# /agency-offer

## Purpose
Walk the user through building a complete agency offer with pricing.

## Skills Required
Load in order: `offer-pricing`, `niche-positioning`

## Step-by-Step Execution

### Step 1: Gather Context
Ask the user:
1. What service(s) will you provide?
2. Who is your target client (niche/industry)?
3. What is your experience level with this service? (beginner / intermediate / expert)
4. Do you have case studies or testimonials? (yes / no / in progress)
5. What do competitors charge for similar services? (if known)
6. How many hours per week will each client require from you?

### Step 2: Bottom-Up Pricing (Shan's Formula)
Calculate:
```
Your hourly rate = Target annual income / 2,080 hours
Time per client/month = Hours/week x 4.33
Your labor cost = Hourly rate x Monthly hours
+ Freelancer/contractor costs
+ Software overhead per client
= Total cost to service
+ 50% margin
= Base price (round up to clean number)
```

### Step 3: Top-Down Market Positioning
Guide through Shan's market research:
1. What do competitors charge? (range)
2. Where are service gaps? (high demand + few providers)
3. Where does the user sit on Dee's positioning grid? (tangibility x customization)

### Step 4: Build the Offer Structure
Choose structure based on experience:
- **Starting out (0 case studies):** Simple base retainer at 50% of competitor rate (Donvesh) -- first client only
- **Building (1-3 case studies):** Base retainer at market rate
- **Established (3+ case studies):** Base + performance component (Shan's 20% profit share model)

### Step 5: Anti-Pattern Warnings
Flag any violations:
- NEVER hourly pricing (Dee: knowledge work is not time-linear)
- NEVER discount -- add sweeteners instead (Dee)
- NEVER money-back guarantee on retainers (Shan)
- NEVER show price in the pitch deck (Shan)
- Contract terms: month-to-month when starting (Donvesh), 3-6 month after proving value (Shan)

### Step 6: Output
Deliver complete offer document:
1. **Service Description:** What you deliver
2. **Base Fee:** Monthly retainer amount with justification
3. **Performance Component:** (if applicable) Revenue share / profit share structure
4. **Contract Term:** Recommended length with rationale
5. **Scope Boundaries:** What is included vs. excluded
6. **Foot-in-Door Option:** Paid audit/strategy session as entry point (Dee's model)
