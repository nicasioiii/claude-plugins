# /agency-sales-prep

## Purpose
Prepare the user for a specific sales call.

## Skills Required
Load in order: `sales-closing`, `offer-pricing`

## Step-by-Step Execution

### Step 1: Gather Prospect Info
Ask the user:
1. Prospect name and company
2. What do they do? (industry, size, revenue if known)
3. How did this lead come in? (hot outreach, cold email, referral, inbound)
4. What problems have you identified?
5. What service would you propose?
6. Is this a first call or second call?
7. What is your target price range for this client?

### Step 2: Pre-Call Research Checklist
Generate tailored research tasks:
- Check their website, social media, content quality
- Look at their competitors (who is doing it better?)
- Check Meta Ads Library for their ad activity (Shan's MVP approach)
- Review any previous correspondence or notes
- Identify 2-3 specific gaps or opportunities you can speak to
- Prepare the ANGLE: what specific thing can you solve? (Shan)

### Step 3: Customized Deck Outline
Generate 8-slide deck structure (Shan's framework):
1. **Cover:** Clean, include their logo
2. **Agency Overview:** 2-3 slides with past clients, key successes
3. **Client Highlight:** Something great about THEIR brand
4. **Problem Discovery:** 2-3 specific issues identified from research
5. **Solution Proposal:** Your plan to tackle each problem
6. **Value-Driven Examples:** Case studies, proof of impact
7. **Risk Mitigation:** How you handle concerns
8. **Process & Timeline:** Roadmap, timelines, touchpoints

Remind: NO price tags in deck. NO generic info. Present multiple approaches if applicable.

### Step 4: Qualifying Questions
Customize based on Donvesh's 7-question framework plus Dee's pull method:
- How have you been handling [service area] until now?
- What results are you currently getting?
- What is your budget range for this type of service?
- What does success look like for you in 90 days?
- Who else is involved in this decision?
- What is your timeline for getting started?
- What has held you back from solving this before?

### Step 5: Objection Preparation
Identify 3-5 likely objections based on prospect type and prepare responses using Dee's Chris Voss techniques:
- **Mirroring:** Repeat back unreasonable requests with upward inflection
- **"How am I supposed to do that?":** Pose it back for them to solve
- **Fairness:** "After all, we just want what is fair"
- **Carrots-or-Peas:** Controlled optionality (two good options, both work for you)

### Step 6: Pricing Recommendation
Based on offer-pricing skill:
- Bottom-up cost calculation for this specific client
- Market positioning recommendation
- Suggested structure: base fee + performance component (if applicable)
- Whether to use foot-in-door paid audit first (Dee's model)

### Step 7: Output
Deliver complete sales prep package:
1. Pre-call research checklist (customized)
2. Deck outline (8 slides, tailored to prospect)
3. Qualifying questions (tailored)
4. Top 5 objections with prepared responses
5. Pricing recommendation with structure
6. Call etiquette reminders (camera setup, join 2 min early, share tab not desktop)
