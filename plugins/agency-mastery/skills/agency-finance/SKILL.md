## Revenue Waterfall (Dee Deng)

```
Revenue
  - Operating Costs (execution team salaries + software)
  - Overhead (non-client-facing staff: CEO, coaches, managers)
  - Miscellaneous (travel, team lunches, L&D, utilities)
  = EBITDA
  - Interest, Tax, Depreciation, Amortization
  = Cash in the bank
```

**Mantra:** "Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. Cash is king."

## Cash Buffer Benchmarks (Dee Deng)

| Level | Buffer | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Bare minimum | 3 months of costs | Survival mode |
| Comfortable | 6 months of costs | Stability |
| Bill Gates (Microsoft) | 12 months | Productive paranoia |
| Wrong answer | 0 months | Danger zone |

Dee and Scott paid themselves NOTHING when starting, to build cash buffer first.

## The Key Metric: Profit Per Team (Dee Deng)

Get this right and everything else clicks. Track:
- Revenue generated per execution team
- Costs per execution team
- Profit per execution team
- Compare across teams to identify optimization opportunities

## Revenue Concentration Risk (Dee Deng)

If one client grows to 20-30-60% of total revenue, you are at extreme risk. Set triggers:
- If any client reaches 20% of revenue, have a meeting
- Actively diversify portfolio
- Manage concentration like stock investors manage diversification

## Over-Servicing Economics (Dee Deng)

12-client capacity team with only 2 clients = tendency to fill time with excessive work. Sets unsustainable precedent. Better: allocate surplus to knowledge base building, SOP creation, experimentation.

## Time Tracking (Dee Deng)

Categories:
- Rev Gen work (directly serving clients)
- Non-Rev Gen work (admin, internal emails)
- Self-development
- Internal meetings
- Training

**[CONTRARIAN]:** Time tracking feels "Big Brother" but explain the WHY. It is about optimization, not surveillance.

## Acquisition Cost Math (Donvesh)

**Rule:** Keep acquisition cost < lifetime value (LTV).

Example for $10K/month prospect:
- Cost of omni-channel to 100 people: $500
- Cost of direct mail to 100 people: $11,000
- Total: $11,500
- Close 3 deals at $10K/month = 3x investment

**Deal size determines strategy:** Small deals ($2-3K) = lean channels. Large deals ($10K+) = invest heavily in acquisition.

## Financial Projections (Dee Deng)

1. Start with conservative case (always)
2. Chart: leads expected -> calls -> proposals -> revenue
3. Track target vs actual every month
4. When actuals deviate, diagnose using KPI chain
5. "Hope is not a strategy" -- always project conservatively
