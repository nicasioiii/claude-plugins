## Agency Types

### Cash Flow Agency (Shan Hanif)
- Designed purely to generate personal income in the short term
- You + freelancers, arbitrage model
- No real brand name, no equity, no exit value
- Key man risk is 100% -- you ARE the agency
- Ceiling: ~$10-20K/month before hitting diminishing returns
- Common trajectory: grow to ceiling, then pivot to info products
- Difficulty: Easy to Medium

### Equity Agency (Shan Hanif)
- Designed to be sold one day for a life-changing exit
- Requires hiring real team members, giving equity, building culture
- Long-term contracts, multiple services, market leadership
- 3-5 years of hardcore focus required
- Branded agencies get ~15x EBITDA multiple at exit
- Difficulty: Hard

**Decision framework:** If you want freedom and income now, start cash flow. If you want to build something larger, commit to equity from day one. You cannot easily switch from cash flow to equity later -- the foundations are different.

## The Agency Triangle (Dee Deng)

Three sides that operate simultaneously (not sequentially):
1. **Acquisition** -- bringing clients in
2. **Fulfillment** -- serving those clients
3. **Finance** -- keeping the money in

Plus two connectors:
- **Systems/Processes** as connective tissue
- **Leadership Clarity** as the driving force

All three sides must be roughly balanced. If acquisition outpaces fulfillment, you over-promise and under-deliver. If fulfillment outpaces acquisition, your best people sit idle and leave. If finance is ignored, you grow into bankruptcy.

## CIGAR Methodology (Dee Deng)

Universal meta-framework for any goal:
- **C** = Current State (where you are now, described in detail)
- **I** = Ideal State (vivid vision of where you want to be)
- **G** = Gap (I minus C = what is missing)
- **A** = Actions (steps to close the gap)
- **R** = Review (assess progress, learn, adjust)

Formula: **I - C = G** (Ideal minus Current equals Gap)

Key principles:
- Works for personal life, business, clients, team members -- universal
- NOT a set-and-forget exercise -- must be reviewed regularly
- Current state changes constantly, so re-assess frequently
- Tony Robbins question: "What will I tolerate no longer in my life?"

## Ikigai (Dee Deng)

Four overlapping circles:
1. What am I good at?
2. What does the world need?
3. What do I love?
4. Where can I extract value / make money?

The intersection = your purpose + value exchange with the market. Use this BEFORE selecting your agency service -- it prevents the trap of choosing a service purely based on what pays the most (Shan's warning: 8 out of 10 agency owners are doing something they are not actually skilled at).

## Skills Audit & Service Selection

### Hard Skills vs. Soft Skills Exercise (Shan Hanif)
- **Hard skills:** Technical know-how (editing, running ads, building websites, coding, data analysis)
- **Soft skills:** People-centered strengths (sales, communication, niche knowledge, trend-spotting, negotiation)
- Task: Write down ALL hard skills and soft skills honestly

### SWOT Analysis for Yourself (Shan Hanif)
- **Strengths:** Skills you already possess forming your competitive edge
- **Weaknesses:** Honest gaps (corporate language, trust issues, product design)
- **Opportunities:** Skills you can learn in 2-3 months that complement strengths (AI tools, CRO, new markets)
- **Threats:** Things that could make your skills obsolete (new tech, competitors, platform changes)

### Service Selection Framework -- 4 Requirements (Shan Hanif)
1. Service must be based on your ACTUAL skills
2. You must be genuinely interested and experienced as a consumer of that service
3. There must be clear market demand visible in the real world
4. You can easily find competitors doing exactly what you want to do (proof of concept)

**[CONTRARIAN] Shan Hanif:** You cannot make ice cream for others if you have never eaten ice cream. You should not provide a service you cannot do yourself at a base level.

### Skill Development Cycle (Shan Hanif)
Threat (e.g., AI editing) -> Turn into opportunity (learn AI tools) -> Dedicate 2-3 months -> Turn into strength -> Sell it

## Core Values Creation Process (Dee Deng)

Right Hook's process:
1. Founders brainstorm: "How we want to be" vs "How we do NOT want to be"
2. Eliminate and consolidate into V1
3. Communicate to team, gather feedback
4. Test resonance over several quarters
5. Use in recruitment (interview for values alignment)
6. Include in performance reviews
7. Use peer recognition at meetings to reinforce

**[CONTRARIAN] Dee Deng:** First version was too inside-baseball. Mission statement "Growth or Death" initially caused discomfort but became galvanizing. Mission should be in plain English, can be aspirational, and should guide every decision.

## Vivid Vision Exercise (Dee Deng)

From Cameron Harold's book:
- Put on a metaphorical VR headset projecting 3 years into the future
- Write what that future looks, feels, sounds like
- Cover: relationships, financial security, team composition, client types, lifestyle
- NO censorship or editing during the exercise
- Do it once, then revisit 2-3 days later for a second pass
- Repeat every ~3 months to check if vision still resonates
- Personal version informs company version

## Finding Your Why (Dee Deng)

**[CONTRARIAN] Dee Deng:** Initially thought "Start with Why" was a waste of time, wanted to skip to closing clients. Got punished when things went well but had no purpose to hold people together during hard times.

- Your personal why informs: company mission, values, hiring decisions, client selection, point of difference
- Not having a why = everyone is only there for money = they leave when money stops flowing
- "Companies are just long shadows of their founders"

## Foundational Principles

### Core Rules (All Instructors Agree)
- An agency is one of the HARDEST businesses because you depend on other people (Shan)
- The first 30 days sets the tone for the entire client relationship (Shan)
- Your first-phase end goal is getting your first case study/testimonial, not revenue (Donvesh)
- Manually onboard first 3-5 clients before building systems (Donvesh)
- After getting first client, stop outreach for 45-60 days to focus on delivery (Donvesh)

### [CONTRARIAN] Shan Hanif: Most agency education is from failed agency owners
Pattern: start agency > struggle 6 months > pivot to teaching > make more from courses than the agency. If someone is teaching agency but does not currently run one at scale, their advice is suspect.

### [CONTRARIAN] Shan Hanif: The "offer" obsession is wrong
Had never even heard the word "offer" until 6 months before recording. It has always been: provide a service for a price. Stop trying to craft "irresistible offers" and instead become genuinely good at what you do.

## Agency Evolution Stages (Shan Hanif)

| Stage | Revenue | Clients | Team | Key Focus |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Freelancer | $0-10K/month | 1-5 | Solo | Build skills, get case studies |
| 2. Side Hustle | Variable | Small | Solo + VA | Test market, build audience |
| 3. Branded (freelancers) | $10-20K/month | 5-10 | 5-10 remote | One service, systematize |
| 4. Branded (team) | Up to $100K/month | 10+ | Hired experts | Multiple services, long-term clients |
| 5. Market Leader | $15-50K/client/month | 100+ | 100+ team | Industry leadership, equity, 2-year contracts |

### Revenue Benchmarks (Genflow)
- Year 1-3 (2016-2018): Cash flow phase
- 2022: $12M revenue
- 2023: $19M revenue
- 2024: On track for $30M
- Cumulative 8-year revenue: ~$100M
- Premium contracts: $15K-$50K/month, 2-year fixed terms

## The 7 Deadly Sins of Agency Owners (Dee Deng)

1. **Not recognizing your worth** -- treating yourself as a utility
2. **Over-promising** -- "Don't worry, it'll be fine" delays pain, creates misalignment
3. **Not delegating** -- you cannot conduct the orchestra while playing every instrument
4. **Not hiring smarter** -- hiring slightly less capable creates cascading mediocrity
5. **Not investing in self-development** -- higher quality problems require higher quality minds
6. **Over-servicing** -- 2 clients getting 12-client capacity of attention sets unsustainable precedent
7. **Skipping purpose and culture** -- without it, people are only there for money
