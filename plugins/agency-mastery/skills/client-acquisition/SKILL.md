## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: Outreach Style

- **Shan Hanif:** Use ALL 6 channels simultaneously; hot outreach is most important when starting
- **Dee Deng:** Sample platter outreach (spec work for dream clients); community-first personal branding
- **Donvesh:** CTA escalation ladder; fake/real video audits; omni-channel by deal size; cold calling for high-value

**Resolution:** At $0-10K/month, prioritize manual personalized outreach (all three agree). At $10K+, layer on automation and paid channels. Donvesh's deal-size framework is the best guide for WHICH channels to combine. Dee's spec-work approach and Donvesh's video audit approach are both high-conversion variants of the same principle: give value before asking.

## Master Framework: The 6 Acquisition Channels (Shan Hanif)

1. **Inbound - Personal Brand:** Content on social media that attracts leads
2. **Inbound - Landing Pages/Funnels/VSLs:** Conversion assets that turn traffic into calls
3. **Outbound - Hot Outreach:** Manual, personalized outreach to targeted prospects
4. **Outbound - Cold Automated Outreach:** Automated email sequences to lead lists
5. **Paid Ads + Email Marketing:** Running ads to drive leads into your funnel
6. **Referrals:** Existing clients referring new business (earned, not bought)

### Channel Selection by Stage
- **$0/month (starting):** Hot outreach + personal brand content (free, effort-based)
- **$10K/month:** Add cold automated outreach + landing page/VSL
- **$20K/month+:** Add paid ads, scale all channels

### 5 Mistakes to Avoid (Shan)
1. Relying on only ONE channel
2. Not following up (most deals close on follow-up 2-5)
3. Pitching too early before understanding problems
4. Copy-pasting generic messages
5. Thinking a single tactic will solve everything

## Hot Outreach (Shan Hanif)

Most important channel when starting. Requires research and customization for every prospect. Combine EMAIL + DM + COMMENT (hit them on 3 channels).

### 3-Section Email Template

**Section 1 -- Peak Interest:**
- State the problem you found
- Mention something not obvious (shows you did research)
- Personal connection (shared interest, recent event)

**Section 2 -- The Value:**
- Give the answer FOR FREE in the email
- Specific, actionable insight they can use immediately
- Re-edited content, analysis, or data point

**Section 3 -- About You + CTA:**
- Brief intro: who you are, what you have done
- Always end with a QUESTION (never "let me know" or "let's get a call")
- Good CTAs: "Have you considered X?" / "What is your current strategy for Y?"

### Lead Qualification Criteria (Genflow Example)
- Is the person real? (not meme/review account)
- Minimum follower/revenue threshold
- Relevant audience demographics
- Active across multiple platforms
- Growth rate positive

## Follow-Up Strategy (Shan Hanif)

- **Day 2:** Continuation of the same messaging angle
- **Day 5:** NEW angle/message (compose fresh email)
- **Day 10:** Continuation of Day 5's angle

**Follow-up angle options:** Problem -> Solution / Opportunity -> Money / Personal connection -> Trust / Competitor comparison -> You can do this too

**NEVER say:** "Just following up" / "Did you get a chance" / "Just wanted to know if interested"

**[CONTRARIAN] Shan:** Hot outreach is about planting seeds, not instant conversion. People come back months later when they need your service.

## Sample Platter Tactic (Dee Deng)

1. Build list of dream clients (Instagram, LinkedIn)
2. Find the DECISION MAKER (marketing manager, not customer service)
3. Create spec work samples for their specific brand
4. Send ONE sample with message: "I loved your brand, created this. I have 4-5 more if you'd like."
5. Open loop gets them to reply -> warm conversation -> discovery call
6. Keep pace consistent (1-5/day, not 50 in one day)
7. Make them feel SPECIAL, not just receiving utility

## Video Audits (Donvesh)

### Real Video Audit (11 Steps)
Use Loom or Vidyard, keep under 4 minutes, show your face. See ref_client_acquisition.md for full script.

### Fake Video Audit Method
Screenshot prospect's homepage, overlay play button image. In email, act as if sending a real video. When lead clicks and finds no video, interested ones reply asking to resend. These replies are VERY WARM -- they are actively requesting content.

**Results:** 17 calls from 86 leads (19.8% call booking rate) with personalized video audits.

## Channel Selection by Deal Size (Donvesh)

| Deal Size | Channels |
|---|---|
| $2K-$3K/month | 1-2 channels, experiment |
| $5K-$10K/month | 2-3 channels (email + LinkedIn + video audit) |
| $10K+/month | Omni-channel (email + LinkedIn + video audit + cold call + direct mail) |

### By Target Type
- **B2B:** LinkedIn + cold email + cold calling
- **Local businesses:** Outbound calls (LinkedIn and cold email less effective)
- **High-value ($10K+):** Handwritten notes + direct mail + video audits + calls + LinkedIn

## Personal Branding (Shan Hanif)

### Content Pillars
- Your expertise/craft (how you do what you do)
- Client wins and case studies (with permission)
- Behind-the-scenes of agency life
- Industry insights and trends
- Personal stories and lessons learned

### Platform Strategy
- **LinkedIn:** Highest-intent for B2B agency leads
- **YouTube:** Builds strongest authority and trust
- **Instagram:** Reels for reach, carousels for engagement
- **Email newsletter:** Direct relationship, not algorithm-dependent

## Community as Conversion (Dee Deng)

Facebook group or Discord for prospects not yet ready to buy. Answer questions, do monthly town halls. Creates closed loop back into pipeline. Many eventually return and become clients.

## Paid Ads for Lead Gen (Shan Hanif)

### Ad Grid Template
Matrix: Personas (columns) x Angles (rows)
- Personas: New business owner, Established brand, Agency owner
- Angles: Case study/testimonials, Uncertainty, Frustration

### Lead Magnet Types
| Type | Use Case |
|---|---|
| Webinar | High purchase intent, authority, strategy calls |
| Free Training | Longer conversion window, extended nurture |
| Instant Resource | Mass leads at lowest cost per lead |

### Key Rule
90% of marketing = top-of-funnel prospecting. Quality of lead magnets drives success.

## Reply Rate Benchmarks (Donvesh)

| Method | Target Reply Rate |
|---|---|
| Minimum viable | 15-20% |
| Comfortable range | 20-30% |
| Unscalable methods (video audits, manual) | 40-60% |

### Testing Protocol
- Test open rate in batches of 20 leads (target: 85% open rate)
- Test reply rate in batches of 75-100 leads (target: 15-20%)

### 3 Factors Driving Replies
1. **CTA** (most important)
2. **Volume** (number of outreach messages)
3. **Personalization, angle, and script**
