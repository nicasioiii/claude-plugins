# Client Acquisition Reference

## DM Framework Templates (Shan Hanif)

### Rules for DMs
1. Never sell in the first DM
2. Never ask for a call in the first DM
3. Do not send a video straight up
4. Use their name
5. Make it obviously not automated
6. Actually put effort into it

### Red Flags (Never Say These)
- Done-for-you / DFY / DWY
- Pay-on-results
- We will pay you
- Guarantees
- Get X results in Y days

### Framework 1: Open Loop
```
Hey {first name},
Seen {brand}, love {something relevant}.
I looked through {some problem} and think can improve it by {insert value}.
What's your current plan on {something re problem}?
```

### Framework 2: Example Work
```
Hey {first name},
Seen {brand}, love {something relevant}.
I looked through {some problem} and think you can improve it by {insert value}.
I am X and do this for XYZ.
Got my team to do some examples, what's the best email to send it to?
```

### Framework 3: Lead Magnet
```
Hey {first name},
Been following your {brand} for a while, love XYZ.
I looked through {problem} and think you can improve it by {insert value}.
I have put together a simple cheatsheet (for free), you can get it here {link}.
```

### Framework 4: Just Conversation
```
Hey {first name},
Have a question.
{insert an actual relevant question to start a conversation}
```

### CTA Mastery
Always end with a QUESTION, not a statement.
- BAD: "Let me know" / "Let's get a call" / "Check this out"
- GOOD: "What do you think of your short form content?" / "What's your current strategy for X?"

## Cold Email Sequence Cadence (Shan Hanif)

### Subject Line Variations
- {{company_name}} <> [Your Agency]
- Scaling framework
- Team of {{Headcount}}
- Your work with {{Client_name}}

### Sequence
- **Email 1:** Initial value-based outreach (lead with insight, not pitch)
- **Email 2 (day 3-5):** Different angle, same value proposition
- **Email 3 (day 7-10):** Direct resource/link with free content
- **Subsequence (if engaged):** Send resource, mention limited availability, offer consultation

## VSL Structures

### Detailed VSL (15-20 min -- Shan Hanif)
1. Hook: grab attention immediately
2. Overview: brief on what is coming
3. Introduction: who you are, why agency exists
4. Success to date: past results for credibility
5. Pain points: specific problems target audience faces
6. Deliver VALUE: tangible, relevant insights
7. More proof: case studies, testimonials
8. Pain points (revisited): circle back for urgency
9. Call to action: book a call

### Simple VSL (Shorter -- Shan Hanif)
1. Hook: strong opening
2. What we do: high-level overview
3. How we do it: brief process
4. Who we serve: types of clients
5. How we can help you: benefits and results
6. Call to action: book a call

### Donvesh VSL Structure
1. Short intro mentioning painful problem (relatable)
2. Explain the problem well (builds trust)
3. Tell alternative solutions that are NOT yours
4. Talk about YOUR solution
5. Explain in detail
6. CTA: Book a call
7. Use Google Slides, record with Loom

## Video Audit Script -- 11 Steps (Donvesh)

Use Loom or Vidyard. Keep under 4 minutes. Show your face.

1. Introduce yourself and thank the viewer
2. First 100 seconds on THEIR content/business
3. Talk about what they are doing RIGHT
4. Talk about what they are doing WRONG or could improve
5. Talk about the IMPLICATIONS of what they are doing wrong
6. Show them you have a better system + show results
7. Show what the competition is doing (that they are not)
8. Show your system/approach
9. Give 3 actionable steps they can use right now
10. Talk about your BIG promise
11. Pitch a call if they need help with implementation

Bonus: edit the audit document and give it as a cheatsheet.

### Fake Video Audit Method (Donvesh)
1. Screenshot the homepage of prospect's website
2. Overlay a play button image (from Google) using Figma/Canva
3. Embed in email as if sending a real video
4. Interested leads reply asking to resend the video
5. Add respondents to Google Sheet, tag in GoHighLevel
6. Follow up with actual video audit or pitch call

### Results
Campaign with personalized video audits: 17 calls from 86 leads (19.8% booking rate). VA cost: ~$5 per 3-minute video.

## Follow-Up Sequence Template (Shan Hanif)

| Day | Action | Angle |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | Initial outreach | Problem -> Value -> CTA |
| Day 2 | Continue Day 0 angle | Add more detail or proof |
| Day 5 | NEW angle | Different hook entirely |
| Day 10 | Continue Day 5 angle | Final push with new data |

### 3-Month Follow-Up (Donvesh)
Last email gives 3 options:
1. "I don't need this service"
2. "I have already hired someone"
3. "Follow up with me in 3 months / 60 days"

If option 3: actually follow up. If too many "already hired": you are late to market. If too many "don't need": revisit niche/audience research.

## CTA Escalation Ladder (Donvesh)

From most scalable to least:
1. **Direct pitch for call:** "Are you free for a quick call Friday?"
2. **Lead magnet:** 9-page PDF with service, case studies, results
3. **Audits:** Free consultation call or fake video audits
4. **Conversation:** SDRs/VAs have actual email exchanges, pitch call after 4-8 messages

## Email Deliverability Quick Reference (Donvesh)

- Open rate below 65% = deliverability problem
- Set up DKIM, SPF, DMARC
- Warmup with Lemwarm ($29/month) -- not cheap $5 tools
- Verify all leads through NeverBounce (target <1% bounce)
- Sending limits per domain/day: text only 50-60, with images 25, with video 15
- Multiple domains needed for scale; use GlockApps for monitoring

**Note:** For deep cold email infrastructure setup, refer to the cold-outbound-mastery plugin.

## Direct Mail & Gifts -- $10K+ Deals (Donvesh)

### ROI Math
If CLTV per client is $500K, you can afford to send an iPhone to 100 leads.
- Cost of omni-channel to 100 people: ~$500
- Cost of direct mail gifts to 100 people: ~$11,000
- Total: ~$11,500
- Close 3 deals at $10K/month = 3x your investment

### Rule
Keep acquisition cost < lifetime value.

### Gift Ideas
AirPods, handwritten notes, cards, Kindles, plants, anything creative that nobody else is doing.
