## 5 Pillars of Account Management (Shan Hanif)

1. **Communication & Touchpoints:** Timely, consistent, proactive, appropriate
2. **Consulting:** Thought-through, problem-solving, identifying opportunities
3. **Delivery:** Project management, follow-through, execution
4. **Reporting:** Consistent, insightful, value-add
5. **Relationship:** Trust, rapport, influence

### 5 Lessons from Managing 100+ Clients (Shan)
1. You cannot communicate too much
2. Good account management is one of the most valuable agency skills
3. The first 30 days sets the tone for everything
4. Great account management = ability to problem-solve in REAL TIME with a client
5. Manage well and you will never lose a client

## Communication Principles (Shan Hanif)

1. **PROACTIVE, not reactive:** Send updates before they are requested
2. **RESPONSIVE, not unresponsive:** Set and police SLAs
3. **POSITIVE, not draining:** Clients should look forward to hearing from you
4. **EFFECTIVE, cut to the point:** Solve the problem, give context, outline options, recommend

### Communication SLAs
| Channel | Response Time |
|---|---|
| Slack | Within 2 hours |
| WhatsApp | Within 2 hours |
| Email | Within 24 hours |
| Meeting minutes | Same day or next morning |

**Key stat:** 70% of client issues arise from poor communication. 70% of those can be resolved with strong communication skills.

## Weekly Cadence Framework (Shan Hanif)

| Frequency | Coverage | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Daily | Comms responses, delivery management, fire-fighting | Ad hoc via Slack |
| Weekly | Status updates, plan for week, next actions | Video call or email |
| Monthly | Top-line performance, goals, financials, 3-month strategy | Meeting + report |

## Client Meeting Framework -- 5 Pillars (Shan Hanif)

1. **Prep:** Ideal outcome? Materials? Who attends? Align on narrative before.
2. **Opening:** Context, rapport, agenda, housekeeping from last meeting.
3. **Facilitation:** Time management, discussion guidance, decision capture.
4. **Closing:** AOB, follow-ups, actions with owners and timings.
5. **Follow-ups:** Email same day with actions, owners, timings, decisions.

## Monthly Report Template -- 8 Sections (Shan Hanif)

1. Business Scorecard (spend, revenue, CPA, ROAS, performance rating)
2. Summary Performance Commentary
3. Combined Business Performance (charts)
4. Funnel Performance (conversion metrics)
5. Channel Performance (platform-specific + best assets)
6. Secondary Channel Performance
7. Content/Creative Performance
8. Next Month (goals, operations actions, strategic priorities)

### Reporting Principles
- Input metrics (actions) + Output metrics (results)
- Compare vs YTD average (exclude outliers)
- Industry benchmarks where relevant
- Translate metrics to financial equivalents
- Format so client can forward/share (make them look good)
- Report size of MISSED opportunity from unimplemented recommendations

## Testimonials (Donvesh)

### Type 1: Interview Style (10-20 minutes)
Get on Zoom, ask questions that handle common prospect objections:
- "How was the quality of leads?"
- "How much ROI per month?"
- "What about our transparency and communication?"
Record on Zoom, upload to YouTube.

### Type 2: Selfie Video (60-180 seconds)
Client records selfie answering 1 objection. Must include:
1. Name, business model, niche
2. Position BEFORE working with you
3. Position AFTER working with you
Use Testimonial.to to collect, host, and embed.

**[CONTRARIAN] Donvesh:** Get DIFFERENT types from EACH client to cover different objections.

## Transparency = Retention (Donvesh)

The more transparent you are with clients, the better retention and stronger testimonials. Share KPI dashboards, be honest about challenges, proactive about solutions.

## Over-Servicing Warning (Dee Deng)

2 clients getting 12-client capacity of attention sets an unsustainable precedent. Better: allocate surplus time to knowledge base building, SOP creation, experimentation. Time expands to fill available capacity.
