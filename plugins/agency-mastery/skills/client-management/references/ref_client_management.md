# Client Management Reference

## Communication SLA Table (Shan Hanif)

| Channel | Response Time | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Slack | Within 2 hours | Notifications always ON |
| WhatsApp | Within 2 hours | Check 2x daily on weekends/holidays |
| Email | Within 24 hours | Acknowledge receipt ASAP |
| Meeting minutes | Same day or next morning | If EOD meeting |

## Weekly Cadence Detail (Shan Hanif)

### Daily
- Respond to all Slack messages within SLA
- Monitor client delivery boards
- Flag any blockers or fire-fighting needs
- Quick internal sync if issues arise

### Weekly
- Video call or written status update covering:
  - What was accomplished this week
  - Plan for next week
  - Any decisions needed from client
  - Next meeting date and agenda preview

### Monthly
- 60-minute strategy meeting covering:
  - Top-line performance vs targets
  - Last month assessment (what worked, what did not)
  - Goals and priorities for next month
  - Financials review (if relevant)
  - Next 3 months strategic outlook

## Meeting Framework -- 5 Pillars Detail (Shan Hanif)

### Pillar 1: Prep
- What is the ideal outcome of this meeting?
- How do you get the client there?
- Materials: Agenda (sent in advance), client input, pre-read, slides
- Team: Who needs to be there? Role of each (presenting, minutes)
- Align on narrative before the meeting

### Pillar 2: Opening
- Context: Intros, timings, strategic context
- Rapport: Ask them something, give them something
- Agenda: Objectives and expected outcomes
- Housekeeping: Review agreements from last meeting

### Pillar 3: Facilitation
- Time management (stay on track)
- Discussion guidance (keep it productive)
- Decision capture (document decisions in real-time)

### Pillar 4: Closing
- AOB (any other business)?
- Follow-ups: Discussions to pick up separately
- Actions: Clear owners and timings for everything

### Pillar 5: Follow-ups
- Send email same day or next morning
- Include: actions, owners, timings, decisions made
- Attach any materials discussed

## Monthly Report Template -- 8 Sections (Shan Hanif)

### Section 1: Business Scorecard
| Metric | This Month | Last Month | Change | Target | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Spend | $X | $X | +/-% | $X | Good/Fair/Low |
| Revenue | $X | $X | +/-% | $X | Good/Fair/Low |
| CPA | $X | $X | +/-% | $X | Good/Fair/Low |
| ROAS | X.Xx | X.Xx | +/-% | X.Xx | Good/Fair/Low |

### Section 2: Summary Commentary
4-part structure:
1. What happened (factual)
2. Why it happened (analysis)
3. What we are doing about it (action)
4. What we recommend (strategic)

### Example Commentary (Shan)
"[Month] was a [good/challenging] month for our campaigns, delivering [headline metric]. This was driven by [cause]. We [maintained/improved] [supporting metric], giving confidence we can [next step]. [Concerning metric] needs to become our goal for [next month]."

### Section 3: Combined Performance
Charts showing trends over last 3-6 months (revenue, CPA, ROAS, conversion)

### Section 4: Funnel Performance
Impressions -> Clicks -> Leads -> Conversions with rates between each

### Section 5: Primary Channel
Platform-specific metrics + screenshot of best-performing asset with explanation

### Section 6: Secondary Channel
Supporting channels (social, email, content) with key metrics

### Section 7: Creative/Content Performance
Top 3 performers with why they worked. Include visual examples.

### Section 8: Next Month
- Revenue/metric goals
- Operations actions: What | Who | When
- Strategic priorities for the quarter

## Testimonial Collection Templates (Donvesh)

### Interview Questions (Zoom, 10-20 min)
1. Tell us about your business and what you were struggling with before working with us.
2. How was the quality of [deliverable] that you got from us?
3. How much ROI did you get per month from our service?
4. What do you think about our transparency and communication?
5. How many [results] did you get from working with us?
6. Would you recommend us to other businesses? Why?

### Selfie Video Instructions (Send to Client)
"Record a 60-180 second selfie video covering:
1. Your name, business model, and niche
2. Where you were BEFORE working with us
3. Where you are AFTER working with us
Upload to [Testimonial.to link]"

### When to Ask
- After a major win or milestone
- After positive feedback in a meeting
- At the 90-day mark (enough results to speak to)
- Before contract renewal

## Client Churn Warning Signs Checklist (Shan Hanif + Donvesh)

Early detection prevents cancellation. Review weekly for every active client.

| Warning Sign | Severity | Immediate Action |
|---|---|---|
| Client stops replying to Slack/email within SLA | High | Escalate -- personal call from account lead within 24h |
| Missed or cancelled weekly call two weeks in a row | High | Send re-engagement email; propose agenda reset |
| Negative tone shift in written messages | Medium | Schedule face-to-face video check-in |
| Client starts asking for deliverables outside scope | Medium | Revisit agreement; propose upsell or scope clarification |
| Decision-maker delegates all comms to junior contact | Medium | Request quarterly strategy session with decision-maker |
| No engagement with monthly report (no questions, no feedback) | Medium | Follow up with commentary highlights and direct question |
| Client mentions competitor or "exploring options" | Critical | Escalate to senior leadership; prepare retention offer |
| Results trending below target for 2+ consecutive months | High | Proactive strategy pivot meeting with revised roadmap |
| Invoice payment delayed beyond terms | Medium | Finance follow-up + account lead check-in on satisfaction |
| Client asks for contract terms or cancellation policy | Critical | Same-day call; address root concern before discussing terms |

## Client Health Dashboard KPI Template (Donvesh)

Transparency drives retention. Share KPIs with clients via Notion or equivalent dashboard.

| KPI Category | Metrics to Track | Update Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| Performance | Primary KPI (leads, revenue, ROAS), secondary KPI (CPA, CTR, CVR) | Weekly |
| Delivery | Tasks completed vs planned, on-time delivery rate | Weekly |
| Communication | Response time compliance, meetings held vs scheduled | Monthly |
| Satisfaction | NPS or informal pulse (1-10 rating at monthly review) | Monthly |
| Financial | Spend vs budget, invoicing status | Monthly |

### Dashboard Rules (Donvesh)
- Could keep KPIs in a private team-only database, but sharing with the client is always better
- The more transparent you are, the stronger the retention and testimonials
- Open live dashboards on calls instead of using static screenshots in slides

## Communication Principles -- 4 Rules (Shan Hanif)

1. **Proactive, not reactive:** If the client is chasing you, there is already a problem. Send updates before they are requested.
2. **Responsive, not unresponsive:** Clients hate being ignored. Set and police communication SLAs.
3. **Positive, not draining:** Clients should look forward to hearing from you.
4. **Effective, cut to the point:** Solve the problem, address the issue, answer the question. Give context, explain process, outline options, give your recommendation.

### Key Stat (Shan)
- 70% of client issues arise from poor communication
- 70% of those issues can be resolved with strong communication skills
- Common pitfalls: lack of responsiveness, inadequate follow-up, failing to explain clearly, not addressing concerns, not updating on changes
