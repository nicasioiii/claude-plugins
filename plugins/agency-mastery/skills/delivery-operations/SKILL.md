## SOP Framework (Shan Hanif)

### SOP Types
- **Handbooks:** Information, instructions, policies (slides or long-form docs)
- **Visual SOPs:** Flowcharts, checklists (Miro boards)
- **Video SOPs:** Loom recordings walking through processes

### SOP Rollout Framework (6 Steps -- Shan)
1. **Identify** need for SOP
2. **Assign owner** -- who owns it
3. **Draft** -- owner creates SOP
4. **Email** -- distribute to affected people, update library
5. **Live session** (if significant) -- demo to team
6. **Govern** -- owner ensures process is followed

**Critical rules:** Only roll out 1-2 new SOPs at a time. Too many at once = execution suffers.

### SOP Template Ingredients (Dee Deng)
1. Last updated date + who updated
2. Average execution time
3. Goal: what are we trying to achieve?
4. Ideal outcome: what does success look like?
5. Dependencies/prerequisites
6. Context: why is this important?
7. Where: in what tool/platform?
8. When: SLA terms, after what preceding step?
9. Who (RACINDA): Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed, Next steps, Dates, Asana

## Function Chart (Dee Deng)

**[CONTRARIAN]:** Start with FUNCTION chart, not org chart. Org charts start with people's names (creates bias). Function charts start with what needs to get done.

Even as a 1-person team, you are juggling: strategic leadership, finance, operations, admin, payroll, client services, sales, marketing. Seeing all functions helps identify where to get help first.

### Layout Principles
- Top to bottom: seniority
- Left to right: decentralized/autonomous (left) to centralized/controlled (right)
- Centralized: payroll, bookkeeping, legals, data management
- Decentralized: creative work, innovation, strategy

### PSIU Framework (Dee Deng)
For each function, identify the dominant trait needed:
- **P**roducer: "How soon can we start?"
- **S**tabilizer: "What do we need before we start?"
- **I**nnovator: "Is this really the best way?"
- **U**nifier: "Let's do this together."

CEO role = iU (Innovation + Unification, not Production or Stabilization).

## Matrix Management (Dee Deng)

### Right Hook Structure
Multiple execution teams, each with: Strategist (captain), Media Buyer, Visual Creator, Copywriter, Client Liaison. Each team has its own client portfolio.

Department heads (Head of Client Performance, Operations, Media Buying, Creative) do NOT manage client work. They COACH execution team members.

**Critical principle:** "Every time I take a problem away from you, I am weakening you. Coach through problems, do not solve them."

## Priority SOPs -- Top 5 to Build First (Dee Deng)

1. **Discovery call agenda** (questions + pricing discussion + proposal mention)
2. **Prospect fit criteria** (ideal level + acceptable level filters)
3. **New client onboarding** (consistent experience, pre-flight checklist)
4. **Client meeting agenda** (wins -> challenges -> strategies -> Q&A -> close high)
5. **Client reporting** (standardized format, how to present data)

**Prioritization rule:** Start with SOPs that are (1) most commonly occurring AND (2) flow the most value to the customer.

**[CONTRARIAN] Dee Deng:** Too many SOPs kill creativity. Too few kill consistency. Systems exist to SERVE you. The moment you start serving them, you have gone too far.

## Centralized vs. Creative (Dee Deng)
- Safety, compliance, routine = centralized SOPs
- Creative, innovative, multi-decision = decision trees, coaching, loose structure

## Process Library (Shan Hanif)
Structure: Department > Function > Category > Process > Subprocess
Fields: Last Updated, Owner, Action Needed, SOP link, Loom link, Visual link

## Knowledge Management (Dee Deng)
Self-serve LMS with video modules. Company wiki by department. Archived calls. Subject matter experts build their own knowledge bases. Department read access across teams.
