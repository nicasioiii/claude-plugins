# Delivery Operations Reference

## SOP Template (Dee Deng)

### Header
- SOP Title: ________
- Last Updated: ________ by ________
- Average Execution Time: ________

### Body
1. **Goal:** What are we trying to achieve?
2. **Ideal Outcome:** What does success look like?
3. **Dependencies:** What must happen first?
4. **Context:** Why is this important? What cannot happen if this does not?
5. **Where:** In what tool/platform?
6. **When:** SLA terms, after what preceding step?
7. **Who (RACINDA):**
   - R = Responsible (can be multiple)
   - A = Accountable (ONE person)
   - C = Consulted (stakeholders with veto)
   - I = Informed (need to know, no authority)
   - N = Next steps (explicitly documented)
   - D = Dates (owned or tracked)
   - A = Asana (put in project management)

### Steps
- Step 1: ________
- Step 2: ________
- Step 3: ________

### Quality Check
- [ ] Output matches ideal outcome
- [ ] All RACINDA roles notified
- [ ] Next steps assigned with dates

## Function Chart Layout (Dee Deng)

```
                    STRATEGIC LEADERSHIP
                           |
    +-----------+-----------+-----------+-----------+
    |           |           |           |           |
  SALES    CLIENT SVCS  OPERATIONS   FINANCE    MARKETING
    |           |           |           |
  Lead Gen  Onboarding  SOP Mgmt    Invoicing
  Closing   Delivery    PM          Payroll
  CRM       Reporting   HR          Budgeting
```

- Left side = decentralized/autonomous
- Right side = centralized/controlled
- Each box = a function, not a person
- One person can fill multiple functions (especially early on)
- As you grow, functions get dedicated people

## Matrix Management Team Structure (Dee Deng)

### Execution Team (per portfolio)
| Role | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| Strategist (Captain) | Client strategy, team coordination |
| Media Buyer | Campaign execution, optimization |
| Visual Creator | Creative assets, design |
| Copywriter | Ad copy, email copy, content |
| Client Liaison | Communication, reporting |

### Department Heads (coaching, not managing)
| Head | Coaches |
|---|---|
| Head of Client Performance | Strategists across all teams |
| Head of Media Buying | Media buyers across all teams |
| Head of Creative | Visual creators and copywriters |
| Head of Operations | Client liaisons, process |

Department heads do NOT manage client work. They coach and develop their discipline.

## Project Board Template (Shan Hanif -- Asana)

### Client Delivery Board
Columns: Backlog | In Progress | Review | Client Approval | Done

Each task includes:
- Assignee
- Due date
- Description
- Subtasks (if complex)
- Priority tag
- Client name tag

### Team Management Board
- Weekly capacity review: who has bandwidth, who is overloaded
- Prioritize client-facing deadlines over internal tasks
- Give teams autonomy over daily task order, but own priorities

## Top 5 SOPs Detailed (Dee Deng)

### 1. Discovery Call Agenda
- Demographic questions (revenue, team size, current approach)
- Psychographic notes (collaborative? realistic expectations?)
- Pricing discussion framework
- Proposal mention and next steps

### 2. Prospect Fit Criteria
- Ideal level filters (revenue, niche, ad spend, collaborative)
- Acceptable level filters (minimum thresholds)
- Disqualification triggers (too small, adversarial, unrealistic)

### 3. New Client Onboarding
- Pre-flight checklist (all access, assets, agreements)
- Consistent experience regardless of team member
- Timeline from signing to first deliverable

### 4. Client Meeting Agenda
- Recent wins (start positive)
- Current challenges (honest assessment)
- Strategies for overcoming (collaborative problem-solving)
- Q&A (address concerns)
- Close on a high note (next steps, optimism)

### 5. Client Reporting
- Standardized format (same structure every time)
- How to present data (input + output metrics)
- How to tell a story with data (not just numbers)
- Commentary template (what happened, why, what next)

## Knowledge Management System (Dee Deng -- "The Dojo")

### Structure
- Self-serve LMS with video modules by topic
- Company wiki with department-specific knowledge bases
- Archived calls for reference
- Subject matter experts build their own sections

### Access
- Department read access across departments (cross-pollination)
- Write access limited to subject matter experts
- Regular content reviews for freshness

### Goal
Taiichi Ohno's "The Wise Company" -- organizational learning that is not dependent on any one person.
