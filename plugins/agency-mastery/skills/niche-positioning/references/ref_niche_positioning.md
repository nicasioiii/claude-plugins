## Audience Research Question Templates

### For Paid Expert Consultations ($200/hour -- Donvesh)
1. What are the top 3 problems businesses in [niche] face with [service area]?
2. What solutions have they tried that did not work?
3. What would an ideal solution look like?
4. How much do they typically spend on this?
5. Who makes the buying decision -- founder, marketing manager, someone else?
6. What objections come up most when pitching this type of service?
7. What makes them trust or distrust an agency?
8. What results would make them stay with an agency for 12+ months?

### For Free Research (Reddit/Quora -- Donvesh)
- Search: "[niche] + agency + problems"
- Search: "[niche] + marketing + frustrated"
- Search: "[niche] + hired + agency + bad experience"
- Look for: recurring complaints, unmet needs, budget discussions

### For 2nd-Degree Research (Donvesh)
Questions to ask your client's clients:
1. How do you find [niche service providers]?
2. What made you choose the one you are using now?
3. What would make you switch?
4. How much do you spend on this per month/year?
5. What is your biggest frustration with current providers?

## TAM Validation Checklist

- [ ] Can you identify 100+ potential prospects matching your IACP? (minimum viable TAM)
- [ ] Are there at least 5 competitors successfully serving this niche? (proof of demand)
- [ ] Is the niche growing or stable? (avoid shrinking markets)
- [ ] Can prospects afford your target price point? (revenue band check)
- [ ] Can you reach them through 2+ acquisition channels?
- [ ] Is the niche large enough that losing a few clients does not threaten your business?

**If any are NO:** Consider broadening your niche definition or testing a different vertical.

## Positioning Grid Application (Dee Deng / Jeffrey Liker)

### Map Your Position

```
                    TANGIBLE DELIVERABLE
                           |
    Productized     -------|------- Customized
    (Routine)              |       (Personalized)
                           |
                  INTANGIBLE EXPERIENCE
```

### Quadrant Descriptions

| Position | Description | Pricing Power | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Routine + Tangible | Commodity service, fixed deliverable | Lowest | Basic logo design package |
| Routine + Intangible | Productized experience | Low-Medium | Monthly reporting service |
| Custom + Tangible | Bespoke deliverables | Medium-High | Custom website build |
| Custom + Intangible | Deep partnership experience | Highest | Full growth strategy + execution |

### How to Move Up the Grid
1. **Layer experience on deliverables:** Bi-weekly strategy calls, Zoom report walkthroughs, kickoff meetings
2. **Personalize routine work:** Even templatized deliverables can have personalized check-ins
3. **Treat their business as your own:** Proactive recommendations, not just reactive execution
4. **Create switching costs through relationships:** The deeper the partnership, the harder to leave

### The "Why Us" Checklist
For every messaging touchpoint (website, social, content, discovery call), answer:
- [ ] Why should they stay on this page?
- [ ] Why should they book a call?
- [ ] Why should they pick YOU over the 10 other agencies they are evaluating?
