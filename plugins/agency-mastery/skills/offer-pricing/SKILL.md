## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENTS

### Disagreement 1: Pricing Methodology
- **Shan Hanif:** Bottom-up (cost + 50% margin) AND top-down (market research); custom offers only; base + performance hybrid
- **Dee Deng:** Value = deliverables + experience; never hourly; never discount; sweeteners instead; foot-in-door paid offers
- **Donvesh:** 10% of value created; charge 50% of competitors initially (first client only); raise until 3 consecutive objections

**Resolution:** All three agree: never compete on price, never discount. For starting out, Donvesh's 50% entry pricing for the first client only is valid as a case-study acquisition tactic. Shan's dual approach (bottom-up + top-down) is the most rigorous for ongoing pricing. Dee's value-based framing should be the default philosophy. Performance components (Shan's 20% profit share) should be layered on once you can track results.

### Disagreement 2: Contract Length
- **Shan Hanif:** 6-month minimum, then rolling 1-month; premium clients on 2-year fixed
- **Donvesh:** NEVER multi-month contracts; always month-over-month

**Resolution:** Stage-dependent. Starting (0-5 clients): month-over-month to reduce friction (Donvesh). Building (5-15 clients): 3-6 month minimums (Shan). Scaling (premium): 1-2 year fixed terms (Shan). You earn longer terms by proving value.

### Disagreement 3: Money-Back Guarantees
- **Shan Hanif:** NEVER for agencies
- **Dee Deng:** Uses refundable foot-in-door paid offers for small entry engagement

**Resolution:** No money-back guarantees on retainers or ongoing services (Shan is correct). A refundable paid audit/strategy session as foot-in-door is acceptable (Dee's model) because scope is small and defined.

## Bottom-Up Pricing (Shan Hanif)

### Formula
```
Hourly Rate = Target Annual Income / 2,080 hours
Time per Client/Month = Hours/Week x 4.33
Your Labor Cost = Hourly Rate x Monthly Hours
+ Freelancer/Contractor Costs
+ Software Overhead per Client
= Total Cost to Service
+ 50% Margin
= Base Price (round up to clean number)
```

### Worked Example (YouTube Management)
- Target income: $100,000/year -> $48.08/hour
- Time per client: 1 day/week (8 hours) = $1,538.46/month
- Freelancers: Thumbnail ($500) + Editor ($500) = $1,000/month
- Software overhead: $80/month / 3 clients = $26.67
- Total cost: $2,565.13
- Plus 50% margin: $769.54
- **Price: $3,335 -> Round to $3,500/month**

## Top-Down Pricing (Shan Hanif)

1. Spot trends: where is high demand?
2. Check competitor pricing: what do similar services cost?
3. Find service gaps: high demand + few providers
4. Calculate profit margins against industry standards
5. Find the "grey area" between low-budget and premium
6. Highlight your unique selling points

### Pricing Research Method (Dee Deng)
1. Google competitors in your discipline + geography
2. Look at published pricing, funnels, content, team pages
3. Book calls with competitors (AB test: sometimes reveal you are also an agency, sometimes pose as prospect)
4. Understand supply-demand curve position
5. Position yourself deliberately on that curve

## The 10% Value Rule (Donvesh)

Charge **10% of the value you create** for the client.
- Help client make $50K -> charge $5K
- Help client make $100K -> charge $10K
- Ensures long retentivity because the ROI is always clear

---

## Price Escalation Rule (Donvesh)

Keep raising prices until you get **3 consecutive price objections from 3 different prospects**. Only then recalibrate. You should charge:
1. More than competitors (signals quality/expert positioning)
2. Just below prospect's threshold

**[CONTRARIAN] Donvesh:** Charging more is the single easiest way to achieve expert positioning. Most people undercharge by a huge margin.

## Performance Models (Shan Hanif)

### Two Levers of Every Offer
- **Lever 1: Base Cost** -- covers your service costs + profit margin
- **Lever 2: Performance Compensation** -- results-based incentive

### Deal Structure Types
| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Revenue percentage | 20% of revenue generated |
| Deals closed | 10% commission on new business |
| Profit share from ads | 20% of ad profit |
| Base + whichever is higher | $7.5K/month OR 20% of profit |
| Base + usage | $1K/month + per-unit fulfillment fees |

### Key Rule (Shan)
Genflow has NEVER done pay-per-lead (result-only pricing). Always have a base retainer that covers costs.

## Anti-Discounting Rules

### Never Discount (All Three Agree)
- **Dee Deng:** Lower prices = clients treat you WORSE. Instead of discounting, add value ("sweeteners") or restructure the deal.
- **Shan Hanif:** All options must be profitable including downsells. Pricing flexibility exists because each offer is custom.
- **Donvesh:** Charging more is expert positioning.

### Never Do Hourly Pricing (Dee Deng)
**[CONTRARIAN]:** Per-hour pricing is derived from manual labor models. Knowledge work output is not time-linear. Nuclear power plant parable: two movements = $50; knowing WHICH two movements = $49,950. Track hours internally for cost management but NEVER communicate hourly to clients.

## Foot-in-Door Offer (Dee Deng)

Fragment a smaller paid engagement from the larger work:
- Examples: audit, consulting session, strategy blueprint, scoping with mockups
- **Tested free vs paid: paid converted significantly better**
- Even a few hundred dollars disqualifies tire-kickers
- Risk mitigation: offer full refund if unsatisfied, rebate into larger engagement if they sign

Example: $2,000 paid discovery/audit -> if they sign the bigger deal, rebate $2K into first month's invoice.

## Social Currency / Lighthouse Clients (Dee Deng)

Early on, prioritize clients that provide:
- Case studies and testimonials
- Network access and referrals
- Authority positioning
- Consider "founding customer" rates, grandfathered pricing
- Social currency can bring more oxygen (revenue) than extracting every cent from early deals

## First Client Pricing (Donvesh)

1. Research what competitors charge
2. If 0 results and no experience: charge **50% of competitor pricing** for first client only
3. After getting first client results + testimonial: **immediately double your prices**
4. Example: competitors charge $2K -> first client at $1K -> second pitch at $2K

**[CONTRARIAN] Donvesh:** End goal of first client is NOT revenue -- it is the testimonial. After first case study, acquisition becomes 10x easier.

---

## Offer Principles (Shan Hanif)

1. **No money-back guarantee** -- ever, for agencies
2. **Custom offers only** -- never fixed packages
3. **No showing prices in pitch deck** -- price comes after value is demonstrated
4. **All options must be profitable** including downsells
5. Smaller target markets (B2C): Lower base (e.g., $3K for email for new clothing brand)
6. Larger target markets (B2B): Higher base (e.g., $10K for email for Nike-sized brand)

---

## ACT-FAST Bonus (Donvesh)

When prospect is on the fence, offer an ACT-FAST Bonus:
- **Not a discount** -- add extra value instead
- Lead gen: give 2 more leads than initially promised
- Systems building: integrate extra software or write additional email sequence
- Creates urgency without destroying pricing integrity
