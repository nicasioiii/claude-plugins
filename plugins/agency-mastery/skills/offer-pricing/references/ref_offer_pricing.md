| # | Service | Offer Structure |

| 1 | Creator Management | 20% of revenue |

| 5 | Launch a course | 20% revenue + $5K/month |

## Performance Pricing Worked Example (Shan Hanif)

**Scenario:** Meta and Google ads management
- Client ad budget: $20K/month, wants 2x ROI
- Standard offer: $5K/month retainer
- **Actual offer created:** $3K/month base + 20% of ad profit

| Month | Ad Spend | Ad Profit | Base Fee | Performance (20%) | Total Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $20K | $20K | $3K | $4K | $7K |
| 2 | $30K | $30K | $3K | $6K | $9K |
| 3 | $40K | $40K | $3K | $8K | $11K |

**Result:** Agency earns more than the standard $5K retainer while the client gets aligned incentives.

## Price Escalation Decision Tree (Donvesh)

```
Start: Set initial price based on competitor research + value calculation
  |
  v
Pitch to prospect
  |
  +--> Closed? YES -> Raise price 15-25% for next prospect
  |
  +--> Price objection?
       |
       +--> 1st objection -> Keep current price, refine pitch
       |
       +--> 2nd objection -> Keep current price, check if pitch/value is clear
       |
       +--> 3rd consecutive objection -> RECALIBRATE
            |
            +--> Lower price 10-15% OR add more value at same price
            +--> Test again with 3 new prospects
```

**Rule:** Never lower price after a single objection. Three consecutive from three different prospects is the signal.

## Foot-in-Door Offer Framework (Dee Deng)

### Structure
- **Offer:** Paid audit / strategy session / scoping with mockups
- **Price:** $500 - $2,000 (enough to disqualify tire-kickers)
- **Deliverable:** Clear, bounded output (report, strategy doc, mockups)
- **Refund option:** Full refund if unsatisfied (risk mitigation)
- **Rebate:** If they sign the main engagement, rebate foot-in-door fee into first month

### Why It Works
- Paid converted significantly better than free in testing
- Proves your listening skills and shows deliverable quality
- Creates mini-experience of working together
- Disqualifies people who will not invest in their business

### Who Should Use This
- Agencies with $5K+/month retainers (the foot-in-door de-risks a larger commitment)
- Agencies where the initial strategy/audit work is genuinely valuable standalone

## Contract Clause Checklist -- 10 Essentials (Dee Deng)

1. **Scope of services/work:** Deliverables, timelines, max revisions
2. **Change requests/amendments:** Process, advance notice, additional fees
3. **Third-party cost disclaimer:** Client pays directly for tools/licenses
4. **Ownership and assignment:** IP rights, work products, future sale provisions
5. **Confidentiality and safeguard of property**
6. **Billing and payment processes:** Due dates, late fees, collection costs
7. **Duration and termination:** Start date, end date, notice periods, auto-renewal
8. **Indemnities:** Balanced (not skewed to one party)
9. **Non-exclusivity:** Retain right to work with other clients in same industry
10. **Applicable law:** Jurisdiction, governing law

### Contract Term Recommendations by Stage

| Stage | Recommended Term | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| First client (0 case studies) | Month-to-month (Donvesh) | Lower friction, easier close |
| Building (1-5 clients) | 3-month minimum (Shan) | Enough time to prove value |
| Established (5-15 clients) | 6-month minimum (Shan) | Revenue predictability |
| Premium/scale (15+ clients) | 1-2 year fixed (Shan) | Maximum revenue stability |

### Proposal Structure (Dee Deng)
Two sections in every proposal:
1. **Plain English section:** What you get, what to expect, commencement date, deliverables, experience elements, what-ifs, overages, dependencies, timelines, mockups
2. **Legal section (bottom):** Terms and conditions, indemnity, IP, confidentiality

**Reduce friction:** E-signature + payment gateway (Stripe) in ONE step.

## Lighthouse Client Criteria (Dee Deng)

A lighthouse client provides more value than just revenue:
- [ ] Will generate a compelling case study
- [ ] Will provide a strong testimonial (video preferred)
- [ ] Has a network you want access to
- [ ] Has a brand name that builds your authority
- [ ] Is collaborative and will allow you to do your best work

**Decision:** If a prospect meets 3+ criteria, consider "founding customer" rates or grandfathered pricing. The social currency may be worth more than the revenue discount.

## Pricing Market Size Adjustments (Shan Hanif)

| Market Size | Base Fee Range | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Small/new B2C brand | $2K-$5K/month | Limited budget, growth stage |
| Mid-market brand | $5K-$15K/month | Proven revenue, can invest |
| Enterprise/large brand | $15K-$50K/month | Large budgets, complex needs |

Always price relative to the value you create, not just your costs. A $3K retainer that generates $30K in revenue for the client is a no-brainer.
