## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: Sales Scripts vs. No Scripts

- **Shan Hanif:** Do NOT use a sales script; use a framework/guideline; prepare a customized deck
- **Dee Deng:** "If you see a course that tells you to use a script, please run"; lead with genuine curiosity
- **Donvesh:** Provides specific qualifying question scripts and video audit scripts; more structured approach

**Resolution:** Never use a rigid word-for-word sales script. Use frameworks and question guides (all three agree). Donvesh's qualifying questions are preparation tools, not read-aloud scripts. The consensus: prepare thoroughly, personalize to the prospect, but never sound rehearsed.

## Sales Pipeline Stages (Shan Hanif)

1. **Lead Sourced** -- identified as potential prospect
2. **Outreach Sent** -- initial contact made
3. **Response Received** -- prospect replied (any response)
4. **Follow-up** -- in nurture sequence
5. **Negotiation** -- terms being discussed
6. **Signed** -- deal closed

Use Pipedrive or similar CRM. Track daily with EOD email to manager.

## Pre-Call Preparation (Shan Hanif)

1. Analyze the lead: find their problems, gaps, competitors doing better
2. Find the ANGLE: what specific thing can you solve?
3. Prepare a customized deck (10-20 slides max)
4. If lacking experience, compare the lead to successful competitors (check Meta Ads Library)
5. Find specific things they are NOT doing that competitors ARE
6. Present these insights as your expertise

### Call Setup Etiquette
- Use Google Meet (free, better screen sharing)
- Camera at face level, facing forward
- Headphones in (not laptop speakers)
- Clean background, professional appearance
- Join 2 minutes EARLY, always be first on the call
- Always have video ON
- Share TAB only, not full desktop

---

## First Call Framework (Shan Hanif)

1. Be first to join
2. Say hello, introduce yourself in 60 seconds (tell your story, not read a script)
3. Listen to their introduction
4. State the call objective (client-focused, not self-focused)
5. Share screen (tab only, not full desktop)
6. Keep their face visible while presenting
7. Guide through slides -- explain, do not read
8. Stop at natural points to spark conversation
9. If conversation flows well, be confident and speak freely
10. Focus on showing VALUE -- avoid price discussion
11. Follow up with email: attach deck + brief call summary
12. Propose times for follow-up call
13. Give them something to think about before next call

---

## Second Call Framework (Shan Hanif)

1. Start with small talk
2. Say you have been thinking about/working on their project
3. Ask for feedback on the first call
4. Present slides showing what you can do for them
5. List specific services you would provide
6. Describe communication and workflow
7. Share pricing, linking it to ROI
8. Open for Q&A
9. If ready: send agreement and next steps

## Deck Framework (Shan Hanif)

10-20 slides max:
1. **Cover Slide:** Clean, include client's logo
2. **Agency Overview (2-3 slides):** Past clients, key successes
3. **Client Highlight (1 slide):** Something awesome about THEIR brand
4. **Problem Discovery (2-3 slides):** Issues you have identified from research
5. **Solution Proposal (2-3 slides):** Your plan to tackle each problem
6. **Value-Driven Examples (3-4 slides):** Case studies, proof of impact
7. **Risk Mitigation (1-2 slides):** How you handle concerns
8. **Process & Timeline (1-2 slides):** Roadmap, timelines, touchpoints

### Design Rules
- No price tags in deck
- No generic/one-size-fits-all info
- Present multiple approaches if applicable
- Clean backgrounds, consistent fonts, minimal text

## The MVP Pitch (Shan Hanif)

MVP = Most Valuable Pitch

Before every pitch, ask: "What is the angle?"
- Research the prospect deeply -- look at their competitors
- Compare their execution to industry leaders (check Meta Ads Library)
- Find specific things they are NOT doing that competitors ARE
- Present these insights as your expertise, even if you have not done them yourself yet
- Fill in knowledge gaps by studying what works for others

## Discovery Call Pull Method (Dee Deng)

**[CONTRARIAN] Dee Deng:** "Wipe all the AIDA, Always Be Closing, one-call-close bullshit off the table. It did not work."

### Three Objectives of Every Discovery Call
1. Close the sale
2. Set the tone for the relationship
3. Manage expectations

### The Approach
- Lead with **genuine curiosity**, not scripts
- Open with: "Hi, welcome. Tell me, how is business?" Then LISTEN.
- Take notes visibly: "Don't mind me typing, what you say is important."
- Ask questions that signal you care (activates subconscious trust)
- Use **stories** to convey case studies (peak pain -> peak pleasure narrative)
- Let prospect put themselves into the hero position
- After stories: "Would it be meaningful for me to tell you about what working with us looks like?"
- Present pricing, then **sit in silence.** Let it hang. "It is your move now."
- Duration: 45-60 minutes

### The "Selling Too Well" Trap (Dee Deng)
If you are too convincing, you close clients who are not the right fit. Results: misaligned expectations, unreasonable demands, refund requests. Being inquisitive > being persuasive. It is OK to say: "It might be too early for us to work together. Here is why."

### Trust Transfer (Dee Deng)
The person closing does not have to be the person doing the work. Introduce the strategist/executor: "You are talking to the dumbest person here. My team did all that. Meet Sally."

## Qualifying Questions (Donvesh)

Pre-call script: "I will ask you 5-6 basic questions just to make sure we are the right people for you. If I find I cannot help you, I will either refer you to someone else or say we are not a good fit."

### The 7 Questions
1. How have you been getting clients till now?
2. How many clients are you currently working with?
3. How does the sales process go? Do you get on calls or have sales closers?
4. How much do you usually charge your clients (a range is good)?
5. Which offer of yours is the best-selling?
6. What is your specific audience for this offer?
7. How many case studies do you have which we can use for future outreach?

### Qualification Checklist
- Has been getting clients through ads, referrals, etc.
- Has minimum revenue of $X/month
- Charges at least $5,000 per deal
- Looking for more clients but does not want to build in-house prospecting
- Has a crystal clear offer and niche (can be worked on if not present)

## Live Proof on Sales Calls (Donvesh)

Do not insert screenshots of campaign dashboards in slides. Instead, open a new tab and show the LIVE dashboard during the call. Much more trust and believability than static screenshots.

---

## ACT-FAST Bonus (Donvesh)

When prospect is on the fence, offer extra value (NOT a discount):
- Lead gen: give 2 more leads than initially promised
- Systems building: integrate extra software or write additional email sequence
- Creates urgency without destroying pricing integrity

## Outsourcing Sales (Donvesh)

### When to Outsource
- Not confident about sales skills
- Self-conscious about accent
- Unable to close even with results
- Want to work ON the business, not IN it

### Commission-Based Closers
- Find on Closify (no upfront cost)
- They only charge when they close a deal
- Onboarding: teach them everything about your business, give list of all objections
- They are the FACE of your agency on calls -- onboard more thoroughly than normal employees

### SDR Commission Structure
| Tier | Rate |
|---|---|
| First 50 meetings/month | 15% per booked appointment |
| 50-75 meetings/month | 20% per booked meeting |
| 75-100 meetings/month | 25% per booked meeting |

## Negotiation Tactics (Dee Deng -- from Chris Voss)

1. **Mirroring:** Repeat back the last portion with upward inflection. Let them hear the lunacy of their own request. Most people self-correct.
2. **"How am I supposed to do that?":** When unreasonable requests come, pose it back. Let THEM solve it.
3. **Fairness:** "After all, we just want what is fair." Everyone understands fairness.
4. **Carrots-or-Peas:** Instead of "Would you like vegetables?" say "Would you like carrots or peas?" Controlled optionality.
5. **Shift from "you/I" to "we":** Linguistic signal of partnership. Listen for when prospects shift from "you guys" to "we" -- invisible tipping point of buy-in.
