# Sales Closing Reference

## Sales Pipeline Stages Template (Shan Hanif)

### CRM Setup (Pipedrive Recommended)
| Stage | Definition | Actions |
|---|---|---|
| Lead Sourced | Identified as potential prospect | Added to CRM with research notes |
| Outreach Sent | Initial contact made (email/DM/call) | Log outreach type and date |
| Response Received | Prospect replied (any response) | Categorize: positive/neutral/negative |
| Follow-up | In nurture sequence | Day 2/5/10 follow-ups scheduled |
| Negotiation | Terms being discussed | Proposal sent, pricing shared |
| Signed | Deal closed | Agreement signed, onboarding triggered |

## Pre-Call Checklist (Shan Hanif)

- [ ] Reviewed prospect's website thoroughly
- [ ] Checked their social media (all platforms)
- [ ] Looked at competitor execution (Meta Ads Library, SimilarWeb)
- [ ] Identified 2-3 specific gaps or opportunities
- [ ] Determined the ANGLE (the specific problem you will solve)
- [ ] Prepared customized deck (10-20 slides)
- [ ] Tested screen sharing setup
- [ ] Camera at face level, headphones in, clean background
- [ ] Joining link tested, will join 2 minutes early
- [ ] Reviewed any previous correspondence or notes

## First Call Framework -- Detailed Steps (Shan Hanif)

| Step | Action | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Be first to join | Shows professionalism |
| 2 | Introduce yourself (60 seconds) | Tell your story, do not read a script |
| 3 | Listen to their introduction | Take notes, show interest |
| 4 | State call objective | Client-focused: "I want to understand your business" |
| 5 | Share screen (tab only) | Not full desktop -- keep it focused |
| 6 | Keep their face visible | Split screen if possible |
| 7 | Guide through slides | Explain, do not read |
| 8 | Pause at natural points | Spark conversation, ask questions |
| 9 | Be confident if conversation flows | Speak freely, do not rigidly follow slides |
| 10 | Focus on VALUE | Avoid price discussion entirely |
| 11 | Follow-up email | Attach deck + brief summary |
| 12 | Propose follow-up call times | Give 2-3 options |
| 13 | Give homework | Something for them to think about |

## Deck Structure -- 8 Slide Types (Shan Hanif)

### Slide 1: Cover
- Clean design
- Include their logo alongside yours
- Date and meeting title

### Slides 2-3: Agency Overview
- Who you are (brief, 1-2 sentences)
- Past clients (logos if possible)
- Key results achieved (3-5 headline metrics)

### Slide 4: Client Highlight
- Something you genuinely admire about THEIR brand
- Shows you did research
- Builds rapport through recognition

### Slides 5-6: Problem Discovery
- Specific issues identified from your research
- Show screenshots or data points
- Frame as opportunities, not criticisms

### Slides 7-8: Solution Proposal
- Your plan to address each identified problem
- Be specific about approach and timeline
- Show how your method differs from generic approaches

### Slides 9-12: Value-Driven Examples
- Case studies relevant to their industry/problem
- Before/after metrics
- Client testimonials (quotes or video screenshots)

### Slide 13: Risk Mitigation
- How you handle concerns (communication, reporting, flexibility)
- Exit terms (shows confidence)

### Slide 14: Process & Timeline
- Month-by-month roadmap
- Key milestones and checkpoints
- What they can expect when

**Design rules:** No price tags anywhere. No generic info. Multiple approaches if applicable. Clean, minimal, professional.

## Qualifying Questions -- Full Framework (Donvesh)

### Pre-Call Script
"I will ask you 5-6 basic questions just to make sure we are the right people for you. If I find I cannot help you, I will either refer you to someone else or say we are not a good fit."

### The 7 Questions with Intent
| # | Question | What You Learn |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | How have you been getting clients till now? | Current acquisition sophistication |
| 2 | How many clients are you currently working with? | Capacity and revenue baseline |
| 3 | How does the sales process go? | Sales maturity and pain points |
| 4 | How much do you usually charge (range)? | Whether they can afford you |
| 5 | Which offer is best-selling? | Product-market fit strength |
| 6 | What is your specific audience? | Niche clarity |
| 7 | How many case studies do you have? | Social proof availability |

### Qualification Checklist
- [ ] Has been getting clients through some channel (not zero)
- [ ] Has minimum revenue of $X/month (your threshold)
- [ ] Charges at least $5,000 per deal
- [ ] Looking for more clients but does not want to build in-house
- [ ] Has clear offer and niche (or willing to work on it)

## Negotiation Tactics -- 5 Techniques (Dee Deng / Chris Voss)

### 1. Mirroring
- **How:** Repeat back the last portion of what they said with upward inflection
- **Example:** Client: "Would you waive your fees and subsidize ad spend?" Response: "So... we did great work, your supplier let you down, you got refunds, and you want us to work for free?"
- **Effect:** They hear the lunacy. Most self-correct.

### 2. "How Am I Supposed to Do That?"
- **How:** When unreasonable requests come, pose it back as a genuine question
- **Effect:** Forces THEM to solve the problem or acknowledge the unreasonableness

### 3. Fairness
- **How:** "After all, we just want what is fair"
- **Effect:** Appeals to universal principle. Difficult to argue against fairness.

### 4. Carrots-or-Peas Trick
- **How:** Instead of "Would you like vegetables?" say "Would you like carrots or peas?"
- **Effect:** Controlled optionality -- both options work for you

### 5. "You/I" to "We" Shift
- **How:** Shift language from "you guys" to "we" in conversation
- **Diagnostic:** Listen for when the prospect shifts from "you" to "we" -- this is the invisible tipping point of buy-in

## Proposal Structure (Dee Deng)

### Section 1: Plain English
- What you get (deliverables)
- What to expect (timeline, process)
- Commencement date
- Experience elements (calls, reports, strategy sessions)
- What-ifs and overages
- Dependencies (what you need from the client)
- Timelines and milestones
- Mockups (if applicable)

### Section 2: Legal
- Terms and conditions
- Indemnity
- IP rights
- Confidentiality

### Delivery
- E-signature + payment gateway (Stripe) in ONE step (not 3-4 separate touches)
- Offer Zoom walkthrough for questions about the proposal

### Notion-Based Proposals (Donvesh)
- Use Notion for proposals
- Host on custom domain using Super (e.g., agency.com/proposal-clientname)
- Professional appearance, easy to update

## Closer Onboarding Steps (Donvesh)

1. Teach them EVERYTHING about your business
2. Walk through all services and pricing
3. Give complete list of objections you have received
4. Share case studies and proof points
5. Have them shadow 3-5 of your calls before going solo
6. They should handle objections independently after onboarding

### SDR Commission Tiers (Donvesh)
| Tier | Meetings/Month | Commission Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Base | 0-50 | 15% per booked meeting |
| Mid | 50-75 | 20% per booked meeting |
| High | 75-100 | 25% per booked meeting |

Hire from Upwork at $10-$15/hour. Hire experienced SDRs to avoid micro-management.

## Troubleshooting Sales (Dee Deng)

### The Discount Trap
When clients are not closing, the easiest (and worst) response is to lower prices.

### Diagnostic Approach -- Measure Every Stage
| Stage | Metric |
|---|---|
| Content/ads -> Email captures | Conversion rate |
| Email captures -> Form completions | Conversion rate |
| Forms -> Discovery calls booked vs held | Show rate |
| Calls -> Verbal yes vs no | Close rate |
| Proposals sent vs signed | Sign rate |
| Foot-in-door -> Main proposal | Upsell rate |

### Record All Discovery Calls
Cross-compare calls, critique one another. Look for:
1. **Specificity:** On this call, you missed X
2. **Pattern recognition:** Across calls, is there a systematic issue?

**The trap is always blaming price.** Look at every step in the funnel first.

## Emotional vs. Logical Selling (Dee Deng)

The sales process is driven by the emotional brain, backed up by the logical brain. Not the other way around.

- **Peak pain:** Make them FEEL the cost of not solving this
- **Peak pleasure:** Make them FEEL what life looks like after the problem is solved
- Use **stories** to convey both (prospect puts themselves into the hero position)

Lead with emotion. Support with logic (numbers, case studies, timelines).
