## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: First Hire Priority

- **Shan:** Hire people whose skills COMPLEMENT yours
- **Dee:** Function chart first, then hire for most critical function; missionaries over mercenaries
- **Donvesh:** Hire basic VA or freelancer after first client; hire SDR for cold calling at scale

**Resolution:** First hire = VA/admin to free your time (Donvesh). Then follow Dee's function chart to identify highest-leverage role. Use Shan's complementary-skills principle for WHO to hire. Use freelancers for execution overflow; hire missionaries for core long-term functions.

## Hiring Process (Shan Hanif -- 5 Steps)

1. **Job Description:** Clear role, responsibilities, requirements
2. **Application Task:** Test actual ability (not just CV)
3. **Interview:** Culture fit, communication, problem-solving
4. **Trial Period:** Paid trial on real work (1-2 weeks)
5. **Contract:** Formal agreement

### What to Look For (Shan)
- Complement YOUR skill gaps (do not hire clones)
- Skills-based assessment > experience/resume
- Communication quality (writing and verbal)
- Self-starter mentality, driven by targets
- Cultural fit with agency values

**[CONTRARIAN] Shan:** Bad hiring nearly destroyed the business. People from corporate backgrounds often cannot adapt to agency pace. Test skills, not interview performance.

## Interview Framework (Dee Deng)

1. Application + video intro + resume (filter for effort/research)
2. Culture fit interview (standardized)
3. Assessment/challenge (real scenario, time-boxed)
4. Tandem interview (2 interviewers -- one asks, one notes)
5. Offer + onboarding
6. 30/60/90-day probation with structured check-ins

**Key question:** "What can we do to be part of your personal growth journey?"

**Have ready before interviewing:** KPIs for the role, responsibilities/accountabilities, development pathway.

**A bad hire costs ~15x that hire's salary** (Dee Deng / Brad Smart Topgrading).

## Talent-Sales Staircase (Dee Deng)

The fundamental tension: you cannot do just-in-time hiring. Knowledge workers need ramp time.

1. X people can handle Y clients at capacity
2. You cannot stop/start the acquisition pipeline
3. You go OVER capacity (team stretched, good for revenue, bad for morale)
4. You hire to next level (now UNDER capacity, more overhead)
5. Pressure goes to sales to fill the gap
6. Cycle repeats

**The game:** Minimize the size of the over/under triangles. Start recruiting conversations early.

**Each additional service line creates its own staircase.** This is why specialization matters.

## Team Composition: Missionaries vs Mercenaries (Dee Deng)

- **Mercenaries:** Complete mission, get paid, leave. Contractors/freelancers.
- **Missionaries:** Drive the cause, long vision, build the tribe.

Right Hook chose missionaries for core team + mercenaries on standby.

### Missionary Recruitment -- Currencies Beyond Salary
1. Career advancement / experience exposure
2. Being developed by skilled leaders
3. Tackling harder problems
4. Resume building
5. Promise of unlocking future value

### Mercenary Management
Build a Rolodex of tested freelancers. Categories: discipline, tested/untested, availability, lead time. Optionally create a freelancer community for overflow work.

## Equity & Co-Founder Decisions (Dee Deng)

### Co-Founder Alignment Checklist
- Life stage alignment (similar financial needs)
- Complementary (not overlapping) skill sets
- Shared values but different strengths
- "Can this person traverse the CIGAR gap with me?"

### Equity Principles
- 100% of nothing is still nothing
- Vesting: 4-year vest with 1-year cliff (standard)
- Alternative: unlock equity via KPIs
- 50/50 voting creates deadlock risk; 3 founders enables majority
- Be cautious giving out points early

## Leadership (Dee Deng)

### Alignment/Autonomy Grid
- Low alignment + low autonomy = chaos
- High autonomy + low alignment = hope someone is working on it
- High alignment + low autonomy = dictatorship
- **High alignment + high autonomy = ideal**

### Delegation Principle
"Every time I take a problem away from you, I am weakening you." Coach through problems, do not solve them.

### Scaling Lessons (Shan Hanif)
1. Culture does not exist without real team members (freelancers are transactional)
2. Give equity to key people early
3. Hire complementary skills
4. Trust must be earned -- verify then trust
5. Freelancer-to-team transition is the hardest inflection point

## Retention Strategies (Shan Hanif -- 8 Strategies)

1. Build Connection: Regular check-ins, video on, quarterly activities
2. Strong Onboarding: Clear expectations from day one
3. Growth Opportunities: Career development, new projects
4. Foster Autonomy: Results-based, not micromanagement
5. Clear Communication: 1-1s, Loom feedback, praise in the moment
6. Recognize Contributions: Friday shoutouts, performance bonuses
7. Create Flexibility: Fixed meetings/deadlines, flexible work hours
8. Clear Advancement Path: Long-term vision, promote from within

## Partnerships (Dee Deng)

6 types:
1. Reciprocal referral
2. Joint client service
3. Co-branded content
4. White label (vet quality thoroughly)
5. Platform partnerships (treat reps as partners)
6. Speaker/ambassador relationships

**Community-first personal branding:** Add genuine value in communities. Zero pitch. People talk. Conference organizers notice. Speaking photos become authority content.
