# /build-forecast

Let's build a realistic 12-month forecast. I'll ask questions to understand your business, then walk you through the process.

**1. What's your revenue model?**
- Single revenue stream (e.g., product sales, services, subscriptions)?
- Multiple streams (product + services, or seasonal mix)?
- Paste or describe how revenue breaks down

**2. How much historical data do you have?**
- 12+ months (full year history): Great, we have seasonality patterns
- 6–11 months: Workable, we'll note seasonal assumptions
- <6 months: New business—we'll build from ground up with strategic inputs

**3. What's changing in your business next 12 months?**
- Are you launching new products or services?
- Scaling marketing spend?
- Hiring additional team?
- Market conditions shifting (competition, regulation, economic)?
- Geographic or customer expansion?

**4. What external factors matter to your forecast?**
- Seasonality (holidays, weather, budget cycles)?
- Industry cycles (B2B vs B2C sales cycles)?
- Macro trends (interest rates, inflation, consumer spending)?
- Competitive dynamics?

**5. What's your biggest forecasting uncertainty?**
- Pricing (will customers pay more / will I need to discount)?
- Volume (will I keep growing at current rate or plateau)?
- Costs (will expenses scale with revenue or stay fixed)?
- Market (how is market growth/competition affecting me)?

---

## Once You Answer, Here's What I'll Do:

**Step 1: Analyze Historical Trends**
- Vertical analysis: What % of revenue is each cost?
- Horizontal analysis: How have they changed month-to-month?
- Identify seasonality patterns (if you have enough history)

**Step 2: Apply Strategic Modifiers**
- Growth modifier: What's your realistic growth rate next 12 months?
- Seasonal adjustments: Apply month-by-month % variations
- Strategic changes: Layer in impact of new initiatives

**Step 3: Build Three Scenarios**
- Base case (most likely): Current trajectory + planned changes
- Optimistic (best case): Growth accelerates, costs track well
- Pessimistic (worst case): Growth slows, unexpected costs arise

**Step 4: Identify Key Drivers**
- Revenue assumptions (units, price per unit, growth %)
- Cost assumptions (% of revenue, fixed vs variable)
- Hiring/headcount timing
- Capex or one-time investments

**Step 5: Validate for Realism**
- Challenge assumptions that seem too aggressive
- Check for second-order consequences (if you grow 50%, can operations handle it?)
- Stress-test against downturn scenarios

**Share your answers above** and we'll build your forecast together.
