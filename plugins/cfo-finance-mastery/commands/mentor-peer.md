# /mentor-peer

Let's coach your peer through a financial challenge or learning opportunity. I'll help you frame guidance that drives understanding and action.

**1. What's the core issue or topic?**
- They need help analyzing a specific financial problem?
- They're struggling to understand a financial concept?
- They made a decision and need perspective on implications?
- They're building a model or process and need guidance?
- Something else?

**2. What's their experience level?**
- Experienced CFO or business owner (knows financials)?
- Learning but has foundation (intermediate)?
- Newer to finance (wants to build basics)?
- Non-financial background (learning from scratch)?

**3. What's their learning style?**
- They want to understand the "why" (strategic context)?
- They want step-by-step "how" (procedural)?
- They want to see the impact "what-if" (scenarios)?
- They want definitions and structure "what" (framework)?
- Not sure—you'll tell them to ask?

**4. What outcome are you going for?**
- Help them understand a concept deeply?
- Coach them through a decision?
- Correct a mistake or misunderstanding (gently)?
- Validate their thinking?
- Challenge them to think deeper?

**5. What's the relationship?**
- Formal mentor-mentee (they expect guidance)?
- Peer collaboration (equal standing)?
- Senior-to-junior (you're more experienced)?
- Expert-to-beginner (big knowledge gap)?

**6. What's your concern about the conversation?**
- They might get defensive or dismiss your input?
- You're not sure how to explain it clearly?
- They're overwhelmed or frustrated?
- You want to challenge them without hurting feelings?
- No concerns—they're open and ready to learn?

**7. Share the specifics:**
- What's the financial issue or decision they're facing?
- What's their current thinking or mistake?
- What do you think they should do instead?
- What data or context do they have?

## Once You Answer, I'll Provide:

**Mentoring Strategy**
- Whether to lead with questions or recommendations (based on learning style and relationship)
- How to position yourself (guide vs equal vs expert)
- The tone to use (supportive, challenging, validating)
- When to go deep vs stay high-level

**Coaching Framework**
- Opening statement (how to start the conversation)
- Guiding questions (help them arrive at insights themselves)
- Alternative framing (how to reposition a mistaken assumption)
- Supporting data (what evidence to reference)
- Restatement (how to summarize for clarity)

**Translation by Learning Style**
- For "Why" learner: Strategic context and business relevance
- For "How" learner: Step-by-step process and mechanics
- For "What" learner: Definition and framework structure
- For "What-If" learner: Impact modeling and scenario consequences

**Response to Common Reactions**
- If they push back: How to validate and dig deeper
- If they get defensive: How to acknowledge and redirect
- If they're confused: How to simplify and break down
- If they're ready to act: How to solidify next steps

**Specific Conversation Script**
- Opening (how to frame the conversation)
- Explanation or guidance (the core teaching)
- Handling objections (what they might say, how to respond)
- Closing (how to leave them with clarity and agency)

**Guiding Questions (Instead of Telling Them What to Do)**
- How to ask questions that lead them to insights
- When to guide with questions vs give direct feedback
- Examples of powerful questions for their specific situation

**Validation + Challenge Balance**
- How to validate what they're doing well
- How to challenge them to think deeper
- How to avoid sounding condescending or know-it-all
- How to make it about growth, not correction

## Specific Coaching Examples I'll Provide:

**Peer is discouraged:** "Our margins are terrible and I don't know how to improve them"

I'll coach you to say:
- First: "Walk me through what you've already looked at. What have you tried?"
- Then: "I notice COGS is your biggest variable. What would happen if you negotiated supplier terms?"
- Guide them: "What do you think the leverage is there? Annual spend? Payment reliability?"
- Then let them discover: "So if you negotiate 10 days more payment time, that frees how much working capital?"

**Peer made a questionable financial decision:** They took an MCA

I'll coach you to say:
- Validate: "I see why it felt urgent at the time. You needed cash."
- Clarify: "Let's look at the real cost structure. What are you actually repaying?"
- Educate gently: "The daily repayment structure means the true APR is much higher than it looks. Here's the math."
- Guide options: "What are the alternatives? Could you refinance to an SBA loan? Apply for a business credit card?"
- Support: "This is fixable. Here's how we'd do it."

**Peer doesn't believe a financial analysis:** "Your forecast seems too optimistic"

I'll coach you to say:
- Invite them in: "I may be wrong. Help me stress-test this."
- Show your work: "Here's my baseline assumption. Here's my growth modifier. Here's my seasonal adjustment."
- Challenge them: "Where do you see the gap? What would you change?"
- Model the alternative: "If growth is 10% instead of 15%, profit drops to X. Is that more realistic?"
- Validate: "Maybe you have context I'm missing. Walk me through your thinking."
