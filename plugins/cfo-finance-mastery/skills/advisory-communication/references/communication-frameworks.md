# Communication Frameworks Reference

## Simplification Framework -- Implementation Guide (Leikauf)

### The Three Components

#### 1. One Key Insight

**Rule:** Never present more than one key insight per interaction segment.

**How to find the key insight:**
1. Review all the data you have analyzed
2. Ask: "If the client could only remember ONE thing from this conversation, what should it be?"
3. That is your key insight
4. Everything else is supporting evidence or secondary

**Examples of strong key insights:**
- "Your business is profitable but you are running out of cash because customers pay too slowly"
- "Your highest-margin product is your least-marketed product"
- "You can fund your expansion without borrowing by collecting AR 15 days faster"

**Examples of weak key insights:**
- "Revenue is up and margins are down and inventory is growing and..." (too many insights)
- "Your current ratio is 1.3" (a number, not an insight)
- "Things look okay" (too vague)

#### 2. Clear Visual

| Data Type | Best Visual | Why |
|-----------|-----------|-----|
| Trend over time | Line chart | Shows direction at a glance |
| Composition / breakdown | Pie or stacked bar | Shows proportions |
| Comparison | Bar chart | Side-by-side is intuitive |
| Health / status | Stoplight (Red/Yellow/Green) | Universal understanding |
| Single metric | Large number with arrow | Grabs attention |
| Budget vs actual | Variance bar | Shows magnitude and direction |

**Rules for visuals:**
- One chart per insight (never combine unrelated data)
- Remove chart junk (gridlines, 3D effects, excessive labels)
- Use color intentionally (red = bad, green = good, blue = neutral)
- Label axes and provide context (vs. prior period, vs. budget)

#### 3. Story to Anchor It

**The anchoring analogy should:**
- Be relatable to the specific audience
- Use everyday language (4th-grade English, per Leikauf)
- Connect the financial concept to something they already understand

**Example analogies by concept:**

| Concept | Analogy |
|---------|---------|
| Cash flow vs profit | "You can sell lemonade all day for profit, but if you spent all your cash on lemons and nobody has paid you yet, you cannot buy cups for tomorrow" |
| Working capital | "It is like your car's gas tank. Even if you earn money from driving, if the tank is empty, you are stuck" |
| CCC | "How fast a dollar goes out the door and comes back bigger. Shorter trips = more trips per year = more money" |
| Gross margin | "For every dollar your customer pays, how many cents do you keep before paying the bills?" |
| DSCR | "For every dollar you owe the bank, how many dollars does your business earn? Above 1.25 means you can breathe" |
| Depreciation | "Your equipment loses value every year, like a new car losing value when you drive it off the lot" |

## Recommendation-Data-Recommendation (R-D-R) -- Detailed Guide

### Structure

```
STEP 1: RECOMMENDATION (the action)
  "I recommend we [specific action]."

STEP 2: DATA (the evidence, 2-3 points max)
  "Here is why: [data point 1], [data point 2], [data point 3]."

STEP 3: RECOMMENDATION (restated, with next step)
  "So I recommend we [action] and the first step is [specific next step]."
```

### Why R-D-R Works

- Decision-makers want the **answer first**, evidence second
- Leading with data buries the recommendation
- Restating the recommendation at the end ensures it is remembered (recency effect)
- Limits data to 2-3 points, preventing overload

### R-D-R Examples by Audience

**For a CEO:**
> "I recommend we raise prices 8% on the premium tier. Margins compressed 4 points this year, customer satisfaction remains at 92%, and competitors charge 15-20% more. We should raise prices next quarter starting with new customers."

**For a board:**
> "I recommend approving the $200K equipment investment. It increases production capacity 35%, has a 14-month payback, and our DSCR remains above 1.5 even with the new loan. I recommend we move forward and begin vendor selection this month."

**For an employee:**
> "I recommend we change our invoicing to same-day electronic invoicing. We are currently billing 5-7 days late, which means $40K in cash is stuck waiting. The change will get cash in the door a week faster and I recommend we start next Monday."

## Learning Styles -- Applied to Finance Presentations

### Building a Complete Explanation

**For any financial topic, cover all four styles in sequence:**

| Style | Time | Example: Explaining a Margin Decline |
|-------|------|--------------------------------------|
| **What** | 1 min | "Gross margin declined from 45% to 41% this quarter." |
| **Why** | 2 min | "This matters because every point of margin at your revenue level equals $50K in profit." |
| **How** | 3 min | "The decline was caused by two factors: supplier cost increases (2 points) and higher shipping rates (2 points)." |
| **What If** | 2 min | "If we renegotiate with suppliers and switch carriers, we can recover 3 of the 4 points by Q3." |

### Audience Style Adaptation

| If Audience Leans | Expand | Compress |
|------------------|--------|----------|
| **What-dominant** (wants definitions) | Definitions, precise numbers | Scenarios, strategy |
| **Why-dominant** (wants purpose) | Business impact, outcomes | Technical details |
| **How-dominant** (wants mechanics) | Process, formulas, data | Big-picture strategy |
| **What-If-dominant** (wants scenarios) | Options, models, projections | Historical analysis |

## Open Loops -- Engagement Technique (Leikauf)

### How to Create an Open Loop

1. **Start with a hook:** A surprising fact, a provocative question, or a promise
2. **Build tension:** Present the context that makes the answer valuable
3. **Close the loop:** Deliver the insight

### Examples

**Hook:** "There is one ratio that predicts whether your business will run out of cash in the next 90 days."
**Tension:** "Most business owners have never calculated it. Their accountant does not track it."
**Close:** "It is your Cash Conversion Cycle. Yours is 67 days. Here is how we get it to 40."

**Hook:** "Your biggest competitor's margins are 12 points higher than yours."
**Tension:** "And they charge the same prices. The difference is not revenue."
**Close:** "It is vendor terms. They pay Net 60 while you pay Net 15. Let me show you how to fix that."

### Rules
- Only use open loops for genuinely important insights (not everything)
- Always close the loop (leaving it open frustrates audiences)
- Limit to 1-2 per meeting (overuse feels manipulative)

## Concrete vs. Conceptual -- Communication Adaptation

### Identifying the Type in Real Time

**Signals of a concrete thinker:**
- Asks "What exactly should I do?"
- Wants specific numbers and examples
- Gets frustrated by abstract strategy discussions
- Takes detailed notes
- Prefers checklists and step-by-step instructions

**Signals of a conceptual thinker:**
- Asks "How does this fit the big picture?"
- Wants frameworks and patterns
- Gets bored by excessive detail
- Thinks in analogies and metaphors
- Prefers strategic summaries over data dumps

### Communication Templates

**For concrete thinkers:**
```
"Here is what we need to do:
1. [Specific action] by [specific date]
2. [Specific action] by [specific date]
3. [Specific action] by [specific date]

The numbers: [exact figures]
The result: [quantified outcome]
Who does what: [clear assignments]"
```

**For conceptual thinkers:**
```
"Here is the pattern I am seeing:
[Framework/trend description]

This means:
[Strategic implication]

The direction we should go:
[Big-picture recommendation]

We can get into details next, but the strategic question is:
[Key decision]"
```

## Three Essential Reports -- Design Specifications

### Report 1: Cash Flow Snapshot

**Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly during tight cash periods; monthly otherwise

```
CASH FLOW SNAPSHOT -- [Period]

Opening Cash:           $XXX,XXX
+ Cash Received:        $XXX,XXX
  (AR collected, other income)
- Cash Paid:            $XXX,XXX
  (AP, payroll, rent, debt service)
= Net Cash Flow:        $XX,XXX   [Arrow: up/down vs prior]
= Closing Cash:         $XXX,XXX  [Stoplight: green/yellow/red]

Cash Runway:            X.X months
Key Alert:              [One sentence if applicable]
```

### Report 2: Profitability Dashboard

**Frequency:** Monthly

```
PROFITABILITY DASHBOARD -- [Month/Quarter]

Revenue:        $XXX,XXX  [vs prior: +/- X%] [Arrow]
Gross Profit:   $XXX,XXX  [Margin: XX%]      [Stoplight]
Net Profit:     $XXX,XXX  [Margin: XX%]      [Stoplight]

Top 5 Expenses:
  1. [Category]: $XX,XXX  [vs budget: +/- X%]
  2. [Category]: $XX,XXX  [vs budget: +/- X%]
  3. [Category]: $XX,XXX  [vs budget: +/- X%]
  4. [Category]: $XX,XXX  [vs budget: +/- X%]
  5. [Category]: $XX,XXX  [vs budget: +/- X%]

Key Insight:    [One sentence -- the R-D-R recommendation]
```

### Report 3: KPI Summary

**Frequency:** Monthly

```
KPI SUMMARY -- [Month]

                        Actual    Target    Trend    Status
Revenue Growth:         X.X%      X.X%      [Arrow]  [Stoplight]
Customer Retention:     XX%       XX%       [Arrow]  [Stoplight]

CPA:                    $XXX      $XXX      [Arrow]  [Stoplight]

Key Insight:    [One sentence]
Action Item:    [One sentence with owner and deadline]
```

## Five-Day Close -- Implementation Checklist (Leikauf)

### Day-by-Day Close Process

| Day | Tasks | Owner |
|-----|-------|-------|
| **Day 1** | Reconcile all bank accounts; post final transactions | Bookkeeper |
| **Day 2** | Reconcile AR and AP; post accruals | Bookkeeper/Controller |
| **Day 3** | Review and post depreciation, amortization, prepaid adjustments | Controller |
| **Day 4** | Review P&L and BS for accuracy; investigate anomalies | Controller/CFO |
| **Day 5** | Generate reports; prepare CFO summary; distribute | CFO |

### Prerequisites for 5-Day Close

1. **Standardized chart of accounts** -- No cleanup or reclassification needed at close
2. **Automated bank feeds** -- Transactions categorized in real time
3. **Clear cut-off procedures** -- Everyone knows the last day of the period
4. **Pre-close checklist** -- Run during the last week of the month (not after close)
5. **Dedicated close time** -- Block calendars for the close team on days 1-5

### Board Meeting Preparation Checklist

1. **Financial summary slide** -- Headline numbers, trend, one key insight
2. **P&L summary** -- Revenue, margins, variance to budget, explanation of material variances
3. **Cash position** -- Current cash, runway, working capital status
4. **Key initiatives** -- Status of strategic projects, ROI tracking
5. **Forward outlook** -- 12-month forecast summary, key assumptions, scenarios
6. **Risks** -- Top 3 risks with likelihood, impact, and mitigation
7. **Recommendations** -- 2-3 specific decisions or approvals needed
8. **Backup slides** -- Detailed data for anticipated questions (do not present unless asked)
