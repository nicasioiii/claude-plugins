# Financing Options Reference

## Financing Decision Matrix

### By Business Need

| Need | Best Option | Second Option | Avoid |
|------|-----------|---------------|-------|
| **Working capital (short-term)** | Business line of credit | Invoice factoring | MCA, payday loans |
| **Equipment purchase** | Equipment loan | Equipment lease | Credit cards |
| **Real estate** | Commercial mortgage | SBA 504 loan | Short-term financing |
| **Seasonal cash gap** | Revolving credit line | Seasonal factoring | MCA |
| **Growth/expansion** | SBA 7(a) | Bank term loan | Multiple MCAs |
| **Debt refinancing** | Bank term loan / SBA | 0% credit card (small amounts) | MCA to pay off MCA |
| **Emergency cash** | Line of credit draw | Short-term bank loan | MCA (last resort only) |

### By Timeline

| Urgency | Options | Typical Approval |
|---------|---------|-----------------|
| **1-3 days** | Existing credit line draw, credit card, MCA (avoid) | Pre-approved |
| **1-2 weeks** | Online lenders, fintech loans | 3-7 days |
| **2-4 weeks** | Bank line of credit, equipment loan | 2-3 weeks |
| **1-3 months** | SBA 7(a), bank term loan, commercial mortgage | 30-90 days |

### By Credit Profile

| Credit Score | Available Options | Expected Rates |
|-------------|------------------|----------------|
| **750+** | All options; best terms | Prime + 1-3% |
| **700-749** | Most traditional options | Prime + 2-5% |
| **650-699** | Limited traditional; may need collateral | 8-15% |
| **600-649** | Online lenders, secured loans | 12-25% |
| **< 600** | Very limited; personal guarantee required; avoid MCA | 20%+ (dangerous) |

## MCA (Merchant Cash Advance) True Cost Analysis

### How MCAs Work
- Lender buys a percentage of future sales at a discount
- Daily or weekly automatic repayment from revenue
- Not technically a "loan" (avoids lending regulations)
- Factor rate of 1.2-1.5 (meaning you pay back $1.20-$1.50 for every $1 borrowed)

### True APR Calculator

```
MCA True Cost Example:
  Amount received: $50,000
  Factor rate: 1.3
  Total repayment: $50,000 x 1.3 = $65,000
  Cost: $15,000

  If repaid over 6 months:
  Approximate APR = (Cost / Amount) x (365 / Days) x 100
  = ($15,000 / $50,000) x (365 / 180)
  = 0.30 x 2.028
  = 60.8% APR

  If repaid over 4 months:
  = ($15,000 / $50,000) x (365 / 120)
  = 0.30 x 3.042
  = 91.3% APR
```

### MCA Danger Signs

| Warning | What It Means |
|---------|--------------|
| Factor rate > 1.3 | Extremely expensive; effective APR likely 60%+ |
| Daily repayment | Severe cash flow drain; no relief during slow periods |
| Stacking MCAs | Taking a second MCA to pay the first; debt spiral |
| Confession of judgment clause | Lender can seize assets without court process |
| No prepayment benefit | You pay the full factor regardless of early payoff |
| Unclear factor rate | They quote weekly amounts instead of total cost |

### MCA Alternatives

Before accepting an MCA, explore:
1. **SBA Microloans** (up to $50K, 6-8% interest)
2. **Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs)** -- mission-based lenders
3. **Invoice factoring** -- sell receivables instead of future revenue
4. **Working capital optimization** -- free trapped cash first (see working-capital)
5. **Credit card with 0% promo** -- for smaller amounts
6. **Negotiating with existing creditors** -- extend terms, reduce payments

## Good Debt vs. Bad Debt -- Decision Framework

### The ROI Test

```
If: Expected ROI on borrowed capital > Cost of debt
Then: Good debt (proceed)

If: Expected ROI < Cost of debt OR ROI is uncertain
Then: Bad debt (do not proceed)
```

### Examples

| Scenario | ROI | Cost | Verdict |
|----------|-----|------|---------|
| Equipment loan at 8% for machine that increases output 25% | ~25% | 8% | Good debt |
| Inventory loan at 10% for inventory that turns in 60 days | ~30%+ | 10% | Good debt |
| Line of credit at 7% to bridge seasonal AR gap | Preserves operations | 7% | Good debt (strategic) |
| MCA at 60% APR for unclear purpose | Unknown | 60% | Bad debt |
| Credit card at 24% for non-essential spending | 0% | 24% | Bad debt |
| Loan to cover operating losses | Negative | Any rate | Bad debt (fix operations first) |

### The Cash Flow Test

Even if ROI is positive, debt is "bad" if the repayment schedule destroys cash flow:
- Monthly payment > 15-20% of monthly cash flow = dangerous
- Daily repayment (MCA) during slow seasons = potentially fatal
- Multiple debt payments stacking = needs restructuring

## Credit Card Strategy -- Detailed Playbook (Leikauf)

### Recommended Cards and Use Cases

| Card | Best Feature | Optimal Use |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Amex Business Gold** | 4x points on top 2 categories (auto-selected) | Digital advertising, shipping, gas stations |
| **Chase Ink Business Preferred** | 3x on travel, shipping, internet, advertising | Diverse spending categories |
| **Capital One Spark Cash** | 2% unlimited cash back | Simplicity; default card for everything |
| **Amex Platinum** | Airport lounge, hotel status, travel insurance | Travel-heavy business owners |

### Credit vs. Charge Card Decision

| Feature | Credit Card | Charge Card |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| Balance carryover | Yes (interest applies) | No (must pay full balance monthly) |
| Spending limit | Pre-set credit limit | Flexible (based on spending patterns) |
| Interest cost | 15-29% APR on balances | None (forced full payment) |
| Cash flow discipline | Lower (can defer payments) | Higher (forces monthly clearing) |

**Leikauf recommendation:** Use charge cards for operating expenses (enforces discipline). Use credit cards only for strategic 0% APR financing.

### The 0% APR Refinancing Tactic (Leikauf)

**Situation:** Business has high-interest credit card debt.

**Strategy:**
1. Apply for a new business credit card with 0% introductory APR (12-21 months typical)
2. Route ALL new business spending to the 0% card
3. Stop using the old high-interest card completely
4. All cash that would have gone to new purchases now goes toward paying down old debt
5. After old debt is paid: close old card or keep for emergencies

**Key distinction:** This is NOT a balance transfer (which incurs 3-5% fees). You are simply redirecting new spending to eliminate the interest burden on new charges while focusing cash on paying down old expensive debt.

**Math example:**
```
Old card: $30,000 balance at 22% APR = $6,600/year in interest
New card: 0% APR for 15 months on new spending

Monthly spending: $10,000
Previously: $10,000 on old card (22% on running balance)
Now: $10,000 on new 0% card; redirect cash flow to pay down old balance

After 15 months: Old balance significantly reduced or eliminated
Savings: $6,000-$8,000 in interest charges
```

## SBA Loan Programs

### SBA 7(a) -- Most Common

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Max amount** | $5 million |
| **Term** | 7-25 years (depends on use) |
| **Interest rate** | Prime + 2.25-2.75% (variable) |
| **Down payment** | 10-20% typical |
| **Guarantee** | SBA guarantees 75-85% to the lender |
| **Personal guarantee** | Required for 20%+ owners |
| **Best for** | Working capital, equipment, real estate, acquisition |

### SBA 504 -- Real Estate and Equipment

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Max amount** | $5.5 million |
| **Term** | 10-25 years |
| **Interest rate** | Fixed, below market |
| **Down payment** | 10% (vs. 20-30% for conventional) |
| **Structure** | Bank provides 50%, CDC provides 40%, borrower 10% |
| **Best for** | Owner-occupied real estate, major equipment |

### SBA Microloans

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Max amount** | $50,000 |
| **Term** | Up to 6 years |
| **Interest rate** | 6-8% typical |
| **Best for** | Startups, very small businesses, working capital |

### Grant Programs

- **Federal grants:** grants.gov (highly competitive, specific eligibility)
- **State/local grants:** Economic development agencies, small business development centers
- **Industry-specific grants:** Technology, agriculture, minority-owned, women-owned
- **Rule:** Grants are free money but require significant application effort and reporting

## Lender Relationship Building -- Tactical Guide (Leikauf)

### Building Your Lender Network

1. **Maintain relationships with 3-5 financial institutions**
   - 1-2 regional banks (relationship banking, flexibility)
   - 1 national bank (broad products, technology)
   - 1 credit union (lower rates for simple needs)
   - 1 SBA-preferred lender (for government-backed options)

2. **Proactive outreach cadence**
   - Quarterly: Send updated financials to your primary banker
   - Semi-annually: Schedule in-person meetings to discuss business
   - Annually: Review all banking products and pricing
   - As needed: Alert them to issues before they discover them

3. **What to share proactively**
   - Updated financial statements (even when not borrowing)
   - Business achievements and milestones
   - Future plans and capital needs
   - Industry insights that affect your business

### Making the Underwriter's Job Easy

| What to Provide | Why It Matters |
|-----------------|---------------|
| Organized binder of financials | Shows professionalism and preparation |
| Executive summary (1 page) | Gives context before they dig into numbers |
| DSCR calculation (your own) | Demonstrates financial sophistication |
| Clear use-of-funds breakdown | Answers their first question proactively |
| Risk acknowledgment + mitigation plan | Shows awareness and preparation |
| References from vendors and clients | Supports Character assessment |

### Leikauf's Key Principle
"Money is a commodity; trust and access are not." Anyone can find money to borrow. The best business owners build relationships that give them access to the best terms, fastest approvals, and most flexibility when they need it most.
