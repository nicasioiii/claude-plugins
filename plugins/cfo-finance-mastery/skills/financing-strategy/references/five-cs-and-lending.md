# Five Cs of Credit and Lending Reference

## Five Cs -- Deep Dive

### Character (Trustworthiness)

**What lenders look at:**
- Personal credit score (700+ preferred, 750+ ideal)
- Business credit (Dun & Bradstreet Paydex)
- Payment history on existing obligations
- References from other lenders or vendors
- Industry reputation and tenure
- Criminal background (for SBA loans)

**How to strengthen Character:**
1. Pay all personal and business obligations on time (never late)
2. Build a track record with small loans before applying for large ones
3. Maintain clean public records (no liens, judgments, bankruptcies)
4. Build Paydex score: open accounts with D&B-reporting vendors
5. Maintain consistent banking relationship (show stability)

### Capacity (Ability to Repay)

**What lenders look at:**
- **DSCR** (Debt Service Coverage Ratio): EBITDA / Total Annual Debt Service
- Historical cash flow trends (2-3 years)
- Revenue stability vs. volatility
- Existing debt obligations
- Owner's other income sources

**Key thresholds:**

| Metric | Minimum | Preferred | Strong |
|--------|---------|-----------|--------|
| DSCR | 1.0 | 1.25 | 1.5+ |
| Revenue trend | Flat | Growing 5%+ | Growing 10%+ |
| Cash flow consistency | Some volatility | Generally consistent | Very consistent |

**How to strengthen Capacity:**
1. Improve DSCR: cut costs, boost revenue, refinance existing debt to lower payments
2. Demonstrate cash flow stability: smooth seasonal patterns, diversify revenue
3. Reduce existing debt obligations before applying for new debt
4. Show a clear plan for how borrowed funds will generate repayment capacity

### Capital (Skin in the Game)

**What lenders look at:**
- Owner's equity investment in the business
- Retained earnings (profits kept in the business)
- Down payment offered (typically 10-30% for term loans)
- Owner's personal net worth
- Additional equity sources (investors, partners)

**How to strengthen Capital:**
1. Build retained earnings by not over-distributing profits
2. Offer a meaningful down payment (20%+ signals commitment)
3. Show owner's personal investment in the business
4. Demonstrate willingness to share risk with the lender

### Collateral (Pledged Assets)

**What lenders look at:**
- Type and value of assets available to pledge
- Liquidity of collateral (how fast it can be sold)
- Current liens on proposed collateral
- Loan-to-value (LTV) ratio

**Collateral hierarchy (most to least preferred by lenders):**

| Collateral Type | LTV Ratio | Lender Preference |
|----------------|----------|------------------|
| Cash / CDs | 90-100% | Most preferred |
| Accounts receivable | 70-85% | Preferred |
| Equipment | 50-80% | Good |
| Inventory | 30-60% | Acceptable |
| Real estate | 70-80% | Preferred but slow to liquidate |
| Intangible assets | 0-20% | Least preferred |

**How to strengthen Collateral:**
1. Maintain clean asset records with current valuations
2. Know what you can pledge before applying
3. Clear existing liens where possible
4. Consider SBA loans (government guarantee reduces collateral requirements)

### Conditions (External Environment)

**What lenders look at:**
- Industry outlook (growing vs. declining)
- Economic conditions (recession risk, interest rate environment)
- Regulatory changes affecting the industry
- Competitive landscape
- Purpose of the loan (expansion vs. survival)

**How to address Conditions:**
1. Acknowledge industry risks proactively
2. Present a STEP analysis showing you understand external factors (see forecasting-strategy)
3. Show mitigation plans for identified risks
4. Demonstrate resilience: "Here is how we performed during the last downturn"
5. Purpose matters: loans for growth are viewed more favorably than loans for survival

## DSCR -- The Key Lending Ratio

### Formula
```
DSCR = EBITDA / Total Annual Debt Service
  Where Total Annual Debt Service = All loan payments (principal + interest) per year
```

### Interpretation

| DSCR | Meaning | Lender View |
|------|---------|-------------|
| < 1.0 | Business cannot cover its debt payments | **Decline** -- too risky |
| 1.0-1.15 | Barely covering debt | **High risk** -- unlikely approval |
| 1.15-1.25 | Thin but adequate coverage | **Marginal** -- may require additional collateral |
| 1.25-1.50 | Healthy coverage | **Acceptable** -- standard approval range |
| 1.50+ | Strong coverage | **Preferred** -- best rates and terms |

### DSCR Improvement Strategies

1. **Increase EBITDA:** Revenue growth, cost reduction, operational efficiency
2. **Reduce debt service:** Refinance at lower rates, extend terms, pay off small loans
3. **Timing:** Apply when EBITDA is at its strongest (avoid seasonal lows)
4. **Pro forma DSCR:** If borrowing for a specific ROI project, show the pro forma DSCR including projected revenue from the investment

## Lender Red Flags (Leikauf)

What causes automatic scrutiny or decline:

| Red Flag | Why It Matters | How to Fix |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| DSCR < 1.25 | Cannot safely cover payments | Improve EBITDA or reduce existing debt |
| High debt-to-equity | Over-leveraged | Pay down debt, retain more earnings |
| Poor current ratio (< 1.0) | Cannot cover short-term obligations | Improve working capital (see working-capital) |
| Unexplained financial fluctuations | Signals poor management or hidden issues | Clean financials, provide explanations |
| Off-balance sheet obligations | Hidden liabilities | Disclose all obligations transparently |
| Mixing personal and business finances | Unprofessional, unclear picture | Separate immediately |
| Tax returns that do not match financials | Credibility destroyed | Reconcile before applying |
| Multiple MCA obligations | Debt spiral signal | Disclose and show payoff plan |

## Personal Guarantee Requirements

### What It Means
- Owner is **personally liable** if the business cannot repay
- Lender can pursue personal assets (home, savings, investments)
- Standard for most small business loans (under $5M)
- SBA loans require personal guarantee from any 20%+ owner

### Negotiating Personal Guarantees
- Request **limited guarantee** (capped at loan amount, not unlimited)
- Ask for **guarantee release** after meeting specific milestones (3 years on-time payments, DSCR above threshold)
- Build business credit to eventually qualify for **business-only loans**
- Multiple owners: negotiate **proportional guarantees** based on ownership percentage

## Business Credit Building -- Paydex Roadmap

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)
1. Get a DUNS number from Dun & Bradstreet (free)
2. Ensure business is properly registered (EIN, state registration, business address)
3. Open a business bank account and business credit card

### Phase 2: Trade Lines (Months 3-6)
1. Open accounts with D&B-reporting vendors:
   - Office supply companies
   - Shipping companies (UPS, FedEx)
   - Technology vendors (Dell, Apple)
2. Use accounts regularly and pay on time or early

### Phase 3: Growth (Months 6-12)
1. Apply for small business credit lines
2. Continue paying all obligations on time
3. Monitor Paydex score monthly
4. Dispute any errors promptly

### Phase 4: Leverage (Month 12+)
1. Use Paydex score in lending applications
2. Request business-only credit (no personal guarantee) based on business credit strength
3. Continue building trade lines and maintaining perfect payment history

## Lending Interview Preparation

### Questions Lenders Will Ask

| Question | What They Are Really Asking | How to Answer |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------|
| "Why do you need this loan?" | Is this productive or survival? | Specific purpose with ROI projection |
| "How will you repay it?" | Do you have capacity? | Show DSCR and cash flow projections |
| "What is your business plan?" | Do you have a strategy? | Provide 12-month forecast and narrative |
| "What are your biggest risks?" | Are you aware and prepared? | Acknowledge risks with mitigation plans |
| "Why this amount?" | Is the request justified? | Break down exactly how funds will be used |
| "What collateral can you offer?" | What is our downside protection? | Know your assets and their values |

### Presentation Tips (Leikauf)
- **Make the underwriter's job easy** -- organized, clean, complete documentation
- **Present a compelling financial story** -- narrative that connects numbers to strategy
- **Be specific and confident** -- vague answers destroy credibility
- **Proactively address weaknesses** -- "Here is what happened, here is what we did about it"
- **Bring your own DSCR calculation** -- shows financial sophistication
