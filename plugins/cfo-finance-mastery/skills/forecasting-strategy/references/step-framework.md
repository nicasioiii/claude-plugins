# STEP Framework Applied to Forecasting

## What Is STEP?

STEP is a structured framework for identifying **external, macro-level factors** that impact your business forecast. It stands for:

- **Societal** — Demographics, cultural shifts, consumer behavior, labor availability
- **Technological** — AI, automation, digital transformation, new platforms, disruption
- **Economic** — Interest rates, inflation, consumer spending, employment, cost of capital
- **Political** — Regulation, legislation, tax policy, tariffs, insurance requirements

Unlike Porter's Five Forces (which focus on competitive positioning), STEP focuses on **broad environmental shifts** that affect entire markets.

---

## How to Apply STEP in Forecasting

### Step 1: Conduct STEP Audit (30 minutes)

Gather a small team (finance, ops, sales) and brain storm factors in each category affecting your business **over the next 12 months**.

**Societal Factors:**
- Are customer demographics shifting (younger/older, different regions, income levels)?
- Are consumer preferences changing (remote work, sustainability, experience vs. stuff)?
- Is labor availability changing (immigration, skilled labor shortage, wage pressure)?
- Are cultural attitudes shifting (privacy, diversity, AI ethics)?

**Technological Factors:**
- Are emerging technologies disrupting your market (AI replacing manual work, new platforms)?
- Are competitors adopting new tech faster (cloud, automation, AI)?
- Are your customers demanding new capabilities (API integrations, mobile, real-time data)?
- Is your tech stack becoming obsolete?

**Economic Factors:**
- What's the interest rate outlook (Fed policy, cost of capital)?
- What's inflation in your input costs (labor, materials, software)?
- How's consumer discretionary spending (recession risk, unemployment rising)?
- What's your small business cost of capital (30% is baseline; rates rising = higher)?

**Political Factors:**
- Are regulations changing in your industry (data privacy, labor laws, environmental)?
- Are tax policies shifting (corporate tax rate, credits, R&D deductions)?
- Are tariffs or trade policies affecting input costs?
- Are insurance/compliance costs rising?

### Step 2: Estimate Impact (30 minutes)

For each factor, estimate:
1. **Direction:** Positive (tailwind) or Negative (headwind)?
2. **Magnitude:** Small (0–2% impact), Medium (2–5%), Large (>5%)?
3. **Probability:** High (>70%), Medium (40–70%), Low (<40%)?

**Example STEP Audit:**

| Factor | Category | Direction | Magnitude | Probability | Impact on Forecast |
|--------|----------|-----------|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| Rising interest rates | Economic | Negative | Medium | High (80%) | CAC increases as customer borrowing costs rise; demand slips 2–3% |
| AI productivity tools | Technological | Positive | Medium | High (85%) | Our efficiency improves; OpEx growth slows; margins expand 2% |
| Labor shortage easing | Societal | Positive | Small | Medium (55%) | Wage pressure moderates; hiring timeline improves 1–2 months |
| Tariff on imports | Political | Negative | Large | Medium (50%) | COGS rises 5–8%; margin compression unless we pass to customers |
| Shift to remote work (permanent) | Societal | Negative | Medium | High (75%) | Office-related products decline; home office products rise |

### Step 3: Incorporate Into Forecast

**For high-probability, large-impact factors**, adjust your base forecast explicitly.

Example:
```
Base growth assumption (trend continuation): +12%

Adjust for STEP factors:
- Rising interest rates (80% probability, -3% impact): -3% × 0.8 = -2.4%
- AI efficiency gains (85% probability, +1.5% margin improvement): +0.5% gross margin → +0.3% net (using 50% OpEx ratio)
- Tariff risk (50% probability, -5% COGS impact): -3% net if passed to customers partially = -1.5%

Adjusted forecast: +12% - 2.4% + 0.3% - 1.5% = +8.4%
```

## STEP Category Deep Dives

### Societal Factors

**Sub-areas to monitor:**

1. **Demographics**
   - Age composition shift (Millennials now core workforce; Gen Z entering)
   - Geographic migration (urban → suburban; sunbelt growth)
   - Immigration and labor availability
   - Income inequality and wealth gaps

   **Forecast implication:** If customer base is aging, growth may slow. If moving to lower-cost regions, willingness to pay declines.

2. **Consumer Behavior**
   - Shift from ownership to access (car rentals, streaming, subscriptions)
   - Experience-first mindset (travel, events, customization)
   - Sustainability preferences (eco-conscious, ethical sourcing)
   - Convenience premium (willingness to pay for fast delivery, ease)

   **Forecast implication:** If industry shifting to subscriptions, recurring revenue models become necessary. If sustainability critical, competitors adopting green practices may force pricing pressure or R&D investment.

3. **Cultural Attitudes**
   - Stance on privacy, data collection
   - Diversity and inclusion expectations
   - Work-life balance (remote work, flexible schedules)
   - AI ethics and automation backlash

   **Forecast implication:** If privacy concerns rising, compliance costs may increase. If remote work permanent, office space costs declining (positive), but cybersecurity costs rising (negative).

4. **Labor Market**
   - Wage pressure in tight labor markets
   - Skill shortages (software engineers, skilled trades)
   - Burnout and turnover rates rising
   - Gig economy shifting permanent employment models

   **Forecast implication:** If wage pressure rising, OpEx inflation above general inflation. If skill shortage acute, may need higher compensation or outsourcing.

**Societal Forecast Checklist:**
- [ ] Demographics shifting toward or away from my customer base?
- [ ] Consumer preferences changing in ways that help or hurt my product?
- [ ] Cultural attitudes creating tailwinds (e.g., sustainability) or headwinds (e.g., privacy)?
- [ ] Labor costs rising faster than inflation?

### Technological Factors

**Sub-areas to monitor:**

1. **Disruption & Displacement**
   - New platforms replacing incumbents (YouTube displacing cable; Shopify disrupting retail)
   - AI replacing manual work (ChatGPT disrupting customer support, coding)
   - Automation accelerating (robotics, workflow automation, RPA)

   **Forecast implication:** If your competitive advantage is manual labor (content moderation, data entry), automation threatens margins. Invest in new value or prepare for margin compression.

2. **New Opportunities**
   - Emerging platforms (TikTok changed influencer marketing, e-commerce)
   - AI-powered tools (ChatGPT APIs enabling new products, analytics)
   - Interoperability (APIs, integrations connecting previously isolated systems)

   **Forecast implication:** If competitors adopting AI APIs faster, you're at relative disadvantage. Forecast R&D spending to keep pace.

3. **Supply Chain & Operational Tech**
   - Cloud infrastructure scaling down costs
   - Data analytics becoming commoditized
   - Cybersecurity spending increasing

   **Forecast implication:** If moving to cloud, infrastructure costs declining. If compliance/security regulatory, costs rising.

4. **Customer Experience Tech**
   - Mobile-first expectations
   - Real-time data and personalization
   - Omnichannel experiences (web, app, in-store seamlessly)

   **Forecast implication:** If customers demanding omnichannel, tech investment required. If competitors faster, market share risk.

**Technological Forecast Checklist:**
- [ ] Is my market being disrupted by new technology?
- [ ] Are competitors adopting tech faster than me?
- [ ] Do I need to invest in new tech to stay competitive?
- [ ] Are technology costs (cloud, security, data) rising faster than inflation?

---

### Economic Factors

**Sub-areas to monitor:**

1. **Macro Conditions**
   - Interest rate trajectory (Fed policy, inflation)
   - Unemployment and labor availability
   - Consumer discretionary spending (GDP growth, confidence)
   - Currency fluctuations (if export-focused or import-dependent)

   **Forecast implication:** Rising rates → customers delay purchases, cost of capital rises, cash flow tightens. Recession risk → consumer discretionary declines 10–20%.

2. **Input Cost Inflation**
   - Materials (metals, energy, commodities)
   - Labor (wage growth above inflation)
   - Software/services subscriptions
   - Freight and logistics

   **Forecast implication:** If COGS inflation >2% above revenue growth, margin compression. Model cost-pass-through or find efficiencies.

3. **Cost of Capital**
   - Small business cost of capital baseline: ~30%/year (risk premium)
   - Financing availability (easier in low-rate environments)
   - Credit card rates, SBA loan terms, vendor financing

   **Forecast implication:** If cost of capital rising, growth investments less attractive. Debt-funded expansion becomes riskier.

4. **Customer Purchasing Power**
   - B2C: Consumer income, savings, confidence
   - B2B: Corporate profit/cash flow, budget allocation
   - Government: Budget cycles, appropriations

   **Forecast implication:** If customer purchasing power declining, CAC rises (need more discounts/promotion to convert), LTV declines (customers buy less).

**Economic Forecast Checklist:**
- [ ] How is interest rate environment affecting customer willingness to invest?
- [ ] Are input costs (labor, materials) rising faster than I can pass to customers?
- [ ] What's my cost of capital, and how does it affect ROI of growth investments?
- [ ] Is recession risk high? If so, what's my downside scenario?

---

### Political Factors

**Sub-areas to monitor:**

1. **Regulation & Compliance**
   - Industry-specific regulation (healthcare, financial, food safety)
   - Data privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, sector-specific)
   - Labor laws (minimum wage, benefits, classification)
   - Environmental regulations

   **Forecast implication:** New regulation → compliance costs spike. Model headcount (compliance staff), software (compliance tools), legal fees.

2. **Tax Policy**
   - Corporate tax rate
   - R&D credits, manufacturing credits
   - Depreciation rules (bonus depreciation on equipment)
   - Deduction limits (R&D, meals, entertainment)

   **Forecast implication:** R&D credits can offset 15–25% of tech spending (material benefit). Tax rate changes affect net income projections.

3. **Trade & Tariffs**
   - Tariff changes on imports (directly affects COGS if import-dependent)
   - Trade agreements (access to markets, supply chain resilience)
   - Export controls (if selling internationally)

   **Forecast implication:** 10% tariff on Chinese imports → 2–5% COGS increase (depending on import reliance). Model pricing power (can you pass it through?).

4. **Government Spending & Procurement**
   - Government contracts (if B2G): Budget authority, appropriations timing
   - Infrastructure spending (affects construction, supply chain)
   - Stimulus/subsidies (if eligible for pandemic relief, green energy credits)

   **Forecast implication:** Government budget cuts → revenue risk if dependent on gov contracts. Government stimulus → demand surge if your product is in stimulated sector.

**Political Forecast Checklist:**
- [ ] Are regulations changing that affect my cost structure?
- [ ] Are tax policies favorable or headwind to my growth investments?
- [ ] Do tariffs/trade changes affect my supply chain?
- [ ] Am I dependent on government contracts/funding affected by budget cycles?

Use this template to document your STEP analysis:

```
  ...

TECHNOLOGICAL FACTORS
[Repeat structure above]

[Repeat structure above]

[Repeat structure above]

Contingency: [If [major risk factor], then we will [action]]
```

## Common STEP Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Confusing STEP with strategy** → STEP is external environment. Your strategy is how you respond.
2. **Ignoring low-probability factors** → They can still blow up (Black Swan events). List them as downside risks.
3. **Extrapolating trends without limits** → If inflation rising, assume it moderates, not accelerates forever.
4. **Missing second-order effects** → If tariffs rise COGS, customers may also buy less (lower volume) compounding impact.
5. **Updating STEP only at forecast time** → Monitor key factors quarterly; reforecast if major shift occurs.

## Monitoring & Reforecasting Based on STEP

**Set up quarterly monitoring:**
1. Identify 3–5 **key economic indicators** relevant to your business (e.g., unemployment, Fed Funds rate, competitor funding)
2. **Track monthly** (pull from reliable sources: Federal Reserve, Bureau of Labor Stats, industry reports)
3. **Reforecast if indicator moves >2 standard deviations** from assumption

Example:
```
STEP Assumption: Interest rates stay 4.5–5.0% through 2026
Monitoring: Fed Funds Rate (check FRED monthly)
If: Fed Funds Rate exceeds 5.5% OR drops below 3.5%
Then: Recalculate forecast (interest-sensitive businesses reassess)
```

Done consistently, STEP monitoring ensures your forecast **stays current** and you're not blindsided by macro shifts.
