# Marketing Efficiency & CAC/LTV Analysis

## Core Metrics

### Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
```
CAC = Total Marketing Spend / New Customers Acquired

Example:
- Monthly ad spend: $30K
- New customers from ads: 60
- CAC: $30K / 60 = $500 per customer

By channel (track separately):
- Organic: $0 CAC (no paid spend, but opportunity cost of time)
- Referral: $100 CAC (small incentive program)
- Paid ads: $500 CAC
- Outbound sales: $2,000 CAC (salary + overhead)
```

### Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
```
LTV = (Monthly Gross Margin) × (Lifespan in months)

For subscription:
- Monthly margin: $400
- Average customer lifespan: 20 months (5% monthly churn = 1/0.05)
- LTV: $400 × 20 = $8,000

Profitability threshold: LTV > 3× CAC (healthy SaaS metric)
Example: LTV $8,000 / CAC $500 = 16x ratio (excellent)
```

### Return on Ad Spend (ROAS)
```
ROAS = Revenue from Ads / Ad Spend

Example:
- Ad spend: $10K
- Revenue generated: $40K
- ROAS: 4x

Profitability analysis:
- Revenue $40K at 65% gross margin = $26K gross profit
- Ad cost: $10K
- Net profit: $26K - $10K = $16K
- ROI: $16K / $10K = 1.6x (160% return)
```

## Channel-Specific CAC Analysis

### Organic / Search
```
Characteristics:
- CAC: Low to free (but opportunity cost of time)
- Conversion lag: 6–12 months (slow build)
- Customer quality: High intent (actively searching)
- Churn: Lower (committed buyers)
- Scalability: Limited by available keywords

Economics:
- If CAC $0, LTV:CAC ratio infinite (best scenario)
- But requires 6+ months of effort before seeing ROI
- Best for: Long-tail keywords, thought leadership
```

### Paid Ads (Google, Meta, etc.)
```
Characteristics:
- CAC: $200–$1,000+ (depends on competition, conversion)
- Conversion: Immediate to 7 days
- Customer quality: Varies (intent level differs)
- Scalability: High (unlimited budget theoretically)
- Efficiency: Decreases with scale (audience saturation, CPM rise)

Profitability threshold:
- CAC must be <30% of LTV
- Example: LTV $1,000, max CAC $300
- If CAC rising above $300, pause or optimize campaign
```

### Referral
```
Characteristics:
- CAC: Lowest ($0–$200 depending on incentive)
- Conversion: High (warm intro, trusted source)
- Quality: Highest (referred by existing customer)
- Churn: Lowest (referred customers sticky)
- Scalability: Limited (depends on existing customer satisfaction)

Mechanics:
- VIP customers refer 2–3 new customers per year
- Mid-tier customers refer 0.5 customers per year
- Program incentive: $100–$500 per referral

Economics:
- Incentive: $100 per referral
- LTV of referred customer: $8,000
- Net profit per referral: $8,000 - $100 = $7,900
- ROI: 79:1 (phenomenal)
- Scalability: If you have 100 happy customers, could generate 100–200 referrals/year
```

### Content / Thought Leadership
```
Characteristics:
- CAC: Very low ($500–$2,000 for content creation)
- Conversion: Slow (3–12 months to see results)
- Quality: High (educated prospects)
- Churn: Low
- Scalability: High (content compounds over time)

Economics:
- Monthly blog post ($2,000 to create + distribute)
- 12 articles/year = $24K investment
- Attracts 30 new leads/month = 360 leads/year
- CAC: $24K / 360 = $67 per lead (very low)
- Note: Lead ≠ customer (conversion rate 5–10% typical)
- CAC to customer: $67 / 0.07 = ~$957 per customer

Time-to-ROI: 6+ months, but compounds with more content
```

## Optimizing Channel Mix

### Profitability Calculation by Channel

```
Channel          CAC    LTV    Ratio   Gross Margin   Net Profit per Customer
Organic          $100   $8,000  80x    $4,000         $3,900
Referral         $150   $9,000  60x    $4,500         $4,350
Paid Ads         $400   $6,000  15x    $3,000         $2,600
Outbound Sales   $2,000 $7,000  3.5x   $3,500         $1,500

Recommendation: Focus on Organic and Referral (best margins)
Reduce Paid Ads if CAC rising above $400
Deprioritize Outbound Sales (lowest net profit)
```

### Scaling Rules by Channel

**Organic (Build first, then maintain):**
- Invest heavily in content (Year 1-2)
- Expect 6-month lag before seeing ROI
- Once established, compounding effect
- Allocation: 20-30% of marketing budget

**Referral (Low-hanging fruit):**
- Easy to implement (just incentivize happy customers)
- Allocation: 20-30% of marketing budget
- Expected contribution: 10-20% of new customers

**Paid Ads (Scale when profitable):**
- Only scale if LTV:CAC >3:1
- Expect CAC to rise 5-10% every 3 months (audience saturation)
- Allocation: 30-50% of marketing budget (rest reserved for organic/referral)

**Outbound Sales (Use for high-ticket only):**
- Only profitable for deals >$10K ACV
- For small deals (<$1K ACV), too expensive
- Allocation: 10-20% for B2B, rarely used for B2C

## When to Scale vs. Cut

### Scale Marketing Spend If:
1. LTV:CAC ratio >3x (or >2x for e-commerce)
2. CAC stable or declining (not rising)
3. Conversion rate consistent across channels
4. Cash runway sufficient (avoid cash burn trap)

**Math:**
```
Current spend: $50K/month, generating 50 customers, LTV $8,000
CAC: $1,000, Ratio: 8x (excellent)

Increase spend to $75K/month (50% increase)
Expect 75 customers (proportional increase)
CAC: $1,000 (should stay same if channel hasn't saturated)
ROI: Still healthy

But monitor: If CAC rises to $1,200+ after increase, stop scaling.
Signal: Channel saturation or auction price inflation.
```

### Cut Marketing Spend If:
1. LTV:CAC ratio <2x (for SaaS) or <1.5x (for e-commerce)
2. CAC rising >10% month-over-month
3. Conversion rate declining
4. Cash burn unsustainable

**Example:**
```
Channel: Facebook Ads
- CAC was $400
- Now $600 (50% increase in 3 months)
- LTV: $5,000
- Ratio: 8.3x (still healthy)

Decision: Don't cut yet. But:
1. Optimize creative (refresh ads, test new messages)
2. Narrow targeting (reduce wasted impressions)
3. Improve landing page conversion (lower CAC through conversion improvement)

If CAC hits $1,000+ with no improvement, then cut.
```

## A/B Testing Channels

### Test Framework

```
Control: Current channel/campaign
Test: Alternative channel/creative/audience

Metrics:
- CAC (cost per customer acquired)
- Conversion rate (visitors → customers)
- LTV (are test customers as valuable?)

Sample size: Minimum 100 customers per group (30 days)

Example:
Control: Facebook Ads, broad targeting, CAC $400, conversion 2%
Test: Facebook Ads, narrow lookalike targeting, CAC $320, conversion 2.5%

Result: Test wins (lower CAC, higher conversion)
Action: Shift more budget to test approach
```

## Key Principles

1. **Track CAC by channel separately** — Aggregate CAC is useless (hides underperformers)
2. **Calculate true LTV** — Include actual churn, not optimistic retention
3. **Scale profitably** — Don't grow spend just to grow; only if ROI positive
4. **Monitor CAC trends** — Rising CAC signals saturation; adjust early
5. **Referral is best** — Highest LTV, lowest CAC; invest in customer happiness first
6. **Organic compounds** — Slow initially, but most scalable long-term
7. **Test before scaling** — A/B test on small budget before committing

Done well, marketing efficiency ensures every dollar drives profitable growth.
