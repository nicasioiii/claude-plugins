# Pricing Strategy Reference

## The Pricing Spectrum (Leikauf)

```
VALUE-BASED PRICING (top)
  Price just below the value you create for the client
  Best for: differentiated offerings, consulting, professional services
  Example: If your work saves a client $200K/year, charge $50-80K

SWEET SPOT
  Intersection of your cost floor + client affordability + perceived value
  This is where most healthy businesses should price

COST-PLUS MARKUP (bottom)
  Cover expenses + desired profit margin
  Minimum 3x markup on delivery cost
  Best for: commodity products, standardized services
  Example: If delivery costs $2K, charge minimum $6K

DANGER ZONES:
  TOO LOW:
  - Insufficient profit to reinvest
  - Cannot scale (no margin for hiring, marketing)
  - Attracts price-sensitive clients who drain resources
  - Leads to burnout (working more for less)

  TOO HIGH:
  - Client dissatisfaction and churn
  - Reputation risk if value does not match price
  - Competitors undercut successfully
```

## Value-Based Pricing Deep Dive

### Core Principle
Price based on **outcomes delivered**, not time or inputs. The client pays for the value received, not the hours spent.

### Implementation Steps

1. **Quantify the value you create**
   - Revenue increase: "Our work added $X in new revenue"
   - Cost savings: "We reduced expenses by $Y"
   - Risk avoidance: "We prevented a $Z compliance penalty"
   - Time savings: "We freed up 20 hours/month of owner time"

2. **Set price as a fraction of value**
   - Rule of thumb: charge 20-40% of the value created
   - Example: If you save $100K/year, charge $20-40K/year
   - This creates a clear ROI story: "For every $1 you pay me, you get $2.50-5 back"

3. **Communicate value before price**
   - Always present the value first, then the price
   - Frame as investment, not cost
   - Use Recommendation-Data-Recommendation format (see advisory-communication)

### Value Pricing for Different Business Types

| Business Type | Value Metric | Pricing Approach |
|--------------|-------------|-----------------|
| E-commerce | ROAS improvement, margin increase | % of incremental profit |
| Services | Revenue growth, cost savings | Fixed project fee based on scope |
| Manufacturing | Waste reduction, throughput increase | Share of savings |
| Retail | Inventory turn improvement, margin lift | Flat fee + performance bonus |

## Anchoring (Leikauf)

### How It Works
Present a high reference price first so the actual price feels reasonable by comparison.

### Tactics

1. **Three-tier proposals** -- Show a premium option ($50K), a recommended option ($25K), and a basic option ($15K). Most clients pick the middle.

2. **Industry benchmark anchoring** -- "Companies your size typically invest $X in this area. We offer it for $Y."

3. **Value-first anchoring** -- "This will save you $200K/year. The investment is $40K."

4. **Previous price anchoring** -- "This service typically runs $X. Because of [reason], your price is $Y."

### Rules
- The anchor must be credible (not absurdly high)
- Always anchor before revealing your actual price
- Use anchoring in proposals, not just verbal conversations (written prices anchor harder)

## Decoy Pricing (SaaS Menu Model)

### How It Works
Offer three options where one is designed to make another look more attractive.

### Classic Structure

```
OPTION A (Basic): $99/month
  - Core features only
  - Limited support

OPTION B (Professional): $199/month  <-- TARGET
  - All features
  - Priority support
  - Monthly review

OPTION C (Enterprise): $499/month    <-- DECOY
  - Same as B + dedicated account manager
  - The price jump from B to C makes B look like great value
```

### When to Use
- Subscription or retainer pricing
- Service packages with multiple tiers
- Product bundles

### Rules
- The decoy should be noticeably more expensive but only marginally better
- The target option should be clearly the best value
- Limit to 3 options (more creates decision paralysis)

## Price Testing

### Methods

| Method | How | Best For |
|--------|-----|----------|
| **A/B test** | Show different prices to different segments | E-commerce, digital products |
| **Geographic test** | Different prices by region | Multi-location businesses |
| **Time-based test** | Test new price for 30-60 days | Services, B2B |
| **Segment test** | Different prices by customer tier | Businesses with clear segments |

### Implementation Playbook

1. **Select test segment** -- Choose your least price-sensitive group (often your best customers)
2. **Set test duration** -- Minimum 60 days to account for buying cycles
3. **Define success metrics** -- Revenue change, conversion change, churn rate
4. **Run the test** -- Increase price 5-10% on the test segment
5. **Measure churn** -- If churn is under 5%, the increase was too conservative
6. **Roll out or adjust** -- Expand to full base or modify based on results

### Price Increase Communication

1. Send email with 30-day notice of new pricing
2. If pushback: **call to explain value** (never negotiate over email)
3. Script: "We have been together [time], gotten you [results], and in order to continue the same level of service, the new price will be [X]."
4. Typically 80% stay without further discussion
5. If resistance: offer small concession (e.g., $100 off)
6. Less than 1% of clients actually leave over price increases

## Pricing Anti-Patterns

1. **Billing hourly** -- Creates adversarial relationship; client wants fewer hours, you want more
2. **Discounting without strategy** -- Trains clients to wait for sales; erodes brand value
3. **Undercharging** -- Leads to overwork, no growth, bad clients, owning a job instead of a business
4. **Skipping the pricing conversation** -- Address price on the first call; do not defer
5. **Competing on price alone** -- Race to the bottom; only works at massive scale

## Industry Pricing Benchmarks (Leikauf)

These are reference points, not rules:

| Category | Benchmark |
|----------|-----------|
| Healthy service firms | 50-60% operating margins |
| Professional services delivery | 80%+ gross margin target |
| Minimum effective hourly rate | $300/hour (target $500+) |
| CFO-level services | $1,500-$5,000/month recurring |
| Product businesses | Minimum 3x markup on COGS |

**Key insight from Leikauf:** "7% of any market spends 70% of the money." Focus on premium clients, not price-shoppers.
