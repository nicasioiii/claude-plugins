# Profit Levers Reference

## Profit Tree -- Detailed Breakdown

### Revenue Branch

#### Price Levers
- Value-based pricing (see pricing-strategy.md)
- Price increases on existing products/services
- Premium tier creation
- Reduce discounting (especially for VIP customers)
- Bundling to increase average order value

#### Volume Levers
- Customer acquisition (new channels, new markets)
- Customer retention (reduce churn, loyalty programs)
- Customer reactivation (win-back campaigns for lapsed buyers)
- Referral programs (existing customers bring new ones)
- Conversion rate optimization (better sales process)

#### Mix Levers
- Shift sales toward higher-margin products/services
- Discontinue or reprice low-margin offerings
- Introduce premium versions of existing products
- Geographic or channel mix optimization

### COGS Branch

#### Materials
- Strategic procurement: consolidate vendors, commit volume
- Negotiate every 6 months with all suppliers (see working-capital skill)
- Source alternatives without sacrificing quality
- Reduce waste and scrap in production
- Bulk purchasing where carrying cost is justified

#### Labor (Direct)
- Loaded labor cost analysis: salary + benefits + taxes + overhead + equipment
- Productivity improvement: output per labor hour
- Automation of repetitive production tasks
- Cross-training to reduce idle time
- Outsource non-core production activities

#### Overhead (Production)
- Utilization rate: % of capacity actually used
- Step-fixed cost awareness: plan for threshold triggers
- Lease vs. buy decisions for equipment
- Energy and facility optimization

### SG&A Branch

#### Fixed Costs
- Renegotiate leases and contracts annually
- Right-size office/warehouse before scaling
- Evaluate technology stack ROI quarterly
- Shared services or co-location to reduce overhead

#### Variable Costs
- Marketing ROI analysis by channel (ROAS, CPA, CLV:CAC ratio)
- Commission structure alignment with profit (not just revenue)
- Travel and entertainment policy with clear limits
- Subscription and recurring charge audit (credit card trick)

## Cost Reduction Tactics

### The Systematic Approach

1. **Categorize all expenses** -- Fixed, variable, step-fixed, discretionary
2. **Rank by magnitude** -- Sort expenses by dollar amount, largest first
3. **Evaluate ROI** -- For each line item: "What does this produce?"
4. **Identify quick wins** -- Expenses that can be reduced without operational impact
5. **Plan medium-term reductions** -- Requires process changes or renegotiation
6. **Strategic investments** -- Some costs should increase if ROI is positive

### Cost Reduction by Category

| Category | Tactic | Typical Savings |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| Software/subscriptions | Audit and cancel unused | $500-$5K/month |
| Vendor contracts | Renegotiate every 6 months | 5-15% per vendor |
| Insurance | Shop rates annually, bundle policies | 10-20% |
| Shipping | Consolidate carriers, negotiate volume rates | 8-15% |
| Professional services | RFP or benchmark every 2 years | 10-25% |
| Facilities | Right-size, sublease unused space | Variable |
| Marketing | Kill underperforming channels, reallocate | 15-30% of waste |

### The Credit Card Trick (Leikauf)

1. Issue a new credit card number to the business
2. Route ALL new charges to the new card
3. The old card: every charge that appears is a recurring subscription or hidden cost
4. Review and cancel anything that does not produce clear ROI
5. Typical finding: $500-$5,000/month in unnecessary recurring charges

## Productivity Metrics -- Deep Dive

### Revenue Per Employee

```
Revenue Per Employee = Total Revenue / Total FTE
```

- Track monthly or quarterly
- Compare against industry benchmarks
- Declining metric signals: overstaffing, declining revenue, or both
- Service businesses: target $150K-$300K per employee
- Product businesses: varies widely by industry

### Profit Per Employee

```
Profit Per Employee = Net Income / Total FTE
```

- The true productivity measure
- Reveals whether headcount adds are creating value
- If revenue per employee rises but profit per employee falls: cost structure problem

### Loaded Labor Cost

```
Loaded Cost = Base Salary + Benefits + Payroll Taxes + Equipment + Training + Overhead Allocation
```

- Rule of thumb: loaded cost is 1.25-1.4x base salary
- Use for all hiring decisions and pricing calculations
- Compare loaded cost against revenue generated per person

### Utilization Rate (Services Businesses)

```
Utilization = Billable Hours / Total Available Hours
```

- Target: 65-80% for professional services
- Below 60%: overstaffed or pricing too low
- Above 85%: at risk of burnout and quality issues

## Customer Lifetime Value (CLV)

### Basic Formula

```
CLV = Average Revenue Per Customer Per Year x Average Customer Lifespan (years)
```

### Advanced Formula

```
CLV = (Average Order Value x Purchase Frequency x Gross Margin %) x Average Lifespan
```

### CLV:CAC Ratio

```
CLV:CAC = Customer Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost
```

| Ratio | Interpretation |
|-------|---------------|
| < 1:1 | Losing money on every customer acquired |
| 1:1 to 3:1 | Breaking even or marginally profitable |
| 3:1 to 5:1 | Healthy, sustainable growth |
| > 5:1 | Under-investing in growth (or great unit economics) |

### CLV Improvement Levers
1. **Increase average order value** -- Bundles, upsells, premium options
2. **Increase purchase frequency** -- Loyalty programs, subscriptions, reorder reminders
3. **Increase lifespan** -- Reduce churn, improve onboarding, deliver exceptional service
4. **Improve margin** -- Pricing optimization, cost reduction on delivery

## RFM Analysis -- Implementation Guide

### Step 1: Score Each Customer (1-5 scale)

| Dimension | Score 5 (Best) | Score 1 (Worst) |
|-----------|---------------|-----------------|
| **Recency** | Bought this week | Has not bought in 6+ months |
| **Frequency** | Buys weekly/monthly | Bought once ever |
| **Monetary** | Top 20% by spend | Bottom 20% by spend |

### Step 2: Create Segments

| RFM Score | Segment | Strategy |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| 5-5-5 | **Champions** | VIP treatment, early access, premium offers. NEVER discount. |
| 4-4-4 to 5-5-4 | **Loyal** | Cross-sell, reward program, testimonial requests |
| 3-3-3 to 4-4-3 | **Potential** | Upsell, education, nurture toward loyalty |
| 2-3-4 to 3-2-3 | **At-risk** | Win-back campaign, personal outreach, "we miss you" offers |
| 1-1-1 to 2-2-2 | **Low-value** | Automate outreach, minimal human effort (80/20 rule) |

### Step 3: Allocate Resources

Apply the 80/20 rule:
- Spend 80% of sales/marketing effort on top 20% of customers (Champions + Loyal)
- Automate engagement for the bottom 50%
- Invest moderately in "At-risk" and "Potential" segments

### Key Insight (Leikauf)
"High-value customers get VIP experiences and upsells -- NOT discounts. Discounting your best customers trains them to wait for sales and destroys your premium positioning."

## Revenue Diversification Framework

### Concentration Risk Assessment

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|--------|---------|---------|----------|
| Top customer % of revenue | < 15% | 15-30% | > 30% |
| Top 5 customers % | < 40% | 40-60% | > 60% |
| Single product/service % | < 50% | 50-70% | > 70% |
| Single channel % | < 60% | 60-80% | > 80% |

### Diversification Tactics

1. **Customer diversification** -- Acquire new customers in adjacent segments
2. **Product/service diversification** -- Add complementary offerings
3. **Channel diversification** -- Sell through new channels (online, wholesale, partnerships)
4. **Geographic diversification** -- Expand to new regions or markets
5. **Revenue model diversification** -- Add recurring revenue alongside project-based

### Building a Revenue Culture (Leikauf)

Every team member contributes to growth:
- **Sales team:** Direct revenue generation
- **Operations team:** Efficiency that funds growth investment
- **Customer service:** Retention and upsell opportunities
- **Finance team:** Identifying margin opportunities and pricing insights
- **Leadership:** Setting revenue targets and accountability

## Profit Opportunity Scoring Matrix

Use this to prioritize which levers to pull first:

| Lever | Impact ($) | Effort (1-5) | Speed (days) | Risk | Score |
|-------|-----------|-------------|-------------|------|-------|
| Price increase | High | 1 (low) | 30 | Low churn risk | Pull first |
| Cancel unused subscriptions | Low-Med | 1 | 7 | None | Quick win |
| Vendor renegotiation | Medium | 2 | 60 | Minimal | High value |
| RFM-based marketing shift | Medium | 3 | 90 | Low | Medium-term |
| Process automation | Medium | 4 | 120 | Execution | Strategic |
| New revenue stream | High | 5 (high) | 180+ | Significant | Long-term |

**Process:** Score every opportunity, rank by Impact/Effort ratio, execute in order.
