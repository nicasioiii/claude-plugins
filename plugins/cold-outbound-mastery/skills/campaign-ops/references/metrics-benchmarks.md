# Campaign Metrics & Benchmarks by Channel

## Email Outreach Metrics

### Primary KPIs (What to Track)
| Metric | Target | Formula | Importance |
|--------|--------|---------|-----------|
| Open Rate | 80% | Opens / Emails Sent | Critical - indicates subject line + deliverability |
| Reply Rate | 15% | Replies / Emails Sent | Critical - indicates copy quality |
| Meeting Book Rate | 4-8% | Meetings Booked / Emails Sent | Primary revenue metric |
| Bounce Rate | <8% | Bounces / Emails Sent | Critical - indicates list quality |
| Click Rate | 0% | Clicks / Emails Sent | Should stay low (no links wanted - reduces meeting bookings) |
| Response Time | Day 1-3 | % replies within first 3 days | 60-70% respond same day or day 2 |
| Follow-Up Response | 60% | % replies come from follow-ups | 70% of conversions happen in follow-ups |

### Volume Metrics
- **Email per domain per day:** 50-100 (starting conservative, scaling up)
- **Emails per day (multi-domain):** 100 → 250 → 500 → 1000+ (each domain adds capacity)
- **Domains needed for scale:**
  - 100-250/day: 1 domain
  - 250-500/day: 3-5 domains
  - 500-1000/day: 5-10 domains
  - 1000+/day: 10+ domains with infrastructure

### Campaign-Level Example
- **Batch size:** 100 emails
- **Expected outcomes:** 80 opens, 15 replies, 4-8 meetings booked, <8 bounces

### List Quality Indicators
| Issue | Signal | Solution |
|-------|--------|----------|
| High bounces | >8% bounce rate | List is outdated or poor quality - validate with NeverBounce |
| Spam folder placement | Emails arriving in SPAM, not Primary | Deliverability issue - check domain authentication |
| Low engagement | <70% opens, <10% replies | Copy/offer is weak OR domain needs warmup |
| Segmentation problem | Some segments 80% open, others 30% | Tailor copy to each segment separately |

## Cold Calling / Phone Outreach Metrics

### Core KPIs (Sean Longden Framework)
| Metric | Target | Formula | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------|-------|
| Contact Rate | 46.5% | Contacts Reached / Total Calls | Benchmark from 5000+ call study |
| Contact-to-Appointment | 22.2% | Appointments / Contacts Reached | Of those you reach, 1 in 4-5 books |
| Overall Call-to-Meeting | ~10% | Meetings Booked / Total Calls Dialed | Combined contact + conversion rate |
| Voicemail Rate | 53.5% | Voicemails / Total Calls | Majority are voicemails (address in sequence) |
| Voicemail Response | Varies | Return calls from voicemails | Follow-up cadence determines response |
| Avg Call Time | 3-5 min | Contact calls only | Disconnects typically <30 sec |
| Qualified Calls per Day | 3+ | Meetings booked | Daily KPI target for SDRs |

### Daily Calling Schedule
- **Best days:** Wednesday-Thursday (Monday/Friday lower reach rate)
- **Best times:** 8-10 AM and 4-5 PM
- **Do not call:** Before 8 AM, after 9 PM, on Sundays or holidays
- **Daily blocks:** 2 hours morning + 1 hour afternoon = 3 hours dialing/day
- **Expected dials:** 40-50 calls per day (with 20-30 min of prep/follow-up)

### Voicemail Strategy (Critical)
- **Length:** 20-25 seconds max
- **Content:** Name, company, reason for call, specific value prop, call-back number
- **Follow-up:** 3-5 voicemail drops across different times/days
- **Combined with email:** Day 1 (call) → Day 1-2 (voicemail drop) → Day 2 (email) → Day 4-5 (second call)
- **Expected voicemail callback:** 5-10% of voicemails

### Role-Based Conversion Rates
- **CEO/President:** 10-15% contact rate (harder to reach)
- **VP/Director:** 40-50% contact rate (good reach window)
- **Manager:** 60-70% contact rate (easier to reach, lower buying power)

## Direct Message (DM) Outreach Metrics

### Core KPIs (Jason Fox Framework)
| Metric | Target | Formula | Notes |
|--------|--------|---------|-------|
| New Conversations/Day | 20-50 | Messages sent that initiate conversation | Volume target varies by channel |
| Follow-ups/Day | 100+ | Total follow-up messages sent | Bulk of work is follow-up not initial outreach |
| Qualified Calls/Day | 3 | Meetings booked from DM sequences | Daily KPI goal |
| Conversation Response Rate | Varies | % of initial messages getting replies | 30-50% reply on good messaging |
| Warm DM Conversion | 10-20% | DMs to qualified calls (warm audience) | Much higher than cold |
| Cold DM Conversion | 2-5% | DMs to qualified calls (cold audience) | Lower conversion, use for content visibility |
| Engagement Rate on Content | Varies | Likes/comments on posted content | Leading indicator for DM receptiveness |

### Platform-Specific Benchmarks
| Platform | Primary/Secondary | New DMs/Day | Conversion | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Facebook | Primary | 20-30 | 10-20% (warm) | Best for warm audience DMs |
| Instagram | Primary | 15-25 | 10-20% (warm) | Best for visual/lifestyle businesses |
| LinkedIn | Secondary | 10-20 | 5-15% (warm) | Professional positioning (lower conversion initially) |
| Twitter/X | Secondary | 10-20 | 2-5% | Community building + organic reach |
| TikTok | Secondary | 5-15 | 2-5% | Newer audience, lower conversion |

### DM Conversion Phases
1. **Cold/Rapport (Phase 1):** 3-7 messages building genuine connection (no pitch)
2. **Warm/Situational (Phase 2):** Introduce business, ask if they're open (casual)
3. **Hot/Close (Phase 3):** CTA to call or product (only after rapport built)

### Content + DM Strategy
- **Content first:** Post content, let audience engage, then DM engaged users (converts better)
- **Conversion improvement:** Warm DMs (from engaged followers) = 3-5x better than pure cold
- **Time to conversion:** 5-7 days of DM sequence average before booking

## List & Lead Quality Metrics

### List Health Indicators
| Indicator | Good | Warning | Critical |
|-----------|------|---------|----------|
| Bounce Rate | <5% | 5-8% | >8% |
| Unsubscribe Rate | <0.5% | 0.5-1% | >1% |
| Complaint Rate | <0.1% | 0.1-0.3% | >0.3% |
| Open Rate Trend | Stable/Growing | Declining slowly | Declining fast |
| Reply Rate Trend | Stable/Growing | Declining slowly | Declining fast |

### List Age & Engagement Decay
- **New list (0-2 weeks):** Highest engagement, best results
- **2-4 weeks:** Engagement starts declining (5-10% drop in open rate)
- **4-8 weeks:** Significant decline (20-30% drop in open rate)
- **8+ weeks:** List fatigue (40-50% lower open rate than week 1)
- **Reactivation:** Need break of 2-4 weeks, then re-test with new angle/offer

| Emails Sent | 50-100 | 250-500 | 1000-2000 |
| Follow-ups Completed | 20-30 | 100-150 | 400-600 |
| Show-up Rate on Calls | 80%+ | 80%+ | 80%+ |
| Conversion Rate (Call → Deal) | Varies by close | Varies | 10-30% |

### Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) by Channel
- **Email:** $2-10 per meeting (assuming $200-500 software, 15% conversion to customer)
- **Phone:** $15-40 per meeting (accounting for SDR time + voicemail tools)
- **LinkedIn:** $5-20 per qualified connection (lower conversion initially)
- **DM:** $10-30 per meeting (requires content creation + platform time)
- **Combined:** Often 20-40% lower CPA than single-channel

### Team Scaling Economics
- **1 SDR:** 60-90 meetings/month (3/day × 20-30 work days)
- **2 SDRs:** 120-180 meetings/month (compound growth + systems)
- **5 SDRs:** 300-450 meetings/month (specialized roles: warm, phone, DMs)
- **10 SDRs:** 600-900 meetings/month (with specialized channel managers)

## Diagnostics Quick Reference

### When metrics are GOOD
- Open rate 75-85% → Subject lines and deliverability working
- Reply rate 12-20% → Copy and offer resonating
- Meeting book 6-10% → Follow-up sequence is strong
- Bounce <5% → List quality is excellent

### When metrics are DECLINING
- Open rate dropping week-over-week → Deliverability issue or list fatigue (change angle/segment)
- Reply rate dropping → Copy is stale or audience no longer fit (test new angle)
- Bounce rate jumping → List source changed or quality degraded (validate list immediately)
- No meetings despite replies → Follow-up sequence broken or objection handling weak

### Realistic Funnel Model

**500 emails → 100 opens (20%) → 25 replies (5%) → 8 positive replies (30%) → 4 meetings (50%) → 1 client (25% close)**

~**0.2% end-to-end conversion** for average performers. This is why volume and targeting matter at scale.

### Performance Levers (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Hook type** — Timeline hooks outperform problem hooks by 3.4x in meetings
2. **Personalization depth** — Up to 250% more replies with deep personalization
3. **Brevity** — 25–75 words optimal, 83% more replies under 75 words
4. **Targeting precision** — ≤50 contacts per campaign = 2.76x higher reply rates
5. **Follow-up strategy** — First follow-up adds 49% more replies
6. **Reading level** — 3rd–5th grade = 67% more replies
7. **Send timing** — Thursday peaks at 6.87% reply rate

### Declining Effectiveness Trend

Reply rates dropped from 7–8% (2020–2022) to 4–5.8% (2024–2025), **~15% YoY decline**.

**Drivers:**
- Inbox saturation (10+ cold emails/week, 20% say none relevant)
- Stricter anti-spam filters (Google's threshold: 0.1% complaints)
- AI email flood (more volume, less quality signal)

**Key insight:** Writing craft matters more, not less. Gap between average and excellent is widening.

### Response Rates by Seniority

- **Entry-level:** 8% reply, 50% open (highest engagement)
- **C-level:** 6.4% reply (23% more likely to respond than non-C-suite)
- **CTOs/VP Tech:** 7.68% reply
- **CEOs/Founders:** 7.63% reply
- **Heads of Sales:** 6.60% reply (most targeted role, highest saturation)

### Industry Variation

**Highest responding:** Nonprofits (16.5%+), legal (10%), EdTech (7.8%), chemical (7.3%), manufacturing (6.1%).

**Lowest responding:** SaaS (3.5%), financial services (3.4%), IT services (3.5%).

### Cultural Calibration

| Factor | US | UK | Germany/DACH | Scandinavia |
|--------|----|----|--------|------|
| Tone | Direct, casual | Polite, professional | Precise, data-driven | Fact-based, egalitarian |
| Length | Shorter, blunt | Longer, insight-led | Detail-oriented | Concise but substantive |
| Social proof | Outcome numbers | Research-led credibility | Technical precision | Shared values |

**Geographic performance:**
- North America: 4.1% response
- Europe: 3.1%
- Asia-Pacific: 2.8%

Shorter, more direct sequences work better in US. UK needs more insight/personality. GDPR affects European tone.
