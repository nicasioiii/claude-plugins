# Multi-Channel Orchestration: Email + LinkedIn + Phone + DMs

## Multi-Channel Strategy Framework

### Why Multi-Channel?
Single channel metrics:
- Email only: 4-8% meeting book rate
- LinkedIn only: 3-5% meeting book rate
- Phone only: 8-12% meeting book rate
- DMs only: 2-5% meeting book rate

Multi-channel combined:
- Email + LinkedIn: 8-12% meeting book rate (50% improvement)
- Email + LinkedIn + Phone: 12-20% meeting book rate (150% improvement)
- Email + LinkedIn + Phone + DM: 15-25% meeting book rate (2-3x improvement)

**Key insight:** Each channel "warms up" the prospect for the next. Email gives context. LinkedIn confirms it's real person. Phone breaks through. DM follows after engagement.

---

## The 10-Day Multi-Channel Sequence

### Goal
Comprehensive outreach with 4 distinct touchpoints, each adding context and warming them for the next.

### Day-by-Day Breakdown

#### **Day 0: Email #1 (Initial) - 8:00 AM**
**Goal:** Introduce yourself, provide context, soft ask

**Content:**
- Subject: Personalized opening (mention something about them)
- Body: 3C structure (Compliment, Case study, CTA)
- CTA: Yes/no question (not calendar link yet)
- Length: 50-100 words
- No links (except to company website context if needed)

**Example:**
```
Subject: Saw your Q2 earnings call re: CAC challenges

Hi [Name],

Saw your recent earnings call where you mentioned CAC challenges for [product]. Caught my attention because we've worked with 12 similar companies to reduce CAC by 34% using [approach].

Quick question - are you actively looking to improve that metric, or waiting until next quarter?

[Your name]
[Your company]
[Phone]
```

**Follow-up:** Monitor for replies. Expect 20-30% reply rate on good first lines.

---

#### **Day 1: Wait + Prepare**
- Do NOT email same person today
- Monitor replies from previous batch
- Prepare LinkedIn message for tomorrow
- Prepare phone script for Day 5

---

#### **Day 2: LinkedIn Message (2:00 PM)**
**Goal:** Confirm real person, add social proof context, soften objections

**Action 1: Send LinkedIn Connection Request**
- Connection message: Reference email from 2 days ago
- Example: "[Name], sent you an email about [specific context] last week. Thought we should connect."
- No pitch yet, just context + connection

**Action 2: If They Connect, Send Message**
- Delay 1-2 hours after connection
- Reference email again (shows coordination)
- Add specific value or social proof
- Example: "Thanks for connecting! BTW - similar company in your space (also hit $50M ARR) reduced customer acquisition by 34%. Found that interesting given your recent earnings call. Wanted to share if relevant."

**Why Day 2?**
- Enough time for them to read email
- LinkedIn message acts as "reminder" and confirms legitimacy
- Adds social proof layer without pushing for meeting

**Expected response:** 15-25% reply rate on LinkedIn from email responders

---

#### **Day 3: Email #2 (Follow-up 1) - 9:00 AM**
**Goal:** Reference LinkedIn, add new angle or social proof, move closer to meeting ask

**Structure:**
1. Reference previous email + LinkedIn
2. Add new angle (different from initial angle)
3. Soft ask for meeting

**Example:**
```
Subject: RE: [Original subject] + one more angle

Hi [Name],

Saw you connected with me on LinkedIn yesterday - appreciate that.

Quick follow-up on my first email. Beyond the CAC reduction, we also helped companies in your space improve LTCV by focusing on [specific tactic]. Seems like something worth exploring given [specific context about them].

If this resonates, worth a quick 15-minute call to see if there's fit. If not, no worries - I'll stop bothering you after this!

[Your name]
```

**Key elements:**
- Reference previous touches (shows attention, builds trust)
- New angle (not repeating first email)
- Soft ask ("quick 15-minute call")
- Permission to stop (lowers resistance)

**Follow-up:** Expect 40-50% of email responders to reply at this stage

---

#### **Day 4: Wait**
- Do NOT email same person today
- Monitor email #2 replies
- Prepare voicemail for tomorrow
- Prepare phone script for Day 5

---

#### **Day 5: Phone Call (4:00 PM)**
**Goal:** Break through via phone, qualify quickly, book meeting

**Pre-Call Preparation:**
1. Research person: LinkedIn profile, recent news, company updates
2. Prepare talking points (3-5 max)
3. Have calendar ready to book time
4. If voicemail likely: Script 20-25 second voicemail

**Phone Script (Sean Longden Framework):**

```
[Ring]
[If they answer]
Hi [Name], this is [Your name] from [Company]. I know you probably get a bunch of these calls.
Quick reason for calling - I noticed [specific signal: earnings call, LinkedIn post, funding news].
Got curious because we've been working with similar companies to solve [specific problem].
Do you have 30 seconds to see if this is even relevant to you?

[If they say yes]
Quick question - is [problem area] something you're actively working on right now, or is it more of a back burner item?

[If they say yes to problem]
Good. That's really the only reason I called. Might be worth 15 minutes to see if there's fit. You free Thursday at 2pm?

[If they say no]
No problem. Who would be the right person on your team to talk to about [problem area]?

[If voicemail]
Hi [Name], this is [Your name] from [Company]. Reason for calling - I noticed [signal].
Been working with similar companies to [solve problem]. Thought you might find it relevant.
My number is [number]. If not a fit, no worries. Thanks!
```

**Why afternoon (4 PM)?**
- Better success rate in early evening
- People less rushed than morning
- Still business hours (8 AM - 9 PM compliance)

**Expected outcome:** 10-15% answer rate, 30-40% of those agree to meeting

---

#### **Day 6: Voicemail Drop (10:00 AM)**
**Goal:** If they didn't answer Day 5 phone, leave voicemail + send email

**Action 1: Send Email**
- Subject: "Left you a voicemail"
- Body: Brief email saying "Tried to call you yesterday about [topic]. Seems relevant given [context]. Calendar link below to schedule time if it makes sense."
- Include calendar link (now you can ask for calendar booking)

**Action 2: If Time, Call Again**
- Different time of day than Day 5 (try 8:30 AM instead of 4 PM)
- Slightly different angle: "Tried reaching you yesterday. Wanted to follow up quickly about [different angle]."

**Expected outcome:** 5-10% callback rate from voicemail

---

#### **Day 7: Email #3 (Follow-up 2) - 10:00 AM**
**Goal:** New angle, case study, permission-based (last attempt)

**Structure:**
1. Acknowledge previous touches (email 1, LinkedIn, phone, voicemail)
2. Completely new angle or social proof
3. Make ask very small (permission to send resource, one specific question)
4. Give permission to opt out

**Example:**
```
Subject: Different angle on [problem] - might be relevant

Hi [Name],

Know you're probably busy. Quick follow-up on my previous messages about [problem].

Wanted to share something different that came up with [similar company]. They were also dealing with [specific challenge] and found that [specific tactic] moved the needle more than expected.

Not trying to push you into a call. Just thought it was worth sharing given [context about their company].

If this seems completely off-base, I'll respect that. Just reply with "not interested" and I'll remove you from future emails. If it does seem relevant, would love to chat for 15 minutes.

[Your name]
```

**Key elements:**
- NEW angle (3rd distinct approach)
- Specific case study
- Give permission to opt out ("not interested")
- Small ask (15 min call)

**Follow-up:** This is final soft ask before "final attempt"

---

#### **Day 8: Wait**
- Monitor Email #3 replies
- If they replied: Book meeting (move to close)
- If no reply: Prepare final attempt

---

#### **Day 9: Email #4 (Follow-up 3, Final) - 8:30 AM**
**Goal:** Last attempt, different channel or high-value angle

**Option 1: Standard Final Email**
```
Subject: One last thing (and then I'll stop)

Hi [Name],

This will be my last email. Promise.

Just wanted to share one more thing: [Different angle or social proof]. Thought you might find it valuable.

If now's not the right time, I totally get it. I'll check back in [3 months]. If that timing doesn't work, just let me know.

All the best,
[Your name]
```

**Option 2: DM-Based Final Attempt** (if they're active on LinkedIn)
Send via LinkedIn DM instead of email:
"Hi [Name], I know I've sent a few emails. Just wanted to say I get it if now's not the right time. Wanted to share one more resource that came up with a similar company though [specific resource]. If you ever want to chat, feel free to reach out."

**Option 3: Phone Call (Day 9, 4 PM)**
If they haven't responded: Final voicemail
"Hi [Name], [Your name] again from [Company]. This is actually my last attempt. Just want to make sure you didn't miss something important. Attached I sent [specific resource]. Thought it was worth your time. My number is [number]. Hope you find it useful."

---

#### **Day 10+: Sunset**
- If no response by Day 10: Mark as "not interested" in CRM
- Do NOT continue emailing
- Restart sequence only if they show new engagement signal (e.g., visit website, open old email link)

## Segment-Specific Sequences

### For Cold Audiences (No Prior Interaction)
- Use full 10-day sequence
- Longer spacing between touches (every 2-3 days)
- Lower volume per day (don't overwhelm)
- Focus on value/case studies (build trust)
- Expected booking rate: 4-8%

### For Warm Audiences (Previous Engagement)
- Compress to 5-7 days
- Closer spacing (every 1-2 days)
- Higher volume acceptable
- Can be more direct in CTA
- Expected booking rate: 12-20%

### For "Hot" Prospects (Recent Engagement Signal)
- 2-3 day sequence max
- Can call immediately
- Direct meeting ask
- High urgency appropriate
- Expected booking rate: 30-50%

## Channel-Specific Best Practices

### Email Best Practices

**Timing:**
- Send between 9-11 AM (best open rates)
- Or 4-6 PM (secondary peak)
- Monday-Thursday (Friday lowest engagement)
- Avoid Sunday

**Format:**
- Plain text (no HTML formatting)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- One clear CTA per email
- No multiple links
- No attachments (use Google Drive)

**Personalization:**
- Custom first line (not copy-paste)
- Reference company-specific signal
- Use first name (not "Hi Team")
- Signature with phone number

### LinkedIn Best Practices

**Connection Message:**
- Keep to 300 characters max (LinkedIn limit)
- Reference something specific about them
- No pitch in connection request
- Example: "Hey [Name], saw your post on [topic]. Thought we should connect."

**Follow-up Message (after connected):**
- Wait 2-4 hours after connection
- Reference email they received
- Ask genuine question (not pitch)
- Example: "Thanks for connecting! BTW - curious about your experience with [topic]?"

**Timing:**
- Best time: 2-4 PM on Tuesday-Thursday
- Avoid early morning (annoying in notifications)
- Avoid late evening (not opened until next day)

**Content:**
- 2-3 short messages (not long paragraphs)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Add emoji sparingly (1-2 max, only if brand-appropriate)
- Add value: Link, insight, resource

### Phone Best Practices

**Timing:**
- Best times: 8-10 AM or 4-5 PM
- Best days: Tuesday-Thursday
- Avoid: Monday, Friday, lunch hours (12-1 PM)
- Avoid: Before 8 AM or after 9 PM

**Script structure:**
1. Name + company (quick)
2. Reason for call (1 sentence)
3. Ask permission (30 seconds?)
4. Qualifying question (not pitch)
5. Book or move to next step

**Voicemail:**
- 20-25 seconds max
- Include name, company, reason, phone number
- No pitch
- No call to action (voicemail won't answer)

### DM Best Practices (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn)

**Timing:**
- Best: When they're online (varies by platform)
- Facebook: 2-4 PM, 8-10 PM
- Instagram: 11 AM, 7-9 PM
- LinkedIn: 2-4 PM

**Sequence:**
- Message 1: Rapport (no pitch) - wait 6 hours
- Message 2: Value add (insight/resource) - wait 8 hours
- Message 3: Soft business intro - wait 12 hours
- Message 4 (if no reply): Bump message - wait 3 days
- Message 5: Different angle - wait 3 days
- Message 6: Final attempt - then move on

**Content:**
- Keep messages short (1-2 sentences each)
- Use line breaks
- Add emoji if platform-appropriate
- Include link only after rapport built
- Never immediate CTA

## Multi-Channel Timing Matrix

### Optimal Sequence (10-day window)

```
Day 0 │ 8 AM      │ EMAIL #1 Initial
Day 1 │ -         │ Wait (monitoring)
Day 2 │ 2 PM      │ LINKEDIN Message + Connection
Day 3 │ 9 AM      │ EMAIL #2 Follow-up 1
Day 4 │ -         │ Wait (monitoring)
Day 5 │ 4 PM      │ PHONE Call (or voicemail)
Day 6 │ 10 AM     │ EMAIL #3 + Calendar link (if no answer)
Day 7 │ 10 AM     │ EMAIL #4 Follow-up 2 (new angle)
Day 8 │ -         │ Wait (monitoring)
Day 9 │ 8:30 AM   │ EMAIL #5 Final (or DM alternative)
Day 10│ -         │ Sunset (no more outreach)
```

### Why This Specific Timing?

- **Day 0 morning:** Emails highest open rate in morning
- **Day 2 afternoon:** LinkedIn message reaches them after they've read email
- **Day 3 morning:** Follow-up email while memory fresh
- **Day 5 afternoon:** Phone in late afternoon (better answer rate)
- **Day 6 morning:** Email with calendar link (if no answer)
- **Day 7 morning:** New angle when previous email might be forgotten
- **Day 9 morning:** Final attempt before workday ends (week winding down)

## Troubleshooting Multi-Channel

### Problem: "They're getting too many emails"

**Reality check:**
- Email every 2-3 days = not overwhelming
- Plus 1 LinkedIn message + 1 phone call = 4 touches over 10 days
- This is actually light compared to enterprise sales (10-15 touches standard)

**If still concerned:**
- Reduce to 3 emails instead of 5 (Day 0, Day 3, Day 7)
- Keep phone call
- Keep LinkedIn message
- Still 4 total touches

### Problem: "Not getting meetings from cold outreach"

**Diagnostic:**
1. Is email converting to replies? (15% reply rate expected)
   - If NO: Copy issue, go to diagnostics
   - If YES: Continue below
2. Are you following up after replies? (Phone call is essential)
   - If NO: Add phone follow-up (this is often the missing piece)
   - If YES: Continue below
3. Are you offering calendar booking?
   - If NO: Add calendar link in Day 6 email
   - If YES: Continue below
4. Are you handling objections?
   - If NO: See objection handling scripts in email diagnostics

**Most common issue:** No phone follow-up. Email alone converts at 4-8%. Email + phone converts at 12-20%. The phone call is the "warm handoff."

### Problem: "LinkedIn message is decreasing email reply rate"

**Likely cause:** LinkedIn message is too pitchy or sent too early

**Solution:**
1. Wait longer between email and LinkedIn (move to Day 3 instead of Day 2)
2. Make LinkedIn message softer (not business ask, just connection + insight)
3. Reference email casually (not pushy)
4. Example good message: "Thanks for connecting! Saw your recent [post/achievement]. Curious how you're approaching [problem area]?"

## Multi-Channel CRM Tracking

### Fields to Track per Prospect

- [ ] Email #1 sent (date/time)
- [ ] Email #1 opened? (yes/no)
- [ ] Email #1 replied? (yes/no, date)
- [ ] LinkedIn message sent? (date)
- [ ] LinkedIn connected? (yes/no)
- [ ] Phone call attempt #1 (date, reached/voicemail)
- [ ] Voicemail left? (yes/no)
- [ ] Email #2 sent (date)
- [ ] Email #2 opened/replied? (yes/no)
- [ ] Phone call attempt #2 (if needed)
- [ ] Email #3 sent (date)
- [ ] Email #3 opened/replied? (yes/no)
- [ ] Calendar booking sent? (yes/no, date)
- [ ] Meeting booked? (yes/no, date/time)
- [ ] Meeting attended? (yes/no, outcome)

### CRM Segmentation

Create these CRM views to track progress:
- "Not yet contacted" (ready to start sequence)
- "Email 1 sent, waiting" (Day 0-2)
- "Email 1 reply, follow-up ready" (ready for Day 3 email)
- "Phone call scheduled" (upcoming calls)
- "Meeting booked" (move to sales team)
- "No response after 10 days" (sunset list)

## Scale Multi-Channel Operations

### 1 SDR Multi-Channel

- 100 initial emails/week
- 20 LinkedIn messages/week
- 30-40 phone calls/week
- Expected: 5-10 meetings/week

### 3 SDRs Multi-Channel

- 300 initial emails/week
- 60 LinkedIn messages/week
- 90-120 phone calls/week
- Expected: 15-30 meetings/week

### Hiring for Multi-Channel

**Specialized roles:**
- "Email specialist" - Owns email copy and follow-up sequences
- "Phone closer" - Owns phone calls and objection handling
- "LinkedIn researcher" - Owns LinkedIn + DM outreach
- "Data analyst" - Owns list building and CRM tracking

**Training focus:**
- Each role gets 5-10 example successful sequences from their channel
- Track KPIs per person (meetings booked)
- Weekly review of best-performing sequences
