# Gatekeeper Strategy & Scripts

Complete guide to getting past gatekeepers—the people hired to stop unwanted calls from reaching decision makers.

---

## Understanding Gatekeepers

### What is a Gatekeeper?
A person specifically hired to protect the decision maker from unwanted calls and interruptions. Their job is to filter calls and prevent salespeople from getting through.

### The Reality
- Gatekeepers and salespeople don't naturally mix well
- Most salespeople struggle with gatekeepers
- Gatekeepers are highly trained to say no
- BUT: Getting past them is a learned skill with consistent principles

### Key Insight
**Gatekeepers are part of the process. Something you'll encounter. Something you have to position yourself to overcome.**

---

## What NOT to Do (Anti-Patterns)

### Anti-Pattern 1: Don't Lie
**DON'T say:** "Yeah, John's expecting my call" (if he's not)
- Burns bridges
- Makes the prospect angry if you do get through
- Not worth the short-term gain
- Damages your reputation

**DO say:** If they ARE expecting your call, absolutely tell them. Build on the fact you've warmed them.

### Anti-Pattern 2: Don't Come Across as a Salesperson
**DON'T say:**
```
"Hi Julie, yeah Sean from Company, is John there please?"
```

**Why it doesn't work:**
- You're immediately identified as a salesperson
- The gatekeeper's job activates: stop you
- You'll get hit with: "Who is it again? What are you calling about? Does John know you?"

**DO say:**
- Short, direct answers
- Act like you should be put through
- Speak with assurance

### Anti-Pattern 3: Don't Treat Them as the Enemy
**DON'T:**
- Be rude or dismissive
- Try to "trick" them
- Get frustrated if rejected
- Burn the bridge

**DO:**
- Be professional and respectful
- Understand their job
- Appreciate them when they help ("Thank you for checking!")

### Anti-Pattern 4: Don't Burn Bridges
**If you're rude:**
- They'll recognize your voice next time you call
- They'll be hostile
- They might tell the decision maker you were rude
- You kill your chances

**Always stay professional** - even if they say no, you might call back later.

### Anti-Pattern 5: Don't Befriend Them (Usually)
**Exception:** If you only have 10 high-value prospects and you'd love to work with them, befriend the gatekeeper and try to get their help.

**For most:** This isn't practical. Use the confidence and assurance method instead (see "What TO Do" below).

## What TO Do (The Strategy)

### Principle 1: Understand They're Part of the Process
Accept that gatekeepers exist. They're not the enemy. They're a filter you navigate by positioning yourself correctly.

### Principle 2: Position Yourself as Equal or Higher Standing
**Act like you're a peer or someone higher in the hierarchy.**
- You're not a salesperson
- You're a manager, director, business owner, or consultant
- You're someone with value who should be taken seriously
- You're someone the prospect would want to speak to

**How to execute:**
- Speak with authority and certainty
- Use short, confident answers
- Don't over-explain
- Act like you belong on the call

### Principle 3: Talk With Urgency and Assurance
**Come across as:**
- Busy (not desperate)
- Important (not pushy)
- Assured (not defensive)
- Impatient (but not rude)

**How to execute:**
- Speak at a normal or slightly faster pace
- Use direct language
- Don't fill pauses
- Sound like someone important

### Principle 4: Use Silence (The Secret Weapon)
People hate silence. It throws people off and can work massively in your favor with gatekeepers.

**How to use silence:**
- After you respond to a question, stop talking
- Use short, succinct answers
- Then go silent
- Give them no more information
- Make them ask for more
- This puts the burden on them

**Example:**
```
GK: "What's this about?"
You: "Business." [SILENCE - don't say more]
GK: [Has to ask] "What kind of business?"
You: "Is John not in?" [Back to YOUR question]
```

**Why it works:**
- Silence creates discomfort
- They feel compelled to fill it
- It shifts power to you
- They have to work harder to stop you

## The Complete Gatekeeper Script (Word-for-Word)

This is the framework. Adapt it to your situation, but follow the principles.

### The Scenario
You're calling John. The gatekeeper answers.

---

### THE SCRIPT

**GK:** "Hello, is John in?"

**You:** "Hi, is John in?"
```
[You ask about John, implying you know him/should be put through.
Short, simple, confident tone. You're not asking permission—
you're asking a simple question.]
```

---

**GK:** "Who's calling?"

**You:** "It's Sean"
```
[Just your first name. Spoken with assurance.
Act like they should know who you are.
Don't say "It's Sean FROM..." yet—let them ask for more.]
```

---

**GK:** "Sean...?"
[They want more information]

**You:** "Sean Longden"
```
[Full name. Said with assurance and certainty.
Like they should know who you are.
Slight impatience/urgency in tone.]
```

---

**GK:** "What's it about?"

**You:** "Business... Is John in please?"
```
[Simple one-word answer: "Business"
Then immediately ask your question back.
This takes control back.
Don't over-explain what business.
Use silence after "Business" - make them ask for more if they want.]
```

---

**GK:** "Let me check, what company is it please?"

**You:** "Mycompany - he'll know the company"
```
[State your company name.
If you've warmed them with email/LinkedIn, add: "he'll know the company"
If it's a pure cold call, just state: "Mycompany"
Speak with assurance like this is expected.]
```

---

**GK:** "Can I take more information?"

**You:** "There's no information for you to take. It's John I need to speak to - is he not in?"
```
[CRITICAL: Don't give information to the gatekeeper.
You're not calling the gatekeeper.
You're calling John.
Assert control.
Put the burden on them to put you through or tell you he's not available.
Don't be rude—just direct and assured.]
```

---

**GK:** "Let me put you through"

**You:** "Thank you"
```
[Appreciate them. They're helping you.
They've done their job (filtering) and determined
you should go through.
Always be appreciative.]
```

---

### KEY PRINCIPLES OF DELIVERY

1. **Confidence is Everything**
   - Deliver this with complete confidence
   - Hesitation shows through and fails
   - You sound like you should be put through = they put you through

2. **Don't Give Information Away**
   - Minimize what you say to the gatekeeper
   - The more you say, the more ammunition they have to stop you
   - Your goal is John, not convincing the gatekeeper

3. **Use Short, Succinct Answers**
   - "It's Sean" (not "Hi, it's Sean Longden from Company")
   - "Business" (not "I'm calling to see if we can help with...")
   - Brevity = power

4. **Use Silence (Pause Strategy)**
   - After you answer, pause and don't say more
   - Makes them ask follow-up questions
   - Puts burden on them to keep the conversation going
   - You maintain control

5. **Speak with Urgency (But Not Rudeness)**
   - Tone: slightly impatient, like you're busy
   - Not angry or rude
   - Like you have other important things to do
   - This makes them think you're important

6. **Act Like You Should Be Put Through**
   - Your entire demeanor says: "This is a normal call. John will want to speak to me."
   - Not: "Please can I speak to John?"
   - But: "Is John in?" (statement, not question)

## Alternative Gatekeeper Tactics

### Tactic 1: The Warm-Up (If You've Emailed or LinkedIn Connected)

**Use this if you've already warmed them:**

```
"Hi [gatekeeper name], it's [your name] from [company]. John and I
have been going back and forth on email about [topic], and he mentioned
calling him today would be great. Is he available?"
```

**Why it works:**
- You're not a cold call
- You've already had contact
- You're expected (or at least, they've heard from you)
- Gatekeeper lets you through more easily

### Tactic 2: The Specific Time Reference

**If you know they have a meeting or event:**

```
"Hi, it's [your name] from [company]. I know John has [specific meeting
at specific time]. Is he available for 10 minutes before that?"
```

**Why it works:**
- Shows you've done research
- Shows you respect their time
- Specific time request is easier to fulfill

### Tactic 3: The Voicemail First

**Call the main number, get voicemail, then call the gatekeeper:**

```
[After leaving voicemail to John]

"Hi, it's [your name] from [company]. I just left John a voicemail,
and I wanted to see if I could catch him quickly. Is he available?"
```

**Why it works:**
- Now you've already contacted John directly
- Gatekeeper knows you've reached out
- Makes your call seem legitimate

### Tactic 4: The Callback Request

**If gatekeeper says John is not available:**

```
"No problem. When would be a good time to call back? [Get specific time]

Is there a direct line I could reach him on, or is this the best number?"
```

**Why it works:**
- You get a specific time (not vague "later")
- You might get a direct line (bypass gatekeeper next time)
- Professional approach

### Tactic 5: The Referral Name-Drop

**If someone referred you:**

```
"Hi, it's [your name] from [company]. [Referrer name] suggested I give
John a call about [topic]. Is he available?"
```

**Why it works:**
- Name-dropping a referral gives you credibility
- Makes it seem like John is expecting you
- Gatekeeper is more likely to put you through

## If The Gatekeeper Says "He's Unavailable" or "He's Busy"

### Response 1: Get a Callback Time
```
GK: "He's not available right now."
You: "No problem. When would be a good time to call back?"
[Get specific time]
"And is this the best number to reach him on?"
```

### Response 2: Ask for Alternative Routing
```
GK: "He's in a meeting."
You: "I understand. Do you have a direct line I could reach him on?
Or what time would be good to try back?"
```

### Response 3: Try the Decision Maker's Decision Maker (If Applicable)
```
GK: "John's busy all day."
You: "I understand. Who else might handle [topic] in the company?
Or is there someone I could speak to while I wait to reach John?"
```

## If The Gatekeeper Says "What's This Regarding?"

### Strategy: Keep It Vague But Assured

**DON'T say:**
```
"I'm calling to see if he needs help with cold calling..."
```

**DO say:**
```
"It's regarding an opportunity we discussed. Is John available?"
or
"Business. Is John in?"
or
"I'll discuss it with John directly."
```

**Why vague works:**
- Gives the gatekeeper less ammunition
- Creates curiosity
- Puts control with John, not the gatekeeper

---

## If The Gatekeeper Says "Does He Know You?"

### Strategy: Imply Yes (Truthfully)

**If you've warmed them:**
```
"Yeah, we've been going back and forth on email."
```

**If it's a pure cold call:**
```
"He should - I've reached out. Is he available?"
```

**If you want to be completely honest:**
```
"Not yet, but he'll want to hear what I have to say. Is he in?"
```

## If You Keep Getting Blocked

### Strategy 1: Try Different Times
```
- Call early morning (7-8 AM) before gatekeeper is there
- Call late afternoon (4-5 PM) when gatekeeper might be less focused
- Call mid-afternoon when they're less likely to be strict filtering
```

### Strategy 2: Try Different Numbers
```
- Direct dial if you can find it (LinkedIn, email signature, Google)
- Switchboard main line and ask to be transferred
- Alternative departments and ask to be transferred
```

### Strategy 3: Go Around With Voicemail
```
- Leave compelling voicemail
- Call once or twice more without leaving message
- They might call you back wondering who you are
- Then you're warmed
```

### Strategy 4: Use LinkedIn to Warm Them
```
- Connect with John on LinkedIn
- Engage with his posts
- Message him directly
- Then the gatekeeper is less likely to block you
```

## The Anti-Pattern: What Most Salespeople Do Wrong

**Most salespeople sound like this:**

```
GK: "Who's calling?"
Sales: "Hi Julie, yeah it's Sean from Company. I'm calling to see if
John has a moment to discuss how we help companies like his improve
their sales process..."

[Gatekeeper immediately blocks]
```

**Why it fails:**
- They sound desperate
- Too much information
- Identified as salesperson immediately
- They're asking permission instead of expecting to go through

**Compare to the correct approach:**

```
GK: "Who's calling?"
You: "It's Sean"
GK: "Sean...?"
You: "Sean Longden from Mycompany - is John in?"
[SAID WITH CONFIDENCE AND ASSURANCE]

[Gatekeeper puts you through because you acted like you belong there]
```

## Summary: The Five Gatekeeper Rules

| Rule | What This Means |
|------|-----------------|
| **Confidence is Everything** | Deliver with absolute assurance. Hesitation fails. |
| **Minimize Information** | Short answers = power. Long answers = ammunition for them. |
| **Use Silence** | Pause after answers. Make them work to stop you. |
| **Act Like You Belong** | You're an equal or superior. You should go through. |
| **Stay Professional** | Never rude. They might block you next time if you're not. |

## Practice Exercise

1. **Write out the script** - Practice with the exact script above
2. **Practice with confidence** - Record yourself. Does it sound assured?
3. **Practice with silence** - Pause after each answer. Don't fill space.
4. **Do practice calls** - Call business switchboards (not your targets) and practice getting through
5. **Analyze failures** - When you don't get through, what went wrong? Confidence? Too much info? Not enough assurance?

The more you practice, the better your gatekeeper skills become. Most salespeople never get good at this because they don't practice consistently.

**Practice on non-target calls first. Once you're confident, you'll get through easily.**
