# Gatekeeper Bypass, Voicemails & Metrics

## Gatekeeper Bypass (30MPC)

### Philosophy
Gatekeepers are hardwired to detect strangers. The WRONG approach is to befriend them or treat them like a prospect. Instead, make them think: "I better get out of this person's way, otherwise my boss is going to be pissed."

### Three Bypass Attempts (Without Lying or Being Disrespectful)

**1st Bypass: The Slide-By**
> GK: "Aubrey Graham's office."
> You: "Hey, could you get me over to Aubrey? It's Nick."

Rules: Confident tone, cool, calm. First name only -- you don't have to share your company. End with a question.

**2nd Bypass: The Context**
> GK: "What's this regarding?"
> You: "I work with a few other Skadden partners in the LA office. Would you let him know that it's Nick Cegelski?"

Rules: Share context, NOT product. If you start pitching, you're toast. End with a question.

**3rd Bypass: Social Proof**
> GK: "I need to see if he's available. What company are you with?"
> You: "We help a couple other partners in the office with their tax planning; it's Northwestern Mutual. I sent him a note the other day, mind letting him know it's Nick?"

### If Stonewalled After 3 Attempts
- Ask to leave a voicemail
- Don't repeatedly fight -- some gatekeepers cannot be passed
- Workarounds:
  - **Call mobile numbers** -- gatekeeper won't answer their iPhone
  - **Call during off-hours** -- early morning, lunch, after 5pm (exec works longer than gatekeeper)
  - **Try a different channel** -- email, LinkedIn, direct mail

## Longden Gatekeeper Script

**Positioning:** Equal or higher standing to the decision maker. Urgent and assured (impatient but NOT rude). Use SILENCE. Give minimal information.

```
GK: "Who's calling?"
You: "It's Sean."
GK: "Sean...?"
You: "Sean Longden..." [silence]
GK: "What's it about?"
You: "Business... Is John not in?" [silence]
GK: "Let me check, what company is it please?"
You: "[Company name] -- he'll know the company"
GK: "Can I take more information?"
You: "There's no more information for you to take. It's John I need to speak to, is he not in?"
```

The gatekeeper is now in a position where NOT putting you through risks them getting in trouble if John actually wants the call.

**What NOT to do:**
- Don't lie ("They're expecting my call") -- burns bridges
- Don't sound like a salesperson ("Hi, is John in please?")
- Don't try to befriend them (unless very small, high-value list)
- Don't be rude -- they'll recognize your voice next time

## Voicemails

### Longden's 3 Voicemail Tactics

**Ideal length:** 20-25 seconds maximum.

**Tactic 1: Don't Leave a Voicemail (Local Businesses Only)**
- Landscapers, plumbers, roofers operate from mobile
- Missed call without voicemail = potential customer -> they call back
- Use for first 2 attempts, switch to tactic 2 or 3 after

**Tactic 2: Vague Voicemail**
"Hi Johnny, Sean. Can you give me a call on [number]? Again, that's [repeat number]. Thanks!"
- Sounds like a familiar contact
- Prospect thinks: "Who's Sean? Should I know him?"
- Peaks curiosity

**Tactic 3: Value Prop Voicemail (4-Part Structure)**
1. Interruptive statement: "Hi John, it's Sean from Company. Is generating new business and increasing revenue a priority for you?"
2. Build credibility: "We just helped [company] generate $10,000 in new business last month."
3. Offer: "We'd love to do a free audit of your current outreach strategy."
4. Contact details: Leave phone number TWICE.

### 30MPC Double Tap Method

**Voicemail data (Gong):**
- Email reply rates DOUBLE when you leave one or two voicemails
- After two voicemails, rates DECREASE
- Voicemails have 90%+ open rate (prospects at least see transcription)

**Three key components:**
1. **Lead with context** -- Pique interest, leave the pitch for the email. The moment you sell, they delete it.
2. **Direct back to the email** -- Never ask them to call back. Direct all attention to: "look for our email."
3. **Keep it really short** -- Max 30 seconds. No one listens to 2-minute voicemails.

**Voicemail #1 (15 sec):** Context only. Save entire pitch for email.

**Voicemail #2 (30 sec):** Context + touch of social proof. Still leave pitch for email.

**Integration with sequences:**
Call -> Leave voicemail -> Point to email in voicemail (give subject line) -> Voicemail drives prospect to read the email with the full pitch.

## The Golden 3 Metrics

### Three Conversion Metrics
1. **Connect Rate** -- % of prospects who picked up
2. **Set Rate** -- % of conversations that turned into meetings
3. **Show Rate** -- % of meetings that actually show up

### Average Rep vs Top Rep (Gong, 800 dials/month)

| Metric | Average (50th %ile) | Top (75th %ile) |
|--------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Connect Rate | 5.5% | 13.3% |
| Conversations | 43 | 106 |
| Set Rate | 4.6% | 16.7% |
| Meetings Booked | 2 | ~18 |
| **Result** | **2 meetings** | **~18 meetings** |

Same dials, 13x the results.

### Longden Benchmarks (Agency/Service Business)

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Weekly calling goal | 100 calls |
| Weekly contact goal | 50 contacts |
| Weekly meeting goal | 5 meetings |
| Calls to contacts rate | ~46.5% |
| Contacts to appointments rate | ~22.2% |
| Overall calls to appointments | ~10.3% |

## Maximizing Connect Rate (4 Tactics)

1. **Prioritize mobile and direct lines** -- Skip gatekeepers and phone trees
2. **Mark your tracks** -- Color-code numbers as red/yellow/green so you never dial a bad number twice
3. **Law of diminishing returns** -- Max 5 dials in 4 weeks per prospect, stop after 2 voicemails, avoid impassable gatekeepers
4. **Prevent spam tagging:**
   - Register at freecallerregistry.com
   - Rotate phone numbers
   - Test your number regularly (call your personal line)
   - Call during business hours only (8am-9pm)
   - Don't repeatedly call bad numbers

## Maximizing Set Rate

**Priority order for who to call:**
1. People who have a problem NOW (highest conversion)
2. People who have a problem
3. Anyone else in your ICP

**Step 1: Pull a qualified list.** Use 30-second rule to disqualify bad fits.

**Step 2: Tier accounts A/B/C.** Build top 5 buying triggers, prioritize timing-based signals.
- A Tier = Problem now
- B Tier = Problem
- C Tier = Anyone in ICP
- Add extra dials to A and B tier

**Step 3: Call the right people.**
- All "above the line" (VP, SVP, C-Level)
- All "at the line" (Director level)
- Some "below the line" (Manager level)
- Be bold calling high -- old-school execs are best reached by cold call
- Don't go too low -- analysts rarely influence purchases

**Step 4: Prioritize by intent signals.**
- Opens email 5x
- Visits pricing page
- Replies with objection
- New compelling event

## Maximizing Show Rate

**Day-before confirmation email:** Send prep research to show goodwill.

**No-show protocol:**
1. Email at 2-minute mark: "We're on! Putting the Zoom at your fingertips."
2. Call at 5-minute mark
3. Call again at 8-minute mark (only acceptable time to double-dial)
4. If no-show: NEVER delete the invite. Move 3-4 days out, send backup times with a touch of guilt.
5. Stay on it -- far easier to keep a meeting than find a new one.

## SDR Hiring & Management (Longden)

### Hiring Process
1. Only hire experienced SDRs (Upwork: $5K-10K+ earned, 90-95%+ job success)
2. Filter question in job post: "First words of cover letter should be: umbrella-avocado"
3. Problem-solving question: "What would you do if you called and it was the wrong number?"
   - Correct answer: Find right number, inform you, update system, call correct number
4. Hire native to target country (timezone, language, accent)
5. 1-2 week trial basis, renew on performance

### SDR Commission Structure (Tiered)

| Appointments/Month | Commission |
|---------------------|-----------|
| 1-25 | 15% |
| 25-50 | 20% |
| 50-75 | 25% |
| 75-100 | 30% |
| 100+ | 40% |

### SDR Management
- Provide everything: value prop, case studies, offer, CTA, decks
- Set KPIs: Expected calls/hour and appointments/hour
- End-of-day reports: calls made, calls booked, follow-up notes
- Review 3-5 random recorded calls per week with feedback

### Managing Calls for Clients (Appointment Setting)
- Always use your real name (fake names impact quality)
- Call from client's company name: "Hi John, it's Sean from Pete's Pizza Shop"
- CTA = getting prospect to agree to second call WITH the client
- 3 things on screen: CRM, client info spreadsheet, client's calendar
- Closing: "Our CEO David is available Monday at 2pm. Would you have time?"
- Must obtain: mobile, email, qualifying answers, decision-making process
- Send call recordings to client

## Software Stack

### Primary: Twilio + Go High Level (GHL)
**Twilio:**
- Calls, SMS, ringless voicemails
- Less than $0.01/minute for US calls
- Local area code numbers (game changer -- appears to call from same area)

**Go High Level:**
- Basic: $97/month (one account)
- Agency: $297/month (unlimited sub-accounts)

**Power Dialer Setup (in GHL):**
1. Campaign with two events: Manual Call + Voicemail
2. Upload pre-recorded voicemail MP3
3. "Allow multiple" = OFF, "Stop on response" = ON
4. Add contacts via tags
5. Click Start -- auto-dials sequentially

**Missed Call Automation:**
1. Pipeline in GHL with "Called Me" stage
2. Zapier: New Twilio call -> Update Google Sheet + GHL opportunity
3. Trigger: "Called Me" stage -> auto-send SMS: "Hi, sorry missed your call. Here's my calendar link"

### Alternatives
- RingCentral, Vonage, Nextiva
