# Objection Handling (Phone)

## Core Insight

Most objections are NOT objections to your product -- they are reactions to the interruption itself. Handle the reaction before handling the objection. When you counter with a pitch, you only get them to kick harder. More pressure = more resistance.

---

## The Mr. Miyagi Method (30MPC)

Like Mr. Miyagi redirecting punches instead of blocking head-on. Three steps for ANY objection:

### Step 1: Agree with the Objection
Remove all pressure. When you agree, it feels dumb to keep fighting someone who isn't fighting you.

### Step 2: Incentivize Conversation
You still don't have enough information. Get them talking by promising value in exchange.

**Incentive phrases:**
- "Just so no one calls you again..."
- "Just so I mark this one as dead..."
- "Just so I don't reach out at the wrong time..."
- "I'd hate to bug you again when you're already swamped..."

**Key technique: Multiple choice questions.** People clam up at open-ended questions but will answer multiple choice.

### Step 3: Sell the Test Drive, Not the Car
Do NOT sell the product. Sell what they'll get from a meeting even if they never buy.

"Hey, you're probably not gonna buy this thing now. But if budget ever frees up, the people who get it at least have a directional sense of what they'd want. Open to taking a look, so you at least know what's out there?"

## Specific Objection Scripts (Mr. Miyagi)

### "No Budget"
- **Agree:** "I hear ya. Nowadays, it's hard enough to keep your budget, let alone add something new."
- **Incentivize:** "Just so no one calls you again, is it that you're out of budget for this fiscal year or you get put through the wringer every time you try to buy something?"
- **Test Drive:** "You're probably not gonna buy this now. But if budget ever frees up, the people who get it at least have a directional sense of what they'd want. Open to taking a look?"

### "Not Interested"
- **Agree:** "Totally get that. This one's my bad. You probably would have reached out to us if you were."
- **Incentivize:** "Just so no one calls you again, is it because you're doing this in-house or you already have a solution in place?"
- **Test Drive:** Tailor based on what they share.

### "We're Using a Competitor" (Unknown)
- **Agree:** "Oh, I should've assumed you had something in place."
- **Incentivize:** "Just so I mark this one as dead, must be CompEx, Compensatory, or Comptastic, right?" (multiple choice to get the competitor name)
- Then run Miyagi again on the known competitor.

### "We're Using a Competitor" (Known)
- **Agree:** "Oh CompEx! They're a great group. Honestly, it rarely makes sense to switch if you like them."
- **Incentivize (trap question):** "Just so we don't bug ya again, that must mean you're not doing stock option bonuses, right?"
- **Test Drive:** "Other HR teams found that managers would request bigger cash bonuses because most platforms don't let you see the value of both cash and stock. My guess is you won't switch, but could I give you a sense of how you might manage those together?"

**Never badmouth a competitor.** When you explain that a similar customer faced a similar problem, their actions speak louder than your words.

### "Stuck in a Contract"
- Same flow as known competitor: agree, find the gap, sell the test drive
- "My guess is you won't switch until your contract is up, but I could give you a sense of how you might manage those things together, just so you know what's out there."
- Discuss differentiators and contract buyouts on the MEETING, not the cold call.

### "Send Me Some Information"
- **Agree:** "Oh, for sure. I'd wanna digest it all first too."
- **Incentivize:** "Just so I don't clutter your inbox, was there a specific question you had in mind or just a general overview?"
- **Test Drive:** "I'll be honest: those PDFs aren't gonna be relevant at all... and you'll know if this is a fit in the first 15 minutes. Opposed to taking a look, and if it's not a fit, I can give you 15 back?"
- **If still no -- Disarmingly Blunt:** "When folks tell me to send them some info, they're just too nice to tell me to go away. Is that what's happening?"
- **The Dart:** "I'll send you the info, call out exactly what's relevant, and send a placeholder invite for next week. If you don't like it, just decline the invite. Fair?"

### "Call Me in 6 Months"
- **Agree:** "Totally makes sense. I guess if you were ready now, you would've reached out to us."
- **Incentivize:** "Just so I don't sound like a goon when I call back, was there something specific happening in 6 months or are you just swamped?"
- If real reason: Sell test drive to prepare for that event
- If no real reason (9/10 times): Use disarmingly blunt line from "Send Info"

### "No Resources / Bandwidth"
- **Agree:** "I hear you. Literally every marketer we talk to today has 3 jobs."
- **Incentivize:** "I'd hate to bug you again when you're already swamped. Mind me asking, is this just a temporary phase, or does it look like you're gonna be swamped until 2032?"
- **Test Drive:** "I know you're swamped. But our best customers are lean teams who need extra firepower. You're probably not gonna buy this still. But could I at least show you how we help other folks, just so you know what's out there?"

### Hang Up (Prospect Hangs Up)
- Do NOT call back immediately saying "I think we got disconnected" -- success rate is basically zero
- Note: call date, call time, exact words they said before hanging up
- Call back ONE WEEK LATER using those 3 things as context:
- "[Name], you and I last spoke on [date] around [time]. At the time, you couldn't talk because [reason]. I know I was an interruption. But I promise I prepped for this one. Could I at least tell you why I called you back?"

### "I Thought You Were Someone Else"
- **Agree (humor):** "Well, I can change my tone of voice and pretend to be someone different if you'd like! But actually, I hate when I'm waiting for a call, then pick up and someone's trying to sell me SiriusXM radio."
- **Incentivize:** "I'd hate to do this to you twice. Could I just tell you why I called?"
- Let them know they can hang up if the other person calls back.

### "Is This a Cold Call?"
- Own it with humor and transparency, then pivot to permission.

### "Not My Responsibility"
- Ask who would be the right person
- Ask for permission to share why you called in case they know someone

### "I'm in a Meeting"
- "Totally my bad. Would it be better if I called you back at [specific time]?"

### "Need to Hire Someone First"
- Agree, then test drive: "The people who do end up getting it are the ones who already know what they want when the new hire arrives."

## The Trap Question (Advanced)

After incentivizing, use a question that reveals a gap in their current solution.

Example for Pave selling against CompEx:
"That must mean you're not doing stock option bonuses, right?"
Prospect: "Well, we are... why?"

This reveals a problem their competitor cannot solve = natural reason to take a look.

## The Dart Technique

Trade information for a calendar hold:
"I'll send you the info. And in return, I'm just going to throw a hold on the calendar because both of us get really busy. If that time doesn't work, just pick one of the backup times I include in the same email."

## AREQ Framework (Longden)

Simpler framework. Good for beginners. Works for ANY objection.

1. **Acknowledge** -- "I understand"
2. **Repeat** -- Restate their objection back to confirm understanding
3. **Empathize** -- Show sympathy for their position
4. **Question** -- Ask a question using value prop to steer back toward close

### 5 Common Objections (AREQ Style)

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | "I understand. But if I can provide results generating [revenue], wouldn't you agree that's incredible ROI?" + stress LTV math |
| "Need time to think" | "I appreciate it's not something you decide straight away. What are your hesitations so I may address them? On a scale of 1-10, how seriously are you considering?" |
| "Send me information" | "What information would you need that I haven't provided? On a scale of 1-10, how seriously are you considering? Let's schedule a call once you've reviewed." |
| "Never heard of your company" | "I'm fortunate to choose who I work with. I don't advertise -- I reach out to businesses I'm 100% confident I can guarantee results for." + guarantee + social proof |
| "Not a priority right now" | Express genuine shock: "I was under the impression this was a priority based on the problems we identified together. What's changed?" |

### All Objections Reduce to 3 Categories
1. **Financial** -- Too expensive / not in budget
2. **Trust** -- Never heard of you / lack of proof
3. **Hesitation** -- Not sure / need time / send info

After every objection: always circle back to the close.

## Pricing Objection Tactic (Longden)

"In all honesty, I've underpriced my service. My plan is to generate [revenue] over 3 months and then review my price because of the value I'm bringing to you."

## Prevention First

Role-play calls with friends/family to identify common objections BEFORE they arise, then address them proactively in your script. The best objection handling is preventing the objection from ever coming up.
