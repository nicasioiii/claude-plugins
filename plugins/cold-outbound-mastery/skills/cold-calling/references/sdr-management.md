# SDR Hiring, Training & Management Guide

Complete framework for hiring, training, and managing Sales Development Representatives (SDRs) to scale your cold calling operations.

---

## What is an SDR?

**SDR = Sales Development Rep**

A person you hire to make cold calls and set appointments on your behalf. They handle the initial outreach and qualification, passing qualified prospects to you for closing.

**Key Role:**
- Make calls to prospects
- Identify and qualify leads
- Set appointments with decision makers
- Keep detailed notes for closing calls

---

## The Hiring Process (5 Critical Steps)

### STEP 1: Only Hire Experienced SDRs

**Why?**
- You don't have time to train someone from scratch
- Experience means they hit the ground running
- They already understand cold calling fundamentals
- Training takes resources you don't have

**Minimum Requirements:**
- **5,000-10,000+ calls minimum** (proof they've done the work)
- **90-95%+ job success score** on freelance platforms (Apollo, Upwork, Fiverr)
- Already skilled in the fundamentals of cold calling

**Where to Hire:**
- Upwork (search "SDR", "appointment setter", "cold calling")
- Freelance platforms (Fiverr, Guru, PeoplePerHour)
- LinkedIn (search for "Sales Development Representative")
- Your network (referrals from other agency owners)

**Job Post Template (Key Elements):**
```
Title: Experienced SDR / Appointment Setter (Proven Track Record)

Requirements:
- Minimum 5,000-10,000+ personal cold calls completed
- Proven appointment-setting experience
- 90%+ job success score on freelance platforms
- Experience in [your industry] preferred
- Native English speaker [specify if needed]

Responsibilities:
- Make 50-100+ calls per day
- Set 5-10+ appointments per day
- Qualify prospects according to our criteria
- Document all interactions in CRM
- Follow our call scripts and framework

Commission Structure:
[Your offer - see commission section below]

Application Instructions:
[Include test - see Step 2 below]
```

### STEP 2: Weed Out Poor Candidates

**First Filter: The Attention Test**

In your job post, include:
```
"The first words in your cover letter should be 'umbrella-avocado'"
```

**Why this works:**
- Weeds out applicants who don't read instructions
- If they can't follow simple instructions, they won't follow your scripts/processes
- Ignore any applications without this phrase exactly
- This single filter reduces applications by 50%+ and keeps quality high

**Second Filter: Problem-Solving Test**

In your job post, include a scenario:
```
"In your cover letter, answer this question:

You call a prospect and realize you have the wrong number - it's not
the person you were looking for. What do you do?"
```

**Poor Answer:**
```
"I would just move on to the next one"
```

**Good Answer:**
```
"I would:
1. Find the relevant contact telephone number
2. Inform the hiring manager
3. Update the system with correct information
4. Call the correct number to try to set an appointment"
```

**Why this matters:**
- Shows problem-solving ability
- Shows they understand qualification process
- Shows they'll document and report
- Reveals if they're lazy or proactive

**Only consider candidates with good answers.**

---

### STEP 3: Hire Someone Native to the Country You're Contacting

**Why This Matters:**

- **Accent/Familiarity:** They sound natural when calling prospects in that country
- **Time Zone:** Similar time zone = better availability
- **Cultural Understanding:** They understand the language, culture, and business practices
- **Quality of Calls:** Native speakers connect better with prospects

**Example:**
- Calling US prospects? Hire US-based SDR
- Calling UK prospects? Hire UK-based SDR
- Calling Australia? Hire Australian-based SDR

**Cost Consideration:**
- US SDRs might cost more than overseas options ($800-2000/month vs. $300-500/month)
- BUT: Quality of calls and conversion rates are dramatically better
- Higher cost = higher results = net positive ROI
- Worth it for premium results

**Where to Find:**
- Upwork (filter by location)
- LinkedIn (location filter)
- Local job boards in target country
- Referrals from people in that country

### STEP 4: Use Commission Structure as Incentive

**Why Commission?**
- Aligns their incentive with your success
- They earn more when you earn more
- Creates motivation for quality over quantity
- Attracts ambitious, driven people

**Base Commission Structure (Simple):**
```
15% per appointment set

Example:
- Appointment price: $200
- SDR commission: $200 × 15% = $30 per appointment
- 10 appointments/month = $300 commission
```

**Pyramid Commission Structure (Recommended):**

This incentivizes them to book MORE appointments as they hit higher volumes.

```
0-25 appointments/month:    15% commission
25-50 appointments/month:   20% commission
50-75 appointments/month:   25% commission
75-100 appointments/month:  30% commission
100+ appointments/month:    40% commission
```

**How it works:**
```
Example Month:
- Appointments 1-25: 15% = $750 (at $200/appt)
- Appointments 25-50: 20% = $1,000
- Appointments 50-75: 25% = $1,250
- Appointments 75+: 30% = $1,800

Total Commission: $4,800 for 85 appointments

Result: SDR is highly motivated to hit 100+ because
40% commission kicks in at that level.
```

**Why Pyramid Commission Works:**
- Seems like you're paying more, but you're making more revenue
- More appointments = more revenue = can afford higher commission
- Fair to SDR for hitting targets
- Motivates continuous improvement

**Possible Concerns:**
```
"Isn't this expensive?"

Answer: More appointments = more revenue for you.
- 50 appointments × $200 = $10,000 revenue
- Commission might be $1,500 (15%)
- Net profit: $8,500

- 100 appointments × $200 = $20,000 revenue
- Commission might be $4,000 (20% average)
- Net profit: $16,000

You make MORE money at higher volume, even with higher commissions.
```

**Optional: Add Hourly Rate**
```
Base: $15/hour (or local minimum wage equivalent)
Plus: Commission structure above

This gives them security while incentivizing performance.
```

### STEP 5: Hire on Trial Basis

**Why Trial Period?**
- You need to see if they actually perform
- Not all experienced SDRs work for every business
- Gives both of you time to evaluate fit
- Allows you to adjust before committing long-term

**Trial Length:**
- **1-2 weeks minimum** (you need time to evaluate)
- **Ideally 2-4 weeks** for more data

**During Trial:**
- Have them make calls following your process
- Track appointments set
- Review call quality
- Get them feedback
- Evaluate their attitude and responsiveness

**End of Trial:**
- **Renew:** If they're a good fit, continue
- **Part-ways:** If not working, end politely
- **Modify:** If close but not perfect, adjust expectations/terms

**Set Expectations at Start:**
```
"Hey [name], thanks for starting. This is a 2-week trial period.
During this time, we'll see if this is a good fit for both of us.
I'll be giving you feedback regularly. At the end of 2 weeks,
we'll decide if we move forward. Sound good?"
```

## Managing SDRs (The Operating System)

### CRITICAL: Provide Everything They Need

**Before they make their first call, they should have:**

1. **Value Proposition Document**
   - Written exactly how you want it delivered
   - Examples of good vs. bad delivery
   - Your target audience definition
   - Why you stand out from competitors

2. **Case Studies & Social Proof**
   - Specific client results (named if possible)
   - Testimonials
   - Before/after metrics
   - Success stories they can reference

3. **Offer Details**
   - Exactly what you're selling
   - Pricing
   - What's included
   - What's NOT included
   - Common questions and answers

4. **Call to Action (CTA)**
   - What you want them to book (demo, call, consultation)
   - How to present it
   - How to handle when prospect asks "what is this?"

5. **Decks/Slides**
   - Visual materials they can reference
   - What to send prospects who ask for info
   - Presentation materials for calls

6. **Marketing Materials**
   - Company information
   - Any collateral that supports the value prop
   - Social proof materials

7. **Go High Level Setup**
   - Fully configured for them
   - Power dialer ready to use
   - Contact list uploaded
   - Call scripts saved in the system
   - Integration with phone (Twilio if using)

8. **Calendar Access**
   - Access to your (or client's) calendar
   - Can see availability in real-time
   - Can propose times to prospects
   - Know when you're available to close

9. **CRM/Contact System**
   - Contacts uploaded
   - Contact information complete
   - Follow-up system in place
   - Where to log calls and notes

10. **Training Materials**
    - This SKILL.md document (cold calling framework)
    - Scripts they should follow
    - The objection handling framework
    - Gatekeeper strategies
    - Your specific process

### SET CLEAR GOALS AND EXPECTATIONS (KPIs)

**Define These BEFORE They Start:**

**Daily Goals:**
```
- How many calls per hour? (typical: 8-15 calls/hour)
- How many calls per day? (typical: 50-100 calls/day)
- How many appointments per day? (typical: 5-10/day)
```

**Quality Goals:**
```
- Appointment quality (qualified leads only)
- Lead accuracy (calling the right people)
- Call notes completeness
- Professionalism in tone/delivery
```

**Weekly Goals:**
```
- Total calls: [X]
- Total appointments: [X]
- Conversion rate: [X%]
```

**Monthly Goals:**
```
- Total appointments: [X]
- Hitting commission targets
- Maintaining call quality
```

**Set expectations in writing:**
```
"Hey [name], here's what success looks like this month:

- Daily: 80 calls, 8+ appointments
- Weekly: 400 calls, 40+ appointments
- Monthly: 1,600 calls, 160+ appointments

This is what needs to happen to hit your commission targets.
I'll track this daily and we can adjust as needed.

Sound good?"
```

### TRAINING & CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

**Week 1: Initial Training**
```
1. Provide all materials listed above
2. Walk them through your process
3. Record practice calls with them
4. Review 5+ practice calls
5. Critique delivery, positioning, listening
6. Have them improve
7. Record real calls once ready
```

**Ongoing (Weekly):**
```
1. Review 3-5 random calls per week
2. Identify what's working
3. Identify what needs improvement
4. Provide specific feedback
5. Coach on objection handling
6. Celebrate wins and improvements
7. Adjust approach based on performance
```

**Monthly:**
```
1. Review full month performance
2. Celebrate progress/hits
3. Discuss challenges
4. Adjust commission/expectations if needed
5. Identify training needs
6. Plan improvements for next month
```

**Benefits to You AND Them:**
- **You:** Improve call quality, fix problems, increase appointments
- **Them:** Get better at calling, improve commission earning, develop skills

## Go High Level Setup for SDR Calling

### Core Features to Use

**1. Smart Lists (Automated Audience Building)**
```
Create triggers that automatically populate lists:

Example:
- List Name: "Email Opened, No Reply"
- Trigger: Contact opened email AND didn't reply
- Use: Power dialer calls these prospects
- Result: Warm prospects more likely to answer
```

**Other Smart List Ideas:**
```
- Opened email + Accepted LinkedIn
- Viewed landing page but didn't convert
- Clicked link in email but didn't reply
- Accepted LinkedIn + Not called yet
- Bounced email + Has phone number
- Open rate over 3x average (highly engaged)
```

**2. Power Dialer (Click-to-Call)**
```
- Pre-loaded contact list
- One-click calling
- Automatic logging
- Built-in recording
- Notes integration
- Follow-up automation
```

**3. Call Recording**
```
- Records all calls automatically
- Store in system for review
- Review with SDR weekly
- Identify improvement areas
- Learn what works
- Preserve for training
```

**4. CRM Integration**
```
- Logs all calls automatically
- Captures call data
- Notes saved to contact record
- Follow-up tasks created
- Full history visible
- SDR can focus on calling, not admin
```

**5. Twilio Integration (Optional But Recommended)**
```
- Use local phone numbers based on prospect location
- US prospect? Call from US local number
- UK prospect? Call from UK local number
- Answer rates increase dramatically with local numbers
- Built into Go High Level
```

**6. Workflow Automation**
```
Example Workflow:
- SDR sets appointment
- Trigger: Automatically send calendar invite to prospect
- Trigger: Add task to your queue to prepare
- Trigger: Create follow-up task if no-show
- Trigger: Log to CRM with appointment details
```

**7. Call Tracking**
```
Dashboard shows:
- Calls made per day/week/month
- Calls per SDR
- Appointments set
- Conversion rates
- Best performing SDRs
- Best performing time blocks
```

## Common SDR Performance Issues & Solutions

### Issue 1: Low Appointment Rate

**Possible Causes:**
- Not following your process/scripts
- Not qualifying properly
- Weak value proposition delivery
- Not handling objections
- Moving to close too quickly

**Solutions:**
```
1. Review recordings of calls
2. Identify where it's breaking down
3. Provide specific coaching
4. Have them practice with you
5. Review improved calls
6. Celebrate improvement
```

### Issue 2: High Voicemail Rate (Not Getting Through)

**Possible Causes:**
- Calling wrong people/numbers
- Calling wrong times of day
- Numbers are outdated
- Not using local numbers (Go High Level/Twilio)
- Not warming prospects first

**Solutions:**
```
1. Check data quality
2. Switch to local numbers (Twilio)
3. Adjust calling times
4. Warm prospects before calling
5. Use direct lines when possible
6. Try early morning (7-8 AM) calls
```

### Issue 3: Low Quality Appointments

**Possible Causes:**
- Not qualifying properly
- Booking anyone who says yes
- Not understanding ideal buyer
- Rushing to close

**Solutions:**
```
1. Remind them of ideal customer profile
2. Have them ask more qualifying questions
3. Review "good" vs "bad" appointments
4. Provide objection handling training
5. Require 2 qualifying questions before booking
```

### Issue 4: Not Following Scripts/Process

**Possible Causes:**
- Don't understand the scripts
- Think they know better
- Lack of coaching/oversight
- No accountability

**Solutions:**
```
1. Review scripts together
2. Record practice calls
3. Identify where they deviate
4. Explain why scripts work
5. Weekly call reviews with feedback
6. Make compliance a KPI
7. Adjust commission for non-compliance
```

### Issue 5: Low Morale / Motivation

**Possible Causes:**
- Not making enough appointments (commission too low)
- Getting rejected frequently
- Lack of appreciation
- No clear feedback

**Solutions:**
```
1. Celebrate small wins
2. Show them the math (their appointments = your revenue)
3. Give specific, actionable feedback
4. Provide encouragement
5. Increase incentive (bonus for hitting targets)
6. Have 1-on-1 check-ins
7. Remind them why what they do matters
```

## SDR Compensation Models

### Model 1: Pure Commission
```
15% of appointment value

Example:
- Appointment price: $200
- Appointment set: $200 × 15% = $30
- 10 appointments: $300/month

Pro: No baseline cost; they only earn if productive
Con: Can lead to low morale if struggling
Best for: Experienced SDRs with track record
```

### Model 2: Hourly + Commission
```
Base: $15/hour (or local minimum wage)
Commission: 10% of appointment value

Example:
- 160 hours/month @ $15 = $2,400 base
- 10 appointments × $200 × 10% = $200 commission
- Total: $2,600/month

Pro: Security + incentive; better for less experienced
Con: Higher baseline cost
Best for: Building trust; longer-term relationships
```

### Model 3: Tiered Commission (Recommended)
```
0-25 appointments:   15% commission
25-50 appointments:  20% commission
50-75 appointments:  25% commission
75+ appointments:    30% commission

Average payout increases as they hit higher volumes
Rewards performance without excessive cost to you

Example: 60 appointments in a month
- Appts 1-25: 15% = $750
- Appts 25-50: 20% = $1,000
- Appts 50-60: 25% = $600
- Total: $2,350
```

### Model 4: Hybrid Tiered
```
Base: $10/hour
Plus tiered commission above

Provides security + high upside potential
```

## Building a Remote SDR Team (Scaling)

### When to Hire Multiple SDRs

**Trigger Point 1: Volume**
```
You're getting 20+ appointments per week from one SDR
But you can only close 15/week
Result: Bottleneck on your end
Solution: Hire 2nd SDR for 5+ more appointments/week
```

**Trigger Point 2: 100+ Prospect List**
```
One SDR can effectively handle 100-200 prospects
Once you have 300+ prospects, you need 2 SDRs
Keeps call volume high without burning out one person
```

**Trigger Point 3: Multiple Markets/Industries**
```
SDR for each industry/market
Different value props and processes
More specialized = better results
```

### Team Management

**1. Establish Standard Processes**
```
- Same scripts for all SDRs
- Same CRM setup for all
- Same KPI tracking
- Same quality standards
- Consistent approach across team
```

**2. Create Competition (Healthy)**
```
- Display daily/weekly leaderboards
- Show who's top performer
- Public recognition for wins
- Friendly competition for bonuses
- Increases motivation
```

**3. Weekly Team Calls**
```
- 30 minutes every Monday
- Review metrics for previous week
- Discuss challenges
- Share successful approaches
- Celebrate wins
- Set goals for week ahead
```

**4. Pair Higher Performers with Newer SDRs**
```
- New SDR shadows high performer for 1-2 weeks
- Learns best practices
- Hears good calls (recordings)
- Gets one-on-one coaching
- Accelerates improvement
```

**5. Monthly Incentive Bonus**
```
- Top SDR gets $500 bonus
- 2nd place: $250
- Or team bonus if group hits collective target
- Drives motivation and performance
- Creates positive team culture
```

## Key Takeaway

**Good SDRs are hard to find. Great SDRs are rare. Once you find one:**

1. **Train them properly** (give them everything they need)
2. **Set clear expectations** (KPIs, goals, timeline)
3. **Coach them continuously** (weekly call reviews)
4. **Incentivize performance** (commission structure)
5. **Appreciate good work** (recognition, bonuses)
6. **Scale slowly** (one proven SDR before hiring more)

An excellent SDR can generate 50-100+ appointments per month, which could be $100,000+ in additional revenue for your business at a small commission cost.

Invest in getting this right.
