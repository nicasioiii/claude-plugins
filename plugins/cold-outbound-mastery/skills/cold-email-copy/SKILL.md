# Cold Email Copywriting

## Core Principle

**A cold email has one job: book a meeting.** You will never sell a high-ticket deal through email alone. The email initiates a conversation; the phone call closes. Every word must earn the next word until the prospect hits reply.

**The 8-Second Rule (30MPC):** Prospects spend 8 seconds reading an email after deciding to open it. If your email cannot be read and understood in 8 seconds, it fails.

## The Three Major Frameworks

### Framework 1: Berman 3-C (Scale B2B Lead Gen)

**Best for:** Agencies, SaaS, service businesses sending 30-100+ emails/day to mid-market ($5M-$150M revenue) companies.

**Structure:**
1. **Compliment** -- personalized first line (from `first-lines` skill)
2. **Case Study** -- one sentence proving you deliver results
3. **Call to Action** -- yes/no question driving toward a meeting

**The Million Dollar Email template:**
```
Subject: Quick question

Hi {first_name},

{CUSTOM FIRST LINE}

I specialize in {what you do for their type of company}. Recently, we helped {CLIENT NAME}, a {description of client}, {achieve RESULT} in {TIMEFRAME}.

{CTA -- e.g., "Can you take on more clients at {Company}? Let me know and I can send over a few times to chat."}

Thanks,
{SIGNATURE}
```

**Key rule:** The more specific your case study is to the prospect's industry, the LESS specific your first line needs to be. A treasury department case study pitched to treasury departments can use "Big fan of the work you do" as the first line.

---

### Framework 2: 30MPC REPLY Method (Enterprise SDR/AE)

**Best for:** SDRs and AEs at B2B companies selling to mid-market and enterprise. Problem-first approach backed by Gong data from 300M+ cold calls.

**Structure:**
- **R** = Reason (first line -- shows up in preview text)
- **P** = Problem (demonstrate the problem, not the solution)
- **L/V** = Value & Social Proof (customer name + outcome)
- **Y** = CTA (interest-based question, never ask for time)

**Critical rules:**
- Subject line: 5 words or less, no gimmicks, internal camouflage
- Reason MUST appear in the first 5-10 words (flip sentence structure to lead with personalization)
- Problem line uses the messaging matrix (see `prospect-research` skill)
- Use customer voice, not product voice -- kill Nothing Nouns ("single source of truth") and Vomit Verbs ("automate," "centralize," "streamline")
- Social proof impact: 2.25x reply rate for managers, 1.5x for directors (Gong)

---

### Framework 3: Bree Weber Ethical Cold Pitching (High-Value Freelance)

**Best for:** Freelancers, consultants, and agencies pitching $5K-$50K+ projects to 1-5 prospects per week. Quality over quantity -- 50%+ conversion rate reported.

**Structure (8 steps):**
1. **Make a Connection** -- relevant, specific, complimentary opening tied to what is top of mind for them
2. **Shift the Conversation** -- connect to larger goal, flip the script to reveal a problem/gap/opportunity
3. **Present the Solution** -- specific project (not you), loosely scoped with fascination bullets
4. **Introduce Yourself** -- OPTIONAL, 1-2 lines max, comes AFTER the solution
5. **Reveal Urgency** -- restate problem, show it getting worse, authentic scarcity only
6. **Future Pace** -- hyper-specific visualization of their life after the solution
7. **Call to Action** -- minimum viable ask, probably NOT a call
8. **Subject Line** -- write LAST, mix: goal + problem + relevancy + urgency

**Key principle:** The pitch is 90%+ about THEM. Your self-introduction is optional.

## Where Instructors Disagree

### Email Length
| Instructor | Position |
|---|---|
| Alex Berman | Ultra-short (3-5 sentences, under 100 words) |
| 30MPC | Short but problem-rich (under 150 words) |
| Bree Weber | Long-form (multi-paragraph, 90%+ about recipient) |
| Acquisition X | Under 150 words, 2-3 paragraphs max |

**Resolution:** Match length to context. B2B lead gen at scale = short (Berman/Acquisition X). High-value freelance pitches to 1-5 prospects/week = long (Bree Weber). Enterprise SDR with personalized triggers = medium (REPLY Method).

### Calendar Links in Emails
| Instructor | Position |
|---|---|
| Alex Berman | Never include links -- target 0% click rate |
| Cold Email Wizard | Avoid in initial emails, OK in follow-ups after interest |
| Acquisition X | No links in first email; OK after reply |

**Resolution:** No links in the initial cold email. Calendar links are acceptable after a positive reply or in later follow-ups.

### CTA Style
| Instructor | Position |
|---|---|
| Berman | Yes/no questions ("Can you take on more clients?") |
| Bree Weber | Minimum viable ask, often NOT a call |
| 30MPC | Interest-based, never ask for time (-44% reply rate per Gong) |
| Cold Email Wizard | Qualifying questions ("Do you use Klaviyo?") |

**Resolution:** Default to interest-based CTAs. Only ask for calls when genuine urgency exists or after qualifying via email. The Cold Email Wizard "One Question" angle is the highest-converting initial approach for tool/service qualification.

**Key insight (Cold Email Wizard):** First line quality matters LESS than question structure. A mediocre first line paired with a killer qualifying question (one you already know the answer to) outperforms a god-level first line with a weak CTA. The question does the heavy lifting -- it gives the prospect an easy, low-commitment reply path. This is why the "One Question" angle converts at 15-30% even with basic personalization.

## Manual Work vs. Automation (Cold Email Wizard)

Manual email work beats automation for call quality. When you personally research prospects, write custom first lines, and craft individual emails, the resulting booked calls are dramatically higher quality -- prospects arrive pre-sold because the outreach felt human. Automated sequences generate more volume but lower show rates and worse call quality. For agencies and consultants selling $3K+/month retainers, the manual approach produces better ROI despite lower send volume. Use automation only for follow-up sequences after the initial manual touch.

## Technical Copy Rules (Non-Negotiable)

These rules come from `email-infrastructure` and directly constrain your copy:

1. **Under 150 words** -- what looks spacious on desktop becomes a wall on mobile
2. **Plain text only** -- NO HTML, no headers, no formatted signatures (50-60% deliverability improvement)
3. **No links in first email** -- custom tracking domain already counts as 1 link
4. **No images** -- word-to-image ratio triggers promotional filters
5. **No spam trigger words** -- "guaranteed," "limited time offer," "buy now," "free," "lowest price"
6. **Use spintax on CTAs** -- "Let's jump on a call" | "Are you free today?" | "Keen for a demo?"
7. **No link shorteners** (TinyURL, Bitly) -- ESPs associate these with spammers
8. **Include unsubscribe option** -- CAN-SPAM requirement
9. **2-3 paragraphs max** -- 1-2 sentences per paragraph

## The 4C Formula (Acquisition X)

Every cold email must pass all four tests:

1. **Concise** -- brevity wins in a short-attention-span world
2. **Clear** -- one goal per email, easy to understand, use white space
3. **Compelling** -- spikes curiosity, grabs attention, creates engagement
4. **Credible** -- back claims with facts, testimonials, or portfolio proof

## Three Problem + Value Formulas (30MPC)

### Formula 1: The Deeper Problem
> Problem: The hard part isn't {surface level problem}, it's {deeper problem}.
> Value: We help {social proof} {achieve outcomes}.

### Formula 2: The Want/But
> Problem: {Personas} want {aspiration}... but run into challenges {challenge}.
> Value: {Social proof} are using our help to {outcome}.

### Formula 3: The Before/After
> Problem: {Social proof} ran into challenges...
> Value: They used our help to {aspirations}... {include testimonial quote}

## Four Questions to Write Triggering Emails (30MPC)

1. **What's the crappy job if you invert the solution?** Don't sell the drill, sell the hole.
2. **So what?** What does that crappy job mean for the business?
3. **Why do they have that problem?** What does a prospect look like when they have it?
4. **How do you prevent the so-what?** Combine into: Personalization + Problem + Solution + CTA.
