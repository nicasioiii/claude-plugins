# Case Study Formula

## The One-Sentence Case Study (Berman)

### Formula
> "Recently we helped {COMPANY NAME} {achieve RESULT} in {TIME} using {YOUR SERVICE}."

### What Makes a Killer Case Study Sentence

A strong case study sentence contains ALL of these:
1. **A recognizable company name** -- or a descriptive identifier if mid-market (e.g., "an NYC-based developer")
2. **Specific numbers** -- revenue, percentage growth, time to result
3. **Relevance** to the prospect's industry
4. **Uses the prospect's language** -- "finding new home buyers" for realtors, not "finding new customers"

If done correctly, this sentence does 90% of the selling before you get on the phone.

## Translating Case Studies to Different Niches

### The Translation Exercise
1. Take your best case study result
2. Rewrite the outcome using the target prospect's industry language
3. Replace the company description with something the prospect identifies with
4. Keep the specific number -- it is the anchor

### Example Translation
- **Original:** "Helped a SaaS company increase MRR by 40% in 3 months"
- **For e-commerce:** "Helped an online retailer increase monthly revenue by 40% in 3 months"
- **For agencies:** "Helped a creative agency increase recurring retainer revenue by 40% in 3 months"
- **For local business:** "Helped a dental practice increase new patient revenue by 40% in 3 months"

Even for non-revenue services (logo design, website design) -- tie to revenue impact whenever possible.

## Social Proof Integration (30MPC)

### Gong Data on Social Proof Impact
- Including social proof for managers and below: **2.25x** impact on reply rate
- For directors: **1.5x** impact on reply rate
- Social proof becomes LESS impactful as you go higher in the org -- VPs and C-suite care more about the problem than your customer list

### Social Proof Formula for Email
```
{Company name similar to prospect} {ran into / faced / struggled with} {specific problem}.

{They used our help to / We helped them} {achieve specific outcome}.
```

### Including Testimonial Quotes (30MPC Before/After Formula)
```
"{Direct quote from customer}" -- {Name, Title at Company}
```

This is especially powerful when the quoted person holds the same title as the prospect.

## Three Case Study Requirements (Berman)

A good case study must be:
1. **Hyper-specific** -- "grew dozens of Instagram accounts for realtors" not "grew Instagram accounts for everyone"
2. **Increased a key number** -- ideally revenue (e.g., "$1M in 6 months")
3. **Appeals to similar companies** -- the result must be generalizable to the target market

## "No Case Studies" Strategies

### Option 1: Borrow Credibility from Mentors (Bree Weber)
When you lack big client names, name-drop your teachers/mentors:
- "I've studied conversion principles from CRO top guns like Joanna Wiebe and ConversionXL"
- This is legitimate because it signals training and methodology, not fake results

### Option 2: Frame Modest Results Optimally (Bree Weber)
- A $12K total launch becomes "$12K in the first hour" (if most came in the first hour of a flash sale)
- 3x conversions is still 3x even if original baseline was low
- "Email open rates consistently above 60%" works even for small, highly engaged lists
- "Everything you have done counts and can be incorporated into a pitch"

### Option 3: Borrow Another Agency's Case Studies (Berman)
- Partner with a bigger agency and use their results (with permission)
- Be transparent that you are partnering: "Our team, in partnership with {Agency}, helped..."

### Option 4: Use Your Own Results as the Case Study
- Only if you have a major client in your target space AND massive results
- Be honest about what is YOUR work vs. team work

### Option 5: Lead with the Problem Instead (Bree Weber / 30MPC)
- Skip the case study section entirely
- Make the problem so specific and well-researched that the prospect says "this person gets it" without needing proof
- Bree Weber's Run the World pitch included NO credibility section and still got a response in 1 hour

### Option 6: Offer Risk-Reversal (Berman / Cold Email Wizard)
- "Let's do $1,000 for one campaign, if you don't make the money back, I'll refund it."
- Performance-based pricing eliminates the need for proof: "you only pay when we deliver profitable results"

## Case Study Homework (Berman)

1. Select 3 case studies you want to clone with new clients
2. Translate each into a one-sentence case study using the formula
3. Test which case study resonates best with each target vertical
4. A/B test case study sentences in your email campaigns

## Case Study Selection Criteria

### For Scale Outreach (Berman / Acquisition X)
Choose the case study closest to the prospect's:
- Industry vertical
- Company size
- Specific problem
- Geographic relevance (when applicable)

### For High-Value Pitches (Bree Weber)
The case study is less critical than the problem identification. Spend research time on understanding their specific situation rather than finding the perfect case study match.

### For Enterprise (30MPC)
Use case studies that match the prospect's:
- Persona level (manager-level proof for managers, VP-level for VPs)
- Specific problem from your messaging matrix
- Include the testimonial quote format when available

## Niche-Specific Case Study Formulas

### SaaS / Tech Companies
> "We helped {COMPANY} reduce {METRIC} by {X}% in {TIME}, freeing their team to focus on {CORE ACTIVITY}."

Use language like "reduce churn," "increase MRR," "shorten sales cycle," "improve activation rate." SaaS buyers respond to metrics they track in their own dashboards.

### E-commerce / DTC Brands
> "One of our clients, a {SIZE} {PRODUCT TYPE} brand, went from {BEFORE METRIC} to {AFTER METRIC} in {TIME} using {YOUR SERVICE}."

E-commerce buyers care about revenue, ROAS, AOV, and email revenue percentage. Always tie to revenue even if your service is not directly revenue-generating (e.g., "better product photography led to a 22% increase in conversion rate").

### Local / Service Businesses
> "We helped a {TYPE OF BUSINESS} in {LOCATION} generate {NUMBER} new {CLIENTS/PATIENTS/CUSTOMERS} in {TIME}."

Local businesses respond to tangible outcomes -- new customers, phone calls, appointments booked. Avoid abstract metrics like "impressions" or "reach."

### Recruiting / Staffing
> "We successfully placed {NUMBER} {ROLE TYPE} for clients in the past {TIME PERIOD}, with an average time-to-fill of {DAYS} days."

Recruiting buyers care about speed, quality of candidates, and retention rates. Include retention data if available (e.g., "92% still employed after 12 months").

### Agency-to-Agency
> "We white-labeled {SERVICE} for {AGENCY NAME}, helping them add ${REVENUE} in recurring revenue without hiring a single new team member."

Agencies buying from other agencies care about margin preservation, scalability, and client retention.

## Common Case Study Mistakes

1. **Too vague** -- "We helped a company grow" (no specifics, no numbers, no timeframe)
2. **Wrong audience** -- Using an enterprise case study when pitching SMBs (or vice versa)
3. **Outdated results** -- Case studies older than 2 years feel stale. Update the framing or find newer examples.
4. **Too many case studies** -- One well-chosen case study per email is enough. Multiple case studies create decision fatigue and dilute impact.
5. **Leading with the case study** -- The case study supports the problem statement, it does not replace it. Always lead with the prospect's problem, then prove you can solve it.
6. **Forgetting the timeframe** -- "$1M in revenue" means nothing without "in 6 months." The timeframe creates urgency and believability.
7. **Using jargon the prospect does not use** -- "Increased ROAS by 4.2x" means nothing to a business owner who thinks in terms of "dollars spent vs. dollars earned"
