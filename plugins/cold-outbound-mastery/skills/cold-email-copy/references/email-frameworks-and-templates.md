# Email Frameworks & Templates Library

## Framework 1: Berman 3-C -- The Million Dollar Email

### Template (Exact Structure)
```
Subject: Quick question

Hi {first_name},

{CUSTOM FIRST LINE -- e.g., "Been following {COMPANY} for a while and love your work. Awesome job with {SPECIFIC PROJECT}."}

I specialize in {what you do for their type of company}. Recently, we helped {CLIENT NAME}, a {description of client}, {achieve RESULT} in {TIMEFRAME}.

{CTA -- e.g., "Can you take on more clients at {Company}? Let me know and I can send over a few times to chat."}

Thanks,
{SIGNATURE}
```

### Customization Rules
- Case study specificity inversely correlates with first line specificity needed
- Use prospect's language (realtors say "finding new home buyers" not "finding new customers")
- Every element targets one goal: booking a meeting

### CTA Variants
- **Simple ask (beginners):** "Interested? Let me know and I can send over a few times to chat."
- **Specific benefit (advanced):** "Can you take on more clients at {Company}? Let me know and I can send over a few times to chat."
- Use specific benefit when selling lead gen/marketing; simple ask for everything else

## Framework 2: 30MPC REPLY Method

### Structure
```
Subject: {5 words or less, internal camouflage}

{REASON -- personalization trigger in first 5-10 words, shows in preview text}

{PROBLEM -- demonstrate the problem using messaging matrix, customer voice}

{VALUE -- social proof customer name + outcome achieved}

{CTA -- interest-based question, never ask for time}
```

### Three Problem + Value Formula Templates

**The Deeper Problem:**
```
The hard part isn't {surface level problem}, it's {deeper problem}.

We help {social proof company} {achieve specific outcomes}.

{Interest-based CTA}
```

**The Want/But:**
```
{Personas} want {aspiration}... but run into challenges {challenge}.

{Social proof companies} are using our help to {outcome}.

{Interest-based CTA}
```

**The Before/After:**
```
{Social proof company} ran into challenges with {specific problem}.

"{Testimonial quote}" -- {Name, Title}

{Interest-based CTA}
```

### PS Line
People always read the PS. Add one personalization nugget to humanize yourself and prove a human wrote it.

## Framework 3: Cold Email Wizard -- The One Question Angle

### Why It Works
Pre-research their tech stack, then ask a qualifying question you already know the answer to. Creates an easy, low-commitment reply. Reply rate: 15-30%.

### Template
```
Subject: quick question

Hi {first_name},

{PERSONALIZED FIRST LINE -- can even be mediocre quality}

Curious, do you use {TOOL/SERVICE} for {FUNCTION}?
```

**That is the ENTIRE email.**

### Follow-Up When They Say Yes (Semi-Scripted)
```
That's great {name}. Thanks for letting me know.

Not sure if it'd help or not, but my team is offering free {Service Name} strategy sessions.

If you're interested, I'll share my calendar link so you can schedule a time.

Have an awesome rest of your day! Cheers
```

### Variations by Offer
- "Curious, do you use Klaviyo for email marketing?"
- "Quick question, would you be interested in receiving debt financing for {Company}?"
- "Quick question, would you be interested in getting {Company}'s products in more online retailers?"
- "Quick question, would you be interested in a free audit of your Google ads account?"
- "Quick question, would you be interested in seeing who exactly is visiting your website?"

### The Negative Framing Variant
- "You wouldn't be interested in {service/benefit}, would you {Name}?"
- Invites a contrarian "actually yes" response

## Framework 4: Bree Weber 8-Step Ethical Cold Pitch

### Step-by-Step Template

**Step 1 -- Make a Connection:**
Message-match with what is top of mind for them. Reference specific details (recent launch, feature, event). Be genuinely complimentary.

**Step 2 -- Shift the Conversation:**
- Connect to their larger goal
- Flip the script: "But here's the thing..." / "The truth is..." / "What if you..."
- Identify pain point in 1-2 lines
- Empathize deeply -- pull from research and VOC

**Four types of "problems" to reveal:**
- Problem/Obstacle -- something blocking their path
- Challenge -- an intense goal they are driving towards
- Gap -- something they have potentially missed
- Opportunity -- something available but not yet leveraged

**Step 3 -- Present the Solution:**
Introduce the specific project (NOT you). Outline loosely with fascination bullets:
- Bookend with most compelling bullets
- Bold the benefit portion
- Use "so that" to chain features to benefits

**Step 4 -- Introduce Yourself (OPTIONAL):**
1-2 lines max. Formula: "As a {type of freelancer}, I help {how prospect sees themselves} (like you) to {accomplish outcome}."

**Step 5 -- Reveal Urgency:**
Restate problem, show it worsening, call out authentic deadlines. Never manufacture urgency.

**Step 6 -- Future Pace:**
Hyper-specific visualization. Name their dog, reference their children, describe their afternoon. Focus on results, not process.

**Step 7 -- Call to Action:**
Minimum viable ask. "Tell me what's left on your plate, and I'll send you a video walkthrough." NOT "Let's hop on a call."

**Step 8 -- Subject Line (Write LAST):**
Mix: highlight a goal + empathize with a problem + show relevancy + reveal urgency.

## Framework 5: Acquisition X 4C Formula

### Template Structure
```
Hi {first_name},

I saw {company/website} struggling with {pain_point}.

We provide {your_service} for {target_market}.

I have {number} ideas that you can implement right away:
- Idea 1: {actionable_tip}
- Idea 2: {actionable_tip}
- Idea 3: {actionable_tip}

Our previous clients: {testimonial/review -- one line}

Keen for a chat?

{signature}
```

### Compliance Checklist
- [ ] Under 150 words
- [ ] No inline images
- [ ] No links (except custom tracking domain)
- [ ] No spam trigger words
- [ ] Personalization included
- [ ] Spintax on CTA and key variables
- [ ] No HTML elements
- [ ] Plain text only

## Niche Scripts Library (Berman)

### Instagram Management for Realtors
Subject: "A question about {COMPANY}" / "Love your instagram"
> Hey {FIRST_NAME}, Love what you're posting on Instagram - your content is awesome! You deserve a much higher reach. I'd love to work with you. I've managed and grown dozens of Instagram accounts for realtors over the last few years, and specialize in finding new customers, not just building followers. Mind if I send over some details?

### Google Ads for Rehab Facilities
Subject: "Quick Question" / "Help?"
> Hi {First Name}, huge fan of the work you do at {Company}! You're really changing lives. We've been running Google ads for 12 years and have filled over 11,000 beds for rehab facilities. I would love to discuss your Google Ads account and see if we can identify any easy wins. Mind if I send over a few times for a quick call?

### App Development for City Governments
Subject: "New library app?" / "Quick question"
> Hey {Name}, {first line}. I've been working with city governments over the last 7 years to update their legacy applications, and would love to discuss rewriting your tax collection software. Just wrapped a project with the City of San Diego treasury department. Mind if I send a few times to talk further?

### E-commerce SEO
Subject: {Company Name} / "Quick Question"
> Hey {Name}, Huge fan of {STORE NAME}, my wife loves the shoes. Anyway, made me want to reach out. I help eCommerce stores get more organic customers and would love to do the same for you. For instance, a client of ours just went from 0-30,000 visitors last year. Let's hop on a call? Would love to do similar for {Company} (or give you free advice).

### Local Business Marketing
Subject: "Good rating on Yelp"
> Hi {Name}, I see {company} on Yelp and we are very impressed that you have a great rating :) Recently we helped a hair studio in Dusseldorf that needed more people to work there, and we generated 9 new people in 7 days. I'd love to do the same for you. Are you around this week for a call?

### Lead Gen / Copywriting with Risk Reversal
> Hi {firstName}, {Personal Line}. I specialize in writing emails for tour companies. Just last month, one client increased her sales by 141%, and another client in the entrepreneurship niche made $4,000 with just one newsletter I wrote for him. Let's talk? Would love to do similar for {companyName}.

Risk-reversal follow-up: "Let's do $1,000 for one campaign, if you don't make the money back, I'll refund it. If we do make money, you hire me monthly."

### Pay-Per-Call Lead Gen
Direct offer with transparent pricing:
- "$300 per qualified call" with 3 qualification criteria listed
- Share close rate data: "Our clients typically see a 10-20% close rate"
- Use ROI math: "If you were spending $3000 for 10 calls, closed 2 retainer clients at $2500/mo..."

Figured I'd send a quick email as well in case you aren't as active on LinkedIn as I am :) If you're open to it, choose a good time for us to connect using my calendar link below...

## Offer-Specific Patterns (Cold Email Wizard)

### Klaviyo / Email Marketing
- Question angle: "Curious, do you use Klaviyo for email marketing?"
- Value follow-up: "Got a few Klaviyo flow ideas that could increase your email revenue by 500% or more."
- After yes: "Not sure if it'd help or not, but my team offers free Klaviyo strategy sessions."

### Consulting / High-Level
- Softer approach: "Would you be open to connecting for a quick call to explore a professional connection in the spirit of networking?"
- Follow-up: "I really enjoy connecting with other peer leaders who are passionate about what they do."

### Full-Service Marketing (Multi-Angle Sequence)
- Email 1: Question about website visitor tracking
- Email 2: "Would you be interested in connecting to explore ways {Company} would benefit from account-based marketing?"
- Email 3: "How're your sales guys keeping {Company}'s offer relevant to prospects' needs?"
