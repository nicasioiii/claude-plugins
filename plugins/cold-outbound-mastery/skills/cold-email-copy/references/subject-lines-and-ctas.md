# Subject Lines & CTAs

## Subject Line Performance Data

### Berman's Top 10 Subject Lines (Ranked by Effectiveness)
1. Quick question
2. {First Name}, quick question
3. Quick question, {Company Name}
4. {Relevant Emoji} (controller for gaming, robot for tech)
5. Question
6. Something for you, {First Name}
7. Interview invite
8. I've got a story for you
9. {Name} recommended I get in touch
10. Intro

**"Quick question" performs best across ALL niches, ALL industries, ALL testing.** Test in order: start with #1, if it underperforms, try #2, etc.

### 30MPC 7 Frameworks for 40%+ Open Rates

**Golden Rules:**
- 5 words or less (highest open/reply rates)
- Little to no capitalization (first letter of first word only)
- Internal camouflage -- use language they would use internally
- No gimmicks -- leave out first name and company name (with rare exception)

**Framework 1: Pervasive Problems**
Macro problems most everyone is experiencing.
- "Unfinished learning" / "Manual welds" / "Participant dropouts"

**Framework 2: Industry Trends**
A trend every prospect is thinking about.
- "Tiered support" / "Welders" / "Remote engagement"

**Framework 3: Company/Org Initiatives**
When you can identify a specific project.
- "WCUS achievement gaps" / "AI weld inspections" / "Trial efficiency"

**Framework 4: Exec Leadership Quotes**
Pull quotes from execs on webinars, podcasts, LinkedIn, quarterly calls.
- "Improving student equity in 2026" / "2030 welding automation in Ford factories"

**Framework 5: People on Their Team & Shared Connections**
Specific people experiencing the problem.
- "Tom's district-wide learning outcomes" / "Dave said to reach out"

**Framework 6: Short Pattern Interrupts**
Feel like off-the-cuff internal email.
- "Student engagement idea" / "DET question" / "delays"

**Framework 7: Competitor Share**
Works after competitor analysis.
- "LAUSD / OCPS" / "John Deere, Kubota, Caterpillar" / "See Moderna?"

### Bree Weber's 4 Approaches (Mix and Match)
1. **Highlight a goal** -- reference their specific business goal
2. **Empathize with a problem** -- reference a pain point
3. **Show relevancy** -- signal timeliness and specificity
4. **Reveal urgency** -- natural deadlines, events, market shifts

**Examples:**
- "Everyday Copy Launch" -- goal + relevancy
- "24 Days until T&W goes to SXSW" -- countdown + goal + urgency
- "Scaling Run The World in the wake of the coronavirus outbreak" -- goal + relevancy + urgency

**Write the subject line LAST.** Early drafts of the email body often inspire the best subject lines.

### Cold Email Wizard Observed High-Performers
- "quick question" (lowercase, most common)
- "Re: LinkedIn" (implies prior conversation)
- "Re: {Person's Name}" (for referral strategy -- near 100% open)
- "{Company Name}" (just the prospect's company)
- "{Topic}" (e.g., "financing")

## Preview Text Strategy (30MPC)

**The personalization MUST show up in the first 5-10 words of the first line.**

Flip sentence structure to lead with personalization:
- Bad: "Frank, with the recent approval of the FAPI study..."
- Good: "With the recent approval of the FAPI study, Frank..."

The preview text is visible in the inbox before opening. If it reads like a mass email, they delete without opening.

## CTA Performance Data (Gong, 5,000+ Cold Emails)

| CTA Type | Impact on Reply Rate |
|---|---|
| Asking for a meeting/time | **-44%** (worst) |
| Asking about a problem (open-ended) | Slightly negative |
| Asking for interest | **Positive** |
| Making an offer | **+28%** (best) |

**Time is the worst thing you can ask for.** The prospect thinks: "What am I going to get in return for my time?"

**58% of meetings buyers attend with reps are a waste of time.** Your demo is NOT value -- they have seen demos from all your competitors.

**Only 3% of your market is in buying mode.** To engage the other 97%, they need a reason to get on a call beyond your solution.

## Three Types of Offers (30MPC)

### Offer 1: Pitch the Blind Date (Easiest)
Position WHO the buyer will meet. Hype the AE, solutions engineer, or expert.
- "Our VP of Manufacturing Automation, who spent 15 years at John Deere, would love to share how other VPs are approaching this."

### Offer 2: 1:Many Offers (Reusable)
High-quality, reusable insights that feel tailored.
- "We published a benchmark with hundreds of clinical ops teams studying how they approach trials."

### Offer 3: 1:1 Offers (Highest Converting)
Custom-tailored experiences for the prospect.
- Free risk analysis benchmarked against competitors
- Personalized audit of their website checkout flow
- Free data, licenses, or pilots
- Hosting workshops with their team

## Interest-Based CTA Library

### Standard Interest CTAs
- "Would it be a bad idea to chat further?"
- "Is this relevant to you?"
- "Interested in a quick intro to {name}?"
- "Want to see how you stack up?"
- "Does this align with your 2025 priorities?"
- "Worth a quick chat?"
- "Did any of that even sound moderately interesting?"

### Berman Yes/No CTAs
- "Can you take on more clients at {Company}?"
- "Mind if I send over some details?"
- "Mind if I send a few times to talk further?"
- "Interested? Let me know and I can send over a few times to chat."

### Cold Email Wizard Qualifying CTAs
- "Curious, do you use {tool} for {function}?"
- "Quick question, would you be interested in {specific offer}?"
- "You wouldn't be interested in {service}, would you {Name}?"

### Bree Weber Minimum Viable Ask CTAs
- "Tell me what's left on your plate, and I'll send you a video walkthrough"
- "Do you have 15 minutes this week?" (ONLY with genuine time urgency)
- "Would you be open to me sending a quick Loom walkthrough of what I'm thinking?"

## Three CTA Tricks (30MPC)

### 1. Softening Language
"Are you opposed to..." instead of "Are you interested?" Reduces pressure.

### 2. No-Based Questions
"Are you opposed to..." -- prospects feel safer saying no, which paradoxically leads to more yeses.

### 3. Mini Push-Away Statements
"My guess is this came out of left field" -- reduces perceived sales pressure.

## Call Naming (Cold Email Wizard)

Do NOT call it an "intro call" or "sales call" -- give it a branded name:
- "Free Klaviyo Strategy Session" > "quick call"
- "Branding Strategy Call" > "intro call"
- "Free Google Ads Strategy Call" > "discovery call"
- "20-minute intro call" with duration specificity also works

The name should communicate value, not sales intent.

## A/B Testing Subject Lines (Acquisition X)

### The 10/10/80 Method
1. Take 20% of your lead list for testing
2. Send 10% with Subject Line A
3. Send 10% with Subject Line B
4. Identify winner (e.g., 50% open vs. 25% open)
5. Use winner for remaining 80%

### What to A/B Test (Priority Order)
1. Subject lines (highest impact on open rates)
2. CTA style (soft vs. hard, reply vs. calendar link)
3. Email body structure (short vs. long)
4. Offers (different service angles for same audience)
5. Audience segments (same message to different ICPs)
