# Cold Email Copywriting & Sequences

## Before You Start: Check for Product Marketing Context

**CRITICAL:** Before helping write cold emails, confirm:
1. Do you have a proven case study or result to reference? (NOT required but significantly improves response)
2. Have you hyper-specified your offer? (E.g., not "copywriting services" but "increase newsletter revenue 30%")
3. Do you have a warm-up email account ready? (New inboxes need 2-3 weeks warmup before sending)

If NO case study exists → see `offer-construction` reference file first. If NO warm-up → pause sending for 2-3 weeks.

See reference file `offer-construction.md` for building irresistible offers. See `first-lines` skill for crafting personalized opening lines.

## The 3C's Framework (Alex Berman)

Every high-converting cold email has three elements:

### 1. Compliment - Custom Opening Line
- Personalized first line showing research
- NOT generic ("I've been following your work")
- Reference something specific to THEM: recent news, specific detail, observation about their business
- **Benchmark impact:** Custom first lines increase response rates 10x (1% → 10%)

### 2. Case Study - One-Sentence Proof
- Recent success with similar company/situation
- Format: "Recently we helped [COMPANY] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME]"
- Must include: Specific client, specific metric/outcome, timeframe
- Hyper-specific > generic ("dozens of clients" doesn't work)
- **Alternative:** If no case study, see offer-construction for "no-brainer offer" approach

### 3. Call To Action - Yes/No Question
- Simple, binary question requesting meeting
- Examples:
  - "Can you take on more clients at {{company}}?"
  - "Are you around this week to talk?"
  - "Would it make sense to grab 15 minutes?"
- Avoid: "Let me know your thoughts" (too vague), "Let's partner" (too big)

## Core Benchmarks (Target Metrics)

**What to expect from a well-executed campaign:**

| Metric | Target | Reality Check |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Open Rate | 80% | High-quality leads + good subject line = 80%+ |
| Response Rate | 15% | 1 in 7 people reply to first email |
| Meeting Book Rate | 4-8% per 100 | 4-8 meetings from 100 cold emails |
| Bounce Rate | <8% | Use verified email lists (UpLead) |
| Click Rate | 0% | Links DECREASE meetings (goal is calls, not clicks) |

**First-time reality:** Don't expect perfection on first campaign. These benchmarks come after 2-3 test cycles with optimization.

## The Email Writing Process: 5-Step System

### Step 1: Research the Prospect (15-30 minutes)
**Before writing, research:**
- Recent company news (Series funding, new hire, product launch)
- Their public messaging (what they're talking about)
- Social media activity (posts, tone, what resonates)
- Their content (blogs, emails, podcasts they've been on)
- Industry context (current trends in their sector)

**Goal:** Find ONE specific, recent detail to reference in opening line.

### Step 2: Write the Opening Line
See `first-lines` skill for detailed framework. Short version:
- Specific to THEM (not generic)
- Based on research
- Authentic compliment OR relevant observation
- 1 sentence maximum

### Step 3: State Your Case Study (Or Offer)
Choose ONE approach:

**Option A: Case Study Approach**
"Recently we helped [COMPANY] achieve [RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME]"

Requirements for good case study:
- Hyper-specific client (not "dozens of clients")
- Key metric increased (revenue, customers, efficiency, cost savings)
- Generalizable (result possible for similar companies)
- Relevant to target market

**Option B: No-Brainer Offer Approach** (if no case study yet)
Position a small, specific offer with clear ROI:
"Let's do $1,000 for one campaign. If you don't make the money back, I'll refund it."

See `offer-construction.md` for full framework.

### Step 4: Ask a Direct Question
Binary yes/no question:
- Specific ("Can you take on more clients?")
- Small ask ("15 minutes to chat?")
- Easy to answer

### Step 5: Add Professional Signature
Clean signature includes:
- First and last name
- Title
- Company name
- Address
- Phone
- Email
- Website URL

**AVOID:** Images, avatars, multiple links, PS statements, Calendly links.

## Email Structure Template

```
Hi {{first_name}},

[CUSTOM OPENING LINE - specific to them]

[CASE STUDY or OFFER - one sentence proof]

[CTA - yes/no question]

Thanks,
[Your Name]
[Signature]
```

**Length:** 4-6 sentences maximum. Respect their time.

## Sales Asset Strategy: Offer Assets Instead of Meetings

The highest-performing cold emails don't ask for a call — they offer a **sales asset** — something your ICP would find extremely valuable and would use to self-qualify.

### What counts as a sales asset:
- Custom Loom video explaining how you'd help their specific company
- Industry-specific report or benchmark doc
- Free audit of their current setup
- Strategy doc tailored to their business
- Guide solving a problem they'd pay $500+ to fix

**Distinction:** These assets are for outbound prospecting qualification. For assets used in active deal negotiations, see the sales-negotiation-mastery plugin.

### Why this works:
When prospects watch your Loom or read your audit, they self-qualify. If they're interested in the asset, the next step is a natural conversation, not a cold pitch. This creates massive differentiation from typical "let's schedule a call" approach.

### The play:
1. **Offer the asset in your email** — "I recorded a 5-minute Loom explaining how we could [deliver result] for [Company] — want me to send it over?"
2. **Only create it for prospects who say yes** — Don't pre-build assets for everyone. Wait for interest, then deliver personalized value.
3. **The asset does the selling** — They self-qualify through engagement with the asset. Conversion rates spike when they come to you asking for the next step.

### Lead Magnet Variation (for broader outreach):
1. Find 5 problems your prospects would pay $500+ to solve
2. Build lead magnets (on Lovable, Google Docs, Gamma)
3. Gate each behind a landing page
4. Use cold email to drive traffic to the magnet
5. Nurture signups into conversations

## Follow-Up Sequences (The Real Money)

**Key insight:** Most cold email responses come on follow-ups, not initial email.

### Three-Email Sequence (Recommended)

**Follow-up #1 (Day 3)** - Quick Bump
```
Hi {{first_name}},

I'm sure you're busy and wanted to make sure this didn't get buried!
```
- 1-2 sentences
- Acknowledge they're busy
- Non-assumptive

**Follow-up #2 (Day 7)** - Value/Angle Shift
```
Hi {{first_name}},

Just had another win with a similar company. Are you around this week to talk?

[OPTIONAL: Add new angle, new result, or different frame]
```
- 2-3 sentences
- Add new information (don't repeat initial email)
- Can introduce limited-time offer or new angle

**Follow-up #3 (Day 14)** - Breakup Email
```
Hi {{first_name}},

At this point I'll assume improving [OUTCOME] isn't a priority this year.
Feel free to reach out if that changes.

All the best
```
- Professional but warm
- Opens door for future outreach
- Stops here (don't email again)

See `follow-up-sequences.md` for variations and industry-specific versions.

## Campaign Setup Strategy

### Sending Volume (Gradual Ramp)
**CRITICAL:** Too many emails too fast = spam folder.

- **Week 1:** 5-10 emails/day max
- **After Week 1:** Increase by 30-40%
- **Month 1 Target:** 30-40 emails/day max
- **Check:** After month 1, test spam score on mail-tester.com

### Best Times to Send
**By prospect time zone (NOT yours):**
- Early morning: 7-9am (before staff arrives)
- Mid-day: 12-1pm (lunch break)
- Late day: 5-6pm (end of workday)

**Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (NOT Monday morning or Friday)

**For executives:** Early morning or late evening (unprotected times)

### Email Accounts & Warm-up
**New email account requirements:**
1. Use Google Workspace Starter ($6/month) with dedicated domain
2. Complete profile: photo, first name, last name, phone, recovery email
3. Enable IMAP and less-secure app access
4. **Warm-up for 2-3 weeks** before sending campaigns

**Warm-up tools:** WarmupInbox (free or $9/month recommended)
- Simulates organic behavior
- Builds sender reputation with Google
- Prevents new account spam folder
- Don't skip this step

### A/B Testing Framework

For every 100 emails, test ONE variable:

**Test 1: Subject Lines**
- 50 emails with Subject Line A
- 50 emails with Subject Line B
- Track opens
- Winner becomes baseline for next 100

**Test 2: Case Study Angle**
- 50 emails with Angle A (revenue focus)
- 50 emails with Angle B (cost savings focus)
- Track response rate
- Winner gets scaled

**Test 3: Sending Time**
- First 50: Thursday 8:30am
- Second 50: Tuesday 7am
- Track which day/time performs best

See `subject-lines.md` for tested subject line formulas.

## Anti-Patterns & Mistakes to Avoid

**DO NOT:**
- ❌ Send to unverified email lists (use UpLead for verified data)
- ❌ Use generic openings ("I've been following your work")
- ❌ Reference irrelevant case studies (wrong industry/company size)
- ❌ Include multiple links (decreases meetings)
- ❌ Use images in signature (increases spam flags)
- ❌ Follow-up more than 3 times (feels like harassment)
- ❌ Wait more than 3 hours to reply to responses (slow response kills meetings)
- ❌ Email multiple people at same company simultaneously (they'll compare notes)

**DO:**
- ✓ Use verified email lists (UpLead, Hunter with verification)
- ✓ Research each prospect before sending (15-30 minutes per person)
- ✓ Warm up inbox for 2-3 weeks before campaigns
- ✓ Test and iterate (expect <1% success on first send)
- ✓ Respect their time (4-6 sentences, direct CTA)
- ✓ Follow-up consistently but not excessively (Days 3, 7, 14)
- ✓ Reply to responses within 1 hour (if possible)
- ✓ Wait 2 weeks between emails to same company (if targeting multiple people)

## Cold Email vs. Cold Pitching

**Cold Email (Alex Berman Framework):**
- Focus: Direct sales/meetings from email
- Length: 4-6 sentences
- Structure: 3C's (Compliment, Case Study, CTA)
- Goal: Get on a call
- Timeline: 2-week sequence

**Cold Pitch (Bree Weber Framework):**
- Focus: Personalized, problem-focused outreach
- Length: Short to medium
- Structure: Problem → Solution → CTA
- Goal: Start conversation
- Timeline: Extended follow-up with angles
- More relationship-focused than transactional

**Both work.** Alex Berman's 3C's is faster/more direct. Bree Weber's approach builds deeper relationships. Choose based on your business model.

See `offer-construction.md` for Bree Weber's pitch positioning framework.

## Industry-Specific Adjustments

See `campaign-examples.md` for real examples by industry. Quick adjustments:

**SaaS/Software:**
- Focus on expansion/upsell opportunities
- Case study: User growth, feature adoption, NPS
- Best time: Tuesday-Thursday mornings

**Agencies/Services:**
- Focus on new business/revenue
- Case study: Client results (revenue increase, lead generation)
- Warm outreach critical (personal touch matters)

**E-Commerce/Retail:**
- Focus on conversion and AOV
- Case study: Conversion rate increase, revenue per customer
- Subject lines should reference seasonal relevance

**B2B Lead Gen:**
- Focus on pipeline/deal volume
- Case study: Qualified leads generated, deal value
- Follow-ups should include new data/research

## Deliverability Essentials

### DNS/DMARC Configuration
Add DMARC record to domain:
- Type: TXT
- Name: @
- Value: `v=DMARC1; p=none`

Improves deliverability to inboxes (vs. spam folder).

### Email List Verification
- Use UpLead for automated database with verified emails
- Clean data before importing (no special characters, proper capitalization)
- Remove duplicates
- MailShake has built-in verification if needed

### Spam Score Monitoring
After first 100 emails:
- Check spam score at mail-tester.com
- If >5: Slow sending, warm-up longer, or open new account
- If >8: New domain needed (you're in spam folder)

## The Decision Tree: Which Approach?

**Do you have a proven case study or result?**
- YES → Use 3C's framework (Compliment → Case Study → CTA)
- NO → Use no-brainer offer approach OR build case study first (see `offer-construction.md`)

**Are you targeting warm prospects or completely cold?**
- Warm (warm outreach) → Use Bree Weber's pitch structure (more personalization)
- Cold (database) → Use Alex Berman's 3C's (more efficient)

**Do you have time for deep personalization?**
- YES → 15-30 min research per person, higher response rates expected
- NO → Use templates with light personalization, lower response rates expected

## Quick Start: 7-Day Action Plan

**Day 1:** Research and build lead list (100 verified emails with first names/company names)
**Day 2:** Set up email account, complete profile, enable IMAP
**Day 3:** Start warm-up (WarmupInbox)
