# Follow-Up Sequences: Complete Cadences & Timing

Complete reference of follow-up email sequences with timing, templates, and industry variations. Follow-ups often produce 50%+ of final responses—they're not optional.

---

## Core Principle

**Most responses come on follow-ups, not initial email.**

Typical breakdown (by day):
- Day 0 (Initial): 20-30% of all responses
- Day 3-5 (FU #1): 30-40% of all responses
- Day 7-10 (FU #2): 20-30% of all responses
- Day 14+ (FU #3): 10-15% of all responses

**Implication:** Don't judge initial email performance until full sequence completes.

---

## The Standard 3-Email Sequence (Recommended)

**Total timeframe:** 14 days

### Email #1 (Day 0): Initial Pitch
```
Hi {{first_name}},

[CUSTOM OPENING LINE]

[CASE STUDY OR OFFER]

[CALL TO ACTION]

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Expected response rate:** 6-8%

**What to track:** Opens (should be 75-85%), Response rate (should be 8-12%)

---

### Follow-up #1 (Day 3): Quick Bump
```
Hi {{first_name}},

I'm sure you're busy and wanted to make sure this didn't get buried!
```

**Length:** 1 sentence only

**Tone:** Helpful, not desperate

**Expected response rate:** 3-5% of remaining non-responders

**Key insight:** This email is VERY short. It's a reminder, not a new pitch. Don't repeat the offer.

**When to send:** Exactly 3 days after initial email

**Time of day:** Same time as original (e.g., if sent Thursday 8:30am, send this Sunday 8:30am)

---

### Follow-up #2 (Day 7-10): Value Add or New Angle
```
Hi {{first_name}},

Just had another win with a {{industry}} company similar to yours.

[OPTIONAL: Add new angle, new data, or different frame of the original offer]

Still interested?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Length:** 3-5 sentences

**Tone:** Helpful, with new information (not repetitive)

**Expected response rate:** 5-7% of remaining non-responders

**New angle options:**
- Different benefit of same solution
- New case study/result
- Addressing common objection
- Time-sensitive element ("opening up 2 spots")
- Different frame of original problem

**Example variations:**

*Original pitch was revenue-focused:*
```
Hi Sarah,

Following up on my previous email about increasing newsletter revenue.

Actually just realized another angle: Most creators struggle with *consistency*
first (writing regularly), which naturally impacts revenue.

Should we address that first?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

*Original pitch was problem-focused:*
```
Hi Mike,

One quick thought on what I mentioned before about {{company}}'s client acquisition...

Just helped another agency in your space fill 3 new client slots in 30 days
by doing [SPECIFIC TACTIC].

Worth exploring?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

### Follow-up #3 (Day 14): Breakup Email
```
Hi {{first_name}},

At this point I'll assume improving [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] isn't a priority this year.

Feel free to reach out if that changes.

All the best,
[Your Name]
```

**Length:** 2-3 sentences

**Tone:** Professional, warm, respectful of their time

**Expected response rate:** 2-4% of remaining non-responders

**Key insight:** This should be final email. Don't continue after this without significant change in circumstances.

**Soft version (warmer):**
```
{{first_name}},

Final check-in on this—no pressure at all if it's not the right time right now.

If you're ever interested in exploring [SPECIFIC OUTCOME], I'm here.
Feel free to circle back if circumstances change.

All the best,
[Your Name]
```

## Extended Sequence (4-5 Emails, For High-Value Targets)

**Use only for:**
- Dream clients
- High-ticket deals (>$50k)
- Long sales cycles
- Executive targeting

### Timeline: 30 days

**Email #1 (Day 0):** Initial pitch

**Follow-up #1 (Day 3):** Quick bump (1 sentence)

**Follow-up #2 (Day 7):** Value add with new angle

**Follow-up #3 (Day 14):** Soft breakup OR new angle

**Follow-up #4 (Day 30):** Win notification or new hook
```
Hi {{first_name}},

Thought you'd find this interesting: Just helped {{similar_company}}
achieve {{specific_outcome}}.

If you're ever interested in exploring {{specific_outcome}} for {{company}},
I'm here.

[Your Name]
```

**Expected response rate:** 15-25% (much higher due to persistence on high-value targets)

## What NOT to Do in Follow-Ups

**❌ AVOID:**

1. **Repeating the exact same email** - They read it the first time
2. **Adding urgency/pressure** - "This is your last chance!" feels spammy
3. **Being defensive** - "Not sure why you haven't responded..." feels aggressive
4. **Making excuses** - "Sorry to bother you again..." - undercuts your confidence
5. **Following up more than 3x cold** - After 3 times, stop
6. **Changing your ask dramatically** - Stick to original offer
7. **Getting too casual** - Keep professional tone throughout
8. **Ignoring timezone** - Send at their time, not yours
9. **Generic templates** - Personalize each follow-up slightly
10. **Sending same follow-up to multiple people at same company** - They talk

## Follow-Up Timing: Best Practices

### Optimal Day/Time Combinations

**Email #1 (Day 0):**
- Send: Tuesday-Thursday, 7-9am (prospect's timezone)
- Avoid: Monday morning (low decision-making), Friday (weekend coming)

**Follow-up #1 (Day 3):**
- If Day 0 was Tuesday 8am → Send Friday 8am
- If Day 0 was Wednesday 8am → Send Saturday 8am (Sunday is actually good for execs)
- If Day 0 was Thursday 8am → Send Sunday 8am

**Follow-up #2 (Day 7):**
- Different day of week from both previous emails
- Example: Email #1 = Tuesday, FU#1 = Friday, FU#2 = Tuesday = OK (different week)
- Example: Email #1 = Wednesday, FU#1 = Saturday, FU#2 = Wednesday = OK

**Follow-up #3 (Day 14):**
- 7-10 days after FU#2
- Can be any day of week
- Earlier in day better (before inbox gets full)

## Sequence Customization Checklist

Before sending any follow-up:

- [ ] Check they haven't responded to previous email
- [ ] Don't repeat previous email verbatim
- [ ] Add new information/angle (if FU #2)
- [ ] Keep it short (1-5 sentences)
- [ ] Personalize with their name
- [ ] Use their first name (not "Hi" + nothing)
- [ ] Check timing (their timezone, not yours)
- [ ] Proofread for typos
- [ ] Don't add images or links (unless critical)
- [ ] Match tone to relationship (warm vs. professional)

## Response to Follow-Ups: What to Do When They Reply

**Rule: Reply within 1 hour if possible (or next business day max)**

**Their response types:**

### Positive Response ("Yes, let's talk")
```
Your reply:

Hi {{first_name}},

Perfect! Here are some times that work for me this week:
- [TIME 1]
- [TIME 2]
- [TIME 3]

Which works best?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Question/Objection ("How much does this cost?")
```
Your reply:

Great question, {{first_name}}.

[ANSWER BRIEFLY - 2-3 sentences max]

Should we hop on a quick call to discuss details?

[Your Name]
```

### No/Not Interested ("Not a good fit")
```
Your reply:

Totally understand, {{first_name}}.

Is there anyone else on your team who should be aware of this?

All the best,
[Your Name]
```

**Key:** Keep replies short. Goal is to get on call, not to sell via email.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Never skip follow-ups** - They're 50%+ of final responses
2. **3 emails is standard** - Initial + 2 follow-ups = 14-24% total response
3. **Timing matters** - Days 3, 7, 14 are optimal
4. **Add new information** - Don't repeat; add new angle in FU #2
5. **Keep them short** - FU #1 is especially short (1 sentence)
6. **Stop after 3** - Don't become annoying
7. **Track everything** - Document what works for your niche
8. **Warm prospects** - Shorten sequence, higher response rates
9. **Timezone matters** - Send at their time, not yours
10. **Breakup email** - Professional close that opens door for future
