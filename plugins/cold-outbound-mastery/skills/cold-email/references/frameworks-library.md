# Complete Copywriting Frameworks Library

Frameworks beat templates — they teach thinking patterns, not copy-paste shortcuts.

---

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

**Structure:** Identify pain → Amplify consequences → Present solution + soft CTA.

**Best for:** Problem-aware but not solution-aware prospects. The workhorse framework.

**When to use:** Prospects who know they have a problem but haven't found a solution yet.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Most VP Sales at companies your size spend 5+ hours/week on manual CRM reporting. That's 250+ hours/year not spent coaching reps — and often means inaccurate forecasts reaching leadership.

We built a tool that auto-generates CRM reports in real time. Teams like Datadog reduced reporting time by 80%.

Would it make sense to see how?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## BAB — Before, After, Bridge

**Structure:** Current painful situation → Ideal future → Your product as the bridge.

**Best for:** Transformation-driven offers with clear before/after. Emotional decision-makers.

**When to use:** When you can show a dramatic transformation or lifestyle change.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Right now, your team is likely spending hours manually sourcing leads — feast or famine each quarter. Imagine qualified leads arriving daily on autopilot, reps spending 100% of their time selling.

That's what our platform does. Companies like HubSpot saw a 40% pipeline increase within 90 days.

Can I show you how?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## QVC — Question, Value, CTA

**Structure:** Targeted pain question → Brief value → Direct next step.

**Best for:** C-suite prospects who prefer brevity. Qualify interest immediately.

**When to use:** When targeting busy executives with limited time.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Are your SDRs spending more time researching than selling?

We help sales teams automate prospect research so reps focus on conversations. Clients see 3x more meetings per rep per week.

Worth a 10-minute demo?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

**Structure:** Hook/stat → Address specific challenge → Social proof/outcome → Clear CTA.

**Best for:** Data-driven prospects, high-ticket pitches with strong stats.

**When to use:** When you have impressive statistics or benchmarks to lead with.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Companies in pharma lose 30% of leads due to manual outreach. Given {{Company}}'s growth this quarter, pipeline velocity is likely top of mind.

Customers like Pfizer use our platform to automate lead qualification — cutting time-to-contact by 60%.

Worth a 15-minute call?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## PPP — Praise, Picture, Push

**Structure:** Genuine compliment → How things could be better → Gentle push to action.

**Best for:** Senior prospects who respond to relationship-building. Requires genuine trigger.

**When to use:** When you have something authentic to compliment (speech, article, achievement).

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Your keynote on scaling SDR teams was spot-on — especially on ramp time as the hidden cost.

What if you could cut that in half? Our in-inbox coach helps new reps write effective emails from day one with real-time scoring.

Open to a quick chat about how this could support your growth?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## Star-Story-Solution

**Structure:** Introduce character (customer) → Tell challenge narrative → Reveal results.

**Best for:** Strong customer success stories. Humanizes the pitch.

**When to use:** When you have compelling customer narratives with good storytelling.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Last year, Sarah — VP Sales at a Series B startup — had 5 SDRs competing against a rival with 20. Her team was getting crushed on volume.

They adopted our AI prospecting tool and sent hyper-personalized emails at 3x pace without losing quality. Within 90 days, they booked more meetings than their competitor's entire team.

Happy to share how this could work for {{Company}}.

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## SCQ — Situation, Complication, Question

**Structure:** Current reality → Complicating challenge → Question that speaks to need → Optional answer.

**Best for:** Consultative selling. Mirrors how professionals present to leadership.

**When to use:** When you want to engage prospects through conversation-starters rather than pitches.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Your team doubled this year. That usually means onboarding is eating into selling time.

How are you handling ramp for new hires?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## ACCA — Awareness, Comprehension, Conviction, Action

**Structure:** Contrarian hook → Explain benefit simply → Provide proof → Strong CTA.

**Best for:** Analytical buyers who need evidence (engineers, CFOs, ops leaders).

**When to use:** When you have strong data or contrary evidence that challenges assumptions.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Most sales teams measure rep activity. The top 5% measure rep efficiency instead.

When Acme switched, they booked 40% more meetings with fewer emails.

Worth seeing how?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

---

## 3C's — Compliment, Case Study, CTA

**Structure:** Compliment → Case Study → CTA.

**Best for:** Agency/services cold outreach. Case study does the heavy lifting.

**When to use:** When you have strong, recent case studies and need simplicity.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Big fan of [Company]. We just built an app for [Competitor] that does XYZ.

I have a few more ideas. Interested?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Why it works:** Minimal, direct, and lets the case study speak. No need for extensive explanation.

## Mouse Trap — Observation + Binary Question

**Structure:** Observation + Binary value-prop question. 1–2 sentences total.

**Best for:** Maximum brevity. Impulsive reply based on curiosity.

**When to use:** When you've done deep research and have a specific observation.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Looks like you're hiring reps. Would it be helpful to get a more granular look at how they're ramping on email?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Key principle:** The observation creates relevance, the binary question creates easy response.

## Justin Michael Method

**Structure:** Trigger/Pain → Solution hint → Binary CTA. 1–3 sentences, no intro.

**Best for:** High-velocity SDR teams. Mobile-optimized. Deliberately polarizing.

**When to use:** When you want to filter for truly interested prospects (lower response but higher intent).

**Framework:**
- Spend max 1 minute on personalization
- Use industry/persona-level signals
- For top-tier prospects, quote their own words from interviews — they almost always respond

**Example:**
```
Hiring developers but struggling with pipeline?

Quick thought: most agencies you're competing with aren't following up systematically.

Worth a 5-minute call to see the process?
```

## Vanilla Ice Cream

**Structure:** Observation → Problem/Insight → Credibility → Solution → Call-to-Conversation.

**Best for:** Universal "base" framework that works everywhere. Five parts.

**When to use:** When you need a complete, well-rounded approach.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

[OBSERVATION: Specific thing about their business]

Most teams like yours struggle with [PROBLEM] because [INSIGHT].

I've worked with [SIMILAR COMPANIES] on this and found [APPROACH] works best.

[SPECIFIC RESULT]: One client saw [METRIC] improve by [%] in [TIMEFRAME].

Would exploring this make sense?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## PASTOR — Problem, Amplify, Story, Testimony, Offer, Response

**Structure:** Problem → Amplify → Story → Testimony → Offer → Response.

**Best for:** Longer-form or multi-email sequences. Consulting, education, complex B2B services. Each element can be developed across separate touches.

**When to use:** For high-ticket deals, complex solutions, or multi-email campaigns where you can develop each element.

**Example (multi-touch):**

**Email 1 - Problem:**
```
Most [ROLE] struggle with [PROBLEM] because [WHY].
```

**Email 2 - Amplify:**
```
This typically leads to [CONSEQUENCES], which costs companies like yours [METRIC/MONEY] annually.
```

**Email 3 - Story:**
```
Here's how we solved this for [COMPANY]. They had [SIMILAR PROBLEM], so we [APPROACH].
```

**Email 4 - Testimony:**
```
Result: [SPECIFIC OUTCOME]. [CLIENT NAME] said: "[QUOTE]"
```

**Email 5 - Offer:**
```
We can do similar for {{company}}. Here's what it would look like: [SCOPE].
```

**Email 6 - Response:**
```
Interested? Let's schedule a quick call.
```

## Problem-Focused (Bree Weber Style)

**Structure:** Observation of problem → How most solve it wrong → Your approach → Proof → CTA.

**Best for:** Consultative selling, thought-leadership angle.

**When to use:** When you have a unique methodology or unconventional approach.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Here's what I've noticed: [COMMON PROBLEM in their industry]

Most approaches to solving this [COMMON WRONG APPROACH] don't work well.

What I've found works: [YOUR SPECIFIC APPROACH/METHODOLOGY]

[PROOF - client outcome]. We helped [SIMILAR COMPANY] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT].

Should we explore this for {{company}}?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## News Hook Framework

**Structure:** News/milestone → Why it matters → How you help → Soft CTA.

**Best for:** Recently funded companies, new hires, product launches, market expansions.

**When to use:** When you have specific, recent news about the prospect.

**Example:**
```
Hi [Name],

Saw your Series B announcement — congratulations on the funding!

Companies at your stage usually face [SPECIFIC CHALLENGE] as they scale. The ones winning are [YOUR APPROACH].

I just helped [SIMILAR COMPANY] through this with [SPECIFIC TACTIC].

Worth a conversation?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

## Quick Framework Selection Guide

| Your Situation | Best Framework | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Prospect knows problem, needs solution | PAS | Shows solution to known pain |
| Want to show transformation | BAB | Creates emotional motivation |
| Busy executive, limited time | QVC | Fast, direct, clear |
| Have strong statistics | AIDA | Data-driven argument |
| High-value relationship play | PPP | Builds genuine connection |
| Strong customer story | Star-Story-Solution | Humanizes pitch |
| Consultative approach | SCQ | Conversation-starter |
| Analytical buyer, needs proof | ACCA | Evidence-based |
| Services/agency, strong case study | 3C's | Simplicity + social proof |
| Deep research, single insight | Mouse Trap | Brevity + curiosity |
| High velocity, SDR teams | Justin Michael | Quick, polarizing |
| Complete rounded approach | Vanilla Ice Cream | Universal fit |
| Long cycle, multi-touch | PASTOR | Developed over emails |
| Unique methodology | Bree Weber Problem-Focused | Consultative authority |
| Recent company milestone/news | News Hook | Timely, relevant |

## Framework Combination Strategy

**Advanced move:** Layer frameworks together for maximum impact.

**Example:**
- Start with **News Hook** in initial email (grabs attention with relevance)
- Follow-up with **PAS** (show the problem their situation created)
- Third touch with **Star-Story-Solution** (prove it with narrative)
- Final touch with **PASTOR** framework across multiple messages (develop case)

**Key principle:** Different frameworks appeal to different decision-making styles. Test which one your audience prefers, then layer in others for follow-ups.

---

## How to Apply Any Framework

1. **Identify your hook** — What's your opening (news, observation, question)?
2. **Choose framework** — Which thinking pattern matches your situation?
3. **Fill in the blanks** — Adapt the structure to your offer
4. **Keep it short** — Most frameworks work best at 4-6 sentences
5. **Test and iterate** — A/B test framework against framework in same audience

**Golden rule:** The framework is scaffolding. Your research and personalization are what make it sing. A generic PAS email performs worse than a highly researched Mouse Trap email every time.
