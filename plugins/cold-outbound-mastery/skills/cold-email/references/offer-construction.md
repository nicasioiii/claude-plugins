# Offer Construction: Frameworks & Value Positioning

Complete reference for building irresistible offers and positioning them for cold email. Strong offer construction is 50% of cold email success—the rest is execution.

---

## Three Components of a Winning Offer

**Alex Berman Framework: All three must exist**

1. **Case Studies** - Who you've worked with and results achieved
2. **Offer** - What you're selling to target audience
3. **Target Audience** - People who want to buy your offer

**Success requirement:** All three must be optimized. Missing one = failure.

**Example failure scenarios:**
- ❌ Great case studies, bad offer, wrong audience = no sales
- ❌ Great offer, no case studies, right audience = low trust
- ❌ All three, but offer too vague = low response rates

---

## The Irresistible Offer Formula (Bree Weber)

Every offer must answer these 5 questions:

### 1. What's the transformation?
**Format:** "Help [SPECIFIC PERSON] [DESIRED OUTCOME] through [YOUR METHOD]"

**Example:**
- ✓ "Help coaches increase enrollments 30% through conversion email sequences"
- ❌ "Copywriting services"

**Key:** Specific outcome, specific person, specific method.

---

### 2. What's the proof?
**Format:** "Recently helped [CLIENT TYPE] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT]"

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Recently helped a coach in the business niche increase enrollments by 40% in their next launch"
- ✓ "Just worked with an e-commerce store that increased AOV by 35%"
- ❌ "Have many happy clients"

**Key:** Specific client type, specific metric, specific percentage.

---

### 3. What's the scope? (What exactly do they get?)
**Format:** Clear list of deliverables

**Examples:**
- ✓ "I'll write 3 email sequences for your launch (subject lines, copy, follow-ups)"
- ✓ "We'll audit your sales page, identify 5 conversion bottlenecks, and recommend specific fixes"
- ❌ "Marketing services"

**Key:** Specific number of deliverables, specific format, specific result.

---

### 4. What's the result timeline?
**Format:** "Results in [SPECIFIC TIMEFRAME]"

**Examples:**
- ✓ "See results in the first 30 days"
- ✓ "Implement in 2 weeks, results within 60 days"
- ✓ "3-week project, 90-day ROI"
- ❌ "Results vary"

**Key:** Be specific with both project timeline and result timeline.

---

### 5. What's the call to action?
**Format:** Single, specific next step

**Examples:**
- ✓ "Let's schedule a 20-minute call to see if this is a fit"
- ✓ "Interested? Reply and we'll find a time"
- ✓ "Want to explore this? Calendar link: [URL]"
- ❌ "Let me know if you're interested"

**Key:** Single, specific, easy to say yes to.

## Cold Email Specific Offer Construction

### The Three Offer Types for Cold Email

#### TYPE 1: The Proven Case Study Offer (Highest Response Rate)
**Use when:** You have 2+ case studies with specific metrics

**Formula:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

[CUSTOM OPENING]

Recently we helped [CLIENT TYPE] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME].

[CALL TO ACTION - usually just asking to talk]

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Example:**
```
Hi Sarah,

Impressed by how quickly you've grown Run the World.

Recently we helped an events platform implement community features that
increased session time by 30% and repeat user rate by 45% in 60 days.

Should we explore what this could do for your platform?

Thanks,
Alex
```

**Performance:**
- Open rate: 75-85%
- Response rate: 12-18%
- Meeting book rate: 50-60% of responses

**Key success factor:** Case study must be relevant (similar company, similar problem, similar scale).

---

#### TYPE 2: The No-Brainer Offer (When No Case Study Yet)
**Use when:** You have testimonials/results but not formal case studies

**Formula:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

[CUSTOM OPENING]

I specialize in [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] for [SPECIFIC PEOPLE].

Let's do [SMALL SPECIFIC OFFER]. If you don't [SPECIFIC SUCCESS METRIC],
I'll refund it. If we do, you hire me [ONGOING/PROJECT].

Interested?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Example:**
```
Hi Mike,

Saw you're actively hiring engineers—smart move.

I specialize in recruiting top engineers from FAANG companies.

Let's do $1,000 for finding and vetting 3 perfect-fit candidates.
If you don't move forward with at least one, I refund it.
If you do, we discuss ongoing work.

Interested?

Thanks,
Nathan
```

**Performance:**
- Open rate: 70-80%
- Response rate: 10-15%
- Meeting book rate: 40-50% of responses

**Key success factor:** The "money-back guarantee" reduces friction. Removes objection of risk.

---

#### TYPE 3: The Problem-Focused Pitch (Bree Weber Style)
**Use when:** Target audience has specific pain point you solve

**Formula:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

[CUSTOM OPENING - specific to their business/problem]

Here's what I've noticed: [COMMON PROBLEM THEY FACE]

Most approaches to solving this [COMMON WRONG APPROACH] don't work well.

What I've found works: [YOUR SPECIFIC APPROACH/METHODOLOGY]

[PROOF - result or client outcome]

Should we explore this for {{company}}?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Example:**
```
Hi Sarah,

Following your journey growing Everyday Copy—impressive product-market fit.

Here's what I've noticed about education platforms: Most struggle with
user retention after purchase. They nail the sales side but miss the
delivery/community side.

What I've found works: Building a community experience alongside the course.

I helped another education platform like yours add weekly Q&As, group projects,
and peer reviews. Increased completion rates from 30% to 67%, plus 40% more
testimonials and referrals from engaged students.

Should we explore what this could do for Everyday Copy?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Performance:**
- Open rate: 78-85%
- Response rate: 15-22%
- Meeting book rate: 60-70% of responses

**Key success factor:** Solves a real problem they're facing. Feels consultative, not salesy.

## Building Strong Case Studies

### What Makes a Case Study "Cold Email Ready"?

#### 1. Hyper-Specific Client
**WRONG:** "I've worked with dozens of e-commerce companies"
**RIGHT:** "I specialize in increasing AOV for subscription box companies"

The more specific your niche, the more relevant to prospects in that niche.

#### 2. Specific Metric That Matters
**WRONG:** "We increased their Instagram followers"
**RIGHT:** "We increased their customer acquisition cost from $15 to $8 per customer"

Metrics that matter:
- Revenue/sales growth
- Cost reduction
- Customer acquisition
- Conversion rate increase
- Repeat purchase rate
- Customer lifetime value
- Efficiency gains
- Market share growth

#### 3. Specific Timeframe
**WRONG:** "We've achieved results with several clients"
**RIGHT:** "Recently helped [CLIENT] achieve [RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME]"

Timeframe options:
- "in 30 days"
- "in 90 days"
- "in 6 months"
- "in their first quarter"
- "by end of year"

#### 4. Generalizable to Your Target
**Question:** Could this result happen for your other prospects?

**WRONG case study for lead gen agencies:**
- "Helped a digital agency book 5 consulting clients"
- (Too specific, not generalizable—most agencies aren't ready for 5 new clients)

**RIGHT case study for lead gen agencies:**
- "Helped a web development firm fill 2 new project slots at $50k each"
- (Generalizable—most dev shops can handle 2 projects)

#### 5. Recent
**Formula:** "Recently" or "Just" + specific timeframe

**WRONG:** "Worked with a client 2 years ago..."
**RIGHT:** "Recently helped [CLIENT] in [MONTH/SEASON]"

"Recently" = past 6-12 months (current year ideally)

---

### Translating Service Results into Case Studies

**If you offer:** Generic services
**Translation:** Specific problem → specific outcome

| Your Service | Case Study Frame |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Copywriting | "I increased email open rates 35% for [COACH TYPE]" |
| Web design | "Redesigned [COMPANY TYPE]'s site, increased conversions 40%" |
| Social media | "Built audience from 10K to 100K in 90 days for [CREATOR TYPE]" |
| SEO | "Ranked [KEYWORD] #1 in 6 months for [INDUSTRY]" |
| Ads management | "Reduced CAC from $25 to $8 for [E-COMMERCE TYPE]" |
| Recruiting | "Filled 15 engineering positions in 6 months at [COMPANY TYPE]" |
| Sales training | "Increased close rate from 20% to 45% for [SALES TEAM TYPE]" |
| Consulting | "Restructured operations, saved [COMPANY TYPE] $200K/year" |

---

### If You Don't Have Case Studies Yet

**Option 1: Build one (fastest path)**
- Pick one client willing to give a testimonial
- Document specific metric before/after
- Ask permission to use in cold emails
- "Recently I worked with [COMPANY] and achieved..."

**Option 2: Use the No-Brainer Offer instead**
- Skip the case study
- Position a specific, limited offer
- Include money-back guarantee
- "If you don't see [RESULT], I'll refund it"

**Option 3: Use a framework/methodology**
- Skip specific client names
- Talk about framework/process
- "Companies using [MY PROCESS] typically see [RESULT]"
- "My approach to [SERVICE] typically increases [METRIC] by X%"

## Offer Positioning: How You Talk About What You Do

### The Positioning Formula

**Format:** "I help [SPECIFIC PERSON] achieve [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] through [YOUR METHOD]"

### Examples (Service to Specific Offer)

**Service:** "Copywriting"
**Positioned offer:** "I help SaaS companies increase email conversion rates 25%+ through clarity-focused copy that addresses objections"

**Service:** "Web design"
**Positioned offer:** "I help e-commerce stores reduce cart abandonment and increase AOV through data-driven UX optimization"

**Service:** "Lead generation"
**Positioned offer:** "I help staffing agencies fill senior engineering roles 60% faster through targeted LinkedIn outreach campaigns"

**Service:** "SEO"
**Positioned offer:** "I help local service companies (plumbers, electricians) get 20+ qualified leads monthly through location-based SEO"

**Service:** "Recruiting"
**Positioned offer:** "I help Y Combinator companies fill engineering positions from FAANG talent pools in under 30 days"

## Price Positioning for Cold Email

### Pricing Approach #1: No-Brainer Offer (Project-Based)

**Formula:** Small specific offer with low entry price ($500-$5k)

**Example:**
- "Let's do $1,500 for a 3-email launch sequence. If it doesn't generate ROI, refund."
- "Let's audit your sales page for $1,000. If we don't find 5+ high-impact changes, refund."
- "Let's find 3 vetted candidates for $1,000. If you don't move forward with one, refund."

**Advantage:** Low barrier to entry removes objection
**Disadvantage:** You must deliver fast and prove value quickly

### Pricing Approach #2: Discovery Call (No Price Mentioned)

**Formula:** Get on call first, price later

**Example:**
- "Can we grab 15 minutes to discuss if there's a fit?"
- "Interested in exploring this? Let's set up a quick call."
- "Should we hop on a conversation about what's possible?"

**Advantage:** More flexibility, can customize to their budget
**Disadvantage:** Lower conversion (more objections on call)

### Pricing Approach #3: Retainer (Ongoing)

**Formula:** Specific monthly commitment

**Example:**
- "I work on a $3,000/month retainer model. Interested?"
- "Most clients commit to 3-month engagements at $5,000/month."
- "Can we add you as a $2,000/month project for 90 days?"

**Advantage:** Predictable revenue, higher lifetime value
**Disadvantage:** Bigger ask, lower initial yes rate

### Pricing Approach #4: Hybrid (Project + Retainer)

**Formula:** Small project to prove value, then retainer

**Example:**
- "Let's start with a $2k audit project. If the results are solid, we move to a $3k/month retainer."
- "$1,500 for the first email sequence. If we hit our targets, $2,500/month ongoing."

**Advantage:** Best of both worlds—low barrier + proven value
**Disadvantage:** Most complex, requires strong follow-through

## Offer Construction by Industry

### For SaaS / Product Companies

**Type:** Usually no-brainer offer or discovery call (they need to understand product fit first)

**Formula:**
"I help [SAAS TYPE] increase [SPECIFIC METRIC] through [YOUR METHOD].

Recently worked with [SIMILAR SAAS] and achieved [SPECIFIC RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME].

Worth a 15-minute conversation to explore if there's a fit?"

**Specific metrics SaaS cares about:**
- User growth
- Activation rate
- Retention rate
- Expansion revenue (upsells)
- Customer lifetime value
- Churn reduction
- Feature adoption

---

### For Coaching / Personal Brands

**Type:** Case study offer or discovery call (they want to see social proof)

**Formula:**
"I help [COACH TYPE] [SPECIFIC OUTCOME] through [YOUR METHOD].

Just helped another [COACH TYPE] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT]."

**Specific outcomes coaching audiences care about:**
- Enrollment increase
- Average ticket price increase
- Client acquisition cost reduction
- Launch revenue
- Email list growth + engagement
- Content distribution/syndication

---

### For E-Commerce / Retail

**Type:** No-brainer offer (they want to see ROI fast)

**Formula:**
"Let's increase your [METRIC] by X%.

If you don't see results in 30 days, I'll refund it."

**Specific metrics e-commerce cares about:**
- Conversion rate
- Average order value (AOV)
- Cart abandonment rate
- Repeat purchase rate
- Customer lifetime value
- Site speed (affects conversion)

---

### For Lead Gen / B2B Services

**Type:** Case study offer (they want proven results with similar companies)

**Formula:**
"Recently brought on [BIG CLIENT] for [SIMILAR COMPANY] and closed $[AMOUNT] in [TIMEFRAME].

Can you take on more clients?"

**Specific outcomes lead gen cares about:**
- New clients/opportunities
- Deal value
- Sales qualified leads (SQLs)
- Cost per qualified lead
- Time to close
- Sales pipeline growth

## Offer Homework: Build Yours Now

### Step 1: Define Your Service
What do you actually do? (Write in 1-2 sentences)

Example: "I write sales pages and email sequences for SaaS companies"

---

### Step 2: Who Do You Help?
Be hyper-specific.

Example: "B2B SaaS companies with $1M-$10M revenue looking to increase signups"

---

### Step 3: What's the Outcome?
What transformation do you create? (Metric + timeframe)

Example: "Increase free trial signups 25-40% in the first 90 days"

---

### Step 4: Build Your Case Study
Do you have proof? (If yes, write it below)

Example: "Recently helped [COMPANY], a project management SaaS, increase signups from 50/month to 85/month through optimized sales page copy"

---

### Step 5: Write Your Positioned Offer
Combine steps 1-4 into your cold email frame

Example:
"I help B2B SaaS companies with $1M-$10M revenue increase free trial signups 25-40% through optimized sales page copy and email sequences.

Recently helped [COMPANY] move from 50 to 85 signups monthly in 90 days.

Can we do similar for {{company}}?"

## Common Offer Construction Mistakes

**❌ MISTAKE #1: Too vague**
- WRONG: "I help businesses grow"
- RIGHT: "I help agencies fill project slots faster through LinkedIn prospecting"

**❌ MISTAKE #2: No proof**
- WRONG: "I can help you increase revenue"
- RIGHT: "Recently helped a similar company increase revenue 30% in 6 months"

**❌ MISTAKE #3: Undefined scope**
- WRONG: "Interested in working together?"
- RIGHT: "Let's do a 30-day project at $2,000. If you don't see [RESULT], refund."

**❌ MISTAKE #4: Wrong target audience**
- WRONG: Selling social media management to B2B companies (they don't care)
- RIGHT: Selling social media management to personal brands/coaches (high value)

**❌ MISTAKE #5: Overcomplicating the CTA**
- WRONG: "Schedule a 90-minute strategy session and bring your team"
- RIGHT: "15 minutes to chat?"

**❌ MISTAKE #6: No differentiation**
- WRONG: "I'm a copywriter" (hundreds of thousands of copywriters)
- RIGHT: "I help coaches increase email-to-sale conversions 30%" (much more specific)

**❌ MISTAKE #7: Generic case study**
- WRONG: "Worked with a client and increased conversions"
- RIGHT: "Worked with an e-commerce store selling pet products, increased AOV from $35 to $50 (+43%) in 60 days"

## Offer Testing Framework

### Test #1: Case Study Angle (Weeks 1-4)

Send to first 100 prospects with different case study angles:
- 50 with case study emphasizing speed (result in X days)
- 50 with case study emphasizing outcome (X% increase)

Track response rates. Winner becomes new baseline.

### Test #2: Scope/Price (Weeks 5-8)

Test two different project sizes:
- 50 offering $1k project
- 50 offering $3k project

Track response and actual close rates. May find cheaper option gets more yeses but lower perceived value.

### Test #3: Positioning (Weeks 9-12)

Test two different ways of describing what you do:
- 50 with "I help [PERSON] [OUTCOME]" format
- 50 with "I specialize in [SPECIFIC SERVICE]" format

Track response rates. Usually the [PERSON + OUTCOME] format wins.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Strong offer = 50% of cold email success** (rest is execution)
2. **All three must exist:** Case studies, Offer, Target Audience
3. **Be hyper-specific:** Not "consulting" but "increase signups 25% for SaaS"
4. **No case study yet?** Use no-brainer offer with money-back guarantee
5. **Case study must be relevant:** Similar company, similar problem, similar size
6. **Price matters less than perceived value:** $1k with proof > $5k without proof
7. **Test continuously:** A/B test offers, positioning, case studies
8. **Document everything:** Track what works in your niche
9. **Positioning is key:** How you talk about your offer matters as much as the offer itself
10. **Start simple:** Master one offer type before adding complexity

## Offer Construction Checklist

Before sending any cold email, verify your offer answers:

- [ ] **PERSON:** Who exactly do you help? (Hyper-specific)
- [ ] **PROBLEM:** What problem do you solve for them?
- [ ] **OUTCOME:** What specific result do you create? (With metric + timeframe)
- [ ] **PROOF:** Do you have a recent case study or testimonial? (If no, use no-brainer approach)
- [ ] **SCOPE:** What exactly do they get? (Specific deliverables)
- [ ] **PRICE:** What's the entry point? (Project cost or monthly retainer)
- [ ] **CTA:** What's the single next step? (Easy yes/no question)
- [ ] **DIFFERENTIATION:** Why you over competitors? (Positioning/specialization)

If any of these are missing or vague, go back and strengthen before sending.
