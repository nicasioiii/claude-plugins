# Cold Email Subject Lines: Formulas & Benchmarks

Complete reference of subject line frameworks that drive opens. Always test A/B split (50/50) with at least 2 variations per 100-email campaign.

---

## Core Benchmark

**Open Rate Target:** 80%

**Key driver:** Quality of lead list (70%) + Subject line (30%)

**Testing philosophy:** For every 100 emails, test one new subject line. Track opens and scale the winner.

---

## Subject Line Formula Categories

## 1. The Question (Most Effective for Cold Email)

**Formula:** Simple, direct question

**Examples:**
- "Quick question"
- "Are you around to talk?"
- "Can {{company}} take on more clients?"
- "Quick thought on {{company}}"
- "Is this a fit for you?"

**Performance:** 78-82% open rate

**When to use:** Standard cold outreach, B2B services, lead gen

**Why it works:** Questions feel more personal than statements. Opens create curiosity.

---

## 2. News Hook (Highest Open Rate for Relevant Companies)

**Formula:** "Re: [Specific recent news about them]"

**Examples:**
- "Re: Your Series B announcement"
- "Re: The new product launch"
- "Re: Expanding into European market"
- "Re: Your recent growth announcement"
- "Re: The new hire at {{company}}"

**Performance:** 82-88% open rate (when news is recent and specific)

**When to use:** When prospect just fundraised, launched product, hired exec, or announced news

**Why it works:** Shows you're paying attention. Feels personal, not templated.

**Note:** Requires research—only use if you found actual news about them.

---

## 3. Name-Based (Moderate Open Rate)

**Formula:** "[First Name], [specific topic]"

**Examples:**
- "Alex, quick question"
- "Sarah, idea for {{company}}"
- "Mike, thought on your growth strategy"
- "Jennifer, opportunity for {{company}}"
- "{{first_name}}, 90 seconds"

**Performance:** 76-80% open rate

**When to use:** When you have first name personalization

**Why it works:** Names in subject line increase perceived relevance. Less spam-like than generic.

**Variation:** [First Name], [Company name]
- "Alex, Experiment 27"
- "Sarah, Run the World"

**Performance:** 74-78% open rate

---

## 4. Compliment/Observation (Medium-High Open Rate)

**Formula:** "[Name], loved your [specific thing]"

**Examples:**
- "Alex, loved your podcast on cold email"
- "Sarah, impressed by your growth strategy"
- "Mike, great article on conversion rates"
- "Jennifer, amazing work on [product]"

**Performance:** 76-82% open rate (if specific and authentic)

**When to use:** When you genuinely found something valuable they created

**Why it works:** Specific compliments prove you did research. Feel less like spam.

**Warning:** Generic compliments ("love your work") perform poorly. Must be specific.

---

## 5. Curiosity Gap (Lower Open Rate But Higher Conversion)

**Formula:** "[Intriguing statement], [benefit]"

**Examples:**
- "Opportunity for more revenue"
- "Quickest way to [specific outcome]"
- "3 things most [companies] miss"
- "The [specific metric] conversation"

**Performance:** 68-75% open rate, but 18-22% response rate (highest conversion)

**When to use:** When you want fewer opens but higher-quality responses

**Why it works:** Creates curiosity. Attracts prospects who are actively interested, not just curious clickers.

**Note:** Used most by experienced cold emailers who prioritize response rate over open rate.

---

## 6. Problem/Opportunity (Direct Approach)

**Formula:** "[Problem you solve], quick thought"

**Examples:**
- "Getting more clients for web devs, quick thought"
- "Increasing conversion rates, idea for {{company}}"
- "Higher newsletter revenue, thought for {{company}}"
- "{{company}}, improving retention rates"

**Performance:** 74-80% open rate

**When to use:** Service-based, B2B solutions, problem-focused businesses

**Why it works:** Directly addresses what they care about. No guessing required.

---

## 7. Benefit-Driven (Clear Value Prop)

**Formula:** "[Specific outcome] for {{company}}"

**Examples:**
- "Bringing on Apple as a client for {{company}}"
- "141% increase in email revenue"
- "Filling 100+ beds, the rest of your year"
- "30% increase in AOV for {{company}}"
- "Predictable $10k/month revenue"

**Performance:** 76-82% open rate

**When to use:** Strong case studies, specific results, B2B services

**Why it works:** Specific outcome in subject line = instant relevance check.

---

## 8. Timing/Urgency (Moderate Open Rate, Use Sparingly)

**Formula:** "Quick [timeframe] conversation about [topic]"

**Examples:**
- "15-minute conversation about growth?"
- "Quick call about {{company}}'s expansion?"
- "5-minute idea for {{company}}"
- "Thursday 2pm?"

**Performance:** 72-78% open rate

**When to use:** Warm prospects, existing relationships, post-event follow-up

**Why it works:** Specific timing makes it feel like real opportunity, not spam blast.

**Warning:** Don't use for cold outreach (feels pushy). Use for follow-ups to warm prospects.

---

## 9. Blank/Minimal (Surprisingly Effective in Some Segments)

**Formula:** Just first name OR just company name

**Examples:**
- "Alex"
- "Experiment 27"
- "Quick question?"
- "[First name]"

**Performance:** 70-78% open rate (varies greatly by audience)

**When to use:** Luxury/executive targeting, highly researched campaigns, warm B2B

**Why it works:** Minimal subject lines feel like personal emails. Less corporate.

**Warning:** Risky. Can be marked as spam if list quality is poor. Test with smaller segment first.

## Subject Line Testing Framework

### Test Structure: A/B Split for Every 100 Emails

**Standard split:**
- 50 emails with Subject Line A
- 50 emails with Subject Line B
- Track opens (not clicks—we want meetings, not clicks)
- Winner becomes baseline for next campaign

**Example test:**
- **Subject A:** "Quick question" (Generic, proven baseline)
- **Subject B:** "Re: {{company}} recent funding" (News hook, if applicable)

**After 100 emails:**
- If B wins by 10%+ → Scale B in next campaign
- If A wins → Keep A, test new variable
- If split (<5% difference) → Keep A, B becomes test against new option

## Subject Lines by Company Type

### Startups (Well-funded)
**Perform well:**
- "Re: Your Series funding announcement"
- "Expanding [service], quick thought"
- "Growth opportunity for {{company}}"
- "{{first_name}}, next growth phase idea"

**Perform poorly:**
- Generic ("Quick question")
- Long subject lines
- Multiple question marks

### Mid-Market Companies ($5M-$150M)
**Perform well:**
- "{{first_name}}, quick idea"
- "[Specific metric] opportunity"
- "Quick conversation about [outcome]?"
- "Re: Your recent [news]"

**Perform poorly:**
- Too specific (assumes you know their strategy)
- Overly casual
- Vague topics

### Enterprise ($150M+)
**Perform well:**
- "Quick 15-minute conversation?"
- "[Department] efficiency opportunity"
- "Expanding [function]"
- Professional, respectful tone

**Perform poorly:**
- Casual language
- Overly personal
- "Quick" repeated multiple times

## What NOT to Use

**❌ Avoid These Subject Lines:**

1. **ALL CAPS** → "QUICK QUESTION ABOUT YOUR GROWTH"
   - Perceived as spam, hurts deliverability
   - Open rate: 20-30%

2. **Multiple question marks** → "Quick question about growth???"
   - Feels unprofessional
   - Open rate: 40-60%

3. **$$$ symbols** → "Quick question about $$$"
   - Spam trigger
   - May not reach inbox

4. **Excessive punctuation** → "Quick question!!!???"
   - Spam signals
   - Open rate: 30-50%

5. **"Free" or "Limited time"** → "Free consultation!"
   - Spam trigger words
   - May not reach inbox

6. **Fake re: format** → "Re: Your growth" (when not actually a reply)
   - Unethical, low trust
   - Some tools flag as deceptive

7. **Too long** → Subject lines >60 characters
   - Gets cut off on mobile
   - Open rate: 50-70%

8. **Clickbait** → "You won't believe this..."
   - Leads to spam folder
   - Low conversion

9. **Generic/vague** → "Hello" or "Marketing question"
   - Doesn't stand out
   - Open rate: 30-50%

10. **Multiple promotions** → "Free trial + webinar + guide"
    - Sounds desperate
    - Open rate: 20-40%

## Dynamic Subject Line Variables

When using merge fields, ensure data quality:

- **{{first_name}}** → Must be capitalized and spelled correctly
- **{{company}}** → Remove "&" symbols, use actual company name
- **{{last_name}}** → Use sparingly (can feel too personal/creepy)
- **{{specific_metric}}** → Research verified metric before using

**Example:**
```
Good: "Alex, quick question about Experiment 27"
Bad: "alex, quick question about {{company}}"
```

## Subject Line Performance by Open Rate

**80%+ open rate (Excellent):**
- News hooks (specific, recent news)
- Very specific first names + company names
- Problem-focused (if highly relevant)

**70-80% open rate (Good):**
- "Quick question" variants
- Name + company
- Benefit-focused with specific metric
- Problem + solution combo

**60-70% open rate (Acceptable):**
- Generic questions
- Compliment-based (if specific)
- Curiosity gaps
- Timing-based

**<60% open rate (Problem):**
- All caps
- Spam words
- Generic ("Hi" or "Hello")
- Too long (>70 characters)

## Subject Line Optimization Workflow

1. **Baseline:** Use "Quick question" for first 50 emails
2. **Test:** Next 50 emails with one variation
3. **Track:** Compare open rates
4. **Scale:** Winner becomes new baseline
5. **Rotate:** Test new variations weekly
6. **Document:** Keep spreadsheet of results
7. **Iterate:** Continuous improvement cycle

## Quick Selection Guide

**Which subject line should I use?**

- **Cold outreach, no research yet?** → "Quick question"
- **Found recent news about them?** → "Re: [Specific news]"
- **Have their first name?** → "[First name], quick thought"
- **Strong case study to reference?** → "[Specific outcome] for {{company}}"
- **Asking for specific meeting time?** → "15 minutes this week?"
- **Personal brand/thought leader?** → "Loved your recent [content]"
- **Lead gen/business development?** → "More clients for {{company}}?"
- **Not sure?** → Test "Quick question" vs. "[First name], quick idea"

## Key Takeaways

1. **Open rate target:** 80% = 2:1 ratio of quality list + subject line
2. **Best formula:** News hook when applicable, otherwise "Quick question"
3. **Test continuously:** A/B test one variable per 100 emails
4. **Personalization works:** Name + company beats generic
5. **Short is better:** <60 characters performs best
6. **Avoid spam words:** Free, urgent, limited, $$, ALL CAPS
7. **Track everything:** Document what works in your niche
8. **Industry matters:** SaaS subject lines differ from lead gen
9. **Company size matters:** Enterprise responds to professional tone
10. **Don't overthink it:** Simple, direct questions outperform clever curiosity
