# Cold Email Templates Library

Complete collection of email templates organized by type and industry. Use these as starting frameworks—always personalize with research-based opening lines and specific case studies.

---

## Template 1: Basic 3C's (Universal)

**Subject:** Quick question

**Email:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

[CUSTOM OPENING LINE - something specific about them]

Recently we helped [COMPANY] achieve [SPECIFIC RESULT] in [TIMEFRAME].

Can we do similar for {{company}}?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Use when:** New to cold email, need simplest framework, targeting any industry.

**Response rate:** 10-15% with good research.

---

## Template 2: No-Brainer Offer (When No Case Study)

**Subject:** [Company], quick idea

**Email:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

{{first_line}}

I specialize in [YOUR SPECIFIC OFFER].

Let's do [SMALL SPECIFIC OFFER]. If you don't [SPECIFIC SUCCESS METRIC], I'll refund it.
If we do, you hire me [ONGOING/PROJECT TERMS].

Interested?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Example:**
```
Hi Sarah,

Saw you just launched the new product—nice work on the positioning.

I specialize in writing emails for SaaS companies looking to increase activation rates.

Let's do $500 for a 5-email onboarding sequence. If your activation rate doesn't increase by at least 10%, I'll refund it. If we hit the goal, we discuss ongoing work.

Interested?

Thanks,
Nathan
```

**Use when:** No proven case study yet, want to build social proof.

**Response rate:** 12-18% (lower barrier to say yes).

**Note:** Set clear success metrics upfront to avoid disputes.

## Template 5: Recruiting/Staffing

**Subject:** Candidate for your {{job_title}} role

**Email:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

{{first_line}}

I have a candidate that would be a great fit for your {{job_title}} role.

CANDIDATE DETAILS:
- [KEY QUALIFICATION 1]
- [KEY QUALIFICATION 2]
- [RELEVANT EXPERIENCE]
- Skills: [LIST]
- [UNIQUE CREDENTIAL - Stanford, Google, etc.]

This candidate is actively seeking, and I thought they'd be perfect for you.

How would you feel about discussing this further?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

**Follow-up #1 (3 days):**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

Circling back on the [ROLE] candidate I mentioned.

I've successfully placed [X] engineers this year alone and this candidate is top tier.

Thoughts?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

**Follow-up #2 (5 days):**
```
Subject: {{company}} candidates

Hi {{first_name}},

I have profiles of additional candidates perfect for {{job_title}} roles.

Each worked at [TOP COMPANIES] and has [RELEVANT EXPERIENCE].

Their profiles: [LINK]

Which candidates pique your interest?

Best
```

**Use when:** Recruiting/staffing, have candidate pipeline.

**Response rate:** 15-20% (hiring is always top priority).

## Follow-Up Email Templates

```

```

**Variation for newer accounts:**
```
Hey {{first_name}},

Just circling back on my previous email. Did it get lost in the noise?
```

```
Hi {{first_name}},

Quick update: Just had another win with a similar company. Still interested in talking?
```

**Variation with new angle:**
```
Hi {{first_name}},

Been thinking more about {{company}} and realized [NEW INSIGHT/ANGLE].

Thanks,
[YOUR NAME]
```

```
Hi {{first_name}},

All the best,
[YOUR NAME]

**Variation (softer):**

```

```
Hi {{first_name}},

If you're ever ready to explore this, happy to chat.

[YOUR NAME]
```

**Use when:** Dream client, long sales cycle, want to re-engage.

## Quick Customization Checklist

Before sending ANY template:

- [ ] Research prospect (15-30 min)
- [ ] Customize opening line (specific to THEM)
- [ ] Verify case study is relevant (similar company/situation)
- [ ] Update company/person names ({{}} fields)
- [ ] Test email to self first
- [ ] Check for typos and formatting
- [ ] Verify email address is correct
- [ ] Note the date/time sending
- [ ] Schedule follow-ups in advance

## Template Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Template | Reason |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| No case study yet | Template 2 (No-Brainer Offer) | Lower barrier to entry |
| Have proven case study | Template 1 (Basic 3C's) | Most direct, highest ROI |
| Content/writing results | Template 3 (Newsletter Focus) | Specific to content creators |
| Lead generation service | Template 4 (Lead Gen) | Proven pain point |
| Building authority | Template 9 (Podcast) | Long-term brand play |
| Warm referral | Template 12 (Referral) | Trust already exists |
| Existing customer | Template 14 (Expansion) | Easiest sells |

## Industry-Specific Template Variations

### For Coaches/Personal Brands
Use Template 3 (Newsletter Focus) or custom framework:
- Lead: Specific content reference
- Proof: Student results or client transformations
- CTA: Coffee chat or strategy call

### For B2B Agencies
Use Template 4 (Lead Gen) or Template 6 (Agency Services):
- Lead: Project they did or client they work with
- Proof: New clients or revenue brought to similar agency
- CTA: "Can you take on X new clients?"

### For Tech/SaaS
Use Template 7 (SaaS Growth) or Template 1 (3C's):
- Lead: Growth metric or product announcement
- Proof: Specific adoption/revenue/user metric
- CTA: "Worth a conversation about next steps?"

### For Freelancers (Design, Copy, Dev)
Use Template 6 (Agency Services) or Template 2 (No-Brainer):
- Lead: Specific project or portfolio piece
- Proof: Project result or client case study
- CTA: Small project or time commitment

### For Recruiters/Staffing
Use Template 5 (Recruiting):
- Lead: Their open role or hiring needs
- Proof: Similar candidate placements
- CTA: "Interested in discussing this candidate?"

### For E-Commerce
Use Template 8 (E-Commerce):
- Lead: Their product or store
- Proof: AOV, conversion, or repeat purchase increase
- CTA: "Worth discussing conversion improvement?"

## Do's and Don'ts for Templates

**DO:**
- Use as frameworks, not copy-paste
- Customize opening line heavily
- Test variations and iterate
- Keep to 4-6 sentences
- Ask clear yes/no questions
- Include specific results

**DON'T:**
- Send exact template to multiple people
- Use generic openings
- Reference irrelevant case studies
- Include multiple links
- Use images or fancy formatting
- Follow-up more than 3 times

## Template Performance Notes

**Higher response rates (15-20%):**
- Warm referrals (existing trust)
- Very specific case studies (perfect fit)
- Deep research (shows real effort)
- Clear ROI (specific offer)

**Medium response rates (10-15%):**
- Basic 3C's with good case study
- Relevant to their industry
- Professional personalization
- Correct person targeted

**Lower response rates (5-10%):**
- Cold outreach to unverified emails
- Generic templates
- Wrong person targeted
- Weak case study

**Reality:** Response rates improve 2-3x after 2-3 test cycles with optimization.
