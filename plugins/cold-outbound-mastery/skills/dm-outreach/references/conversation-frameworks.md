# Complete Conversation Frameworks & Flows

## THE COMPLETE 3-PHASE CONVERSATION STRUCTURE

All successful DM conversations follow this exact progression. Each phase has specific goals and language patterns.

---

## PHASE 1: COLD/RAPPORT BUILDING

**Duration:** 1-3 messages
**Goal:** Genuine connection and trust
**Mindset:** Person first, business second
**Success Indicator:** They're responding with substance (not one-word answers)

### Framework

```
Message 1: THE GENUINE OPENER
- Observe something specific from their profile/post
- Ask a genuine question (not selling)
- Make it conversational, not formal

Example:
"Hey NAME! I noticed you've been posting about [specific thing].
How long have you been doing that?"

Not: "Hi, I help people with [topic]. Want to chat?"
```

---

## PHASE 2: WARM/SITUATIONAL AWARENESS

**Duration:** 2-5 messages
**Goal:** Understand their situation and uncover pain points
**Key Question:** Is there a pain point that matches our solution?
**Success Indicator:** They mention a specific problem or challenge

### The Pain Discovery System (Level 1, 2, 3)

```
LEVEL 1 (Surface Problem):
What they say: "I want 10k/month"
Your response: "Cool! What does getting to 10k/month look like for you?"

Why Level 1 isn't enough: General goals alone don't drive conversions.
Their motivation is theoretical, not urgent.

---

LEVEL 2 (Specific Problem - THIS IS THE MINIMUM):
What they say: "I need better DM selling skills"
OR: "I'm struggling to get consistent leads"
OR: "I don't know how to close people in DMs"

Your response: "What's the biggest challenge you're facing with that right now?"

Why Level 2 works: Concrete, specific obstacles create urgency.
This is the minimum required to move to Phase 3 (invitation).

---

LEVEL 3 (Personal/Emotional Pain - IDEAL):
What they say: "I want financial freedom to spend time with my family"
OR: "I'm stressed about my income being inconsistent"
OR: "I want to prove to my team that I can scale this"

Your response: "That makes total sense. When do you want to hit that by?"

Why Level 3 is ideal: Emotional drivers are the strongest.
This creates urgency and commitment to change.
```

### Discovery Message Framework

```
Message Structure for Pain Discovery:
[Acknowledge their previous response] + [Market assumption or probe] + [Open-ended question]

Example 1:
"Got it, so you're getting clients but not consistently. What's your main bottleneck—
is it lead generation, the pitch, or something else?"

Example 2:
"Nice! So you're doing the 1:1 coaching. How are you typically getting your new clients right now?"

Example 3:
"That makes sense. What do you think is holding you back from hitting that 10k/month goal?"

Example 4:
"Cool. What do you feel like you need most right now to scale to [their goal]?"

Example 5:
"I see. If this [problem] wasn't holding you back, where would you want your [metric] to be by end of year?"
```

### Market Assumption Framework (Takes 3-5 Messages)

```
Structure: [Observation] + [Assumption] + [Question]

Example 1:
"I noticed you started coaching people in [niche]. Usually when people get into
that space, they struggle with [common problem]. Is that something you're dealing with?"

Example 2:
"I saw you're pretty active on Instagram with posts. Most coaches I know who post consistently
are either getting tons of leads or not getting ANY. Which camp are you in?"

Example 3:
"You seem like you've got the knowledge and credibility down. The one thing most people
struggle with in your position is [X]. How are you handling that?"

Example 4:
"I noticed you mention [goal] a lot. Usually when people want that, the thing that stops them
is [specific problem]. Are you running into that?"

Key: Don't hit the pain on first assumption. Test 3-5 before finding their real obstacle.
```

### If They Say "Things Are Going Well" (Stonewalled)

```
Don't give up. Try this:

"Sounds like things are going pretty well for you then! Just out of curiosity,
is there anything you'd like to improve on if you could?"

Or pivot:

"That's awesome. So when you think about the next 12 months, what's one area
you'd like to improve or optimize?"

Or try a different angle:

"Got it. I'm just curious—if you had unlimited [resource, time, knowledge],
what's the ONE thing you'd change about your business right now?"

Strategy: Different pain questions often unlock where they're actually struggling.
```

## PHASE 3: HOT/INVITATION & BOOKING

**Duration:** 1-4 messages
**Goal:** Get them on a call (position as value play, not sales)
**Success Indicator:** They've either booked or clearly said no
**Maximum follow-ups on offer:** 3-4 messages before moving on

### The Soft Invitation Framework

```
THE POSITIONING:
NOT: "Buy my program" or "Let me sell you something"
YES: "Let's see if we can actually help you" or "Quick strategy chat to see if we're a fit"

THE STRUCTURE:
[Recap what they told you] + [Your belief in a solution] + [Soft invitation] + [CTA]

Example 1:
"Based on what you've told me, I think we might actually be able to help you
with [their specific problem], as we've helped dozens of [profession]s in your exact situation.

How would you find it to hop on a quick chat to see if we could help in the first place?
No strings attached, just to see if it makes sense. [CALENDLY LINK]"

Example 2:
"So if I'm hearing you right, you're struggling with [specific problem].
We've built a whole system around this, and it's worked for X number of people just like you.

Would you be open to a 15-minute gameplan call this week? Just to see if we can help and
go from there. [CALENDLY LINK]"

Example 3:
"Okay so the main thing is [their pain], got it. Here's what I'm thinking—
a lot of coaches we work with had the same problem, and we've figured out a way to solve it.

How would you find it if I shared the exact framework we use with you over a quick chat?
[CALENDLY LINK]"

Example 4:
"For someone in your position trying to hit [goal] and dealing with [pain], I think a quick
strategy session could be really valuable. No pitch, just us looking at your situation and seeing
if we have something that could help. Sound good? [CALENDLY LINK]"
```

### If They Say "I'm Interested But Need to Think About It"

```
Acknowledge and follow up in 3-5 days:

"Totally understand. Let me send you a quick resource on [topic] that might help
you think through it. [SEND PDF OR VIDEO LINK]

Let me know your thoughts and we can chat more if it makes sense. :)"

Then 3-5 days later:

"Hey! Just checking in—did you get a chance to look at that? What were your thoughts?

Still interested in hopping on that quick call to map this out?"
```

### If They Have an Objection (See objection scripts in templates-library.md)

```
BASIC OBJECTION FLOW:

1. Acknowledge their objection
2. Normalize it (others have felt the same)
3. Reframe the value
4. Soft re-invite

Example:

Them: "I'm not sure if I can afford it right now."

You: "Totally get it, that's super common. Here's the thing though—
most people I talk to in your position end up making the investment back
3-5x within the first month just from implementing one of our frameworks.

That's why it makes sense to jump on a quick call—not to sell you, but to
make sure this is actually a fit before you commit any money. Sound fair? [LINK]"
```

## CONVERSATION VELOCITY & TIMING

### Message Spacing by Phase

**Phase 1 (Rapport):**
- First opener: Send immediately or within 1 hour of follow
- Messages 2-3: Space out 1-3 hours apart (let them respond)
- If no response after 3 messages: Wait 24 hours, then send fresh angle

**Phase 2 (Pain Discovery):**
- Ask a question, wait for their response (don't spam)
- Each follow-up should be a NEW question or insight, not a reminder
- If they go silent: Wait 24-48 hours, re-engage with value or new angle

**Phase 3 (Invitation):**
- Send calendar link with first invitation
- If no response in 24 hours: Send one follow-up with slight reframe
- Maximum 3-4 follow-ups on the offer before moving them to "NOT_NOW" tag

### Response Rate Indicators

**Good (40%+ response rate):**
- Messages are personalized
- Each message ends with a question
- You're messaging warm leads first

**Mediocre (10-30% response rate):**
- Messages lack personalization
- Some messages don't have clear questions
- Mix of warm and cold without priority

**Poor (Under 10% response rate):**
- Messages are templated or generic
- No personalization or observation
- Messaging wrong audience
- Weak openers that don't spark curiosity

## COMMON CONVERSATION KILLERS TO AVOID

```
❌ Making it about you instead of them
"I help coaches book calls through DMs..." → Focus on them first

❌ Jumping to pitch before pain discovery
"You should join my program..." → Still in Phase 1 rapport building

❌ Messages without questions
"Here's my free resource" → Always end with a question for engagement

❌ Generic copy-paste messages
"Hey! How are you?" → Personalize at least one observation

❌ Too many messages without response
If they haven't answered: Don't send 5 more messages. Wait 24 hours.

❌ Pitching instead of inviting
"This is $997/month..." → "Quick strategy chat to see if we're a fit"

❌ Long messages in Phase 1
Keep openers short (3-4 sentences max in cold outreach)

❌ Not confirming understanding
"So if I understand correctly..." → Shows you were listening

❌ Ending on weak note
"Let me know!" → Specific CTA: "Does Friday at 2pm work for you?"
```
