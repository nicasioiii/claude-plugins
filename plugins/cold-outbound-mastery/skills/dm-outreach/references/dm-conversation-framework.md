# DM Conversation Framework

## The 4-Step Consulting Framework

### Stage 1: Awareness
- Get them connected (friends list / following you)
- They can put their hand up to talk further
- No selling, no pitching, no qualifying
- Just visibility and connection

### Stage 2: Cold Phase
Build genuine connection and trust. They may not be ready to buy or aware of their problem.

**Goals:**
1. Make genuine connections and build trust
2. Keep conversation going (don't let it die)
3. Get them to open up and transition to warm stage

**Questions for the Cold Phase:**
- "What do you do for clients?"
- "Is that mostly one-on-one? Group coaching?"
- "How are you currently getting most of your clients?"

### Stage 3: Warm Phase
Understanding where they are. Uncovering if and how you can help. You may need to help them realize they have a problem.

**Goals:**
1. Find a problem that matches your solution
2. Get their desired outcome
3. Get what's keeping them from desired outcome

**Surface Problem Discovery:**
- "What would you want your goal to be by end of year?"
- "If [your service area] wasn't a problem, what would your goals be?"
- "If you could fix this problem, how much revenue would you want to see?"

**Level 2 Pain Discovery:**
- "What do you feel you need in order to hit your goal?"
- "How long has that been going on for?"
- "Why do you think that is causing that?"
- "What have you tried to fix that?"

### Stage 4: Hot Phase
Get them on a call.

**Goals:**
1. Book a meeting by proposing a call
2. Overcome possible resistance

**Invitation Pitches:**
- "How would you find it if I shared the frameworks for [specific result] that's been working for my clients?"
- "I can let you jump on a call with my team to see if we can help you get to those specific goals."
- "Here's what I'm thinking. 15 minutes. I can give you a call right here on Facebook and map out a plan on how you can [achieve result]."
- "It's really just to see how we'd vibe and if this could be something for you personally, no strings attached."
- "Does that make sense jumping on that call?"

**Triage Call Framing:**
"This is not a sales call. I'm not going to pitch you, so you can leave your credit card at home."

**Call Booking Follow-Up:**
"Did you run into any problems finding a time slot that suits your personal agenda?" -- Described as extremely effective.

## Three Levels of Pain (Deep Dive)

### Surface / Level 1
General problem or goal they're already aware of.
- "I want to go from $5K to $10K/month"
- "I want to lose weight"
- "I need more clients"

**Trap:** Most setters stop here and propose a call. This is too early. The prospect hasn't felt the pain deeply enough to commit.

### Level 2
The specific problem preventing them from achieving Level 1.
- "I need to get better at DM selling to close more deals"
- "I don't know what foods to eat or how to track macros"
- "I don't have a system for getting leads consistently"

**This is your minimum threshold for booking a call.** You need at least Level 2 pain before proposing.

### Level 3
Personal/emotional pain underneath the business problem.
- "If I can't book more calls, I can't grow and provide for my family"
- "I won't be able to play with my kids because of my health"
- "I'm stressed all the time because revenue is unpredictable"

**Explore Level 3 on the actual sales call, not in DMs.** DMs are for identifying Level 2 and creating enough urgency to book.

### The Critical Rule
**No Pain, No Next Step.** Only book a call when you've identified at least Level 2 pain. Skipping this step = low show-up rates, unqualified calls, and wasted time.

## Market Assumption Questions

Questions based on common industry pain points that allow you to lead the conversation toward booking.

### Structure
[Understanding of their current situation] + [Question/assumption about a common industry problem]

### Examples
- "I saw you have your profile set up as a funnel -- great way to connect with leads in [industry]. Is Facebook your main platform?"
- "I noticed you had 3 kids! Must be a lot to keep up with. Have you found you can keep up with their energy levels?"
- "I see you're the CEO of your business. How do you manage running a business and taking care of you?"
- "I see you started a group. Always a great way to get leads. Have you been able to generate many through there?"

### Why They Work (Regardless of Answer)
- If crushing it: "Awesome, so you have a full calendar of great calls consistently?" (assumes they don't)
- If struggling: You've found the pain point directly

### Tips
- Multiple assumption lines may be needed before hitting a pain point
- Keep a record of questions that work for future conversations
- Keep questioning aligned with current conversation topic
- Once you connect with a good assumption, still dig to Level 2 pain

## Show-Up Rate Optimization

### Core Principle
How prospects FEEL about you and your offer is far more important than what they see. Create a sales EXPERIENCE, not a sales process.

### Reminder Sequence (Before Every Call)
1. **Immediately:** Confirmation email (include training video, ensure Zoom downloaded)
2. **48 or 24 hours before:** Reminder email
3. **30 minutes before:** Reminder email
4. **Text message reminder(s)** -- at least one
5. **DM reminder** on booking platform (Facebook/Instagram)

### Pre-Call Funnel Elements (Must-Haves)
1. **Overview** of what you do (clear who you help and why)
2. **Training on your "vehicle"** -- the method that takes client from A to Z. Replaces long webinars. Converts better.
3. **Testimonials** -- Quality over quantity. Categorize by client type so prospects see someone like them.
4. **Option to binge content** -- They'll research you while waiting for the call.

### Post-Booking Sequence (Differentiator for Close Rate)
1. Confirm date/time in DM: "Got you locked in at [time] on [date]. Sound right?"
2. Send pre-call training link + funnel page
3. Tag them in an offer post (social proof)
4. Triage questions (casual, no interrogation): "Can I get some details? Your funnel, landing pages, some stats -- nothing major"
5. Tag them in a relevant client interview/testimonial (matching their niche)
6. Confirm pricing alignment (reverse engineer revenue from triage info)
7. 24 hours before: "Excited for our call tomorrow. Did you get the emails?"
8. Morning of: "Hey John, just confirming you're still available at 3pm"
9. 15 min before: "Here's the Zoom link in case any tech issues"

### Cancellation Scripts (For Non-Qualified Leads)
- "I saw your booking came in. I noticed the budget section -- for context, pricing is [X]. Lowest installment is [Y]. Would that sit within your budget?"
- For clear non-fits: "I can hold off the call until you're in a better place to purchase" (then downsell)

## Daily Action SOP Summary

### Target KPIs
| Metric | Daily Target |
|--------|-------------|
| New conversations | 20-50 |
| Follow-ups | At least 100 |
| Qualified calls booked | 3 |

### Daily Flow
1. Send call reminders (~5 min)
2. Clear inbox
3. Follow up with yesterday's Calendly events (~5 min)
4. Follow up with calls proposed yesterday (~10 min)
5. New outreach: group requests, polls, action posts, external groups, post engagers
6. Follow-ups: 24-hour, hot tags, scroll-back, welcome post, story watchers
7. Clear inbox again at end of day
8. Fill in KPI tracker
9. Send end-of-day report

### Weekly Special Tasks
- Follow up with everyone who had a call in past 2 months
- 30 days back in inbox
- Clean through old CRM leads

## Pricing Model Notes

### No-Brainer Gap
Value should be minimum 2x the price (ideally 3-5x or more).

### Value Equation (Hormozi)
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)

### Pay-in-Full Discount (Not "Broke Tax")
Reward pay-in-full customers with a discount rather than charging more for payment plans. Example: $5,000 PIF or 3x $1,900 = $5,700 (payment plan costs more but isn't framed as a penalty).
