# Facebook DM Scripts

## Warm Lead Openers

### 1. Facebook Group Members (No Survey Response)
- Add as friend first, wait for acceptance
- "Hey, thanks for joining my group [name]! I just released my free course -- would you be against it if I sent it over to you?"
- Wait for reply, THEN send resource + ask a question
- Never send the resource before they reply

### 2. Facebook Group Members (With Survey Response)
- "Hey, just accepted you into the group! Love the goal you have of [their goal from survey]. How's that going?"
- Follow-up: "Also -- would you want me to reach out to see how I might be able to help you with that? Are you keen to connect on a quick call this week?"

### 3. Old Group Members (Inactive)
- Scroll to members who joined 6+ months ago
- "Hey, did you know I have [action post/training] available? Would you like me to send it?"
- If no response: check their profile, look for personalized conversation starter

### 4. Story Responders
- Respond naturally to their reaction
- "Thanks for the support! [Relate to content] Have you been working on [topic] for a while?"
- Keep it conversational, not pitchy

### 5. Story Poll Voters
- "Hey, thanks for voting on my story! Let me send you [training] -- would you be interested?"
- The poll already indicates engagement -- they're primed

### 6. Action Post Commenters
- See comment, respond to comment publicly FIRST, THEN send DM
- "Hey, thanks for your comment on my [topic] post! So I can send it over, here it is. What has your [relevant metric] been looking like the past couple months?"
- **Wait for reply before sending resource** -- the question is the priority

### 7. Action Post Likers (No Comment)
- "Hey, I saw you liked my post about [topic]. Would you be against it if I sent over that training?"
- Lower engagement signal than commenters, but still worth messaging

## The Welcome Post System

Pin a welcome post in your Facebook group. Tag every new member.

Script: "Thanks for joining! Would love to send you some free training -- send me a message and I'll get it to you."

This creates inbound DMs from new members, making the conversation feel like THEIR idea.

## Cold Lead Openers

### 1. Old Group Members
- Scroll back to people who joined months ago
- "Noticed you've been in my group for a while. Hope you're doing amazing and getting a lot of value!"
- Non-threatening, re-engagement focused

### 2. Older Friends (Unrecognized/Un-communicated)
- Go to friends list, scroll back to people you haven't spoken to
- "We've been friends for a while! Hope you're doing amazing."
- Check their profile for personalized follow-up

### 3. Content Engagers
- Check likes on recent posts, message those people
- "Appreciate the engagement on my recent post about [topic]. Hope you're not having a [related problem] as well!"
- Lighthearted, opens pain discussion naturally

### 4. Story Viewers
- Message people who watched ALL stories (higher engagement signal)
- People who watched the entire story sequence are worth messaging -- indicates real interest

### 5. People in Similar Groups
- Add people from other relevant groups
- DO NOT cold message before they accept friend request
- Risk of account ban in competitive niches (marketing, health, dating)
- Wait for acceptance, then use warm opener

## Critical Rules for All Facebook DMs

### Always Ask a Question
If you don't have an obvious way for the conversation to continue and you're not asking questions, you will get stonewalled. Every message should either answer a question or ask one.

### The Goal of Sending Free Resources
NOT to get them to watch it. The goal is to START A CONVERSATION.

If they say "I'll watch it tonight," respond: "That's awesome, hope you get a lot of value! Have you been working on [topic] for a while?"

The resource is bait for dialogue, not the deliverable.

## Facebook Group Onboarding for Quick Call Booking

### 3 Membership Questions (Set in Group Settings)
1. "Where are you currently at in your business?" (current situation)
2. "Where do you want to be in the next 12 months?" (desired situation)
3. "Would you like me [your name] to reach out to see how we can help you get some help with that?" (permission)

### Response Flows

**YES to Q3 + provided a goal:**
"I saw you wanted me to reach out to see how I could help you with that. Are you keen to connect on a quick call this week?"
Works 9 out of 10 times when someone has already said yes and stated a clear goal.

**No goal provided:**
"Hey, just accepted you into the group! Love that [topic] goal. How's that going?"
Then dig for pain using the 4-step framework.

**No response at all:**
"Thank you for joining! I'm always aiming to deliver as much value as possible. Would you be against it if I sent over my [framework/training]?"

## Daily Action SOP -- Facebook DM Flow

### Morning Sequence
1. Send call reminders for the day (~5 min)
2. Clear inbox (use mbasic.facebook.com/messages/?folder=unread for speed)
3. Follow up with past Calendly events from yesterday and last week (~5 min)
4. Follow up with calls proposed from yesterday (~10 min)

### New Outreach Sequence
5. Facebook group requests -- add as friend first (~3 new reach-outs)
6. Story poll sweep (~5 new reach-outs)
7. Action post sweep (~5 new reach-outs)
8. External FB groups (~10 new reach-outs)
9. Most recent post engagers (~10 new reach-outs)

### Follow-Up Sequence
10. 24-hour follow-ups (72 hours on Monday) (~50 follow-ups)
11. Hot tags follow-up (~20 follow-ups)
12. Scroll-back follow-ups (~10 follow-ups)
13. Group welcome post follow-up (~5 follow-ups)
14. Story watchers, inbound friend requests, older group members, older friends

### End of Day
- Clear the inbox completely
- Fill in KPI tracker
- Send end-of-day report

### Weekly Special Tasks
- Follow up with everybody who had a call in past 2 months
- 30 days back in the inbox
- Clean through old CRM leads

## Content Strategy for Facebook DM Outreach

### Action Posts
Content pieces that prompt engagement (comments). Format:
- Share a framework or insight
- End with: "Comment [keyword] and I'll send you [resource]"
- Every commenter gets a DM AND funneled into opt-in

### Story Strategy
- Minimum 1 poll per story (2x/week)
- Polls create engagement signals you can DM about
- Stories keep you visible in the feed

### Posting Cadence
- 1 Facebook content post per week (action post)
- Facebook stories with polls (2x/week)
- 1 TikTok/Reel + FB Reel
