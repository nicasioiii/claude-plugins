# Outsourcing First Lines

## Economics of Outsourced First Lines

### Cost Targets

- **Target effective cost:** $0.70 per good first line
- **Hiring rate:** $7/hour on Upwork (not $15/hour)
- **Batch pricing:** 100 first lines for $70
- **Quality threshold:** 7 out of 10 minimum (assessed on god-level spectrum)

### When to Outsource

- You are sending 50+ cold emails per day and cannot research every lead yourself
- You have a proven first line formula and need to scale it
- You have trained yourself on god-level quality and can QA someone else's work

### When NOT to Outsource

- You have not mastered first lines yourself yet
- You cannot articulate what makes a line good vs. bad
- You do not have a QA process in place
- You are sending fewer than 30 emails/day (do them yourself for practice)

**Critical rule (Berman):** Master the skill yourself FIRST before outsourcing. You need to understand the craft before you can evaluate someone else's work.

## Hiring on Upwork

### Job Posting Template

**Title:** "Cold Email First Line Writer - Personalized Opening Lines"

**Description:**
We need personalized first lines for cold email outreach. Each first line must be:
- Based on real research about the prospect (case studies, blog posts, awards, LinkedIn)
- 1-2 sentences maximum
- Conversational and natural sounding (pass the read-aloud test)
- Specific enough that it could ONLY be sent to that one person
- NOT generic flattery ("love your company" = rejected)

**Deliverables:**
- 100 first lines per batch
- Each line must cite the source (website, LinkedIn post, podcast, etc.)
- Turnaround: 48 hours per batch

**Budget:** $70 per 100 lines ($0.70 per line)

**To Apply:** Please include the phrase "[your filter phrase]" at the top of your cover letter, and write a sample first line for [your company/a specific person].

### Hiring Criteria

| Filter | Requirement |
|--------|------------|
| Job success score | 90%+ |
| Hourly rate | $10 or below |
| Hours billed | At least 1 hour on platform |
| Cover letter | Customized, references your specific criteria |
| Filter phrase | Included (proves they read the posting) |
| Sample line | Provided and meets quality threshold |

### Hiring Process

1. Post the job with the template above
2. Filter applications by criteria
3. Hire 3 freelancers simultaneously as a test
4. Give each the same batch of 20 leads
5. Compare output quality
6. Keep the best performer, provide feedback to the others
7. 1 of 3 hires will typically produce god-level quality

## Training Guide for Freelancers

### What to Send Your Freelancer

Share these materials with every new first line writer:

**1. The Quality Spectrum**
Show them 3-5 examples of each:
- BAD first lines (with explanation of why they are bad)
- MEDIOCRE first lines (with explanation of what is missing)
- GOD-LEVEL first lines (with explanation of what makes them great)

**2. The 6 Formulas**
Provide the complete formula list with examples:
1. Specific Achievement + Understated Comment
2. Specific Content + Deep Industry Insight
3. Case Study Stat + Real-World Connection
4. Specific Reference + Humor/Cleverness
5. Specific Info + Genuine Question
6. Specific Blog/Podcast + Genuine Deeper Thought

**3. The Research Hierarchy**
Tell them exactly where to look and in what order:
1. Case studies (best)
2. Accomplishments / awards
3. Podcasts / interviews
4. Career milestones
5. Company website / blog
6. LinkedIn posts

**4. The Formatting Rules**
- Max 1 exclamation point
- Use "&" instead of "and"
- Always use contractions
- Max 2 sentences
- Use dashes and dots to connect ideas
- Never use "amazing"
- Smiley faces: 1 in every 5-7 maximum

**5. The Anti-Pattern List**
Explicitly prohibit:
- Generic flattery
- Self-references ("I noticed," "I was impressed")
- Starting with "I"
- Using "amazing," "awesome," "excellent" without context
- Mentioning their name in the line (redundant)
- Shallow observations (years in industry, college, endorsements)

**6. The Read-Aloud Test**
Instruct: "Read each first line out loud 3 times. If you would not say this when meeting someone for the first time, rewrite it."

### Lead Spreadsheet Format

Share a spreadsheet with freelancers containing:

| Column | Content |
|--------|---------|
| First Name | Prospect's first name |
| Last Name | Prospect's last name |
| Title | Job title |
| Company | Company name |
| LinkedIn URL | Direct link to their profile |
| Website | Company website URL |
| Email | Prospect email (for context) |
| First Line | (freelancer fills this in) |
| Source | (freelancer cites where they found the info) |
| Quality Score | (you fill this during QA) |

## QA Process

### Scoring Each First Line

Use a 1-10 scale aligned with the quality spectrum:

| Score | Quality Level | Action |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| 1-3 | Bad | Reject, provide specific feedback |
| 4-6 | Mediocre | Reject with fix guidance, or rewrite yourself if close |
| 7-8 | Good | Accept |
| 9-10 | God-Level | Accept, highlight as an example for future batches |

### Quality Threshold: 7/10 Minimum

A batch of 100 first lines should have:
- 80%+ scoring 7/10 or above (80+ acceptable lines)
- 0% scoring below 4/10 (zero terrible lines)
- If a batch has more than 20% below 7/10: send back for revision with specific notes

### QA Checklist (Per Line)

- [ ] Is it specific to this one person? (Could not be sent to anyone else?)
- [ ] Does it include a deep second thought? (Not just a surface observation?)
- [ ] Does it flow naturally? (Pass the read-aloud test?)
- [ ] Is the source cited? (LinkedIn post, website, podcast, etc.?)
- [ ] Is it under 2 sentences?
- [ ] No filler words? (really, very, amazing, awesome without context?)
- [ ] No self-references? (No "I" at the start?)
- [ ] Proper formatting? (Contractions, dashes, max 1 exclamation point?)

### Feedback Loop

After each batch:
1. Score every line
2. Highlight 3 best lines as examples of what you want more of
3. Highlight 3 worst lines with specific notes on what went wrong
4. Provide the fix pattern (e.g., "you keep using 'amazing' -- replace with specific adjectives tied to what impressed you")
5. Track batch-over-batch quality trends

### When to Fire a First Line Writer

- Quality does not improve after 3 batches of feedback
- Consistently submits generic or copy-paste lines
- Misses deadlines
- Does not follow the research hierarchy (writes from LinkedIn only when case studies exist)
- Cannot pass the read-aloud test consistently

### When to Increase Pay

- Consistently delivers 90%+ at 7/10 or above
- Requires minimal editing
- Proactively cites creative sources (podcasts, interviews, case studies -- not just LinkedIn)
- Delivers ahead of deadline
- Increase to $1.00-$1.50 per line for premium writers you want to retain

## Scaling First Line Operations

### Volume Guidelines

| Daily Send Volume | First Line Approach |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Under 30/day | Write yourself (practice and quality) |
| 30-50/day | Write yourself or 1 freelancer |
| 50-100/day | 1-2 freelancers with QA |
| 100-200/day | 2-3 freelancers with dedicated QA time |
| 200+/day | Small team of freelancers with QA manager |

### Quality at Scale

As volume increases, quality naturally pressures downward. Maintain standards by:
1. Never accepting below 7/10 -- reject and retrain
2. Rotating in new freelancers periodically (fresh perspective)
3. Updating your example library monthly with new god-level lines
4. Random-sampling 10% of each batch for deep QA (even when a freelancer is trusted)
5. A/B testing lines from different writers to identify top performers
