# Follow-Up & Sequencing

## Core Principle

**70% of replies come from emails sent AFTER the first email.** A one-off email has less than 1% chance of getting a reply. If you are not following up, you are leaving 70% of your results on the table.

**80% of successful sales via cold email happen after 5+ follow-ups.** Only 8-10% of senders have a proper structure of 5+ emails (Acquisition X).

## The K.I.S.S. Sequencing Framework (30MPC)

**Keep It Simple Sequencing** -- the foundation for all outbound sequence design.

### Key Stats
- Adding phone calls nearly **doubles** reply rate
- Phone + voicemail = **3.24x** increase in reply rate
- Triple tap (call + voicemail + email + social) = **3.1x** increase in contact rates
- **14-28 days** is the ideal sequence duration (too short = unsubscribes, too long = lost burst effect)

### Weekly Touch Pattern
- **Day 1 (Tuesday):** Triple tap -- Call + voicemail + email + social touch
- **Day 3 (Thursday):** Double tap -- Call + email
- Repeat with new problem angle in week 2

### Two Problem Angles Structure
- **Emails 1-3:** Focus on Problem #1 (same email chain)
- **Emails 4-6:** Focus on Problem #2 (new email chain, new subject line)
- **Final emails:** "Get the truth" / breakup emails

## Six Bump Email Frameworks (30MPC)

### 1. The Breakup Bump
> "Hate to bother you if this isn't relevant -- is there someone else on the team you think I should reach out to?"

Variant: "Would this be more relevant for Todd over in operations?"

### 2. The Get the Truth Bump
> "Hey {Name}, I shared a few ways to help with your team's {problem}, but I'd hate to keep reaching out if we aren't a fit. Mind giving me the thumbs-up or thumbs-down?"

### 3. The CTA Bump (1-Sentence Follow-Up)
- "Does this align with your 2025 priorities?"
- "Any chance this is top of mind for you?"
- "Worth a quick chat?"
- "Wanna try it out? (doesn't cost anything)"

### 4. The Any Thoughts? Bump
> "Any thoughts?"

Variant: "{Name}, any thoughts on reducing the need for devs?"

### 5. The Quick Chat? Bump
> "Any chance you're free next Tuesday at 2pm or 3pm PT?"

### 6. The Case Study Bump
> "Here's how Ops leaders at Caterpillar are leveraging automation to replace welders."

The only purpose of a bump email is to get another email into the inbox drawing attention back to the original. Do NOT write another full-length email.

## Berman's 7 Follow-Up Types

1. **Quick Bump** -- short mail pushing thread to top of inbox
2. **Two Ideas Email** -- two specific ideas for their niche, showing you understand their business
3. **Big News** -- share a recent win or different case study for a similar company
4. **Breakup Email** -- "I'll assume {their goal} isn't a priority this year"
5. **Personal Connection** -- mention something in common (sports team, alma mater)
6. **Excitement** -- "Excited to get started?" as reply to thread
7. **News Item** -- mention a recent achievement or news about their company

## Cold Email Wizard's 6 Levels of Creativity

### Level 1 -- Gentle Bump
- "Bump :)"
- "{First Name}?"
- "Thoughts?"

### Level 2 -- Re-engagement
- "Hey {Name}, happy Monday! What does your week look like? Would love to connect for that intro call."
- "Just bumping this thread to the top of your inbox."

### Level 3 -- Offer Value/Content
- Share a LinkedIn post, case study, YouTube video, or portfolio piece
- "Just wanted to share a quick reel showcasing our most recent work"

### Level 4 -- Reverse Psychology / Breakup
- "My guess is timing might be off on this, or there's less than zero interest in having a conversation."
- "You wouldn't be interested in {service}, would you {Name}?"

### Level 5 -- The Spam Check Hack
- "Important question for you, {Name}... Have you been receiving my recent emails? Had a few people tell me they were finding them in spam :/"
- Creates obligation to respond; even negative responses reopen the conversation

### Level 6 -- Holiday/Event Anchoring
- "Hi {Name}, hope you enjoyed your Memorial Day weekend! Any availability this week?"
- "Just checking in before things get crazy with the holidays :)"

## Follow-Up Timing Rules

### Email Cadence (Berman)
| Timing | Email |
|---|---|
| Day 0 | Initial cold email |
| +3 days | Quick bump |
| +4-6 days | Two Ideas / Value email OR Big News |
| +3-4 days | Breakup: "I'll assume {goal} isn't a priority this year" |

### Email Cadence (Cold Email Wizard, Observed Patterns)
| Follow-Up | Timing |
|---|---|
| Follow-up 1 | 3 days after initial |
| Follow-up 2 | 3-5 days after FU1 |
| Follow-up 3 | 5-7 days after FU2 |
| Follow-up 4+ | 7-11 days, escalating creativity |

Some threads had 5-6+ follow-ups before booking.

### Acquisition X 4-Email Sequence
| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | Day 1 | Introduction + value proposition |
| Email 2 | Day 3-5 | Gentle reminder |
| Email 3 | Day 7-10 | New angle/additional value |
| Email 4 | Day 14-21 | Case study/social proof |

### Critical Timing Insights (Cold Email Wizard)
- Friday afternoon emails (after 2pm) rarely get same-day responses -- expect Monday midday replies
- If sending Friday, wait until Tuesday/Wednesday for next follow-up
- Respond to positive replies AS FAST AS POSSIBLE -- same hour if possible
- Prospects can reply 18+ days after initial email and still book
- Some conversations span 3+ weeks before booking

## Follow-Up Best Practices

### Do's
- Add new value, angle, or proposition in EACH follow-up (Acquisition X)
- Mix questions and statements to engage prospects
- Personalize based on research
- Space emails appropriately -- respect their schedule and time zone
- Chase deals until you get a definitive YES or NO (Berman)
- Never send all follow-ups at same time/day (they may have recurring meetings)

### Don'ts
- Never just say "Did you get my last email?" without adding value (Acquisition X)
- Do not write another full-length email for a bump (30MPC)
- Do not give up after 1-2 follow-ups

## Meeting Booking Flow

1. Prospect replies with interest
2. Offer specific times with timezone: "I'm free Thursday 2-4pm EST or Friday 10am-12pm EST"
3. Confirm meeting and send calendar invite
4. Day-before confirmation email with prep research
5. No-show protocol if they miss (see `references/meeting-booking-mechanics.md`)

## Post-Sales-Call Follow-Up Sequence (Berman)

| Timing | Email |
|---|---|
| Day 0 (within minutes) | "Great chat today. Attaching the proposal." |
| Next day | "Wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Did you get the signup link?" |
| End of week | "I have your word that we're starting today, right?" |
| Next week | "Did you have any more questions on us building the site?" |
| 2 weeks later | "Just signed another {type} client. Getting close to being booked solid. Should I mark you down as a no?" |
| 1 week later | "Hey {Name}, are you ghosting me? lol" + same day cold call |
| 2 weeks later | Price increase urgency: "We're increasing prices by 50%." |

## Momentum Maintenance (Bree Weber)

When a prospect responds positively but scheduling stalls:
- Send a Loom video walkthrough of your proposed solution
- Keep conversation warm with value-add content
- The Run the World pitch got a response in 1 hour but died because scheduling took too long
- Lesson: send additional proof-of-concept while waiting for the call to keep momentum
