# Email Follow-Up Sequences

## 30MPC 7-Email + LinkedIn Sequence Template

### 1st Problem Block (Same Email Chain)

**Email 1 -- Personalized Problem Email:**
Full REPLY method email with personalization trigger, problem, social proof, interest-based CTA.

**Email 2 -- Bubble-Up (reply to previous):**
> "Hey {Name}, just tried you over the phone. Any thoughts?"

**Email 3 -- Make It Real (reply to previous):**
> "Left you a VM. Here's a 1-minute video where we {demo}. Open to trying it out?"

### 2nd Problem Block (New Subject Line)

**Email 4 -- Next Best Problem:**
New subject line, different problem angle from messaging matrix.
> "Sometimes that Enterprise AE won't come to you because the best reps often aren't looking..."

**Email 5 -- First Pushaway (reply to Email 4):**
> "Hate to bother you if this doesn't fall on your plate. Is there someone else on the team?"

### Get the Truth Block (New Subject Line)

**Email 6 -- Get the Truth:**
> "Shared a few ways to help... but I'd hate to keep reaching out if we weren't a fit. Mind giving me the thumbs-up or thumbs-down?"

**Email 7 -- Final Pushaway:**
> "I'll assume this isn't a fit for now. In case you'd ever like to consider us: {one-sentence summary}, {1-minute demo video}, {case study}. I usually reconnect every 30 days to see if anything has changed. Mind letting me know if I shouldn't reach back out?"

### LinkedIn Messages (Parallel)
- **Connection Request:** Leave blank (do NOT pitch)
- **LI Message 1 (Context Only):** "Noticed your {trigger} and sent you an email. I know everyone hates the connect-and-pitch thing, so I won't restate it all here. Did you see my note by chance?"
- **LI Message 2 (Bubble-Up):** "Any thoughts?"
- **LI Message 3 (Make It Real):** "Recorded a 60-second video to give you a sense of what it looks like. Mind giving me the thumbs-up or thumbs-down?"

## Berman Pre-Meeting Follow-Up Sequence

| Timing | Email Type | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | Initial cold email | Full 3-C email |
| +3 days | Quick bump | "Hey {Name}, I'm sure you're busy and wanted to make sure this didn't get buried!" |
| +4-6 days | Two Ideas / Value OR Big News | "Two quick ideas for getting more {result} at {Company}: 1) {Idea}. 2) {Idea}." |
| +3-4 days | Breakup | "At this point I'll assume {their goal} isn't a priority this year. Please feel free to reach out if that changes." |

## Acquisition X 7-Step Advanced Sequence

| Email | Purpose | Content Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| 1 -- Opener | Confirm right person, establish credibility | Introduce yourself, highlight recent success, open-ended question. NO links, NO images. |
| 2 -- Gentle Reminder | Keep conversation going | Short, friendly, restate value proposition, invite questions. Same thread. |
| 3 -- Educational Lead Magnet | Add value through teaching | Video case study or educational content. Builds credibility. |
| 4 -- Value Gift | Demonstrate goodwill | Something prospect usually pays for. Introduce CTA with calendar link (links now OK). |
| 5 -- Quick Follow-Up | Check-in email | Keeps conversation going without heavy content. |
| 6 -- Another Lead Magnet | Show commitment to value | Additional resource + landing page or calendar link. |
| 7 -- Closing Attempt | Prompt decision | Short, direct, aimed at engagement or graceful exit. |

**Key principle:** Add new value, new angle, new proposition in EACH follow-up. Never just say "Did you get my last email?" without adding value.

## Cold Email Wizard 6 Levels of Follow-Up Creativity

### Level 1 -- Gentle Bump (Days 1-3 after initial)
- "Bump :)"
- "{First Name}?"
- "Thoughts?"

### Level 2 -- Re-engagement (Days 4-7)
- "Hey {Name}, happy Monday! What does your week look like? Would love to connect for that intro call. Let's make it happen! :)"
- "Hi {Name}, just bumping this thread to the top of your inbox. Let me know if you'd have time for a quick intro call this week."

### Level 3 -- Offer Value/Content (Days 7-14)
- Share LinkedIn posts, case studies, YouTube videos, portfolio pieces
- "Just wanted to share a quick reel showcasing some of our most recent video work: {link}. Would you be completely opposed to working on some video projects together?"

### Level 4 -- Reverse Psychology / Breakup (Days 14-21)
- "My guess is timing might be off on this, or there's less than zero interest in having a conversation. Let me know either way."
- "You wouldn't be interested in {service}, would you {Name}?"

### Level 5 -- The Spam Check Hack (Days 21-28)
- "Important question for you, {Name}... Have you been receiving my recent emails? Had a few people tell me they were finding them in spam :/ If you could let me know, I'd really appreciate it."
- Creates obligation to respond. Even negative responses reopen the conversation.

### Level 6 -- Holiday/Event Anchoring (Opportunistic)
- "Hi {Name}, hope you enjoyed your Memorial Day weekend! Any availability this week or next to connect?"
- "Just checking in before things get crazy with the holidays :)"

## Follow-Up Subject Lines

### Same Thread (Bumps)
Reply to the original email chain -- no new subject line needed. This keeps the conversation in one thread and creates a sense of ongoing dialogue.

### New Thread (Problem Angle Switch)
When switching to a new problem angle (emails 4-6 in 30MPC), start a fresh email chain with a new subject line drawn from the 30MPC 7 frameworks or Berman top 10.

### Follow-Up Specific Subjects (Cold Email Wizard)
- "{Company Name}" (just their company name)
- "Thoughts?"
- "{First Name}?" (just their name as subject)
- "quick question" (can be reused)

## When to Stop Following Up

### Definitive Stop Signals
- Prospect explicitly says "not interested" and does not engage with reframe
- Prospect asks to be removed from your list
- Email bounces (remove from list immediately)
- Spam complaint

### Continue Following Up
- No response (silence is NOT a "no")
- "Not right now" / "Follow up in X months" (set calendar reminder)
- Positive engagement signals (opens, clicks) with no reply
- Referred to someone else (this is GOLD -- see cold-email-copy objection handling)

### Maximum Follow-Ups by Source
- Berman: 3-4 follow-ups before breakup
- Cold Email Wizard: 5-6+ observed before booking
- 30MPC: 7 emails + 3 LinkedIn messages
- Acquisition X: 7 emails in advanced sequence

**Resolution:** 5-7 total touches per email sequence is the sweet spot. Add multichannel touches (LinkedIn, call, voicemail) on top.

## Berman Post-Sales-Call Sequence

| Timing | Email | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 (within minutes) | Proposal delivery | "Great chat today. Attaching the proposal. Please sign up here: {link}" |
| Next day | Bump | "Wanted to make sure this didn't get buried. Did you get the signup link?" |
| End of week | Commitment check | "I have your word that we're starting today, right? Here's the signup link again." |
| Next week | Question check | "Did you have any more questions on us building the site? Ready to get started!" |
| 2 weeks later | Scarcity | "Just signed another {type} client. Getting close to being booked solid. Should I mark you down as a no?" |
| 1 week later | Humor + call | "Hey {Name}, are you ghosting me? lol" + same day cold call |
| 2 weeks later | Price increase | "We're increasing prices by 50%. The $10K site we discussed would be $15K. I can hold old pricing if you get back today." |

## Cancelled Call / No-Show Recovery (Cold Email Wizard)

### When They No-Show
- "Sorry we missed each other the other day. Are you still interested in connecting to discuss {topic}? If so, feel free to use the link in my previous email to schedule a new time."

### When YOU Missed the Call
- "Sorry we missed each other the other day. I scheduled a new time on your calendar for tomorrow @ {time}. Talk then."
- Always reschedule proactively -- do not let it die

### Critical Rule
Always reschedule. Never delete the calendar invite. A meeting already booked is far more valuable than finding a new prospect.
