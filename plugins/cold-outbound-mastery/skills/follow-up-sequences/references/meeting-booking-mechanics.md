# Meeting Booking Mechanics

## Calendar Link Timing Rules

### Never in Initial Cold Email
- Calendar links hurt deliverability and can land emails in spam
- People have an "ego-tistical grudge against calendar links" (Cold Email Wizard)
- Target click rate of 0% in initial email (Berman)

### OK After Interest
- After prospect says "yes" to a call
- In follow-up reply threads (not initial outreach)
- When prospect specifically asks for easy scheduling
- When working with an assistant

### Manual Scheduling (Higher Booking for High-Ticket)
- Be specific: "I have availability tomorrow from 9:10am EST to 7:10pm EST"
- Offer multiple days: "Tomorrow @ 7am EST or 5:30pm EST, Thursday @ 7-11am EST or 5-530pm EST"
- Always include timezone
- Ask for confirmation: "Just let me know which of these times works best for you."

## Naming the Call (Cold Email Wizard)

Do NOT call it an "intro call" or "sales call." Give it a branded value name:

| Generic Name | Branded Name |
|---|---|
| Quick call | Free Klaviyo Strategy Session |
| Intro call | Branding Strategy Call |
| Discovery call | Free Google Ads Strategy Call |
| Meeting | 20-minute Growth Diagnostic |

The name should communicate value, not sales intent. When the prospect sees the calendar invite, it should feel like they are getting something, not being sold to.

## No-Show Prevention Protocol (30MPC)

### Day-Before Confirmation Email
Send prep research to show goodwill. If booked further out, send one week prior then bubble up again day-before.

Content should demonstrate you have done homework:
- Reference something specific about their company
- Share a relevant insight or data point
- Confirm logistics (time, timezone, Zoom link)

### Day-Of Text (LinkedIn Client Lab / 30MPC)
Send a short text or LinkedIn message the morning of the call:
- "Looking forward to our chat today at {time}!"
- This alone boosts show-up rates by 10%+ (30MPC)

### No-Show Escalation Protocol (30MPC)

| Timing | Action |
|---|---|
| 2 minutes past | Email: "We're on! Putting the Zoom at your fingertips." + Zoom link |
| 5 minutes past | Call their phone |
| 8 minutes past | Call again (only acceptable time to double-dial a prospect) |
| After no-show | NEVER delete the calendar invite. Move it 3-4 days out and send backup times with a touch of guilt. |

**Key principle:** Stay on it -- it is far easier to keep a meeting than to find a new one.

## Rescheduling Rules (Berman)

### Maximum Reschedules
- Allow up to 3 reschedules before applying pressure
- After 3 reschedules, use scarcity/urgency: "Getting close to being booked solid"

### Proactive Rescheduling
- When YOU need to reschedule: "I scheduled a new time on your calendar for tomorrow @ {time}. Talk then."
- When THEY reschedule: Immediately offer 2-3 alternative times
- Never let a rescheduled meeting drift into the void -- pin down a new date immediately

### When They Ghost After Rescheduling
- "Hey {Name}, are you ghosting me? lol" (Berman)
- Cold call them the same day you send this email
- If still no response, wait 1-2 weeks then try price increase urgency

## Post-Meeting Email (Berman)

### Timing: Within Minutes of the Call Ending

**Template:**
```
Great chat today, {Name}.

Attaching the proposal as discussed.

Please sign up here: {link}

Looking forward to getting started!

{Signature}
```

**Key rules:**
- Send within minutes, not hours or days
- Attach the exact proposal discussed
- Include the direct signup/action link
- Keep it short -- they already had the full conversation

## Meeting Duration Guidelines (Berman)

### Initial Meetings
- 10-15 minutes for initial discovery
- Do NOT book 30-60 minute meetings for first conversations
- Short meetings feel lower-risk to the prospect

### Giving Specific Duration (Cold Email Wizard)
- "20-minute intro call" with duration specificity works well
- The specificity signals you respect their time
- "30-minutes, free of charge" for strategy sessions

---

## Speed of Booking Data (Cold Email Wizard)

### Response Speed Matters
- Respond to positive replies AS FAST AS POSSIBLE -- same hour if possible
- Even 18+ days after initial email, prospects can still book (observed in real conversations)
- Some conversations span 3+ weeks before booking
- The faster you respond to interest, the higher the booking rate

### Day/Time Patterns
- Friday afternoon emails rarely get same-day responses -- expect Monday midday replies
- If sending Friday, wait until Tuesday/Wednesday for next follow-up (not Monday morning)
- Morning sends (7-9 AM prospect's timezone) get fastest responses
- Sunday afternoon can work for C-level targets who are planning their week

## Meeting Confirmation Workflow

### After Booking
1. Send calendar invite immediately with Zoom/meeting link
2. Include brief agenda in calendar description
3. Use branded call name in the event title

### 1 Week Before (If Booked Far Out)
- "Hi {Name}, looking forward to our {Branded Call Name} next {day}. In the meantime, I came across {relevant insight}. Thought you'd find it interesting."

### Day Before
- Confirmation email with prep research showing goodwill
- Restate time and timezone
- Include Zoom link again

### Day Of
- Short text/LinkedIn message: "Looking forward to our chat at {time}!"

### 5 Minutes Before
- Be in the Zoom room early
- Have their LinkedIn profile, website, and notes open

## Show-Up Rate Optimization Checklist

- [ ] Day-before confirmation sent with value-add content
- [ ] Day-of text/LinkedIn message sent
- [ ] Calendar invite includes Zoom link and agenda
- [ ] Call has a branded value name (not "intro call")
- [ ] Meeting is 10-15 minutes (low commitment)
- [ ] No-show protocol is ready (2/5/8 minute escalation)
- [ ] Backup times pre-identified in case of reschedule

## Assuming the Close in Follow-Ups (Cold Email Wizard)

After building rapport through several email exchanges:
- "Looking forward to connecting shortly and learning more about your business"
- This assumes they WILL book without explicitly saying so
- Only use after multiple interactions, never in the initial email
- Works especially well combined with providing specific available times
