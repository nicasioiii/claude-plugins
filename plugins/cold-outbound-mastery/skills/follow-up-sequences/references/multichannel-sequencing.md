# Multichannel Sequencing

## The Multichannel Advantage (30MPC Data)

| Approach | Impact on Reply Rate |
|---|---|
| Email only | Baseline |
| Email + phone calls | **2x** reply rate (even if you don't connect live) |
| Email + phone + voicemail | **3.24x** reply rate |
| Triple tap (call + VM + email + social) | **3.1x** contact rate increase |

**Voicemail data (Gong):** Email reply rates double with 1-2 voicemails. After two voicemails, rates decrease. Voicemails have 90%+ open rate (prospects at least click to see transcription).

### Week 1: Problem Angle #1

| Day | Channel | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Tuesday | Email + Call + VM + LinkedIn | Triple tap: Send personalized email, call, leave voicemail pointing to email, send LinkedIn connection request (blank) |

|---|---|---|

### Week 3: Get the Truth

| Day | Channel | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Tuesday | Email + LinkedIn | Get the Truth email + LinkedIn DM #3 (video) |
| Thursday | Email | Final pushaway email |

## The Double Tap Method for Voicemails (30MPC)

### Integration with Email Sequences
Call -> Leave voicemail -> Point to the email in the voicemail (give them the subject line).

The voicemail drives the prospect to read the email, which has the full pitch.

### Voicemail #1: 15 Seconds, Context Only
Lead with the most important piece of context. Save the entire pitch for the email. Keep it under 15 seconds.

### Voicemail #2: 30 Seconds, Context + Social Proof
If context-only did not work, add a touch of social proof. Still leave the pitch for the email. Max 30 seconds.

### Critical Voicemail Rules
- Never ask them to call you back -- direct every ounce of attention to "look for our email"
- Keep it extremely short -- no one listens to a 2-minute voicemail
- Max 2 voicemails per prospect (rates decrease after 2)
- The moment you sell in the voicemail, they delete it

## Two Tiers of Sequences (30MPC)

### High Effort (A-Tier Accounts -- Most Valuable Meetings)

**Characteristics:**
- All manual phone calls
- Personalized emails (trigger-based)
- LinkedIn touches (connection + DMs)
- Full triple/double tap pattern
- Two deeply personalized long emails per sequence
- Case study bumps, video bumps
- 14-28 day sequence duration

**When to use:** Target accounts with strong buying triggers (funded recently, hiring for relevant role, mentioned relevant initiative). These accounts justify the time investment.

### Low Effort (B/C-Tier Accounts)

**Characteristics:**
- Fully automated emails OR automated emails + call people who open
- Pervasive problems instead of personalized triggers
- Same email structure but templated
- Use "Not sure if you're running into this, but..." language
- Shorter sequence (7-14 days)

**When to use:** Accounts in your ICP without specific timing triggers. Volume play with lower conversion but lower effort.

## Sequence by Persona (30MPC)

Different personas need different sequences:

### Above-the-Line (VP/C-Suite)
- Chunk up problems to business impact
- Reference board-level concerns, strategic initiatives
- Use exec leadership quote subject lines
- Longer between touches (these people are busier)
- More emphasis on LinkedIn (execs check LinkedIn)

### Below-the-Line (Manager/Director)
- Focus on day-to-day process pain
- Reference specific workflow problems
- Social proof has higher impact (2.25x at manager level vs 1.5x at director)
- Standard timing between touches
- More emphasis on email + phone

### Trigger-Based Sequences
Build purpose-built sequences for key triggers:
- "Recently funded" sequence
- "Hiring for {role}" sequence
- "Launched new product" sequence
- "Competitor change" sequence

## Follow the Engagement (30MPC)

Use your sales engagement platform to identify:
- People who open your email 5x (call them immediately)
- People who visit your pricing page (call them immediately)
- People who reply with an objection (high priority -- they are engaged)
- People with a new compelling event (trigger-based follow-up)

Activity-based signals = higher pickup and reply rates. Prioritize dials by intent signals, not by list order.

---

## Productivity Hack (30MPC)

Queue up emails to go out during your call block so you can just hit the phones while emails send automatically. This creates natural triple/double taps without extra scheduling effort.

## LinkedIn Integration Points

### Connection Request Timing
- Send with the first email (Day 1)
- Leave the connection request BLANK -- do NOT pitch in it
- The connection request creates familiarity before DMs

### LinkedIn DM Sequence (Parallel to Email)
1. **Context Only:** "Noticed your {trigger} and sent you an email. Did you see my note?"
2. **Bubble-Up:** "Any thoughts?"
3. **Make It Real:** "Recorded a 60-second video. Mind giving me the thumbs-up or thumbs-down?"

### When to Phase Out LinkedIn
- After email 5-6, LinkedIn touches add diminishing returns
- Shift effort to phone in the final sequence block

## Cross-Channel Follow-Up Modalities

### When Email Is Not Working (Low Opens)
- Switch to LinkedIn DMs
- Add phone calls
- Check deliverability (may be infrastructure issue, not copy issue -- see `email-infrastructure`)

### When Phone Is Not Working (Low Connect Rate)
- Prioritize mobile/direct numbers
- Try early morning or after-5pm calls (bypass gatekeepers)
- Leave voicemails pointing to email

### When LinkedIn Is Not Working (Low Acceptance)
- Check profile optimization (see `linkedin-outreach`)
- Try email as primary with LinkedIn as support
- Consider DM outreach on other platforms (see `dm-outreach`)

## Sequence Duration Guidelines

| Sequence Type | Duration | Touches |
|---|---|---|
| High-effort A-tier | 21-28 days | 7 emails + 3-4 calls + 3 LinkedIn |
| Standard B-tier | 14-21 days | 5-7 emails + 2 calls + 2 LinkedIn |
| Low-effort C-tier | 7-14 days | 4-5 automated emails |
| Post-sales-call | 4-6 weeks | 6-7 follow-ups with escalating urgency |

### After Sequence Completion
- Add to 30-day reconnect cadence for cold prospects
- Mark warm prospects (opened, clicked, partial reply) for future re-engagement
- Remove bounced/unsubscribed immediately
