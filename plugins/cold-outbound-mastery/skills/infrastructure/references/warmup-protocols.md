# Email Warmup Protocols & Volume Ramp Strategies

Complete guide to warming up new inboxes and ramping sending volume safely.

---

## Why Email Warmup Is Mandatory

**The Problem:**
- New inboxes with 0 sending history + 500 cold emails on day 1 = spam folder
- Email providers see: "This account is brand new and immediately blasting emails"
- Provider response: Route to spam as protective measure
- Result: All your work wasted, domain reputation damaged

**The Solution:**
- Gradual sending with organic-looking replies
- Makes providers think: "This is a real person building legitimate conversations"
- Builds sender reputation from day 1
- Enables higher cold email volume after warmup period

**Timeline:**
- Minimum warmup: 2-3 weeks before campaigns
- After warmup: Can send 500+ cold emails with good inbox placement
- Without warmup: 500 emails = spam folder

## WarmupInbox Setup Steps

### Step 1: Create Account (5 minutes)
1. Go to coldemailuniversity.com/warmup or warmupinbox.com
2. Sign up with your new email (firstname@yourdomain.com)
3. Verify email address

### Step 2: Enable Gmail Security Settings (10 minutes)
These settings are CRITICAL—without them, warmup tool can't connect.

**In Gmail:**
1. Go to myaccount.google.com
2. Click "Security" (left sidebar)
3. Enable: "Allow less secure apps" → Turn ON
4. Enable: "Allow per-user outbound gateways" → Turn ON
5. Enable: "IMAP" in Gmail Settings
   - Go to Gmail Settings → Forwarding & POP/IMAP
   - Select: "Enable IMAP"
   - Save

**Critical:** WAIT 1 HOUR after enabling IMAP before proceeding to next step. This sync delay is essential for warmup tool to function properly.

### Step 3: Connect WarmupInbox to Gmail (20 minutes)
1. Return to WarmupInbox dashboard
2. Click "Connect Gmail Account" or "Add Inbox"
3. Follow OAuth flow (you'll be taken to Google)
4. Authorize WarmupInbox to access your Gmail
5. Grant permission for: "Send emails as you", "Read your emails"
6. Return to WarmupInbox
7. Should show: "Success: First email on this inbox will be sent"

**If it fails:**
- Verify IMAP is enabled (go back to Gmail Settings)
- Check 1 hour has passed since enabling IMAP
- Try disconnecting and reconnecting

### Step 4: Configure Warmup Schedule (Paid Account) (10 minutes)
1. Go to WarmupInbox dashboard
2. Click "Settings" or "Schedule"
3. Configure warmup parameters:

**Initial Setup:**
- Starting emails per day: 15 (day 1)
- Daily increase: 5-10 emails per day
- Maximum: 30-40 emails/day total

**Advanced Settings (if available):**
- Send time: Distribute throughout business hours (9 AM - 5 PM)
- Warmup type: Natural (replies, opens, reads)
- Maintenance mode: Keep running during campaigns (recommended)

### Step 5: Monitor Dashboard (Daily, 5 minutes)
1. Log in to WarmupInbox daily
2. Monitor:
   - Emails sent (should increase each day)
   - Warmup status (green = healthy)
   - Spam complaints (should be 0)
   - Ready-to-send status (appears after 2-3 weeks)

## Email Warmup Best Practices

### Do's
✓ Keep warmup running during your cold email campaigns (maintenance)
✓ Monitor dashboard daily for issues
✓ Allow full 2-3 weeks before campaigns (don't rush)
✓ Use downtime during warmup to build lead lists, write copy, refine templates
✓ Start with paid account ($9/month) for faster ramp and better control
✓ Maintain warmup: 15-20 emails/day even when running campaigns

### Don'ts
✗ Don't skip warmup to send cold emails faster (will destroy reputation)
✗ Don't disable IMAP or less-secure apps (warmup won't connect)
✗ Don't add multiple domains to single warmup account (separate accounts per domain)
✗ Don't exceed 30-40 emails/day combined warmup + campaigns (too aggressive)
✗ Don't stop warmup once campaigns start (kill the momentum)
✗ Don't mix warmup with spam (send legitimate, valuable emails always)

## Cold Email Volume Ramp Formula

**Use this when:**
- Starting new domain
- Starting new sending IP
- Recovering from deliverability damage
- Launching new campaigns after warmup period

**Principle:** Gradually increase volume to build reputation without triggering spam filters

|-----|--------|-------|

| 2 | 75-125 emails | Engaged segment |
| 3 | 100-150 emails | Engaged segment |
| 4 | 125-175 emails | Engaged segment |
| 5 | 150-200 emails | Engaged segment |
| 6 | 175-225 emails | Engaged segment |

**Cumulative by day 7:** ~1,000 emails total

#### Week 2: Moderate Increase (Days 8-14)

| 11 | 325-375 emails | Warm segment |

**Cumulative days 8-14:** ~2,400 emails
**Total by day 14:** ~3,400 emails

#### Week 3: Full Ramp (Days 15-21)

| Day | Volume | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| 15 | 450-500 emails | All segments |
| 16 | 475-525 emails | All segments |
| 17 | 500-550 emails | All segments |
| 18 | 525-575 emails | All segments |
| 19 | 550-600 emails | All segments |
| 20 | 575-625 emails | All segments |
| 21 | 600-700 emails | All segments |

**Cumulative days 15-21:** ~3,800 emails
**Total by day 21:** ~7,200 emails

#### Week 4+: Steady State (Days 22-30+)

| Days | Volume | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| 22-30 | 700-1000 emails/day | Can scale further if metrics healthy |
| 31+ | 1000+ emails/day | Full scaling mode |

**Cumulative days 22-30:** ~7,500 emails
**Total by day 30:** ~14,700 emails

### Volume Ramp Rules

**Mandatory conditions:**
1. Only send to truly engaged segments (recent activity, high open history)
2. Never skip this ramp for new domains—damages reputation permanently
3. Monitor bounce rate daily (must stay <2%)
4. Monitor spam complaints daily (must stay <0.1%)
5. Stop volume increase if bounce rate exceeds 2%

**If bounce rate exceeds 2%:**
- Pause volume increase immediately
- Run list through NeverBounce (identify bad addresses)
- Remove invalid/risky addresses
- Resume ramp once bounce rate returns to <2%

**If spam complaints spike:**
- Pause volume increase
- Review email content (too salesy? too many links?)
- Segment to more engaged only
- Resume once complaints back to normal

**If getting blacklisted:**
- Stop all sends immediately
- Request delisting (takes 24-48 hours typically)
- Investigate root cause (list quality? content? volume?)
- Don't resume until blacklist removal verified

## Sending Schedule Best Practices

### Optimal Send Times
- **Peak:** 9 AM - 12 PM (morning work hours)
- **Good:** 1 PM - 3 PM (post-lunch check)
- **Acceptable:** 3 PM - 5 PM (end of day)
- **Avoid:** 5 PM - 9 AM (off-hours)
- **Avoid:** Weekends (unless B2C)
- **Avoid:** Holidays (check calendar)

### Distribution Strategy
- **Spread sends** throughout business hours (don't send 500 at 9:00 AM sharp)
- **Intervals:** Send 10-20 emails every 30 minutes (spreads inbox delivery)
- **Timezone consideration:** Send during recipient's business hours (use email tool's timezone feature)

### Time Zone Handling
- **If B2B USA:** Send 9 AM - 5 PM EST (1 hour later for each zone moving west)
- **If B2B Global:** Spread sends across time zones (9 AM sender's time through 5 PM sender's time will hit various zones)
- **Email tools with timezone:** MailShake, LemList (automatically adjust by recipient timezone)

## Common Warmup Problems & Solutions

### Problem: Warmup Tool Won't Connect

**Causes:**
- IMAP not enabled in Gmail
- Less secure apps not allowed
- Didn't wait 1 hour after enabling IMAP
- Incorrect credentials

**Solution:**
1. Go to Gmail Settings → Forwarding & POP/IMAP
2. Verify IMAP is enabled
3. Go to myaccount.google.com → Security
4. Verify "Allow less secure apps" is ON
5. Wait 5 minutes
6. Try reconnecting warmup tool
7. If still fails: Revoke WarmupInbox access and reconnect from scratch

### Problem: Warmup Tool Connected But No Emails Sending

**Causes:**
- IMAP sync delay
- Free account (paid features)
- Warmup pool empty

**Solution:**
1. Wait 24 hours (sometimes takes time for warmup to populate inboxes)
2. Upgrade to paid account ($9/month) if on free
3. Check dashboard for error messages
4. Try disabling/enabling in settings

### Problem: Warmup Emails Bouncing

**Causes:**
- Gmail account not fully verified
- Warmup inboxes no longer active
- Issue with warmup tool

**Solution:**
1. Verify Gmail is receiving normal emails (check your inbox)
2. Check if you're getting other emails
3. Contact WarmupInbox support if persists

### Problem: Warmup Shows Ready But Cold Emails Still Go to Spam

**Causes:**
- Wrong email authentication (SPF/DKIM/DMARC)
- Email content is spammy
- List quality is poor
- Still warming up despite "ready" status (need 2-3 consistent weeks)

**Solution:**
1. Verify SPF, DKIM, DMARC with MX Toolbox
2. Run email through Mail-Tester (should be 9+/10)
3. Clean list with NeverBounce
4. Wait additional week if just completed 2-week warmup
5. Reduce first batch to 50 emails, test placement
6. If placement good at 50, ramp to 100, then 200, etc.
