# LinkedIn Outreach

## Source Synthesis

This skill synthesizes three sources:
- **LinkedIn Client Lab (Ty Frankel)** -- Primary source. Complete organic LinkedIn system: profile optimization, 6 warmth levels, multi-thread DM framework, macro/micro strategies, voice messages, follow-up system, content strategy. Generated $3.7M+ via organic LinkedIn outreach.
- **LinkedInbox (Alex Berman)** -- Supplementary. Connect-then-pitch framework, message patterns, follow-up escalation. More template-based, higher-volume approach.
- **Cold Email Wizard** -- LinkedIn-to-email bridge strategy, referral leverage from LinkedIn conversations.

**Where sources disagree:**
- **Connection request notes:** Ty Frankel says ALWAYS send empty requests (no note). Berman includes brief notes. **Resolution:** Default to empty requests -- they consistently outperform noted ones per Frankel's data. Exception: If you have highly specific context (mutual connection, recent event), a short note can work per Berman's patterns.
- **DM style:** Berman uses template-based, direct pitches after connecting. Frankel uses handwritten multi-thread conversations with psychology-first approach. **Resolution:** Frankel's approach for companies 1-50 employees and high-ticket ($5K+). Berman's approach acceptable for high-volume, lower-ticket or when scaling with templates.
- **Automation:** Berman recommends Zopto for automation. Frankel says automation ONLY for connection requests (Dripify), NEVER for DMs. **Resolution:** Never automate DMs. Automation for connection requests is optional but manual gets 10-15% higher acceptance rates.

## The 3-Prong LinkedIn System

LinkedIn outbound requires three pillars working together:

1. **Profile** (one-time setup) -- acts as your landing page
2. **Content** (ongoing, 2-5 posts/week) -- builds credibility and creates warm prospects
3. **Outbound DMs** (daily, ~1 hour) -- the revenue driver

Profile + content alone = 5-15 calls/month. Add outbound DMs = 25-40+ calls/month.

## The 6 Levels of Prospect Warmth

Every prospect falls into a warmth level. Match your approach accordingly:

1. **Neutral (70-95%)** -- Connected but no engagement. Use full multi-thread DM framework. Target: 5-10% call book.
2. **Profile Viewers (5-15%)** -- Viewed your profile. Reach out ASAP. Target: 10-20% call book.
3. **Content Engagers (5-10%)** -- Liked/commented on your post. Use their engagement as conversation starter. Target: 15-25% call book.
4. **Lead Magnet Engagers (5-30%)** -- Commented to get a resource. Send resource, then ask pain-priming questions. Target: 10-30% call book.
5. **Inbound Leads (1-10%)** -- Messaged you first. Hard to mess up. ~50% will be unqualified even with premium positioning.
6. **Referrals/Warm Intros** -- Warmest. More direct approach, less personalization needed upfront.

## Initial DM Framework (Multi-Thread)

Every initial outreach follows this structure, sent as 3-6 separate messages:

1. **Greeting** -- "Hey [First name]." (separate message, no exclamation, no "Hello sir")
2. **Restrained compliment** -- 4-6/10 enthusiasm. NOT "I LOVE your case study!" YES: "Pretty solid case study with the gummy bears brand -- I like it."
3. **Rephrase their offer** -- Restate what they do as a question. Shows research, pries pain naturally.
4. **Business question** -- Mandatory in every initial DM regardless of warmth level. Pry pain points.
5. **Personal connection** -- Separate thread from business. Multi-threading is essential.
6. **Answer WIIFM** -- Give them a reason to engage. Name-drop relevant contacts, mention friends in their space.

**Critical rule:** Every single message is handwritten. No copy-paste scripts. Copy the psychology and energy, not exact words.

## Contrarian Principles (LinkedIn Client Lab)

**Outbound beats inbound 80-90% of the time for qualified leads.** Ty Frankel's data shows that 80-90% of his highest-qualified calls come from outbound DMs, not inbound leads. Inbound leads (people who message you first) are ~50% unqualified even with premium positioning. Outbound lets you choose exactly who you talk to, pre-qualify before the conversation, and control the frame from the start. Do not over-invest in inbound strategies at the expense of daily outbound activity.

**Commenting on LinkedIn is overrated for client acquisition.** While many LinkedIn gurus push a "comment on 30 posts per day" strategy, Frankel's system prioritizes direct DMs and connection requests over commenting. Commenting builds general visibility but has an extremely low conversion rate to booked calls. The ROI of 1 hour spent on targeted DMs (25-40+ calls/month) far exceeds 1 hour spent commenting (near-zero direct attribution to calls). Use commenting sparingly -- to engage with warm prospects who already know you, not as a primary acquisition channel.

## Three Macro Strategies That Matter Most

### 1. The Friendly Helper Mindset
The single most important DM mindset. Help at all costs; completely detach from making the sale. Prospect's well-being comes first. NOT giving free services -- find high-leverage ways to convey genuine care: endorse skills, like posts, send free resources, foreshadow intros, give authority-building observations.

### 2. Multi-Thread Conversations
Run 2 conversations simultaneously: 1 personal, 1 business. Over time the personal thread naturally drops as warmth builds. LinkedIn is a social selling platform -- multi-threading is essential.

### 3. Frame Control
Frame = how you view reality. Every conversation is a frame battle. Control it by asking questions, using humor, staying non-reactive, and enforcing boundaries with love. Not angry control -- loving, grounded control.

## Key Micro Strategies

- **Contractions always** -- "you're" not "you are." No contractions = robotic.
- **Vary line lengths** -- Short, medium, long, medium, short. Looks off-the-cuff.
- **Conscious typo** -- Occasionally typo a plausible word, then correct it. Shows you're real.
- **Non-needy call pitches** -- "I might be down to...", "Could maybe find a pocket of time..."
- **SURE language** -- "This will help you" not "This might help you maybe."
- **Question attacks** -- 2-4 questions at once. Shows genuine curiosity.
- **"Wait so..."** -- Opens questions spontaneously.
- **Never DM green rings** -- "Open to Work" = unqualified 99/100 times.
- **Never edit or delete DMs** -- Shows "edited" tag, looks contrived.

## Voice Messages

Voice messages are a force multiplier. Ty books 1 call every 3-5 voice messages sent.

**When to send:** NEVER as first contact. After initial 2-6 DM series with no reply, mid-conversation to escalate, to save a dying conversation.

**What to convey:** Outcome independence, low excitement, friendly helper mindset, positivity, DO NOT read from a script.

**Structure:** Greeting + acknowledgment of their work + social proof (friends in their space) + specific offer/value + casual time suggestion.

## LinkedIn-to-Email Bridge (Cold Email Wizard)

When LinkedIn conversation stalls or you get a referral:
- Subject: "Re: LinkedIn"
- Reference the LinkedIn conversation in the email body
- Attach screenshot of LinkedIn messages as proof of prior contact
- "Figured I'd send a quick email in case you aren't as active on LinkedIn as I am"
- When prospect says "reach out to [person] instead" -- that is your golden referral opportunity
