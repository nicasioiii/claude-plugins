# LinkedIn DM Frameworks

## The Multi-Thread DM Framework (Ty Frankel)

Every initial outreach uses this structure. Send as 3-6 separate messages, NOT one wall of text.

### Step 1: Greeting (separate message)
"Hey [First name]."
- No exclamation points
- No "Hello sir", no "Hey Zach!"
- No "Hope you're doing well!" or "Hope this finds you well!"
- Period, not exclamation mark

### Step 2: Restrained Compliment (4-6/10 enthusiasm)

**Bad (7-10/10 enthusiasm):**
- "I ABSOLUTELY LOVE your case study with the gummy bears e-com brand!"
- "Your content is AMAZING! I've learned so much from it!"

**Good (4-6/10 enthusiasm):**
- "Pretty solid case study with the gummy bears e-com brand -- I like it."
- "Dig the LinkedIn content, solid stuff in there."
- "Nice work with [specific thing]. Cool approach."

Restrained enthusiasm = expert who has been there before, outcome independent. Over-enthusiasm = desperate, just starting out, no value to offer.

### Step 3: Rephrase Their Offer as a Question

Restate what they do as a question. Shows research; pries pain in a genuine, non-threatening way. Used in 20-40% of initial DMs.

Example: "NBM Digital is your thing, yeah? You're helping web dev agencies sign clients?"

### Step 4: Business Question (Mandatory)

Mandatory in every initial DM regardless of warmth level. Pry pain points.

- "How's that going for you?"
- "What's been working best for client acquisition?"
- "Are you doing mostly inbound or outbound?"
- "What's the main bottleneck right now?"

### Step 5: Personal Connection (Separate Thread)

Start a personal thread that runs alongside business thread:
- "You guys do mostly Google ads for what kinds of companies? I always wanted to visit Utrecht by the way. Saw some 4K drone videos of the mountains over there. So beautiful."
- Reference something from their profile, location, interests, photos

### Step 6: Answer WIIFM ("What's In It For Me")

Give them a reason to engage. Especially critical for Level 1 (neutral) prospects.

**Don't answer directly:** "I've helped people like you make millions"
**Do answer genuinely:** Mention friends killing it in their space, name-drop relevant people.

Example: "Solid niche. Quite a few friends of mine are killing it in the leadgen space." + name specific person.

## Macro DM Strategies

### The Friendly Helper Mindset
The single most important DM mindset. Makes or breaks outreach.
- Help at all costs; completely detach from "making the sale"
- Prospect's well-being comes first
- NOT giving free services -- high-leverage ways to convey genuine care:
  - Endorse skills, like posts, send free resources
  - Foreshadow intros to relevant people
  - Give authority-building observations
  - "Got quite a few friends" -- social proof through association
  - Send sales assets (lead magnets, free training videos, PDFs, book recommendations)

### Three Communication Modes (Only Use #3)
1. **Me, me, me** -- Ego-centric, selfish. How most DM outreach is done. AVOID.
2. **Horse trading** -- "I'll do you a favor, you do me a favor." Transactional. AVOID.
3. **Your needs are my needs** -- Genuine care, well-being first. THE way to communicate.

### Multi-Thread Conversations
Have 2 conversations simultaneously: 1 personal, 1 business. Over time personal thread naturally drops, warmth is built, transitions to business only. LinkedIn is a social selling platform -- multi-threading is essential.

### Conveying Spontaneity
Anything showing you're typing off the dome, not using automation:
- "Was actually just thinking..."
- "Actually it's funny..."
- "Hmmm"
- "Sitting here on your site right now"
- "Huh interesting"
- "I was actually wondering if this'd make sense for you..."

Without spontaneity: look formulaic, fake, contrived = the arch nemesis of booking calls.

### Frame Control
- Frame = how you view reality; every conversation is a "frame battle"
- Control it by asking questions, using humor, being non-reactive, enforcing boundaries with love
- When prospect goes off-topic: mirror/reflect what they say, then redirect back to your agenda
- Not angry control -- loving, grounded control

### Restrained Compliments
Everyone knows genuine compliments work, but 95% do it wrong with excessive enthusiasm.

### Specificity
Specificity = authority and authenticity. Must cascade across ALL DM conversations.
- Generic advice/observations = looks like everyone else
- Specific niche knowledge, specific numbers, specific names = authority

### Playing Devil's Advocate
Sound skeptical -- don't be 100% sold on prospect's business.
- "Why do that?"
- "Interesting. Huh."
- "I have a completely different approach actually"
- Balance: mix warmth/connection with skepticism

### SURE Language
Complete conviction in everything you say:
- NOT: "This might be able to help you maybe"
- YES: "This will help you" / "Check this out"
- Don't ask: "Is it okay if I send you this?"
- Do say: "Check this out" and send it

### Throwing Prospects Rope
Give prospects a reason (the bigger the better) to reply:
- Foreshadowing valuable/relevant intros
- Giving away relevant value (course-level quality)
- Building RELEVANT social proof
- Making authority-building observations
- Sending sales assets (lead magnets, free training videos, PDFs)

## Micro DM Strategies

### Contractions Always
"you're" not "you are", "I'm" not "I am", "it's" not "it is." Without contractions = robotic and automated-sounding.

### Differentiate Line Lengths
Don't send all long paragraphs or all short lines. Flow: short, medium, long, medium, short, long. Looks off-the-cuff and genuine.

### The Conscious Typo
Make a typo ON PURPOSE, then correct it. Shows you're real, not automated. Typo words that are plausibly misspelled (e.g., "SAS" -> "SaaS*"). Use every now and then.

### Foreshadow Intro Before Business Questions
Before asking business questions, foreshadow a potential introduction. Frames you as friendly helper; prospect is more responsive to questions after.

### Non-Needy Call Pitches
- "I'm willing to..."
- "I might be down to..."
- "Could maybe find a pocket of time..."
- "I should be able to..."
- "I should have some time..."

### Be Unsure of Time Availability
"Hmm, when do I actually have time... I might have time here..." Positions you as busy with high-value work.

### "If I Can Help" vs. "How I Can Help"
Always "Let's see IF and how I can help you" not "Let me show you HOW I can help." "If" = qualifying them. "How" = already decided you want to work with them = needy.

### The Question Attack
Ask 2-4 questions at once. Shows genuine curiosity; gives prospect multiple chances to respond. Stack questions with authority-building observations.

### "Wait so..."
Opens questions spontaneously. Conveys genuine thinking and processing.

### WDYM (What Do You Mean) Phrases
Drop a proprietary term that prompts "What do you mean?" When they ask, you explain = paints you as authority. Example: "backend upside" -- when prospect asks, you demonstrate offer structuring expertise.

### Mention Relevant Big Names
When prospect is in a niche where you know big names, always mention them. Successful friends' traits rub off on you in prospect's perception.

### "Please Keep This Between Us"
When sending a resource: "Please keep that between us." Creates exclusivity and intimacy.

### The Breakup Message
After 3-4 follow-ups with no response:
- "Have you given up on [problem you solve]?"
- "Should I close this out for us?"
- "Are we moving ahead or closing it out?"
Great at reviving dead prospects. Now-or-never creates urgency.

## Voice Message Framework

### When to Send
- NEVER as first contact -- need initial 2-6 DM series first
- After initial DMs with no reply (follow-up)
- After they replied but then stopped responding
- Mid-conversation to escalate
- To save a dying conversation
- To take prospect from 2-6/10 interest to 7-9/10

### Sub-Communication (What to Convey)
1. **Outcome independence** -- "If you book, cool. If not, cool." Need 25-30+ calls/month lead flow to genuinely access this energy.
2. **Low excitement** -- Grounded, restrained enthusiasm. Not "Hey John! It's nice to meet you! Your offer is AMAZING!"
3. **Friendly helper mindset** -- Foreshadow intros, offer free value in the voice message itself.
4. **Positivity and love** -- Lead with warmth and courage. "Hey, what's up brother? Pleasure to meet you."
5. **Don't read from a script** -- Close your eyes and talk naturally. Free-flowing > perfectly worded but stilted.

### Voice Message Structure
"Hey [Name], pleasure. Good talking to you so far. I think it's really cool what you're doing with [company]. Actually, I know some killers in this space doing really well. I've worked with 3-4 companies like yours and helped them go from [X] to [Y] with [service]. I'm down to show you exactly how I did that. I should be free early next week sometime."

Optional value add: "Oh by the way, my friend [Name] is amazing at [relevant skill]. I noticed you [relevant detail]. I'd be down to make the intro if that could help."

## Berman Message Patterns (Connect-Then-Pitch)

For higher-volume, template-based approach:

**Pattern A: Direct After Connect**
"Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I'd like to run a couple of ideas by you that could help you increase sales and brand awareness at [Company]. We specialize in [service]. Thought it might be worth a chat, what do you think?"

**Pattern B: Reconnect**
"We've been connected for a while but I just realized we never actually got to speak. Would love to take you out for coffee and see what we're both up to."

**Pattern C: Referral Frame**
"One of my colleagues noticed the great work you're doing and asked me to make contact. He'd love to speak with you. When would you have 20 minutes?"

**Pattern D: Interest-Based**
"Thanks for accepting my connection request. I see you have a keen interest in [topic]. A trait I appreciate. Would love to have a call sometime."

**Pattern E: Credential/Specificity**
"Looked over your LinkedIn profile and was impressed by your specialties in [industry]. The [specific feature] part was particularly interesting. Would love to connect and talk more."

## Objection Handling in DMs

### "Are You Trying to Sell Me?"
Stay grounded. Don't get fearful or prideful. "Not trying to sell you. Just want to see if I can help, genuinely." Then non-needy pitch: "Yeah, sure, I do both. If I can help in an official capacity, cool. If not, just want to get a deep dive on what you're doing."

### When Prospect Calls You Out on Outreach
Don't answer "why I reached out" right away. Foreshadow value FIRST (controls frame). Then get into it in very non-needy, casual, spontaneous tonality.

### When Someone Declines a Call
Don't keep pushing. Leave with genuine value-first message: "Let me know if there's any way I can help." Follow up in 1-2 weeks.

### "Not Looking for Coaching/Services"
- "100%" (separate message)
- "Yeah I'm down for just a chat where I get under the hood of what you do & find blind spots"
- "No strings or anything"
- Confidently drop calendar link: "Feel free to book with that. Let me know once you did it"
- With cold outbound: frame call as value/audit, NOT "potentially working together"
- With hot inbound: DO frame as potentially working together

### When Someone Agrees But Books Far Out
1. "Here let's do this"
2. "Book this with me" (send link)
3. "I'll adjust it to the same time next week for us"
4. "Let me know once you did it and I'll adjust"

### Using WDYM as Objection Handler
Drop proprietary phrase -> prospect asks what you mean -> explain with authority -> give advice -> smoothly transition to call pitch.

## Multiple Touchpoints (Before/During Initial DM)

Before sending your first DM, warm the prospect:
- Follow their company page
- Endorse their top skills
- Like and comment on their posts
- Comment twice on one post (looks more human)
- Mention them in comments on other posts

## Energy Matching Rule

Mirror prospect's energy and take it up a small notch.
- Prospect at 3/10? Don't reply at 9/10.
- Prospect says "Yup." -> Reply: "Yeah that makes sense... 100%. Have you tried Smartlead? Crazy how fast..."
- NOT: Prospect says "Yup." -> "Wow that's amazing!!! Instantly is the BEST software ever :)"
