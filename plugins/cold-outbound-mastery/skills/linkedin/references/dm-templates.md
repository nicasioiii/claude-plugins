# LinkedIn DM Templates & Conversation Scripts

Framework-based DM sequences for different prospect types and industries. All messages are EXAMPLES—never copy/paste. Use these as templates to build your own unique messages around the framework.

---

## Core DM Framework Structure

Every LinkedIn DM sequence follows this pattern:

1. **Initial Message** (Connection request or first DM)
   - Short, simple, no exclamation marks
   - One genuine observation or compliment
   - No ask, no pitch

2. **Follow-up #1** (Next day or 2-3 days later)
   - Value-first approach (advice, reference, intro offer)
   - Still no hard pitch
   - Build on previous message

3. **Follow-up #2** (3-5 days later)
   - Soft pitch with value proposition
   - Reference similar client win or result
   - Non-needy CTA (make it optional)

4. **Follow-up #3** (5-7 days later)
   - Light re-engagement
   - New angle or different value prop
   - Final attempt before moving on

---

## DM Micro-Tactics

**Essential Rules**:
- No copy-paste across conversations
- Every message is handwritten (unique per prospect)
- Use contractions ("you're," "it's," not "you are," "it is")
- No excessive exclamation marks (max 1-2 per message)
- "Hmm," "Huh," "By the way..." convey spontaneity
- Mirror their energy and tone slightly
- Don't use green dot indicators (they're working; worse timing)
- Never edit or delete sent messages (looks desperate)
- Use "if I can help" language (non-pushy)
- Say "please keep this between us" to build rapport
- Foreshadow introductions before pitching

**Spontaneity Phrases**:
- "Hmm, that's interesting..."
- "Huh, gotcha. Well why would you...?"
- "Oh, I just thought of someone to introduce you to..."
- "By the way, just thought of something..."
- "Yeah that makes sense... 100%"
- "Okay I think that makes sense. I'm trying to think of..."

These sound genuine and engaged rather than robotic.

## Template 1: Service-Based Cold Outreach (Agencies, Coaches, Consultants)

### Connection Request Message
```
Hey [First Name],

I came across your profile and loved what I saw. I work with [niche] to [specific outcome]. Thought it'd be worth connecting.

Thanks
```

OR (Research angle):
```
Hey [First Name],

Your work at [company/focus] is solid. I specialize in [your offer] and think we might have some synergies. Worth connecting?
```

### Follow-up #1 (Next day)
```
Thanks for connecting, [First Name].

I was going through your profile and you've clearly built something impressive. Quick question—are you handling your own client acquisition right now, or do you have a team?
```

**Why this works**: Not asking about their clients or revenue (too obvious). Asking about team shows you understand their business structure. Gives them an easy conversation starter.

### Follow-up #2 (3-5 days later)
```
[First Name], random but I just worked with someone in a super similar position to you. They went from booking 5-10 calls/month to hitting 30+ calls/month in about 90 days.

Their biggest blocker was their [specific problem related to their profile].

Not sure if this applies to you, but thought it might resonate. Want to hop on a quick 20-min call to see if I can help?
```

**Why this works**:
- References a similar client win (Results Database)
- Specific problem mentioned (makes it relevant)
- Soft pitch ("want to hop on...?")
- Time-boxed (20 min, not vague)
- Non-needy (optional feel)

### Follow-up #3 (5-7 days later)
```
Hey [First Name], I know you're probably swamped.

Honestly, I just thought of someone from your industry I should introduce you to. Would be a good conversation anyway. Let me know if you're open to it, no pressure.
```

**Why this works**:
- Acknowledges they're busy (empathy)
- Different value prop (introduction, not service pitch)
- Non-pushy CTA
- Sets up for meeting in non-sales context

## Template 3: High-Ticket/Complex Sale

### Connection Request
```
Hey [First Name],

I came across your background in [specific area] and was impressed by [specific accomplishment/company/initiative]. Thought it'd be worth connecting.
```

### Follow-up #1 (Day 2-3)
```
Thanks for connecting, [First Name].

I've been doing some work in the [their niche] space and noticed something interesting about how most [their role] approach [specific challenge].

Do you deal with [specific problem]?
```

**Why this works**: Asks a question that qualifies them. If they answer, you have engagement.

### Follow-up #2 (3-5 days)
```
I actually just completed a case study with a company similar to yours that shows how [specific transformation].

The biggest thing that surprised them was [counterintuitive finding].

Curious if this resonates with what you're seeing on your end?

Here's the case study if you want to check it out: [link]
```

**Why this works**:
- Provides value first (case study)
- Counterintuitive finding grabs attention
- Asks engagement question
- Case study = social proof without hard pitch

### Follow-up #3 (5-7 days)
```
[First Name], just wanted to follow up one more time.

I genuinely think there's potential here based on what you're building. Would love to have a conversation on this, but totally understand if timing isn't right now.

Whenever you're ready, just let me know.
```

## Template 6: Objection Handling & Follow-ups

### "Not interested / Can't help"
```
Hey [First Name], totally get it.

Honestly, you might not even need what I do. But if something changes or you think of someone I could help, just let me know.

No hard feelings.
```

### "Busy right now"
```
Totally understand. No rush.

Whenever you have bandwidth, whether that's in a month or in 3 months, feel free to reach out. I'll be here.
```

### "Need to think about it"
```
Cool. Take your time.

I'm genuinely confident we could help, but I also don't want to rush you. Whenever you're ready, let's talk. No pressure.
```

### "Let me get back to you"
```
No problem. I'll check back in with you next [week/month].

In the meantime, if anything comes up, just send me a message.
```

### Silent non-response
```
Hey [First Name], I know I'm probably getting lost in your inbox.

Not upset at all—I get it. Just wanted to leave this here in case you ever want to chat. My door's always open.

Take care.
```

## Template 7: Multi-Thread Conversation (Advanced)

Once someone responds positively, open multiple conversation threads to prevent the conversation from feeling like a linear pitch.

### Thread 1: Advice/Value
```
[First Name], I was just thinking about what you said about [their challenge].

Have you considered [specific advice]? I worked with someone who had a similar problem and this approach changed everything for them.
```

### Thread 2: Introduction Offer
```
By the way, I just thought of someone I should introduce you to. They're in [specific area] and have seen great results with [relevant outcome].

Want me to make that intro?
```

### Thread 3: Social Proof/Case Study
```
I just wanted to share a case study with you that shows how someone in your position handled [similar situation].

Case study link: [link]

This is what you might be able to achieve.
```

### Thread 4: The Ask (Final message in thread)
```
Okay so based on all this—I think it makes sense for us to hop on a call.

I can help you [specific outcome], and I genuinely think you'd be a fit for what we do.

What does your week look like? I can do Tuesday or Wednesday afternoon.
```

```
It took about 90 days and the biggest shift was fixing his [specific problem].

I think the same approach could work for your business. Want to jump on a quick call to explore?
```

## Key Principles

1. **Never Copy-Paste**: Every message should feel unique. Take these frameworks and build your own.

2. **Spontaneity is Key**: Phrases like "Hmm," "By the way," "Just thought of..." signal you're naturally thinking about their problem, not running a script.

3. **Value First**: Give before asking. Reference, advice, introduction, case study—something of value before CTA.

4. **Multi-Thread**: Don't let conversation be one linear pitch. Multiple threads of value build stronger relationships.

5. **Soft CTAs**: "Want to chat?" "Would you be open to a conversation?" "Let's see if this applies to your situation?" These feel less pushy than "Let's book a call."

6. **Energy Matters**: Your emotional state comes through in your writing. Write from genuine care, not desperation.

7. **Timing**: Space messages 2-3 days apart. Follow up 3-5 times if no response before moving on.

8. **Respect Silence**: If someone doesn't respond after 3-4 touches, move on. Many will come back later when they're ready.

## Industries & Specific Angles

### Agencies
- Focus on: Client acquisition, revenue growth, hiring bottlenecks
- Angle: "I help agencies scale from $X to $Y"
- Results Database: Other agencies' revenue progression

### Coaches/Consultants
- Focus on: Selling more of existing offer, client quality, automation
- Angle: "I help coaches book more premium clients"
- Results Database: Other coaches' client counts/rates

### SaaS
- Focus on: Customer acquisition, retention, product-market fit
- Angle: "I help SaaS teams scale their CAC efficiency"
- Results Database: User growth, retention improvements

### Service Providers (Designers, Developers, Marketers)
- Focus on: Client quality, project pricing, recurring revenue
- Angle: "I help [service] providers build retainer-based businesses"
- Results Database: Service providers' revenue transformation

### E-commerce
- Focus on: AOV, repeat purchase rate, product launches
- Angle: "I help e-com brands scale revenue per customer"
- Results Database: Store revenue growth, customer lifetime value

### B2B Founders
- Focus on: Sales pipeline, enterprise deals, market expansion
- Angle: "I help founders close larger deals faster"
- Results Database: Deal sizes, sales cycle compression
