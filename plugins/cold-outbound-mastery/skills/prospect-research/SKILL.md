## WHAT THIS SKILL DOES

This skill is the bridge between knowing WHO to contact (list-building) and knowing WHAT to say (channel-specific writing skills). It answers the question: "What research do I need, and how do I turn that research into messaging angles?"

Every outreach channel benefits from this skill. A cold email, cold call, LinkedIn DM, and Instagram DM all start with the same research foundation -- the messaging matrix tells you what problems to lead with, and trigger research tells you when to reach out.

## THE MESSAGING MATRIX (Core Framework)

The messaging matrix is a structured map of your personas to what matters to them. It drives every piece of outbound messaging.

### Structure

For each persona you target, document four dimensions:

| Dimension | What It Answers | Example (Comp Software Selling to HR) |
|---|---|---|
| **Priorities** | What is most important to them right now? | Equitable compensation decisions, retaining top talent |
| **Current Solutions** | How are they solving it today? | Spreadsheets with VLookups, manual merit cycles |
| **Problems** | What challenges do they face with current solutions? | 17,000 VLookups, 2 hours of pipeline hygiene daily, stale data |
| **Aspirations** | What do they ultimately want to achieve? | Real-time comp data, managers making fair decisions independently |

### Why It Matters

- Different personas get different messaging. A VP of HR cares about equitable decisions (business problem). A Comp Analyst cares about eliminating spreadsheet hell (process problem).
- The matrix drives what triggers to look for in research.
- Personalization connects to problems your product actually solves -- not random flattery.
- Each persona should have its own outreach sequence.

### Building the Matrix

1. Interview your sales team or review closed-won deals: "Why did they buy?"
2. For each persona, fill in all four columns.
3. Validate by asking: "If I said this problem to this persona, would they nod?"
4. Use the 4 Questions to Write Painfully Triggering Messages (see reference file).

## THE TRIGGER HIERARCHY

Not all research signals are equal. Prioritize by trigger quality:

### Tier 1: Hard Triggers (Timing-Based) -- Highest Priority
These signal the prospect has a problem RIGHT NOW:
- Recent funding round (within 3 months)
- New executive hire in your buyer's department
- Product launch or expansion
- Earnings call mention of a relevant initiative
- Strategic initiative announcement
- Job posting for a role your product replaces or supports

### Tier 2: Soft Triggers (Situational)
These signal the prospect likely has the problem but may not feel urgency:
- Industry trends affecting their segment
- Pervasive problems in their company size/stage
- Competitor moves in their space
- Regulatory changes in their industry

### Tier 3: Activity-Based Triggers
These signal interest or attention:
- Opened your email 5+ times
- Visited your pricing page
- Engaged with your LinkedIn content
- Replied with an objection (still engaged)
- New compelling event after initial outreach

### The First-Best Principle
Rank your top 5 triggers for each major problem. Run the list top to bottom. The moment you find one, stop searching and write the outreach. Do not keep digging for a "better" trigger -- the first good one is the best one because speed matters more than perfection in outbound.

## ACCOUNT TIERING

Use triggers to tier accounts and allocate effort accordingly:

| Tier | Definition | Outreach Effort |
|---|---|---|
| **A Tier** | Problem NOW (hard trigger found) | Full personalization, multichannel sequence, extra call dials, manual research |
| **B Tier** | Problem (soft trigger or situational fit) | Personalized emails, standard sequence, targeted calls |
| **C Tier** | Anyone else in ICP | Templated-but-relevant emails, pervasive problem angles, automated where possible |

A and B tier accounts get extra dials on cold calls. C tier accounts use "Not sure if you're running into this, but..." language with pervasive problems rather than specific triggers.

## VOC MINING (Voice of Customer)

VOC is the actual language your prospect uses to describe their needs -- not your interpretation, their literal words and phrases.

### Where to Mine VOC (12 Sources)
1. Social media posts and captions
2. Facebook Lives and videos
3. Blog posts they write
4. Podcast episodes (hosted or guested)
5. Interviews (press, webinars)
6. Email newsletters they send
7. About pages and website copy
8. Community/group posts (Facebook, Slack, Reddit)
9. Public conversations on Twitter/X
10. Press releases
11. Earnings call transcripts
12. Product review sites / G2 / Capterra

### How to Use VOC in Outreach
- Mirror their language for goals (e.g., "aggressive growth goals" -- their exact phrase)
- Use their descriptors in your messaging (e.g., "fast-growing disruptors" from how they describe themselves)
- Pull pain point language from their public shares
- Reference specific names, details, and phrases from their content
- Use VOC to pair audience objections with product features
- Reflect brand values and mission statements back to the prospect

## AI RESEARCH WORKFLOW (Prompt-Dig-Summarize)

### Step 1: Prompt
Educate the AI about what your company does and what specifically to look for. Feed it your messaging matrix.

### Step 2: Dig
Tell the AI where to look:
- Publicly available reports and strategic plans
- Company websites
- News articles
- Earnings call transcripts (Seeking Alpha for public companies)
- LinkedIn content
- Quarterly reports and budgets

### Step 3: Summarize
Ask it to make the information digestible. 30 pages of raw information is useless if it takes as long to sift through as finding it yourself.

### Key Principle
AI is only as smart as the person using it. Do the manual work of prioritizing your best triggers first. Identify your top 5 triggers. Train the AI to look for those specific signals.

## TRANSLATING RESEARCH INTO MESSAGING

### For Cold Email (Problem + Value)
Use the 4 Questions:
1. What is the crappy job if you invert your solution?
2. So what? What does that crappy job mean for the business?
3. Why do they have that problem? What does a prospect with this problem look like?
4. How do you prevent the so-what? Combine into: Personalization + Problem + Solution + CTA.

### For Cold Calls (Problem Proposition)
Describe the problem so specifically it triggers a memory. Layer descriptors:
- **Persona:** "Most CFOs of insurance defense law firms..."
- **Annoyances:** "...those deductions, rejections, and appeals..."
- **Scenery:** "...after winning a big case..."
- **Emotion:** "...think it's ridiculous that they have to deal with..."

Apply the Hairy Lollipop Test: if you cannot literally visualize the problem, it is not specific enough.

### Chunk Up for Senior Personas
- Below the line (managers/directors): Speak to the day-to-day process problem
- Above the line (VP/C-suite): Attach a business problem to the process problem

### For LinkedIn DMs
Use research for restrained compliments (4-6/10 enthusiasm, not 7-10/10). Reference specific business details, not generic praise. Make authority-building observations that demonstrate you understand their space.

## RESEARCH TIMELINE GUIDELINES

| Prospect Value | Time Investment | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| High-value / A-tier account | 1-3 days (Bree Weber's standard) | Deep research: website audit, podcast listening, VOC mining, full messaging matrix |
| Standard B-tier account | 15-30 minutes | Trigger scan, quick LinkedIn review, one pain angle |
| Volume C-tier outreach | 4-6 minutes per prospect | First-best trigger, templated problem angles |
| AI-assisted at scale | 2-4 minutes per prospect | Prompt-Dig-Summarize workflow with pre-built trigger list |

## WHERE INSTRUCTORS AGREE

All sources converge on these principles:
- Research that does not connect to a problem your product solves is wasted effort
- Personalization in the preview text / first 5-10 words is where research pays off most
- The prospect's own language (VOC) is always more persuasive than your language
- Trigger-based outreach dramatically outperforms spray-and-pray
- Senior personas need business-level problems, not process-level problems
