# Messaging Matrix Framework

## WHAT THE MESSAGING MATRIX IS

A messaging matrix maps your personas to their priorities, current solutions, problems, and aspirations. It tells you WHAT to look for when researching and ensures personalization connects to problems your product actually solves.

---

## MATRIX STRUCTURE

For each persona you sell to, fill in this grid:

| Dimension | Question to Answer | How to Find It |
|---|---|---|
| **Priorities** | What is most important to them right now? | Job postings, LinkedIn posts, earnings calls, industry reports |
| **Current Solutions** | How are they solving it today? | Technographic tools, website analysis, competitive intel, sales conversations |
| **Problems** | What challenges do they face with current solutions? | Customer interviews, churned accounts, VOC mining, community forums |
| **Aspirations** | What do they ultimately want to achieve? | Mission statements, executive interviews, strategic plans, press releases |

### Example: Compensation Software (Pave) Targeting HR

**Below-the-Line Persona: Compensation Analyst**
| Dimension | Content |
|---|---|
| Priorities | Running merit cycles accurately, maintaining comp data integrity |
| Current Solutions | Excel spreadsheets with VLookups, manual data entry across HRIS systems |
| Problems | 17,000 VLookups per cycle, stale data, no equity visibility, hours of manual work |
| Aspirations | One place for all comp data, never run merit in spreadsheets again |

**Above-the-Line Persona: CHRO / VP of People**
| Dimension | Content |
|---|---|
| Priorities | Equitable compensation decisions, retaining top talent through comp transparency |
| Current Solutions | Relying on comp team's spreadsheets, quarterly reviews with incomplete data |
| Problems | Risk of inequitable decisions, managers requesting oversized cash bonuses because equity value is invisible, attrition from comp dissatisfaction |
| Aspirations | Real-time comp benchmarking, managers making fair decisions independently, comp as a retention lever |

## 4 QUESTIONS TO WRITE PAINFULLY TRIGGERING EMAILS

These four questions transform your messaging matrix into outreach copy that makes prospects physically uncomfortable enough to reply.

### Question 1: What Is the Crappy Job If You Invert Your Solution?

Do not sell the drill -- sell the hole. Take your solution and flip it:
- CRM Note Taker that auto-updates opportunities -> "So I don't have my manager nagging me to do 2 hours of pipeline hygiene every day?"
- Compensation platform that integrates data -> "So I don't have to run merit cycles in spreadsheets with 17,000 VLookups?"
- Clinical ops platform that tracks trials -> "So I'm not buried in scheduling sessions, tracking counts, and making sure tutors get paid?"

### Question 2: So What? What Does That Crappy Job Mean for the Business?

Connect the process pain to a business consequence:
- Manager nagging for pipeline hygiene -> "That means there's risk in your forecast because you're going off last Monday's data."
- Spreadsheet merit cycles -> "That means your managers might be making inequitable comp decisions because they can't see equity and cash side by side."
- Manual clinical operations -> "That means your startup timelines stretch from 4 weeks to 12 weeks."

### Question 3: Why Do They Have That Problem? What Does a Prospect With This Problem Look Like?

This becomes your trigger -- the observable, researchable signal:
- Risk in forecast from old data -> "Your VP of Sales talked about MEDDPICC on a podcast" (they care about forecast rigor)
- Inequitable comp decisions -> "They just fundraised and are hiring a Head of Compensation" (comp becoming strategic)
- Slow startup timelines -> "They posted 3 clinical ops roles in the last month" (scaling trial volume)

### Question 4: How Do You Prevent the So-What?

Combine everything into the email formula:
- **Personalization** (why they have the problem -- the trigger you found)
- **Problem** (the crappy job + the so-what business consequence)
- **Solution** (how you prevent it in HUMAN LANGUAGE -- not product buzzwords)
- **CTA** (interest-based, not time-based)

## KILL WORDS (Words That Tank Reply Rates)

### Nothing Nouns -- Vague Buzzwords That Mean Nothing
- "Single source of truth"
- "All-in-one platform"
- "Single pane of glass"
- "End-to-end solution"
- "Leading provider"
- "Comprehensive suite"

### Vomit Verbs -- Generic Actions That Every Product Claims
- "Automate"
- "Centralize"
- "Streamline"
- "Optimize"
- "Leverage"
- "Empower"

### The Fix
Describe the damn problem in literal human terms. Not "streamline your workflows" but "stop copying deal notes from Gong into Salesforce at 6pm on a Friday."

## PROBLEM PROPOSITION STRUCTURE (For Cold Calls)

The problem proposition replaces the traditional value proposition on cold calls. Gong data from 300M cold calls shows problem language at 16% success rate vs. buzzwords at 5.5% -- a 3x difference.

### Three Steps

**Step 1: Triggering Problem**
Describe the problem so specifically that it triggers a memory and creates a visual.

Layer descriptors for sharpness:
- **Persona:** "Most CFOs of insurance defense law firms..."
- **Annoyances:** "...those deductions, rejections, and appeals..."
- **Scenery:** "...after winning a big case..."
- **Emotion:** "...think it's ridiculous that they have to deal with..."

**For simple solutions (one big problem):**
"Most talent teams find it mind-numbing to sift through 2,000 inbound applications just to figure out which five candidates you should even talk to."

**For complex solutions (three-part chronological problem):**
"Most tutoring teams are bogged down in minutia with scheduling all the sessions, tracking how many each student has left, and then making sure the tutors get paid."

**The Hairy Lollipop Test:** Imagine dropping a lollipop on a shag rug. That visceral "ugh" is the reaction your problem description needs to create. If the prospect cannot literally see the problem in their mind, it is not specific enough.

**Step 2: One-Sentence Solution**
Formula: "We do [single biggest differentiator] so that [the problem goes away]."
- "Pave integrates your HRIS, your equity, and your comp data into one place so you literally never have to run merit cycles in spreadsheets again."

**Step 3: Interest-Based CTA**
Do NOT ask for a meeting, demo, or specific time. Just validate interest:
- "I know I totally cold called you out of the blue, but would you be open to learning more sometime?"
- "Did any of that even sound moderately interesting?"

## CHUNK UP FOR SENIOR PERSONAS

The same product needs different problem framing depending on seniority:

| Level | Problem Focus | Example (Pave) |
|---|---|---|
| Below the line (Manager/Director) | Day-to-day process problem | "During Q1 merit season, most compensation teams are buried under a mountain of spreadsheets with 17,000 VLookups." |
| Above the line (VP/C-suite) | Business problem attached to the process | "During Q1 merit season, most people leaders freak out when they see their team is buried under a mountain of spreadsheets because it becomes really hard to ensure their managers are making equitable compensation decisions." |

### Rule: Only ask your prospect to make one decision or answer one question at a time.

## PROBLEM + VALUE FORMULAS FOR EMAIL

### Formula 1: The Deeper Problem
> Problem: The hard part isn't [surface level problem], it's [deeper problem].
> Value: We help [social proof] [achieve outcomes].

Example: "The hard part isn't providing tiered levels of support, it's giving every student a voice so that they feel motivated and inspired to learn."

### Formula 2: The Want/But
> Problem: [Personas] want [aspiration]... but run into challenges [challenge].
> Value: [Social proof] are using our help to [outcome].

Example: "Many ops leaders want to remove production bottlenecks, but the shortage of welding talent is tough right now."

### Formula 3: The Before/After
> Problem: [Social proof] ran into challenges...
> Value: They used our help to [aspirations]... [include testimonial quote]

Example: "Ultronomics ran into challenges with spreadsheets and double data entry across systems causing delays. 'XYZ solution helped us cut startup timelines from 12 weeks to four weeks.' -- Christine Brady, Head of Clinical Ops"

---

## SOCIAL PROOF IMPACT DATA (Gong)

| Prospect Level | Social Proof Effect on Reply Rate |
|---|---|
| Managers and below | 2.25x increase |
| Directors | 1.5x increase |

Always include social proof. Name a customer you helped with a similar problem and share the outcome -- not what your solution does.

---

## PS LINE STRATEGY

People always read the PS. Add one more personalization nugget to humanize yourself and prove a human wrote it. This is low-effort, high-impact real estate in any cold email.
