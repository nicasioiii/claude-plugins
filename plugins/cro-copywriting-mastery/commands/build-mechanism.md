# /build-mechanism -- Discover Your Unique Mechanism

## Purpose
Guide the user through Todd Brown's 5-step mechanism interrogation process to identify, name, and position their unique mechanism. Produces a mechanism brief and campaign thesis.

## Prerequisites
Ask the user to provide:
- What product/service they sell
- Who their target audience is
- What problem their product solves
- What their current process/method is (even if unnamed)

If they have completed `/research-audience` or `/diagnose-awareness`, reference those outputs.

---

## Step 1: Identify the Method

**Ask the user:**
"Walk me through HOW your product/service produces its result. What is the step-by-step process, system, method, or framework? Don't worry about naming it yet -- just describe what actually happens."

**Follow-up probes if needed:**
- "What happens first? Then what?"
- "How many steps or phases are there?"
- "What tools, techniques, or principles does each step use?"

**Output:** Document the raw process as a numbered list.

---

## Step 2: Interrogate the Method

**This is the most valuable activity.** Ask these questions one by one:

1. "Why do you do it in THIS order? What would happen if you rearranged the steps?"
2. "What happens if you SKIP a step? Which steps are absolutely essential?"
3. "What if you ADDED a step? Is anything missing?"
4. "Why does each piece play the role it does? What's the purpose of each step?"
5. "If someone asked 'Why is your method superior to every other method?' -- what would you say?"

**For each answer, document:**
- The claim (what makes this step/element superior)
- The proof (why it works -- the reason-why)
- The benefit (what this means for the customer)

These become CPB chunks for the marketing argument.

---

## Step 3: Test for Uniqueness

Run three checks:

**Check 1: The Google Slap Test**
"If a prospect paused your video or left your page and Googled [your method in generic terms], would they find free alternatives from competitors?"
- If YES: The mechanism needs further differentiation
- If NO: You have a unique mechanism

**Check 2: The Commodity Test**
"If you slapped a fancy name on three common steps, would you have anything to say when asked to PROVE it's superior?"
- If NO: You have a unique name on a commodity, not a unique mechanism. Go deeper.

**Check 3: The Replacement Test**
"Is this positioned as a REPLACEMENT for the old way, or an IMPROVEMENT on the old way?"
- Best answer: Replacement. Different AND better. Not a tweak -- a new approach.

---

## Step 4: Name the Mechanism

**Rules for naming:**
- DO NOT name it something benefit-driven or commercial ("The Explosive Growth System")
- DO name it something that sounds scientific, official, or like it could appear in a news article
- It should sound legitimate, not marketed

**Naming patterns:**
- The [Adjective] [Noun] Method (The Compound Revenue Method)
- The [Number]-[Noun] Framework (The 5-Layer Framework)
- [Adjective] [Noun] Protocol (Dynamic Sequencing Protocol)
- The [Proper Noun] System (The Meridian System)
- [Acronym] Method (The CPB Method, The E5 Method)

**Ask the user to brainstorm 5 names, then evaluate each against:**
1. Does it sound scientific/official?
2. Would it look at home in a college textbook title?
3. Does it pass the Google Slap test (unique enough that Googling leads back to you)?
4. Does it NOT sound like a marketing gimmick?

---

## Step 5: Write the Campaign Thesis

**Formula:**
"The [statement of superiority] way to [Primary Promise] is with [Unique Mechanism]."

**Extended formula with Catalyst:**
"The [statement of superiority] way to take advantage of [Catalyst] so you can [Primary Promise] is with [Unique Mechanism]."

**Ask the user to fill in:**
- Statement of superiority: most effective, fastest, most reliable, safest, etc.
- Primary Promise: the one big measurable result
- Unique Mechanism: the name from Step 4
- Catalyst (optional): a timely market event that makes this relevant NOW

---

## Output: Mechanism Brief

Generate a summary document:

```
MECHANISM BRIEF

Product/Service: [Name]
Target Audience: [One Reader]
Problem Solved: [Core problem]

UNIQUE MECHANISM
Name: [Mechanism name]
Description: [1-2 sentence summary of how it works]
Key Differentiators:
1. [What makes it different from alternatives]
2. [Second differentiator]
3. [Third differentiator]

CAMPAIGN THESIS
"[Full thesis statement]"

CPB CHUNKS (for marketing argument)
Chunk 1: [Claim] -- [Proof] -- [Benefit]
Chunk 2: [Claim] -- [Proof] -- [Benefit]
Chunk 3: [Claim] -- [Proof] -- [Benefit]
Chunk 4: [Claim] -- [Proof] -- [Benefit]

GOOGLE SLAP TEST: [Pass/Fail]
COMMODITY TEST: [Pass/Fail]
REPLACEMENT TEST: [Replacement/Improvement]

NEXT STEPS:
- Use headline-hook-craft to develop hooks around this mechanism
- Use sales-page-vsl to build the marketing argument from these CPB chunks
- Use benefit-translation to dimensionalize the benefits in each chunk
```
