# Build Page Command

## What This Command Does

Walks you through selecting the right landing page type for your product and audience, then delivers the exact section-by-section template with copywriting guidance for each section.

---

## Step 1: Gather Context

Before selecting a page type, I need to understand your situation. Please provide:

1. **What are you selling?** (Course, SaaS, physical product, service, coaching, etc.)
2. **Who is the primary audience?** (Brief description of your ideal customer)
3. **What awareness stage is your traffic?** (Unaware / Problem Aware / Solution Aware / Product Aware / Most Aware -- if unsure, describe where traffic comes from)
4. **What is the primary traffic source?** (Paid social, Google ads, email, organic, referral)
5. **What action do you want visitors to take?** (Buy, opt-in, book a call, start trial, etc.)
6. **Do you have an existing page to improve, or is this new?**

---

## Step 2: Select Page Type

Based on the context, I will recommend one of these 6 page types:

| Page Type | Use When |
|-----------|----------|
| **Digital Product LP** | Selling courses, SaaS, digital downloads, memberships, coaching programs |
| **Physical Product LP** | Selling physical goods, e-commerce products, DTC items |
| **Service LP** | Selling services, booking calls, generating leads for agencies/consultants |
| **Listicle LP** | Running paid ads to cold traffic, wanting editorial/advertorial feel |
| **Editorial LP** | Needing story-driven trust building before any pitch |
| **Homepage** | Building main brand page, trust checkpoint, multi-audience gateway |

**Decision rules I apply:**
- If product is digital and price is above $97 --> Digital Product LP (long form)
- If product is physical and sold online --> Physical Product LP
- If selling services or booking calls --> Service LP
- If running cold paid social and want to feel native --> Listicle LP
- If audience is skeptical and needs trust first --> Editorial LP
- If building the main brand page --> Homepage

---

## Step 3: Deliver the Template

Once the page type is selected, I will provide:

### For each section of the template:
1. **Section name and purpose** -- What this section does
2. **Exact placement** -- Where it goes in the page order
3. **Content requirements** -- What to write, how long, what format
4. **Template with fill-in prompts** -- Ready-to-complete scaffolding
5. **Rules and anti-patterns** -- What to do and what to avoid
6. **Example** -- A real-world example or worked template

### Page-level guidance:
- The 10/90 Rule application for this page type
- Hero section element checklist (what to include/exclude)
- CTA strategy and placement
- Message matching requirements (if traffic is from ads)
- Mobile optimization notes

---

## Step 4: Hero Section Deep Dive

For every page type, I provide an expanded hero section breakdown:

1. **Primer copy** -- Whether to include it and what to write
2. **Headline** -- Formula recommendation based on awareness stage
3. **Subheadline** -- How to expand the headline
4. **Body copy** -- Whether needed and what to include
5. **CTA button** -- Copy and placement recommendation
6. **Hero image** -- What type of image to use

---

## Step 5: Ad-LP Matching Check (If Applicable)

If the traffic source is paid ads, I run the 3 gut-check questions:

1. Is the subject matter consistent with what got them interested in the ad?
2. Is the tone consistent and familiar?
3. Does the design/flow make sense based on what they just saw?

And I flag any potential mismatches.
