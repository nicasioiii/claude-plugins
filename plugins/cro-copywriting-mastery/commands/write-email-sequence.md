# Write 7-Email Welcome Sequence

## PURPOSE
Guide the user through building a complete 7-email welcome sequence using Amarsi's framework. Produces all 7 emails with subject lines, preview text, body copy, and strategic notes.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Gather Context

Ask the user for the following (do not proceed without answers):

1. **What is your product/service?**
   - Brief description of what you sell
   - Price point (this affects tone and directness)
   - Is there a specific offer to promote in the sequence? If so, what?

2. **Who is your One Reader?**
   - Describe your ideal subscriber
   - What problem brought them to your list?
   - What language do they use to describe their pain?
   - What is their awareness stage? (Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware)

3. **What did they opt in for?**
   - Lead magnet name and type (PDF, video, checklist, template, etc.)
   - What does the lead magnet promise?

4. **What is your brand voice/personality?**
   - Casual and funny? Professional and authoritative? Warm and empathetic?
   - Any pop culture references or humor styles that fit your brand?
   - How do you naturally sign off? (Formal? Casual? Branded?)

5. **Do you have testimonials or case studies?**
   - At least 1-2 client results with specific numbers
   - Even informal results work ("My client Sarah went from X to Y")

6. **Long Version or Short Version?**
   - Long Version: More personal, story-driven, philosophical. Best for personal brands, coaches, consultants, thought leaders.
   - Short Version: More direct, case-study driven. Best for SaaS, ecommerce, professional services.
   - If unsure, recommend based on their product type.

### Step 2: Confirm the Sequence Blueprint

Before writing, present the 7-email arc to the user for approval:

```
EMAIL 1: Welcome — Deliver lead magnet, introduce yourself,
         set rules, be transparent about selling

EMAIL 2: Bond — Share your philosophy/worldview,
         connect it to their goal

EMAIL 3: Content Setup (Pt 1) — Teach something valuable,
         challenge a belief, open a loop

EMAIL 4: Promotional Payoff (Pt 2) — Close the loop from
         Email 3, weave in a subtle pitch via P.P.S.

EMAIL 5: Testimonial — Share a client transformation story,
         end with contemplation questions

EMAIL 6: Philosophy/Case Study — Share your worldview through
         content or a deeper case study

EMAIL 7: Sales Email — Direct pitch wrapped in genuine content
```

Get confirmation or adjust based on their needs.

### Step 3: Write Email 1 (Welcome)

This is the MOST important email. Write it first and get feedback before proceeding.

**Include:**
- Subject line (with pop culture reference if brand-appropriate)
- Preview text
- Full body copy following the template from `welcome-sequence-templates.md`
- Numbered list of 5-6 items (personal detail, sending expectations, whitelist request, folder creation request, participation invite)
- Transparent selling statement
- Lead magnet delivery link placeholder
- Offer reference (if applicable)
- Sign-off and P.S.

**Reference:** `email-sequences/references/welcome-sequence-templates.md` — Email 1 template

### Step 4: Write Emails 2-4

Write these as a batch because Emails 3 and 4 are the "One-Two Punch" and must be developed together.

**Email 2 (Bond):**
- Subject line (bold claim or philosophical statement)
- Philosophy/worldview connected to their goal
- Link back to lead magnet

**Email 3 (Content Setup - Part 1):**
- Ominous subject line with "Part 1"
- Genuine value/teaching content
- Challenge a belief or misconception
- Bullet points selling a link (odd number, end with "And so much more...")

**Email 4 (Promotional Payoff - Part 2):**
- Continuation subject line with "Part 2"
- Completes the story/teaching from Email 3
- Subtle pitch woven into the content
- P.S. summarizing the email
- P.P.S. calling back to offer from Email 1

**Reference:** `email-sequences/references/welcome-sequence-templates.md` — Emails 2-4 templates

### Step 5: Write Emails 5-7

**Email 5 (Testimonial):**
- "From X to Y" subject line
- Client/customer story in their own words
- Context the testimonial leaves out
- Contemplation questions at the end
- Soft CTA to offer

**Email 6 (Philosophy/Case Study):**
- Conversational subject line
- Personal observation or experience
- Connected to their problem/goal
- One-sentence summary of the key point
- Actionable guidance

**Email 7 (Sales Email):**
- Numbered/dramatic subject line
- Admission of doing something different
- ONE focus point explored in depth
- Link to sales page (hyperlink text + raw URL)
- P.S. with a directive

**Reference:** `email-sequences/references/welcome-sequence-templates.md` — Emails 5-7 templates

### Step 6: Subject Line Review

After all 7 emails are written, present all subject lines together for review:

```
Email 1: [Subject line] | Preview: [Preview text]
Email 2: [Subject line] | Preview: [Preview text]
Email 3: [Subject line] | Preview: [Preview text]
Email 4: [Subject line] | Preview: [Preview text]
Email 5: [Subject line] | Preview: [Preview text]
Email 6: [Subject line] | Preview: [Preview text]
Email 7: [Subject line] | Preview: [Preview text]
```

Check:
- Do the subject lines create variety? (Not all the same format)
- Do Emails 3 and 4 create an open loop across the Part 1/Part 2?
- Is there a mix of patterns? (bold claim, curiosity, transformation, numbered, conversational)
- Would YOU open these if they appeared in your inbox?

**Reference:** `email-sequences/references/subject-line-bank.md`

### Step 7: Deliver with Implementation Notes

Provide the complete sequence along with:

**Timing Schedule:**
| Email | Day |
|-------|-----|
| 1 | Immediately on opt-in |
| 2 | Day 2 |
| 3 | Day 4 |
| 4 | Day 5 |
| 5 | Day 7 |
| 6 | Day 9 |
| 7 | Day 11 |

**Implementation Checklist:**
- [ ] Set up email automation tool with correct timing
- [ ] Test all links in each email
- [ ] Preview on mobile (50%+ of readers are mobile)
- [ ] Set up tracking for opens, clicks, and replies per email
- [ ] Have a post-sequence plan (broadcast schedule or repeat the cycle)

**Key Metrics to Watch:**
- Email 1 open rate: Target 50-70%
- Overall sequence open rate: Target 30-50%
- Click-through rate per email: Target 5-15%
- Reply rate: Track but do not benchmark (any replies are good)
- Unsubscribe rate: Should be under 2% per email
- Conversion rate: Track purchases attributed to the sequence

**If Results Are Below Target:**
- Low opens → Fix subject lines (A/B test)
- Low clicks → Fix body copy and CTA clarity
- Low conversions → Fix the offer or sales page the emails link to
- High unsubscribes → Fix frequency or relevance of content
