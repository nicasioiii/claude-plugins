# Write Email Sequence

This command builds complete email sequences (welcome, launch, nurture, or re-engagement) with subject lines and copy outlines for each email.

## What You'll Get

- Full email sequence (3-10 emails depending on type)
- Compelling subject line for each email
- Copy outline with key sections and talking points
- Arc and flow that builds urgency and belief
- CTA strategy for each email
- Spacing recommendations

## Questions I'll Ask

1. **What type of sequence do you need?**
   - Welcome series, launch sequence, nurture drip, re-engagement, abandoned cart

2. **What's the product or offer?**
   - Name, price point, main benefit

3. **Who's the audience?**
   - Stage in buyer journey, pain points, objections

4. **How many emails?**
   - Typically 3-5 for welcome, 5-10 for launch or nurture

## My Process

I'll reference these skills to build your sequence:
- **email-sequence-architecture** — Design overall sequence strategy
- **story-narrative-copywriting** — Create compelling email narratives
- **headline-hook-engine** — Write powerful subject lines

## Output Format

Your sequence will include:

For each email:
```
EMAIL [#]: [SUBJECT LINE]
─────────────────────────
Type: [Type of email - story, pitch, proof, objection handler, urgency, etc.]

Copy Outline:
- Opening hook: [Specific opening]
- Main body: [Key talking points and story beats]
- Bridge to CTA: [How we transition to ask]
- CTA: [Specific action requested]

Send timing: [Recommended day/time relative to previous]
```

Final section includes:
- Overall sequence strategy
- How each email builds on previous
- Key metrics to track
- Alternative versions for A/B testing
