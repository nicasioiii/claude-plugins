# Write Story Section

## PURPOSE
Guide the user through selecting the right story structure, developing the narrative arc, and producing a finished story section ready for use in sales pages, emails, ads, or content.

## PROCESS

### Step 1: Gather Context

Ask the user for the following (do not proceed without answers):

1. **Where will this story live?**
   - Sales page "Your Story" section
   - Email (which email in a sequence?)
   - Ad copy (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn?)
   - Landing page hero section
   - Content / blog post
   - VSL script
   - Other (specify)

2. **What is the story about?**
   - Founder/brand origin story
   - Client transformation / case study
   - Personal experience related to the product
   - Discovery of the mechanism / solution
   - Industry problem / paradigm shift
   - Other

3. **Who is the One Reader?**
   - Describe your ideal prospect (or provide an existing avatar)

4. **What is the audience's awareness stage?**
   - Unaware, Problem Aware, Solution Aware, Product Aware, Most Aware

5. **What is the desired outcome after reading the story?**
   - They feel connected to the brand
   - They understand why the mechanism works
   - They see themselves in the transformation
   - They are ready to read the offer section
   - Other

6. **Approximate word count?**
   - Short (100-300 words) — ads, emails, hero sections
   - Medium (300-800 words) — story sections on pages
   - Long (800-2,000 words) — full origin stories, VSL sections
   - Extended (2,000+ words) — editorial content, full campaigns

### Step 2: Recommend a Story Structure

Based on the answers, recommend the best structure from:

| Structure | Best For |
|-----------|----------|
| Before-After-Bridge | Hero sections, quick ads (50-200 words) |
| IRS Method | Short ads, emails, social posts (100-500 words) |
| 60-Second Sales Hook | Bios, intros, elevator pitches (60-150 words) |
| PAISA Formula | Organic posts, ad copy (200-800 words) |
| Confessions Template | Stage 5 sophistication reset ads (500-2,000 words) |
| Freytag's Pyramid | Sales page story sections (500-2,000 words) |
| Hero's Journey | VSLs, long-form sales pages (1,000-5,000 words) |
| 12-Point Framework | Full campaigns, editorial (2,000+ words) |

Present the recommendation with reasoning. If multiple structures could work, present 2 options and let the user choose.

### Step 3: Develop the Narrative Arc

Walk the user through the key decisions for their chosen structure:

**For all structures, establish:**
- The central character (reader as hero, founder as guide, or client as case study)
- The conflict (which of the 6 types: person vs. person/nature/self/society/technology/supernatural)
- The transformation moment (pivotal point where everything changes)
- The resolution (how the conflict is overcome)
- The connection to the product/mechanism

**For the 12-Point Framework, also establish:**
- Where the curiosity gap will open and close
- At least 2 planned zigzag redirects
- The sensory details for the opening (which senses will you use?)
- How the story connects back to the title

**For the Confessions Template, also establish:**
- The confessional opening line
- The deepest pain point (this becomes 40-50% of the story)
- The paradigm shift / breakthrough moment
- The mechanism name to introduce

### Step 4: Write the Story

Using the chosen structure and developed arc, write the complete story section.

**Writing rules to follow:**
- Read the `body-copy-craft` SKILL.md for sentence-level craft rules
- Use binary writing (one thought per sentence)
- Choose strong, sensory verbs (reference `verb-selection-guide.md`)
- Show, do not tell (use action and sensory details instead of labels)
- Keep the reader as the hero when possible
- Open and close all curiosity gaps
- Follow the Zigzag Approach — never be predictable
- Apply the Narrative Nexus: Emotion + Experience + Expression must ALL be present

### Step 5: Review and Refine

After writing, check the story against:

- [ ] **Narrative Nexus:** Does it have emotion, experience, AND expression?
- [ ] **Conflict present:** Is there genuine struggle?
- [ ] **Show not tell:** Are emotions shown through actions, not labels?
- [ ] **Sensory language:** Does it engage at least 2 senses?
- [ ] **Curiosity gaps:** All opened loops are closed?
- [ ] **Connection to product/mechanism:** Does the story naturally lead to the offer?
- [ ] **The "So What" test:** Would the One Reader care about this story?
- [ ] **Read aloud:** Does it flow naturally when spoken?
- [ ] **Binary writing:** Is each sentence one thought?
- [ ] **Verb quality:** Are verbs strong and sensory (no dead verbs)?

### Step 6: Deliver with Usage Notes

Provide the finished story along with:
- Where exactly it should be placed on the page/in the email
- What comes before it (transition-in suggestion)
- What should follow it (transition-out suggestion)
- Any A/B testing recommendations (e.g., test story lead vs. problem-solution lead)
