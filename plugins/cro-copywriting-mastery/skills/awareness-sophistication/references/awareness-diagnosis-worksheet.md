# Awareness & Sophistication Diagnosis Worksheet

## Purpose

Complete this worksheet before writing any copy. It takes 15-20 minutes and determines the strategic foundation for everything you write. Wrong diagnosis = wrong copy = failed campaign.

---

## PART 1: AWARENESS STAGE DIAGNOSIS

### Question 1: Does your prospect know they have this problem?

**If NO** -> They are **Unaware**. Your copy must create problem awareness through story, entertainment, or revelation. Do NOT mention your product or solution category.

**If YES** -> Continue to Question 2.

### Question 2: Does your prospect know that solutions to this problem exist?

**If NO** -> They are **Problem Aware**. Your copy must show that the problem is solvable. Lead with empathy for the problem, then introduce the concept of a solution. Do NOT name your product yet.

**If YES** -> Continue to Question 3.

### Question 3: Does your prospect know about YOUR type of solution specifically?

**If NO** -> They are **Solution Aware**. Your copy must differentiate your approach from alternative solution types. Explain WHY your approach is different and better.

**If YES** -> Continue to Question 4.

### Question 4: Does your prospect know about YOUR specific product?

**If NO** -> They are **Product Aware**. Your copy must prove you are the best choice within the solution category they have already chosen. Social proof, comparisons, and risk reversal are critical.

**If YES** -> Continue to Question 5.

### Question 5: Has your prospect already decided you are the best option?

**If YES** -> They are **Most Aware**. Your copy just needs to give them a reason to buy NOW. Lead with the offer, deal, or urgency.

**If NO** -> They are **Product Aware** with objections. Identify and address the specific objections holding them back.

## PART 2: SOPHISTICATION STAGE DIAGNOSIS

### Question 1: How many competitors are making similar claims in your market?

- **None or very few** -> Stage 1 (First to Market)
- **Several, with escalating claims** -> Stage 2 (Ramp-Up/Hype)
- **Many, with mechanism-level explanations** -> Stage 3 or 4 (Mechanism)
- **Market feels saturated and cynical** -> Stage 5 (Identity/Story Reset)

### Question 2: What does your prospect's skepticism look like?

- **Low skepticism, excited by the concept itself** -> Stage 1
- **Moderate skepticism, wants bigger proof** -> Stage 2
- **High skepticism about claims, wants to know HOW** -> Stage 3
- **High skepticism about mechanisms, wants "without the downside"** -> Stage 4
- **Total cynicism, "I've heard it all before"** -> Stage 5

### Question 3: What language does the market use about solutions?

- **"I didn't know this existed!"** -> Stage 1
- **"Which one delivers the best results?"** -> Stage 2
- **"How exactly does this work?"** -> Stage 3
- **"Can I get the result without [specific negative]?"** -> Stage 4
- **"They're all the same. Nothing really works."** -> Stage 5

### Question 4: What are competitors leading with in their copy?

- **Simple claims and descriptions** -> Stage 1-2
- **Named mechanisms and frameworks** -> Stage 3
- **"Without" language and negative removal** -> Stage 4
- **Story-driven, identity-focused, paradigm-shifting** -> Stage 5

## PART 3: THE STRATEGY OUTPUT

### Based on your awareness diagnosis, complete this section:

**My prospect's awareness stage is:** _______________

**Therefore, my hook should:**
- [ ] Tell a story / create revelation (Unaware)
- [ ] Name their problem and empathize (Problem Aware)
- [ ] Differentiate my approach with unique insight (Solution Aware)
- [ ] Lead with my value proposition and proof (Product Aware)
- [ ] Lead with the offer / deal (Most Aware)

**My offer should be:**
- [ ] Content opt-in (Unaware / Problem Aware)
- [ ] Educational content or lead magnet (Problem Aware / Solution Aware)
- [ ] Free trial or low-commitment ask (Solution Aware / Product Aware)
- [ ] Ask for the sale (Product Aware / Most Aware)
- [ ] Special promotion or urgency offer (Most Aware)

**My copy length should be:**
- [ ] Very long -- must do the most educational work (Unaware)
- [ ] Long -- need to establish problem and introduce solution type (Problem Aware)
- [ ] Medium-long -- need to differentiate and build case (Solution Aware)
- [ ] Medium -- they know the problem and solution type (Product Aware)
- [ ] Short -- get to the offer fast (Most Aware)

**My ad approach should be:**
- [ ] Indirect -- story, content, education (Stages 1-3)
- [ ] Direct -- clear offer, direct pitch (Stages 3-5)
- [ ] Test both with the 2x2 framework (Stage 3 / uncertain)
