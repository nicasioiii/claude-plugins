# Awareness Hooks by Stage -- Templates and Examples

## How to Use This Reference

For each stage of awareness, this file provides the hook strategy, template patterns, and real examples. The hook is what goes in the TOP 10% of your page or ad -- the part that determines whether the prospect keeps reading.

---

## STAGE 1: COMPLETELY UNAWARE

### Strategy
The prospect does not know they have a problem. You cannot mention solutions, products, or even the problem directly. Lead with a story, a revelation, or a problem that "others like them" have encountered.

### Hook Templates

**Story Lead:**
"[Relatable character] was [doing something normal] when [unexpected revelation changed everything]..."

**Revelation Lead:**
"[X number] of [people like the reader] are unknowingly [suffering from / missing out on] [hidden problem]..."

**Social Proof Lead:**
"Other [identity group] have struggled to [desired outcome] -- and most never discover why..."

**Curiosity Lead:**
"What if the reason you [symptom] has nothing to do with [assumed cause]?"

### Example Hooks

- Wall Street Journal "Two Young Men" -- Pure story. No headline. Two men leave college, one succeeds wildly, one does not. The only difference: the Journal.
- "They All Laughed When I Sat Down at the Piano" (John Caples) -- Story hook for piano lessons that never mentions lessons in the hook.
- Confessions ad template -- "Confessions of a [role]" opens with voyeuristic intrigue, deep dive into shared pain, then a breakthrough.

### Rules for Unaware Hooks
1. NEVER mention your product or service
2. NEVER mention the solution category
3. Lead with entertainment, story, or shared human experience
4. Make it about THEM, not about you
5. Create enough emotional resonance that they self-identify with the problem

## STAGE 2: PROBLEM AWARE

### Strategy
The prospect knows they have a problem but does not know solutions exist. Name their problem. Make them feel seen. Build empathy BEFORE offering any solution.

### Hook Templates

**Problem Identification:**
"Do You [Make This Mistake / Experience This Symptom]?"

**Empathy Lead:**
"If you [specific symptom], you are not alone. [X number] of [identity group] face this every [day/week/month]..."

**Pain Amplification:**
"The real cost of [problem] is not just [obvious cost] -- it is [hidden emotional/financial/time cost]..."

**Question Lead:**
"What if [common problem] is actually caused by [unexpected root cause]?"

**Validation Lead:**
"You have tried [common solution 1], [common solution 2], and [common solution 3]. None of them worked. Here is why..."

### Example Hooks

- "Do You Make These Mistakes in English?" -- Names the problem (mistakes in English) without naming the product
- "Tired of Waking Up Exhausted Even After 8 Hours of Sleep?" -- Symptom-first for a sleep product
- "Why 73% of Small Business Owners Burn Out Before Year 3" -- Statistical problem hook

### Rules for Problem Aware Hooks
1. Name the problem in THEIR language (use VOC research)
2. Demonstrate empathy before offering solutions
3. You CAN mention that solutions exist, but do NOT name your product yet
4. Address symptoms first, root cause later
5. The emotion here is empathy and validation: "I understand you"

## STAGE 3: SOLUTION AWARE

### Strategy
The prospect knows solutions exist and is evaluating types of solutions. Differentiate your APPROACH (not your product). Show unique insight into the solution space.

### Hook Templates

**Unique Insight:**
"The [solution technology / methodology] that [created the problem / gave competitors an advantage] is now available to [you / your identity group]..."

**Differentiation:**
"Most [solutions] focus on [common approach]. But [unique insight] shows that [different approach] produces [specific result]..."

**Mechanism Introduction:**
"There is a new [method / approach / system] that [produces result] by [unique mechanism]..."

**Category Disruption:**
"Forget everything you know about [solution category]. [New paradigm] changes the game by [specific difference]..."

### Example Hooks

- "The brewing technology that gave overpriced coffee shops all the power is now available in your kitchen" -- Solution-aware hook for Tassimo
- "There is a new kind of project management built specifically for [specific team type] that cuts meeting time by 60%" -- Differentiates approach
- "Why the 'just eat less' approach to weight loss is scientifically wrong -- and what to do instead" -- Invalidates competing solutions

### Rules for Solution Aware Hooks
1. Position your approach as DIFFERENT from what they have already tried
2. You CAN mention solution categories but differentiate yours
3. Lead with the unique insight, mechanism, or angle
4. Create curiosity about HOW your approach works differently
5. The emotion here is hope: "Maybe this is what I have been missing"

## STAGE 4: PRODUCT AWARE

### Strategy
The prospect knows your product and is deciding between you and alternatives. Lead with your value proposition. Provide proof, reassurance, and specific reasons to choose you.

### Hook Templates

**Value Proposition:**
"[Product]: [Specific benefit] for [specific audience]"

**Social Proof Lead:**
"[X number] of [identity group] use [Product] to [achieve specific result]..."

**Comparison:**
"While other [solution category] tools [common limitation], [Product] [unique advantage]..."

**Risk Reversal:**
"Try [Product] for [time period]. If [specific result] does not happen, [guarantee]."

**Testimonial Lead:**
"[Customer name]: '[Specific result quote]'"

### Example Hooks

- "Tassimo: because you don't have time to grind beans" -- Direct value proposition
- "Over 7,000 marketing teams use [Product] to ship campaigns 3x faster" -- Social proof + specific result
- "So yeah, we tried Slack" -- Slack's product-aware homepage era

### Rules for Product Aware Hooks
1. Name your product directly
2. Lead with your strongest differentiator or proof point
3. Social proof and testimonials are critical at this stage
4. Address the top 1-2 comparison questions explicitly
5. The emotion here is assurance: "You are making the right choice"

## STAGE 5: MOST AWARE

### Strategy
The prospect already knows and trusts you. They need a reason to buy NOW. Lead with the offer, deal, or new feature.

### Hook Templates

**Offer Lead:**
"[Discount/Deal]: Get [Product] for [price] (normally [higher price]) -- [deadline]"

**New Feature:**
"Introducing [Feature]: Now [Product] also [new capability]..."

**Urgency:**
"[Time-limited offer]: [Specific deal]. Ends [date]."

**Exclusive:**
"For [identity group / list subscribers] only: [Special offer]"

**Reactivation:**
"We have made [X changes] since you last looked. Here is what is new..."

### Example Hooks

- "[80% OFF] Grab this copywriting course" -- Straight to the deal
- "New: AI-powered templates now included in every plan" -- Feature announcement
- "Flash Sale: 48 hours only. $199 $49. No coupon needed." -- Pure urgency

### Rules for Most Aware Hooks
1. Get to the offer in the FIRST line
2. Price, discount, or deal should be visible immediately
3. Create genuine urgency (deadline, limited quantity)
4. This is NOT the time for education or storytelling
5. The emotion here is excitement: "Finally, this is the deal I was waiting for"

## ADAPTING HOOKS FOR DIFFERENT FORMATS

### Facebook/Social Ads
- The first 2-3 lines visible before "See More" function as the hook
- Must answer: "Why should I read this?" and "What's this doing for me?"
- Image/video is seen FIRST, then headline, then body copy

### Email Subject Lines
- Map subject line to awareness stage of the list segment
- Cold list = Problem Aware hooks
- Engaged subscribers = Product Aware hooks
- Past buyers = Most Aware hooks

### Landing Page Headlines
- The hook IS the headline + subhead combination
- Must pass the 7-Second Test (answers: What is it? Why should I care? What now?)
- 84% of attention goes to above-the-fold content

### Organic Social Posts
- The "real estate" (visible portion before "See More") functions as the hook
- Match the hook to where your audience sits on the awareness spectrum
- Hooks ride on curiosity and intrigue; payoffs answer "what's in it for me"
