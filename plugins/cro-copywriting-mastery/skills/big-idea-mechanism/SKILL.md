# Big Idea, Unique Mechanism & Primary Promise

## THE ONE BIG IDEA

Every landing page, every campaign, and every ad must follow the Rule of One: One Reader, One Big Idea, One Promise, One Offer.

Big ideas are great big benefits -- something your prospect badly wants and believes you can offer. The Big Idea must be immediately graspable and stick with the reader throughout the entire page.

### What a Big Idea Is NOT
- "Save time AND money" is two ideas. Choose one.
- "A comprehensive solution" is no idea. It is a category.
- A list of features is not an idea. It is a brochure.

### How to Find Your One Big Idea
1. Use on-site polls to discover which idea matters most to readers
2. Survey paying customers: "What is the single biggest benefit?"
3. Interview prospects: dig deep into specifics -- they write the page for you
4. Research determines the Big Idea, not guesswork

## THE UNIQUE MECHANISM

### What It Is
The unique piece, part, component, aspect, process, system, method, or framework behind your product that solves the problem and produces the result. It is HOW your product works. The reason WHY your product works.

It gives the prospect hope and justification for that hope: "Maybe this is what I have been missing."

### The Unique Mechanism Is a Marketing Invention

**Contrarian insight:** The overwhelming majority of unique mechanisms are marketing inventions, not actual proprietary innovations. They are ways of positioning, repositioning, and presenting a product or service.

As long as you are not selling a pure commodity (a glass of tap water), you can identify a unique mechanism. Every single thing that has a name -- somebody named it. You have every right to name your process.

### The Google Slap Test

Never sell or market a solution that can be viewed as a commodity. If you get buy-in on "Facebook ads are the best way to grow" -- the prospect pauses your video, Googles "Facebook ads," and finds free information everywhere. You sold them on a commodity.

Instead, get buy-in on YOUR proprietary method for using Facebook ads (the unique mechanism). Now all roads lead to you.

### Interrogating the Mechanism -- The Most Valuable Activity

Naming is the LEAST important part. The single most important activity is INTERROGATING your method. Spend time understanding HOW and WHY it works.

**The 5 Interrogation Questions:**

1. Why do you do it THAT way instead of other ways?
2. Why in THAT order specifically?
3. What happens if you skip a step or do it out of order?
4. Why is each piece necessary? What role does it play?
5. If someone asked "Why is your method superior to every other method?" -- what would you say?

The answers give you the reasons why your method is superior -- which becomes the body of your campaign argument.

### Different vs. Better vs. Superior

- **Different gets attention. Superior generates the sale.**
- Different alone is not enough. Just because something has a different UI does not mean it is better.
- Superior must always be different. But different is not always superior.
- Present your mechanism as REPLACEMENT, not improvement. "This is a different way that is better" -- not "this is a tweak to the old way."
- Replacement gives hope because prospects who have tried the old way doubt that a small tweak will change everything.

### Why a Unique Name on a Commodity Fails

If you take three common steps, slap a fancy name on them ("The Palm Tree Process"), and call them unique -- you have a unique name for a commodity, NOT a unique mechanism. When it comes time to prove superiority, you will have nothing to say.

The power is NOT in the name. The power is in understanding how and why the mechanism works differently and better.

## NAMING THE MECHANISM

### The One Naming Rule

Do NOT name it something that sounds benefit-driven or commercial. Do NOT call it "The Explosive Muscle Building System."

Name it something that sounds scientific, official, like it could appear in a news article or college textbook. Sound legitimate, not commercialized.

**Good names:** "The E5 Method," "Pixel Refreshing Protocol," "The Timing Accelerator," "Zone-2 Metabolic Reset"

**Bad names:** "The Ultimate Fat Blaster System," "The Money Machine Formula," "The Dream Body Blueprint"

## THE PRIMARY PROMISE

### Crafting It

The chief result, outcome, or transformation. The one overarching change. It must be:

- **Big, bold, audacious** AND true and believable
- **Specific, concrete, tangible** (NOT vague)
- **Measurable** -- the prospect must know with certainty when it has been experienced
- **Envisionable** -- they must be able to picture it in their mind

**BAD:** "Get a better night's sleep."
**GOOD:** "Sleep so soundly you wake up refreshed at the crack of dawn without ever needing an alarm clock."

### Primary Promise vs. Ancillary Benefits

Every campaign has ONE primary promise (the big overarching change). Ancillary benefits (sleep better, more energy, more confidence) appear in the copy but are secondary. The hook conveys the primary promise.

### Two Exercises for Identifying Your Primary Promise

1. **The Magic Wand Technique:** If you could wave a magic wand and grant your prospects anything related to their problem, what would they ask for?
2. **The Ideal Client Exercise:** Imagine your greatest future success story. What exact results did that client get? What was the transformation?

## THE CAMPAIGN THESIS (BUYING BELIEF)

### Formula

"The [statement of superiority] way to [Primary Promise] is with [Unique Mechanism]."

**Example:** "The most effective way to get new buyers every day is with the E5 Method."

### Extended Formula with Catalyst

"The [statement of superiority] way to take advantage of [Catalyst] so you can [Primary Promise] is with [Unique Mechanism]."

**Example:** "The most effective way to leverage the iOS tracking update so you can get flawless marketing metrics is with Pixel Refreshing."

Everything in the marketing argument exists for ONE reason: to establish this buying belief.

## THE 75/25 CAMPAIGN SPLIT

- **75%** of a front-end campaign = getting buy-in on the MECHANISM (not the product)
- **25%** = presenting the offer

In the 75%, there is NO mention of the product, no pitching, no product features. Only education about why the mechanism is superior. This is "education-based marketing."

When done correctly, prospects are GRATEFUL for the opportunity to buy. They thank you for the offer.

## THE HOOK FOUNDATION

**Promise + Curiosity = Hook Foundation**

Every good hook is both:
- **Emotionally compelling** = big bold promise + unique mechanism (creates hope)
- **Intellectually interesting** = storyline or angle they have never heard before (creates curiosity)

### Hook Checklist
- [ ] Follows the Rule of One (one narrative, one promise, one emotion)
- [ ] Big, believable promise of result
- [ ] New and fresh (timely)
- [ ] Specific, concrete, tangible
- [ ] Intellectually interesting
- [ ] Easy to grasp

## THE CATEGORICAL IMPERATIVE (Bill Bonner / Agora)

When humans encounter information, they try to categorize it: "Can I put this in a bucket I already know?" If they CAN, they dismiss it. Your hook must PREVENT this categorization.

## THE BENEFIT-BELIEF MATRIX (David Deutsch)

| | High Belief | Low Belief |
|---|---|---|
| **High Benefit** | SOLD (the goal) | HYPE (they roll their eyes) |
| **Low Benefit** | WHO CARES (believed but boring) | START OVER |

### Benefit Dimensions
- **Impact:** How dramatic have you made the benefit?
- **Relevance:** Does it relate to something they actually feel?

### Belief Dimensions
- **Trust:** If they trust you, anything is believed without documentation
- **Proof:** Studies, track record, testimonials, common sense
- **Story:** Juries vote based on which story they believe; so do prospects

### The Third Dimension: Interest
All benefit and proof is worthless if they do not stay on the page. Maintain fanatical interest throughout.

## THE PMS FORMULA (Andrew Milligan)

Three components for differentiation, positioning, and persuasion:

1. **P = Paradigm** -- Introduce something brand new OR slay a false belief
2. **M = Mechanism / "IP"** -- The secret/proof behind WHY the new way works
3. **S = Sell** -- Scoop your demographic, overcome "will this work for me?"

### PMS Headline Formula
**[Proof] + [Mechanism / "IP"] + [Core Benefit Claim]**

- "Why Wayne Gretzky's 'puck-cue' lets you become unforgettable with customers"
- "The 'K-Signs' to adding $4,327 to your portfolio in 16 hours"

## THE DO/DON'T WORD STRATEGY (Arman Assadi)

Create two lists for your brand:
1. **DO words:** Words that resonate with your brand and mission
2. **DON'T words:** Words you want to push away from

Saying what you DON'T believe in is sometimes as powerful as saying what you DO believe in. It creates separation. Example: EVO Planner positioned around "flow" instead of "productivity" to create a blue ocean.

## THE EXTRAORDINARY PRESENTATION OF THE ORDINARY

When you cannot come up with something absolutely different, take the ordinary and find a different angle, perspective, or storyline to make it feel extraordinary. Not superlatives or exaggerated claims -- finding a unique angle nobody else is telling.
