# Mechanism Interrogation Guide

## Why Interrogation Is the Most Valuable Activity

Naming your mechanism is the LEAST important part. Understanding HOW and WHY it works is everything. The interrogation gives you the reasons why your method is superior -- which becomes the body of your entire campaign argument (the CPB chunks).

If someone asked you "Why is your method superior to every other method?" you must be able to answer clearly and specifically. The answers come from interrogation.

---

## THE COMPLETE INTERROGATION QUESTION BANK

### Tier 1: Foundation Questions (Always Start Here)

1. **What is the specific process, system, or method behind your product?**
   - Describe it step by step as if explaining to a smart 12-year-old
   - Do not use jargon or technical terms initially

2. **Why do you do it THIS way instead of other ways?**
   - What alternatives did you consider or try before arriving at this approach?
   - What was wrong with those alternatives?

3. **What makes your approach fundamentally DIFFERENT from what everyone else does?**
   - Not slightly better -- how is the underlying approach different?
   - If someone said "that's the same as [competitor]," how would you respond?

4. **Why does this specific order matter?**
   - What happens if you do step 3 before step 2?
   - Why does the sequence produce results that a different sequence would not?

5. **What happens if you remove one component?**
   - Take out each step individually. What breaks?
   - This reveals which components are essential (all of them should be)

### Tier 2: Superiority Questions

6. **Why is each piece necessary?**
   - What specific role does each component play?
   - What would the customer lose if that piece were missing?

7. **What result can someone expect that they CANNOT get from alternatives?**
   - What is the unique outcome only your method produces?
   - Why can competitors not replicate this result?

8. **What common approach does your method specifically replace?**
   - What is the "old way" that your market has tried and been disappointed by?
   - Why does the old way fail where yours succeeds?

9. **Is there scientific, research-based, or logical evidence for why this works?**
   - Any studies, data, or established principles that support the mechanism?
   - Any expert endorsements or third-party validation?

10. **What is the single most important insight that makes your method work?**
    - If you could only teach ONE thing to your customer, what would it be?
    - Why does this insight change everything?

### Tier 3: Story and Origin Questions

11. **How did you discover or develop this method?**
    - What problem were you trying to solve?
    - What failures or iterations led to the current approach?

12. **What was the "aha moment" that made everything click?**
    - When did you realize this approach was fundamentally different and better?
    - What were you doing or experiencing at that moment?

13. **Who were the first people to benefit from this method?**
    - What specific results did they get?
    - What did they say about the experience?

14. **What skepticism did you face initially?**
    - Who doubted this would work?
    - How were they proven wrong?

### Tier 4: Deepening Questions (For Richer Arguments)

15. **Why has nobody else done this before?**
    - What knowledge, experience, or insight do you have that others lack?
    - What makes this non-obvious?

16. **What is the biggest misconception about solving this problem?**
    - What does the market believe that is WRONG?
    - How does your mechanism correct this misconception?

17. **If you had to explain why this works to a scientist, what would you say?**
    - Strip away all marketing language
    - What is the objective, logical reason this produces results?

18. **What is the Unaddressed Root Problem (URP) your mechanism solves?**
    - Is there a deeper underlying cause that nobody else is addressing?
    - If so, how does your mechanism address it specifically?

19. **What would your best customer say is the reason this works?**
    - How do actual users explain the mechanism in their own words?
    - Their language is often more compelling than yours

20. **What makes this a REPLACEMENT, not just an improvement?**
    - Why is this not "a tweak to the old way"?
    - How is the fundamental approach different?

## WORKED EXAMPLE: ONLINE COURSE PLATFORM

### The Product
An online course platform that helps creators build and sell courses.

### Interrogation Answers

**Q: What is the specific process?**
A: A 3-phase system -- Validate (test course idea with minimum viable content), Build (structured template that guides course creation section by section), and Launch (built-in sales page + email sequence + payment processing).

**Q: Why this way instead of other ways?**
A: Most platforms make you build the entire course FIRST, then figure out if anyone wants it. We reverse the order -- validate demand BEFORE building. This means creators never waste months building something nobody buys.

**Q: What makes this fundamentally different?**
A: The validation phase. No other platform has a built-in demand-testing system. Competitors assume you already know what to teach. We assume you do not (because most new creators do not) and give you the tools to find out.

**Q: Why does the order matter?**
A: If you build before validating, you risk months of wasted effort. If you launch before building, you have nothing to deliver. Validate > Build > Launch is the only sequence that works.

**Q: What happens if you remove one component?**
A: Without Validate, you build in the dark. Without Build, you have a validated idea but no product. Without Launch, you have a product but no way to sell it. All three are essential.

### Campaign Thesis Generated from Interrogation

"The most effective way to create and sell an online course without wasting months building something nobody buys is with the Validate-First Method."

### CPB Chunks Generated from Interrogation

**CPB 1:** Most course creators fail because they build first and test demand later (Claim). 67% of first-time courses sell fewer than 10 copies (Proof). The Validate-First Method ensures you only build what people have already told you they want to buy (Benefit).

**CPB 2:** Traditional platforms assume you know what to teach (Claim). But research shows most new creators' assumptions about their audience are wrong (Proof). The built-in demand-testing system lets you discover exactly what your audience will pay for before you create a single lesson (Benefit).

**CPB 3:** Other platforms separate building from selling (Claim). This means creators spend weeks switching between tools, reformatting content, and troubleshooting integrations (Proof). Everything from course hosting to sales pages to payment processing lives in one system, so you launch in days instead of months (Benefit).

## WORKED EXAMPLE: FITNESS PROGRAM

### The Product
A 12-week strength training program for people over 40.

### Key Interrogation Answers

**Q: Why this way?**
A: Traditional programs are designed for 20-year-olds and modified for older bodies. This program is DESIGNED from the ground up for joints, recovery rates, and hormonal profiles of people over 40.

**Q: What makes this different?**
A: The recovery-first programming model. Every workout is built around your body's recovery capacity, not arbitrary volume targets. The program adapts weekly based on recovery markers.

**Q: What is the URP?**
A: Most people over 40 who try strength training get injured within the first 8 weeks because programs designed for younger bodies push their joints past their recovery capacity. The URP is not "lack of exercise" -- it is "exercise programs that ignore age-specific recovery needs."

### Campaign Thesis

"The safest, most effective way to build real strength after 40 without joint pain or injury is with Recovery-First Programming."

## TURNING INTERROGATION INTO COPY

The interrogation answers become your campaign argument:

1. Each answer to "why is this different/better?" becomes a CLAIM
2. Each supporting reason becomes PROOF
3. Each outcome for the customer becomes a BENEFIT
4. Stack 3-5 CPB chunks = complete buying belief established

The entire marketing argument section of your sales page or VSL is built from interrogation answers. This is why interrogation is the most valuable activity in the entire copywriting process.

---

## ADVANCED INTERROGATION QUESTIONS

### Tier 5: Market Positioning Questions

21. **What would your ideal client's life look like if they never found your method?**
    - Paint the "do nothing" scenario to sharpen the urgency of your mechanism

22. **What is the single thing your competitors get WRONG about solving this problem?**
    - The answer becomes a powerful contrast point in your marketing argument

23. **If a journalist asked you to explain why your approach works in 30 seconds, what would you say?**
    - Forces the most concise, compelling explanation of the mechanism

24. **What common advice in your industry do you believe is actively harmful?**
    - Contrarian positioning material that separates you from the market

25. **What is the smallest, simplest version of your method that still produces results?**
    - Reveals the essential core, which is often the most compelling selling point

## WORKED EXAMPLE: MARKETING AGENCY

### The Product
A marketing agency selling a done-for-you lead generation service to B2B SaaS companies.

### Key Interrogation Answers

**Q: What is the specific process?**
A: Three phases -- Audit (analyze the client's current lead sources and identify leaks), Build (create a multi-channel outbound system with email, LinkedIn, and retargeting working together), and Optimize (weekly data reviews that shift budget to the highest-performing channel in real time).

**Q: Why this way instead of other ways?**
A: Most agencies run a single channel in isolation -- just cold email, or just LinkedIn, or just paid ads. When one channel underperforms, results drop. We run all three simultaneously so channels support each other and we can shift budget weekly based on real data.

**Q: What makes this fundamentally different?**
A: The weekly reallocation. No other agency we know of moves budget between channels on a weekly basis. Most lock you into a 90-day plan and hope it works. We treat your budget like a living system that adapts every 7 days.

**Q: What is the URP?**
A: The real reason most B2B lead gen fails is not bad copy or wrong targeting -- it is channel dependency. When your entire pipeline runs through one channel, a single platform change (like LinkedIn's connection limit update) can kill your pipeline overnight. The URP is single-channel risk.

### Campaign Thesis

"The most reliable way to generate qualified B2B leads without being dependent on any single platform is with the Adaptive Pipeline System."

### CPB Chunks Generated

**CPB 1:** Most B2B companies rely on a single lead channel (Claim). When LinkedIn changed its connection limits in 2023, agencies using only LinkedIn outreach saw lead volume drop 40-60% overnight (Proof). The Adaptive Pipeline System runs three channels simultaneously so no single platform change can shut down your pipeline (Benefit).

**CPB 2:** Traditional agencies set a plan and run it for 90 days before reviewing (Claim). But market conditions shift weekly -- what worked Monday may not work Thursday (Proof/Reason-Why). The weekly reallocation cycle means your budget always flows to wherever your prospects are most responsive THIS week, not where they were responsive last quarter (Benefit).

**CPB 3:** Running multiple channels sounds expensive, but in practice it reduces cost-per-lead (Claim). Because channels reinforce each other -- a prospect who sees your LinkedIn content AND receives your email AND encounters your retargeting ad converts at 3.2x the rate of single-channel contact (Proof/Data). So you get more leads for the same budget (Benefit).
