# Big Idea & Unique Positioning

## What is a Big Idea? (And What It's NOT)

Your Big Idea is NOT:
- Your unique selling proposition (USP)
- Your mechanism (the HOW)
- Your offer (the WHAT you're selling)
- Your hook (the attention-grabber)

A Big Idea is an **intellectual and emotional concept** that sits BEHIND all your copy.

It's an idea so interesting that someone would forward it to a friend just because it's interesting—before you even mention your offer.

---

## The Three Components of a Big Idea

A Big Idea has exactly three components. Without all three, it's not big enough.

### Component 1: Intellectually Interesting

The reader thinks "I didn't know that" or "I never thought of it that way."

**Examples of intellectually interesting:**
- "The reason your diet fails isn't willpower. It's that you're fighting your neurobiology"
- "Most productivity tools make you LESS productive because they create their own busywork"
- "Your biggest competitors aren't other agencies. They're the client's internal team"

**Key:** It's unexpected. It reveals something the reader didn't see before. It's novel or a fresh angle on something known.

**Questions to ask:**
- What's counterintuitive about your solution?
- What do competitors get wrong?
- What does your customer not understand about their own problem?

### Component 2: Emotionally Compelling

The reader FEELS something. It prods their core buying emotions or aspirations.

**Examples of emotionally compelling:**
- "Imagine the freedom of a full day with zero meetings"
- "What if your body finally worked WITH you instead of against you?"
- "Picture yourself as the expert everyone comes to for answers"

**Key:** It connects to desire, aspiration, fear, or identity. It's not just interesting—it's *resonant*.

**Emotions to consider:**
- Desire (what do they want?)
- Fear (what do they want to avoid?)
- Identity (who do they want to be?)
- Status (how do they want to be seen?)

### Component 3: Unique to Your Mechanism

Competitors cannot claim the same thing.

This is where your mechanism (your unique method/process/approach) becomes the foundation of your Big Idea.

**Examples:**
- "You can't build muscle by doing 1-hour workouts. You need the right 15-minute protocol." (Unique mechanism: 15-minute protocol)
- "Lead generation software doesn't work because it treats all prospects the same. You need to segment by intent." (Unique mechanism: intent-based segmentation)

**Key:** Your Big Idea is ONLY yours because of how you solve the problem.

**Questions to ask:**
- What's unique about how you solve this?
- Why does your way work when others don't?
- What's the insight that others are missing?

## Testing if Your Idea is Big Enough

**The Forwarding Test:**

"Would a prospect forward this idea to a friend purely because it's interesting?"

- If YES → Your idea is big enough
- If NO → Your idea isn't big enough; keep developing

**Examples:**

**Too Small (wouldn't forward):**
- "We have better customer service"
- "Our software is easier to use"
- (These are table stakes, not ideas)

**Big Enough (would forward):**
- "Your CRM is making you lose deals because it's designed for record-keeping, not selling"
- "Most weight loss fails in week 3, not week 1. That's because your brain chemistry resets. Here's what actually works"
- (These are interesting ideas worth sharing)

## From Mechanism to Big Idea

Your mechanism (HOW you solve the problem) is the foundation.

### Step 1: Interrogate Your Mechanism

This is the single most valuable activity according to Todd Brown.

Don't focus on NAMING your mechanism. Focus on UNDERSTANDING it.

**Questions to ask:**

1. **Why do we do it this way?**
   - Example: "Why do we segment by intent?"
   - Answer: "Because intent determines readiness to buy"

2. **Why is this step important?**
   - Example: "Why is intent assessment first?"
   - Answer: "Because wasting energy on unready prospects kills conversion"

3. **What's the logic flow?**
   - Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3, and why this order?

4. **What's superior about our approach vs. others?**
   - We do X vs. competitors do Y
   - Our way is better because Z

5. **What's the insight that makes this work?**
   - What did we discover that others miss?

### Step 2: Extract the Core Insight

From interrogating your mechanism, pull out the core insight.

**Example interrogation:**
- Q: "Why does your 15-minute workout protocol work?"
- A: "Because muscle growth happens in the first 15 minutes. After that, cortisol rises and breaks down muscle."
- Insight: "You've been wasting time after the growth window closes"

**Example interrogation:**
- Q: "Why do you segment by intent first?"
- A: "Because intent tells us if someone is ready to buy. All other factors matter less than readiness."
- Insight: "Most tools focus on fit; we focus on readiness"

### Step 3: Build the Idea

From your core insight, build the Big Idea:

**Formula:**
[Core Insight] + [Emotional Impact] + [Uniqueness] = Big Idea

**Examples:**

**Insight:** "You've been wasting time after the growth window closes"
+ **Emotional:** "What if your body actually worked with you?"
+ **Unique:** "Our 15-minute method"
= **Big Idea:** "You're wasting 75% of your workout time. Muscle growth peaks in the first 15 minutes, then cortisol destroys your gains. One simple protocol fixes everything."

## The Three Core Questions for Your Big Idea

Before finalizing, answer these:

1. **Is this intellectually interesting to your customer?**
   - Would they think "I didn't know that"?
   - If not, make it more unexpected/novel

2. **Is this emotionally compelling?**
   - Does it touch on desire, fear, identity, or status?
   - If not, connect it to a deeper emotion

3. **Is this unique to YOU?**
   - Can competitors claim the same thing?
   - If yes, dig deeper into what makes YOUR way different

## Primary Promise (The Promise Side of Your Idea)

Once you have your Big Idea, distill it into your Primary Promise.

Your Primary Promise is:
- **Big, bold, and audacious** (if it's not exciting, nothing else matters)
- **True and believable** (grounded in reality)
- **Specific, concrete, tangible** (NOT vague)
- **Measurable** (has weight and impact)
- **Visualizable** (prospect can conjure it in their mind)

### Formulation Techniques

**The Magic Wand Technique:**
"If I could wave a magic wand and grant your customers anything, what's the one thing that would change everything?"

**The Ideal Client Exercise:**
"Imagine your greatest success story. What EXACT result did they get? What did they experience? What was the transformation?"

### Specificity Rule

- Vague: "Lose weight"
- Specific: "Lose 10 pounds in 30 days without hunger"
- Better: "Lose 10 pounds in 30 days, keep it off, and never feel deprived"

Specificity = credibility.

**Examples of strong primary promises:**
- "Get from zero to 100K Twitter followers in 90 days using a repeatable system"
- "Cut your lead generation cost in half while tripling lead quality"
- "Go from brand unknown to recognized authority in your niche in 6 months"

## Unique Mechanism (The HOW Behind Your Idea)

Your Unique Mechanism is the HOW. It's what gives your promise credibility.

Don't name it with a branded term (it sounds scammy). Instead, explain it clearly.

**Bad (Branded naming):**
- "The XYZ Method"
- "The 5-Step Blueprint"
- (Prospect doesn't know what it is)

**Good (Clear explanation):**
- "We start by segmenting your list by purchase intent, not demographics"
- "Your results depend on the order: foundation first, then expansion, then optimization"
- "We use a three-layer proof model: claim proof, social proof, and the power of three"

### Interrogating Your Mechanism (Deep Dive)

For each step of your mechanism:

1. **What is this step?** (Define it clearly)
2. **Why do we do this step?** (Reasoning)
3. **What's different about how we do it?** (Our unique approach)
4. **Why does our approach work better?** (Why ours > others)

This interrogation becomes the language in your copy.

## The Offer Solution Framework

Once you have your Big Idea, Primary Promise, and Unique Mechanism, you have your Offer Solution.

### The Bare Bones Formula

Three components:

1. **Targeted Problem** — What problem are you solving?
2. **Primary Promise** — What's the big result?
3. **Unique Mechanism** — How do you do it?

### The Formula

"The [statement of superiority] way to [Primary Promise] is with [Unique Mechanism]."

**Examples:**

- "The most effective way to go from zero to recognized authority is with the three-layer positioning framework"
- "The fastest way to cut lead costs in half is by segmenting by purchase intent first"
- "The only way to keep muscle gains is to understand your growth window is 15 minutes"

### With a Catalyst (Timely Hook)

If there's a timely trigger or urgency:

"The [statement of superiority] way to take advantage of [Catalyst] so you can [Primary Promise] is with [Unique Mechanism]."

**Example:**
"The most effective way to leverage the iOS update so you can maintain tracking accuracy is with our first-party data collection method"

## Big Idea Development Process

### Brainstorm Phase (Weekly thinking)

Spend dedicated time on idea generation.

- Generate LOTS of ideas (volume leads to breakthroughs)
- Look for unusual combinations
- Jump into the conversation already happening in prospects' minds
- Question: What is top of mind for your prospect? What are they discussing?

### Execution Phase

1. **List 5 potential Big Ideas** (don't edit, just brain dump)
2. **For each, test the three components:**
   - Is it intellectually interesting?
   - Is it emotionally compelling?
   - Is it unique to your mechanism?
3. **For each, ask the forwarding test:**
   - "Would someone share this just because it's interesting?"
4. **Select your strongest idea**
5. **Build Primary Promise from it**
6. **Articulate your Unique Mechanism**

### Refinement Phase

- Only write copy for ideas YOU are wildly excited about
- If you're not excited, your copy will feel flat
- Go back to idea development
- Avoid "curse of knowledge" — present ordinary info in extraordinary ways

## Common Big Idea Mistakes

**Too Generic:**
- "We provide the best customer service"
- (Competitors claim same thing)

**Not Intellectually Interesting:**
- "Our software is easy to use"
- (Yes, so is everything else)

**Not Unique to You:**
- "You can automate your email with artificial intelligence"
- (So can 50 competitors)

**Tries to Appeal to Everyone:**
- "Works for solopreneurs and enterprises"
- (Rule of One: pick one audience, one message)

**Not Connected to a Real Mechanism:**
- "Change your life in 30 days"
- (How? Why 30 days specifically?)
