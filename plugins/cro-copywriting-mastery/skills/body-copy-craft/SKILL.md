# Body Copy, Sentence Craft & Writing Technique

This skill covers every decision you make at the sentence and paragraph level. Headlines get you the click. Body copy closes the deal. Every sentence must earn its place, move the reader forward, and make the next sentence irresistible.

---

## CORE PRINCIPLE: THE SLIPPERY SLIDE

Joe Sugarman's foundational concept: the job of the first sentence is to get the reader to read the second sentence. The job of the second sentence is to get them to read the third. Every sentence exists to propel the reader forward. The moment a sentence stops pulling, the reader leaves.

This means every line must do one of three things:
1. Advance the argument
2. Deepen the emotion
3. Create curiosity that demands resolution

If a sentence does none of these, delete it.

## THE VERB SELECTION PROCESS (3 Steps)

Weak copy is driven by adjectives and adverbs. Strong copy is driven by VERBS. This is the single most impactful writing improvement you can make.

### Step 1: Ask "What IMAGE am I trying to convey?"
Speed? Volume? Ease? Passivity? Aggression? Precision?

### Step 2: Ask "What FEELING do I want to trigger?"
Fear? Excitement? Relief? Confidence? Urgency?

### Step 3: Find a verb that conveys BOTH simultaneously.

**Examples:**
- "Get more leads" → "Flood your business with leads" (volume + excitement)
- "He was behind the counter" → "He lurked behind the counter" (hiding + fear)
- "Bob came on the scene" → "Bob rushed onto the scene" (speed + urgency)
- "slowly walk" → "strut" / "gently touch" → "caress" / "loudly yell" → "scream"

**Rule: When choosing between conciseness and the right image, prioritize the image.** A slightly longer sentence with the right verb beats a shorter sentence with a dead verb.

### Dead Verbs to Eliminate
Have, do, get, show, use, make, put, go, take, give. These are vague, fuzzy, and static. Replace every one.

### Verb Enhancement Techniques
- **Present tense for immediacy:** Grew → Growing; Scaled → Scaling; Saved → Saving
- **Pluralize for ownership:** "Put music in your life" → "Puts music in your life"
- Use PowerThesaurus.com to find alternatives

## BINARY WRITING

Each sentence should contain ONE dominant thought. If you can split that thought into one or two sentences, you win.

**Binary Done RIGHT:**
```
It flies smack in the face of everything we've ever known about growing older.

Because the key to aging well isn't your heart, brain, or your immune system.

And it's not your blood, collagen, or anything else like that.

It's not even your hormones.

It's a strange code hidden deep inside your natural DNA.
```

**Binary Done WRONG (chunky, harder to read):**
```
It flies smack in the face of everything we've ever known about growing older because the key to aging well isn't your heart, brain, or immune system. And it's not your blood, collagen, or anything else like that. It's a strange molecule hidden deep inside your genetic code.
```

### Binary Writing Rules
1. One thought per sentence, maximum
2. Short sentences create pace and urgency
3. Long sentences slow the reader for emphasis — use deliberately
4. Mix sentence length to create rhythm: short-short-short-LONG-short
5. Pattern: "It either works... or it doesn't." / "You're with me... or against me."

## ACTIVE VS. PASSIVE VOICE

Active voice is more engaging, more direct, and more pleasant to read. Use it as default. Reserve passive voice only when the actor is unknown or unimportant.

| Passive (Weak) | Active (Strong) |
|----------------|----------------|
| The passengers were asked to return | The captain forced passengers back |
| Clicks were generated like crazy | The ad attracted clicks like crazy |
| The report was written by the team | The team wrote the report |
| Results were achieved | They achieved results |

**Test:** If you can add "by zombies" to the end and it makes sense, it is passive voice. "The report was written [by zombies]" = passive. Fix it.

## SENTENCE STRUCTURE PRINCIPLES

### Primacy and Recency
The most important idea belongs at the BEGINNING or END of a sentence. Never buried in the middle.

- GOOD: "It's astonishing the amount of fat you lose when you do this."
- BAD: "You'll be astonished, when you do this, at the amount of fat you drop."

### The Power of Three
Lists of three are inherently satisfying and memorable.
- "Stop, Drop, and Roll"
- "Life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness"
- "Lights, Camera, Action"

Use groups of three for benefits, proof points, and descriptors. Two feels incomplete. Four feels excessive. Three is perfect.

### Pronoun Clarity
When a pronoun is ambiguous, replace it with the noun.
- BAD: "Tom got to John's house. And he was pissed." (Who?)
- GOOD: "Tom got to John's house. And John was pissed."

## WORD SUBSTITUTIONS (Quick Reference)

| Replace | With | Why |
|---------|------|-----|
| "And" | "But" | "But" negates what came before; use "And" when both are positive |
| "If" | "When" | "When" presupposes certainty: "When you're struggling" > "If you're struggling" |
| Embarrass | Humiliate | Stronger emotional charge |
| Take | Swipe | More visceral |
| Beat | Pummeled | More dramatic |
| Won | Dominated | More commanding |
| Tired | Exhausted | Deeper fatigue |
| Broke | Crushed | More devastating |

**Trim "that":** Overuse makes writing sound weak (Donald Miller). Replace "that" with "which" where possible, or delete entirely.

---

## ADJECTIVE RULES

1. Only use adjectives when they ADD information the reader does not already have
2. Never use fluff adjectives: amazing, incredible, fantastic, superb, outstanding, great, excellent, extraordinary, awesome, top-shelf
3. Replace adverb + adjective with one better adjective: "Really giving" → "Generous"
4. If an adjective can be removed without losing meaning, remove it

---

## EVOCATIVE, NOT EMOTIVE

Do not share YOUR emotions. Stimulate THEIR emotions.

- BAD: "I'm so excited to tell you..."
- GOOD: "You'll be excited when you hear..."
- BAD: "We're thrilled to announce..."
- GOOD: "This changes everything about how you..."

Pair evocative, sensory language with the pain: "No more sitting in silence, no more feeling anxious on calls."

## READABILITY TARGETS

- **Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level:** Target below 8 (middle school reading level)
- Write like you talk. 50% of coaching copywriters is asking: "Would you ever say this to someone in person?"
- Use simple words. Bill Bonner at Agora would strike "utilize" and replace with "use." Nobody talks that way in conversation.
- George Orwell: "Never use a long word where a short one will do."

## FORMATTING FOR SKIMMERS

People do not read — they scan. Format for scanning first, reading second.

### White Space Rules
- One to two sentences per paragraph, maximum
- One-line paragraphs are ideal for most copy
- The less intimidating the page looks, the more people read

### Scannable Elements
- **Bold** key phrases (the ones that carry meaning even if the reader reads nothing else)
- Use subheads every 3-5 paragraphs to re-engage skimmers
- Bullet points for lists of 3+ items
- Pull quotes or callout boxes for critical insights
- Subheads must TEASE, not summarize (create curiosity to keep reading)

### Odd Number Rule
When listing bullet points, always use odd numbers: 3, 5, 7. Odd numbers feel more credible and specific. End bullet lists with "And so much more..." as the 3rd or 5th item.

## PRESUPPOSITIONAL LANGUAGE

### Presuppositional Statements
- "If [activity prospect does], then [action you want]." — Presupposes they are the right person.
- "Every successful [identity] knows that [claim]." — Presupposes established wisdom.
- "Every time you [wrong action], [negative consequence]." — Presupposes the problem exists.

### Presuppositional Questions
- "So, what makes [mechanism] so effective at [result], you may be wondering?" — Presupposes effectiveness.
- "So, how quickly will you [result] when using [mechanism]?" — Presupposes the result will happen.

### Framing Claims as Facts
- "You already know that..."
- "There's no question that..."
- "Obviously..."
- "The fact is..."
- "We can agree that..."

## COPY MASSAGING — STRENGTHENING SENTENCES

Start with a basic benefit and progressively add layers:

1. MEH: "How to alter your mindset"
2. + INTRIGUE: "How to naturally alter your mindset using nothing but your index finger"
3. + WORD PLAY: "How to rapidly unleash a warrior mindset using nothing but your index finger"
4. + TIME: "How to alter your mindset in 17 minutes using nothing but your index finger"
5. + SPECIFICS: "How to reboot your warrior mindset 97% using nothing but your index finger (takes less than 17 minutes)"
6. + PROOF: "Why Michael Jordan's mindset practice helps reboot your state in as little as 17 minutes"
7. + POSITIONING: "Why this Aztec Warrior mindset practice helps reboot your mindset to 97% clarity base"

**Power words for massaging:** "without," "even if," "plus," "in just," "starting today"

## LOADED LANGUAGE

A word is "loaded" when it carries a secondary evaluative meaning beyond its primary descriptive meaning. Use this strategically.

| Neutral | Loaded (Negative) | Loaded (Positive) |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Plant | Weed | Herb |
| Insect | Pest | Pollinator |
| Reduced | Slashed | Streamlined |
| Pro-Abortion | — | Pro-Choice |

Choose words that steer perception toward your desired frame.

---

## THE DAMAGING ADMISSION

Admit an insignificant negative and turn it into a positive. This builds massive credibility.

- "Maybe it's not the prettiest looking book, but it will certainly give you a thick lawn."
- "This only does one thing. That's because we wanted to focus on making it the best."

**Advanced:** Combine damaging admission + takeaway: "So if you're looking for a mediocre 'one app does it all' kind of thing, this is not for you."

---

## "YOU" AND SECOND-PERSON EMPHASIS

"You" is the most important word in copy. Center everything on the prospect. Count the ratio of "you/your" to "I/we/our" in every paragraph. The ratio should be at least 3:1 in favor of "you."

---

## SCOOPABILITY — WRITING FOR WIDE AUDIENCES

On a sales call, you talk to ONE person about THEIR specific problem. In copy, you need to "scoop" as many of the right people as possible.

**Scoopable tactics:**
- "So it doesn't matter if you're 18 or 55..."
- "Works even if you're 35 or 75"
- Address the demographic broadly, then show it is naturally within their reach
- Use universal scenarios the majority can insert themselves into

---

## TONE OF VOICE AND PERSONA

Tone = mix of word choice + sentence structure + writing technique + consistency.

**Key insight from Porter Stansberry:** "It's not the person the reader responds to — it's the PERSONA." The persona (worldview, style, attitude) can be replicated by studying tone, word choices, and perspective.

### Tone Checklist
- Formal vs. informal?
- Technical vs. conversational?
- Authoritative vs. friendly?
- Provocative vs. reassuring?

Match tone to your audience. Never default to formal when conversational works. Bill Bonner: "No one says that in conversation."
