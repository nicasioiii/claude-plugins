# Customer Research & Voice-of-Customer Mining

## THE FOUNDATIONAL RULE

Research comes before writing. Always. A-list copywriters spend 80% of their time researching and 20% writing. The best copy is not invented -- it is discovered in the language, pain, and desires of your prospects. Skip research and you are guessing. Guessing loses.

## THE ONE READER DECISION

Every landing page, email, and ad speaks to ONE reader. Not two. Not a vague demographic. One specific human being.

### How to Identify Your One Reader

1. Survey your paying customers and your site visitors separately
2. Compare the two groups -- who BUYS is different from who VISITS
3. Write for the 20-35% of traffic most likely to be your ideal segment
4. The ideal prospect has five qualities: needs what you offer, buys from you, is happy with the purchase, tells their peers, and keeps buying

**Rule:** Multiple landing pages for different segments beats one catch-all page every time.

### Person vs. Persona (The Eddie Shleyner Principle)

A buyer persona is a Frankenstein -- a composite of made-up characteristics. Consumers detect what is fake. Instead:

- Base your copy on ONE real person's singular point of view
- Conduct a personal interview with an ideal prospect
- Use mirroring: repeat the last thing they said back as a question
- Record the interview (do not take notes -- it distracts)
- The authentic language of one real person resonates more than any composite

**When to use personas:** When you have zero access to real customers (pre-launch). Switch to real-person research as soon as you have paying customers.

## THE EXAMINATION TRIAD

Research in this exact order (Todd Brown):

1. **Prospects** -- who you are talking to (pain, desires, beliefs, awareness level)
2. **Competitors** -- what the market has already heard (hooks, claims, mechanisms, offers)
3. **Product** -- your own offering examined fresh (as if you were an outside copywriter)

Never start with the product. You will fall in love with features nobody cares about.

## PROSPECT RESEARCH -- WHAT TO UNCOVER

For every project, answer these questions before writing a single line:

| Dimension | Key Questions |
|-----------|--------------|
| **Problem** | What is their most pressing, most urgent problem? |
| **Wants/Desires** | What is the ideal outcome? What do they want to experience that they are not? |
| **Emotions** | Is their core state more fear-based or hope-based? |
| **Beliefs** | Are there beliefs that, if contradicted, would cause disconnect? |
| **Awareness Level** | Where are they on the Schwartz scale: Unaware through Most Aware? |
| **Language** | What exact words and phrases do they use to describe their problem? |
| **Buying Triggers** | What makes them buy? What makes them NOT buy? |
| **Objections** | What specific fears prevent action: financial loss, loss of face, time waste, hassle? |

### The Sandy Franks 6 Research Questions

Apply these in LAYERS -- answer one, then ask again to go deeper (Tree Branch Effect):

- **WHO**: Central character, who aided them, stakeholders, personality traits
- **WHAT**: What happened, what created the situation, what reactions occurred
- **WHERE**: Setting, where they operate, what the environment is like
- **WHEN**: Timeline, when did the problem start, when does it peak
- **WHY**: Why this choice, why does it matter, why can they not solve it alone
- **HOW**: How is it possible, how do they feel about it, how have they tried to fix it

Each answer branches into new questions. One research finding leads to another. This branching is how rich customer understanding is built.

## VOC DATA SOURCE DECISION MATRIX

| Method | Best When | Time | Data Quality | Sample Size |
|--------|-----------|------|--------------|-------------|
| **Interviews** | You need deep motivators and emotional language | 45-60 min each | Highest | 7 interviews = ~500 survey responses |
| **Surveys** | You need to validate patterns across segments | 1-2 weeks to collect | High for patterns | 100+ respondents |
| **Review Mining** | You need sticky language and objections | 2-4 hours per source | High for language | N/A |
| **On-Site Polls** | You need to understand why visitors leave | Ongoing | Medium-high | Varies |
| **User Testing** | You need to see how people interact with pages | 3-5 tests per round | Uniquely high for UX | 3-5 users |
| **Chat/Support Transcripts** | You need real objections and friction points | 1-2 hours to review | High for objections | N/A |

**VOC messages have approximately a 2-year shelf life** unless the business or audience changes significantly.

## TOP 5 SURVEY QUESTIONS

These are the highest-value open-ended questions from the Copy Hackers methodology:

1. **"In your own words, what is [Product]?"** -- Reveals value proposition language your prospects actually use.
2. **"What was going on in your world that compelled you to look for a solution like [Product]?"** -- Reveals top motivators and triggering events.
3. **"What nearly stopped you from buying?"** -- Reveals primary objections to address in copy.
4. **"What is the single biggest benefit you have experienced?"** -- Reveals the One Big Idea candidates.
5. **"How would you describe [Product] to a friend?"** -- Reveals natural positioning language.

**Survey rules:**
- Survey ONLY to find what to say and how to say it -- not demographics
- Survey different segments separately: visitors, trial users, new customers, long-term customers, former customers
- Use logic jumps to route respondents to relevant questions
- Offer a simple incentive ($25 gift card) -- enough to show up, not so much they feel bought

## REVIEW MINING PROCESS (STEP BY STEP)

1. Identify 5-10 review sources: Amazon book reviews in your niche, Reddit threads, forums, Yelp, TripAdvisor, G2/Capterra, niche marketplaces
2. Read reviews WITHOUT interpreting or summarizing -- document verbatim
3. Look for sticky language: phrases that are emotionally charged, specific, or unusually vivid
4. Copy phrases verbatim with quotation marks
5. Categorize findings: pain language, desire language, objection language, transformation language
6. Look for patterns: what phrases or themes appear across multiple reviews?
7. Flag the strongest 10-15 phrases as headline/hook candidates

**If you mirror prospects' own words on the page, they see themselves and connect.**

### Proven Review Mining Results

- "If you think you need rehab, you do" (Amazon book review) = 20%+ more leads for a rehab center
- "Big bum, thick waist, not super perky boobs" (forum language) = 125% more clicks to start a trial
- "Eliminate up to 99% of paper" (testimonial) = winning headline when all others failed

## INTERVIEW BEST PRACTICES

- **7 interviews = messaging gold equivalent to approximately 500 survey respondents**
- Schedule 20 interviews to get 7 (people flake)
- Use two-person interviewing teams (one drives conversation, both take notes)
- Set expectations: duration (45-60 min), anonymization, no pitching
- Do NOT over-script -- let their answers guide follow-up questions
- In person > video call > phone
- Reward with $25 gift card

### Interview Question Bank (Top 10)

1. Walk me through the moment you decided you needed a solution
2. What had you already tried before finding us?
3. What was the hardest part of your old approach?
4. When you describe this problem to a friend, what do you say?
5. What nearly stopped you from buying/signing up?
6. What was the single biggest result you got?
7. What surprised you most about using the product?
8. If you had to describe us in one sentence, what would you say?
9. What would you miss most if we disappeared tomorrow?
10. Is there anything I should have asked but did not?

## COMPETITOR RESEARCH CHECKLIST

For each of your top 3-5 competitors, document:

- [ ] Their primary hook/headline angle
- [ ] Their unique selling proposition (if any)
- [ ] Their unique mechanism claim
- [ ] Their offer structure (price, bonuses, guarantee)
- [ ] Their primary proof elements
- [ ] Common language and phrases they use
- [ ] What they do NOT address (gaps = your opportunity)
- [ ] Where their reviews and social proof come from
- [ ] What objections their customers raise in reviews

**The goal:** Know what the market has already heard so you know what NOT to say. Different beats same.

## QUICK AVATAR WORKSHEET

Complete this for every new project (5 min per section):

### Section 1: Demographics (Skip if Irrelevant)
- Age range, occupation, income, education

### Section 2: Frustrations and Fears
- What keeps them up at night?
- What is standing in their way?
- What are they afraid of losing?

### Section 3: Wants and Aspirations
- What do they want for themselves?
- What are their secret desires (go deeper than surface level)?
- What does success look like to them?

### Section 4: Buying Triggers
- Must-have features
- Price/budget expectations
- Deal killers and common objections

### Section 5: Before-After Grid (5 Dimensions)
- Have: What they have vs. will have
- Feel: Emotional state before vs. after
- Average Day: Daily life before vs. after
- Status: How they/others see them before vs. after
- Good vs. Evil: What philosophical evil they fight vs. defeat

## NICHE DRILLING (Arman Assadi Method)

Go from broad to hyper-specific, layer by layer:

**Example:** Pet Industry > Dogs > Small Dogs > Shih Tzu > Shih Tzu Show Dog Training

The narrower your niche, the more specific your language, and the higher your conversion rate. Broad targeting means broad copy that resonates with nobody.

**Validation tools:**
- Google Trends (is the niche growing or declining?)
- Related keyword research (what adjacent terms do they search?)
- Competitor density (is there money being spent here?)

## JOBS-TO-BE-DONE FRAMEWORK

Beyond demographics and psychographics, understand what JOB the customer is hiring your product to do:

- What progress are they trying to make in their life?
- What are the functional, emotional, and social dimensions of that progress?
- What are they firing (the old solution) and hiring (your solution)?
