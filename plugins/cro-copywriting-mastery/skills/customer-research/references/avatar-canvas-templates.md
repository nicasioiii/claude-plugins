# Customer Avatar Canvas Templates

Three frameworks side by side: Ryan Deiss (DigitalMarketer), Arman Assadi (7-Figure Copywriting), and OnePeak Creative. Choose the one that fits your project, or combine elements from all three.

---

## FRAMEWORK 1: RYAN DEISS CUSTOMER AVATAR CANVAS

### Overview

The Deiss framework is the most structured. It combines demographic, psychographic, and behavioral data with the Before-After Grid. Best for established businesses with customer data.

### The Canvas (5 Minutes Per Section)

#### Section A: Demographics
- Age range
- Gender (if relevant)
- Occupation / Industry
- Household income
- Education level
- Location / Geography
- Marital / Family status

**Rule:** Skip any demographic that is NOT a meaningful differentiator for your product. If income does not affect buying behavior, leave it blank.

#### Section B: Frustrations and Fears
- What is the biggest frustration in their [relevant area of life]?
- What do they worry about at 2 AM?
- What keeps coming back as an unsolved problem?
- What are they afraid will happen if they do not act?
- What do they dread about their current situation?

*Go deeper than surface level.* "Frustration with their weight" is surface. "Dreading the family beach trip because they cannot stand how they look in a swimsuit" is deep.

#### Section C: Wants and Aspirations
- What do they want for themselves in the next 12 months?
- What are their secret desires (the ones they would not post on social media)?
- What does success look like in their mind?
- Who do they admire and want to emulate?
- What lifestyle do they picture for themselves?

*Secret desires are where the real copy lives.* The stated desire is "grow my business." The secret desire is "prove my parents wrong about leaving my corporate job."

#### Section D: Key Purchase Drivers
- Must-have features (deal-breakers if missing)
- Price sensitivity / budget range
- Quality expectations
- Service expectations
- Deal killers (what would make them walk away immediately)
- Common objections (what makes them hesitate)

#### Section E: Before-After Grid
Complete the 5-dimension grid from the before-after-grid.md reference file:
- Have, Feel, Average Day, Status, Good vs. Evil

### How to Use the Deiss Canvas

1. Complete one canvas per market segment
2. Involve customer-facing team members (support, sales, community managers)
3. Use post-it notes for brainstorming, then prioritize
4. The top items from each section become your messaging hierarchy
5. Revisit every 6-12 months or when business/audience changes

## FRAMEWORK 2: ARMAN ASSADI "LITTLE JOHNNY" AVATAR

### Overview

The Assadi framework is the most empathetic. It starts with naming your avatar and "falling in love" with them. Best for creators, coaches, and personal brands who serve a passionate niche.

### The Process

#### Step 1: Name Your Avatar

Give your target customer a real name. "Little Johnny" is Arman's example. This forces you to think of them as a real human being, not a demographic profile.

**Why this matters:** When you write to "women aged 25-34 interested in fitness," you write vague, watered-down copy. When you write to "Sarah, the 29-year-old marketing manager who has been trying to lose the 15 pounds she gained during COVID," you write copy that resonates.

#### Step 2: Audience Profile Checklist

Answer every question in detail:

**Identity:**
- Who are they? (role, life stage, self-image)
- How do they like to be spoken to? (formal, casual, motivational, tough love)
- What do they love? (hobbies, interests, values)
- Where do they hang out? (online and offline)
- What brands do they follow and trust?

**Psychology:**
- What are their biggest fears and pain points?
- What makes them buy?
- What makes them NOT buy?
- What are their trigger words? (words that get their attention)
- What do they want their life to be like in 5-10 years?

**Behavior:**
- What is their typical buying process?
- How long do they research before purchasing?
- What information do they need to make a decision?
- Who influences their decisions?
- What platforms do they use to find solutions?

#### Step 3: Niche Drilling

Go from broad to hyper-specific, layer by layer:

**Example progressions:**
- Health > Weight Loss > Women's Weight Loss > Post-Pregnancy Weight Loss > Post-Pregnancy Weight Loss for Working Moms
- Business > Marketing > Digital Marketing > Email Marketing > Email Marketing for E-Commerce Brands Under $1M
- Pets > Dogs > Small Dogs > Shih Tzu > Shih Tzu Show Dog Training

Each layer of specificity makes your copy sharper and more resonant.

#### Step 4: Research Methods

- Talk to actual humans (primary research is the most important)
- Go into stores and randomly talk to strangers who fit the avatar
- Deep research on Google, Facebook, blogs, places they read
- Use data collection tools like Typeform
- Identify buying patterns and competitor pricing

#### Step 5: Validation

- Use Google Trends to confirm the niche is growing, not declining
- Look for related keywords to expand your understanding
- Verify there is money being spent in the niche (competitor ads running = money being made)

## FRAMEWORK 3: ONEPEAK 4-COMPONENT AVATAR

### Overview

The OnePeak framework is the most action-oriented. It focuses specifically on the four components that directly impact copy decisions. Best for performance marketers and media buyers.

### The 4 Components

#### Component 1: The Problem
- What is the specific, urgent problem they need solved?
- How is this problem affecting their daily life?
- How long have they been dealing with it?
- What have they tried that has not worked?

**Go specific:** Not "they want to lose weight" but "they have tried 3 different diets in the last year, lost weight each time, and gained it back plus more. They are now heavier than when they started and feel like failures."

#### Component 2: The Desired Outcome
- What does their ideal outcome look like in concrete, measurable terms?
- What does success feel like?
- How quickly do they expect results?
- What is the minimum outcome that would satisfy them?

**Be measurable:** Not "feel better about their body" but "fit into the jeans they wore 3 years ago and feel confident enough to post a beach photo."

#### Component 3: The Objections
- What are their top 3 reasons for NOT buying?
- What past experiences have created skepticism?
- What do they believe about products like yours?
- What financial, time, or effort barriers do they perceive?

**Each objection needs a proof point.** If the objection is "this won't work for someone my age," you need a testimonial from someone their age.

#### Component 4: The Buying Triggers
- What specific event or moment triggers them to start looking for a solution?
- What emotional state are they in when they are ready to buy?
- What external pressure (deadline, event, health scare) creates urgency?
- What level of social proof do they need to pull the trigger?

## JOBS-TO-BE-DONE LAYER (ADDITIVE)

Add this to any framework above for deeper insight:

### The Core Question
"What job is the customer hiring your product to do?"

### Three Dimensions of the Job

**Functional Job:**
- What practical task do they need accomplished?
- What process are they trying to improve?
- What output do they need to produce?

**Emotional Job:**
- How do they want to feel during the process?
- How do they want to feel after?
- What emotional state are they trying to avoid?

**Social Job:**
- How do they want to be perceived by others?
- What group do they want to belong to?
- What identity do they want to project?

### Hiring and Firing

- **Firing:** What solution are they currently using (and what is wrong with it)?
- **Hiring:** What would the ideal replacement do differently?
- **Switching cost:** What effort, money, or risk is involved in switching from the old to the new?

## CHOOSING THE RIGHT FRAMEWORK

| Situation | Recommended Framework |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Established business with customer data | Deiss Canvas (most structured, uses real data) |
| Coach, creator, or personal brand | Assadi "Little Johnny" (most empathetic) |
| Performance marketing / paid ads | OnePeak 4-Component (most action-oriented) |
| Pre-launch with no customers | Assadi + JTBD (empathy + job focus fills data gaps) |
| Complex B2B with multiple stakeholders | Deiss Canvas per stakeholder + JTBD layer |

## THE ONE-PAGE AVATAR SUMMARY

After completing any framework, distill into this one-page format:

**Avatar Name:** [Name]

**In one sentence:** [Name] is a [role/identity] who struggles with [core problem] and wants [core desire] but is held back by [core objection].

**Top 3 Pains:**
1. [Pain 1 -- in their words]
2. [Pain 2 -- in their words]
3. [Pain 3 -- in their words]

**Top 3 Desires:**
1. [Desire 1 -- in their words]
2. [Desire 2 -- in their words]
3. [Desire 3 -- in their words]

**Top 3 Objections:**
1. [Objection 1 -- in their words]
2. [Objection 2 -- in their words]
3. [Objection 3 -- in their words]

**Buying Trigger:** [The specific event or moment that makes them start looking]

**Core Emotional State:** [Fear-based / Hope-based / Both]

**Language to Use:** [3-5 verbatim phrases from their world]

**Language to Avoid:** [3-5 words or phrases that would alienate them]

This one-page summary is your copy brief. Pin it above your desk for every project.
