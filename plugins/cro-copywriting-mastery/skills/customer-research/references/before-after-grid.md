# Before-After Grid -- The Complete Framework

## Source: Ryan Deiss (DigitalMarketer)

Ryan Deiss considers this the single most valuable copywriting exercise at DigitalMarketer. Start every copywriting project here before writing a single word.

---

## THE 5-DIMENSION GRID

| Dimension | Before State | After State |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Have** | What do they have or not have? | What will they have or not have? |
| **Feel** | What is their emotional state? | What is their emotional state? |
| **Average Day** | What does a typical day look like? | What does a typical day look like? |
| **Status** | How do they see themselves? How do others see them? | How do they see themselves? How do others see them? |
| **Good vs. Evil** | What greater philosophical evil are they fighting? | What evil have they defeated? |

---

## DIMENSION 1: HAVE

This is the most surface-level dimension. It covers tangible possessions, tools, resources, and capabilities.

### Questions to Ask

**Before:**
- What physical things do they have that relate to the problem?
- What tools or resources are they using (poorly)?
- What do they NOT have that they wish they did?
- What information are they missing?

**After:**
- What new things will they possess?
- What tools will they now have access to?
- What knowledge will they have gained?
- What capabilities will they have that they lacked before?

### Worked Example: Project Management Software

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Scattered spreadsheets across 5 tools | One central hub for all projects |
| No visibility into team workload | Real-time dashboard of every team member's capacity |
| Missing deadlines because tasks slip through cracks | Automated reminders and dependency tracking |
| No standardized process for project intake | Templated workflows for every project type |

### Worked Example: Online Fitness Program

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| A gym membership they never use | A structured 12-week program on their phone |
| A closet full of clothes that do not fit | Confidence to wear anything in the closet |
| No meal plan or nutrition guidance | A weekly meal plan with grocery lists |
| Confusion about which exercises to do | Clear daily workout with video demos |

## DIMENSION 2: FEEL

This dimension captures the emotional landscape. It is where the real persuasive power lives. People buy to change how they feel.

### Questions to Ask

**Before:**
- What emotions dominate their daily experience?
- What are they frustrated by?
- What makes them anxious or fearful?
- What embarrasses them?
- What do they dread about their current situation?

**After:**
- How will they feel once the problem is solved?
- What confidence will they have?
- What relief will they experience?
- What pride or excitement will they feel?
- What fear will be gone?

### Worked Example: Project Management Software

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Overwhelmed by the volume of tasks | In control and on top of everything |
| Anxious before every status meeting (nothing to show) | Confident walking into meetings with real data |
| Frustrated by constant "where is this?" questions | Calm because the answer is always one click away |
| Embarrassed when the CEO asks for a progress update | Proud to share a polished project dashboard |

### Worked Example: Online Fitness Program

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Ashamed when they see photos of themselves | Proud to be in photos again |
| Anxious about their health at their next checkup | Excited to see their doctor and share progress |
| Guilty after every unhealthy meal | At peace with a sustainable nutrition approach |
| Defeated -- they have tried and failed before | Confident because the program is working |

## DIMENSION 3: AVERAGE DAY

This dimension paints the picture of daily life. It is the most powerful dimension for writing copy that makes prospects think "this person is describing MY life."

### Questions to Ask

**Before:**
- Walk through a typical morning. What is frustrating?
- What recurring problems eat up their afternoon?
- What do they dread about tomorrow?
- What daily tasks are inefficient or painful?

**After:**
- What does the new morning routine look like?
- What tasks are now effortless or eliminated?
- What can they do with the time they saved?
- What is the highlight of their new average day?

### Worked Example: Project Management Software

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Morning starts with 47 unread Slack messages asking for updates | Morning starts with a quick glance at the dashboard -- everything is green |
| Spends 2 hours in status meetings that could be emails | 15-minute weekly standup with real-time data |
| Afternoon lost to chasing down files and approvals | Automated approval workflows clear tasks without nagging |
| Leaves work at 7pm still feeling behind | Wraps up by 5:30 knowing nothing critical will slip |

### Worked Example: Online Fitness Program

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Hits snooze 3 times, dreading the day | Wakes up energized, looking forward to the workout |
| Grabs fast food for lunch because "there is no time" | Eats a prepped meal they actually enjoy |
| Crashes at 3pm, needs coffee to survive | Sustained energy through the afternoon |
| Collapses on the couch after work with no energy for family | Plays with the kids after dinner, still has energy |

## DIMENSION 4: STATUS

This is the dimension most copywriters miss. If you figure out the "average day" dimension, you capture attention. If you change the story someone tells about themselves (status), you capture their heart.

### Questions to Ask

**Before:**
- How do they see themselves in relation to peers?
- How do others (boss, spouse, friends) perceive them?
- What story do they tell themselves about why they are stuck?
- What label do they secretly fear applies to them?

**After:**
- How do they see themselves now?
- How do others perceive them?
- What new story do they tell about themselves?
- What label do they proudly claim?

### Worked Example: Project Management Software

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Seen as "the bottleneck" by their team | Seen as "the person who gets things done" |
| Peers view them as disorganized | Peers ask them for advice on project management |
| Self-story: "I am not a natural leader" | Self-story: "I run a tight ship" |
| Boss questions their ability to handle bigger projects | Boss trusts them with the company's largest initiative |

### Worked Example: Online Fitness Program

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| The "unhealthy one" in the friend group | The friend everyone asks for fitness advice |
| Avoids the annual company photo | Volunteers to be front and center |
| Self-story: "I just do not have the discipline" | Self-story: "I am someone who takes care of themselves" |
| Partner worries about their long-term health | Partner brags about the transformation |

## DIMENSION 5: GOOD VS. EVIL

Every significant brand battles a larger philosophical evil. This dimension connects your product to a bigger purpose and mission.

### Questions to Ask

**Before:**
- What greater wrong exists in their industry or life?
- What systemic problem are they up against?
- Who or what is the "villain" in their world?
- What feels fundamentally unfair about their situation?

**After:**
- What evil have they defeated or escaped?
- What change have they contributed to?
- What injustice have they overcome?
- How are they now part of the solution?

### Worked Example: Project Management Software

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Evil: "Busy work" culture that values hours over outcomes | Victory: A team that ships great work and goes home on time |
| Evil: Meetings that exist because nobody has visibility | Victory: Radical transparency that eliminates unnecessary meetings |

### Worked Example: Online Fitness Program

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| Evil: A fitness industry that profits from confusion and yo-yo dieting | Victory: A sustainable approach that works for real humans with real lives |
| Evil: Society that judges people by appearance while making health impossible | Victory: Taking control of health on your own terms |

### Famous Brand Examples

| Brand | Philosophical Evil | Their Victory |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|
| Apple | One-size-fits-all computers forcing conformity | Individuality and creativity for everyone |
| Patagonia | Corporate environmental destruction | Business as a force for good |
| Slack | Email overload destroying workplace happiness | A team that communicates with joy |

## HOW TO RUN THE EXERCISE

1. **Time:** 5 minutes per dimension, 25 minutes total
2. **Format:** Post-it notes work best for brainstorming
3. **Team:** Include customer care reps, salespeople, and community managers -- not just marketers
4. **Process:** Stack overlapping ideas, rank by importance
5. **Output:** The top 2-3 items per dimension become your core messaging hierarchy
6. **One worksheet per market segment.** Never combine two avatars on the same worksheet.

## USING THE GRID IN COPY

- **Headlines:** Pull from the single strongest "After" state
- **Subheads:** Contrast "Before" and "After" states
- **Body copy:** Paint the "Average Day" in vivid detail
- **Social proof:** Match testimonials to specific "Before > After" transformations
- **CTA:** Frame the action as the bridge from "Before" to "After"
- **Guarantee:** Address the biggest "Before > Feel" fear

The grid is your copy brief. Every element on the page should map back to one cell in this grid.
