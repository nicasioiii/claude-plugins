# Customer Interview Guide -- Complete Protocol

## Why Interviews Are the Highest-ROI Research Method

Seven interviews produce messaging gold equivalent to approximately 500 survey respondents (Copy Hackers). Interviews let you explore tangents revealing deeper motivators, see pain in their eyes, hear it in their voice, and repeat back what you hear (they further refine the message).

---

## PRE-INTERVIEW LOGISTICS

### Scheduling

- Schedule 20 interviews to get 7 (people flake at roughly 65% rate)
- Book 45-60 minute slots with 15-minute buffers between
- Send calendar invite with clear expectations: duration, anonymization promise, no pitching
- Send a reminder 24 hours before and 1 hour before

### Incentives

- Offer $25 gift card -- valuable enough to show up, not so much they feel bought
- Send the incentive AFTER the interview, not before
- Amazon gift cards have the broadest appeal

### Recording

- Always record the interview (audio minimum, video preferred)
- Do NOT take handwritten notes during the interview -- it distracts you from listening
- Get verbal consent to record at the start
- Use Otter.ai, Rev, or Zoom transcription for automatic transcript

### Format Preference (Best to Worst)

1. **In person** -- Highest fidelity, can read body language
2. **Video call** (Zoom/Google Meet) -- Good for remote, can see facial expressions
3. **Phone** -- Last resort, loses visual cues

### Team Structure

Use two-person interviewing teams:
- **Person A (Driver):** Asks questions, keeps conversation on track, manages time
- **Person B (Observer):** Takes sparse notes on key themes, watches for body language cues, prepares follow-up questions

## THE INTERVIEW STRUCTURE

### Opening (5 minutes)

1. Thank them for their time
2. Restate purpose: "We are trying to understand your experience so we can communicate better with people like you"
3. Confirm recording consent
4. Set expectations: "There are no right or wrong answers. We want YOUR experience, not what you think we want to hear."
5. Start with a warm-up question: "Tell me a bit about yourself and what you do."

### Core Questions (35-45 minutes)

Organize questions into four blocks. Do NOT read these rigidly -- let the conversation flow naturally. Use these as a guide, not a script.

#### Block 1: Problem Discovery (10 minutes)

1. **"Walk me through the moment you first realized you had this problem."**
   - Follow-up: What was happening in your life at that time?
   - Follow-up: How long had it been building before that moment?

2. **"What had you already tried before looking for a solution?"**
   - Follow-up: Why did those not work?
   - Follow-up: How did that make you feel?

3. **"When you describe this problem to a friend or colleague, what do you say?"**
   - This is gold -- their natural language becomes your copy language

4. **"What was the hardest part of dealing with this problem day to day?"**
   - Follow-up: Can you give me a specific example from the last week?

#### Block 2: Solution Discovery (10 minutes)

5. **"How did you first hear about [Product/Solution type]?"**
   - Follow-up: What made you pay attention?
   - Follow-up: What were you skeptical about?

6. **"What nearly stopped you from buying/signing up?"**
   - This reveals your top objections to address in copy

7. **"Was there a specific moment or trigger that made you decide 'I need to do this now'?"**
   - Reveals urgency triggers and buying moments

8. **"Did you compare us to any alternatives? What made you choose us?"**
   - Reveals competitive positioning opportunities

#### Block 3: Results and Transformation (10 minutes)

9. **"What was the single biggest result or change you experienced?"**
   - Follow-up: How quickly did that happen?
   - Follow-up: Were you surprised by that?

10. **"What does a typical [day/week] look like now compared to before?"**
    - Average Day dimension of the Before-After Grid

11. **"What surprised you most about using [Product]?"**
    - Unexpected benefits are often the most compelling in copy

12. **"If you had to describe [Product] to a friend in one sentence, what would you say?"**
    - Natural positioning language

#### Block 4: Deep Dive (5-10 minutes)

13. **"What would you miss most if [Product] disappeared tomorrow?"**
    - Reveals the core value that retention depends on

14. **"Is there anything about your experience -- good or bad -- that we should know?"**
    - Open-ended catches surprises

15. **"Is there anything I should have asked but did not?"**
    - Let the interviewee guide you to what matters most to them

### Closing (5 minutes)

1. Thank them sincerely
2. Ask if they would be willing to provide a testimonial (capture it while emotion is fresh)
3. Ask for referrals: "Do you know anyone else who might be willing to chat with us?"
4. Confirm where to send the gift card
5. Let them know how their input will be used (anonymized, for marketing improvement)

## INTERVIEW TECHNIQUES

### Mirroring (Eddie Shleyner)

Repeat the last 2-4 words someone said back to them as a question. This prompts them to keep talking and go deeper.

**Example:**
- Interviewee: "I was just so frustrated with the whole process."
- You: "Frustrated with the process?"
- Interviewee: "Yeah, like every time I tried to get my team aligned, someone would be working off an old version of the brief, and we would waste entire meetings just getting on the same page..."

Mirroring is the single most powerful technique for getting rich, detailed language.

### The Silence Technique

When they finish an answer, count to 3 silently before responding. People fill silence with deeper, more honest answers.

### Emotion Probing

When they express an emotion, ask about it directly:
- "You said that was frustrating. Can you tell me more about what that frustration felt like?"
- "It sounds like that made you anxious. What was the anxiety about specifically?"

### The "Specific Example" Redirect

When answers are vague or general, redirect to specifics:
- "Can you give me a specific example of that?"
- "Think about the last time that happened. Walk me through exactly what occurred."
- "Who was involved? What did they say?"

## COMMON INTERVIEW MISTAKES

1. **Chasing tangents too far** -- Let interesting tangents run for 2-3 minutes, then redirect. Have your question list visible.

2. **Interviewee speaking for general population** -- When they say "people usually think..." redirect with "What about YOUR experience specifically?"

3. **Becoming too friendly and losing objectivity** -- Stay warm but professionally curious. You are a researcher, not their friend.

4. **Leading questions** -- Do NOT ask "Did you love the onboarding?" Ask "How was the onboarding experience?"

5. **Confirming your own bias** -- Do NOT ask "Was the speed of results the biggest benefit?" Ask "What was the biggest benefit?"

6. **Not going deep enough** -- If an answer feels surface-level, probe: "Tell me more about that" or "Why do you think that was?"

## POST-INTERVIEW PROCESSING

### Within 24 Hours

1. Review the recording/transcript
2. Highlight verbatim phrases that are emotionally charged, specific, or vivid
3. Categorize highlights into: Pain Language, Desire Language, Objection Language, Transformation Language, Trigger Language
4. Note any unexpected themes or surprises

### After All Interviews Complete

1. Compare themes across all 7 interviews
2. Identify patterns: what appears in 3+ interviews becomes a messaging priority
3. Rank themes by emotional intensity and frequency
4. Map the strongest themes to your Before-After Grid
5. Pull the top 15-20 verbatim phrases for your swipe file
6. Write a 1-page research summary: Top 3 pains, Top 3 desires, Top 3 objections, Top 3 transformations

### Turning Research into Copy

The research summary becomes your copy brief:
- **Top pain** -> Headlines and opening hooks
- **Top desire** -> Primary promise and CTA framing
- **Top objections** -> Proof sections and guarantee language
- **Top transformations** -> Social proof and testimonial selection
- **Verbatim phrases** -> Exact headline candidates, subhead candidates, and body copy language

## ADDITIONAL QUESTION TEMPLATES BY RESEARCH GOAL

### When You Need Headline Language
- "If you were recommending [Product] to a colleague, what would you say in one sentence?"
- "What was the first thing you noticed or felt after using [Product]?"
- "Complete this sentence: Before [Product], I was ___. After [Product], I am ___."

### When You Need Objection-Handling Copy
- "What almost stopped you from buying?"
- "If a friend said 'I'm not sure about [Product],' what would you tell them?"
- "What did you need to see or hear before you felt confident enough to purchase?"

### When You Need Urgency Triggers
- "What would have happened if you had NOT solved this problem when you did?"
- "Was there a deadline, event, or consequence that made this feel urgent?"
- "How long did you wait before taking action, and what finally pushed you?"

### When You Need Before-After Grid Content
- "Describe a typical Monday morning before you had [Product]."
- "Now describe a typical Monday morning after."
- "How would your coworkers or family describe the change they have seen in you?"
