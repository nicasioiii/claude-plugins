# Survey Templates -- Complete Question Bank

## Core Principles

- Survey ONLY to find what to say and how to say it -- not demographics
- "You don't buy the Wall Street Journal because you're 40, married, and graduated from an Ivy League school -- you buy it for deeper reasons" (Rewired Group)
- Ask open-ended questions to find actual language and phrasing
- Use logic jumps to route different respondents to relevant questions
- Segment responses to tailor messaging

---

## SURVEY BY RESPONDENT TYPE

### Survey A: Paying Customers (Most Valuable)

These people have already bought. Their language is your most reliable copy source.

**Question 1 -- Trigger Event:**
"What was going on in your world that compelled you to look for a solution like [Product]?"

*Why this works:* Reveals the triggering moment that drives people from passive problem-awareness to active solution-seeking. This language goes directly into your hooks and opening copy.

**Question 2 -- Natural Description:**
"In your own words, what is [Product]?"

*Why this works:* Reveals how real customers describe your value proposition. Often produces better positioning language than anything your marketing team writes.

**Question 3 -- Nearly Stopped:**
"What nearly stopped you from buying?"

*Why this works:* Reveals your top objections. These become the backbone of your proof section and guarantee language.

**Question 4 -- Biggest Benefit:**
"What is the single biggest benefit you have experienced since using [Product]?"

*Why this works:* Reveals the One Big Idea candidates -- the benefit that matters most to people who actually use the product.

**Question 5 -- Friend Description:**
"How would you describe [Product] to a friend who was considering it?"

*Why this works:* Produces natural, conversational copy language. Often captures the exact phrasing for testimonials and social proof.

**Question 6 -- Life Before:**
"Describe your life before [Product]. What was the most frustrating thing about your old approach?"

*Why this works:* Paints the "Before" state for your Before-After Grid with authentic emotional detail.

**Question 7 -- Alternatives:**
"What would you use if [Product] no longer existed?"

*Why this works:* Reveals your true competitive set (which may differ from who you think you compete with).

**Question 8 -- Improvement:**
"If you could change one thing about [Product], what would it be?"

*Why this works:* Identifies friction points that may appear as objections in your copy. Also useful for product development.

---

### Survey B: Trial Users / Free Users (Second Most Valuable)

These people are considering buying. Their objections are fresh and active.

**Question 1:**
"What brought you to [Product] today?"

**Question 2:**
"What problem were you hoping [Product] would solve?"

**Question 3:**
"What is the ONE thing you need to see or know before upgrading to the paid version?"

*Why this works:* Directly reveals the conversion barrier. Whatever they say goes into your upgrade page copy.

**Question 4:**
"What is the biggest risk you see in committing to [Product]?"

**Question 5:**
"Have you tried any similar products? How does [Product] compare?"

**Question 6:**
"What feature or benefit matters most to you?"

---

### Survey C: Website Visitors (Non-Buyers)

These people visited but did not convert. Use on-site polls to capture them.

**Timed Poll (after 30-60 seconds on page):**
"What brought you to this page today?"

**Exit Intent Poll:**
"What, if anything, is keeping you from [signing up / buying] today?"

*Why this works:* Captures the exact objection or friction point at the moment of abandonment.

**Exit Intent Poll (Alternative):**
"Is there any information missing from this page?"

**Post-Browse Poll:**
"How would you rate the information on this page? What could we improve?"

---

### Survey D: Former Customers / Churned Users

These people left. Their reasons for leaving reveal what your copy promised but your product did not deliver.

**Question 1:**
"What was the primary reason you stopped using [Product]?"

**Question 2:**
"What would we need to change for you to come back?"

**Question 3:**
"What are you using instead? How does it compare?"

**Question 4:**
"Looking back, what was the best part of using [Product]?"

---

### Survey E: Non-Customers in Your Market

When you have no customers yet, survey the broader market.

**Question 1:**
"What is the single biggest challenge you face when it comes to [problem area]?"

**Question 2:**
"What have you tried to solve this challenge? Why did it not work?"

**Question 3:**
"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about [problem area], what would it be?"

**Question 4:**
"How much time/money do you spend on [problem area] each month?"

**Question 5:**
"What would a perfect solution look like to you?"

**Tool for non-customer surveys:** Ask Your Target Market (aytm.com) -- approximately $1,000 for 100 respondents.

## ON-SITE POLL BEST PRACTICES

### Setup

- Use non-invasive slide-up polls (Hotjar, Qualaroo)
- Two trigger types: **Timed** (appears after X seconds or scroll depth) and **Exit Intent** (cursor moves to leave)
- Start with the question: "What copy do I need my visitors' help writing today?"

### Question Starters

Best question starters: what, why, how, when

### Format Decision

- **Open-ended questions:** Fewer responses but provide actual swipeable phrasing
- **Multiple-choice questions:** More responses, removes friction of typing, but gives less language to mine
- **Rule:** Start with open-ended to gather language, then switch to multiple-choice to validate patterns

### Top On-Site Poll Questions

**For understanding visitor intent:**
- "What are you hoping to find on this page?"
- "What brought you to [website] today?"

**For identifying friction:**
- "Is there anything preventing you from [desired action] right now?"
- "What questions do you have that this page does not answer?"

**For understanding objections:**
- "Why did you choose not to [buy/sign up] today?"
- "What would need to change for you to [buy/sign up]?"

**For improving messaging:**
- "How would you describe what [Product] does to a friend?"
- "What is the single most important thing [Product] should communicate on this page?"

## SURVEY LOGISTICS

### Tools

- **Email surveys:** Typeform (best UX), SurveyMonkey
- **On-site polls:** Hotjar, Qualaroo
- **Advanced analysis:** FourEyes (free with advanced analysis)
- **Non-customer panels:** AYTM (aytm.com)

### Reducing Annoyance

- Offer incentives for longer surveys ($10-25 gift card)
- Only survey engaged subscribers (opened 3+ emails in 30 days)
- Keep surveys under 10 questions for email, under 3 for on-site
- Show progress bar
- Send a reminder 3-5 days after initial send

### Segmentation

Survey different groups separately and compare results:
- Premium vs. free users
- New customers vs. long-term customers
- High-engagement vs. low-engagement
- Different use cases or industries

**Why segment:** The language, pain points, and desires differ dramatically between segments. Combining them produces watered-down messaging.

### Response Targets

- Email surveys: 100+ responses for statistical patterns
- On-site polls: 50+ responses per question variant
- If response rates are low: shorten the survey, improve the incentive, survey a more engaged list

## PROCESSING SURVEY RESPONSES

### Step 1: Read Every Response

Do not skim. Read every open-ended response in full. Highlight vivid, specific, or emotionally charged phrases verbatim.

### Step 2: Categorize

Sort highlighted phrases into buckets:
- **Pain language** -- how they describe the problem
- **Desire language** -- how they describe the ideal outcome
- **Objection language** -- what nearly stopped them or what concerns them
- **Transformation language** -- how they describe the change after using the product
- **Trigger language** -- what prompted them to start looking for a solution

### Step 3: Count Frequencies

Tally how often each theme or phrase appears. Themes appearing in 20%+ of responses are messaging priorities.

### Step 4: Extract Top Phrases

Pull the 10-15 strongest verbatim phrases for your swipe file. These become direct candidates for headlines, subheads, testimonials, and body copy.

### Step 5: Map to Copy Brief

- Most frequent pain -> Hook and opening
- Most frequent desire -> Primary promise
- Most frequent objection -> Proof section and guarantee
- Most frequent transformation -> Social proof selection
- Most frequent trigger -> Ad targeting and hook timing
