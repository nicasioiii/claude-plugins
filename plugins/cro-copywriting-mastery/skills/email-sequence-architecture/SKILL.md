# Email Sequences & Multi-Touch Campaigns

## WHY EMAIL SEQUENCES OUTPERFORM SINGLE EMAILS

### The Mere Exposure Effect
**Principle:** Repeated exposure to a stimulus increases liking of it.

**In email:**
- Email 1: "Who is this?"
- Email 2: "Maybe they have something useful"
- Email 3: "I'm starting to trust them"
- Email 4: "I'm ready to buy"

Each email builds credibility, familiarity, and desire.

### The Research
- First email: 2-5% conversion typical
- Multi-email sequence (7 emails): 15-30% conversion
- Email #4 often has highest conversion (after trust is built)

## THE 7-EMAIL WELCOME SEQUENCE (Adil Amarsi Formula)

This formula converts 30-91.4% depending on market fit and lead magnet quality.

### Email 1: Welcome to the Party
**Subject:** "Welcome To The Party, Pal!"

**Jobs:**
- Set expectations
- Build personality
- Explain future sales (transparency)
- Remind of lead magnet value

**Key Elements:**
1. **Greetings + Credibility** (Who you are, brief authority signals)
2. **Personal touch** (2-3 interests/personality traits—humanize)
3. **Set expectations** (What they'll receive, frequency)
4. **Key instructions:**
   - Ask them to reply (ensures deliverability + engagement)
   - Request they create dedicated folder (re-engagement trick)
   - Set up email filter/rule (keeps emails organized)
5. **Transparency on selling** ("I'm going to sell you stuff; here's why")
6. **Reinforce lead magnet** (Remind what they got)
7. **Call to action** (Next step)
8. **Personality sign-off** (Friendly, authentic)
9. **P.S. + P.P.S.** (Reinforce key points; create urgency if appropriate)

**Length:** 200-400 words

**Key Insight:** This email builds relationship and GETS DELIVERABILITY by asking reply. Critical first step.

---

### Email 2: Wine & Dine (Rapport + Value Delivery)
**Purpose:** Build rapport; deliver genuine value; establish expertise

**Jobs:**
- Continue conversation tone
- Deliver value (not selling yet)
- Tell story or share lesson
- Build authority subtly
- Soft CTA

**Key Elements:**
1. **Continue conversation** (Reference Email 1; feel like ongoing dialogue)
2. **Deliver real value** (Insight, framework, tip they can use)
3. **Keep it personal** (Story or experience, not generic advice)
4. **Establish authority subtly** (Results you've seen; experience; credentials)
5. **Soft CTA** (Light action; "check this out" not "buy this")

**Length:** 250-400 words

**Key Insight:** This email proves you deliver value before you ask for money. Trust-building phase.

---

### Email 3: On-Two Setup (Create Curiosity)
**Purpose:** Build anticipation; create open loop; set up next offer/value

**Jobs:**
- Reference something from previous emails
- Create open loop (curiosity gap)
- Prime reader for next email/offer
- Leave them wanting more

**Key Elements:**
1. **Reference previous email** (Show continuity)
2. **Create curiosity** (Hint at something coming)
3. **Tease benefit** (Don't fully explain yet)
4. **Build anticipation** ("In the next email, I'm going to share...")
5. **Soft CTA** (Keep engagement going)

**Length:** 150-250 words (shorter email okay here)

**Key Insight:** This email is the hook for Email 4. Create intrigue and anticipation.

---

### Email 4: Call to Arms (Emotional Activation)
**Purpose:** Move toward action; identify with challenge; emotionally activate

**Jobs:**
- Identify problem or challenge they face
- Make it emotionally resonant
- Show what's at stake
- Position your solution/offer as path forward
- Use stronger CTA language

**Key Elements:**
1. **Identify problem** (Paint the pain)
2. **Make emotional** (Appeal to feelings, not just facts)
3. **Show stakes** ("If nothing changes...")
4. **Introduce solution** (Your offer as answer)
5. **Stronger CTA** (More action-oriented)

**Length:** 300-500 words

**Key Insight:** This is where you shift from relationship-building to selling. Emotional activation is key.

---

### Email 5: Testimonial Email (Social Proof)
**Purpose:** Establish credibility through third-party validation

**Jobs:**
- Lead with compelling testimonial
- Explain what they experienced
- Show results/transformation
- Include multiple testimonials for redundancy
- CTA to your offer

**Key Elements:**
1. **Strong testimonial** (Video transcript, quote, or full case)
2. **Explain what they experienced** (Context + result)
3. **Show transformation** (Before → After)
4. **Multiple testimonials** (3+ is better; shows pattern)
5. **CTA to offer** (Call to action)

**Length:** 400-600 words

**Key Insight:** Peer proof is more credible than self-promotion. Use this email to build conviction.

---

### Email 6: Case Studies (Deep Proof)
**Purpose:** Provide detailed proof of your mechanism/results

**Jobs:**
- Tell detailed client/customer story
- Establish their starting situation
- Explain what they did differently
- Show specific results
- Explain why it worked
- Include CTA

**Key Elements:**
1. **Story setup** (Who, where, situation)
2. **Their challenge** (What they were struggling with)
3. **What changed** (What they did differently; your solution)
4. **Specific results** (Numbers, metrics, transformation)
5. **Why it worked** (The mechanism)
6. **CTA to offer**

**Length:** 600-900 words

**Key Insight:** Case studies are credible and specific. Good final proof before sales email.

---

### Email 7: Sales Email (Direct Offer)
**Purpose:** Close the sale; present offer directly

**Jobs:**
- Reference relationship built
- Remind of value/transformation possible
- Present offer clearly
- Include guarantee/risk reversal
- Strong CTA
- P.S. with urgency or sweetener

**Key Elements:**
1. **Reference relationship** ("After 6 emails together...")
2. **Remind of transformation** (What's possible for them)
3. **Deliver on promise** (What they get)
4. **Present offer clearly** (Price, what's included)
5. **Guarantee/risk reversal** (Remove fear)
6. **Strong CTA** (Clear action)
7. **P.S.** (Urgency + offer sweetener)

**Length:** 400-600 words

**Key Insight:** This email closes the loop. Make offer clear, benefit obvious, action easy.

## THE WELCOME SEQUENCE IN ACTION

### Structure:

```
Email 1 (Day 1): Welcome + Instructions
Email 2 (Day 3): Value Delivery
Email 3 (Day 5): Curiosity Setup
Email 4 (Day 7): Emotional Activation + Soft Offer
Email 5 (Day 9): Testimonial/Proof
Email 6 (Day 11): Case Study/Deep Proof
Email 7 (Day 13): Hard Sales Email + Offer
```

**Spacing:** 2-3 days between emails is ideal. Allows time to read, think, build trust.

### Conversion Expectations
- **Cold list:** 10-20% conversion typical
- **Warm list:** 30-50% conversion
- **Hot list:** 50-91.4% conversion

## BROADCAST EMAIL STRATEGY (After Welcome Sequence)

Once welcome sequence completes, send broadcast emails to full list.

### Email Types (Rotation)

**Type 1: Educational Email** (Pure value)
- No selling
- Teaching concept/framework
- 20-30% of sends

**Type 2: Soft Sell Email** (Value + light pitch)
- Provide value
- Light product mention
- 30-40% of sends

**Type 3: Hard Sell Email** (Direct offer)
- Present specific offer
- Full sales pitch
- 20-30% of sends

**Type 4: Announcement Email** (News)
- New product/feature
- Update/news
- 10-20% of sends

**Type 5: Story Email** (Pure narrative)
- Story that illustrates principle
- No direct CTA (or soft CTA)
- 10-20% of sends

**Rule:** Mix types; don't send 3 hard sells in a row = unsubscribes and list decay

## STORY ARCS IN EMAIL SEQUENCES

### Hero's Journey Arc
**Email 1:** Hero's ordinary world (reader's current situation)
**Email 2-3:** Call to adventure (problem/opportunity)
**Email 4-5:** Hero meets mentor (you introduce solution)
**Email 6:** Testing and trials (how others overcame it)
**Email 7:** Return with wisdom (offer solution; reader becomes hero)

### Before-After Arc
**Email 1:** Introduce before-state (reader's pain)
**Email 2-3:** Explore problem (make it real)
**Email 4-5:** Introduce after-state (vision of solution)
**Email 6:** Show how others got there (proof)
**Email 7:** Offer path to after-state (sales email)

### Testimonial Series Arc
**Email 1:** Customer A's story (transformation)
**Email 2:** Customer B's story (different situation, same solution)
**Email 3:** Customer C's story (another angle)
**Email 4:** Your story (why you built it)
**Email 5-6:** Results/data (pattern established)
**Email 7:** Sales email (leverage proof built)

## EMAIL COPYWRITING PRINCIPLES

### Subject Line Rules (Different from Landing Pages)
- Curiosity works better in email
- Personalization = 20-50% lift
- Benefit clarity still matters but less
- Preview text extends message

### Body Copy Rules
- **Conversational tone** (like one friend to another)
- **Short paragraphs** (2-3 lines max)
- **Short sentences** (8-12 words ideal)
- **Active voice** (not passive)
- **Strong verbs** (not adjectives)

### P.S. Rules
- **P.S. is often highest-read section**
- Reinforce main benefit
- Create urgency (if appropriate)
- Alternative angle

### Email-Specific Formats
- One main point per email
- Multiple CTAs okay (top, middle, bottom)
- White space important
- Mobile-first design

## PSYCHOLOGY IN EMAIL SEQUENCES

### The Frequency Question
**Principle:** More emails = higher conversion BUT risk of unsubscribes

**Balance:**
- Cold list: 1x per week ideal
- Warm list: 2-3x per week acceptable
- Very warm list: Daily emails work

**Testing:** Vary frequency; measure unsubscribe rates alongside conversion

### Building Belief Over Time
**Email 1:** "Who is this?"
**Email 2:** "They know something"
**Email 3:** "I'm interested"
**Email 4:** "I believe they can help"
**Email 5:** "Others succeeded, so can I"
**Email 6:** "Deep proof = I'm convinced"
**Email 7:** "I'm ready to buy"

Each email systematically builds the buying belief.

### The Open Loop Effect
- Email 3 creates curiosity
- Email 4 partially answers
- Reader must open Email 5 to find out more
- Natural pull forward

## BROADCAST EMAIL SEQUENCE (After Welcome)

**Recommended Structure (Weekly Sends):**

**Week 1:**
- Monday: Educational email (pure value)
- Thursday: Soft sell email (value + light pitch)

**Week 2:**
- Monday: Story email (narrative)
- Thursday: Hard sell email (direct offer)

**Week 3:**
- Monday: Educational email (pure value)
- Thursday: Soft sell email (value + light pitch)

**Week 4:**
- Monday: Story email (narrative)
- Thursday: Hard sell email (direct offer)

**Repeat indefinitely.** This rotation keeps list engaged while selling consistently.

## KEY PRINCIPLES

1. **Sequences outperform single emails.** Mere Exposure Effect is real. Repetition builds trust.

2. **Relationship before transaction.** First 4 emails build trust. Email 5-6 provide proof. Email 7 closes. Wrong order = low conversion.

3. **Value delivery first, selling second.** If first 2 emails are sales pitch, unsubscribe rate spikes. Deliver value; trust builds; conversions happen.

4. **Storytelling is most powerful.** Stories convert better than facts across all channels. Use stories throughout sequence.

5. **Broadcast emails need mixing.** 3 hard sells in a row = list decay. Rotate: educational, soft sell, hard sell, story.

6. **Frequency matters but test it.** More emails = more conversions BUT more unsubscribes. Find your audience's sweet spot.

7. **Email is different medium.** Different from landing pages. Curiosity works better. Personalization = higher lift. Preview text matters.
