# Email Sequences & Multi-Touch Campaigns

Email is the highest-ROI channel in marketing. Amarsi's 7-email welcome sequence framework has generated over $300 million in sales across clients. This skill covers the complete system.

## THE 7-EMAIL WELCOME SEQUENCE ARC

Two versions exist: Long (more personal, story-driven) and Short (more direct, case-study driven). Both follow the same structural arc.

| Email # | Long Version | Short Version | Core Purpose |
|---------|-------------|---------------|--------------|
| 1 | Welcome to the Party | Welcome to the Party | Set tone, deliver lead magnet, establish rules |
| 2 | The Build (Stories Sell) | Wine & Dine Your Reader | Deepen bond, share philosophy |
| 3 | The One-Two Setup (Pt 1) | The One-Two Setup (Pt 1) | Content email that sets up Email 4 |
| 4 | A Call To Arms (Pt 2) | A Call To Arms (Pt 2) | Promotional payoff of the 1-2 punch |
| 5 | Testimonial Email | Testimonial Email | Social proof through client stories |
| 6 | Philosophy / Case Studies | Case Studies | Philosophy or case study to trigger desire |
| 7 | The Sales Email | The Sales Email | Direct pitch wrapped in content value |

### Key Principle: The Sequence Is Cyclical
After Email 7, the pattern REPEATS. You can write beyond 7 emails by cycling through the same types with fresh content. One student wrote a 14-email sequence in 6 hours by repeating the pattern.

### Sequence Results
- Over $300 million in sales across clients
- One client: $120K/year at $10K profit → $390K/year at $80K profit
- 28% increase in subscriber retention
- Students completing sequences in 4-6 hours

## EMAIL-BY-EMAIL PURPOSE AND KEY ELEMENTS

### Email 1: Welcome Email
**THE most important email in the sequence.** Sets the tone for the entire relationship.

Key elements:
- Deliver what they opted in for (lead magnet/download)
- Introduce yourself with personality
- Set expectations (what you will send, how often)
- Ask them to reply + whitelist your email
- Ask them to create a dedicated folder for your emails
- Be truthful that you WILL sell them things — give an honest reason why
- Links go to TWO places: your offer OR your download

### Email 2: The Build / Wine & Dine
Deepen the relationship. Transition from "what's in it for me" to personality-driven connection.

Key elements:
- Share your philosophy or worldview
- Connect your philosophy to their goal
- Disrupt their thinking with a new concept
- Link back to your download/freebie

### Email 3: The One-Two Setup (Part 1)
Content email that creates investment and sets up Email 4.

Key elements:
- Ominous subject line with "Part 1"
- Challenge beliefs and misconceptions
- Use a third-party quote and question it
- Link to content on your site
- Sell the link with odd-number bullet points
- End with "And so much more..."

### Email 4: A Call To Arms (Part 2)
The promotional payoff. Wraps up Email 3 as the "One-Two Punch."

Key elements:
- Continue the subject line from Email 3
- Explain why there is a Part 2
- Weave a subtle pitch into the story
- P.S. summarizes the email
- P.P.S. calls back to the offer from Email 1 (creates continuity)

### Email 5: Testimonial Email
Use testimonials to bridge content and build proof.

Key elements:
- Set expectation this email is different
- Share a client/customer story with their testimonial in their own words
- Add context the testimonial leaves out
- End with contemplation questions (probing self-assessment)

### Email 6: Philosophy / Case Studies
Instill your philosophy through content or use case studies to drive desire.

Key elements:
- Share a personal observation or conversation
- Relate it to a story about your past
- Highlight your key philosophical points
- Summate in one sentence
- Give actionable guidance

### Email 7: The Sales Email
Direct sales pitch wrapped in genuine content value.

Key elements:
- Admit you are doing something different
- Bait emotion with a statistic or claim
- Pick ONE focus point and go deep
- Link to sales page/video
- Give enough to act on but leave them needing MORE clarity
- P.S. with a directive ("take notes while watching")

## THE ONE-TWO PUNCH STRATEGY

Pair a content email (Email 3) with a promotional email (Email 4) that continues the same theme. The content email creates investment; the promotional email delivers the payoff with a pitch woven in.

This technique generated $15,000 from a list of only 200 people selling a $500 product.

**How it works:**
1. Email 3 teaches a concept and opens a loop
2. Email 4 closes the loop and transitions into a pitch
3. The reader does not feel sold to because the pitch is the natural conclusion of the content

## THE TRANSPARENT SELLING PHILOSOPHY

In Email 1, explicitly state: "I'm going to sell you stuff over the course of our relationship." Give the honest reason why.

**Why this works:**
- Sets expectations from day one
- Removes the "ick" factor from future sales emails
- Builds trust through radical honesty
- When the sales email arrives, it is expected — not a surprise

## SUBJECT LINE PRINCIPLES

### Proven Patterns from the Sequence
| Email | Subject Line | Pattern |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | Welcome To The Party, Pal! | Pop culture + welcome |
| 2 | Stories sell everything | Bold claim / theme |
| 3 | Not having this = death part 1 | Ominous + Part 1 |
| 4 | Not having this equals death part 2 | Continuation |
| 5 | from burn out to so fresh, so clean... | Transformation + pop culture |
| 6 | self-esteem plays a big part... | Conversational |
| 7 | 3 reasons why emails die... | Numbered + dramatic |

### Subject Line Rules
- Test three capitalizations: Title Case, Sentence case, all lowercase
- Pop culture references create instant personality
- Part 1/Part 2 creates open loops across emails
- Preview text should expand or contrast the subject line (never repeat it)
- "From X to Y" transformation format works for testimonial emails

## STORY-BASED EMAIL SELLING

Stories are the foundation of ALL email selling. The market has shifted from "what's in it for me" to "who are you, what can you do for me, and would we get along?"

### Email Content Sources
- Your personal story and life experiences
- Lessons from failures and successes
- Daily observations and random discoveries
- What you talk about with friends in your marketplace
- Client case studies and testimonials
- Book reviews and content you are consuming
- Philosophical positions on your industry

### The Contemplation Technique
In testimonial and case study emails, end with probing questions that force self-assessment:
- Are you emailing your list as often as you should?
- Do they know who you REALLY are?
- Are you showing up in the best possible way?
- Is your audience entertained?

## OPT-IN PAGE FORMULA

Amarsi's opt-in page formula converts between 30-91.4%, with average conversions of 40-50%.

### Elements
1. Testimonials on the opt-in page (always increases subscribers since 2014)
2. Layout with specific bullet count (odd numbers)
3. Testimonial placement for maximum results
4. Clear description of what they are getting

### Pre-Headline Formulas
- "Your Download Is On Its Way, But First..."
- "Looking For An Unbiased Review Of [Product], Read On..."
- "For your eyes only..."
- "For [Market] Facing [Problem]..."

## EMAIL FORMATTING AND DELIVERABILITY

### Formatting Rules
- Short lines (40-60 characters per line ideal)
- Maximum 2 sentences per paragraph
- One-line paragraphs preferred
- Hyperlink text + raw URL for sales links
- P.S. acts as a second subject line — many readers skip to it first

### Deliverability Best Practices
- Ask for reply and whitelist in Email 1
- Ask subscribers to create a dedicated folder for your emails
- Ask them to set an automated rule to move your emails to that folder
- These steps train engagement signals that improve inbox placement
