# Dead List Revival Strategy

## WHEN A LIST IS "DEAD"

A list is considered dead or dying when:
- Open rates have dropped below 10% consistently
- No engagement (opens, clicks, replies) for 30+ days
- Significant portion of list has not opened in 60-90 days
- Deliverability is declining (more spam folder placement)
- Unsubscribe rates spike on sends

---

## WHY LISTS DIE (THE 3 REASONS)

1. **You don't connect with your audience** — Your content feels generic, impersonal, or irrelevant
2. **Your subject lines are bad** — People scan past your emails without opening
3. **You don't know what to write about** — So you either stop sending or send boring content

The fix for all three: return to story-based, personality-driven email using the welcome sequence framework.

---

## THE REVIVAL STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Segment the Dead
Before sending revival emails, segment your list:

**Segment A — Recently Dead (30-60 days no engagement)**
These subscribers are the most likely to revive. They remember you but have stopped engaging.

**Segment B — Long Dead (60-120 days no engagement)**
These subscribers have largely forgotten you. Revival is harder but possible.

**Segment C — Ancient Dead (120+ days no engagement)**
Very low revival rates. Consider cleaning these off the list entirely after one final attempt.

### Phase 2: The Revival Sequence (3 Emails)

#### Revival Email 1: The "I Miss You" Email
```
Subject: been a while...
Preview: are you still interested in [topic]?

Hey [Name],

I noticed you haven't opened my emails in a while.

No hard feelings — I get it. Life gets busy.
Inboxes get crowded. Priorities shift.

But I wanted to check in because I've got some
[new content / exciting things / updates] coming
that I think you'll genuinely find useful.

If you're still interested in [topic/benefit],
just hit reply and let me know.

Even a simple "I'm here" works.

If not — no worries at all. I'd rather you be
on this list because you WANT to be, not because
you forgot to unsubscribe.

[Your name]

P.S. If you want to stay but my emails keep
ending up in spam or promotions, here's a quick fix:
[whitelist instructions]
```

**Purpose:** Re-establish connection. The reply request improves deliverability signals.
**Timing:** Send to Segment A first, Segment B 3 days later.

#### Revival Email 2: The "Value Bomb" Email
```
Subject: [Your best content topic] — for free
Preview: I made this for you

Hey [Name],

I've been working on something I'm pretty proud of,
and I wanted to share it with you before anyone else.

[Describe the free resource/content — make it genuinely valuable]

Here's the link: [Link]

No catch. No pitch. Just something I think will help
you [achieve specific result].

[Your name]

P.S. If this is useful, hit reply and let me know.
I love hearing from you.
```

**Purpose:** Provide genuine value to remind them why they subscribed.
**Timing:** 3-4 days after Revival Email 1.

#### Revival Email 3: The "Last Chance" Email
```
Subject: should I keep sending you emails?
Preview: honest question — no hard feelings either way

Hey [Name],

I've sent you a couple emails recently and
I haven't heard back.

I totally respect your inbox, so I want to ask
directly: do you still want to hear from me?

If yes — just click this link or hit reply:
[Link / "Yes, keep me on the list"]

If I don't hear from you in the next 7 days,
I'll remove you from the list.

No hard feelings at all. I'd rather have a smaller
list of people who actually want to hear from me
than a big list of people who don't.

Thanks for being here,
[Your name]

P.S. If you DO want to stay, I've got [exciting thing]
coming soon that I think you're going to love.
```

**Purpose:** Force a decision. Those who click stay. Those who don't get removed.
**Timing:** 5-7 days after Revival Email 2.

### Phase 3: Clean the List
After 7 days from Revival Email 3:
- Keep everyone who opened, clicked, or replied to ANY of the three emails
- Remove everyone who showed zero engagement across all three
- This improves your sender reputation and deliverability for remaining subscribers

---

## POST-REVIVAL: RESTART THE WELCOME SEQUENCE

For subscribers who re-engaged, do NOT just dump them back into your regular broadcast schedule. Instead:

1. Send a "fresh start" email thanking them for staying
2. Re-deliver your best lead magnet or content piece
3. Start a mini welcome sequence (condensed 3-email version)
4. Then transition to regular broadcasts

## LIST HYGIENE BEST PRACTICES

### Regular Maintenance Schedule
- **Monthly:** Remove hard bounces and invalid emails
- **Quarterly:** Segment inactive subscribers (no opens in 90 days)
- **Bi-annually:** Run revival sequence on inactive segment
- **Annually:** Full list audit and clean

### Engagement Scoring
Assign scores based on behavior:
- Opened email: +1 point
- Clicked link: +3 points
- Replied: +5 points
- Purchased: +10 points
- No engagement: -1 point per week

Send your best offers to high-score subscribers. Send revival sequences to zero-score subscribers.

### Deliverability Protection
- Never buy email lists (instant reputation damage)
- Always use double opt-in for new subscribers
- Remove subscribers who report you as spam immediately
- Monitor your sender reputation score regularly
- Keep bounce rate below 2% on every send
- Keep complaint rate below 0.1% on every send

---

## PREVENTION: HOW TO KEEP LISTS ALIVE

The best dead list strategy is prevention. Following the welcome sequence framework prevents list death because:

1. **Email 1** trains whitelist and folder behavior (deliverability)
2. **Emails 2-3** establish personality-driven connection (engagement)
3. **Emails 4-7** cycle through value and sales (ongoing relevance)
4. **The cyclical pattern** means you always have fresh content

### Frequency Guidelines
- Minimum: 1 email per week
- Optimal: 2-3 emails per week
- Maximum: Daily (only if your content quality supports it)
- Gap danger zone: If you go 2+ weeks without sending, engagement drops significantly
- After a 4+ week gap: Consider a mini revival before resuming regular sends

### Content Ratio
- 70% value/content/story emails
- 20% promotional/sales emails
- 10% community/engagement emails (polls, questions, replies)

This ratio keeps the list engaged while generating revenue. Shift too far toward promotion and engagement drops. Shift too far toward content and revenue drops.

## ANNUAL PLAN CONVERSION FROM EMAIL

For SaaS or subscription products, email is the best channel for converting monthly subscribers to annual plans.

**Strategy:** Send a dedicated email sequence to monthly subscribers offering annual plan conversion with:
- Clear savings calculation (monthly x 12 vs. annual price)
- Exclusive annual-only bonuses
- Limited-time conversion window
- Testimonials from annual plan members

This strategy has been shown to significantly increase LTV while reducing churn.
