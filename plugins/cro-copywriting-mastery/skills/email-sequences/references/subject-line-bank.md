# Subject Line Bank

## CORE SUBJECT LINE PRINCIPLES

1. **Test three capitalizations:** Title Case, Sentence case, all lowercase. Amarsi tests all three.
2. **Pop culture references** create instant personality and stand out in inbox
3. **Part 1/Part 2** creates open loops across emails
4. **Preview text** should expand or contrast the subject line — NEVER repeat it
5. **"From X to Y"** transformation format works for testimonial emails
6. **Keep under 50 characters** for full mobile display
7. **Avoid spam trigger words:** free, guarantee, limited time, act now, buy now, click here

## SUBJECT LINE FORMULAS BY CATEGORY

### The Bold Claim
State something provocative or surprising as if it were absolute truth.

- "Stories sell everything"
- "Email is not dead. Your emails are."
- "The #1 reason your funnel doesn't work"
- "Copywriting is 80% science"
- "Your landing page is lying to you"

### The Curiosity Gap
Withhold just enough to force the open.

- "Not having this = death"
- "The email I almost didn't send"
- "What I found when I looked at the data"
- "The one thing nobody tells you about [topic]"
- "This changes everything (no, really)"
- "I was wrong about this..."

### The Transformation
Show before-and-after in the subject line.

- "from burn out to so fresh, so clean..."
- "From $0 to $10K in 90 days"
- "from struggling to sold out"
- "How [name] went from stuck to $47K/month"
- "[Name]: from bankruptcy to 7 figures"

### The Numbered List
Numbers signal easy-to-consume content.

- "3 reasons why emails die..."
- "7 things your landing page is missing"
- "5 mistakes killing your conversions"
- "The 1 change that doubled our revenue"
- "11 subject lines that got 40%+ open rates"

### The Continuation (Open Loop Across Emails)
Creates urgency to open by referencing an unresolved thread.

- "Not having this = death part 1"
- "Not having this equals death part 2"
- "[Topic] — here's what I meant (part 2)"
- "Remember what I said yesterday?"
- "I promised you this — here it is"

### The Pop Culture Reference
Instantly recognizable, creates personality and warmth.

- "Welcome To The Party, Pal!" (Die Hard)
- "from burn out to so fresh, so clean..." (Outkast)
- "Houston, we have a problem" (Apollo 13)
- "I'll be back" (Terminator) — for re-engagement
- "May the [benefit] be with you" (Star Wars)
- "One does not simply [challenge]" (Lord of the Rings)

### The Conversational
Sounds like a text from a friend, not a marketing email.

- "self-esteem plays a big part..."
- "How many emails?!"
- "quick question for you"
- "have you seen this?"
- "weird thing happened today"
- "honestly?"
- "you're not going to believe this"

### The Question
Forces the reader to mentally answer, creating engagement.

- "Are you making this mistake?"
- "What would you do with an extra $5K/month?"
- "Ready for the truth about [topic]?"
- "Can I be honest with you?"
- "What if everything you knew about [topic] was wrong?"

### The Ominous/Dramatic
Creates urgency through tone, not fake scarcity.

- "Not having this = death part 1"
- "the mistake that's costing you everything"
- "this is why you're stuck"
- "the uncomfortable truth"
- "we need to talk about [topic]"

### The Personal Story
Signals vulnerability and authenticity.

- "I almost quit last Tuesday"
- "the worst advice I ever took"
- "my biggest failure (and what it taught me)"
- "the email I wrote at 3am"
- "what my mentor said that changed everything"

### The Direct Benefit
Clearly states what the reader gets. Best for Most Aware audiences.

- "Your [resource] is ready"
- "here's the template I promised"
- "the exact script I use for cold outreach"
- "your step-by-step guide to [result]"

## PREVIEW TEXT (PREHEADER) FORMULAS

Preview text appears after the subject line in most email clients. It is your second chance to earn the open.

### Rules
- NEVER repeat the subject line
- Either expand on it or create contrast
- Keep to 40-90 characters
- Use it to answer "why should I open this?"

### Formulas
| Subject Line | Preview Text |
|-------------|-------------|
| "3 reasons why emails die..." | "And how to make sure yours never do" |
| "Welcome To The Party, Pal!" | "Here's your download + what happens next" |
| "Not having this = death part 1" | "Relax, it's not as dramatic as it sounds" |
| "from burn out to so fresh, so clean..." | "How [name] turned it all around in 90 days" |
| "I was wrong about this" | "And it cost me $14,000 to find out" |

## SUBJECT LINE TESTING METHODOLOGY

### A/B Test Protocol
1. Split your list 50/50
2. Test ONLY the subject line (keep everything else identical)
3. Wait 24 hours before declaring a winner
4. Measure open rate as primary metric
5. Track click-through as secondary metric (opens that do not click = bad subject-to-content match)

### What to Test
- Capitalization: Title Case vs. sentence case vs. all lowercase
- Length: Short (3-5 words) vs. medium (6-10 words) vs. long (11+ words)
- Format: Question vs. statement vs. number list
- Tone: Dramatic vs. conversational vs. direct
- Personalization: With name vs. without name
- Emoji: With emoji vs. without (test carefully — can hurt deliverability)

### Benchmark Open Rates
- Welcome email (Email 1): 50-70% (highest in sequence)
- Early sequence (Emails 2-4): 30-50%
- Mid sequence (Emails 5-6): 25-40%
- Sales email (Email 7): 20-35%

If your rates are significantly below these, subject lines need work. If rates are above but click-through is low, the email body needs work.

## SUBJECT LINE ANTI-PATTERNS

### Never Do These
1. **ALL CAPS** — Triggers spam filters and feels aggressive
2. **Multiple exclamation marks!!!** — Looks desperate
3. **Clickbait that doesn't deliver** — Opens mean nothing if trust is broken
4. **"Re:" or "Fwd:" tricks** — Deceptive and damages trust permanently
5. **Overly salesy language** — "Buy now," "Limited time offer," "Act fast"
6. **Too vague** — "Hey" or "Check this out" (no reason to open)
7. **Too long** — Gets cut off on mobile; loses impact
8. **Lying** — "Your order has shipped" when nothing was ordered

### The Golden Rule
Write subject lines that you would WANT to open if they appeared in your own inbox. If you would delete it, your subscribers will too.

## ADVANCED FORMULA CATEGORIES

### The Confession
Signals vulnerability and insider access. Creates a sense of privilege in the reader.

- "confessions of a failed copywriter"
- "I lied to you about [topic]"
- "the truth about how I got started"
- "something I've never told anyone"
- "my dirty little [industry] secret"

### The Contrast / Paradox
Juxtaposes two ideas that seem contradictory, forcing the reader to resolve the tension by opening.

- "why lazy marketers outperform hard workers"
- "the expensive way to save money"
- "do less, convert more"
- "the counterintuitive trick to [result]"
- "why your best traffic source is killing your business"

### The Social Proof Tease
Uses other people's results to create FOMO.

- "[Name] just hit $10K/month (here's how)"
- "she did ONE thing differently"
- "47 people used this template last week"
- "what top performers do that you don't"
- "read this before you read another email"

### The Deadline / Urgency (Use Sparingly, Must Be Real)
Only use when the deadline is genuine. Fake urgency destroys trust permanently.

- "last chance: [specific offer]"
- "closing tonight at midnight"
- "I'm pulling this down tomorrow"
- "48 hours left"
- "this won't be here next week"
