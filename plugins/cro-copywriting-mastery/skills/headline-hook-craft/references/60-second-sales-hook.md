# The 60-Second Sales Hook Framework

**Source:** Kevin Rogers (Copy Chief founder, ex-standup comic, 10-year freelance copywriter) via Traffic & Funnels Copywriting Masterclass
**Origin:** Derived from a standup comedy joke formula. When the stakes are high and you cannot afford to fail (TV debut, first impression), this is the formula comics use.

---

## The Joke Formula (Comedy Origin)

**Structure:** Identity > Struggle > Discovery > Surprise

The power of comedy is the surprise -- the unexpected twist that releases tension as laughter. Marketing adapts this by replacing the surprise with a result.

**Comedy example:**
- Identity: Seemingly a mother (Karen Ronkowski on Letterman)
- Struggle: Kids acting crazy in Walmart
- Discovery: She pulled a fly swatter off the shelf and smacked them
- Surprise: She does not have kids

---

## The Marketing Adaptation

Change "Surprise" to "Result," then add a Call to Action:

### Identity > Struggle > Discovery > Result > Call to Action

This creates a compressed hero's journey in 60 seconds or less.

---

## The Five Components

### 1. Identity
Who you are. A simple, relatable introduction.

**Rules:**
- State your name and one identifying detail
- The identifying detail should make you relatable to your audience
- Keep it to one sentence
- The identity should set up the struggle

**Templates:**
- "Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm a [relatable identity]."
- "Hi, I'm [Name], and [number] years ago I was [relatable situation]."
- "Hi, I'm [Name]. Like you, I [common experience]."

**Examples:**
- "Hi, I'm Kevin Rogers."
- "Hi, I'm Sarah. Three years ago I was a burnt-out marketing manager working 70-hour weeks."
- "I'm Mike. I'm a former accountant who couldn't stand the thought of another tax season."

---

### 2. Struggle
The problem you faced -- one that mirrors your audience's problem.

**Rules:**
- Must be a struggle your audience can see themselves in
- Be specific and vivid -- not vague
- Include emotional weight (how it felt, not just what happened)
- One to two sentences maximum

**Templates:**
- "I spent [timeframe] struggling with [specific problem]."
- "For years, I [painful situation] and nothing seemed to work."
- "I was [negative state] -- [specific painful detail] -- and I couldn't figure out why."

**Examples:**
- "I spent years as a dead broke standup comedian."
- "For three years, I tried every diet, every program, every supplement. Nothing moved the scale."
- "I was losing clients faster than I could find them, working 14-hour days just to stay afloat."

---

### 3. Discovery
What you found that changed everything. THIS IS WHERE YOU NAME YOUR MECHANISM.

**Rules:**
- Name your unique mechanism here -- do NOT use generic terms
- The discovery should feel like a turning point
- It must be specific enough that Googling leads back to you
- Frame it as a discovery, not as something you invented (more humble, more believable)

**Critical rule -- Name It and Claim It:**
- BAD: "Then I discovered a way to convert cold traffic" (generic -- they will Google it and find competitors)
- GOOD: "Then I discovered the 60-Second Sales Hook" (unique, Googling only leads to you)
- When you name something and call it a formula/system/method, it shows you put work into it
- They picture you in the lab with smoking beakers -- expertise signaling

**The Google Slap Test (Todd Brown):** If they can Google the generic term and see millions of results from competitors, you have lost them. Name it something unique.

**Templates:**
- "Until I discovered [Mechanism Name] -- a [brief descriptor] that [key differentiator]."
- "Until I stumbled onto what I now call [Mechanism Name]."
- "That's when I found [Mechanism Name], a [category] that [what makes it different]."

**Examples:**
- "Until I discovered how a simple joke formula can be used as an irresistible sales hook I call the 60 Second Sales Hook"
- "Until I discovered what I now call the Revenue Flywheel -- a 3-step system that turns one client into five."
- "That's when I found the Clarity Canvas -- a framework that replaces 20 hours of market research with a single 90-minute session."

---

### 4. Result
The outcome of using your discovery. Specific, measurable, believable.

**Rules:**
- Use specific numbers when possible
- Make it aspirational but believable
- Connect it to what your audience wants
- Keep it to one to two sentences

**Templates:**
- "...and began [achieving result] for [who]."
- "...which allowed me to [specific measurable result] in [timeframe]."
- "Since then, I've helped [number] [audience] [achieve specific result]."

**Examples:**
- "and began teaching marketers how to use it to skyrocket sales and grow their businesses."
- "which allowed me to go from $3K months to $47K months in under 90 days."
- "Since then, I've helped over 200 coaches and consultants add $100K+ to their annual revenue."

---

### 5. Call to Action
The next step. Clear, low-friction, specific.

**Rules:**
- One action only -- do not give multiple options
- Low commitment (opt-in, watch, read) rather than high commitment (buy, schedule)
- Tell them exactly what to do and what they will get
- Include the mechanism name in the CTA when possible

**Templates:**
- "If you'd like to discover [Mechanism Name], [specific action] below."
- "To see how [Mechanism Name] can work for you, [action]."
- "Enter your [info] below to get the [Mechanism Name] delivered to your inbox."

**Examples:**
- "If you'd like to discover this powerful four-sentence formula, put it to work in your business, enter your name below."
- "To see the Revenue Flywheel in action, click the button below to watch the free 12-minute walkthrough."
- "Download the Clarity Canvas template free -- just enter your email."

## Complete 60-Second Sales Hook Examples

### Example 1: Digital Marketing
- **Identity:** "I'm Jordan, a former school teacher."
- **Struggle:** "For two years after leaving teaching, I couldn't figure out how to get clients for my new marketing agency. I tried cold emails, LinkedIn DMs, even door knocking. Nothing worked."
- **Discovery:** "Then I stumbled onto what I call the Referral Engine System -- a method that turns every client into three more without asking for a single referral."
- **Result:** "In the last 18 months, we've added 94 clients and crossed $2M in revenue -- all from a team of 4."
- **CTA:** "If you'd like to see exactly how the Referral Engine works, enter your email below for the free training."

### Example 2: Health/Fitness
- **Identity:** "Hi, I'm Dr. Anna Chen."
- **Struggle:** "After my second child, I gained 45 pounds that refused to come off. I tried keto, intermittent fasting, even expensive meal delivery services. Nothing budged."
- **Discovery:** "Until I discovered what I now call the Metabolic Reset Protocol -- a simple sequence of food timing adjustments based on circadian biology."
- **Result:** "I lost 47 pounds in 5 months without restricting any food groups, and I've since helped over 3,000 women do the same."
- **CTA:** "Take the free Metabolic Assessment to see if the Reset Protocol is right for you."

### Example 3: SaaS/Software
- **Identity:** "I'm Matt. I ran a small e-commerce store."
- **Struggle:** "I was spending $4,000 a month on ads and losing money on every sale. My ROAS was 0.7x and I couldn't figure out where the leak was."
- **Discovery:** "Then I built what became the Funnel Diagnostic Dashboard -- a single view that shows you exactly where customers drop off and why."
- **Result:** "Within 60 days, I went from losing money to a 3.2x ROAS. Now over 500 e-commerce brands use the Dashboard to find and fix their funnel leaks."
- **CTA:** "Start your free 14-day trial -- connect your store in 3 minutes and see your first diagnostic report today."

## Naming Your Mechanism: Rules and Patterns

### DO:
- Sound scientific or official: "The E5 Method," "The Clarity Canvas," "Pixel Refreshing"
- Use something that could appear in a news article or textbook
- Make it 2-4 words
- Include an unexpected combination of words
- Consider acronyms: "The R.A.D. Framework" (Require, Acquire, Desire)

### DON'T:
- Sound benefit-driven or commercial: "The Explosive Growth System" (sounds like hype)
- Sound generic: "The Marketing System" (no differentiation)
- Use someone else's trademarked name
- Make it so obscure nobody can remember it

### Naming patterns that work:
- [Adjective] + [Noun]: "The Clarity Canvas," "The Revenue Flywheel"
- [Acronym]: "The CPB Framework," "The E5 Method"
- [Number] + [Noun]: "The 60-Second Sales Hook"
- [Unexpected Noun] + [Category]: "The Metabolic Reset Protocol"
- [Authority Figure] + [Possessive] + [Noun]: "The Schwartz Awareness Ladder"

## Where to Use the 60-Second Sales Hook

1. **About page** -- Replace your boring bio with the hook
2. **Ad copy** -- Structure your ad as a compressed hook
3. **Landing page hero** -- The hook can BE the hero section copy
4. **Webinar intro** -- Open any presentation with your hook
5. **Podcast guest intro** -- When introducing yourself on podcasts
6. **Email welcome sequence** -- Email 1 should tell your hook story
7. **Social media bio** -- Compress to 2-3 sentences for bios
8. **Networking/elevator pitch** -- The original use case

**Key insight:** You can tell DIFFERENT stories for DIFFERENT products or lead magnets. The hook is not a single biography -- it is a flexible formula that adapts to the offer.
