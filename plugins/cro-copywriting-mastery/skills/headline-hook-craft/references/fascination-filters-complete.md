# Complete Fascination Filters & Rules

**Source:** Eddie Shleyner - Transformational Landing Pages (via Ryan Deiss course)
**What fascinations are:** 1-3 sentence statements that tease what the reader will learn inside content. They promise value but do NOT give it away. They create curiosity and intrigue like a cliffhanger in a TV show.
**Why they matter:** Many direct marketers consider fascinations the single most important copywriting skill. Eugene Schwartz had a 90% control-beating win rate largely through fascination-driven copy.

---

## Why Fascinations Work

1. They promise a benefit (what is in it for the reader)
2. They create curiosity and intrigue (cliffhanger effect)
3. The only options are: convert or keep reading more fascinations (win-win for you)
4. Reading begets reading -- the more a prospect reads, the more likely she converts
5. Even ONE fascination that hits the right nerve can compel conversion
6. The more fascinations you stack, the more curiosity compounds

---

## The Three-Step Fascination Writing Process

### Step 1: Find the Nugget
Identify a piece of valuable information inside your content. Ask:
- What is the most surprising thing someone will learn?
- What is the most counterintuitive insight?
- What is the most practical tip with the biggest impact?
- What result or data point is most impressive?
- What mistake do most people make that this content corrects?

### Step 2: Tease the Value and State the Benefit
Hint at what the reader will gain WITHOUT giving away the answer:
- Name the benefit they will experience
- Reference the specific piece of information
- Create enough intrigue that they NEED to know more
- Do NOT reveal the actual answer, method, or name

### Step 3: Apply a Filter
Choose one of the 18 fascination filters below and pour your nugget through it.

## The 18 Fascination Filters

### Filter 1: Mechanisms
**Template:** "The [mechanism/method/system] that [benefit]"
**Examples:**
- "The 3-step morning routine that eliminates brain fog by 10am"
- "The pricing framework that doubled our average order value in 30 days"
- "The hiring question that reveals toxic candidates in under 60 seconds"

**When to use:** When your content reveals a specific process, method, or system

---

### Filter 2: Statement + Interest + Benefit
**Template:** "[Interesting statement]. [Why it matters] -- and [benefit to reader]"
**Examples:**
- "Most landing pages break a critical rule before the reader scrolls once. Find out what it is -- and how fixing it can double your conversion rate"
- "Your competitors are making this mistake every single day. Learn to spot it -- and use it to your advantage"

**When to use:** When you have a provocative opening statement

---

### Filter 3: "What"
**Template:** "What [surprising thing] does for [result]"
**Examples:**
- "What a cereal box can teach you about writing landing page copy"
- "What the top 1% of sales reps do in the first 30 seconds that everyone else skips"
- "What your bounce rate is really telling you (it's not what you think)"

**When to use:** When content reveals an unexpected connection

---

### Filter 4: "What Never"
**Template:** "What you should never [do] when [situation]"
**Examples:**
- "What you should never say in a sales email subject line"
- "What you should never do when a prospect says 'I need to think about it'"
- "The one word you should never put on a CTA button"

**When to use:** When content includes a critical warning or mistake to avoid

---

### Filter 5: "Right/Wrong"
**Template:** "The right/wrong way to [action]"
**Examples:**
- "The right way to follow up after a demo (most reps do the opposite)"
- "The wrong way to write testimonials for your landing page"
- "The right way to introduce pricing on a sales call"

**When to use:** When content contrasts correct vs. incorrect approaches

---

### Filter 6: "Why"
**Template:** "Why [counterintuitive claim]"
**Examples:**
- "Why your best customers are actually hurting your growth"
- "Why shorter landing pages can outperform longer ones (but usually don't)"
- "Why your guarantee is costing you money instead of making you more"

**When to use:** When content explains something counterintuitive

---

### Filter 7: "When"
**Template:** "When to [action] for [maximum benefit]"
**Examples:**
- "When to send your sales emails for 3x the response rate"
- "When to raise your prices (the signals most businesses miss)"
- "The exact moment to introduce your price on a landing page"

**When to use:** When timing is a key factor in the content

---

### Filter 8: Specific Question
**Template:** "[Specific question about reader's situation]?"
**Examples:**
- "Is your headline passing the 7-second test?"
- "How many of these 5 conversion killers are on your checkout page right now?"
- "Are you leaving money on the table with your current guarantee?"

**When to use:** When you want the reader to self-diagnose

---

### Filter 9: "If, Then"
**Template:** "If you [situation], then [consequence/benefit]"
**Examples:**
- "If you're spending more than $5K/month on ads, then this one tweak could save you 30%"
- "If your cart abandonment rate is above 65%, then you're likely making one of these 3 mistakes"
- "If you've ever felt like your copy sounds 'salesy,' this framework fixes it instantly"

**When to use:** When the content applies to a specific segment

---

### Filter 10: "Quickest, Easiest"
**Template:** "The quickest/easiest way to [desired result]"
**Examples:**
- "The quickest way to write a landing page that converts (even if you hate writing)"
- "The easiest method for doubling email open rates this week"
- "The fastest path from idea to published landing page"

**When to use:** When speed or simplicity is the key benefit

---

### Filter 11: Number
**Template:** "[Number] [things] that [benefit]"
**Examples:**
- "7 headline formulas that work in every industry"
- "3 words that make any CTA button more clickable"
- "12 phrases that kill landing page conversions (you're probably using at least 4)"

**When to use:** When content contains a list or collection of items

---

### Filter 12: Big Promise
**Template:** "[Bold, specific promise of result]"
**Examples:**
- "Double your landing page conversion rate in one afternoon"
- "Write headlines that score 50%+ on the emotional analyzer -- every time"
- "Generate 100 qualified leads per month with a single landing page"

**When to use:** When you can make a bold, believable promise backed by proof

---

### Filter 13: "How"
**Template:** "How to [achieve result] without [obstacle]"
**Examples:**
- "How to write sales copy without sounding pushy or manipulative"
- "How to get 47% email open rates without clickbait subject lines"
- "How to build a high-converting page without touching code"

**When to use:** The most versatile filter -- works for almost any content

---

### Filter 14: "Caution"
**Template:** "Caution: [warning about common mistake]"
**Examples:**
- "Caution: this common headline mistake is silently killing your conversions"
- "Caution: if your landing page loads in more than 3 seconds, you're losing 40% of visitors"
- "Warning: most testimonials actually REDUCE trust (here's why)"

**When to use:** When content reveals a danger or critical mistake

---

### Filter 15: "Truth"
**Template:** "The truth about [misconception]"
**Examples:**
- "The truth about A/B testing (and why most tests fail before they start)"
- "The truth about long-form landing pages vs. short-form"
- "The real reason your Facebook ads aren't converting (it's not the targeting)"

**When to use:** When content debunks a myth or corrects a misconception

---

### Filter 16: "Best"
**Template:** "The best [method/approach] for [specific result]"
**Examples:**
- "The best time to publish landing pages for maximum organic traffic"
- "The best CTA button color (according to 1,200 A/B tests)"
- "The best way to handle the 'I need to think about it' objection"

**When to use:** When content identifies a clear winner among options

---

### Filter 17: "Surprising"
**Template:** "[Surprising fact] about [topic]"
**Examples:**
- "The surprising reason 16% of your visitors never see your CTA"
- "A surprising study shows stories increase perceived value by 2,706%"
- "The surprising link between cereal boxes and high-converting hero sections"

**When to use:** When content contains data or facts that defy expectations

---

### Filter 18: "Are You"
**Template:** "Are you [making this mistake/in this situation]?"
**Examples:**
- "Are you accidentally training your audience to ignore your emails?"
- "Are you using the wrong landing page type for your product?"
- "Are you still writing headlines the old way?"

**When to use:** When you want to create urgency through self-identification

## The 9 Rules for Writing Fascinations

### Rule 1: Write More Than You Need
Overproduction yields the best material. If you need 8 fascinations, write 25. The cream rises to the top through volume.

### Rule 2: The More Specific the Benefit, the Better
Vague: "A way to get more leads." Specific: "The 3-word subject line tweak that generated 47 more leads in a single week." Specificity creates pictures in the mind.

### Rule 3: Prove It
Back claims with evidence. Numbers, percentages, timeframes, and named sources add weight. "The method that doubled our conversion rate" is weaker than "The method that took our conversion rate from 2.1% to 4.7% in 14 days."

### Rule 4: Clarity Beats Concision
Do not sacrifice clarity to be brief. A 20-word fascination that is crystal clear beats a 10-word fascination that is ambiguous. The reader must instantly understand the promised benefit.

### Rule 5: Use Simple Words
No jargon, no complexity, no industry-insider language. Write at a middle school reading level. If your fascination requires a glossary, rewrite it.

### Rule 6: Use the Prospect's Vocabulary
Mirror the exact language your prospects use. If they say "get more customers," do not say "increase customer acquisition velocity." Review mining and interview transcripts give you this language.

### Rule 7: Avoid Transactional Words
Words that feel like selling (buy, purchase, invest, order, subscribe) trigger sales resistance. Replace with value words (discover, learn, get, see, find out).

### Rule 8: Avoid Work Words
Words that imply effort or labor (implement, execute, complete, study, practice) create friction. Replace with ease words (just, simply, in minutes, without effort).

### Rule 9: Create Variance
Vary sentence structure, length, and rhythm across your fascination set. If every fascination starts with "How to," the reader tunes out by fascination 4. Mix the filters.

## Fascination Placement on the Page

**Eddie Shleyner's approach:** Fascinations go in Divider 2 of the landing page, right after the hero section. They build desire before proof and authority sections.

**Bullet copy sections:** Fascinations are the foundation of bullet copy in any long-form page, sales letter, or email. Use them in:
- Landing page "what you'll learn" sections
- Email body copy (3-5 fascinations per email)
- Sales page "here's what's inside" sections
- Webinar registration pages
- Course/product feature lists
- Social media ad copy

**Volume guidelines:**
- Landing page fascination section: 6-10 fascinations
- Sales page bullet section: 15-25 fascinations
- Email: 3-5 fascinations
- Ad copy: 1-3 fascinations
