# Complete Headline Formula Bank

**Sources:** Copy Hackers (Joanna Wiebe), Ryan Deiss, Sandy Franks, Andrew Milligan (PMS), Todd Brown, Sean Vosler, Arman Assadi, OnePeak, Copywriting Made Simple (Tom Albrighton)
**Count:** 55+ formulas organized by type
**Usage:** Pick formulas matching your awareness stage and page type. Write at least 20-25 variations minimum.

---

## HOW-TO HEADLINES

The most reliable headline category. Works at every awareness stage above unaware.

1. `How to [achieve result] without [obstacle]`
2. `How to [achieve result] in [timeframe]`
3. `How to [achieve result] even if [common objection]`
4. `How [notable person] [achieves notable thing] (and how you can too)`
5. `How we [achieved impressive result] with [simple mechanism]`
6. `How to [desirable thing] [counter to expectations]`

**PMS Variant (Andrew Milligan):**
7. `How [proof/authority]'s '[mechanism name]' lets you [core benefit claim]`

**Examples:**
- "How to Write Landing Pages That Convert 3x Better Without Hiring a Copywriter"
- "How We Built a $100K/Month Business With One Simple Funnel"
- "How Michael Jordan's 'mindflow' practice rebuilds your mindset in 17 minutes"

## QUESTION HEADLINES

Engage the reader by inviting mental participation. Leading questions get mental "yes" responses.

8. `Who else wants [most desirable outcome]?`
9. `Are you still wasting money on [thing] without anything to show for it?`
10. `Do you make these mistakes in [activity]?`
11. `Is it worth [low price] to you to [outstanding result]?`
12. `Do you have the courage to [desirable thing]?`
13. `What if you could [dream outcome] in [timeframe]?`
14. `What would you do with [benefit] -- starting tomorrow?`

**Rules for question headlines:**
- Leading questions that invite "yes" are powerful
- Reflective questions starting with "what" or "how" invite deeper engagement
- NEVER use "why not" -- it invites the reader to give reasons NOT to buy
- If the reader can answer "no" and move on, the question failed

## RESULT/BENEFIT HEADLINES

Lead directly with the outcome. Best for product-aware and most-aware audiences.

15. `[Desired end result] for [avatar]` (Ryan Deiss -- simplest, most reliable)
16. `Get [desired result] from your [existing asset], starting now`
17. `[Do something hard] in [period of time]`
18. `Get rid of [problem] once and for all`
19. `[Result] guaranteed -- or [consequence to you]`
20. `The secret to [desired outcome] is simpler than you think`

**PMS Formula (Andrew Milligan):**
21. `[Proof] + [Mechanism/"IP"] + [Core Benefit Claim]`
- "The 'K-Signs' to adding an extra $4,327 straight to your portfolio in the next 16 hours"
- "Why Wayne Gretzky's 'puck-cue' lets you become unforgettable with your customers"

## CLARITY/PRODUCT HEADLINES (TechCrunch Style)

Best for SaaS, tools, and products where clarity is the primary goal.

22. `[Product] is a [category] that [different thing it does best]` (Copy Hackers)
23. `The only [product category] made exclusively to [most desirable outcome]`
24. `The only [product category] that doesn't [objection/anxiety]`
25. `[Product] helps [avatar] [achieve result] -- without [obstacle]`

**Kernel Sentence foundation:** Before writing any of these, reduce to "Blank is Blank." If you cannot, you do not have clarity.

---

## TRANSFORMATION HEADLINES

Frame the headline around changing from a before state to an after state.

26. `Transform your [existing asset] into [desired end result]` (Ryan Deiss)
27. `Turn [something they have] into [desired end result]` (Ryan Deiss)
28. `From [negative current state] to [positive desired state] in [timeframe]`
29. `[Product] took me from [before state] to [after state] -- here's how`
30. `Stop [current pain]. Start [desired new reality].`

---

## NEWS/ANNOUNCEMENT HEADLINES

Leverage novelty. Everyone likes what is new. Must have genuine news.

31. `Introducing [product]: [benefit]`
32. `Now you can [desirable thing] [counter to expectations]` (Copy Hackers)
33. `Finally: [solution to long-standing problem]`
34. `New: [product feature] that [benefit]`
35. `[Year] update: [what changed and why it matters]`

---

## COMMAND HEADLINES

Direct and forceful. Can be inspirational or urgent.

36. `Stop [known pain point]. We'll do it for you.` (Ryan Deiss)
37. `Make your [constituent group] better at [core skill/benefit]` (Ryan Deiss)
38. `Easily [end result] with [feature]` (Ryan Deiss)
39. `Everyone can now [something simple] and [accomplish desired end result]` (Ryan Deiss)

---

## OBJECTION-BUSTING HEADLINES

Acknowledge the objection head-on and counter it.

40. `[Objection.] But/And it works.` (Copy Hackers)
41. `You're tired of [objection]. But you [desired outcome]. So it's time you met [product].` (Copy Hackers)
42. `Tired of [known pain point]? [Product] makes it easy to [overcome pain]` (Ryan Deiss)
43. `We promise you this: [result] or [consequence]` (Copy Hackers)
44. `[Accomplish desired result] without [known roadblock]` (Ryan Deiss)

---

## STORY/CURIOSITY HEADLINES

Best for unaware and problem-aware audiences. Create curiosity gaps.

45. `[Notable person] shows you how to [do notable thing like they do]` (Copy Hackers)
46. `[Eliminate pain in unexpected way]` (Copy Hackers)
47. `The peculiar [story detail] that [surprising result]` (Sandy Franks style)
48. `Confessions of a [role/identity]` (Traffic & Funnels -- their most successful ad template)
49. `They all laughed when I [did thing] -- but when I [result]...` (John Caples)

---

## TWO-PART HEADLINES (Sandy Franks' Preference)

Two-part headlines work best for business origin stories and long-form content.

50. `[Intriguing detail about person] + [surprising outcome they achieved]`
51. `[Setup creating curiosity] -- [payoff teasing the result]`

**Real headline examples with open rates:**
- "The Peculiar Tale of the Black Bear That Saved Lives and The Man Who Turned a $54 Investment Into a 2.2 Billion Fortune" -- 49% open rate
- "This Husband and Wife Team Found Their Eureka Moment When They Paired Gasoline With a Necessity All Humans Crave" -- 48% open rate

## SOCIAL PROOF HEADLINES

Lead with proof rather than promise.

52. `[X] [people/companies] already [achieved result] -- here's how`
53. `Why [X] [identity group] switched to [product] this [time period]`
54. `Rated #1 by [authority] for [specific use case]`
55. `[Specific number] [result metric] -- and counting`

## HEADLINE FORMULAS BY AWARENESS STAGE

| Awareness Stage | Best Formula Categories | Avoid |
|----------------|------------------------|-------|
| Unaware | Story/Curiosity, Two-Part | Product names, direct offers |
| Problem Aware | Question, How-To | Product features, pricing |
| Solution Aware | How-To, Transformation, Clarity | Generic claims, "best" superlatives |
| Product Aware | Objection-Busting, Result/Benefit, Social Proof | Problem agitation (they know) |
| Most Aware | Command, News/Announcement, direct offer | Long story builds (they know you) |

## THE IMITATION GAME METHOD (Todd Brown)

A structured process for generating headlines by adapting proven winners:

1. Collect 10-20 winning headlines from swipe files (any industry)
2. Identify the structural pattern of each (e.g., "How to X without Y")
3. Replace X and Y with YOUR product's specifics
4. Generate 3-5 variations per template
5. This reliably produces 30-100 headline candidates in under an hour

---

## THE AMAZON R&D METHOD (Todd Brown)

Mine best-selling book subtitles for headline inspiration:

1. Go to Amazon in your topic category
2. Look at top 20 best-selling books
3. Read the SUBTITLES (not the main titles)
4. Subtitles contain the benefit promise that sells the book
5. Adapt the subtitle structure for your headline

Example: Book subtitle "The Surprising Science of How to Lead, Energize, and Get Out of the Way" becomes "The Surprising Method Behind How to Generate Leads, Energize Sales, and Let Your Funnel Do the Work"

## THE "EVEN IF" TECHNIQUE (Todd Brown)

Add "even if" to any headline to pre-empt objections:

- "[Result headline] -- even if you've never [done this before]"
- "[Result headline] -- even if [common objection]"
- "[Result headline] -- even if you only have [limited resource]"

This single addition can significantly increase click-through by addressing the reader's self-doubt before it crystallizes.
