# 12+ Headline Formulas with Real Examples

## 1. BENEFIT-FOCUSED FORMULA

**Structure:** "[Outcome] [Without/For] [Common Fear/Effort]"

**Rules:**
- Lead with the transformation
- Eliminate the objection in headline itself
- Must be specific (not vague benefits)

**Examples:**

*From Copy Hackers 10x Training:*
- "Get 50 Qualified Leads Daily Without Cold Calling"
- "Build Your Dream Body in 90 Days Without Giving Up Your Favorite Foods"

*From Arman Assadi 7-Figure Copywriting:*
- "Cut Your Costs in Half (Without Sacrificing Quality)"
- "Get More Leads While Spending LESS on Marketing"

*From Glenn Fisher Art of the Click:*
- "Free up 21 minutes every week" (Coffee maker example)
- "Write better copy 3X faster without industry experience"

**When to use:** Strong product, clear problem, high-intent audience

**Why it works:** Leads with transformation; removes biggest objection preemptively

---

## 2. THE RULE OF ONE BENEFIT FORMULA

**Structure:** "[Specific Result] For [Specific Person]"

**Rules:**
- One reader, one promise, one outcome
- Specificity in both parts
- No mixed audiences
- Don't dilute with secondary benefits

**Examples:**

*From Copy Hackers (Rule of One):*
- "The Ultimate Sales Page Formula for Digital Product Creators"
- "Email Sequences That Convert for B2B SaaS Companies"

*From Eddie Shleyner Transformational Landing Pages:*
- "Stop Wasting Ad Spend: For Coaches Who Want Qualified Leads"
- "The Landing Page Formula for Service Providers in Tech"

*From Todd Brown A-Z Workshop:*
- "How to Get Your First Paying Client in 30 Days (For New Coaches)"
- "Scale Your Email List From 0 to 10,000 (For Consultants)"

**When to use:** Multiple audience segments, need to be specific, high-competition markets

**Why it works:** Reader immediately knows this is for them; no confusion = no scroll away

---

## 3. CURIOSITY GAP / INFORMATION GAP FORMULA

**Structure:** "[Interesting Fact/Problem] [Hint at Why/How]"

**Rules:**
- Create gap without fully answering
- Must be relevant to reader
- Deliver on promise inside (not clickbait)
- Often works with numbers

**Examples:**

*From Glenn Fisher Art of the Click:*
- "At 60 Miles an Hour, the Loudest Noise in a New Rolls-Royce Comes From the Electric Clock"
  - Gap: Why the electric clock? (curiosity pull)
  - Implicit benefit: So quiet, only clock audible

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "What 47,000+ Entrepreneurs Know About Productivity That You Don't"
- "The Single Biggest Issue Plaguing Every Copywriter on Earth (& the Simple Fix)"

*From Sandy Franks Story Framework:*
- "What Almost Destroyed This $100M Company (And Made It Stronger)"
- "The Secret That Changed Everything For This Struggling Solopreneur"

*From Adil Amarsi Encyclopedia of Copy:*
- "Discover The Blueprint That Generated Nearly A Billion Dollars In 400+ Markets"
- "Revealed: The Insider Secrets of a 9-Figure Copywriter"

**When to use:** Cold traffic, competitive markets, when benefit alone insufficient, education angle

**Why it works:** Brain naturally tries to close gaps; creates pull to read more

## 4. PROBLEM AGITATION FORMULA

**Structure:** "[Problem Identification] [Emotional Trigger] [Hint at Solution]"

**Rules:**
- Name SPECIFIC problem (not generic)
- Create identification (reader thinks "yes, that's me")
- Include emotion, not just facts
- Don't just complain—hint solution exists
- Often phrased as question

**Examples:**

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "Can't Get Your Kids to Sleep Without Fighting?"
- "Exhausted From Your High CPA On Facebook Ads?"
- "Trapped in the Feast-or-Famine Cycle?"

*From Eddie Shleyner:*
- "Your Landing Page Isn't Converting (Here's Why)"
- "You've Built Something Good, But It Feels Stuck at This Revenue Level"

*From Todd Brown:*
- "Tired of Losing Money Every Time You Run a Facebook Ad?"
- "How Frustrated Are You With Your High Customer Acquisition Cost?"

*From Copywriting Made Simple:*
- "You've Tried Everything and Still Can't Get Your Work Done On Time"
- "Feeling Like You're Not Cut Out for Sales? You're Not Alone"

**When to use:** Problem-aware audiences, email sequences, objection handling, existing lists

**Why it works:** Creates emotional resonance; makes reader feel seen and understood

## 5. NUMBER/SPECIFICITY FORMULA

**Structure:** "[Number] [Type] To [Outcome]"

**Rules:**
- Number must match content (promise 7, deliver 7)
- Odd numbers slightly better (3, 5, 7, 9, 11)
- Lower numbers work best (3-7 range)
- Specific numbers > round (7 > several)
- Can combine with benefit formula

**Examples:**

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "7 Proven Strategies To Lower Your Insurance Premiums"
- "3 Ways To Triple Your Productivity In 30 Days"
- "10-Step Copywriting Framework For Landing Pages"

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "8 Psychological Triggers For Clicks"
- "The 6 Common Copy Mistakes (And How to Fix Them)"

*From Todd Brown:*
- "The 3 Elements of Persuasion (Aristotle's Framework)"
- "5 Mistakes Preventing Your Hair Growth"

*From Adil Amarsi:*
- "The 13-Module Sales Page Framework"
- "7-Email Welcome Sequence Template"

**When to use:** Always safe, listicles, how-tos, educational content, blog posts

**Why it works:** Numbers reduce cognitive load ("I know what I'm getting"); odd slightly more interesting than even

## 6. HOW-TO / SOLUTION FORMULA

**Structure:** "How to [Specific Outcome] [In Time Period/Without Effort/For Audience]"

**Rules:**
- Outcome must be specific and desirable
- Time period makes it more compelling
- Can stack with benefit or without
- Most reliable formula when stuck
- Works across all mediums

**Examples:**

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "How to Write Sales Copy That Converts 2X Better"
- "How to Get Your First Paying Client in 30 Days"
- "How to Scale Your Email List From 0 to 10,000"

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "How to Master Direct Response Copywriting"
- "How to Get People to Click Almost Anything"

*From Todd Brown:*
- "How to Develop Your Big Idea in 7 Steps"
- "How to Position Your Offer As Superior"

*From Adil Amarsi:*
- "How To Write The Greatest Sales Letter (With Template)"
- "How To Build Email Sequences That Convert"

**When to use:** When stuck, educational angles, authority positioning, evergreen content

**Why it works:** Promise of knowledge/solution; natural pull forward

## 7. WHY FORMULA (Contrarian Angle)

**Structure:** "Why [Expected Thing] Actually [Unexpected Insight/Opposite]"

**Rules:**
- Contrarian vs. expected wisdom
- Must deliver on promise (not just clickbait)
- Often used for authority/thought leadership
- Can be provocative if authentic
- Pairs well with surprising data

**Examples:**

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "Why 90% of Copywriters Plateau at $50K/Year"
- "Why Most Landing Pages Fail (Even With Good Traffic)"

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "Why Long Copy Actually Works Better Than Short"
- "Why Your Headline Matters More Than Your Offer"

*From Todd Brown:*
- "Why 'Creating Desire' Is The Wrong Approach (You Should Channel It Instead)"
- "Why Better Copy Doesn't Always Mean Higher Conversions"

*From Copywriting Made Simple:*
- "Why You're Not Making Money (It's Not Your Fault)"
- "Why The Best Copy Feels Like A Conversation"

**When to use:** Contrarian positioning, expertise authority, breaking expected patterns

**Why it works:** Challenges assumption; creates cognitive dissonance that pulls reader in

## 8. QUESTION FORMULA (Rhetorical)

**Structure:** "[Relevant Question to Reader's Situation]?"

**Rules:**
- Must be relevant (not random)
- Presupposes reader's situation/challenge
- Open-ended better than yes/no
- Creates natural mental answer attempt
- Sometimes implied answer in sub-headline

**Examples:**

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "What's Keeping You From Your First Six Figures?"
- "What If You Could Write Copy in Half the Time?"

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "How Frustrated Are You With Your High CPA?"
- "Are You Still Using Old-School Sales Tactics?"

*From Eddie Shleyner:*
- "What Does Your Landing Page Do For A Living?"
- "Is Your Copy Actually Talking To The Right Person?"

*From Todd Brown:*
- "What's The One Thing Stopping Your Sales Growth?"
- "How Much Are You Leaving On The Table Every Month?"

**When to use:** Self-awareness campaigns, email openers, blog titles, presuppositional language

**Why it works:** Brain naturally tries to answer questions; creates engagement loop

## 9. NEWS/ANNOUNCEMENT FORMULA

**Structure:** "[New/Breakthrough/For the First Time] [Benefit/Discovery]"

**Rules:**
- Must feel genuinely timely
- Can leverage trend angles
- Platform updates/algorithm changes work well
- Research/study angles work
- Don't overuse (loses power with repetition)

**Examples:**

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "New Study Reveals Best Time To Exercise For Fat Loss"
- "Announced: The First AI-Powered Copywriting Tool"

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "Breaking: Facebook's Algorithm Change Affects 47% of Brands"

*From Eddie Shleyner:*
- "The Latest Research on Landing Page Conversions (2026)"
- "New Framework Discovered for Email Subject Lines"

**When to use:** Actual innovation, product launches, study releases, trend angles

**Why it works:** Novelty catches attention; recency bias at play

## 10. COMMAND/IMPERATIVE FORMULA

**Structure:** "[Action Verb] [Object] [Benefit Hint]"

**Rules:**
- Must feel congruent with offer
- Authoritative tone (can feel pushy if misused)
- Best with known/warm audiences
- Pair with benefit for maximum power
- Often stronger in email than cold landing pages

**Examples:**

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "Claim Your Free Report Before We Hit Capacity"
- "Discover the Framework That Changed Our Business"

*From Adil Amarsi:*
- "Get Started With Your Greatest Sales Letter"
- "Learn the 13-Module Framework Today"

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "Take the Direct Response Copywriting Masterclass"
- "Master These 8 Psychological Triggers"

**When to use:** Email CTAs, action-oriented audiences, existing relationships, clear offers

**Why it works:** Direct instruction creates authority; urgency through command tone

## 11. BEFORE/AFTER FORMULA

**Structure:** "[Before State] → [After State] [Without/For] [Easy Method]"

**Rules:**
- Paint both states vividly
- Before state must resonate emotionally
- After state must feel achievable
- Solution must seem simple
- Contrast creates value perception

**Examples:**

*From Todd Brown A-Z:*
- "From Struggling Marketer to Flourishing Entrepreneur (In 90 Days)"
- "Go From Confused About Copywriting to Confident in 7 Days"

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "From Feast-or-Famine to Consistent $10K Months"
- "From Exhausted Agency Owner to Hands-Off Business"

*From Glenn Fisher:*
- "No More Wasted Ad Spend → Maximum ROI With These 5 Strategies"

**When to use:** Transformation-focused offers, before/after case studies, webinar hooks

**Why it works:** Contrast shows value; reader projects themselves into after state

---

## 12. PRESUPPOSITIONAL FORMULA

**Structure:** "[When/If] [You Do Action], [Desired Result/Outcome]"

**Rules:**
- Assumes reader is already considering action
- Presupposes interest/readiness
- Often used with "when" not "if"
- Can be paired with other formulas
- Works for upsells/continuation

**Examples:**

*From Todd Brown:*
- "When You Master The Big Idea, Conversions Skyrocket"
- "Once You Understand Benefit Laddering, Sales Letters Become Easier"

*From Adil Amarsi:*
- "After You Get Your First Customer, Use This Upsell Formula"
- "Once You Complete The Welcome Sequence, Launch This Broadcast Series"

*From Arman Assadi:*
- "If You Apply This Framework, You'll See Results Within 7 Days"
- "When You Learn These 3 Secrets, Your Copy Transforms"

**When to use:** Mid-funnel emails, upsells, sequences, educated audiences, continuation hooks

**Why it works:** Assumes positive outcome; creates expectation of success

## FORMULA SELECTION DECISION TREE

**Start here when choosing a formula:**

1. **Is the reader problem/pain obvious?**
   - YES → Problem Agitation or Before/After formula
   - NO → Continue to #2

2. **Do you have unique data or angle?**
   - YES → Curiosity Gap or News/Announcement formula
   - NO → Continue to #3

3. **Is transformation central to offer?**
   - YES → Before/After or How-To formula
   - NO → Continue to #4

4. **Do you need to narrow audience?**
   - YES → Rule of One Benefit formula
   - NO → Continue to #5

5. **Do you have numbers/specifics?**
   - YES → Number/Specificity formula
   - NO → How-To or Benefit-Focused formula

## COMBINING FORMULAS (ADVANCED)

Most powerful headlines combine 2-3 formulas:

**Examples:**

*Number + Benefit + Without:*
- "7 Ways to Cut Your Costs in Half (Without Sacrificing Quality)"
  - (Number + Benefit + Without formula)

*How-To + Specificity + Time:*
- "How to Get Your First $10K Client in 30 Days"
  - (How-To + Number + Time formula)

*Benefit + Question + Audience:*
- "Ready to Stop Wasting $500/Month on Wasted Ad Spend?"
  - (Benefit + Question + Presuppositional)

*Curiosity + Problem + Solution Hint:*
- "The Surprising Reason Your Copy Isn't Converting (And the 3-Step Fix)"
  - (Curiosity + Problem + Solution)

## TESTING HEADLINES ACROSS FORMULAS

Best practice: Test different formulas, not just variations of same formula

**Example A/B test lineup:**
1. **Benefit formula:** "Get 50 Leads Daily Without Cold Calling" (control)
2. **Curiosity formula:** "The Secret Most Agencies Never Discover"
3. **Number formula:** "5 Ways to 3X Your Lead Generation"
4. **Problem formula:** "Exhausted From Low-Quality Leads?"
5. **How-To formula:** "How to Build Your Lead Machine"

Run these against each other; see which formula resonates best with YOUR audience.

---

## KEY INSIGHT: FORMULAS AS SCAFFOLDS

These aren't rigid templates—they're conversation scaffolds. Adapt them to your voice while preserving the structure. The mechanism is the talking points and psychology, not the exact words.

The best headline you write will often be a hybrid of 2-3 formulas, customized to your offer and audience.
