# Headline Testing Process: A/B Testing Methodology

## THE TESTING PHILOSOPHY

**Principle:** Your guess is worth less than data.

Never bet on one headline. Always test multiple angles and let the market decide.

### Why Headline Testing Matters

- Headlines determine 50-80% of copy effectiveness
- Small changes can create 2-3x lift
- Different audiences respond differently
- One winning formula doesn't work forever (market saturation)
- Testing compounds: 10% improvement × 10 tests = doubling conversions

---

## THE TESTING PROCESS (5 STEPS)

### STEP 1: GENERATE OPTIONS (Write 10+)

**Goal:** Create volume. Quantity breeds quality.

**Process:**
1. Spend 30 minutes writing headlines
2. Use 8 different formulas (benefit, curiosity, number, how-to, etc.)
3. Aim for 10-20 headlines minimum
4. Don't filter as you write (quantity first)

**Example Output:**
- Benefit: "Cut Your Energy Bills in Half"
- Curiosity: "The Weird Trick Your Utility Company Doesn't Want You to Know"
- Number: "7 Ways to Lower Your Energy Costs This Month"
- Question: "Ready to Stop Wasting Money on Overpriced Energy?"
- How-To: "How to Get Free or Cheap Energy (Here's the Secret)"
- Problem: "Exhausted by Your Skyrocketing Energy Bills?"
- Before/After: "From $300/Month to $150/Month (Without Solar)"
- Presuppositional: "Once You Know This Trick, You'll Never Pay Full Price Again"

**Why this works:** Volume generates options; options create winners

---

### STEP 2: NARROW TO BEST 3-5

**Goal:** Identify strongest candidates for testing.

**Selection Criteria:**

**Criterion 1: Clarity**
- Does average reader understand it in 5 seconds?
- Can they grasp benefit without explanation?

**Criterion 2: Relevance**
- Does it signal "this is for me"?
- Does it match audience pain point or desire?

**Criterion 3: Differentiation**
- Is it different from competitor headlines?
- Would it stand out in a crowded inbox/feed?

**Criterion 4: Authenticity**
- Can you deliver on this promise?
- Is it true to your actual offer?

**Criterion 5: Testability**
- Is the benefit/claim concrete enough to measure?
- Not: "Amazing results" (vague)
- Yes: "Cut costs by 50%" (measurable)

**Example Narrowing:**

Starting list: 12 headlines

**Round 1 (Clarity):** Remove 2-3 that are too clever/vague
- ❌ "Energy Revolution Coming" (too vague)
- ✓ "Cut Your Energy Bills in Half" (clear)

**Round 2 (Differentiation):** Remove if competitor is saying it
- ❌ "Save Money on Energy" (everyone says this)
- ✓ "Cut Your Energy Bills in Half Without Solar" (specific, differentiated)

**Final 5:**
1. "Cut Your Energy Bills in Half" (benefit + specific)
2. "The Weird Trick Your Utility Company Doesn't Want You to Know" (curiosity)
3. "7 Ways to Lower Your Energy Costs This Month" (number)
4. "Ready to Stop Wasting Money on Overpriced Energy?" (question)
5. "How to Get Free or Cheap Energy (Here's the Secret)" (how-to)

---

### STEP 3: TEST AGAINST CONTROL

**Goal:** Find winner; identify if control is beaten.

**Setup:**

**Control Group (Current/Best Headline):**
- Use existing best performer
- Or if brand new, use most "safe" option
- Control = baseline to measure against

**Test Group:**
- Test one variation against control
- Change only ONE element (not multiple)
- Run simultaneously (not sequentially)

**Example Test Structure:**

**Week 1: Control vs. Test 1**
- Control: "Cut Your Energy Bills in Half"
- Test 1: "The Weird Trick Your Utility Company Doesn't Want You to Know"

**Week 2: Control vs. Test 2** (if Test 1 wins, Test 1 becomes new control)
- Control: Test 1 winner
- Test 2: "7 Ways to Lower Your Energy Costs This Month"

**Sample Size Requirements:**

| Metric | Minimum Sample |
|--------|-----------------|
| Email open rate | 100+ emails |
| Landing page clicks | 50+ clicks |
| Conversion event | 10-20+ conversions |
| Small list test | At least 3x results needed |

**Duration Requirements:**
- Email: Full send (day/hour doesn't matter much)
- Landing page: Run for 3-7 days (get weekday/weekend data)
- Ads: Run for 3-5 days minimum (algorithm needs time)

---

### STEP 4: MEASURE & ANALYZE

**Goal:** Identify winner with confidence.

**Metrics to Track:**

**Primary Metric (What You Optimize For):**
- Email: Open rate %
- Landing page: Click-through rate % or conversion rate %
- Ads: Cost per click or conversion rate
- Social: Engagement rate or click rate

**Secondary Metrics (Context):**
- If headline wins on opens but loses on clicks = wrong promise
- If headline wins on clicks but low conversion = attracts wrong person
- Track full funnel (top to bottom)

**Statistical Significance:**

**Rule:** Never make conclusion on small sample sizes

**Confidence Levels:**
- 80 conversions control, 100 conversions test = CONFIDENT (clear winner)
- 5 conversions control, 7 conversions test = NOT CONFIDENT (too small)
- 100 clicks control, 110 clicks test = NOT CONFIDENT (too close, run longer)

**Winner Determination:**

**Clear Winner:** (>20% lift)
- Control: 100 opens, 10% conversion = 10 conversions
- Test: 100 opens, 12% conversion = 12 conversions
- Lift: 20%
- **Action:** Use test as new control

**Inconclusive:** (<15% lift, small sample)
- Control: 100 opens, 10% conversion = 10 conversions
- Test: 100 opens, 11% conversion = 11 conversions
- Lift: 10%
- **Action:** Run longer or test new angle

**Clear Loser:** (<10% performance)
- Test performs worse than control
- **Action:** Archive; test new variation instead

---

### STEP 5: ITERATE & REPEAT

**Goal:** Continuous improvement cycle.

**After Winner Identified:**

1. **Use new winner as control**
   - Control: "Weird Trick" headline (winner from Step 3)
   - Test: New variation you create

2. **Test against new variations**
   - Keep winning element (whatever made it win)
   - Change one other element

3. **Run indefinitely**
   - Small gains at scale = huge revenue
   - 10% improvement × 12 months = 214% annual improvement

**Example Iteration:**

Month 1 Winner: "The Weird Trick Your Utility Company Doesn't Want You to Know"

Month 2 Test:
- Same curiosity structure
- Different positioning: "The Weird Trick Energy Companies Are Hiding From You" (more accusatory)

Month 3 Test (if Month 2 wins):
- Keep winning element
- Test benefit clarity: "The Weird Trick That Could Save You $2,000/Year"

## TESTING DIFFERENT ELEMENTS

### What to Test First (Priority Order)

**Priority 1: HEADLINE FORMULA (Highest Impact)**
- Benefit vs. Curiosity vs. Number vs. How-To
- These drive 20-40% differences
- Test first; impact is biggest

**Priority 2: SPECIFIC BENEFIT/PROMISE (Medium Impact)**
- "$100/month savings" vs. "$50/month savings"
- "Cut bills in half" vs. "Save $2,000/year"
- Drives 10-20% differences

**Priority 3: TARGETING/SPECIFICITY (Medium Impact)**
- "Homeowners Only" vs. "Anyone Can Do This"
- "For Renters" vs. "Universal"
- Different audiences respond differently

**Priority 4: TONE/APPROACH (Lower Impact)**
- Funny vs. serious
- Urgent vs. calm
- Formal vs. conversational
- Drives 5-10% differences

**Priority 5: MINOR WORDING (Lowest Impact)**
- "Amazing" vs. "Incredible"
- "Here's the secret" vs. "Here's how"
- Drives <5% differences

## TESTING HEADLINES IN DIFFERENT MEDIUMS

### EMAIL TESTING

**Variables to Test (In Order):**

1. **Subject line formula**
   - "Curiosity" vs. "Benefit" vs. "Question"
   - Highest impact

2. **Personalization**
   - "[Name], [Hook]" vs. generic
   - 20-30% lift expected

3. **Preview text**
   - Extends subject vs. repeats vs. adds new info
   - 10-15% impact

4. **Send time**
   - Tuesday 9am vs. Wednesday 2pm, etc.
   - 10-20% impact (secondary to subject line)

**Sample Size:**
- Minimum: 100 opens per variation
- Best practice: Full send list split 50/50

**Duration:**
- Single send = all variation data in one day
- Sequential sends = need multiple sends to compare

---

### LANDING PAGE TESTING

**Variables to Test (In Order):**

1. **Headline formula**
   - Benefit vs. Curiosity vs. Before/After
   - Highest impact (15-30% lift potential)

2. **Headline specificity**
   - Specific benefit vs. vague benefit
   - 10-20% impact

3. **Subheadline addition/removal**
   - Headline + subheadline vs. headline alone
   - 10-15% impact

4. **Imagery**
   - Visual support for headline
   - Secondary to copy (5-10%)

**Sample Size:**
- Minimum: 50 clicks per variation
- Better: 100+ clicks
- Best: Run until statistical significance (online calculator)

**Duration:**
- Minimum: 3 days (captures weekday/weekend traffic)
- Better: 7 days
- Continuous: Run winner indefinitely; add new test when confident

**Testing Platform:**
- Unbounce, Leadpages, Instapage, or native website A/B tools
- Split traffic 50/50 between variations

---

### AD COPY TESTING (Facebook/Google)

**Variables to Test:**

1. **Headline (Primary Ad Copy)**
   - First benefit vs. curiosity vs. problem
   - Highest impact

2. **Deck (Body Copy)**
   - Secondary message; reinforces headline

3. **Image**
   - Visual + headline must align
   - Test image + copy combinations

**Sample Size:**
- Minimum: 50-100 clicks per variation
- Better: 500+ impressions per variation
- Duration: 3-5 days (algorithm needs time)

**Testing Platform:**
- Facebook Ads Manager split testing
- Google Ads A/B test feature
- Platform automated testing

## INTERPRETING RESULTS CORRECTLY

### Scenario 1: Clear Winner
**Data:** Control: 100 opens/10 clicks | Test: 100 opens/15 clicks

**Interpretation:** Test is 50% better
**Action:**
- Use test as new control
- Create new variation to test against it
- Document what made it win (curiosity? specificity? personalization?)

**Learning:** "When we use curiosity + personalization, opens increase 50%"

---

### Scenario 2: Inconclusive
**Data:** Control: 100 opens/10 clicks | Test: 100 opens/11 clicks

**Interpretation:** Only 10% difference; could be random variation
**Action:**
- Run longer (need bigger sample)
- OR test different variation
- Don't make conclusion yet

**Learning:** "This small change doesn't matter; let's try bolder variations"

---

### Scenario 3: Clear Loser
**Data:** Control: 100 opens/10 clicks | Test: 100 opens/7 clicks

**Interpretation:** Test is 30% worse
**Action:**
- Don't use this variation
- Archive it
- Create totally different variation for next test

**Learning:** "This angle doesn't work; let's try opposite approach"

---

### Scenario 4: Surprising Winner (Different Metric)
**Data:**
- Opens: Control 10%, Test 8% (test loses on opens)
- Conversions: Control 2%, Test 3% (test wins on conversions)

**Interpretation:** Test attracts fewer people but better-qualified ones
**Action:**
- Depends on goal (opens or conversions)
- If conversion is goal = test wins
- If warm audience, test might be better (qualified people)

**Learning:** "This headline attracts fewer but better prospects"

## STATISTICAL SIGNIFICANCE CALCULATOR

**Quick Rule of Thumb:**

For 10% improvement to be "real" (95% confidence):
- Sample: 100+ conversions per variation

For 20% improvement to be "real" (95% confidence):
- Sample: 30+ conversions per variation

For 50% improvement to be "real" (95% confidence):
- Sample: 10+ conversions per variation

**Tools:**
- Online: Optimizely's stats calculator
- Spreadsheet: Create simple sample size calculator
- Logic: More conversions = faster to declare winner

## COMMON TESTING MISTAKES

### 1. Testing Multiple Changes At Once
**Wrong:** Change headline + subheadline + color simultaneously
**Right:** Change only headline; keep everything else same

**Why:** Can't tell which change caused the win

### 2. Stopping Too Early
**Wrong:** Control: 10 clicks, Test: 12 clicks → "Test wins!"
**Right:** Run until 100+ clicks per variation

**Why:** Small samples = random variation, not real difference

### 3. Declaring Winner Too Fast
**Wrong:** Run 1 day, see 15% lift, switch immediately
**Right:** Run full week; check if it holds; ensure not random

**Why:** One day can be statistical anomaly

### 4. Not Documenting Learning
**Wrong:** Test 10 headlines, forget which won and why
**Right:** Keep spreadsheet of every test, winner, lift %, and learning

**Why:** You need pattern recognition to improve faster

### 5. Testing Too Many Variations At Once
**Wrong:** Test 5 headlines simultaneously (split traffic 5 ways)
**Right:** Test 2 at a time; declare winner; test new variation

**Why:** Multiple tests dilute power; testing 1 vs. 1 is fastest

## KEY INSIGHT: COMPOUND IMPROVEMENTS

Small testing gains compound dramatically:

- Month 1: +10% improvement
- Month 2: +10% improvement (1.1 × 1.1 = 1.21 = +21% cumulative)
- Month 3: +10% improvement (1.21 × 1.1 = 1.33 = +33% cumulative)
- Year: 12 tests × 10% average = 215% improvement

One 10% win per month = 3x better performance in one year.

Test ruthlessly.
