# Objection Response Templates

**Sources:** OnePeak ($1M LP Blueprint), Todd Brown (A-Z Workshop), Copy Hackers (10x Landing Pages)

---

## The 7 Universal Objections

Every prospect has some combination of these objections. Your copy must address the top 3-5 BEFORE the close -- not after. Objections left unresolved become reasons to leave.

---

## Objection 1: Price ("It costs too much")

### The Psychology
Price objection is rarely about the actual dollar amount. It's about perceived value relative to cost. If the perceived value is 10x the price, nobody objects.

### Template A: Anchoring + Reframe
```
Most [solutions in this category] charge [$high anchor].
And honestly? Many of them deliver less than what you'll get here.

[Product] is just [$your price].

That's [$pennies-per-day reframe] -- less than [relatable daily purchase].

And unlike that [daily purchase], this one [specific measurable result].
```

### Template B: Cost of Inaction
```
Here's what [problem] is actually costing you:

- [Cost 1]: [$X per month/year in lost revenue, wasted time, etc.]
- [Cost 2]: [$X in opportunity cost]
- [Cost 3]: [Intangible cost -- stress, reputation, confidence]

Total cost of doing nothing: [$sum] per [time period].

[Product] costs [$your price]. Once.

The math speaks for itself.
```

### Template C: Investment Frame
```
This isn't an expense. It's an investment with a measurable return.

The average [customer type] sees [specific result] within [timeframe].

At [$your price], that's a [X]x return in the first [period] alone.
```

### Template D: Comparison Reframe
```
For the price of [relatable thing they already buy],
you get [complete list of what's included].

Which one moves the needle on [their goal]?
```

## Objection 2: Trust ("I don't believe you")

### The Psychology
Trust is not built by claiming trustworthiness. It's built through proof, specificity, and letting others vouch for you.

### Template A: Proof Power of Three
```
Don't take our word for it.

[Proof 1 -- strongest, most specific]: "[Specific testimonial with name, result, and timeline]"

[Proof 2 -- data/authority]: In a study of [N] users, [specific metric] improved by [exact percentage].

[Proof 3 -- unexpected source]: Even [industry publication/competitor/skeptic] called it "[specific praise]."
```

### Template B: Transparency Disarm
```
We know what you're thinking. "Sounds too good to be true."

Fair. So here's what we WON'T promise:
- We won't promise [unrealistic thing]
- We won't promise [another unrealistic thing]
- We won't promise [thing competitors promise but can't deliver]

What we WILL promise:
- [Specific, realistic, provable result]
- [Another specific result]
- [Your guarantee]

And we'll prove it with [30-day trial / money-back guarantee / free audit].
```

### Template C: Named Source Credibility
```
Trusted by [number] [specific type of customer] including:

[Logo bar of recognizable companies]

"[Short, specific testimonial]" -- [Full name, Title, Company]
"[Short, specific testimonial]" -- [Full name, Title, Company]
"[Short, specific testimonial]" -- [Full name, Title, Company]
```

## Objection 3: Complexity ("It looks too hard")

### The Psychology
People overestimate effort and underestimate their ability. Your job is to reduce perceived effort to near-zero.

### Template A: Three Easy Steps
```
Getting started takes less than [time]:

Step 1: [Simple action] (takes [X] minutes)
Step 2: [Simple action] (takes [X] minutes)
Step 3: [Simple action] (takes [X] minutes)

That's it. You're up and running.

No [scary technical thing]. No [another scary thing]. No [learning curve].
```

### Template B: Beginner Success Story
```
"I'm not tech-savvy at all. I was honestly terrified to try this.

But I followed the steps, and within [timeframe], I had [specific result].

If I can do this, anyone can." -- [Name, relevant detail showing they're like the reader]
```

### Template C: DFY Emphasis
```
You don't need to [scary technical skill].
You don't need to [another scary skill].
You don't even need to [yet another scary skill].

We've done the hard part for you.

All you do is [the simple thing they actually do].
```

## Objection 4: Good Enough ("What I have works fine")

### The Psychology
Inertia is the most powerful anti-conversion force. People prefer familiar pain over unfamiliar solutions. You must make the cost of the status quo viscerally painful.

### Template A: Hidden Cost of Status Quo
```
Your current [solution/approach] is working.

But is it working as well as it could?

Here's what you might not see:
- [Hidden inefficiency]: costing you [$ or time] every [period]
- [Hidden risk]: one [bad event] away from [painful consequence]
- [Hidden opportunity cost]: while you [current approach], [competitors/peers] are [better result]

"Good enough" has a price. You're just not seeing the invoice.
```

### Template B: Upgrade Framing
```
You wouldn't drive a car with a cracked windshield just because "it still works."

Your current [solution] gets you from A to B.
[Product] gets you from A to Z -- in half the time, with none of the [pain point].

Same goal. Dramatically different experience.
```

## Objection 5: Urgency ("I'll do it later")

### The Psychology
"There's always tomorrow" is Mind Reader Shortcut #20. Combat it with real consequences of delay, not fake scarcity.

### Template A: Authentic Urgency
```
This offer is available until [real date/condition].

After that, [what actually changes]:
- Price goes to [$higher price]
- [Bonus] is no longer included
- [Limited element] sells out (we had [number] last time)

We're not manufacturing urgency. [Explain why the deadline is real.]
```

### Template B: Cost of Delay
```
Every week you wait, you're:
- Missing [specific number] of [opportunities/leads/sales]
- Falling [specific amount] further behind [competitors/peers/goals]
- Paying [$X] more in [waste/inefficiency/manual labor]

There's no penalty for acting today. There IS a penalty for waiting.
```

### Template C: Invitational Urgency (Soft)
```
Whenever you're ready, we're here.

But if [specific time-sensitive factor] matters to you,
now is the best time to start.

[Specific reason why timing matters -- seasonal, market shift, enrollment cap]
```

## Objection 6: Ethics/Legitimacy ("Is this legitimate?")

### The Psychology
Sophisticated buyers are wary of scams, hype, and too-good-to-be-true offers. Authority proof is essential.

### Template A: Institutional Credibility
```
[Product] is used by teams at:
[Logo bar: recognizable companies]

Featured in:
[Media logos: publications that covered you]

[Certification/compliance badge if applicable]

We've been in business since [year], serving [number] customers
across [number] countries.
```

### Template B: Transparent Business Practices
```
We believe you should know exactly what you're buying.

- No hidden fees. Ever.
- No auto-renewals without notification.
- No fine print that contradicts the big print.
- Cancel anytime with one click.

Questions? [Real person name] is available at [real email/phone].
```

## Objection 7: Mistake Fear ("What if it doesn't work for me?")

### The Psychology
This is Mind Reader Shortcut #21 ("I'm the exception"). Combat with: guarantee + case studies from people LIKE them + specific conditions for success.

### Template A: "People Like You" Proof
```
"I was sure this wouldn't work for me because [specific concern the reader shares]."

"I was wrong. Within [timeframe], I [specific result]."

-- [Name], [Detail showing they're similar to the reader]

[Add 2 more testimonials from diverse "exception" types]
```

### Template B: Guarantee + Success Conditions
```
Here's our promise:

Follow [specific process] for [specific timeframe].
If you don't see [specific measurable result], we'll [guarantee action].

No hoops. No fine print. No "well, actually."

We can make this promise because [reason why -- based on data/track record].
```

### Template C: "Who This Is NOT For" (Takeaway)
```
This is NOT for you if:
- [Disqualifying condition -- shows you're selective]
- [Another disqualifying condition]
- [Condition that eliminates tire-kickers]

This IS for you if:
- [Qualifying condition -- reader says "that's me"]
- [Another qualifying condition]
- [Condition that describes their situation]

If you see yourself in the second list, you're exactly who we built this for.
```

## Objection Placement Map

| Page Section | Objections to Address |
|-------------|----------------------|
| Above the fold | None -- focus on promise and curiosity |
| Marketing argument | Trust (#2), Good Enough (#4) -- via proof and mechanism superiority |
| Social proof blocks | Trust (#2), Mistake Fear (#7) -- via testimonials |
| Offer section | Price (#1) -- via anchoring and value stack |
| Guarantee section | Mistake Fear (#7) -- via risk reversal |
| Close section | Urgency (#5) -- via consequences of inaction |
| FAQ section | Complexity (#3), Ethics (#6), remaining Price (#1) objections |
| P.S. | Whatever the #1 objection is for your audience |

## Quick Diagnostic: Which Objections to Prioritize

**If your audience is...**
- Price-sensitive buyers: Lead with #1 (Price) and #7 (Mistake Fear)
- First-time buyers in your category: Lead with #2 (Trust) and #3 (Complexity)
- Switching from a competitor: Lead with #4 (Good Enough) and #7 (Mistake Fear)
- Procrastinators/researchers: Lead with #5 (Urgency) and #1 (Price)
- Sophisticated/skeptical buyers: Lead with #6 (Ethics) and #2 (Trust)
