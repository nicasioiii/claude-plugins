# Proof Hierarchy Guide

**Sources:** Todd Brown (A-Z Copywriting Workshop), Copy Hackers (10x Landing Pages), Copywriting Made Simple

---

## The Proof Imperative

No proof = no campaign. Without proof, you have claims. Claims without proof = puffery. Claims WITH proof = argument. Claims + Proof + Benefit = Marketing Argument.

**Foundational rules:**
1. Assume prospects will NOT believe you unless you offer evidence
2. Do NOT claim what you cannot prove -- remove unsubstantiated claims
3. Unsubstantiated claims stack to create cumulative disbelief
4. Your biggest claim must be backed by your biggest proof point
5. No claim bigger than your biggest proof point

---

## Two Types of Proof

### Type 1: Claim Proof (For the Mechanism)
Proves claims about HOW and WHY your method works. Used in the marketing argument (the 75% section of a campaign).

**Does NOT require testimonials.** Uses logic, data, analogy, demonstration, and reason-why.

**Where it goes:** CPB chunks in the body of the sales page, marketing argument sections, educational content.

### Type 2: Social Proof (For the Product)
Proves claims about RESULTS and EXPERIENCE. Used in the offer section (the 25% section).

**Uses:** Testimonials, case studies, client results, reviews, user numbers.

**Where it goes:** Offer section, social proof blocks, near CTAs, in the re-close.

**Critical mistake to avoid:** Using testimonials to prove mechanism claims. Testimonials prove the PRODUCT delivers results. Reason-why proof proves the MECHANISM is sound. Keep them separate.

## The Reason-Why Technique

The simplest and most powerful form of claim proof. The default for the majority of claims in your marketing argument.

### The Two Magic Phrases
1. **"The reason why is..."**
2. **"Because..."**

What comes after these phrases serves as evidence.

### How It Works
The reason-why technique connects the claim to its logical basis. It gives the prospect a rational foothold for believing what you're saying.

**Claim without proof (puffery):**
"Our system generates leads on autopilot."

**Claim with reason-why proof (argument):**
"Our system generates leads on autopilot. The reason why is that it uses behavior-triggered sequences that fire based on 14 different prospect actions -- so every lead gets the right message at the exact moment they're most receptive."

### Where Reason-Why Proof Comes From
Interrogating the unique mechanism. The questions you ask during mechanism interrogation -- "Why do you do it that way? Why in that order? What happens if you skip a step?" -- generate the answers that become reason-why proof.

**This is why mechanism interrogation is the most valuable activity in campaign development.** It doesn't just name the mechanism -- it gives you the PROOF for why it works.

### Examples Across Industries

**SaaS:**
"Your reports generate automatically every Monday at 6 AM. Because the system pulls directly from your connected data sources and applies the same analysis framework your best analyst would use -- in 1/100th of the time."

**Health:**
"Your energy levels stabilize within 72 hours. The reason why is the bioavailable magnesium crosses the blood-brain barrier faster than standard supplements, activating your cellular energy production at the mitochondrial level."

**Business:**
"Your ad costs drop by 30-40% in the first month. Because the algorithm identifies and eliminates the 3 highest-waste audience segments that consume budget without converting -- something manual optimization misses."

**Course/Training:**
"Students complete the program in 21 days, not 12 weeks. The reason why is we eliminated 80% of the curriculum that doesn't directly produce results, leaving only the 20% that matters -- then structured it as daily 30-minute implementation sprints."

## The Proof Power of Three

One piece of evidence = an event (could be a fluke).
Two pieces = a coincidence.
Three pieces = a PATTERN.

### Structure
For bold claims that stretch believability, use three consecutive paragraphs of proof:

1. **Strongest proof first** -- devote the most copy to it (full paragraph)
2. **Second proof** -- half the length of the first
3. **Third proof** -- half the length of the second

### Variation Rule
Vary the TYPE of proof across the three. Don't use three testimonials or three data points. Mix them:

**Example combinations:**
- Reason-why logic + Data/statistic + Analogy
- Case study + Expert endorsement + Demonstration
- Customer testimonial + Industry data + Reason-why explanation
- Before/after data + Expert quote + Logical proof

### Example: Power of Three in Action

**Claim:** "The XYZ Method produces results 3x faster than traditional approaches."

**Proof 1 (Reason-why -- longest):**
"The reason is architectural. Traditional approaches require you to build from scratch every time -- new campaign, new research, new copy, new creative. The XYZ Method uses modular templates derived from 2,300 tested campaigns. You're not starting from zero; you're starting from a proven foundation and customizing the 20% that needs to be unique to your offer."

**Proof 2 (Data -- medium length):**
"In our benchmark study of 847 campaigns, XYZ Method users reached their first 1,000 leads in an average of 11 days. The control group averaged 34 days."

**Proof 3 (Analogy -- shortest):**
"It's the difference between building a house from lumber versus assembling one from pre-fabricated walls. Same end result. Fraction of the time."

## Monster Proof Points

Extraordinarily strong, specific, memorable proof points that can anchor an entire campaign.

### What Makes a Proof Point "Monster"
- Unusually specific number or result
- Comes from an authoritative or unexpected source
- Creates an immediate emotional reaction
- Is hard to dismiss or rationalize away
- Stands alone as compelling evidence

### Examples of Monster Proof Points
- "$3M revenue difference from a single headline change" (OnePeak case study)
- "7 interviews = messaging gold equivalent to 500 survey responses" (Copy Hackers)
- "Copy-heavy version beat design-heavy version by 25% -- worth $2M+ annually" (OnePeak)
- "Single focused CTA button = 45% boost in account starts" (Crazy Egg)
- "Review-mined headline: 20%+ more leads = tens of thousands monthly" (Copy Hackers rehab center)

### Building Campaigns Around Monster Proof Points

**Contrarian approach:** Instead of finding an idea first and then looking for proof, start with a monster proof point and find an idea to wrap around it.

**Why this works:** Ideas are unlimited. Monster proof points are rare. When you find one, it becomes the most credible element of your entire campaign.

**Process:**
1. During research, flag any data point that makes you say "wow"
2. Ask: "Can I build a campaign angle around this single proof point?"
3. If yes, the proof point becomes the hook: "After testing 847 campaigns, we discovered something that changes everything..."
4. The mechanism explains WHY the proof point exists
5. The offer delivers the system behind the proof point

## Strategic Testimonial Placement

### Match Testimonials to Specific Claims
Do NOT dump all testimonials in one section. Place each testimonial adjacent to the specific claim it proves.

| Claim on Page | Testimonial Needed |
|--------------|-------------------|
| "Easy to get started" | Testimonial about ease of onboarding |
| "See results in 14 days" | Testimonial with a timeline matching the claim |
| "Works for beginners" | Testimonial from a self-identified beginner |
| "Responsive support" | Testimonial praising support experience |
| "Risk-free guarantee" | Testimonial about using the guarantee and being treated well |

### Testimonial Quality Hierarchy
From weakest to strongest:

1. Anonymous text testimonial (weakest -- almost worthless)
2. Named text testimonial
3. Named testimonial with photo
4. Named testimonial with photo and title/company
5. Named testimonial with photo, title, and SPECIFIC result
6. Video testimonial with specific result
7. Case study with before/after data (strongest)

**Rule:** Never use anonymous testimonials. If you can't attribute it, don't use it.

### Social Proof as Message Delivery (Copy Hackers)
Social proof is not just a persuasion tactic -- it is a message said more credibly by the right person.

**Line-by-line audit process:**
Go through every claim on your page and ask:
- Should a customer/user say or reinforce this in a testimonial?
- Should an influential person/business endorse this?
- Should a GIF or screenshot demonstrate this?
- Should a video demo show this?
- Should live data reinforce this?

If someone OTHER than you can make the claim more credibly, let them.

## Proof Placement by Page Section

| Page Section | Primary Proof Type | Secondary Proof Type |
|-------------|-------------------|---------------------|
| **Hero/Above the fold** | Credibility indicator (logo bar, user count) | None -- keep clean |
| **Marketing argument (CPB chunks)** | Reason-why proof | Data, analogy, demonstration |
| **Social proof blocks** | Testimonials (matched to preceding claims) | Case study snippets |
| **Offer section** | Value justification (anchoring, comparison) | Testimonials about ROI |
| **Guarantee section** | Testimonials about guarantee experience | Company credibility |
| **Close/Re-close** | Strongest testimonial (best result) | Urgency proof (real scarcity data) |
| **P.S.** | Single most compelling proof point | Restate guarantee |

## The "So What?" / "Prove It" Audit Process

Apply to EVERY line of copy:

**Step 1:** Read the line aloud.
**Step 2:** Ask "So what?" -- If you can't answer with a clear benefit, rewrite or remove.
**Step 3:** Ask "Prove it." -- If you can't support it with evidence, add proof or soften the claim.

**Example audit:**

| Line | "So What?" | "Prove It" | Action |
|------|-----------|------------|--------|
| "Our AI is the most advanced" | Why does that matter to me? | No proof. Puffery. | Remove or replace with specific capability |
| "Saves you 8 hours/week" | I get my evenings back | Calculator showing time saved per task | Add calculator or testimonial with time saved |
| "Trusted by 10,000+ teams" | I'm not alone in choosing this | Real number, logo bar | Add logos of recognizable companies |
| "Get started in 5 minutes" | Low effort, quick win | Screenshot of setup flow | Add GIF walkthrough |
