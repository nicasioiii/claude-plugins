# Offer Engineering & Value Proposition

## THE OFFER SUPREMACY PRINCIPLE

**The better the offer, the weaker the copy can be.** The offer is the single most valuable use of marketing time. An irresistible offer makes the copywriter's job dramatically easier. Copy cannot save a bad offer. But a great offer can compensate for mediocre copy.

The single best thing you can do for your business is invest in offer creation BEFORE obsessing over copy.

**Contrarian insight:** Fix AOV (average order value) before conversion rate. Most marketers obsess over conversion rate. But increasing AOV through better offer engineering often produces faster revenue growth with less effort.

## TWO COMPONENTS OF AN IRRESISTIBLE OFFER

### 1. Attractive Solution

How compelling, interesting, and attractive is the solution itself? Most marketers skip this and jump straight to the proposition. A hideous solution wrapped in an irresistible proposition still fails.

### 2. Irresistible Proposition

Deliverables, features, advantages, benefits, price, terms, risk reversal, premiums, and reason to respond now. This is what most people think of as "the offer."

**Always start with engineering an attractive solution, THEN wrap it in an irresistible proposition.**

## THE 9 ELEMENTS OF AN ATTRACTIVE SOLUTION

Score your solution 1-10 on each element:

1. **Solves a significant problem** -- Is the problem painful enough that people will pay to fix it?
2. **Produces a big, desirable result** -- Is the outcome something they badly want?
3. **New, not simply better** -- Is it a REPLACEMENT, not an improvement? (Different > Better)
4. **Viewed as unique and different** -- Can they find this elsewhere?
5. **Seen as superior to other options** -- Can you articulate specific superiority?
6. **Quick and easy to implement** -- How much effort to get started?
7. **Produces fast results** -- How quickly do they see the first win?
8. **At least partially DFY (done for you)** -- Have you done some of the work for them?
9. **Gives the prospect success confidence** -- Do they believe THEY can succeed with this?

**The two biggest factors:** Easy/quick to implement + fast results. These dwarf everything else.

**Scoring:** 70+ out of 90 = strong. Below 50 = rework the solution before writing copy.

## THE 7 ELEMENTS OF AN IRRESISTIBLE OFFER (OnePeak)

Evaluate every offer across these seven dimensions:

| Element | Question | Optimization |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Outcome** | What result does the customer get? | Make it specific and measurable |
| **Price** | What does it cost relative to perceived value? | Anchor high, reveal low |
| **Time** | How quickly do they get the result? | Compress timelines, show early wins |
| **Effort** | How much work does the customer put in? | Reduce steps, provide templates |
| **Likelihood** | How confident are they it will work for them? | Add proof, show people like them |
| **Risk** | What happens if it does not work? | Guarantee reverses this completely |
| **Urgency** | Why should they act now instead of later? | Authentic deadlines and scarcity |

### The One-Liner Test

You should be able to communicate your solution in 5-15 words. The more enticing that one-liner, the stronger your offer will be.

- **Weak:** "A comprehensive marketing program"
- **Strong:** "100 new leads per month in 90 days or your money back"

If you cannot pass this test, the offer needs work.

### Offer Formula Template

Get [value prop] in [time period] with [risk reversal/guarantee] and [bonuses for a limited time only] -- do [CTA].

## VALUE STACKING PROCESS

The perceived value of everything the customer receives should be 5-10x what they pay. If the offer is $100, show $500-$1,000 in total value.

### Step 1: Break Down the Core Offer
List every sub-component of your main deliverable. Each has independent value.

### Step 2: Assign Dollar Values
For each sub-component, determine a legitimate stand-alone price. Use market comparables.

### Step 3: Add Bonuses
Add 2-4 bonuses that are RELEVANT to the core offer and ACCELERATE results.

### Step 4: Assign Bonus Values
Same process -- legitimate stand-alone prices.

### Step 5: Stack and Present
Show the total value, cross it out, reveal the actual price. The contrast between total value and price creates the "no-brainer" perception.

### Bonus Strategy Rules

1. Bonuses MUST be relevant to the core offer (irrelevant bonuses are worthless psychologically)
2. The best bonuses ACCELERATE RESULTS (help them get outcomes faster)
3. Good bonus examples: phone call with technician, special PDF breakdown, accelerated training, templates
4. Start with ONE bonus, add more over time based on testing
5. Each bonus needs its own value explanation -- do not just list them

## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY (TOP 6 TACTICS)

### 1. Anchoring
Mention a high price before stating the actual price. Readers compare every subsequent number with the first one they see.
- "While a premium solution costs $5,000+, [Product] is just $497"

### 2. Bigness Bias
Smaller amounts sound less significant next to larger amounts.
- "Your investment of $997 is a small price when a single new client is worth $10,000+"

### 3. Pennies Per Day
Divide the total cost into tiny daily amounts.
- "For less than $3.29 a day, you get the entire system"

### 4. Comparison Reframing
Compare price to something the reader already buys without thinking.
- "For the price of a coffee each morning, you could have a fully automated lead pipeline"

### 5. Sunk Cost Leverage
Promote products that let people get more value from something they already own.
- "If you have already invested in a website, this tool lets you actually convert visitors into customers"

### 6. Whole-Life Cost Reframing
Encourage thinking about cost over a longer timeframe rather than the upfront amount.
- "Over 12 months, that comes out to $41/month -- less than most marketing tools charge"

**Golden Rule:** If you just state the price, the reader compares it to zero (the cost of not buying). Always anchor first.

## GUARANTEE FRAMEWORK

### The Dual Guarantee Structure (Todd Brown)

**Component 1: Unconditional Guarantee**
A risk-free trial period with no strings attached.
- "Try it for 30 days. If you are not satisfied for any reason, get a full refund."

**Component 2: Conditional Guarantee**
A stronger promise tied to specific actions and results.
- "Follow the program for 90 days, implement the 5 core strategies, and if you do not see at least [specific result], we will refund every penny AND let you keep the materials."

**Why dual works:** The unconditional guarantee removes fear of loss. The conditional guarantee demonstrates confidence in results and creates a roadmap for success.

### Guarantee Positioning Rules

1. State the guarantee in the POSITIVE: "You are either thrilled with the results, or just return within 60 days for a full refund"
2. Address their specific fears, not generic ones
3. Make the guarantee LONGER than they expect (60-90 days instead of 30)
4. You will make more from people who would not have bought without the guarantee than you lose from refunds
5. A strong guarantee is a NET POSITIVE for revenue

## AUTHENTIC URGENCY AND SCARCITY

### The Authenticity Rule

Fake urgency and scarcity DESTROYS trust. You may earn the short-term sale but lose the long-term relationship. Play the long game.

### Authentic Scarcity Examples
- "I can only take on 5 clients at a time because I choose quality over quantity"
- Beta pricing that genuinely increases later
- Limited enrollment that genuinely closes
- Physical product inventory limits

### Authentic Urgency Examples
- Price goes up after a specific date (and actually does)
- Bonus or coupon available only until a specific deadline
- Seasonal relevance that naturally expires
- Live cohort with a real start date

### The Warning Element
Before closing the offer, warn them directly:
- "This is for real. Enrollment closes [date] regardless."
- "The cost of NOT doing this: [specific consequence]"

## THE TAKEAWAY SALE (Arman Assadi)

Instead of hard-selling, present the offer with "take it or leave it" confidence:
- "If it is not right for you, that is okay"
- "This is only for people who are serious"
- Disqualify non-ideal buyers to create desire in ideal buyers

The takeaway sale shows you believe so much in what you offer that you do not need them -- they need you.

## ELEMENTS-OF-THE-OFFER CLOSING FORMULA (Copy Hackers)

For closing sections on landing pages:

1. Here is what you are going to get (list deliverables)
2. Establish the value (show total worth)
3. Offer a conditional bonus
4. State the price
5. Trivialize the price (comparison, pennies per day)
6. State the guarantee
7. Risk reversal statement
8. Scarcity/urgency

## PRICE REVEAL METHODS

### On Sales Pages
Show overall stacked value, visually cross it out, reveal actual price. The visual contrast between total value and actual price creates the "no-brainer" perception.

### In Video/Live
Tell the story of WHY this price is special:
- "This is a way to thank my community"
- "As a special offer for our beta testers"
- Create feelings of EXCLUSIVITY

### Key Rules
- Start with ONE price point -- keep it simple
- Do NOT present price until value is clearly understood
- $1,000 is a great deal for a Ferrari, terrible for a paperclip -- context first, price second
- Showing a round number with confidence creates strength
