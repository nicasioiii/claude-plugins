# Guarantee Templates & Language Patterns

## The Core Principle

You will make more from people who would not have bought without a strong guarantee than you will lose from people requesting refunds. Guarantees are a NET POSITIVE for revenue. If you believe in your product, put your money where your mouth is.

---

## THE DUAL GUARANTEE STRUCTURE

The most powerful guarantee structure combines an unconditional guarantee with a conditional guarantee. Together, they address both emotional fear and rational skepticism.

### Component 1: Unconditional Guarantee

**Purpose:** Remove all financial risk. Make the decision to try completely safe.

**Template:**
"Try [Product] for [time period]. If you are not completely satisfied for any reason -- or no reason at all -- simply [contact method] and we will refund your full investment. No questions asked. No hoops to jump through."

**Time period options:**
- 14 days (minimum for low-ticket digital products)
- 30 days (standard for most products)
- 60 days (strong; recommended for most offers)
- 90 days (exceptional; creates maximum confidence)
- 365 days (only for very high-confidence offers; builds extreme trust)

**Rule:** A longer guarantee period almost always REDUCES refund requests because it removes the urgency to decide whether to keep it. People who feel pressured to evaluate quickly are MORE likely to refund.

### Component 2: Conditional Guarantee

**Purpose:** Demonstrate supreme confidence in your product. Show that if they do the work, results are virtually guaranteed.

**Template:**
"Follow the [program/system/method] for [time period]. Complete [specific actions]. If you do not experience [specific, measurable result], we will refund every penny AND [additional bonus]. You keep all the materials."

**Specific action examples:**
- "Complete all 6 modules"
- "Implement the 3 core strategies"
- "Follow the daily protocol for 30 consecutive days"
- "Send at least 10 outreach messages using our templates"

**Why conditional guarantees work:**
1. They demonstrate YOUR confidence in the product
2. They create a roadmap for the customer (do X, get Y)
3. They filter out non-serious buyers who would never succeed anyway
4. They signal that the product works IF the customer participates

## GUARANTEE COPY TEMPLATES BY PRODUCT TYPE

### Digital Course / Training Program

**Standard:**
"Enroll today with complete peace of mind. If within 60 days you feel this program has not delivered on its promise, email us at [email] for a full, no-questions-asked refund."

**Conditional:**
"Complete all modules, do the exercises, and implement the strategies. If after 90 days you have not [specific result], show us your completed work and we will refund 100% of your investment. You keep all the materials and bonuses."

**Bold:**
"We are so confident in this program that we offer a double guarantee. First: try it risk-free for 60 days. If it is not for you, get a full refund, no questions asked. Second: complete the full program, and if you do not [specific result] within 6 months, we will refund your investment AND give you $100 for wasting your time."

### SaaS / Software

**Standard:**
"Start your free trial today. No credit card required. If you decide [Product] is not right for your team, simply cancel before day 14. You will not be charged a penny."

**Post-purchase:**
"Not satisfied after your first month? Contact our team within 30 days for a full refund. We want you to love [Product] -- if you do not, we would rather have you back as a friend than keep you as an unhappy customer."

**Performance:**
"If [Product] does not save you at least [X hours/dollars] in your first 90 days, we will extend your subscription free of charge until it does."

### Coaching / Services

**Standard:**
"After our first session, if you feel this is not the right fit, we will refund your full investment with no questions asked."

**Results-based:**
"Work with us for [time period]. Follow our process. If you do not see [specific, measurable improvement], we will continue working with you at no additional charge until you do."

**Satisfaction-based:**
"If at any point during our engagement you feel you are not receiving exceptional value, tell us. We will either fix it immediately or refund the unused portion of your investment."

### Physical Products

**Standard:**
"Love it or return it within 30 days for a full refund. We will even cover the return shipping."

**Extended:**
"Try it for 90 days. If it does not [specific benefit], return it for a full refund. We stand behind our product because we know it works."

**Steal my product:**
"If for any reason you are not 100% satisfied, keep the product and we will refund your purchase price. We would rather you have it for free than be an unhappy customer."

## GUARANTEE LANGUAGE PATTERNS

### Positive Framing (Always Use This)

Frame the guarantee in the affirmative -- describe the positive outcome, then mention the refund as a secondary safety net.

**DO:** "You are either head-over-heels thrilled with the results, or just return within 60 days for a full refund."

**DO NOT:** "If you are not satisfied, you can request a refund within 60 days." (This frames dissatisfaction as the primary scenario.)

### Fear-Addressing Language

Call out the specific fears your audience has at the point of purchase:

"We know investing in [category] can feel risky -- especially if you have been burned before. That is exactly why we offer..."

"You might be wondering: what if this does not work for me? Here is our promise..."

"The biggest risk you face is not trying this at all. But just in case, here is how we protect you..."

### Confidence-Building Language

"We have helped [number] of [identity group] achieve [result]. We are so confident this will work for you that..."

"In [X years], fewer than [low percentage] of customers have ever requested a refund. That is how confident we are."

"We would not offer this guarantee if we were not absolutely certain in the results."

### Making It Easy

"No hoops to jump through. No complicated forms. Simply email [address] with the word 'refund' and we will process it within [time period]."

"One email. That is all it takes. We respect your decision and your time."

## GUARANTEE POSITIONING ON THE PAGE

### Where to Place It

1. **First mention:** Near the price reveal (reduces sticker shock)
2. **Detailed explanation:** In its own dedicated section with a visual badge/seal
3. **Final mention:** In the closing CTA section (removes last objection before clicking)

### Visual Treatment

- Use a guarantee badge or seal graphic (builds credibility)
- Set the guarantee section apart with a distinct background color
- Include your signature or headshot for personal accountability
- Bold the specific terms (time period, conditions, what they keep)

### Common Mistakes

1. **Hiding the guarantee in fine print.** Display it prominently. A hidden guarantee might as well not exist.
2. **Making the refund process complex.** Every hoop you add reduces the trust-building power of the guarantee.
3. **Using negative language.** "If you are unhappy..." vs. "When you see the results..."
4. **Forgetting to restate at the close.** The guarantee should be the LAST thing they see before the CTA button.
5. **Not having one at all.** No guarantee = maximum perceived risk = minimum conversions.

## HANDLING GUARANTEE OBJECTIONS FROM CLIENTS

When clients resist offering a guarantee:

**"What if everyone refunds?"**
"In practice, refund rates for digital products with strong guarantees average 5-10%. The increase in conversions far exceeds the cost of refunds."

**"Does it not attract freebie seekers?"**
"Some, yes. But the revenue gained from legitimate buyers who would not have purchased without the guarantee dramatically outweighs the cost of the occasional bad actor."

**"How long should the guarantee be?"**
"Longer is almost always better. A 60-day guarantee typically produces FEWER refund requests than a 14-day guarantee because customers do not feel pressured to decide quickly."

**"What about services that cannot be un-done?"**
"Offer a satisfaction guarantee on the first milestone or session. If they are not satisfied after the initial engagement, refund in full."

## ADDITIONAL GUARANTEE TYPES

### The "Better Than Free" Guarantee
If the customer follows the process and does not get results, refund their money AND pay them for their time.

**Template:**
"Complete the program. If you do not [result], we refund 100% of your investment AND send you $[amount] for your trouble. You keep everything."

**When to use:** High-ticket offers where the product creator has extreme confidence. The psychological impact is massive because it implies the seller has MORE confidence than the buyer.

### The Performance Guarantee
Tie the guarantee to a specific, measurable performance metric.

**Template:**
"If [Product] does not [specific measurable outcome] within [time period], we will [extend service / refund / provide additional support] at no extra cost."

**Example:** "If our SEO service does not increase your organic traffic by at least 30% within 6 months, we will continue working for free until it does."

### The Emotional Guarantee
Address the emotional risk, not just the financial risk.

**Template:**
"If at any point you feel overwhelmed, confused, or unsupported, tell us. We will either solve it immediately or return every penny. We would rather lose a sale than leave you feeling disappointed."

**When to use:** Educational products, coaching programs, or any offer where the prospect fears feeling stupid, lost, or abandoned after purchasing.

## REFUND RATE BENCHMARKS

Understanding typical refund rates helps clients make informed guarantee decisions:

| Product Type | Typical Refund Rate (with 30-60 day guarantee) |
|-------------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Digital courses ($200-$2,000) | 5-10% |
| SaaS (monthly) | 3-7% churn/month |
| Physical products | 5-15% (varies by category) |
| High-ticket coaching ($3,000+) | 3-8% |
| Low-ticket digital ($7-$97) | 8-15% |

**Key insight:** Longer guarantee periods almost always REDUCE refund rates. A 60-day guarantee produces fewer refunds than a 14-day guarantee because customers do not feel pressured to make a snap judgment.
