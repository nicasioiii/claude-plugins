# Ad-to-Landing-Page Matching Flowchart

**Sources:** $1M Landing Page Blueprint (OnePeak Creative) | Eddie Shleyner - Transitional Congruence | Copy Hackers - Message Matching
**Purpose:** Ensure every ad leads to a landing page that feels like a natural continuation of the conversation

---

## The Core Principle

Your landing page must feel like a natural continuation of the ad. Mismatches between ad and landing page kill conversions. This is called **transitional congruence** (Eddie Shleyner) or **message matching** (Copy Hackers).

**Why it matters:** The visitor clicked because the ad promised something. If the landing page does not immediately deliver on that promise, the visitor bounces. It is a "campaign killer" -- it can plummet conversion rates overnight.

## Three Critical Gut-Check Questions

Before launching ANY new ad, answer these three questions:

### Question 1: Is the subject matter on my landing page consistent with what got them interested in the ad?

**What to check:**
- Does the headline reference the same topic as the ad?
- Does the offer match what was promised?
- Are the same benefits emphasized?

**Red flags:**
- Ad talks about "saving time" but LP leads with "making money"
- Ad promises a specific free resource but LP leads with a paid product
- Ad discusses one problem but LP addresses a different one

### Question 2: Is the tone consistent and familiar -- does the page feel like the same conversation?

**What to check:**
- Casual ad = casual landing page (not suddenly corporate)
- Urgent ad = urgent landing page (not suddenly relaxed)
- Story-based ad = story continues on landing page
- Humor in ad = at least some warmth on landing page

**Red flags:**
- Ad uses casual language but LP is formal and corporate
- Ad is emotional and personal but LP is feature-focused and dry
- Ad has a fun, colorful vibe but LP is gray and minimal

### Question 3: Does the design/flow make sense for the viewer based on what they just saw?

**What to check:**
- Same color palette
- Same or similar visual style
- Same faces/people appear
- Same imagery or imagery style
- Layout feels like a continuation, not a jarring redirect

**Red flags:**
- Ad has warm, lifestyle photography but LP has stock photos
- Ad features a specific person but LP has no people
- Ad is bright and colorful but LP is dark and moody
- Ad is on-brand but LP looks like a different company

## Message Matching Checklist (Copy Hackers)

Apply this checklist to every ad-to-LP pair:

### Copy Matching
- [ ] Exact words from the ad headline appear on the LP (ideally in the headline or eyebrow copy)
- [ ] The same promise/benefit is stated first on the LP
- [ ] Keywords from the ad are visible above the fold
- [ ] The offer is clearly the same offer promoted in the ad
- [ ] If the ad mentions a specific number or claim, the LP repeats it

### Visual Matching
- [ ] Same faces/people from the ad appear on the LP
- [ ] Same or harmonious color palette
- [ ] Same visual style (photo style, illustration style, typography)
- [ ] Product images match between ad and LP
- [ ] The LP "feels" like the same brand as the ad

### Emotional Matching
- [ ] Same emotional tone (urgency, curiosity, excitement, empathy)
- [ ] Same level of formality/casualness
- [ ] If ad tells a story, LP continues or references that story
- [ ] The visitor feels like they are in the same conversation

## The Order of Operations (Eddie Shleyner)

**The correct workflow:**

1. Create your landing page FIRST
2. Then let the hero copy, art, and layout inform your ads
3. Literally screenshot your hero and paste it into social posts when possible
4. If limited ad space, use as much of the hero as you can, starting with the headline

**Why this order matters:** Building the ad first and then trying to make the LP match is backwards. The LP is the destination; the ad is the invitation. Build the destination first.

## What High-Bounce Landing Pages Get Wrong

The most common reasons for high bounce rates when ad traffic arrives:

1. **Tone mismatch** -- Ad is casual and friendly, LP is corporate and cold
2. **Visual disconnect** -- Different faces, different style, different colors
3. **Promise mismatch** -- Ad promises X, LP talks about Y
4. **Headline disconnect** -- Ad headline and LP headline have nothing in common
5. **Jarring detour** -- The visitor feels like they clicked to one place and ended up somewhere else
6. **Speed mismatch** -- Ad creates urgency, LP is slow and long-form (or vice versa)

## The 2x2 Ad Testing Framework (Ashton Shanks)

When testing ads against landing pages, use this systematic approach:

| | Direct Headline | Indirect Headline |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct Ad** | Variation 1 | Variation 2 |
| **Indirect Ad** | Variation 3 | Variation 4 |

- **Direct ads:** Shorter, clear what is being offered, targets ready buyers
- **Indirect ads:** Longer, gradual approach, targets broader market
- **Direct headlines:** "[Free Workshop] How to [Result]"
- **Indirect headlines:** "Do You Make These Mistakes in [Area]?"

**Testing protocol:**
1. Run all 4 variations
2. One will clearly win -- kill the other three
3. Sometimes keep both a direct and indirect running (different times of day reach prospects in different buying states)
4. Media buying sequence: Indirect to cold markets > Retarget engagers with direct ads

**Each ad variation may need its own LP variant** to maintain transitional congruence. A direct ad to an indirect LP (or vice versa) creates a mismatch.

## Matching Rules by Awareness Stage

| Awareness Stage | Ad Type | LP Hero Strategy | Message Match Priority |
|----------------|---------|------------------|----------------------|
| Unaware | Story/entertainment | Continue the story | Narrative continuity |
| Problem Aware | Problem-focused | Restate the problem | Problem language match |
| Solution Aware | Solution/mechanism | Lead with your solution | Solution language match |
| Product Aware | Product-focused | Product benefits | Feature/benefit match |
| Most Aware | Offer/deal | Lead with the offer | Price/deal match |

## Quick Diagnostic: Is My Ad-LP Pair Working?

**Symptom: High click-through rate but high bounce rate on LP**
- Diagnosis: The ad is compelling but the LP does not deliver on the promise
- Fix: Audit message matching -- ensure the LP immediately confirms what the ad promised

**Symptom: Low click-through rate on ad**
- Diagnosis: Not an LP problem -- the ad itself needs work
- Fix: Test new hooks and creatives (see headline-hook-craft skill)

**Symptom: Good LP time-on-page but low conversion**
- Diagnosis: The argument is engaging but the offer or CTA is weak
- Fix: Audit the offer section (see offer-engineering skill) and CTA (see cta-conversion-elements skill)

**Symptom: Visitors scroll past hero immediately**
- Diagnosis: Hero does not match what they expected from the ad
- Fix: Strengthen message matching in the hero section specifically
