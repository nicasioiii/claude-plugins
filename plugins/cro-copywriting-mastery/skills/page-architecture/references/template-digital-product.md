# Digital Product Landing Page Template

**Source:** $1M Landing Page Blueprint (OnePeak Creative) -- based on $10M+ in sales and $6M+ in A/B testing
**Best for:** Courses, SaaS, digital downloads, membership programs, coaching programs
**Traffic:** Cold to warm paid traffic
**Key insight:** Copy-heavy versions consistently outperform design-heavy versions by 25%+

---

## Complete 13-Section Structure (Top to Bottom)

### Section 1: 5-to-15-Word Header
**Purpose:** Define the offer in a punchy, clear headline

**Rules:**
- Must be immediately understandable -- no cleverness
- Should pass the 7-Second Test: visitor knows what this is in under 7 seconds
- Include the primary benefit or transformation
- Use numbers when possible for specificity

**Template:** `[Specific Result] [For Whom] [In Timeframe/Without Obstacle]`

**Examples:**
- "The Complete System for Launching a Profitable Online Course in 90 Days"
- "Write Copy That Sells -- Without Sounding Salesy or Sleazy"
- "Build a 6-Figure Coaching Business Using One Simple Funnel"

**Pair with:** Subheadline or eyebrow copy for additional context. End primer copy with a colon to push into the headline.

---

### Section 2: Offer Definition
**Purpose:** One or two sentences describing the offer in more depth

**Rules:**
- Expand on the headline promise with specificity
- Answer "what do I get?" in plain language
- Keep to 1-3 sentences maximum, 30-60 words
- Include what makes this different from alternatives

**Template:** `[Product Name] is a [format] that teaches/gives you [specific outcome]. Unlike [alternative], it [key differentiator] so you can [benefit].`

**Examples:**
- "The Funnel Builder Academy is a step-by-step video training that walks you through building your first high-converting sales funnel. Unlike generic marketing courses, every lesson includes done-for-you templates you can deploy today."

---

### Section 3: VSL (Video Sales Letter)
**Purpose:** Tell "your story" -- the founder/brand narrative in video format

**Rules:**
- This is the story of who you are, why you built this, and why it works
- Keep under 15 minutes for cold traffic, can go longer for warm
- Open with the viewer's problem, not your credentials
- Use the 60-Second Sales Hook structure for the opening
- Include social proof within the video (screenshots, results, testimonials)
- Always provide a text alternative below for non-video viewers

**Structure within the VSL:**
1. Hook (0-30 seconds): State the viewer's problem
2. Story (30 sec - 3 min): Your journey from struggle to discovery
3. Mechanism (3-7 min): What you discovered and why it works
4. Proof (7-10 min): Results, testimonials, case studies
5. Offer (10-12 min): What they get
6. CTA (12-15 min): Clear next step

---

### Section 4: CTA (First Call-to-Action)
**Purpose:** First opportunity to convert after the VSL

**Rules:**
- Button must visually pop against the background
- Copy should be specific: "Get Instant Access" or "Start the Training" not "Submit" or "Get Started"
- Include reassuring text below button: "Instant access. 30-day money-back guarantee."
- First-person button copy tests well: "Give Me Instant Access"

---

### Section 5: Social Proof Block (Brief)
**Purpose:** Quick credibility boost -- not the main proof section

**Rules:**
- 3-5 customer quotes, reviews, or highlights
- Include name, photo, and relevant credential/result
- Brief quotes work best here (1-2 sentences each)
- Can include: number of customers/clients, star ratings, media mentions
- DM screenshots and authentic messages add credibility

**Format options:**
- Grid of 3 testimonial cards with photos
- Single scrolling carousel
- Star rating summary + 3 short quotes

---

### Section 6: Your Story Section (THE MAKE-OR-BREAK SECTION)
**Purpose:** Extended copy-heavy narrative introducing who you are, what you built, why, and the impact

**This is the most important section on the entire page.** This is where the reader decides if they trust you enough to buy.

**Rules:**
- This is long-form copy -- do not be afraid of length
- Tell your origin story: the problem you faced, the struggle, what you discovered, the result
- Include images for context (you working, your team, screenshots of results)
- Write in first person -- this should feel like a letter from a friend
- Use the Before-After-Bridge structure: life before > life after > the bridge (your product)
- Include specific numbers and timeframes
- Be vulnerable about your struggles -- damaging admissions build trust

**Structure within the story section:**
1. The relatable struggle (reader sees themselves)
2. The failed attempts (reader nods along)
3. The breakthrough discovery (mechanism reveal)
4. The results (specific, measurable)
5. Why you decided to share this (mission/purpose)
6. Who this has already helped (transition to more proof)

---

### Section 7: CTA (Second Call-to-Action)
**Purpose:** Catch readers who are convinced by the story

Same rules as Section 4. Can use slightly different copy for variety: "Yes, I Want In" or "Join [X] Members Inside"

---

### Section 8: More Social Proof
**Purpose:** Deep proof section with authentic messages

**Rules:**
- This is where you go heavy on proof
- Customer highlights with specific results
- DM screenshots, email screenshots, authentic messages
- Video testimonials if available (most powerful form of proof)
- Organize by objection: group testimonials that address specific concerns
- Include diverse voices so different segments see themselves

**Types to include:**
- Before/after results (with numbers)
- "I was skeptical but..." testimonials
- "This was easier than I expected" testimonials
- Industry authority endorsements
- Media mentions or press coverage

---

### Section 9: What You Get
**Purpose:** Itemized list of everything included in the product

**Rules:**
- Break this out as extensively as possible to maximize perceived value
- Every module, bonus, template, resource gets its own line item
- Include product mockup image or visual representation
- Assign dollar values to individual components (value stacking)
- Use benefit-driven descriptions, not just feature names
- Format: Feature name + 1-sentence benefit description

**Template per item:**
```
[Component Name] (Value: $X)
[1-sentence description of what it is and the specific result it delivers]
```

**Example:**
```
Module 3: The Traffic Engine (Value: $497)
The exact paid ad system that drove 14,000 leads in 90 days -- including the campaign structures, audiences, and ad templates you can copy.
```

---

### Section 10: The Offer
**Purpose:** Payment plans, bundles, or single option with price, terms, and guarantee

**Rules:**
- Present price AFTER value has been established (never before)
- If offering payment plans, show both options with clear comparison
- Anchor the price against the total value: "Total value: $4,997 -- Your investment today: $497"
- Make the most popular/recommended option visually prominent
- Include a brief guarantee statement here (detailed guarantee comes next)

**Pricing display template:**
```
TOTAL VALUE: $[high anchor]

YOUR INVESTMENT TODAY:

Option A: One Payment of $[price] (Best Value -- Save $[amount])
Option B: [X] Payments of $[lower price]

[CTA Button]

[Brief guarantee: "Protected by our 30-day money-back guarantee"]
```

---

### Section 11: The Guarantee
**Purpose:** Put your money where your mouth is. Remove risk.

**Rules:**
- Guarantees are net-positive for revenue -- more sales gained than lost to refunds
- State in the positive/affirmative: "You're either head-over-heels thrilled with the results, or just return within 60 days for a full refund"
- Consider dual guarantees: unconditional (30 days, no questions) + conditional (90 days, show you did the work)
- Give the guarantee a name for memorability
- Include a guarantee badge/seal graphic

**Template:**
```
[Guarantee Name] -- Our [X]-Day Money-Back Guarantee

Try [Product] for a full [X] days. If you don't [specific measurable result],
simply email [support email] and we'll refund every penny. No questions, no hassle.

We can make this guarantee because [reason why you're confident].
```

---

### Section 12: Even More Social Proof
**Purpose:** Final credibility push before FAQ

**Rules:**
- Case studies with detailed before/after narratives
- 2-3 longer-form success stories (3-5 sentences each)
- Video testimonials if not used earlier
- Results screenshots with context

---

### Section 13: FAQ
**Purpose:** Answer common questions and bust remaining objections

**Rules:**
- 6-10 questions maximum
- Every FAQ answer should end with a benefit or CTA nudge
- Address the real objections: price, time commitment, skill level, refund process
- Do NOT include questions nobody actually asks
- Final FAQ can be "What happens after I order?" to reduce friction

**Must-include questions:**
1. "Who is this for?" (address skill level/prerequisites)
2. "How long do I have access?" (remove time anxiety)
3. "What if it doesn't work for me?" (restate guarantee)
4. "How quickly will I see results?" (set realistic expectations)
5. "Is this different from [competitor/alternative]?" (differentiation)
6. "What format is the training in?" (practical logistics)

## Page-Level Technical Rules

- **No global navigation** -- Landing pages get no nav bar. Only the logo (linked to homepage for trust) and CTA
- **Mobile-first** -- 60%+ of traffic is mobile. Every section must read well on a phone
- **Loading speed** -- Compress images, lazy-load below-fold content. Every second of load time kills conversions
- **CTA repetition** -- Minimum 3 CTA buttons: after VSL, after story, after offer
- **Sticky CTA** -- Consider a sticky bottom bar on mobile with CTA button always visible
- **Tools referenced:** Kajabi (hosting), Convert.com (A/B testing), WiserNotify/Proof (social proof notifications)
