# Page Structure & Template Selection

## The Five Core Page Types

Not all pages are created equal. The wrong page type for your goal will underperform no matter how good your copy is.

Each page type has a specific structure, purpose, and psychology.

---

## Choosing Your Page Type: Decision Tree

**First question: What's your primary goal?**

1. **Sell a digital product** (course, software, template, guide) → Digital Product Page
2. **Sell a physical product** (e-commerce) → Physical Product Page
3. **Sell a service** (coaching, agency, consulting) → Service Page
4. **Capture emails** (lead magnet, newsletter, webinar signup) → Opt-In Page
5. **Build trust & convert multiple ways** (homepage) → Homepage

Each has a different structure because each serves a different purpose.

---

## Page Type 1: Digital Product Landing Page

**Used for:** Online courses, software, digital templates, guides, memberships

**Goal:** Sell a digital product you've created once that sells to many

**Psychological foundation:** Reader needs to understand WHAT they're getting and see proof that it works

### Structure (In Order)

1. **Hero Section (Above Fold)**
   - 5-15 word headline
   - 1-2 sentence offer description
   - VSL (video sales letter) or compelling image
   - Primary CTA button

2. **Story Section**
   - Why you created this
   - Problem you solved
   - Transformation the product offers
   - THIS is where conversions happen

3. **Social Proof**
   - Customer testimonials
   - Results/transformation stories
   - Number of customers

4. **What You Get**
   - List everything included
   - Break it out (modules, templates, bonuses)
   - Make it feel extensive

5. **The Offer**
   - Pricing
   - Payment options
   - Guarantee

6. **More Social Proof**
   - Different format (case studies, messages, quotes)

7. **FAQ Section**
   - Answer objections preemptively

8. **Final CTA**
   - Reminder + strong call to action

### Key Principles

- **Story is everything** — This is where most conversions happen
- **Social proof repeatedly** — Place 3+ times on page
- **Guarantee is critical** — Money-back removes friction
- **Above fold:** Headline + offer + CTA is enough to convert some

### Copy Focus

- What problem does this solve?
- What transformation do they get?
- Why should they trust you?
- What's included?

## Page Type 2: Physical Product E-Commerce Page

**Used for:** Physical products, merchandise, hardware, consumables

**Goal:** Sell inventory-based products

**Psychological foundation:** Reader needs to see the product, understand what's special about it, and trust the quality

### Structure (In Order)

1. **Hero Section**
   - Product name/benefit headline
   - Brief description
   - Product image/gallery or GIF showing it in action

2. **Price & Primary CTA**
   - Clear price
   - "Add to Cart" button (prominent)
   - Shipping/delivery info

3. **You Might Also Like**
   - Cross-sell section
   - 3 complementary products

4. **Comparison**
   - Direct contrast with competitors
   - Why yours is better/different

5. **Social Proof**
   - Customer reviews/ratings
   - Testimonials
   - How many sold/happy customers

6. **Features Section**
   - Visual breakdown of attributes
   - Quality, durability, design, etc.

7. **More Social Proof**
   - Different format (customer photos, messages)

8. **Why We Made This**
   - Brief origin story
   - Problem it solves

9. **FAQ**
   - Shipping, returns, material questions, etc.

10. **Final CTA**
    - "Add to Cart"

### Key Principles

- **Imagery is critical** — Product must be shown clearly in action
- **Comparison section works** — Direct vs. competitor contrast
- **Reviews matter** — Star ratings and testimonials are trust signals
- **FAQ reduces returns** — Answer shipping, material, fit questions

### Copy Focus

- What makes this special?
- What problem does this solve?
- Why should they buy THIS vs. competitor?
- How good is the quality?

## Page Type 3: Service-Based Landing Page

**Used for:** Coaching, consulting, agencies, implementation services

**Goal:** Generate qualified leads and book consultations/calls

**Psychological foundation:** Service is intangible, so reduce anxiety through clarity and proof

### Structure (In Order)

1. **Hero Section**
   - Benefit headline ("Get [result] for [audience]")
   - Who this is for
   - Primary CTA ("Book Consultation", "Schedule Call", "Request Demo")

2. **How It Works**
   - Step-by-step process breakdown
   - This reduces anxiety about the service delivery
   - Visual timeline helps

3. **Social Proof**
   - Client testimonials
   - Case studies (with results)
   - Before/after examples
   - Number of clients served

4. **Brand Story**
   - Why you got into this
   - Your unique perspective
   - What makes your approach different

5. **Your Team** (if applicable)
   - Photos and bios of who they'll work with
   - Build confidence in the people

6. **Results/Outcomes**
   - Specific results clients achieved
   - Transformation they experienced

7. **Investment Section**
   - Pricing (if applicable)
   - Payment structure
   - Or: "Cost varies by project scope"

8. **FAQ**
   - Timeline questions
   - Scope questions
   - Process questions

9. **Final CTA**
   - "Schedule Your Free Consultation"

### Key Principles

- **"How It Works" is critical** — Reduces anxiety about intangible service
- **Process clarity = confidence** — Step-by-step breakdown matters
- **Team matters** — Show who they'll work with
- **Results beat features** — Show transformations, not services

### Copy Focus

- What transformation do clients experience?
- What's your proven process?
- Who should hire you?
- What results can they expect?

## Page Type 4: Opt-In / Email Capture Page

**Used for:** Lead magnets, newsletter signups, webinar registrations, free trials

**Goal:** Capture emails (often with a valuable freebie)

**Psychological foundation:** Reader is giving up their email for something valuable

### Structure (In Order)

1. **Hero Section**
   - Benefit headline ("Get [valuable result]")
   - What they're receiving (specific)
   - Email capture form

2. **The Lead Magnet Details**
   - What exactly will they get?
   - How will it help them?
   - Why is it valuable?

3. **Social Proof**
   - Number of people who've downloaded
   - Testimonials about the value
   - What customers say they got from it

4. **What's Inside**
   - Chapter/module breakdown
   - What they'll learn
   - Results they can expect

5. **FAQ**
   - "What's this about?"
   - "Do I have to buy anything?" (No)
   - "When will I get it?" (Immediate)

### Key Principles

- **Lead magnet must be specific** — "Free guide on X" > "Free resource"
- **Form should be simple** — First name + email minimum
- **Emphasize immediate access** — "Instant access" matters
- **Social proof critical** — Show it's popular and valuable

### Copy Focus

- What problem does this lead magnet solve?
- What will they learn?
- Why is it valuable?
- Why should they give their email?

## Page Type 5: Homepage

**Used for:** Main website entry point, brand trust building, multiple audience segments

**Goal:** Establish trust, clarify what you do, direct to relevant conversion pages

**Psychological foundation:** Reader clicked to verify legitimacy. Trust is primary job.

### Structure (In Order)

1. **Hero Section (84% attention)**
   - Clear headline: "What do you do?"
   - Why should I care?
   - What do I do next?
   - Primary CTA

2. **Top Navigation**
   - Logo visible
   - Primary CTA prominent
   - Essential links only

3. **Below Hero: What We Do / How It Works**
   - Simple explanation
   - Metaphor for clarity
   - Visual representation

4. **Social Proof**
   - Customer logos (if impressive)
   - Testimonials
   - Case studies

5. **Trust Builders**
   - Awards, affiliations
   - Years in business
   - Number of customers served

6. **Multiple Audience Sections** (if applicable)
   - "For [audience 1]"
   - "For [audience 2]"
   - Help each segment see relevance

7. **Call-to-Action Section**
   - Primary action (what should they do?)
   - Secondary actions

8. **Footer**
   - Extended navigation
   - Contact info
   - Legal links (required for ads)

### Key Principles

- **84% rule:** 84% attention goes above fold. Spend 84% of effort there
- **Clarity > cleverness:** Make it obvious what you do
- **Three 7-second questions:** What is it? Why should I care? What do I do next?
- **Trust first, conversion second:** Homepage won't close the sale, but a bad one WILL lose it

### Copy Focus

- What do you do (in one sentence)?
- What problem do you solve?
- Who should care?
- Where should they go next?

## The 84% Rule (Above-the-Fold Strategy)

84% of homepage attention goes above the fold. 16% of visitors never scroll.

**Therefore: 84% of effort should go to above-fold messaging**

### Above-the-Fold Must Include

1. **Headline** (what do you do?)
2. **Subheadline** (why should they care?)
3. **Visual** (reinforces headline, not distraction)
4. **Primary CTA** (what's the first action?)

Everything else is secondary.

## Seven Essential Page Elements

Every high-converting page has these elements (regardless of type):

### 1. Clear Headline + Subheadline
- **Headline:** What are you offering? (5-15 words)
- **Subheadline:** Why should they care? (1-2 sentences)

### 2. Hero Visual / CTA
- Image, video, or design that reinforces headline
- Or prominent call-to-action
- Purpose: Stop scrolling

### 3. Body Copy / Story
- Problem identification
- Solution introduction
- Transformation promise
- Purpose: Build belief

### 4. Social Proof**
- Testimonials, case studies, logos
- Results, numbers, credentials
- Purpose: Build trust

### 5. How It Works / Features**
- Clear explanation of solution
- Step-by-step if service
- Feature benefits if product
- Purpose: Reduce anxiety

### 6. Offer / Price / Guarantee**
- What are they getting?
- What does it cost?
- What if it doesn't work?
- Purpose: Remove objections

### 7. Clear CTA**
- Specific action
- Prominent placement
- Repeated multiple times
- Purpose: Enable conversion

## Design for Clarity, Not Beauty

This is a critical principle that contradicts most design thinking.

**Reality:** Copy-heavy pages outperform design-heavy pages by 25%+

**Why:** Clarity > visual complexity

### Design Principles for CRO

- **Clean layout** > Fancy design
- **Clear typography** > Decorative fonts
- **White space** > Cramped elements
- **Text hierarchy** > Visual complexity
- **Simple contrast** > Multi-color design

### Why Clarity Wins

1. Readers can understand your offer
2. Copy is readable (no fighting design)
3. Eyes naturally flow through page
4. CTAs stand out clearly

## Wireframing Before Design

**Always wireframe before designing.**

### Wireframe Template

```
HEADER [Logo + Nav + CTA Button]

HERO
[Headline + Subheadline]
[Image/Video OR CTA Button]

STORY
[Paragraphs + maybe image]

SOCIAL PROOF
[Testimonials / logos / numbers]

FEATURES / HOW IT WORKS
[List or steps + visuals]

OFFER
[What they get + price + guarantee]

CTA
[Primary button]

FOOTER
[Navigation + legal]
```

### Wireframing Benefits

- Clarifies structure before design
- Saves designer time
- Ensures all elements included
- Makes messaging clear to designer

## Message Matching (Ad to Landing Page)

If you're running ads, your landing page must feel like continuation of the ad.

**Three gut-check questions:**

1. Are the faces/stories from the ad present on the LP?
2. Is the tone consistent and familiar?
3. Does it still make sense for the viewer?

If all three are YES, message match is good.

If any is NO, create a new landing page.
