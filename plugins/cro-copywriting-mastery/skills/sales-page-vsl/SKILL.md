# Sales Pages, VSLs & Long-Form Persuasion

This skill covers the architecture and writing of sales pages, video sales letters, and any long-form persuasion piece. Multiple frameworks are available — choose based on your price point, audience sophistication, and format.

---

## THE 6-PART CAMPAIGN ARCHITECTURE (Todd Brown)

This is the master structure for any front-end campaign. Every sales page, VSL, and webinar follows this skeleton:

1. **Hook/Headline** — Get attention. Convey the promise + mechanism.
2. **Lead** — Set the emotional hook. Tease the most exciting benefits.
3. **Credibility** — Brief answer to "why should I listen to you?" Two sentences max.
4. **Marketing Argument** — Made up of CPB chunks (Claim-Proof-Benefit). This is 75% of the page.
5. **Segue to Offer** — Transition from mechanism buy-in to product presentation.
6. **Offer** — Present the product as the fulfillment vehicle for the mechanism.

---

## THE 75/25 CAMPAIGN SPLIT

**75% of a front-end campaign** = getting buy-in on the MECHANISM (not the product).
**25%** = presenting the offer.

In the 75%, there is NO mention of the product. No pitching. No product features. Only education about why the mechanism is superior.

This is "education-based marketing" — marketing that uses education as its vehicle. The prospect's sales resistance is low because you are teaching, not pitching. When done correctly, prospects are GRATEFUL for the opportunity to buy.

**Rule:** Only apply 75/25 to front-end campaigns (cold/warm traffic). Back-end campaigns (existing customers) can be much more direct.

## THE CPB CHUNK SYSTEM

The core unit of the marketing argument. Each chunk handles one belief the prospect needs to accept.

**CPB = Claim + Proof + Benefit**

- **Claim:** A statement about your mechanism that the prospect needs to believe
- **Proof:** Evidence supporting the claim (reason-why, data, story, testimonial)
- **Benefit:** What this means for the prospect — "what's in it for YOU"

### How Chunks Build the Buying Belief
CPB1 + CPB2 + CPB3 + CPB4 = Buying Belief (Campaign Thesis)

Like a prosecutor building a case:
- Motive (claim + proof) + Violent tendency (claim + proof) + At the scene (claim + proof) = Guilty verdict

### The Benefit Component Is What Makes It Marketing
Claims + Proof = Argument. Claims + Proof + BENEFIT = Marketing Argument. The benefit tells the prospect "what this means for YOU."

## THE LEAD — SETTING THE EMOTIONAL HOOK

The lead comes right after the headline. Think of it as the elevator pitch for the campaign.

**Purpose:** Tease the most exciting benefits of the mechanism. Do NOT explain or unpack them. Just tease.

**After the lead, the prospect should think:** "Wow, this sounds amazing! Now prove to me this works, and will work for me."

### Two Lead Types
1. **Story Leads:** Can be killer but tend to boom or bust. The prospect must see themselves in the story.
2. **Problem-Solution Leads:** Hit the problem, agitate, then get to the solution early. Hope comes from the solution. More reliable.

## CHOOSING YOUR FRAMEWORK

| Framework | Best For | Price Point | Complexity |
|-----------|----------|-------------|------------|
| OnePeak 7-Step | Quick sales pages, ad scripts | $10-$200 | Low |
| Assadi 10-Step | Complete sales letters, course launches | $100-$2,000 | Medium |
| Amarsi 17.5-Step | Premium sales pages, high-ticket | $500-$10,000+ | High |
| Todd Brown CPB System | Mechanism-first campaigns, VSLs | Any | High |

### Sales Page Length by Price Point (Assadi)
- **$10-$50:** Short page, benefits-focused, minimal story
- **$50-$200:** Medium page, story + benefits + social proof
- **$200-$1,000:** Long page, full framework, extensive proof
- **$1,000+:** Very long page or VSL, deep mechanism argument, multiple proof sections

## THE SPIT DRAFT PROCESS

Borrowed from TV scripting. A spit draft lays out WHAT you will say, not HOW you will say it.

### How It Works
Write a description of each section's purpose, not the actual copy:
```
Eyebrow copy with exact phrase message match,
headline introducing the pain of [problem],
line that agitates expense of [current solution],
three-line story about problems with [topic],
introduction of idea that solutions exist,
introduction of product,
value prop for a pain-aware prospect...
```

### The Letter Writing Technique
For the bottom 90% of the page:
1. Write a persuasive letter to your One Reader arguing why they should take your offer
2. Parse the letter into bullets/messages
3. Lay them out as a spit draft
4. Set aside 2-3 days, return, find gaps in argument flow
5. Fill gaps, set aside again
6. Return for the first actual written draft

## OPEN LOOPS AND PATTERN INTERRUPTS

### Open Loops
- "More about that in a minute..."
- "In a moment I'll tell you... First, let me..."
- Keep the prospect reading by creating unresolved tension
- Every loop opened MUST be closed

### Pattern Interrupts on Sales Pages
- Myth-busting sections: "Myth: You need 10,000 followers to make money. Reality:..."
- Disqualification at top: "Who this is for" / "Who this is NOT for"
- Unexpected tone shifts (humor after serious content)
- Callout boxes with surprising data points

---

## FRONT-END VS. BACK-END CAMPAIGNS

### Front-End (Cold/Warm Traffic)
- Full 75/25 structure required
- Big marketing idea, strong hook, mechanism buy-in before product
- The marketing argument IS the education

### Back-End (Existing Customers)
- Can be much more direct
- Lead with: "Here's what I've got, here's what it does, here's why it's great, here's how to get it"
- Leverage existing trust and goodwill
- Deeper into the back end = more direct

## THE CLOSE

### Psychology of Closing
Tell the prospect exactly what is going to happen and stay congruent. This allows people to rehearse the purchase in their mind — and then their body follows.

### Close Structure (Products)
```
To get started, here's what you do next.

Click the order button below where you'll be taken to our secure
checkout page where all you have to do is enter your regular details
and complete your investment.

You'll then be taken to a registration page and receive an email
with your login details.

Within [timeframe] you'll be able to get started and I highly
recommend you [first action to take].
```

### Close Structure (Services)
```
To get started, here's what you do next.

Enter your name and email below and you'll be taken to my calendar page.

There, you'll be able to select a time for our call.

There are [number] questions I need you to answer as it'll help
us get the most out of the call.
```

**Key phrase:** "Enter your regular details" — normalizes the purchase action.

## THE RE-CLOSE

Amarsi's term for a summary before asking for the sale again. Place it after the guarantee and bonuses.

```
To get started with [product], all you have to do is [action].

Remember, you're covered by my [XX] guarantee, meaning you can
[go through the program], and [future-paced result].

You'll also receive [X] bonuses:
- Bonus #1: [Name]
- Bonus #2: [Name]
- Bonus #3: [Name]

Total value is $[amount]

Click the button below to begin.
```

## P.S. STRATEGY (3 Formulas)

**P.S. #1 — Remind them of what they're getting:**
"P.S. Remember, all I'm offering is [opportunity/transformation]."

**P.P.S. — Price and a little pain:**
"P.P.S. You can get started for $[price]. Either you'll implement this or your competition will."

**P.P.P.S. — Scarcity + competition:**
"P.P.P.S. There's only a limited number available until [date]..."

---

## PRICE REVEAL SEQUENCE

1. Build value through the entire page before revealing price
2. Stack the value (show total worth of everything included)
3. Show the anchor price (strikethrough)
4. Reveal the actual price
5. Justify with honest reasoning
6. Show comparison (cost of NOT buying, or cost of alternatives)

> Read `references/price-reveal-techniques.md` for complete anchoring, justification, and reveal methods.

## UPSELL AND DOWNSELL PRINCIPLES

- Upsells: Offered immediately after purchase. Must complement the core offer.
- Downsells: Offered when they decline the upsell. Lower price, simpler version.
- Fix AOV before fixing conversion rate (contrarian: much easier to increase order value than to convert more non-buyers)
