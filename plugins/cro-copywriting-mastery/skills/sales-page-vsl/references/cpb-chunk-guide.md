# CPB Chunk Construction Guide

## WHAT IS A CPB CHUNK?

The core building block of every marketing argument. Each chunk handles ONE belief the prospect needs to accept before they can accept the overall buying belief (Campaign Thesis).

**CPB = Claim + Proof + Benefit**

- **Claim:** A statement about your mechanism the prospect needs to believe
- **Proof:** Evidence supporting the claim
- **Benefit:** What this means for the prospect personally

### Why Benefit Matters
Claims + Proof = an Argument.
Claims + Proof + **Benefit** = a **Marketing** Argument.

Without the benefit, you are just proving a point. With the benefit, you are showing the prospect why they should CARE.

---

## HOW CHUNKS BUILD THE BUYING BELIEF

The Campaign Thesis (Buying Belief) is the overarching statement you need the prospect to accept:

**Formula:** "The [statement of superiority] way to [Primary Promise] is with [Unique Mechanism]."

**Example:** "The most effective way to get new buyers every day is with the E5 Method."

Each CPB chunk establishes one sub-belief needed for the overall buying belief:

```
CPB Chunk 1: [Sub-belief about WHY old approach fails]
    +
CPB Chunk 2: [Sub-belief about WHAT makes your mechanism different]
    +
CPB Chunk 3: [Sub-belief about HOW your mechanism works]
    +
CPB Chunk 4: [Sub-belief about WHO has succeeded with this mechanism]
    =
BUYING BELIEF: "The [superior] way to [promise] is with [mechanism]"
```

Think of it like a prosecutor building a case:
- Motive (claim + proof) + Violent tendency (claim + proof) + At the scene (claim + proof) = Guilty verdict

---

## CPB CHUNK TEMPLATE

```
[CLAIM]
State the claim clearly and directly. One sentence.
"[Thing] is true because of [reason]."

[PROOF]
Support the claim with evidence. 1-3 paragraphs depending
on claim boldness. Use the most appropriate proof type.

[BENEFIT]
Bridge to what this means for the prospect.
"What this means for you is..."
"So you can..."
"Which means..."
```

## PROOF TYPES (Ranked by Power)

### 1. Reason-Why Proof (Default — Use Most Often)
The simplest and most powerful proof form. Two magic phrases:
- "The reason why is..."
- "Because..."

What comes after "because" or "the reason why" serves as evidence.

**Example:** "This ensures your ad account never gets banned BECAUSE it leverages only compliant copy patterns that Facebook's algorithm recognizes as organic content."

You can build an ENTIRE marketing argument using only reason-why proof. It comes directly from interrogating your unique mechanism.

### 2. Third-Party Evidence
Studies, research, data from credible institutions.
- "According to research from [institution]..."
- "A study published in [journal] found that..."

### 3. Expert Authority
Quotes or endorsements from recognized authorities.
- "[Expert name], who [credibility], says..."

### 4. Case Study / Testimonial
Client results that prove the specific claim (not generic praise).
- Match the testimonial to the specific claim being made
- If you claim it is easy to start, use a testimonial about ease of setup

### 5. Demonstration
Show the mechanism working. Screenshots, walkthroughs, before/after.

### 6. Common Sense / Analogy
When the claim is intuitive, connecting it to something they already believe.
- "You already know that [accepted truth]. Well, [your claim] works the same way."

---

## THE PROOF POWER OF THREE

- One piece of evidence = an event (could be a fluke)
- Two pieces = a coincidence
- Three pieces = a pattern

For bold claims that stretch believability:
1. First proof: Strongest, most copy devoted to it
2. Second proof: Half the length of first
3. Third proof: Half the length of second

Vary the TYPE of proof across the three (reason-why + data + testimonial).

## FIVE WORKED EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Online Course (Mechanism: "The Outcome Architecture")

**Campaign Thesis:** "The most effective way to escape the time-for-money trap is with the Outcome Architecture."

**CPB Chunk 1 (Why the old way fails):**
- Claim: "Selling hourly consulting is fundamentally broken because your income is capped by the number of hours in a day."
- Proof (Reason-Why): "The reason why is simple math. At $200/hour and 30 billable hours a week, your absolute ceiling is $312,000/year — and that assumes zero vacation, zero sick days, and zero admin time. In reality, most consultants bill 15-20 hours and top out at $150K while working 50-hour weeks."
- Benefit: "Which means you are working harder than most employees, taking on all the risk, and still hitting an income ceiling that has nothing to do with your talent."

**CPB Chunk 2 (What makes the mechanism different):**
- Claim: "The Outcome Architecture decouples your time from your revenue by packaging your expertise around results instead of hours."
- Proof (Reason-Why + Case Study): "The reason this works is that clients do not actually want your time — they want results. When Sarah switched from $150/hour consulting to a $5,000 Outcome Architecture package, her clients got BETTER results because the package was focused on outcomes, not on stretching hours."
- Benefit: "What this means for you is that your income becomes a function of how many clients you serve, not how many hours you work. Sarah serves 6 clients per quarter and earns more than she did with 17."

**CPB Chunk 3 (How the mechanism works):**
- Claim: "The Architecture works in three phases: Package, Price, and Position — and the entire setup takes less than a week."
- Proof (Demonstration): "In Phase 1, you identify the 3 outcomes your clients care about most. In Phase 2, you assign a value based on what those outcomes are worth — not your hours. In Phase 3, you position the package so clients see it as an investment with a measurable return. The entire process follows a template you fill out in 90 minutes."
- Benefit: "So you can have a new, premium offer ready to sell before the end of this week — without creating any new content, courses, or deliverables."

**CPB Chunk 4 (Who has succeeded):**
- Claim: "This is not theory — 347 consultants have implemented the Outcome Architecture in the last 18 months."
- Proof (Social proof + data): "On average, they increased their monthly revenue by 2.3x while reducing client hours by 40%. The highest performer went from $8K/month to $47K/month in 90 days."
- Benefit: "And because the framework is based on YOUR existing expertise — not a new skill you need to learn — you can start where you are right now, with the clients you already serve."

### Example 2: SaaS Product (Mechanism: "Pixel Refreshing")

**CPB Chunk 1 (Problem with current approach):**
- Claim: "iOS tracking updates have destroyed the accuracy of your Facebook ad data."
- Proof: "Since iOS 14.5, Facebook's own data shows a 30-40% underreporting of conversions. That means for every 10 sales your ads generate, Facebook only reports 6-7 of them — making your campaigns look like they're losing money when they're actually profitable."
- Benefit: "Which means you're killing winning campaigns based on bad data and wasting thousands of dollars on what you THINK are your best-performing ads."

**CPB Chunk 2 (Why the mechanism is different):**
- Claim: "Pixel Refreshing bypasses the iOS limitation entirely by reading conversion signals from a different source."
- Proof: "The reason why this works is that Pixel Refreshing uses server-side event tracking combined with probabilistic matching. Instead of relying on the pixel that Apple blocks, it reads the purchase event directly from your payment processor and matches it back to the ad click using encrypted identifiers. Apple's privacy framework does not affect server-side events."
- Benefit: "So you see EVERY conversion your ads produce — not just the 60-70% that Facebook currently reports. Your ROAS numbers become accurate again, and you can make decisions based on reality instead of guesswork."

### Example 3: Health Product (Mechanism: "The Cortisol Reset Protocol")

**CPB Chunk 1:**
- Claim: "The reason you cannot lose belly fat has nothing to do with calories, exercise, or willpower."
- Proof: "Research from the University of California shows that elevated cortisol — your body's stress hormone — triggers a specific type of fat storage called visceral fat accumulation. This type of fat wraps around your organs and is resistant to calorie restriction because it serves as a cortisol buffer."
- Benefit: "What this means is that no amount of dieting or exercise will flatten your stomach unless you address the cortisol issue first. It explains why you can eat perfectly and still see no change."

## CPB CHUNK RULES

1. **One claim per chunk.** Never combine multiple claims. Each chunk handles exactly one sub-belief.
2. **No claim bigger than your biggest proof point.** If your best case study shows 3-day results, do not claim 24 hours.
3. **The benefit bridges to the prospect.** Without it, you have an argument — not a marketing argument.
4. **Use reason-why as your default proof.** It comes directly from interrogating the mechanism.
5. **Vary proof types across chunks.** Do not use the same proof type in every chunk.
6. **Remove unsubstantiated claims.** Unsubstantiated claims stack together to create disbelief.
7. **Strategic testimonial placement.** Match testimonials to specific claims, not generic use.
8. **3-5 chunks per marketing argument.** Fewer than 3 feels thin. More than 5 feels exhausting.

---

## THE CLAIM-PROOF FLOW

For each chunk, write in this order:

1. **State the claim** in one clear sentence
2. **Ask yourself:** "Why should they believe this?" Write the proof.
3. **Ask yourself:** "So what? What does this mean for THEM?" Write the benefit.
4. **Read back:** Does the proof actually prove the claim? Does the benefit actually connect to the prospect's life?
5. **Tighten:** Cut any proof that does not directly support the specific claim. Cut any benefit that is vague.

## PROOF TYPE HIERARCHY (When to Use What)

Match the proof type to the claim's boldness and the prospect's skepticism:

| Claim Boldness | Recommended Proof Types | Example |
|----------------|------------------------|---------|
| Mild (intuitive) | Reason-why alone | "We use templates because structure removes guesswork" |
| Moderate (new info) | Reason-why + third-party data | "Research from [institution] confirms..." |
| Bold (surprising) | Three-proof stack: reason-why + data + testimonial | Strongest proof first, tapering length |
| Extraordinary (paradigm shift) | Monster proof point + expert authority + multiple case studies | Build an entire section around the proof |

### Monster Proof Points

During research, watch for extraordinarily strong proof points. Sometimes you can build an entire HOOK around a single monster proof point. 90% of marketers start with an idea and look for proof. You can reverse this: start with a monster proof point and build the idea around it.

Monster proof points are rare but disproportionately powerful. They include: government studies, celebrity endorsements of the mechanism (not the product), viral news stories that validate the approach, or a single client result so dramatic it defies expectation.

## WORKED EXAMPLE 4: E-COMMERCE DTC (Mechanism: "The Story-First Launch")

**Campaign Thesis:** "The most effective way to launch a physical product without a massive ad budget is with the Story-First Launch."

**CPB Chunk 1 (Why the old way fails):**
- Claim: "Pouring money into Facebook ads on launch day is gambling, not marketing."
- Proof (Reason-Why + Data): "The reason why is that new products have zero social proof, zero reviews, and zero pixel data. Facebook's algorithm needs approximately 50 conversions per week to optimize delivery. On day one, you have none — so the algorithm is guessing, and your cost-per-acquisition skyrockets."
- Benefit: "Which means the harder you push ads on launch day, the faster you burn through your budget with nothing to show for it."

**CPB Chunk 2 (What makes the mechanism different):**
- Claim: "The Story-First Launch builds demand BEFORE the product is available by turning your origin story into a pre-launch content sequence."
- Proof (Case Study): "When Norse Organics used this approach, they generated 4,200 email subscribers and $38,000 in pre-orders before spending a single dollar on ads. Their launch-day ad costs were 60% lower because they had warm pixel data from day one."
- Benefit: "So instead of gambling with cold traffic, you launch to an audience that is already emotionally invested in your product and eager to buy."
