# The 17.5-Step Sales Page Framework (Adil Amarsi)

This framework generated nearly $1 billion in sales across 420+ niches. It mirrors natural conversation — how you would sell face to face. The reader mentally rehearses the conversation that allows them to buy.

---

## THE COMPLETE STRUCTURE

1. Pre-Headline
2. Headline
3. Sub-Headline
4. Opening Paragraph
5. Introduction (Who You Are)
6. Story of Discovery / Origin Story
7. Introducing Your Product/Service
8. Product/Service Descriptions
9. Testimonials
10. Price Reveal and Justification
11. The Close
12. Guarantees
13. Bonuses
14. The Re-Close
15. Sign Off
16. P.S.
17. P.P.S.
17.5. P.P.P.S.

---

## STEP 1: PRE-HEADLINE

Teaser copy that calls out the audience specifically. Sets up the headline.

**Rules:**
- Great for calling out your audience specifically
- Not always necessary — test with and without
- Works especially well on post opt-in pages, upsells, downsells

**Templates:**
- "For [Market] Facing [Problem]..."
- "Your Download Is On Its Way, But First..."
- "For your eyes only..."
- "Attention [specific audience identifier]..."

---

## STEP 2: HEADLINE

Drives home a benefit and sparks enough curiosity to read on. 80% of your work is in the headline.

**The 5 Core Headline Formulas:**

1. **Discover:** "Discover The Single Biggest Issue Plaguing Every [Market] On Earth & The Simple Fix That'll Put You In The [Result] Range..."

2. **Uncover:** "Uncover The Blueprint That Has Generated [Big Claim] In Over [Number] Markets."

3. **If, Then:** "If You're Looking to [Desire] And Have Less Than [Timeframe] To Get It Right, Then Read Every Word Below."

4. **Finally:** "Finally: Take A Sneak Peek Behind A [Big Claim] & Replicate The Same Success."

5. **Revealed:** "Revealed: The Insider Secrets Of A [Authority Claim] To [Big Benefit]..."

---

## STEP 3: SUB-HEADLINE

Continues the conversation from the headline. Sets up another point that keeps reading.

**Critical Rule:** NEVER start the sub-headline with the same word as the headline.

**Purpose:** Continue the story, add a second layer of benefit or curiosity.

---

## STEP 4: OPENING PARAGRAPH

**Three Rules:**
1. Always start as a continuation of the sub-headline
2. Use one-sentence paragraphs throughout
3. Create an open loop that piques curiosity — do NOT pay it off yet

**Four Length Options:**
- **Short:** Best for eCom, obvious problems. Summarize quickly.
- **Medium:** Best for personal brand, service businesses. Build trust.
- **Long:** After curiosity headlines. Explain, build open loops.
- **None (Old School):** Straight into "Dear Friend" then copy.

**Shortcut:** If in doubt, start with "It wasn't that long ago when..."

---

## STEP 5: INTRODUCTION (Who You Are)

Social media changed this step. It is no longer just "what can you do for me." Now it is: "Who are you, what can you do for me, and would we get along?"

**Introduction Greeting Formulas:**
- Classic: "Dear friend" / "Dear reader" / "Hello [name]"
- Advanced: "Dear disgruntled [market trait]" / "Dear future [result]"

**Long Introduction Structure:**
1. Start with your name
2. Background of who you are
3. Why you (authority building)
4. Throw in actual value to demonstrate expertise
5. Tease the transformation you are about to reveal

**Short Introduction (low-end products or warm audiences):**
- Name + authority claim + career stats
- What you are about to share
- Transition to the journey story

---

## STEP 6: STORY OF DISCOVERY / ORIGIN STORY

People want to know there is a backstory. Origin stories allow the audience to map the journey with you.

**Rules:**
- Does NOT have to be a pain/horror story unless it truly was
- Must be real for you to connect
- Can be fun, funny, or just an everyday tale

**Structure:**
1. The origin of how things started
2. What happened / benefits of understanding the situation
3. What led to the discovery that allowed you to create what you are selling

---

## STEP 7: INTRODUCING YOUR PRODUCT/SERVICE

**Products:** Center text, sub-headline, Title Case. Use "Introducing [Name]..." then detail features, duration, what is inside + advantages and benefits.

**Services:** Use: "That's why I'm writing to you today... I want to see if I can truly create something great just for you and your business." Or lead with a case study into your offer.

---

## STEP 8: PRODUCT/SERVICE DESCRIPTIONS

**Product Description Structure:**
1. Title for the module/product section
2. Describe what is inside
3. Detail with an ODD number of bullets (always odd: 3, 5, 7)
4. Finish with application or potential outcome

**Key Formula for Bullets:**
- "The [Number] [Type] That [Authority Claim] In Nearly Every [Context]"
- "What [Things] To Avoid — and why this could make the difference between [success] or [failure]"
- Always end with "And so much more..."

---

## STEP 9: TESTIMONIALS

Place after product descriptions to prove the claims you just made.

**Most important element:** Show the TRANSFORMATION. Ask: "What was life like before?" and "How's life now?"

**Format:** Transformation + photo + full name + title/company.

---

## STEP 10: PRICE REVEAL AND JUSTIFICATION

**Structure:**
1. Transition from testimonials
2. State the price
3. Justify with honest reasoning

**Template:**
```
What's the investment?

It's only $[amount].

That's it.

The reason it's only $[amount] is simple.

[Honest reason — accessibility, ROI, belief in product]

Think about it this way...

Would you rather spend the next [timeframe] trying to
figure out [struggle]?

Or...

Would you rather spend the next [timeframe] implementing
[proven solution]?

The choice is entirely yours.
```

**High-Ticket:** If you do not want to reveal exact price, mention a range to filter leads.

---

## STEP 11: THE CLOSE

Tell them EXACTLY what happens when they click.

**Product Close:**
```
To get started, here's what you do next.

Click the order button below where you'll be taken to our
secure checkout page where all you have to do is enter
your regular details and complete your investment.

You'll receive an email with your login details.

Within [timeframe] you'll be able to get started and I
recommend you [first action].
```

**"Enter your regular details"** — psychologically normalizes the purchase.

---

## STEP 12: GUARANTEES

**Longer guarantees = lower refund rates.** Amarsi uses 363-day guarantees. People procrastinate on refund requests; the longer window reduces urgency to refund.

**Template:**
```
Is there a refund?

Yes there is.

If, for whatever reason, you're not 100% satisfied with
[product] within the next [timeframe], all you have to do
is reach out to me through [channel].

You can keep the bonuses as a thank you and we'll
part as friends.
```

---

## STEP 13: BONUSES

**Bonuses go AFTER the guarantee, not before.** Amarsi's testing shows this placement strengthens the close.

**Purpose:** Move "no" to "maybe," "maybe" to "yes," "yes" to "hell yes."

**Rules:**
1. Relevant to the core offer
2. Placed AFTER guarantee
3. Use odd number of bullets (3 or 5)
4. End with "And so much more..."
5. Give TRUE value — no inflated numbers

---

## STEP 14: THE RE-CLOSE

Summary before asking for the sale again.

```
To get started with [product], all you have to do is [action].

Remember, you're covered by my [XX] guarantee.

You'll also receive:
- Bonus #1: [Name]
- Bonus #2: [Name]
- Bonus #3: [Name]

Total value: $[amount]

Click the button below to begin.
```

---

## STEPS 15-17.5: SIGN OFF AND P.S. FORMULAS

**Sign Off:** Make it unique and brand-specific.

**P.S. #1 — Remind:** "P.S. Remember, all I'm offering is [transformation]."

**P.P.S. — Price + Pain:** "P.P.S. Get started for $[price]. Either you implement or your competition will."

**P.P.P.S. — Scarcity:** "P.P.P.S. Limited availability until [date]..."
