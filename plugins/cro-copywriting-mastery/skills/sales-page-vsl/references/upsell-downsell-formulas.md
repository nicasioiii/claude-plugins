# Upsell & Downsell Formulas

## THE UPSELL FORMULA

An upsell is presented immediately after the initial purchase. The customer has already committed — momentum is in your favor.

### Upsell Page Structure
```
[Pre-Headline]
"Wait! Your Order is Not Complete..."

[Headline]
"Before You Go, I Have a Special Offer Just For You..."

[Sub-Headline]
Expand on the benefit of the upsell and its connection to
what they just bought.

[Why This Offer Exists]
Explain the logical reason you are making this offer NOW.
Connect it directly to the product they just purchased.

[What It Is]
Clear description of the upsell product/service.
Use odd-number bullet points.

[How It Enhances What They Just Bought]
Bridge the upsell to the core product. Show how it
accelerates, deepens, or expands results.

[Social Proof]
1-2 testimonials specific to the upsell product.

[The Offer]
Price (always lower perceived friction than the core offer).
One-click purchase — no re-entering payment details.

[CTA Button]
"Yes! Add This To My Order"

[Decline Link]
"No thanks, I don't want [benefit statement]"
— Frame the decline as declining the BENEFIT,
   not just declining a product.
```

### Upsell Rules
1. **Must complement the core offer.** An upsell that feels disconnected confuses and annoys.
2. **Must accelerate or deepen results.** The best upsells help them get results faster or better.
3. **One-click purchase.** NEVER make them re-enter payment details.
4. **Price should feel like a no-brainer** relative to what they just spent.
5. **Frame the decline as declining a benefit,** not just a product: "No thanks, I don't want faster results" instead of "No thanks."

### Upsell Types That Work
- **Done-For-You upgrade:** "Let us do it for you" — always the highest-converting upsell
- **Accelerator:** Additional training/tools that speed up results
- **Templates/swipe files:** Ready-made resources they can use immediately
- **Extended access:** Longer access period, additional modules
- **Group coaching add-on:** Live support on top of self-paced product
- **Premium tier:** VIP version with additional features

## THE DOWNSELL FORMULA

A downsell is presented when they DECLINE the upsell. It is a lower-priced, simpler version of the upsell.

### Downsell Page Structure
```
[Pre-Headline]
"I Understand - Let Me Make This Easier..."

[Headline]
"What If You Could Get [Core Benefit] For Just $[Lower Price]?"

[Acknowledge Their Decision]
Show you respect their decision not to take the upsell.
Do NOT be pushy or guilt-trip.

[Present the Lighter Version]
Explain what this simpler version includes.
Make it clear this is a DIFFERENT offer, not a discount
on the same thing.

[Why This Works]
Brief explanation of why the lighter version still delivers value.

[The Offer]
Significantly lower price than the upsell.
One-click purchase.

[CTA Button]
"Yes, I'll Take The [Lighter Version]"

[Decline Link]
"No thanks, I'll figure it out on my own"
```

### Downsell Rules
1. **Must be a genuinely different offer,** not just a discounted upsell. Discounting the same thing trains customers to always decline first.
2. **Lower price, lower scope.** Remove components to justify the lower price.
3. **Acknowledge their decision respectfully.** "I understand" not "Are you sure?"
4. **Still one-click purchase.** No friction.
5. **The decline statement should create mild regret** but not guilt: "No thanks, I'll figure it out on my own."

### Downsell Types That Work
- **Lite version:** Fewer modules, fewer features, still valuable
- **PDF/digital version** instead of video course
- **Self-paced** instead of coached version
- **Single module** instead of full program
- **Payment plan** for the upsell price (lower monthly, same total)
- **Trial period** at reduced cost

## UPSELL/DOWNSELL MISTAKES AND ANTI-PATTERNS

### Mistake 1: Upselling Something Unrelated
If someone buys a copywriting course and you upsell a fitness program, you lose trust instantly. The upsell MUST be a natural extension of what they just bought.

### Mistake 2: Too Many Upsells
The maximum is 2 upsells (with a downsell for each). More than that creates "upsell fatigue" and buyer's remorse for the original purchase.

Recommended flow:
```
Core Purchase → Upsell 1 → (if declined) Downsell 1 → Thank You Page
```

Or for higher-ticket:
```
Core Purchase → Upsell 1 → (if accepted) Upsell 2 → Thank You Page
Core Purchase → Upsell 1 → (if declined) Downsell 1 → Thank You Page
```

### Mistake 3: Making the Upsell More Expensive Than the Core
The upsell should feel like a small additional investment, not a second major purchase. Exception: Done-for-you services can be priced higher because the value differential is obvious.

### Mistake 4: Discounting the Upsell as the Downsell
If you offer the same product at a lower price as the downsell, you train customers to ALWAYS decline the upsell knowing a discount is coming. The downsell must be a different, simpler product.

### Mistake 5: No Connection to the Core Product
Every upsell must clearly answer: "How does this make the thing I just bought work BETTER?"

### Mistake 6: Reusing the Same Copy Style
The upsell page should feel different from the sales page. It should be shorter, more direct, and conversational. The hard selling was done on the sales page — now you are talking to someone who already trusts you enough to buy.

### Mistake 7: Forgetting the Thank You Page
After all upsells/downsells, the thank you page should:
- Confirm what they purchased
- Set expectations for next steps
- Deliver access/download links
- Include a welcome video or message
- NOT try to sell anything else (let them breathe)

## THE AOV PRIORITY (CONTRARIAN)

Fix AOV before fixing conversion rate.

- Doubling conversion rate from 2% to 4% is a MONSTER leap with unconverted non-buyers (the hardest group to move)
- But bringing AOV from $200 to $400 through upsells, order bumps, and slack adjusters is much more achievable
- CPA has a fixed floor ($0). AOV has infinite upside.
- The higher you can afford to pay for CPA, the more secure your business is.

**Order form optimization:** When click-through from the sales page is strong but order form abandonment is high, strip the order form to a "skeleton" (product, price, payment fields only). Extra elements on the order form can TALK PEOPLE OUT of buying.

## DECLINE BUTTON COPY (The "Negative Choice" Technique)

Frame the decline button so that clicking it means explicitly rejecting the BENEFIT, not just the product:

**DO:**
- "No thanks, I don't want more leads"
- "No thanks, I prefer to do it the hard way"
- "No thanks, I don't need faster results"
- "No thanks, I'll figure it out on my own"

**DO NOT:**
- "No thanks" (too easy to click without feeling anything)
- "I'm not interested" (too neutral)
- "Skip this offer" (no emotional weight)

**Warning:** This technique is powerful but can feel manipulative if overdone. Use mild regret, not guilt. The reader should think "hmm, I kind of do want that" -- not feel shamed into buying.

## UPSELL PRICING GUIDELINES

| Core Product Price | Upsell Price Range | Downsell Price Range |
|-------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| $7-$47 (tripwire) | $27-$97 | $7-$27 |
| $97-$297 (mid-ticket) | $47-$197 | $27-$67 |
| $497-$997 (high-ticket) | $197-$497 | $47-$197 |
| $2,000+ (premium) | $497-$1,497 | $197-$497 |

**Exception:** Done-for-you upsells can exceed the core product price because the value differential is obvious and the customer is trading money for time/effort.

## UPSELL CONVERSION BENCHMARKS

- A well-structured upsell typically converts 10-25% of buyers
- A well-structured downsell typically converts 5-15% of those who declined the upsell
- Combined, upsell + downsell flows can increase AOV by 30-60%
- The highest-converting upsell type across niches: Done-For-You upgrades
- The simplest upsell to implement: templates and swipe files (high margin, zero fulfillment complexity)
