# The 12-Point Sandy Franks Framework: Complete Walkthrough

Sandy Franks' proprietary framework for writing any story that converts. This framework is used in ALL types of storytelling: sales copy, emails, testimonials, case studies, content.

## POINT 1: THE TITLE

**Job:** Catch attention at the reptilian brain level (survival instinct)

**Rules:**
- Must intrigue or signal importance
- Create curiosity
- Set up story expectation

**Examples:**
- "How I Almost Lost Everything (But Discovered the Secret)"
- "The Day Everything Changed"
- "The Mistake That Made Me Rich"
- "Why My Biggest Fear Led to Success"

**What works:**
- Conflict signal: "How I Almost Lost..." "The Crisis..."
- Mystery: "The Secret..." "What Nobody Knows..."
- Transformation: "The Day I Changed..." "From X to Y..."
- Emotional hook: "The Worst Moment..." "The Best Day..."

**What doesn't work:**
- Too generic: "My Story," "How I Got Here"
- Too cute/clever: Risk over clarity
- Too long: Keep it short (5-8 words ideal)

---

## POINT 2: THE OPENING HOOK / UNUSUAL ENTRY POINT

**Job:** Startle reader; make them compulsively keep reading

**Rules:**
- Don't start at chronological beginning
- Start in middle of action/drama/intensity
- Say something that makes reader think "I have to know what happens"
- Create pattern interrupt (unexpected statement)

**Examples:**

*Unusual entry point (mid-action):*
- "I was dead broke. Completely broke. And I was about to lose my house."
- "She looked me in the eye and said, 'You're a failure.'"
- "That's when the door burst open and everything changed."

*Pattern interrupt:*
- "I made $10,000 my first month in business. Then I lost it all in 30 days."
- "The expert told me I had 6 months to live."
- "I was the guy nobody wanted to hire."

**What works:**
- Specific detail (dollar amount, time, emotion)
- Contradiction or surprise
- Something emotionally charged
- Statement that raises question: "Why? How? What happens?"

---

## POINTS 3-4: BACKSTORY + CONTEXT

**Job:** Build empathy; help reader understand the situation

**Rules:**
- Paint before-state vividly
- Show reader's thinking/perspective
- Include sensory details
- Make reader FEEL what character felt
- Establish stakes (why it matters)

**What to include:**
- Where/when this happened
- What character's life was like before
- What they wanted/dreamed of
- What obstacles existed
- How they felt about the situation

**Example:**

"I was 23 years old. Fresh out of college. No job prospects. My parents kept asking, 'What's your plan?' And I had nothing. I was sleeping on my friend's couch. My degree in communications felt useless. I had $47 in my bank account. Every morning, I'd look at my phone hoping for a job callback. Never came. I felt like a failure."

**Key:** Make reader think "I've been there" or "I can relate to that feeling"

---

## POINTS 5-6: CONFLICT / TENSION / THE PROBLEM

**Job:** Create engagement; trigger reader's amygdala (emotional brain)

**Rules:**
- Someone wants something and can't get it
- Create emotional stakes (why it matters)
- Build anticipation ("How will this end?")
- Make reader's brain release chemicals (dopamine, adrenaline)

**Types of conflict:**
- External: Outside force preventing goal
- Internal: Character's own beliefs/fears preventing goal
- Two people wanting same thing; only one can get it

**Example:**

"I wanted to quit my job. I dreamed of building my own business. But I had a family to support. A mortgage to pay. I was the only income. If I failed, we'd lose everything. My wife was supportive but terrified. Every night, I'd lie awake thinking: 'What if I'm not good enough? What if I fail? What if I lose everything?'

That was the conflict. I wanted to leap. But I was too afraid."

**Key:** Make reader understand BOTH sides of conflict. Make stakes feel real.

---

## POINT 7: THE PIVOTAL MOMENT / TURNING POINT

**Job:** Create the moment when everything changes

**Rules:**
- Moment when character's thinking shifts
- Could be big or small (size doesn't matter; impact does)
- Must shift direction/perspective
- Reader should connect: "I've had a moment like this"

**Examples:**

*Moment of realization:*
- "I was sitting in a coffee shop, broke and defeated. I saw someone with a business book. I didn't ask why. I just asked to borrow it. That book changed everything."

*Moment of decision:*
- "I was on the floor, crying. My daughter walked in and said, 'Daddy, are you okay?' And I realized: She doesn't care if I'm rich. She just wants me present. That's when I decided to build something that let me be there for her."

*Moment of discovery:*
- "I failed. Again. I was furious. I threw the plan against the wall. And one page fell open to a sentence I'd missed: 'The real secret isn't the product. It's the customer.' And everything clicked."

**Key:** Make it relatable. Reader should think: "I could have this moment too."

---

## POINT 8: CURIOSITY GAP SETUP / CREATING MYSTERY

**Job:** Create information gap that reader wants closed

**Rules:**
- Hint at something interesting/important coming
- Hold back full information
- Make reader think: "I need to know what happens"
- You WILL close this gap later (not clickbait)

**Example:**

"So I borrowed the book. I came home and read it cover to cover in one night. By page 47, I realized something that changed everything. But I'm getting ahead of myself. Let me back up..."

**Key:** Tease without answering. Make reader desperate to know.

---

## POINT 9: BUILDING ANTICIPATION / PACING

**Job:** Maintain engagement; keep reader reading

**Rules:**
- Use pacing and rhythm strategically
- Sometimes speed up (action moments)
- Sometimes slow down (reflection moments)
- Build toward revelation
- Create nested open loops (one closes, another opens)

**Example (Fast pacing):**
"I quit my job. I called my business partner. We launched that week. The first day: zero sales. The second day: three sales. By the end of the week: $10,000."

**Example (Slow pacing):**
"I sat down to write the email. My hands were shaking. I'd never done this before. Every word felt risky. What if people thought I was crazy? What if they laughed? I almost hit delete three times. But I kept going. Because something inside me knew this was right."

**Key:** Vary your pacing. Keeps reader engaged.

---

## POINT 10: CURIOSITY GAP CLOSURE

**Job:** Reveal what you withheld earlier

**Rules:**
- Satisfy the curiosity you created
- Make revelation feel earned
- Deliver promised information
- Reader thinks: "Ah! That makes sense!"

**Example:**

"The secret from page 47 was simple: 'The customer's transformation matters more than your product.' Everything changed when I understood that. Instead of selling software, I was selling freedom. Instead of listing features, I was painting futures. That one insight doubled my sales within 30 days."

**Key:** Make the revelation satisfying. It should feel like the payoff to the promise.

---

## POINT 11: TITLE CONNECTION

**Job:** Connect back to title

**Rules:**
- Reader reads title → waits for payoff → story delivers connection
- Connection can be direct (use title words) or indirect (allude to it)
- Must happen somewhere in story (start, middle, or end)
- When connected: reader feels satisfaction ("This all fits together")

**Example (Title: "How I Almost Lost Everything"):**

"I almost lost my house. I almost gave up on my dream. I almost let fear win. But I didn't. Instead, I discovered something more valuable than money: I discovered what I was capable of."

**Key:** Make reader feel the full arc. Title → story → connection.

---

## POINT 12: THE OMG MOMENT / REVELATION / AHA

**Job:** Give reader an excited realization or takeaway

**Rules:**
- Moment of discovery/revelation
- Something fascinating to reader
- "Oh my god, I didn't know that!"
- Often the main lesson/insight

**Examples:**

*Insight/AHA:*
- "That's when I realized: Money doesn't buy happiness, but freedom does. And freedom comes from building something you believe in."

*Revelation/Discovery:*
- "The framework I used worked for anyone, not just me. I tested it with 50 people. 48 succeeded. That's when I realized I had something worth sharing."

*Profound realization:*
- "I spent 20 years trying to be rich. But the real wealth was the time I saved to spend with family. That changed my whole perspective."

**Key:** The OMG moment should make reader think: "I needed to hear that" or "I didn't know that was possible."

## THE FRAMEWORK IN CONTEXT

### When Story Goes in Sales Copy

The 12-point framework usually appears as 2-4 paragraph section within larger copy, often as social proof.

**Template placement:**
- Headline → Problem → Solution → Benefits → [STORY SECTION HERE] → Objections → Offer → CTA

**Story section** usually includes:
- Point 1: Title (as section subheading)
- Points 2-11: Abbreviated story (hitting main beats)
- Point 12: OMG moment that connects to benefit

### When Story Is the Entire Copy

If story is the complete piece (email, long-form sales letter, testimonial), expand all 12 points fully.

Each point becomes 2-4 paragraphs instead of 1 sentence.

### Abbreviated Version (When Space Is Limited)

Hit these points minimum:
- Point 2: Hook (unusual opening)
- Points 5-7: Conflict + pivotal moment
- Point 10: Curiosity gap closure
- Point 12: OMG moment

This abbreviated version still converts; includes core elements.

---

## 12-POINT FRAMEWORK CHECKLIST

Before finalizing story, verify all 12 points:

- [ ] **Point 1:** Title is intriguing + creates curiosity
- [ ] **Point 2:** Opening hook is unusual/startling
- [ ] **Points 3-4:** Backstory establishes context; reader understands before-state
- [ ] **Points 5-6:** Conflict is clear; emotional stakes exist
- [ ] **Point 7:** Pivotal moment is relatable; everything changes
- [ ] **Point 8:** Curiosity gap created (held info)
- [ ] **Point 9:** Pacing varies (speed up/slow down); keeps reader engaged
- [ ] **Point 10:** Curiosity gap closed; promise delivered
- [ ] **Point 11:** Title connected back; arc feels complete
- [ ] **Point 12:** OMG moment makes reader think "I didn't know that"

## REAL EXAMPLE: COMPLETE 12-POINT STORY

**[POINT 1: TITLE]**
"How a Broke 23-Year-Old Made $100K in His First Year (And How You Can Too)"

**[POINT 2: HOOK]**
"I was dead broke. Completely broke. No job. $47 in the bank. Sleeping on a friend's couch. And I was about to discover something that changed everything."

**[POINTS 3-4: BACKSTORY]**
"Fresh out of college with a communications degree I couldn't use. Job interviews went nowhere. My parents asked, 'What's your plan?' I had nothing. Every morning, I'd check my phone for callbacks. Nothing. I felt like a failure. Twenty-three years old and already convinced my life was over."

**[POINTS 5-6: CONFLICT]**
"I wanted to build my own business. I dreamed about it constantly. But I had nothing: no money, no experience, no network. And if I tried and failed, I'd have even less. My friend let me crash on his couch. I couldn't afford to fail. The conflict was brutal: I wanted to leap, but I was too afraid."

**[POINT 7: PIVOTAL MOMENT]**
"One night, I'm sitting in a coffee shop (couldn't afford the coffee, but I sat there anyway). A guy at the next table left a book on his seat. I didn't ask permission. I just picked it up and read it. By page 47, something clicked. The secret wasn't complicated. It was just different from what everyone told me."

**[POINT 8: CURIOSITY GAP SETUP]**
"That discovery led to my first client. Then my second. By week three, I'd made $3,000. By month one, I'd made $15,000. But here's what's interesting: It wasn't the money that changed. It was something else entirely."

**[POINT 9: ANTICIPATION BUILDING]**
"I quit my friend's couch. Got my own apartment. My second month: $22,000. Third month: $28,000. The income was doubling. But more important was what I learned about myself. I wasn't broken. I wasn't incapable. I just needed the right framework."

**[POINT 10: CURIOSITY GAP CLOSURE]**
"The secret from that borrowed book was this: 'The customer's transformation matters more than your product.' Once I understood that, everything changed. I stopped trying to impress people with features. I started showing them the life they could have. Sales tripled."

**[POINT 11: TITLE CONNECTION]**
"By year end, I'd made $100,000. But more importantly, I went from broke, desperate, and afraid—to believing in myself. From $47 to security. From 'What's your plan?' to 'Here's my plan.' And I did it in one year."

**[POINT 12: OMG MOMENT]**
"Here's what I didn't expect: The framework that worked for me works for anyone. I've since taught it to 500+ people. 85% reach five figures. 40% reach six figures. And they all say the same thing: 'Once I understood this principle, my whole business changed.' That's the real power of the framework. It's not magic. It's just a different way of thinking about your customer."

---

## KEY INSIGHT

The 12-point framework is a scaffold, not a straitjacket. You can abbreviate, expand, or rearrange based on context. But hitting all 12 points creates the most compelling story. Master this framework, and you can write any story that converts.
