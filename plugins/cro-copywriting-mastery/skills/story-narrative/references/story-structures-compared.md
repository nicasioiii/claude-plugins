# Story Structures Compared

## QUICK SELECTION GUIDE

| Structure | Best For | Word Count | Skill Level |
|-----------|----------|------------|-------------|
| Before-After-Bridge | Hero sections, quick ads | 50-200 | Beginner |
| IRS Method | Short-form ads, emails, posts | 100-500 | Beginner |
| 60-Second Sales Hook | Elevator pitches, bios, intros | 60-150 | Beginner |
| PAISA Formula | Organic posts, ad copy | 200-800 | Intermediate |
| Confessions Template | Stage 5 sophistication resets | 500-2,000 | Intermediate |
| Freytag's Pyramid | Sales page story sections | 500-2,000 | Intermediate |

| 12-Point Framework | Full campaigns, editorial | 2,000-10,000 | Advanced |

## 1. BEFORE-AFTER-BRIDGE

The simplest story structure in copywriting. Use it when you need a story in 3 sentences or less.

### Structure
1. **Before** — Life before the product (the problem state)
2. **After** — Life after using the product (the transformed state)
3. **Bridge** — The product that connects the two states

### Template
```
[Describe the painful before-state in vivid, relatable terms]

[Describe the desirable after-state in specific, enviable terms]

[Name your product/mechanism as the bridge that makes the transformation possible]
```

### Example
```
Before: You spend 3 hours every morning manually sending follow-up emails,
wondering if any of them will ever convert.

After: Your inbox fills with replies from interested prospects while you
sleep — 12 new qualified leads every morning before your first coffee.

Bridge: That's exactly what the AutoReach Sequence does. Set it up once,
and it works for you 24/7.
```

### When to Use
- Landing page hero sections
- Email opening paragraphs
- Social media posts
- Ad copy (especially short-form)
- Quick pitch decks

## 2. IRS METHOD (Sandy Franks)

Three-part story structure for any length of content.

### Structure
- **I** — Intriguing Introduction
- **R** — Riveting Middle
- **S** — Satisfying Ending

### Template
```
[INTRIGUING INTRODUCTION]
Open with something unexpected, unusual, or in the middle of action.
Never start chronologically. Use one of the 11 opening techniques
(action scene, startling fact, memorable statement, sensory imagery, etc.)

[RIVETING MIDDLE]
Build the story with conflict, context, curiosity gaps, and zigzags.
Control pacing with sentence length variation.
Show, don't tell, using sensory language.

[SATISFYING ENDING]
Resolve the conflict. Deliver the pivotal moment.
Tie back to the introduction. Leave the reader changed.
```

### When to Use
- Short ads and social posts
- Email stories (especially emails 2, 5, and 6 in welcome sequences)
- Blog posts and content marketing
- When you need a simple framework and don't need the full 12-point system

## 3. THE 60-SECOND SALES HOOK (Kevin Rogers)

Adapted from standup comedy's joke formula. When the stakes are high and you cannot afford to fail, this is the formula.

### Structure
**Identity → Struggle → Discovery → Result → Call to Action**

### Template
```
[IDENTITY]
"Hi, I'm [Name]."

[STRUGGLE]
"I spent [time period] as [relatable negative state]."

[DISCOVERY]
"Until I discovered how [unique mechanism name] [what it does]."

[RESULT]
"And began [impressive result] for [who benefits]."

[CALL TO ACTION]
"If you'd like to [benefit], [simple next step]."
```

### Example
```
Hi, I'm Kevin Rogers.
I spent years as a dead broke standup comedian.
Until I discovered how a simple joke formula can be used as an
irresistible sales hook I call the 60-Second Sales Hook.
And began teaching marketers how to use it to skyrocket sales
and grow their businesses.
If you'd like to discover this powerful four-sentence formula
and put it to work, enter your name below.
```

### Critical Rule
**Name your mechanism in the Discovery step.** If they Google a generic term, they find competitors. If they Google your named mechanism, they find only you.

### When to Use
- Elevator pitches and introductions
- About page hero sections
- Podcast guest intros
- Lead magnet landing page copy
- Email signature bios

## 4. PAISA FORMULA (Andrew Milligan)

Organic post and ad copy framework with a built-in competitive positioning move.

### Structure
- **P** — Problem: State the problem your market faces
- **A** — Agitate: Stir the pain, make it vivid
- **I** — Invalidate: Discredit other solutions they have tried
- **S** — Solve: Present your solution
- **A** — Action: Call to action (optional; use PAIS for general posts)

### Template
```
[PROBLEM]
State the painful problem using their language.

[AGITATE]
Make the pain vivid. Show consequences of not solving it.
Use specific scenarios and emotional details.

[INVALIDATE]
Discredit what they have already tried and why it failed.
This is your competitive positioning moment.
"The reason [common solution] doesn't work is because..."

[SOLVE]
Introduce your unique solution/mechanism.
Show how it addresses the root cause that others miss.

[ACTION] (Optional)
Soft or direct CTA depending on awareness level.
```

### The Power of "I" (Invalidate)
This is the key differentiator of PAISA. Most frameworks skip this step. By invalidating what they have already tried, you:
- Position yourself apart from competitors
- Explain why past failures were not their fault
- Create openness to a new approach

### Story Types to Use with PAISA
- Client transformation stories
- New discoveries
- Personal backstory (what makes you the ideal guide)
- Personal experiences with the problem

### When to Use
- Organic social media posts
- Facebook/Instagram ad copy
- LinkedIn articles and posts
- Warm audience email content

## 5. FREYTAG'S PYRAMID (5-Stage Story Structure)

Classical narrative structure adapted for sales copy.

### Structure
1. **Exposition** — Introduction with inciting incident
2. **Rising Action** — Start and escalation of conflict
3. **Climax** — Peak of conflict / the transformation moment
4. **Falling Action** — Aftermath and consequences
5. **Resolution** — Tying together loose ends

### Template for Sales Copy
```
[EXPOSITION]
Set the scene. Introduce the character (reader or founder).
Present the inciting incident — the event that started everything.
"It all started when..."

[RISING ACTION]
The problem escalates. Solutions are tried and fail.
Tension builds. Stakes increase.
"Things went from bad to worse when..."
"Every solution I tried made it worse..."

[CLIMAX]
The breakthrough moment. The discovery.
The mechanism is revealed or the pivot happens.
"Then everything changed when I discovered..."
"That's when it hit me..."

[FALLING ACTION]
The results begin to show. Proof appears.
Others start achieving the same results.
"Within [timeframe], [specific result]..."
"And I wasn't the only one..."

[RESOLUTION]
The new reality. The transformation is complete.
Connect to the offer — this is now available to the reader.
"Today, you can access the same [mechanism] that..."
```

### When to Use
- Sales page "Your Story" sections
- VSL scripts
- Case study content
- Origin stories on about pages

## 6. THE HERO'S JOURNEY (Condensed for Copywriting)

Joseph Campbell's 12-step structure condensed for sales copy.

### Condensed 7-Step Version for Copy
1. **Ordinary World** — The reader's current painful reality
2. **Call to Adventure** — They discover a solution might exist
3. **Refusal of the Call** — Doubt and objections arise
4. **Meeting the Mentor** — They encounter you/your brand (the guide)
5. **Crossing the Threshold** — They commit to trying the new approach
6. **Tests, Allies, Enemies** — The journey of implementation (results, setbacks, proof)
7. **Return with the Elixir** — The transformation is complete; they share their success

### Key Copywriting Adaptation
The reader is always the hero. You are always the mentor/guide. Your product is the "magical weapon" that helps them succeed on their quest.

### Template
```
[ORDINARY WORLD]
"You know the feeling. Every morning you [painful daily reality]..."

[CALL TO ADVENTURE]
"What if there was a way to [desired outcome]?"

[REFUSAL]
"I know what you're thinking. You've tried [past solution].
It didn't work. Why would this be different?"

[MEETING THE MENTOR]
"That's exactly what [name/brand] thought — until they discovered
[mechanism name]."

[CROSSING THE THRESHOLD]
"Here's what happens when you take the first step..."

[TESTS AND ALLIES]
"[Client name] started with nothing. Within [timeframe],
[specific result]. Then [another client] did the same..."

[RETURN WITH THE ELIXIR]
"Now imagine yourself [vivid after-state]. That's not a fantasy.
It's what [number] people have already experienced."
```

### When to Use
- VSL scripts (15-45 minutes)
- Long-form sales pages ($500+ offers)
- Webinar presentations
- Brand story videos
- Case study compilations

## 7. THE CONFESSIONS TEMPLATE

Traffic & Funnels' most successful ad ever. Running 3-4+ years. Millions in revenue.

### Structure
1. **Controversial/Conflict Opening** — The "real estate" (first 2-3 visible lines)
2. **Pain Section** — Deep dive into market pain
3. **Breakthrough** — The shift/paradigm change
4. **Solution/Pleasure** — How the new way works and delivers
5. **Call to Action** — Direct next step

### Template
```
[CONTROVERSIAL OPENING]
"Confessions of a [role]..."
Start with something that creates voyeuristic curiosity.
Something that makes them think "Wait, what?"

[PAIN SECTION]
Go DEEP into the pain the market is experiencing.
Use their exact language. Show you understand every detail.
Make them feel seen and understood at a visceral level.

[BREAKTHROUGH]
"Then everything changed."
Introduce the paradigm shift — the new way of thinking.
Position it as a revelation, not a product pitch.

[SOLUTION/PLEASURE]
Show how the new approach works and what it delivers.
Use specific results, timeframes, and outcomes.
Keep it about the MECHANISM, not the product.

[CALL TO ACTION]
Soft or direct depending on the offer.
"If this resonates, [next step]."
```

### Why the Confessions Template Works
- Indirect approach — not immediately clear there is something for sale
- "Confessions of a [role]" framing is deeply voyeuristic
- Dives deeply into pain, creating massive resonance
- The "confessional" format builds radical trust and authenticity
- It is a Stage 5 sophistication ad that resets to Stage 1

### Adaptation Guide
- Replace [role] with the audience's role: "Confessions of a struggling agency owner" / "Confessions of a burnt-out fitness coach"
- The pain section should be the longest part (40-50% of total copy)
- The breakthrough should feel like a genuine revelation, not a sales pitch
- The CTA should match the intimacy of the confessional tone — soft, invitational

## STRUCTURE COMBINATION RULES

You can combine structures within a single piece:

1. **Hero section:** Before-After-Bridge (quick hook)
2. **Your Story section:** Hero's Journey or Freytag's Pyramid (depth)
3. **Individual emails:** IRS Method or PAISA (efficiency)
4. **Social proof sections:** 60-Second Sales Hook format for each testimonial
5. **Full campaign:** 12-Point Framework as the master structure, with sub-structures within

**Rule:** Never use two complex structures simultaneously. One master structure, then simpler sub-structures for individual sections.
