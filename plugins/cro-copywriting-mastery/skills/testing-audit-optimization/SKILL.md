# Testing, Auditing & CRO Optimization

## Core Principle: Learning IS Winning

Never guarantee a test win. Guarantee a learning. The best CRO practitioners have a portfolio of learnings, not a streak of wins. Early tests figure out what works vs. what does not work. Non-winners are just as valuable as winners when you extract the learning.

**Key rules:**
- Never guarantee results from services -- only guarantee on-time, best-ability delivery
- Never expose 100% of traffic to something untested
- Make hypotheses based on research, but know you will not know until you test
- Be honest when conversion rates drop -- learn from non-winners too

## 1. Testing Philosophy and Priority

### Test Big Differences Before Small Ones

Eugene Schwartz philosophy applied to testing: dramatic differences teach you more than minor tweaks.

**Test priority hierarchy (highest impact first):**

| Priority | What to Test | Expected Impact | Example |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|---------|
| 1 | **Offer** | Massive (2-10x) | Different offer entirely, pricing structure, guarantee |
| 2 | **Headline/Hook** | High (20-100%+) | Completely different headline angle, awareness stage targeting |
| 3 | **Page type/template** | High (20-50%) | Long-form vs. short-form, video vs. text, listicle vs. direct |
| 4 | **Body copy/argument** | Medium (10-30%) | Different proof strategy, story vs. logic, benefit order |
| 5 | **CTA copy/design** | Medium (5-20%) | Button text, color, placement, reassuring text |
| 6 | **Visual design** | Low-Medium (5-15%) | Images, layout, font, whitespace |
| 7 | **Micro-copy** | Low (1-5%) | Fine-tuning word choices, punctuation, formatting |

**Rule:** Always test from priority 1 downward. Testing button colors when your offer is wrong is like rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic.

### Fix AOV Before Fixing Conversion Rate

**Contrarian insight (Todd Brown):** 99% of the time, fix the Average Order Value (AOV) before trying to improve conversion rate.

- Doubling conversion rate from 2% to 4% is a MONSTER leap with unconverted non-buyers (the hardest group to move)
- Bringing AOV from $200 to $400 through upsells, order bumps, and slack adjusters is much more achievable
- CPA has a fixed floor ($0). AOV has infinite upside
- The higher you can afford to pay for CPA, the more secure your business

**Translation:** If conversions are decent but revenue is low, look at upsells, order bumps, and pricing before optimizing the landing page.

## 2. The 20-Point Copy Audit

Synthesized from all 8 source courses. Use this to audit any landing page, sales page, or ad.

**Scoring:** Rate each element 0-5 (0 = missing, 1 = poor, 5 = excellent). Total possible: 100.

### Foundation (Points 1-5)
| # | Element | What to Check |
|---|---------|--------------|
| 1 | **One Reader** | Is the copy written for a single, specific reader segment? Or does it try to speak to everyone? |
| 2 | **Awareness Match** | Does the page match the visitor's awareness stage? Does the hook meet them where they are? |
| 3 | **Message Match** | Does the page match the ad/email/link that brought them here? Same words, same images, same promise? |
| 4 | **One Big Idea** | Is there a single, clear big idea? Or is the page trying to communicate multiple ideas? |
| 5 | **One Offer** | Is there exactly one offer? No backup offers, no competing CTAs? |

### Hook & Above the Fold (Points 6-9)
| # | Element | What to Check |
|---|---------|--------------|
| 6 | **Headline Clarity** | Can a stranger understand the value proposition in under 5 seconds? Clarity over cleverness? |
| 7 | **7-Second Test** | Does the page answer: What is it? Why should I care? What do I do next? -- in 7 seconds? |
| 8 | **Unique Mechanism** | Is the mechanism named and positioned as different/superior? Or are you selling a commodity? |
| 9 | **Primary Promise** | Is there a big, bold, specific, measurable, envisionable primary promise? |

### Body Copy & Argument (Points 10-14)
| # | Element | What to Check |
|---|---------|--------------|
| 10 | **Benefit Depth** | Are benefits dimensionalized (3 tiers: functional > dimensionalized > emotional)? Or are they surface-level? |
| 11 | **Proof Strategy** | Is every major claim supported with proof? Reason-why for mechanism claims, testimonials for product claims? |
| 12 | **Objection Handling** | Are the top 3-5 objections addressed before the close? Or left unresolved? |
| 13 | **Story/Narrative** | Is there a compelling story element? Does the reader see themselves as the hero? |
| 14 | **Readability** | Is copy at middle-school reading level (Flesch-Kincaid < 8)? Short paragraphs? Scannable formatting? |

### Offer & Close (Points 15-18)
| # | Element | What to Check |
|---|---------|--------------|
| 15 | **Offer Presentation** | Is the offer clearly presented with value stacking? Do they know exactly what they get? |
| 16 | **Price Justification** | Is the price anchored, reframed, and trivialized? Or just stated cold? |
| 17 | **Guarantee** | Is there a clear, prominent guarantee? Does it reverse risk completely? |
| 18 | **CTA Strength** | Are CTAs clear, value-driven, in first person? Is reassuring text present? Is contrast high? |

### Technical & Congruency (Points 19-20)
| # | Element | What to Check |
|---|---------|--------------|
| 19 | **Congruency** | Does the promise match the guarantee? Does the ad match the page? No contradictions anywhere? |
| 20 | **Formatting & Friction** | No global nav on landing pages? Coupon fields hidden if unused? Forms minimal? Load time fast? |

**Interpretation:**
- 80-100: Strong page. Test micro-optimizations (priority 5-7)
- 60-79: Good foundation. Fix weakest 3-5 elements for significant lift
- 40-59: Major gaps. Rewrite needed. Focus on priorities 1-3
- Below 40: Start over. The page has fundamental strategic problems

## 3. The 7-Point Homepage Audit

Based on Ryan Deiss's framework. Score each element 1-4.

| # | Element | Score 1 (Poor) | Score 2 (Okay) | Score 3 (Good) | Score 4 (Excellent) |
|---|---------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------------|
| 1 | **Clarity** | Confusing, no clear value prop | Value prop exists but vague | Clear value prop, somewhat specific | Crystal clear, specific, compelling |
| 2 | **Relevance** | Generic, could be any company | Somewhat targeted | Speaks to a clear audience | Deeply resonant with specific avatar |
| 3 | **Trust** | No trust elements | Logo bar or basic testimonial | Multiple trust signals, named testimonials | Comprehensive trust: logos, testimonials, media, data |
| 4 | **Navigation** | Confusing, too many options | Okay but cluttered | Clean, clear main paths | Intuitive with clear primary + secondary paths |
| 5 | **CTA** | Missing or confusing | Present but weak text | Clear with decent copy | Compelling, value-driven, contrasting, with reassurance |
| 6 | **Above the Fold** | Fails 7-second test | Passes 1-2 of 3 questions | Passes all 3 questions | Passes all 3 questions AND creates desire |
| 7 | **Mobile** | Broken on mobile | Functional but awkward | Good mobile experience | Optimized mobile-first experience |

**Scoring:**
- 25-28: Excellent homepage
- 18-24: Good with room for improvement
- 11-17: Significant issues to address
- 7-10: Fundamental redesign needed

## 4. The 7-Second Test Protocol

### What It Tests
Whether your page communicates its core message within 7 seconds (the real-world attention window).

### How to Run It
1. Recruit 5-10 people who match your target audience (or close to it)
2. Explain their role: "You are [avatar description]"
3. Do NOT explain your business or product
4. Show them the page for exactly 7 seconds, then close it
5. Ask three questions:
   - "What is this page about?" (tests clarity)
   - "Is this for you?" (tests relevance)
   - "What would you do next?" (tests CTA visibility)
6. Do NOT argue with feedback. Document everything.

**Key insight:** The 7 seconds you get in a test is MORE focused attention than real visitors give. Real visitors are multitasking, distracted, driving. If they fail the test in controlled conditions, they will definitely fail in the wild.

## 5. The 5-Second Test Protocol

### What It Tests
Whether specific page sections communicate their message clearly.

### How to Run It
1. Take a screenshot of the section you want to test
2. Post to FiveSecondTest.com or UsabilityHub
3. Show for exactly 5 seconds
4. Ask: "What is this section about?" and "What stood out most?"
5. If the answer does not match your intent, the section needs work

**Best for:** Headlines, hero sections, pricing tables, CTA areas, above-the-fold layouts.

## 6. Congruency Checks

### Ad-to-LP Congruency (The LP Flow Chart)
Three gut-check questions before launching any new ad:

1. **Subject matter:** Is the landing page content consistent with what got them interested in the ad?
2. **Tone:** Is the tone consistent and familiar -- does the page feel like the same conversation?
3. **Design/Flow:** Does the visual design make sense based on what they just saw?

**Red flags:**
- Different faces on the ad vs. the page
- Different tone (casual ad, corporate page)
- Promise in the ad not addressed above the fold on the page
- The visitor feels a "jarring detour" instead of a natural next step

### Promise-to-Guarantee Congruency (Todd Brown)
The most common incongruency error:
- Page promises: "Double your leads in 30 days"
- Guarantee says: "60-day money-back guarantee"

**Problem:** If you promised 30-day results but the guarantee is 60 days, the prospect wonders: "If it takes 30 days, why do I need 60 to decide?" This creates doubt.

**Fix:** Align guarantee timeline with promise timeline, or explain the gap explicitly.

## 7. Before/After Measurement (Low-Traffic Pages)

When A/B testing is not possible (traffic too low for statistical significance):

1. Record the current conversion rate over a meaningful period (minimum 2 weeks, ideally 4)
2. Make the change
3. Record the new conversion rate over the same period length
4. Compare

**Rules:**
- This is a signal, not proof. Do not publish it as a case study
- Account for seasonality and traffic source changes
- 3% to 6% is a clear signal. 3% to 3.5% is noise
- Best used for directional decisions, not definitive conclusions

## 8. Testing Methods Overview

| Method | Best For | Traffic Needed | Confidence Level |
|--------|---------|----------------|-----------------|
| **A/B test** | Any single element change | 1,000+ visitors/variant | High (statistical) |
| **Before/After** | Low-traffic pages, major redesigns | Any | Low-Medium (directional) |
| **5-second test** | Headlines, hero sections, CTAs | 20-50 respondents | Medium (qualitative) |
| **7-second test** | Full page assessment | 5-10 respondents | Medium (qualitative) |
| **User testing** | UX issues, belief gaps, friction points | 3-5 users | Medium-High (behavioral) |
| **Heatmap/recording** | Scroll depth, click patterns, rage clicks | 500+ sessions | Medium (behavioral) |
| **Multivariate** | Multiple elements simultaneously | 10,000+ visitors | High but complex |

## 11. Common Mistakes (Synthesized Anti-Patterns)

### Foundation Mistakes
- Writing for everyone instead of One Reader
- Skipping research and jumping straight to writing
- Modeling most-aware brands when your audience is problem-aware
- Selling a commodity without a unique mechanism

### Strategy Mistakes
- Leading with features instead of dimensionalized benefits
- No clear campaign thesis or buying belief
- Trying to communicate multiple big ideas on one page
- Skipping the marketing argument and going straight to the offer

### Writing Mistakes
- Adjective-driven copy instead of verb-driven copy
- Passive voice throughout
- Faux benefits (one step removed from what the prospect actually wants)
- Reading level too high (Flesch-Kincaid > 8)
- Emoting instead of evoking ("I'm so excited" vs. triggering THEIR excitement)

### Conversion Mistakes
- No proof for major claims
- Generic testimonials not matched to specific claims
- CTA that describes work ("Submit your information") instead of value ("Get your results")
- Coupon code fields visible when no coupons are offered
- Global navigation on landing pages
- Proposing marriage on the homepage (hard sell to unaware visitors)

### Testing Mistakes
- Testing button colors before testing the offer
- Exposing 100% of traffic to untested changes
- Declaring winners too early (insufficient sample size)
- Ignoring learnings from losing tests
- Never testing at all
