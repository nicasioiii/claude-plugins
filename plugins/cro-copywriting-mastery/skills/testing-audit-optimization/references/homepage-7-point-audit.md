# The 7-Point Homepage Audit Framework (Ryan Deiss)

## What This Framework Is

The 7-Point Homepage Audit is a **rapid evaluation tool** for assessing homepage quality. It answers whether your homepage effectively:
1. **Communicates what you do** (clarity)
2. **Shows why a visitor should care** (relevance)
3. **Gives them clear next steps** (CTA)
4. **Builds trust** (credibility)
5. **Shows how it works** (function)
6. **Proves it works** (social proof)
7. **Provides navigation** (user experience)

**Scoring System:** Each element scores 1-4 points. Total = 28 points max

**Benchmarks:**
- **28 points:** Exceptional homepage
- **21-27 points:** Competent, converting well
- **14-20 points:** Needs improvement
- **7-13 points:** Unsatisfactory, significant redesign needed

---

## Audit Element #1: Top Section (Headline, Subheadline, Imagery, CTA)

### What Gets Graded
The **hero section** above the fold — everything in the first visual area when someone lands on your page.

### The 3 Critical Questions
Does the hero section answer these in 7 seconds?
1. **What is it?** (Clear value proposition)
2. **Why should I care?** (Is this for me? Benefit?)
3. **What do I do next?** (Clear primary CTA)

### Scoring Rubric

**4 Points: EXCEPTIONAL**
Immediately and clearly answers all three questions
- Visitor knows **exactly what you do** in 5 seconds
- Visitor **sees themselves** in the solution
- **Primary CTA is obvious** (above fold, high contrast)
- Copy is **specific and benefit-focused** (not vague)
- Imagery **reinforces the promise**

**Signs of 4-point hero:**
- "We help [specific avatar] achieve [specific result]"
- Strong hero image showing desired "after" state
- Clear, prominent call-to-action
- No confusion about what this page is about
- Visitor thinks "This might be for me"

**3 Points: GOOD**
Engaging copy/imagery but visitor must scroll to fully understand
- Mostly clear on value proposition but requires some inference
- Visitor sees **relevance but not immediately**
- **CTA is present and above fold** but could be more prominent
- Good design but messaging could be clearer
- Visitor thinks "This looks interesting, let me scroll"

**2 Points: WEAK**
Vague on problem/solution; no obvious CTA above fold
- Visitor **doesn't understand quickly** what you do
- Audience **unclear** (too broad)
- **CTA is below fold** or weak ("Learn More")
- Relies on design to communicate (design ≠ messaging)
- Visitor thinks "Interesting design, but what does it do?"

**1 Point: POOR**
No clarity on problem solved; audience unclear; no CTA
- **Completely unclear** what you do
- **No specific audience** (appeals to everyone = appeals to no one)
- **No CTA** or CTA is hidden
- Imagery is confusing or unrelated
- Visitor thinks "I don't know what this is" and bounces

### How to Score Your Homepage

**Use the 7-Second Test:**
1. Show homepage to someone unfamiliar with your business
2. Give them **exactly 7 seconds** to view
3. Ask: "What does this company do? Is this for you? What should you do next?"
4. Grade based on how clearly they answered those three questions

**Grade Higher If:**
- They could clearly identify the problem solved
- They could identify the target customer
- They knew the CTA before scrolling

**Grade Lower If:**
- They were confused about what you do
- They didn't see themselves as target customer
- They didn't know what action to take

### Common Issues (And Fixes)

**Issue: Beautiful design but no clarity**
- **Fix:** Add clear headline stating what you do in simple terms
- Example: Instead of cryptic tagline, try "Help for [Specific Problem]"

**Issue: Says what you are, not what it does**
- **Fix:** Focus on benefit, not feature
- Instead of: "AI-powered analytics platform"
- Try: "See exactly which customers are about to leave—and how to stop them"

**Issue: CTA is weak**
- **Fix:** Make CTA specific and benefit-focused
- Instead of: "Learn More"
- Try: "Get Your Free Audit"

**Issue: Multiple CTAs competing**
- **Fix:** Pick ONE primary CTA; others should be softer alternatives
- Primary: "Start Free Trial"
- Secondary: "Watch Demo" (for less committed visitors)

## Audit Element #2: Imagery & Visual Communication

### What Gets Graded
How well the **visual elements** (images, videos, graphics) support and reinforce the messaging.

### The Key Question
Does the imagery show the prospect the **desired "after" state** they're hoping for?

### Scoring Rubric

**4 Points: EXCEPTIONAL**
Customer as hero showing ideal "after" state; all images support messaging
- **Imagery shows the desired outcome** (not the product, but the result)
- Prospect can **visualize themselves** in the "after" state
- **All images support the messaging** (reinforce, don't distract)
- Photos show real people (not stock photos) when possible
- Before-after visual progression is clear
- **Consistency:** All images align with brand and message

**Signs of 4-point imagery:**
- Hero image shows someone succeeding with solution
- Images progress through transformation (from problem to solution)
- Real customer photos paired with testimonials
- Diagrams/visuals clarify complex concepts

**3 Points: GOOD**
Product shown in action; demonstrates ideal state but not perfectly
- **Product shown in realistic use** (better than product shots)
- **Outcome is visible** but could be clearer
- Images support message but some are generic
- Mix of stock and real photos
- Visual design is professional but could be stronger

**2 Points: WEAK**
Visually pleasing but distracts or doesn't align with message
- **Beautiful images but unrelated to message**
- Focus on **aesthetics over communication**
- Product hidden or unclear
- Stock photos feel disconnected from copy
- Images don't reinforce the promised outcome

**1 Point: POOR**
Crude/unprofessional; vague/inaccurate representation
- **Unprofessional or low-quality images**
- Images **contradict or confuse messaging**
- Product is never shown
- Imagery creates questions instead of answers
- Visitor thinks "This looks cheap/untrustworthy"

### How to Improve Image Scores

**Score 1→2 Fix:** Get professional photos (stock photos are OK, but choose carefully)

**Score 2→3 Fix:** Show product in use/action, not just beauty shots

**Score 3→4 Fix:**
- Lead with customer as hero (not product)
- Show the transformation (before → after)
- Use real customer photos
- Ensure visual consistency throughout

## Audit Element #3: Top Navigation Menu

### What Gets Graded
The **header/navigation area** at the very top of the page.

### The Key Questions
1. Is your **logo visible** (building brand recognition)?
2. Is **navigation minimal** (not cluttered)?
3. Is **primary CTA prominent** in header (top-right position)?
4. Does header **establish professionalism**?

### Scoring Rubric

**4 Points: EXCEPTIONAL**
Logo visible, minimal nav, primary CTA prominent (top-right)
- **Logo clearly visible** (brand building)
- **Navigation is minimal** (3-5 items maximum)
- **Primary CTA in top-right** (standard placement)
- CTA **stands out visually** (color, size)
- Professional appearance builds instant trust
- Header is **uncluttered and clean**

**Signs of 4-point navigation:**
- "Logo | Home | About | Contact | [Primary CTA Button]"
- Dropdown menus for additional navigation
- Clear visual hierarchy

**3 Points: GOOD**
Logo visible, CTA present, but 1+ non-essential elements cluttering
- Logo visible but could be larger
- Navigation present with 1-2 extra items
- CTA visible but could be more prominent
- Professional but slightly cluttered

**2 Points: WEAK**
Cluttered nav, no room for CTA, confusion about brand
- **Navigation is cluttered** (6+ items)
- **CTA not visible** in header
- Logo small or hard to find
- Too much competing for attention

**1 Point: POOR**
Very cluttered, confusing, no CTA or direction
- **Navigation is a mess** (10+ items)
- **No logo** or logo is hidden
- **No CTA in header** (must scroll to find action)
- Unprofessional appearance

### How to Improve Navigation Scores

**Score 1→2 Fix:** Remove non-essential navigation items (keep to essentials only)

**Score 2→3 Fix:** Add primary CTA button to header (usually top-right)

**Score 3→4 Fix:**
- Minimize navigation to 3-5 essential items
- Make CTA stand out (color, size, position)
- Ensure logo is prominently visible

## Audit Element #4: Call-to-Action (Repetition & Clarity)

### What Gets Graded
How many times your **primary CTA appears** and how clearly the visitor knows what to do next.

### The Key Question
Does the visitor know the **exact next step** at every point where they might be ready to act?

### Scoring Rubric

**4 Points: EXCEPTIONAL**
Clear primary + secondary, repeated 3+ times; visitor knows exact next step
- **Primary CTA appears 3+ times:**
  - Above fold (hero)
  - Mid-page (after proof)
  - Below fold (final chance)
- **Secondary CTA appears** (alternative action for less-committed visitors)
- **CTA is specific and compelling** (not "Submit")
- **Copy around CTA** explains why they should click now
- Visitor never has to search for next step

**3 Points: GOOD**
At least 2 clear CTAs; CTA is specific and compelling
- CTA appears at least 2x (above fold + below fold)
- CTA is specific ("Get Your Free Guide" not "Click Here")
- Copy around CTA explains benefit
- Visitor knows what happens after click

**2 Points: WEAK**
CTA present but action/next step vague
- Only 1 CTA (or CTAs are scattered/unclear)
- CTA is generic ("Submit," "Learn More")
- Visitor unsure what happens after click
- No reinforcement of why to click

**1 Point: POOR**
No direct CTA or visitor unsure what to do
- **No obvious CTA** or CTA is hidden
- Multiple conflicting CTAs (confusing choices)
- No clarity on what happens after clicking
- Visitor leaves without knowing next step

### How to Improve CTA Scores

**Score 1→2 Fix:** Add a clear primary CTA above fold

**Score 2→3 Fix:** Repeat CTA 2-3 times; make it more specific

**Score 3→4 Fix:**
- Add secondary CTA (alternative action)
- Repeat primary CTA 3+ times (top, middle, bottom)
- Ensure specificity ("Get Free Checklist" not "Start")

## Audit Element #5: Body Copy (Benefits, How It Works, Clarity)

### What Gets Graded
The **main copy of the page** — does it explain benefits, show how the product works, and connect features to customer outcomes?

### The Key Questions
1. Is it clear **HOW the product works**?
2. Is it clear **WHO benefits** from this?
3. Is it clear **WHAT the visitor does next**?

### Scoring Rubric

**4 Points: EXCEPTIONAL**
Clear HOW/WHO/WHAT; sections labeled; copy compelling
- **"How It Works"** section explains step-by-step
- **"Who This Is For"** clearly identifies target customer
- **Features connected to benefits** ("This means you..." language)
- Copy is **benefit-focused** (what it does for customer, not product specs)
- Sections are **clearly labeled** (not wandering around)
- Compelling language that speaks to desires/fears

**3 Points: GOOD**
Features explained but not clearly connected to benefits
- How it works is understandable but could be clearer
- Some customer language but could speak more to desires
- Benefits mentioned but feature-heavy
- Sections present but organization could improve

**2 Points: WEAK**
Company-centric; fails to connect with customer challenges
- **Reads like company brochure** (all about company, not customer)
- **Features listed without benefit translation**
- Doesn't speak to customer pain points
- Copy feels detached from what customer actually wants

**1 Point: POOR**
Missing or completely disjointed from promises
- **No explanation of how it works**
- **Doesn't speak to customer at all**
- Copy contradicts headline promise
- Rambling, unclear, confusing organization

### How to Improve Body Copy Scores

**Score 1→2 Fix:** Add "How It Works" section with step-by-step explanation

**Score 2→3 Fix:**
- Translate features to benefits ("This feature means you...")
- Add customer-focused language (speak to their desires)
- Organize with clear section headings

**Score 3→4 Fix:**
- Make copy more compelling (tap into emotions, desires, fears)
- Add specific examples of customer transformation
- Deepen benefit language (go beyond surface benefits)

## Audit Element #6: Trust Builders (Social Proof)

### What Gets Graded
How well the page **establishes credibility** through social proof, customer stories, credentials, and authority signals.

### The Key Questions
1. Are there **impressive client logos**?
2. Are there **specific customer testimonials**?
3. Are there **customer stories or case studies**?
4. Is there **third-party proof** (awards, certifications, media mentions)?

### Scoring Rubric

**4 Points: EXCEPTIONAL**
Impressive customer logos; testimonials & stories visible; links to case studies
- **Client logos featured prominently** (recognizable, impressive brands)
- **Specific testimonials** with names, titles, companies (not vague quotes)
- **Customer stories** showing transformation (before → after results)
- **Multiple types of proof** (logos + quotes + case studies)
- **Case study links** for deep diving
- **Refund rate or satisfaction stat** ("99% satisfaction")
- Testimonials feature **specific results** (numbers, metrics)

**3 Points: GOOD**
Logos and testimonials present but no specific customer stories
- Client logos visible
- Testimonials present with names
- But missing deeper customer stories
- Proof feels surface-level rather than deep

**2 Points: WEAK**
Logos visible but no quotes/stories
- Some logos shown
- No specific testimonials or quotes
- No customer stories
- Proof is visual only (logos)

**1 Point: POOR**
No logos, quotes, or stories
- **No social proof at all**
- Or proof is generic/vague
- No customer credibility
- No trust signals

### How to Improve Trust Builder Scores

**Score 1→2 Fix:** Add 3-5 customer logos

**Score 2→3 Fix:** Add specific customer testimonials (name + quote)

**Score 3→4 Fix:**
- Add detailed case studies (before → after → results)
- Feature diverse customers (different industries/sizes)
- Add quantified proof (satisfaction %, refund rate, etc.)
- Include video testimonials
- Add media mentions or awards

## Audit Element #7: Footer (Navigation, Legal, Resources)

### What Gets Graded
The **footer section** — does it provide essential company info, legal compliance, and helpful resource links?

### The Key Questions
1. Are all **essential links** present (nav, contact, legal)?
2. Is the **layout organized** (not cluttered)?
3. Are **additional resources** available (case studies, blog)?
4. Is **legal compliance** covered (privacy policy, terms)?

### Scoring Rubric

**4 Points: EXCEPTIONAL**
All essential info + resource directory (case studies, content)
- **Navigation links** (Home, About, Contact, etc.)
- **Company information** (address, phone, email)
- **Legal compliance** (Privacy Policy, Terms of Service)
- **Resource directory** (Case Studies, Blog, Testimonials, etc.)
- **Organized layout** (not cluttered)
- **Social media links** (LinkedIn, Twitter, etc.)

**3 Points: GOOD**
All essential info visible but cluttered layout
- All essential elements present
- But organization could improve
- No additional resources

**2 Points: WEAK**
Only essential info; no additional resources
- Legal/contact info present
- Navigation links present
- But no case studies or resource links
- Very basic footer

**1 Point: POOR**
Missing essential info or footer missing entirely
- **Missing legal pages** (Privacy Policy, Terms)
- **Missing contact information**
- **Footer missing entirely**
- Cluttered or unnavigable

### How to Improve Footer Scores

**Score 1→2 Fix:** Add Privacy Policy & Terms of Service links

**Score 2→3 Fix:** Organize footer into clear columns (Company | Resources | Legal)

**Score 3→4 Fix:**
- Add resource directory (links to case studies, blog, testimonials)
- Add social media links
- Clean up organization

## How to Use This Audit

### Step 1: Grade Your Homepage
Go through each of the 7 elements. Score honestly (1-4 points).

### Step 2: Identify Weakest Elements
Find the elements scoring 2 or below. These are your biggest opportunities.

### Step 3: Prioritize Improvements
Fix lowest-scoring elements first (biggest impact).

### Step 4: Implement Changes
Use the "How to Improve" sections for each element.

### Step 5: Re-Audit After Changes
Re-run the audit after implementing changes to measure improvement.

---

## Common Homepage Problem Patterns

### Pattern 1: Beautiful Design, Unclear Message
- **Symptoms:** Scores 4 on imagery, 2 on top section
- **Fix:** Add clear headline stating exactly what you do in simple terms

### Pattern 2: Clear Message, No Proof
- **Symptoms:** Scores 4 on top section/copy, 1 on trust builders
- **Fix:** Add customer testimonials, logos, and case studies

### Pattern 3: Scattered CTAs
- **Symptoms:** Scores 1-2 on CTA element
- **Fix:** Choose ONE primary action; repeat it 3+ times

### Pattern 4: Company-Focused Copy
- **Symptoms:** Scores 2 on body copy
- **Fix:** Rewrite to focus on customer results, not company features

### Pattern 5: Bad Navigation
- **Symptoms:** Scores 1-2 on navigation
- **Fix:** Simplify nav to 3-5 items; add prominent CTA button

---

## Testing After Audit

After improving your homepage based on audit scores, test these changes:

- **A/B test headline** (original vs. clearer version)
- **A/B test CTA placement** (different number of CTAs)
- **A/B test trust builders** (with/without customer logos and testimonials)
- **A/B test body copy** (company-focused vs. customer-focused)

Track improvement in:
- Click-through rate (% clicking CTA)
- Bounce rate (% leaving without action)
- Conversion rate (% completing desired action)
