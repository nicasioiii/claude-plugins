# 7-Point Homepage Audit Rubric

**Source:** Ryan Deiss (Craft A High-Converting Homepage v2)

Score each element 1-4. Total possible: 28.

---

## Element 1: Clarity

**What it measures:** Can a first-time visitor immediately understand what you do and the value you provide?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 - Poor | Confusing or missing value proposition. Jargon-heavy. Visitor has no idea what the company does. | "Empowering synergistic solutions for tomorrow's challenges" |
| 2 - Okay | Value prop exists but is vague or generic. Could apply to many companies. | "We help businesses grow" |
| 3 - Good | Clear value prop with some specificity. Visitor understands the general offering. | "Email marketing software that helps e-commerce stores send better campaigns" |
| 4 - Excellent | Crystal clear, specific, and compelling. Visitor immediately understands what you do, who it's for, and why it matters. | "Send emails that make your Shopify store $5K more per month -- without hiring a copywriter" |

**Fix checklist:**
- Does the headline pass the "blank is blank" kernel sentence test?
- Can a 12-year-old understand it?
- Would a stranger know what you sell in 3 seconds?

---

## Element 2: Relevance

**What it measures:** Does the homepage speak to a specific audience, or does it try to be everything to everyone?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 - Poor | Generic messaging that could apply to any company in any industry. No audience targeting. | "Solutions for modern business" |
| 2 - Okay | Somewhat targeted but broad. Industry or role mentioned but not deeply addressed. | "Marketing tools for growing companies" |
| 3 - Good | Speaks to a clear audience segment. Language resonates with their specific challenges. | "Built for SaaS founders who are tired of guessing what converts" |
| 4 - Excellent | Deeply resonant with a specific avatar. Uses their exact language. Reader says "this is for me." | "Stop losing free trial users at Day 3. Our onboarding flows keep SaaS users engaged through the critical first week." |

**Fix checklist:**
- Is a specific audience named or implied?
- Does the language match how this audience describes their problems?
- Would someone OUTSIDE the target audience know this isn't for them?

---

## Element 3: Trust

**What it measures:** Does the homepage provide evidence that the company is legitimate, credible, and trustworthy?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 - Poor | No trust elements. No logos, testimonials, data, or credentials. Feels like it could be a scam. | Bare page with no social proof whatsoever |
| 2 - Okay | Basic trust: one or two elements (a logo bar or a single generic testimonial). | "Trusted by companies worldwide" (no specifics) |
| 3 - Good | Multiple trust signals. Named testimonials with results. Media logos or partner badges. | Logo bar with recognizable companies + 2 specific testimonials |
| 4 - Excellent | Comprehensive trust stack. Specific testimonials, media mentions, user numbers, case study snippets, integration partners. | "Trusted by 12,847 SaaS teams" + named testimonials + media logos + case study link |

**Fix checklist:**
- Is there a trust bar (logo bar) above the fold or immediately below?
- Are testimonials specific (name, company, result) or generic?
- Is there a "featured in" media section if applicable?
- Are there user numbers or data points?

---

## Element 4: Navigation

**What it measures:** Can visitors easily find what they need? Is the navigation intuitive without being overwhelming?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 - Poor | Confusing navigation. Too many options. No clear primary path. Dropdown menus with 20+ items. | Mega menu with every product, feature, and resource in one dropdown |
| 2 - Okay | Navigation exists but is cluttered. Primary action competes with secondary links. | 8+ nav items with no visual hierarchy |
| 3 - Good | Clean navigation. Clear main paths. Primary CTA is visible. | 4-5 nav items + prominent CTA button |
| 4 - Excellent | Intuitive navigation with clear primary and secondary paths. Logo left, minimal links, CTA button far right. | Logo + 3 nav links + "Get Started" button (contrasting color) |

**Fix checklist:**
- Is the primary CTA in the top navigation?
- Are there fewer than 6 main navigation items?
- Does the CTA button contrast visually with other nav elements?
- Is the logo clickable and links to homepage?

---

## Element 5: Call to Action

**What it measures:** Is there a clear, compelling CTA that tells visitors what to do next?

| Score | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| 1 - Poor | No CTA or CTA is "Learn more" or "Submit." Invisible or confusing. | Text link saying "click here for more information" |
| 2 - Okay | CTA present but generic text, poor contrast, or unclear about what happens next. | Gray button saying "Sign up" |
| 3 - Good | Clear CTA with decent copy and visual contrast. Communicates value. | Green button: "Start your free trial" |
| 4 - Excellent | Compelling, value-driven CTA with high contrast, reassuring text, and clear next step. | Contrasting button: "Get my free growth plan" + "No credit card required" below |

**Fix checklist:**
- Is the CTA above the fold?
- Does the button text describe the VALUE, not just the action?
- Is there reassuring text below the button?
- Does the button contrast sharply with the page background?
- Is there a secondary CTA (ghost button) for lower-commitment visitors?

---

## Element 6: Above the Fold

**What it measures:** Does the above-the-fold area pass the 7-Second Test?

The 7-Second Test requires answering three questions:
1. **What is it?** (What do you do?)
2. **Why should I care?** (What's the benefit for me?)
3. **What now?** (What action should I take?)

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 - Poor | Fails all three questions. Auto-playing video or carousel. No clear message. |
| 2 - Okay | Answers 1-2 of the three questions. Missing a clear CTA or benefit. |
| 3 - Good | Answers all three questions clearly. Visitor understands what to do. |
| 4 - Excellent | Answers all three questions AND creates desire to scroll or act. Emotionally compelling AND clear. |

**The 84% Rule:** Above-the-fold content gets approximately 84% of all attention. 16% of visitors will never scroll. Therefore, 84% of your time, budget, and effort should be spent on the above-the-fold message.

**Fix checklist:**
- Run the actual 7-Second Test with 5 people
- Is there a single, clear headline (not a rotating carousel)?
- Is the hero image reinforcing the value prop, not distracting from it?
- Is there a visible CTA above the fold?

---

## Element 7: Mobile Experience

**What it measures:** Does the homepage work well on mobile devices?

| Score | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| 1 - Poor | Broken layout, tiny text, buttons too small to tap, horizontal scrolling required. |
| 2 - Okay | Functional but awkward. Content is there but not optimized for mobile interaction. |
| 3 - Good | Good mobile experience. Content reflows cleanly. CTAs are tappable. |
| 4 - Excellent | Mobile-first design. Fast loading. Touch-optimized. CTA buttons are prominent and easy to tap. |

**Fix checklist:**
- Test on actual mobile devices (iPhone and Android), not just browser emulators
- Are buttons at least 44x44 pixels?
- Does the page load in under 3 seconds on mobile?
- Is the headline readable without zooming?
- Is the CTA visible without scrolling on mobile?

---

## Score Interpretation

| Total Score (out of 28) | Assessment | Action |
|------------------------|-----------|--------|
| 25-28 | Excellent | Minor optimizations. A/B test specific elements. |
| 18-24 | Good | Fix the 2-3 lowest-scoring elements for meaningful improvement. |
| 11-17 | Significant issues | Multiple elements need attention. Prioritize Clarity and Above the Fold first. |
| 7-10 | Fundamental redesign | The homepage has structural problems. Start with the 7-Second Test and rebuild from there. |

---

## Recommended Fix Priority

1. **Clarity (Element 1)** -- If people don't understand what you do, nothing else matters.
2. **Above the Fold (Element 6)** -- 84% of attention lives here. Fix it first.
3. **CTA (Element 5)** -- Without a clear action, even interested visitors won't convert.
4. **Relevance (Element 2)** -- Speak to a specific audience or lose everyone.
5. **Trust (Element 3)** -- Add proof elements progressively.
6. **Navigation (Element 4)** -- Simplify.
7. **Mobile (Element 7)** -- Ensure nothing is broken. Optimize touch targets.

---

## COMMON HOMEPAGE FAILURES (Patterns from Audits)

### Failure 1: Modeling Most-Aware Brands
Businesses copy Apple, BMW, or Slack's current homepage without realizing those brands speak to most-aware audiences. If your audience is problem-aware or solution-aware, modeling a most-aware homepage is exactly wrong. Default to problem-aware messaging when in doubt.

### Failure 2: The Clever Headline
Headline uses wordplay, metaphor, or abstraction instead of clearly stating what the business does. Example: "When we experience true breakthrough, it changes everything" -- fails the 7-Second Test entirely. The clear version almost always outperforms.

### Failure 3: Auto-Playing Video or Carousel in the Hero
Rotating hero banners are almost universally bad. They create decision paralysis, slow load times, and distract from the primary CTA. A single, clear headline with a static hero image consistently outperforms.

### Failure 4: No Secondary CTA Path
Not every homepage visitor is ready for the primary action. Without a secondary CTA (a ghost button, a "learn more" link, a content path), visitors who are not ready to commit have no next step and leave.

### Failure 5: Trust Bar with Unknown Logos
If the logos in your trust bar are not recognizable to your specific audience, they add no trust. Either use well-known brands or replace with specific data points ("12,847 teams" or "$2.3M generated for clients").

## SCORING EXAMPLE WALKTHROUGH

**Hypothetical SaaS Homepage Audit:**

| Element | Score | Notes |
|---------|-------|-------|
| Clarity | 2 | Headline says "The platform for modern teams" -- vague, could be any SaaS |
| Relevance | 2 | No specific audience called out |
| Trust | 3 | Has logo bar + 2 testimonials with names |
| Navigation | 4 | Clean, 4 links + CTA button |
| CTA | 2 | Button says "Get Started" -- no value, no reassurance below |
| Above the Fold | 2 | Answers "what now" but not "what is it" or "why should I care" |
| Mobile | 3 | Functional, buttons tappable, but headline too long for small screens |
| **Total** | **18/28** | **Assessment: Good but clarity and CTA need urgent attention** |

**Priority fixes:** (1) Rewrite headline with kernel sentence structure: "[Product] is [category] that [specific benefit]." (2) Change CTA to value-driven: "See how [Product] saves you [X hours/week]" with "Free -- no credit card required" below.
