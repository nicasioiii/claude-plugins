# Discovery Questionnaire & Checklist

Use this as a template for client interviews and discovery calls. Adapt based on project type.

---

## Pre-Call Preparation

### Materials to Request Before the Call

- Existing brand guidelines (if any)
- Current website or competitor examples
- Content (copy deck)
- Any previous design work or inspirations
- Technical requirements or constraints
- Budget and timeline documentation

### Goal of This Call

Understand: Who is this for? What problem are we solving? Why does this matter?

---

## Company Identity (10-15 minutes)

### Name & Origin

1. **Customer-facing name:** What's the official company/product name?
2. **Origin story:** How did this company start? What's the meaning behind the name?
3. **Company age:** Founded when? Market presence?

### Vision & Mission

4. **Vision statement:** Where do you want to be in 5 years?
5. **Mission statement:** Why do you exist? What value do you provide?
6. **One-sentence description:** Describe your business in one clear sentence for someone who's never heard of you
7. **Tagline:** Do you have a company tagline? (If not, what would it be?)

### Core Values

8. **Five core values:** What does your company stand for? (Examples: Transparency, Innovation, Community, Sustainability, Trust)

---

## Brand Personality (10-15 minutes)

### Descriptive Characteristics

9. **Brand descriptors (5 words):** How would you describe your brand?
   - Professional? Playful? Bold? Trustworthy? Creative?
   - Provide examples: "We're like Airbnb (adventurous) meets Apple (premium)"

10. **Brand voice:** How do you speak to customers?
    - Formal or casual?
    - Technical or conversational?
    - Example copy from your brand: "Here's how we'd describe our product"

### Emotions & Positioning

11. **Customer emotions (5):** What should customers feel when using your product?
    - Examples: Empowered, inspired, supported, confident, delighted

12. **What you're NOT:** Define the opposite. "We are NOT:"
    - Cheap? Exclusive? Corporate? Confusing?
    - This clarifies boundaries better than describing what you ARE

13. **Celebrity equivalent:** If your brand were a famous person, who would it be?
    - Example: "We're like Jason Momoa—adventurous, authentic, caring about environment"
    - This reveals personality instantly

---

## Target Audience (10-15 minutes)

### Demographics

14. **Age range:** Who is the primary user? (18-25? 35-50? Spread?)
15. **Income level:** What's their household income?
16. **Occupation:** What do they do for work?
17. **Location:** Geographic region? Urban/rural?
18. **Education level:** High school, college, graduate degree?

### Psychographics

19. **Values:** What does this audience care about?
    - Money, family, health, environment, adventure, security?

20. **Lifestyle:** How do they spend their time?
    - Fitness enthusiasts? Foodies? Travelers? Parents?

21. **Shopping habits:** How do they buy?
    - Online only? Research extensively first? Impulse buyers?
    - Influenced by reviews, peer recommendations, or social proof?

22. **Where they spend time (online):**
    - Instagram? LinkedIn? TikTok? Specific communities?
    - What websites do they visit?

23. **Where they spend time (offline):**
    - Gyms? Coffee shops? Conferences? Travel?
    - What stores do they shop at?

### Pain Points

24. **What problems does your audience have?**
    - What keeps them up at night?
    - What frustrates them about current solutions?
    - What are they searching for?

---

## Competitive Landscape (10 minutes)

### Direct Competitors

25. **Who are your direct competitors?**
    - List 3-5 companies doing the same thing

26. **What do you do better?**
    - Faster? Cheaper? Better quality? Better support?
    - More ethical? More innovative?

27. **What do they do better?**
    - Be honest. Where are you behind?

### Indirect Competitors

28. **What are alternative solutions?**
    - People solving the same problem differently
    - Example: Uber vs. Taxis vs. Rental cars (all solve "transportation")

---

## Project Goals (10-15 minutes)

### Primary Goals

29. **What's the #1 goal of this website/product?**
    - Increase sales? Build community? Educate? Support existing customers?

30. **How will you measure success?**
    - More sign-ups? Longer time on site? More support tickets? Better retention?
    - What metric matters most?

### Secondary Goals

31. **Ranked list of 3-5 goals:**
    1. (Most important)
    2. (Important)
    3. (Nice to have)
    4. (Nice to have)
    5. (Nice to have)

---

## Technical & Business Requirements (10 minutes)

### Timeline & Budget

32. **Timeline:** When do you need this launched?
    - 1 month? 3 months? 6 months?

33. **Budget:** What's your investment?
    - Don't be vague: "What range are we talking? $5k-10k? $20k-50k?"

### Technical Constraints

34. **Existing systems:** Do you have to work with existing tech?
    - Shopify? WordPress? Custom backend? Legacy system?

35. **Special requirements:**
    - Payment processing? Membership system? Multi-language support?
    - Accessibility requirements (legal mandate or best practice)?
    - Security/compliance needs (HIPAA, GDPR)?

---

## Visual Direction (10-15 minutes)

### Existing Brand

36. **Do you have brand guidelines?** (Request if so)

37. **Existing colors:** What's your current color palette?
    - Are you open to evolving it?
    - Any colors that are off-limits?

38. **Existing typography:** What fonts do you currently use?

39. **Logo:** Do you have one? Open to redesign or must keep?

### Visual Inspiration

40. **Reference websites:** What sites do you admire?
    - Not for copying, but for direction
    - Collect 3-5 websites you like
    - For each: "What do you like about this?"

41. **Reference imagery style:** How should the site look?
    - Photography? Illustration? 3D elements? Minimal/flat?
    - Realistic photos or stylized/playful?
    - Photography of real people? Lifestyle? Product-focused?

42. **Brand archetype:** Which of these resonates?
    - The Hero (bold, inspiring, overcoming challenges)
    - The Sage (wisdom, analysis, truth-seeking)
    - The Lover (relationships, connection, passion)
    - The Caregiver (supporting, helping, nurturing)
    - The Everyman (relatable, friendly, approachable)
    - The Explorer (adventure, freedom, discovery)
    - The Innocent (hope, simplicity, optimism)
    - The Sage (wisdom, analysis, truth-seeking)
    - The Magician (transformation, innovation, mystery)
    - The Creator (self-expression, innovation, authenticity)
    - The Ruler (order, power, control)
    - The Jester (fun, humor, entertainment)

43. **Visual mood:** What's the feeling?
    - Luxury? Playful? Trustworthy? Modern? Established? Disruptive?

---

## Content & Messaging (5 minutes)

### Copy Deck

44. **Do you have copy written?** (Request full content deck)
    - All page content, button labels, error messages

45. **Key message:** What's the most important thing people should know?

46. **Elevator pitch:** 30-second summary of what you do

---

## Project Scope

### Deliverables

47. **What are you delivering?**
    - Figma designs only?
    - Responsive design (mobile, tablet, desktop)?
    - Prototype in Figma?
    - Built website (Webflow, custom, etc.)?
    - How many pages?

### Timeline for Each Phase

48. **When do you need:**
    - Discovery/research complete? (Week X)
    - First designs (mood boards, concepts)? (Week X)
    - Revised designs/feedback? (Week X)
    - Final designs? (Week X)
    - Built/launched? (Week X)

## Follow-Up Actions

### Document After the Call

- [ ] Send summary email confirming all answers
- [ ] Request any materials not provided during call
- [ ] Share timeline expectations for next steps
- [ ] Schedule next review/feedback session
- [ ] Get logo files, existing guidelines, content

### Create These Documents

- [ ] Problem statement (1 sentence)
- [ ] Complexity assessment (high/medium/low)
- [ ] Sitemap (if high/medium complexity)
- [ ] User flows (if high/medium complexity)
- [ ] Brand brief (summary of brand personality)
- [ ] Project scope document (what's included/excluded)

## Red Flags During Discovery

Watch for these signals that indicate potential project issues:

1. **Unclear problem:** Client can't articulate what they're solving
   - **Solution:** Keep asking "why" until you understand the real problem

2. **Too many stakeholders with different visions:** No alignment on goals
   - **Solution:** Schedule alignment meeting; document final decision

3. **Unrealistic timeline + budget:** Want a $200k project for $10k and 2 weeks
   - **Solution:** Adjust scope or reset expectations

4. **No success metrics:** "Just make it look good" (subjective)
   - **Solution:** Define measurable goals before designing

5. **Constantly changing requirements:** Scope creeping mid-discovery
   - **Solution:** Document everything; get written approval on scope

6. **No access to target audience:** Designing without user input
   - **Solution:** Request user interviews or research

## Time Investment Summary

- **Discovery call:** 1-1.5 hours
- **Document review & research:** 2-3 hours
- **Competitive analysis:** 2-4 hours
- **Create sitemap/flows:** 2-3 hours
- **Write brief & scope:** 1-2 hours

**Total discovery phase:** 8-14 hours

This investment prevents 20-40 hours of rework during design phase. Worth it.
