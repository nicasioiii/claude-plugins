# Project Scoping & Process Selection

## DESIGN PROCESS FRAMEWORKS (FULL DETAIL)

### MDS 8-Step Process

1. **Understand the problem** -- top priority before designing anything
2. **Determine complexity** -- this dictates your entire process
3. **Use lists, content maps, flows** to guide the project
4. **Decide to wireframe or not** based on complexity
5. **Set design direction** -- creative style, look and feel
6. **Present early and often** to get feedback
7. **Explore more styles, iterate, nail down** the system
8. **Create the system and expand it**

### Designership 10-Step Process

1. Empathize -- understand user needs
2. Define -- problem statement
3. Ideate -- sitemaps, user flows, brainstorming
4. Wireframe -- low/medium/high fidelity based on needs
5. Prototype -- interactive mockups
6. Test -- usability testing
7. Iterate -- refine based on feedback
8. Visual design -- apply visual foundations
9. Development handoff -- specs, assets, documentation
10. Launch and maintain

### Flux Starter Process

1. Read the brief -- extract: technical limitations, format, brand guidelines, content, budget, audience, deadlines, review process, goal
2. Make notes -- underline keywords, identify mood
3. Sketch first -- rough, small, fast on paper
4. Evaluate sketches against 7 layout principles (hierarchy, focal point, grouping, alignment, balance, scale, white space)
5. Build 2-3 options in software quickly, refine strongest
6. Check in preview -- scroll through full design for cinematic experience

## COMPLEXITY ASSESSMENT MATRIX

### Questions to Determine Complexity

| Question | High | Medium | Low |
|----------|------|--------|-----|
| How many unique page types? | 10+ | 4-9 | 1-3 |
| Novel IA or established patterns? | Novel | Mixed | Established |
| User roles? | Multiple | 2-3 | Single |
| Data-driven features? | Yes, complex | Some | None/minimal |
| Content volume? | High, diverse | Moderate | Minimal |
| Integration requirements? | Multiple APIs | 1-2 | None |
| Stakeholder count? | 5+ | 2-4 | 1 |

**Scoring:** Mostly High = full process. Mostly Medium = standard process with wireframes. Mostly Low = jump to design.

## WIREFRAME FIDELITY LEVELS [Designership]

### Low Fidelity
- Boxes, lines, placeholder text
- Focus on layout and content hierarchy only
- No visual design decisions
- Use for: initial exploration, stakeholder alignment on structure

### Medium Fidelity
- Approximate sizing, real content outlines
- Typography hierarchy suggested (but not final)
- Use for: most projects at medium complexity

### High Fidelity
- Near-final layout with real content
- Typography, spacing, and component details
- Use for: complex projects before visual design phase

When starting any project, capture these nine elements:

```
PROJECT BRIEF
─────────────
1. Technical constraints: [platforms, tech stack, CMS]
2. Format: [responsive targets, device priority]
3. Brand guidelines: [existing assets, colors, fonts, voice]
4. Content: [what exists, what needs creation]
5. Budget: [range, payment structure preference]
6. Target audience: [demographics, behaviors, needs]
7. Deadlines: [hard dates, launch windows, milestones]
8. Review process: [who approves, rounds, format]
9. Success metric: [business outcome, not deliverable]
```

## DISCOVERY CALL SYSTEM [Flux / Design Career Kickstarter]

### Pre-Call Preparation
- Review client's current website/brand -- note areas for improvement
- Research their industry and competitors
- Prepare questions tailored to their situation
- Send reminders: 24 hours and 1 hour before call

### Discovery Call Structure (30 min scheduled, 1 hour blocked)

| Phase | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Rapport | 2-3 min | Build connection, set agenda |
| Business discovery | 5-8 min | How they started, what they sell, target audience |
| Project scoping | 5-8 min | Current site problems, goals, timeline, budget |
| Process overview | 3-5 min | Your phases, client responsibilities, deliverables |
| Budget alignment | 3-5 min | Give range, check alignment |
| Next steps | 2-3 min | When they hear from you, what format |

### Key Discovery Questions
- "What problems are you experiencing with your current website?" -- keep asking WHY until you reach the root cause
- "What made you drawn to what I do?" -- reveals style preferences and motivations
- "Did you have a launch date in mind?" -- surfaces hidden deadlines

### Budget Discussion Approach
Give a range, not a fixed number:
> "I will give you a specific quote in the proposal, but based on what we discussed, this sits around the $3-5K mark. Does that align with your budget?"

This meets the client halfway and takes pressure off (finalize exact number later in proposal).

## PROPOSAL SYSTEM [Flux / Design Career Kickstarter]

### Timing
Send within 24 hours of discovery call. Client may be talking to other designers -- first proposal in inbox has advantage.

### Proposal Structure
1. **Introduction** -- personalize, reference specific things from the call
2. **Project goals** -- use CLIENT'S vocabulary from the call (makes them feel heard; serves as anchor when scope creep occurs)
3. **Deliverables** -- specific list of what they receive
4. **Process/phases** (3-6 phases) -- what happens at each, what client receives, their responsibilities, approximate timeline
5. **Relevant case studies** -- similar industry or similar problem; include testimonial if available
6. **Fees and payment terms** -- clear, no ambiguity
7. **FAQ section** -- pre-answer lingering questions
8. **Next steps** -- crystal clear instructions on how to approve

### Payment Structure Options
- **50/50:** 50% upfront, 50% on completion (simplest)
- **Monthly retainer:** Client pays monthly regardless of project phase
- **Milestone-based:** Client pays when predefined milestones are reached

## CLIENT ONBOARDING [Flux / Design Career Kickstarter]

### Onboarding Sequence
Proposal approved → Contract signed → Deposit invoice paid → Welcome email

### Welcome Email Should Include
1. Confirm project is officially underway
2. Outline next steps -- what they need to do (fill a form, book strategy call)
3. Set expectations for communication cadence
4. Reassure client they made the right decision

[CONTRARIAN] Send contract separately from proposal (not combined), because: the contract is a different kind of document, and if the proposal needs changes, the contract may need changes too -- creates complex back-and-forth.

Include a proposal validity clause (e.g., "This proposal is valid for 14 days") to prevent clients returning months later expecting same terms.
