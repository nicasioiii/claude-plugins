# Discovery Calls & Proposals

## DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT [Flux/Career]

### Opening (2 minutes)

"Thanks for jumping on the call. The purpose is for us to get to know each other and for me to understand your business and design needs to see if we're a good fit. How about you start by telling me about you and your business?"

### Business Discovery (5-8 minutes)

Listen to their business journey. Ask:
- "What are your short and long-term goals?"
- "What is currently in the way of reaching those goals?"
- "What are you struggling with?"

**Key:** Listen more than you talk. Note their exact vocabulary -- you will use it in the proposal.

### Project Scoping (5-8 minutes)

- "From your inquiry, I understand you are looking to [X]. Tell me more about what you would like."
- Dive into specifics: How many pages? Blog? E-commerce? How many products? Key functionalities?
- Share your own thoughts and suggestions from pre-call research
- Show you are committed to their success, not just selling a service

### Your Process Overview (3-5 minutes)

Present 3-6 phases briefly. Highlight what matters most to THIS client.

Example phases:
1. Research/discovery call
2. Wireframes and mockups
3. Development/build
4. Review and revisions
5. Launch and training

Mention client responsibilities at each phase.

### Budget Conversation

Transition: "Did you have a launch date in mind?"

Then: "What budget did you have in mind for this project?"

If they want to hear from you first:
"I will give you a specific quote in the proposal, but based on what we discussed, this sits around the $X-Y mark. Does that align with your budget?"

This meets the client halfway and takes pressure off you.

### Wrap-Up

- Ask if they have questions
- Express excitement about potentially working together
- Set expectations: when they will hear from you, what format (proposal), what they should do with it
- **Send recap email immediately after call** repeating next steps in writing

## PROPOSAL STRUCTURE (FULL DETAIL) [Flux]

### 1. Personal Introduction
- Reference specific things from the call
- Express genuine excitement about their project
- Show you were listening and understand their situation

### 2. Project Goals
- Use the CLIENT'S vocabulary from the call
- Frame goals as business outcomes, not deliverables
- This section becomes your scope creep anchor: "Remember, this is what we are working toward"

### 3. Deliverables (Be Precise)
Bad: "Website design and development"
Good: "5-page website including:
- Home (hero, services overview, testimonials, CTA)
- About (team, story, values)
- Services (3 service detail sections)
- Blog (CMS-powered with category filtering)
- Contact (form, map, business details)
- Mobile-responsive across all pages
- 2 rounds of design revisions
- Content migration from existing site"

### 4. Process/Phases (3-6 phases)
For each phase include:
- What happens
- What the client receives
- Their responsibilities
- Approximate timeline
- Any milestones or approval gates

### 5. Case Studies
- Choose similar industry or similar problem
- Include testimonial if available
- Best positions: after deliverables, after process, or after fees

### 6. Fees
- Total project estimate
- Broken into payment milestones
- Do NOT price individual deliverables

### 7. FAQ Section
Pre-answer lingering questions:
- What is my client still wondering about?
- What might stop them from signing?
- What came up on the call that was not fully covered?

### 8. Next Steps
Crystal clear instructions:
- How to approve the proposal
- What to sign and where
- What happens after approval
- When they hear from you next

## PAYMENT PLAN OPTIONS [Flux]

### Monthly Payment (Recommended)
- Client pays monthly regardless of project phase
- Advantage: if project drags on, you still get paid on time
- Easier cash flow planning
- Example: $6,000 project = $2,000/month for 3 months

### Milestone Payment
- Client pays when predefined milestones are reached
- Risk: if project stalls before milestone, payment is delayed
- Example: 40% deposit, 30% after design approval, 30% at launch

## BUDGET RANGE BENCHMARKS

Research method for setting your rates:
1. Search Upwork for similar project types -- note bid ranges
2. Search Fiverr top-rated sellers for similar services
3. Use as benchmarks, not ceilings
4. Factor in: your experience, portfolio strength, market positioning

**Pricing mindset:** Start competitive to build reputation and portfolio, then increase gradually with each successful project. Flexibility is valuable during early stages.

## FOLLOW-UP CADENCE

If client has not responded after sending proposal:
- **48 hours:** Friendly follow-up email
- **5-7 days:** Second follow-up with added value
- **14 days:** Final check-in, reference proposal validity clause
- Include a validity clause in proposal (e.g., "valid for 14 days") to prevent clients returning months later at old prices
