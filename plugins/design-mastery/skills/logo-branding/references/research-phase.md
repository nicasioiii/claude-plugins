# Research Phase Deep Dive

## Business Discovery Questions

### Positioning & Value

- What problem does this business solve?
- Who is the target audience? (Demographics + psychographics)
- What makes them different from competitors?
- What is their core promise or value proposition?

### Brand Personality

- If this brand were a person, who would they be?
- Descriptors: Bold/Subtle? Playful/Serious? Modern/Classic? Aggressive/Nurturing?
- What emotional response should the logo trigger?
- Who admires/uses this brand?

### Industry & Context

- What industry are they in?
- What do competitors' logos look like?
- How do they want to position against competitors?
- Is this for B2B, B2C, or hybrid?

### Application Scope

- Where will this logo appear? (Business cards, websites, billboards, product packaging, app icons?)
- Does it need to work in motion? (Animated logo)
- What colors dominate their brand? (Any color restrictions?)

## Competitive Analysis Framework

Audit 5-8 direct and indirect competitors. For each, note:
- Logo type (wordmark, icon, combination, abstract)
- Primary color palette
- Personality (bold/subtle, playful/serious)
- Strength (what works?)
- Weakness (what could be better?)

**Key insight:** Your logo should be visually different from all major competitors.

## Target Audience Definition

Don't just say "25-40 year old women." Go deeper:

### Psychographics

- What values matter to them?
- What aspirations do they have?
- What frustrates them?
- What brands do they admire? (Other industries)
- Online/offline behavior?

**Design implications:** A logo for premium/luxury audiences looks different than budget-friendly. Educational vs. entertaining. Modern vs. timeless.
