# Mood Board & Brand Vibe

Establish visual direction before opening design software. This skill covers the complete workflow from raw brand idea to formalized visual direction using both manual inspiration methods and AI-powered branding tools.

## CORE PRINCIPLE: DIRECTION BEFORE DESIGN

Every instructor agrees: research and direction come before pixels. Written direction often produces better results than diving into design software.

- [Gareis] "Consider the mood I want to create before opening design software"
- [Lane] "Start with the FEELING you want to create -- everything flows from emotional direction"
- [MDS] "Set design direction" is Step 5 of 8 -- after understanding the problem and determining complexity

**Consensus:** Written direction and analysis BEFORE visual execution produces vastly better results.

## THE 3-STEP AI BRANDING SYSTEM [Lane]

An AI-powered system that transforms rough ideas into consistent, high-impact visual concepts.

### Step 1: Brand Vibe Report

Start with the FEELING you want to create. Everything flows from emotional direction.

**Input options:**
- Type in basic brand details (name, niche, audience, what you sell)
- Paste website content or competitor URLs
- Upload screenshots of brands you admire
- Describe the founder story and brand energy

**Output:** Structured report covering:
- Brand name and niche
- Sales messaging direction
- Ideal client avatar
- Founder story integration
- Brand energy and feeling
- Vibe strategy summary

**Critical rule:** If you skip the vibe report and jump straight to image generation, the AI has to do too much thinking at once -- results suffer dramatically.

**Iterate in written form first.** Much faster to edit text than regenerate images.

[CONTRARIAN] Written instructions often produce BETTER results than image references -- AI tends to just recreate images you give it, whereas written guidelines let AI apply its own creative interpretation. [Lane]

### Step 2: Mood Board Image Generation

Paste the brand vibe report into an AI image generator (ChatGPT recommended).

**Key principles:**
- The more information you provide, the more narrow/precise (less creative) the results
- For maximum creativity, give less detail
- Create 4-6 variations by running different chats simultaneously
- Compare results side by side to identify preferred direction
- Iterate feedback: "more blue, less yellow, more expensive looking"
- If AI goes off the rails, start new chat rather than trying to fix
- Better to refine the brand vibe report text and regenerate than iterate at the image level
- Optimal mood board image aspect ratio: **2:3**

### Step 3: Visual Brand Guidelines

Upload mood board image to a visual brand guidelines tool. AI analyzes and produces:
- Specific hex codes for colors
- Typography direction (font styles, not specific fonts)
- Imagery style guidance
- Overall design system description

**Why written guidelines matter:** AI is better at reading/interpreting text instructions than analyzing images. Written guidelines act as reusable instructions for any future content creation.

**Final setup:** Upload brand vibe report + mood board image + visual guidelines into a ChatGPT Project for persistent brand memory.

## AI IMAGE PROMPT ENGINEERING [Lane]

### Seven Key Principles

1. **Explain what you are trying to do** -- tell AI the task in plain words
2. **Give context** -- prepare AI for what is coming (what images you will upload, what output you want)
3. **Set non-negotiables** -- crystal clear rules: "do not alter the design, no stretching, recoloring, or distortion"
4. **Guide how it should think** -- use verbs: "analyze the design," "choose suitable products"
5. **Describe possible outputs** -- examples of what good looks like
6. **Tie it back to brand style** -- "reflect the brand's mood in background, props, textures, and lighting"
7. **Build in variation** -- "produce several images with different framings; keep tone unified, identity intact"

### Cross-Platform Testing

| Platform | Best For | Notes |
|----------|---------|-------|
| ChatGPT | Mood board images | Most consistent quality |
| Google AI Studio | Some use cases | Square output by default; decent for specific needs |
| Google Gemini | Product mockups | Stricter content policies; results can look more dated |

## MANUAL INSPIRATION WORKFLOW [Gareis]

### Sources

- **Instagram:** Save feature to organize by category (typography, color, layout)
- **Physical magazines:** Eye Magazine, Vogue, Spike -- print provides tactile experience; take screenshots, upload, annotate
- **Books:** Grid Systems (Muller-Brockmann), Thinking With Type (Lupton), Interaction of Color (Albers), Designing Brand Identity (Wheeler)
- **Websites:** SAVE (wide range, Pinterest-like), Fonts In Use (30,000+ analyzed font combinations), Irena (photography and art direction)

### The Process

1. Always gather targeted inspiration BEFORE opening design software
2. Spend even just 1 hour of focused search -- saves days of directionless execution
3. Create dedicated artboards per project for different aspects (typography, color, layout)
4. Do not save entire websites -- screenshot specific sections that stand out
5. Write notes on WHY you are saving something
6. Analyze references systematically across: grid structure, typography, white spacing, colors, pacing
7. [CONTRARIAN] Sometimes starting with blank canvas and NO inspiration is valuable -- palette cleanser for creativity [Gareis]

### Reference Selection Criteria

Evaluate on four factors:
1. Composition structure
2. Mood alignment
3. Practical usability
4. Originality

Reject about 80% of initial selections after evaluation. For client projects, present 12-50 reference images, each chosen for specific attributes.

## THE ONE-THING RULE [MDS]

Only lift ONE specific thing (typography, color, OR layout) from an inspiration source -- never copy multiple elements from the same source.

- Do not lift anything synonymous with an existing brand (e.g., Stripe's angle gradient)
- **Process:** Make a list of things you like about a design, then close it -- do not look at it again
- Copy work (recreating pixel-by-pixel) is valuable for LEARNING -- it exposes decisions you would not have made on your own

### Copying vs. Being Inspired [Gareis]

- **Copying** = replicating solution without understanding thinking
- **Being inspired** = extracting principles and applying to your unique problem

**Framework:**
1. Study MULTIPLE sources (not one)
2. Extract principles, not visual elements
3. Understand the WHY behind decisions
4. Translate concepts across different contexts
5. Preserve your unique perspective

Diversifying sources forces your brain to make new connections, making direct copying impossible.

## BRAND VIBE REPORT BRAINSTORMING [Lane]

For when the brand direction is unclear:

1. Input minimal description of business/idea
2. Generate three unique angles -- each with distinct name, positioning, and energy
3. Copy preferred angle into mood board generator
4. Run multiple angles simultaneously in separate chats (4+ images generating at once)
5. Compare results across angles to find best direction
6. Iterate feedback at the brand vibe level, not the image level

**Key workflow:** Go WIDE first with exploration, then NARROW once direction is clear.

## FOUNDER STORY INTEGRATION [Lane]

- Founder stories matter more than ever -- consumers want to know the human behind the brand
- As AI generates brands from thin air, having an authentic story becomes a key differentiator
- Include founder story in brand vibe report to inform all downstream content
- If no founder story exists, AI will invent something simple -- but real stories always perform better

## INDUSTRY-SPECIFIC DIRECTION [Gareis]

| Industry | Visual Direction | Typography Tendency | Color Tendency |
|----------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Fashion | Bold, creative, unseen compositions | Display + serif pairings | High contrast, signal colors |
| Tech startups | Modern, clean, bold or light | Sans-serif primary | Bright accents on neutral |
| Financial | Traditional, trust-focused | Thin serif + clean sans | Blue, dark, conservative |
| Food/beverage | Warm, inviting, organic | Script or display + serif | Warm palette, earth tones |
| Hospitality | Elegant, experiential | Serif or editorial sans | Sophisticated, muted |

## PRACTICAL DECISION FRAMEWORK (Synthesis)

1. Define emotional direction (Brand Vibe Report)
2. Visualize direction (Mood Board + inspiration collection)
3. Extract design principles (systematic reference analysis)
4. Select typography (font selection process: mood > typeface personality > technical quality)
5. Establish grid system (content analysis > grid type selection)
6. Apply hierarchy (size, weight, contrast, white space, alignment)
7. Refine (texture, rhythm, emphasis, harmony checks)

---

## ANTI-PATTERNS

- Opening design software before establishing visual direction
- Copying multiple elements from a single inspiration source
- Skipping the written brand vibe report and jumping to image generation
- Iterating at the image level instead of refining the brief/vibe report
- Saving entire websites instead of specific elements with notes
- Following trends instead of developing project-appropriate direction
- Not testing AI-generated mood boards across multiple platforms
