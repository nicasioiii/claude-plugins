# Platform Selection Guide

Framework for advising clients on whether Shopify is the right platform.

---

## THE 60% RULE

**If selling products will make up more than 60% of the website content, Shopify is the best option.**

Shopify is purpose-built for e-commerce. Every feature, app, and theme is geared toward selling. Other platforms (Squarespace, WordPress) treat e-commerce as an add-on.

---

## SHOPIFY STRENGTHS

### E-Commerce Infrastructure
- Closed-source software: secure, no hacking risk, no backups needed
- Theme code is open and accessible for customization
- Scales from small startups to enterprise (KKW Beauty, Kylie Cosmetics, Fitbit, Red Bull)

### Payment & Checkout
- 40+ payment options (Apple Pay, Amazon Pay, Klarna, installments, subscriptions)
- Shopify checkout is familiar and trusted across brands -- increases conversion
- Compare: Squarespace only offers Stripe, Square, or PayPal

### Point of Sale
- Advanced inventory management
- Staff permissions
- Buy-online-pick-up-in-store (BOPIS)
- Unified online + in-person selling

### Developer Ecosystem
- Thousands of apps and integrations
- Extensive developer documentation
- Active theme marketplace (70-80 themes)
- Liquid templating language with full customization

---

## SHOPIFY WEAKNESSES

### Blogging
- Basic: images and text only
- One or two layout options per theme
- No advanced content blocks, scheduling, or collaboration tools
- Workaround: WordPress subdomain (blog.yourdomain.com) but SEO gains go to subdomain and creates clunky switching experience

### Content-Heavy Sites
- Limited page builder capabilities compared to Squarespace/Webflow
- Information architecture tools are basic
- Scheduling tools not built in
- Portfolio features limited

## SHOPIFY LITE HYBRID STRATEGY

**For businesses less than 60% e-commerce but needing serious selling tools.**

### Setup
- Build site on Squarespace or Webflow
- Add Shopify Lite for e-commerce backend
- Cost: ~$23/mo Squarespace + ~$9/mo Shopify Lite = ~$32/mo

### Client Gets
- Shopify's product management tools
- Payment processing
- Customer profiles and analytics
- Finance reports
- Trusted Shopify checkout

### Best For
- Service businesses that also sell products
- Content-heavy sites with a small shop section
- Businesses transitioning gradually into e-commerce

## DECISION QUESTIONS

Ask these questions during discovery to determine platform:

### Business Model
1. Will selling products make up more than 60% of website content?
2. How many products/SKUs now and in the next 12 months?
3. Do you sell in person (POS needs)?
4. Do you sell internationally?
5. Do you need fulfillment center integration?

### Content Needs
6. How important is blogging to the business?
7. Do you need advanced content management (scheduling, collaboration)?
8. Do you need a portfolio or gallery?
9. How much non-product content exists?

### Technical Requirements
10. Do you need custom functionality beyond standard themes?
11. What's the budget for monthly platform costs?
12. Who will manage the site day-to-day? (Technical comfort level)
13. Are there existing integrations that must be maintained?

### Future Planning
14. **Always ask about NOW and FUTURE needs** -- platform migration is painful and costly
15. Common migration trigger: fulfillment center integration (clients switch FROM Squarespace TO Shopify for this)

| Scenario | Platform | Reasoning |
|----------|----------|-----------|

## SHOPIFY PARTNER PERKS FOR DESIGNERS

### Becoming a Shopify Partner
1. **Recurring referral revenue** -- earn monthly for each client that stays on Shopify
2. **Unlimited free development stores** -- fully functional except checkout, no trial limits
3. **Partners do not count toward staff member limits** -- client's staff allocation unaffected
4. **All stores accessible from one dashboard** -- no separate logins
5. **Client can restrict your access** -- you cannot view customer info or orders (reduces liability)
6. **Create a team under your account** -- client will not see who you hire; no access approval needed

## SCOPING SHOPIFY PROJECTS

### Quoting and Pricing Rules
- Include up to 10 products in standard quotes
- For sites with hundreds of products, use CSV import (scope separately)
- Include basic tutorials with all sites (how to add products, edit homepage)
- Budget more tutorial time if client is new to Shopify
- Always state in proposal that prices are subject to change (clients sometimes return 8+ months later)
- Frame client problems as goals in proposals for higher acceptance rates
- Suggest smaller refresh over full rebrand if that is what client actually needs -- builds trust

### Shopify Sales Call Questions
Ask these during the discovery call to determine scope:
1. Show me sites you like (enable screen sharing) -- clients often describe "super interactive" but mean a few hover effects
2. Is branding complete? Need fonts, colors, not just a logo
3. Photography and copywriting sorted? If not, use placeholders and schedule extra day rate for design changes once final images arrive
4. What platform are they currently on? Domain bought through Squarespace/Wix requires redirect work
5. How many products? Include adding 10 or fewer in quote; hundreds may need CSV import

## NICHING INTO SHOPIFY [Luna Templates]

### The Niche Advantage
- "When you speak to everyone, you speak to no one" (Seth Godin principle applied to design)
- Niching into beauty industry produced a "huge uptick in inquiries" -- clients specifically cited specialism as reason for hiring
- Create a tagline combining niche + platform + style: e.g., "playful Shopify websites for beauty brands"

### Niche Timing
- [CONTRARIAN] Do not niche too early -- work with diverse businesses for a few years first to discover what you enjoy, then specialize
- Only niche early if you already know your target
- Your portfolio attracts more of the same -- showing beauty brand work attracts beauty brand clients

### Client Acquisition for Shopify Designers
Priority-ordered channels:
1. Instagram -- post snippets of homepage designs as tasters; show face in stories
2. Word of mouth / referrals -- becomes top source over time
3. LinkedIn -- bigger budgets, startups with investment
4. Facebook groups -- industry-specific groups for your niche
5. Pinterest -- visual search engine, snowballs over time
