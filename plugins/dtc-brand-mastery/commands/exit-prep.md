# Exit Preparation Wizard

## Step 1: Gather Business Financials

Ask the user these questions before proceeding. Do not skip any.

### Required Inputs
1. **Annual revenue**: Trailing 12-month total revenue?
2. **Annual net profit** (or SDE -- Seller's Discretionary Earnings): After all expenses including your salary?
3. **Revenue channels**: Which platforms and approximate % from each?
4. **Growth trajectory**: Is revenue growing, flat, or declining YoY? What % change?
5. **Subscription revenue**: What % of revenue is recurring/subscription?
6. **Accounting basis**: Cash or accrual accounting?
7. **Entity type**: Sole proprietor, LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp?
8. **IP portfolio**: Any registered trademarks? Patents? Brand Registry?
9. **Team**: How many employees/contractors? Are operations owner-dependent?
10. **SOPs**: Do you have documented processes? (none, partial, comprehensive)
11. **Timeline**: When would you ideally want to exit? (6 months, 1 year, 2+ years)

## Step 2: Estimate Valuation Range (Load: 15-legal-exit)

Based on inputs, calculate estimated valuation range:

### Determine Base Multiple
- Amazon-only: 2x-4x annual net profit
- Amazon + DTC: 3x-6x annual net profit
- DTC-first with Amazon: 4x-8x annual net profit
- Subscription-heavy DTC: 5x-10x annual revenue or EBITDA

### Apply Multiplier Adjustments

| Factor | Adjustment |
|---|---|
| Growing YoY 20%+ | +0.5x to +1.5x |
| Flat or declining | -0.5x to -1.5x |
| Subscription > 30% of revenue | +0.5x to +1x |
| Strong IP portfolio (TM + patents) | +0.5x |
| Comprehensive SOPs & systems | +0.5x |
| Large email/SMS list (10K+ engaged) | +0.25x to +0.5x |
| Owner-dependent operations | -1x to -2x |
| Cash-basis accounting | -0.5x (plus restatement cost) |
| Single-channel dependency | -0.5x to -1x |

### Output Valuation Estimate
- Low estimate: [base multiple low] x annual profit
- Mid estimate: [base multiple mid] x annual profit
- High estimate: [base multiple high] x annual profit
- Note: these are rough estimates; actual valuation requires professional broker assessment

### Priority 2 (Should-Fix for Better Multiple)

### Priority 3 (Nice-to-Have)

## Step 5: Exit Timeline & Documentation Plan

Based on user's target timeline, create a phased plan:

### If exiting in 6 months:
- Month 1-2: Fix Priority 1 gaps, switch to accrual accounting, organize Google Drive
- Month 3-4: Document SOPs, clean financials, prepare manufacturer spreadsheet
- Month 5: Engage broker or begin aggregator outreach
- Month 6: Due diligence and negotiation

### If exiting in 12+ months:
- Months 1-3: Build systems (Escala methodology), fix all Priority 1 and 2 gaps
- Months 4-6: Grow revenue trajectory (buyers pay premium for upward trend)
- Months 7-9: Add revenue channel for diversification
- Months 10-11: Engage broker, prepare data room
- Month 12: Due diligence and negotiation

## Step 6: Deal Structure Guidance (Load: 15-legal-exit)

Based on user's situation, recommend deal structure preferences:
- **Full cash upfront**: best if user wants clean exit and certainty
- **Partial + earnout**: best if user is confident in continued growth trajectory
- **Majority sale**: best if user wants liquidity but believes in future upside

Provide negotiation preparation tips:
1. Know your walk-away number before any conversations
2. Always have multiple potential buyers in process simultaneously
3. Never accept first offer
4. Get professional representation (e-commerce-specialized accountant + lawyer)
