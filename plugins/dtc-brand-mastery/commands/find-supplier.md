# Supplier Search Wizard

## Step 1: Determine Sourcing Strategy

### Required Inputs
1. **Product type:** What are you manufacturing?
2. **Category:** Ingestible/cosmetic, or physical good?
3. **Volume needed:** How many units for first order?
4. **Budget:** Total available for first inventory order?
5. **Timeline:** When do you need product ready?
6. **Geography preference:** Open to China, or prefer domestic?

### Routing Rule
- Ingestibles/supplements/cosmetics: recommend domestic manufacturing
- Physical goods: recommend Alibaba with Kian's method
- Heavy/bulky products: evaluate domestic for freight savings
- Genuinely novel IP: develop locally, then reverse-engineer globally

---

## Step 2: Generate Search Plan

### For Alibaba (Kian's Method)
1. Search by MANUFACTURERS, not products
2. Filter: Verified + Trade Assurance + ISO + BSCI
3. Target 5+ years verified
4. Check company name format for specialization
5. Verify export markets match yours
6. Apply 10-3-1 method: contact 10, narrow to 3, order from 1

### For Domestic (Matt's Method)
- Google: `"[product type]" private label -site:alibaba.com`
- Check ThomasNet.com and WholesaleCentral.com
- Verify certifications and capability

---

## Step 3: Supplier Vetting Checklist

For each supplier, verify:
- [ ] Minimum 3 years on platform
- [ ] Correct certifications for your product category
- [ ] Production equipment matches your needs (not just packing equipment)
- [ ] Export experience to your target market
- [ ] Responsive communication (within 24 hours)
- [ ] Reasonable MOQ for your volume
- [ ] Competitive pricing (within market range)
- [ ] Trade Assurance available
- [ ] Willing to provide samples

---

## Step 4: Generate First Contact Message

Customize Jason Wong's template:
- Position as "expanding product line" (not "starting a business")
- Ask: MOQ, quantity discounts, freight costs, sample time/cost, production time
- Include shipping address
- Send from professional domain email

---

## Step 5: Sample Ordering Plan

- Order from 3 different suppliers
- Budget $50-$100 per sample including shipping
- Request "sample of no commercial value" on invoice
- Ask for sample cost refund with first bulk order
- Plan stress testing upon receipt
