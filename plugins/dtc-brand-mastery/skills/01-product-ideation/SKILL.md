# Product Ideation & Validation

Generate and validate product ideas for DTC, Amazon, and TikTok Shop. This skill covers the complete journey from raw idea generation through data-driven validation, using platform-specific frameworks.

## BEFORE STARTING: DETERMINE CONTEXT

Ask these questions if not already provided:

1. **Platform:** Are you selling on Shopify/DTC, Amazon, TikTok Shop, or all three?
2. **Stage:** Do you have product ideas already, or need help generating them?
3. **Budget:** What is your available startup capital? ($3K-$5K minimum realistic)
4. **Experience:** First product or expanding an existing line?
5. **Advantage:** Do you have social media skills, capital for ads, or domain expertise?

## IDEA GENERATION FRAMEWORKS

### Gretta's PSSP Framework (Problem > Stat > Solution > Product)

The foundational ideation method. Start with a real problem, not a product.

1. **Problem:** Identify a clear, tangible pain point you have personally experienced ("scratching your own itch")
2. **Stat:** Find supporting data demonstrating the problem's scale or urgency
3. **Solution:** Describe how to solve the problem at the category level
4. **Product:** Define the actual product offering

Real examples:
- Dropbottle: Dehydration (64% of body is water; 1-2% loss = fatigue) > Creative hydration solutions > Fruit-infused glass drink bottle
- SkinnyMeTea: Demand for fast/safe weight loss > Health-approved detox > 14-day / 28-day detox tea pack
- Reusable straw: 500M straws used daily in US > Make items reusable > Retractable metal straw in matchbox-sized case

Action: Complete PSSP worksheet for 5 problem ideas, then narrow to 3.

### Product Innovation Framework (Gretta Van Riel)

Core principle: You do NOT need to reinvent the wheel. Innovation means changing ONE dimension of an existing product.

**1. DESIGN** -- Customization (monogramming, engraving -- harder to scale) vs. Personalization (targeted segments, like "Share a Coke"). Materials/sustainability: upcycled ocean plastic, compostable. Color/style: rose gold, matte black. Warning: Shoes of Prey failed despite $30M funding -- mass customization can be a trap.

**2. FUNCTION (Most Powerful)** -- Research method: Read 2-3 star negative Amazon reviews to find common complaints. Hybridization: combine two products (PopSocket = handle, Lumi Case = phone case + selfie light). Fix the specific pain point competitors miss.

**3. PRICE** -- Accessible DTC: Movement Watches, Warby Parker (under $100) for mass market. Luxury/Exclusive: requires strong brand equity first.

**4. TIME (Exclusivity by Scarcity)** -- The Fifth Watches: sold ONLY on the 5th of each month for 5 days, creating FOMO. Result: $100K+ day-one revenue. Other examples: Yeezys, flash sales, limited editions.

### Jason Wong's Problem-First MVP Approach

Core insight (contrarian): "Your job is not to validate your product, it's to validate the PROBLEM. Who has it and what is the best way to solve it?"

- Three key MVP points: (1) costs less to produce, (2) functions close enough that people understand it, (3) channels consumer feedback to improve the product
- Single-product launch examples: Casper (one mattress), Bonobos (one style of pants), Harry's (one razor)
- The opportunity cost of NOT launching earlier -- lacking real feedback -- costs more than imperfection
- "Version one is better than version none"

## PLATFORM-SPECIFIC VALIDATION

### Matt Clark's Four Criteria (DTC/Shopify)

All 4 criteria MUST be met before proceeding:

**1. Big Market Potential** -- Product must appeal to 1M+ people. DTC uses interruption marketing (ads), not search-based discovery, so you need a large audience. Validate via Google Trends volume, competitor audience size, market research. Red flag: low Google Trends volume = market too small.

**2. Healthy Margins** -- Must sell product for 4x landed cost minimum. Matt's breakdown: Manufacturing $10 + Fulfillment $5 + Packaging $2 + Marketing $10-15 + Payment processing $1 + Overhead $2-3 = Profit $4-8 on $40 retail. Critical nuance: the DOLLAR MARGIN matters more than the multiplier. A $12 product at 4x only has $9 margin -- Facebook ads cost $10-20+ per acquisition.

**3. Messaging Potential** -- Benefits communicable in under 10 seconds. Test: write one-sentence benefit, show product image to 5 people -- do they immediately "get it"?

**4. Media Buying Potential** -- Product must photograph/film well and stop the scroll in a social feed. Must create a hook in the first 3 seconds of video.

### INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: Minimum Markup

| Instructor | Rule | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Matt Clark | 4x landed cost | Paid-ads-driven DTC brands |
| Gretta Van Riel | 2.5x manufacturing cost | Organic/influencer-first brands |
| Jason Wong | 5x cost (COGS as 20% of retail) | Most conservative, safest default |
| Melisa Vong | 30%+ profit after ALL Amazon fees | Amazon-specific |

Resolution: Use 4x for paid-ads DTC. Use 2.5x for organic-first. For Amazon, use 30%+ after fees. Jason's 5x is the safest default. Always calculate fully loaded unit economics.

### Melisa Vong's PRIME Method (Amazon FBA)

**P -- Positioning Potential:** Can you rank on page 1 for main keywords? Look for products with <1,000 reviews on page 1. If 5+ sellers on page 1 desktop (or 2+ on mobile top 15) have <200 reviews, opportunity exists.

**R -- Researching Reviews:** Mine 1-star and 2-star competitor reviews for product improvement opportunities. Also mine positive reviews to identify must-have features.

**I -- In-Demand Product:** Main keyword must have >2,000 searches/month. Find 3-5 relevant keywords totaling >5,000 searches/month collectively. Competitors should be doing >$5,000/month in sales.

**M -- Margins & Profit:** Target >30% profit margin after ALL Amazon fees. Products must sell for >$12 on Amazon to be viable after fees.

**E -- Easy to Make & Ship:** Ideally <5 lbs, <18 inches on all sides. Lightweight = lower FBA fees and shipping costs.

### Melisa Vong's 10-Step Validation Checklist (Amazon)

1. High search volume: main keyword 2,000+ searches/month; 3-5 keywords totaling 5,000+
2. Potential monthly sales: 200+ units based on competitor data
3. At least 2 sellers on page 1 mobile with <200 reviews
4. Products on page 1 with poor ratings (3.5 stars or less = opportunity)
5. Profitable: >30% margins, sells for >$12
6. Top 3 sellers do NOT dominate >80% of category revenue
7. Competitor listings are NOT fully optimized (missing images, weak bullets, no A+ content)
8. Not seasonal, not restricted, not an Amazon Basics product
9. Product can be improved (better packaging, accessories, fix complaints)
10. Product is NOT patented (check patents.google.com)

### Michelle's 5 Keys to TikTokability (TikTok Shop)

Products must meet at least 3 out of 5 criteria:

1. **Demonstrability** -- Can it be demonstrated quickly in a short video? Appeal must be visible in first seconds
2. **Unique Attributes** -- Distinct features that stand out (even simple differentiators like unique color)
3. **Serves a Niche** -- Caters to a specific, passionate audience (survivalists, beauty enthusiasts)
4. **Solves a Problem** -- Can quickly demonstrate both the problem AND solution in video
5. **Optimized for Impulse Buying** -- Keep pricing below $50 for higher success rates

Key difference: Unlike Amazon (need-based shopping), TikTok thrives on impulse buying. Customer journey is integrated -- awareness to checkout without leaving the app.

## E-COMMERCE VIABILITY CHECKLIST

Before committing to any product, verify:

- [ ] Not too heavy (shipping costs eat margins)
- [ ] Not too fragile (refund/replacement issues -- Dropbottle's glass was heavy AND breakable, requiring luxury pricing at $40+)
- [ ] Not dangerous or heavily regulated (lithium batteries, aerosols, health claims)
- [ ] Able to ship worldwide without special handling
- [ ] Shipping costs coverable in product pricing (enables free shipping offers)
- [ ] Avoid supplements/ingestibles unless experienced or well-capitalized (FDA/TGA regulatory risk, $100/day fines possible)
- [ ] Not a declining category on Google Trends (5-year view)
- [ ] Not dominated by Amazon's own brands

### Gretta's Idea Validation Process (20-Person Interviews)

Before spending money on physical product:
- Talk to ~20 people matching your customer personas
- Script: "I'm developing a new product idea. Could I quickly ask you 5 questions?"
  1. Would you purchase this product?
  2. Would you recommend it to a friend?
  3. If not, why?
  4. If you could change one thing, what would it be?
  5. Any other feedback?
- If majority say yes: proceed
- If not: tweak idea/UVP or iterate
- The best validation is your first sale -- someone pulling out a credit card

## KIAN GOLZARI: BRANDING vs. SELLING DECISION

Before developing any product, decide your approach:

**Branding (Long-Term):** Build community first, launch products to community. Core values drive everything. Example: Peak Design started with camera clips on Kickstarter, later launched tripod for $13M+ to existing community. Requires more upfront investment but yields dramatically higher multiples at exit.

**Selling (Quick Revenue):** Products you may not be passionate about but have proven demand and low competition. Lower budget, faster time to profit, lower risk.

Decision factors: budget, profit timeline, risk appetite, passion level.

## ANTI-PATTERNS: WHAT TO AVOID

- Declining markets on Google Trends
- Regulated spaces without experience (supplements, cosmetics, medical devices)
- Mass customization trap (Shoes of Prey: $30M raised, still failed)
- Ultra-niche hobbies with <1M addressable market for DTC
- Products requiring 5+ minute explanations
- Commodity items with no differentiation
- Electronics with high manufacturing cost and low margin
- Products that do not photograph or film well
- Entering a price war ("Don't try to be as cheap as possible" -- Jason Wong)
- Copying without differentiating (Chinese sellers compete on price; you must compete on brand)
