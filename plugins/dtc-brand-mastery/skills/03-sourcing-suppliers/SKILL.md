# Sourcing & Supplier Management

Find, vet, negotiate with, and manage manufacturing suppliers. Covers Alibaba, domestic US manufacturing, import record research, and the complete supplier relationship lifecycle from first contact through ongoing orders.

## BEFORE STARTING: DETERMINE CONTEXT

Ask these questions if not already provided:

1. **Product type:** What are you sourcing? (Ingestible/cosmetic vs. physical good)
2. **Volume:** First order or reorder? Estimated quantity needed?
3. **Budget:** How much capital is available for first inventory order?
4. **Geography preference:** Open to China, or prefer domestic?
5. **Experience level:** First time sourcing, or have existing supplier relationships?

## ALIBABA SOURCING PROCESS

### Kian Golzari's Filtering Method

Search by MANUFACTURERS, not products -- do not be incentivized by cheapest price.

1. Select "Manufacturers" tab (not products tab)
2. Filter: Verified Manufacturers only
3. Filter: Trade Assurance (payment protection)
4. Filter: ISO certification + BSCI (business social compliance)
5. First word of company name = city/province = manufacturing cluster (e.g., Wenzhou = glasses hub)
6. Target factories verified 5+ years on Alibaba
7. Ignore ratings/reviews (make your own assessment from direct interaction)
8. Hover over "Products" section -- all products should be in same category (not random hot products = trading company)
9. Check export markets align with your target market (30%+ North America = good for US sellers)
10. Check establishment date vs. Alibaba years (company may be older than Alibaba presence)

### Chinese Company Name Format (Jason Wong)
Regulated by law: (1) City of registration, (2) Actual company name, (3) Product category specialization, (4) Company structure (typically LLC). Understanding this helps you identify specialized manufacturers.

### Manufacturer vs. Trading Company Detection
- Companies must declare which they are on Alibaba
- Spotting trading companies: check their address -- real factories are in rural areas, not cities
- Search their address on maps; bonus: coastal facilities save on ground transport to seaport
- Trading companies mark up price; you pay more for a middleman
- "When you think of China producing low quality goods, it's oftentimes due to them working with a non-specialized manufacturer" -- Jason Wong

### Key Certifications to Look For
- ISO 9001: quality management (gold standard)
- ISO 14000: environmental management
- BSCI: business social compliance
- Factory-level certifications are harder to obtain than product certifications
- Trade Assurance: Alibaba protection for defective products, delayed shipments

## KIAN GOLZARI'S 10-3-1 METHOD

1. Contact 10 manufacturers with your spec sheet (identical specs for apples-to-apples comparison)
2. Narrow to top 3 based on: communication quality, pricing, alignment with your needs
3. Order samples from all 3
4. Place production order with the 1 winner

### Knowledge-Building Hack
- Identify 3-5 suppliers you will NOT work with
- Ask them all your "beginner" questions to build product knowledge
- Then approach preferred suppliers with expert-level specifications
- Suppliers respect knowledgeable customers and give better pricing and treatment

### Supplier Relationship Value
- Ask good suppliers: "What new products are you developing? What's selling in other markets?"
- Deep relationships = better prices, priority production, exclusive innovations
- "People buy from people" -- invest in real relationship

## JASON WONG'S 5-SECTION SUPPLIER AUDIT

When narrowing suppliers, audit these 5 sections of their Alibaba profile:

1. **Company Profile:** Employee count, certifications, main export markets (verified by third-party firm like SGS Group)
2. **Production Capacity:** Product lines, equipment types. KEY: ensure their equipment matches what you need. If they only have gluing/packing machines but not production machines, they are outsourcing and you will pay more
3. **Quality Control Equipment:** Machines used to test product quality. "I tend to stay away from factories that do not disclose this regardless of their reasons"
4. **R&D Capacity:** Larger/more credible suppliers have internal R&D teams with their own certifications
5. **Export Capabilities:** Trade shows attended, countries exported to. Work with suppliers experienced in your region

Efficiency tip: Do not read the full audit PDF until you have narrowed to 1-2 suppliers. It is "really time-consuming and not necessary for the initial seeding stage."

## DOMESTIC MANUFACTURING (US)

### When US Manufacturing Wins
- Ingestibles, supplements, cosmetics: domestic strongly preferred (FDA/TGA regulatory compliance)
- Heavy/bulky products: freight savings offset higher production costs
- Genuinely novel IP: enforceable contracts protect your innovation
- "Made in USA" prestige badge for marketing

### Pros and Cons (Jason Wong)
**Pros:** (1) Shorter lead time (days vs. weeks), (2) Better communication, (3) IP protection (enforceable contracts), (4) Prestige badge
**Cons:** (1) More expensive (labor cost ratio: 1 US employee = ~5 Chinese employees), (2) Less customization capability

### US Supplier Directories
- **ThomasNet.com** -- more industrial/commercial, higher MOQs
- **WholesaleCentral.com** -- lower MOQs but less customization

### Google Search Technique (Matt Clark)
Use Google for products that go IN or ON your body (supplements, beauty, food, cleaning).
- Search: `"product type" private label -site:alibaba.com`
- Or: `"product type" manufacturers -site:alibaba.com`
- The `-site:alibaba.com` excludes Alibaba results

### Gretta's "Start Local, Scale Global" Framework
- Local first because: lower MOQs, easier communication, no cultural barriers, faster iteration, can visit in person
- China manufacturers are great at REVERSE ENGINEERING but struggle to INNOVATE from scratch
- Process: develop product locally > send sample to global manufacturer > they replicate it
- Contrarian: "SkinnyMeTea is still made in Australia. Being made in Australia was one of our unique value propositions"

## IMPORT RECORD RESEARCH (Jason Wong)

~25% of quality suppliers do not have Alibaba listings. Find them through import records.

### How It Works
- Bill of Lading (BOL): document with all shipment info including importer name, supplier name, and shipment contents
- US government makes ALL import records publicly accessible

### Finding Company Registered Names
Look at the brand's privacy policy (usually in website footer). Example: Hydroflask = "Steel Technology LLC DBA Hydroflask," Alo Yoga = "Alo LLC"

### Tools
- **PortExaminer.com (Free):** Filter by supplier name, importer name, cargo description. Limited data (mainly pre-2016)
- **Import Genius ($99-$399/month):** More accurate, recent data. Worth it for serious research

### Protecting YOUR Import Records
- Air freight shipments do not appear in these databases (BOL only issued for sea shipments)
- For sea freight: create a "ghost entity" LLC with an unpublicized name solely for importing. Consult lawyer first

## ALIBABA ACCOUNT SETUP & MESSAGING

### Account Setup (Matt Clark)
- Use a NEW email dedicated to this business (you will get flooded with supplier messages)
- Domain emails (you@yourbrand.com) look more professional than Gmail/Yahoo
- Provide company name when registering (planned business name)

### Messaging Limits
- New accounts have daily message limits
- Send only 10-50 messages per day when starting
- If researching multiple products, contact suppliers for only ONE product per day
- "Contact All Suppliers" button sends to 10-15 at once -- use max 1-2 times or risk account throttling

### Compare Feature Hack
- Click "Compare" checkbox on products to add to comparison tray (up to 20 suppliers)
- Bookmark the comparison URL to return to your curated list anytime

## GRETTA'S SUPPLIER SELECTION CRITERIA

- Look for "one-stop shop" -- avoid ordering 20 parts from 20 suppliers
- Criteria: location (close as possible), history (worked with brands you know), capacity to innovate, quality of samples
- Good communication skills and fast reply rates
- "Trust your instincts -- if you find them hard to deal with from the outset, it's unlikely that's going to change"
- Google search formula: [product type] + "manufacturer" + [nearest major city]
- Create list of minimum 5 options

### Key Questions for Manufacturers
1. Basic cost for your product type
2. Scaled pricing tiers for MOQ
3. Lead time (order submission to stock receipt)
4. Maximum order quantity (capacity ceiling)
5. Sample availability and cost

## SAMPLE ORDERING PROCESS

### Matt Clark's Process
- Order at least 3 samples, each from a DIFFERENT supplier
- Samples cost $50-$100 including shipping
- Payment: PayPal preferred; TT/wire transfer not recommended for samples (bank fees $30-$50)
- Samples typically arrive in 5-10 days
- Template asks supplier to reimburse sample cost with first bulk order

### Jason Wong's Sample Checklist
1. Email inquiry with quantity needed
2. State color and describe what sample should look like
3. State shipping address
4. Ensure supplier writes "sample of no commercial value" on invoice
5. Tell supplier you will deduct sample charge from first order
6. Declare proper sample value (do not under-declare a $99 item at $8)
7. Provide visual of product (design mockup or drawing)

### Customs Tips for Samples
- Ask supplier to write "sample of no commercial value" on sample invoice
- Can ask supplier to lower declared value, but keep it credible
- Items with built-in batteries may not be sendable via airmail/courier

### Stress Testing (Jason Wong)
Drop it, scratch colors, test functionality to failure. Document limitations. Example: Jason found sample lashes could be worn 20+ times (expected only a handful) -- became core marketing message.

## PAYMENT TERMS

### INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: Payment Terms

| Instructor | Position | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Gretta | 50% upfront, 50% on delivery | Standard for her experience |
| Jason Wong | 30% deposit, 70% on completion; 100% upfront = RED FLAG | Standard for experienced buyers |
| Matt Clark | 30% deposit, 70% on completion; wire transfer for bulk | Standard for bulk orders |
| Melisa Vong | First order may require 100% upfront; evolves to 30/70; long-term net-60 | Reality for new sellers |
| Kian Golzari | Negotiable; split shipments to reduce upfront risk | Advanced tactic |

Resolution: First-ever order may require higher upfront (Melisa's reality). Standard is 30% deposit / 70% on completion. Never 100% upfront to an unproven supplier. Build toward net-60 as relationship matures.

### Payment Methods
- Alibaba payment services: for orders above $1,000, even if communicating via WeChat
- PayPal or Payoneer: Chinese vendors commonly accept. Benefit: pay with credit card for points + extra 30 days cash flow. Suppliers add ~4% processing fee
- Wire transfer (TT): for bulk orders. Get WRITTEN CONFIRMATION of payment receipt
- Credit card stacking: pay suppliers via Melio for extended terms + rewards points
