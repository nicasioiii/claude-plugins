# Negotiation Scripts & Supplier Communication

## JASON WONG'S FIRST CONTACT TEMPLATE

> "Hi John. My name is Jason. I'm the founder of ABC Company. We're expanding our product line and I'm looking to work with a manufacturer to create a yoga mat. I want to order 500 units for our sample order and would like to know the following: What is your MOQ? What are the discounts if I order a certain amount? The air and sea freight costs? Sample time? Sample cost? Production time? And here's my address."

### Positioning Trick
Say "We're expanding our product line" rather than "I'm starting a new business" -- positions you as established with experience. Suppliers prioritize customers who sound like they will bring repeat orders.

## JASON WONG'S 5 NEGOTIATION TACTICS

### 1. Be Respectful and Professional
Creates perception you know what you are doing. Builds trust for better pricing. Suppliers give better terms to buyers they respect and want to keep long-term.

### 2. Tell Them Your Future Intentions
Suppliers want long-term customers for consistent/predictable revenue. Position as "sample order now, larger order later." Use language like "We plan to scale to 5,000 units within the first year."

### 3. Diversify Suppliers
Always have at least 3 suppliers you are talking to simultaneously. This provides leverage ("another supplier offered us...") and backup in case your primary fails.

### 4. Ask What Can Change to Lower Price
Ask supplier what drives the cost. Work backwards to see if different material, coating, or color would lower price/MOQ. Sometimes a small change in material can reduce cost by 15-20%.

### 5. Create Urgency
"We're looking to finalize the numbers today." "We have a deadline with our launch timeline." Time pressure motivates suppliers to offer their best terms faster.

## JASON WONG'S MOQ REDUCTION SCRIPT (1000 to 400 Units)

**You:** "We want to make sure the first production run is correct. I would like to order 400 units to test production quality."

**Supplier:** "400 units is too low, we'll lose money."

**You:** "I understand, not my intention for you to lose money. I value our long-term working relationship. My company invested a lot of time and money in the design and will lose money if this doesn't go right."

**Supplier pulls "I need to check with my boss" card.**

**You:** "Would it be possible for me to talk to your boss? We've been in business for many years. We're talking to a few suppliers and this is the only company that hasn't accepted a lower MOQ."

**Follow-up:** "I don't want you to lose this order because of your MOQ. We're deciding between two manufacturers. The deadline to consider a supplier is tomorrow. Please let me know if 400 units is possible."

**If they reject due to setup/raw material cost:** "How about we lower the MOQ to 500 or 600 and I'm willing to pay a little bit more for the product cost per unit. If this is acceptable, let me know by today and I'll issue the PO."

### Negotiation Limits
It is difficult to lower the price or MOQ beyond 20-25%. Asking for 30-40% reduction is unrealistic. Aim for 20-25% and let them meet in the middle.

## KIAN GOLZARI'S PRICE NEGOTIATION STRATEGIES

### 1. Find Market Price First
Send identical spec sheet to 5-10 verified manufacturers. Prices cluster around true market price. Cancel out outliers (too cheap = quality shortcuts; too expensive = excessive margin). Focus on suppliers in the range.

### 2. Sample Leverage
Get your favorite sample from Supplier A ($5.50). Tell them: "I received a quote for $4.80 from another manufacturer. If you match, I'll give you the PO." You know $4.80 is legitimate because another verified manufacturer quoted it.

### 3. Upsell Quantity Technique
Ask for pricing at 1,000 / 5,000 / 10,000 units. Even if ordering 1,000, you now know the floor price ($4.20 at 10K). Tell supplier: "This is a trial order. If you can get close to $4.20, I'll pass savings to customers, sell more, reorder faster = more orders for your factory." They may meet you halfway ($4.60 instead of $5.00).

### 4. Ask About Materials in Stock
Factories hold bulk materials or have cancelled orders. Materials in stock = discounted pricing. Always test quality of stock materials first (may have been cancelled for quality reasons).

### 5. Use Non-Target Suppliers as Knowledge Sources
Identify 3 suppliers you will NOT work with. Ask them all "silly questions" about materials, techniques, pricing. Build product knowledge, then approach preferred suppliers with specific technical requirements. Supplier respects knowledgeable customers and gives better pricing.

### 6. HTS Code Optimization
Different product descriptions = different import duty rates. Changing from one material to another can shift duty from 12% to 2-3%. That is a 10% savings on COGS from a classification change alone.

### 7. Factory Visits (Relationship Leverage)
See product in all its component pieces. Once you see 30 components, you have 30 reference points for cost reduction OR quality improvement. Understanding each production step gives you negotiation power. Use language: "long-term partnership," "build together," "develop more products."

### Warning on Price Negotiation
"In China, you can get ANY price you want. If a product is $8, you can get $7. But there WILL be shortcuts -- cheaper materials, skipped QC steps. If a price is too good to be true, it usually is. You'll find out in your negative reviews and returns."

## KIAN GOLZARI'S MOQ REDUCTION TACTICS (9 Strategies)

1. **State first order is trial order:** "We normally order 5,000-10,000 but always trial new manufacturers first. If everything goes smooth, immediate reorder at much larger quantity."
2. **Show strength as customer:** Share your brand portfolio, sales volume, history
3. **Long-term partnership language:** Repeat throughout all communication
4. **Show China visit history:** Share photos of previous factory visits (proves you are a serious buyer)
5. **Choose smaller factories:** 1-50 workers more flexible than 2,000-worker operations
6. **Use stock materials:** Factory's existing materials = lower MOQ (they need to clear stock)
7. **Split shipments:** Order MOQ (e.g., 1,000 units) but ship only 300 first; store rest at factory warehouse. Pay deposit on 1,000 but balance only on 300 shipped. Sell those, then ship rest.
8. **Get forward orders:** Pre-sell to retailers/customers at discount before placing factory order
9. **Crowdfunding:** Collect orders via Kickstarter/Indiegogo, then place factory order with committed revenue

### Understanding MOQ Drivers
MOQ is driven by raw materials (e.g., fabric roll makes exactly 1,200 units). Sometimes MOQ is driven by packaging (packaging factory MOQ = 5,000 boxes), not product (product MOQ = 800 units). Solution: order 800 products + 5,000 boxes.

## PAYMENT TERMS PROGRESSION

### Stage 1: First Order (No Trust Yet)
- May require 50-100% upfront (Melisa Vong's reality for new sellers)
- Pay through Alibaba's payment services for protection
- RED FLAG: supplier demanding 100% upfront for a large order = financial trouble

### Stage 2: Standard Terms (After First Successful Order)
- 30% deposit upon order placement
- 70% upon order completion (product ready to ship)
- This gives you leverage for quality control

### Stage 3: Relationship Terms (After Several Orders)
- Negotiate net-30 or net-45
- Request supplier commitment discount (10% off per unit for 1-year commitment)

### Stage 4: Long-Term Terms
- Net-60 or better
- If you sell items before vendor credit expires, vendor effectively funds your purchases
- This is how companies achieve negative Cash Conversion Cycle

### Payment Method Notes
- For orders above $1,000: use Alibaba payment services
- PayPal/Payoneer: ~4% processing fee added by supplier, but you get credit card points + extra 30 days
- Wire transfer (TT): for bulk orders; get written confirmation of receipt
- Melio: pay suppliers via credit card; they receive bank transfer. You earn points and extend payment terms

## POST-NEGOTIATION RELATIONSHIP BUILDING

- Ask Chinese suppliers for WeChat or WhatsApp (communication moves off Alibaba)
- Build personal relationship through instant messaging
- The supplier agent will be the same person you work with long-term
- Send holiday greetings (Chinese New Year is critical)
- Share product success stories and customer feedback
- Visit factory when possible (builds strongest possible relationship)
- Ask about their other products and capabilities for future development
