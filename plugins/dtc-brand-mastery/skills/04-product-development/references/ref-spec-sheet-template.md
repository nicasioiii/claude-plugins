# Spec Sheet Templates & Development Checklists

## KIAN GOLZARI'S PRODUCT SPEC SHEET TEMPLATE

Create this as a single-page PDF to send to all manufacturers. Include ALL information upfront.

### Section A: Product Identity
- **Brand name:** [Your brand]
- **Product name:** [Specific product]
- **Product code/SKU:** [Internal reference]
- **Launch season:** [Target date or season]

### Section B: Materials & Construction
- **Primary material:** [e.g., Polyester 300D] with specific weight/grade
- **Secondary materials:** [Lining, padding, coating -- each specified separately]
- **Pantone colors:** [Exact Pantone numbers -- give same number to ALL suppliers]
- **Hardware/components:** [Zippers: brand/grade, Buckles: type, Elastic: width/stretch]
- **Thread color:** [Match to body or contrast]

### Section C: Dimensions & Specifications
- **Product dimensions:** Length x Width x Height (in cm and inches)
- **Product weight:** Target weight range
- **Size guide:** [If applicable -- include measurement chart]
- **Capacity/volume:** [If applicable -- liters, oz, etc.]

### Section D: Features & Modifications
- **Feature list:** Numbered list of all required features
- **Design modification annotations:** Arrows pointing to specific changes on images/mockups
- **New feature requests:** With sketches or reference images
- **Competitor images:** Show what exists; annotate what to improve

### Section E: Packaging
- **Packaging type:** Poly bag, color box, hang tag, blister pack, etc.
- **Packaging dimensions:** Must fit product with protection
- **Labeling requirements:** Country-specific (UPC, care labels, warnings)
- **Barcode/UPC:** Number and placement

### Section F: Compliance
- **Certification requirements:** ISO, CE, FDA, CPSC as applicable
- **Testing requirements:** Lab testing for materials, safety, durability
- **Compliance needs:** Region-specific regulations

### Section G: Production Notes
- **Master carton specifications:** Units per carton, carton dimensions, weight
- **Special instructions:** Assembly notes, color-matching priority, quality focus areas

## KIAN GOLZARI'S PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT MASTER CHECKLIST (16 Steps)

1. Review top 5 competitors online (listings, images, copy, reviews)
2. Visit retail store holding competitor products (touch, feel, ask staff)
3. Read both negative AND positive reviews online
4. Read the Q&A sections (reveals unexpected use cases)
5. Purchase competitor samples and test them yourself extensively
6. Check with suppliers what they are doing for other customers/countries
7. Answer: what is the clear USP over the market leader?
8. Can you deconstruct the product to reveal improvement opportunities?
9. Create mood board (print all images, put on wall, find connections)
10. Create specification sheet (use template above)
11. Cost up improvements to ensure product is still competitively priced
12. Discover packaging options and how they can improve the product
13. Can a complimentary free product improve conversion and perceived value?
14. Align with manufacturer that fits your purpose (best price/quality)
15. Order samples from shortlisted suppliers (minimum 3)
16. Test your products yourself and provide feedback in clear document

## JASON WONG'S PACKAGING DESIGN DETAILS

### Four Elements to Decide
1. **What to communicate:** Brand name, product name, key benefits, usage instructions, warnings, certifications
2. **Packaging type:** Poly bag (cheapest), corrugated box, rigid box, sleeve, blister pack
3. **Packaging material:** Cardboard, corrugated, plastic, cloth, wood. Consider sustainability
4. **Design:** Layout, colors (match brand Pantone), typography, images

### Operational Consequences of Design
- Consider whether the box is difficult to assemble during fulfillment
- Whether it adds weight (increasing shipping cost per unit)
- Whether it gets dirty during delivery (all-white boxes show damage easily)
- Jason's candle brand failure: glass shattered during delivery in first month because packaging did not protect the glass

### Retail-Ready Packaging
Prepare packaging as if selling in retail even if DTC-only at launch. Having barcodes and shelf-ready design helps when retailers request samples.

### Cost Sources
- Ask your supplier if they can print inserts or packaging (much cheaper done locally in China)
- Alibaba: search "business card printing" for affordable Chinese insert printing
- Vistaprint for US-based products
- Budget designer sources: Upwork, 99designs, The Dieline

## GRETTA'S PACKAGING CONSIDERATIONS

### 5 Key Factors
1. **Weight:** heavier packaging = higher shipping cost per unit
2. **Size:** larger packaging = more storage space needed = higher fulfillment cost
3. **Presentation:** must match brand positioning (premium brand needs premium packaging)
4. **Protection:** must protect product during shipping (glass requires extra protection)
5. **Sustainability:** increasingly important to consumers; compostable/recyclable preferred

### Packaging Manufacturer Decision Rule
- **100-300 units:** Use stock/generic packaging with branded stickers or simple labels
- **500+ units:** Invest in custom packaging (economies of scale make it affordable)

### Merch as Perceived Value
- Package product with branded accessories (tote bag, stickers, mesh bag)
- List accessory at retail price on website so customer understands value
- Example: "Buy a Dropbottle and receive free tote bag" (tote listed at $40 on site)

## PROTOTYPE EVALUATION CRITERIA

### Functional Assessment
- [ ] Does it solve the problem identified in PSSP/ideation?
- [ ] Does it function as specified in the spec sheet?
- [ ] Are all requested features present and working?
- [ ] Is the weight within target range?
- [ ] Are dimensions accurate to spec?

### Quality Assessment
- [ ] Material quality matches specification?
- [ ] Color matches Pantone swatch?
- [ ] Stitching/assembly quality acceptable?
- [ ] Hardware (zippers, buttons, clasps) function smoothly?
- [ ] Printing/labeling is clear and properly aligned?

### Durability Assessment
- [ ] Drop test passed? (drop from various heights)
- [ ] Stress test passed? (pull, twist, stretch to failure)
- [ ] Color fastness? (rubbing test, sun exposure)
- [ ] Water resistance? (if applicable)
- [ ] Repeated use test? (use 20+ times to check degradation)

### Comparison Assessment
- [ ] Better than competitor samples?
- [ ] Addresses pain points found in competitor reviews?
- [ ] Justifies target price point?
- [ ] Photographs well for listing images?

## SAMPLE STRESS TESTING PROTOCOL

1. **Impact test:** Drop from table height (3 feet) onto hard floor, 3 times each side
2. **Scratch/abrasion test:** Rub with coin, keys, fingernail. Document marks
3. **Functional test:** Use the product 20+ times in real conditions
4. **Color test:** Expose to sunlight for 48 hours, check fading
5. **Water test:** If relevant, submerge or spray; check for leaks
6. **Weight test:** Load to maximum intended capacity; check for deformation
7. **Assembly/disassembly test:** If product has parts, assemble and disassemble 10+ times
8. **Packaging test:** Ship a sample to yourself; check if packaging protected it
9. **Document everything:** Photograph all failures, measurements, and observations
10. **Compare to competitor:** Run same tests on competitor product for baseline

## REGULATORY COMPLIANCE TYPES

| Category | Regulation | Applies To | Key Requirements |
|---|---|---|---|
| Safety standards | CPSC | Children's toys, apparel, furniture | Product-specific standards per category |
| Substance regulations | Prop 65 (US), REACH (EU) | All consumer products | Lead content testing; chemical safety |
| Documentation | DoC (EU), GCC (US) | Imported goods | Conformity certificates at customs |
| Labeling | Various | Toys, apparel, food, cosmetics | Country-specific label requirements |
| Testing | FDA, FTC | Medical claims, cosmetics, skincare | Lab-certified testing for claims |

### CPSC Regulatory Robot
- Tool at cpsc.gov to identify which regulations apply to your specific product
- Input product category and get applicable standards, testing requirements, and documentation needs
- Use BEFORE placing first order to avoid Jason Wong's condom story scenario

### When to Consult Professionals
- Products making health/medical claims: always consult regulatory lawyer
- Products for children under 12: mandatory third-party testing
- Products containing batteries/electronics: shipping and safety regulations
- Food and beverages: FDA registration and labeling compliance
- Cosmetics/skincare: ingredient disclosure and safety testing
