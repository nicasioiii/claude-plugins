## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICES

### Disagreement 1: Minimum Markup Rule

| Position | Advocate | Core Argument |
|---|---|---|
| **2.5x manufacturing cost** | Gretta Van Riel | Accommodates wholesale at 50% discount. $20 cost -> $50 retail. |
| **4x landed cost** | Matt Clark | Dollar margin must cover $10-$20+ Facebook CAC. |
| **5x total cost** | Jason Wong | COGS as 20% of retail = multiply by 5. Most conservative. |
| **30%+ after ALL fees** | Melisa Vong | Amazon-specific: must clear referral fees, FBA, PPC. |

**RESOLUTION:** Use 4x landed cost for paid-ads-driven DTC brands (Matt's context). Use 2.5x for organic/influencer-first brands (Gretta's context). For Amazon, use Melisa's 30%+ after-fees rule. Jason's 5x formula is the most conservative and safest default. Always calculate fully loaded unit economics including fulfillment, platform fees, returns, and marketing allocation.

### Disagreement 2: Profit-First vs. LTV-First Acquisition

| Position | Advocate | Core Argument |
|---|---|---|
| **Profit now** | Jason Wong | "Revenue doesn't pay the bills, profit does." Bootstrapped founders need positive cash flow. |
| **LTV-based spending** | Matt Clark | Spend up to 12-month LTV on acquisition to outspend competitors. LifeBoost proved this. |

**RESOLUTION:** Start with day-one break-even (Jason's approach). Once you have repeat purchase data, gradually extend to 2-month, 6-month, 12-month payback (Matt's approach). LTV-based spending requires capital reserves and subscription/repeat purchase data to execute safely.

## MARKUP RULES BY PLATFORM & CHANNEL

### DTC / Shopify (Paid Ads as Primary Channel)
**Use Matt Clark's 4x landed cost minimum.**
- Critical nuance: the DOLLAR MARGIN matters more than the multiplier
- Example: $3 product at 4x = $12, but $9 margin may not cover $10-$20+ CAC
- Solution for low-cost products: increase AOV through bundles/multi-packs
- Rule of thumb: you need enough dollar margin to cover acquisition costs

### DTC / Shopify (Organic/Influencer Primary Channel)
**Use Gretta's 2.5x manufacturing cost minimum.**
- $20 cost -> $50 retail
- Accommodates wholesale (50% discount = $25 wholesale on $20 cost = $5 margin -- tight)
- Works when CAC is near-zero from organic traffic

### Amazon FBA
**Use Melisa Vong's 30%+ profit after ALL fees.**
- Must account for: referral fee (8-15%), FBA fees, PPC spend, storage fees, returns
- Take top 15 competitor price points, remove outliers, calculate average
- Do not be cheapest (perceived low quality) or most expensive (lose sales)
- Position in competitive middle range

### Jason Wong's 5x Formula (Conservative Default)
**Safest default when uncertain about channel mix.**
- Add all variable costs per product
- Target those costs as 20% of final retail price (multiply by 5)
- Example: $12.28 total cost x 5 = $61.40 base price
- Then verify fixed costs (rent, software, insurance, labor) are covered

## COGS BREAKDOWN

### Gretta's Complete COGS Categories (11 Items)
1. Production costs (price per unit x units)
2. Freight shipping costs (factory to fulfillment center)
3. Picking and packing costs (3PL fees)
4. Storage fees (warehouse holding costs)
5. Transaction fees (PayPal, Stripe, bank fees)
6. Software costs (store hosting, email, website)
7. Staff costs (customer service, operations)
8. Branding and graphic design
9. Product inspections
10. Legal advice
11. Product samples

### Jason Wong's Variable Cost Categories
1. **COGS** -- price paid to manufacturer per unit
2. **Freight costs** -- factory to fulfillment (FOB only covers factory to seaport)
3. **Receiving fees** -- 3PL unloading, stocking, barcoding, kitting
4. **Transaction fees** -- 2.1%-2.9% of order + $0.30 per transaction (extra 1% if non-Shopify payment processor)
5. **Fulfillment fees** -- pick-and-pack costs
6. **Postage fees** -- shipping label cost (varies by weight, dimensions, distance)

## GROSS PROFIT MARGIN

### Formula and Targets
```
GPM = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue x 100
```
- Minimum target: 30%
- Ideal target: 60%
- SkinnyMeTea first year: ~80% (but was not reinvesting enough into growth)
- Jason Wong benchmark: 25% gross profit as percentage of total revenue

### "Profit Margin Is Your Mistakes Margin" (Gretta)
- Higher margin = more room for error and calculated risks
- Higher margin = can spend more to acquire customers
- Higher margin = can negotiate bulk deals with manufacturer
- Warning: "$100K in sales with 10% profit margin = only $10K actual profit"

### Three Tactics When Margin Is Too Low
1. **Cut production costs** -- find different manufacturer if needed (do not sacrifice quality)
2. **Bundle** -- pair low-margin items with high-margin items (themed packs, upsells, cross-sells)
3. **Raise the price** -- start high, reduce later based on market feedback

## PSYCHOLOGICAL PRICING

### Three Variations (Gretta)
- Limit to 3 price tiers to reduce decision fatigue; base on VALUE tier ($20, $40, $60)
- **Anchor pricing:** show expensive option to make mid-tier seem reasonable
  - SkinnyMeTea: 14-day pack ($35) vs. 28-day pack ($65) -- better daily value
  - Doe Lashes: $100 twin pack (anchor) vs. $55 bundle of 5 vs. $12.50 single
- **Dollar Shave Club effect:** make perceived value ($220) seem like a bargain price

### Wholesale Consideration
- Need 2.5x minimum because wholesale requires ~50% discount
- $50 retail / 2 = $25 wholesale (with $20 cost = only $5 margin)

## AMAZON PRICING TACTICS

### Competitive Pricing Method (Melisa Vong)
- Take top 15 competitor price points
- Remove outliers (cheapest and most expensive)
- Calculate average -- position within competitive range
- Do not be cheapest or most expensive

### Launch Pricing Strategy
- Launch at lower price or break-even to build sales velocity
- Once consistent sales established, increase to profitable price point
- Under $10 pricing: may qualify for 8-12% referral fee (vs. standard 15%) and "under $10" filters

### Price Testing
- Track daily sales at each price point
- Change price, monitor sales changes
- Huge sales drop = price too high
- A good price today may not be good tomorrow -- monitor continuously

### Anti-Price War Warning (Jason Wong)
Do NOT try to be cheapest in the market. Price wars turn markets into buyer's markets and take a year or longer to recover from.

## EXPENSE OPTIMIZATION (Jason Wong -- 5 Strategies)

1. **Negotiate with every PO increase:** Lower per-unit price, bulk discounts, lower price for upfront payment, lower MOQ, better payment terms
2. **Monthly expense audits:** Review every service; distinguish essential vs. nice-to-have; check market rates
3. **Break down cost of delivery:**
   - Lighten primary and secondary packaging
   - Choose sea freight over air for non-urgent shipments ($195 vs. $1,000)
   - Negotiate pick-and-pack fees with competing 3PL quotes
4. **Prioritize capital to high-ROI activities with short payback:** Advertising returns money faster than R&D or inventory
5. **Hire only when necessary:** Start with freelancers, transition to full-time when budget allows. Full-time employee true cost: 1.5x base salary

## LTV-BASED CPA TARGETING (Matt Clark)

### Progressive CPA Expansion
Start conservative, expand as data builds:

| Stage | CPA Target | When to Use |
|---|---|---|
| Day-one break-even | AOV x Gross Margin % | Default starting point |
| 2-month payback | Factor in 2-month cumulative LTV | After 60+ days of repeat purchase data |
| 6-month payback | Factor in 6-month cumulative LTV | Established repeat purchase patterns |
| 12-month payback | Factor in 12-month cumulative LTV | Subscription brands with proven retention |

**LifeBoost Example:**
- Month 1 AOV: ~$70
- 12-month cumulative LTV: $166
- At 50% margin: $83 LTV profit
- Could spend up to $83 per customer and break even in 12 months

### Ad Spend Benchmarks by Stage
- Testing phase: $5-10/day per platform
- Early scaling: 15-35% of revenue
- Long-term target: 30-35% of revenue
- With 50% gross margin and 35% ad spend: 15% left for operating expenses + compensation

## CASH CONVERSION CYCLE

### Formula
```
CCC = DIO + DSO - DPO
DIO = Days Inventory Outstanding
DSO = Days Sales Outstanding
DPO = Days Payable Outstanding
```

### Benchmarks
- Typical DTC: 40-100 days
- Gymshark: negative 101 days (suppliers effectively fund inventory)
- At $3,000/day sales, CCC of 40-100 days = $120K-$300K cash stuck in operations

### Three Levers to Improve CCC
1. **Increase AP (pay later):** Negotiate vendor credit; if you sell before credit expires, vendor funds your purchases
2. **Reduce AR (get paid faster):** Set Shopify payouts to DAILY. Pre-orders, batch ordering, annual memberships for negative AR
3. **Reduce inventory:** Ask vendors to hold inventory for later shipment. Audit and eliminate underperforming SKUs

## DAILY AD TRACKING REPORT (Matt Clark)

Track these fields daily to maintain visibility on profitability. Full template structure in ref-financial-formulas.md.

**Store-level daily:** Total sales, orders, AOV, estimated gross profit %, total ad spend.
**Per channel (Facebook, Google, etc.):** Daily spend, clicks, CPC, orders (platform-reported), orders (third-party), revenue, AOV, CPA, profit/loss calculation.

**Key rule:** Compare platform-reported conversions vs. third-party tracking. Consistent directional difference = investigate. Random bouncing = less concerning (timing/attribution).

## BUILDING FOR PROFIT (Jason Wong)

- "Revenue is vanity. Revenue doesn't pay the bills, profit does."
- Average e-commerce company sells for 3x EBITDA. Low net profit = low sale price
- Supplier commitment discount: commit to 1-year relationship for 10% per-unit cost reduction
- Bootstrapped founders should aim to profit now, not later
- LTV-based spending requires capital reserves and is difficult for bootstrapped businesses
