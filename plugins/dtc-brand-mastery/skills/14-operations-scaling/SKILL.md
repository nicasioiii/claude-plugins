## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICES

### Disagreement: Hiring Timing
| Position | Advocate | Core Argument |
|---|---|---|
| **Automate before hiring** | Jason Wong | Hiring too early is a common mistake. Systematize manually first, then automate, then hire. |
| **Build systems before hiring** | Eli Lipshatz (Escala) | Most sellers hire for urgency, not scale. Each reactive hire adds complexity without systems to support them. Pause to build systems first. |
| **Hire for speed** | Matt Clark | At scale ($600K+/month), senior hires accelerate growth. Top-down hiring absorbs their knowledge from past companies. |
| **RESOLUTION** | -- | Below $100K/month: freelancers and automation first (Jason + Escala are right). Above $200K/month: full-time hires become necessary (Matt's approach). The Escala methodology applies at ALL revenue levels -- build systems before or alongside hiring. |

## 1. DEMAND FORECASTING FRAMEWORK (Jason Wong)

### Sales Velocity vs. Simple Average
- Use sales velocity (rate of sales omitting out-of-stock days) rather than simple average
- If you include stockout days, you underestimate future demand
- Track velocity per SKU separately

### Seasonality Adjustment
- Do not use October-December data for January forecasting -- those are holiday outliers
- Build separate seasonal models for Q4 vs. rest of year
- Account for promotional spikes vs. organic baseline

### Forecasting Formula
`Previous sales + seasonality adjustment + average monthly growth rate = projected sales (30/60/90 days)`

### New Product Forecasting
- Look at previous new product launch patterns
- Every product gets attention at launch, then sales decrease until finding a baseline number (sustained sales without promotions)
- Use baseline, not launch spike, for ongoing forecasting

### Competitor Revenue Estimation
- Commerce Inspector: competitor best sellers, monthly revenue, traffic sources
- Order number subtraction: place orders day 1 and day 30, subtract order numbers for monthly volume, multiply by estimated AOV

### Three Benefits of Forecasting
1. **Inventory**: know exactly how much to order to conserve cash
2. **Marketing**: allocate proper budget per channel; reverse-engineer required orders/AOV/visitors
3. **Staffing**: increase customer support and fulfillment team for high-demand seasons

## 2. JASON WONG'S FOUR ZONES OF TASK MANAGEMENT

This framework determines what to delegate and in what order:

| Zone | Definition | Action | Jason's Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Incompetence** | Tasks others do better than you | Hire/outsource FIRST | Media buying, graphic design |
| **Competence** | Tasks you do fine but others can match | Hire/outsource SECOND | Influencer and social media management |
| **Excellence** | Tasks you are great at but do not love | Hire/outsource THIRD | Leading marketing team |
| **Genius** | Tasks only you can do with greatest impact | Keep FOREVER | Business development, product design, partnerships |

**Application**: List every task you do weekly. Categorize each into one of the four zones. Start delegating from Zone of Incompetence downward.

## 3. HIRING FRAMEWORK BY REVENUE LEVEL (Jason Wong)

### Revenue-Based Thresholds

| Monthly Revenue | Hiring Strategy | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Under $50K/month | Freelancers only | Not enough daily work for a team. Takes at most 1-2 hours/day to run marketing. |
| Under $100K/month | Freelancers + agencies | Spend 60% of time on hire-out jobs, 40% on growth |
| $200K/month | Transition to full-time | At 20% net margin = $40K available. Allocate ~$20K to payroll for 2-3 mid-level employees covering four pillars: marketing, operations, product, creatives |

### Full-Time Employee True Cost
- Budget 1.5x base salary (benefits, equipment, taxes, overhead)
- Example: $50K salary = $75K true cost

### Bottom-Up vs. Top-Down Hiring

| Approach | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| **Bottom-up** (junior first) | Cheaper, moldable | Slower growth, requires heavy training, founder cannot step away |
| **Top-down** (senior first) | Strategic, absorb past-company knowledge, avoid known mistakes | More expensive, need to also hire their reports |

### 8-Step Interview Process (Jason Wong)
1. Define company goals (you may think you need a media buyer but actually need a growth marketer)
2. Create ideal candidate persona (qualifications, personality, expertise)
3. Determine interview tone (casual conversation vs. structured panel)
4. Prepare questions (phone screens 20-30 min: 5-10 questions; in-person: 10-15 open-ended)
5. Outline process (skill tests, practical assignments, video links, time limits)
6. Ask different types of questions (avoid surface-level standard questions)
7. Stay in touch (weekly updates to candidates even with no decision)
8. Wait for the right person -- never hire someone who is just good enough

**Jason's Actual Interview Questions (Doe Lashes)**:
- "What would your first 30 days be spent doing?"
- "What do you prioritize?"
- "What do you do when you are asked to do something you have never done before?"
- "Can you give me an example of a new process that you implemented at work?"

## 4. ESCALA BUSINESS SYSTEMIZATION (Eli Lipshatz)

### Core Philosophy
- Most Amazon/DTC sellers who hire do so for urgency, not scale -- this creates a destructive loop
- Take 1-2 steps back to take 10 steps forward
- Goal: shift from people-reliant business to systems-reliant business
- Even implementing 50% of Escala puts you in the top 1% of e-commerce businesses

### Three Business Pillars
Every business system must address:
1. **People** -- vision, accountability chart, org chart
2. **Process** -- process maps, SOPs, wiki
3. **Technology** -- tools list, tool owners, task/project management workflows

### 5-Level Process Hierarchy
See `ref-systemization-frameworks.md` for the complete hierarchy table and implementation steps.

| Level | Name | Where Documented |
|---|---|---|
| L1 | Core Functions (departments) | Miro Masterboard |
| L2 | Processes (what teams do) | Miro Masterboard |
| L3 | Tasks & Decisions | Miro + ClickUp (bridge point) |
| L4 | Subtasks | ClickUp Wiki + Workflows |
| L5 | Instructions (step-by-step) | ClickUp Wiki |

### Three-Phase Implementation
1. **Assessment**: process walkthroughs, current state mapping, Areas For Improvement (AFI) list
2. **Design**: accountability chart, technology audit, future state process maps
3. **Integration**: build ClickUp wiki + workflows, sprint methodology for SOP documentation

### ClickUp Dual System
- **Wiki** (knowledge repository): company info, SOPs organized by L1/L2/L3, glossary, tools directory, FAQ
- **Workflows** (day-to-day operations): daily task management, reduces emails/meetings/Slack, central business hub
- Every workflow task links back to its Wiki SOP page (bidirectional)

### Sprint Methodology for SOPs
1. Choose one core process to sprint on
2. Create L4 subtask skeleton
3. Identify process owners and instruction types needed (video, screenshot, criteria, checklist, FAQ)
4. Conduct sprint kick-off meeting
5. Process owners submit work instructions (Days 1-5)
6. Transfer into Wiki SOP pages
7. Offline review (Days 6-9), make changes, finalize
- Tools: Scribe or Tango (Chrome extensions that auto-generate step-by-step guides)

## 5. TECHNOLOGY SYSTEMIZATION FRAMEWORK (Escala)

### The Technology Dilemma
- **Too little tech**: repetitive manual tasks in Excel, scattered platforms, gut-feel decisions, answer to every problem is "hire"
- **Too much tech**: workarounds compensate for lack of integrations, no single source of truth, tech spend out of control

### Three Guiding Principles
1. Work from the problem backwards, not the technology forwards
2. Less is usually more -- discriminate
3. Centralization is more important than personal preference

### Technology Audit Process
1. Audit ALL tools (check credit card statements -- monthly AND annual; expect 20+ platforms)
2. Map each tool to L1 core function and L2 process
3. Document utility (one sentence per tool-process combination)
4. Separately list every Excel/Google Sheets template (reveals upgrade opportunities)
5. Start/Stop/Continue assessment per tool
6. Retain/Remove/Add decisions -- finalize technology roadmap

### Internal Technology Owners (6 Responsibilities)
1. **In the Loop**: track new features, best practices, beta programs
2. **Relationship Building**: build rapport with account manager, manage pricing
3. **User Management**: onboarding new users, billing
4. **Learning & Development**: prepare and deliver internal upskilling sessions
5. **Quality Control**: regular auditing of usage (efficiency, effectiveness, economy)
6. **Competitor Research**: identify new tools, enhance utility

## 6. SYSTEM MAINTENANCE & CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT (Escala)

### Maintenance Philosophy
- Continuous, gradual improvement beats major intervention projects
- If team loses trust in the system, the entire system falls apart
- Fix problems on the spot (within day/week), not 90 days later

### 90-Day Quarterly Review Process
1. Current state assessment via focus groups (individual contributors, not leaders)
2. Compile master AFI list (company-wide survey)
3. Work backwards from vision to consider future state updates
4. Determine integration project owners and timeline
5. Roll out changes with FORMAL training (never just an email)

### Day-to-Day Integration Principles
- Embrace problems as improvement opportunities
- Persist on ClickUp collaboration -- no falling back to email/Slack
- Include "system orientation" as a hiring scorecard criterion
- Reward adoption (awards for ClickUp activity, process contributions)
- Focus on input metrics over output metrics

## 7. ONBOARDING SYSTEM (Escala)

### Three-Phase Onboarding Workflow (in ClickUp)
1. **Pre-Onboarding** (assigned to HR/hiring manager): contract signing, Slack announcement, Google Workspace account, ClickUp access, equipment setup
2. **General Onboarding** (assigned to new hire): company Wiki review -- history, vision, team structure, values
3. **Job-Specific Onboarding** (assigned to new hire): transpose ClickUp workflows into onboarding board, review each linked Wiki SOP, mark "Done" after each section, due dates spread across 2-week period

**Key insight**: The wiki and workflows already contain everything needed. No new content creation required. Onboarding manager needs only ~30 minutes/day after the first day.

## 8. DTC MODEL WHEEL / OMNICHANNEL EXPANSION (Matt Clark)

### Revenue Channels (Build in Sequence)
1. **Owned Channel (Shopify)** -- direct revenue, highest margin, full customer relationship
2. **Email & SMS** -- repeat purchases, 40x ROI, owned audience
3. **Paid Advertising** -- Facebook, Instagram, Google, TikTok; scale acquisition
4. **Organic Social** -- content marketing, brand building, community
5. **Influencer Marketing** -- affiliate partnerships, niche audience access
6. **Affiliate Program** -- leverage others, pay only for results
7. **Amazon** -- third-party selling (FBA/FBM), different customer base
8. **Wholesale** -- retail partnerships, higher volume, lower margins
9. **Subscription/Recurring** -- predictable cash flow, higher valuation multiple

### Revenue Distribution at Maturity
- Shopify: 40-50% (highest margin)
- Amazon: 20-30% (good volume, lower margin)
- Email/SMS: 10-15% (repeat purchases)
- Wholesale: 5-10%
- Subscription: 5-10%
- Other: 5-10%

### Amazon + TikTok Funnel Integration (Tim Jordan)
- Amazon over-indexes on purchase intent; TikTok over-indexes on awareness/discovery
- Combining both creates a stable funnel
- Amazon Brand Referral Bonus: 10% gross sales back for external traffic with attribution
- Stop being "Amazon sellers" -- become "product sellers who use Amazon"

## 9. THREE KEY LEVERS FOR REVENUE GROWTH (Jason Wong)

1. **Traffic**: increase visitors (ads, organic, email, influencers, SEO)
2. **Conversion Rate**: increase % who purchase (product page, social proof, pricing, friction reduction). 1% to 2% conversion = 100% revenue increase at same traffic.
3. **AOV**: increase revenue per order (prices, bundles, upsells, free shipping threshold). Easiest lever -- no new customers required.

## 12. PROJECT MANAGEMENT & OPERATIONS TOOLS

### Core Project Management
- **Asana** or **Monday.com**: operational workflows
- **Alloy**: workflow automation (triggers and conditions)
- Map key workflows before automating
- Systematize manually FIRST, then automate

### Inventory Management
- Track inventory turnover ratio
- Replenishment scheduling aligned with demand forecast
- Return rate indicates product or expectation issues
- Budget rule: 2.5x initial inventory cost (Melisa Vong)
- 3PL for overflow when Amazon limits inbound inventory

### Key Metrics Dashboard (Jason Wong)
- **Acquisition**: CAC, CPA, ROAS
- **Retention**: repeat purchase rate, LTV, churn
- **Product**: conversion rate, AOV, return rate
- **Operations**: fulfillment time, support response time
- **Financial**: contribution margin, payback period, burn rate
- Trailing data beats real-time for strategy -- do not overreact to daily fluctuations

### Analytics Tools
- Tydo: performance analytics dashboard
- Google Analytics: website analytics
- Shopify analytics: built-in order/sales data
- UTM parameters for channel attribution
- Model multi-touch attribution (not just last-click)

## 13. AI AGENTS & AUTOMATION (Steve Simonson, Mark Degrasse, Clement Wan)

### AI Agent Architecture for E-commerce (Priority Order)
1. **Brand Bot** (website chatbot): 24/7, trained on product knowledge, FAQs, warranties
   - Klarna case study: 66% of inquiries (2.3M chats) routed to AI in one month, equivalent to 700 agents
   - Equal satisfaction scores to humans; 25% fewer repeat inquiries
2. **Telephone Agent Bot** (Bland.AI): 9 cents/minute, answers calls 24/7
3. **Internal Database Chatbot**: talk to your ERP/data ("What was my top seller yesterday?")
4. **Bot Army** (multi-agent systems): manager bot delegates to specialist bots for hashtag research, SEO, content creation
   - Tools: n8n.io (visual bot builder), CrewAI.com (code-based multi-agent)

### AI Brand Blueprint (Mark Degrasse)
- Define 9 Brand Pillars (name, slogan, values, customer avatar, brand avatar, etc.) loaded into GPT
- AI maintains brand consistency even when agencies change
- Vision must be Ambitious + Quantifiable (with deadline and number)

### No-Code App Building for Sellers (Clement Wan)
- Stack: ChatGPT/Claude ($20/mo) + Replit ($20/mo) + Cursor ($20/mo) + Vercel ($20/mo) = $60-80/month
- Replaces custom developer for landing pages, coupon code distribution, API integrations
- Describe what you want as if talking to a tech friend

## ANTI-PATTERNS

1. **Hiring for urgency, not scale** -- each reactive hire adds complexity without systems (Escala)
2. **Hiring too early** -- automate first, then systematize, then hire (Jason Wong)
3. **Not documenting SOPs** -- makes hiring, scaling, and exit much harder (Melisa Vong)
4. **Falling back to email/Slack** instead of using project management tool
5. **Technology sprawl** -- adding tools without auditing what you already have
6. **Overreacting to daily data** -- use trailing averages for strategic decisions
7. **Scaling before unit economics work** -- many brands scale unprofitably
8. **Single-channel dependency** -- build multiple revenue channels in sequence
