# Systemization Frameworks & Hiring Reference

## 1. ESCALA 5-LEVEL PROCESS HIERARCHY (Complete)

| Level | Name | Description | Example | Where Documented |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| L1 | Core Functions | Teams/departments | Brand Management | Miro Masterboard |
| L2 | Processes | What teams do | Product Listing Creation | Miro Masterboard |
| L3 | Tasks & Decisions | How processes are completed | Perform Keyword Research | Miro + ClickUp (overlap) |
| L4 | Subtasks | Breakdown of each task | Run Helium 10 X-Ray Extension | ClickUp Wiki + Workflows |
| L5 | Instructions | How to complete each subtask | Step-by-step with screenshots | ClickUp Wiki |

**Key rule**: L1-L3 documented on Miro (Escala Masterboard). L3-L5 documented in ClickUp (wiki + workflows). L3 is the bridge point.

### Standard Amazon Private Label L1 Core Functions
1. **1.0 Research & Development** -- product ideation, product research
2. **2.0 Supply Chain Management** -- sourcing, logistics, inventory
3. **3.0 Brand & Product Management** -- listing creation, marketing strategies, PPC, optimization
4. **4.0 Product & Customer Support** -- customer service, returns, reviews

### DTC/Shopify L1 Core Functions (Extended)
1. **1.0 Product & R&D** -- ideation, development, sourcing
2. **2.0 Supply Chain** -- manufacturing, logistics, inventory, fulfillment
3. **3.0 Marketing** -- paid ads, organic, influencer, email, content
4. **4.0 Sales & Revenue** -- Shopify store, Amazon, wholesale, subscriptions
5. **5.0 Customer Experience** -- support, returns, reviews, community
6. **6.0 Finance & Admin** -- bookkeeping, legal, HR

## 2. ESCALA MASTERBOARD STRUCTURE (Miro)

Central visual artifact containing:
1. **Organizational Chart** -- who reports to whom
2. **Accountability Chart** -- who is accountable for what (different from org chart)
3. **High-Level Process Map** -- L1 core functions and L2 processes with relationships
4. **L3 Process Maps** -- detailed maps with swim lanes showing role assignments
5. **L3 Tasks & Decisions** -- specific task boxes that link to ClickUp

**Numbering system**: Everything gets a hierarchical code (1.0, 1.1, 3.1.4, etc.) for traceability.
**Clickable links**: L3 task boxes on Miro link directly to ClickUp tasks/wiki pages.

## 3. CLICKUP HIERARCHY MAPPING

| ClickUp Level | Maps To | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Spaces | L1 Core Functions (+ HR/Admin) | "3.0 Brand & Product Management" |
| Folders | L2 Processes | "3.1 Product Listing Creation" |
| Lists | L3 Tasks | "3.1.4 Keyword Research" |
| Tasks | L3 Tasks with detail | "Perform keyword research and submit to copywriter" |
| Subtasks | L4 Subtasks | "Run Helium 10 X-Ray extension" |

### Building ClickUp Structure
1. Build spaces, folders, lists first (skeleton)
2. Do not go to subtask level until sprint methodology kicks in
3. Customize statuses per list/folder/task
4. Expect iteration -- perfection not required on first pass
5. Link every task to its corresponding Wiki page (bidirectional)

## 5. SPRINT KICK-OFF PROCESS & TIMELINE

### Sprint Management Board
- Created from L3 process maps
- Contains outline of L4 subtasks
- Documents needed instructions and files for SOPs
- Validated with team during Sprint Kick-Off

### Sprint Kick-Off Steps
1. Choose one core process to sprint on
2. Create L4 subtask skeleton
3. Identify process owners and specific instruction types needed (video, screenshot, criteria, checklist, FAQ)
4. Prepare Sprint Kick-Off deck
5. Schedule and conduct kick-off meeting with involved team
6. Repeat for each core process

### Sprint Timeline
- Days 1-5: Process owners submit work instructions; transfer into Wiki SOP pages; fill in policies, criteria, guidelines; collaborate with process owners
- Days 6-9: Request offline review; make changes based on review comments
- Day 10: Finalize

### After Sprints
1. Add L4 subtasks to Wiki (copy from Sprint Management Board)
2. Add subtasks to Workflows (review Sprint Board, add to ClickUp Workflows)
3. Link Workflows to Wiki (paste Wiki page links into workflow descriptions)

## 7. MAINTENANCE PHILOSOPHY & UPDATE AUTHORIZATION

### Continuous Improvement Rules
- Fix problems on the spot (within day/week), not 90 days later
- As soon as team loses trust in the system, the entire system falls apart
- Manage expectations: system will not be perfect after initial implementation

### Update Authorization
- Define formal approvers for each component
- Approver must understand their area AND the impact to other business areas
- Example: changing PPC optimization process must consider inventory/supply chain impact

### Day-to-Day Integration Checklist
1. **AFIs**: embrace problems as improvement opportunities; use process maps and SOPs as workshop tools
2. **Adoption**: persist on ClickUp collaboration; no falling back to email/Slack; ClickUp must be first app opened daily
3. **Hiring**: include "system orientation" as scorecard criterion; hire process-driven people
4. **Reward adoption**: awards for ClickUp activity, process map contributions; input metrics over output metrics
5. **Assess**: highlight efficiency improvements to team; understand what works and does not from Escala methodology

## 8. ONBOARDING WORKFLOW STRUCTURE (in ClickUp)

### Phase 1: Pre-Onboarding (assigned to HR/hiring manager)
- Contract signing
- New hire Slack announcement
- Pre-onboarding to-do list sent to new hire
- Create Google Workspace account
- Add to ClickUp workspace
- Work setup (computer, desk, or remote perks)

### Phase 2: General Onboarding (assigned to new hire)
- "Get to know you / get to know our company" session with COO
- New hire form
- Company Wiki review (company history, vision, team structure)

### Phase 3: Job-Specific Onboarding (assigned to new hire)
- Transpose existing ClickUp workflows into onboarding board
- New hire reviews each Wiki SOP linked from the workflow
- Click "Done" after reading each section
- Checklists for verification
- Due dates spread across 2-week onboarding period
- 30-minute daily meeting with onboarding manager for Q&A

## 9. TECHNOLOGY MASTER LIST AUDIT PROCESS

### Step-by-Step
1. **Audit all tools**: check credit card statements (monthly AND annual charges); expect 20+ platforms minimum
2. **Map each tool** to L1 core function and L2 process -- where is each tool used?
3. **Document utility**: one sentence per tool-process combination
4. **Special Excel treatment**: list Excel/Google Sheets as a separate tool and document every template; reveals opportunities to move to bespoke platforms
5. **Start/Stop/Continue assessment**: decide for each tool/feature
6. **Retain/Remove/Add decisions**: finalize technology roadmap

### Delegation/Automation Index
- Review accountability chart for high D/A index scores (accountabilities that can be automated)
- Cross-reference master AFI list for items technology can solve
- Record D/A index scores in master tech list

### Two Most Common Technology Upgrades
1. Moving processes away from Excel into bespoke technology platforms
2. Consolidating multiple tools doing the same thing into a single platform

**Caution**: Excel is NOT always the problem. Sometimes Excel is more efficient than a bespoke tool. Do not apply a blanket rule.

## 10. AMAZON SELLER HERO JOURNEY (Failure Pattern to Avoid)

1. **Side hustle phase**: launch product, hit first $10K/mo, then $100K/mo
2. **Full-time transition**: quit job, launch 2nd/3rd products, consider hiring
3. **Amazon Treadmill**: too much to do, cannot go out of stock, cannot slow down
4. **Crisis point**: three options only -- automate, delegate, or implode
5. **Hiring-for-urgency loop**: hire VAs reactively, realize they are inefficient, need systems, no time to build systems, hire again -- repeat until implosion

**The Escala Circuit Breaker**: Take 1-2 steps back to take 10 steps forward. Shift from people-reliant to systems-reliant business.

## 11. HIRING DECISION TREE BY REVENUE LEVEL

```
Monthly Revenue < $50K?
  YES -> Freelancers only (Upwork, Fiverr)
  NO -> Continue

Monthly Revenue < $100K?
  YES -> Freelancers + agencies; 60% on hire-out jobs, 40% on growth
  NO -> Continue

Monthly Revenue < $200K?
  YES -> Begin transitioning from freelancers to full-time
         Identify Zone of Incompetence tasks first
  NO -> Continue

Monthly Revenue $200K+?
  YES -> 2-3 mid-level full-time employees (~$20K payroll)
         Four pillars: marketing, operations, product, creatives
         Consider top-down hiring for strategic acceleration
```

### Interview Red Flags
- Candidate cannot articulate first-30-day plan
- Only has surface-level answers to process questions
- Cannot give examples of processes they have implemented
- Does not ask questions about the role or company
