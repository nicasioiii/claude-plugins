## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICES

### Disagreement: Pre-Launch vs. Speed

| Position | Advocate | Core Argument |
|---|---|---|
| **Audience-first** | Gretta Van Riel | Build audience months before product exists. The Fifth had 70K followers and 8,800 email waitlist before launch, generating $100K+ day one. |
| **Speed-first** | Matt Clark | 12-week sprint; 6-month total timeline. "Action trumps everything else nine times out of ten." |
| **Speed-first** | Jason Wong | Opportunity cost of delayed launch outweighs imperfection. |

**RESOLUTION:** If you have social media skills and time, audience-first yields lowest CAC (Gretta's approach). If you have capital, Matt's speed approach works. Jason is right that delayed launches cost more than imperfect ones. Route by available resources and skills.

## PRE-LAUNCH AUDIENCE BUILDING

### Gretta's Market-Product Fit Funnel
Build audience BEFORE product exists. She calls it "Market-Product Fit" (not Product-Market Fit).

**5-Stage Funnel:**
1. **Content Community** -- create content around problem/niche before product exists
2. **Engaged Audience** -- grow following with consistent, valuable content (engagement rate > follower count)
3. **Email List** -- convert social followers to email subscribers; launch waitlist (target: 500-8,800+ before launch)
4. **Paying Customers** -- convert email list to buyers on day one
5. **Brand Advocates** -- turn customers into repeat buyers and referrers

### Gretta's Case Studies

**Dropbottle (Audience-First):**
- Noticed Pinterest trend: fruit-infused waters (not yet on Instagram)
- Created @detoxwater account; reposted Pinterest images with credit + hashtags
- Result: 800K+ followers in months
- Analyzed audience demographics, created personas, reverse-engineered product
- Oprah's team found Dropbottle on social -> Oprah's Favorite Things -> Amazon front page

**The Fifth Watches (Pre-Launch):**
- Pre-launch: giveaways, shout-outs, influencer marketing
- Results: 70,000+ Instagram followers, 8,800+ email waitlist
- Sent 50 watch samples to 50 influencers before launch to create content and hype
- Launch day (December 5): 1,000+ watches sold, $100K+ revenue

## DTC / SHOPIFY LAUNCH STRATEGY

### Matt Clark's 12-Week Launch Timeline

| Weeks | Phase | Key Activities |
|---|---|---|
| 1-2 | Product & positioning | Validate Four Criteria, source supplier, define persona |
| 2-4 | Store build | Shopify setup, photography, copy, payment, email capture |
| 3-5 | Brand development | Logo, social accounts, brand guidelines, first content |
| 5-6 | Initial testing | 3-5 ad creatives, Facebook/Instagram at $100-500, build email list |
| 6-8 | Launch prep | Inventory order, email automation, influencer outreach, customer service |
| 8-10 | Soft launch | Email list early access, influencer activations, limited paid ads, feedback |
| 10-12 | Full launch | Inventory arrives, scale winning ads, full email automation, organic social |

**Expected Week 12 outcomes:** 100-1,000+ email subscribers, initial ad data, 50-500 customers, revenue run rate visible.

### Matt Clark's 6-Month Timeline (End-to-End)
- Month 1: Find product, find supplier, order samples, finish branding, place bulk order
- Months 2-3: Manufacturing (45-60 days)
- Months 4-5: Shipping from China (45-60 days)
- Month 6: Launch -- all marketing, promotions, everything live
- Domestic sourcing can compress manufacturing + shipping significantly

### Initial Traffic Generation Sequence (Matt Clark)
1. Create big discount code (e.g., "LAUNCH20" for 20% off) with urgency
2. Post on brand social media with professional photos + hashtags
3. Boost best-performing organic post ($100-200 Facebook, $50-100 Instagram)
4. Post on personal social media (friends, family, LinkedIn)
5. Create simple Google Search ad for brand + product name ($100-200)
6. Notify past customers through every channel
7. Ask for reviews 3-5 days after purchase; use best reviews in marketing

**Traffic goals:** 500-1,000 visitors, 10-20 initial sales, 50-100 email subscribers.

## AMAZON LAUNCH STRATEGY

### Launch Budget (Melisa Vong)
- Budget for inventory should be **2.5x initial inventory run cost**
- Example: $5,000 budget / 2.5 = $2,000 for first inventory order
- Reserve remainder for reorder before stock runs out
- Start with 200-1,000 units depending on product cost and budget

### Amazon Honeymoon Period
- New listings get a ranking boost as Amazon tests placement
- No historical data = Amazon relies heavily on listing text + image content for initial placement
- **Critical: have perfectly optimized listing BEFORE launch**
- Semantic relevance determines initial keyword placement
- Cross-reference: use `08-amazon-listing` to optimize before launching

### Vine Review Strategy

**Six-Star Review Hack:**
- Vine reviewers see basic product image when claiming
- What they receive: premium gift box packaging + bonus complementary product
- Include insert card with tips/instructional video (value-add, not review solicitation)
- Bonus product ideas: complementary item (e.g., baby bottle warmer + copy of "Good Night Moon")
- Result: reviewers are delighted, high probability of 5-star reviews

**Vine Stacking (Variation Method):**
- Create many size/color variations
- Each variation gets up to 30 Vine units
- Example: 10 colors x 2 sizes = 20 variations x 30 = 600 potential Vine reviews
- Hold off on merging variations until you see which get good reviews

### Surprise Insert Strategy (Bradley Sutton)
- Use Helium 10's "frequently bought together" data to find cheap complementary products
- Include as unadvertised bonus ($0.20-0.25 per unit, e.g., spooky stickers with coffin shelf)
- Drives review velocity: reviewers mention the surprise bonus
- Creates "six-star experience" (Tomer Rabinovich concept)

### "Lowest Price in 30 Days" Badge Hack
1. List product at higher-than-target price before inventory arrives
2. When inventory is active, lower price
3. Amazon awards red "lowest price in last 30 days" badge
4. Badge makes listing taller on mobile + catches deal shoppers
5. Stack with green coupon badge (% off or $ off) for double visibility
6. Some buyers purchase without clicking coupon = full price sale with badge benefit

### Time-Release Launch Strategy (Izabela Hamilton)
Case study: $800K/month to $2M/month in 5 months (140% revenue growth).

**Three hidden levers Amazon uses to pick winners:**
1. **Velocity control** -- steady acceleration, not one-time spike
2. **Shopper behavior signals** -- dwell time, scroll depth, compare-then-return, add-to-cart patterns
3. **Traffic authority injection** -- external traffic from Google, TikTok, blogs

**Execution:**
- Spread purchases across days/weeks showing consistent growth (20 > 30 > 40 orders/day)
- Do NOT blast entire email list at once; stagger to show Amazon consistent upward trend
- Review velocity matching: maintain 1-10% review rate relative to orders
  - 100% review rate = suspension for manipulation
  - Near-zero review rate = de-ranking
- Month-by-month rollout: (1) Time-release ranking, (2) External traffic, (3) Competitor keyword targeting, (4-5) Competitor brand name targeting

### Social Media Promo Code Sales Spikes (Jon Derkits)
Took products from 2 units/day to 360 units/day using social media promo codes.

**Process:**
1. Gradually increase price in days/weeks before promotion
2. Offer 40%+ discount (50% is sweet spot for Facebook deal groups to pick up organically)
3. Check box "make promo code available to Associates and Influencers" (critical)
4. Cultivate relationships with Facebook deal group admins
- Sales spike creates halo effect -- elevated sales persist after promotion

## TIKTOK SHOP LAUNCH STRATEGY

TikTok Shop launch is a **22-step sequence** -- see ref-launch-checklists.md for full walkthrough.

**Key launch principles:**
- Launch via creator content + GMV Max ads (not brand-produced content alone)
- Start with Video Shopping Ads if limited content; graduate to GMV Max once converting content exists
- Import reviews from Amazon via Shopify/Yotpo pipeline before launch
- Run 3 simultaneous promotions from day one: free shipping + discount + coupon

## REVIEW STRATEGY (ALL PLATFORMS)

### Compliant Amazon Review Generation (5 Methods)
1. **Amazon Vine Program** -- Brand Registry required; product must be excellent (can't control review quality)
2. **Request a Review Button** -- 100% TOS-safe; in order details; automate with Helium 10 (trigger 7 days post-delivery)
3. **Product Inserts** -- Direct to review funnel; "Our small, family-owned business relies on your reviews... If not satisfied, email support@yourbrand.com"
4. **High Quality Products** -- Best long-term strategy; naturally generates positive reviews
5. **Brand Registry Customer Contact** -- Contact customers who left 3-star or lower; offer courtesy refund

### Review Policy Warnings (Amazon)
- NO incentivized reviews (financial reward, discount, free products for reviews)
- NO friends/family reviews (Amazon tracks IP addresses, WiFi connections, Facebook friends lists)
- NO diverting negative reviews while sending positives to Amazon
- NO manipulation through variation review aggregation
- NO review solicitation in packaging/inserts
- Amazon tracks geographic clustering (if business in Miami and early reviews all from Miami = flagged)
- Set Facebook friends list to private

### Free UGC Strategy for Reviews (Gracey Ryback)
1. Reach out to existing customers with promo code incentive (50-100% off)
2. In exchange: photo/video with product, share to social media
3. Create branded hashtag for tracking
4. Vet content, reach out to best creators for usage rights
5. Repurpose on Amazon listing, social media, paid ads -- all at no cost

### Review Importing Pipeline (Amazon -> TikTok Shop)
Three-step process: Amazon Reviews -> Shopify/Website (via Helium 10 export) -> TikTok Shop (via Yotpo).

A website is REQUIRED as intermediary -- cannot import directly from Amazon to TikTok Shop. Full step-by-step process in ref-launch-checklists.md.

### Review Benchmarks
- Younger shoppers expect 150-200 reviews; older shoppers content with < 100
- Minimum 10 reviews before scaling PPC campaigns
- Time-release review velocity: maintain 1-10% review rate relative to orders

## LAUNCH ANTI-PATTERNS
- Launching without optimized listing (wastes honeymoon period)
- Blasting entire email list on day one (undermines time-release strategy)
- Scaling PPC before 10+ reviews ("throwing money into a fire pit")
- Incentivizing reviews in any form (suspension risk)
- Ordering more than MOQ on first order (Gretta: "I've definitely been burned")
- Skipping test orders before marketing spend
- Launching GMV Max ads without proven converting content
- Ignoring geographic clustering in early reviews
