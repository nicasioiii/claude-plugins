## CONTENT STRATEGY FRAMEWORK (Gretta Van Riel)

### The 3 C's of Community
1. **Content** -- must do three things: add value, build trust, inspire action
2. **Collaboration** -- secret weapon for social growth
3. **Consistency** -- post every day, no exceptions

**Content maxim:** "Content is king, context is queen, and engagement is the kingdom."

### Engagement as Self-Perpetuating Cycle
- More engagement = more reach assigned by algorithm = more people see content = more engagement
- Posts getting 20%+ more engagement than average = your Top Performing Content (TPC)
- Weight content heavily toward TPC

### Content Territories (Pillars) Framework
Define 3-5 topic categories for all content across social, blog, email, ads.

**Two universal territories that work for any brand:**
1. Products & Promotions
2. Celebrating community/customers

Map remaining 2-3 territories to customer personas.

**Examples by brand:**
- **The Fifth:** Inspire & Aspire, Wandering & Well-being, Rest & Relaxation, Products & Promotions, The Fifth Fam
- **Yoga pants brand:** In the Studio, Balance & Well-being, Daily Wisdom (quotes/mantras)
- **Dropbottle:** Fruit-infused water recipes, Health/well-being/detox tips, Places & Spaces

### Top Performing Content (TPC) Method
1. Review posts; identify those getting significantly more engagement than average
2. Example: average 200 likes, one type consistently gets 600 likes = TPC
3. Use Instagram business account Insights: Content tab > See All > sort by engagement
4. Post more of what works, less of what you want to see
5. "You won't believe how many brands just keep posting content THEY want to see"

### Content Curation vs. Creation
- Curation = gathering existing content and sharing with credit
- Better than creation when starting out: access to high-quality images elevates unknown brand
- Avoid screenshots (reduces quality) -- save images directly or use Repostly app
- Credit with "via @accountname" or camera emoji + account name
- Two types to save: (1) influencer content in niche, (2) viral content in niche

### Consistency Math
- Post once/week at 300 likes = 300 interactions
- Post daily at 300 likes = 2,100 interactions per week (7x)
- Daily posting also reveals: best times to post, which content types resonate most

### Call-to-Action Tactics (12 CTAs)
1. Ask audience to comment if they agree/disagree
2. Choose left or right
3. Comment 1, 2, 3, or 4
4. Tag a friend who'd love this
5. Share with somebody who can relate
6. Swipe up to read more
7. Tap link in bio to shop
8. Incentivize: 10% off first order when joining waitlist
9. Free gift with purchase
10. "Perceptual set theory" -- people act when it feels like the next logical step
11. Ask a question that prompts debate
12. "Save this for later" (bookmark-driving CTA)

### Collaboration Tactics
1. **Mutual engagement:** agree to like/comment on each other's posts
2. **Shout-out for shout-out (S4S):** find similar-sized account in niche, both tag/mention each other
3. **Paid shout-outs:** pay larger niche/vertical accounts (topic-based, not person-based)

## INFLUENCER MARKETING FRAMEWORK

### Influencer Tier Framework (Jason Wong)

| Tier | Followers | Engagement | Cost | ROI | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mega | 1M+ | Medium (diluted) | $10K-$100K+ | Often lower | Brand awareness only |
| Macro | 100K-1M | Medium-High | $5K-$50K | Moderate | Awareness + some conversion |
| Micro | 10K-100K | High | $500-$5K | Often best | Conversions, niche targeting |
| Nano | <10K | Very high | $100-$500 or product exchange | Often highest per dollar | Authentic endorsements |

**Key insight:** "Nano-influencers often outperform macro on ROI." Offer multiple nano/micro instead of one macro.

### Influencer Selection Criteria
1. **Audience quality:** Does audience match target customer? Engagement rate 3%+? Comments genuine or bot-like? Sudden follower spikes = bought followers
2. **Demographic match:** Location, age range, interest alignment, values alignment
3. **Authenticity:** Do they actually use the product category? Will endorsement feel natural? Have they endorsed competitors? (dilutes authority)

### Campaign Structure
1. **Brief development:** clear creative guidelines, specify deliverables (posts, stories, reels), product usage suggestions, timeline/deadlines
2. **Tracking:** unique promo code per influencer; alternative: affiliate links
3. **Relationship models:** flat fee, affiliate (commission), hybrid (base + commission), product exchange (nano only)
4. **Duration:** one-off (limited impact) vs. campaign (2-4 weeks) vs. long-term partnership (ongoing, best results)

**Key insight:** "Long-term relationships > one-off campaigns." Build partnerships over time.

### Outreach Process (Jason Wong)
- Personalized DM (not template-feeling)
- Explain why the fit matters (not just follower count)
- Include product info and media kit
- Lead with relationship, not transactional ask
- Do not assume first price is final -- negotiate
- Create tiered options (video + 3 posts vs. just posts)

## TIKTOK SHOP CREATOR OUTREACH

### Volume Benchmarks
- Target: 30-50 creators saved/invited per day via Target Collaborations
- For mass outreach: 1,000-2,500 creators per day using bots
- Expected conversion rate: ~10% (outreach to completed content)
- Formula: want 100 pieces of content = reach out to ~1,000 creators

### Daily KPIs to Track
- Number of creators reached out to
- Number of sample requests received
- Number of samples shipped
- Content pending count
- Completed content count

### Creator Outreach Workflow
1. **Find and Save** -- search creators by keywords, hashtags, interests
2. **Create Target Collaboration** -- daily with 30-50 saved creators
3. **Share via IM** -- copy/paste invite message and share Target Collaboration card via DM
4. **Follow up** -- continue messaging invited creators

### What Creators Look For
- Lifestyle fit with their content
- Commission structure
- Ease of product demonstration
- Clear communication about product value and benefits

### Sample Request Management (Automated in TikTok Shop)
1. **Review tab** -- new sample requests
2. **Ready to Ship tab** -- approved requests
3. **Shipped tab** -- after shipping
4. **Content Pending tab** -- after delivery confirmed
5. **Completed Content tab** -- finished content

### Spark Code Management
- Unauthorized videos in GMV Max = shoppable posts without spark ad codes
- These represent organic converting content you cannot promote via ads
- Action: request spark code from creator or ask them to extend validity
- Check unauthorized videos regularly -- untapped ad potential

## UGC & CREATOR PLATFORMS

### Three Types of Influencer Platforms (Tim Jordan)
1. **Commission-based selling** (e.g., Levanta, Amazon Associates)
   - Brand lists product; creators earn commission per sale
   - Offer 20-30% commission (still cheaper than PPC)
   - Provide assets (photos, copy, USPs) to make creators' jobs easy
2. **Paid creator matching** (branded content deals)
   - Pay creator to create AND share on their platforms
   - Can include free sample platforms for nano/micro influencers
3. **UGC production platforms** (e.g., Join Brands, Billo, UGC Factory, InsensePro)
   - Creators make content; brand OWNS IP
   - Used for running ads on Facebook, TikTok, Amazon DSP
   - Creator shares in Type 2; brand shares in Type 3

**Flywheel effect:** Commission traffic (Levanta) -> capture non-converters via Pixel Me/DSP -> retarget with UGC (Join Brands) on Facebook/TikTok -> build custom audiences -> feed back to Amazon DSP.

### Creator Retainer Model
- $500/month for 10-20 pieces of content
- Focus commission-based compensation over flat fees for better ROI
- Build stable of 3-5 reliable creators for consistent content pipeline

### Viral UGC Content Strategy (Gracey Ryback)
- Best-performing format: Problem > Product > Solution (under 30 seconds)
- Put ad money behind whatever performs best organically
- One piece of content can become 3-10 videos with different edits, text overlays, hooks
- Do not repost creator content as TikTok Shop tagged video on brand account (violation)

### Content Repurposing Workflow
Single piece of content can go across:
- TikTok (original)
- Facebook Reels
- Instagram Reels
- YouTube Shorts
- Pinterest Idea Pins
- Amazon Influencer storefronts
- Blog embeds
- YouTube long-form compilations

**Editing tool:** CapCut (free, made by TikTok's parent company)

## INSTAGRAM AS BRAND EXTENSION (Mary Harcourt)

- 70% of consumers check Instagram before visiting brand website
- Set up business profile (not personal/creator)
- Pin 3 videos: (1) brand story, (2) product demo/commercial, (3) customer testimonial UGC
- Repost every customer story tag (builds loyalty + exposes to their audience)
- Use LinkTree for multiple destinations (Amazon, website, YouTube, newsletter)
- QR code on packaging: "Join our community" -> Instagram -> landing page funnel
- Instagram presence increases exit valuation

### TikTok SEO Hack
- Add keywords as small text on video
- TikTok algorithm reads text for categorization
- Improves discovery for product-related searches

## MARKET-PRODUCT FIT (Gretta's Audience-First Model)

Build audience BEFORE product exists. "Market-Product Fit" not "Product-Market Fit."

**Dropbottle case study:**
- Noticed Pinterest trend: fruit-infused waters
- Created @detoxwater, reposted curated content with credit
- Built 800K+ followers, then reverse-engineered product from audience demographics
- Cross-reference with 11-launch-reviews for full pre-launch funnel

**Gretta's social following:** 16M+ Instagram followers across all brands since 2012, used to launch four multi-million dollar e-commerce brands.
