## 1. ENTITY TYPES & SELECTION GUIDANCE (Jason Wong)

| Entity | Best For | Key Features |
|---|---|---|
| **Sole Proprietor** | Testing, side hustles | Simplest setup, no liability protection, personal tax filing |
| **LLC** | Most e-commerce businesses | Liability protection, pass-through taxation, flexible structure |
| **S-Corp** | Profitable businesses paying owners salary | Pass-through taxation, salary + distribution split saves self-employment tax |
| **C-Corp** | Businesses seeking investors or going public | Double taxation but required for VC funding, stock options, unlimited shareholders |

### Selection Guidance
- Start as LLC for most e-commerce businesses
- Convert to S-Corp when consistently profitable and paying yourself salary (consult accountant for breakeven point)
- Choose C-Corp only if raising institutional capital or planning IPO
- Structure for success, not just saving taxes -- many pick LLC for tax reasons but need C-Corp for investors (Jason Wong)

### Formation Timing (Melisa Vong)
- Not required immediately
- Needed when hitting $10K/month for 3 consecutive months (Amazon requires liability insurance at this level)
- LLC/business formation cost: varies by state, typically $50-$500 filing

## 2. TRADEMARK STRATEGY

### Gretta Van Riel's Practical Guidance
- Only trademark ONE thing (your brand name)
- Do research to avoid infringing on existing trademarks
- Stay away from words too close to big brands ("they will just hold you up in court")
- Do not go overboard trademarking taglines -- "not necessary especially in e-commerce"
- Owning a domain does NOT mean you own the trademark
- Trademarks are registered under classifications (SkinnyMeTea under food/drink -- a car company could use same name)

### Trademark Cost Reality (Gretta)
- SkinnyMeTea has a $2 million trademark portfolio
- One trademark cost $180,000 -- had to buy a company called "Skinny Tea" in Australia that was not even selling much tea
- "Trademarks can be really expensive and really complicated to maintain"

### Trademark Filing (Melisa Vong + Rich Goldstein)
- Cost: $500-$2,000 via lawyer
- US registration timeline: 9-12 months
- EU trademark: 3-5 months
- No specific time bar, but competitors filing first creates $10K+ cleanup costs vs. $2K standard filing
- Online legal services: LegalZoom, TrademarkYear.com
- Often first lawyer meeting is free

### Amazon Brand Registry
- Benefits: A+ Enhanced Brand Content, Vine access, Brand Analytics, customer review management, hijacker protection
- File early via Amazon IP Accelerator if trademark is not registered yet
- IP Accelerator speeds up Brand Registry access while trademark pending
- Trademark NOT required for TikTok or Amazon brand selling (but strongly recommended)

## 3. PATENT STRATEGY

### When to Patent (Rich Goldstein -- BDSS)
- US is first-to-file system; first to patent office wins (inventor notebooks no longer matter)
- Public disclosure starts 1-year clock for patent filing (but you can lose rights within that year)
- International patents: must file BEFORE any public disclosure (no grace period)
- Common mistake: "I will patent it if it sells well" -- by then often too late (2+ years)

### When NOT to Patent (Gretta Van Riel)
- "You do not need a patent unless you have some kind of scientific proprietary formula"
- "For most online stores it is an unnecessary expense that will hold up your timeline"
- Do not worry about patents unless altering one of the four product dimensions (design, function, price, time)
- Example: Lindsay from Byte Toothpaste Bits did not let patent hold her up

### Patent Costs
- Approximately $8,000 for filing (Melisa Vong)
- Provisional patent: cheaper, establishes priority date, gives 12 months to file full patent
- Defense costs can be much higher than filing costs

### IP and Exit Value
- IP assets significantly increase exit multiples
- Trademark your brand name immediately -- cheaper to file than to clean up
- Even without patents, documented trade secrets and proprietary processes add value

## 4. BUSINESS REGISTRATION REQUIREMENTS

### For Amazon
- Seller Central Professional plan: $39.99/month
- Brand Registry: requires trademark filing (or IP Accelerator enrollment)
- LLC recommended once hitting $10K/month consistently

### For TikTok Shop
- SS4 letter required (IRS confirmation of EIN)
- JPG format for document upload
- US-based social account with geo-locking requirements
- Business registration does NOT require trademark

### General
- Business bank account + credit card + PayPal
- Accounting software: QuickBooks ($5/month) or Xero
- Sales tax collection required in states where applicable (Shopify handles for most US states, quarterly remittance)

## 5. FUNDING OPTIONS (Jason Wong + Melisa Vong)

| Source | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bootstrapping** | Full ownership, no debt | Limited capital | Most first-time sellers |
| **Friends & Family** | Flexible terms, relationship-based | Can damage relationships | Early stage with trusted network |
| **Angel Investors** | Smart money + mentorship | Equity dilution | Validated product needing growth capital |
| **Venture Capital** | Large capital, connections, credibility | Significant equity dilution, loss of control | High-growth scalable businesses |
| **Debt Financing** | No equity dilution | Repayment obligation, interest | Established revenue stream |
| **Revenue-Based (Clearco)** | No equity dilution, scales with revenue | Higher effective interest rate | E-commerce doing $2K+/month revenue |
| **Charge Cards (AMEX)** | Qualifies on personal credit for new business | High interest if not paid monthly | Cash flow bridge |
| **Local Grants** | Free money | Competitive, time-consuming to apply | Early stage in supported regions |
| **Pitch Competitions** | Cash prizes + networking | Time investment, uncertain outcomes | Businesses with compelling story |

### Search Strategy for Grants
- Google "[city/state] + business grants" for local options
- Look for small business accelerators and incubators
- SBA (Small Business Administration) resources

## 6. PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENTS & EQUITY

- Clear partnership agreements prevent expensive lawsuits (Jason Wong)
- Equity split models must be documented before significant revenue
- Equity alignment matters more than salary for early-stage hires
- Get basic partnership/operating agreement via LegalZoom or lawyer
- Include: roles, responsibilities, decision authority, exit triggers, vesting schedules
- NDA + partnership agreement if multiple partners (Melisa Vong)

## 7. EXIT VALUATION FACTORS (Melisa Vong + Matt Clark)

### What Drives Valuation Multiples

| Factor | Impact on Valuation | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **YoY sales trajectory** | HIGH | Sell when on upward trajectory = highest multiple |
| **Documented SOPs** | HIGH | Systems-dependent business > people-dependent |
| **Clean financials** | HIGH | Accrual accounting preferred over cash accounting |
| **Brand assets** | MEDIUM-HIGH | Packaging, logos, content, social presence |
| **Supplier documentation** | MEDIUM | Contacts, lead times, pricing, which products manufactured where |
| **Product catalog expansion potential** | MEDIUM | Pipeline of validated product ideas |
| **IP portfolio** | MEDIUM-HIGH | Patents and trademarks significantly increase multiples |
| **Customer list / email list** | HIGH | Owned audience is one of most valuable assets |
| **Channel diversification** | HIGH | DTC business valued 2-3x more than Amazon-only by brokers |

### Valuation Multiple Ranges (Matt Clark)
- E-commerce businesses: 3x profit up to 2x revenue
- Some cases reach 20x profit
- 3x EBITDA is average (Jason Wong)
- DTC/Shopify brands command premium over Amazon-only businesses
- Subscription revenue increases multiples significantly

### Matt Clark's Asymmetric Risk Framework
- Estimate maximum realistic downside risk
- If you can survive it, move forward
- E-commerce: $5K-$10K maximum downside risk with massive upside potential
- Matt started ~20 different businesses before e-commerce worked
- "There is no failure unless you quit" -- setbacks are not failures, only quitting is

## 9. DEAL STRUCTURES

| Structure | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Full cash upfront** | Clean exit, receive 100% at closing | Seller wanting complete exit, lower risk tolerance |
| **Partial + earnout** | Percentage upfront + additional payments over 1-3 years tied to performance milestones | Higher total price but with execution risk |
| **Majority sale** | Sell 51%+ with continued involvement + future upside | Seller who wants liquidity but believes in continued growth |

### Key Considerations
- Consider tax implications of timing and structure
- Earnout risk: buyer may not execute as well as you would
- Majority sale: get clear governance terms and decision authority documented
- Retained stake: negotiate anti-dilution protections

## 10. NEGOTIATION TACTICS (Melisa Vong)

1. **Never accept first offer** -- always counter
2. **Shop around** to multiple aggregators simultaneously
3. **Create urgency**: tell aggregators "We have received an offer from someone else"
4. **Let them compete** against each other
5. **Know your walk-away number** before negotiations begin
6. **Get professional representation** -- e-commerce-specialized accountant and lawyer
7. **Document everything** -- sales dips need explanations, growth needs attribution

## 12. DONATION TAX DEDUCTION STRATEGY (Tony Brink -- BDSS)

### The Opportunity
- Most sellers dispose of returns = throwing money away
- Example: 100K units/year, 5% return rate = 5,000 units disposed = $75K loss

### Strategy
- Donate returns to charity instead of disposing
- Tax deduction = units x retail value (e.g., 5,000 units x $30 = $150K deduction)
- Deduction is NOT a credit (reduces taxable income, not dollar-for-dollar tax reduction)
- High-return categories (clothing, 20%): 20,000 units x $60 = $1.2M annual deduction
- Unused deductions roll forward 5 years

### Implementation
- Change removal address to charity
- Get tax receipts annually
- For expiring supplements: donate direct from 3PL (skip Amazon fees, lower total losses)
- Donating expiring inventory can actually make you MORE money than selling at discount

## 13. CASH FLOW vs. PROFIT vs. REVENUE

### Key Distinctions (Multiple Speakers)
- Revenue is not profit; profit is not cash flow
- Free Cash Flow = Net Profit - Net Working Capital (inventory investment)
- Most Amazon businesses do not generate much FCF; sellers make most money at exit
- Cash flow is life or death; profitability is success (Jason Wong)
- You can be profitable on paper and still die from cash flow

### Building for Profit (Jason Wong)
- Revenue is vanity, profit is sanity
- EBITDA valuation at 3x average
- Build unit economics before scaling -- many brands scale unprofitably

### Cash Flow Reinvestment (Matt Clark)
- First year: reinvest ALL profits, do not take a single penny out
- Once at $600K-$700K/month, started paying themselves
- Rule: if business makes $100K/month profit and you take $5K-$20K, it is only 5-20% and will not hurt growth

## ANTI-PATTERNS

1. **Using cash accounting instead of accrual** -- creates massive headache during exit due diligence (Melisa Vong)
2. **Not documenting SOPs** -- makes hiring, scaling, and exit much harder
3. **Delaying trademark filing** -- competitors filing first creates $10K+ cleanup costs
4. **Patenting before validating** -- expensive and delays launch for most products
5. **Taking too much money out early** -- starves growth when reinvestment matters most
6. **Single-channel business** -- Amazon-only businesses valued at 50% discount vs. DTC + Amazon
7. **No partnership agreement** -- prevents expensive lawsuits later
8. **Disposing of returns instead of donating** -- losing both the product value and the tax deduction
