## 2. VALUATION MULTIPLE RANGES BY BUSINESS TYPE

| Business Type | Multiple Range | Basis | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amazon-only (private label) | 2x-4x | Annual net profit (SDE) | Lower range for single-product, higher for diversified |
| Amazon + DTC (Shopify) | 3x-6x | Annual net profit | DTC channel adds 2-3x premium over Amazon-only |
| DTC-first with Amazon | 4x-8x | Annual net profit or EBITDA | Highest premiums for strong brand + owned audience |
| Subscription-heavy DTC | 5x-10x | Annual revenue or EBITDA | Predictable recurring revenue commands premium |
| Exceptional cases | Up to 20x | Profit | Strong brand, high growth, defensible moat |

### What Increases Your Multiple
- Year-over-year growth trajectory (selling on upswing = highest multiple)
- Channel diversification (DTC + Amazon + wholesale)
- Subscription/recurring revenue
- Strong IP portfolio (trademarks, patents)
- Documented SOPs and systems-dependent operations
- Large, engaged email/SMS list
- Product catalog with expansion potential

### What Decreases Your Multiple
- Declining sales trajectory
- Single-channel dependency
- Owner-dependent operations (no SOPs)
- Cash-basis accounting
- Undocumented supplier relationships
- Pending legal issues
- High customer concentration

## 3. AGGREGATOR LANDSCAPE OVERVIEW

### Types of Buyers
- **Amazon Aggregators**: raised capital specifically to acquire Amazon FBA brands (Thrasio model)
- **Private Equity**: broader e-commerce portfolio builders
- **Strategic Acquirers**: larger brands wanting to expand product lines or enter categories
- **Individual Buyers**: entrepreneurs looking to acquire existing businesses

### Major Platforms
- **Empire Flippers**: marketplace-style, listings vetted, earn-out options, wide buyer pool
- **Quiet Light**: brokerage model, more hands-on advisory, higher average deal size
- **FE International**: tech and e-commerce focus
- **Aggregator-direct**: approach known aggregators directly (Thrasio, Perch, Branded, etc.)

### Broker vs. Direct
- Brokers typically charge 8-15% commission
- Direct deals save commission but may get lower price without competitive bidding
- Best approach: have broker create competitive process among multiple buyers

## 4. DEAL STRUCTURE COMPARISON TABLE

| Element | Full Cash | Partial + Earnout | Majority Sale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Upfront payment** | 100% at close | 60-80% at close | Purchase price for equity portion |
| **Additional payments** | None | 20-40% over 1-3 years based on milestones | Ongoing distributions + potential future sale |
| **Seller involvement post-close** | Transition period (30-90 days) | Often required during earnout period | Continued operational role |
| **Risk to seller** | Lowest | Medium (buyer may not hit targets) | Highest (tied to continued performance) |
| **Total payout potential** | Known, fixed | Higher total if targets met | Potentially highest if business continues to grow |
| **Tax implications** | Capital gains on full amount in year of sale | Spread recognition over earnout period | Complex, consult tax professional |
| **Best for** | Clean exit, certainty-oriented sellers | Sellers confident in growth trajectory | Sellers wanting liquidity + continued upside |

## 5. NEGOTIATION SCRIPTS FOR ACQUISITION DISCUSSIONS

### Initial Response to Inbound Interest
"Thank you for reaching out. We are currently evaluating strategic options for the business. Could you share more about your acquisition criteria and typical deal structures? We are in discussions with several parties and want to ensure alignment before sharing detailed financials."

### When Receiving First Offer
"We appreciate the offer. Based on our trailing twelve months of [revenue/profit], our growth trajectory, and comparable transactions in the space, we believe the valuation should be closer to [your target]. We have received interest from other parties as well. Would you be open to discussing adjusted terms?"

### Creating Competitive Tension
"We are in active conversations with [number] interested parties. We want to move efficiently but also ensure we find the right partner for the business and our team. Our timeline for making a decision is [timeframe]."

### Earnout Negotiation
"We are open to an earnout structure, but the milestones need to reflect what is achievable given normal market conditions, not aggressive growth targets. We would also want to discuss what operational support and resources you would provide during the earnout period to ensure success."

### Walk-Away Response
"Based on the current terms, we do not see alignment on valuation. We are going to continue building the business and may revisit a sale in 6-12 months when our trajectory makes the gap in valuation expectations clearer."

## 6. ACCRUAL VS. CASH ACCOUNTING COMPARISON

| Aspect | Cash Basis | Accrual Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue recognition** | When cash received | When earned (even if not yet collected) |
| **Expense recognition** | When cash paid | When incurred (even if not yet paid) |
| **Inventory treatment** | Expensed when purchased | Expensed when sold (COGS) |
| **Prepaid expenses** | Expensed immediately | Amortized over benefit period |
| **Accuracy** | Can be misleading (large inventory purchases distort P&L) | More accurate picture of business economics |
| **Exit due diligence** | Creates massive headaches -- buyers will require restatement | Preferred by all acquirers |
| **When to switch** | -- | Switch when seriously considering exit (or at $500K+ annual revenue) |

**Critical**: Using cash accounting creates a massive headache during exit due diligence (Melisa Vong). Buyers will either walk away or demand significant restatement work at your expense.

## 7. TRADEMARK FILING TIMELINE & PROCESS

### Step-by-Step
1. **Research** (Week 1): Search USPTO TESS database for conflicts; search state databases; check domain and social media availability
2. **Classify** (Week 1): Identify correct Nice Classification(s) for your goods/services
3. **File application** (Week 2): Direct via USPTO ($250-$350/class) or through attorney ($500-$2,000 total)
4. **Examination** (Months 3-4): USPTO examiner reviews; may issue Office Action requiring response
5. **Publication** (Months 5-6): Published in Official Gazette for 30-day opposition period
6. **Registration** (Months 9-12): If no opposition, registration certificate issued
7. **Maintenance**: Declaration of Use due between years 5-6; renewal every 10 years

### EU Trademark (Faster)
- Filed through EUIPO
- Timeline: 3-5 months typical
- Covers all EU member states with single filing

### Key Rules
- Only trademark ONE thing initially (your brand name) -- Gretta Van Riel
- Owning a domain does NOT mean you own the trademark
- Trademarks are registered under classifications (food/drink, clothing, etc.)
- Online legal services: LegalZoom, TrademarkYear.com
- Amazon IP Accelerator: speeds up Brand Registry while trademark pending

## 10. NEWSLETTER/EMAIL LIST AS EXIT ASSET (Kevin King)

### Three Most Valuable Business Assets (Ranked)
1. **Physical mailing address** -- can follow customer for 44+ years via National Change of Address service
2. **Mobile phone number** -- direct, high-open-rate channel
3. **Email address** -- control contact for 4+ years

### Why This Matters for Exit
- With a list, you can always pivot even if brand/IP fails
- Facebook group post reach: ~15% of members; Email open rate: 56% = 4x more reach
- Smart marketer formula: get attention where it is easy (Amazon/social) then transfer to owned channel (email/phone/mail)
- 2,500+ engaged subscribers can generate 7 figures
- Newsletter exit valuations demonstrate list value (Morning Brew: $75M, The Hustle: $27M)

### Building List Value
- Always give compelling reason to share contact info (not just "sign up for warranty")
- Use excess/slow-moving inventory as lead magnets
- Free plus shipping model builds list of people who showed purchase intent
