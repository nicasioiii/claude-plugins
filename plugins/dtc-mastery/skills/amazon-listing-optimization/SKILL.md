## Amazon Ranking Model: Performance x Relevancy (Brandon Young)

**Ranking score = Performance x Relevancy**

- **Performance** = CTR x CVR x revenue on keyword
- **Relevancy** = score from 0 to 1 based on keyword placement in listing
- If relevancy = 0.25, a performance score of 1,000 becomes 250 -- drops you from page 1 to page 2-3

**Relevancy hierarchy (strongest to weakest):**
1. Exact match keyword at BEGINNING of title (strongest)
2. Title (any position)
3. Bullet points
4. Backend search terms
5. Description / A+ content

**Implication:** Every listing optimization decision should consider relevancy impact on core keywords.

## Reduce Cognitive Load = More Sales (Matt Kostan)

- Amazon data: 28% of purchases happen in under 3 minutes, 50% in 15 minutes
- Costco model: fewer choices = more buying (4,000 products vs Walmart's 150,000)
- **Takeaway 1:** Tell shoppers WHY to buy (not just specs) in secondary images + A+ content
- **Takeaway 2:** Simplify images -- 1-2 highlights per image maximum
- Anker: sold $1B on Amazon with dead-simple listing images (just "portability" and "high-speed charging")
- Case study: face painting kit went from 10% to 13.5% conversion (35% increase) by simplifying images
- Tool: imagecheck.com -- see how Amazon AI interprets your images

## Rufus/Cosmo Optimization (Vanessa Hung)

- **Rufus** = Amazon's front-end AI shopping assistant
- **Cosmo** = backend algorithm complementing A9

**Audit process:**
1. Collect all Rufus questions from your listing + competitor listings (40-50 questions)
2. Feed listing content + questions into ChatGPT
3. Ask: "Does my listing answer these questions? Score 1-5 for each"
4. Focus on scores of 1-3 and rewrite content to address those gaps

**Key insight:** Images are powerful for Cosmo. A photo showing 3 sleeping positions tells Cosmo the pillow works for all positions WITHOUT needing text. Repeat audit regularly as Rufus questions evolve.

## Semantic Relevance (Leo Sgovio)

[CONTRARIAN] Stuffing trendy keywords (e.g., celebrity names, trending terms) DILUTES semantic relevance and costs rankings on core keywords.

**Case study:** Product with 22K reviews and trendy keywords does $90K/month. Competitor with 7K reviews and tight semantic focus does $250K/month.

**Process:**
1. Use ChatGPT as NLP expert: "Analyze semantic relevance of this keyword list, find the central topic, exclude irrelevant/branded keywords"
2. Keep listing focused on your core product category
3. Amazon reads images for relevance signals -- optimize images too
4. Reference Amazon NIN patents on semantic ranking

## Title Density Concept (Bradley Sutton)

**Title density** = how many page 1 listings have your exact keyword phrase in their title.

- Low title density (2-3 out of 48 results) = you can hit page 1 just by putting keyword at beginning of title during honeymoon period
- **Pattern field hack:** Amazon shows bold text from "pattern" backend field in search results -- put main keyword there for extra visibility

## Flat File Completeness (Kevin King)

Fill EVERY field in flat file -- even if "N/A" or "no."

**Why:** Missing fields create vulnerability for competitors to add trigger words or change your listing. Every blank field is a door left open.

**IQD Score:** Amazon assigns a numerical IQD score to every listing. Must be 90+ to be fully included in algorithm.

**Requirements for 90+ IQD:**
- 5+ bullet points
- 5+ high-resolution images
- 1+ review of 3+ stars
- A+ content enabled
- Correct leaf node assignment

Ask your SAS rep for an IQD report to see what is missing.
