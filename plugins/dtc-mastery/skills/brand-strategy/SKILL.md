## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICES

### Disagreement: Audience First vs. Product First
(Same as product-ideation -- cross-reference for context)

| Position | Advocate | When It Applies |
|---|---|---|
| Build audience FIRST ("market-product fit") | Gretta van Riel | Social-media-native founders; grow niche account -> create product for audience |
| Find product first, build brand around it | Matt Clark | Data-driven founders; Amazon BSR + Google Trends -> brand identity |

**Resolution:** Both are valid. Gretta's approach creates stronger brand DNA from day one. Clark's approach is faster to revenue. Jason Wong adds: PMF can be achieved by changing the target audience, not just the product.

## Why Brand Matters

**Store vs. Brand**: A store = renting a house. A brand = owning a house (sellable asset).

**Acquisition benchmarks:**
- Native (natural deodorant): acquired by P&G for $100M after 2.5 years
- Movement Watches: acquired by Movado for $200M (2x+ revenue)
- Dollar Shave Club: $1B acquisition by Unilever

**The omnichannel model (Matt Clark):** Sell highly differentiated product -> build real brand -> sell on Shopify first -> price at premium -> capture customer info -> expand to Amazon/Walmart/retail.

**DTC valuation premium:** DTC businesses valued at 2-3x more than Amazon-only businesses (Matt Clark, confirmed by brokers).

## Minimum Viable Brand Pyramid (Gretta van Riel)

Six elements from Love & Money Agency:

### 1. Target Audience
Demographics + psychographics from customer personas (built in product-ideation).

### 2. Benefits
Rational benefits your product offers. Brand features, not just product features.

### 3. Values
Emotional ground between brand and audience. "Reasons to Believe" (RTBs).

**Example (Drop Bottle):** Pragmatic, Altruistic, Transparent, Conscious.

### 4. Aphorism
Brand value proposition in one sentence. E.g., "Every drop counts."

### 5. Vision
What your business aspires to achieve beyond the bottom line.

### 6. Brand Personality
Using Millward Brown brand archetypes. Pick 1 core + 2 sub-archetypes.

**Drop Bottle:** Core = The Wise (humility, credibility); Sub = The Dreamer (creative) + The Friend (community).

**Brand Positioning**: Own a single word. Volvo = safety. BMW = driving. Shopify = e-commerce.

## Naming Frameworks

Both Gretta and Jason identify the same 4 categories:

| Category | Description | Examples | Guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Generic | Describes the product directly | Hotels.com, Cars.com | Avoid unless you own domain + trademark |
| Descriptive | Describes what product does | SkinnyMeTea, All Brand | Clear but can box you in |
| **Associative** | Implies through imagery/emotion | Drop Bottle, Nike, Apple, Uber | **RECOMMENDED** -- gives sense without being literal |
| Coined/Abstract | Made-up words | Kodak, Xerox, Google, Adidas | Blank slate but harder to remember |

### Name Storming Process
1. Word dump: 15+ words related to brand, product, benefits, feelings (5-minute timer)
2. Take 5-10 favorites to thesaurus.com, follow the rabbit hole
3. Generate 3-5 name options
4. Check availability (domain, trademark, social handles) before getting attached
5. Consider: double meanings, noun + adjective usage, how name extends to other assets

### Naming Lessons
- "Skinny" was acceptable in 2012 but became controversial (media backlash)
- Separate product name from brand name when possible
- Frank Body: "Frank" = guy's name + adjective (honest); enabled hashtag "let's be frank"
- The Fifth: inspired by Fifth Avenue; enabled "Five O'Clock" blog, selling on 5th for 5 days
- Doe Lashes: attributes + benefits -> "round eyes" observation -> deer picture -> "doe-like eyes"

## Core Values (Jason Wong)

- **Existing brands:** Look at what you already do; find concrete examples of values in action
- **New brands:** Research brands you admire; analyze their customer engagement
- Transparency wins with Millennials/Gen Z (keen eye for inauthenticity)
- **Capsule collection strategy:** If margins don't allow ongoing charity, launch short-term capsule collection with nonprofit

## Tone of Voice (Jason Wong)

### 4 Scales
Rate your brand on each: Funny vs. Serious | Formal vs. Casual | Respectful vs. Cheeky | Authoritative vs. Matter-of-fact

### Definition Exercise
- "I want my brand to make people feel ___"
- "I want people to ___ when they come into contact with my brand"
- Define what you're NOT: "We're funny but not sarcastic. We're friendly but not sappy."

Brand voice = what you say. Brand tone = how you say it (tone shifts by platform/audience).

## Category-of-One Positioning (Janelle Page)

Don't compete in existing category ladder -- create a new category class.

**Examples:**
- Red Bull: didn't compete as cola, created "energy drinks" category
- TrueEarth: created laundry sheets category, disrupted detergent market
- Dr. Eckberg electrolyte: positioned specifically for intermittent fasters
- Sean Kerr: all-in-one motorcycle detailer (not generic car care)

**"Tiny touches" strategy:** Surprise/delight in packaging drives reviews without asking. Example: motorcycle gloves with American flag lining + commemorative tin.

## Brand Aesthetic (Jason Wong)

### 3 Components
1. **Logo** -- 7 types: lettermark, wordmark, pictorial, abstract, mascot, combination, emblem
2. **Color** -- Color psychology predicts customer response. Choose 2-3 core colors tied to brand values.
3. **Typography** -- Serif (traditional), Sans Serif (modern), Script (elegant), Decorative (unique)

**Budget options:** Canva (DIY) -> Fiverr/99designs (freelance) -> Dribbble (custom) -> Agency ($2K-$10K+)

**Logo advice (Gretta):** Logo doesn't need to be icon-based; can just be brand name in stylized font. Provide 4 options to community and let them vote.

**Mood boards:** Create 2 creative directions for designer -- one aspirational/future, one closer to current state.

## Content Territories (Gretta van Riel)

3-5 content categories/topics across all channels.

**The Fifth example:**
1. Inspire & Aspire
2. Wandering & Well-Being
3. Rest & Relaxation
4. Products & Promotions
5. The Fifth Fam (community celebration)

**Universal territories** (always include): Products & Promotions + Celebrating Community.
Shape remaining 2-3 around customer persona mindsets.

## The /build-brand Command

When the user runs `/build-brand`, execute this guided workshop:

### Step 1: Foundation
Ask: What product/category? Who is the target customer? What problem does it solve? What aspirational brands do you admire (brand SPO)?

### Step 2: Brand Pyramid
Walk through all 6 MVB elements. Output a completed Brand Pyramid worksheet.

### Step 3: Naming
Run the name storming process. Generate 5-7 options across associative and coined categories. Check availability guidance.

### Step 4: Tone of Voice
Rate on 4 scales. Generate "We are ___ but not ___" statements. Write 3 sample social posts in the defined voice.

### Step 5: Positioning Statement
Format: "For [target audience] who [need/want], [brand name] is the [category] that [key differentiation] because [reason to believe]."

### Step 6: Content Territory Plan
Define 4-5 content territories mapped to customer personas. Assign content types to each.

### Step 7: Output
Deliver complete brand strategy document with: pyramid, name recommendations, tone guide, positioning statement, content territories, and next steps (-> product-development for spec sheet + packaging).
