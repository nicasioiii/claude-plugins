# Brand Strategy Frameworks -- Deep Reference

## Naming Framework Details

### Gretta's Name Criteria (from Love & Money Agency)
**Should be:** Easy to remember, distinct, pique interest, make sense, open to change/development.
**Should NOT:** Try to tell the whole story, be too literal, box you in, steal the show, slow things down.

### Jason's Doe Lashes Naming Process (Worked Example)
1. Listed product attributes + benefits
2. Observation: "round eyes" effect
3. Googled "round eyes" -> deer picture appeared
4. "Doe-like eyes" phrase anchored on gentle personality
5. Checked domain + social availability
6. Registered and committed

### Domain Tips (Jason Wong)
- .com preferred
- Creative alternatives work: tryhint.com, wearpepper.com
- Don't need perfect domain initially
- Claim social handles immediately

### Name Testing
- Provide 4 logo/name options to community, let them vote (comment 1/2/3/4)
- Facebook group for validation
- Check trademark availability via TESS (USPTO search tool)

## Tone of Voice Deep Dive

### 4 Scales Exercise
Rate 1-10 on each scale:
1. **Funny** (1) ←→ (10) **Serious**
2. **Formal** (1) ←→ (10) **Casual**
3. **Respectful** (1) ←→ (10) **Cheeky**
4. **Authoritative** (1) ←→ (10) **Matter-of-fact**

### Brand Voice Examples

**BarkBox:** High energy, exclamation points, conversational, warm. FAQ uses "not to worry though" -- upbeat and helpful.

**Frank Bod:** Persona named "Frank" -- unapologetic, honest, calls customers "babes." First-person voice creates intimate relationship.

**Doe Lashes (Jason):** Soft, inviting, bubbly. Pastel blue/white palette reinforces gentle personality.

### Voice vs. Tone Distinction
- **Voice** = consistent personality (what you say) -- doesn't change
- **Tone** = emotional inflection (how you say it) -- shifts by platform, context, audience mood

### "We Are / We Are Not" Exercise Template
Complete 5+ pairs:
- "We are [positive trait] but not [adjacent negative]"
- Example: "We are approachable but not unprofessional"
- Example: "We are bold but not aggressive"
- Example: "We are playful but not childish"

## AI Brand Blueprint Process (Mark Degrasse)

### 9 Pillars in Detail

**1. Brand Name:** Associative/suggestive preferred (see naming section)

**2. Slogan:** Memorable phrase capturing core promise. Under 8 words. Test: can someone recall it after hearing once?

**3. Values:** 3-5 core values with "Reasons to Believe" evidence for each

**4. Customer Avatar:** Demographics + psychographics + buying behavior + objections + aspirations

**5. Brand Avatar:** The brand as a person -- age, personality, speaking style, values, what they wear, how they enter a room

**6. Visual Style Guide:** Colors (primary, secondary, accent), typography hierarchy, image style (photography vs. illustration), spacing/layout principles

**7. Tonal Style Guide:** Voice characteristics, do/don't language rules, sample copy per channel

**8. Community Style Guide:** How to engage fans, response templates, user content curation rules, community rituals

**9. Interactive/Experiential Style Guide:** Unboxing experience, event presence, customer touchpoint design

### Implementation
- Build all 9 pillars into a custom GPT or brand document
- Hand to any agency, contractor, or VA for instant brand consistency
- VisionCraft GPT: free tool to generate brand vision in 2 minutes

## Instagram as Brand Landing Page (Mary Harcourt, Kevin King Summit)

### Why It Matters
70% of consumers check Instagram before visiting a brand's website.

### Setup Checklist
1. Business profile (not personal)
2. Logo as profile picture
3. SEO keywords in bio (discoverable via search)
4. Shop integration enabled
5. Highlight reels organized by topic
6. Pin 3 videos: brand story, product demo/commercial, UGC testimonial
7. QR code on product inserts -> Instagram profile (acts as curated landing page)
8. Link tree in bio: Amazon listing, website, YouTube, newsletter, TikTok

### Content Strategy
- Repost customer stories to build community + reach their audience
- Use carousel posts for education content
- Reels for product demos and behind-the-scenes
- Stories for daily engagement and polls

## The 3 C's of Community (Gretta van Riel)

### 1. Content
- Must add value, build trust, inspire action
- "Content is king, context is queen, engagement is the kingdom"
- Use CTAs: comment, tag a friend, share, swipe up, tap link in bio
- Incentivize: 10% off for waitlist signup, free gift for bio link purchase

### 2. Collaboration
- Mutual like/comment agreements with similar-sized accounts
- Shout-out for shout-out (S4S): two similar accounts tag each other
- Pay larger niche accounts for paid shoutouts
- Co-branded giveaways with complementary brands

### 3. Consistency
- Post daily; more posts = more total engagement = more algorithmic reach
- 1 post/week x 300 likes = 300 interactions. Daily = 2,100/week.
- Content territories keep you from running out of ideas

## Omnichannel Brand Model (Matt Clark)

### Old Model (Amazon-Only)
1. Find keyword opportunities on Amazon
2. Create similar product to fit keyword
3. Rank via PPC + reviews
4. Rankings stick once reviews accumulate
5. Increase price over time

### New Omnichannel Model
1. Sell highly differentiated product (mass market, not niche keywords)
2. Build real brand (social media, email, recognition)
3. Sell on Shopify first
4. Price at premium
5. Capture customer info for remarketing
6. Expand to Amazon, Walmart.com, retail

**Key insight:** You no longer need to find small Amazon niches. Go after mass markets because you compete via social advertising, not search rank. Supplements, beauty, and top-100 BSR products that were "avoid" categories for Amazon are fair game for DTC Shopify.

## Two Ways to Build Wealth (Matt Clark)

1. **Cash flow** -- Reinvest profits to grow sales base, then extract profit
2. **Asset value** -- E-commerce businesses valued at 3x profit up to 2x revenue

**LifeBoost case study:**
- Year 1: Reinvested ALL profits
- Current: $2.6M/month ($30M/year run rate)
- ~90% Shopify, ~10% Amazon
