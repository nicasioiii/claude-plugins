# Content Creator & Influencer Strategy -- Reference

## Section 1: Creator Outreach Workflow & KPIs

### TikTok Shop Creator Outreach (Michelle -- E06)

**6-Step Daily Workflow:**
1. Find and save creators by keywords, hashtags, interests relevant to brand/products
2. Create a Target Collaboration daily with 30-50 saved creators
3. After inviting, share the Target Collaboration invite via IM (instant message)
4. Copy/paste invite message and share the Target Collaboration card
5. Track outreach vs content completed to build conversion math equation
6. Over time, know: "To get X content pieces, outreach to Y creators"

**Volume Benchmarks:**
- Standard daily outreach: 30-50 creators saved and invited
- Aggressive/bot-assisted: 1,000-2,500 creators/day
- Content conversion rate: ~10% (100 pieces requires ~1,000 outreach)

**Daily KPI Tracking:**

| Metric | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Creators outreached | Daily |
| Sample requests received | Daily |
| Samples shipped | Daily |
| Content pending | Daily |
| Content completed | Daily |
| Outreach-to-content ratio | Monthly aggregate |

**What Creators Look For:**
- Lifestyle fit with their existing content
- Commission rate/structure
- Ease of demonstration of the product
- Clear communication about product value and benefits

### Sample Management Pipeline (Michelle -- E06)

**Tab-Based System:**
1. Review Tab -- new sample requests arrive here
2. Ready to Ship Tab -- approved requests move here
3. Shipped Tab -- auto-tracked via fulfillment integration
4. Content Pending Tab -- after delivery confirmed
5. Completed Content Tab -- view content, engage with comments

**Approval Criteria:**
- Creator's sales performance history
- Creator's fulfillment percentage (past content completion)
- Relevance of brand to their existing content

**Post-Sample Messaging:**
1. When approved: send templated message with product features and creation instructions
2. When in Content Pending: send follow-up with instructions + spark code/link requests

### Spark Code Management (Michelle -- E06)
- "Unauthorized" videos in creative tab = shoppable posts without spark codes or with expired codes
- If an organic video generates orders without ad spend, contact that creator immediately
- Extend spark codes proactively -- this is money left on the table
- Import spark codes to ad creative library for GMV Max amplification

### Outsourcing (Michelle -- E06)
- Outsourceable tasks: creator searching, sorting, saving, inviting, validation, sample management, review requests, spark code importing
- Recommended: trained VAs (VAA Philippines or similar)

## Section 2: Influencer Tiers & Partnership Models

### Micro vs Macro (Gretta -- E01)
- **Micro (<100K followers):** Accept free product, no manager, easier communication, volume game (4 out of 10 PR boxes get posted)
- **Macro (>100K followers):** Rate cards, may have manager, larger reach but more expensive

**Product-for-Post Model:**
- Send free product in exchange for content/posts
- The Fifth: sent 50 watches to 50 influencers before launch
- Focus on micro-influencers for better engagement rates and ROI
- Outreach via DM and email; be personal, genuine, and brief

### 3-Step Partnership Framework (Jason Wong -- E02)
1. **Brand awareness post** -- just using product, tagging brand; no selling; associates influencer with brand naturally
2. **Sales promotion post** -- straightforward promotion with coupon codes/offers; directs followers to shop
3. **Retargeting post** -- reactivates fan base; sometimes takes multiple exposures before purchase

### Attribution Methods (Jason Wong -- E02)
1. **Coupon codes** -- easiest to track; downside: makes partnership obvious (less effective)
2. **Post-purchase survey** -- ask "where did you find us?" on thank-you page; preferred method
3. **Caveat:** Influencer attribution will never be perfectly trackable

### Creator Compensation Models

| Model | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Product-for-post (free product) | Micro-influencers, seeding | Volume game, 40% post rate |
| Commission-based | TikTok Shop affiliates | Performance-aligned, scalable |
| Retainer ($500/mo for 10-20 pieces) | Proven top performers | Gracey Ryback recommendation |
| Flat fee | Macro-influencers, campaigns | Negotiate on engagement rate, not followers |

### Three Types of Influencer Platforms (Tim Jordan -- E04)
- Type 1: Commission-based selling (Levanta, Amazon Associates) -- 20-30% cheaper than PPC
- Type 2: Paid creator matching -- pay for content AND distribution to their audience
- Type 3: UGC production (JoinBrands, Billo) -- content you OWN for your own ads/listings

## Section 3: UGC Flywheel & Content Production

### UGC Flywheel (Tim Jordan -- E04)
- Single input (one UGC video) feeds multiple outputs: DSP, Facebook, TikTok Shop, Amazon listing
- "1 + 1 = 7" -- compound returns from one piece of content
- Capture non-converting traffic from creator links as segmented DSP audience
- Build lookalike audiences from creator-driven purchasers
- Free sample platforms emerging for micro-creator content at zero cost

### JoinBrands TikTok Shop Blast (Gracey Ryback -- E04)
- Creator posts with TikTok Shop tag + you get content + ad code for spark ads
- Do NOT repost same content as TikTok Shop tagged video on brand account (violation)
- Additional platforms: Billo for general UGC, Billow.app, UGC Factory, InSensePro

### Free UGC from Existing Customers (Gracey Ryback -- E04)
- Incentivize customers with promo code to create content with branded hashtag
- Vet best content, reach out for rights/repurposing permission
- Benefits: organic content from real users, no product gifting needed

### FEMSA Framework (VisCap Media via Jason Wong -- E02)
- **F**rameworks, **E**lements, **S**egments, **S**tructure, **A**uditing
- Pre-production is the most critical stage
- Elements of an ad: Hook, headline, problem/agitation, product intro, authority, guarantee, social proof, UGC, PR, features/benefits, desired end result, use case, CTA, offer
- Modular content approach: create interchangeable elements
- $1,000 video splits into 10 variations ($100/piece), splits again ($10/piece)

### Element Mutation (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Take successful ad elements and reproduce as UGC pieces
- UGC acts as "element mutator" -- cheaply multiplies content library
- 4-screen UGC mashup (Shackelford): four different UGC videos playing simultaneously with text overlay -- #1 performing format

### Three Content Types (Gretta -- E01)
1. **Brand Generated Content (BGC)** -- in-house or professional; you own rights
2. **User Generated Content (UGC)** -- created by customers; more genuine; incentivize with monthly giveaway
3. **Influencer Generated Content (IGC)** -- created by influencers for product/payment; higher quality than UGC, more authentic than BGC

## Section 4: Content Repurposing & Short-Form Strategy

### TikTok Content Strategy (Gracey Ryback -- E04)
- Best converting structure: Problem > Product > Solution in under 30 seconds
- Content doesn't have to be high production to convert
- SEO hack: minimize keywords as tiny text off to side of TikTok videos -- algorithm reads it but viewers don't see it

### Cross-Platform Repurposing Map (Gracey Ryback -- E04)

| Source | Short-Term Repurpose | Evergreen Repurpose |
|---|---|---|
| TikTok video | FB Reels, IG Reels, YouTube Shorts | Pinterest pins, Amazon storefront, blogs |
| UGC testimonial | All short-form platforms | YouTube long-form compilations |
| Product demo | All social platforms | Amazon listing images/video |

### Creator Retainer Model (Gracey Ryback -- E04)
- $500/month for 10-20 pieces of content
- Focus on commission-based compensation over flat fees for ROI alignment
- Put proven creators on retainer after initial product-for-post testing

### Content Curation vs Creation (Gretta -- E01)
- Curation = sharing existing content with credit to elevate brand perception
- High-quality curated content communicates professionalism without expensive photography
- Two key types to curate: influencer content in your niche (no competing brands visible) + viral niche content
- Use Repostly app for resharing (maintains quality)

### Creative Testing Protocol -- PLERT (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Test ONLY the video creative; keep proven thumbnails + winning copy constant
- Gold mining system: Red (kill) / Yellow (optimize) / Green (scale)
- Metrics: playthrough, CTR, ROAS, CPC, average watch time
- 80/20 rule: 80% proven frameworks, 20% testing new offers/angles
- Soft CTA insertion: if ad has semi-good ROAS but high CPC and 12-second watch time, insert soft CTA earlier

### VisCap Media Assembly Line (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Week 1: Monday kickoff > research > copy > storyboard > tighten > present to client
- Week 2: set review > shoot Tuesday-Thursday > Friday reshoots
- Post-shoot: footage uploaded same day > overseas editing team > first draft in 48 hours
- 2 internal revisions > client gets ad ~4 days after shoot > 2 client revisions > finished in 7 days
- Key rule: only start on Mondays; storyboards double as on-set shot lists

### Content Top Performing Analysis (Gretta -- E01)
- Identify TPC through engagement metrics (20%+ above average)
- Post MORE of what works, less of what doesn't
- Use Instagram Insights: Content tab > See All > sort by engagement
- Common mistake: brands keep posting content THEY want to see, not what audience engages with

### Buyer Journey Content Mapping (Gretta -- E01)
- **Awareness (Top)** -- cold leads; problem/brand-focused: social posts, blog posts, giveaways, educational videos
- **Evaluation (Middle)** -- warm deciding; social proof: reviews, UGC, press, influencer endorsements + product demos, FAQs
- **Purchase (Bottom)** -- hot ready to buy; offers: free shipping, free returns, guarantee, free gift, first-order discount

### Post-Purchase Content Moments (Gretta -- E01)
Four micro-moments to capture content:
1. **Right after purchase** -- capitalize on dopamine/excitement; encourage social sharing
2. **Product arrival/unboxing** -- first physical impression; #unboxing has 1M+ posts on IG
3. **Product in action** -- customers using product in real life
4. **Transformation/results** -- before/after, outcomes achieved
- Each moment is a content capture opportunity; incentivize sharing at each stage
