## VALUATION MULTIPLE REFERENCE TABLE

| Business Type | Typical Multiple | Notes |
|--------------|-----------------|-------|
| Amazon-only FBA business | 2-4x annual net profit | Standard SDE multiple; platform-dependent risk |
| Amazon + Shopify DTC | 4-8x annual net profit | DTC premium from channel diversification |
| DTC-first with subscription | 6-12x annual net profit | Recurring revenue + direct customer relationships |
| Premium brand (strong IP + team) | Up to 2x annual revenue | Rare; requires all 8 value factors scoring high |
| Exception category | Up to 20x annual profit | Category leaders with explosive growth trajectory |

**Key insight (Matt Clark):** The jump from Amazon-only to Amazon + DTC is a 2-3x multiplier on the multiple itself. This is the single highest-ROI action for exit value.

## THE 8 VALUE-INCREASING FACTORS -- SCORING GUIDE

### Factor 1: Brand Registry & Trademark Protection
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No trademark filed, no Brand Registry |
| 1 | Trademark application submitted |
| 3 | Trademark registered, Brand Registry active |
| 5 | Full trademark portfolio with international filings |

### Factor 2: Documented SOPs
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | All knowledge in founder's head |
| 1 | A few processes written down informally |
| 3 | Core processes documented with SOPs |
| 5 | Full 5-level process hierarchy in a Company Wiki (Escala standard) |

### Factor 3: Revenue Channel Diversification
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Single channel (Amazon-only or Shopify-only) |
| 1 | Primary channel + minor secondary (<10% revenue) |
| 3 | Two channels each contributing 20%+ of revenue |
| 5 | Three or more channels with no single channel exceeding 60% |

### Factor 4: Review Profile & Brand Recognition
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Fewer than 50 reviews, no brand recognition |
| 1 | 50-200 reviews, some brand search volume |
| 3 | 500+ reviews, measurable branded search traffic |
| 5 | 1,000+ reviews, strong brand following, PR coverage |

### Factor 5: Owned Customer List (Email/SMS)
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No email list |
| 1 | Under 5,000 subscribers |
| 3 | 5,000-25,000 active subscribers with segmentation |
| 5 | 25,000+ active subscribers generating measurable repeat revenue |

### Factor 6: Subscription Revenue
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No subscription component |
| 1 | Subscription available but under 10% of revenue |
| 3 | 10-30% of revenue from subscriptions |
| 5 | 30%+ of revenue from subscriptions (LifeBoost benchmark: ~50%) |

### Factor 7: Clean Financial Records
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | No accounting system; personal and business finances mixed |
| 1 | Basic bookkeeping but incomplete or inconsistent |
| 3 | QuickBooks/Xero with monthly reconciliation |
| 5 | Professionally prepared financial statements, clean P&L, clear SDE calculation |

### Factor 8: Founder Independence
| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 0 | Business stops if founder takes a week off |
| 1 | Can survive a week without founder but not a month |
| 3 | Team runs daily operations; founder handles strategy only |
| 5 | Business operates independently; founder role is optional |

### Scoring Interpretation
- **Total 0-10:** Not exit-ready. Focus on building foundational value.
- **Total 11-20:** Early-stage exit potential. Address the lowest-scoring factors first.
- **Total 21-30:** Approaching exit readiness. Fine-tune and begin broker conversations.
- **Total 31-40:** Exit-ready. Maximize timing and negotiate from a position of strength.

## IP FILING TIMELINE

### Recommended Sequence
1. **Day 1 (before product development begins):** File trademark application for brand name
2. **Before first product listing:** Ensure trademark is at least in "serial number" status for Amazon Brand Registry
3. **Before any public product disclosure:** File provisional patent application if product has novel features (12-month protection window)
4. **Within 12 months of provisional:** Convert to full utility patent if product is succeeding
5. **Before international expansion:** File international trademark in target markets (Madrid Protocol)

### Patent Decision Framework
Ask these questions before spending money on patents:
1. Does the product have a genuinely novel mechanism, design, or process?
2. Is the innovation visible and replicable by competitors?
3. Are you planning to sell the business within 3-5 years?
4. Can you afford the filing cost ($5K-$15K provisional; $15K-$50K full utility)?
5. Would a patent create a competitive moat that a buyer would value?

If YES to questions 1, 2, 3, and at least one of 4 or 5: file a patent.
If NO to question 1: do not file (you cannot patent what is not novel).

### Trademark Cost Reference
| Action | Approximate Cost |
|--------|-----------------|
| USPTO filing (per class) | $250-$350 |
| Trademark attorney (optional but recommended) | $500-$1,500 |
| Contested trademark defense | $10,000-$50,000+ |
| International filing (Madrid Protocol, per country) | $300-$600 per designation |
| Trademark acquisition (buying from another holder) | Highly variable; Gretta's example: $180K for one trademark |

## DONATION TAX STRATEGY -- DETAILED IMPLEMENTATION

### Eligibility Requirements
- Must be a C-corporation or S-corporation (check with tax advisor for LLC pass-through treatment)
- Donations must go to qualified 501(c)(3) organizations
- Must maintain documentation: donation receipts, fair market value assessments, charity acknowledgment letters

### Setup Process
1. Identify a qualified charity that accepts product donations
2. Add charity's warehouse address as a removal destination in Amazon Seller Central
3. Set up monthly auto-removal orders for returned and unsellable inventory
4. Track all donations with: product name, quantity, retail value, date, charity name
5. Provide tracking information to charity for acknowledgment letters
6. File Form 8283 with tax return for non-cash donations over $500

### Calculation Examples

**Example 1: Standard Return Rate (5%)**
- Annual units sold: 100,000
- Returns at 5%: 5,000 units
- Retail value per unit: $30
- Total donation value: $150,000
- At 25% tax rate: $37,500 in tax savings
- Versus disposal: $0 recovered + disposal fees paid

**Example 2: High-Return Category (Clothing, 20%)**
- Annual units sold: 100,000
- Returns at 20%: 20,000 units
- Retail value per unit: $60
- Total donation value: $1,200,000
- At 25% tax rate: $300,000 in tax savings
- Deductions roll forward 5 years if not fully used

### Expiring Inventory (Supplements)
- Donate from 3PL directly before expiration
- Skip Amazon disposal fees entirely
- Get tax deduction plus lower losses on the books
- Double benefit: avoid disposal cost AND gain tax deduction

## EQUITY AND PARTNERSHIP STRUCTURE

### Vesting Schedule Template (Jason Wong)
| Parameter | Recommended |
|-----------|-------------|
| Total vesting period | 4 years |
| Cliff period | 1 year (no equity vests before 12 months) |
| Vesting frequency after cliff | Monthly or quarterly |
| Acceleration clause | Single-trigger on acquisition is standard |
| Good leaver vs. bad leaver | Define explicitly in operating agreement |

### Operating Agreement Must-Haves
1. Roles and responsibilities for each partner
2. Equity split rationale (contribution-based, not default 50/50)
3. Decision-making authority and voting thresholds
4. Salary and distribution policies
5. Exit terms: buyout process, valuation method, right of first refusal
6. Dispute resolution mechanism
7. Non-compete and non-solicitation clauses
8. Death or disability provisions

## EXIT READINESS CHECKLIST

### Financial Readiness
- [ ] 12+ months of clean P&L statements
- [ ] SDE (Seller's Discretionary Earnings) clearly calculated
- [ ] All add-backs documented and defensible
- [ ] Tax returns filed and consistent with financial statements
- [ ] No outstanding tax liabilities or disputes

### Operational Readiness
- [ ] Core processes documented in SOPs
- [ ] Business can operate without founder for 30+ days
- [ ] Key supplier relationships transferable (not personal)
- [ ] Customer support system in place with response-time benchmarks
- [ ] Inventory management system with demand forecasting

### Legal Readiness
- [ ] Trademark registered and Brand Registry active
- [ ] Patent applications filed (if applicable)
- [ ] Operating agreement in place (if partners)
- [ ] No pending legal disputes or IP challenges
- [ ] All contracts (supplier, 3PL, software) transferable

### Growth Readiness
- [ ] Clear growth trajectory (at least 3 consecutive months of growth)
- [ ] Multiple revenue channels contributing
- [ ] Email/SMS list with measurable revenue attribution
- [ ] Subscription revenue component (if applicable)
- [ ] Pipeline of product expansion opportunities

### Timing Considerations
- Best time to sell: when the business is growing and you do not need to sell
- Worst time to sell: when you are burned out or the business is declining
- Seasonal businesses: list after your strongest quarter's financials are complete
- Plan 6-12 months in advance of desired exit date for preparation
