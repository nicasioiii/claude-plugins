# External Traffic & List Building -- Reference

## Section 1: Perpetual Sales Machine & Blog Funnels

### Perpetual Sales Machine (Travis Zigler -- E04)

**Core Strategy:**
- Focus on PROBLEM-based keywords (not product keywords) for dramatically lower CPC
- Build blog around the problem your product solves
- Blog includes: attribution links to Amazon + lead magnet for email capture
- Welcome email sequence (3-12 emails) drives back to blogs and Amazon cyclically

**Case Study -- Acid Reflux Brand:**
- $400/week Google spend = 2,000 clicks to blog + 350 email leads + $1,200 Amazon sales (3x ROAS)
- Lead cost: ~$1.50 per lead while simultaneously driving profitable Amazon sales
- Built 15,000 email list in under 2 months
- Previous brand reached 150,000 emails before multimillion-dollar exit

**The Perpetual Cycle:**
```
Google Ads (problem keywords) -> Blog Post -> Lead Magnet (email capture)
     |                                              |
     v                                              v
Amazon Attribution Link              Welcome Email Sequence (3-12 emails)
     |                                              |
     v                                              v
Amazon Sales + Ranking Boost          Drives back to Blog + Amazon
```

**Scaling:**
- Just increase Google spend -- ROAS decreases slightly but email list + total sales increase
- Key: blog creates perpetual cycle that compounds over time

### Problem-Based Keyword Strategy (Travis Zigler -- E04)
- Product keywords: high CPC, high competition ("acid reflux supplement" = expensive)
- Problem keywords: low CPC, lower competition ("why do I get heartburn after eating" = cheap)
- Blog content answers the problem, naturally introduces product as solution
- Amazon attribution link embedded in blog for direct ranking juice

### Branded Search Spillover (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Shopify/social media presence creates branded searches on Amazon
- Customers who see brand on social -> search brand name on Amazon -> purchase
- This organic Amazon traffic is essentially free acquisition
- Brand recognition from any channel spills over to all channels

### Customer Reactivation Campaigns (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Re-engage past customers with promotions
- Plan one promotion per week for 4 consecutive weeks
- Every holiday/event is an opportunity (Labor Day example: $1,000 from one 5-minute email)
- Use every possible channel: email, SMS, social, retargeting

## Section 2: Google Business Profile

### Google Business Profile for Amazon Brands (Norm Farrar -- E04)

**Setup:**
- Online retailers CAN register -- Google added this option for e-commerce
- Verify via website snippet (not video -- too much hassle)
- Product listings in GBP are SHOPPABLE -- link directly to Amazon, Shopify, Walmart

**Case Study:**
- 0 to 273,000 impressions driving traffic to Amazon listing
- 10 minutes/day of updates yielded this result

**Optimization Tactics:**
- Open holiday hours when competitors default to "closed" -- capture all holiday search traffic
- Post events (not just offers) -- Google gives events higher weighting
- Upload customer photos from different cities to boost local SEO in those regions
- Q&A section: load 50-100 keyword-rich questions and answers
- Regular posting schedule (events, offers, products, updates)

**Product Listings:**
- Each product links directly to purchase destination (Amazon, Shopify, etc.)
- Include high-quality images, descriptions, and pricing
- Update regularly to reflect inventory and seasonal changes

## Section 3: Owned Audience Hierarchy & Attribution

### Build, Own, Engage Audience (Kevin King -- E04)

**Three Most Valuable Business Assets (in order):**
1. Email address (highest value)
2. Mobile phone number
3. Physical mailing address (follow customer for life via national change-of-address databases)

**Key Data:**
- Facebook group post reach: ~60 out of 414 members (14%)
- Email open rate: 56% of 841 subscribers (469 people reached)
- Email crushes social for actual reach and engagement

**Smart Marketer Formula:**
- Get attention where easiest (social media, Amazon) -> transfer to owned channel (email, phone, mail)
- Always give VALUE for contact info -- warranty registrations are NOT enough value
- Carnival of Dreams party: captured 621 verified emails from ~1,000 attendees

### Attribution Link Keyword Hack (Kevin King -- E04)
- Standard Amazon Attribution links don't populate the search bar
- Add "field-keywords=your+keyword" to end of attribution URL
- Result: keyword appears in search bar when clicked, giving extra rank juice for that keyword
- Use on: blog posts, email links, social media links, any external traffic to Amazon

### Email List Building Fundamentals

**Email ROI Benchmarks (Gretta -- E01):**
- Email converts 6.05% vs social media 1.9% (3x+ higher)
- Email ROI: $44 for every $1 spent (4,400%)

**Capture Methods:**
- Landing page with lead magnet (free content, discount, exclusive access)
- Shopify popup with 15% off (Klaviyo integration)
- Blog lead magnets (Zigler perpetual sales machine model)
- Post-purchase email capture for Amazon-only customers
- Product inserts with QR code to email signup

**Landing Page Tools:** Lander, Unbounce, Leadpages, Launchrock; can store emails in Google Sheet initially

### Affiliate & Referral Programs (E05 -- Off-Amazon Week 5)
- Add affiliate program with best possible commission without losing money
- Implement customer referral program (promote on thank-you page + autoresponders)
- Test additional traffic sources where CPC < average profit per visitor:
  - Affiliates/review sites, YouTube ads, TikTok ads, Google search/display, Pinterest, Snapchat, influencer promotions, Taboola, Criteo

## Section 4: Social Media Organic & Community

### Instagram as Brand Landing Page (Mary Harcourt -- E04)
- 70% of consumers check Instagram before visiting a brand's website
- Setup: business profile + logo + SEO keywords in bio + shop integration + highlight reels
- Pin 3 videos: brand story, product demo/commercial, UGC testimonial
- QR code on product inserts directing to Instagram profile (acts as curated landing page)
- Repost customer stories to build community + reach their audience
- Link tree in bio: Amazon listing, website, YouTube, newsletter, TikTok

### 3 C's of Community (Gretta -- E01)
1. **Content** -- must add value, build trust, inspire action
   - "Content is king, context is queen, and engagement is the kingdom"
   - Use CTAs: ask to comment, tag a friend, share, swipe up, tap link in bio
   - Incentivize: 10% off for waitlist signup, free gift for bio link purchase
2. **Collaboration** -- secret weapon for social growth
   - Mutual like/comment agreements with similar-sized accounts
   - Shout-out for shout-out (S4S) between similar accounts
   - Pay larger niche accounts for paid shoutouts
3. **Consistency** -- post daily; more posts = more total engagement = more algorithmic reach
   - 1 post/week x 300 likes = 300 interactions; daily = 2,100 interactions/week

### Content Territories (Gretta -- E01)
- 3-5 categories/topics for content across all channels
- Two universal territories: Products & Promotions + Celebrating Community
- Shape remaining 2-3 around customer persona mindsets
- The Fifth: Inspire & Aspire, Wandering & Well-Being, Rest & Relaxation, Products & Promotions, The Fifth Fam
- Identify Top Performing Content (TPC) through engagement (20%+ above average)
- Post MORE of what works; "figure out the kind of content your audience loves and give them more of it"

### Content Curation vs Creation (Gretta -- E01)
- Curation = sharing existing content with credit to elevate unknown brand
- High-quality curated content communicates professionalism without expensive photography
- Use Repostly app for resharing (maintains quality)
- Always credit original creator ("via @accountname")
- Two types to curate: influencer content in your niche + viral niche content

### SEO Fundamentals (Jason Wong -- E02)
- SEO is long-term organic traffic investment
- Focus on content marketing alongside technical SEO
- FTC compliance: must disclose paid partnerships/sponsored content

### Promotion Channel Checklist (E05)
Choose 10+ promotional methods daily: YouTube video, email to list, 2 Instagram stories, 2 Facebook stories, Instagram post, Facebook post, blog article, text message. Maximize each channel before adding new ones.

### Buyer Journey for External Traffic (Gretta -- E01)
- **Awareness (Top)** -- cold leads: social posts, blog posts, giveaways, educational videos, ebooks
- **Evaluation (Middle)** -- warm audience: social proof (reviews, UGC, press, influencer endorsements) + product-focused content
- **Purchase (Bottom)** -- hot audience: offers (free shipping, free returns, guarantee, free gift, first-order discount)

### Social Proof Categories (Gretta -- E01)
- Popularity (likes, comments, views)
- Public praise (reviews, testimonials)
- Endorsements from experts
- Proof of demand (waitlist numbers, sold-out indicators)
- 83% of consumers trust recommendations from friends/family (Nielsen)
- Branded content trust: ~30%

### External Traffic Anti-Patterns
- Relying solely on Amazon PPC without building owned audience
- Not using attribution links with keyword parameter (losing ranking juice)
- Warranty registration as only email capture method (not enough value)
- Neglecting Google Business Profile (free traffic left on table)
- Posting inconsistently on social media (algorithm punishes gaps)
- Not using problem-based keywords (paying premium for product keyword CPCs)

### Off-Amazon Week 5 Expanded Traffic Sources (E05)
Test additional sources only where CPC < average profit per visitor:
- Affiliates and review sites
- YouTube ads
- TikTok ads
- Google search and display ads
- Pinterest ads
- Snapchat ads
- Influencer promotions
- Taboola ads
- Criteo ads
- End goal: $1M+/month from diverse traffic + repeat + subscription
