## INSTRUCTOR SYNTHESIS

### Gretta van Riel (E01) -- Audience-First Launch
- Build audience BEFORE product exists; "market-product fit" not "product-market fit"
- Instagram growth + email waitlist as primary pre-launch engine
- Tag-to-win giveaways for rapid follower growth (20K followers in 24 hours)
- Launch-day urgency with limited stock messaging and psychology triggers
- The Fifth: 70K followers, 8,800 waitlist, $100K+ first-day sales

### Matt Clark (E03) -- 6-Week Scaling Roadmap
- Week 1: Maximize RPV. Week 2: Best landing page. Week 3: Test ads. Week 4: Scale one source. Week 5: Multiple sources. Week 6: Repeat/subscription
- 5-hook landing page test method to find winning angle before scaling
- 9-step initial traffic generation before spending on paid ads
- Asymmetric risk framework: max loss $5-10K, potential multimillion-dollar outcome

### Izabela Hamilton (E04) -- Time-Release Launch Framework
- Three hidden levers: velocity control, shopper behavior signals, traffic authority injection
- Gradual ramp (not spike) to show Amazon consistent growth signals
- Review velocity matching: keep at 1-10% of orders (500 orders = 20-50 reviews)
- Case study: $800K/month to $1.9M/month in 5 months using layered approach

### Jason Wong (E02) -- Mock Launch Validation
- Launch with Photoshop mockup, no inventory; measure conversion signals
- Holy Meme Bible: single Photoshop picture generated $250K+ first weeks
- If orders come in and you can't fulfill, refund (avoid chargebacks)

## LAUNCH PATH DECISION TREE

**Step 1: Which platform is your primary channel?**

### Shopify-First Launch
1. Maximize RPV before driving any traffic (Matt Clark 9-step system)
2. Build email waitlist with 7-component landing page (Gretta)
3. Create 5 hook variations on landing pages (Matt Clark)
4. Generate initial traffic with 9 organic/low-cost steps
5. Test hooks with small ad budgets ($10-20/day per hook)
6. Scale winning hook; expand to additional traffic sources
- -> See reference Sections 1, 3

### Amazon Launch
1. Ensure listing is live with 10+ reviews before heavy PPC
2. Use time-release framework: gradual traffic ramp over weeks (Izabela)
3. Layer in external traffic Month 2 (TikTok, Google, blogs, influencers)
4. Match review velocity to order volume (1-10% ratio)
5. Month 3-4: competitor keyword targeting via comparison content
- -> See reference Sections 2, 4

### TikTok Shop Launch
1. Complete 33-step setup sequence (see tiktok-shop-setup)
2. Set up 3 always-on promotions (free shipping, flash sale, coupon)
3. Begin daily creator outreach 30-50/day (see content-creator-strategy)
4. Wait for sufficient converting content before GMV Max ads
5. Start Video Shopping Ads if limited content; graduate to GMV Max
- -> See tiktok-shop-setup for platform details, content-creator-strategy for outreach

## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: AUDIENCE FIRST VS PRODUCT FIRST

| Position | Advocate |
|---|---|
| Build audience FIRST, then create product | Gretta van Riel |
| Find product opportunity first using data, then build brand | Matt Clark |
| PMF is dynamic; can change target customer instead of product | Jason Wong |

**Resolution:** Both paths are valid. Audience-first works when you have social media skills and can grow a niche account quickly (Gretta's playbook). Product-first works when you have sourcing skills and want data-driven validation (Clark's playbook). Note Jason's insight: PMF can be achieved by changing the audience, not just the product.

## MARKETING PSYCHOLOGY TRIGGERS FOR LAUNCH

Three most powerful for e-commerce launch (Gretta -- E01, Cialdini-based):

1. **Reciprocity** -- give something to get something (free content for email signup; SkinnyMeTea 14-day Booty Challenge drove 8,000 signups in 1 day)
2. **Social Proof** -- 83% trust friend/family recommendations vs ~30% trust branded content; show reviews, UGC, press, waitlist numbers
3. **Scarcity** -- limited availability increases perceived value (The Fifth: only available 5 days/month; countdown timers, limited stock messaging)
