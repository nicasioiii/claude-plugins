# Launch Strategy -- Reference

## Section 1: Pre-Launch Audience Building

### Audience-First Model (Gretta -- E01)
- "If you build it they will come" is the biggest myth in e-commerce
- Build audience FIRST, then create product for them ("market-product fit")
- Drop Bottle: started @detoxwater reposting fruit-infused water images from Pinterest; grew to 800K+ followers; reverse-engineered audience to create product
- The Fifth: giveaways + shoutouts + influencer marketing grew Instagram to 70K followers pre-launch; 8,800 email waitlist; 1,000+ watches sold on day one; $100K+ first-day sales

### Email Waitlist (Gretta -- E01)

**7 Components of Great Waitlist Landing Page:**
1. Headline (attention, FOMO, anticipation)
2. Subhead (one-liner: what product is, who it's for)
3. Hero image (product photo, prototype, or 3D render; show context of use)
4. Clear benefits-driven copy (from brand pyramid rational benefits)
5. Social proof (even pre-launch -- get friend or influencer testimonial)
6. Clickable CTA connected to waitlist signup
7. Scarcity (countdown timer, scarcity-driven copy)

**CTA Language:** "Get on the VIP waitlist", "Be the first to know", "Reserve your spot", "This launch is exclusive to 100 VIPs"

**Target:** Minimum 100 subscribers before launch; The Fifth had 8,000+

**Tools:** Lander, Unbounce, Leadpages, Launchrock; can store emails in Google Sheet initially

**Benchmarks:** Email converts 6.05% vs social media 1.9% (3x+ higher); email ROI: $44 per $1 spent (4,400%)

### Giveaway Mechanics -- Tag-to-Win (Gretta -- E01)

**The Fifth Results:** 20,000 new followers in 24 hours; 80,000+ comments; most engaged brand on Instagram that day; only gave away 2 watches

**Caption Template:**
"Tag to win a [product] for you and your friend. How to participate: 1. Follow @[brand] 2. Tag a friend under this photo and ask them to follow @[brand]. Bonus entry: 1 comment = 1 entry and you can tag 1 friend per comment. You must both be following to win. Competition ends in 48 hours."

**Best Practices:**
- Strong, clear CTA immediately
- Make entry easy (tag 1 friend per comment, not 5)
- Prize must be attainable (one watch, not entire collection)
- Give away TWO items (relevant for friend tagging)
- Limit timeframe (48 hours creates urgency)
- Use paid shoutouts or influencer co-hosting for scale

**Bambi Boutique Case:** 0 to 150,000 followers in 1 week using 3 macro influencer giveaways ($2,500+ prize packs each); 465,000+ comments

### Co-Branded Giveaways (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Partner with complementary brands to cross-pollinate audiences
- Both brands contribute product; both gain email subscribers

### Mock Launch Validation (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Launch product with Photoshop mockup, no inventory; measure conversion signals
- Cost: designer for assets + paid ads + web hosting = a few thousand dollars
- If orders come in and you cannot fulfill, refund (avoid chargebacks + trust loss)
- Holy Meme Bible: single Photoshop picture generated $250K+ first weeks

## Section 2: Launch Execution Frameworks

### Time-Release Launch Framework (Izabela Hamilton -- E04)

**Three Hidden Levers:**
1. **Velocity control** -- gradual traffic ramp, not spike; show Amazon consistent growth
2. **Shopper behavior signals** -- dwell time, scrolling, add-to-cart patterns matter for ranking
3. **Traffic authority injection** -- external traffic from blogs, influencers, Google, TikTok

**Review Velocity Matching:**
- Keep reviews at 1-10% of orders (500 orders = 20-50 reviews)
- Do NOT send entire email list at once -- time-release over weeks

**Month-by-Month Layered Strategy:**
- Month 1: Time-release launch to stabilize rankings
- Month 2: External traffic (TikTok, Google, blogs, influencers)
- Month 3: Competitor keyword hijacking via comparison content
- Month 4-5: Target competitor brand name keywords
- Case study: $800K/month to $1.9M/month in 5 months (140% growth)

**Bonus:** Optimize for Google SEO -- "collagen for wrinkles" ranking on Google boosts Amazon rank even more

### Attribution Link Keyword Hack (Kevin King -- E04)
- Standard Amazon Attribution links don't populate the search bar
- Add "field-keywords=your+keyword" to end of attribution URL
- Result: keyword appears in search bar when clicked, giving extra rank juice

### Launch-Day Urgency (Gretta -- E01)
- Launch to email waitlist first (warmest audience)
- Limited stock messaging ("Only X remaining")
- Countdown timers
- The Fifth model: only available 5 days per month (scarcity by time, not price)

## Section 3: Traffic Testing & 5-Hook Method

### 5-Hook Landing Page Test Method (Matt Clark -- E03)

**Process:**
1. Create one landing page with best-guess headline/hook
2. Duplicate 4 times (total of 5 pages)
3. Change ONLY the headline/hook on each page (body stays identical)
4. Each hook = different primary reason someone would buy

**Hook Generation Exercise:**
Imagine ideal customer at a coffee shop. They know all options. What ONE thing would convince them to buy YOUR product?

**Example (Tactical Flashlight):**
1. Social proof: "People across America are securing this flashlight"
2. Self-defense: "The biggest, baddest flashlight for self-defense"
3. Brightness: "The world's brightest flashlight"
4. Durability: "The indestructible flashlight"
5. Preparedness: "The best flashlight for your go bag"

**Testing:** Push small traffic ($10-20/day per hook, 1 week); identify best converter; double down on winner

### Initial Traffic Generation -- 9 Steps Before Paid Ads (Matt Clark -- E03)
1. Create big discount code for main product
2. Add discount to product page
3. Post on brand social media about promotion
4. Boost Facebook post as ad
5. Boost Instagram post about promotion
6. Post on personal social media about new store
7. Create simple Google Search ad
8. Notify past customers through every possible channel
9. Ask for reviews and use them everywhere

### 6-Week Scaling Roadmap (Matt Clark -- E03)
- **Week 1:** Maximize RPV (9-step system from shopify-store-setup)
- **Week 2:** Create best landing page (5-hook test method)
- **Week 3:** Launch and test ads (redundant tracking, $10-20/day per ad set)
- **Week 4:** Scale one traffic source (calculate revenue/profit per visitor)
- **Week 5:** Multiple traffic sources (affiliate program, referral program, test additional channels where CPC < profit/visitor)
- **Week 6:** Repeat and subscription sales

### Redundant Tracking (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Do NOT rely solely on Facebook/Google reported conversions
- Use third-party tracking (AffiliatelyApp) as secondary verification
- Compare platform-reported vs third-party vs actual Shopify orders
- Platforms systematically over-report conversions

### Daily Ad Performance Checklist (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Total Shopify sales, number of orders, AOV
- Estimated gross profit %
- Total ad spend (all platforms)
- Per-platform: spend, clicks, CPC, reported orders, third-party verified orders
- ROAS and break-even ROAS

## Section 4: Multi-Platform Launch Sequencing

### Recommended Launch Order
1. **Shopify first** (Matt Clark: "sell on Shopify first, expand to Amazon")
   - Validates product-market fit with full customer data
   - Tests hooks and creative before Amazon launch
   - Captures email addresses for long-term owned audience
2. **Amazon second** (once Shopify validates demand)
   - Listing must be live with 10+ reviews before PPC (Melisa Vong)
   - Use time-release framework for gradual ranking growth
   - Branded search spillover from Shopify/social drives Amazon organic
3. **TikTok Shop third** (requires existing inventory)
   - Needs Amazon MCF or 3PL inventory already in place
   - Content pipeline must be active before ads

### Amazon + TikTok Integration (Tim Jordan -- E04)
- Amazon = high intent, low awareness; TikTok = high awareness, low intent
- Combine into a stable funnel: TikTok drives awareness, Amazon converts search intent
- TikTok Shop makes awareness-to-purchase transition smoother
- Do not choose one platform over another -- integrate them

### Off-Amazon 6-Week Program -- Launch Phase (Matt Clark -- E05)
- **Week 3:** Launch and test ads (Facebook: 5 ad sets per hook, $10-20/day each; Google search: $20/day, long-tail keywords)
- **Week 4:** Scale one traffic source (calculate revenue/profit per visitor first)
- **Week 5:** Add affiliate program, customer referral program, test additional sources where CPC < profit/visitor
- End goal: $1M+/month from diverse traffic + repeat + subscription

### Promotion Channel Checklist (E05)
Daily promotional activity across 10+ channels: YouTube video, email to list, 2 Instagram stories, 2 Facebook stories, Instagram post, Facebook post, blog article, text message. Maximize each channel before adding new ones.

### Pre-Launch Product Seeding (Gretta -- E01)
- At The Fifth: sent 50 watches to 50 influencers before launch to create content
- Focus on micro-influencers (10K-100K) for better engagement and ROI
- Content from seeded influencers becomes launch-day social proof
- Negotiate usage rights for influencer content to repurpose across channels

### Amazon Launch Velocity Requirements
- Minimum 10 reviews before increasing PPC budget (Melisa Vong -- E05)
- Products with under 50 reviews can still do $5K+/month if positioned well
- Use Vine program for initial reviews (Brand Registry required)
- "Request a Review" button automation via Helium 10

### Launch Anti-Patterns
- Sending entire email list at once (kills gradual velocity signals on Amazon)
- Launching on Amazon before listing has reviews (wasted PPC spend)
- Running GMV Max on TikTok without sufficient converting content
- Not testing hooks before scaling ad spend (Matt Clark's biggest emphasis)
- Ordering more than MOQ for first production run (Gretta: "I've been burned")
- Not using redundant tracking from day one (platform data over-reports)

### SAVE Marketing Framework (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Strategic planning approach for marketing initiatives
- Pre-iOS14.5: targeting/audiences were king; creative was afterthought
- Post-iOS14.5: only 3 things matter: Offer, Landing Page, Content
- 70% of budget should go to content creation, 30% to distribution
