## COMPLETE BUSINESS CHECKLIST (Melisa Vong)

Nine categories covering every operational area of an Amazon/DTC business:

### 1. Legal & Corporate
- Corporation setup and operating agreement
- NDA for suppliers and partners
- Trademark: shortlist names, select, file, register
- Web domain and social media handle audit

### 2. Administration
- Business bank account and credit card
- PayPal and payment processing
- Hosting and Shopify setup
- Slack, Google Drive, QuickBooks
- Amazon Seller Central setup, Brand Registry, category ungating

### 3. Brand, Web & Design
- Brand identity: name, slogan, values
- Target audience personas
- Competitive analysis
- Brand design: logo and packaging (99Designs)
- Shopify store, blog, email marketing (Klaviyo)

### 4. Supply Chain
- Shortlist products and determine formulation
- Shortlist manufacturers and calculate CPUs
- Place orders, packaging, labels, accessories
- Documentation: FDA, cGMP, organic certifications

### 5. Content & Social Media
- Keyword research and professional copywriting
- Listing images and storefront
- Blog SEO and website product info
- Social media setup across all platforms
- Automation and scheduling tools
- Influencer lists: 100 bloggers + 100 YouTubers + 100 Instagrammers + 100 TikTokers

### 6. Customer Service
- Zendesk or Gorgias setup
- Voicemail and automated messaging
- Customer care handbook
- Hire customer service representative

### 7. Data & Performance
- Helium 10 or Sellerboard for Amazon analytics
- Daily sales tracker
- Google Analytics for Shopify
- Keyword tracking tools

### 8. Campaign & Sales
- Product launch schedule
- Daily velocity rate tracking
- Execute launches and gather 50 reviews per product
- Page 1 positioning for top 5-10 keywords

### 9. Distribution
- Website payment gateway
- Affiliate plugin
- Subscribe-and-save setup
- Amazon Canada (French labels), Amazon Europe (VAT, consultant)

## SOP DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS

### What Makes a Good SOP (Synthesized from Escala + Melisa Vong)
- Written at Level 5 (Working Instructions): step-by-step with screenshots and videos
- Includes criteria for success (how do you know it was done correctly)
- Includes FAQ section for common edge cases
- Links to the workflow task that executes it
- Version-controlled with last-updated date
- Stored in central Wiki, not in personal folders or email threads

### SOP Priority Matrix
Prioritize documentation based on:
1. **Revenue impact:** Processes that directly generate or protect revenue
2. **Frequency:** Processes performed daily or weekly before monthly or quarterly
3. **Dependency risk:** Processes that only one person knows how to do
4. **Error cost:** Processes where mistakes are expensive or irreversible
5. **Delegation readiness:** Processes you plan to hand off to a hire or VA soon

## INVENTORY MANAGEMENT DEEP DIVE (Jason Wong)

### Demand Forecasting Framework
- Track sell-through rate (units sold per day/week)
- Calculate reorder point: daily sell-through x (manufacturing lead time + shipping time + safety buffer)
- SKU system with barcodes for accuracy
- Monitor cash tied up in inventory -- this is the biggest DTC challenge (Adii Pienar / Cogsy)

### Inventory Replenishment Formula
```
Reorder Point = (Average Daily Sales x Lead Time in Days) + Safety Stock
Safety Stock = (Maximum Daily Sales - Average Daily Sales) x Maximum Lead Time
```

### Shipping Strategy Selection (Jason Wong)
- Sea freight for planned inventory (4-6 weeks but cheapest)
- Air freight only for urgent or small shipments (1-2 weeks, 5-10x more expensive)
- Free shipping threshold must generate enough additional margin to cover shipping costs
- Calculator: if proposed threshold is $50 and current AOV is $39.75, the extra $10.25 at 81% gross margin = $8.32 additional profit; minus $7.41 shipping cost = $0.92 extra profit per order

## CUSTOMER SUPPORT SYSTEM (Jason Wong)

### Gorgias Setup Best Practices
- Centralize all support channels (email, chat, social) into one platform
- Create macros and templates for the top 20 most common questions
- Set response time targets (every email answered within 1 hour = brand value differentiator)
- Use Wonderment for proactive shipping notifications to reduce WISMO tickets
- Social proof via Junip: display photo and video reviews prominently

### Customer Support Hiring
- First hire for most DTC brands should be customer service
- Create customer care handbook before hiring
- Train on SOP documentation methodology as part of onboarding

## AI BOT IMPLEMENTATION GUIDE (Steve Simonson)

### Brand Bot (Priority 1)
- Deploy in 10 minutes: upload product files, scrape website, test, launch
- Handles FAQ, order status, product recommendations
- 24/7 availability eliminates timezone constraints
- Klarna benchmark: 66% of inquiries resolved by AI at equal satisfaction

### Telephone Bot (Priority 2)
- bland.AI at $0.09/minute
- 24/7 multilingual phone support
- Handles basic inquiries and routes complex issues to humans

### Internal Database Chatbot (Priority 3)
- Query business data in natural language
- Essential at $5M+ revenue for quick decision-making
- Eliminates dependency on analyst for basic data questions

### Multi-Agent Bot Army (Priority 4)
- Specialized bots working in concert
- Examples: hashtag researcher, SEO bot, content writer, scheduler, quality checker
- Tools: n8n.io for visual bot building, CrewAI.com for multi-agent orchestration
- Non-English sellers: AI eliminates language and culture barrier for US market entry

## OPERATIONS MATURITY ASSESSMENT TEMPLATE

### Scoring Rubric (0-5 per dimension)
| Dimension | Score 1 (Ad Hoc) | Score 3 (Developing) | Score 5 (Optimized) |
|-----------|-----------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Process Documentation | No SOPs; tribal knowledge only | Some SOPs exist but inconsistent | All core processes documented to Level 5 |
| Technology | Spreadsheets for everything | Mix of dedicated tools and spreadsheets | Purpose-built tools with no redundancy |
| Team Structure | Founder does everything | 1-3 hires or contractors | Defined roles with clear accountabilities |
| Automation | Manual everything | Some email/notification automation | AI bots and workflow automation in place |
| Knowledge Management | In founder's head | Shared Google Drive with some docs | ClickUp Wiki with linked workflows |
| Onboarding | Shadow the founder for weeks | Some training materials | Self-service onboarding via Wiki + SOPs |

### Interpreting Results
- **Total 0-10:** Crisis mode. Focus on hiring first customer service person and documenting top 3 daily processes
- **Total 11-20:** Foundation building. Run SOP sprints for revenue-critical processes. Start technology audit
- **Total 21-30:** Scaling ready. Implement ClickUp dual-system. Deploy AI bots. Build automation index
