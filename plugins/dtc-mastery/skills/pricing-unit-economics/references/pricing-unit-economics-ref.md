# Pricing & Unit Economics -- Reference

## Section 1: Markup Rules & Pricing Frameworks

### 2.5x Markup Rule (Gretta -- E01)
- Sell for at least 2.5x COGS per unit ($20 COGS = $50 minimum retail)
- Reason: need room for wholesale (50% cut) + production/startup costs
- Max 3 product variations, all value-based (e.g., $20/$40/$60)
- SkinnyMeTea: 14-day ~$35, 28-day ~$65; 28-day priced to drive higher purchases
- Luxury markups can be 10-20x production cost (perceived value + brand equity)

### 4x Landed Cost Rule (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Landed cost = manufacturer cost + duties/taxes + shipping
- If product + freight + packaging costs $5, sell for $20+
- Validates by checking Amazon: are products selling well at 2x average price?
- Example: average poncho $18-19, military poncho sells at $45 doing $60K/month
- High margins enable: quantity discounts, ad spend, bundle offers

### One-Third Rule -- Amazon (Melisa Vong -- E05)
- Price point sweet spot: $12-$150 (below $12 margins too thin; above $150 manufacturing costly)
- Profit margin target: 30-33%
- Approximately: 1/3 product cost, 1/3 Amazon fees, 1/3 profit

### Pricing Tactics When Margin Too Low (Gretta -- E01)
1. Cut production costs (negotiate, alternative manufacturer, different materials)
2. Bundle low-margin with high-margin items (themed packs, upsells)
3. Raise the price ("when in doubt, raise the price")
- Board shorts example: started at $100, reduced to $80 when market resisted; maintained good margin

### AOV & Offer Benchmarks (Shackelford via Wong -- E02)
- Best profitable AOV: $67+ target
- Average CPA: ~$32 (US + international)
- Best performing offers: BOGO (boosts AOV + sells units), Buy Two Get One, Bundles of complementary products
- "The person who can spend the most money to acquire a customer will win."

### TikTok Shop Pricing Strategy (Michelle -- E04/E06)
- TikTok Shop price can be HIGHER than Amazon -- Amazon bots cannot see TikTok prices
- Build into retail pricing: referral fees, fulfillment, discounts, coupons, affiliate commissions, ad costs
- Keep products under $50 for impulse-buy optimization on TikTok
- Combine free shipping + flash deals + coupons for maximum conversion (16% conversion lift from free shipping alone)

## Section 2: Landed Cost & Tariff Optimization

### Landed Cost Calculator (Golzari -- E05)
**Complete Formula:** Landed Cost = Product Price + Import Duty + Freight Cost

**Components:**
- Product price from factory (per unit)
- Carton dimensions and quantity per carton
- CBM calculation for container sizing (20ft vs 40ft vs 40ft HC)
- Import duty rate via HTS code
- Freight cost per unit (sea vs air)
- Delivery cost to customer

### Tariff Optimization (Afolabi Oyerokun -- E04)
- Most freight forwarders use WRONG HTS codes -- could be overpaying by $275K+/year
- Check Form 7501 from freight forwarder for current tariff codes
- Free tools at tariffterminator.com: HTS code lookup, classification, duty calculator, bulk verification
- Always verify HTS codes BEFORE launching -- include in ROI calculations
- Example: changing HTS code saved 27% in duty costs

### HTS Code Optimization (Golzari -- E05)
- Change product description or materials to hit different import duty rate
- Example: 12% down to 2-3% = instant 10% cost savings
- This is legal optimization, not evasion -- work with customs broker

### External Factors Affecting Price (Golzari -- E05)
- Raw material costs (verify with multiple suppliers)
- Labor costs (verify with multiple suppliers)
- Exchange rates (check xe.com; can save significantly on timing)
- Factory's production capacity utilization (slow periods = better pricing)

## Section 3: RPV, ROAS & Profitability Math

### Revenue Per Visitor Formula (Matt Clark -- E03)
**RPV = Average Order Value x Conversion Rate**

| AOV | Conv Rate | RPV | Profitable if CPC < |
|---|---|---|---|
| $30 | 2% | $0.60 | $0.60 |
| $60 | 2% | $1.20 | $1.20 |
| $60 | 5% | $3.00 | $3.00 |

- If RPV > CPC, you are profitable on paid traffic
- Case study: member went from $100K/month to $800K/month (8x) by focusing solely on maximizing RPV

### Break-Even ROAS (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Break-even ROAS = 1 / Gross Profit Margin %
- Example: 50% gross margin = 2.0 ROAS break-even
- Example: 33% gross margin = 3.0 ROAS break-even
- Any ROAS above break-even = profit on ad spend

### Retail Math P&L Framework (Jon Derkits -- E04)
**Revenue = Traffic x Conversion x AOV** (wrapped in inventory availability)

**Full P&L:**
- Revenue - COGS - freight - referral fees - FBA fees - placement fees = Contribution Profit
- Contribution Profit = break-even ACOS ceiling
- Contribution Profit - PPC = Net Profit
- Net Profit - net working capital (inventory investment) = Free Cash Flow
- Rule #1: don't go out of stock

### Revenue Formula (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Revenue = Product sales + shipping revenue - sales tax
- Sales tax is NOT revenue -- you owe it to government
- Fixed costs: rent, utilities, payroll, subscriptions (incurred regardless of sales)
- Variable costs: COGS, freight/receiving, transaction fees (2.1-2.9% + $0.30), fulfillment, postage

### CAC Calculator (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Formula: Gross profit - target net profit = maximum CAC
- Variable cost benchmark: keep below 50% of revenue
- Transaction fees: Shopify Payments = 2.1-2.9% + $0.30; non-Shopify payment adds 1% base fee

### DTC Marketing System Key Metrics (E05)
- 4X markup minimum from Shopify/DTC product
- If product + freight + packaging = $5, sell for $20+
- Calculate average revenue and profit per VISITOR (not just per order)
- Test additional traffic sources only where CPC < average profit per visitor

## Section 4: Cash Flow, Storage & Financial Planning

### Unit Economics & Storage Multiplier (Chelsea Cohen -- E04)

**Three Profit Layers:**
1. Pre-holding profit (before storage costs)
2. Current profit (with accumulated storage costs)
3. Projected profit (with future storage costs)

**Storage Multiplier:**
- 2 months inventory = 1.5x multiplier per unit
- 6 months inventory = 4x multiplier
- Example: 29% pre-holding profit drops to 19% projected after storage costs
- Actionable: cut low-margin products dragging down cash flow BEFORE launching new ones
- Case study: dropping underperformers freed $24K positive cash flow while maintaining $146K revenue

### Cash Conversion Cycle (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Time between paying for inventory and receiving customer payment
- Shorter CCC = healthier cash flow
- Strategies: negotiate longer supplier payment terms, shorter collection from customers

### Credit Card Cash Flow Strategy (Josh Haley -- E04)
1. Request Amazon increase ad billing threshold to $10,000
2. ALWAYS pay ad spend with credit cards (preserve cash + earn rewards)
3. Open multiple cards with STAGGERED statement dates (every 3-7 days apart)
4. Only charge each card during first 5 days of its statement period
5. Result: 55-60 day payback on normal AMEX, 90 days on AMEX Plum
6. Recommended cards: AMEX Gold (4x on first $150K), AMEX Plum (60 day terms), Chase Business Preferred (3x on $150K)

**Supplier Payment Hack (Melio):**
- Pay supplier invoices via credit card through Melio (2.9% fee offset by 2.5% rewards = 0.4% net cost)
- Converts supplier terms to net 60-90 without negotiating
- With negotiated net 90 + this strategy = net 150 day payment terms

### Expense Optimization -- 5 Strategies (Jason Wong -- E02)
1. Negotiate with every PO increase (lower per-unit, bulk discounts, quicker payment discounts)
2. Monthly expense audit (essential vs nice-to-have; review all fees)
3. Break down cost of delivery (lighter packaging, sea over air, negotiate 3PL fees)
4. Prioritize high-ROI with short payback (ads over R&D; negotiate terms for long-payback items)
5. Hire only when necessary (freelancers first; US full-time = ~1.5x base salary)

### Asymmetric Risk Framework (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Maximum startup risk: $5K-$10K
- Potential: multimillion-dollar business, asset worth 3-20x profit
- As long as you can survive the maximum loss, take the bet
- Every test (new product, new ad, new channel) should follow this framework
- Three rules: (1) Estimate realistic downside, (2) Move fast, (3) Always test small

### Finance 101 (Gretta -- E01)
**Gross Profit Margin:** (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue x 100

**COGS Components:** production costs, freight to fulfillment, picking/packing (3PL), storage fees, transaction fees, software costs, staff, branding/design, inspections, legal, samples

**Benchmarks:**
- Minimum 30% gross profit margin; ideally closer to 60%
- SkinnyMeTea first year: ~80% (wasn't investing enough back into growth)
- "Your profit margin is your mistakes margin" -- more profit = more room for error

### Budget Guidelines (Matt Clark -- E03)
- Inventory: $3,000-$5,000 (including shipping to fulfillment)
- Total all-in (inventory + ads + photography + everything): $5,000-$10,000
- Do NOT go above $10,000 for first product launch

### Two Ways to Build Wealth (Matt Clark -- E03)
1. **Cash flow** -- reinvest profits to grow sales base, then extract profit
2. **Asset value** -- e-commerce businesses valued at 3x profit up to 2x revenue (can be 20x profit)
- LifeBoost timeline: Year 1 reinvested ALL profits; past Year 1: $600-700K/month; current: $2.6M/month; ~50% subscription

### Sales Tax Automation (Jason Wong -- E02)
- Use automated sales tax collection (Avalara, TaxJar)
- Must collect in states where you have nexus
- Sales tax is NOT revenue -- you owe it to government
- Separate from pricing strategy but must be factored into cash flow
