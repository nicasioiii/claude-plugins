## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICES

### Disagreement: Initial Inventory Size

| Position | Advocate | When It Applies |
|---|---|---|
| Start with MOQ (as low as possible) -- "I've been burned" ordering more | Gretta van Riel | First-time founders, limited capital, DTC-first brands |
| 300-500 units is fine because you're not trying to rank on Amazon | Matt Clark | DTC Shopify-first brands (recommended default for new DTC) |
| Need 1,000+ units for Amazon launch velocity | Traditional Amazon advice | Amazon-first sellers needing ranking velocity |

**Resolution:** For DTC-first brands (Shopify primary), 300-500 units is correct (Clark). For Amazon-first sellers, higher quantities may be needed. For TikTok Shop, have sufficient FBA inventory since you cannot predict viral content timing.

### Disagreement: Breaking the Mold vs. Private Label

| Position | Advocate | When It Applies |
|---|---|---|
| Modify existing design for first product (recommended) | Gretta van Riel | First-time founders. Change 1 dimension. Lower risk. |
| Start with simplest MVP, iterate from customer feedback | Jason Wong | Get to market fast. Don't over-engineer v1. |
| Full custom product with complete spec sheet | Kian Golzari | Experienced sellers. Novel product innovations. |

**Resolution:** First product = modify existing design or private label. Second+ product = custom development with full Golzari process.

## MVP Validation (Gretta van Riel)

### Three MVP Options (in order of preference)
1. **Hand-make a demo version** -- SkinnyMeTea MVP = tea blend in Ziploc bags with stickers
2. **Get a 3D render/prototype designed digitally** -- Budget: $250-$500 for quality freelance
3. **Get local manufacturer to create physical prototype** -- Higher cost but tangible product

### MVP Process
1. Show MVP to original 20 validation people (from product-ideation)
2. Watch reactions, film interactions
3. Iterate until product matches what people want (Lean Startup concept)
4. Do NOT listen to friends/family for feedback
5. Sequence: validate -> start local -> move fast -> get feedback -> scale big

## Three Paths to Product (Gretta van Riel)

| Path | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Modify existing** | Change 1 dimension of existing product | First product (RECOMMENDED) |
| **Break the mold** | Custom product from scratch | Strong supplier relationship, novel innovation |
| **Private label** | Pre-existing design, add your logo | Already have audience; fastest to market |

For custom products: develop locally first, then send sample to global manufacturer for reverse engineering.

## 10-Step Product Creation Process (Kian Golzari)

1. **Self-actualize:** Create the product YOU want to use yourself
2. **Start with passion:** Your vision advantage = competitive moat
3. **Express it in product/packaging/bundling**
4. **Solve a real problem** (TRTL travel pillow: sold 1M+ units)
5. **Align with core values** (sustainability, quality, etc.)
6. **Complete specification sheet** (one-pager with ALL details)
7. **Select best supplier** (-> sourcing-suppliers skill)
8. **3-stage sampling process** (Development -> Pre-production -> Pre-shipment)
9. **Test rigorously** (yourself, 3rd party, influencer beta testers, customer avatar)
10. **Make or deconstruct one item yourself** -- see product in 30 pieces, not 1 unit

## Specification Sheet (Golzari)

Critical one-pager sent to manufacturers. Must include:
- Brand name, product name, product code, launch season
- Fabrics/materials breakdown per component
- Pantone colors
- Material specifics (e.g., Polyester 300D vs 900D, Nylon, Waterproof)
- Product dimensions and size range
- Product images and competitor images
- New feature requests
- Label, hangtag, packaging requirements
- Product sketches and comments
- Request for quote sheet

Fill this out yourself even though supplier builds one internally -- single reference document for you.

## 3-Stage Sampling Process (Golzari)

| Stage | Quality Target | Key Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Development sample** | 90-95% correct | Iterate based on spec sheet until satisfied |
| **Pre-production sample** | 100% perfect | Label, tag, date it. Sign off with photos. THEN place deposit. |
| **Pre-shipment sample** | Must match pre-production 100% | Taken from actual production run. Get third-party PSI. |

**Critical rule:** NEVER place a deposit until you have the 100% perfect pre-production sample in hand.

## Packaging Design

### Five Considerations (Gretta van Riel)
1. **Unboxing experience** -- first physical touchpoint with customer
2. **Shareability on social media** -- package worth photographing/filming
3. **Shape** -- doesn't need to be standard box
4. **Perceived value alignment** -- luxury product needs premium packaging
5. **Functionality** -- fragile product needs proper padding

### Three Purposes (Jason Wong)
1. Represent brand identity
2. Protect product during transit
3. Create memorable unboxing experience

### Packaging as Feature (Golzari)
- Packaging should ENHANCE the product, not just contain it
- Different options have different costs (hang tag vs color box)
- Eco-friendly packaging aligns with sustainability values
- Consider packaging as a product development opportunity

### Packaging Sources
- **100-300 units:** Use packaging company (Lumi.com, Ink Packaging)
- **500+ units:** Go direct to manufacturer (Alibaba)
- Same designer who did logo/branding can do packaging design
- Inspect for typos and spec compliance (biggest issues)

### Product Inserts (Matt Clark)
- Powerful tool for building direct customer relationships
- Include QR code to email signup or review page
- Drive repeat purchases
- Include satisfaction guarantee messaging
- Include customer support contact info

## Designing Your Prototype (Gretta)

- **Budget:** $250-$500 for quality freelance prototype design
- **Source:** Upwork, 99designs -- search "industrial designer" or "product designer"
- **Render vs. Prototype:** Render = digital 3D visual; Prototype = physical sample
- **Three key briefing elements:**
  1. Product features (non-negotiables)
  2. Materials
  3. Start & Scale framework: lightweight, high perceived value, easy to ship
- **Pro tip:** Plan 3D renders as MARKETING ASSETS, not just manufacturing files
- DIY sizing: cut cardboard, use measuring tape (The Fifth team stuck paper cutouts to wrists)

## Regulatory Compliance (Jason Wong)

### 5 Types of Regulatory Requirements
1. **Product-specific safety standards** -- Use CPSC Regulatory Robot tool
2. **Substance regulations** -- California Prop 65 (US); REACH (EU)
3. **Documentation requirements** -- General Certificate of Conformity (US); Declaration of Conformity (EU)
4. **Labeling requirements** -- Vary by product + destination
5. **Testing requirements** -- Medical claims, cosmetics, SPF levels

**Horror story:** Ramen Noodle Condom -- condoms are Type 2 medical devices (FDA); tens of thousands in costs + years for approval; had to throw away all inventory.

**Rule:** Always consult a test lab or product compliance consultant before committing to regulated categories.

## The /spec-sheet Command

When the user runs `/spec-sheet`, execute this guided process:

### Step 1: Gather Product Details
Ask: What is the product? What materials? Key dimensions? Target price point? Target market (US/EU/both)?

### Step 2: Generate Specification Sheet
Output a complete spec sheet document with:
- Product name, brand name, product code, target launch season
- Materials breakdown per component
- Pantone colors (or color descriptions to convert)
- Dimensions and size range
- Feature requirements (functional + aesthetic)
- Packaging requirements
- Label/hangtag specifications
- Competitor reference images to include
- Quote request section for suppliers

### Step 3: Sampling Plan
Output the 3-stage sampling timeline with:
- Development sample targets and iteration expectations
- Pre-production sample sign-off checklist
- Pre-shipment inspection requirements
- Estimated timeline (30 days for samples, 45-60 days manufacturing)

### Step 4: Compliance Check
Flag any regulatory requirements based on the product category and target market. Recommend compliance consultant if category is regulated.

### Step 5: Next Steps
Direct to sourcing-suppliers skill for the 10-3-1 method and supplier outreach.
