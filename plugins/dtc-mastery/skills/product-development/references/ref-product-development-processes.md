# Product Development Processes -- Deep Reference

## Specification Sheet Template (Kian Golzari)

### Required Fields
| Section | Contents |
|---|---|
| **Header** | Brand name, product name, product code, launch season, date |
| **Materials** | Fabrics/materials per component (e.g., Polyester 300D vs 900D, Nylon, Waterproof) |
| **Colors** | Pantone codes for all colors |
| **Dimensions** | Product dimensions and full size range |
| **Visuals** | Product images, competitor images for reference |
| **Features** | New feature requests with priority ranking |
| **Labeling** | Label placement, content, regulatory requirements |
| **Hangtags** | Design, material, attachment method |
| **Packaging** | Box type, dimensions, materials, inserts, QR codes |
| **Sketches** | Product sketches with annotations and comments |
| **Quote Request** | Request pricing at 500 / 1,000 / 5,000 / 10,000 units |

### Spec Sheet Best Practices
- Keep it to ONE page (front and back OK)
- Include both your product images AND competitor images
- Specify exact material grades (not just "polyester" -- specify denier)
- Include tolerances where relevant
- Send identical spec sheet to all suppliers for fair comparison
- Fill this out yourself even though supplier creates their own version

## Product Development Checklist (Kian Golzari)

- [ ] Review top 5 competitors online
- [ ] Visit retail store holding competitor products
- [ ] Read ALL negative AND positive reviews online
- [ ] Read the Q&A sections on Amazon listings
- [ ] Purchase competitor samples and test them yourself
- [ ] Check with suppliers what they're doing for other customers/countries
- [ ] Answer: What is the clear USP over the market leader?
- [ ] Deconstruct the product to reveal improvements
- [ ] Create mood board/vision board
- [ ] Create specification sheet
- [ ] Cost up improvements to ensure still competitive
- [ ] Discover packaging options to improve product
- [ ] Can a complimentary free product improve conversion and perceived value?
- [ ] Align with manufacturer that fits your purpose (best price/quality)
- [ ] Order samples from suppliers
- [ ] Test products yourself, provide feedback in clear document

## Competitor Research Process (Golzari)

### Track A: Competitor Listings Analysis
- Read ALL positive and negative reviews
- Read Q&A sections thoroughly
- Analyze competitor images and sales copy
- Identify: likes/dislikes, features/benefits, wants/needs

### Track B: Competitor Sample Analysis
- Purchase competitor samples and physically test them
- **Reverse engineer construction:** Identify raw materials, individual parts
- **Function and usage tests:** Actually USE the product
- **Packaging analysis:** Study how packaging enhances experience
- Wash in cold/hot temperatures; test durability to failure point
- Create action list of all improvements needed

## 3-Stage Sampling Process Details (Golzari)

### Stage 1: Development Sample
- **Quality target:** 90-95% correct based on spec sheet
- **Purpose:** Verify manufacturer can execute your vision
- **Iteration:** May take 2-3 rounds to get right
- **Cost:** Usually 20-50% markup over production price
- **Timing:** 1-3 weeks per iteration
- **Key question:** Does the sample demonstrate capability to produce to spec?

### Stage 2: Pre-Production Sample
- **Quality target:** 100% perfect
- **Purpose:** Final sign-off before production begins
- **Actions required:**
  1. Label the sample with date
  2. Tag with approval status
  3. Take detailed photos from all angles
  4. Sign off in writing with photos attached
  5. THEN (and only then) place deposit
- **Critical rule:** NEVER place deposit without 100% perfect pre-production sample in hand

### Stage 3: Pre-Shipment Sample
- **Quality target:** Must match pre-production sample 100%
- **Purpose:** Verify mass production matches approved sample
- **Source:** Taken from ACTUAL production run (not a separate sample)
- **Actions required:**
  1. Get third-party pre-shipment inspection (PSI)
  2. Compare against signed-off pre-production sample
  3. Check for defects per AQL 2.5 standard (-> quality-inspection skill)
- **If mismatch:** Do not pay balance. Renegotiate or reject.

## Regulatory Compliance Details (Jason Wong)

### 5 Types with Examples

**1. Product-Specific Safety Standards**
- Tool: CPSC Regulatory Robot (cpsc.gov)
- Children's products: CPSIA testing required
- Electronics: UL listing may be required
- Furniture: flammability standards

**2. Substance Regulations**
- **California Prop 65:** Any product sold in CA must not contain listed carcinogens/reproductive toxins above safe harbor levels (or must carry warning)
- **EU REACH:** Registration, evaluation, authorization of chemicals
- **RoHS:** Restriction of hazardous substances in electronics

**3. Documentation Requirements**
- **US: General Certificate of Conformity (GCC)** -- required for all consumer products
- **US: Children's Product Certificate (CPC)** -- required for products for children 12 and under
- **EU: Declaration of Conformity (DoC)** -- self-declaration of meeting EU standards
- Keep on file; may be requested by Amazon, Customs, or retailers

**4. Labeling Requirements**
- Country of origin ("Made in [Country]")
- Material composition (textiles: fiber content)
- Care instructions (textiles)
- Warning labels (small parts, choking hazard)
- Varies by product category AND destination country

**5. Testing Requirements**
- Medical claims require FDA approval
- Cosmetics: ingredient lists, allergen warnings
- SPF claims: must be tested and documented
- Food contact materials: FDA food-grade certification
- Organic claims: USDA organic certification

### FTC Advertising Compliance
- Cannot make health claims without substantiation
- "All natural" requires all ingredients to be natural
- Endorsement disclosures required for paid partnerships
- Penalties: fines, injunctions, inventory destruction

## 365 Product Model (Kian Golzari)

### Calendar Planning Framework
1. **Start with hero product** -- highest value, builds brand loyalty
2. **Follow with complementary accessories** -- higher margin, lower cost
3. **Time seasonal products** to land BEFORE peak selling periods
4. **Plan launches sequentially** across the calendar year

### Implementation
| Quarter | Product Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Q1 | Hero product launch | Establish brand, build reviews |
| Q2 | Accessory #1 | Cross-sell, increase AOV |
| Q3 | Seasonal variant or new color | Capture trend/season demand |
| Q4 | Gift bundle + accessory #2 | Maximize holiday revenue |

### Key Principles
- Each product builds on the last (brand ecosystem)
- Accessories are higher margin than hero products
- Bundle hero + accessories for maximum AOV
- Plan production timelines backwards from desired launch dates
- Account for: spec sheet (2 weeks) + sampling (4-6 weeks) + production (6-8 weeks) + shipping (4-6 weeks)

## Packaging Deep Dive

### Packaging Types and Cost Tiers
| Type | Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Poly bag with label | Lowest | Basic products, accessories |
| Kraft/cardboard box | Low-medium | Standard products |
| Custom printed box | Medium | Brand experience, gifting |
| Rigid box | High | Luxury/premium products |
| Custom molded insert | Medium-high | Fragile products, premium unboxing |

### Unboxing Experience Design
1. **Outer packaging:** Branded shipping box or mailer (compostable mailer = sustainability signal)
2. **Inner reveal:** Tissue paper, custom sticker seal
3. **Product presentation:** Custom insert holding product in place
4. **Extras:** Thank-you card, product insert with QR code, free sample/gift
5. **Social trigger:** Design worth photographing -- drives UGC

### Product Insert Strategy (Matt Clark)
Must include:
- Brand story (1-2 sentences)
- QR code to email signup or review page
- Satisfaction guarantee messaging
- Customer support contact info
- Social media handles
- Repeat purchase incentive (optional: 10% off next order)

## Prototyping Budget Guide

| Method | Cost Range | Timeline | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| DIY hand-make | $0-$50 | 1-3 days | Concept validation |
| 3D digital render | $250-$500 | 1-2 weeks | Marketing assets + manufacturer briefing |
| Local manufacturer sample | $500-$2,000+ | 2-4 weeks | Physical prototype for testing |
| Mold creation | $1,500-$5,000+ | 4-8 weeks | Custom plastic/metal products |
| Full prototype with packaging | $1,000-$3,000 | 3-6 weeks | Complete product experience testing |

### Designer Sourcing
- **Upwork:** Search "industrial designer" or "product designer" (filter for physical products)
- **99designs:** Contest format for multiple design options
- **Fiverr:** Budget-friendly for simple products
- **Dribbble:** Higher quality, higher cost
- **Pro tip:** Plan 3D renders as marketing assets from the start

## Manufacturing Framework (Gretta van Riel)

### Motto: "Start Local, Scale Global"
- Local first for prototype and initial product (1-50 units)
- Lower MOQs negotiable with local manufacturers
- Global manufacturers (China) great at REVERSE ENGINEERING but struggle to innovate from scratch
- Send locally-developed product to global manufacturer for replication
- Find a one-stop shop supplier; don't order 20 parts from 20 suppliers
- Listen to manufacturers' market expertise -- they often know your market better than you

### SkinnyMeTea Lesson
- Still made in Australia -- "Made in Australia" became a UVP
- Tried global manufacturing and went back to local
- "Made in [Country]" can be a selling point for certain markets
