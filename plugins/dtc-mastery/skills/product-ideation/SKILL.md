## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICES

### Disagreement: Audience First vs. Product First

| Position | Advocate | When It Applies |
|---|---|---|
| Build audience FIRST, then create product for them ("market-product fit") | Gretta van Riel (E01) | You have social media skills and can grow a niche account quickly. Gretta built @detoxwater to 800K+ followers, then reverse-engineered the product. |
| Find product opportunity first using data, then build brand around it | Matt Clark (E03) | You have sourcing/manufacturing skills and want data-driven validation via Amazon BSR, Google Trends, and margin math. |
| PMF is dynamic -- can change target customer instead of product | Jason Wong (E02) | Your product exists but the initial target market is wrong. Pivot the audience, not the product. |

**Resolution:** Both paths are legitimate. Choose based on your strengths. If social-media-native, go audience-first. If analytical/sourcing-native, go product-first. Note Jason's insight that PMF can be achieved by changing the audience, not just the product.

## Core Ideation Frameworks

### 1. PSSP Framework (Gretta van Riel)

**Problem -> Stat -> Solution -> Product**

The foundational ideation method. Start with a real problem, not a product idea.

1. **Problem**: What pain point exists? Ask "What problem can I solve?" not "What product can I sell?"
2. **Stat**: Find a statistic that quantifies the problem (builds credibility and marketing copy)
3. **Solution**: What category of solution addresses this problem?
4. **Product**: What specific physical product delivers that solution?

**Techniques for finding problems:**
- "Scratch your own itch" -- solve a problem you personally experience
- Polar opposite technique -- frame the problem, think of its opposite (disposable -> reusable)
- Research tools: Trend Hunter, Kickstarter, Pinterest (DIY tips and trends)

**Action**: Generate 5 broad problems, fill out PSSP for each, rank by viability.

### 2. UVP Innovation Framework (Gretta + Jason)

Find your Unique Value Proposition by changing ONE dimension of an existing product.

**Gretta's 4 dimensions:**
1. **Design** -- personalization, customization, sustainable materials, color trends
2. **Function** -- add/subtract/change features (read 2-3 star Amazon reviews for pain points)
3. **Price** -- accessible DTC (Movement Watches) vs. luxury exclusive (Tiffany)
4. **Time** -- limited edition, flash sale, time-restricted availability (The Fifth Watches)

**Jason's 5 elements:**
1. Design, 2. Branding, 3. Function, 4. Distribution, 5. Price

Key difference: Jason adds **Distribution** (subscription models, DTC-only) and **Branding** as separate levers.

**Critical insight**: You do NOT need a crazy new product idea. Changing a single dimension is enough.

**Product hybridization**: Combine functionality of two products (e.g., selfie light + phone case = Lumi).

**Negative review mining**: Read 2-3 star reviews on Amazon and Trustpilot. Ignore 1-star (outlier bad experiences). Find the most common complaints and fix them.

### 3. Matt Clark's 4 Criteria

| # | Criterion | Test |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Big Market Potential** | Appeals to millions (interruption-based ads need large audiences). Mass appeal product narrowed to focused niche to start. |
| 2 | **Good Demand** | 2+ products on Amazon with BSR 10,000 or less. Google Trends 5-year view shows steady or growing. |
| 3 | **Ability to Differentiate** | Can target unique buyers, unique packaging, superlative claims ("-est" strategy), environmental focus, or contrarian positioning. |
| 4 | **High Profit Potential** | Can sell for 4x landed cost. Products on Amazon selling well at 2x the average price. |

**Interest + Search Method**: List personal interests -> combine with Amazon terms (accessories, gear, gadgets, tools) -> append demographics -> browse for mass appeal + personal interest overlap -> build list of 10+ products.

### 4. PRIME Method (Melisa Vong)

| Letter | Step | Key Action |
|---|---|---|
| P | Positioning Potential | Framework for finding and positioning without overwhelm |
| R | Researching Reviews | Leverage competitor reviews to create standout products |
| I | In-Demand Product | Data-driven demand verification before ordering samples |
| M | Margins & Profit | "Maximum margin math" ensures profitability from day one |
| E | Easy to Make & Ship | Filter for lighter, easier-to-manufacture products |

**Helium 10 Black Box Thresholds:**
- Monthly revenue: $5,000+ minimum ($50 profit/day target)
- Price range: $12-$150
- Max review count: <200 (or <100 for stronger opportunities)
- Review rating max: 3.5 stars (room for improvement)
- Shipping size: Small/medium (avoid large oversized)
- Images: <5 images = underoptimized listing = opportunity
- Keyword search volume: >10,000 monthly for long-tail keywords
- Profit margin target: 30-33%

### 5. TikTokability Scoring (Michelle Barnum Smith)

Product must score **3 out of 5** to proceed on TikTok Shop.

| # | Key | Evaluation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Demonstrability** | Can be shown working in a short video within seconds |
| 2 | **Unique Attributes** | Something visually distinctive (even simple: unique color, texture) |
| 3 | **Serves a Niche** | Caters to a specific, passionate audience |
| 4 | **Solves a Problem** | Problem + solution shown in <10 seconds |
| 5 | **Impulse-Buy Optimized** | Priced under $50 with compelling discounts |

## Validation Methods

### Talk-to-20 Validation (Gretta)
- Interview 20 people from your target audience (in person preferred)
- Script: "Would you purchase? Recommend to friend? If not, why? What would you change?"
- Do NOT ask friends/family -- they will just say yes
- Do NOT spend money building product until validated

### Mock Launch Validation (Jason Wong)
- Launch with just a Photoshop mockup, no inventory
- Measure site visitors, conversion rate, engagement
- If orders come in, refund immediately (avoids chargebacks)
- Jason's Holy Meme Bible: single mockup generated $250K+ in first weeks
- Cost: designer + paid ads + hosting = a few thousand dollars

### Product-Market Fit Signals (Jason Wong)
1. Are customers interested in trying? (new order volume)
2. Do they see value and want to reuse? (returning customer rate)
3. Are they staying for months/years? (product lifecycle completion)

### Amazon Data Validation (Matt Clark)
Amazon validates 3 of 4 criteria: demand (BSR), differentiation (read reviews), profit potential (2x price products exist).

### TikTok Manual Validation (Michelle)
1. Search product keywords directly on TikTok
2. Analyze sales counts and engagement on competitor products
3. Assess market demand from live results

## Customer Personas

Build 2-3 personas. Make them **transitional** (by age bracket) or **oppositional** (opposing motivations).

**Include**: Demographics, behavioral patterns, motivations, goals, psychographics (interests, habits, attitudes, emotions), purchase triggers, objections.

**SkinnyMeTea example** (Gretta):
1. "Detox-Retox Repeater" (18-28) -- casual exerciser, Instagram-influenced
2. "Day-to-Dayer" (29-39) -- learned fad diets fail, prefers "cleanse" over "detox"
3. "Pre-Eventer" (18-39) -- needs fast results for event, short-term thinking

**Jason's Buyer Persona Checklist**: Age, gender, family, education, income, career goals, environment, needs, hobbies, shopping behavior, purchase barriers.

## Products to Avoid

**Across all platforms (all instructors agree):**
- Regulated/dangerous products (flammable, lithium batteries, vitamins without experience)
- Products prohibited from Facebook/Google advertising
- Very heavy/breakable products (shipping and returns nightmare)

**Amazon-specific (Melisa Vong):**
- Amazon Basics branded categories
- Products with many variations (clothing, phone cases)
- Seasonal-only products
- Electronics with batteries (HAZMAT)
- Restricted/gated categories without certifications
- Dominant established brands
- Handmade products (not scalable)
- Perishable foods

**Budget guidelines (Matt Clark):**
- Inventory: $3,000-$5,000
- Total all-in: $5,000-$10,000
- Do NOT exceed $10,000 for first product launch

## The /validate-product Command

When the user runs `/validate-product`, execute this guided scoring sequence:

### Step 1: Gather Product Details
Ask: What is the product? Who is the target customer? What problem does it solve? What is the approximate price point? Which platform(s) are you targeting (Shopify, Amazon, TikTok Shop)?

### Step 2: Run All Frameworks
Score the product against:
1. **PSSP clarity** (0-10): Is there a clear Problem -> Stat -> Solution -> Product chain?
2. **Clark 4-criteria** (0-4): Big market? Good demand? Differentiable? 4x margin?
3. **PRIME check** (0-5): Positioning? Reviews mined? In-demand? Margins? Easy to ship?
4. **TikTokability** (0-5): Demonstrable? Unique visual? Niche? Problem-solution? Impulse price?

### Step 3: Output Viability Scorecard
Present a table with all scores, weighted total, and a **GO / CONDITIONAL GO / NO-GO** recommendation with specific reasons and next steps.

- GO: Total score 70%+ across frameworks, no critical fails
- CONDITIONAL GO: 50-69%, or one framework fails but others pass -- list what to fix
- NO-GO: Below 50% or multiple critical fails -- recommend pivoting
