# Product Ideation Frameworks -- Deep Reference

## PSSP Framework Details (Gretta van Riel)

### Worked Examples

**Drop Bottle (hydration angle):**
- Problem: Most people don't drink enough water; dehydration causes fatigue
- Stat: Bodies are 64% water; 1-2% loss causes fatigue; fatigue is first sign of dehydration
- Solution: Hydration solutions that encourage drinking enough water
- Product: Fruit-infused reusable glass drink bottle

**Drop Bottle (sustainability angle):**
- Problem: Waste from single-use plastic bottles
- Stat: Single-use plastic from everyday products = over half of world's plastic waste
- Solution: Make the drink bottle reusable
- Product: Reusable glass drink bottle

**SkinnyMeTea:**
- Problem: Need fast, safe weight loss solution
- Stat: (health-approved detox data)
- Solution: 14-day detox with proven results
- Product: Detox tea program

### PSSP Process
1. Come up with 5 broad problems you want to solve
2. Fill out PSSP worksheet for each
3. Rank by viability (see Viability Checklist below)
4. Scrap bottom 2, keep top 3
5. Proceed to UVP framework with top 3

## Viability Checklist (Gretta van Riel)

Three screening questions for every product idea:

### 1. Is There a Market?
- Google: "[product] industry market size" or "[product] global market size"
- Look for billion-dollar total addressable markets
- Smaller markets OK if niche is passionate and underserved

### 2. Is There Demand / Is It Trending?
- Google Trends: set to worldwide, last 5 years
- Compare terms head-to-head
- Steady or growing = good. Declining = avoid.
- Anti-pattern: Garcinia Cambogia spiked after Dr. Oz, then continuous decline

### 3. Is It Viable for E-Commerce?
- Lightweight and easy to ship?
- Not too breakable? (refund nightmare -- Drop Bottle lesson)
- Not regulated or dangerous to ship? (flammable, lithium, aerosols)
- Can you cover shipping in product price for free shipping?

## Matt Clark 4-Criteria Deep Dive

### Criterion 1: Big Market Potential
- Mass appeal products: watches, phone cases, hats, food, vitamins, coffee, toothbrushes, drinkware
- Strategy: take mass-appeal product, narrow to focused niche to start
- Example: Avian watches targeting military aviators but appealing broadly
- Check Amazon best sellers for categories with broad appeal

### Criterion 2: Good Demand
- 2+ products on Amazon with BSR of 10,000 or less
- Must NOT be declining -- use Google Trends 5-year view
- Amazon validates demand directly

### Criterion 3: Ability to Differentiate
- Target unique buyers (women, veterans, vegans, aviators)
- Unique packaging (Apple simplicity, BarkBox experience)
- Superlative claims: biggest, smallest, lightest, brightest, softest (the "-est" strategy)
- Environmental focus (Patagonia model)
- Contrarian packaging (Boxed Water vs Fiji)
- TOMS buy-one-give-one model

### Criterion 4: High Profit Potential
- Can you sell for **4x landed cost**?
- Landed cost = manufacturer cost + duties/taxes + shipping
- Validation: products on Amazon selling well at 2x average price
- Example: Average poncho $18-19; military poncho sells at $45 doing $60K/month

### Interest + Search Method (Step by Step)
1. List personal interests/activities (10+)
2. Combine with Amazon search terms: accessories, gear, gadgets, tools, stuff, gifts, products, supplies, must-haves, essentials
3. Append demographics: "for women" / "for men" / "for kids"
4. Browse results looking for: personally interesting + mass appeal
5. Build list of 10+ potential products
6. Do NOT worry about differentiation yet

## PRIME Method Detailed Thresholds (Melisa Vong)

### Helium 10 Black Box Settings
| Parameter | Setting | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly revenue | $5,000+ minimum | $50/day profit target |
| Price range | $12-$150 | Below $12 margins too thin; above $150 mfg cost rises |
| Review count | <200 (or <100) | Lower reviews = easier to compete |
| Review rating | max 3.5 stars | Room for product improvement |
| Shipping size | Small/medium only | Avoid large oversized (expensive fees) |
| Images | <5 | Underoptimized listing = opportunity |
| Keyword search volume | >10,000/month | Sufficient demand for long-tail keywords |
| Profit margin | 30-33% | One-third rule for Amazon |

### 10 Product Research Methods Compared
1. Amazon Best Sellers browsing (free, updated hourly)
2. Helium 10 Black Box (filter by revenue, reviews, rating, price, size)
3. Helium 10 Keyword search (high-volume long-tail)
4. Helium 10 Cerebro reverse ASIN (shared competitor keywords)
5. Amazon Movers & Shakers (gaining rank in 24h)
6. TikTok hashtag browsing (#AmazonFinds)
7. Social media algorithm training (follow brands, receive competitor ads)
8. Facebook Ad Library transparency
9. Google Trends validation (confirm upward trend)
10. Retail store observation (demand already vetted)

## TikTokability Scoring Rubric (Michelle Barnum Smith)

### Scoring Each Key (0-1 per key)

**1. Demonstrability:**
- 1 = Can show product working in 3-5 seconds of video
- 0 = Requires lengthy explanation to understand value

**2. Unique Attributes:**
- 1 = Visually distinctive element (even simple: unique color, unusual shape)
- 0 = Looks identical to every other product in category
- Benchmark: blue Liquid IV video sold 100K+ units on color alone

**3. Serves a Niche:**
- 1 = Defined passionate audience (survivalists, campers, beauty enthusiasts)
- 0 = Generic mass market with no community identity

**4. Solves a Problem:**
- 1 = Problem + solution shown in <10 seconds
- 0 = Solution requires education or explanation

**5. Impulse-Buy Optimized:**
- 1 = Priced under $50 with compelling discounts
- 0 = Priced above $50 or discount structure is weak

**Threshold**: 3/5 minimum to proceed with TikTok Shop.

### TikTok vs Amazon Fundamental Difference
- Amazon = need-based shopping (search intent)
- TikTok = impulse buying (entertainment -> shoppable content -> checkout)
- Do NOT treat TikTok like a search-based marketplace

## Validation Methods Detailed

### Mock Launch Mechanics (Jason Wong)
1. Create product mockup in Photoshop (or hire designer)
2. Build simple landing page with product images and pricing
3. Drive small amount of paid traffic
4. Measure: site visitors, add-to-cart rate, conversion rate, engagement
5. If orders come in without inventory: refund immediately (avoid chargebacks)
6. Cost: designer + ads + hosting = few thousand dollars maximum

### Lean Canvas (Gretta van Riel)
Adapted from Ash Moria's business model canvas:
- Market Problems
- Solution
- Metrics (visitors -> checkout -> convert)
- UVP
- Unfair Advantage (harder in e-com than tech -- OK to leave blank)
- Channels
- Customer Segments
- Cost Structure
- Revenue Streams

### TAM/SAM/SOM Model (Jason Wong)
- **TAM** = Total Addressable Market (entire market)
- **SAM** = Serviceable Available Market (your segment)
- **SOM** = Serviceable Obtainable Market (realistic capture)

## Products to Avoid -- Complete List

### Universal Avoid List
- Regulated products without experience/capital (vitamins, supplements, medical devices)
- Products prohibited from Facebook/Google/TikTok advertising
- Very heavy or very breakable products
- Products requiring complex certifications (FDA, CPSC)
- Products with continuous decline on Google Trends

### Amazon-Specific Avoid (Melisa Vong)
- Amazon Basics branded products
- Many-variation products (clothing, phone cases)
- Seasonal-only products
- Electronics with batteries (HAZMAT classification)
- Restricted/gated categories without certifications
- Categories dominated by large established brands
- Handmade products (not scalable)
- Perishable foods (short shelf life)

### TikTok Shop Restricted/Prohibited
- Beauty and personal care (requires additional approval)
- Medical devices, electronic products
- Packaged food and beverages, pet food
- CO2 cylinders, products with batteries
- Surprise-based products
- Children's products under 12 (stringent safety requirements)
- Prohibited = no appeal process; restricted = need documentation

### Budget Anti-Patterns
- Exceeding $10,000 on first product launch (Matt Clark)
- Ordering more than MOQ for first order (Gretta: "I've been burned")
- Skipping validation and going straight to manufacturing (all instructors agree)
