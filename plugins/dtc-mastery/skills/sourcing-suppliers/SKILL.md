## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICES

### Disagreement: Payment Terms

| Position | Advocate | Context |
|---|---|---|
| 50% upfront, 50% on delivery | Gretta van Riel | May reflect Australian/local manufacturer norms |
| 30% deposit, 70% on completion | Matt Clark + Jason Wong | Industry standard for China manufacturing |
| 100% upfront = RED FLAG | Jason Wong | Never accept; signals untrustworthy supplier |

**Resolution:** 30/70 is the industry standard (Clark and Wong agree). Negotiate for 30/70. Flag any supplier requesting 100% upfront as a red flag. Josh Haley's credit card strategy (Kevin King Summit) can extend effective payment terms to 90+ days regardless of deposit split.

### Disagreement: Price Negotiation Intensity

| Position | Advocate |
|---|---|
| Negotiate hard -- always have 3+ suppliers to leverage | Jason Wong |
| Don't negotiate too hard -- "you can get any price you want" but quality shortcuts will show in reviews | Kian Golzari |

**Resolution:** Negotiate firmly but fairly. Always understand the REAL market price by comparing 5+ suppliers. Use Golzari's 7 tactics but never push below the quality floor.

## The 10-3-1 Method (Kian Golzari)

The foundational supplier selection framework:

1. **Contact 10 suppliers** with identical spec sheets
2. **Sample from 3** best candidates (based on communication, pricing, capability)
3. **Commit to 1** best manufacturer for production

### Supplier Search Channels
- **Alibaba** (primary for China suppliers)
- **Google** (for domestic/US suppliers)
- **Import records** (for finding competitors' suppliers)
- **Trade shows** (Canton Fair for direct manufacturer access)
- **Sourcing firms** (for complex/custom products)

## Alibaba Sourcing Process

### Pre-Search Filters
- **Gold Supplier 3+ years** (minimum platform age shows experience)
- **Trade Assurance** (buyer protection for defective products/delayed shipment)
- **Verified** badge (third-party factory inspection)
- Check customer satisfaction ratings and production flow documentation

### Identifying Real Factories vs. Trading Companies
- **Trading companies** = middlemen with markup, less reliable
- **Check address:** Real factories are in rural areas, not city centers
- **Coastal locations** = closer to seaport = lower ground transport costs
- **Product range:** Concentrated range = specialized factory = better quality + pricing
- **Chinese company name format:** [City] + [Company Name] + [Product Category] + [Company Structure]

### Key Certifications
- **ISO 9000:** Quality management standards
- **ISO 14000:** Environmental management
- Look for industry-specific certifications relevant to your product

## Alibaba 5-Point Supplier Audit (Jason Wong)

| # | Check | What to Look For |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Company profile** | Employees, certifications, export markets |
| 2 | **Production capacity** | Product lines, equipment (missing equipment = outsourcing = higher price) |
| 3 | **Quality control equipment** | Testing machines present; avoid factories that don't disclose |
| 4 | **R&D capacity** | Internal R&D teams signal credibility + innovation |
| 5 | **Export capabilities** | Trade show history, countries exported to; prefer experience with your region |

## Import Record Research (Jason Wong)

### Why It Matters
A quarter of good suppliers DON'T list on Alibaba -- they find it attracts low-quality leads.

### Process
1. Find company's registered name via their Privacy Policy page (e.g., "Steel Technology LLC DBA Hydroflask")
2. Search import databases (US government makes all Bill of Lading records publicly accessible)

### Tools
| Tool | Cost | Coverage |
|---|---|---|
| **PortExaminer.com** | Free | Data before 2016 only; limited detail |
| **ImportGenius** | $99-$399/month | Last few months of data; brands rarely switch manufacturers |
| **Import Yeti** | Free | Same data as Panjiva; competitor factories and volumes |

### Protecting Your Own Records
- Air freight shipments are NOT in these databases (only sea freight BOL)
- Create a **ghost LLC** for sea freight importing so competitors can't find your suppliers
- Block your import data: submit request to CBP government website (free, 15-30 days, renew every 2 years)

## Local vs. Global Strategy (Gretta van Riel)

**Motto: "Start local, scale global"**

| Stage | Source | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Prototype (1-50 units) | Local manufacturer | Lower MOQs, faster iteration, easier communication |
| Initial production | Local or global | "Made in [Country]" can be UVP; test quality |
| Scale production | Global (China) | Lower unit costs, higher volume capability |

- Global manufacturers excel at REVERSE ENGINEERING but struggle to innovate from scratch
- Send locally-developed product to global manufacturer for replication
- Find a one-stop shop supplier; don't source 20 parts from 20 suppliers

## Communication Templates Overview (Matt Clark)

7 templates covering the full supplier relationship:
1. **Initial Supplier Contact** -- first outreach: products, pricing, MOQ
2. **Sample Order** -- requesting product samples
3. **Sample Tracker** -- follow-up for tracking number
4. **Product Packaging** -- packaging specs/requirements
5. **Price Negotiation** -- negotiating better pricing
6. **Pre-Order & Shipping Estimate** -- shipping quotes before ordering
7. **Inventory Order** -- placing the actual order

**Tips:** Modify templates to fit your voice (suppliers receive the same templates from many people). Best time to negotiate price is AFTER making lots of sales (supplier sees potential).

## Negotiation Quick Reference

### Jason Wong's 5 Tactics
1. Be respectful -- position yourself as experienced ("expanding product line" not "starting out")
2. Tell future intentions -- suppliers want long-term customers
3. Diversify -- always have 3+ suppliers; leverage one against another
4. Ask what can change to lower price/MOQ (different material, coating, color?)
5. Create urgency -- "deciding between two manufacturers by tomorrow"

### Golzari's 7 Price Negotiation Tactics
1. Spec sheet comparison (send identical specs to multiple suppliers)
2. Sample leverage (use best sample from expensive supplier as benchmark)
3. Upsell quantity (ask pricing at 1K/5K/10K; use 10K price as target)
4. Stock materials (ask what factory holds in stock from cancelled orders)
5. Long-term partnership language ("build together," "long-term")
6. Relationship building (visit factory, attend events)
7. HTS code optimization (change description/materials for lower duty rate)

## The /find-supplier Command

When the user runs `/find-supplier`, execute this guided process:

### Step 1: Gather Product Details
Ask: What product? Do you have a spec sheet? Target price point? MOQ flexibility? Target market? Timeline?

### Step 2: Supplier Outreach Plan
Generate:
- 10 supplier search criteria for Alibaba (Gold 3+yr, Trade Assurance, product-specific filters)
- Initial outreach message customized for the product
- Evaluation scorecard template for comparing responses

### Step 3: Negotiation Strategy
Provide:
- Price negotiation script customized to the product
- MOQ reduction talking points (select from 9 strategies based on situation)
- Payment terms recommendation (30/70 standard, with credit card extension strategy)

### Step 4: Sample Checklist
Output:
- Identical spec sheet requirements for fair comparison
- Sample evaluation criteria
- Stress test protocol for received samples

### Step 5: Next Steps
- After samples received: compare against evaluation scorecard
- After supplier selected: proceed to product-development for 3-stage sampling
- After order placed: proceed to quality-inspection for pre-shipment inspection
