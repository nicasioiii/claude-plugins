# Sourcing & Suppliers Tactics -- Deep Reference

## Alibaba 5-Point Supplier Audit Details (Jason Wong)

### Point 1: Company Profile
- Number of employees (match to your order scale)
- Certifications held (ISO, industry-specific)
- Export markets served (preference for your target market)
- Years on platform (minimum 3 years)
- Company history and ownership structure

### Point 2: Production Capacity
- Product lines offered (concentrated = specialized = good)
- Equipment list (critical: does equipment match YOUR product needs?)
- Missing equipment = outsourcing to subcontractor = higher price + less quality control
- Monthly/annual output capacity
- Current utilization rate (busy factories may deprioritize small orders)

### Point 3: Quality Control Equipment
- Testing machines present and disclosed
- QC team size and training
- Defect rate history
- **Red flag:** Factories that don't disclose QC capabilities -- stay away

### Point 4: R&D Capacity
- Internal R&D teams signal credibility and innovation capability
- Patents or proprietary processes
- Ability to develop from spec sheet vs. only replicate existing products
- Design/engineering support available

### Point 5: Export Capabilities
- Trade show participation history (Canton Fair, industry shows)
- Countries exported to (preference for your destination)
- Experience with destination-specific regulations
- Freight/logistics partnerships

## Import Record Research Process (Jason Wong)

### Step-by-Step
1. Identify target brand's products you want to find suppliers for
2. Go to brand's website -> Privacy Policy page -> find registered legal entity name
3. Search import databases with legal entity name
4. Review Bill of Lading records for: factory name, port of origin, shipment frequency, volumes
5. Contact identified factories directly (bypass Alibaba entirely)

### PortExaminer.com (Free)
- Data before 2016 only
- Limited detail but useful for established brands
- Search by company name or product description

### ImportGenius ($99-$399/month)
- Last few months of data (most current)
- Brands rarely switch manufacturers -- recent data is highly accurate
- More detailed shipment information
- Export format for analysis

### Import Yeti (Free)
- Same data as Panjiva
- Find competitor factories AND shipment volumes
- Good for understanding competitive landscape

### Protecting Your Own Import Records
- **Air freight:** Shipments NOT in import databases (only sea freight BOL)
- **Ghost LLC:** Create separate LLC for sea freight importing
- **CBP block request:** Submit to CBP website (free, 15-30 days processing, renew every 2 years)

## Full Communication Templates

### Template 1: Initial Supplier Contact (Matt Clark)
Subject: Product Inquiry - [Product Type] for [Brand Name]

"Hi [Supplier Name],

My name is [Your Name], and I'm the founder of [Brand Name]. We're looking for a reliable manufacturer for [product description]. We're interested in:

1. Your product catalog for [product type]
2. Pricing for [estimated quantity] units
3. Minimum order quantity
4. Lead time for samples and production
5. Available certifications

Could you please send us your catalog and pricing details?

Best regards,
[Your Name]"

### Template 2: Sample Order (Matt Clark)
"We'd like to order samples of [specific products]. Please confirm:
- Sample cost per unit
- Shipping cost to [your address]
- Can sample cost be deducted from first production order?
- Expected shipping time

Please use this shipping address: [address]"

### Template 3: Price Negotiation (Matt Clark)
"Thank you for the quote. We're comparing offers from several manufacturers. Could you provide better pricing at the following quantities: [500 / 1,000 / 2,500 units]? We're looking for a long-term manufacturing partner and expect to place regular orders as we scale."

### Jason Wong's Initial Outreach Script
"Hi [Name]. My name is [Your Name]. I'm the founder of [Company]. We're expanding our product line and looking to work with a manufacturer to create [product]. I want to order [quantity] units for our sample order and would like to know: MOQ? Volume discounts? Air and sea freight costs? Sample time and cost? Production time? Here's our address: [address]."

### Jason Wong's Full MOQ Negotiation Script
1. "We've invested significant time in design and want to ensure first production run is correct. We'd like 400 units to test quality."
2. Supplier: "400 is too low, we'll lose money."
3. "I value our long-term relationship. My company has invested a lot and will lose money too if quality isn't right."
4. Supplier: "My boss said no."
5. "Can I speak to your boss? We've been in business for years. Other suppliers have accepted lower MOQ. We feel confident working together but need your help."
6. "I don't want you to lose this order because of MOQ. We're deciding between two manufacturers. Deadline is tomorrow."
7. If rejected due to setup costs: "How about we lower MOQ to 500-600 and I pay a bit more per unit? If acceptable, I'll issue the PO today."
- Realistic range: 20-25% reduction on MOQ; aim higher and meet in the middle
- After acceptance: get WeChat/WhatsApp for instant messaging; build personal relationship

## 7 Price Negotiation Tactics Details (Kian Golzari)

### Tactic 1: Spec Sheet Comparison (Market Price Discovery)
- Send IDENTICAL spec sheet to multiple verified suppliers
- Compare apples-to-apples quotes
- Eliminate outliers: too cheap = quality shortcuts; too expensive = inflated margin
- The average of middle quotes = real market price

### Tactic 2: Sample Leverage
- Get best sample from the expensive supplier
- Use cheaper supplier's verified price as leverage
- "We received a higher quality sample from another supplier at this price point. Can you match quality at your price?"

### Tactic 3: Upsell Quantity
- Ask for pricing at 1,000 / 5,000 / 10,000 units
- Use 10K price as aspirational target even when ordering 1K
- Framing: "I'll pass savings to customer = sell more units = faster reorders for you"

### Tactic 4: Stock Materials
- Ask what materials factory holds in stock
- Cancelled orders = cut-price materials available
- ALWAYS test quality of stock materials before committing

### Tactic 5: Long-Term Partnership Language
- Use keywords: "long-term," "partnership," "build together"
- Supplier thinks in years of orders, not single transaction
- Frame small first order as beginning of large relationship

### Tactic 6: Relationship Building
- Visit factory in person when possible
- Attend industry events and trade shows together
- Personal relationships yield best pricing
- "People buy from people" -- this is especially true in Chinese manufacturing

### Tactic 7: HTS Code Optimization
- Change product description or materials to hit different import duty rate
- Example: 12% duty down to 2-3% = instant 10% cost savings
- Work with customs broker or use tariffterminator.com tools
- Always verify HTS codes BEFORE launching (include in ROI calculations)

## 9 MOQ Reduction Strategies (Kian Golzari)

| # | Strategy | How It Works |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Use stock materials | Factory holds existing materials -- no minimum raw material order |
| 2 | Accept standard colors/materials | Factory's defaults = no custom material sourcing needed |
| 3 | Order during slow season | Factory needs to keep workers busy -- more flexible on MOQ |
| 4 | Start with existing product | Minimal customization = lower setup costs |
| 5 | Commit to long-term | Verbal commitment to future orders builds trust |
| 6 | Pay higher per-unit price | Explicitly offer premium pricing for smaller quantity |
| 7 | Frame as "trial order" | Reduces perceived risk for factory |
| 8 | Ask about cancelled inventory | Other buyers' cancelled orders = ready-made stock |
| 9 | Use trading companies | Lower MOQs than direct factory (but higher per-unit price) |

## Landed Cost Calculator (Kian Golzari)

### Formula
**Landed Cost = Product Price + Import Duty + Freight Cost**

### Calculation Steps
1. Get product price from factory (FOB or EXW)
2. Calculate carton dimensions and quantity per carton
3. Calculate CBM for container sizing (20ft vs 40ft vs 40ft HC)
4. Look up import duty rate via HTS code
5. Get freight cost per unit (sea vs air)
6. Add delivery cost to customer
7. Calculate selling price for target gross margin

### Container Reference
| Container | Internal Volume | Max Weight |
|---|---|---|
| 20ft | ~33 CBM | ~28,000 kg |
| 40ft | ~67 CBM | ~28,000 kg |
| 40ft HC | ~76 CBM | ~28,000 kg |

### External Factors Affecting Price (Golzari)
- Raw material costs (verify with multiple suppliers)
- Labor costs (verify with multiple suppliers)
- Exchange rates (check xe.com; can save significantly)
- Factory's production capacity utilization

## Payment Terms and Methods

### Standard Terms
| Split | Context |
|---|---|
| 30% deposit / 70% on completion | Industry standard (Clark + Wong) |
| 50% / 50% | May reflect local/Australian norms (Gretta) |
| 100% upfront | RED FLAG -- never accept |

### Payment Methods
| Method | Best For | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Wire transfer (TT) | Orders $1,000+ | Standard for China; called "TT" |
| Alibaba payment | Orders $1,000+ | Safest; buyer protection |
| PayPal/Payoneer | Smaller amounts | Earn credit card points; extra 30 days cash flow; 4% fee |
| Melio | Any invoice | Pay via credit card (2.9% fee offset by rewards) |

### Credit Card Cash Flow Strategy (Josh Haley, Kevin King Summit)
- Pay supplier invoices via Melio using credit card (2.9% fee, offset by 2.5% rewards = 0.4% net cost)
- Stagger multiple credit cards with different statement dates
- Charge each card during first 5 days of statement period
- Result: 55-60 day payback on standard AMEX, 90 days on AMEX Plum
- With negotiated net 90 supplier terms + this strategy = net 150 day payment terms

## Working with Sourcing Firms (Jason Wong)

### When to Use
- Complex or custom products beyond simple color/material changes
- Products requiring multiple components from different factories
- When you lack manufacturing industry connections

### How to Find
- Google: "[category] development company" (e.g., "food and beverage development company")
- Industry trade associations
- Referrals from other brand founders

### Key Tip
Do as much homework as possible BEFORE engaging:
- Provide reference pictures, materials, dimensions, functions
- Not just a few sentences of description
- More detail = better results = lower costs
- Sourcing firms charge for their time; preparation reduces billable hours

## Getting Samples Checklist (Jason Wong)

1. Give ALL suppliers identical specs for fair comparison
2. Don't filter by price first (cheapest often gives least effort)
3. Most suppliers charge 20-50% markup on samples
4. Ask for sample cost refund on first production order
5. Shipping: state "sample of no commercial value" on invoice
6. Declare reasonable value (not too low -- raises customs suspicion)
7. Sample request email must include: number of pieces, color, product description, shipping address, deduct-from-order request, declared value, visual mockup
8. Stress test received samples as if you're the end customer -- test limits
9. Doe Lashes discovery: samples could be worn 20+ times (became core marketing message)
