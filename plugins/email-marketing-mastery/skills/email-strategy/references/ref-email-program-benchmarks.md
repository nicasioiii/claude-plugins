# Email Program Benchmarks & KPIs

## Revenue Attribution Targets

### Ecommerce Brands (E02 -- Boyan, E03 -- MuteSix)

| Brand Size | Email Revenue Target | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Starting out (<$50K/mo) | 10-15% of total revenue | Focus on building flows first |
| Growing ($50K-$500K/mo) | 20-30% of total revenue | Flows + regular campaigns |
| Established ($500K-$5M/mo) | 25-40% of total revenue | Full flow suite + 2-3x weekly campaigns |
| Large ($5M+/mo) | 30-50% of total revenue | One client: $70M/yr, 34% from email (E02) |

### Healthy Account Indicators (E03)
- Email accounts for 20-25% of total revenue
- Split roughly evenly between flows and campaigns = healthy
- If campaigns drive disproportionately more than flows: automation gaps exist
- If flows drive disproportionately more than campaigns: campaign cadence is too low

## Standard KPIs to Track (E03 -- MuteSix)

### Core Email Metrics
1. Number of emails delivered
2. Open rate
3. Click-through rate (CTR)
4. Click-to-open rate (CTOR) -- the key optimization metric
5. Unsubscribe rate
6. Total revenue
7. Number of orders placed
8. Average order value (AOV)
9. Revenue per email
10. Revenue per recipient
11. Conversion rate / placed order rate

### Reporting Cadence
- Month to date
- Month over month
- Year over year
- Track flows AND campaigns separately

### Metric Health Ranges

| Metric | Healthy | Warning | Critical |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open rate (engaged segment) | 25-50% | 15-25% | <15% |
| Open rate (welcome email 1) | 48-65% (E03) | 35-48% | <35% |
| Open rate (welcome emails 2-4) | 23-25% (E03) | 15-23% | <15% |
| Click-to-open rate | 5-15% | 2-5% | <2% |
| Abandon cart open rate | 35%+ (E03) | 25-35% | <25% |
| Unsubscribe rate per campaign | <0.4% | 0.4-0.8% | >0.8% |
| Spam rate | <0.08% | 0.08-0.2% | >0.2% (Google danger zone) |
| Deliverability rate | 99.5%+ | 98-99.5% | <98% (red flag) |
| Abandon checkout recovery rate | 15-25% (E02) | 10-15% | <10% |

## Metric Interrelations Framework (E02 -- Boyan)

### What Affects Each Metric
- **Open rate (7 variables):** subject line, preview text, sender name, profile picture, segmentation, time of day, deliverability
- **Click rate:** offer quality + messaging clarity. Prospect must understand core offer within 1-3 seconds
- **Conversion rate:** click intent + landing page conversion rate. Great offer converts even on bad landing page
- **Deliverability:** sender reputation (volume + spam rates + bounce rates + email content weight)
- **Spam/churn rate:** sending frequency + content relevance to segment

### The Subject Line Math (E02 -- Why Subject Lines Matter Most)
- Control: 10K sends, 10% click-of-opens, 30% convert
- 10% open rate = 1,000 opens -> 100 clicks -> 30 conversions
- 20% open rate = 2,000 opens -> 200 clicks -> 60 conversions
- Doubling open rate is easier, takes less time, and less brain power than doubling CTR

### Diagnostic Decision Framework (E02)
- Good click rate but low/no revenue = change the offer or landing page
- Good conversion rate but low clicks = change subject line, segment, or preview text
- Terrible opens no matter what = check deliverability
- Optimize flows from top down (Email 1 first, not last emails)
- Only extend a flow if final email still converts 3-5%+ of recipients

## Newsletter-Specific Benchmarks (E05)

### Top Performer Benchmarks
- 1440 Media: 4.4M+ subs, 60%+ open rate, $1M+ revenue per employee
- CJ Gustafson (Mostly Metrics): 57K subs, $700K+ annual revenue
- The Assist: 40K quality subs/month via paid acquisition
- Paid acquisition CPL range: $0.95-$3.00 per subscriber

### Newsletter Health Metrics
- Open rate above 40% = excellent product
- Open rate 27-40% = good, can grow
- Open rate below 27% = product problem, not distribution problem (E05 -- 1440 Media)
- Holy grail: 100% revenue/spend ratio (ad spend fully recouped within 30 days)

## Client/Brand Qualification Criteria (E02)

When assessing whether a brand is ready for professional email marketing:
- Minimum: $50K/month revenue
- Look for: high-res product photography, professional videos, meaningful review counts
- Brand at $100K/month will have minimum 5,000-10,000 email list (existing customers alone)
- Email revenue benchmarks by size: brands $2M-$70M/year achieve 20-50% from email
- Email is lower barrier to entry than Facebook ads, SEO, or paid search

## Five Email Myths Debunked (E02 -- Boyan)

1. **"Email is dead"** -- False. Largest communication channel worldwide
2. **"The more I send the more I make"** -- Oversending destroys sender reputation
3. **"Email is set and forget"** -- Flows need updating 1-4x/year; metrics need monitoring
4. **"Too much complexity"** -- Only 7-12 variables matter; optimize the 10% that drives 100%
5. **"Highly aesthetic emails convert better"** -- Multi-seven-figure brands with terrible-looking emails convert extremely well

## SMS Channel Benchmarks (E03 -- MuteSix)

### SMS vs. Email Performance Comparison

| Metric | Email | SMS |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 15-30% | 90-99% |
| Click-through rate | 2-5% | 40-100% |
| Revenue per message (Nov/Dec) | Baseline | 3-5x email RPM |

### SMS Channel Rules
- SMS does NOT replace email -- the two channels work together
- Be mindful of who receives email vs. SMS vs. both (avoid double-messaging)
- SMS is especially powerful during peak seasons (BFCM, holiday)
- Higher engagement rates justify reserved use for high-value messages
