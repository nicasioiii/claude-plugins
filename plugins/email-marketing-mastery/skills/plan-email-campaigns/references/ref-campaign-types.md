# Reference: Campaign Types -- Deep Framework

## SALES CAMPAIGN DEEP DIVE

### Banner Design Rules
- Convey offer without scrolling, within 3 seconds
- Maximum 5-7 words on the banner text
- If offer requires "mental gymnastics" (tiered logic), simplify
- Feature the product prominently
- Always include a CTA button visible above the fold
- Mobile-optimized aspect ratio is critical (80%+ of traffic is mobile)

### Mystery Discount Strategy (E02)
- Email CTA sends people to checkout where they discover their personalized discount code
- Psychology: similar to trying on clothes in a store -- once engaged, 50% more likely to buy
- Drives checkout page visits even from browsers who might not normally click
- Best for: engaged segments who respond to gamification

### Sales Campaign Structure
- Banner (full or header) + Offer + Single CTA above fold
- Below fold: product recommendations, testimonials, or bestsellers (optional)
- 90% of people who see a strong banner click immediately
- Below-banner content is secondary -- invest time in the banner and offer

### Discount Stacking in Campaigns (E03)
- Email 1-3: Initial discount (10%)
- Email 4+: If no conversion, increase to 15-20%
- Use unique expiring codes in Klaviyo for real urgency
- Tiered approach: Buy 1 = 10% off, Buy 2 = 15%, Buy 3 = 20%

## ANNOUNCEMENT CAMPAIGN DEEP DIVE

### Priming vs. Selling Modes
- **Priming:** Inform without CTA. "Something big is coming." Optimize for awareness, not conversion. Use countdown timers to build anticipation.
- **Selling:** Post-launch. Educate on benefits + sell with clear CTA. Use header banner (50% screen) when body content matters.

### Product Launch Tactics (E03)
- Send to engaged/VIP segments first for exclusivity
- Subject line tactics that drive opens: "Early VIP Access" (40% open rate), "Limited Stock Available"
- Follow announcement with 1-2 follow-up emails over 3-7 days
- Include product-specific deep dive (value props, use cases, reviews)

### Three-Campaign Launch Blend
1. Pre-launch priming (no CTA, build anticipation)
2. Launch day (full sell, exclusivity, scarcity)
3. Follow-up / last chance (urgency, social proof)

## HOLIDAY CAMPAIGN DEEP DIVE

### Invention vs. Convention Holidays (E02)
| Type | Examples | Duration | Discount Level | Use For |
|------|----------|----------|----------------|---------|
| Invention | National Dog Bed Day, Brand Anniversary | 24-48 hours | Lower (10-15%) | Testing new offers, flash sales |
| Convention | Valentine's Day, Mother's Day, 4th of July | 3-7 days | Medium (15-25%) | Planned seasonal promotions |
| Tier 1 | BFCM, Christmas | 5-14 days | Highest (20-40%) | Largest revenue opportunity |

### Holiday Ideation
- Check nationaltoday.com/category/world for fitting holidays
- If nothing fits, invent one that ties to your product category
- Flash sales on invented holidays are low-risk ways to test discount levels and offer framing

## CONTENT CAMPAIGN DEEP DIVE

### Two Content Objectives
1. **Revenue-driving:** Educational content that naturally leads to product purchase
2. **Rapport-building:** Pure value content that builds trust for future sales

### Content Email Tactics (E02)
- Drive traffic to content hosted ON YOUR SITE, never external platforms
- Embedding content on-site enables pixel data capture and product discovery
- Split one product's benefits across 3 separate emails instead of cramming into one
- Link to high-performing content, not SEO blog articles (blogs have outbound links)

### Content Campaign Structure
- Hook headline or story opener
- Educational value (one concept per email)
- Soft product connection or CTA to content
- Below fold: product recommendations or bestsellers

## REVIEW CAMPAIGN DEEP DIVE

### Review Selection Strategy (E02)
- Choose reviews that address specific objections
  - Shipping speed concerns: feature review praising fast delivery
  - Quality doubts: feature review detailing long-term use
  - Price objections: feature review emphasizing value-for-money
- Correct grammar mistakes in reviews before publishing
- Blend review text with UGC photos/videos for maximum impact

### Review Email Structure
- "Don't take it from us, take it from them"
- 2-3 featured reviews with customer names/photos
- Product images alongside relevant reviews
- CTA to shop the reviewed products

## PRODUCT LAUNCH CAMPAIGN FRAMEWORK (E03)

### Simple Ecom Product Launch
- Distinct from full launch sequences (which are for info products/courses)
- 2-3 email series: tease > launch > follow-up
- VIP/engaged segment gets first access
- Include product education, benefits, lifestyle imagery
- Scarcity and exclusivity language throughout

### Advanced Product Launch Mix
| Email | Timing | Content | Segment |
|-------|--------|---------|---------|
| Tease | 3-5 days before | "Coming soon" + sneak peek image | VIP + 30-day engaged |
| Launch Day | Launch day | Full product details, benefits, scarcity | Full engaged segment |
| Deep Dive | Day 2-3 | Product education, use cases, reviews | Openers of launch email |
| Last Chance | Day 5-7 | Urgency, limited stock, countdown | Full engaged minus purchasers |

## PLAIN TEXT CAMPAIGN TACTICAL GUIDE

### When to Deploy Plain Text (E02, E03)
- After 3-4 branded designed emails as a pattern breaker
- During heavy promotional periods (BFCM after several designed emails)
- When you have no time for creative production
- For CEO/founder messages that need to feel personal
- During IP warm-up or deliverability recovery (first campaigns should be plain text)

### Plain Text Advantages
- Higher readability (feels like a personal email)
- Super lightweight; bypasses spam filters more easily
- "Generally see the highest conversion rates from plain text" (E03)
- Nearly doubled open rates for a brand with 10% open rates (E02)

### Plain Text Disadvantages
- Not effective for visual products (fashion, jewelry)
- Offers do not stand out as visually
- No brand reinforcement through design

### Ratio Rule
- 80/20 or 90/10 designed-to-plain overall
- Strategic weapon, not the default
