# Write Email Copy

Write high-converting email copy using proven frameworks. Covers both ecommerce (designed/modular) and creator/personality (plain text/story) contexts.

## BEFORE WRITING: DETERMINE CONTEXT

Ask these questions if not already provided:

1. **Business type:** Ecommerce/DTC or Creator/Service/Info-product?
2. **Email type:** Campaign, flow, broadcast, launch, or standalone?
3. **Goal:** Sale, click, engagement, or relationship-building?
4. **Audience awareness level:** Unaware, Problem-aware, Solution-aware, Product-aware, or Most-aware?
5. **Tone/voice:** Brand voice, founder voice, or personality-driven?

## CORE COPYWRITING FRAMEWORKS

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)
- **Attention:** Hook them -- compete with extremely good content
- **Interest:** Incentivize continued reading until the CTA
- **Desire:** Make them want it -- talk about benefits, what the product DOES for them
- **Action:** If the first three steps land, the CTA closes naturally
- **Best for:** Cold audiences, top-of-funnel, prospecting campaigns

### PAS (Problem-Agitation-Solution)
- **Problem:** Point out a problem they have -- many people have problems they do not recognize
- **Agitation:** Explain in detail why this problem is serious
- **Solution:** Present the product as the resolution
- **Best for:** Warm audiences, retargeting, people already somewhat sold

### Combined A-PASTA (AIPASDA)
- Attention > Interest > Problem > Agitation > Solution > Desire > Action
- The most complete framework -- use when you have space (long emails, multi-email sequences)

### UPPER Framework (Emails That Crush)
Flexible building blocks, not a rigid formula:
- **A -- Attention:** First lines grab with problem/symptom/counter-intuitive statement/bold claim/story opening
- **P -- Perspective:** Go deeper into WHY it is a problem (hell) or WHY the benefit is amazing (heaven). Give a NEW way of looking at something
- **P -- Tease Solution:** Present the solution uniquely. Choose blind (higher clicks), hints at mechanism, or direct (more pre-sold clicks)
- **E -- Amplification:** Make the advantage bigger with credibility, proof, testimonials, specificity, "even better" stacks, "works for anyone" amplifiers
- **R -- Action:** CTA using CARE PAIR framework (Convenience / Access / Relief / Enhancement / Cost savings / Personal transformation / Easily replace)

### Problem-Solution Quick Template
Fastest possible email format:
- Hey [name]. It is [sender] from [brand].
- [Fun/personal warm-up line -- 1-2 sentences]
- [Problem statement -- 1 sentence]
- [Solution = product -- 1-2 sentences]
- [Offer/discount -- 1 sentence]
- CTA

## THE TABLE METHOD (ECOM EMAILS)

Structure ecom emails as an 8-row table:

| Row | Element | Purpose |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | Subject Line | Hook / reason to open |
| 2 | Preview Text | Supporting detail for subject line |
| 3 | Header | Title/purpose -- MAX a few words |
| 4 | Body (above fold) | Primary details + discount/offer |
| 5 | Call to Action | Shop now, Buy now |
| 6 | Subheader | Secondary title (testimonials, bestsellers, founder message) |
| 7 | Body (below fold) | Secondary supporting details |
| 8 | Call to Action | Bookend CTA for scrollers |

**Speed Writing (Reverse Order):** Fill the EASIEST parts first:
1. CTAs (always the same)
2. Header (usually the offer)
3. Below-fold content (pull from site)
4. Subject line, preview text, above-fold body (the only "hard" parts)

## AWARENESS LEVELS (Schwartz) -- MATCH COPY TO AUDIENCE

| Level | State | Copy Approach |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| Unaware | Do not know the problem exists | Super emotional, shock/inspire |
| Problem-Aware | Know the problem, not solutions | Emotional, easy to inspire |
| Solution-Aware | Know solutions exist, not yours | Best offer wins, comparison |
| Product-Aware | Know your product specifically | Us vs. them, inside references |
| Most Aware | Extremely familiar with industry | Logic-based, specific data |

**Critical insight:** 75% of the time copy underperforms, it is because you are speaking to the wrong awareness level. Emotional copy to solution-aware people feels corny. Logical copy to unaware people feels intimidating.

## ANGLE LAYERING TECHNIQUE

Generate infinite angles from a single product problem by asking "what happens because of this?" at each level:

- Layer 1: Surface problem (e.g., bad skin)
- Layer 2: Consequence (e.g., looks unattractive)
- Layer 3: Social impact (e.g., people treat you differently)
- Layer 4: Internal impact (e.g., low confidence)
- Layer 5: Behavioral change (e.g., avoid putting yourself out there)
- Layer 6: Opportunity cost (e.g., miss career/social opportunities)
- Layer 7: Life impact (e.g., unfulfilled life)

Each layer = a separate email angle, subject line, or hook. 7 layers = 14+ unique email concepts from a single problem. See `ref-copy-angles.md` for the complete technique.

## NINE PROVEN EMAIL ANGLES

Use these battle-tested structures for any niche (see `ref-copy-angles.md` for full templates):

1. **Schoolgirl Screech** -- Pure excitement, no claims, opinion-based hype
2. **Oprah** -- Lighthearted announcement of bonuses/gifts/extensions
3. **Three Rules** -- Present three proven rules/steps leading to the product
4. **Optical Illusion** -- Counterintuitive reframe of the problem
5. **Micro Story** -- Short story: person + problem + obstacles + solution + benefit
6. **Two Types** -- Contrast two types (aspirational or educational)
7. **Nit-Rat** -- Transformation surprise after doing less/different
8. **Less Is More** -- Counterintuitive easy approach beats hard approach
9. **Caveman Invite** -- Stripped-down direct offer with zero filler
10. **Mission Impossible** -- Doing everything right but not getting results
11. **Rocky Balboa** -- Aspirational cart-close with pop culture reference

## FOUR LENSES (Content Delivery Perspectives)

Rotate through all four to reach all buyer types:

1. **Aspirational:** Inspiring through stories, future-pacing the result
2. **Insightful:** Shining a light on blind spots, connecting dots
3. **Actionable:** Tactical, implementable advice with step-by-step
4. **Analytical:** Facts, stats, research breakdowns

Most writers default to their own buying type. Deliberately rotate across a launch or campaign series.

## WRITING VOICE AND STYLE

- Write like you talk -- the game is being relatable and human
- Find the sweet spot between too stiff and too casual
- Exercise: speak copy out loud first, then transcribe
- Free-write aggressively, then pull back to palatable
- For brand voice: offer celebrity comparisons to calibrate tone
- Brand email opener: "Hey [First Name]. It is [Founder Name] from [Brand]. Thought of you this morning because [seasonal/topical connector]..."

### Storytelling Principles
- Use non-linear narrative for power (hook first, then context)
- Five C's: Circumstances, Conflict, Crisis, Climax, Conclusions
- Rule of Threes for descriptors (least to most intense)
- Sensory words to describe how something FEELS
- Stories exist to flesh out character by showing REACTIONS, not just events (the Sitcom Secret)
- Horror stories are the most persuasive narrative type

### Writing Rules (Sam Bennett)
- 3:1 "you" to "I" ratio
- One topic per email
- One exclamation point max
- Eliminate filler: really, just, very, so
- One-sentence paragraphs for scannability
- Each email: clear desired result + clear next step

## CONTRARIAN INSIGHT: Emails Are the New Sales Letters (E06 -- Throssell)

In many cases emails are supplanting the traditional sales letter. A good email marketing game basically eliminates the need for long sales letters -- you can send people straight to an order form. One email should not take someone from cold to sold (that is the sales letter's job), but a cumulative email sequence over time can do the same work more effectively. The sequence builds trust, handles objections across multiple touches, and primes the reader so the final email only needs a short pitch + link. This reframes email copy: each email is a chapter, not the whole book.

## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENTS (SYNTHESIZED)

| Topic | Positions | Resolution |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| Market research depth | E01: 30 min max (contrarian); E06: deeper knowledge = better copy | Use 10-question checklist for speed; deeper research for unfamiliar markets |
| Bullets in emails | E06: overrated (people skip); E05: visual cue to pay attention | Good for utility emails; bad for story-driven sales emails |
| Questions in copy | E06: amateur tell, use stories; E05: curiosity questions work | Rhetorical sales questions are weak; curiosity questions in hooks are strong |
| Plain text vs. designed | E02: plain text nearly doubled opens; E03: brand consistency | 80/20 designed-to-plain ratio; plain text as pattern breaker during promos |
| Copy: emotional vs. logical | Common: copy is emotional not logical; E06: it is BOTH | Make logical arguments with emotion woven throughout |
