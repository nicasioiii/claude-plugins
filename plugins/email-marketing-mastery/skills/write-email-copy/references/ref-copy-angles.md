# Email Angles, Life Force Eight, High Click Concepts & TIMER Framework

## ANGLE LAYERING TECHNIQUE (Infinite Angles)

### The Problem with Layer-1 Thinking
Most copywriters only address surface-level problems and run out of angles quickly.

### The Method
Ask "what happens because of this?" at each level:

**Example (Skincare Brand):**
- L1: Bad skin
- L2: Makes you unattractive -> "Three tips to looking more handsome"
- L3: People treat you differently -> "How to command respect from strangers"
- L4: Low confidence -> "How to feel like the absolute man every morning"
- L5: Avoid putting yourself out there -> "How to get over your fear of rejection"
- L6: Miss opportunities -> "How to make new friends everywhere without feeling corny"
- L7: Unfulfilled life -> "How to make your life better in three easy steps"

### Practical Output
- 7 layers = 7 angles minimum
- Each angle produces 2+ subject lines = 14+ unique email concepts
- Deeper layers find people whose REAL problem is at Layer 5 or 6, not Layer 1
- As you go deeper, you find MORE people who resonate because the problems become universal

## NINE PROVEN EMAIL ANGLES (Full Templates)

### 1. The Schoolgirl Screech (Pure Excitement)
- Core: "This thing is SO freaking cool, you NEED it"
- Pure hype with no specific claims
- When you do not make claims, you do not bring up objections
- Use super believable claims surrounded by enthusiasm
- Lean into OPINION: "The fact that I think this is cool carries weight"
- Best used: Late in launches after logical/feature emails have been sent
- Template: Personal reaction to the product > Why you are excited > Easy believable claim > CTA

### 2. The Oprah Email (Lighthearted Announcement)
- Fun way to announce bonuses, extensions, new spots, added benefits
- Opens with relatable pop culture reference
- Delivers typically dull announcements entertainingly
- Adapt the reference to your audience (Santa, movie character, any beloved figure)
- Template: Pop culture reference > "Because I am bringing gifts" > Announcement details > CTA

### 3. The Three Rules
- Three proven rules/ideas/strategies the reader can check against
- Ideally they already do one (they just need 1-2 more puzzle pieces)
- Each rule leads logically to the product
- Cross-pollinate across industries
- Template: Subject with result > "Here are three [proven] [rules] that cannot be ignored" > Rule 1 > Rule 2 > Rule 3 > Solution follows all three > CTA

### 4. The Optical Illusion (Reframe)
- Present a counterintuitive way of looking at a problem
- Creates the "aha" moment
- Ties into why the product works
- Template: "[Specific issue] -- is [obvious solution] the fastest way to [result]? Surprisingly no." > Reveal real driver with specific numbers > Connect to product
- Example: "Erasing wrinkles the fastest way to look younger? Surprisingly no. Wrinkles = 18% of what someone sees. Your neck = 54%. That is 3x more."

### 5. The Micro Story
- Short story: person + problem/symptom + relatable obstacles + new solution + desired benefit
- Super versatile, works repeatedly across niches
- "Most unlikely person" variant: someone who should NOT have gotten the result actually did
- Paint vivid, visceral pictures of the "before" state
- Swipe the ANGLE and STRUCTURE from different industries, never the same industry
- Template: "[Name] was [relatable situation] > [Specific problem/symptom] > [What they tried] > [Discovery of new approach] > [Result]"

### 6. The Two Types
- "Probably the most versatile winner. Almost every time it works really well."
- Two delivery methods:
  - **Aspirational:** Two types of people (desirable vs. undesirable life)
  - **Educational:** Contrast two types of something (teach through comparison)
- Works on email, social, AND ads
- Template: "Two types of [people/thing]" > Type A description > Type B description > Connect to product > CTA

### 7. The Nit-Rat (Transformation Surprise)
- Someone doing "expected" things to get results
- Disappears for weeks/months
- Returns totally transformed
- Hook: "What did they do during that time?"
- Twist: they were doing LESS or something DIFFERENT
- Template: "I used to see [person] [grinding at activity] > Disappeared > Ran into them again > Almost did not recognize them > What changed: [product/approach]"

### 8. The Less Is More
- Doing something with LESS effort gives BETTER results
- Counterintuitive to what they think they need
- Uses TIMER currencies
- Template: "[Easy thing] can be more effective than [hard thing]. Most [common approach] is too [intense]. This causes [bad outcome]. The most effective way is [different approach]."

### 9. The Caveman Invite (Stripped-Down Direct Offer)
- Remove ALL filler -- speak like a caveman
- Key elements only: Avatar + Hell (problem) + Heaven (desired state) + Offer + Scarcity + Promise + "Without [hate 1], [hate 2], [hate 3]" + Risk reversal + Proof + "This works." + Scarcity + Next step
- Great for social media repurposing
- Best toward end of launches as recap

### 10. The Mission Impossible (Spinning Your Wheels)
- Targets the feeling of doing everything right but not getting results
- Core perspective: "If you are doing everything right but not getting results, then you are not doing everything right"
- Personal or client case study showing years of grinding
- Includes "what used to work is not working anymore" angle

### 11. The Rocky Balboa Final Countdown (Aspirational Cart Close)
- Fun way to deliver cart-close urgency people enjoy reading
- Replace "Rocky" with any relatable reference for the audience
- Include aspirational quote/lesson connected to the offer
- Structure: Pop culture reference > "Today is the last day" > If you already know you want [result], use this link > Aspirational quote > Connect to product > Ease objections > Final urgency
- Use "two paths" close or "best time to plant a tree was 10 years ago" approach

## LIFE FORCE EIGHT -- HOOK FORMULAS

### 1. Survival / Life Extension
"93% of people between age 35 and 49 are at risk of [X] in the next six months. Here is why you are likely one of them."

### 2. Food / Beverages
"We guarantee you have never tried this flavor before."

### 3. Freedom from Fear / Pain / Danger
"How to protect yourself from the increasing rate of [threat] in [location]."

### 4. Sexual Companionship / Status
"Three keys to finding a partner who will never stop loving you." / "The one crucial tip to never get rejected again."

### 5. Comfortable Living
"Consistently waking up with [pain]? Try this."

### 6. Superiority / Keeping Up
"Do this to get yourself into the top 1% in 30 days." / "This [blank] will make you the envy of your entire neighborhood."

### 7. Care / Protection of Loved Ones
"If your kid's principal will not help them from being bullied at school, this is for you."

### 8. Social Approval
"Here is what you have in common with [celebrity]." / "Five tips for navigating [social situation]."

### Targeting Insight
Call out demographics in hooks. The more targeted, the more likely someone buys. They need to feel like the only customer in the world.

## HIGH CLICK CONCEPTS

### Definition
Words, phrases, and ideas that trigger action. Emotionally charged language specific to your market.

### How to Build Your Library
- Most come during EDITING, not drafting
- Checklist question: "Am I pressing on the hottest emotional button?"
- Collect market-specific phrases that consistently drive clicks

### Examples (Health/Fitness)
- "Stress fat" / "Stress-related weight gain"
- "Extra fat stuck to your body"
- "Cry for help"
- "Revving metabolism"
- "Burning stubborn stress fat"
- "Super wife, super mom, super career woman"

### Visceral Language Principle
Paint visual pictures. Make symptoms concrete and sensory. Not "she looked old" but "creases spreading from her eyes that looked like spider webs."

## TIMER FRAMEWORK

Five currencies that matter to people. Use across launches to touch different motivations:

| Currency | Description | Email Application |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **T**ime | How much time does this save/cost? | "Get back 2 hours every morning" |
| **I**dentity | How they view THEMSELVES (internal) | "Become the kind of person who..." |
| **M**oney | Financial cost/savings | "Save $200/month by switching" |
| **E**nergy | Effort, intensity required | "Without spending hours at the gym" |
| **R**eputation | How they believe OTHERS view them (external) | "Your colleagues will ask what changed" |

**Key distinction:** Identity = internal self-image. Reputation = external perception. Both matter but appeal to different motivations.

In multi-day launches, deliberately touch different TIMER currencies across different emails.

## FOUR LENSES -- DETAILED APPLICATION

### Aspirational Lens
- Stories of transformation
- Future-pacing: "Imagine waking up six months from now and..."
- Motivational energy
- Best for: Cart-close emails, testimonial emails, lifestyle emails

### Insightful Lens
- "I never thought of things that way" moments
- Connect dots, shine light on blind spots
- Why we do things, biases, habits
- Best for: Mid-launch emails, belief-shifting emails, newsletter content

### Actionable Lens
- Step-by-step tactical advice
- The what, the why, and the how
- Implementable immediately
- Best for: Welcome sequences, value emails, content emails

### Analytical Lens
- Facts, stats, research breakdowns
- Data-driven arguments
- Complex concept simplification
- Best for: Objection-handling emails, comparison emails, product-aware audiences

**Rotation principle:** Most writers default to their own lens. Deliberately rotate through all four across a launch or campaign series to reach all buyer types.

## STORYTELLING IN EMAIL -- ADVANCED TECHNIQUES

### The "Based on a True Story" Principle
Take creative license. Compress timelines. Eliminate unnecessary characters. Rewrite dialogue for clarity or humor. Do not lie to make yourself look better.

### The Sitcom Secret
Stories exist NOT to relate interesting events but to show how you RESPOND to events. Focus on your REACTIONS. Ask: am I casting myself as the character I want readers to perceive?

### Horror Stories for Persuasion
Humans are hard-wired to be more persuaded by horror stories than anything else. Show a clear, horrific vision of the fate awaiting them without the solution. They persuade THEMSELVES.

### The Exaggeration Technique
Most boring stories need exaggeration. Amplify emotion. Turn statements into screams. People instantly teleport, burst into rooms. You are not surprised -- your eyes are wide with shock.

### Triforce of Email Leads
Three factors: a) relevance to your pitch; b) entertainment value; c) brevity. If short on any one, compensate in the other two.

## SWIPING ACROSS INDUSTRIES

### What to Swipe
Swipe the HOOK and ANGLE (the big idea), not the specific copy. Execute in your own voice.

### Hype Meter Adjustment
Any email can be dialed up or down in intensity:
- High hype: "European secret that erases years of aging in just a few short days"
- Low hype: "This morning skincare routine"
- Same angle, same structure, dramatically different tone

### Never Swipe Within Your Industry
Looking at different industries for hooks and applying them to your niche is creative. Swiping within the same industry is copying.
