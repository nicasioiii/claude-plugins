# Copy Frameworks, Templates & Design Modules

## AIDA DEEP DIVE

### Structure for Email Context
- **Attention (Line 1-2):** Hook that stops the scroll. Compete with inbox noise, not just other marketing
- **Interest (Lines 3-5):** Incentivize continued reading. If they stop reading here, everything below is wasted
- **Desire (Body):** Benefits, not features. What the product DOES for the prospect. Emotional pull
- **Action (CTA):** If the first three steps land, the CTA is almost automatic

### When to Use AIDA in Email
- Cold subscribers (new to list)
- Campaign emails to broad segments
- First email in a flow sequence
- Product launch announcements to mixed-awareness audiences

---

## PAS DEEP DIVE

### Structure for Email Context
- **Problem:** Call out a specific, relatable problem. Be precise -- vague problems get vague responses
- **Agitation:** Explain why this problem is SERIOUS. Layer consequences. Nobody fixes a problem that is not a big deal
- **Solution:** Clear the clouds. Present product as the path to resolution

### When to Use PAS in Email
- Retargeting/remarketing emails
- Mid-funnel flow emails (abandon cart email 2+)
- Story-based daily emails
- Warm audience broadcasts

---

## A-PASTA (COMBINED FRAMEWORK)

Full structure: Attention > Interest > Problem > Agitation > Solution > Desire > Action

Use when you have room (500+ word emails, long-form sales emails, final cart-close emails). This is the most complete persuasion arc in a single email.

## UPPER FRAMEWORK DETAILED APPLICATION

### Attention -- Lead Types
Choose one per email:
- **Story lead:** Open with a vivid moment ("This morning I read a crazy story about...")
- **Bold statement lead:** Counter-intuitive claim that demands attention
- **Secret/tease lead:** Imply insider knowledge
- **Problem-solution lead:** State the problem immediately
- **Promise lead:** Make a specific benefit promise
- **Offer/direct lead:** Lead with the deal itself

### Perspective -- The "New Way of Looking at It"
This is where unique mechanisms shine. Not just agitation -- give them a genuinely new understanding.
- "Stress is not just about having a bad day, it also comes from the food we eat"
- "Doctors call the hidden kind death fat because it causes serious health problems"
- Copy is BOTH emotional AND logical. You are making a logical argument with emotion tied into it.

### Tease Solution -- Blind vs. Direct
- **Blind:** Talk about the advantage without naming the product. Higher click rates because curiosity drives action
- **Hints:** Give clues about the unique mechanism. Middle ground
- **Direct:** Name it, say what it does. Lower clicks but higher quality, more pre-sold clicks

### Amplification Techniques
- Credibility or proof (research, authority endorsements)
- Make core promise bigger or bolder
- Show why solution is different from everything else
- One-line testimonials: "[Name] + [unique identifier] + got [result] + all while [saving time/money/energy]"
- Specificity about effort: "For best results, do this three times a week"
- "Even better" stack: layer additional benefits
- "Works for anyone" amplifier: broaden applicability

### Action -- CARE PAIR CTA Framework
Choose the CTA framing that fits:
- **C**onvenience -- how easy the solution is
- **A**ccess -- information/resources they did not have before
- **R**elief -- straightforward problem resolution
- **E**nhancement -- improving what they already have
- **C**ost savings -- saving money
- **P**ersonal transformation -- identity-level change
- **E**asily replace -- swap current inferior solution

## THE TABLE METHOD (ECOM) -- FULL TEMPLATE

### Above-the-Fold Components
- **Header:** MAX a few words. Short and clean. Usually the offer
- **Body:** Primary supporting details. What are they buying? Why? How?
- **CTA:** Shop now, Buy now. Include the link in body copy text for users who cannot find buttons

### Below-the-Fold Options (Choose 1-2)
1. **Product Recommendations:** "Haven't seen these yet?" + product images + short copy + buy now per item
2. **Testimonials:** "Don't take it from us, take it from them." + 2-3 customer quotes
3. **Founder Message:** Plain text personal note (good for brands with forward-facing founders)
4. **Bestsellers:** "Use code WELCOME15 on our top picks for you"

### Product-Specific Below-Fold Copy Formula
One line per product connecting product to benefit/use case:
- "Eye Stick -- for the morning after"
- "Face Mask -- to showcase your dreams of beauty"
- "Sunscreen -- for your skin's final days of summer"

## SPEED WRITING METHOD (REVERSE ORDER)

Fill easiest parts first to build momentum:
1. CTAs (always the same -- "Shop Now," "Buy Now")
2. Header (usually the offer -- straightforward)
3. Below-fold subheader + body (social proof or bestsellers -- pull from site)
4. Subject line, preview text, above-fold body (the only "hard" parts)

### Speed Hacks
- Set a timer BELOW your expected time. 12 emails expected all day? Try 45 minutes
- Get first words on page immediately: "Hey [first name], it is [name] from [brand]..."
- Starter phrases: "A lot of people have been asking me about..." / "Thought of you this morning because..." / "I was just [doing something] when I realized..."
- Waste a line or two on fun warm-up copy. It often becomes the actual hook
- Free-write first, edit after. Worst case: done fast + 3 minutes to edit
- Target: 30-60 minutes per email for a familiar market. Never more than 2 hours

## EMAIL DESIGN -- MODULAR FRAMEWORK (ECOM)

### Required Modules (Every Email)
1. **Goal:** Measurable objective (engagement or sales)
2. **Brand logo:** Always at the top
3. **Call to action:** Conveys purpose of email
4. **Unsubscribe link:** Legal requirement

### Optional Modules
1. **Banner:** Most important visual. Conveys offer in first 3 seconds. 5-7 words max. Feature the product
2. **Reviews:** Handle specific objections through selected reviews. Correct grammar. Blend with UGC
3. **Countdown timer:** Toward end of sale or code expiration. Tool: countdownmail.com
4. **UGC:** Real product usage photos. Puts faces behind customers
5. **Product display:** Table/grid. Dynamic or designed
6. **Authority factors:** "As seen on" logos. Hijack authority of publications
7. **Discount code:** Add random characters (e.g., WELCOME10DKWS) to prevent scraping and create exclusivity
8. **Guarantees:** Longer guarantee = LOWER refund rate (180-day beats 30-day) [CONTRARIAN]
9. **Body copy:** Focus on selling the click. Consider plain text for messages you want actually read

### 8 Rules for Bulletproof Ecommerce Guarantee
1. Start with "100% unconditional money back guarantee"
2. Sell benefits/transformation, NOT features
3. Integrate USPs in the wording
4. Personalize the guarantee
5. Give the longest guarantee possible
6. Demonstrate that returns are easy and hassle-free
7. Assure no small text or hidden terms
8. Name your guarantee

## THE DIRECT RESPONSE VS. BRAND COPY SPECTRUM

### When to Use Direct Response
1. Need quick sales (day 1 matters, day 30 does not)
2. Product but no brand
3. Lower quality / lower awareness leads
4. All competitors emphasize brand
5. Older audience (45-65)

### When to Use Brand Copy
1. Longer sales cycle (products over $1,000)
2. Rely on subscription revenue or LTV customers
3. Higher awareness leads (millennials, ad-savvy)
4. Cannot compete on direct response
5. All competitors use direct response

## MARKET RESEARCH -- 10-QUESTION CHECKLIST

Complete in 30 minutes or less for familiar markets:
1. What is the product?
2. What are the brand's values?
3. What does the brand want the customer to know?
4. Who is the customer avatar?
5. What problem do they have?
6. What do most customers say about this problem?
7. What do most customers say about this product?
8. How are customers getting this message? (Funnel position)
9. What action do you want them to take?
10. How do they like to be spoken to?

### Review Mining Strategy
- Read ONLY 5-star and 1-star reviews
- 5-star: Extract what people love (features, benefits, service)
- 1-star of COMPETITORS: Find weaknesses your product solves
- Use exact customer language in your copy

## CREATIVITY FRAMEWORKS

### Creativity Formula
Creativity = combining two existing things that have not been combined before.

### Three Laws of Creativity (The 3 R's)
1. **Relevance:** Must tie back to the product/goal
2. **Realism:** Must be believable and justifiable
3. **Resolution:** Must resolve toward the goal/CTA

### Application to Email
- Go through finished writing -- wherever you start falling asleep, inject excitement
- Number one rule: do not be boring
- Throw in jokes, TV references, unexpected turns
- Works best when you have more information than your audience

## THE PS AS POWER TOOL

- Use PS for TL;DR summaries (skimmers read the PS)
- Use PS for the main CTA for skimmers
- Use PS for additional urgency reminders
- Use PS for personality/humor that does not fit the main body
- Use PPS and PPPS liberally -- people read them
