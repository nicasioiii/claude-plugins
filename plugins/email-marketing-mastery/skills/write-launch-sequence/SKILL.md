# Write Launch Sequences

Write complete product/course launch email sequences -- pre-launch seeding, cart open, mid-cart objection busters, cart close, fast-action bonuses, soft opt-outs, competitive affiliate launches, and real-time responsive writing.

---

## THE LAZY LAUNCH MODEL (Belgray -- E04)

Generate six figures from emails alone -- no webinar, no pre-launch content (PLC), no Facebook challenges, no ads. Just emails plus a few social posts.

**Track record:**
- Inbox Hero launch: ~$170K in sales from emails alone
- B-School affiliate: ~$150K in commissions from ~8,000-subscriber list
- Money Bootcamp affiliate: #1 affiliate against partners with much bigger lists

**Core philosophy:** [CONTRARIAN] Most launches follow PLF (Product Launch Formula) with 3 free videos + webinar + challenge. The Lazy Launch skips all of it. The emails themselves ARE the pre-launch content, the webinar replacement, and the sales mechanism.

**The "more than you think" rule:** When asked how many emails to send, the answer is always "more than you think." The email you almost did not send is often the one that converts someone. First Inbox Hero launch: 25 total emails from seeding to final cart close.

## LAUNCH PHASES & EMAIL COUNT

| Phase | Duration | Email Count (Bare Minimum to All-In) |
|-------|----------|--------------------------------------|
| Pre-Launch / Seeding | 1-4 weeks | 1-10 emails |
| Cart Open Period | 1-2 weeks | 3-10 emails |
| Cart Close Day | 1 day | 1-6 emails |

**Throssell's nuance (E06):** Keep promos between 3-7 days. Beyond 7 days, you tire people out. Less than 3 days, people say they missed the emails. 4-5 days is probably best. But you CAN tease a product weeks or months in advance.

**Synthesized approach:** Pre-launch seeding can be long (1-4 weeks). Active selling window: 3-7 days. Cart-close day: 1-6 emails depending on list tolerance and entertainment value.

## PHASE 1: PRE-LAUNCH SEEDING

Purpose: Introduce the idea, build anticipation, promote pre-launch content. These seed excitement before the cart opens.

### Six Pre-Launch Email Types

**1. Sneak Peek** -- "Check out this thing that's in the works!"
- Share work-in-progress (screenshot, behind-the-scenes)
- Nugget of info about the product
- CTA: "Click here to be first to know when it's available" (interest list)
- PS: "If you've ever asked me 'How do you [objective]?' you're going to love this."

**2. Enviable Lifestyle / Results** -- "I owe it all to [concept]"
- Paint a picture of how good you have it because of [concept product teaches]
- Introduce the creator/product
- Bullet points of what they will learn
- CTA to interest list or free content

**3. Education / "Catch the Wave"** -- "Here's why now's the time"
- Stats on why now is the moment
- Not just for [type of person they think they are not]
- Examples of different ways people apply this
- CTA: Urgent message -- catch the wave or be left behind

**4. Personal Journey** -- "This transformed my life/business"
- Before: the problem, how it felt
- After: what life looks like now
- The bridge: one simple thing -- [concept product teaches]
- CTA to free content or interest list

**5. Contrast** -- "Some people will always struggle. You don't have to."
- Story of someone stuck in [common problem]
- Sad thing is, that is most people
- But you and I know that [concept] solves this
- Bulleted list of enviable outcomes
- CTA to product or free content

**6. Webinar / Masterclass Promo** -- "You should be there live!"
- Name of webinar + bulleted highlights
- Why attend live (bonuses, giveaways, limited replay)
- CTA: Grab your seat
- PS: Soft opt-out link for those who do not want promo emails

## PHASE 2: CART PRE-OPEN EMAIL

Purpose: Get them ready to pounce. Especially important when there is a fast-action bonus.

- Describe the product and why it is exciting
- Announce your bonuses (full description here)
- Explain affiliate relationship transparently (if applicable)
- Instructions on how to claim bonuses
- Fast Action Bonus description + deadline
- "Keep an eye on your inbox. I'll be here at [hour] to tell you it's Go Time."

**Why front-load buyers:** Getting people to buy at the start creates momentum and social proof. In competitive launches, more time thinking = more chance they shop other affiliates.

---

## PHASE 3: CART-OPEN EMAILS

### Cart Open Announcement
- Doors are open! Link near the TOP for people ready to buy (do not make them scroll)
- Why you want in + big mistake most people make
- Bulleted benefits + problems it eliminates
- Bonus package description
- Fast Action Bonus with time frame / limited spots
- PS: TL;DR for skimmers

### Fast Action Bonus Reminder
- Doors are open, people are swarming in
- Fast Action Bonus deadline
- Screenshots of social proof (IG stories, DMs, emails from early buyers)
- PS: "Post your stories and tag me!"

**Never stop reminding about the disappearing bonus.** You want maximum purchases at the beginning for buzz, momentum, and urgency.

## PHASE 4: MID-CART EMAILS

Purpose: Try different persuasion angles, tell different stories, address different objections. See `ref-launch-framework.md` for the complete email-by-email templates for all objection busters.

### Five Objection Busters
1. **"It won't work for me"** -- Story showing your flaw or liability. "If I can do it, you can too."
2. **"This is for suckers"** -- Validate skepticism. Agree marketers are often full of it. Then: why this is different. Stats/quotes/reviews.
3. **"Can't afford it"** -- Share your own scary investment story. "What if it doesn't work?" vs. "What if it does?" Case study of someone who thought they could not afford it.
4. **"I'm not ready"** -- Story of doing something before you were ready. Most who succeed were not ready when they joined.
5. **"I've tried everything"** -- What you tried that did not work. What was different about this. It is not their fault other things failed.

### Additional Mid-Cart Email Types
- **Future Pacing:** Paint vivid picture of their life [timeframe] from now. "Do you like that picture?"
- **FAQ Email:** Questions and answers; "Were you wondering this?" subject line (curiosity > revealing the question)
- **Testimonial / Social Proof:** Feature amazing results with quote + screenshot
- **"How cool is this reply":** Screenshot of real subscriber reply, elaborate on the angle
- **Catch-Up / "What Even Is This?":** Nutshell description for people who missed earlier emails

## PHASE 5: CART-CLOSE EMAILS

**Principle:** Make it 100% clear this is going away. Recommend at minimum a "last day" morning email and a "final hour" email.

### Cart-Close Sequence
1. **Closing Tomorrow** -- Story about your journey. "Don't wait another [timeframe] to do yourself this favor."
2. **Closing Today (Morning)** -- "Last day -- you have till [time]." Empowering message. Full bonus details.
3. **One More Hour** -- Short, empowering, urgent. "Join us here before it disappears."
   - **CRITICAL: Schedule 70 minutes before close, not 60** -- some emails are not delivered instantaneously (E04)
   - PS: "[Product] plus all my bonuses are available for one more hour via this link."

### Final Day Volume (E06)
- 3-8 emails on final day if they are entertaining
- Mix salesy emails with value-based emails
- Expect 1/3 to 1/2 of total sales in the final 24 hours
- Use aspirational/inspirational elements, not just "last chance, buy now" (E06)

## SAVE YOUR AMMUNITION (Throssell -- E06)

Do not throw every element into the first email. Distribute throughout the launch:
- Email 1: Product announcement, core benefits
- Email 2-3: Stories, social proof
- Email 4: Guarantee reveal
- Email 5: Payment plan deadline
- Email 6+: Bonuses, testimonials, FAQ answers

As people become ready, they buy. For holdouts, introduce new persuasion elements progressively.

## SOFT OPT-OUT STRATEGY (E04)

Let people unsubscribe from the promotion while staying on regular list.

- **When to introduce:** When email frequency ramps up, or when people ask to stay on list but stop hearing about the promo
- **Placement:** Top of every email once promotion frequency increases
- **Example language:** "If you DON'T want to hear about [thing] from me, click here: Don't email me about [thing] after this!"
- **Mechanics:** Tag-based suppression in your ESP

---

## FAST ACTION BONUS STRATEGY (E04)

**Always offer a fast action bonus.** More early buyers = more buzz and testimonials during the promo.

### Types
- Live Q&A / coaching call for first buyers within X hours
- Limited spots: "First 50 to purchase get invited to [live event]"
- Time-limited: "Buy within the first 50 hours"

### Urgency Mechanics
- Price going up next time: "This is the best deal you'll ever get"
- Disappearing bonus: Never stop reminding in every email during the bonus window

## COMPETITIVE AFFILIATE LAUNCH TACTICS (E04)

When everyone is selling the same thing:

1. **Make emails worth reading even for non-buyers.** People who already own the product keep reading if emails are entertaining. Some change their mind by the end.
2. **Use personality as differentiation.** When promoting something "not a natural fit," use the disconnect as a selling point.
3. **Pre-launch segmentation.** Survey: (a) Already bought, (b) Interested, (c) Not interested -- opt me out.
4. **The "5 Kinds of People" email.** Five clickable options to self-segment (see `ref-launch-tactics.md`).
5. **Email consistently before the launch.** Years of ghosting then showing up to sell = 2 sales. Regular emails + trust first = $150K+.

## LAUNCH CHECKLIST (E04)

Every launch sequence should cover these:
- [ ] Seed excitement (1-4 weeks before)
- [ ] Personal connection to the product/concept/creator
- [ ] Drool-worthy benefits (via something people envy in you)
- [ ] Why now? (Why now is the perfect moment)
- [ ] Stories about other people's successes
- [ ] Show work in progress (screenshots of product/bonuses)
- [ ] "Keep your eye out" -- announce when it's coming
- [ ] Hint at bonuses, then fully announce
- [ ] Grand opening "It's here" email
- [ ] Expiring fast-action bonus
- [ ] Personal stories emails
- [ ] FAQ email
- [ ] Social proof emails (testimonials, posts, stories)
- [ ] "Look what someone just said" emails (real subscriber replies)
- [ ] Catch-up "what even is this" email
- [ ] "Going away soon" emails
- [ ] "You can do this" empowering message
- [ ] "Closing soon" emails -- several on last day

## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENTS (RESOLVED)

| Topic | Belgray (E04) | Throssell (E06) | Synthesis |
|-------|---------------|-----------------|-----------|
| Launch email volume | "More than you think" (up to 6 on cart close, 25 total) | 3-7 day promos, 3-8 emails on final day | Both agree on aggressive volume; E04 provides more granular phasing |
| Swipe copy | Never use swipe copy | Swipe the HOOK and ANGLE from different industries, not the copy | Use frameworks as soft scripts, swipe angles across industries, bring your own voice |
| Sales expectation timing | Back-loaded (most sales in final 24 hours) | 1/3 to 1/2 in final 24 hours | Both agree; front-load with fast-action bonus to capture early buyers too |
| FAQ emails | "Were you wondering this?" curiosity approach | FAQs are boring unless made fun; sprinkle through multiple emails | Put FAQs in PS sections before cart close; make dedicated FAQ emails entertaining |
