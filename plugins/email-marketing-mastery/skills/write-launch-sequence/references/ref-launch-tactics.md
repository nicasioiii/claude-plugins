# Reference: Launch Tactics -- Advanced Strategies

## SOFT OPT-OUT DETAILED MECHANICS (E04)

### What It Is
A link that lets people unsubscribe from the specific promotion while staying on the regular email list. Requires tag-based suppression in the ESP.

### Implementation Steps
1. Create a tag in your ESP: "opted-out-[promotion-name]"
2. Create a link that applies this tag when clicked
3. Add tag-based exclusion to all subsequent promotion emails
4. After promotion ends, archive or deactivate the tag

### Placement Strategy
- Introduce when email frequency starts to ramp up
- Place at the TOP of every email once promotion frequency increases
- Some prefer bottom placement -- test both

### Language Examples
- "If you DON'T want to hear about [thing] from me, click here: Don't email me about [thing] after this!"
- "Not interested in [product]? No worries. Click here to skip these emails and I'll see you when I'm back to regular programming."
- "Want to keep getting my regular emails but skip the [product] promo? Totally cool. Opt out here."

### Why It Works
- Preserves list health by preventing full unsubscribes
- Shows respect for subscriber inbox
- People who opt out of a specific promo remain warm for future offers
- Reduces spam complaints during heavy promotion periods

## FAST ACTION BONUS DEEP DIVE (E04)

### Why Always Offer One
- More early buyers = more buzz and testimonials during the promo
- Social proof snowball: early buyers share excitement, creating FOMO for fence-sitters
- In affiliate launches, buying early from YOU (vs. shopping around) locks in your commission

### Types of Fast Action Bonuses
| Type | Best For | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| Live Q&A / coaching call | High-ticket products | "First buyers get a live 60-min Q&A with me" |
| Limited spots | Creating urgency | "First 50 to purchase get invited to [exclusive event]" |
| Time-limited | Broad reach | "Buy within the first 50 hours to get [bonus]" |
| Digital bonus | Easy to deliver | "First 100 buyers get my $197 [template/course] free" |

### Urgency Mechanics for Bonuses
- Price going up: "This is the best deal you'll ever get. The price goes up after this launch."
- Disappearing bonus: Remind in EVERY email during the bonus window
- Countdown: "Only 12 hours left to get [fast action bonus]"
- Social proof + scarcity combo: "47 of 50 spots have been claimed"

## THE "5 KINDS OF PEOPLE" SEGMENTATION EMAIL (E04)

### Purpose
Pre-launch segmentation that respects subscriber choice and pre-qualifies interest.

### Five Clickable Options
1. **"SO not in the mood to hear about [thing]"** -- Opt-out link (tags them to suppress promo emails)
2. **"Looking to [achieve result]"** -- Opt-in for promo emails (tags them as interested)
3. **"Already [thing] curious"** -- Express interest + learn about bonuses (highest intent segment)
4. **"Already a grad / not interested but like the emails"** -- Stay subscribed, enjoy the ride (no promo suppression, no special targeting)
5. **Fun wildcard option** -- e.g., "A mushroom pizza?" (personality moment, catches skimmers)

### Why It Works
- Shows you respect their inbox and give them control
- Pre-qualifies interest so you can send more aggressively to interested segment
- Reduces complaints and unsubscribes during heavy promo periods
- Data: tells you the size of your interested audience before you start

### When to Send
- 1-2 weeks before the launch begins
- Can be combined with a pre-launch survey for deeper segmentation

## COMPETITIVE AFFILIATE TACTICS (E04)

### Standing Out When Everyone Sells the Same Thing

**Tactic 1: Entertainment Differentiation**
Make launch emails worth reading even for non-buyers. People who already own the product or have no interest keep reading if emails are entertaining. Some change their mind by the end.

**Tactic 2: Personality as Differentiation**
When promoting something "not a natural fit":
1. Acknowledge the disconnect head-on: "Part of me said, 'Nope. That's some [off-brand] thing right there.'"
2. Use your skepticism as credibility: "I'm making it safe for someone who'd be embarrassed to explore this."
3. Show your journey from skeptic to believer: Initial resistance > What changed your mind > Results
4. Make it safe for skeptics: "Even if you're a skeptic like I am..."

**Tactic 3: Pre-Launch Survey Segmentation**
Before the launch, survey your list:
- (a) Already bought it
- (b) Interested and want to hear about it
- (c) Not interested -- please don't email me about this

**Tactic 4: Consistent Pre-Launch Emailing**
Years of ghosting your list then showing up to sell = 2 sales. Regular emails + trust = $150K+.

**Tactic 5: Transparent Affiliate Disclosure**
"I get a commission, which I like to call 'thank-you money.' It sounds way less... Avon Lady." Transparency builds trust and makes the pitch less sleazy.

## REAL-TIME RESPONSIVE WRITING (E04)

### The Principle
[CONTRARIAN] Do not write all launch emails in advance. Respond to real-time feedback, questions, and events as they happen during the promotion.

### How to Practice It
- When someone asks a question you did not anticipate, turn it into an email: "I didn't expect so many people would ask this..."
- When you hear "this really got me off the fence," repeat that angle in another email
- When noteworthy things happen during the launch, include them
- Screenshots of real DMs, replies, and social posts become tomorrow's email content

### Why It Works
- Emails feel fresh and current, not canned
- Addresses actual objections from your actual audience
- Social proof is real-time, not manufactured
- Creates a sense of community and shared experience around the launch

## THE PS AS POWER TOOL (E04)

### Multiple Uses for PS in Launch Emails
1. **TL;DR for skimmers:** One-sentence summary + main CTA link
2. **Main CTA placement:** For people who scroll to the bottom without reading
3. **Additional urgency:** "Only 3 hours left to get the fast action bonus"
4. **Personality/humor:** Content that does not fit the main body
5. **FAQ answers:** Sprinkle one FAQ answer in the PS of several emails before cart close (E06)

### PPS and PPPS
- Use liberally -- people read them
- Each additional PS can introduce a different element (bonus detail, testimonial snippet, deadline reminder)
- The PS section often has higher read rates than mid-email content

## BONUS DESCRIPTION STRATEGY (E04)

### Describe Fully Once, Then Reference
- Describe bonuses in full in the cart pre-open email and cart-open email
- Going forward, mention by name + link to a bonus/sales page for details
- Do not repeat full bonus descriptions in every email -- it gets tedious
- "Check out the full details here" with a link

### Bonus Page
- Create a dedicated page listing all bonuses with descriptions
- Link to this page from every launch email instead of re-describing
- Include visuals/mockups of bonus materials for Instagram-worthy social proof

## SOCIAL PROOF IN LAUNCH EMAILS (E04)

### Types of Social Proof to Use
- **IG stories:** Visual pop and create FOMO
- **Screenshots of real replies/DMs:** With permission, type out the text too for accessibility
- **Early buyer shoutouts:** Sharing incentivizes others to do the same
- **Product cover graphics:** Makes product Instagram-worthy for sharing

### How to Gather Social Proof During Launch
- Ask early buyers to share IG stories tagging you
- Monitor DMs and email replies for testimonial-worthy quotes
- Screenshot everything promising in real-time
- Ask: "What got you off the fence?" and use those answers in subsequent emails

## LINK PLACEMENT STRATEGY (E04)

### Rules
- Give a link near the TOP for people ready to buy (never make them scroll)
- Also link at the BOTTOM for those who read through
- The PS should always contain a link
- Preview text should be strategic, especially when the first lines contain an opt-out

## BACK-LOADED SALES EXPECTATION (E04, E06)

### The Reality
- 1/3 to 1/2 of total sales come in the final 24 hours (both E04 and E06 agree)
- Exception: pre-order lists or heavily teased products get most sales at door-open
- This means: do NOT panic if early sales are slow
- The job of mid-cart emails is to keep the product top-of-mind for the final push

### How to Maximize Final-Day Sales
- Send 3-8 emails on the final day (only if entertaining)
- Include deadline info in subject lines
- Mix salesy with value-based emails
- Use aspirational "two paths" close alongside urgency
- Schedule final email 70 minutes before close

## DISGUISE PITCH AS ENTERTAINMENT (Throssell -- E06)

### The Technique
When sending multiple sales emails in a day, wrap each in a different creative frame.

### Examples
- Each email written in the "voice" of a different famous copywriter
- One email as a "letter from your future self"
- One email as a mock news report about the product
- One email as a dialogue between your past self and current self

### Why It Works
People read the same pitch multiple times when it is entertainingly packaged. By phrasing it differently each time, the sales message hits from multiple angles without feeling repetitive.

## PROMOTING NON-NATURAL-FIT PRODUCTS (E04)

### When the Product Does Not Match Your Brand Persona
1. **Acknowledge the disconnect head-on.** "Part of me said, 'Nope. That is some goddess-y woo-woo right there.'"
2. **Use your skepticism as credibility.** "I'm making it as safe as possible for someone who'd be embarrassed to explore this."
3. **Show personal journey from skeptic to believer.** Three beats:
   - Initial resistance: "My first reaction was..."
   - What changed your mind: "Then I saw/experienced..."
   - Results you have seen: "And now I..."
4. **Make it safe for skeptics to participate.** "Even if you're a skeptic like I am..."

### Key Insight
The disconnect between your skeptical persona and the product becomes the SELLING POINT rather than something to hide. Authenticity in acknowledging the mismatch builds more trust than ignoring it.
