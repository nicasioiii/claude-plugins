# Subject Line Formulas, Examples & A/B Testing Protocol

## LAUNCH SUBJECT LINE FORMULAS (Belgray)

### Pre-Launch Phase
These build anticipation before cart opens:
- "So? You want in on this? (NEED BY TOMORROW)"
- "Come to my room?"
- "what a pain - srsly"
- "The missing 'click here'"
- "Inbox Hero! (early notice - for your eyes only)"
- "Quick Q - need to know this about you (and you want me to)"
- "You're one of these 5 kinds of people (OPEN to save your sanity!)"
- "re: OMG success! (And pudding.)"
- "You and me and our money stuff [WATCH THIS]"
- "Working too hard and not making enough? Same here. (I swear)"

### Cart-Open Phase
Direct urgency paired with personality:
- "RUN! Inbox Hero - now open with a fast-action bday bonus"
- "Inbox Hero - were you wondering this? [BONUS ends tomorrow am!]"
- "Highs n lows + pics [plus, bonus ending at 11am ET]"
- "Brag - and 45 mins left to get the answers to life"
- "have a few left - want this?"
- "oh, that's a story"

### Cart-Close Phase
Maximum urgency, clear deadline:
- "Last chance to get 33% off any copywriting course I sell" (Throssell)
- Put deadline info IN the subject line so people do not have to open
- Send 1-3 hours before sale ends

## ECOM SUBJECT LINE TACTICS (MuteSix)

### Tactics That Consistently Drive Opens
- **Exclusivity:** "Early VIP Access" -- drove 40% open rate
- **Scarcity:** "Limited Stock Available" / "Limited Quantities Available"
- **Personalization:** First name tag in subject line
- **Urgency:** "Your cart is about to expire"

### Ecom Best Practices
- Keep under 50 characters for mobile-first audiences
- Users decide in 2-3 seconds whether to open
- If running a discount, test placement: subject line vs. preview text
- When promo: put discount in subject OR pre-header -- A/B test which works

## THROSSELL SUBJECT LINE PRINCIPLES (10 Rules)

### Rule 1: Form a Question
Every subject line must create a question in the reader's mind. Evaluate: what question does this form, and how badly would they want the answer?

### Rule 2: Aim for the Double Whammy
Surprise + curiosity > curiosity alone. "Wait, what?!" is stronger than "Hmm, interesting."

### Rule 3: Self-Interest Usually Beats Pure Curiosity
Include the core interest word in most subject lines. Benefit-driven pulls more consistently.

### Rule 4: Pump Up with Specificity
Replace vague claims with specific numbers, dates, dollar amounts, or percentages.

### Rule 5: Pump Up with Credibility
Name-drop authorities, cite known entities, reference recognizable results.

### Rule 6: Push It Further
Your subject line is probably not "out there" enough. Find the craziest legitimate angle.

### Rule 7: Long Lines Can Work
Truncation adds curiosity. Front-load with interesting words. Do not fear length.

### Rule 8: Use the Grafting Pre-Test
Imagine it was sent by someone you rarely read. Would you still open?

### Rule 9: Pay Off What You Tease
Unsatisfied curiosity becomes resentment. Always deliver on the subject line's promise.

### Rule 10: Names Boost Opens
First name helps. Full name helps more. Do not overdo it.

## SUBJECT LINE PATTERN CATEGORIES

### Category 1: Vague + Emotional
Low specificity, high emotional pull. Works best for established sender-reader relationships.
- "Too soon?"
- "oh, that's a story"
- "what a pain - srsly"
- "have a few left - want this?"

### Category 2: Direct + Brackets
Clear topic with bracketed qualifier adding urgency or format.
- "[BONUS ends tomorrow am!]"
- "[WATCH THIS]"
- "(NEED BY TOMORROW)"
- "(OPEN to save your sanity!)"

### Category 3: Personal / Intimate
Mimics personal communication. Creates immediate "who is this?" intrigue.
- "Come to my room?"
- "Let's not fake it, [First Name]"
- "Is this it for us? I don't wanna say goodbye"
- "Bribe you"

### Category 4: Numbered + Curiosity
Specificity of numbers combined with curiosity gap.
- "You're one of these 5 kinds of people"
- "3 humiliating mistakes"
- "These 5 words are a huge turnoff"
- "My #1 favorite writing trick (any guesses?)"

### Category 5: Taboo / Tension
Words or concepts that create micro-tension because they are socially uncomfortable.
- "Brag" -- bragging is taboo, we want to know what about
- "Too soon?" -- implies they have done something premature
- "I saw what you did" -- creates accountability spike
- "We need to talk" -- relationship anxiety

### Category 6: Combination (Emotion + Specifics)
Layer emotional pull with concrete details.
- "Brag - and 45 mins left to get the answers to life"
- "Highs n lows + pics [plus, bonus ending at 11am ET]"
- "Working too hard and not making enough? Same here. (I swear)"

### Category 7: Misleading-but-Fun
Intentionally ambiguous in a playful way.
- "have a few left - want this?" (a few minutes left, not items)
- "This is not the email I wanted to send you today" (implies reluctance)
- "Come to my room?" (personal, not what you think)

## A/B TESTING PROTOCOL (DETAILED)

### Campaign A/B Test Setup (Klaviyo)

**Step 1: Choose What to Test**
- Subject line (highest impact -- test this first, always)
- Preview text (second priority)
- Sender name (third -- test sparingly, do not mislead)

**Step 2: Configure the Test**
- Test size: 10-20% of total list
- Split: Throttle (auto-winner) or 50/50 (manual analysis)
- Duration: 2-4 hours
- Winning metric: Open rate for subject/preview/sender; Click rate for content

**Step 3: Scheduling Math**
If desired delivery = 10 AM and test duration = 2 hours:
- Schedule test start at 8 AM
- Winner auto-sends to remainder at 10 AM
- Tick "Determine recipients at send time"

### Flow A/B Test Setup (Klaviyo)
- Add variation to any flow email; equalize weights
- Test subject lines first (higher opens cascade to higher clicks and conversions)
- Single variables only
- Sample size: 100-500 emails per variation before declaring winner
- Optimize flows in order of revenue contribution

### Binary Search Approach
Do not test incrementally. Test polar opposites first, then narrow:
1. Test: short curiosity subject vs. long specific subject
2. Winner competes against a variation of itself
3. Continue narrowing until diminishing returns

### Key Early Test: Discount Visibility
- Version A: "Save big for Labor Day Weekend" (curiosity/vague)
- Version B: "30% off for Labor Day Weekend" (specificity/direct)
- Run this early with new accounts to learn audience preference
- This single test informs your approach for all future campaigns

## SUBJECT LINES FOR SPECIFIC EMAIL TYPES

### Abandon Cart/Checkout
- "Your cart is about to expire" (urgency)
- "Still thinking about it?" (empathetic)
- "We saved your cart (but not for long)" (scarcity + helpfulness)
- Call out specific behavior: "We know you added [product] and left"

### Post-Purchase
- "Your order is on its way" (transactional -- include keyword for search)
- "A note from [Founder Name]" (personal)
- "You made a great choice" (affirmation)

### Winback
- "We miss you, [First Name]" (emotional)
- "It's been a while" (personal)
- "A gift for old friends" (incentive)

### VIP/Loyalty
- "Early VIP Access" (exclusivity -- 40% open rate benchmark)
- "For your eyes only" (exclusivity)
- "[First Name], this is just for you" (personalization + exclusivity)
