# Reference: Creator Welcome Sequence -- Extended Email Templates

## ONGOING WELCOME EMAIL TYPES (Post Email 4)

After the core 4 emails (deliver, resell, get-to-know-you, I-sell-things), these email types extend the welcome sequence. Add them over time by repurposing your best-performing broadcasts.

---

### Type 1: Story + Copy Lesson + Soft Sell
**Benchmark:** 5.67% CTR to product page

**Template Structure:**
- Tell an entertaining personal story (vivid, specific, funny or relatable)
- Segue to a business/life lesson: "When someone [does X], they're out of there as fast as..."
- Soft sell: "Luckily, you can keep that from happening with [skill/product]. What kind? The kind that doesn't suck."
- Link to relevant product
- PS: Related tip or secondary CTA

**Example subject:** "Should've knocked! (Don't let this happen to your business)"

**Why it works:** The story creates engagement and emotional connection. The lesson feels natural, not forced. The sell is a gentle extension of the lesson, not a pivot.

---

### Type 2: Testimonial + Dual Product Pitch
**Benchmark:** 5.62% CTR

**Template Structure:**
- Tell a relatable story (personal craving, everyday moment)
- Segue: "You want to be [desirable quality] to your audience like [story element]?"
- Showcase a killer testimonial (full quote, customer name, specific result)
- Dual pitch: "Which of these is missing from your arsenal?"
- Descriptions of 2-3 products with links
- PS: Reminder of lead magnet or free resource

**Example subject:** "You have at least half of this! (Deadly combo)"

**Why it works:** The testimonial does the selling. Offering multiple products lets readers self-select based on their current need.

---

### Type 3: Pure Value / Teaching Email
**Benchmark:** High open rates, builds trust bank

**Template Structure:**
- Teach a single actionable principle (one concept per email)
- Give examples: "OK way to do it" vs. "Better way" vs. "Best way"
- Drive to Instagram with a related post OR to a blog post
- No direct sell -- builds trust and authority
- PS: "What's your biggest [topic] struggle? Hit reply and tell me."

**Example subject:** "These 5 words are a huge turnoff (you've been warned)"

**Why it works:** Establishes expertise. Builds the "trust bank" so selling later feels earned. Drives engagement through replies and social follows.

---

### Type 4: Mystery / Curiosity Email
**Benchmark:** 18.3% CTR (highest in entire welcome sequence)

**Template Structure:**
- Build up the value of one specific technique/trick without revealing it
- Create anticipation: "This one trick changed everything for me"
- Link to blog post that reveals it
- "Can you guess what it is without reading the post? Hit me back and tell me, smarty pants. No cheating."
- PS: Plug for relevant product

**Example subject:** "My #1 favorite writing trick (any guesses?)"

**Why it works:** Curiosity is the strongest email driver. The "guess before clicking" element creates engagement beyond just clicking a link. The blog post delivers on the promise AND captures site visitors for retargeting.

---

### Type 5: Myth-Busting Email
**Benchmark:** High reply rates, strong list-building effect

**Template Structure:**
- Call out common bad advice: "'[Bad advice]' -- I respectfully disagree."
- [CONTRARIAN] Bust myths and reveal misinformation your subscriber has been fed
- Explain why the conventional wisdom is wrong
- What to do instead
- Pitch relevant product that addresses the myth
- PS: "Am I wrong? Tell me. I can take it."

**Example subject:** "What if it IS about you?"

**Why it works:** Myth-busting creates an "aha moment" that bonds the reader to you. It positions you as someone who thinks independently, not just repeating the same advice everyone else gives. It builds authority through contrarianism.

---

### Type 6: Survey / Bribe Email
**Benchmark:** 65% open rate, 40% increase in submissions when added to multiple emails

**Template Structure:**
- "I'll trade you info about myself for info about you. Deal?"
- "All you have to do is fill out this 2-question, 90-second survey"
- Bribe: "I'll send you a shameful confession" (or other fun reward)
- PS: Alternative for refusers: "If you outright REFUSE to take the survey, hit reply and tell me [fun question]"

**Example subject:** "Bribe you"
**Preview text:** "It's ethical. I think there's such an easy one for you."

**Survey Questions to Ask:**
1. What are you here for / interested in? (segments for content)
2. Which of my courses/products have you already taken? (segments for offers)

**Key tactic:** Add the survey CTA to 2-3 welcome emails beyond the initial placement. Belgray saw a 40% increase in daily submissions by mentioning it in more emails.

## COLD LIST RE-ENGAGEMENT SEQUENCE (E04)

When you have not emailed in a long time:

### Email 1: Re-Introduction
- Remind them how they got on your list
- "I know you haven't heard from me yet. I'm writing because you signed up for [X]."
- Give something valuable -- do not sell in the first email back
- Re-provide the original lead magnet: "Here it is again, in case you misplaced it"

### Email 2: Value + Connection
- Share your best piece of content
- Ask a question to prompt replies
- "I've got some great stuff planned for you"

### Email 3: Soft Sell
- "Now that we're reacquainted..."
- Introduce your current offers casually
- Link to products in PS

### Key Principle
The years Belgray ghosted her list and then showed up selling, she made 2 sales (one disappeared). When she emailed regularly and built trust first, she cracked $150K.

## FIBONACCI CADENCE MODEL

### What It Is
Space emails with increasing gaps following a pattern similar to the Fibonacci sequence:
- Email 1: Day 0 (immediate)
- Email 2: Day 1
- Email 3: Day 2
- Email 4: Day 4
- Email 5: Day 7
- Email 6: Day 11
- Email 7: Day 18

### Why It Works
- Highest frequency when subscriber is warmest (just opted in)
- Gradually reduces as initial excitement fades
- Prevents inbox fatigue while maintaining presence
- Mirrors natural relationship-building rhythm

### When to Use
- Default cadence for most creator/service welcome sequences
- Adjust based on your content volume and subscriber tolerance
- After the welcome sequence ends, transition to your regular broadcast schedule

## LIST HYGIENE WITHIN WELCOME SEQUENCES (E04)

### Automated 100-Day Scrub
- Subscribers who have been cold (no opens) for ~100 days get removed
- Improves deliverability for remaining engaged subscribers
- Set up as automated flow: "If no open in 100 days, remove from list"

### Handling Replies
- Do not be scared of replies -- they boost deliverability and build connection
- When volume becomes overwhelming, hire someone to respond (transparently, not pretending to be you)
- Only respond personally when truly necessary

- Not recommended for creator welcome sequences

## WEBSITE-TO-EMAIL INTEGRATION (E04)

### Opt-In Strategy
- Do NOT put social media links on your website
- Website purpose: get opt-ins and purchases
- Email purpose: deepen connection and drive to social, content, and products
- Think of Instagram as a retargeting tool -- they see the same content in a different channel

### Thank-You Page Upsell
- After opt-in, redirect to a page offering a product at half price for 10-20 minutes
- Creates immediate revenue from new subscribers
- Because many buy via this upsell, fewer warm prospects remain for welcome sequence -- but the sequence still adds meaningful incremental sales
