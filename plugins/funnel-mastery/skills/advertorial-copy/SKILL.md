## PURPOSE AND FUNCTION

Advertorials solve the problem of insufficient education and belief when going directly from ad to product page.

**Without advertorial:**
Ad -> Short Product Page = CPA too high, AOV too low, can't scale, lose money

**With advertorial:**
Ad -> Advertorial (educates, builds belief, creates urgency) -> Product Page/VSL = better CPA, higher AOV, scalable profitability

### When You Need an Advertorial

Use an advertorial when:
- Target audience is problem-aware or unaware (needs education before product pitch)
- Market is saturated and prospects are skeptical of direct sales pages
- Product requires mechanism explanation (UMP/UMS) before the prospect will consider buying
- Traffic is cold and brand equity is low
- Direct-to-product-page funnels are unprofitable

Skip the advertorial when:
- Audience is product-aware or most-aware (already knows solution category)
- Brand has strong existing recognition
- Product has instant demonstrable proof
- Traffic is warm (email list, retargeting)

## THE 5 GOALS OF AN ADVERTORIAL

Every advertorial must accomplish these five objectives:

1. **Laywork of selling** -- pre-educate and warm up the prospect before they see the product
2. **Curiosity** -- hook them in so they keep reading (not selling yet)
3. **Personal/emotional connection** -- engage through story so they feel invested
4. **Educational** -- teach about the UMP, product category, or problem (the "real reason" framework)
5. **Sell the qualified click** -- get the RIGHT person to click through to the VSL or product page

**The key distinction:** An advertorial does NOT sell the product. It sells the CLICK to the product page. The advertorial's job is to deliver a warm, educated, emotionally engaged prospect to the sales page -- NOT to close the sale itself.

---

## 7-STEP ADVERTORIAL STRUCTURE

1. **Curiosity + emotional hook** (headline + opening story)
2. **Personal/emotional engagement** (story of someone with the pain point)
3. **Education about the problem** (UMP introduction, why existing solutions fail)
4. **Solution tease** (friend/discovery/product introduction -- but don't fully reveal yet)
5. **Product reveal + benefits** (how it works, key features)
6. **Social proof** (testimonials, reviews)
7. **CTA** (click through to full sales page or product page)

## LENGTH GUIDELINES

| Length | Word Count | Best For | Awareness Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Short** | 150-500 words | Highly aware markets, strong demonstrable proof products | Product-aware, most-aware |
| **Medium** | 500-1,500 words | Most common; education + story + product reveal | Solution-aware |
| **Long** | 1,500-3,000+ words | Low-awareness markets; heavy education + mechanism + belief building | Problem-aware, unaware |

### Length Decision Rule
The less aware your audience is, the more education the advertorial must provide before the click-through. Problem-aware audiences need the advertorial to do heavy lifting -- introducing the mechanism, building belief in the solution category, and creating emotional investment. Product-aware audiences just need a quick story and social proof to click through.

## PLATFORM DIFFERENCES: NATIVE vs. META

### Native Advertorials (Taboola, Outbrain)

- Must look and feel like **editorial pieces or news stories**
- Longer format is typical (1,000-3,000 words)
- Designed to maintain the reading experience they were already having
- Headlines should look like article headlines, not ad headlines
- Layout should resemble the publisher's editorial design
- Reader should feel they're reading a news article or feature story, not an ad
- Images should feel editorial (not product shots)

### Meta Advertorials (Facebook, Instagram)

- Can be **shorter** than native (500-1,500 words is common)
- More **story-driven** -- personal narratives, discovery stories
- Designed to feel like content they'd consume on social media
- Can be more visually rich with product images/lifestyle photos
- Voice can be more personal and casual
- Reader should feel they're reading a friend's recommendation or a content creator's post

### Platform Congruency Rule
The advertorial must maintain congruence with the ad experience that preceded it:
- If the ad was editorial/curiosity-based -> advertorial must feel editorial
- If the ad was story-based/personal -> advertorial must feel like a personal story
- Breaking congruence (ad feels like news, landing page feels like an infomercial) disrupts the customer journey and kills conversion rates
