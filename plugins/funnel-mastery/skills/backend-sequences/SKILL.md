# Backend Email Sequences

Design post-purchase email flows that promote additional products to existing customers. Backend sequences are pure profit -- the customer has already been acquired. This skill covers what to promote, when, how many emails per offer, and what to write.

## CORE PHILOSOPHY: BACKEND = PURE PROFIT

The customer is already acquired. Every sale from a backend sequence costs $0 in acquisition. This is where the real money lives.

**Katie's revenue data:** Main product generated $24,672 in one week. Backend email sequences generated $53,431 in the same week -- more than DOUBLE the front-end revenue.

**Why most businesses leave this money on the table:**
- They do not have products to promote (solve by re-using upsell products at a discount)
- They fear "being too salesy" (customers WANT to buy more -- they are already thinking it)
- They do not know the structure (this skill provides exact cadence, copy patterns, and benchmarks)

## BACKEND SEQUENCE STRUCTURE

### Starting Point
Start with ONE backend sequence. Do not build five sequences simultaneously.

If you do not have many products yet, re-use your $197 upsell as the first backend offer, but discounted. A product they already declined in the upsell flow can convert via email at a discount because the context changes -- they have now consumed your content and built more trust.

### 4-Email Sequence Cadence (Per Product)

Each backend product promotion follows this cadence:

| Email | Timing | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| Email 1 | Day 1 (after added to sequence) | Introduce the product and its value |
| Email 2 | Day 2 (1 day after Email 1) | Reinforce with proof, reframe the problem |
| Email 3 | Day 3 (1 day after Email 2) | Stack benefits, deepest pitch |
| Email 4 | Day 3.5-4 (12-24 hours after Email 3) | Urgency/deadline -- "last chance" |

**Critical rule:** Each email stands alone. Every email must be able to sell independently. Do NOT assume they read previous emails.

## BACKEND OFFER ORDERING

Sequence offers from low to high price. Give customers time to consume content and build trust before selling expensive items.

### Recommended Offer Order

**First backend offer:** Under $100 product
- Lowest friction, highest conversion rate
- Can be a discounted version of an upsell they declined
- Goal: get them buying from email (establish the pattern)

**Second backend offer:** Mid-ticket ($100-$300) OR high-ticket if applicable
- They have now consumed some content and trust you more
- If going to high-ticket, this is the bridge

**Third backend offer:** Under $500 product OR high-ticket ($2,000+)
- By now, they know your quality
- High-ticket becomes possible without sales calls

### Conversion Rate Benchmarks by Price

| Backend Product Price | Target Conversion Rate (per 100 customers) |
|---|---|
| Under $100 | 6% or higher |
| Under $300 | 3.5% or higher |
| Under $500 | 1-3.5% or higher |

## HIGH-TICKET BACKEND: SELLING WITHOUT CALLS

Katie generated $42,000 in a single day from 3 emails promoting a high-ticket accelerator (over $5,000). No DM conversations, no email selling back-and-forth, no sales calls. Customers just buy from the link.

### Why This Works
Trust is already built through the low-ticket funnel system. They have bought from you, consumed your content, gotten results, and now believe in your ability to deliver transformation.

### High-Ticket Email Incentives That Work
- **Discount codes** -- proven to move high-ticket purchases
- **Limited spots** -- "only 3 spots left" (and mean it)
- **Bonuses** -- tested but did NOT get significant results for Katie. Discount codes and limited spots outperformed bonuses.

### Sequencing Rule
Do NOT immediately try to sell high-ticket to new low-ticket customers. Put a lower-ticket course as the FIRST backend offer before the multi-thousand dollar pitch.

### Critical Positioning Rule
You cannot sell high-ticket like information. You must sell it like transformation via coaching. "How much faster could we go? How many more breakthroughs could you have if we work together?" Customers already buy courses from you -- high-ticket must be about coaching, mentorship, and direct access, not more information.

### Cannibalization Warning
Make sure your low-ticket offer does NOT cannibalize your high-ticket offer. They must serve different needs or levels. If the low-ticket course teaches the complete system, there is no reason to pay for coaching.

### Mentioning High-Ticket Everywhere
Mention your high-ticket offer inside your micro-courses. At the end of every course: "If you want to keep working together, here's what I would suggest as the best next step." Offer both a course option and a coaching option.

## ABANDON CART SEQUENCE (GEORGI)

A separate but critical sequence. Converting just 15% of cart abandoners can dramatically change unit economics.

**Example math:** 100 visitors, 20 buy at CPA $250 each. Converting 15 more through abandon cart email = CPA drops to $143 per customer, potentially making a losing offer profitable.

### Six Reasons People Abandon (And How to Address Each)

1. **Don't fully believe solution solves their problem** -> Email with proof, testimonials, mechanism explanation
2. **Negative past experience with similar products** -> Email addressing "why this is different"
3. **Lack of confidence in themselves** -> Email with customer stories who started from same position
4. **Price perceived as too high** -> Email reframing value, not discounting. Or offer payment plan.
5. **Life gets in the way** -> Simple reminder email (easiest to convert -- they just need a nudge)
6. **Lack of urgency** -> Email with deadline, scarcity, or cost-of-inaction framing

## KEY BACKEND EMAIL TACTICS

### Discounting Structure
80% off with coupon codes works for backend sequences. A $997 product sold for $147 with a coupon is highly profitable because the customer was already acquired. Deep discounts on the front-end erode brand value, but deep discounts on the backend for existing customers are pure profit margin play.

**Why this is different from front-end discounting:** Hormozi's rule (free or full price, never discounted) applies to acquisition. Backend discounts apply to customers who already know the full value -- they are price-anchored.

### Urgency Mechanisms
- "Price will go up to $97 soon"
- "48-hour coupon"
- "Sale ends TONIGHT at 11:59PM EST"
- "For the next 72 hours only"
- "24 hours left"
- "Flash sale ends TONIGHT"

### Social Proof in Every Sequence
At least one customer success story with specific numbers per sequence. Numbers matter -- "made $50,000" is more compelling than "had great success."

### Ascending Prices Across Sequences
First backend under $100, second around $147-$247, third potentially higher. Each sequence acclimates them to spending more with you.
