## The 15 Factors That Determine Funnel Structure (Georgi)

Each factor rated 1-5. Higher total = shorter funnel needed. Lower total = longer funnel needed.

| # | Factor | 5 = (Less Copy Needed) | 1 = (More Copy Needed) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Market Awareness Level | Hyper aware (GLP-1) | Completely unaware niche |
| 2 | Demonstrable Proof | Instant visual proof (OxiClean) | Results take months, invisible |
| 3 | Price Point | Free/low cost | High ticket, heavy justification |
| 4 | Traffic Temperature | Warm email list | Stone cold traffic |
| 5 | Brand Equity | Coca-Cola recognition / celebrity | Unknown brand |
| 6 | Perceived Risk | Low risk, free trial | High risk, no reviews |
| 7 | Regulatory Compliance | No regulations | Heavy compliance (medical/financial) |
| 8 | Offer Complexity | One product, one price | Many options, decision paralysis |
| 9 | Importance of LTV | One-time purchase | Business built on recurring |
| 10 | Competitive Saturation | First-mover/unique | Saturated, jaded buyers |
| 11 | Emotional Complexity | Simple/routine purchase | Deeply emotional, identity-linked |
| 12 | Post-Purchase Behavior Change | No behavior change needed | Significant lifestyle change |
| 13 | Product Tangibility | Physical, visible | Abstract, intangible info product |
| 14 | Social Proof Availability | Thousands of reviews | No social proof |
| 15 | Purchase Frequency | Frequent repurchase | One-time lifetime purchase |

### Funnel Complexity Tiers Based on Score

**High score (above ~46):** Ad -> Product Page -> Cart/Checkout
- Example: GLP-1 telemedicine (high awareness, known solution)

**Mid score (~30-46):** Ad -> Quiz or Advertorial -> Landing Page -> Checkout
- Not necessarily requiring a VSL

**Low score (below ~30):** Ad -> Story/Pre-sell -> 35-45 min VSL -> Lander -> Checkout -> Email ascension
- Example: Unknown supplement brand in saturated market

## Platform Congruency Rules (Georgi)

Congruence = maintaining a consistent feeling and experience throughout the customer journey. Breaking congruence disrupts conversion.

| Platform | User Intent | Ad Format | Post-Click Destination |
|---|---|---|---|
| YouTube | Education/Entertainment | Video ads (educational/entertaining) | Page with another video |
| Native (Taboola/Outbrain) | Reading articles | Looks like news story links | Advertorial (reading mode) |
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | Consuming content | In-feed video or image | Advertorial, VSL, or PDP |
| TikTok | Short-form entertainment | UGC-style short videos | Product page or short VSL |
| Google | Active search intent | Text/shopping ads | Landing page or PDP (direct match) |

## Gusten's Conversion Equation

**Likelihood of purchase = (Desire + Credibility + Ability) / (Lack of Priority + Risk + Confusion)**

Your funnel architecture must increase the numerator and decrease the denominator at every stage. Each page in the funnel should move at least one of these levers.

## The New Conversion Funnel -- 6-Stage Model (Gusten)

Replaces AIDA with a more granular awareness journey:

1. **Opportunity Aware** -- "Did you know this is possible?"
2. **Problem Aware** -- "I understand your struggle"
3. **Process Aware** -- "Here's how the new method works"
4. **Source Aware** -- "Here's proof it works (me + others)"
5. **Offer Aware** -- "Here's exactly what you get"
6. **Priority Aware** -- "Here's why you should act NOW"

**Emotional journey mapped to stages:**
Desire -> Pain -> Hope -> Belief -> Risk -> Confidence -> Action

Each stage knocks down a domino of resistance. Map your funnel pages to these stages.

## Common 2025 Funnel Patterns (Georgi)

1. **Classic:** Ad -> VSL -> Checkout
2. **Advertorial Path:** Ad -> Advertorial -> VSL -> Checkout
3. **PDP Integration:** Ad -> Advertorial -> VSL -> Product Page -> Checkout (most common for 8-figure businesses)
4. **Direct to Product:** Ad -> Product Page -> Checkout (for high-awareness markets)

There's no single "right way" to sell. Many businesses struggling are trying to make outdated models work. The traditional video sales letter on a landing page often isn't the best choice anymore -- context and awareness level determine everything.

## Decision Tree: Which Funnel Type?

```
IF selling digital product under $100 -> Low Ticket / SLO Funnel
IF selling digital product $100-$2,000 -> Course Funnel
IF selling coaching/consulting $3,000-$25,000+ -> Application Funnel
IF selling physical product (DTC) -> Score with 15 factors, then route
IF selling SaaS -> SaaS Funnel
IF running a live event -> Paid Event Funnel
IF building an email list first -> Lead Generation Funnel
IF need to warm audience over days -> Challenge Funnel
IF need heavy education before sale -> VSL/Webinar Funnel
IF routing between multiple offers -> Homepage / Funnel Hub
IF front-end offer is $7-$27 after opt-in -> Tripwire Funnel
```

See references/funnel-types.md for complete details on each type.
