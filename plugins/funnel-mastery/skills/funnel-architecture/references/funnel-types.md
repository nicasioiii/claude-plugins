# Funnel Types -- Complete Blueprints (Primarily Gusten Sun)

---

## 1. Homepage / Funnel Hub

**Purpose:** Build authority and credibility; route visitors to appropriate funnels.

**Pages:** Homepage -> Freebie -> Confirmation -> (optional) Invitation -> Application/Order Form

**Key Sections:** Promise, Problem, Proof, Process (3 steps), About Us, Products/Offers, CTA

**Key insight:** Homepage is a FUNNEL HUB that routes to other funnels (lead, low ticket, application). Position yourself as an expert people APPLY to work with, not someone begging for clients.

---

## 2. Lead Generation Funnel

**Purpose:** Capture emails with free offer.

**Pages:** Opt-in Page -> Confirmation/Delivery Page -> (optional) Invitation -> Application/Order Form

**Conversion Benchmarks:**
- Cold traffic opt-in: 10-47% (industry standard ~10%, Gusten's best 47%)
- Warm traffic opt-in: 90%+

**Key strategies:**
- Test ultra-short opt-in (just headline + email + image, no scroll) vs. longer pre-qualifying page
- Short = more leads but broader; long = fewer but higher quality
- Confirmation page ALWAYS has next-step CTA: "Book a free call" or "Check out our [next offer]"

---

## 3. Low Ticket / SLO Funnel ($1-$100)

**Purpose:** Acquire customers profitably; self-liquidating offer.

**Pages:** Sales Page -> Order Form (or popup on page) -> Upsell 1 -> (optional) Upsell 2 -> Confirmation

**Flow math (Gusten):**
- 1,000 visitors, 3% buy at $9 = 30 customers ($270)
- 15% buy Upsell 1 at $47 = 5 customers ($235)
- 20% buy Upsell 2 at $197 = 1 customer ($197)
- Total: $658 from 1,000 visitors (AOV ~$22 per buyer)

**Key strategies:**
- Order form as POPUP on sales page converts better than separate page (less friction)
- One-click upsells charge same card (no re-entering payment)
- 1-2 upsells recommended; 3+ hurts completion rate (Gusten)
- "Wow bundle" strategy: massive value at stupidly cheap price ($37 for 37 funnel templates)
- Always have "No thanks" decline link on upsell pages

## 4. Tripwire Funnel ($7-$27)

**Purpose:** Convert free leads into buyers immediately after opt-in.

**Pages:** Opt-in -> Tripwire Offer Page -> Confirmation

**Distinction from low ticket:** Tripwire is offered IMMEDIATELY after free opt-in as a one-time opportunity. Low ticket is a standalone funnel.

---

## 5. VSL / Webinar Funnel

**Purpose:** Build deep relationship before presenting offer; automate high-ticket sales.

**Pages:** Opt-in -> Video/Training Page -> Application/Sales Page -> Confirmation

**Key advantages:**
- Automates relationship building 24/7 (replaces meeting 100 people individually)
- High-converting video plays as your salesperson
- Combine with sales page below video for people who prefer reading

**Strategy:**
- Curiosity-driven opt-in: "Free training reveals how to [outcome] without [struggle]"
- For $5K-$25K offers, don't show price -- use "Apply to work with us"
- Offer stack without price + "Apply before spots are gone"

---

## 6. Challenge Funnel (Free)

**Purpose:** Build community + nurture leads over 3-5 days before pitching paid offer.

**Pages:** Opt-in -> Confirmation (prep instructions) -> Bridge Pages (daily links) -> Sales Page -> Order Form -> Confirmation

**VIP Upsell Strategy:**
- Immediately after free registration, offer VIP upgrade ($27-$97)
- VIP gets: recordings, behind-the-scenes access, special badge on Zoom
- Social status element (golden VIP badge visible to others)

**Post-challenge pitch:**
- Sales page with full framework
- For sub-$100 offers, can add upsells after order form
- For $1,000-$2,000 offers, skip additional upsells

---

## 7. Paid Event Funnel

**Purpose:** Sell tickets to live/virtual event.

**Pages:** Sales Page -> Order Form -> Upsell (VIP) -> Confirmation

**Strategy:**
- Early bird pricing creates natural urgency (price increases as event approaches)
- Tony Robbins example: early bird discount -> gradual price increase
- What/When/Why close section at bottom of page

---

## 8. Course Funnel ($100-$2,000)

**Purpose:** Sell automated digital course.

**Pages:** Sales Page -> Order Form -> (optional Upsell) -> Confirmation

**Key distinction:** Fully automated sale AND delivery -- no calls needed.

**Strategy:** Sell the transformation, not the curriculum. Show student results, not module outlines. Nobody buys "12 modules and 47 videos." They buy "land your first 5 clients in 30 days."

---

## 9. Application Funnel (High Ticket: $5,000-$25,000+)

**Purpose:** Qualify leads before selling high-ticket.

**Pages:** Sales Page -> Application Form -> Calendar Booking -> Confirmation (with prep video)

**Key strategies:**
- Don't show price on sales page; show total value then "Apply to work with us"
- OR: show tons of results, process, big CTAs -- "Apply before spots are gone"
- Optional: $100 refundable deposit to book call (filters price shoppers)
- Pre-frame video on confirmation: "Here's how to prepare for your call" (positions you as expert)
- Warm traffic strategy: VSL on frontend -> email nurture -> send to application page

**Conversion math:** 1,000 visitors -> 7.5% book call (75 people) -> 3-5% close at $25K = $47K-$94K collected

---

## 10. SaaS Funnel

**Purpose:** Drive free trials or demo bookings for software.

**Pages:** Sales Page/Homepage -> Demo Booking OR Free Trial Signup -> Confirmation

**Key differences from info product funnels:**
- Show the UI/dashboard prominently in hero section
- "No credit card needed" / "Cancel anytime" above the fold
- Features framed as benefits: "All-in-one client portal SO THAT you can..."
- Comparison table: replace X tools with one platform
- Pricing tiers on the page (if applicable)
- Annual plan positioning: show monthly equivalent + savings

**Conversion math:** 1,000 visitors -> 7.5% trial (75) -> 2.5% buy annual at $799 = $7,000

## 2025 Funnel Patterns (Georgi)

### Pattern 1: Classic
Ad -> VSL -> Checkout
Still works for warm traffic and mid-awareness markets.

### Pattern 2: Advertorial Path
Ad -> Advertorial -> VSL -> Checkout
Required when traffic is cold and education is needed before the pitch.

### Pattern 3: PDP Integration
Ad -> Advertorial -> VSL -> Product Page -> Checkout
Most common architecture for 8-figure DTC businesses. Multiple touchpoints build belief.

### Pattern 4: Direct to Product
Ad -> Product Page -> Checkout
For high-awareness markets where the product category is well understood. Minimal education needed.

## Order Form Implementation Notes

**Popup vs. separate page:**
- Popup on sales page = less friction, higher conversion
- Separate page = faster page load, cleaner analytics
- Test both for your specific situation

**One-click upsells:** After initial payment, upsell pages charge the same card with one button click. No re-entering payment info. This is critical for upsell take rates.

**Platform recommendations (Katie):**
- ThriveCart: $500 one-time, excellent bump/upsell flow
- SamCart: Subscription-based, similar capability
- Both outperform GoHighLevel for checkout conversion
