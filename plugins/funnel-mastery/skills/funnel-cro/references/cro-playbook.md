# CRO Playbook

## TEN SPLIT TESTS -- DETAILED IMPLEMENTATION

### Test 1: Video vs. Image at Top of Page

**Hypothesis:** Video may outperform static image (or vice versa) as the hero element above the fold.

**Implementation:**
- Variant A: Sales page with hero image (product mockup or lifestyle shot)
- Variant B: Sales page with hero video (VSL preview, product demo, or testimonial reel)
- Also test video PLACEMENT: top (hero), middle (after empathy section), or sidebar

**What to measure:** Conversion rate, time on page, scroll depth.
**Expected outcome:** Video converts 2-4x better than photo in most cases (Gusten's data). However, images load faster and may win on mobile.

### Test 2: Headline A vs. Headline B

**Hypothesis:** Different angles in the headline attract different segments and convert differently.

**Implementation:**
- Choose two different angles from your research (e.g., convenience angle vs. results angle)
- Keep the rest of the page identical
- Example: UV water filter -- "Self-cleaning filter" angle vs. "Clips to any bag" convenience angle

**What to measure:** Conversion rate on the exact same traffic split.
**Critical insight:** In hook testing for ads, one hook generated 62% of all sales out of 4 tested. The planned hook generated ZERO sales. Headlines matter more than any other copy element.

### Test 3: Light Mode vs. Dark Mode

**Hypothesis:** Page color scheme affects conversion differently by niche.

**Implementation:**
- Variant A: Light background, dark text
- Variant B: Dark background, light text

**Guidelines:**
- Dark mode works well for: design, crypto, NFT, tech, gaming, luxury brands
- Light mode is easier to consume for: text-heavy pages, education, health, info products
- Test both regardless of assumptions

### Test 4: Order Form Placement

**Hypothesis:** Where and how the order form appears affects conversion rate.

**Implementation:**
- Variant A: Popup on the sales page (CTA opens modal)
- Variant B: Embedded sidebar (form visible alongside content)
- Variant C: Separate order form page (CTA navigates to new page)

**Key tradeoff:** Popup = less friction. Separate page = faster load. Test for your specific price point and audience.

### Test 5: Platform-Specific Landing Pages

**Hypothesis:** Customizing messaging by traffic source improves conversion.

**Implementation:**
- Create different URLs: /fb, /yt, /ig, /google
- Customize the hero image/video to match the platform experience
- Adjust headline tone (casual for social, professional for search)
- Add platform-specific social proof ("As featured on YouTube" for /yt traffic)

**Benefit:** Better tracking AND better conversion. You see which traffic source converts best AND you can optimize each page independently.

### Test 6: Short vs. Long Copy

**Hypothesis:** Optimal copy length depends on the offer and awareness level.

**Implementation:**
- Variant A: Short page (headline, brief benefits, CTA, testimonials, CTA)
- Variant B: Full 10-step sales page (all sections from the sales-page-copy framework)

**Guidelines:**
- Short works for: freebies (70-90% opt-in with just headline + button), low-ticket to warm traffic
- Long works for: health supplements with story-driven copy, cold traffic, complex offers
- Test BOTH regardless of assumptions

### Test 7: Price Testing

**Hypothesis:** Higher prices may outperform lower prices due to perceived value.

**Implementation:**
- Test $27 vs. $37 vs. $47 for the same offer
- Measure REVENUE PER VISITOR (not just conversion rate)

**Contrarian insight:** Sometimes $37 outperforms $7 because the perceived value is higher. People assume "you get what you pay for." A $7 product may trigger skepticism while a $37 product feels legitimate.

### Test 8: Upsell Page Length

**Hypothesis:** Ultra-short upsell pages may outperform longer ones (or vice versa).

**Implementation:**
- Variant A: Ultra-short -- "Wait! Add this for $47. [Yes / No thanks]"
- Variant B: Full upsell page with Katie's 6-part copy structure, offer stack, social proof, FAQ

**Note from Georgi:** Longer video upsells still work even after a long VSL. Do not assume short always wins.

### Test 9: Payment Plans

**Hypothesis:** Adding a payment plan option increases total revenue.

**Implementation:**
- Variant A: $1,000 one-time only
- Variant B: $1,000 one-time OR 3x $397

**Expected outcome:** Total revenue often increases because you capture buyers who would not have purchased at the full one-time price. Analytical buyers still take one-time. Emotional buyers choose monthly.

### Test 10: Testimonial Placement

**Hypothesis:** Where and how testimonials appear affects conversion.

**Implementation:**
- Variant A: All testimonials in one section (traditional)
- Variant B: Small testimonial above the fold + detailed testimonials scattered near CTAs throughout
- Variant C: Screenshot testimonials + video testimonials mixed

**Best practice:** Sprinkle throughout the page, not just one section. Specific testimonials matching your claims work best (if you promise weight loss, testimonials should mention weight loss).

## COMPOUND TESTING MATH

### Baseline Example
- 50,000 product page views
- 10% add-to-cart rate = 5,000
- 35% begin checkout = 1,750
- 60% complete checkout = 1,050
- 2.10% overall conversion rate
- $100 AOV = $105,000/month revenue

### Scenario 1: Single Improvement
+10% add-to-cart improvement (10% -> 11%)
- 5,500 add-to-cart -> 1,925 checkout -> 1,155 purchases
- Revenue: $115,500 (+$10,500/month)

### Scenario 2: Compound Improvements
+10% ATC + 5% cart + 5% checkout
- 5,500 add-to-cart -> 2,021 checkout -> 1,273 purchases
- Revenue: $127,300 (+$22,300/month)
- Total improvement: 21.28% (multiplicative, not 20% additive)

### Scenario 3: Compound + AOV
+10% ATC + 5% cart + 5% checkout + 20% AOV ($100 -> $120)
- Revenue: $152,760 (+$47,760/month)

**The compounding effect means that testing across MULTIPLE touchpoints simultaneously yields far greater results than optimizing one touchpoint at a time.**

## CRO AUDIT CHECKLIST

### For Each Funnel Touchpoint, Check:

**Sales Page / Landing Page:**
- [ ] Headline clearly states outcome + timeframe
- [ ] Social proof visible above the fold
- [ ] CTA button high-contrast and above the fold
- [ ] Benefits stated as outcomes (not features)
- [ ] Guarantee prominently displayed
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds
- [ ] Mobile experience is smooth (no horizontal scroll, thumb-friendly CTAs)
- [ ] No competing navigation elements

**Checkout:**
- [ ] Trust badges near payment fields
- [ ] Guarantee restated near submit button
- [ ] Product image visible in order summary
- [ ] "No subscription" text if applicable
- [ ] Payment plans available
- [ ] One-click upsell supported

**Upsell Pages:**
- [ ] Hook headline asks the question they are thinking
- [ ] Page is SHORT (not long-form)
- [ ] Product mockup visible
- [ ] "Yes" button clearly displays price
- [ ] "No thanks" link at the very bottom
- [ ] For upsell 3+: "You're almost done" messaging

**Order Bump:**
- [ ] Describes what the product DOES (not just the name)
- [ ] Price is $27-$37
- [ ] Same topic as main product (no mind-disjointing)
- [ ] 4-part copy structure present

## GETTING TESTIMONIALS

### Facebook Group Challenge Method
1. Create a challenge inside your community (Facebook group, Slack, Discord)
2. Tie the challenge to product usage (e.g., "Use the system for 7 days")
3. Entry requirement: submit a video testimonial sharing their experience
4. Give guidelines, NOT scripts (scripted testimonials sound fake)

### Testimonial Guidelines (Not Scripts)
Give them these prompts:
- "Share how much you [metric] before and after"
- "How did you feel before? How do you feel after?"
- "What specifically helped you the most?"
- "Would you recommend this to a friend? Why?"

### For Women's Products (Georgi's Framing)
"If you share your story, you're going to help more women." This framing drives participation without monetary incentives. People want to help others who are in the same position they were in.

### Testimonial Visual Format
- Photo of the person + headline quote + short video = most effective combination
- Screenshot testimonials from social media also work well (feel authentic)
- Mix formats: some text screenshots, some video, some photo + quote

## DISCOUNT RETARGETING METHOD -- DETAILED (GUSTEN)

### Strategy Overview
Run a discount ad ONLY to warm audience (people who already know your product and price). This is NOT a cold traffic strategy.

### Setup
1. Create a retargeting audience: (a) anyone who watched 75%+ of any video ad in past year AND (b) anyone who signed up for webinar/opted in for lead magnet in past 3 months
2. Exclude current customers (upload email list AND add thank-you page visitors for redundancy)
3. No geographic or age restrictions on retargeting (audience is already qualified)

### Creative
- Photo ad outperformed video (the "$300 OFF" sign was harder to ignore than a video)
- Simple, clear, bold text overlay showing the discount amount
- Include a deadline

### Budget and Monitoring
- Budget: $30/day (low to avoid exhausting the warm audience)
- Monitor FREQUENCY metric:
  - 2-3 = healthy
  - Above 5 = wasting money showing to same people
  - Above 7-10 = turn off the ad

### Discount Amount
- Keep smaller than Black Friday discount to preserve BF momentum
- If BF = $400 off, retarget discount = $300 off
- The discount must feel meaningful but not devalue the brand

### Sustainability Requirement
Must have other ads continuously bringing NEW leads into the funnel. The retarget audience is finite and will exhaust without fresh traffic feeding the top of funnel.

### Results
- Added $100K+/month to one course
- Added $40K+/month to another
- Almost doubled weekly revenue and sustained it for 2+ months (unprecedented vs. typical Black Friday spike-then-crash)
