## The Three Numbers That Matter (Hormozi)

Every funnel's health comes down to three metrics:

1. **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** -- All costs to acquire one new customer. Includes ad spend, media buyer salary, creative team, software, sales commissions, manager salaries. This is fully loaded, not just ad spend.
2. **GP (Gross Profit)** -- Price minus variable costs (COGS). Excludes rent, admin, utilities. This is per-customer, not total.
3. **PPD (Payback Period)** -- How many days until cumulative gross profit exceeds CAC. The speed dimension.

**Goal:** Low CAC + High GP + Short PPD = more money, faster.

---

## LTGP:CAC Ratio -- The Viability Test

- **Minimum viable:** 3:1 (lifetime gross profit to CAC)
- Below 3:1 = unworkable business model
- At 3:1 = survivable but fragile
- **Target:** Much higher (Hormozi achieved 31:1+ at gyms, 100:1 at Gym Launch year one)

**Critical anti-pattern (Hormozi):** Never use revenue instead of gross profit in this ratio. A meals company had 5:1 revenue-to-CAC but only 1:1 LTGP-to-CAC because gross margins were 20%. They were fooling themselves with the wrong number.

## Fully Loaded CAC by Channel (Hormozi)

**Formula:** Total acquisition costs / Number of new customers

| Channel | Typical Components | Example CAC |
|---|---|---|
| Outbound | Emailer salary + software + commissions | $500 |
| Content | Media team salary + commissions | $1,100 |
| Paid Ads | Media buyer + ad spend + commissions + software | $3,500 |

**Primary levers to reduce CAC (80/20):**
1. Better offers (higher response rates)
2. Better offers (higher conversion rates)
3. Better offers (lower CPMs from engagement)

**Secondary levers (rarely worth touching):** Cheaper labor, cheaper software. Order-of-magnitude improvements come from offers, not cost-cutting.

## The 4 Vital Questions (Georgi)

For any funnel, answer these:
1. How much does it cost to acquire a customer? (CPA/CAC)
2. How much do you gross on Day 0? (AOV)
3. What is cost to fulfill? (COGS)
4. What is gross profit? (GM = AOV - CPA - COGS)

---

## Gross Margin Targets

- **Service businesses:** 80%+ gross margin (yields ~30% net after overhead)
- **Physical products/supplements:** Variable, but watch COGS closely
- **Digital products/info:** Near 0% COGS, so gross margin approaches 100%

**The arms race (Hormozi):** The business that makes each customer worth more can outspend everyone on advertising. You can only reduce costs to zero, but you can increase customer value infinitely. This creates an ethical monopoly.

---

## AOV Targets by Funnel Type

- **Low-ticket funnel:** Minimum $70 AOV (Katie). Below this, paid ads are unlikely to be profitable.
- **SLO (Self-Liquidating Offer):** Break-even on front-end, profit on backend. AOV must equal or exceed CPA.
- **High-ticket:** AOV inherently high; focus shifts to close rate and CPA per application.

---

## CPA Optimization Levers in Priority Order (Georgi)

**Lower CPA:** Better ads (higher CTR, lower CPM) > Higher landing page conversion rate > Alternate traffic sources > Abandon cart recovery > Branded keywords > Organic halo

**Lower COGS:** Different fulfillment partners > Negotiate shipping/products > Lower merchant fees

**Increase AOV:** AOV money close in VSL > Order bumps > Better upsells > Price split tests > Add packages

**Increase LTV:** Add subscription/continuity > Reduce churn > Email list monetization > Phone/text sales

---

## When to Use Revenue vs. Gross Profit

**Always use gross profit** for LTGP:CAC and break-even calculations. Revenue lies. A $500 sale with 20% margin is only $100 gross profit. Optimizing for revenue leads to scaling losses.
