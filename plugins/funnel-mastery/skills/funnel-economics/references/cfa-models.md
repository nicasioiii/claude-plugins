# Client-Financed Acquisition -- The Three Levels (Hormozi)

## Level 1: Basic Business Viability

**Threshold:** LTGP > CAC (lifetime gross profit exceeds acquisition cost)

- Minimum 3:1 ratio
- This just means you have a viable business -- it does NOT mean you have a growth engine
- Most businesses operate here and grow slowly because they rely on "marketing budgets"

**Hormozi's position:** Having a "marketing budget" is a symptom of a broken money model. If your economics are right, customers fund their own acquisition.

---

## Level 2: Free Growth

**Threshold:** 30-Day GP > CAC (first month gross profit exceeds acquisition cost)

- You never spend your own money to grow
- Why 30 days? Every business can borrow money interest-free for 30 days (credit cards)
- Spend on credit card, collect from customer, pay off card, repeat
- **The constraint shifts from advertising to operations** -- you have more customers than you can handle

**The mechanism:**
1. Spend $5,000 on credit card for ads
2. Acquire customers who generate $6,000+ GP within 30 days
3. Pay off credit card
4. Reinvest profits into more ads
5. Repeat

---

## Level 3: Compounding Growth (Hormozi's Target)

**Threshold:** 30-Day GP > 2x CAC (first month gross profit is DOUBLE acquisition cost)

- One customer pays back your spend AND pays for the next customer
- Doubling happens per customer, not per month -- can be daily
- This is exponential growth limited only by market size and operations

## The 56x Story

Hormozi consulted with a competitor selling the **same number of units per month**:
- Competitor: $300K/year profit
- Hormozi: $17M/year profit
- **56x difference -- entirely from gross profit per customer**

The discrepancy was not in lead generation or close rates. It was in how much each customer was worth after the initial sale. This is the arms race.

## How Hormozi Broke the Gym Industry

### Old Way (Industry Standard "LBO Model")
10 walk-ins -> 8 buy $19-29 trial -> 3 convert at 35% trial rate
- **30-day gross profit: $600**
- Revenue per show-up: $60

### Hormozi's Money Model
10 walk-ins -> sell the goal (mix of $500/$1K/$2K packages) + upsell supplements ($85 GP avg) + meals upsell + annual membership conversion + prepayment offers
- **30-day gross profit: $9,644**
- Revenue per show-up: $960

**Result: Could outspend competitors 25:1 on advertising.** When you outspend 25:1, you get every customer in the market.

### Timeline
- **Day 0:** Attraction offer + core sale + supplements
- **Day 2-7:** Meals upsell
- **Day 14-21:** Continuity conversion (downsell the upsell into membership)
- **Day 21-28:** Prepayment offer on continuity

### The Downsell Flow (for no-sales)
Service -> smaller package -> free trial with penalty -> free trial no commitment -> free orientation

Even complete no-sales got funneled: free nutritional orientation -> sell $300 in supplements. Non-service buyers who bought supplements spent MORE ($300 avg) than service buyers who also got supplements ($200 avg). They wanted the "magic pill," not work. Always have the magic pill available.

## The Contrarian Position on Marketing Budgets

Hormozi considers a "marketing budget" to be a sign that the business model is broken. If you need to set aside a fixed amount for marketing, it means your customers are not paying for their own acquisition fast enough.

The alternative: build a money model where every dollar spent on acquisition returns 2+ dollars within 30 days. Then your "budget" is unlimited -- constrained only by operational capacity.

---

## The Anti-Pattern: "You Need Money to Make Money"

Hormozi's direct rebuttal: "You need to have more skills. Somebody who has fewer skills needs more money." Skills in offer design, pricing psychology, upsell structuring, and sales compress the gap between CAC and 30-day GP. Money compensates for missing skills, not the reverse.
