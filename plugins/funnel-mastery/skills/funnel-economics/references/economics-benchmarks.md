## Conversion Rate Impact -- Georgi's Real Example

Starting point: 1.04% conversion rate at $3K/day ad spend

| Conversion Rate | CPA | Net per Customer | Status |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.04% | $300 | -$146 | Losing money |
| 2.08% (2x improvement) | $150 | +$5 | Barely break-even |
| 2.50% | $125 | +$30 | Profitable at scale |

**Key insight:** The relationship between conversion rate improvement and profitability is exponential, not linear. Doubling conversion rate halves CPA. A 2.4x improvement in conversion rate turned a $146/customer loss into a $30/customer profit.

## Katie's AOV Data (Low-Ticket Digital)

**Make Your Audience Go Crazy funnel ($44 front-end, ~6,000 customers):**
- Bump ($37): 50%+ take rate (exceptional)
- Upsell 1 ($67): 29% take rate
- Upsell 2 ($77): 10%
- Upsell 3 ($197): 1.2% (low but AOV still healthy)
- **Achieved AOV: $99-$103**

**Video Ads course funnel (125 customers measured):**
- Bump ($37): 37% take rate
- $197 upsell: 15% (excellent)
- One upsell: 1% (needs replacement)
- **Achieved AOV: $105**
- **Potential with fixes: $125**

**The $70 AOV rule:** If your AOV is below $70 with at least 30 customers, this is an urgent problem. Fix it immediately using Position/Price/Switch framework. If AOV is at $70+, optimize incrementally -- do not burn anything down.

## Gusten's SLO Math

Self-Liquidating Offer model:
- $5/click, $50/order (using 10-step sales page)
- Average order value through upsells = $50 (break-even on ad spend)
- Backend client acquired for FREE because funnel breaks even
- $3,000/day ad spend = 60 customers/day (break-even) + 1 backend client/day ($3,000 pure profit)
- Plus: 60 customers/day building email list for cross-selling, affiliate commissions, recurring revenue

**The math that works:**
1,000 visitors to low-ticket funnel:
- 3% buy at $9 = 30 customers ($270)
- 15% buy Upsell 1 at $47 = 5 customers ($235)
- 20% buy Upsell 2 at $197 = 1 customer ($197)
- Total: $658 from 1,000 visitors (AOV ~$22 per buyer)

## LTV Calculation and Churn Impact

**Formula:** LTV = Price / Monthly Churn Rate

| Monthly Price | Churn Rate | LTV | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| $100 | 10% | $1,000 | Baseline |
| $100 | 5% | $2,000 | 2x from 5% churn reduction |
| $100 | 3% | $3,333 | 3.3x from 7% churn reduction |

Hormozi: "If you want big LTGP, conquer churn."

## The Big Head Long Tail Model (Hormozi)

**Structure:** $1,000 down + $50/month vs. $200/month flat

Why it reduces churn:
- Big upfront payment = customer "earned" the low ongoing rate
- Psychological ownership of the deal prevents cancellation
- People updated credit cards before expiration to keep their $10/month rate
- Low ongoing price = low churn = higher total LTV than higher flat monthly

## Education Business Churn Problem

The value of education DIMINISHES after learning. Day before learning arithmetic = infinitely valuable. Day after = worthless.

**Fixes:**
1. Sell bulk/annual payments upfront (capture value before it diminishes)
2. Split into one-time value (install sales system) + consumable value (monthly coaching, new content)
3. Price continuity based on CONSUMABLE value only, not blended

## Georgi's Optimization Lever Priority

In order of impact:

1. **Lower CPA** (biggest lever -- better ads, higher CR on landing page)
2. **Increase AOV** (bumps, upsells, package push)
3. **Increase LTV** (continuity, churn reduction, email monetization)
4. **Lower COGS** (smallest lever -- negotiate fulfillment, shipping, fees)

Higher conversion rate = lower CPA = more scale AND more profit. The relationship is exponential.

## The Complete Money Model Definition (Hormozi)

"A good money model makes more profit from a customer than it costs to get and service them in the first 30 days. A $100M money model makes more profit from ONE customer than it costs to get and service MANY customers in the first 30 days."

## Diagnostic: When Metrics Indicate a Problem

| Signal | Likely Cause | First Action |
|---|---|---|
| LTGP:CAC below 3:1 | Fundamental model problem | Redesign offer stack |
| 30-day GP < CAC | No self-funding growth | Add upsells, raise prices, or reduce CPA |
| AOV below $70 (low-ticket) | Insufficient upsells or bad pricing | Position/Price/Switch framework |
| CPA climbing month over month | Ad fatigue or market saturation | New creatives, new angles, new traffic |
| High churn on continuity | Pricing mismatch or value decay | Big Head Long Tail or consumable split |
| Backend underperforming | Sequence issue or offer-market mismatch | Audit backend sequences |
