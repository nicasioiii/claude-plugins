# Audience Psychology

## Gusten's 9 Pre-Funnel Questions

Before building ANY funnel, answer these. This is the quick-start research framework for markets you already understand.

1. **WHO** -- Specific person, not a broad category. Not "people who want weight loss" but "entrepreneur dads with purchase power who value fitness for ROI." Must have: deep pockets, trending market, findable online, problem-solution aware.

2. **WHAT** -- What transformation are you selling? The outcome, not the product. "Help funnel builders charge more and land 5-10K dream clients."

3. **EVEN IF** -- What objections do they have? "Even if they're brand new with no portfolio" / "Even if they've failed before" / "Even if they don't have a big audience."

4. **WHY** -- What are the biggest problems? Why does it matter? Pain points AND their life consequences.

5. **HOW** -- What's the vehicle/mechanism? "Never-skip-dessert fitness protocol" / "YouTube funnel for real estate agents."

6. **WITHOUT** -- What old thing doesn't work? "Without cold calling, Fiverr proposals, or expensive software."

7. **BENEFITS** -- Feature -> Benefit -> Impact. "Coaching -> Learn skills -> Feel confident closing clients."

8. **WHERE** -- Where do you start serving them? Application? Free challenge? Watch this video?

9. **WHEN** -- When can they start? Challenge starts [date]; book a call this week.

## Three Categories of Belief to Overcome (Gusten)

Every prospect has three types of beliefs blocking purchase. Your funnel must address all three.

### 1. Product-Based Beliefs
"Does this even work?"
- "Are funnels right for my business?"
- "Does keto work?"
- "Ads don't work anymore"

**Offer hack:** Demo the vehicle/method with proof. Case studies, before/after, live demonstrations.

### 2. People-Based Beliefs
"Can I actually do this?"
- "I'm not good enough"
- "I'm not a designer"
- "My wife says I always fail"
- "The last course was terrible"

**Offer hack:** Templates, AI tools, plug-and-play resources. Remove the skill requirement. Make it so easy that personal ability becomes irrelevant.

### 3. Priority-Based Beliefs
"Is now the right time?"
- "I'm too busy"
- "I can't afford it"
- "I told my wife I'd stop investing"
- "Do I have the energy?"

**Offer hack:** Automation, speed, convenience. "Even with just 20 minutes per day." Show the cost of inaction.

## The Conversion Equation (Gusten)

**Likelihood of purchase = (Desire + Credibility + Ability) / (Lack of Priority + Risk + Confusion)**

**Increase the numerator:**
- **Desire:** Sell a bigger, more valued outcome
- **Credibility:** Trust you AND your process (source + vehicle proof)
- **Ability:** Confidence they can succeed (templates, support, step-by-step)

**Decrease the denominator:**
- **Priority:** Show cost of inaction; make it easy to start NOW
- **Risk:** Guarantee, free trial, payment plans, testimonials
- **Confusion (Clarity):** FAQ, clear next steps, what/when/why summary

## 12-Step Purchase Decision Checklist (Gusten)

Every sales page must get the prospect to internally agree with ALL 12:

1. I want this outcome and value it a lot
2. I acknowledge I have a problem I want solved ASAP
3. I believe this solution will solve my problem
4. I trust you're the right person to help me
5. It makes sense why I haven't solved this myself yet
6. Your process is better than what I've tried before
7. Others have tried this and gotten results (gives me hope)
8. Financially, this is more valuable than the price
9. I'm willing to make time for this
10. Risk of inaction is bigger than risk of trying
11. I understand the next steps
12. I feel urgency to solve this NOW

If your page is not converting, audit it against this checklist. The missing "yes" is where the page fails.

## The Domino Statement Principle (Gusten)

Sell the IDEA before the offer. If you can get the prospect to believe in one core idea (the "domino"), every other objection falls.

**Structure:** "If [core belief is true], then [everything else follows]."

Example: "If you can build one high-converting funnel, you'll never worry about leads again." Once they accept that one statement, the course/coaching/software is just the vehicle to make it happen.

## Customer Persona Trigger Language

**Use "When" not "If":**
- Wrong: "If you struggle with weight loss..."
- Right: "When you step on the scale and the number hasn't budged..."

"When" assumes the experience is shared. "If" creates distance.

**Positive persona triggers:** "If you're the kind of person who..." + aspirational identity trait. Creates in-group identification.

**Negative persona triggers:** Name the enemy or the old way. "If you're tired of gurus telling you to post 3x a day..." Creates shared frustration and positions you as the alternative.

## Language and Communication Frameworks by Market Type (Georgi)

### BizOpp/Financial Freedom Market
**USE:** Financial freedom, escape the rat race, be your own boss, take back control, build your own empire, smart money moves, leverage, cash flow, passive income, proven system

**AVOID:** Get rich quick, guru, secret formula, guaranteed success, easy money, no work required, overnight success, overly hype-driven language, corporate jargon

### Adapting Messaging to Psychological Profiles

**If market believes wealth is unfairly distributed:** "Finally, you can level the playing field and access the secret that greedy 1% billionaires have been keeping from the rest of us."

**If market believes in meritocracy:** "The same secret that Elon Musk and Steve Jobs used to become billionaires is something anyone can use to start a profitable side hustle if you're willing to put in the work."

**For women 45+ seeking skincare:** Acknowledge prejudice against younger beauty counter reps. Position as "Created by women who understand mature skin because we're living it too."

## Opportunity Selling vs. Improvement Selling (Gusten)

**Wrong (improvement):** "Better funnel builder" / "Write better copy" / "Get stronger"

**Right (new opportunity):** "AI system that writes for you" / "White-label your own SaaS platform" / "Data-driven custom supplements based on YOUR blood test"

New opportunities bypass skepticism because the person hasn't failed at THIS specific thing before. The moment you say "better," they recall every past failure and their walls go up.

Learned from Russell Brunson and Dan Kennedy. Visual technique: create a comparison table showing "How most people do it" (crossed out pain points) vs. "Our method" (checkmarks).

## Instructor Disagreement: AIDA Framework

**Georgi** uses AIDA (Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action) as foundational context for funnel copy and sees it as still valid.

**Gusten** declares AIDA dead and replaces it with his 6-stage awareness model (Opportunity Aware -> Problem Aware -> Process Aware -> Source Aware -> Offer Aware -> Priority Aware).

**Resolution:** Both agree that awareness levels matter and that prospects move through stages. Gusten's model is more granular and maps better to modern funnel stages. Use Schwartz awareness levels (from Georgi) as the canonical research framework. Use Gusten's emotional journey mapping (Desire -> Pain -> Hope -> Belief -> Risk -> Confidence -> Action) as a complementary layer when designing the page flow.
