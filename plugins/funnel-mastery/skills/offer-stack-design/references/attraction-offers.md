# Attraction Offers -- 6 Types (Hormozi)

Attraction offers exist to LIQUIDATE CAC -- break even on customer acquisition so everything after is profit.

---

## Type 1: Win Your Money Back

**Hormozi's #1 money-maker ever.**

**Structure:** Set a goal for the customer. Tell them how to reach it. If they reach it, they get money back (or store credit).

**Three versions:**
1. **Results-based:** Lose 20 pounds = money back (regardless of compliance)
2. **Action-based:** Attend all sessions, log progress, do homework = money back (regardless of results)
3. **Both:** Must follow directions AND hit goal

**Results:** 78% success rate of people hitting their goal.

**Pro tip:** Tie required actions to things that (a) advertise your business and (b) get them the best results. This turns onboarding into a revenue generator instead of a cost center.

**The real money:** Don't wait until end of program to sell more. Upsell DURING the program (supplements, continuity, coaching add-ons). The challenge becomes a high-cash, highly-choreographed onboarding.

**Adaptation by business type:**
- Gym: "Lose 20 lbs in 6 weeks or your money back"
- Course: "Implement all modules and land 3 clients or your money back"
- SaaS: "Hit X metric in 90 days or refund"
- Coaching: "Follow the system for 12 weeks; if no results, full credit toward next program"

## Type 2: Free Giveaways

**Structure:** Advertise a chance to win a big prize in exchange for contact information. Pick a winner. Offer everyone else the big prize at a discounted price.

**Works because:** People opted in for the prize, so they already want it. Offering it at a discount to non-winners converts a percentage who almost won and are emotionally invested.

---

## Type 3: Decoy Offers (Most Flexible -- Works for Any Business)

**Structure:** Advertise free/discounted thing. When lead shows up, present TWO options:
- **Decoy (free/cheap):** Stripped-down version (1 workout/week, at-home course, no guarantee)
- **Premium:** Full version (unlimited workouts, personalized nutrition, guarantee, accountability)

**Target:** 70-80% should take the premium version.

**How to make the decoy:**
- Fewer components
- Lower quality delivery
- Less support/access
- No guarantee
- Less personalization

**Key:** Advertise the RESULT, not the vehicle. "Free 21-day transformation" -- not "free 3 workouts." This lets you choose the vehicle and pricing in the sales presentation.

**Tanning Salon Example (John's Model):**
- $5 VIP 5-day pass distributed on car windshields
- "Turkey talk" -- you can't get tanned in 5 sessions without burning
- Decoy: $25/session OR premium: $9.99/month unlimited membership
- Everyone saw the value and converted to recurring membership

---

## Type 4: Buy X Get Y Free

Offer free stuff in exchange for buying stuff. Higher value of free items = more people buy X.

**Implementation:** Calculate the marginal cost of the free item. If the free item costs you $5 but increases conversion by 20%, the math is obvious.

---

## Type 5: Pay Less Now or Pay More Later

Give choice: pay full price later OR discounted price now with additional benefits. Creates urgency through ascending price.

**Example:** "Early bird: $497 this week. $697 next week. $997 at the door."

---

## Type 6: Free With Consumption (Not in the Book)

Give away massive free value first. The more expensive your eventual offer, the longer and more free stuff you give them first. This is the "free content to high-ticket" model.

**Examples:**
- YouTube videos (free) -> Community (free) -> $18K advisory upsell (Hormozi's own model)
- Blog posts (free) -> Email course (free) -> $2K program
- Podcast (free) -> Free challenge -> $5K coaching

## The "Free Brings Broke People" Myth -- BUSTED

**Hormozi's 20-gym split test:**
- 10 locations used "free" in ads
- 10 locations didn't use "free"
- **Result:** Close rate was IDENTICAL between free and non-free leads
- Best salespeople closed both groups equally
- Bad salespeople couldn't close either group
- **Only difference:** Cost per lead was much lower for "free" group
- Therefore "free" group made MORE total sales at the same close rate

**Conclusion:** Rich people also like free stuff. The only thing that changed was lead cost. "If I had only one offer to make to convert, or my family would be killed, it would be a free offer."

## Create Flow -> Monetize -> Add Friction (Hormozi)

The three-step advertising framework:

### Step 1: Create Flow
Give away the big sexy thing. Get maximum eyeballs and clicks. Do not worry about quality of leads yet. Volume first.

### Step 2: Monetize
Make sure you're converting the right people and getting paid. Validate that the flow produces buyers, not just leads.

### Step 3: Add Friction
Only AFTER you have profitable flow, start adding qualifiers and filters:
- Qualification questions
- Number of steps in the process
- Targeting criteria
- Force content consumption before next step
- Increase ad length

**Critical principle:** You cannot optimize an empty funnel. You cannot optimize zero clicks. Get flow first, then optimize.

---

## Friction Mechanisms (for When Volume is Too High)

- Qualification questions on opt-in
- Multi-step application process
- Tighter targeting criteria
- Required content consumption before next step
- Longer ad copy (self-selects serious prospects)
- Deposit requirement for calls
- Pre-frame video before booking

## The Rental Car Money Model (Perfect Example)

Reserved online for $19/day. Walked out paying $400 for 3 days.

Sequential upsells:
1. **Vehicle upgrade:** "We don't have your reservation car. Is a Tundra okay?" ($19 -> $69/day)
2. **Late checkout:** Half-day fee to avoid made-up penalty
3. **Insurance (implied sale):** "Do you want to UPGRADE your insurance?" (implies base included)
4. **Prepaid gas:** $3.75/gal vs. $3.50 market, BUT prepay the entire tank. Unused gas = pure profit
5. **Damage fee:** Implicit upsell -- scratch it, you pay
6. **Late return fee:** Another invented penalty

**Key insight:** They CREATE fees to AVOID fees. The prepaid gas "to avoid the refueling surcharge" is a fee created to avoid a fee they also created. The naming and framing makes each offer feel protective rather than extractive.
