# Copy Frameworks, Domino Statements & Brief 2.0

## THE 4 TYPES OF COPY (Gusten Sun)

1. **Sales copy** -- Funnel pages (trust + priority). Goal: get them to buy NOW.
2. **Email copy** -- Sequences and broadcasts (nurture + sell). Goal: maintain relationship and drive repeat purchases.
3. **Content copy** -- Social media posts (attract + engage). Goal: build audience and authority.
4. **Ad copy** -- Paid advertising (hook + convert). Goal: stop the scroll and drive a click.

A full-stack funnel builder masters all four. Each type has different rules, different pacing, and different psychological goals.

---

## 3 FUNNEL COPY TYPES (Gusten Sun)

Each funnel page type has a distinct psychological objective:

| Page Type | Primary Objectives | Copy Style |
|---|---|---|
| **Opt-in pages** | Intrigue + desire | Hook to download free thing; minimal copy; promise of value |
| **Sales pages** | Trust + priority | Believe in the person/offer + act now; longest copy |
| **Application pages** | Authority + exclusivity | Must apply, not everyone gets in; scarcity of access |

**Routing rule:** Match your copy style to the page type. An opt-in page written like a sales page will underperform (too much friction). A sales page written like an opt-in will underperform (not enough trust-building).

## THE DOMINO STATEMENT

**Core principle:** Sell the IDEA before the OFFER.

"Once they're sold on the process and outcome, they want the vehicle ASAP." If someone believes the bridge works, the offer is just the boat to cross it faster.

### How to Use Domino Statements

1. **Identify the core belief** your prospect must hold for your product to be the obvious choice
2. **State that belief as a fact** in your copy, then prove it
3. **Only THEN introduce the offer** as the vehicle to act on that belief

**Example flow:**
- Domino belief: "The real reason your funnel isn't converting is that you're selling improvement, not a new opportunity."
- Proof: Case studies, mechanism explanation, before/after
- Offer: "This is exactly what the Funnel Mastery system teaches you to build."

**The key insight (Russell Brunson via Gusten):** "Sell me this pen" misses the point. Don't sell the pen -- sell the social status the pen gives you. Don't sell the course -- sell the transformation the course enables.

## PERSONA TRIGGERS

### Positive Triggers (Future-Pacing)
Paint the picture of life AFTER the transformation:
- "Imagine waking up to Stripe notifications every morning"
- "Picture yourself confidently walking into that boardroom"
- "What would it feel like to look in the mirror and love what you see?"

### Negative Triggers (Pain Amplification)
Name the specific pain they're living with:
- "The shame you feel when you say you should spend more time with family but you're stressed at work"
- "That sinking feeling when another month passes and your revenue is flat"
- "The frustration of watching competitors succeed while you're stuck"

### The "When" Not "If" Rule
- WRONG: "If you implement this system..."
- RIGHT: "When you land your first $5K client..."

"When" assumes the outcome is certain. "If" introduces doubt. Always frame future outcomes with "when" in sales copy.

## THE BRIEF 2.0 -- 25-POINT TEMPLATE (Stefan Georgi)

Before writing ANY sales page, VSL, or advertorial, complete this brief. The more detail you provide, the stronger your copy will be. This is also the primary document to feed to AI tools for copy generation.

### The 25 Points

1. **Who's the Audience?** -- Demographics, psychographics, awareness level
2. **What are the pain points or biggest fears?** -- Identify 1-3 HUGE ones; rest are secondary. Include both short-term AND long-term fears.
3. **What are the big promises about the copy?** -- Promises about what they'll GET from engaging with this content (not product promises -- copy promises)
4. **What does it look like when pain points are solved?** -- Physical, emotional, social, psychological benefits of the transformation
5. **What solutions exist already and why aren't they working?** -- The competitive landscape and failure points
6. **What is the UMP and UMS?** -- Unique Mechanism of the Problem and Solution. Include enough detail for an LLM to write the full section.
7. **Is there a unique nickname for the mechanism?** -- e.g., "7 second bowel releasing ritual," "30 second breakfast hack," "Paper Flipping" for real estate arbitrage
8. **(Optional, For Supplements) Main ingredients** + 2-3 studies per ingredient showing efficacy
9. **Bold product claims** -- Specific promises about results from using the product
10. **What is the product?** -- Features, benefits, fascinations, curiosity bullets. More detail = better.
11. **Background story + story of discovery** -- Hero's journey: spokesperson in pain -> quest for answer -> discovers mechanism -> existing solutions don't work
12. **Who is the main speaker?** -- Quick snapshot + credibility markers
13. **Is there a second speaker or expert?**
14. **Does the spokesperson partner with a brand/company?**
15. **List of testimonials or social proof**
16. **Credibility builders for the opening** -- studies, endorsements, media features
17. **Normal retail price**
18. **Actual price for viewers**
19. **Multiple options/packages and pricing for each**
20. **What is the guarantee?**
21. **Are there any bonuses?**
22. **Any other scarcity elements?**
23. **Specific future-paced results and benefits**
24. **Common objections + how to answer them**
25. **Specific hooks or big ideas to test in the lead**

## RESEARCH PROMPTS FOR COPY (Stefan Georgi)

### Market Awareness Assessment Prompt

Use this before writing to understand where your audience sits:

```
I want your help evaluating the level of market awareness for a product
in [YOUR NICHE]. The specific product is [YOUR PRODUCT]. I would like
for you to deep dive to help me understand the current level of market
awareness for this product in [GEOGRAPHY].

For the intents of this exercise, let's not consider "product-aware" or
"most-aware" to be our specific product and brand. Instead, let's consider
it to be awareness about similar and nearly identical products and brands.

Please provide an overview of the estimated TAM and a rough estimate of
what percentage falls into each category of product market awareness.
```

### Ingredient Deep Research Prompt (When Mechanism Is Known)

```
These are the ingredients for [PRODUCT]: [LIST INGREDIENTS]

I'm attaching our research brief and unique mechanism documents.

Come up with a list of claims for each ingredient tied to specific studies
from NCBI or scientific journals. Give preference to RCTs with humans.

Claims should focus on:
1. How the ingredient supports the unique mechanism
2. Additional studies showing the ingredient helps alleviate market pain points

Please keep claims digestible with references.
```

**Best practice:** Run research BOTH with and without mechanism context. Each approach yields different data. Combine findings.

## EVIDENCE-BASED SELLING FRAMEWORK (Gusten Sun)

### Two Types of Evidence

| Type | Definition | Examples | Trust Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Internal direct** | Claims YOU make | "I've helped 500 clients" / "I generated $2M" | Moderate (you control the narrative) |
| **External indirect** | Claims OTHERS make | Testimonials, reviews, case studies, Forbes mention | High (you can't control what others say) |

**Rule:** External indirect evidence is always stronger because the reader perceives it as uncontrollable and therefore more trustworthy. Lead with external proof wherever possible.

### How to Stack Evidence

1. **Open with external:** "As seen in Forbes" or customer quote above the fold
2. **Support claims with internal:** Your own results after establishing external credibility
3. **Close with external:** Strongest testimonial before CTA

## THE CONVERSION EQUATION (Gusten Sun)

Use this to diagnose WHY a sales page isn't converting:

**Likelihood of Purchase = (Desire + Credibility + Ability) / (Lack of Priority + Risk + Confusion)**

### Increase the Numerator
- **Desire:** Sell a bigger, more valued outcome
- **Credibility:** Trust in you AND your process (source + vehicle proof)
- **Ability:** Confidence THEY can succeed (templates, support, step-by-step)

### Decrease the Denominator
- **Priority:** Show cost of inaction; make it easy to start NOW
- **Risk:** Guarantee, free trial, payment plans, testimonials
- **Confusion:** FAQ, clear next steps, What/When/Why summary

### 12-Step Purchase Decision Checklist

Every sales page must get the prospect to internally agree with ALL 12:

1. I want this outcome and value it a lot
2. I acknowledge I have a problem I want solved ASAP
3. I believe this solution will solve my problem
4. I trust you're the right person to help me
5. It makes sense why I haven't solved this myself yet
6. Your process is better than what I've tried before
7. Others have tried this and gotten results (gives me hope)
8. Financially, this is more valuable than the price
9. I'm willing to make time for this
10. Risk of inaction is bigger than risk of trying
11. I understand the next steps
12. I feel urgency to solve this NOW

**Diagnostic use:** If your page isn't converting, map each section to these 12 items. The one not adequately addressed is likely your conversion bottleneck.

## AWARENESS LEVEL -> COPY LENGTH RELATIONSHIP

| Awareness Level | Copy Length | What the Copy Must Do |
|---|---|---|
| Problem-aware | Long (full 10-step page or long VSL) | Educate on solution category, introduce mechanism, build extensive trust |
| Solution-aware | Medium | Differentiate your approach, prove your method is superior |
| Product-aware | Short | Compare features/price, overcome specific objections |
| Most-aware | Very short | Remind, discount, urgency only |

This directly determines whether you need a 10-step long-form sales page (problem-aware) or a concise product page with a strong offer (most-aware).
