# Sales Page Sections -- Detailed Templates & Guidance

## ABOVE-THE-FOLD REQUIREMENTS

Every sales page must include these elements visible without scrolling:

1. **Logo bar** -- your brand logo (builds instant recognition)
2. **Ratings/reviews snippet** -- star ratings or "Rated 4.8/5 by 2,000+ customers"
3. **Small testimonial** -- one-line quote from a real customer
4. **Pre-headline** -- who this is for: "For [specific person] who wants [outcome]"
5. **Main headline** -- specific outcome + timeframe + objection removal
6. **CTA button** -- primary call to action visible immediately
7. **Guarantee icons** -- money-back badge, secure checkout badge

### Headline Formula

**Structure:** [Specific Outcome] in [Timeframe] Without [Main Objection]

Examples:
- "Lose 10kg in 30 Days Without Going to the Gym"
- "Land Your First 5 Freelancing Gigs in 30 Days Without Cold Calling"
- "Build a $10K/Month Funnel in 14 Days Without Knowing Code"

**Negative framing** (fix problem): "Stop Losing Money on Ads That Don't Convert"
**Positive framing** (become identity): "Become the Go-To Expert in Your Niche"

Test both -- negative framing often converts better for problem-aware audiences; positive framing works for solution-aware.

## STEP 1: BIG PROMISE -- EXPANDED

The promise must pass three tests:
1. **Specific** -- numbers, timeframes, measurable outcomes
2. **Believable** -- ambitious but not outrageous for this market
3. **Desirable** -- something they actually want (not what you think they should want)

**Pre-headline patterns:**
- "Free 5-Day Challenge Starting [Date]"
- "Attention: [Specific Person Type]"
- "New for 2026: [Category] That Actually Works"

---

## STEP 2: EMPATHY -- EXPANDED

**Structure:**
1. Stakes statement: "Here's why you should care about getting this right..."
2. Connection: "I was in your shoes [time] ago..."
3. Pain point list (use their language, not yours):
   - "You're likely feeling overwhelmed by..."
   - "You've probably tried X and felt frustrated when..."
   - "Maybe you've even started to wonder if..."
4. Support bridge: "You don't have to struggle anymore. Keep reading."

**Rule:** The empathy section should make them feel SEEN, not pitied. Write FROM understanding, not condescension.

---

## STEP 3: OPPORTUNITY / NEW SOLUTION -- EXPANDED

**The pattern:**
1. Name what they've tried: "You've likely tried [old method 1], [old method 2], maybe even [old method 3]."
2. Explain why it didn't work: "Here's why those approaches are incomplete..."
3. Introduce the new vehicle/mechanism: "What I discovered is that [new method]..."
4. Prove the vehicle works for others: "When [person] tried this approach, they [specific result]."

**Gap and Bridge framework:**
- THE GAP: "Old way sucks because [specific reasons]..."
- THE BRIDGE: "New way works because [mechanism]..."
- Visual: crossed-out pain points (old way) vs. checkmarks (your method)

## STEP 4: USP / COMPARISON TABLE -- EXPANDED

### Comparison Table Patterns

**Pattern 1: Us vs. Competitors**

| Feature | Others | Us |
|---|---|---|
| Support | Email only (48hr response) | Live coaching + Slack (same day) |
| Templates | Generic, outdated | Updated monthly, niche-specific |
| Results | "Results may vary" | 47% average improvement documented |

**Pattern 2: Old Way vs. New Way**

| Old Way | New Way |
|---|---|
| Cold calling 100 people/day | Automated funnel runs 24/7 |
| 2% response rate | 15-30% opt-in rate |
| 3 months to first client | First client in 14 days |

**Pattern 3: DIY vs. Done-With-You**
Show the cost of doing it wrong (time, money, frustration) vs. the shortcut of working with you.

---

## STEP 5: BENEFITS -- EXPANDED

**The Feature -> Benefit -> Impact chain:**

| Feature | Benefit | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Weekly coaching calls | Learn skills faster | Feel confident closing clients |
| 50 funnel templates | Skip the design phase | Launch in days, not months |
| Private community | Get answers when stuck | Never feel alone in your business |
| AI prompt library | Create content 10x faster | Free up 20 hours/week for family |

**Rule:** Always end the chain at the emotional impact. Features are forgettable. Benefits are interesting. Impacts are what people buy.

## STEP 6: OFFER STACK PRESENTATION -- EXPANDED

### The Offer Stack Structure

Present each component with:
1. **Name** of the component (branded, aspirational)
2. **What it is** (format: course, template, call, etc.)
3. **What it does** (the specific result)
4. **Individual value** (what it would cost separately)

**Example stack:**
- The Funnel Builder Accelerator (8 modules) -- Value: $2,997
- 50 Plug-and-Play Templates -- Value: $997
- Weekly Group Coaching (12 weeks) -- Value: $3,600
- Private Community Access (1 year) -- Value: $1,200
- Bonus: AI Prompt Library -- Value: $497
- **Total Value: $9,291**
- **Your Investment Today: $1,997** (or 3x $697)

### Price Anchoring Rules

- Anchor at 5-10x the actual price: if selling at $497, anchor total value at $2,500-$5,000
- "Get the gasp" -- the value stack should make the actual price feel like a steal
- Never anchor below 3x -- it doesn't create enough perceived discount
- Show crossed-out original prices for each item

### "Smart People Buy More" Push (Georgi)
After the first CTA, add a brief push for the bigger package:
- "Most customers choose the 6-month supply at $49/bottle instead of $69 -- it's the best value and the recommended usage period."
- Keep this to 2-3 sentences, not paragraphs. Don't belabor it.

## STEP 7: SOCIAL PROOF PLACEMENT -- EXPANDED

### The Sprinkle Strategy

Do NOT put all testimonials in one section. Instead:
- **Above the fold:** One small testimonial quote (builds instant credibility)
- **After empathy:** "I felt exactly like [customer name] who said..."
- **After opportunity:** Testimonial showing the new method worked
- **After benefits:** Testimonial matching specific benefit claims
- **Before CTA:** Strongest testimonial (the one that closes)
- **After first CTA:** Additional testimonials for those still scrolling

### Testimonial Visual Format
- Photo of the person (real, not stock)
- Headline quote (the most impressive line, bolded)
- Full testimonial text below
- Video testimonial link (if available -- video converts better)

### Types of Social Proof (Ranked by Strength)
1. **Specific results with numbers:** "I made $14K in my first month"
2. **Before/after transformations:** visual or narrative
3. **Third-party mentions:** Forbes, media logos, awards
4. **Volume proof:** "Join 10,000+ students" / "4.8 stars from 2,300 reviews"
5. **Celebrity/expert endorsements:** if applicable
6. **Peer proof:** "Other [identity] like you are doing this"

## STEP 8: RISK REVERSAL -- EXPANDED

### Options (Pick 1-2)

1. **Money-back guarantee:** "Try it for 30 days. If you don't see results, email us for a full refund."
2. **Payment plans:** Reduce the single-payment barrier. "3 easy payments of $697" feels smaller than "$1,997 today."
3. **Free trial:** "Test drive for 7 days before deciding."
4. **Double guarantee:** "If you don't get [result] in [time], I'll refund you AND give you [bonus]."

### Guarantee Copy Pattern
"I've taken most of the risk for you. Try [Product] for [Time]. If you don't [specific result], simply email [address] and I'll refund every penny. No questions, no hassles, no hard feelings."

## STEP 9: AUTHORITY SECTION -- EXPANDED

**Structure:** Tell your transformation story, NOT your personal biography.

1. "I was struggling with [same problem as reader]..."
2. "I tried [same failed solutions]..."
3. "Then I discovered [the mechanism/method]..."
4. "Now I've [impressive result] and helped [number] people do the same."

**Epiphany moment:** "I almost quit. Then I discovered this one thing that changed everything..." (This is the hook of the authority section.)

**What NOT to include:**
- Personal hobbies ("I love hiking and have two dogs")
- Credentials without context ("I have an MBA" -- so what?)
- Humble brags that don't connect to the reader's problem

**Indirect authority elements:**
- Photos with clients, on stages, at events
- Screenshot of results (revenue, followers, transformations)
- Logos of companies worked with or featured in

---

## STEP 10: URGENCY / SCARCITY -- EXPANDED

### Four Mechanisms

1. **Price scarcity:** "Price goes up from $997 to $1,497 on [date]"
2. **Time scarcity:** "Doors close [date] -- next cohort starts in 3 months"
3. **FOMO scarcity:** "Bonuses worth $2,400 disappear if you don't act now"
4. **Spot limits:** "Only 5 coaching spots remain this quarter"

### What/When/Why Close Section

End the page with a clear summary:
- **WHAT:** "You're getting [product name] -- [one sentence description]"
- **WHEN:** "Access starts immediately / Challenge begins [date]"
- **WHY:** "Because [restate the biggest pain point] and [restate the biggest promise]"

### FAQ Section Structure

Place FAQs after the urgency section (for people who scrolled past everything but are still considering):

Common questions to address:
- "What if I need more support?" -> Describe support options
- "What's included exactly?" -> Brief recap of offer stack
- "When does it start?" -> Timeline/access details
- "What if it doesn't work for me?" -> Restate guarantee
- "Is this right for [my specific situation]?" -> Restate who it's for
- "How is this different from [competitor/category]?" -> Restate USP

## HIGH-TICKET SALES PAGE STRUCTURE (Katie/Floyd)

For $5,000-$25,000+ offers sold via application:

### Section 1: Promo Banner
Current offer or deadline at the very top. Creates immediate urgency context.

### Section 2: Tagline Answering 4 Questions
One powerful statement that covers:
1. How long is this container? ("90 days of hands-on mentorship")
2. What is it? (coaching, mastermind, done-for-you)
3. What benefit? ("refine messaging and build passive income stream")
4. How is it different? ("doesn't involve launching or posting daily")

### Section 3: Heart Section
Your mission, your story, what you believe. Write from genuine feeling. This is NOT a sales pitch -- it's a manifesto. The reader should feel your conviction.

### Section 4: Who It's Perfect For
State the avatar very clearly. Be specific enough that the right person self-selects and the wrong person self-deselects.

### Section 5: Authority Proof
What you've done, specific results, named clients (with permission), revenue generated, transformations created.

### Section 6: What's Inside
Focus on WHY the support is beneficial, not just what information is covered. At this price point, they're buying ACCESS and TRANSFORMATION, not more courses.

### Critical Positioning Rule
You cannot sell high-ticket like information. Customers already buy courses from you -- the high-ticket must clearly be coaching/mentorship/transformation. Frame it as: "How much faster could we go? How many more breakthroughs could you have if we work together?"

## SALES PAGE CHECKLIST

Before publishing, verify:

- [ ] Headline passes the specific + believable + desirable test
- [ ] Above-the-fold includes CTA, social proof snippet, and guarantee icon
- [ ] Empathy section uses THEIR language (from research)
- [ ] Opportunity section introduces a NEW method, not improvement
- [ ] Comparison table differentiates clearly
- [ ] Every feature has a benefit AND an emotional impact
- [ ] Offer stack has price anchoring at 5-10x actual price
- [ ] Social proof is sprinkled throughout, not clumped in one section
- [ ] Risk reversal is specific (time, terms, process)
- [ ] Authority tells a transformation story, not a bio
- [ ] At least one urgency/scarcity mechanism is active
- [ ] FAQ addresses top 5-6 objections
- [ ] Multiple CTAs throughout the page (not just at the bottom)
- [ ] "No thanks" or "I have questions" path exists for non-buyers
