# Upsell, Downsell & Post-Purchase Strategy

Design the complete post-purchase offer flow -- from order bumps through upsells, downsells, and continuity. This skill covers what to sell, when to sell it, how to price it, and how to structure the page.

## CORE PHILOSOPHY: WHY POST-PURCHASE OFFERS MATTER

Upsells are where the real money lives. All four source instructors agree:

- Hormozi's gym model went from $600 to $9,644 per 10 walk-ins by adding upsells, downsells, and continuity
- Katie's backend sequences generated 2x+ front-end revenue ($53K vs $24K in one week)
- Georgi's upsell economics example: adding upsells swings a -$40/customer loss to +$20/customer profit ($60/customer swing)
- Gusten's SLO model depends on upsells to break even so the backend is pure profit

**Binge-buying psychology (Katie):** When someone buys, they enter a heightened buying state. They immediately ask "what else does this store have?" You are doing them a disservice by NOT offering more. The first purchase is the hardest; each subsequent purchase has less friction.

**Hyper-buying cycle (Hormozi):** After a new purchase or identity shift, people enter a hyper-active buying mode. If you do not offer them what they need next, they buy it from your competitor.

## UPSELL TIMING -- WHEN TO SELL

Five optimal timing windows (Hormozi), in order of effectiveness:

| Window | When | Why It Works |
|--------|------|-------------|
| 1. Immediately | Right after first sale | Solving one problem naturally creates the next |
| 2. Next step | 24-48-72 hours | Guide awareness of a new problem they cannot yet see |
| 3. After a big win | When they hit a milestone | Fully solved problem = fully formed new problem |
| 4. Halfway point | Mid-program/delivery | Historically high upsell acceptance |
| 5. Last chance | End of period | Most businesses try here -- but by now you have already lost |

**Cardinal timing rule:** Sell at the point of greatest deprivation, NOT at the point of greatest satisfaction. Do not offer another steak right after someone ate a steak. Create deprivation first, then make the offer.

Four out of five optimal upsell points are BEFORE the halfway point. Waiting until the end is an anti-pattern.

## ORDER BUMP FRAMEWORK

An order bump appears on the checkout page. The customer clicks a checkbox to add it before completing their purchase.

**Pricing:** $27-$37 range. Over $37 lowers conversion rate. Under $27 drags down AOV without enough lift.

**Take rate target:** 40%+ (25-40% is typical; Katie's own bump exceeds 50%).

**The core bump rule:** Your bump must answer "How can I help them get results faster and easier?" It accelerates the main product result -- it does NOT solve a different problem.

**Good bump product types:** Templates, recipes, swipe files, cheat codes, fill-in-the-blank frameworks, done-for-you resources, plug-and-play daily templates, proven scripts.

**Critical mistake -- disjointing the mind:** If the main product is shampoo (hair), do not bump with soap (body). That sends their brain into a different realm. Keep the bump laser-focused on the same topic. A tangential product can be an upsell 2 or 3, but never the bump.

**4-Part bump copy structure (Katie):**
1. **Fast "Yes" Headline:** "Add [product name] for just [$price]?"
2. **One-Sentence Payoff:** "Inside, you'll get [specific asset] so you can [immediate payoff]."
3. **Bonus Value Stack:** "Plus, you'll get [extra templates/resources] not included in [main product]."
4. **Identity Nudge:** "If you're the kind of [identity] who [desire/trait], you'll want this."

Do NOT just use the product name in the headline ("Add Aligned for $37"). Describe WHAT the product does.

## UPSELL PRICING ARCHITECTURE

After the bump, upsells appear as separate pages after checkout. One-click purchase (charges the same card).

**Katie's proven pricing sequence:**

| Upsell | Price Range | Question It Must Answer |
|--------|------------|------------------------|
| Upsell 1 | $67-$97 | What is their next small problem immediately after buying? |
| Upsell 2 | $197 | What is their next bigger problem? |
| Upsell 3 | $44-$77 | What smaller problem follows the big one? (the "clean-up hitter") |
| Upsell 4 | $44-$77 | What is another small problem they will hit next? |

**Take rate benchmarks:**

| Step | Average | Exceptional |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Bump ($27-$37) | 25-40% | 40%+ |
| Upsell 1 ($67-$97) | 10-20% | 20%+ |
| Upsell 2 ($197) | 10-15% | 15-20% |
| Upsell 3 ($44-$77) | 8-12% | -- |
| Upsell 4 ($44-$77) | 5-10% | Higher if framed as "last chance" |

**Starting advice:** If just starting, do one bump + one upsell at $197. Do NOT build four upsells upfront -- it destroys capacity and motivation. Build additional upsells as cash flow comes in.

**Minimum AOV target:** $70 (especially if running ads). Katie achieves $99-$105 AOV on a $44 front-end.

**Pricing psychology:** Cheaper upsells hurt you. Revenue per customer matters more than take rate. If your AOV math requires above-industry-average take rates to hit $70, raise prices instead.

## FOUR UPSELL TYPES (HORMOZI)

Each type is a different selling mechanism. See `references/upsell-types.md` for full implementation details.

1. **Classic Upsell:** Offer the solution to the customer's NEXT problem the moment they become aware of it. "You can't have X without Y."

2. **Menu Upsell (Hormozi's favorite):** Four-step process -- Unsell what they don't need (build goodwill) -> Prescribe what they do need (detailed instructions) -> Ask A or B (assumed close) -> Card on file close. The Unsell technique builds massive trust.

3. **Anchor Upsell:** Present premium item FIRST at 5-10x the price. Customer gasps. Come to the rescue with the real offer -- they feel relieved and buy. If 1 in 10 buys the 10x option, you double your business.

4. **Rollover Upsell:** Credit previous purchase toward the next offer. Four situations: win-back campaigns, rescue upset customers (alternative to refund), steal competitors' upset customers, and regular customer ascension. Credit toward something 5x more expensive.

## GEORGI'S UPSELL CATEGORIES

Frame each upsell by what type of improvement it offers:

- **Better Results** -- premium version
- **Faster Results** -- accelerator
- **Easier Results** -- done-for-you, automated
- **Expanded Results** -- solving a NEW or COMPLEMENTARY problem
- **Savings** -- save more money today (and get better results)
- **Holism** -- you solved PART of the problem, now solve the ENTIRE problem
- **Improved Version** -- upgrade what they just bought

**New problem framing examples:**
- "Now you know how to get dates. How do you not screw it up?"
- "Now you'll be skinny. What about stretch marks/saggy skin?"
- "Now you've fixed your knee pain. What about your back pain?"

## THREE DOWNSELL TYPES (HORMOZI)

**Cardinal rule:** You can offer something DIFFERENT for less. You CANNOT offer the SAME THING for less. Dropping your price is not downselling -- it is discounting, and it destroys trust permanently.

1. **Payment Plan Downsell:** Same product, same total price, different payment structure. "Can't afford $3,000 today? How about $500/month for 6 months?"

2. **Free Trial with Penalty:** Let them try free with compliance terms. If they comply, better chance of converting. If they do not comply, they pay a penalty.

3. **Feature Downsell:** Lower price by changing what they get -- fewer components, lower quality delivery, remove optional pieces. "If you're okay without a guarantee, I can knock off $400."

**Downsell principles:** They said no to THIS offer, not ALL offers. Downsells are trades -- if you give something, get something back. Personalize, do not pressure.

## CONTINUITY OFFER DESIGN

Three types to convert one-time buyers into recurring revenue. See `references/continuity-offers.md` for full frameworks.

1. **Continuity Bonus:** Give an awesome bonus (worth MORE than first payment) if they sign up for continuity today. Focus advertising on the BONUS, not the membership. Example: $1,000 challenge FREE when you become a $200/month gym member.

2. **Continuity Discount:** Give free time in exchange for longer commitment -- front-loaded, back-loaded, spread, or bookended free months.

3. **Waived Fee Offer:** Establish a startup fee (3-5x monthly rate), then offer to waive it with annual commitment. The same reason they signed up is the same reason they will not cancel.

## UPSELL PAGE COPY -- 6-PART STRUCTURE (KATIE)

1. **Interrupt + "Before You Go"** -- Stop the customer and re-open the buying loop
2. **Objection-Prevention Statement** -- Show why they will struggle without this upsell
3. **Irresistible "Value Shift" Promise** -- How this upsell dramatically boosts results
4. **Credibility Boost** -- Social proof or authority (skip if you have no testimonials)
5. **Offer Snapshot + Emotional Payoff** -- Transition from desire to decision
6. **What's Included** -- Switch the brain into logical "justification mode"

**Page layout:** Hook headline > Short blurb > Product mockup > Stack > Testimonials > "Yes" button with price > "No thanks" link at the VERY BOTTOM.

**Upsell pages should be SHORT.** Even for $197 offers. Do not write long-form sales pages for upsells.

**Handling promotion fatigue (upsell 3+):** Tell them "You're almost done" then immediately state the problem this solves.

## FULL STACKING SEQUENCE

Build one layer at a time -- do not launch a full money model on day one.

1. **Attraction offer** -> collect cash, liquidate CAC
2. **Upsells** -> maximize profit per customer
3. **Downsells** -> turn no's into yes's at every stage
4. **Continuity bonus** -> get them subscribed to recurring
5. **Prepayment offer** -> stack even more cash forward
