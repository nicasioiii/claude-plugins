# Continuity Offers

## WHY CONTINUITY MATTERS

One-time vs. continuity comparison:
- 1,000 people at $1,000 one-time = $10K from 10 buyers
- Same 1,000 people at $50/month continuity = $2K now from 40 buyers + $40K over time
- Result: 4x the customers to upsell later + same LTV collected over time

Continuity stabilizes cash flow, increases lifetime value, and creates a larger customer base for future offers.

---

## TYPE 1: CONTINUITY BONUS

Give the customer an awesome thing (worth MORE than the first payment) if they sign up for continuity today.

### Examples
- $24,000 in past courses FREE when you sign up for $399/month newsletter trial
- $800 in dog toys FREE when you sign up for $59/month food delivery
- $1,000 challenge FREE when you become a $200/month gym member (70-80% close rate)

### Rules for Continuity Bonuses
1. **Focus advertising on the BONUS, not the membership.** People buy the bonus; the membership is the vehicle.
2. **Keep bonuses related to core offer.** Do not offer free t-shirts to upsell tech services.
3. **Make bonuses from things you already have.** Past newsletters, courses, onboarding you would do anyway. Zero marginal cost.
4. **Add urgency:** Only available if they join RIGHT NOW.

### Full Stacking Sequence
This is the complete Hormozi money model build order:

1. Attraction offer -> collect cash, liquidate CAC
2. Upsells -> maximize immediate gross profit
3. Downsells -> convert "no's" at every decision point
4. Continuity bonus -> get them subscribed to recurring revenue
5. Prepayment offer on continuity -> stack even more cash forward

Build one layer at a time. Do not launch with a full money model (it will break your business). Start with one offer, then add layers like layers of paint.

## TYPE 2: CONTINUITY DISCOUNTS

Give free time in exchange for longer commitment. Four structures:

### Front-Loaded
First month free, then regular price. Gets them in the door. Risk: higher churn after free month ends.

### Back-Loaded
Last month free on a 12-month commitment. Reward for staying. Creates an incentive to reach the end.

### Spread
Free months distributed throughout the year (e.g., month 3, 6, 9 free on a 12-month plan). Creates multiple retention moments.

### Bookended
First AND last month free. Combines the onboarding ease of front-loaded with the retention incentive of back-loaded.

## TYPE 3: WAIVED FEE OFFER

The most powerful continuity lock-in mechanism.

### Structure
1. Establish a startup/onboarding fee (3-5x the monthly rate)
2. Offer to WAIVE the fee if they commit to 12+ months
3. If they cancel early, the fee becomes due

### Examples

**Gym:**
$5,000 onboarding fee + $1,000/month month-to-month, OR waive the $5,000 with a 12-month commitment at $1,000/month. Cancel early = pay the $5K.

**Consulting:**
$25,000 to start + $4,000/month month-to-month, OR $4,000/month with annual commitment. Cancel early = $25K fee.
Result: Virtually zero churn. Consultant making $5M/year with no employees.

### Why It Works
The same reason they signed up (to avoid the big fee) is the same reason they will not cancel. There is a crossover point (month 5-6) where paying the fee equals the same cost as staying -- so no financial reason to leave the back half.

### Fee Naming Options
Initiation, onboarding, enrollment, startup, setup, processing, activation, build, commencement -- add "fee" to any word. The name matters less than the structure.

## THE "DOWNSELL THE UPSELL" FRAMEWORK

Sell the expensive upfront thing first. Then downsell into continuity at a lower monthly rate but longer term.

**Example:**
- $600 for 6 weeks ($100/week)
- Then $200/month membership ($50/week = half the price)
- Pitch: "You've enjoyed X, Y, Z. If you're in this for the long term, we'll reward you with even more stuff for less."

**Why it works:** People think about monthly price more than total term/commitment value. A 3-year contract at a lower monthly feels better than a higher monthly with no commitment, even if the total cost is higher.

## THE BIG HEAD, LONG TAIL MODEL

**Structure:** $1,000 down + $50/month (vs. $200/month flat)

### Why Big Head + Long Tail Beats Flat Monthly
- Big upfront payment = "earned" the low rate -> customer will not cancel to preserve it
- People called to UPDATE their credit cards before expiration to keep their $10/month rate
- Low ongoing price = low churn = higher total LTV than the higher flat monthly

### Math Comparison
- $200/month with 10% monthly churn: LTV = $200 / 0.10 = $2,000
- $1,000 down + $50/month with 3% churn: LTV = $1,000 + ($50 / 0.03) = $1,000 + $1,667 = $2,667

The Big Head model collects more upfront AND achieves higher total LTV through lower churn.

## EDUCATION BUSINESS CHURN FIX

Education products have uniquely high churn because the value of education DIMINISHES after learning. The day before learning arithmetic = infinitely valuable. The day after = worth nothing.

### Three Solutions

**Solution 1: Sell Bulk/Annual Upfront**
Collect the full value promise before the learning devaluation kicks in. Annual plans, cohort pricing, lifetime access at a premium.

**Solution 2: Split One-Time + Consumable Value**
- One-time value: Install sales system = one-time price
- Consumable value: Monthly coaching calls, new ads, new playbooks = recurring price
- Sell them separately so the recurring price reflects only the consumable (ongoing) value

**Solution 3: Price Continuity on Consumable Value Only**
Do NOT price the membership at the blended rate of one-time education + consumable coaching. That leads to high churn because the education portion depreciates. Price the recurring component based solely on the ongoing value they receive each month.

## LTV CALCULATION

### Back-of-Napkin Formula
**LTV = Price / Monthly Churn Rate**

| Monthly Price | Churn Rate | LTV |
|---|---|---|
| $100/month | 10% | $1,000 |
| $100/month | 5% | $2,000 |
| $100/month | 3% | $3,333 |

A 7-point absolute reduction in churn (10% to 3%) produces a 3.3x improvement in LTV.

### Churn Impact on Business Scale
If you want big lifetime gross profit, conquer churn. Small improvements in churn compound dramatically over time. The relationship between churn reduction and LTV increase is exponential, not linear.

### Churn Benchmarks
- Industry average membership churn: 3-4 months before cancellation
- Waived Fee model churn: near zero (customers locked in by fee structure)
- Big Head Long Tail model churn: significantly lower than flat monthly pricing
- Education/info product churn: typically highest due to value depreciation

## COMPLETE MONEY MODEL TIMELINE (HORMOZI GYM EXAMPLE)

| Timeline | Action | Revenue Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Day 0 | Attraction offer (free 21-day transformation / 6-week challenge) | Lead generation, CAC liquidation |
| Day 0-2 | Core sale (sell the goal: $500-$2K packages) + Supplement upsell ($85 GP avg) | Primary revenue |
| Day 2-7 | Meals upsell | Additional recurring revenue |
| Day 14-21 | Continuity conversion (downsell the upsell into membership) | Recurring revenue lock-in |
| Day 21-28 | Prepayment offer on continuity | Cash acceleration |

At every stage where the customer says "no," run the downsell flow: same product different terms -> stripped version -> free orientation -> sell supplements anyway.

**Result:** 30-day gross profit of $9,644 per 10 walk-ins vs. industry standard of $600. Could outspend competitors 25:1 on advertising. The constraint shifted from marketing budget to operational capacity.
