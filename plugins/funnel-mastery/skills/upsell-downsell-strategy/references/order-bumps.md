# Order Bump Reference

## WHAT MAKES A GOOD BUMP

The bump must accelerate the result of the main product. It does NOT solve a different problem -- it helps them implement faster, easier, or better.

### Good Bump Product Types
- Templates (email, social, sales page, pitch)
- Recipes (meal plans, workout plans, content calendars)
- Swipe files (ads, subject lines, DM scripts)
- Cheat codes (shortcuts, hacks, quick-reference guides)
- Fill-in-the-blank frameworks (proposals, contracts, briefs)
- Done-for-you resources (pre-built assets, configured tools)
- Plug-and-play daily templates (90-day plans, daily checklists)
- Proven scripts (sales calls, onboarding, follow-up)

### Bump Examples by Niche

| Main Product | Good Bump | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Meal-planning guide | 20 done-for-you weekly grocery lists | Removes implementation friction |
| Productivity system | Plug-and-play daily templates | Ready to use immediately |
| Copywriting course | 100 proven scripts + cheat codes | Shortcuts to results |
| Content creation course | AI prompts + sales copy templates | Write content faster |
| Shampoo | Leave-in conditioner / brush / shine gloss | Same topic (hair) |
| Sports car | Leather steering wheel cover / black rims | Same topic (car) |
| Phone case | Camera screen protector / screen protector | Same topic (phone protection) |
| Gym membership | Workout log + nutrition cheat sheet | Accelerates gym results |
| Course on ads | Ad creative template library | Implement ads faster |
| Coaching program | Pre-call prep worksheet + goal tracker | Get more from sessions |

## 4-PART BUMP COPY STRUCTURE (KATIE)

### Part 1: The Fast "Yes" Headline
Quick, low-friction question or statement. Keep it simple.

**Template:** "Add [descriptive product name] for just [$price]?"

**Good:** "Add the 20 Done-For-You Grocery Lists for just $27?"
**Bad:** "Add Aligned for $37?" (nobody knows what "Aligned" is)

Always describe WHAT the product does, never just the product name.

### Part 2: The One-Sentence Payoff
Exactly how this bump makes the main product faster, easier, or better.

**Template:** "Inside, you'll get [specific asset] so you can [immediate payoff]."

**Example:** "Inside, you'll get 100 plug-and-play sales scripts so you can start closing deals today instead of staring at a blank page."

### Part 3: The Bonus Value Stack
1-2 extra perks to amplify perceived value.

**Template:** "Plus, you'll get [extra templates/resources/tools] not included in [main product]."

**Example:** "Plus, you'll get 3 bonus email sequence templates and a cold outreach swipe file not included in the main course."

### Part 4: The Identity Nudge
Identity-based closing line for frictionless buying. Appeals to who they want to be.

**Template:** "If you're the kind of [identity] who [desire/trait], you'll want this."

**Examples:**
- "If you're the kind of entrepreneur who values speed over perfection, you'll want this."
- "If you're the kind of coach who wants to spend time coaching, not writing, you'll want this."
- "If you're the kind of parent who wants healthy meals without the research, you'll want this."

## BUMP COPY MISTAKES

### Mistake 1: Using Only the Product Name
"Add Aligned for $37" tells them nothing. They do not know what "Aligned" is. Describe the function: "Add the Meal Planning Guide for $27."

### Mistake 2: Disjointing the Mind
If main product is about hair care, do NOT bump with body soap. That sends their brain to a different realm (body care, toxins, ingredients). Keep the bump laser-focused on the same topic.

A tangential product (soap) can work as Upsell 2 or 3 where the customer's mind has already expanded to adjacent problems. But never as a bump.

### Mistake 3: Pricing Too High
Over $37 dramatically lowers take rates. The bump should feel like an impulse add-on, not a considered purchase.

### Mistake 4: Pricing Too Low
Under $20 drags down AOV. The sweet spot is $27-$37 where it feels like a no-brainer but still contributes meaningfully to revenue per customer.

### Mistake 5: Solving a Different Problem
The bump must accelerate the SAME result the main product delivers. If the main product teaches them to write better, the bump should be templates that help them write faster -- not a course on email marketing.

## BUMP TAKE RATE BENCHMARKS AND OPTIMIZATION

| Take Rate Range | Interpretation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 40%+ | Exceptional -- bump is a perfect no-brainer | Monitor weekly, do not change |
| 25-40% | Typical range | Try copy improvements using 4-Part Structure |
| 15-25% | Below average | Rewrite copy, verify pricing is $27-$37, check alignment with main product |
| Under 15% | Product or alignment issue | Consider replacing the bump entirely |

**When bump underperforms:**
1. First rewrite the copy (especially the headline -- describe what it does)
2. Then check if price is in $27-$37 range
3. Then verify it is on the same topic as the main product
4. Only if all three fail, replace the product

**Katie's real data:** Her sales copy templates bump for a content course achieves 50%+ take rate. The product perfectly aligns (writing templates for a writing course) and the copy describes what it does.

## BUMP BRAINSTORMING BY BUSINESS MODEL

### Digital Info Products / Courses
The main product teaches a skill or system. The bump should be the tool that accelerates implementation.

- **Main: Course on writing** -> Bump: Swipe file of 100 examples
- **Main: Course on funnels** -> Bump: Pre-built funnel templates
- **Main: Course on ads** -> Bump: Ad creative template library
- **Main: Course on sales** -> Bump: Sales call scripts and objection handlers
- **Main: Course on productivity** -> Bump: Daily planning templates for 90 days
- **Main: Course on investing** -> Bump: Spreadsheet tracker + analysis templates

### Coaching / Consulting
The main product is access to a person. The bump should be a resource that makes their sessions more productive.

- **Main: Coaching program** -> Bump: Pre-call prep worksheets + goal tracker
- **Main: Mentorship** -> Bump: Quick-start implementation guide
- **Main: Group program** -> Bump: Private community access add-on
- **Main: VIP day** -> Bump: Pre-event strategy questionnaire + follow-up templates

### Physical Products / E-Commerce
The main product is a tangible item. The bump should be a complementary accessory on the same topic.

- **Main: Supplement** -> Bump: Companion supplement (same category, different function)
- **Main: Skincare serum** -> Bump: Eye cream or moisturizer (same brand, same skin concern)
- **Main: Fitness equipment** -> Bump: Carrying case or maintenance kit
- **Main: Kitchen appliance** -> Bump: Recipe book specific to that appliance

### SaaS / Software
The main product is a software tool. The bump should be setup assistance or templates.

- **Main: Project management tool** -> Bump: Pre-built project templates for their industry
- **Main: Email platform** -> Bump: Email sequence templates + deliverability checklist
- **Main: Design tool** -> Bump: Template library + brand kit setup guide

### Service Businesses
The main product is a service engagement. The bump should accelerate onboarding or add tangible deliverables.

- **Main: Website design** -> Bump: Brand photography session or stock photo pack
- **Main: Tax preparation** -> Bump: Tax planning guide for next year
- **Main: Cleaning service** -> Bump: Organization consultation add-on

## ADVANCED BUMP STRATEGIES

### The "Speed" Bump
Position the bump as the fastest path to the main product's result. "Want results in 7 days instead of 30? Add [speed resource]."

### The "Complete" Bump
Position the bump as making the main product complete. "Your course is powerful, but [bump] fills the one gap most people struggle with."

### The "Insider" Bump
Position the bump as exclusive insider knowledge. "My personal [templates/scripts/tools] that I use in my own business -- not included in the main course."

### Rotating Bumps
Test different bumps over time. What works as a bump in Q1 may fatigue by Q3. Keep 2-3 bump options ready and rotate when take rate declines.

### Bump as Lead Indicator
Bump take rate is an early signal of traffic quality and offer alignment. If bump take rate drops suddenly while traffic volume stays the same, the traffic quality may have changed (different audience segment arriving). This can be a leading indicator before sales page conversion rate drops.
