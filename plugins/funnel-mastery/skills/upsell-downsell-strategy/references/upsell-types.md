# Upsell Types & Downsell Flows

## HORMOZI'S MENU UPSELL -- 4-STEP PROCESS

The Menu Upsell is Hormozi's favorite and most consistently effective type. It works by building goodwill BEFORE making the offer.

### Step 1: Unsell What They Don't Need
Deliberately have products available that you know most people will not need. Cross items off: "You don't need this. You can get this from down the street."

This is the most deadly component. By telling them NOT to buy, you build massive trust. They drop all sales resistance.

**Example:** In a female-dominant gym, stock weight gainer protein. Cross it off the list for female clients. "You definitely don't need this." The goodwill from one crossed-off item makes the remaining recommendations feel genuinely helpful.

### Step 2: Prescribe What They Do Need
Give detailed, personalized instructions that ASSUME the purchase. "You'll take 2 of these at 8am, 1 of these before bed, and use this before your Monday and Thursday workouts."

The Prescription Close works because the customer thinks "why would he tell me these specific instructions if I'm not buying this?" The level of detail implies the purchase is already happening.

This outperforms vague/general product pitches massively. Specificity = authority = trust = sale.

### Step 3: Ask A or B (Assumed Close)
Do not ask "would you like to buy?" Ask "vanilla or chocolate?" The decision is not WHETHER to buy, but WHICH option to choose.

**Discovery story:** Hormozi went 0-for-19 on supplement sales using traditional pitches. On attempt #20, defeated and exhausted, he just asked "vanilla or chocolate?" She said chocolate. He asked "kiwi or strawberry for pre-workout?" She took it. Then closed the next 12 people the same way.

### Step 4: Card on File Close
"Want to use the card ending in 4696?" Since they already provided payment information for the original purchase, this removes all friction. No new decision to make.

## HORMOZI'S ANCHOR UPSELL -- 5-STEP PROCESS

Brute force pricing psychology. Requires a premium option that genuinely exists.

### Step 1: Present the Anchor (10x Price)
Show the premium item first. This sets the mental price range.

### Step 2: Get the Gasp
If there is no gasp, you did not anchor high enough. The visible shock is confirmation the anchor is working.

**Suit story:** Customer budgeted $500. First suit shown: $16,000. Blood drained from his face. Second suit: $2,200. Felt like a steal. Bought suit + $500 in shirts and accessories. Spent 5-6x his original budget.

**Rolls Royce insight:** They stopped showing at car shows (egregious next to $50K cars). They started showing at plane and yacht shows. After considering a $50M jet, a $400K car feels like candy.

### Step 3: Come to the Rescue
Ask about the secondary features that make the premium item premium. "Do you really need the hand-stitched lining? The Italian silk?" Let them identify what they do not care about.

### Step 4: Present the Main Offer
The customer now feels RELIEVED. What was your intended price all along looks like a bargain compared to the anchor.

### Step 5: Ask How They Want to Pay
Direct to close. No "would you like to buy?" Just "cash or card?"

**Math:** If 1 in 10 buys the 10x option, you DOUBLE your business. Plus everyone else spends more due to the anchoring effect.

## HORMOZI'S ROLLOVER UPSELL

Credit some or all of the customer's previous purchase toward the next offer. Credit toward something 5x more expensive.

### Four Situations to Use

**1. Win-Back Campaigns**
"I owe you money" subject line. Credit past purchase toward new service. People respond strongly to "I owe you money."

**2. Rescue Upset Customers (Alternative to Refund)**
"Everything you paid is on me. Let's credit it toward the thing you really needed." Prefer a redo over a refund every time.

**3. Steal Competitors' Upset Customers**
Scrape 1-star reviews of competitors. Message those people. "I'll credit whatever you spent there toward our services."

**4. Regular Customer Ascension**
Roll current purchase into a longer or higher-tier commitment. Move a $500 monthly client to a $2,500 annual program by crediting their existing payments.

## THE NO-SALE SALE TECHNIQUE (HORMOZI)

"You don't want anything else, do you?"

This works because:
- People are conditioned to say "no" to salesmen
- By saying "anything ELSE" -- it implies the prior items are already included
- They say "no" (meaning "no, nothing else") and accept the bundle

Used effectively in Classic Upsells where bundling items together is the strategy.

## GEORGI'S UPSELL CATEGORIES -- COMPLETE FRAMEWORK

Frame each upsell by what type of improvement it offers. Use this as a brainstorming checklist.

### Better Results
Offer a premium version of what they just bought. Higher potency supplement, advanced course module, priority support tier.

### Faster Results
Offer an accelerator. Done-for-you implementation, priority processing, fast-track program, intensive bootcamp format.

### Easier Results
Offer automation or done-for-you. AI tools, pre-built templates, managed service, "we do it with you" coaching.

### Expanded Results
Solve a NEW or COMPLEMENTARY problem. This is the most powerful category for maximizing revenue because it opens an entirely new value stream.

**Examples:**
- "Now you know how to get dates. How do you not screw it up?"
- "Now you'll be skinny. What about stretch marks/saggy skin?"
- "Now you're going to be rich. How do you avoid freeloaders?"
- "Now you've fixed your knee pain. What about your back pain?"
- "Now you'll have younger-looking eyes. What about the rest of your face?"

### Savings
Offer a bundle deal. "Buy 6 months now, save $X per month." Combines savings messaging with expanded supply.

### Holism
You solved PART of the problem. Now solve the ENTIRE problem. "You've optimized your ads. Now let's optimize your entire marketing system."

### Improved Version
Upgrade what they just bought. Extended warranty, premium materials, additional features, white-glove onboarding.

## KATIE'S 6-PART UPSELL PAGE COPY STRUCTURE

### 1. The Interrupt + "Before You Go" Pattern
Stop them and re-open the buying loop.
**Template:** "Before you go... I want to make sure you don't miss something that will make everything you just bought work even better."

### 2. The Objection-Prevention Statement
Show the real reason they might struggle without this.
**Template:** "[Core thing they just bought] is going to help you [promised outcome]... but without [missing element], you'll still struggle with [specific pain]."

### 3. The Irresistible "Value Shift" Promise
Show how this upsell dramatically boosts results.
**Template:** "Grab the proven [name of method/framework] my students have used to [quantifiable win] simply by shifting the way they [describe action]."

### 4. The Credibility Boost
Social proof or authority statement. Skip if no testimonials are available.
**Template:** "[#] of students/coaches/clients have used this system to [result]. [Insert 1 testimonial if available.]"

### 5. The Offer Snapshot + Emotional Payoff
Transition from desire to decision.
**Template:** "Inside [Upsell Name], I'll show you how to [core transformation] so you can [emotional win] -- without [annoying industry problem]. Get it now for just [$price]."

### 6. What's Included (Clear, Tangible Assets)
Switch the brain into logical justification mode. Break into specific assets with:
- What it is
- The result it creates
- Value (optional but effective for price anchoring)

## UPSELL PAGE LAYOUT AND DESIGN

Physical structure of the page:

1. Hook headline (question that states what they want)
2. Short blurb explaining the offer
3. Product mockup image
4. "What you get" section (the value stack)
5. Testimonials (if available)
6. "Yes, I want to add this" button with price clearly displayed
7. "No thank you" link at the VERY BOTTOM (force scrolling past everything)

**On page length:** Upsell pages should be SHORT. Even for $197 offers, keep it concise: hook, blurb, mockup, stack, testimonials, button. Long-form is not required and can hurt conversion on post-purchase pages.

**On later upsells (3+):** Counter promotion fatigue by letting them know "You're almost done" or "One more step." Then immediately state the problem this solves: "One more step -- don't you need more leads?" Asking the question that states their problem is highly effective.

## COMPLETE DOWNSELL FLOWS

### Hormozi's Gym Service Downsell Flow
For people who said no to the core service:

Service package -> Smaller package -> Free trial with penalty -> Free trial no commitment -> Free orientation

Even for complete "no" sales: "Come in for a free nutritional orientation." Sell $300 in supplements to people who did not buy gym membership. No-sale customers who bought supplements spent MORE ($300 avg) than service-buyers who also bought supplements ($200 avg). They wanted a "magic pill" not work. Have the magic pill available.

### Supplement Downsell Flow
Full bundle -> 4-pack with extras -> 4-pack only -> Continuity subscription -> No continuity (one-time) -> Extras only

### Digital Product Downsell Approach
Full program at $197 -> Payment plan ($67/month x 3) -> Stripped version at $97 -> Different complementary product at $67

### Key Downsell Principle
Downsells are TRADES. If you give something (lower price), get something back (fewer features, longer commitment, different format). Think of downsells as "100 ways to offer what you already have."
