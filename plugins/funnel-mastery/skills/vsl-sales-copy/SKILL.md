## INSTRUCTOR SYNTHESIS NOTES

This skill is primarily built from Stefan Georgi's RMBC II system (the definitive direct-response VSL methodology) with supporting context from Gusten Sun's funnel frameworks.

### Key Insight: The Close Is the Longest Section
Expectation should be that most viewers will NOT finish the VSL. The crescendo = the first CTA. Everything after is "free rolling" -- more shots at the conversion. Pacing matters MORE before the first CTA than after. Time is dilated after the first CTA -- length no longer matters much.

### Key Insight: AI Speed Workflows
With AI tools, 2-4 high-quality VSLs can be written in a single day (vs. weeks in the past). The RMBC Brief 2.0 is the key input document -- feed it to an LLM and it can generate each VSL section. Human editing and strategic thinking remain critical, but production speed is now hours, not weeks.

## CLASSIC VSL OUTLINE (High Level)

**Simple flow:**
Hook/Microlead -> Lead -> Background (Credibility + Origin Story) -> Discovery -> UMP/UMS -> Product Build-Up and Reveal -> Close -> FAQ

**Eight sections, each with distinct purpose:**

| Section | Purpose | Typical Length (Long VSL) |
|---|---|---|
| I. Hook / Microlead | Grab attention, relate to pain | 15-60 seconds |
| II. Lead | Open loops, tease mechanism, build curiosity | 2-5 minutes |
| III. Background Story | Establish credibility via shared struggle | 3-7 minutes |
| IV. UMP (Problem Mechanism) | Reveal the REAL cause they didn't know | 3-7 minutes |
| V. UMS (Solution Mechanism) | The real solution on a macro level | 2-5 minutes |
| VI. Product Buildup + Reveal | Trial/tribulation -> breakthrough -> product born | 3-7 minutes |
| VII. Close | Product details, alternatives dismissed, price, CTAs | 8-15 minutes |
| VIII. FAQ | Friendly tone, clear confusion, final CTA | 2-5 minutes |

## VSL LENGTH DECISION

Use the 15-Factor Scoring System (from `funnel-architecture`) to determine length:

| Score Range | VSL Length | When This Fits |
|---|---|---|
| High (above ~46) | Short (1-8 min) or no VSL needed | High-awareness market, demonstrable proof, low price, warm traffic |
| Mid (~30-45) | Medium (8-20 min) | Moderate awareness, some education needed, mid-price |
| Low (below ~30) | Long (20-60+ min) | Low awareness, needs heavy education, unknown brand, high price |

### Key Factors That Push Toward Longer VSLs
- Low market awareness (problem-aware or unaware audiences)
- No demonstrable proof (results take months with no visible change)
- High price point requiring heavy justification
- Cold traffic from unknown brand
- Saturated competitive market with jaded buyers

### Key Factors That Push Toward Shorter VSLs
- High market awareness (audience already knows the solution category)
- Instant visual proof (before/after, demos)
- Low price / low perceived risk
- Warm traffic (email list, retargeting)
- Strong brand equity

## IN-FEED VSL vs. ON-PAGE VSL

**Critical structural differences:**

### In-Feed VSL (Running as Meta/TikTok Ad)
Structure: Hook(s) -> Education -> Entertainment -> Pain + Solution Promise -> Quick Background -> UMP -> UMS -> Product Reveal -> Close

- Multiple hooks tested against the same body
- Must feel like native content (not a commercial)
- Heavier on curiosity and education upfront
- Product reveal comes later

### On-Page VSL (Landing Page After Click-Through)
Structure: Follows the classic VSL outline more closely

- Stronger lead section: more curiosity, pain callouts, teasing mechanism, dropping fascinations
- Viewer has already clicked (some intent established)
- Can be longer without losing audience (they chose to be here)
- Background story can be more detailed

## KEY VSL WRITING PRINCIPLES

### Trial and Tribulation Increases Conversions
Tested extensively: having a struggle/trial-and-error section before the product reveal increases conversions vs. just saying "and here's the product." This taps into the myth of the innovator tinkering in the garage. Show setbacks, failures, and eventual breakthroughs.

### Price Psychology Trick
Whatever the price is, mentally add a zero. If the product is $69, pretend it's $690 while writing the close. This makes you write with more urgency and gravitas -- as if the stakes are higher. The resulting copy is more persuasive even at the lower actual price.

### AOV vs. Conversion Trade-Off
Being too obsessed with pushing multi-bottle packages before the first CTA can hurt conversion rate. Better approach: get it converting first with a quick push to the bigger package ("6-month supply is the best bet... $49/bottle instead of $69... click now"), THEN optimize AOV as a second step.

### The First CTA Is the Crescendo
Everything in the VSL builds toward the first call to action. After that:
- Second CTA: Additional testimonials, then CTA
- Crossroads Close: Two paths (inaction = keep suffering; action = change your life)
- Third CTA: Urgency (no guarantee how long offer available)
- FAQ section: Tone shift back to being their friend
- Final CTA: After FAQs
