# Unique Mechanism (UMP/UMS) Development

## CORE DEFINITION (Stefan Georgi -- RMBC II)

The unique mechanism explains **why the prospect is struggling** and **how they can overcome** that struggle. It is always a **two-part logical connection:**

1. **UMP (Unique Mechanism of the Problem):** The real, hidden reason behind the problem. Shows the prospect why they've failed in the past.
2. **UMS (Unique Mechanism of the Solution):** The real solution to that problem. Shows the prospect why your product will succeed where alternatives failed.

### Why It Works

By the time prospects come to you, they've usually tried solutions that:
- Didn't work at all
- Worked partially
- Worked fully for a period before stopping

Your job: educate the prospect that their knowledge about the problem is **incomplete**. They've been missing ONE CRUCIAL PIECE OF INFORMATION -- this "knowledge gap" is what held them back.

---

## UMP STRUCTURE PATTERN

Especially effective for health/DTC but applies to any category:

1. **Here's how this system/process is supposed to work** (normal function)
2. **This is why that system isn't working like it's supposed to** (the malfunction)
3. **What external factors or culprits are causing the malfunction** (why it's happening to YOU)

### Example -- Edema Product (Nooroo):
1. Heart sends blood down; calves ("the second heart") pump blood back up
2. When calves weaken, they can't contract properly, blood pools in feet/legs
3. External factors: sitting for hours, aging, less physical activity = weakened calves

### Example -- Skincare Product (Miami MD):
1. Skin health depends on fibroblasts (the "interior designers" of the dermis layer)
2. As you age, fibroblasts get lazy and stop producing collagen efficiently
3. External factors: UV damage, environmental toxins, hormonal changes accelerate fibroblast decline

## RULES FOR GOOD UNIQUE MECHANISMS

1. **Must be logical** -- Someone should go "Oh wow, okay, I get it." The mechanism should create an aha moment, not confusion.

2. **Must be simple** -- Do not overcomplicate with excessive biological or technical details. Even in longer VSLs, you don't need excessive scientific complexity. If you can't explain it to a 12-year-old, simplify it.

3. **After explaining the UMP, summarize it again** in a few sentences. Repetition of the key insight cements understanding.

4. **Must be connected to the product's actual ingredients or method** -- Not pure fiction. The mechanism must logically connect to what your product actually does.

5. **Must align with the awareness level** of your target market segment (see section below).

6. **Must feel like a genuine revelation** -- The prospect should feel like they just learned something important that changes their understanding of the problem.

---

## UMP/UMS MUST MATCH AWARENESS LEVEL

| Targeting | UMP Explains... | UMS Positions Product As... |
|---|---|---|
| **Problem-aware** | Why their general attempts at solving the problem haven't worked | The first real solution to the underlying cause |
| **Solution-aware** | Why the solution category they know about isn't giving full results | A superior approach within the solution category |
| **Product-aware** | Why competitor products in the category are specifically inferior | The best-in-class option with unique advantages |

**Critical insight for scaling:** Businesses plateau at $1-3M because they exhaust product-aware and most-aware segments. To scale to $10M+, you must write mechanisms for problem-aware and unaware audiences (70-80% of most markets). This requires different UMP/UMS framing and longer copy.

## AI-POWERED UMP IDEATION PROCESS (3-Step Prompt Chain)

### Step 1: Ideation Prompt

```
Today I want your help coming up with as many Unique Mechanism ideas for
a [PRODUCT] in the [NICHE].

I have attached a research brief that will help you understand the market
we are speaking to.

A Unique Mechanism explains why our prospect is struggling and how they
can overcome that problem. It is a two-part logical connection:

1. UMP (Unique Mechanism of the Problem): The real, hidden reason behind
   the problem. Shows why they've failed in the past.
2. UMS (Unique Mechanism of the Solution): The real solution. Shows why
   our product will succeed where alternatives failed.

One thing to pay VERY careful attention to is the levels of Product/Market
Awareness we are focusing on. The UMP and UMS need to speak to prospects
where they are.

For this exercise, let's specifically focus on people who are
[Problem Aware / Solution Aware].

Please generate at least 8-10 UMP/UMS pairs with brief explanations.
```

### Step 2: Evaluation Prompt

Feed multiple UM ideas + unified research brief to the LLM:

```
Here are [X] Unique Mechanism ideas I've developed for [PRODUCT].
I'm also attaching our unified research brief.

Please evaluate and rank each mechanism based on:
1. Potential for connecting emotionally with the target market
2. Logical coherence (does the UMP -> UMS chain make sense?)
3. Alignment with the awareness level we're targeting
4. Differentiation from common competitor messaging
5. Ability to make the product the obvious solution

For each, provide a score 1-10 and brief justification.
Identify the top 2-3 strongest mechanisms.
```

### Step 3: Expansion Prompt

Take winning mechanisms and expand:

```
Let's develop [WINNING MECHANISM] into a full UMP/UMS narrative.

Please write:
1. The full UMP explanation (how the system is supposed to work, why it's
   not working, what external culprits are causing the problem)
2. The summary/recap of the UMP in 2-3 sentences
3. The full UMS explanation (the real solution on a macro level, then
   the micro proof points)
4. How this connects to our product's specific [ingredients/method/features]
5. 3 supporting evidence points or citations we should include
6. A catchy nickname for this mechanism (see Mechanism Nicknames below)
```

## MECHANISM NICKNAME CREATION

Great mechanisms have memorable nicknames that become shorthand:

### Examples from the Wild
- "7 second bowel releasing ritual"
- "30 second breakfast hack"
- "Paper Flipping" (for real estate arbitrage)
- "The Second Heart" (for calf-pump mechanism in edema)
- "Sleeping Fibroblasts" (for skincare mechanism)

### Nickname Rules
1. Include a specific number or time element when possible
2. Use everyday language (no jargon)
3. Create curiosity -- the nickname alone should make someone want to learn more
4. Connect to the mechanism's core insight
5. Should be usable in ad hooks, email subject lines, and headlines

### Nickname Creation Prompt

```
I have a unique mechanism for [PRODUCT]:
UMP: [Brief UMP description]
UMS: [Brief UMS description]

Please generate 10 catchy, curiosity-provoking nicknames for this mechanism.
Rules: Include a time/number element when possible, use everyday language,
and make each one hook-worthy for ad headlines and email subject lines.
```

## HOW UMP/UMS FITS IN DIFFERENT VSL LENGTHS

### Short VSL (1-8 min)
- UMP/UMS is the CORE of the video (30-90 seconds)
- Delivered as a quick reveal without extensive scientific backing
- One clear mechanism, one clear product connection

### Medium VSL (8-20 min)
- UMP/UMS gets 3-5 minutes total
- Room for one supporting study or proof point per mechanism part
- Summary/recap after each part

### Long VSL (20-60+ min)
- UMP/UMS gets 5-12 minutes total
- Extensive scientific backing with multiple citations
- Detailed explanations with analogies and visualizations
- "After I explain the UMP, I'll summarize it again in layperson's terms"
- The mechanism IS the centerpiece of the entire VSL

## MECHANISM DEVELOPMENT CHECKLIST

Before using your UMP/UMS in a VSL:

- [ ] UMP creates a genuine "aha moment" (not just restating the problem)
- [ ] UMP -> UMS chain is logically airtight (one causes the other)
- [ ] Mechanism is simple enough to explain in 2-3 sentences
- [ ] Mechanism is connected to what the product actually does (not fiction)
- [ ] Mechanism matches the awareness level of the target audience
- [ ] Mechanism has a memorable nickname
- [ ] UMP addresses why past solutions failed (not just "they're bad")
- [ ] UMS positions your product as the obvious/only solution to the UMP
- [ ] At least 1-2 supporting evidence points exist (studies, data, expert quotes)
- [ ] Mechanism doesn't require excessive scientific knowledge to understand
