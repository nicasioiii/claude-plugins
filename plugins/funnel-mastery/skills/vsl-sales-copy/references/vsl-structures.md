# VSL Structures -- Classic Outline & 8 Short Formats

## CLASSIC VSL OUTLINE -- COMPLETE STRUCTURE

### I. Pre-Lead / Microlead / Hook
- Hook-heavy or attention-grabbing opening line
- Quickly relates to the pain point prospect faces
- Quick relation to the promise of a solution
- Must stop the scroll / keep them watching in the first 3-5 seconds

### II. Lead (Open Loops with Fascinations)
- Call out the problem directly
- Promise solution to pain point + promise to save time/money
- Tease an emotional discovery story
- Tease the unique mechanism behind the solution
- Tease contrarian nature of mechanism ("flies in the face of what you've been told")
- Work in "fascinations" as incentive for continuing to watch
- Briefly mention credibility builders
- Qualifiers -- who this works for
- Briefly address skepticism
- Include a few broad testimonials (about benefits, don't give away product)

### III. Background Story
- Who I am + credibility builders
- "Me or someone close to me was just like you (in pain)" -- emotional story
- Traditional solutions and advice not working
- Trigger event -- pain escalated to the point where something MUST change
- Search for truth/answers begins

### IV. Unique Mechanism of the Problem (UMP)
- Explaining the "real cause" -- you've had 99% of the answer but this is the missing 1%
- Should be surprising, possibly counterintuitive
- Backed by credibility and proof elements
- After explaining, summarize it again in a few sentences

### V. Unique Mechanism of the Solution (UMS)
- Now that you know the real cause, here is the "real solution" on a MACRO level
- Logically connected to the problem mechanism
- Once they have the macro solution (a theory), go deeper to prove it (get into the micro)
- Backed by credibility and proof elements, including citations and scientific sources

### VI. Product Buildup + Reveal
- Avatar knows the solution, looks for an "out-of-the-box" version
- Discovers existing options are FLAWED (why they can't just go buy it on Amazon)
- Has to do it himself/herself -- creates a novel new solution
- **Starts testing. PROBLEMS AND SETBACKS occur** (trial and tribulation section)
- Eventually: success/breakthrough
- Proof product works; others asking for it
- PRODUCT IS BORN

### VII. Close
1. Product details (what's included, what makes it special, USPs)
2. Discuss and dismiss other alternatives (expensive, ineffective, side effects, poor quality)
3. Keep building up value
4. Additional testimonials
5. How to use the product (eliminate confusion)
6. Scarcity: demand is high, supplies limited, out-of-stocks
7. Personal mission of avatar -- tied to emotion/altruism
8. Price justification and reveal -- other solutions cost more; consequences of inaction are costly
9. "Normally $X, but right now just $Y!"
10. **Smart People Buy More** -- brief push for bigger package (2-3 sentences, not paragraphs)
11. **FIRST CALL TO ACTION** <-- This is the crescendo. Everything after is free rolling.
12. What happens after you click the button
13. Optional: valuable free bonuses revealed
14. Guarantee -- great experience likely, but if not, no risk, no hassle
15. SECOND CALL TO ACTION
16. **Crossroads Close** -- two choices: inaction (keep suffering) vs. action (change forever)
17. Urgency -- end of presentation, no guarantee how long offer will be available
18. THIRD CALL TO ACTION
19. Thank you for watching

### VIII. FAQs
- **Tone shift: back to being their friend** -- helping clear up confusion
- Reminding them of product, what it's used for, importance of action, guarantee terms, how to buy
- End with FINAL CALL TO ACTION

## 8 SHORT VSL STRUCTURES (1-8 Minutes)

### Structure 1: Problem -> Mechanism -> Solution
Best for: DTC supplements, health products, skincare

1. **Hook/Problem Callout** (10-15 sec): Direct pain point statement
2. **Mechanism Reveal** (30-60 sec): The "real cause" most people don't know
3. **Solution Reveal** (30-45 sec): Introduce approach, mention compounds/components or tease method
4. **CTA** (15-30 sec): Risk reversal + call to action

### Structure 2: BizOpp Contrarian
Best for: Business opportunity, coaching, investment offers

1. Hook (contrarian investment/income positioning)
2. Opportunity introduction
3. Explain method/system (simple example + key differentiator + analogy + industry validation)
4. Business model reveal (partnership structure + what you do for them + done-for-you promise)
5. Objection handling sequence ("Why don't you just do it yourself?" / "Is this a scam?" / "What's the catch?")
6. Qualification requirements
7. Information gap bridge ("Can't explain everything in this video")
8. Primary CTA with urgency
9. Risk reversal + future pacing
10. Final CTA

### Structure 3: Personal Discovery -> Solution
Best for: Personal transformation products, info products, supplements

1. Audience hook + life change promise
2. Problem agitation (personal struggle with failed solutions)
3. Discovery moment (came across video/article/doctor/friend)
4. Product reveal path (physical products) OR method tease path (info products)
5. Personal transformation (immediate results + specific improvements)
6. Direct sale CTA OR information CTA

### Structure 4: Authority/Affinity + Life-Changing Discovery
Best for: Premium products, expert-positioned brands

1. Contrarian hook + personal authority
2. High-stakes situation setup
3. From old to new solution
4. Experiencing benefits
5. Proof/test results
6. Personal recommendation
7. Product authority and features
8. Comparison/superiority
9. Technical benefits
10. Social proof
11. Current state transformation
12. Offer + CTA

### Structure 5: "Secret" Reveal (Curiosity-Driven)
Best for: Quick hits (1-3 minutes), curiosity-heavy audiences

- **Mystery Hook** (15-20 sec): Intrigue-based opening
- **Personal Transformation Story** (45-75 sec): Before/after narrative
- **Product/Method Reveal** (30-45 sec): The secret unveiled
- **Urgency CTA** (15-20 sec): Act now framing

### Structure 6: Emotional Story
Best for: Health, pain relief, emotional transformation products

1. Hook/Problem callout (10 sec)
2. Personal story as problem amplification (45 sec) -- stakes raising with each beat
3. Authority-backed mechanism reveal (90 sec) -- scientific detail + why conventional treatments fail
4. Solution introduction with more authority (45 sec) -- specific discovery
5. Benefits promise + CTA (30 sec) -- future pacing + free presentation offer

### Structure 7: Contrarian + Expert Reveals Mechanism
Best for: Products where the mechanism is the key differentiator

1. Hook + contrarian twist (15-25 sec)
2. Authority setup + education (20-30 sec)
3. Mechanism reveal with progressive explanation (60-90 sec):
   - How the system is supposed to work
   - What goes wrong
   - Body's/market's failed compensation
   - Escalation
   - Why common solutions fail
4. Real solution introduction (30-45 sec)
5. Social proof story (45-60 sec)
6. Final CTA with benefit stack (25-35 sec)

### Structure 8: SPG BizOpp Template
Best for: Business opportunity, affiliate, partnership offers

Hook -> Transformation story (guided by expert) -> Introduce UMS -> Explain UMS -> "Results not typical but what if they were?" (future pace) -> "Can't teach fully here, created a free presentation" -> CTA with urgency -> Remind of opportunity -> Final CTA

## MEDIUM VSL GUIDANCE (8-20 Minutes)

- Follow the full classic outline but compress sections
- Background story can be very brief (credibility + quick pain acknowledgment)
- UMP/UMS delivered efficiently without excessive scientific detail
- Common format for in-feed Meta ads: multiple hooks/openings tested against the SAME core body
- This is "the format that is working best for most people today" (Georgi)

### Medium VSL Compression Strategy
1. Merge Lead and Background into one section (90 seconds max)
2. Keep UMP/UMS tight (3-4 minutes combined)
3. Shorten product buildup (skip extended trial-and-tribulation)
4. Close can still be the longest section (multiple CTAs still apply)

---

## LONG VSL GUIDANCE (20-60+ Minutes)

- Full classic outline with all sections expanded
- More detailed background stories with emotional depth
- Extensive UMP/UMS with scientific backing and citations
- Longer product buildup with full trial-and-tribulation narrative
- Multiple close sequences with escalating urgency
- FAQ section can be 5-10 questions

### When Long VSLs Are Necessary
- Problem-aware audience that needs heavy education
- Unknown brand with no existing trust
- High price point requiring extensive justification
- Saturated market where differentiation requires deep mechanism explanation
- Complex product where the "how it works" IS the selling point

## IN-FEED VSL vs. ON-PAGE VSL -- STRUCTURAL DIFFERENCES

### In-Feed VSL Structure (Running as Meta/TikTok Ad)

```
Hook or Multiple Hooks (curiosity heavy)
  -> Education
  -> Entertainment (sometimes)
  -> Calling Out Pain Point + Promising Solution
  -> Quick Background Story
  -> UMP
  -> UMS
  -> Product Buildup and Reveal
  -> Close
```

**Key differences from on-page:**
- Must feel like native content, not a polished commercial
- Multiple hooks tested against the same body (the winning hook can generate 60%+ of all sales)
- Education and entertainment come before the sell
- Product reveal is delayed further than on-page

### On-Page VSL Structure (Landing Page After Click-Through)

Follows the classic VSL outline more closely with emphasis on:
- Stronger lead section with more curiosity + pain callouts
- Teasing the mechanism with fascinations
- More credibility builders upfront (they chose to click, now justify that choice)
- Can assume some intent since they already clicked through from an ad or advertorial

## VSL WRITING CHECKLIST

Before finalizing any VSL script:

- [ ] Hook grabs attention AND relates to the specific pain point (not just clickbait)
- [ ] Lead opens at least 3 curiosity loops
- [ ] Background story creates emotional connection through shared struggle
- [ ] UMP is surprising but logical ("Oh wow, that makes sense")
- [ ] UMS is directly connected to UMP (logical two-part connection)
- [ ] Product buildup includes setbacks before breakthrough (trial and tribulation)
- [ ] Close has at least 3 CTAs spaced throughout
- [ ] First CTA is the crescendo moment -- everything builds to it
- [ ] "Smart People Buy More" push exists but is brief (2-3 sentences)
- [ ] Crossroads Close presents two clear paths (inaction vs action)
- [ ] FAQ section shifts tone to friendly/helpful
- [ ] Guarantee is stated clearly and specifically
- [ ] Script matches the awareness level of the target audience
- [ ] If in-feed: feels like native content, not a commercial
- [ ] If on-page: lead section is strong enough to retain post-click viewers
