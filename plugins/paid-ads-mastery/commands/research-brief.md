## Step 5: Awareness Level of Your Audience

**What's your audience's starting point?**

**Option A: Problem-Aware**
- Audience knows they have a problem
- Don't know your solution exists
- Research focus: Understand their pain, validate problem urgency
- Message angle: "Here's the solution to your problem"

**Example:** Colorblind person knows they have color blindness, but doesn't know corrective glasses exist

**Option B: Solution-Aware**
- Audience knows solutions exist
- Don't know about your specific product
- Research focus: Competitive advantages, why choose you
- Message angle: "Here's why OUR solution is better"

**Example:** Colorblind person knows about color correction, comparing options

**Option C: Product-Aware**
- Audience knows about your product
- Haven't bought yet
- Research focus: Objection handling, testimonials, social proof
- Message angle: "Here's why 10,000+ customers chose us"

**Example:** Colorblind person saw your product, now deciding

**Option D: Most-Aware**
- Audience has seen your ads
- Repeat traffic but haven't converted
- Research focus: Urgency, special offers, objection removal
- Message angle: "Don't miss out, limited time offer"

**Which applies most to your situation?** [A / B / C / D]

## Step 6: Research Plan Output

Based on your inputs, here's your structured research plan:

---

## PHASE 1: INTERNAL RESEARCH (3-5 days)

### What to do:
1. **Product Deep Dive**
   - List 5-10 unique benefits of your product
   - Document what makes it different from competitors
   - Write down exact customer language you hear (not marketing-speak)

2. **Customer Interviews** (if you have existing customers)
   - Call/email 5 recent customers
   - Ask: "Why did you buy?" (listen for real reasons, not sales pitch)
   - Ask: "What problem does this solve?" (their exact words)
   - Ask: "What almost stopped you?" (objections to overcome)

3. **Customer Success Stories**
   - Identify 3-5 best customer transformations
   - Document: Before state → After state → Specific result
   - Get permission for testimonial/video

### Deliverable:
- List of 5-10 real customer benefit statements
- 3-5 specific objections you need to overcome
- 3-5 customer transformation stories

---

## PHASE 2: COMPETITIVE RESEARCH (5-7 days)

### What to do:

1. **Facebook Ads Library**
   - Go to facebook.com/ads/library
   - Search for each competitor name
   - Document: Which ads they're running, how many variations, estimated spend
   - Look for patterns: Which message angles appear 3+ times? (Winners)

2. **TikTok Trend Analysis**
   - Check TikTok Ads Library (TikTok.com/ads/library)
   - Search relevant keywords for your category
   - Note: Which video hooks are trending? Which creators?
   - Look for emerging trends you can apply to your product

3. **Amazon/Competitor Reviews**
   - Read 1-star reviews for competitors (objections)
   - Read 5-star reviews for competitors (what people love)
   - Document exact language used in both

4. **Reddit Deep Dive**
   - Search r/[relevant_subreddit] for discussions about problem
   - Look for: Language people use, frustrations, solutions they mention
   - Screenshot valuable threads

### Deliverable:
- 5-10 best-performing competitor ads (screenshots/descriptions)
- 5-10 trending hook types in your category
- Top 3 objections found in competitor reviews
- Top 3 benefits people mention in 5-star reviews

---

## PHASE 3: AUDIENCE RESEARCH (5-7 days)

### What to do:

1. **Forum Research** (Reddit, Facebook Groups, Quora)
   - Find 3-5 forums where your audience hangs out
   - Search keywords related to your product
   - Read 20+ threads, document:
     - Exact language they use for the problem
     - Common objections/concerns
     - Desired outcomes (what do they want?)

2. **Social Listening**
   - YouTube: Search relevant keywords, read top comments
   - Instagram: Follow related hashtags, read captions/comments
   - Newsletters: Subscribe to 2-3 relevant newsletters in your space
   - Document: Pain points, desires, frustrations

3. **Google Search Trends**
   - Go to google.com/trends
   - Search related keywords
   - Note: Search volume trends, related searches
   - Document emerging interest areas

4. **Amazon Product Research**
   - Find 3-5 product alternatives on Amazon
   - Analyze: Top reviews (1-star, 5-star)
   - Document exact language used in reviews

### Deliverable:
- List of 10-15 forum posts with pain point language
- Collection of 20+ social media comments (real audience language)
- 5-10 search trends & related searches
- Top 10 objections from real customer feedback

---

## PHASE 4: MESSAGING ARCHITECTURE (3-5 days)

### What to do:

Based on all research above, develop:

1. **Problem Statement** (What's the pain?)
   - Write: "[Target audience] struggle with [problem]"
   - Validate: Does this match what you found in research?

2. **Desired Outcome** (What do they want?)
   - Write: "[Target audience] want to [desired state]"
   - Validate: Is this mentioned in competitor ads & reviews?

3. **USP Statement** (Why yours?)
   - Write: "[Product] is the only one that [unique thing]"
   - Validate: Confirmed in your competitive research?

4. **Proof Elements** (Why believe you?)
   - List: Testimonials, stats, case studies, guarantees
   - Validate: Do these address top objections?

5. **Objection Handlers** (Answer the doubts)
   - Document: Top 3 objections + your counter-proof
   - Validate: Are these the same objections found in competitor reviews?

### Deliverable:
- Clear problem statement
- Desired outcome statement
- Unique selling proposition
- List of proof elements
- Objection handling framework

---

## PHASE 5: CREATIVE DIRECTION (3-5 days)

### What to do:

1. **Hook Strategy**
   - From TikTok/competitor research: Which hook types work in your space?
   - Decision: Will you use pattern interrupt, benefit-forward, testimonial, etc.?
   - Examples: Find 3 ads using your chosen hook type

2. **Content Format**
   - Which format wins in your category? (Video / Static / Carousel)
   - Best performing video length?
   - Aspect ratio? (9x16 / 1x1 / 4x5)

3. **Visual Direction**
   - Mood board: Collect 10-15 visual examples from competitors
   - Aesthetic: Professional / Casual / Playful / Authoritative?
   - Color scheme: Which colors dominate in your category?
   - Creator type: Professional / UGC / Influencer?

4. **Messaging Angles to Test**
   - Angle 1: [Based on competitor winners]
   - Angle 2: [Based on competitor winners]
   - Angle 3: [Your unique angle]
   - Angle 4: [Emerging trend you spotted]
   - Angle 5: [Objection-focused angle]

### Deliverable:
- Detailed creative brief with:
  - Hook strategy (type + examples)
  - Format specs (dimension, length, placement)
  - Visual direction (mood board, aesthetic)
  - 5 messaging angles to test
  - Estimated production timeline

---

## PHASE 6: BRIEF TO CREATIVE (5-7 days)

### What to do:

1. **Create Design Brief**
   - For: Video producer / Graphic designer / UGC creator
   - Include: All findings from Phase 1-5
   - Reference: Competitor ads + mood board
   - Clarity: Exactly what to create, why, and what success looks like

2. **Production Timeline**
   - Hook 1 variation: [Date]
   - Hook 2 variation: [Date]
   - Hook 3 variation: [Date]
   - Copy/testimonial variations: [Date]
   - Backup creatives: [Date]

3. **Quality Standards**
   - Resolution: [Specify pixels]
   - Format: [Specify exact dimensions]
   - Style: [Reference specific ads/examples]
   - Voiceover: [If applicable, specify tone/accent]

### Deliverable:
- Production-ready creative brief
- Timeline with specific deadlines
- Mood board with 10-15 reference images
- Approved messaging angles
- Quality standards checklist
