# /research-brief -- Creative Research Methodology

## OVERVIEW

This command activates the `research-creative` skill to run the complete 4-step sequential research process. The output is a creative strategy document that feeds ALL creative production skills (create-ad-copy, create-video-ads, create-ai-ads, create-google-assets).

**Skill activated:** `research-creative`

---

## INTAKE QUESTIONS

### Question 1: Brand Context
- Brand name and website URL
- Product(s) to focus on
- Monthly ad spend (approximate)
- Target CPA or Target ROAS
- Platforms currently running ads on

### Question 2: Existing Data Availability
- Do you have access to your ad account? (for Step 1)
- Do you have customer reviews available? (Amazon, website, Trustpilot)
- Do you have a post-purchase survey running?
- How many active ads are currently running?
- Can you share your top 5-10 performing creatives from the past 90 days?

### Question 3: Research Scope
- Is this for a new product launch or iteration on existing creative?
- Specific audience segments to explore?
- Any competitor brands to analyze?

---

## THE 4-STEP PROCESS

### Step 1: Ad Account Analysis

**Sub-step A -- Digging Questions (8 questions to answer):**
1. Which angles are currently running? (UGC, narration, mashup, VSL)
2. What video creative types are in rotation?
3. What is working on statics? (benefit callouts, comparisons, before/after)
4. Which awareness levels are being targeted?
5. Most profitable audience segment? (age/gender breakdowns from Ads Manager)
6. Top 5-10 performing creatives from past 90 days (links + spend + performance)
7. Monthly ad spend (approximate)
8. Target CPA / Target CAC

**Sub-step B -- Turning Dirt into Gold:**
For each top performer, break down:
- Script formula (e.g., Problem > Agitation > Solution > Benefits > CTA)
- Visuals for each sequence
- Personas and energy of people shown
- Concept and creative style
- Pacing (fast, slow, normal)

**Sub-step C -- Comments on Ads:**
- Analyze comments on top-performing ads (both Instagram and Facebook)
- Categorize: Positive reactions, Negative reactions, Specific objections
- These objections feed directly into the off-ramp/sales sequence of future ads

### Step 2: Support Documents

**Document A -- Code Bank:**
- Extract ALL reviews (Amazon, website, Trustpilot)
- Categorize by: personas, outcomes, benefits, pain points
- Look for LONGER reviews with stories
- Identify specific language customers use (e.g., "fresher" not "glowy")
- Find "connecting threads" -- how customers discovered the product

**Document B -- Personas:**
For each persona document:
- Age, gender, country
- Tangible desires (physical/measurable outcomes)
- Intangible desires (emotional/invisible byproducts)
- Tangible pain points
- Intangible pain points
- Past purchases + why they didn't work

**Document C -- Post-Purchase Survey Questions:**
Recommended questions to implement:
1. How would you describe us to a friend?
2. How long did you know about the brand before purchasing?
3. What was going on in your life that made you buy?
4. Why did you buy from us?
5. What almost kept you from buying? (reveals objections)

**Document D -- Awareness Level Assessment**
(Covered in Step 3 below)

### Step 3: Manual Research (Reverse Engineering Customers)

Process for identifying audience awareness level:
1. Google search: "how to get rid of [problem]"
2. Reddit: Open 15-20 threads, identify awareness patterns
3. At each stage: Are people discussing problems? Solutions? Specific products?
4. Where there is most engagement = where most of your audience sits

### Step 4: Competition Research

**AdSpy (primary tool):**
- Filter: keyword, country, total likes (1000+), media type, date range
- Break down 4-6 winning ads: script, visuals, personas, concept, framework

**Meta Ad Library (secondary):**
- Filter: language, format, active status
- Break down 2-3 additional ads

Total: ~8-10 competition ads analyzed

---

## OUTPUT DOCUMENT

The research brief output should contain:

### 1. Code Bank Summary
- Top 10 customer language phrases (exact words)
- Categorized by: outcomes, benefits, pain points, personas
- Connecting threads (how customers discovered the product)

### 2. Persona Profiles (2-3 personas)
- Demographics + psychographics
- Tangible and intangible desires and pain points
- Shopping behavior and past purchase history

### 3. Awareness Level Assessment
- Where the majority of the target audience sits
- Recommended frameworks by awareness level
- Timing rules for product introduction

### 4. Competition Breakdown
- Top 3-5 competitor angles currently in market
- Gaps and opportunities (what competitors are NOT doing)
- Proven hooks and visual structures to adapt

### 5. Hypotheses for Testing (3-5)
- Each hypothesis follows: "We believe [creative approach] will [expected outcome] because [research evidence]"
- Prioritized by ICE score (Impact x Confidence x Ease)

### 6. Creative Direction Recommendations
- Recommended video frameworks by persona
- Recommended copy templates by awareness level
- Recommended angles from the 10-angle framework
- Specific hooks to test first
