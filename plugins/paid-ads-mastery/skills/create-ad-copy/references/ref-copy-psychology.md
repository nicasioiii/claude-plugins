# Copy Psychology, Persuasion Principles & Creative Formulas

## THE 95% RULE

95% of purchasing decisions happen in the subconscious mind. People buy on emotion first, then justify with logic. If your ad only talks about features (speed, price, specs), you are missing what drives conversions.

**Before writing any copy, answer three questions:**
1. How does this product improve someone's life?
2. What negative emotions does it eliminate?
3. What positive emotions does it create?

---

## THE EMOTIONAL SANDWICH FRAMEWORK

The highest-converting ad structure follows: **Emotional > Logical > Emotional**

1. **Open with emotion:** Pain point, desire, fear, or aspiration. Create the feeling.
2. **Middle with logic:** Features, specs, proof, social proof. Justify the feeling.
3. **Close with emotion:** Transformation, identity, future state. Reinforce the feeling.

### Example (Supplement):
1. Emotional: "Imagine waking up tomorrow without that stiffness in your knees."
2. Logical: "Our formula contains 1500mg glucosamine + 1200mg chondroitin, clinically shown to support joint comfort in 14 days."
3. Emotional: "Get back to the hikes, the morning walks, the life you thought you'd lost."

### Example (SaaS):
1. Emotional: "Your team wastes 4 hours every week on manual reporting."
2. Logical: "Our dashboard auto-generates 12 report types, integrates with 50+ data sources, refreshes every 15 minutes."
3. Emotional: "Give your team their Fridays back."

## 10 PERSUASION PRINCIPLES FOR COPY

Pick 2-3 per campaign. More than 3 overwhelms the audience.

### 1. Reciprocity
Give something valuable first (free guide, tip, sample) before asking for the sale. The viewer feels obligated to reciprocate.
**Copy trigger:** "Free [valuable thing] included with every order"

### 2. Commitment / Consistency
Get a small yes first. People who take one step are more likely to take the next.
**Copy trigger:** "Take the 30-second quiz to find your match"

### 3. Social Proof
People follow what others do, especially in uncertainty.
**Copy trigger:** "Join 50,000+ customers who..." or "[Number] 5-star reviews"

### 4. Authority
Expert endorsements, credentials, certifications build instant trust.
**Copy trigger:** "Recommended by 2,000+ dermatologists" or "As seen on [publication]"

### 5. Liking
People buy from people they like. Relatable creators, shared values, humor.
**Copy trigger:** Use first-person testimonial format, casual language, personality

### 6. Scarcity
Limited availability increases perceived value.
**Copy trigger:** "Only 147 left in stock" or "Limited batch -- sold out 3x this year"

### 7. Unity
"People like us do things like this." Tribal identity and belonging.
**Copy trigger:** "For [specific identity group] who refuse to settle for..."

### 8. Loss Aversion
People feel losses 2x stronger than equivalent gains. Frame the cost of NOT acting.
**Copy trigger:** "Every day you wait costs you [specific consequence]"

### 9. Anchoring
Present a high number first to make the actual price feel like a deal.
**Copy trigger:** "$247 value -- yours for $49" or "Competitors charge $200+. We charge $59."

### 10. Pratfall Effect
Small, honest admissions of imperfection increase trust and likability.
**Copy trigger:** "It's not the prettiest packaging -- but what's inside is [remarkable claim]"

## WINNING CREATIVE FORMULAS

### PAS (Problem > Agitation > Solution)
1. State the problem the reader experiences
2. Agitate it -- make them feel the pain more intensely
3. Present your product as the solution

**Best for:** Problem-aware audiences, pain-driven products
**Example:** "Neck pain after every night? (Problem) It gets worse -- bad sleep posture compounds into chronic issues by age 40. (Agitation) The [Product] realigns your spine in your sleep. (Solution)"

### FAB (Feature > Advantage > Benefit)
1. State a feature of your product
2. Explain the advantage it creates
3. Connect to the emotional benefit

**Best for:** Solution-aware audiences, products with clear differentiators
**Example:** "Made from recycled ocean plastic (Feature). 3x stronger than regular nylon (Advantage). Feel good knowing your purchase removes trash from the ocean (Benefit)."

### BAB (Before > After > Bridge)
1. Describe life before your product
2. Describe life after your product
3. Show your product as the bridge

**Best for:** Product-aware audiences, transformation-driven products
**Example:** "Before: 3 hours editing every video, missing deadlines. After: Professional edits in 20 minutes, clients impressed. Bridge: [Product] automates the tedious parts so you can focus on the creative."

### OCR (Objection > Counter > Reframe)
1. State the common objection directly
2. Counter it with evidence
3. Reframe the objection as a reason to buy

**Best for:** Product-aware audiences who are hesitant, competitive markets
**Example:** "'It's too expensive.' (Objection) A single chiropractor visit costs $75. Our pillow costs $89 and works every night for 5+ years. (Counter) The real question isn't whether you can afford it -- it's whether you can afford another year of bad sleep. (Reframe)"

## AWARENESS LEVEL COPY FRAMEWORKS

### Unaware Audience Copy
- Lead with curiosity or shocking fact
- NO product mention in first 50% of copy
- Educate about the problem first
- Use question hooks: "Did you know...?" / "Have you ever wondered...?"
- Longer copy needed (300+ words)

### Problem-Aware Audience Copy
- Lead with the problem: validate their experience
- Agitate before introducing solution
- Product introduction at 30-40% through copy
- PAS formula works perfectly here

### Solution-Aware Audience Copy
- Lead with differentiation: why YOUR solution is different
- Product introduction within first 2-3 sentences
- Focus on unique mechanism or approach
- FAB formula works well here

### Product-Aware Audience Copy
- Lead with social proof or comparison
- Direct product introduction in first sentence
- Address specific objections (price, effectiveness)
- OCR formula is strongest here

### Most-Aware Audience Copy
- Lead with the offer: discount, bundle, urgency
- Copy can be very short (under 50 words)
- Focus on CTA and friction removal
- "Last chance" / "Ends tonight" / "Only X left" language

## LIFE FORCE 8 DRIVES (APPLIED TO COPY)

These 8 human drives are hardwired and can power any ad angle:

1. **Survival / life extension** -- health, longevity, energy
2. **Enjoyment of food/beverages** -- taste, nutrition, convenience
3. **Freedom from fear, pain, danger** -- safety, relief, protection
4. **Sexual companionship** -- attractiveness, relationships, intimacy
5. **Comfortable living conditions** -- home, convenience, luxury
6. **To be superior / winning** -- status, competition, achievement
7. **Care for loved ones** -- family, pets, children, partners
8. **Social approval** -- belonging, reputation, recognition

**How to use:** Identify which 1-2 drives your product connects to. Build your hook and CTA around satisfying that drive. The product is the BRIDGE between the drive and its fulfillment.

## MESSAGE SEQUENCING PLAN (4-EXPOSURE STRATEGY)

For campaigns running multiple touchpoints:

| Exposure | Goal | Copy Focus |
|----------|------|-----------|
| 1st | Introduce the problem | Pain point, curiosity, pattern interrupt |
| 2nd | Reveal the solution | Education, unique mechanism, differentiation |
| 3rd | Provide proof | Social proof, testimonials, demos, data |
| 4th | Present offer with urgency | Discount, guarantee, scarcity, strong CTA |

Same core message across all 4, but different format and angle each time. Cycle through video, static, carousel, and different copy templates.
