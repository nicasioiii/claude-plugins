# Static Creative Anatomy & Design Principles

## THE 5 ELEMENTS OF STATIC AD ANATOMY

Every static ad has these elements, ordered by importance:

### 1. Image/Photo (Primary Visual)
- Relatable, casual aesthetic (iPhone selfie vibe outperforms studio shots)
- **Smiling people = lower CPMs** -- Meta pushes ads with positive emotions because the platform wants users to associate positive feelings with scrolling
- Include people who match the target persona (age, gender, setting)
- Left side typically holds the image; right side holds text overlay

### 2. Headline (Most Important Text Element)
- The hook equivalent for static ads -- this is what makes someone stop scrolling
- Visible immediately without clicking
- Use CAPS, emojis, or contrasting color to make it pop
- Maximum 6-8 words for instant comprehension
- Must address a specific pain point or promise a specific benefit

### 3. Body Text (Supporting Element)
- Bridge between the hook (headline) and the action (CTA)
- 1-2 supporting sentences maximum on the creative itself
- Reinforces the headline promise with a specific detail
- NOT the same as the Meta primary text (that's the copy above the image)

### 4. CTA (Call to Action)
- "Shop Now" for product pages
- "Learn More" for advertorials/articles
- Can be a button overlay on the creative or the Meta CTA button
- Lowest-impact element for statics -- focus energy on headline first

### 5. Product Image (Optional)
- Can replace or supplement the CTA
- Show the actual product clearly
- Works best when product is visually distinctive

## THE "ART OF 1 FRAME" PRINCIPLE

A static ad must communicate its entire message in a single frame. Unlike video, you have zero seconds of motion to explain.

**The test:** Can someone scrolling at full speed understand what you're selling and why they should care in under 1 second?

If the answer is no, simplify:
- Reduce text to headline + one supporting line
- Make the visual self-explanatory
- Remove any element that doesn't serve the core message

## HOOK TEXT CONDENSING TECHNIQUE

Take verbose descriptions and compress them into punchy static headline text:

**Before:** "I used to spend hours at the gym doing cardio but never saw results until I discovered this 15 minute workout routine"

**After:** "I replaced 2 hours of cardio with 15 minutes of THIS"

### Condensing Rules:
1. Keep the contrast (before vs. after)
2. Cut all filler words
3. Add a curiosity element ("THIS," "one thing," "secret")
4. Maximum 8-10 words
5. Use numbers for specificity ("2 hours" not "hours and hours")

### More Examples:
- Long: "After trying dozens of skincare products that didn't work, I finally found one that cleared my acne in two weeks"
- Condensed: "12 products failed. This one cleared my skin in 14 days."

- Long: "My dog used to scratch himself raw every single night until we switched to this new food"
- Condensed: "He stopped scratching DAY ONE."

## STATIC ITERATION PRIORITY (HIGHEST TO LOWEST)

1. **Headline** -- highest impact, always iterate first
2. **Body text / testimonial** -- second priority
3. **Color scheme** -- surprisingly impactful (test warm vs cool, dark vs light backgrounds)
4. **CTA text** -- lowest impact for statics

When iterating, change ONLY ONE element at a time. Run 3-5 day test minimum.

---

## FORMAT SPECIFICATIONS

### Testing Order
1. Start with **1:1 (square)** for feed testing -- works across most placements
2. Once you have a winner, adapt to **9:16 (vertical)** for Stories/Reels
3. Create **4:5** variant for optimal feed real estate

### Safe Zones
- Top 15%: Avoid text (profile/username overlay on Stories)
- Bottom 20%: Avoid text (CTA buttons, swipe-up UI on Stories)
- Center 65%: Primary message zone

### Resolution Requirements
- Minimum 1080x1080 for 1:1
- Minimum 1080x1920 for 9:16
- Keep file size under 30MB for fast load times

## SMILING PEOPLE RULE (CPM OPTIMIZATION)

Meta's algorithm favors ads with positive emotional signals:
- **Smiling faces** in creative = lower CPMs (Meta wants positive user experience)
- **Relatable people** (not models) outperform stock photography
- **iPhone/casual aesthetic** beats studio-polished shots for DR campaigns
- Fashion/luxury brands are the exception -- quality visuals still matter there

### Image Selection Priority:
1. Smiling person holding/using product in natural setting
2. Before/after comparison (where appropriate)
3. Product-in-use lifestyle shot
4. Product hero shot with text overlay
5. Pure text/graphic (lowest performer for most categories)

## STATIC AD TYPES BY AWARENESS LEVEL

### Unaware Audience
- Lead with curiosity or education: "Did you know 73% of [demographic] [surprising fact]?"
- NO product in image -- use relatable person or attention-grabbing visual
- Headline focuses on the problem, not the solution

### Problem-Aware Audience
- Lead with the problem: "[Problem] keeping you up at night?"
- Person experiencing the problem in the image
- Product may appear small/secondary in frame

### Solution-Aware Audience
- Lead with the solution category: "The [solution type] that actually works"
- Show product clearly with supporting evidence
- Include a differentiator ("Unlike [common alternative]...")

### Product-Aware Audience
- Lead with social proof or comparison: "Why 50,000+ chose [Product]"
- Product prominently featured
- Reviews, star ratings, customer count as overlay text

### Most-Aware Audience
- Lead with the offer: "60% OFF -- Today Only"
- Product + price/discount prominently featured
- Strong urgency/scarcity elements

## SHOW, DON'T TELL FRAMEWORK (FOR STATIC VISUALS)

For every product benefit, create a three-column mapping:

| Product Benefit | Desire Fulfilled | How to Show in Static |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Waterproof phone case | Protection, peace of mind | Photo: phone submerged in pool, still working |
| Wrinkle cream | Youthful appearance | Before/after close-up (real person, not model) |
| Dog supplement | Healthy, happy pet | Photo: energetic dog running/playing |
| Standing desk | Back pain relief | Person smiling at desk vs. person hunched at regular desk |

**Visual information is processed 60,000x faster than text.** If you can SHOW the benefit instead of stating it, always show it.

---

## STATIC AD CHECKLIST (PRE-LAUNCH)

1. Headline is under 8 words and creates instant comprehension
2. Smiling or emotionally engaged person in the image
3. Product is visible (unless targeting unaware audience)
4. Text passes the 1-second scroll test
5. Safe zones are respected (no text cut off in Stories)
6. One core message only
7. Color contrast makes text readable over image
8. CTA is clear (even if just the Meta button)
9. Image resolution is minimum 1080px on shortest side
10. File format is JPG or PNG under 30MB
