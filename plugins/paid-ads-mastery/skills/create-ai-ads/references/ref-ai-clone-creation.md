# AI Clone Creation

## GOOGLE FLOW CLONE METHOD (SHORT-FORM, 8-SEC CLIPS)

### Process
1. Use "Frames to Video" mode in Google Flow
2. Upload photo of yourself/client
3. Write dialogue in the prompt field
4. Set to Veo 3 Fast, 4 outputs, portrait or landscape
5. Generate and select best result
6. Repeat for each 8-second script segment

### Photo Tips for Cloning
- Use multiple different photos for different scenes (creates visual variety)
- Sometimes professional photos trigger "prominent figures" block -- try casual photos
- Can create entire ads using different photos of the same person for each scene
- Gather 5-10 usable photos before starting
- Good lighting, looking at camera, clearly visible face

### Best For
- Short social media ads (15-60 seconds, assembled from 8-sec clips)
- Quick iterations and testing
- When you need unlimited generations (included in Ultra subscription)

### Limitations
- 8-second maximum per clip
- Character may not be perfectly consistent across clips
- Voice is AI-generated (not your actual voice)
- Cannot do long continuous takes

## HEYGEN CLONE METHOD (LONG-FORM, UP TO 30 MIN)

### What HeyGen Does
- Creates a full digital twin (avatar) that speaks any script up to 30 minutes
- Clones both appearance AND voice
- Best for: YouTube videos, long-form social, course content, webinar-style ads

### Setup Process
1. Go to HeyGen > Avatars > Create New > Start from a Video
2. Record yourself speaking for minimum 2 minutes (recommended 2-5 min)
3. Upload the video
4. HeyGen processes and creates your avatar (several minutes)
5. Go to avatar > Create with AI Studio
6. Paste any script > Generate > Download

### Recording Requirements
| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Format | 16:9 (landscape) or 9:16 (portrait) |
| Audio | Very quiet environment |
| Lighting | Well lit, lots of light |
| Camera | Phone on tripod or professional camera |
| Speaking | Clear, enunciated, natural pauses |
| Hands | Keep below chest level -- hands near face confuse the AI |
| Movement | Minimal, natural, not excessive |
| Continuity | One continuous take -- no cuts |
| Mouth | Close mouth between words (helps AI model lip movements) |
| Topic | Talk about anything comfortable for 2+ minutes |
| Editing | Only trim beginning (pressing record) and end (pressing stop) |

### Pricing
- $29/month for avatar creation with voice cloning
- Free version available but does NOT clone voice
- Multi-language support available

### Output Quality
- Close but not perfect -- recommend adding B-roll and other elements
- Cut away to product shots, demos, text overlays to disguise AI artifacts
- SubMagic can add automatic B-roll and captions to improve quality

### Google Flow vs HeyGen Comparison
| Feature | Google Flow | HeyGen |
|---------|------------|--------|
| Max length | 8 sec per clip | 30 min continuous |
| Voice | AI-generated (not yours) | Cloned from your recording |
| Appearance | From uploaded photo | From uploaded video |
| Cost | Included in Ultra ($125/mo) | $29/month additional |
| Best for | Short ads, social content | YouTube, long-form, courses |
| Iterations | Unlimited with Ultra | Limited by subscription tier |

## VOICE CLONING WITH ELEVENLABS

### Setup
1. Sign up at elevenlabs.com (free tier available)
2. Record 5-10 minutes of clear audio reading their provided script
3. Quiet environment essential -- background noise degrades clone quality
4. Upload recording
5. AI learns speech patterns and tone

### Use Cases
- Voiceovers for HeyGen videos (clone voice once, never re-record)
- Multi-language versions of content
- Narration for non-narrated UGC (overlay voice on b-roll footage)
- AI receptionist for business phone lines
- Export as MP3 or WAV for any audio project

### Workflow Integration
- ElevenLabs (voice) + HeyGen (avatar) = complete AI clone for unlimited content
- Clone voice once, use across unlimited scripts
- Can use ElevenLabs voice with Google Flow clips (replace AI-generated voice in CapCut)

## AI CLONE SCRIPT OPENING FORMULA

For AI clone ads, the self-aware opening creates novelty and entertainment:

**Formula:**
"Hey, I'm not really [Name], your [role]. I'm just her AI clone."
"The real [Name] is [doing something impressive/busy] right now."

**Important:** Avoid saying the real person is "on vacation" or "relaxing" -- makes it look like they waste client money. Instead: "The real Jess is way too busy booking amazing luxury vacations for other couples right now."

**Closing with AI humor:**
"I'll still be here working because AI doesn't get vacation days."

The AI clone format is itself a differentiator and attention-grabber. The "I'm not really [Name]" hook inherently creates curiosity.

## ANTI-TESTIMONIAL STYLE FOR AI ADS

Since the avatar is AI-generated, it cannot claim to be a real customer testimonial.

**Instead, the AI spokesperson:**
- Discusses product benefits from a knowledgeable perspective
- Explains pain points and solutions
- Describes results without claiming personal experience
- Focuses on why the audience should be interested

**UGC-style AI ads** frame the AI as a casual person talking about the product (not as a customer).

## QUALITY CHECKLIST FOR AI CLONE ADS

1. Dialogue is clear and properly paced (not too slow or fast)
2. Lip sync looks natural (regenerate if off)
3. Character is consistent across all scenes
4. No AI artifacts visible (weird pauses, extra breathing, accent shifts)
5. Dead space trimmed from all clips
6. Multiple scene changes for visual variety
7. Music and sound effects added
8. Captions present and readable
9. Total length under 60 seconds for social platforms
10. Strong hook in first 3 seconds
11. Clear CTA at the end
