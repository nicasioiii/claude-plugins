# Carousel Frameworks Reference

## Attraction Carousel Formats (Detail)

### Format 1: Tips / Step-by-Step Education
- Loose tips, "how to" guides, awareness-raising
- Structure: Hook > Tip 1 > Tip 2 > Tip 3 > Tip 4 > Takeaway > CTA
- Keep each tip to one slide with one visual

### Format 2: News / Commentary
- Grab attention with trending topic, add your expert commentary
- Structure: News hook > Why it matters > Your perspective > What to do about it > CTA
- Timeliness is critical -- post within 24-48 hours of the trend

### Format 3: Famous References
- Hijack a recognizable reference (Harry Potter, Friends, popular culture)
- Structure: Reference hook > Connection to your topic > Lessons > Application > CTA
- Audience already knows the reference = instant engagement

## Differentiation Carousel Formats (Detail)

### Format 1: Opinion
- Share a somewhat polemic point of view with education embedded
- Must be genuine -- fake controversy backfires
- Structure: Bold opinion > Why you believe this > Evidence > Nuance > CTA

### Format 2: Common Enemy
- Unite your audience by blaming a shared external force
- "The algorithm," "traditional education," "outdated industry practices"
- Structure: Enemy identification > How it hurts them > Why it persists > Your alternative > CTA

### Format 3: Duality
- Contrast old vs. new, before vs. after, wrong vs. right
- Structure: What most people do > What actually works > Side-by-side comparison > Result > CTA
- Visual contrast between slides is powerful

### Format 4: Punchline
- Each page delivers a new standalone punchline leading to conclusion
- Every slide must work independently AND build toward the whole
- Structure: Setup > Punchline 1 > Punchline 2 > Punchline 3 > Conclusion > CTA

### Format 5: Timeline
- Chronological progression of events (logos, jobs, experiences)
- Structure: Starting point > Key milestone 1 > Key milestone 2 > Where you are now > What you learned > CTA

### 10 Differentiation Topic Ideas
1. How you began your journey
2. Turning points that shaped your path
3. Achievement you are proud of
4. How you do things differently
5. Inspirational influences
6. Challenges overcome (one per post)
7. Timeline of anything relevant
8. A touching personal story
9. Common challenge you overcame that audience faces
10. Strong opinions about your niche topic

## Hybrid Carousel Structure (The Money Format)

### Requirements
1. Problem or desire awareness
2. Strategic narrative (not just a story)
3. Perspective shift that generates emotions

### Narrative Structure
1. **Context** -- What is happening in their world
2. **New perspective** -- Shift how they see their situation
3. **Truth** -- The real solution or insight
4. **Proof** -- Your results, client results, data

### The Perspective Shift
The goal: Shift audience from wanting X (what they think they need) to wanting Y (what you actually sell).

**Example:** A carousel offering "100 Instagram story ideas" that pivots to explaining why ideas alone do not work -- you need STRATEGIES. This shifts desire from "content ideas" to "a content system."

**Revenue benchmark:** A single well-crafted hybrid carousel with DM automation generated 15,000+ euros in sales. The key: strong narrative > perspective shift > proof > easy CTA (comment emoji) > DM automation > sale.

## The Desire Equation

To generate desire in content, demonstrate four things:

### 1. Context
Does the audience understand what you are talking about? Does it make logical sense from start to finish? Cut excesses.

### 2. Relatability
Are hooks meeting audience where they are? Using their words? Making situations specific (not vague)?

### 3. Emotion
Which SINGLE emotion will you bring? Options: Fear, excitement, shock, aha moment, exclusivity, boosting limitations, surprise. Add curiosity as a booster.

**Rule:** Keep each post to a single emotion to avoid overload. Unless the emotions connect very well.

### 4. Trust
Are you bringing arguments that make them trust you? Testimonials, DM screenshots, past experiences, past failures, past results.

**Exercise:** Take a plain business story and layer in context > relatability > emotion > trust. Each layer transforms generic content into compelling content.

## Audience Research Framework (3 Questions for Carousel Content)

1. **What is your audience struggling with?** Go beyond technical answers. What keeps them up at night? What are the CONSEQUENCES? (Not "they don't know Canva" but "their designs aren't eye-catching, they're losing followers")
2. **What do they want the most and WHY?** Go beyond money/fame. (Not "make more money" but "provide the life they want through their business")
3. **What are they feeling towards your niche?** Name the specific emotion (frustrated, overwhelmed, skeptical, hopeful)

**Target:** At least 10-20 pain points with consequences for question 1.
**If you do not know:** Create feedback systems -- Google Forms, poll stories, 30-minute client conversations.

## Content Ideation System (5 Steps)

1. **Map content idea sources** -- train your brain to notice ideas throughout daily life
2. **Turn each idea into a hook or concept** -- not "no time" but "I don't have time to create content AND run my business"
3. **Keep all ideas in one place** -- Notes app, notebook, DM yourself
4. **Write full scripts/idea development** -- separate from design, use Trello or similar
5. **Create visuals LAST** -- only after text is finalized

### 7 Content Idea Sources
1. Listen to ideal client (FAQs, DM questions, competitor comments)
2. Your offers (every piece of content must relate to what you sell)
3. Trending topics (piggyback on celebrities/news)
4. Seasonality and categories
5. Content research (follow accounts from OTHER niches that match your vibe)
6. Books, blogs, podcasts
7. Client results and your own results

**Contrarian:** Do not sit down to brainstorm ideas. Follow accounts from OTHER niches matching your vibe, cross-reference their ideas into your niche. You will never copy anyone this way.

## Reels vs. Carousels Strategic Split

- **Reels** = Hook to your world (reach, attract new audience)
- **Carousels** = Walking, living, breathing culture for your community and offer (engage, convert, build)

Carousels generate sales even when reels do not. First 13,000 followers from reels generated only 3 sales. Carousels from the same audience generated 2,000 euros.

## Conversion Carousel Formats (Detail)

### Format 1: Testimonial Stack
- Structure: Bold claim > Testimonial 1 > Testimonial 2 > Testimonial 3 > Your offer > CTA
- Each slide = one customer result with specific numbers
- Final slides pivot to "Here's how to get the same results"
- Best for: product-aware audiences, bottom-of-funnel retargeting

### Format 2: Before/After
- Structure: Before state > The problem > The turning point > After state > How > CTA
- Visual contrast between before and after slides is essential
- Use real customer transformations (with permission) over stock imagery
- Best for: visual products (fitness, beauty, home improvement)

### Format 3: Objection Destroyer
- Structure: Common objection > Why it's wrong > Proof > Second objection > Why it's wrong > Proof > CTA
- Each objection gets 2 slides: the objection stated, then the counter with evidence
- Best for: audiences stuck in consideration phase, retargeting

### Format 4: Product Demo
- Structure: Problem > "Watch what happens when..." > Step 1 > Step 2 > Step 3 > Result > CTA
- Each slide shows one step of using the product
- Works well when the product has a satisfying visual transformation
- Best for: physical products with visible results

## Design Rules for Carousel Ads

### Typography
- Maximum 2 fonts per carousel (one for headlines, one for body)
- Headline font: bold, high contrast, readable at small size
- Body font: clean, simple, high legibility
- Minimum font size: 24pt for headlines, 16pt for body text (mobile readability)

### Color
- Use 2-3 brand colors maximum per carousel
- High contrast between text and background (dark text on light, or light text on dark)
- Consistent color palette across all slides (visual cohesion)
- Use color to create hierarchy: one color for headlines, one for emphasis, neutral for body

### Layout
- Keep text in the safe zone (avoid top 15% and bottom 20% of the image)
- One main idea per slide -- never overcrowd
- Consistent placement of elements across slides (text always in same position)
- Leave breathing room -- white space is not wasted space

### Slide Count
- Minimum: 5 slides (fewer feels incomplete)
- Maximum: 10 slides (more than 10 = viewer drop-off)
- Sweet spot: 7-8 slides for most formats
- First slide gets 80% of views; each subsequent slide loses 10-15% of viewers
- Front-load your strongest content

### First Slide Rules
- Must work as a standalone image (most people only see slide 1)
- Include a hook or question that creates curiosity to swipe
- Never put the punchline on slide 1 (give them a reason to continue)
- Visual arrow or "Swipe" indicator increases swipe rate

## Carousel Ad Placement Notes

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
- Format: 1:1 (square) for feed, 9:16 for stories
- Max 10 cards per carousel
- Each card can have its own headline, description, and URL
- For DPA (Dynamic Product Ads): carousel pulls from product catalog automatically

### Organic Instagram Carousels
- Format: 1:1 (square) or 4:5 (vertical, more real estate)
- Max 20 slides per post (as of 2024+)
- First slide = hook, last slide = CTA or save-worthy takeaway
- Instagram algorithm rewards carousels with high save rates and swipe-through rates
- Repost/reshare carousels reset the algorithm distribution cycle
