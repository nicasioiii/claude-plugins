# Thumbnail Design Principles

## The AHQ Framework

A good thumbnail does three things:

### A -- Attention
Grabs the viewer's eye on a crowded homepage or feed.

Attention-grabbing techniques:
- Text (easy to read, minimal words)
- Color and contrast
- Emotion (facial expressions)
- Numbers
- Unique/interesting photos
- Great design (feels safe, credible)
- Things your audience values (money, logos, status symbols)
- Faces (humans relate to other humans)

**Rule:** Present ONE really interesting thing. All techniques should serve one central idea.

### H -- Highlight
Shows what the content is about and creates an expectation.

Two questions to answer:
1. What is this video about?
2. What is the MOST REMARKABLE thing about it?

The thumbnail should highlight the most remarkable aspect, not just the general topic.

### Q -- Question
Creates a question the target viewer wants answered. This generates the curiosity gap that compels the click.

**Example:** "The real reason dogs lick you" with a dog photo and disgusted face -- 7 million views. The question created: "Wait, is it not because they love me?"

## Composition Rules

### Rule 1: Limit to Three Main Elements
- Too many elements (7+) confuse the viewer
- Three elements (person, text, background) is ideal
- The brain should instantly understand what the video is about

### Rule 2: Rule of Thirds
- Divide thumbnail into thirds horizontally and vertically
- Place key elements along lines and intersection points
- Face on one third line, text on another
- Creates satisfying visual balance

### Rule 3: Highlight One Key Thing
- One element must clearly be the focal point
- Everything else supports that one thing
- If everything is emphasized, nothing is emphasized

---

## Color Principles

- Leverage CONTRAST, not just bright colors
- The key is making elements contrast against EACH OTHER
- Use a color wheel for complementary colors (blue and orange = strong contrast)
- Consider both light mode AND dark mode display
- White thumbnails pop dramatically in dark mode
- Small color changes have outsized impact (gray to white text = massive difference)

---

## Text Rules

- **Maximum 6 words** (strong guide, not absolute rule)
- Easy to read (clear font, sufficient size)
- Text should CONNECT with the title (work together, not repeat each other)
- Thumbnail text and title should complete each other's thought

---

## Design Mistakes to Avoid

1. Text overload (more than 6 words)
2. Overcrowding elements (more than 3 main elements)
3. No clear hierarchy (viewer does not know what to look at)
4. No one clear idea
5. Title and image do not relate
6. Poor color contrast (text hard to read, subject bleeds into background)
7. Bad lighting in photos

---

## The 8-Step Thumbnail Creation Process

1. **Make the thumbnail BEFORE the video** -- if they do not click, they do not watch
2. **Allocate enough time** -- 30 minutes minimum, 2 hours maximum
3. **Do your research** -- search your topic on the platform, see what competitors use
4. **Plan one or more concepts** -- sketch or screenshot inspiration thumbnails
5. **Get a good photo** -- take 50-200+ photos. iPhone is fine. One key light in front of you.
6. **Make more than one variation** -- duplicate in Canva, change elements, compare
7. **Preview in context** -- use browser extensions or tools to see how it looks among real content
8. **Review against AHQ checklist** -- Attention? Highlight? Question?

## Canva Production Workflow

### Person Photo Enhancement
1. Remove background from person photo (Canva background remover)
2. Layer cutout person on TOP of original (enables separate adjustments)
3. Brighten person: Brightness +26-30, slight contrast boost, slight highlights, sharpness +10-15
4. Background: Lower temperature (slightly cool/blue), blur 15-25, reduce saturation slightly

### Color Manipulation
- Use Magic Edit to change clothing colors for contrast (blue background = orange shirt)
- Use Magic Expand to extend images when composition does not fit
- Use Magic Eraser to remove distracting background elements

### Export
- 1920 x 1080 PNG for YouTube
- 1080 x 1080 or 1080 x 1350 for Instagram/Facebook
- Always export as PNG (not JPEG) for text clarity

## Thumbnail Photo Tips

- Take LOTS of photos (50-200+)
- Good lighting is the #1 factor -- one key light in front, not overhead
- iPhone photos are perfectly fine
- Screenshots from HD video also work (many podcasts do this)
- Use AI image enhancers (Remini.ai) for lower-quality photos
- Have a photo WITH background AND a photo of JUST the background (makes editing easier)

## Thumbnail in Ad Context

### For Video Ads (Social Media)
- The thumbnail is the static frame shown BEFORE the video auto-plays
- On some placements (feed with auto-play off), thumbnail IS the first impression
- Design thumbnail as part of the hook framework, not as an afterthought

### Five Elements of an Effective Hook (ACA Framework)
1. **Persona** -- who appears (should resemble target audience)
2. **Message** -- bold problem statement
3. **Visual** -- engaging, shocking, or satisfying image
4. **Sound** -- pattern-interrupting audio
5. **Thumbnail** -- the static frame before video plays

The thumbnail works WITH the hook, not separately from it.

---

## Understanding Your Target Viewer

**Contrarian:** Understanding your specific target viewer can override general design principles. Thumbnails that look "wrong" by design standards can outperform when they match the audience aesthetic.

Example: Thumbnails with lots of text and cluttered design performing well in the feminine energy/self-improvement niche because they match that audience's aesthetic preferences.

**The rule:** Learn the design principles first, then break them intentionally when you deeply understand your audience.
