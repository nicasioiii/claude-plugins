# Google Ads Creative & Feed

Google ads span multiple formats: Shopping (product titles + images), Search (text headlines + descriptions), YouTube (video scripts), and Performance Max (multi-asset optimization). This skill covers creative for all.

## GOOGLE SHOPPING: PRODUCT TITLES

### Title Optimization Strategy

**Rule: 50% of Shopping ad success comes from title optimization.**

Product titles are scanned left-to-right. First 40 characters get ~50% visibility. Titles affect both:
- Relevance (matching customer search queries)
- CTR (title clarity encourages clicks)
- Quality Score (accurate titles = higher score)

### Title Structure (Best Performing)

**Format:** [Primary Keyword] [Variant] + [Brand/Model] + [Key Feature/Color]

**Example breakdown:**
```
Primary keyword: "Yoga Mat" (main product category)
Variant: "Non-slip, 6mm thickness" (what customer is searching)
Brand: "Liforme" (brand name)
Key feature: "Eco-friendly Natural Rubber, Blue"

Full title: "Yoga Mat Non-Slip 6mm Liforme Eco-Friendly Natural Rubber Blue"
Character count: 64 (under 70 character limit, optimal)
```

### Keyword Research for Titles

1. **Google Search:** Type product category, review auto-complete
   - Shows real customer search queries
   - Use exact phrasing in title

2. **Google Shopping:** Search your product, note winning competitor titles
   - What keywords appear in top 3 ad titles?
   - Are they in position 1-10 or position 20+?
   - High position titles have better keyword match

3. **Google Keyword Planner:** Research search volume
   - Focus on keywords with 50-1000+ monthly searches
   - Avoid super-niche keywords (low search volume)
   - Avoid overly broad keywords (too much competition)

### What Works in Titles

**Include:**
- Primary keyword (search term you're targeting)
- Product variant (size, color, material that customer specifies)
- Brand name (if brand matters for your category)
- Specific dimensions/quantity (helps customer decide)
- Key benefit if relevant (non-toxic, eco-friendly, waterproof)

**Avoid:**
- Keyword stuffing ("yoga mat yoga mat yoga mats yoga")
- Generic descriptions ("nice yoga mat")
- Excessive punctuation (!!!, ???)
- Superlatives without proof ("best", "premium" unsubstantiated)
- ALL CAPS (reduces readability)

### Title Examples by Category

**Example 1: Yoga Mat**
- Bad: "Yoga Mat"
- Better: "Yoga Mat Non-Slip 6mm Premium Natural Rubber Black"
- Why: Includes variant (non-slip, 6mm), material, color = matches customer search

**Example 2: Coffee Mug**
- Bad: "Coffee Mug Ceramic"
- Better: "Ceramic Coffee Mug 12oz Insulated Vacuum Keep Hot 8 Hours Dishwasher Safe"
- Why: Includes capacity (12oz), key benefit (keep hot 8 hours), practical feature (dishwasher safe)

**Example 3: Phone Case**
- Bad: "Phone Case for iPhone"
- Better: "iPhone 15 Pro Max Case Leather Slim Profile Magnetic MagSafe Compatible"
- Why: Specific model (iPhone 15 Pro Max), material (leather), key feature (MagSafe)

## GOOGLE SHOPPING: PRODUCT IMAGES

### Image Quality Standards

**Minimum images:** 3-5 high-quality images
**Aspect ratio:** Square (1:1) preferred, can test 4:5 (portrait) for fashion

**Image types to include:**
1. **Hero shot:** Product centered on white/clean background (main image)
2. **Detail shot:** Close-up of key feature or material
3. **Lifestyle shot:** Product in use context (person using, or in room)
4. **Alternative angle:** Different view/color/size if applicable
5. **Size/scale reference:** Product next to common object for scale

### Image Best Practices

**What works:**
- Bright, well-lit images (no shadows or dark areas)
- Minimal text overlay (pure product shots beat text-heavy)
- Clean background (white, neutral, or non-distracting)
- Product fills 50-80% of frame (not tiny, not overwhelming)
- Multiple angles (not just one view)

**What doesn't work:**
- Dark/blurry images (reduces CTR)
- Watermarks or heavy text (reduces professional perception)
- Models in bad positioning (awkward poses reduce credibility)
- Inconsistent styling (image 1 professional, image 2 amateur = confuses customer)
- Irrelevant background (product kitchen scene doesn't belong in category)

### Image Formatting

**Technical specs:**
- File size: 1-2MB (larger loads slower)
- Format: JPG or PNG
- Dimensions: 1024x1024px minimum (larger is better)
- Color space: RGB (not CMYK)

## GOOGLE SHOPPING: PRODUCT DESCRIPTIONS

### Description Purpose

Product descriptions serve:
- Secondary keyword targeting (for long-tail searches)
- Customer education (what can they do with this?)
- Objection handling (size, material, care instructions)
- Trust building (mentions certifications, testing, quality)

### Description Structure

**Recommended length:** 50-200 words (balance completeness with readability)

**Structure:**
1. **Opening line (1 sentence):** What is the product, key benefit
2. **Problem it solves (1-2 sentences):** What customer pain does it address?
3. **Key features (3-5 bullet points):** Main specifications, dimensions, materials
4. **Benefits (2-3 sentences):** Emotional outcomes, lifestyle improvement
5. **Use cases (1-2 sentences):** When/how customer uses product
6. **Care/warranty (1-2 sentences):** How to maintain, warranty period

**Example description:**

```
The Liforme Yoga Mat is an eco-friendly, non-slip 6mm mat designed for studios and home practice.

This mat solves alignment confusion—the built-in alignment lines help yogis perfect their pose geometry and avoid common mistakes that lead to injury.

SPECIFICATIONS:
- Dimensions: 68" x 24" x 0.24" (6mm thickness)
- Material: 100% natural rubber, biodegradable
- Weight: 3.5lbs
- Non-slip surface, both sides

BENEFITS:
Transform your practice with a mat engineered for comfort. The premium natural rubber provides superior grip whether you're in power yoga or gentle stretching. Alignment guides prevent form breakdown during challenging poses.

IDEAL FOR:
Professional yogis, studio instructors, home practitioners seeking alignment precision.

CARE:
Hand wash with mild soap, air dry. Do not machine wash. 5-year durability guarantee on seams and material.
```

### Key Guidelines

- Write for customer understanding (not internal jargon)
- Include actual dimensions/measurements (reduces returns)
- Mention materials if relevant to category (people care about quality)
- Be honest about limitations (don't overclaim)
- Answer common questions (care, sizing, durability)

## GOOGLE SHOPPING: PRODUCT PRICING STRATEGY

### Pricing Rules (What Kills Performance)

**Don't:**
- Price everything 50% off (signals low quality, won't sustain)
- Use inflated "Compare-At" pricing (customers see through it)
- Change prices hourly (confuses algorithm, reduces trust)
- Price irrationally vs competitors (if everyone is $50, pricing at $20 attracts wrong traffic)

**Do:**
- Price competitively vs 2-3 main competitors
- Use compare-at pricing ONLY if it's the real retail price (MSRP)
- Price consistently for 2-4 week periods before changes
- Test premium positioning if product warrants it (better quality = higher price OK)

## GOOGLE SEARCH ADS: RSA FORMAT

### RSA (Responsive Search Ads) Structure

**Headlines:** 3 required, up to 15 total
**Descriptions:** 2 required, up to 4 total
**Display URL:** Your domain
**Final URL:** Landing page

**Character limits:**
- Headlines: 30 characters each
- Descriptions: 90 characters each

### Headline Writing (Top Priority)

**Headline 1 (Primary):** Your main keyword + benefit
- Example: "Premium Yoga Mats | Non-Slip & Eco-Friendly"
- Why: Mentions keyword + benefit combo

**Headline 2-3 (Alternative angles):** Different value propositions
- Headline 2: "Yoga Alignment Guides | Perfect Every Pose"
- Headline 3: "Natural Rubber Mats | Built to Last 5+ Years"
- Why: Google tests different angles, keeps performing ones

**Headline formula patterns:**
- [Product] + [Key benefit]
- [Problem solved] + [Solution]
- [Brand] + [Category] + [Differentiator]
- [Category] + [Key feature] + [Outcome]

### Description Writing

**Description 1 (Primary):** Problem-solution flow
- Example: "Struggling with grip during yoga? Our non-slip surface adapts to all practice styles."

**Description 2 (Secondary):** Social proof or offer
- Example: "Join 50,000+ yogis improving alignment. Free shipping on orders over $30."

**Description formula patterns:**
- [Problem] → [Your solution] → [Benefit outcome]
- [Specific feature] + [Emotional benefit] + [Social proof]
- [Offer/scarcity] + [Risk removal] + [CTA urgency]

### Search Ad Examples

**Example 1: Yoga Mat**
```
Headlines:
1. "Yoga Mat Non-Slip | Natural Rubber"
2. "Alignment Guides Built In | Perfect Poses"
3. "Eco-Friendly Mats | 5-Year Guarantee"

Descriptions:
1. "The premium mat yogis choose for perfect alignment every session. Non-slip surface keeps you stable through power flows."
2. "Join 50K+ yogis. Free shipping on orders over $30. 100% satisfaction guaranteed."
```

## YOUTUBE AD SCRIPTS: 7 MUST-HAVE ELEMENTS

### Element 1: Hook (0-5 seconds)
**Purpose:** Stop scrolling, grab attention
**Approach:** Problem statement, surprising visual, pattern interrupt
**Example:** "Wait, you've been sleeping wrong this whole time..."

### Element 2: Problem Identification (5-15 seconds)
**Purpose:** Deepen engagement through relatability
**Approach:** Show specific pain, emotional consequence
**Example:** "Neck pain stops you from work, gym, enjoying life..."

### Element 3: Solution Introduction (15-25 seconds)
**Purpose:** Introduce product as cure
**Approach:** Product reveal, key feature explanation, how it works
**Example:** "This pillow is different because it has 3 pressure zones..."

### Element 4: Social Proof / Results (25-35 seconds)
**Purpose:** Build credibility, reduce skepticism
**Approach:** Customer testimonials, results data, usage numbers
**Example:** "50,000+ people have transformed their sleep. Sarah said she had zero pain for the first time in 5 years..."

### Element 5: Objection Handling (35-45 seconds)
**Purpose:** Address customer doubts
**Approach:** Guarantee, return policy, quality assurance
**Example:** "Not sure? Try it for 30 days. If you hate it, full refund, zero questions."

### Element 6: Call-to-Action (45-55 seconds)
**Purpose:** Drive action, create urgency
**Approach:** Clear next step, limited-time offer, easy access
**Example:** "Order now and get free shipping. Link in description. Limited quantity at launch pricing..."

### Element 7: Final Proof / Reassurance (55-60+ seconds)
**Purpose:** Remove final doubts (optional for shorter ads)
**Approach:** Expert endorsement, award/certification, longer guarantee
**Example:** "Backed by 10-year manufacturer warranty and recommended by chiropractors nationwide."

### YouTube Script Examples

**30-second script (approximately 75 words):**
```
Hook: "Stop waking up with neck pain..."
Problem: "Every morning stiff. Can't focus at work. Missing workouts."
Solution: "This pillow has pressure-zone technology..."
Proof: "50,000 customers report better sleep."
CTA: "Try it free for 30 days. Link below."
(Total: ~25 seconds, leaves 5 sec for visual transitions/music)
```

**60-second script (approximately 150 words):**
```
[Includes all 7 elements with more detail on proof and objections]
```

## PERFORMANCE MAX ASSETS: MULTI-FORMAT OPTIMIZATION

### Asset Requirements

**Minimum assets needed:**
- 4-5 images (square + landscape variations)
- 1-2 videos (15-60 seconds)
- 3-5 headlines
- 2-4 descriptions
- 1-2 logos

**Recommendation:** Provide MORE assets than minimum
- Google tests combinations
- More assets = better optimization
- Aim for 10-15 images, 3-5 videos, 5-8 headlines, 3-5 descriptions

### Image Specifications

- **Aspect ratios to test:** 1:1 (square), 4:5 (portrait), 16:9 (landscape)
- **Size:** 1024x1024px minimum (larger better)
- **File type:** JPG or PNG
- **Key guideline:** All images should be strong individually (not relying on text for context)

### Video Specifications

- **Length:** 15-60 seconds (longer = better for YouTube, shorter = better for feed)
- **Aspect ratio:** 9:16 (vertical) preferred, also test 1:1 and 16:9
- **File size:** Under 1GB
- **Quality:** 1080p minimum
- **Must include:** Subtitles/captions (increases engagement, helps hearing-impaired)

## GOOGLE DISPLAY NETWORK (GDN) CREATIVE

### Static Display Ads

**Standard ad sizes:**
- 300x250 (medium rectangle, most popular)
- 728x90 (leaderboard)
- 160x600 (wide skyscraper)
- 300x600 (half page)

**Design rules:**
- Contrast: High contrast (dark text on light background, or vice versa)
- Text: Keep under 30% text overlay
- Brand consistency: Same logo/colors across sizes
- Call-to-action: Clear and visible
- Image quality: Professional, not pixelated

### Responsive Display Ads

- Upload 3-5 images (different aspect ratios)
- Create 3-5 headlines
- Create 2-3 descriptions
- Google assembles combinations automatically

## KEY BENCHMARKS

**Product Title CTR Improvement:**
- Poor title: 2-3% CTR
- Good title: 4-6% CTR
- Excellent title (perfect keyword match): 7-10% CTR

**Search Ad CTR Range:**
- Brand ads: 3-6% CTR
- Non-brand/generic: 1-3% CTR
- Competitive keywords: 0.5-2% CTR

**YouTube Ad Engagement:**
- View rate target: 20-30% of impressions
- Click-through rate: 0.5-2%
- Conversion rate: 1-4%

**Performance Max ROAS:**
- New campaigns: 1.2-1.8x
- Optimized (60+ days): 2.0-3.0x+
- Best performers: 4.0x+

## COMMON MISTAKES

**Mistake 1:** Vague product titles
- Fix: Include specific variant, dimension, material

**Mistake 2:** Keyword stuffing (repeating keyword)
- Fix: Use keyword once naturally, focus on benefit

**Mistake 3:** Generic headlines ("High Quality", "Best Price")
- Fix: Specific benefit ("Reduces Neck Pain", "30% Cheaper Than Competitors")

**Mistake 4:** Not testing different angles
- Fix: Create 3-5 different headlines, let Google optimize

**Mistake 5:** YouTube scripts too long
- Fix: 30-second scripts = ~75 words, 60-second = ~150 words

**Mistake 6:** Missing social proof
- Fix: Include customer count, reviews, guarantees, expert endorsement
