# Shopping Product Titles, Descriptions & Image Strategy

## SEO TITLE CREATION PROCESS

Product titles are the single most important element for Google Shopping performance. Google uses titles to match your products to search queries.

### The Left-to-Right Importance Rule
Google reads titles left to right, giving more weight to earlier words. Place highest-volume keywords FIRST.

### Title Creation Process (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Identify search terms**
List all keywords people would use to find this product. Include:
- Product type (e.g., "standing desk")
- Key features (e.g., "electric", "adjustable")
- Material (e.g., "bamboo")
- Brand name (if recognized)
- Color, size, style variations

**Step 2: Rank by search volume**
Use Google Keyword Planner or similar tool. Order terms by monthly search volume.

**Step 3: Arrange left to right by volume**
Highest volume terms go first. Each additional term adds specificity.

**Step 4: Add differentiators**
After core keywords, add what makes your product unique: "with cable management" or "for home office"

**Step 5: Check character limit**
Google Shopping title limit: 150 characters. Sweet spot: 70-100 characters (what shows in most placements).

### Title Examples

**Bad:** "Our Amazing Standing Desk" (no keywords, no specifics)

**Good:** "Electric Standing Desk Adjustable Height 48x24 Bamboo Desktop - Home Office with Cable Management"

**Formula:** [Primary Keyword] + [Key Feature] + [Size/Spec] + [Material] + [Use Case/Differentiator]

### Title Rules
- Never start with brand name unless brand is searched (Nike, Apple = yes; unknown brand = no)
- No ALL CAPS (violates Google policy)
- No promotional text in titles ("50% off" "Free shipping")
- No special characters or emoji
- Avoid keyword stuffing (readable by humans, not just algorithms)
- Include color and size when applicable (especially for clothing/fashion)

## PRODUCT DESCRIPTION TEMPLATE

### Structure (50-200 words, original content)

```
[OPENING -- Problem or desire statement, 1-2 sentences]
[PRODUCT INTRODUCTION -- What it is and main benefit, 1 sentence]

Key Features:
- [Feature 1]: [Benefit of feature]
- [Feature 2]: [Benefit of feature]
- [Feature 3]: [Benefit of feature]
- [Feature 4]: [Benefit of feature]

[SOCIAL PROOF -- review quote or stat, 1 sentence]

[SPECS -- dimensions, materials, what's included]

[GUARANTEE / TRUST -- return policy, warranty]
```

### Critical Rules
- Write 100% ORIGINAL descriptions -- Google's algorithm crawls word-for-word and matches against other websites
- If your content matches other dropshipping/scam sites, you get flagged
- Include emotional language alongside specifications
- Never copy from manufacturer, Amazon, or competitor sites
- Minimum 50 words, recommended 100-200 words

## PRODUCT IMAGE SELECTION STRATEGY

### The "Do Opposite of Competitors" Rule
Research your competitor Shopping ads. If they ALL show:
- White background product shots: You use lifestyle/in-use images
- Lifestyle images: You use clean product shots
- Single product: You show the product in a bundle or set

Standing out visually in the Shopping carousel is critical for CTR.

### Image Requirements
- Multiple images per product (3-5 minimum)
- Primary image: clean product shot on white or light background
- Additional images: product in use, lifestyle context, different angles
- High resolution (minimum 100x100, recommended 800x800+)
- No watermarks, no promotional text overlays
- No borders or padding
- Accurate representation of the product

### Image Selection Priority
1. Clean product shot (required by Google)
2. Product in use / lifestyle shot
3. Product from different angle
4. Size/scale reference (product next to common object)
5. Detail/close-up shots of key features

## PRODUCT PRICING STRATEGY

### For Merchant Center Approval
- Realistic pricing -- do NOT set identical discounts across all products
- Do NOT use compare-at price initially (same % off on every product = suspicious pattern)
- Pricing must match what's on your website exactly

### For Shopping Performance
- Products priced $20-80 generally have best Shopping performance (impulse + considered mix)
- Below $10: Shipping cost can exceed product value, hurting conversion
- Above $200: Longer consideration cycle, lower Shopping conversion rates
- Free shipping threshold significantly impacts Shopping CTR and conversion

## FEED OPTIMIZATION FOR CREATIVE IMPACT

### Required Attributes (Creative Impact)
| Attribute | Impact on Performance |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Title | Highest -- determines search match AND click decision |
| Image | High -- determines visual CTR in Shopping carousel |
| Price | High -- immediately visible, drives click/no-click decision |
| Description | Medium -- helps with broad match and PMax targeting |
| Product type | Medium -- helps Google categorize correctly |

### Custom Labels for Creative Segmentation
Use custom labels to segment products by creative approach:
- `custom_label_0`: Margin tier (high/medium/low) -- bid differently
- `custom_label_1`: Product lifecycle (new/bestseller/clearance)
- `custom_label_2`: Season (spring/summer/fall/winter/evergreen)
- `custom_label_3`: Creative type needed (visual/education/demo)

## SHOPPING TITLE A/B TESTING

### Process
1. Identify top 20 products by spend
2. Create title variation for each (change keyword order or add/remove terms)
3. Implement variation in supplemental feed (not primary feed)
4. Run for 14-30 days
5. Compare CTR and conversion rate
6. Winner replaces original

### What to Test
- Keyword order (which term first)
- Brand name inclusion vs exclusion
- Feature emphasis (material vs use case vs size)
- Specificity level (generic vs detailed)
