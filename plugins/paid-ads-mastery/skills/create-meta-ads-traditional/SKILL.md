# Meta Ad Creative (Traditional)

This skill covers traditional ad production (photographer, videographer, UGC creators) for Meta platforms. For AI-powered creative, see create-meta-ads-ai skill.

## THE EVERGREEN VIDEO STRUCTURE

All video ads follow this sequence. Time allocation matters.

**Hook (0-5 sec):** 35%+ hook rate target
**USP/Body (5-15 sec):** Why this is different/better
**Social Proof (15-20 sec):** Testimonials, reviews, results
**Objection Handling (20-25 sec):** Guarantee, return policy, timeline
**CTA (25-30 sec):** Clear next action

**Hook rate benchmark:** Above 35% = good, 15-20% = average, below 15% = weak

---

## HOOK FORMULAS: 6+ TYPES

### Hook Type 1: Problem Hook
**Formula:** "You know that feeling when [specific problem]?"
**Why it works:** Immediately relatable, triggers emotion
**Example:** "You know that feeling when you wake up and your neck is killing you?"
**Best for:** Problem-aware and solution-aware audiences
**Hook rate:** 28-35%

### Hook Type 2: Visual Pattern Interrupt
**Formula:** Shocking/unusual visual cut in first 2 seconds
**Why it works:** Brain reacts to novelty
**Examples:**
- Person cutting onion (totally unrelated to product)
- Fast zoom on product
- Unexpected color flash
- Fast-motion sequence
**Best for:** All audiences (grabs attention universally)
**Hook rate:** 30-40%

### Hook Type 3: Question Hook
**Formula:** "Did you know [surprising fact]?"
**Why it works:** Curiosity drives watch completion
**Example:** "Did you know 73% of sleep problems aren't about the pillow?"
**Best for:** Unaware, problem-aware audiences
**Hook rate:** 25-32%

### Hook Type 4: Statistic Hook
**Formula:** "[Specific number]% of [demographic] [struggle with X]"
**Why it works:** Authority + relatability combo
**Example:** "47% of women over 30 report sleep disruption..."
**Best for:** Data-conscious audiences, solution-aware
**Hook rate:** 28-35%

### Hook Type 5: Story Hook
**Formula:** "I used to [problem]. Then I [discovered solution]..."
**Why it works:** Narrative draws viewers in
**Example:** "I spent $2,000+ on sleep fixes. Then I tried this..."
**Best for:** Product-aware, testimonial-style
**Hook rate:** 25-30%

### Hook Type 6: Benefit Hook
**Formula:** "Imagine [life improved by benefit]..."
**Why it works:** Aspirational, positive emotion
**Example:** "Imagine waking up without neck pain for the first time in 5 years..."
**Best for:** Problem-aware, most-aware audiences
**Hook rate:** 20-28%

**Rule:** Test 3-5 different hooks per ad. Measure hook rate. Keep best performer, rotate others.

## HOLD RATE & SAFE ZONES

**Hold rate (watch-through):** Average 10% finish rate (90% drop by end—that's normal)

**Safe zone strategy:**
- CTA placement: MIDDLE of video (more viewers reach middle than end)
- Video should be shot 9x16 vertical
- Safe zone for multi-platform: Keep critical content in 1x1 square center
- This 1x1 center zone works across: Feed, Stories, Reels

**Format specifications by placement:**
- Instagram Reels: 9x16 full vertical
- Feed: 1x1 square (test here first)
- Stories: 9x16 vertical
- Facebook Feed: Can be 1x1, 4x5, or 9x16

## STATIC AD CREATIVE

**Components:**
1. **Hero Image:** Person using/holding product OR lifestyle scene
2. **Headline:** 5-10 words, benefit or curiosity focused
3. **Body Copy:** 10-30 words, problem + solution
4. **CTA Button:** "Shop Now", "Learn More", "Claim Yours"

**Design rules:**
- People in ads: Should be SMILING (Facebook boosts smile engagement)
- Image style: Casual/relatable beats polish (selfie quality > studio quality)
- Contrast: High contrast between product and background
- Text overlay: Keep under 20% of image (Meta reduces reach if too much text)

**Static ad template variation testing:**
- Test 5-10 different headline variations per image
- Test different CTAs (Shop vs Learn vs Claim)
- Test different color overlays if using text
- Test square (1x1) format first, if winning, recreate in 9x16

## COPY TEMPLATES: 4 MAIN TYPES

### Template 1: Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS)

**Structure:**
**Problem:** [State the problem clearly]
**Agitation:** [Show consequences/emotion of problem]
**Solution:** [Introduce your product as solution]

**Example:**
- Problem: "Back pain is ruining your morning routine"
- Agitation: "You can't play with your kids, can't work pain-free, feel old at 35"
- Solution: "This pillow stops pain in 7 nights. Guaranteed."

### Template 2: Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

**Structure:**
**Before:** [Describe current frustration]
**After:** [Describe desired future state]
**Bridge:** [Your product gets them from Before to After]

**Example:**
- Before: "Waking up groggy, neck stiff, coffee-dependent"
- After: "Waking up refreshed, pain-free, energized naturally"
- Bridge: "One pillow change = complete morning transformation"

### Template 3: AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

**Attention:** [Hook headline]
**Interest:** [Benefit statement]
**Desire:** [Social proof / emotional driver]
**Action:** [CTA]

**Example:**
- Attention: "Sleep pillow that actually works"
- Interest: "Reduces neck pain by 89% in first week"
- Desire: "Used by 50,000+ people, 4.9★ rating"
- Action: "Claim yours today + free shipping"

### Template 4: Listicle / Numbered Format

**Structure:** "3 Reasons Your Sleep Pillow Isn't Working (And What Works Instead)"

**Use for:**
- Comparative positioning (implicit comparison to alternatives)
- Educational angle (appeals to unaware/problem-aware)
- High CTR (people click to see list)

## COPY IMPACT METRICS

**Headline:** 20% of ad performance impact
**Body copy:** 10% of ad performance impact
**Visual/Hook:** 70% of ad performance impact

**Action:** Optimize hook/visual FIRST (biggest impact), then tweak copy.

## VIDEO FORMATS & TYPES

### Format 1: Evergreen Educational
**Structure:** Problem → Solution explanation → Benefits → CTA
**Length:** 15-30 sec
**Best for:** Cold traffic (unaware, problem-aware)
**Production:** Can use iPhone footage, simple graphics

### Format 2: UGC (User-Generated Content)
**Structure:** Real person talking directly to camera about product
**Length:** 15-30 sec
**Best for:** Authenticity, higher conversion than branded
**Production:** iPhone quality preferred, minimal makeup/setup

### Format 3: VSL (Video Sales Letter)
**Structure:** Single person speaking directly, longer narrative
**Hook → Problem → Solution → Proof → Urgency → CTA**
**Length:** Mini (45-90 sec) or Full (120+ sec)
**Best for:** Higher-ticket products, detailed value communication
**Production:** Voiceover + visuals (product, lifestyle, testimonials)

### Format 4: Mashup / Quick Cuts
**Structure:** Fast-paced cuts of product in action, lifestyle integration
**Length:** 15-30 sec
**Best for:** Reels, younger audiences, pattern interrupts
**Production:** Requires editing skill, fast music/sound

### Format 5: Testimonial
**Structure:** Customer talking about transformation
**Hook → Problem they had → How product helped → Results → CTA**
**Length:** 15-30 sec
**Best for:** Product-aware, most-aware audiences
**Production:** Real customer interview (audio might have imperfections = better authenticity)

### Format 6: Before/After
**Structure:** Visual side-by-side or sequence of before state → product use → after state
**Length:** 15-30 sec
**Best for:** Transformation-focused products (weight loss, skincare, organization)
**Production:** Product demo or lifestyle before/after

### Format 7: Lifestyle Integration
**Structure:** Product shown in natural context of daily use
**Length:** 15-30 sec
**Best for:** Aspirational products (coffee equipment, fitness, wellness)
**Production:** Requires production for aesthetic quality

## VSL SPECIFIC TEMPLATES

### Mini VSL (45-90 seconds)

**Structure:**
- Hook (5-10 sec): Problem statement or curiosity
- Problem identification (10-15 sec): Deep dive into customer pain
- Solution presentation (10-15 sec): How product solves it
- Proof/Results (10-15 sec): Customer testimonials or data
- CTA (5 sec): Clear call to action + urgency

### Full VSL (120+ seconds)

**Structure:**
- Hook (10 sec): Compelling opening
- Problem story (20-30 sec): Narrative of struggle
- Transformation story (20-30 sec): How product changed things
- Proof (20-30 sec): Multiple testimonials, data, authority
- Objection handling (15-20 sec): "What if X? Here's Y..."
- Urgency/Scarcity (5-10 sec): Limited time, limited stock
- CTA (5 sec): Clear next action

**Pacing rule:** Average 1 word = 0.5 seconds. 120-second VSL = 240 words max for natural delivery.

## CREATIVE ITERATION: DIMINISHING RETURNS

**When to iterate winners:**
- Creative is hitting above-average account ROAS for 3-5 days
- Hook rate is good (30%+) but copy/visuals could improve
- You want to extend creative lifespan before fatigue

**What NOT to iterate:**
- Losing creatives (they won't improve by tweaking)
- Creatives hitting diminishing returns (new versions underperform)

**Variables to test by priority:**

**Priority 1: HOOK (Highest impact)**
- If hook rate low: Change hook type completely
- If hold rate low: Keep hook, change body footage

**Priority 2: Copy/Messaging**
- Headline: Test 5-10 variations
- Body: Test problem vs benefit focus
- CTA: Test "Shop Now" vs "Learn More" vs "Claim Yours"

**Priority 3: Visual/Aesthetic**
- Color grading
- Music/sound
- Product visibility
- Lifestyle integration

**Iteration quantities:**
- Test 4-5 variations per variable
- Once you identify highest-impact variable, produce 5-10 of that variable
- Stop when new batches score below account average ROAS
- Reallocate budget to either new creatives OR iterate different variable

**Timeline:** 1-week test per variable, 2-3 weeks per high-impact variable before diminishing returns

## CREATIVE NAMING CONVENTION

**Format:** `[BRAND]_[NUMBER]_[TYPE]_[CONCEPT]_[SIZE]_[VARIATION]_[VERSION]`

**Example:** `BLS_1_VIDEO_BodyPain_9x16_HOOK_V1`

**Components:**
- Brand: BLS (3-letter brand initials)
- Number: 1 (sequential creative ID)
- Type: VIDEO, IMAGE, CAROUSEL, COLLECTION
- Concept: BodyPain, BedtimeRoutine, PriceComparison
- Size: 1x1, 9x16, 4x5
- Variation: HOOK (hook variation), COPY (copy variation), COLOR, etc.
- Version: V1, V2, V3

**This naming enables:**
- Easy tracking of what's being tested
- Clear communication with team
- Historical performance analysis
- Relaunch of past winners

## TESTING CHECKLIST: BEFORE LAUNCH

**Creative Quality:**
- [ ] Hook grabs attention in 2-3 seconds
- [ ] Audio quality is clear (if voiceover)
- [ ] Product is clearly visible (not hidden or small)
- [ ] No jarring transitions or confusing edits
- [ ] Subtitles/captions present (improves retention)
- [ ] Safe zone text is readable (not cramped)

**Copy Quality:**
- [ ] Headline is under 15 words
- [ ] Body copy uses customer language (not marketing speak)
- [ ] CTA is action-oriented ("Shop Now", not "Click Here")
- [ ] Benefit is clear in first 10 words
- [ ] No spelling errors

**Format Compliance:**
- [ ] Aspect ratio correct for platform (9x16 for Reels, 1x1 for Feed)
- [ ] Text in safe zone (not cut off at edges)
- [ ] File size under 5MB (fast loading)
- [ ] Frame rate 30fps minimum

**Strategic Alignment:**
- [ ] Matches audience awareness level (unaware gets education, solution-aware gets comparison)
- [ ] Persona matches (right demographic, right language)
- [ ] Product angle aligns with research findings
- [ ] Hook type aligns with market data (if 70% of competitors use testimonials, this should too OR be contrarian if you have reason)

## BUDGET ALLOCATION: VIDEO VS STATIC

**Rule:** Video is 3x more expensive than static due to platform demand

**Strategy by product type:**

**For info products:** 100% static (video often underperforms)
**For ecommerce:** 50/50 or 60% video / 40% static
**For services:** 70% video / 30% static
**For new launches:** Start 100% static, once you find winners, add video iterations

**Reasoning:** Static reaches the 20% of conversion audience at 3x cheaper CPM than video. Video reaches different audience. Test both, but static is more efficient per dollar.

## KEY BENCHMARKS & RULES

**Hook Rate Targets:**
- 35%+ = Excellent
- 25-35% = Good
- 15-25% = Average
- Below 15% = Weak (replace hook)

**Hold Rate (Video Completion):**
- 10% = Normal (not a failure metric)
- 3% or below = Boring footage (replace body, not hook)
- 20%+ = Excellent (rare)

**Copy Testing Frequency:**
- Test 10 different copy variations once per quarter
- Find 1 evergreen winner, use for 3 months
- Refresh after 3 months (audience fatigue)

**Video Length:**
- Optimal: 15-30 seconds
- Acceptable: 10-60 seconds
- Avoid: Above 90 seconds unless VSL (specific format)

**CTA Placement:**
- Best: Middle of video (more viewers reach middle than end)
- Acceptable: End of video (lowest watch rate)
- Avoid: Only at beginning (nobody watches rest)

## COMMON MISTAKES

**Mistake 1:** Testing too many variables at once
- Fix: Change ONE variable per test (hook only, or copy only, not both)

**Mistake 2:** Iterating losing creatives
- Fix: Kill creatives below account average ROAS, don't iterate them

**Mistake 3:** Polished creative for direct response
- Fix: Ugly ads (low production) often outconvert branded polished ads

**Mistake 4:** Hiding product in lifestyle footage
- Fix: Product should be clearly visible (not 2% of frame)

**Mistake 5:** Using generic copy instead of customer language
- Fix: Use exact phrases from code bank, not marketing speak

## WHEN TO BRIEF CREATIVE TEAM

**Include in brief:**
- Persona (who is this for?)
- Problem they're solving
- Audience awareness level
- Hook approach (which formula?)
- Competing ads to reference (good patterns to steal)
- Product angle/USP
- Copy tone (casual vs professional, humorous vs serious)
- Video length and format
- Specific CTA to use
- Success metrics (expected CTR, conversion rate)

**Do NOT tell them:**
- Exact copy (they write it based on research)
- Exact visuals (they create based on direction)
- Every edit decision (they make creative calls)
- Budget (irrelevant to creative quality)
