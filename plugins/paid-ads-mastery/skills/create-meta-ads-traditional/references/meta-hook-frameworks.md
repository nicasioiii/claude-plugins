# Meta Video Hook Frameworks & Formulas

## The 6 Primary Hook Types (With Benchmarks)

### Hook 1: Problem Hook (28-35% hook rate)
**Formula:** "You know that feeling when [specific problem]?"
**Psychological driver:** Relatability + emotion
**Execution:**
- Identify specific, emotional problem
- Make it VIVID (show the consequence, not just state it)
- Keep under 5 seconds
- Use natural voice, not dramatic

**Examples:**
```
"You know that feeling when your neck wakes you up at 3am in pain?"
"You know that feeling when you're at work and your back just... gives out?"
"You know that feeling when everyone's scrolling their phone and you're just... stuck?"
```

**When to use:**
- Problem-aware audiences (know they have issue)
- Products solving pain points
- Cold traffic (ads reaching unaware audience)

**Variations to test:**
- Physical problem hooks
- Emotional problem hooks ("that sinking feeling when...")
- Time-related hooks ("You know that feeling when you only have 10 minutes and...")

---

### Hook 2: Visual Pattern Interrupt (30-40% hook rate)
**Formula:** Shocking/unusual visual in first 2 seconds
**Psychological driver:** Brain reacts to novelty and movement
**Execution:**
- Open with something UNEXPECTED related to product or problem
- Use fast cuts, bright colors, unusual angles
- Match audio to visual (sound effect or music spike)
- Transition to product 3-5 seconds in

**Examples:**
```
1. Person cutting onion (eyes watering) → transition to eye cream → "This stops puffiness"
2. Fast zoom on product → slow-motion reveal → product in action
3. Bright color flash → dramatic music drop → product appears
4. Stop-motion sequence of problem → freeze on solution
```

**When to use:**
- All audiences (universal attention-getter)
- Unaware audiences (doesn't require problem knowledge)
- High-scroll-rate placements (need immediate stop)

**Variations to test:**
- Shock/surprise visuals
- Fast-paced montages
- Aesthetic pattern interrupts (sudden style shift)
- Motion pattern interrupts (fast-slow, still-movement)

---

### Hook 3: Question Hook (25-32% hook rate)
**Formula:** "Did you know [surprising fact/statistic]?"
**Psychological driver:** Curiosity (brain wants to know the answer)
**Execution:**
- Ask question with surprising/unexpected answer
- Make answer NOT obvious from question
- Answer the question in next 5-10 seconds
- Use data if available

**Examples:**
```
"Did you know 73% of sleep problems aren't about the pillow?"
"Did you know most people are doing their morning routine completely wrong?"
"Did you know the average person spends 40+ hours per year on admin tasks?"
```

**When to use:**
- Problem-aware audiences (question implies solution exists)
- Educational angle campaigns
- Data-backed products

**Variations to test:**
- Surprising statistic questions
- Contrary-to-belief questions ("Did you know coffee actually helps sleep?")
- Personal reflection questions ("Did you know you're spending $X on...")

---

### Hook 4: Statistic Hook (28-35% hook rate)
**Formula:** "[Specific number]% of [demographic] [experience problem/want benefit]"
**Psychological driver:** Authority + relatability
**Execution:**
- Use specific statistic (not vague: "many" is weak, "47%" is strong)
- Relate to target demographic
- Show problem or pain point
- Statistic should be noteworthy (not "50% of people eat breakfast")

**Examples:**
```
"47% of women over 30 report sleep disruption due to neck position"
"67% of managers waste 10+ hours weekly on administrative tasks"
"3 out of 4 fitness enthusiasts experience knee pain from improper form"
```

**When to use:**
- Data-backed/scientific products
- Solution-aware audiences
- Building credibility/authority

**Variations to test:**
- Problem statistics ("73% suffer from...")
- Behavior statistics ("67% of people do...")
- Success statistics ("89% report improvement...")

---

### Hook 5: Story Hook (25-30% hook rate)
**Formula:** "I used to [problem]. Then I [discovered solution]..."
**Psychological driver:** Narrative engagement (story pulls viewers in)
**Execution:**
- Open with relatable struggle
- Make struggle SPECIFIC and emotional
- Quickly introduce discovery/solution
- End with transformation hint ("Then everything changed...")

**Examples:**
```
"I spent $2,000+ on sleep fixes. Weighted blankets, white noise machines, pillows. Nothing worked. Then a chiropractor told me about THIS..."
"I tried 5 different diets. Each time, I'd lose weight for 2 weeks then gain it all back. Then my trainer showed me THIS approach..."
"I wasted 15 hours per week on admin tasks. Spreadsheets, manual entry, lost documents. Then I found this tool..."
```

**When to use:**
- Testimonial-style videos
- Personal transformation angle
- Product-aware audiences (they know you exist, need trust)

**Variations to test:**
- Personal failure narratives
- Long-struggle narratives ("For 5 years I...")
- Accidental discovery narratives ("My friend suggested...")

---

### Hook 6: Benefit Hook (20-28% hook rate)
**Formula:** "Imagine [desired outcome after using product]..."
**Psychological driver:** Aspiration + positive emotion
**Execution:**
- Paint vivid picture of improved life
- Make benefit SPECIFIC and emotional
- Show product delivering that benefit
- Keep aspirational (not boring features)

**Examples:**
```
"Imagine waking up without neck pain for the first time in 5 years..."
"Imagine having 10 extra hours per week for things you actually love..."
"Imagine feeling confident in your own skin again..."
```

**When to use:**
- Aspirational products (lifestyle, wellness, confidence)
- Problem-aware audiences (they feel problem, want solution)
- High emotional-resonance categories

**Variations to test:**
- Emotional benefit hooks ("Imagine feeling confident...")
- Time-saving benefit hooks ("Imagine having X hours back...")
- Lifestyle benefit hooks ("Imagine your morning routine looking like...")

## HOOK RATE MEASUREMENT & TESTING

### How to Measure Hook Rate
- In Meta Ads Manager: Monitor "3-second video views" metric
- Hook rate = (3-second video views) / (video views) × 100
- Benchmark: Above 35% = good, 15-20% = average, below 15% = weak

### Testing Framework

**Week 1: Test 3 different hook types**
- Ad A: Problem hook
- Ad B: Visual pattern interrupt
- Ad C: Question hook
- Same audience, budget, body/CTA
- Budget: $50 each, run 3-5 days

**Measure:** Which has highest hook rate?

**Week 2: Iterate winner + test alternative**
- Ad A: Top performing hook (test variations)
- Ad B: Different hook type you haven't tested
- Budget: Scale winner to $150, test new to $50

**Continue:** Until you find 35%+ hook rate OR identify highest-performing hook type for your audience

## ADVANCED HOOK STRATEGIES

### Hook + Hold Rate Connection

**Hook rate:** 0-5 second performance (getting attention)
**Hold rate:** Middle-video performance (keeping attention)

**If hook rate is LOW (<15%):**
- Problem: Opening 2 seconds aren't interesting
- Solution: Change hook type completely
- Don't tweak, replace

**If hook rate is GOOD (30%+) but hold rate is VERY LOW (3%):**
- Problem: Body footage is boring (viewers drop after hook)
- Solution: Change video body/footage, keep hook
- Means hook works but body doesn't

**If both are low:**
- Problem: Hook and content both weak
- Solution: Complete video overhaul (different hook + different body)

## PATTERN INTERRUPT VISUAL TACTICS

**For visual pattern interrupt hooks, use these tactics:**

1. **Color flash:** Bright saturated color (red, yellow) in first 1 second
2. **Fast cuts:** Scene change every 0.5-1 second (rapid editing)
3. **Zoom:** Fast zoom in or out on object/person
4. **Sound effect:** Dramatic sound spike/drop at moment of visual interrupt
5. **Movement:** Sudden movement after still frame
6. **Unexpected element:** Something the viewer doesn't expect (person doing unexpected action, product in weird context)
7. **Text overlay:** Bold text appears suddenly
8. **Contrast:** Sudden shift from dark to bright or vice versa

**Testing approach:**
- Create 5 versions of same product
- Version 1: Fast cuts + music spike
- Version 2: Color flash + zoom
- Version 3: Unexpected element (onion cutting example)
- Version 4: Movement after still frame
- Version 5: Text overlay surprise

**Measure hook rate by tactic, scale winning tactic.**

## WHEN TO CHANGE HOOKS (Diminishing Returns)

**Keep testing new hooks when:**
- Current hook rate is 30%+ but ROAS declining
- Video been running 3+ weeks with high frequency
- Audience fatigue detected (CTR dropping, CPM rising)

**Change hooks when:**
- Statistically significantly lower performance than alternative
- Fatigue signals (same hook, declining performance over days)
- Audience demographic shifts (new audience may respond to different hook)

**Reuse winning hooks when:**
- Tested + proven across multiple products
- Audience hasn't seen it yet (new campaign)
- Format/platform is different (TikTok vs Facebook may respond differently)
