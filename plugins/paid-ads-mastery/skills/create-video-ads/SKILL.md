# Video Ad Scripting & Production

Create video ad scripts, UGC briefs, and production plans for social media advertising. This skill covers traditional (human-produced) video creative.

## PREREQUISITE: RESEARCH FIRST

Before scripting any video, you need output from creative research (research-creative skill):
- Code bank with categorized customer language
- Persona profiles
- Awareness level assessment
- Hypothesis for what to test

Scripts are ASSEMBLED from research data, not invented from thin air. "Copy is not written, it is assembled" (Eugene Schwartz).

---

## THE EVERGREEN VIDEO STRUCTURE

All video ads follow this sequence. This is the default framework when no specific format is requested.

1. **Hook (0-3 seconds):** Stop the scroll. Text visible from frame 1. Target: 35%+ hook rate.
2. **3-5 USPs:** Why this product is different/better. Use customer language from code bank.
3. **Social proof:** Testimonials, customer photos, review counts.
4. **Objection handling:** 30-day guarantee, free returns, shipping info.
5. **CTA:** Clear next action. Place CTA in the MIDDLE of the video (not just the end) -- only ~10% of viewers reach the end.

**Hook benchmarks:**
- Good hook rate: above 35% (US market)
- Bad hook rate: below 15%
- Average video play time: 3-5 seconds; 10 seconds is very good
- Hold rate: ~11% finishing full video is acceptable

## VIDEO AD FORMAT SELECTION

| Format | Best For | Length | When to Use |
|--------|----------|--------|-------------|
| Mini VSL | Older audiences (40+), problem-solving products, high-AOV items | 5-10+ min | Competitive/saturated markets needing indirect approach |
| Branded Mini VSL | D2C brands wanting polished + VSL framework | 1-1.5 min | When brand resists "ugly" VSL look |
| Single-Creator UGC | Any brand needing education with visuals | 45-90 sec | Younger audiences, standard DR creative |
| UGC Mashup | Social proof and credibility | 20-60 sec | Any brand needing volume social proof |
| Non-Narrated UGC | Visually-driven products (jewelry, fashion, pets) | 15-45 sec | When product sells itself visually |
| Organic-Style UGC | Cosmetics, skincare, native-looking content | 30-90 sec | When ad must look like organic content |
| Staged Testimonials | When real testimonials are unavailable | 15-30 sec | Spliced into other formats or standalone |

## SCRIPT WRITING FROM CODE BANKS

### The On-Ramp (Before Product Introduction)

Everything before you name the product. Purpose: stop scroll, retain attention, give reason to keep watching.

**Process:**
1. Place framework structure in script template (e.g., Problem > Educate > Agitate > Intro)
2. Go to code bank and find reviews matching each section
3. Pull customer language directly from reviews
4. Use multiple reviews -- pick the best sentences per section
5. Categorized outcomes in code bank make finding content fast

**Timing rule for awareness levels:**
- Product introduced within 15-20 seconds = product-aware ad
- Product introduced after 20+ seconds = solution-aware ad
- Product introduced after 40+ seconds = problem-aware or unaware ad

### The Off-Ramp (After Product Introduction)

The sales sequence after naming the product. Handle objections here.

**Primary objections FIRST:** Product formula, mechanism, specific concerns ("Is this vegan?" "Does this work on X?")
**Secondary objections LATER:** Money-back guarantee, pricing, payment plans

The off-ramp rarely changes between ads. The ON-RAMP is where most iteration happens.

## THE 5 STAGES OF ITERATING A VIDEO CONCEPT

Go gradually -- do not skip to Stage 5:

1. **Ad Copy & Headline Testing:** Different copy below the video. Dynamic headline testing. Most brands skip this.
2. **Hook Changes:** Change hook TYPE (claim to question) or hook FORMAT (split screen to talking-head).
3. **On-Ramp Changes:** Change first 10-15 seconds while keeping off-ramp. Test different formats (9:16 vs 4:5 vs 1:1).
4. **Sales Sequence Modifications:** Trim the video, rearrange blocks, move explanatory material to end.
5. **Adapting, Paraphrasing & Rhetorical Reframing:** Pull elements from other winners, change voiceover, address skeptics' comments directly.

## DEMOGRAPHIC-SPECIFIC CREATIVE RULES

### Younger Audiences (18-25)
- Faster pacing -- something on screen every 2-3 seconds
- TikTok/Instagram-native editing style
- Visuals over script (visual-first ads)
- "Save time and money" callouts resonate
- Reference the platform: "After seeing this all over my Instagram feed..."

### Older Audiences (40+)
- Slower video pace, bigger fonts, fewer emojis
- Longer-form videos and mini VSLs perform better
- Script over visuals (script-first ads)
- Fewer jump cuts, fewer sound effects
- Editing pace = implicit audience targeting (algorithm matches style to demo)
