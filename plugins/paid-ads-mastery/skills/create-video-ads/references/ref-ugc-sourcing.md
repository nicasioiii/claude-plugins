# UGC Sourcing, Creator Management & Production Briefs

## UGC SOURCING HIERARCHY

In order of recommended priority:

1. **AI-generated content** (fastest, cheapest, most scalable)
   - ElevenLabs for voiceovers, Google Flow/Veo for video, Creatify for AI UGC
   - See create-ai-ads skill for full workflow

2. **Upwork / freelance platforms** (good middle ground)
   - Post detailed briefs, hire per-project
   - Can find experienced UGC creators with portfolios

3. **UGC marketplaces** (Backstage, Billo, Insense, JoinBrands)
   - Backstage actors are trained speakers -- better on-camera presence than random creators
   - Higher quality delivery but costs more

4. **Retained creators** (long-term relationships)
   - Build a database of reliable creators for repeat work
   - Best for brands with ongoing high-volume creative needs

### Why Backstage Over Other Platforms
- Actors come from acting backgrounds (TV, film) -- much better at speaking to camera
- More skilled spokespersons vs. random UGC creators
- Higher quality delivery and on-camera presence

---

## BACKSTAGE SOURCING PROCESS

1. Go to Backstage > Find Talent > Actors & Performers
2. Add filters: Gender, "UGC" keyword, Age range, Location
3. Scrape fast -- first impression based on appearance and ICP fit
4. Open profiles of potential fits -- check media/portfolio for UGC experience
5. Save 20-30+ qualified candidates to a list
6. Qualify further: portfolio quality, camera presence, footage quality, previous UGC work
7. Message qualified candidates: ask for email + portfolio
   - Include "paid" in message -- "Paid UGC [brand name] work opportunity"
8. Move to email for all further communication

### Outreach Email Must Include:
- Type of brand/product (include website/product page link)
- What content will be used for (paid ads, organic, YouTube)
- Overview of project workflow (ship product, send shot list, deliver footage)
- Quantity of work expected (e.g., 18-20 b-roll scenes + 2-3 talking ads)
- Example brief/shot list so they understand volume
- Your budget range

---

## NEGOTIATION GUIDELINES

- Budget below $100 = very hard to get responses
- Always offer a starting price BELOW actual budget (budget $150, offer $125)
- Don't go too low or you lose credibility
- **NEVER increase budget to fit their needs -- REDUCE scope of work instead**
  - Can't afford 20 b-rolls + 3 talking ads for $200? Offer 15 b-rolls + 3 talking ads
- If creator is weak on talking ads but has great footage/b-rolls/lighting/location, hire for b-roll only
- Combine their visual assets with other creators' talking ads

---

## WHITELISTING DEAL STRUCTURES

Running ads from a creator's personal account (appears as organic content):
- Negotiate whitelisting rights upfront as part of the deal
- Typical additional cost: 10-30% premium over standard UGC rates
- Duration: usually 30-90 day usage rights
- Works perfectly with organic-style UGC format

## CREATOR BRIEF STRUCTURE (GOOGLE DOCS)

### Section 1: Setup for Success
- Overview + quick training on navigating the brief
- Filming training: deadlines, effects, tone, acting tips, framing, removing unwanted sounds
- Instructions: Send ALL clips separately as raw footage (individual files)
- This builds a content bank for future reuse/iterations

### Section 2: Upload Instructions
- Link to Google Drive folder
- Naming convention: [Brand Name] / [Round #] / Raw Footage / [Creator Name]
- Creators rename each clip to match shot description before uploading

### Section 3: General Guidelines
- Product-specific notes (e.g., for skincare: "don't wear heavy makeup, keep it light")
- Platform the content is for
- Target audience description

### Section 4: Storyboard
Two parts:

**Talking Ads / Voiceovers:**
- Full script to recite
- Separate into Talking Ad 1, 2, 3
- One long file, editors split later

**Visual B-Rolls:**
Two columns:
| Action / Shot Type | Visual Description |
|---|---|
| Apply foundation | Front angle, close-up, giving unsatisfied reactions |
| Show product to camera | Medium shot, holding product label-forward |
| Unboxing | POV shot, opening package with excitement |

Include visual references (videos, links, GIFs) for shots needing specific execution. Leave references out when you want creative freedom.

## DEMOGRAPHIC-SPECIFIC CREATOR GUIDELINES

### For Younger Audiences (18-25)
- Creators should be 18-28, matching the demo
- TikTok-native delivery style
- Comfortable with quick, energetic speaking
- Settings: bedrooms, cars, casual environments
- Props and trending effects welcome

### For Older Audiences (40+)
- Creators should be 35-55+
- Calm, measured delivery -- not hyper
- Settings: home office, kitchen, living room
- Minimal effects -- clean, straightforward presentation
- Talking-head style connects better with older female audiences

### Critical Insight on Ad Delivery
How you EDIT your ads has a huge impact on WHO Meta delivers them to:
- Slower-paced, slower-cut ads = delivered to older audiences
- Faster-paced, faster-cut ads = delivered to younger audiences
- The algorithm matches editing style to audience. Editing pace = implicit audience targeting.

## FILMING TRAINING (SHARED WITH CREATORS)

Key topics to cover with every creator:
1. Deadlines and clip delivery schedule
2. How to use requested effects (if any)
3. Tone and acting tips for the brand
4. What is a voiceover vs. a talking ad
5. Framing rules (safe zones for 9:16 format)
6. How to remove unwanted sounds
7. Rename clips before uploading (match shot list names)
8. All footage shot on iPhone/smartphone only
9. Good lighting -- natural light preferred, no harsh overhead
10. Clean audio -- quiet environment, no background noise

---

## ASSET MANAGEMENT SYSTEM

**Always collect separate raw clips** so you can reuse and recombine for iterations:
- Each b-roll scene = separate file
- Each talking ad take = separate file
- Build a content bank organized by: brand > creator > content type > date
- Old footage can be remixed with new hooks, new scripts, new creators
- This is what enables the 5-stage iteration process at scale
