# Video Ad Frameworks & Format Specifications

## MINI VSLs (VIDEO SALES LETTERS)

### What They Are
In-feed ads that look like interesting videos, mini-documentaries, or educational content. They do NOT look like ads. They disguise as storytelling/educational content, then slowly sell.

### Best For
- Older audiences (40+) with longer attention spans
- Competitive/saturated markets where audience needs an indirect approach
- Problem-solving products: health, beauty, supplements, gadgets
- Higher-consideration / higher-AOV products requiring more education
- When you need a "unique mechanism" or new root cause explanation

### Structure (5-10+ minutes, some go 30+ min on Facebook)
1. **Hook with open loop** (0-10 sec): Curiosity-driven, NO product mention
2. **Educate about the problem** (10 sec - 2 min): Root cause explanation
3. **Present new mechanism** (2-3 min): Why common solutions fail + what actually works
4. **Introduce product** (3-4 min): Product as the solution to the real root cause
5. **Benefits + USPs** (4-6 min): Specific outcomes using customer language
6. **Objection handling** (6-8 min): Address top concerns
7. **Testimonials + CTA** (final 1-2 min): Social proof + risk-free offer

### Hook Pattern Examples
- "Big people above 250 pounds with sciatica who can't walk more than a couple of minutes -- you're going to want to watch this"
- "Sisters, this is the reason why your hair keeps falling"
- "This is the best sleeping hack for neck and back pain -- but first, what happens when you sleep wrong every night"

## BRANDED MINI VSLs

### What They Are
Same framework as mini VSLs but with more UGC elements, branding, and a spokesperson fitting the ICP. Compressed to ~1-1.5 minutes vs 5-10 min.

### Key Differences from Regular VSLs
- More UGC-style footage and branding elements
- Content creator/actor as spokesperson (not AI voiceover)
- Gets to product faster (within ~20 seconds vs 2-3 minutes)
- Still includes: education, root cause, unique mechanism, objection handling

### Staged Testimonials in Branded Mini VSLs
Prefer organic staged testimonials over scripted deliveries:
1. Hop on video call with actor
2. Set up as if gathering customer feedback
3. Give them character context and key talking points
4. Ask open-ended questions and record
5. Coach between takes -- capture REACTIONS not recitations
6. Extract the most genuine-sounding clips

## SINGLE-CREATOR UGC (AGC -- ACTOR GENERATED CONTENT)

### Best For
Any brand needing education with visual examples -- health, wellness, skincare, beauty, food, pets, supplements. Works better for younger audiences.

### Guidelines
- Length: 45-90 seconds max -- cut the fluff, make scripts direct
- Write scripts as if creator is talking to a friend over video call
- Use as many b-roll clips as possible vs talking head (minimizes faces, prevents fatigue)
- Do NOT present any offer within first 5-10 seconds for cold traffic -- Meta will show to existing buyers if offer appears early
- For sophisticated audiences, delay product reveal (moves toward mini-VSL territory)

## UGC MASHUPS

### Two Approaches
1. **Testimonial Mashup:** Multiple creators each give their testimonial, spliced together
2. **Script Mashup:** One script, multiple creators. Each delivers different sections (0-10s = Actor 1, 10-20s = Actor 2)

### Guidelines
- Show at least 4+ actors/customers (2 is not a mashup)
- Prefer b-rolls over talking ads -- show people USING the product
- Show diversity in users and locations (unless very specific ICP)
- Can work as short as 20 seconds (voiceover + visual mashup)

## NON-NARRATED UGC

### Best For
Products sold visually -- jewelry, clothing, accessories, tech, pet toys. Where visuals do the heavy lifting.

### Guidelines
- 6-8 words max per frame of text
- Length: 15-60 seconds (sweet spot: 15-45 seconds)
- Limit stock content -- keep it organic/UGC-feeling
- Can include DR elements at end (guarantee, urgency, scarcity)
- Buying decision is emotional/impulse -- driven by seeing product in action

---

## ORGANIC-STYLE UGC

### Best For
Cosmetics, skincare, audiences accustomed to buying from influencer content.

### Guidelines
- Delivery of actor is CRITICAL -- must speak naturally on camera
- Person must fit the ICP perfectly to seem like actual customer
- Minimal editing -- no fancy effects, no jump cuts, very "normal" feeling
- Eliminate typical DR visual elements (overlays, text animations)
- Works perfectly paired with whitelisting (running as creator's post)
- Entire ad can be one long talking-head shot of creator demonstrating product
- Fully scripted but sounds completely unscripted

## STAGED TESTIMONIAL PRODUCTION PROCESS

1. Hop on video call with actor
2. Set up call as if gathering customer feedback (not reading a script)
3. Tell them product name and key talking points to hit
4. Give them the "character" -- they are a satisfied customer
5. Ask open-ended questions: "What's your experience?" "How did you find it?"
6. Record entire call
7. Let them do multiple takes -- coach between takes
8. Extract best, most genuine-sounding clips
9. Include specific details from reviews ("lost 15 pounds," "cravings are way down")

**Key:** Capture REACTIONS, not recitations. The best testimonials come from actors getting into character.

## VIDEO FORMAT SPECIFICATIONS

### Aspect Ratios
| Ratio | Use For | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| 9:16 (vertical) | Stories, Reels, TikTok | Primary format -- start here |
| 1:1 (square) | Feed | Good testing format, works everywhere |
| 4:5 | Feed (optimal) | Maximum feed real estate |
| 16:9 (landscape) | YouTube, in-stream | YouTube ads only |

### Safe Zones (9:16)
- **Top 15%:** No critical text (profile/username overlay)
- **Bottom 20%:** No critical text (CTA buttons, swipe-up UI)
- **Center 65%:** Primary message zone for all text and key visuals

### Technical Requirements
- Subtitles on ALL videos -- never assume audio is on
- Subtitle font: large enough for mobile viewing
- Resolution: minimum 720p (1080p preferred)
- File format: MP4
- Max file size: varies by platform (generally under 4GB)

### iPhone Requirement for UGC
All UGC footage should be shot on iPhone or equivalent smartphone. Professional camera footage looks too polished and defeats the authenticity purpose.
