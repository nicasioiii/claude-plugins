# Video Hooks, Retention Techniques & Editing Spectrum

## THE 3-SECOND HOOK RULE

You have approximately 3 seconds (maybe less) to stop the scroll. The hook has two components:
1. **Visual** stops the scroll (pattern interrupt, unusual image, motion)
2. **Text** gives context and reason to stay (visible from FRAME ONE)

### Hook Text Rules
- Must be visible from FRAME ONE -- no animations, no slide-ins, no delays
- Make hook text slightly bigger than regular captions
- Never confuse viewers (they leave) vs. intrigue them (they stay)
- Confusion = "What am I looking at?" / Intrigue = "I need to know how this works"

### Hook Benchmarks
- **Good hook rate:** Above 35% (US market)
- **Average hook rate:** 20-35%
- **Bad hook rate:** Below 15%
- **First Frame Retention:** Must be above 90% (1-second views / impressions)

---

## HOOK TEXT CONDENSING TECHNIQUE

Take long-winded creator intros and compress to punchy hook text:

**Before:** "I used to spend hours at the gym doing cardio but never saw results until I discovered this 15 minute workout routine"
**After:** "I replaced 2 hours of cardio with 15 minutes of THIS"

### Rules:
1. Keep the contrast (before vs. after, old vs. new)
2. Cut all filler words
3. Add curiosity element ("THIS," "one simple thing," ellipsis)
4. Maximum 8-10 words
5. Use numbers for specificity

---

## 13 HOOK SCRIPT FRAMEWORKS

1. **Start with the problem** -- "Tired of [specific frustration]?"
2. **Ask a question about the problem** -- "Why does [problem] keep happening?"
3. **Make a list** -- "3 things you're doing wrong with [topic]"
4. **Start with the product** -- "This [product] just changed everything"
5. **Start with solving the problem** -- "Here's how I fixed [problem] in [timeframe]"
6. **Start with the benefits** -- "[Specific outcome] in just [timeframe]"
7. **Start with the pain points** -- "[Pain point] is ruining your [area of life]"
8. **Be funny** -- Humor hook with unexpected twist
9. **Be aspirational** -- "What if you could [dream scenario]?"
10. **Call out specific audience** -- "[Specific identity], you need to see this"
11. **Personal experience** -- "I tried [product] and honestly..."
12. **Social proof opener** -- "[Number] people can't be wrong about this"
13. **How-to guide** -- "How to [achieve result] without [common sacrifice]"

### Hook Factory System
- Create 3 different hook variations for every new concept
- Create 10 new hooks for top-performing ads (to beat fatigue and unlock new audiences)
- Isolate variables: keep ad body and CTA consistent when testing hooks
- Track: thumbnail retention, 3-second retention, average watch time, conversion rate

## 5 PATTERN INTERRUPT TECHNIQUES

1. **Triggering curiosity** -- "You won't believe what happened when..."
2. **Triggering emotion** -- Show a strong emotional moment immediately
3. **Being relatable** -- "POV: you just [common frustrating experience]"
4. **Being shocking/surprising** -- Unexpected visual or statement
5. **Showcasing opportunity** -- "What if I told you this costs $5?"

## MR. BEAST RETENTION TECHNIQUES (APPLIED TO ADS)

### 1. Open Loops
Stack reveals that each shatter assumptions. Delay product reveal until viewer is emotionally invested.
- Loop 1: Show impossible result (50 lb weight loss)
- Loop 2: Show them eating pizza while fit
- Loop 3: Reveal it happened in just 90 days
- Loop 4: Show testimonials proving it works for everyone
- **Never reveal product too early** -- triggers skeptic mode

### 2. Visual Variety & Pacing
Something must change every 1-3 seconds (new angle, text, animation).
- Match pacing to audience: Gen Z = every second; 60+ = slower, calmer
- Never front-load all effort into the hook then let the rest fizzle
- The middle of the video needs just as much production effort as the opening

### 3. Progress Bar Psychology
Show viewers how much time is left; they commit to finishing.
- Start timer fast (show 50% progress in first 10 seconds), then slow down -- viewer is already invested
- Use countdown timers before product reveal: "Solution revealed in 20 seconds"

## MUSIC & SFX STRATEGY

- Build tension with music, then PAUSE at key moments to emphasize the next line
- **Music pause + key statement = maximum impact**
- Sound effects support visuals: cash register for value, tick sounds for benefits, buzzer for negatives
- Pausing music before product reveal creates anticipation
- 90% of people are more likely to remember funny ads than any other type -- humor + sound design is powerful

## STORY vs. RETENTION EDITING SPECTRUM

There is no single "best" editing style. Position on this spectrum based on platform and audience.

### Story Editing (For Depth)
- In-depth dialogue, thoughtful pacing, subtle foreshadowing
- Visual storytelling through shot composition
- Realistic sound design
- **Pros:** Mental engagement, strong emotions, deep connection, universal appeal
- **Cons:** Slow attention build, dependent on story quality, high time investment
- **Best for:** Mini VSLs, older audiences, high-AOV products

### Retention Editing (For Views)
- Hyper dialogue (bare minimum of most engaging statements)
- Rapid pacing, quick cuts, flashy visuals
- Strong exaggerated sound design
- **Pros:** Quick attention, high view potential, low time investment
- **Cons:** Over-stimulation, joyless engagement, feeling of manipulation
- **Best for:** Short-form UGC, younger audiences, impulse products

### The Hybrid Approach (Recommended for Ads)
Adjust editing style dynamically within a single video:
- **If a section is inherently interesting:** Lean story editing, let it breathe
- **If a section is contextually necessary but dry:** Use retention techniques (fast cuts, animations)
- **Beginning of video:** Lean retention (short attention spans, viewer not invested)
- **Once viewer is invested:** Shift toward story editing (earn deeper engagement)

## CTA PLACEMENT DISCOVERY

CTAs in the MIDDLE of the video get clicked most (not start or end).
- Only ~10% of viewers reach the end
- Middle captures the largest engaged audience
- Include a CTA card at the end anyway, but the primary CTA should be mid-video
- Always include money-back guarantee in the CTA: "Try today risk-free"

---

## WATCHABILITY QUALITY CHECKLIST (PRE-LAUNCH)

Before launching any video ad, editors must verify:

1. **Shot Selection:** Are shots serving the primary goal? Background shots when text is focus; foreground when visuals are selling.
2. **Timing & Pacing:** Does pace match target audience?
3. **Focus:** Is viewer attention directed correctly at each moment?
4. **Information Alignment:** Right info shown at right time?
5. **Design & Text Treatment:** Clean, readable, appropriate for demographic
6. **Audio:** Clean, clear, appropriate volume levels
7. **Subtitles:** Present, readable, properly timed

**Core principle:** Find WINNING ads, not launch volume of mediocre ads. Winners have robust scripts, great pacing, great transitions, great acting -- everything in place.
