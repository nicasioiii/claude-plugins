# Video Script Writing Process & Framework Selection

## SCRIPT WRITING FROM CODE BANKS

Scripts are assembled, not invented. The code bank is your raw material.

### On-Ramp Building Process (Before Product Intro)

1. **Select framework** based on awareness level (see below)
2. **Place framework structure** in script template
3. **Go to code bank** and find reviews matching each section
4. **Pull customer language** directly -- don't rewrite in marketing speak
5. **Use multiple reviews** -- pick best sentences per section
6. **Check timing** -- product intro timing must match awareness level

### Off-Ramp Building (After Product Intro)

**Two types of objections (address in this order):**

1. **Primary objections (FIRST):** Product-specific questions
   - "Is this vegan?" "Does this work on [body part]?" "Is it harmful for pets/kids?"
   - Address these immediately after product introduction

2. **Secondary objections (LATER):** Purchase-process questions
   - "Do you have a money-back guarantee?" "Is it too expensive?" "Payment plans?"
   - Place further down in the video

**Keep financial/monetary objections further down.** Handle product concerns first.

**Three ways to address objections in the off-ramp:**
1. Same narrator answers them directly
2. B-rolls + voiceovers (supporting visuals with voice addressing objections)
3. Staged testimonials or real customer clips addressing specific concerns

**The off-ramp rarely changes between ads.** The on-ramp is where most iteration happens.

## FRAMEWORK SELECTION BY AWARENESS LEVEL

### Unaware Frameworks
**Structure:** Curiosity > Educate > Problem > Personal Experience > Reveal Product > Benefits > How-to > Testimonial > CTA
- Very indirect -- don't mention product for 30-40+ seconds
- More education, storytelling, facts before product reveal
- Longer-form, mini-VSL style
- Use open loops to maintain curiosity

### Problem-Aware Frameworks
**Structure:** Problem > Agitate > Solution > Benefits > Outcome > CTA (basic PAS)
- Start directly with the problem
- Agitate before introducing solution
- Still some education needed
- Product intro at 15-25 seconds

### Solution-Aware Frameworks
**Structure:** Problem > Solution intro > Product features > Benefits > Social proof > CTA
- Somewhat indirect but product introduction happens faster
- Focus on WHY your solution is different
- Product intro at 10-20 seconds

### Product-Aware Frameworks
**Structure:** Product in hook > Demo > Social proof > Objections > CTA
- Direct -- introduce product in the hook or within first 5-15 seconds
- Use formats like: "Do you really think [product] would have gone viral if it didn't work?"
- Reaction videos to winning ads (address comments/objections)
- Live demonstrations, "trying the viral product" format

### Most-Aware Frameworks
**Structure:** Offer > Product > Urgency > CTA
- Push for the sale -- customers just need an incentive
- Sale/offer-focused: "Biggest Black Friday sale ever -- $240 off"
- Short, direct, offer-centric
- Under 30 seconds often sufficient

---

## TIMING RULES FOR IDENTIFYING AWARENESS LEVEL

Use these to analyze existing ads OR guide your own:
- Product introduced within 15-20 seconds = product-aware or most-aware ad
- Product introduced after 20+ seconds = solution-aware ad
- Product introduced after 40+ seconds = problem-aware or unaware ad
- **The hook reveals the awareness level:** Indirect = colder audience; Direct = warmer audience

## HYPOTHESIS CREATION FOR TESTING

A hypothesis = the test you want to run. Multiple angles/concepts sit under one hypothesis.

### Four Hypothesis Brackets
1. **Testing a specific creative type or angle** -- e.g., VSLs vs. UGC
2. **Testing a specific framework** -- e.g., PAS vs. Outcome-Problem-Agitation for same type
3. **Testing a specific awareness level** -- e.g., problem-aware vs. unaware audience
4. **Testing new personas** -- e.g., older audiences when only showing younger creators

**Example:** Client has only run short-form UGC. Hypothesis: Test mini VSLs where product introduction is delayed to 60+ seconds.

## CREATIVE IDEATION FRAMEWORK (OFFER > AUDIENCE > ANGLE)

Produces almost unlimited concepts:

1. Choose the OFFER you want to push (specific product/SKU)
2. Identify 3 DIFFERENT audiences that would buy the product
3. Come up with 2 angles per audience

**Result:** 6 campaign concepts from 1 offer.

**Example (PetLabCo Probiotic Chews):**
- Audience 1 (New pet owners): "Prevent gut bacteria" / "Help your pup grow healthy"
- Audience 2 (Ingredient-conscious): "Locally sourced" / "Formulated by experts"
- Audience 3 (Vet-trusting): "#1 recommended by vets" / Vet endorsement angle

## SCRIPT FORMAT TEMPLATE

```
[HOOK -- 0-3 sec]
Visual: [Describe what viewer sees]
Text on screen: [Hook text -- 8 words max]
Audio: [Music/SFX note]
Script: "[Spoken words]"

[ON-RAMP -- 3-20 sec (adjust by awareness level)]
Visual: [Scene description]
Text: [Supporting text overlay if needed]
Script: "[Spoken words assembled from code bank]"

[PRODUCT INTRODUCTION -- timing varies by awareness level]
Visual: [Product reveal moment]
Script: "[Introduction line]"

[BENEFITS SECTION]
Visual: [B-roll of product in use / results]
Script: "[3-5 USPs from code bank]"

[OFF-RAMP / OBJECTION HANDLING]
Visual: [Testimonials, demos, guarantee badge]
Script: "[Address primary then secondary objections]"

[CTA]
Visual: [Product + offer card]
Text: [Offer details, guarantee, urgency]
Script: "[CTA line with guarantee]"
```

## BIGGER vs. SMALLER ACCOUNT STRATEGY

### Bigger Accounts (High spend + existing winners)
- Play in the "grey area"
- Out of 15 concepts: 10-12 based on proven frameworks, 3-5 are new tests
- Research source: Internal ad account data primarily
- Collect learnings from what already works, then take calculated risks

### Smaller Accounts (Low spend / new products)
- Black or white -- they need ads that perform
- Research source: AdSpy, competition research, manual research
- Focus on externally proven hooks and visual structures
- More conservative with frameworks, bolder with hooks

## SCRIPT EDITING CHECKLIST

1. Distance yourself (take a break before editing)
2. Reorganize the order if flow feels off
3. Remove anything that doesn't advance the story or sell
4. Delete filler words (um, so, like, basically)
5. Read it out loud -- if it sounds unnatural, rewrite
6. Add formatting (bold key lines, note visual changes)
7. Check that timing matches awareness level
8. Get feedback from someone who fits the target persona
9. Verify all claims are supported by code bank data
