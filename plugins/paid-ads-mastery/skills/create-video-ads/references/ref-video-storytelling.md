# Video Storytelling Frameworks & Voiceover Writing

## THE 4 ESSENTIAL STORY ELEMENTS

Every compelling video ad story requires these four elements working together:

1. **Mission** -- The character pursues a clear goal. The viewer must understand what the character wants to achieve. In ads: the transformation the customer seeks.

2. **Conflict** -- Obstacles in the way. Without conflict, there is no tension and no reason to keep watching. In ads: the problem, failed alternatives, skepticism.

3. **Character Transformation** -- The character changes through the journey. They are different at the end. In ads: the before/after state. The customer becomes someone new.

4. **Vulnerability** -- Authentic human weakness makes the character relatable and creates emotional connection. In ads: honest admission of struggle, fear, or failure.

---

## THE 6-STAGE STORY ARC

1. **Hook** -- Grab attention in the first seconds (pattern interrupt, bold statement, visual shock)
2. **Context** -- Give the viewer background to understand the story (the situation, the character, the world)
3. **Setup** -- Establish the goal, stakes, and character situation (what they want, what happens if they fail)
4. **Progression** -- Show the journey with rising tension, setbacks, and small wins (the middle -- maintain engagement)
5. **All-Is-Lost Moment** -- The darkest point where the goal seems impossible (maximum emotional tension)
6. **Finale + Reflection** -- Resolution of the story, followed by the lesson or takeaway (product = the resolution)

**For educational/topic-driven ads:** The "all-is-lost moment" becomes a key insight or perspective shift. The "finale" is the actionable takeaway (your product/solution).

## THE HERO'S JOURNEY (JOSEPH CAMPBELL -- APPLIED TO ADS)

Classic structure audiences are hardwired to follow:

1. **A protagonist** (the customer) is called on to make a journey (solve their problem)
2. They are **reluctant** but embark anyway (they've tried other things, they're skeptical)
3. During the journey, they face **obstacles** (failed alternatives, worsening condition)
4. They find a **mentor/tool** (your product) that helps them overcome
5. The **transformed person** returns to share knowledge (testimonial, recommendation)

**Application to mini VSLs:** The narrator takes the viewer on this journey. "I was just like you... I tried everything... then I found [product]... now my life is [transformed]."

## LINEAR vs. NON-LINEAR STORYTELLING

### Linear Storytelling
Events unfold chronologically. Easier to follow, builds naturally.
- **Best for:** Simple product stories, testimonials, how-to content
- **Structure:** Problem > Attempt > Discovery > Solution > Outcome

### Non-Linear Storytelling
Events rearranged for dramatic effect (starting at climax, flashing back).
- **Best for:** Pattern interrupts, mini VSLs, emotional hooks
- **Structure:** Outcome (hook) > Flash back to problem > Journey > Return to outcome with context
- Creates curiosity and tension: "How did she lose 50 pounds while eating pizza? Let me take you back to 6 months ago..."

## SHOW, DON'T TELL -- LEVELS OF EXPERIENCE

The biggest mistake in video ads: NARRATING events instead of VISUALLY IMMERSING the viewer.

### Application to Ads:
- **Telling (lowest immersion):** "Our cream reduces wrinkles." (Narrator states fact)
- **Showing (medium immersion):** B-roll of cream being applied, skin texture visibly improving
- **Full immersion (highest):** Close-up of woman's face, morning light, touching her skin with surprise and joy, no words needed

### When to Use Which Level:
- **Telling:** Context/transitions between scenes. Quick product specs.
- **Showing:** Emotional peaks, product demonstrations, transformation moments.
- **Immersion:** Hook moments, climactic reveals, the "after" state.

## PHYSICAL AND EMOTIONAL JOURNEY (DUAL TRACK)

Every strong video ad has TWO parallel journeys:

1. **Physical journey** -- External events, actions, progress toward the goal
2. **Emotional journey** -- Internal feelings, doubts, realizations, transformation

### Common Mistakes:
- Only showing the physical journey (becomes a boring product recap)
- Only showing the emotional journey (lacks concrete narrative)
- Not letting them interact at key moments

### How to Do It Right:
Physical events TRIGGER emotional responses. Emotional states INFLUENCE physical decisions.

**Example in a fitness product ad:**
- Physical: Struggling at gym (showing) > Discovers product (turning point) > Crushing workouts (progression)
- Emotional: Frustration, self-doubt > Hope, curiosity > Confidence, pride
- Interaction: The moment of frustration (emotional) drives the search for a solution (physical)

## THE 4-STEP WRITING PROCESS FOR VIDEO SCRIPTS

### Step 1: Research Your Topic
- Deep research using code bank, customer reviews, Reddit, competitor ads
- Use customer language verbatim
- Identify emotional triggers and specific pain points

### Step 2: Outline the Structure
- Define chapters/sections of the video
- Assign story pieces to each section (bullet points of what happens)
- Review flow: Does each section lead naturally to the next?

### Step 3: Write the Script
- Draft with awareness of your writing style
- Balance narrative (story) vs. explanatory (educational) sections
- Read each line out loud -- if it sounds unnatural, rewrite

### Step 4: Edit for Clarity
Apply these 9 editing tips:
1. Distance yourself (take a break before editing)
2. Reorganize the order
3. Remove to progress (cut anything that doesn't advance the story)
4. Delete filler words
5. Read it out loud
6. Add formatting notes (visual changes, music cues)
7. Check spelling and grammar
8. Get feedback from someone in the target demographic
9. List your mistakes (build a personal error database)

## 4 LEVELS OF SCRIPTING

Match the scripting level to the content type:

1. **Tightly written voiceovers** -- Word-for-word scripted narration. For mini VSLs, complex explanations, emotional voiceovers.
2. **Outlined talking points** -- Key points scripted, delivery improvised. For UGC where creator has strong camera presence.
3. **Guided improvisation** -- Loose framework, mostly natural speech. For organic-style UGC, "trying the product" format.
4. **Fully improvised moments** -- Authentic in-the-moment reactions. For testimonials, unboxing, genuine reactions.

---

## VOICEOVER WRITING -- 7 TIPS

1. **Relate to the visuals** -- Voiceover should connect to what's on screen
2. **Add context** -- Fill in what visuals alone cannot communicate
3. **Create a connection** -- Between viewer and character/product
4. **Tell the emotional journey** -- The inner world, feelings, realizations
5. **Compress time** -- Narrate transitions between key moments
6. **Share the takeaway** -- The lesson or insight the viewer should remember
7. **Leave room for moments** -- Silence is powerful. Let visuals breathe at emotional peaks.

---

## AD ANALYSIS METHOD (REVERSE ENGINEERING)

Watch the same winning ad multiple times, each time focusing on a different element:
- **1st watch:** Hook only (first 3 seconds)
- **2nd watch:** Script structure and building block sequence
- **3rd watch:** Visuals (shot types, transitions, text overlays)
- **4th watch:** Supers / text overlays
- **5th watch:** Pacing (new info/visuals should change every ~1.5 seconds)
- **6th watch:** Click through and check congruency (ad must match post-click experience)
