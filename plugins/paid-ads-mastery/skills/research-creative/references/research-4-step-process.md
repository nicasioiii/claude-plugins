# The Complete 4-Step Research Process

## Step 1: Ad Account Analysis

### Sub-Step A: Digging Questions (8 Questions to Answer)

For every client or product, answer these before creating anything:

1. **Which angles are running?** (UGC, narration, UGC mashup, etc.)
2. **What video creative types?** (mini VSLs, UGC, mashups, short-form, longer-form VSLs)
3. **What works on statics?** (benefit callouts, comparisons, before/after, etc.)
4. **Which awareness levels targeted?** (problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, etc.)
5. **Most profitable audience segment?** Check Ads Manager breakdowns by age/gender
6. **Most profitable creatives?** Link top 5-10 performers from past 90 days, filtered by spend + performance
7. **Monthly ad spend?** (approximate)
8. **Target CPA / Target CAC?**

### Sub-Step B: Turning Dirt into Gold (Creative Breakdowns)

For each top performer from the digging questions, break down:
- **Script formula** (e.g., Problem > Agitation > Solution > Benefits > Outcome > CTA)
- **Visuals per sequence** (hook = animation, body = talking ad, creative flex + b-rolls)
- **Personas and energy** (age range, gender, energy level -- excited vs. informative vs. chill)
- **Concept and creative style**
- **Pacing** (fast, slow, normal)
- **Link to the creative**

This reveals your "creative success formula" -- the patterns that work for THIS specific brand.

### Sub-Step C: Comments on Ads

1. Go to ad level > click edit > see previews > Facebook post with comments
2. Analyze comments on MOST ENGAGED / top performing ads (both Instagram and Facebook)
3. Categorize all comments:
   - **Positive reactions** (what people love)
   - **Negative reactions** (what triggers pushback)
   - **Specific objections** ("it doesn't work," "how is this different?," "how long does it last?")
4. These objections feed directly into the off-ramp/sales sequence of future ads

## Step 2: Support Documents

### Document A: Code Bank + Connecting Threads

**Source:** Extract ALL reviews from Amazon, website, Trustpilot, wherever they exist.

**Process:**
1. Categorize reviews by: personas, outcomes, benefits, pain points
2. Look for LONGER reviews with stories, not one-liners
3. For each review, identify the main takeaway
4. Create columns: "fewer joint problems," "better hair," "fresher skin," etc.
5. Identify the SPECIFIC LANGUAGE customers use
   - Example: Customers say "fresher" for skin, not "glowy" -- use "fresher" in scripts
6. Find "connecting threads" -- how customers discovered the product and their journey

### Document B: Personas List

For each persona, document:
- Age, gender, country
- Tangible desires (physical/measurable outcomes)
- Intangible desires (emotional/invisible byproducts)
- Tangible pain points (specific physical problems)
- Intangible pain points (emotional consequences of those problems)
- Past purchases and why they failed

**Tangible desire example:** "Partner to be pain-free during periods"
**Intangible desire example:** "Feel appreciated by partner for getting the best gift"

**Caution:** Do not rely solely on Facebook demographic data -- it is biased by who you show in your ads. If you show 24-year-old models but sell to 55+ women, Facebook will not show to older audiences.

### Document C: Post-Purchase Survey Questions

Recommended questions (pick ~5 to avoid fatigue):
1. What style/flavor/size do you want most that is out of stock?
2. How would you describe us to a friend? (reveals exact customer language)
3. How long did you know about the brand before purchasing?
4. What was going on in your life that made you buy?
5. Why did you buy from us?
6. What almost kept you from buying? (reveals objections)
7. Paint a picture of how you use the product
8. If our product was not available tomorrow, how would your routine change?

Tools: NoCommerce, Hayflow. Push clients to set up surveys immediately.

## Step 3: Manual Research -- Reverse Engineering Customers

### Process for Identifying Awareness Level
1. Think like the customer -- put yourself in their shoes
2. Start with Google search: "how to get rid of [problem]"
3. Go to Reddit -- primary research platform (anonymous, honest sharing)
4. Open 15-20 different threads and dive deep
5. At each stage, identify: Are people talking about problems? Solutions? Specific brands?
6. Move through the awareness funnel step by step

### Awareness Level Detection Signals
| Signal | Awareness Level |
|---|---|
| People discussing specific brands/products heavily | Product-aware |
| People discussing remedies/solutions but not brands | Solution-aware |
| People only discussing the problem itself | Problem-aware |
| People not even recognizing it as a problem | Unaware |

**Where there is the most engagement = where most of your audience sits.** Target that level first.

**Contrarian insight:** The assumption that "there are always more unaware people" is NOT always true. In skincare, for example, many people are already product-aware due to market saturation.

## Step 4: Competition Research

### Primary Tool: AdSpy
- Filter by: keyword, country, total likes (1000+ minimum), media type, date range
- Likes = proxy for scale. 500+ likes = ad has been running for a while
- Provides: link to actual ads, post IDs with comments, engagement data, landing page URLs
- Break down at least 4-6 winning ads from AdSpy

### Secondary Tool: Meta Ad Library
- Filters: language, format, active status, impression by date
- Limitation: No engagement/performance data -- just visibility
- Break down 2-3 ads from Ad Library

### Total Target: 8-10 Competition Ad Breakdowns
Break down each competitor ad the same way as internal ads: script formula, visuals, personas, concept, framework.

### Use Cases for Competition Research
1. **Validate ideas:** If competitors also run similar angles, higher chance of success
2. **Primary source for new brands:** When there is little internal data, competition research is the main input

## Research Data Organization

### Two Storage Locations
1. **Creative Strategy Document (Google Doc):** Digging questions, comments on ads, off-ramp objections, product/survey research
2. **Creative Strategy Sheet (Google Sheet):** Turning dirt into gold (creative breakdowns), code banks, personas, consumer psychology, competition research

### Account Size Strategy

**Bigger accounts (high spend + existing winners):**
- 10-12 of 15 concepts based on proven internal frameworks
- 3-5 are calculated new tests
- Primary source: Internal ad account data

**Smaller accounts (low spend + few bangers):**
- Primary source: AdSpy, competition research, manual research
- Focus on externally proven hooks and visual structures
