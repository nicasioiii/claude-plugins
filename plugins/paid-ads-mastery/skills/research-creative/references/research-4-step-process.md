# Complete 4-Step Research Process

## Step 1: Ad Account Analysis (Deep Dive)

### Account Metrics Review
- Time period: Last 90 days minimum
- Metrics to calculate:
  - Average ROAS across all campaigns (your benchmark)
  - Average CPA across all campaigns
  - Average hook rate for video creatives
  - Average conversion rate across all products

### Creative Performance Deep Dive
**For top 5 performing creatives by ROAS:**

1. **Hook Type Analysis**
   - Visual hook (shot, cut, movement)
   - Problem hook ("You know that feeling...")
   - Question hook ("Did you know...")
   - Statistic hook ("73% of people...")
   - Story hook ("I used to...")
   - Benefit hook ("Imagine if...")

2. **Copy Architecture**
   - Length (short vs long form)
   - Emotion (fear, desire, curiosity, social proof)
   - Pain vs gain focus
   - Urgency/scarcity present (yes/no)

3. **Product Angle**
   - Price/value positioning
   - Lifestyle/aspirational angle
   - Problem-solution frame
   - Comparison to alternatives
   - Guarantee/warranty angle

4. **Audience Segment**
   - Age range that converted best
   - Gender breakdown
   - Interest targeting used
   - Geographic targeting
   - Lookalike % if used

### Account Stage Assessment
- Days since first purchase event: __
- Total conversion events tracked: __
- Pixel maturity: Brand new / 3-6 months / 6-12 months / 2+ years
- Account status: Scaling / Testing / Paused / Mixed

## Step 2: Support Documents - Code Bank Building

### Source 1: Amazon/E-commerce Review Mining

**Process:**
1. Go to product reviews on Amazon (or Trustpilot, Shopify reviews)
2. Sort by: NEWEST first
3. Read 50+ reviews minimum across 1-star and 5-star

**For 1-star reviews** (extract objections):
- What specific problem did customer experience?
- Why wasn't product a good fit?
- What competitor/alternative did they compare to?
- What exact language do they use for disappointment?

**For 5-star reviews** (extract benefits):
- What specific benefit surprised them?
- What problem did it solve?
- What is their aspirational language?
- What exact words do they use for satisfaction?

**Code Bank Template:**

```
OBJECTIONS FROM 1-STAR:
["doesn't stay in place during exercise"]
["fell apart after 3 weeks"]
["much cheaper alternatives work just as well"]
["took 2 weeks to arrive"]

BENEFITS FROM 5-STAR:
["finally found something that actually stays on"]
["lasted 6+ months, worth every dollar"]
["looks way more professional than competitors"]
["arrived next day, excellent packaging"]

EMOTIONAL TRIGGERS:
["frustration": missing key features, poor quality, slow shipping]
["joy": exceeded expectations, long-lasting, great value]
["relief": solves problem they've had for years]
["confidence": feels like premium product]
```

### Source 2: Reddit Research

**Search strategy:**
1. Use site:reddit.com + keyword on Google
   - "site:reddit.com weight loss struggles"
   - "site:reddit.com back pain solutions"
2. Alternate: Go directly to r/[category] subreddits
   - r/skincare, r/loseit, r/bodyweightfitness, r/budgetaudio
3. Read 30+ threads (sort by "Most Commented")

**What to extract:**
- How do they describe their problem? (language mining)
- What solutions are they currently using?
- What are their frustrations with current solutions?
- What objections come up repeatedly?
- What outcome would be life-changing?

**Reddit Code Bank:**

```
PROBLEM LANGUAGE (extracted verbatim):
["can't find a product that doesn't pill after washing"]
["every single one gives me breakouts, what am I doing wrong"]
["trying to find something that doesn't break the bank"]

CURRENT SOLUTIONS & FRUSTRATIONS:
[Using: competitor A, frustration: expensive, need to reapply constantly]
[Using: DIY solution, frustration: inconsistent results, time-consuming]

OBJECTION PATTERNS:
["is this worth the hype or just marketing"]
["how is this different from [competitor] which is cheaper"]
["will it work for my specific situation"]

DESIRED OUTCOME LANGUAGE:
["honestly just want something that works and lasts"]
["would change my life if I didn't have to think about this daily"]
["finally want to feel confident"]
```

### Source 3: Internal Customer Feedback

**Collection method 1: Support Tickets**
- Export last 3 months of support emails
- Identify top 5 recurring questions/complaints
- Extract exact customer phrasing

**Collection method 2: Customer Interviews**
- Target 5-10 customers who gave 5-star reviews
- Ask: "What problem was this solving for you?"
- Ask: "What made you choose us over competitors?"
- Ask: "What almost stopped you from buying?"
- Record exact language they use

**Collection method 3: Testimonials**
- Review all written testimonials
- Pull authentic quotes (even grammatically imperfect ones)
- Note specific outcomes mentioned

**Collection method 4: Surveys**
- Ask: "What was your biggest frustration before this product?"
- Ask: "What surprised you most about this product?"
- Ask: "Would you recommend to a friend? Why/why not?"

**Internal Code Bank Structure:**

```
SPECIFIC PROBLEM THEY HAD:
["tried 5 different brands over 2 years, none worked"]
["spending $200+ per month on alternatives"]
["pain made my job unbearable, affecting income"]

WHY THEY CHOSE YOU:
["your guarantee was the only one that made me feel safe trying"]
["friend recommended, plus cheaper than I expected"]
["honest reviews from real people, not just marketing"]

OBJECTION THEY OVERCOME:
["thought it was too expensive, but calculated cost per use"]
["worried about waste, but product lasts 6+ months"]
["was skeptical it would work, but tried anyway due to guarantee"]

SPECIFIC TRANSFORMATIONS:
["can now work full days without pain flare-ups"]
["saves me 45 minutes daily in my routine"]
["finally feel like myself again"]
```

## Step 3: Manual Market Research

### Google Trends Research

**Process:**
1. Go to trends.google.com
2. Enter your main keyword
3. Review "Rising" tab (emerging interest)
4. Check regional breakdown (which countries/states show interest)
5. Check seasonal patterns (Q4 spike for holidays, Q1 for resolutions)

**What to note:**
- Is interest growing or declining?
- Which regions show highest interest (adjust targeting)?
- Which months show peaks (seasonal strategy)?
- Related searches (alternative keywords for broader reach)?

**Market Awareness insight:**
- High search volume = Problem-aware or Solution-aware market
- Low search volume = Unaware market (needs education)
- Rising trend = Market emerging (first-mover advantage)
- Declining trend = Market saturated (need stronger differentiation)

### YouTube Autocomplete Research

**Process:**
1. Go to YouTube.com
2. In search bar type: "[your product] why..."
3. Review autocomplete suggestions
4. Type: "[your product] problem..." and "[your product] won't..."
5. Note variations

**Insight extraction:**
- What questions are people actually asking?
- What problems are they mentioning?
- What doubts come up?

**Example - "Neck pillow":**
```
Autocomplete shows:
- "neck pillow why painful"
- "neck pillow why doesn't work"
- "neck pillow won't stay in place"
- "neck pillow problem"

Insight: Main objections are comfort + durability
```

### TikTok/Instagram Trends

**Process:**
1. Search hashtags in your product category
2. Filter by "Most Viral" / "Trending"
3. Note which video formats get engagement
4. Document trending sounds (what are creators using?)
5. Identify pattern interrupts (what makes people stop scrolling?)

**What to extract:**
- Which problem angles are getting attention?
- Which solutions are trending?
- What format works best (vertical video, before/after, educational)?
- What tone resonates (serious, humorous, relatable)?

## Step 4: Competitor Research (Ads Library Deep Dive)

### Facebook Ads Library Process

**Access:** facebook.com/ads/library

**Search strategy:**
1. Select country your ads run in
2. Choose "All Ads" category
3. KEYWORD search (not brand search)
   - Search: "weight loss supplement" (NOT brand name)
   - Shows all ads using that keyword phrase
4. Review first 100+ ads
5. Look for patterns

### What Each Ad Tells You

**Hook Analysis:**
- First 1-3 seconds: What grabs attention?
- Is it visual (movement/cuts) or text-based?
- Does it relate to product directly or is it pattern interrupt?

**Copy Analysis:**
- Headline: What's the core promise/curiosity?
- Body: Problem focus vs benefit focus?
- Social proof present? (testimonials, stats, reviews)
- Urgency/scarcity language? ("Limited time", "Only X left")

**Product Angle:**
- Is it priced competitively or premium positioning?
- Is it lifestyle/aspirational or problem-solving?
- Is it educational or emotional?
- Is it compared to alternatives?

**Landing Page Approach:**
- Does ad link to advertorial? (educational article)
- Does ad link to listicle? ("5 Ways to...", "7 Tips for...")
- Does ad link to product page directly?
- Does ad link to collection page?

### Pattern Detection Matrix

```
Hook Type      | Frequency | ROAS Indicator | When to Use |
Problem hook   | 60%       | High          | Problem-aware audiences |
Visual hook    | 25%       | Medium        | Unaware audiences |
Question hook  | 40%       | Medium-high   | Curiosity-driven |
Statistic hook | 35%       | High          | Authority-focused |
Testimonial    | 50%       | Very High     | Product-aware audiences |
```

**Action:** Count patterns in competitor ads. If 70% use testimonials, testimonials are table-stakes. If 20% use problem hooks, problem hooks are unexplored.

### Duplication = Winning Signal

- Ads duplicated 1-2 times: Being tested
- Ads duplicated 3-5 times: Likely working
- Ads duplicated 5+ times: "Heavy hitters" - worth deep copying

**When you see a duplicated ad:**
1. Screenshot the hook
2. Document the exact copy
3. Note the landing page (if visible from ad)
4. Assess: Can we use similar STRUCTURE with our ANGLE?
