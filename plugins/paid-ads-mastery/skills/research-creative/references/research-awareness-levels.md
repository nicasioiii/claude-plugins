# Awareness Levels & Market Sophistication

## Eugene Schwartz's 5 Stages of Market Awareness

### Stage 1: Unaware
**Audience state:** Do not know they have a problem.
**Creative goal:** Pattern interrupt, trigger curiosity, tell stories.
**Ad approach:** Very indirect. Do not mention product for 30-40+ seconds. More education, storytelling, facts before product reveal. Longer-form, mini-VSL style.
**Example hook:** "The hidden reason you're exhausted even after 8 hours of sleep"

**Framework:** Curiosity > Educate > Problem > Personal Experience > Reveal Product > Benefits > How-to > Testimonial > CTA

### Stage 2: Problem Aware
**Audience state:** Recognize the problem, unsure of solutions.
**Creative goal:** Validate experience, educate on the problem, introduce solution category.
**Ad approach:** Start directly with the problem. Agitate before introducing solution. Still needs education.
**Example hook:** "Main causes of chronic fatigue and how science is addressing them"

**Framework:** Problem > Agitate > Solution > Benefits > Outcome > CTA (PAS)

### Stage 3: Solution Aware
**Audience state:** Know solution type exists, have not chosen a product.
**Creative goal:** Differentiate your approach, highlight unique mechanisms.
**Ad approach:** Product introduction happens faster (within 15-20 seconds). Differentiate from alternatives.
**Example hook:** "Why natural supplements outperform synthetic for sustainable energy"

**Framework:** Problem > Solution intro > Product features > Benefits > Social proof > CTA

### Stage 4: Product Aware
**Audience state:** Know your product, undecided.
**Creative goal:** Address objections, provide social proof, demonstrate.
**Ad approach:** Direct -- introduce product in hook or within first 5-15 seconds. Reaction videos, live demos, "trying the viral product" format.
**Example hook:** "Our formula delivered 42% better results in clinical trials"

**Frameworks:** Reaction to winning ads, live demonstrations, "Do you really think this would have gone viral if it didn't work?"

### Stage 5: Most Aware
**Audience state:** Ready to buy, need final push.
**Creative goal:** Present urgency, scarcity, irresistible offer.
**Ad approach:** Push for the sale. Short, direct, offer-centric.
**Example hook:** "Limited edition, money-back guarantee, free shipping this weekend only"

**Framework:** Offer > Urgency > Scarcity > CTA

---

## Key Rule: Walk Through the Levels

If starting at an earlier awareness level, the ad must walk the viewer through each subsequent level. An unaware ad must progress through all 5 levels within one ad. The earlier the awareness stage, the longer and more educational the ad must be.

---

## Timing Rules for Product Introduction

| Product Introduced | Awareness Level |
|---|---|
| Within first 5-15 seconds | Product-aware or Most-aware |
| Within 15-20 seconds | Solution-aware |
| After 20-40 seconds | Problem-aware |
| After 40+ seconds | Unaware |

The timing of product introduction is the biggest tell for what awareness level an ad targets.

## Identifying Your Audience's Awareness Level

### Manual Research Process
1. Google search: "how to get rid of [problem]"
2. Go to Reddit -- open 15-20 threads, dive deep
3. At each stage identify: Are people discussing problems? Solutions? Brands?
4. Where there is the most engagement = where most of your audience sits

### Detection Signals
| What People Discuss | Awareness Level |
|---|---|
| Specific brands and products | Product-aware |
| Remedies and solutions (not brands) | Solution-aware |
| The problem itself | Problem-aware |
| Not recognizing it as a problem | Unaware |

### Contrarian Insight
The assumption that "there are always more unaware people" is NOT always true. In saturated markets (skincare, supplements), most people are already product-aware. Sometimes you must combine solution-aware + product-aware targeting because the product-aware pool alone is too small.

## Market Sophistication Levels

As audiences become more familiar with a category, basic claims lose effectiveness.

### Level 1: Simple Claim
Market is new. "This product solves X." Works because nobody else is saying it.

### Level 2: Enlarged Claim
Competitors have appeared. "This product solves X FASTER / BETTER / CHEAPER."

### Level 3: Mechanism
Claims are crowded. Must explain WHY your product works differently. "Our patented formula uses [mechanism] to solve X."

### Level 4: Enlarged Mechanism
Multiple mechanisms exist. Must prove superiority of YOUR mechanism. "Clinical trials show our [mechanism] outperforms [competitor mechanism] by 42%."

### Level 5: Experience/Identity
Market exhaustion. Claims and mechanisms are all noise. Must connect on identity/experience level. "For people who have tried everything and given up hope."

## Framework Selection by Awareness Level

| Awareness Level | Best Frameworks | Ad Length | Tone |
|---|---|---|---|
| Unaware | Curiosity > Educate > Problem > Solution | 60-120 sec | Educational, storytelling |
| Problem Aware | PAS, Problem > Agitate > Solution > Benefits | 30-60 sec | Empathetic, validating |
| Solution Aware | Differentiation, Unique mechanism, Comparison | 30-45 sec | Confident, specific |
| Product Aware | Testimonials, Demos, Objection handling | 15-30 sec | Direct, proof-heavy |
| Most Aware | Offer, Urgency, Scarcity | 10-15 sec | Urgent, action-focused |

## Winning Creative Formulas

**PAS:** Problem > Agitation > Solution
**FAB:** Feature > Advantage > Benefit
**BAB:** Before > After > Bridge
**OCR:** Objection > Counter > Reframe

### The Emotional Sandwich
Open with emotion (pain or desire) > Provide logical evidence (features, proof, data) > Close with emotion (transformation, future state)

This pattern matches how the brain processes purchase decisions: emotional trigger > rational justification > emotional commitment.

## 10 Types of Creative Angles

| Angle | Core Message | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Promotion | "Because it's on sale" | Most-aware, seasonal |
| Education | "Because it does this" | Problem-aware, solution-aware |
| Social Proof | "Because everyone loves it" | Product-aware |
| Influencer | "Because this person loves it" | Solution-aware, product-aware |
| Gifting | "Because someone you love needs it" | Seasonal, holidays |
| New Release | "Because it's new" | Product-aware, most-aware |
| Emotional Benefit | "Because it'll make you feel better" | Unaware, problem-aware |
| Functional Benefit | "Because it makes this easier" | Solution-aware |
| Comparison | "Because it's better than competitors" | Solution-aware, product-aware |
| Seasonality | "Because now is the time" | All levels, seasonal |
