## THE SCALING DECISION FRAMEWORK

### Before Scaling, Check These Prerequisites (In Order)

**1. Unit Economics: Are You Actually Profitable?**
- Calculate break-even ROAS: 1 / Profit Margin.
- If profit margin is 12%, break-even ROAS is 8.3x -- nearly impossible to scale.
- If contribution margin after ad spend is <5%, scaling will amplify losses.
-> If unit economics are broken, see ref-scaling-prerequisites.md.

**2. Offer Strength: Is Your Offer Irresistible?**
- An irresistible offer is: Valuable, Unique, Urgent, and Trustworthy.
- Google Ads cannot scale a broken offer. Facebook is just an amplifier of reality.
- If conversion rate is below 2% on cold traffic, fix the offer before scaling.
-> If offer is weak, see ref-scaling-prerequisites.md.

**3. Creative Pipeline: Do You Have Enough Creative Fuel?**
- Scaling burns through creative faster.
- Need different angles for different hot pockets (Meta).
- Need fresh hooks and variations (all platforms).
-> If creative is limited, see test-creative skill.

**4. Tracking Quality: Is Your Data Clean?**
- Scaling decisions based on bad data = scaling into losses.
- Verify: Ads Manager ROAS vs bank account ROAS correlation.
- Ensure conversion tracking is complete (Meta CAPI, Google enhanced conversions).
-> If tracking is suspect, see setup-meta-tracking or setup-google-ads.

## SCALING RULES BY PLATFORM (Quick Reference)

### Meta Scaling Rules
| Method | How | When |
|---|---|---|
| Vertical (safe) | 20% budget increase every 48hr | Proven campaigns at higher budgets ($200+/day) |
| Vertical (aggressive) | Up to 70% every 24hr | Strong performers, willing to risk learning reset |
| Horizontal/Nuke | Duplicate winner into 25-100 ad sets at $25-50 each | Want rapid expansion, willing to kill fast |
| Crazy Method | Duplicate identical ad sets in CBO | Amplify proven creative/targeting |
| Manual bid | Cost cap/bid cap with incremental increases | Want volume with cost control |
| Sniper | Very low manual bid | Capture cheapest audience pockets |
| Surge | One ad set, very high budget | Shotgun approach, opposite of nuke |
| CBO consolidation | Push winners into CBO | Maintenance-mode scaling |
| Advantage Shopping | Add winners continuously | Evergreen scaling |

### Google Scaling Rules
| Method | How | When |
|---|---|---|
| GTC budget | $10-15 increase (<$100/day) or 20% (>$100/day) | Entire campaign profitable 7-14 days |
| GTC bid | +$0.01-$0.03 max per change | ROAS >6x or budget not fully spent |
| Standalone campaign | Isolate winner at HIGH priority | Preferred first scaling method |
| PMax budget | 10-30% increase | Budget fully spent + profitable |
| PMax lower target | Reduce ROAS target by small increments | Budget NOT fully spent |
| PMax add campaigns | New per collection/country | Want broader reach |
| Search scaling | Follow 30/30 -> Target CPA/ROAS | After 30 conversions in 30 days |

## THE FUNDAMENTAL SCALING TRUTH

If lowering budget = good ROAS and increasing budget = bad ROAS, the problem is NOT your ad strategy. The problem is one of:

1. **Weak offer** -- not compelling enough at scale.
2. **Bad creatives** -- limited angles, fatiguing creative.
3. **Low AOV** -- insufficient margin to sustain higher CPAs at scale.
4. **Poor conversion rate** -- website/landing page leaks.
5. **Too much competition** -- market saturated at your price point.
6. **Product-market limitations** -- total addressable market is small.

Facebook/Google are amplifiers of reality. Fix business fundamentals first, then scale.

## ANTI-PATTERNS

1. **Scaling without unit economics validation** -- amplifying losses faster.
2. **Scaling unprofitable products hoping they turn profitable** -- almost never works (Shri).
3. **Duplicating same creatives in more campaigns** -- creates daily loop on Meta (same sales, more spend).
4. **Large budget jumps without testing** -- >30% on Google, >70% on Meta risks learning reset.
5. **Scaling based on single good day** -- need 3-5 days of above-average ROAS minimum.
6. **Ignoring the Daily Loop** -- more campaigns =/= more sales if algorithm redistributes.
7. **Not running evergreen campaigns alongside promo campaigns** -- after promo ends, nothing benefits.
8. **Scaling creatives instead of business fundamentals** -- if CVR is 1%, no amount of ad spend fixes it.
9. **Only scaling one way** -- use vertical AND horizontal simultaneously.
10. **Judging YouTube/TOF by direct ROAS** -- misses the halo effect contributing 27%+ to other campaigns.
