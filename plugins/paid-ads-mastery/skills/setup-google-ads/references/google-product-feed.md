# Google Product Feed Optimization Guide

## Product Feed Essentials

### The 50/45/5 Rule: What Drives Sales

**Google Shopping visibility breakdown:**
- 50% = Product title (keyword optimization)
- 45% = Product images (visual appeal)
- 5% = Everything else (description, reviews, etc.)

**Implication:** Invest proportionally. Spend half your optimization effort on titles, 45% on images, 5% elsewhere.

---

## Product Titles: The 50% Factor

### Title Structure & Keyword Placement

**Principle: Left-to-Right Keyword Priority**

Google reads titles left-to-right. The leftmost words carry the most weight.

**Title Formula:**
```
[Highest Volume Keyword] + [Secondary Keywords] + [Brand/Positioning]
```

**Examples:**

| Product | Title | Why It Works |
|---------|-------|--------------|
| Colorblind glasses | "Colorblind Corrective Glasses for Color Blindness \| Premium Testing Eyewear" | Primary keyword "Colorblind Corrective Glasses" first (highest volume) |
| Vintage chair | "Mid-Century Oak Dining Chair \| Vintage Wooden Accent Seating" | "Mid-Century" AND "Dining Chair" first (both high-volume) |
| Throw pillow | "Velvet Throw Pillow Cover 18x18 \| Modern Home Decor Cushion" | "Velvet Throw Pillow" first + size specification + secondary keyword |

**Structure Breakdown:**

```
[Primary Keyword 1] [Primary Keyword 2] | [Secondary Keyword] [Tertiary Descriptor]

Colorblind Corrective Glasses | for Color Blindness Premium Testing Eyewear
```

### Title Rules: What to DO

- **DO keyword optimize** — Include searchable terms in natural positions
- **DO use pipes "|"** — Pipe separator makes title more readable to humans
- **DO include variants** — If product has color/size: "Velvet Throw Pillow 18x18 [Colors]"
- **DO use 3-5 target keywords** — But woven naturally
- **DO read aloud test** — Would a real person click this?
- **DO match search intent** — If users search "buy Victorian chairs," use that language
- **DO vary titles by product** — Even similar items need distinct titles

### Title Rules: What NOT to Do

- **DO NOT keyword stuff** — Multiple instances of same word ("Glasses Colorblind Glasses for Colorblind" is spam)
- **DO NOT repeat keywords** — Each keyword once maximum
- **DO NOT use filler words** — "For people," "for you," "bestseller," "new," "limited" waste space
- **DO NOT include competitor brands** — Even if you sell them (risk: trademark issues)
- **DO NOT use ALL CAPS** — Looks unprofessional, reduces CTR
- **DO NOT truncate for space** — If you need more space, create variant listing
- **DO NOT write generic titles** — "Product" or "Item" wastes the 50% opportunity
- **DO NOT include pricing** — "(On Sale $49.99)" changes daily, keep in price field

### Title Length & Character Limits

- **Max 150 characters** (Google displays 75-80 on mobile, 150 on desktop)
- **Best practice: 50-80 characters** (ensures visibility on all devices)
- **Minimum: 30 characters** (too short leaves keyword opportunity unused)

**Length Testing:**
- Short title (30 chars): "Colorblind Glasses" — Loses secondary keywords
- Medium title (60 chars): "Colorblind Corrective Glasses | Premium Eyewear" — Balanced
- Long title (150 chars): "Colorblind Corrective Glasses for Color Blindness | Premium Testing Eyewear | Unisex | Affordable" — Keyword-optimized

**Recommendation: 60-80 characters** (balances SEO and readability)

### Title Optimization Checklist

- [ ] Highest-volume keyword is LEFTMOST
- [ ] 3-5 distinct keywords in natural English
- [ ] No keyword repetition (each word once)
- [ ] No competitor brand names
- [ ] No all-caps
- [ ] No filler words ("for you," "bestseller," "new")
- [ ] Reads naturally when spoken aloud
- [ ] 50-80 character range
- [ ] Contains primary benefit or differentiator
- [ ] Matches product category collection

## Product Images: The 45% Factor

### Image Hierarchy & Priorities

**Image Priority (by impact):**
1. Lifestyle/usage image (person using product) — HIGHEST CTR
2. Front/primary product shot — SECOND
3. Detail/close-up shot — Product quality signal
4. Alternative angle or color — Preference indication
5. Scale reference (item next to common object) — Size clarity

### Lifestyle vs. Studio Shots: Real Data

**Case Study (from conversion data):**
- Same product: Lifestyle image vs. White background studio shot
- Lifestyle image result: $150 average selling price
- Studio shot result: $100 average selling price
- Difference: **50% price premium with lifestyle photography**

**Why Lifestyle Images Win:**
- Users envision product in their space (mental ownership)
- Creates emotional connection (not just "thing for sale")
- Reduces buyer uncertainty (actual use case visible)
- Signals quality and authenticity (not generic stock photo)

### Image Selection Strategy

**Image 1: Lead Image (Lifestyle Preferred)**
- Show product in actual use context
- Example: Chair in living room, eyeglasses on person's face
- If product never used (décor item): Best angle/view
- Human in frame increases engagement

**Image 2: Alternative Angle or Color Variant**
- If product has multiple colors: Show each color
- If product is complex (furniture): Show from side/back
- Gives user second perspective
- Shows "this is real product, multiple views available"

**Image 3: Detail Shot / Texture / Quality Indicator**
- Close-up of fabric weave, material quality, stitching
- Shows craftsmanship and build quality
- Reassures on durability
- Signals "premium product" vs. cheap knock-off

**Image 4: Scale Reference**
- Product next to common object (hand, coin, ruler, person)
- Eliminates size uncertainty
- Example: Furniture against wall with person standing nearby
- Critical for items without standard size reference

**Image 5: Packaging / Unboxing (Optional)**
- Unboxing image builds trust (shows careful packaging)
- Packaging quality signals premium positioning
- Only if packaging is professional-grade
- Skip if packaging is basic/minimal

### Image Technical Requirements

**File Format:** JPEG (fastest loading, best compatibility)

**Image Dimensions:**
- Minimum: 800×800 pixels
- Recommended: 1200×1200 pixels (higher clarity)
- Aspect ratio: Square preferred (1:1)
- Avoid extreme aspect ratios (too tall/wide)

**File Size:**
- Target: 100-300 KB per image
- Optimize before upload (compress without losing quality)
- Tools: TinyPNG, ImageOptim, or platform auto-compression

**Image Quality Checklist:**
- [ ] Lighting is even and professional (not dark/grainy)
- [ ] Focus is sharp (not blurry)
- [ ] Colors are accurate to actual product
- [ ] No visible watermarks or logos (except your brand)
- [ ] No text overlays (except subtle branding)
- [ ] Image is cropped well (no excess empty space)
- [ ] Image is level (not tilted at weird angles)
- [ ] All images have consistent style/tone
- [ ] No lifestyle image shows product in poor condition
- [ ] No stock photo indicators (generic/too-perfect appearance)

### Image Consistency Across Catalog

**Why Consistency Matters:**
- Customers subconsciously expect professional standard
- Inconsistent image quality signals unprofessionalism
- Mixed quality damages credibility ("some products are real, others questionable?")
- Consistent quality builds trust

**Consistency Checklist:**
- [ ] All product images have similar lighting (not some dark, some bright)
- [ ] All images have similar background treatment (consistent aesthetic)
- [ ] All product shots have similar scale (zoom level)
- [ ] All lifestyle images use similar models/people
- [ ] Font/branding (if visible) is consistent across products
- [ ] Color accuracy consistent (not oversaturated on some, dull on others)
- [ ] Photography style consistent (professional vs. amateur visible?)

### Competitor Image Analysis

**How to Analyze Competitor Images:**

1. Search product on Google Shopping
2. Identify top 5 ranking products (highest position = best performing)
3. Screenshot each competitor's lead image
4. Note: Lifestyle vs. studio, colors shown, angles used
5. Analyze why that image might rank well:
   - Is it cleaner/sharper than alternatives?
   - Does it show product in real use?
   - Is person/scale visible?
   - What makes it visually appealing?

**What to Replicate (Ethically):**
- Image quality level (professional standard)
- Photography style (lifestyle vs. studio preference)
- Angle and perspective (what view works best?)
- Color variation shown (important for matching)
- NOT: Don't copy competitor's exact photo or branding

**Differentiation Strategy:**
- Match quality standard, add unique angle
- Use different lifestyle context (different room, different person, different use case)
- Include unique detail shots (show YOUR product's unique craftsmanship)
- Add unboxing/packaging if competitors don't (competitive advantage)

## Product Descriptions: The 5% Factor

### Description Best Practices

**Length:** 50-200 words (optimal)
- Too short (<50): Insufficient detail, looks unprofessional
- Too long (>300): Users don't read, wastes space
- Sweet spot: 100-150 words (enough detail, scannable)

**Structure (Recommended):**

```
Paragraph 1 (1-2 sentences): What is it? What problem does it solve?
Paragraph 2 (2-3 sentences): Materials, dimensions, specifications
Paragraph 3 (2-3 sentences): Benefits or unique features
Paragraph 4 (1 sentence, optional): Care instructions or warranty info
```

**Example Description:**

```
Colorblind Corrective Glasses for Color Blindness

These premium colorblind glasses use advanced lens technology to help
people with red-green color blindness see colors more vividly and accurately.
Perfect for work, hobbies, or everyday wear.

Made from durable acetate frames with mineral glass lenses, these glasses
are unisex and fit most face shapes. Lightweight and comfortable for all-day
wear. Available in black and tortoiseshell.

Users report improved color discrimination in everyday tasks like reading
charts, selecting produce, or enjoying nature. Can be worn over regular
glasses with optional strap. Backed by 30-day satisfaction guarantee.

Care: Clean with provided cloth, store in protective case. Avoid extreme heat.
```

**Word count: 95 words** (concise, informative, benefit-focused)

### Content Rules

- **DO write original descriptions** — Google detects copied/spun text from AliExpress, eBay, competitors
- **DO include actual benefits** — How does this product improve customer's life?
- **DO use natural paragraphs** — Don't write walls of text; break into 2-3 short paragraphs
- **DO include care/maintenance** — If relevant (washing, storage, durability tips)
- **DO mention warranty/guarantee** — If applicable (builds trust)

- **DO NOT copy from competitors** — Plagiarism risk + Google catches it
- **DO NOT exaggerate claims** — "Best in world" without evidence damages trust
- **DO NOT include price** — Keep in price field; don't write "$XX value"
- **DO NOT write fluff** — Every sentence must add information
- **DO NOT use all CAPS** — Looks unprofessional
- **DO NOT include HTML tags** — Use plain text (Google handles formatting)

### Writing Process for Original Descriptions

**Step 1: Product Knowledge**
- Spend time with actual product (hold it, use it, test it)
- Take notes: materials, dimensions, weight, construction quality, unique features

**Step 2: Customer Perspective**
- Who buys this product? (age, profession, lifestyle)
- What problem does it solve? (what pain point?)
- How does it make their life better?

**Step 3: First Draft**
- Write like you're explaining to a friend
- Don't worry about perfection
- Include: What it is, materials, benefits, care

**Step 4: Edit**
- Remove redundancy
- Tighten sentences (shorter = more readable)
- Read aloud for natural flow
- Check spelling/grammar

**Step 5: Competitor Comparison**
- Look at competitor descriptions (inspiration, not copying)
- Are they missing a benefit you can highlight?
- Is there a unique angle competitors don't mention?
- Add your unique insight

## Pricing Strategy in Product Feed

### Price Point Validation

**Before Adding Product to Feed:**

1. Search product on Google Shopping
2. Identify top 5 competitors
3. Note their selling prices
4. Your price should fit within market range

| Price Situation | Action |
|-----------------|--------|
| Competitors selling $20-$50, you price $15 | RED FLAG: You're too aggressive, quality appears cheap |
| Competitors selling $20-$50, you price $35 | GOOD: Competitive, price signals quality |
| Competitors selling $20-$50, you price $100+ | RED FLAG: Overpriced, will get no sales |
| All competitors selling suspiciously low ($2-5) | RED FLAG: Scam market, avoid product |
| ONE competitor at $500, others at $20 | Use the $20 anchors (outlier likely error) |

**Pricing Competitiveness:**
- Your price within 10-20% of average competitor = competitive
- Your price 30%+ lower than competitors = quality question
- Your price 30%+ higher than competitors = luxury positioning (needs justification)

### Dynamic Pricing in Feed

**Static Pricing:**
- Set price once, rarely change
- Example: Furniture, collectibles, unique items
- Update in feed monthly or quarterly

**Dynamic Pricing:**
- Price changes based on competitor pricing
- Example: Electronics, commodities, high-competition products
- Update daily or weekly

**Tool Options for Dynamic Pricing:**
- Manual CSV upload (weekly updates)
- Automated feed URL (daily automatic updates)
- eCommerce platform sync (real-time if connected)
- Price monitoring software (auto-adjusts to compete)

### Sale Pricing & Discounts

**Communicating Discounts in Feed:**

| Field | Purpose | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| `price` | Current selling price | $35.99 |
| `compare_at_price` | Original price before discount | $59.99 |
| Discount signal | Tells customer they save money | Shows as "$35.99 (40% off $59.99)" |

**Discount Best Practices:**
- Original price (compare_at) must be recent (not 6 months old)
- Discount must be real (not inflated original price)
- Use sparingly (every product on sale = no credibility)
- Test discount vs. no discount (which converts better?)

**Discount Approval Risk:**
- Discounts attract bargain hunters (lower AOV)
- Discounts signal urgency (drives urgency-motivated buyers, not loyal)
- Overuse of "compare_at" flags Google as misleading

**Recommendation:** Use compare_at for genuine sales (seasonal clearance, bulk discounts) but NOT permanently on every product.

## Advanced Feed Optimization

### Product Attributes That Improve Performance

**Critical Attributes:**
- `material` — Fabric type, wood type, metal composition
- `color` — Color options available (if variants)
- `size` — Size range (XS, S, M, L, XL)
- `brand` — Your brand or product brand
- `gtin` (barcode) — UPC/EAN if available (increases trust)
- `mpn` (part number) — Manufacturer part number (authenticity signal)

**Optional Attributes (Improve Visibility):**
- `gender` — Unisex, men's, women's (if applicable)
- `age_group` — Adult, teen, children (if applicable)
- `pattern` — Striped, floral, plaid (visual search relevant)
- `sport_type` — Basketball, running, yoga (for athletic products)

### Seasonal Product Strategy

**Seasonal Products (Highly Variable Demand):**

**Identification:**
- Product sells 10x more in certain months
- Example: Winter jackets (peak: Oct-Dec), beach items (peak: May-Aug)

**Strategy:**
1. Validate demand across ALL 12 months (must be viable year-round)
2. Price higher in peak season (higher demand = higher price tolerance)
3. Price lower in off-season (clear inventory, maintain volume)
4. Consider temporary "out of stock" in dead season (vs. fire-sale pricing)
5. Monitor quarterly (adjust pricing per season)

**Example Pricing:**
```
Winter Jacket:
- Peak (Oct-Dec): $79.99
- Shoulder (Jan, Apr-Sep): $59.99
- Trough (Feb-Mar): $44.99 or out-of-stock
```

**Rule:** If product can't generate sales in off-season, it's too seasonal. Exclude it.

## Feed Upload & Update Process

### Feed Format Options

**CSV (Recommended for Most):**
- Simple spreadsheet format
- Easy to create and edit
- Supports all product fields
- Upload frequency: Weekly recommended
- Tool: Google Sheets → Export as CSV

**XML/Atom Feed (Recommended for Scale):**
- Machine-readable format
- Can be generated automatically from inventory system
- Supports real-time updates
- Hosted on your server or feed management service
- Google fetches every 24 hours

**Google Sheets (Easiest):**
- Link directly to Merchant Center
- Auto-syncs as you edit
- Limited to 5,000 products
- Good for testing before CSV/XML

### Feed Validation Checklist

Before uploading, verify:

- [ ] All required fields present (id, title, availability, price, image_link, link, brand, condition, product_type)
- [ ] No duplicate product IDs (each ID unique)
- [ ] All image URLs functional (check 5-10 random images)
- [ ] All product links point to real product pages (not homepage)
- [ ] Prices have currency (USD, EUR, etc.)
- [ ] Availability status accurate (in stock vs. out of stock)
- [ ] Product type matches collection structure
- [ ] Description length 50-200 words (not too short, not too long)
- [ ] No missing titles
- [ ] No suspicious pricing (50% off EVERYTHING)
- [ ] Date format consistent (YYYY-MM-DD if dates used)

### Monitoring Feed Health

**Weekly Review:**
- Check Merchant Center > Diagnostics > Data Quality
- Review any new disapprovals (fix immediately)
- Monitor feed processing time (should be <24 hours)

**Monthly Review:**
- CTR by product (products with low CTR need image/title improvement)
- Conversion rate by product (products not converting need pricing or description review)
- Top-performing products (double down on similar products)
- Underperforming products (improve or remove)
