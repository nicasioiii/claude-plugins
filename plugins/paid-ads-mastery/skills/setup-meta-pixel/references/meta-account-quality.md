# Meta Account Quality: Health, Prevention, & Recovery

## Account Quality Score Fundamentals

### What Is Account Quality?

**Definition:** Meta's internal rating of your account's trustworthiness and reliability

**Scale:** 0-100 (Meta doesn't show exact number, but indicates as Good/Average/Below Average)

**Impact:** Acts as FIRST filter on targeting before pixel or audience type applies

### The Targeting Layer Model

**How Meta Processes Targeting (In Order):**

```
1. ACCOUNT QUALITY FILTER
   ↓
   Only "good" segment of audience available to new accounts
   (If targeting 5M people, poor account = 1M "bad" subset)

2. PIXEL/DATA TRAINING
   ↓
   Targets ideal customers within available segment

3. AUDIENCE TYPE
   ↓
   Interest, lookalike, or broad targeting applied
```

**Implication:** Even with trained pixel + perfect audience, poor account quality = poor traffic quality

## Account Quality Factors & Scoring

### Quality Assessment Checklist

| Factor | Positive Signal | Negative Signal | Weight |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Payment History** | Consistent, on-time payments | Failed charges, disputes | HIGH |
| **Account Age** | 2+ years established | <6 months old, brand new | HIGH |
| **Historical Behavior** | Clean history, no warnings | Prior violations, warnings | HIGH |
| **Spending Pattern** | Regular, predictable spend | Erratic, stop-start | MEDIUM |
| **Policy Compliance** | No violations, approved ads | Rejected ads, warnings | HIGH |
| **Complaint Rate** | Low/none, quick resolution | High complaints, unresolved | MEDIUM |
| **Industry Type** | Mainstream business | High-risk industry | MEDIUM |
| **Geographic Location** | Established markets | Emerging/high-fraud countries | LOW |

### Quality Scoring Interpretation

| Rating | What It Means | Typical ROAS Impact | Recovery Time |
|--------|---------------|-------------------|----------------|
| **Excellent** | 2+ years clean history, consistent payment | +15-20% ROAS boost | N/A |
| **Good** | No violations, 1+ year history | +0-5% ROAS boost | N/A |
| **Average** | New account, some caution flags | -5-10% ROAS penalty | 3-6 months |
| **Below Average** | Multiple warnings or violations | -20-30% ROAS penalty | 6-12 months |
| **Poor/Restricted** | Repeated violations, high-risk behavior | -50%+ ROAS penalty or disabled | 12+ months |

## Building Account Quality (New Accounts)

### Foundation Phase (Months 1-3)

**Goals:**
- Establish payment reliability
- Demonstrate legitimate business
- Build clean spending history

**Action Items:**

1. **Set Up Autopay**
   - Prevents payment failures (critical)
   - Ensures no billing gaps
   - Meta prioritizes accounts with autopay

2. **Start with Small Budget**
   - Begin with $20-50/day (not $500/day)
   - Demonstrate control and intent
   - Build spending history gradually

3. **Run "Safe" Campaigns**
   - Use only industry-standard creatives
   - Avoid edge cases (health claims, get-rich-quick, etc.)
   - Stick to approved ad categories
   - Clear product/service descriptions

4. **Complete Business Profile**
   - Full legal business name
   - Real business address
   - Real phone number
   - Legitimate website with privacy/terms pages
   - Complete About/Contact information

5. **Avoid Violations**
   - Read Meta's advertising policies thoroughly
   - Avoid prohibited items (weapons, drugs, counterfeit)
   - No misleading claims
   - No restricted content (gambling, dating, finance schemes)
   - No personal data collection in ads

### Growth Phase (Months 3-12)

**Goals:**
- Increase budget steadily
- Maintain clean history
- Build pattern of success

**Action Items:**

1. **Gradual Budget Increases**
   - Increase 10-20% per month if performing well
   - Example: Month 3: $100/day → Month 6: $200/day → Month 12: $500/day
   - Demonstrates stability, not erratic growth

2. **Diversify Campaigns**
   - Run multiple campaigns (not just 1)
   - Test different audiences (shows sophistication)
   - Iterate on winning strategies
   - Document results (shows legitimacy)

3. **Maintain Payment Reliability**
   - Never miss a payment (critical)
   - Proactive: Set up payment buffer (extra credit on account)
   - Update payment method before expiration
   - Resolve billing issues immediately

4. **Monitor Account Notifications**
   - Check Message Center regularly (daily if possible)
   - Respond to Meta requests within 24 hours
   - Don't ignore warnings or policy notices
   - Address issues proactively

5. **Track Performance Metrics**
   - Document ROAS, CPA, conversion rate
   - Build case study of success
   - Shows sophisticated advertiser (improves quality rating)

### Maturity Phase (Months 12+)

**Goals:**
- Achieve "Excellent" account quality rating
- Unlock all targeting options
- Enjoy quality traffic and results

**Action Items:**

1. **Consistent Spending Pattern**
   - Maintain regular monthly budget (even if amount varies)
   - Never "pause indefinitely" (stop spending)
   - If scaling: Do so gradually and predictably

2. **Proactive Communication**
   - Respond to Meta support within 24 hours
   - Provide documentation if requested
   - Be cooperative and professional

3. **Expand Responsibly**
   - As budget grows, maintain same rigor
   - Add team members (assign roles, not share login)
   - Document changes to account
   - Keep contact information current

4. **Business Documentation**
   - Maintain accurate business records
   - Prepare documentation if Meta requests verification
   - Bank statements, business license, tax records
   - Website history (WayBack Machine confirms longevity)

## Early Warning Signs (Before Account Gets Flagged)

### Red Flags You Can Fix

| Flag | Cause | Fix | Urgency |
|------|-------|-----|----------|
| Ad disapproved | Policy violation in creative | Remove ad, rewrite compliant version | HIGH |
| Payment failed | Credit card declined | Update payment method immediately | CRITICAL |
| Spending pattern unusual | Budget fluctuations | Stabilize at consistent daily amount | MEDIUM |
| Low click-through rate | Ad quality issue | Improve creative, test new audiences | LOW |
| High cost-per-click | Competition or targeting too narrow | Expand audience, improve targeting | MEDIUM |
| Account age warning | New account, Meta cautious | Just spend consistently, time will help | LOW |

### Preventing Account Restrictions

**Most Common Causes of Restrictions:**
1. Payment failure (let billing lapse)
2. Repeated ad disapprovals (keep violating policies)
3. Suspicious spending pattern (sudden spike or erratic stop/start)
4. Complaints/disputes (customer issues escalated to Meta)
5. Policy violations (misleading claims, prohibited products)

**Prevention Checklist:**
- [ ] Autopay enabled with valid payment method
- [ ] All ads pre-reviewed against Meta's policies
- [ ] Spending increases gradual, not sudden
- [ ] Responsive to customer service issues
- [ ] Business information accurate and current
- [ ] No misleading or prohibited content
- [ ] Account checked daily first week, weekly ongoing
- [ ] Message Center monitored for Meta alerts

## Account Recovery (If Already Flagged)

### Restriction Levels & Recovery

| Level | Symptoms | Recovery Time | Recovery Steps |
|-------|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Warning** | Yellow alert in Message Center | 1-2 weeks | Fix issue, wait for review |
| **Limited** | Some campaign types disabled | 2-4 weeks | Request review, show compliance |
| **Restricted** | Can't run new campaigns | 4-8 weeks | Formal appeal with documentation |
| **Disabled** | Account shut down completely | 8+ weeks | Appeal to support, may be permanent |

### Recovery Steps by Situation

**Scenario 1: Single Ad Disapproval**
1. Read disapproval message carefully
2. Identify policy violation
3. Create compliant version
4. Resubmit for review
5. Wait 24-48 hours for approval
6. Continue running other ads (don't pause)

**Scenario 2: Payment Failure**
1. Update payment method immediately
2. Pay any outstanding balance
3. Set up autopay (prevent recurrence)
4. Wait 1-3 days for account re-enablement
5. Test with small campaign to confirm

**Scenario 3: Suspicious Activity Warning**
1. Read warning message (understand complaint)
2. Stop spending for 3-7 days (cooling-off period)
3. Review account for issues
4. Resume spending at 50% previous level
5. Increase gradually over 4-6 weeks
6. Document spending pattern shows legitimacy

**Scenario 4: Low-Quality Account Status**
- Most painful: slow recovery
- Timeline: 6-12 months to rebuild
- Strategy: Spend consistently (no gaps), follow all policies, build history

**Recovery Process:**
- Month 1: Consistent small spend ($20-50/day)
- Months 2-3: Maintain consistency, increase 10% monthly
- Months 3-6: If no new violations, quality perception improves
- Months 6-12: Gradually increase to previous budget
- Month 12+: Account quality restored (if clean)

**Accelerators (if account is restricted/disabled):**
1. **Submit Appeal** (if option available)
   - Explain situation
   - Show corrective actions taken
   - Request review
   - Provide documentation

2. **Contact Meta Support**
   - Via Business Manager Message Center
   - Respond within 24 hours to requests
   - Provide evidence of compliance
   - Be professional and honest

3. **Escalate if Needed**
   - Account restricted but no explanation?
   - Submit formal appeal through Business Manager
   - Provide supporting documentation (business docs, bank statements)
   - Request specific feedback on what violates policy

### Appeals & Formal Requests

**What Meta Wants to See:**
1. Clear explanation of what happened
2. Evidence you've fixed the problem
3. Demonstration you understand policy
4. Commitment to compliance going forward
5. Supporting documentation (if requested)

**Example Appeal:**

```
Subject: Appeal - Account Restriction Review

Dear Meta Support,

My account [ACCOUNT_ID] was restricted on [DATE] for [REASON].

I have reviewed Meta's advertising policies and identified the issue:
[SPECIFIC POLICY VIOLATION]

I have taken the following corrective actions:
1. Removed the disapproved creative
2. Updated all ads to comply with policy
3. Implemented review process for future ads

I understand the policy requires [SPECIFIC REQUIREMENT] and I am committed to
full compliance going forward. My business is legitimate
[BUSINESS TYPE] and I have been spending consistently since [DATE].

I respectfully request that my account be reviewed for re-enablement.

Documentation attached:
- Business License
- Tax ID / Business Registration
- Website showing legitimate business
- Previous compliance history

Thank you for your consideration.

Best regards,
[YOUR NAME]
[CONTACT PHONE]
```

## Maintaining Quality Long-Term

### Monthly Health Check

**First of Each Month:**
- [ ] Check Message Center (any alerts or warnings?)
- [ ] Review all active campaigns (any disapprovals?)
- [ ] Verify payment method still valid
- [ ] Check spending trend (trending up, stable, or down?)
- [ ] Reconcile billing statement (charges match expected)
- [ ] Update business information if changed

### Quarterly Deep Dive

**Every 3 Months:**
1. Review account history (any warnings, restrictions?)
2. Analyze spending pattern (stable and legitimate-looking?)
3. Check ROAS trend (improving or declining?)
4. Review creatives (all compliant with policies?)
5. Document business growth (shows legitimacy over time)

### Annual Account Audit

**Once Per Year:**
1. Complete account health review
2. Update all business information
3. Review and update audience definitions
4. Analyze which campaigns work best
5. Plan budget for next year (predictable growth)
6. Train new team members on compliance
7. Archive documentation (for potential audits)

### High-Risk Account Monitoring

**If Account Shows Quality Issues:**
- Check daily for first 2 weeks
- Respond to ANY Meta messages within 4 hours
- Don't change campaigns (stability signals)
- Document all actions taken
- Consider hiring Meta representative (if qualified for support)

## Industry-Specific Risks

### High-Risk Industries (More Scrutiny)

| Industry | Main Risk | Mitigation |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| Health/Medical | Misleading health claims | Avoid health claims, FDA compliance |
| Finance | Scams, unrealistic returns | Full transparency, realistic claims |
| Gambling | Addictive behavior, minors | Age gate, responsible gambling message |
| Dating | Scams, catfishing | Verified profiles, safety messaging |
| Supplements | Unproven benefits, legality | Transparent ingredients, no health claims |
| MLM/Network Marketing | Pyramid schemes | Clear income disclosures, FTC compliance |
| Cryptocurrency | Scams, fraud | Legitimate exchange, security emphasis |
| Dropshipping | Scams, quality issues | Transparent dropshipping, quality assurance |

### Best Practice by Industry

**Legitimate eCommerce/Dropshipping:**
- Complete, professional website
- Clear product descriptions (accurate)
- Real customer reviews
- Transparent shipping/return policy
- Professional brand presentation
- Consistent, moderate spending
- → Quality rating: Good to Excellent

**Service-Based Business:**
- Case studies showing results
- Transparent pricing
- Clear service limitations
- Professional testimonials
- Documented business history
- → Quality rating: Good to Excellent

**High-Risk (Health/Finance):**
- Extreme scrutiny from Meta
- Requires more documentation
- Slower account quality improvement
- More likely to get restrictions
- → Quality rating: Often stays Average or Below Average initially

## Account Quality & Pixel Training Connection

### How Account Quality Affects Pixel Training

**Account quality → Pixel quality → Optimization quality**

**Relationship:**

```
Poor Account Quality
↓
Access only to "bad" audience segment
↓
Poor-quality conversions from pixel
↓
Pixel trains on low-value customers
↓
Optimization poor, high CPA, low ROAS
↓
Results terrible despite pixel effort
```

vs.

```
Excellent Account Quality
↓
Access to "good" audience segment
↓
High-quality conversions from pixel
↓
Pixel trains on high-value customers
↓
Optimization excellent, low CPA, high ROAS
↓
Results excellent
```

**Implication:** Even if pixel is perfectly trained, poor account quality caps your results.

### Combined Strategy

1. **Build Account Quality First (Months 1-3)**
   - Establish payment reliability
   - Clean spending history
   - Basic compliance

2. **Implement Pixel (Month 1)**
   - Start training immediately
   - Run conservative campaigns
   - Pixel and account both maturing

3. **Scale as Both Mature (Months 6+)**
   - Account is now "established"
   - Pixel has training data
   - Can use advanced targeting
   - Results dramatically improve
