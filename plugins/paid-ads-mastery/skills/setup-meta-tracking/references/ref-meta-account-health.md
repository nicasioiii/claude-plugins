# Meta Account Health & Training Reference

## Ad Account Training Journey

### How Ad Accounts Learn
- Ad accounts develop their own "intelligence" over time through consistent spending
- A trained account separates good traffic (buyers) from bad traffic (browsers) at the account level
- This happens ABOVE the pixel level -- even before pixel targeting kicks in

### New Account Phase (First 30 Days)
- Results will be volatile -- expect wild swings in performance
- Do NOT judge campaign viability in the first 2 weeks
- Start with purchase optimization from day one (never traffic or engagement objectives)
- Keep spend consistent -- avoid $50 one day, $0 the next, $200 the following day
- Budget consistency trains the account faster than sporadic high spending

### Growing Account Phase (30-90 Days)
- Performance stabilizes as account accumulates purchase data
- CPMs should begin normalizing across campaigns
- Start testing broader targeting as pixel data builds
- This is when lookalike audiences become viable (~100 purchase events)

### Seasoned Account Phase (90+ Days)
- Campaigns hold for weeks or months without dying
- Broad targeting becomes consistently profitable
- ASC (Advantage Shopping) campaigns start outperforming interest-based campaigns
- Account can handle larger budget increases without destabilizing

## Payment Hygiene Protocol

### Rules (Non-Negotiable)
1. **Use a credit card with high limit** -- never a debit card with balance limits
2. **Never let a payment fail** -- one declined payment damages account health permanently
3. **Maintain a backup payment method** -- add a second card to the ad account
4. **Pay off balances promptly** -- don't let spending thresholds trigger holds
5. **Use a business credit card** -- personal cards trigger more fraud reviews

### Why Payment Matters for Performance
- Failed payments trigger Meta's risk assessment systems
- Multiple failures = account flagged as unreliable = restricted delivery
- Even after resolving payment issues, the account "remembers" the failure
- Some advertisers report 2-4 weeks of suppressed delivery after a single declined payment

### Spending Threshold Management
- Meta increases your spending threshold as account matures
- New accounts: ~$25-$50 threshold (charged frequently)
- Mature accounts: $750-$2,500+ threshold
- Rapid threshold increases happen with consistent, on-time payments
- Do NOT try to artificially accelerate thresholds

## Shadow Ban Detection & Recovery

### What Is a Shadow Ban
- Account flagged by Meta's automated systems but not officially restricted
- No notification in Ads Manager -- account appears "active"
- Delivery drops to near zero or CPMs spike 2-3x above normal

### Detection Checklist
- [ ] CPMs suddenly 2-3x higher than account average with no market explanation
- [ ] Campaigns show "Active" but spending $0 or near $0
- [ ] New campaigns launched die within hours
- [ ] Account Quality tab shows no violations (but delivery is still suppressed)
- [ ] Other advertisers in same niche report normal performance

### Recovery Steps
1. **Wait 48-72 hours** -- some flags auto-resolve
2. **Contact Meta support** via Business Help Center
3. **File appeals** through Account Quality section
4. **Reduce ad volume** -- pause all campaigns, restart with 1-2 campaigns only
5. **Check creative compliance** -- review all active ads for borderline content
6. **If unresolved after 2 weeks:** Open new ad account under same Business Manager
7. **Last resort:** New Business Manager (only if BM itself is flagged)

### Prevention
- Never run ads to restricted categories without proper verification
- Avoid frequent on/off campaign toggling (appears bot-like)
- Don't run too many ads to zero conversions simultaneously
- Keep ad rejection rate below 10% of total submissions
- Avoid dramatic spending changes ($50/day to $5,000/day overnight)

## Business Manager Structure

### Recommended Setup
- **One Business Manager** per actual business entity
- **2-3 ad accounts** per Business Manager (for redundancy and testing)
- **One primary ad account** for main spending
- **One backup ad account** initialized and running at low spend
- This way, if primary gets restricted, backup is already warm

### Account Warming Protocol for Backup
- Create the backup ad account immediately
- Run $5-10/day on a simple campaign (even engagement or traffic is fine for warming)
- Maintain consistent low spend for 30+ days
- This keeps the account alive and past initial risk assessment
- When needed as primary, you can scale up from a warm state

## Working with Facebook Representatives

### What Reps Want vs. What You Need
- **Reps want:** You to spend more money on awareness/engagement campaigns
- **You need:** Sales and leads at profitable cost
- Reps are measured on account spend growth, not your profitability
- They will suggest broad awareness campaigns, conversion API upgrades (good), and spending increases

### What to Accept from Reps
- CAPI setup assistance (genuinely helpful)
- Account verification help
- Policy violation explanations and appeal guidance
- Access to beta features (sometimes valuable)
- Credit offers for testing new features

### What to Decline from Reps
- Suggestions to run traffic or engagement campaigns
- Recommendations to consolidate into one campaign (test first)
- Advice to increase budgets without performance justification
- Suggestions to use automatic placements if you have data showing otherwise

### How to Get a Rep
- Accounts spending $1,000+/month may qualify
- Apply through Meta Business Help Center
- Having a Meta Business Partner manage your account can get you rep access
- Higher spending = higher quality rep assigned

## Account Restrictions -- Common Causes

| Cause | Risk Level | Fix |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Payment failure | High | Update payment, contact support |
| Ad policy violation (content) | Medium | Edit creative, appeal |
| Too many rejected ads | High | Slow down, review policies, appeal |
| Unusual spending pattern | Medium | Wait 48h, contact support |
| New account + high spend | High | Ramp gradually over 2-4 weeks |
| Restricted product category | High | Get category verification or stop |
| Multiple accounts for same business | Critical | Consolidate to one BM |

## Page Quality Score

### What It Measures
- Customer feedback on your business (from ad interactions)
- Shipping speed and accuracy
- Product quality vs. advertisement claims
- Customer service responsiveness

### Impact on Advertising
- Low page quality score = restricted ad delivery, higher CPMs
- Score below 1 = potential account disable
- Check at: facebook.com/business/quality

### How to Maintain High Score
- Ship within advertised timeframes
- Product quality must match ad creative (no bait and switch)
- Respond to customer messages within 24 hours
- Address negative feedback proactively
- Monitor score weekly -- drops take weeks to recover
