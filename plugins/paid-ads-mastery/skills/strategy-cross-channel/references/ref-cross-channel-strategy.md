# Cross-Channel Strategy Reference

## Budget Allocation Framework

### Starting Budget Allocation by Business Type

**E-commerce with Existing Search Demand:**
| Channel | % of Budget | Role |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Google Shopping/PMax | 35-45% | Primary revenue driver (demand capture) |
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | 25-35% | Demand generation + retargeting |
| Google Search (Brand + Non-brand) | 10-15% | Intent capture |
| YouTube | 5-10% | Brand building + remarketing |
| TikTok | 5-10% | TOF awareness + younger demo |

**New/Novel Product (No Search Demand):**
| Channel | % of Budget | Role |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Meta (Facebook/Instagram) | 40-50% | Primary demand creation |
| TikTok | 15-25% | Awareness + virality potential |
| YouTube | 10-15% | Education + brand building |
| Google Search (Brand only) | 5-10% | Capture demand you create |
| Google Display/Demand Gen | 5-10% | Retargeting + awareness |

**Lead Generation / B2B:**
| Channel | % of Budget | Role |
|---------|-----------|------|
| Google Search | 40-50% | High-intent lead capture |
| Meta | 20-30% | Lead generation + retargeting |
| YouTube | 10-15% | Education + authority building |
| LinkedIn | 5-10% | Professional targeting |

### Rebalancing Rules
- Shift budget TO the channel showing best efficiency (lowest CPA at scale)
- Never allocate 100% to one channel (single point of failure)
- Ramp push channels BEFORE seasonal peaks, ramp pull channels DURING peaks
- Review allocation monthly, major rebalance quarterly

## Platform-Specific Creative Adaptation

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram)
**Format:** Mix of UGC, lifestyle, polished creative
**Tone:** Conversational, relatable, benefit-driven
**Length:** 15-60 seconds (shorter for stories/reels, longer for feed)
**Hook:** First 3 seconds must stop scroll. Problem statement, surprising fact, or relatable scenario.
**CTA:** "Shop Now" for e-commerce, "Learn More" for consideration, "Sign Up" for lead gen
**Formats needed:** 9:16 (stories/reels), 1:1 (feed), 4:5 (feed, highest real estate)

### TikTok
**Format:** Native, raw, entertainment-first
**Tone:** Fun, authentic, trend-aware. Must NOT look like an ad.
**Length:** 20-35 seconds sweet spot
**Hook:** Entertainment or shock value. Sound-on environment.
**CTA:** Softer CTAs work better. "Link in bio" or comment-driven.
**Formats needed:** 9:16 only. Safe zones: avoid top 20% and bottom 20%.

### Google Search
**Format:** Text ads (RSA)
**Tone:** Direct, benefit-driven, keyword-relevant
**Key:** Headlines must match search intent exactly. Include keywords in headlines.
**CTA:** Action-oriented ("Get Free Quote," "Shop Now," "Book Today")

### YouTube
**Format:** In-stream (skippable), Bumper (6 sec), Shorts
**Tone:** Storytelling, educational, cinematic
**Length:** 90-180 seconds for in-stream (sweet spot). 6 seconds for bumpers.
**Hook:** First 5 seconds determine whether viewer skips. Must hook immediately.
**CTA:** Overlay CTAs, companion banners, end cards

### Google Display / Demand Gen
**Format:** Responsive display ads (images + headlines + descriptions)
**Tone:** Clean, eye-catching, brand-consistent
**Key:** Multiple image sizes needed. Strong headline + clear value proposition.
**CTA:** Simple, clear ("Shop Now," "Learn More")

## The Four Pillars of Google Ads (Ezra/Brett Framework)

1. **Google Search** -- Reaches shoppers at learn/do/buy moments
2. **Google Shopping / PMax** -- Workhorse for e-commerce (up to 60%+ of Google spend)
3. **Remarketing** (YouTube, Display, Discovery) -- Re-engage the 95-99% who visit but do not buy
4. **Top of Funnel** (YouTube, Display) -- Not 100% necessary but needed for stable/rapid growth

**Rule:** You need at least 3 of 4 pillars for stable, predictable growth. Ideally all 4.

## Demand Capture vs. Demand Generation

### Demand Capture (Pull)
- Active shoppers already looking for solutions
- Focus: Search + Shopping
- Best for: auto parts, appliances, known product categories
- Metric: immediate ROAS

### Demand Generation (Push)
- People unaware product exists but would love it
- Focus: YouTube + Display + Meta + TikTok
- Best for: novel products, impulse items, problem-unaware audiences
- Metric: brand awareness, new customer rate, long-term LTV

Almost ALL businesses need BOTH. Scale requires demand generation. Profitability requires demand capture.

## Three Ways to Grow a Business (Google Impacts All Three)

1. **Get more new customers** (acquisition)
2. **Get customers to buy more** (increase AOV through upsells, bundles)
3. **Get customers to buy more often** (increase frequency through remarketing, email)

Google Ads touches all three through Search (new customers), Shopping (AOV through product visibility), and Remarketing (repeat purchases).

## Cross-Channel Attribution Problem

If you add all conversion value from all channels, the total will exceed actual revenue. Each platform takes full credit for the same purchase.

**Solutions:**
- Third-party attribution tools (Northbeam, Triple Whale) as single source of truth
- MER (Media Efficiency Ratio) = Total Revenue / Total Ad Spend across all channels
- Incrementality testing: turn off one channel, measure total business impact
- Accept that perfect attribution is impossible -- directionally correct is sufficient

## Platform Personality Guide

### Meta (Facebook/Instagram) -- The Demand Creator
- **Strength:** Finding people who did not know they wanted your product
- **Weakness:** Attribution is messy post-iOS; reported ROAS often under-counts
- **Personality:** Emotional, visual, scroll-stopping. People are browsing, not searching.
- **Best for:** Novel products, impulse purchases, visually appealing items, brand building
- **Worst for:** High-consideration B2B, products requiring extensive research before purchase

### Google Search -- The Intent Harvester
- **Strength:** Catches people at the exact moment they are looking for a solution
- **Weakness:** Limited by search volume; cannot create demand that does not exist
- **Personality:** Rational, keyword-driven, intent-matching. People are actively looking.
- **Best for:** Known product categories, services, high-consideration purchases
- **Worst for:** Novel products nobody knows to search for

### Google Shopping / PMax -- The Product Showcaser
- **Strength:** Shows your product with image, price, and reviews directly in search results
- **Weakness:** Requires Merchant Center approval, product feed maintenance, Smart Bidding maturity
- **Personality:** Transactional, comparison-driven. Buyers are evaluating options side by side.
- **Best for:** E-commerce with competitive pricing and strong product images
- **Worst for:** Services, digital products, categories Google restricts

### YouTube -- The Storyteller
- **Strength:** Long-form engagement, builds trust and authority, massive reach
- **Weakness:** Longer attribution windows, harder to measure direct ROAS
- **Personality:** Educational, entertaining, cinematic. Viewers are engaged in content.
- **Best for:** Brand building, remarketing, complex products needing explanation
- **Worst for:** Low-AOV impulse products that cannot justify video production cost

### TikTok -- The Trend Rider
- **Strength:** Viral potential, younger demographics, low CPMs, trend amplification
- **Weakness:** Volatile performance, short creative lifespan, weaker purchase intent
- **Personality:** Raw, entertaining, trend-driven. Content must not look like advertising.
- **Best for:** Products that photograph/video well, brands targeting 18-35, trend-responsive categories
- **Worst for:** B2B, complex products, audiences 55+

## Budget Allocation Decision Matrix

### When to Shift Budget TO a Channel
- Channel shows lowest CPA at scale (not just low spend)
- Channel shows positive incrementality when tested
- Channel has room to scale (not impression-share capped)
- MER improves when channel spend increases

### When to Shift Budget AWAY From a Channel
- CPA rising despite creative refresh and optimization
- Diminishing returns evident (each $1,000 increment produces less)
- MER declines when channel spend increases
- Channel is impression-share capped with no room to grow

### Monthly Review Protocol
1. Compare MER this month vs. last month
2. Check each channel's contribution to new customer acquisition
3. Identify which channel has the best marginal CPA (the next $1,000 of spend)
4. Reallocate 10-15% of budget maximum per month (avoid drastic shifts)
5. Document changes and measure impact over 30 days before adjusting again
