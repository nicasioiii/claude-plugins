# Google Performance Max (PMax) Strategy

## Overview
- **What it is:** One campaign serving across all Google channels (Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Maps)
- **When to use:** After Shopping/Search campaigns profitable (50+ conversions)
- **Data requirement:** MINIMUM 50 conversions recent history to optimize properly
- **Budget minimum:** $100/day (technically works), $150+/day recommended
- **Expected timeline:** 3-4 weeks to learning phase exit; 6-8 weeks to stable performance
- **ROAS expectation:** 2.5-3.5 (usually lower than Shopping initially, improves with scale)

---

## WHEN TO ADD PMAX (NOT START WITH IT)

**Don't start with PMax if:**
- ✗ New account with zero conversion history (need data to optimize)
- ✗ First time running Google Ads (too complex for learning; start with Shopping)
- ✗ Have <$150/day budget (not enough to learn across 5+ channels)

**DO add PMax when:**
- ✓ Shopping campaign has 100+ conversions (proven product market fit)
- ✓ Search campaign running (if service business) with 50+ conversions
- ✓ Account is 2+ weeks old (aged accounts have better quality)
- ✓ Budget $150+/day available

---

## CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE: EFFICIENCY vs GROWTH

### Efficiency Campaign (Conservative Testing)
**Goal:** Maximize ROAS; don't worry about volume

**Setup:**
- Campaign name: "[PMax Efficiency] - [Date]"
- Daily budget: $100-150
- Target ROAS: Set to 3.5-4.0 (high bar)
- Asset group: 1 asset group (single set of headlines, images, etc.)
- Bidding: Conversion value (maximize ROAS)

**Expected:** 10-20 conversions/day; high ROAS; lower volume

**When to use:** Testing new asset angles; proving efficiency of messaging

### Growth Campaign (Aggressive Scaling)
**Goal:** Maximize conversions; don't worry about ROAS

**Setup:**
- Campaign name: "[PMax Growth] - [Date]"
- Daily budget: $200-500
- Target ROAS: Don't set (let Google maximize conversions)
- Asset group: 2-3 asset groups (different messaging angles)
- Bidding: Conversions (maximize volume)

**Expected:** 30-50 conversions/day; variable ROAS; higher volume

**When to use:** Scaling proven winners; don't care about per-unit cost

**Recommendation:** Run both simultaneously
- Efficiency: $150/day (find best messaging)
- Growth: $300/day (scale winners)
- Total: $450/day coordinated strategy

## ASSET GROUPS (The Core of PMax)

**What is an asset group?** A bundle of creative elements (headlines, descriptions, images, videos) that Google uses to create variations automatically across channels.

### Asset Group Checklist (Minimum Requirements)

- [ ] **Headlines:** 3-5 variations minimum (Google tests combinations)
  - Example: "Lose Weight Fast", "Drop 10 Pounds in 30 Days", "Weight Loss Guaranteed"

- [ ] **Descriptions:** 2-5 variations (supports headlines)
  - Example: "Doctor-recommended program with meal plans", "Join 100K+ success stories"

- [ ] **Images:** 4-6 minimum (feeds Display, Gmail, Maps)
  - Recommendation: Mix of product, lifestyle, customer testimonial images
  - Formats: 1200x628, 1500x1500, 600x600
  - Include: 1 lifestyle, 1 close-up product, 1 testimonial/before-after

- [ ] **Videos (Optional):** 1-2 videos
  - Duration: 15-30 seconds (YouTube ads play automatically)
  - Can be: Product demo, customer testimonial, lifestyle montage
  - Not required but improves performance

- [ ] **Logo:** 1 required (128x128 minimum)

- [ ] **Business Name:** Auto-filled from account

### Asset Group Quality Standards

**Headlines (5 total):**
```
1. "Premium [Product] for [Benefit]" (brand positioning)
2. "[Benefit] in [Timeframe]" (urgency/specificity)
3. "[#] of [Category] Owners [Verb]" (social proof)
4. "[Problem] Solved with [Product]" (problem-solution)
5. "[Guarantee] or [Refund]" (risk reversal)

Real example:
1. "Premium Weight Loss Programs Online"
2. "Lose 10 Pounds in 30 Days"
3. "Trusted by 100,000+ People"
4. "End Yo-Yo Dieting with Science"
5. "Results or Your Money Back"
```

**Images (6 total):**
```
1. Lifestyle (person using product; 30% boost to CTR)
2. Close-up detail (show quality; 20% boost)
3. Before-after transformation (emotional; 25% boost)
4. Logo + white background (clean; 10% boost)
5. Infographic (data-driven; 15% boost)
6. Customer testimonial (social proof; 20% boost)

Avoid: Stock images (detected + penalized), watermarked images, low quality
```

## PMAX MATRIX (Bidding Strategy Selection)

| Metric | Maximize Conversions | Target ROAS | Target CPA |
|---|---|---|---|
| **When to use** | Proven winners; scale for volume | Good ROAS history (50+ conversions) | Tight margins; need cost control |
| **Data requirement** | 50+ conversions | 100+ conversions | 100+ conversions |
| **Setup time** | 5 minutes | 10 minutes | 10 minutes |
| **Control** | None (Google decides bids) | Set profitability goal | Set cost ceiling |
| **Volume** | Highest | Medium-high | Medium |
| **ROAS stability** | Variable | More stable | Stable but lower |
| **Best for** | Scaling; don't care about CPA | Profitability-focused | Ultra-tight margins |

**Recommendation for beginners:** Use Maximize Conversions. Switch to Target ROAS only if you have clear ROAS data (150+ conversions with consistent 3.0+ ROAS).

## PMAX SCALING STRATEGY

### Phase 1: Testing (Week 1-2)
- Daily budget: $100-150
- Asset group: 1 (test messaging)
- Bidding: Maximize Conversions
- Goal: 20-30 conversions; identify strongest headlines/images
- Action: Monitor which assets get most impressions; mental note of winners

### Phase 2: Refinement (Week 3-4)
- Daily budget: $150-250
- Asset group: 2 (test variant messaging angle)
  - Asset Group 1: Original (proven headlines)
  - Asset Group 2: Alternative angle (different benefit focus)
- Bidding: Maximize Conversions
- Goal: 50-80 conversions total; see which asset group outperforms
- Action: Identify winning asset group; consider retiring loser

### Phase 3: Scaling (Week 5+)
- Daily budget: $300-800
- Asset group: 2-3 (keep winners; add new angle if growth stalled)
- Bidding: Switch to Target ROAS if 100+ conversions at 3.0+ ROAS
- Goal: 100+ conversions; prove sustained performance
- Action: Continue scaling 20% per week if ROAS maintains 2.5+

### Scaling Math
```
Week 5: $300/day, ROAS 2.8
Week 6: $360/day (20% increase), target ROAS 2.6+
Week 7: $430/day (20% increase), target ROAS 2.4+
Week 8: $515/day (20% increase), target ROAS 2.2+
Stop when: ROAS drops below 2.0 (saturation)
```

## AUDIENCE SIGNALS (Optional But Helpful)

**What are audience signals?** Hints to Google about your ideal customer (not targeting, just signals for optimization).

**How to add:**
1. Go to PMax campaign
2. Click "Audiences" section
3. Add optional signals:
   - Customer match (your email list, highest quality)
   - In-market audiences (people actively shopping category)
   - Similar audiences (look-alikes; use if you have customer list)

**Best signal:** Customer match (upload your email list; Google matches to Google accounts and shows ads preferentially to warm audience).

**Impact:** 5-15% ROAS improvement if you have customer data.

**When to skip:** First month (don't restrict before algorithm learns). Add after 100+ conversions.

## TROUBLESHOOTING PMAX

### Problem: Learning Phase Taking Too Long (Week 3+, still volatile)
**Diagnosis:** Not enough data yet OR bid too conservative
**Solution:**
1. Increase daily budget 25% (gives algorithm more to work with)
2. Add more images/videos to asset group (more creative combinations for algorithm to test)
3. Wait until Week 4 minimum before judging

### Problem: ROAS 1.8 (Below Breakeven)
**Diagnosis:** Either assets weak OR audience too broad OR account quality poor
**Solution:**
1. Check asset quality (are images professional? Headlines compelling?)
2. Check targeting: Is campaign set to worldwide? Narrow to US only if applicable.
3. Check: Do you have 100+ conversions yet? If not, still in learning; wait.
4. If confirmed bad: Kill campaign; try different asset group angle.

### Problem: Spending budget but getting clicks, no conversions
**Diagnosis:** Landing page issue (not ads)
**Solution:**
1. Check product page loads in <2 seconds
2. Check mobile responsiveness (most PMax traffic is mobile)
3. Check conversion tracking is firing (test by buying yourself)
4. Verify: Is your conversion event the right event? (Should be "Purchase" not "View")

### Problem: Asset Group 1 ROAS 3.5, Asset Group 2 ROAS 2.1
**Diagnosis:** Asset Group 1 winning; Asset Group 2 needs refinement
**Solution:**
1. Identify which headlines/images in Asset Group 1 are outperforming
2. Refresh Asset Group 2 with winning elements from Asset Group 1
3. Run new version of Asset Group 2 for Week 2
4. Or: Just delete Asset Group 2; keep winning one

## PMAX vs SMART SHOPPING

| Aspect | PMax | Shopping |
|---|---|---|
| **Channels** | All (5+ Google channels) | Products feed only |
| **Control** | Lowest (algorithm decides everything) | Higher (you manage products) |
| **Learning time** | 4-6 weeks | 2-3 weeks |
| **Asset complexity** | High (headlines, images, videos, logos) | Medium (just product data) |
| **ROAS typical** | 2.5-3.5 | 3.0-4.5 |
| **Setup effort** | 30 minutes | 20 minutes |
| **When to use** | Scaling established products | First campaign; fast learning |
| **Layering** | Add AFTER Shopping proves concept | Start here |

**Recommendation:** Launch Shopping first (faster results); layer PMax once Shopping profitable.

---

## PMAX SUCCESS INDICATORS

✓ **Working if:**
- Learning Phase exit by Week 3-4 (stable metrics)
- ROAS 2.5+ maintained for 2+ weeks
- Asset Group 1 outperforms Asset Group 2 (identifies winning messaging)
- Spend concentrated on best-performing channels (usually Search 40%, YouTube 30%, Display 20%)

✗ **Should be killed if:**
- Week 5+ ROAS still <2.0
- Asset groups all performing equally bad (messaging issue, not algorithm)
- Spending decreasing (algorithm losing confidence)
- Conversion volume zero/near-zero despite spend

---

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Need 50+ conversions** minimum to add PMax (not start with it)
2. **Asset groups are everything** in PMax success (spend time on quality)
3. **Maximize Conversions** best bidding for learning; Target ROAS after 100+ conversions
4. **Efficiency vs Growth split** ($150 test / $300 scale) is optimal
5. **Learning phase = 3-4 weeks** (longer than Shopping due to multi-channel)
6. **Asset Group A vs B testing** identifies which messaging resonates
7. **Audience signals optional** but boost ROAS 5-15% if you have customer list
8. **Scale 20% per week** if ROAS above 2.5; stop when below 2.0
9. **Layer on top of Shopping**, not instead of it
10. **Typical ROAS 2.5-3.5**, which is good (Shopping higher due to intent)
