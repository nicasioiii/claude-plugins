# Google Ads Scaling Strategy (GTC & Bidding)

## Overview
- **GTC = General Testing Campaign** (standard methodology)
- **Purpose:** Methodically test, identify winners, then scale
- **Timeline:** 4-8 weeks from start to 3x scaling
- **Budget progression:** $100 → $200 → $300 → $500+ (sustainable scaling)
- **Expected scaling:** 20% per week once profitable (Week 1: $100 → Week 4: $240)

---

## GTC: GENERAL TESTING CAMPAIGN METHODOLOGY

**GTC is a proven 4-phase framework for scaling Google Ads:**

### Phase 1: Single Testing Campaign (Week 1-2)
**Goal:** Identify winning products/keywords at scale

**Setup:**
- Campaign name: "[GTC Phase 1] All Products" or "[GTC Phase 1] General Search"
- Daily budget: $50-100
- Bidding: Maximize Clicks
- All products/keywords in one campaign
- Product selection: Top 20-30 best products (if Shopping)
- Keyword scope: Tier 1 + Tier 2 only (high-intent)

**Data collected:**
- Which products get clicks? (Shopping)
- Which keywords get clicks? (Search)
- What's click volume? (at your budget level)
- Early ROAS signal (10-20 conversions expected)

**Decision point (Day 14):**
- Have 15-20 conversions? → Proceed to Phase 2
- Have 0-5 conversions? → Increase budget 50% OR optimize product feed/landing pages
- Have 20+ conversions with ROAS 2.0+? → Skip to Phase 3

### Phase 2: Stratified Campaigns (Week 3-4)
**Goal:** Separate winners from losers; concentrate budget on winners

**Setup - Shopping example:**
```
Campaign A: [GTC Phase 2 Winners]
├─ Products: Top 5 products from Phase 1 by ROAS
├─ Daily budget: $50 (50% of total budget)
├─ Bidding: Manual CPC ($0.50-0.75, higher than Phase 1)
└─ Goal: Scale profitable products

Campaign B: [GTC Phase 2 Testing]
├─ Products: Middle 10-15 products from Phase 1
├─ Daily budget: $30 (30% of total budget)
├─ Bidding: Maximize Clicks ($0.30-0.50)
└─ Goal: Find new winners among remaining products

Campaign C: [GTC Phase 2 Low Performers]
├─ Products: Lowest performers from Phase 1
├─ Daily budget: $20 (20% of total budget)
├─ Bidding: Maximize Clicks ($0.15-0.30)
└─ Goal: Either eliminate or find unexpected winners
```

**Search example:**
```
Campaign A: [GTC Phase 2 Tier 1]
├─ Keywords: Branded + top-performing high-intent keywords
├─ Daily budget: $60
├─ Bidding: Manual CPC, high bids
└─ Expected ROAS: 3.0-4.0

Campaign B: [GTC Phase 2 Tier 2]
├─ Keywords: Mid-performing high-intent keywords
├─ Daily budget: $40
├─ Bidding: Manual CPC, medium bids
└─ Expected ROAS: 2.0-2.5
```

**Data collected:**
- Winners confirmed (ROAS 3.0+)
- Mid-tier products (ROAS 2.0-3.0)
- Clear losers (ROAS <1.5)
- Total: 40-60 conversions (ready for smart bidding)

**Decision point (Day 28):**
- Winners identified? Have 40+ conversions? → Proceed to Phase 3
- Still unclear? → Continue Phase 2 another 7 days

### Phase 3: Smart Bidding (Week 5-6)
**Goal:** Optimize winners with smart bidding; scale aggressively

**Setup:**
```
Campaign A: [Winners] High-Performance
├─ Products/Keywords: Confirmed winners only (ROAS 3.0+)
├─ Daily budget: $150
├─ Bidding: Target ROAS (set 3.0-3.5 target) OR Maximize Conversions
├─ Separation: Each winner may get own campaign or combined
└─ Goal: Scale to $300-500/day

Campaign B: [Growth] Mid-Performance
├─ Products/Keywords: ROAS 2.0-2.5 range
├─ Daily budget: $75
├─ Bidding: Manual CPC or ECPC (not yet smart bidding)
└─ Goal: Prove these can scale to winner tier

Campaign C: [Testing] Low-Performance
├─ Products/Keywords: ROAS <2.0
├─ Daily budget: $25 (minimal spend; testing only)
├─ Bidding: Maximize Clicks (cheap exploration)
└─ Goal: Find surprise winners or eliminate
```

**Data collected:**
- Winners at scale (60+ conversions each)
- Growth tier confirmed or rejected
- Clear differentiation of campaign tiers
- Scalable, profitable structure proven

**Decision point (Day 42):**
- Winners sustaining 3.0+ ROAS at $150+/day? → Proceed to Phase 4
- Good momentum but not peak scale? → Continue Phase 3, increase to $250/day

### Phase 4: Scaling (Week 7+)
**Goal:** Maximize profitable spend

**Setup:**
```
Campaign A: [Winners - Tier 1]
├─ Daily budget: $300+
├─ Bidding: Target ROAS (3.0-3.5)
├─ Frequency: Bid reviews every 7-14 days
└─ Scaling: +20% per week if ROAS maintained

Campaign B: [Growth - Tier 2]
├─ Daily budget: $150
├─ Bidding: Manual CPC or Target CPA
├─ Goal: Prove to Tier 1 level
└─ Scaling: Conditional on ROAS reaching 2.5+

Campaign C: [Expansion]
├─ Daily budget: $100 (NEW)
├─ Scope: New products/keywords not tested yet
├─ Bidding: Maximize Clicks (learning new segment)
└─ Goal: Find next tier of winners
```

**Scaling math:**
```
Week 7:  $300 ROAS 3.2
Week 8:  $360 (20% increase) ROAS target 3.0+
Week 9:  $430 (20% increase) ROAS target 2.8+
Week 10: $515 (20% increase) ROAS target 2.6+
Week 11: $615 (20% increase) ROAS target 2.4+
Week 12: $730 (20% increase) ROAS target 2.2+
Stop when: ROAS falls below 2.0 (saturation)
```

## STANDALONE CAMPAIGN STRATEGY

**Instead of GTC phases, some accounts prefer separate campaigns from start:**

### Standalone Approach (Shopping)
```
Campaign 1: [High-Bid] Testing Phase
├─ Products: 15-20 your best products
├─ Budget: $200/day
├─ Bid: $0.50-0.70 per product
└─ Duration: Weeks 1-3 (identify winners)

Campaign 2: [Low-Bid] Testing Phase
├─ Products: 15-20 different products
├─ Budget: $150/day
├─ Bid: $0.20-0.35 per product
└─ Duration: Weeks 1-3 (test value angle)

Campaign 3: [Winners Only] Scaling
├─ Products: Top 5 from Campaign 1 + Top 5 from Campaign 2
├─ Budget: $250/day (after Week 3)
├─ Bid: $0.60+ (scale winners)
└─ Duration: Weeks 4+
```

**Pros:** Cleaner separation; easier to analyze; prevents cross-contamination
**Cons:** More campaigns to manage; requires more budget upfront

## BIDDING PROGRESSION BY PHASE

### Phase 1 Bidding (Learning)
- **Strategy:** Maximize Clicks
- **Manual bid:** $0.30-0.50 (medium range)
- **Why:** Build click data; find products that get traffic
- **Expected CPC:** $0.20-0.40

### Phase 2 Bidding (Early Optimization)
- **Strategy:** Manual CPC (based on Phase 1 learnings)
- **Winners:** Increase to $0.60-0.80 (higher investment)
- **Losers:** Decrease to $0.15-0.25 (minimal spend)
- **Mid-tier:** Keep at $0.35-0.45 (testing phase)
- **Why:** Concentrate budget on items you know work

### Phase 3 Bidding (Smart Bidding)
- **Strategy:** Target ROAS (or Maximize Conversions)
- **Target setting:** Based on Phase 2 proven ROAS
  - If Phase 2 winners averaging 3.5 ROAS: Set Target ROAS 3.0-3.2
  - If Phase 2 growth averaging 2.3 ROAS: Keep Manual CPC (not ready for smart)
- **Why:** Algorithm now has 60+ conversions to learn from; safe to automate

### Phase 4 Bidding (Scaling)
- **Strategy:** Target ROAS (locked in on winners)
- **Bid reviews:** Every 7-14 days
- **Adjustment:** Only adjust ROAS target if circumstances change
  - Increase target: Only if ROAS consistently above target (not limited by bid)
  - Decrease target: Only if ROAS consistently below target
- **Why:** Consistent bidding prevents re-training; let algorithm optimize

## 20% WEEKLY GROWTH RULE

**Sustainable scaling follows the 20% per week pattern:**

**Why 20%?**
- Growth rate fast enough to see results (doubles in 4 weeks)
- Conservative enough to maintain quality (doesn't shock system)
- Balances algorithm optimization with budget expansion

**Application:**
```
Week 1: $100
Week 2: $100 (accumulate data; no scale yet)
Week 3: $100 (confirm winners)
Week 4: $120 (+20%) First scaling
Week 5: $144 (+20%)
Week 6: $173 (+20%)
Week 7: $207 (+20%)
Week 8: $248 (+20%)
Month 2 total: $1,090 (10x growth in one month)
```

**When to slow scaling:**
- ROAS drops 15-20% → Reduce to 10% weekly
- ROAS drops 30%+ → Stop scaling; hold budget
- If CPC rising trend → Be more conservative

**When to accelerate:**
- ROAS consistently 3.5+ → Can safely go 25% weekly
- Market opportunity (seasonal spike) → Go 30% for limited period

## EXPANSION TACTICS (Finding More Winners)

### Tactic 1: Expand Within Winner Category
```
Phase 2: [Winners] has 5 best products making $1M/month
Phase 4: Expand to "Related Products"
├─ Add 10-15 similar products to that category
├─ Budget: $100/day new allocation
├─ Result: Often 50-70% of new products become winners too
└─ Math: New winners add $500K-700K/month
```

### Tactic 2: New Product Categories
```
Winner category proven
├─ Launch Phase 1 (GTC) in new category
├─ Budget: $50-100/day (small test)
├─ Timeline: 2-3 weeks to determine if viable
└─ Decision: If ROAS 2.0+, expand; if <1.5, abandon
```

### Tactic 3: Geographic Expansion
```
If winning in United States
├─ Test: Canada, UK, Australia (one at a time)
├─ Budget: 20-25% of US budget per country
├─ Timeline: 4 weeks per country
└─ Expected: 70% success rate (audience similar)
```

### Tactic 4: Audience Expansion
```
If Shopping winners clear at US only
├─ Add: In-market audiences (similar reach, different people)
├─ Add: Similar audiences (look-alikes)
├─ Add: Remarketing (past visitors)
├─ Budget: 10-25% per audience type
└─ Expected ROAS: 20-30% lower than core, but more volume
```

## TROUBLESHOOTING SCALING ISSUES

### Problem: Budget increased; ROAS dropped 50%
**Diagnosis:** Audience saturated too quickly OR bidding broke
**Solution:**
1. Check bidding strategy (if using smart bidding, make sure data sufficient)
2. Check: Did you hit same audience size ceiling? (Only so many searchers for keyword)
3. Revert to previous budget level
4. Expand to new products/keywords instead of bidding more on existing

### Problem: Winners became losers (ROAS 3.5 → 1.8)
**Diagnosis:** Seasonal shift, competition increase, or audience fatigue
**Solution:**
1. Check: Is this seasonal? (Some products have season)
2. Check: Did you add new landing pages that converts worse?
3. Check: Have competitors increased bids? (CPC rising?)
4. Kill campaign if can't identify cause within 7 days

### Problem: Can't find second tier of winners
**Diagnosis:** Your selection is tight; new products aren't as good
**Solution:**
1. Test smaller bid increments (not Phase 4 level)
2. Expand category broadly (go from "shoes" to "footwear" larger)
3. Accept: You may have found best products already
4. Scale existing winners deeper rather than find new

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **GTC methodology ensures tested scaling** (not guessing)
2. **4 phases: Testing → Stratifying → Smart Bidding → Scaling**
3. **20% weekly growth is sustainable** (doubles monthly)
4. **Don't skip phases** (each phase provides data for next)
5. **Standalone campaigns** work if you have sufficient budget upfront
6. **Bidding progression:** Maximize Clicks → Manual CPC → Target ROAS → Optimize
7. **Expansion tactics:** Category expansion, geo expansion, audience expansion
8. **Monthly reviews essential** to catch issues early
9. **Budget 30-day outlook** before committing to scaling
10. **If something breaks, revert** and diagnose before re-scaling
