# Google YouTube Ads Strategy

## Overview
- **Best For:** Brand awareness, product discovery, scaling after Search/Shopping profitable
- **When to launch:** After Search or Shopping campaign has 100+ conversions
- **Budget:** $50-200/day (secondary channel; layer on top)
- **Timeline:** 4-8 weeks to identify winning formats
- **Expected ROAS:** 1.5-3.0 (lower than Search; awareness-focused)
- **NOT for:** First campaign (start with Search/Shopping)

---

## 7 MUST-HAVE AD ELEMENTS

**Every YouTube ad needs these 7 elements:**

1. **Hook** (0-2 seconds)
   - Stop scroll; pattern interrupt
   - Visual surprise or compelling statement
   - Example: "This costs $5 but looks like $500"

2. **Value Proposition** (2-4 seconds)
   - What's the problem you solve?
   - Why should user care?
   - Example: "Get luxury furniture without the luxury price"

3. **Product Demonstration** (4-6 seconds)
   - Show product in action
   - Real use case or result
   - Example: Person using product; before/after

4. **Social Proof** (6-8 seconds)
   - Testimonials, reviews, user count
   - "100K+ happy customers"
   - "5-star rated on Google"

5. **Objection Handling** (8-10 seconds)
   - Address main barrier to purchase
   - "Ships in 24 hours", "30-day money-back guarantee"
   - "No credit card required"

6. **Call-to-Action** (10-15 seconds)
   - Clear next step
   - "Shop now", "Claim your free trial", "Learn more"
   - Make it easy/urgent ("Limited time 50% off")

7. **Frequency Cap** (Visual indicator)
   - Brand logo/watermark throughout (builds recall)
   - Consistent color scheme
   - Helps viewers remember brand during recall phase

## CRITICAL: THE 1:30 - 3:00 SWEET SPOT

**Rule: YouTube ads perform best between 1:30-3:00 length**

### Why?
- **Skip threshold:** Users can skip at 5 seconds
- **Retention cliff:** 50% of viewers stop by 2 minutes
- **Optimal window:** 1:30-3:00 catches the 50% who stay + adds 30-45 seconds of engagement

### Duration Strategy by Goal

| Goal | Duration | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | 30-45 seconds | Quick; gets message across before skip |
| **Consideration** | 1:30-2:30 | Time to explain benefits; keeps viewers |
| **Conversion** | 2:30-3:00 | Time for objection handling + CTA |
| **Avoid** | 5+ minutes | Too long; loses viewers by minute 1 |

### Specific Guidelines
```
0-5 sec: SKIPPABLE → Hook must stop scroll BEFORE skip button appears
5-15 sec: CRUCIAL → After skip threshold; hooks who stayed are committed
15-90 sec: ENGAGEMENT → Explore value, demo, proof
90-180 sec: RETENTION → Dedicate to convincing (objections, social proof, CTA)
180+ sec: DANGER → Completion rate drops sharply; avoid unless exceptional content
```

## AUDIENCE TARGETING

### Best Audiences for YouTube

**Cold Traffic (Awareness):**
- In-market audiences (shopping category, but not your brand)
- Similar audiences (similar to your customers)
- Interests (shopping, lifestyle, industry-related)
- Placements (specific YouTube channels, publishers)

**Warm Traffic (Consideration/Conversion):**
- Remarketing lists (past website visitors)
- Customer match (email list)
- Shopping audiences (if e-commerce)

### Audience Segmentation Strategy
```
Campaign 1: Cold (Awareness)
├─ Audience: In-market + Similar audiences
├─ Budget: 60% of YouTube budget
├─ Goal: Build awareness among 100M+ people
└─ Expected ROAS: 1.0-1.5 (top-funnel is break-even)

Campaign 2: Warm (Retargeting)
├─ Audience: Website visitors (past 30 days)
├─ Budget: 40% of YouTube budget
├─ Goal: Re-engage engaged visitors
└─ Expected ROAS: 2.5-4.0 (warm traffic converts better)
```

## BIDDING FOR YOUTUBE ADS

### Cost Models

**CPV (Cost Per View)** - Primary model
- Pay only when user watches 30+ seconds or clicks ad (whichever comes first)
- Typical range: $0.05-0.50 per view (depends on competition, audience)
- Budget: If $100/day with $0.20 CPV = 500 views/day

**CPM (Cost Per 1000 Impressions)** - Alternative
- Pay per 1000 ad impressions (whether user watches or not)
- Use when: Want reach over engagement
- Typical range: $5-20 per 1000 impressions

**Recommendation for e-commerce:** Use CPV (pay for engagement, not just impressions)

### Bid Strategy Progression

**Week 1-2: Maximize Views**
- Let Google optimize for most views at your budget
- Bid: Automatic (you set daily budget; Google decides CPV)
- Goal: See 1000+ views; identify which ads get watched

**Week 3+: Target CPA**
- If you have conversion data: Set target cost per conversion
- Google bids to hit your cost target
- Requires: Conversion tracking + 50+ conversions historical data
- Goal: Sustainable ROAS

## YOUTUBE CAMPAIGN SETUP

### Campaign Structure
```
Campaign 1: YouTube Cold - Awareness
├─ Audience: In-market + Similar + Interest targeting
├─ Budget: $60/day (if $100/day total YouTube)
├─ Bidding: Maximize Views initially, then Target CPA
├─ Creatives: 4-6 awareness-focused ads (template 1-3)
└─ Goal: Brand awareness among broad audience

Campaign 2: YouTube Warm - Retargeting
├─ Audience: Website visitors (past 30 days, <10 days avg page duration)
├─ Budget: $40/day
├─ Bidding: Target CPA (set aggressive; warm traffic converts)
├─ Creatives: 2-3 conversion-focused ads (template 4-5)
└─ Goal: Convert interested visitors
```

### Ad Group Organization (Within Campaign)
- Ad Group 1: Awareness ads (3-4 videos)
- Ad Group 2: Consideration ads (2-3 videos)
- Ad Group 3: Conversion ads (2-3 videos)
- Each targets same audience but focuses on different message

## SCALING YOUTUBE ADS

### Phase 1: Testing (Week 1-3)
- Budget: $50-100/day (small test)
- Creatives: 6-8 different ad variations
- Goal: 500-1000 views; identify which ads get best watch rate
- Metric: Watch rate (target 25%+ of viewers watch to 30 seconds)

### Phase 2: Optimization (Week 4-6)
- Budget: Keep at $50-100/day (don't scale yet)
- Creatives: Keep top 2-3 from Phase 1; add 2-3 new variations
- Goal: 1000-2000 views; see conversion data
- Metric: Cost per conversion (target <50% of your CPA limit)

### Phase 3: Scaling (Week 7+)
- Budget: Increase 25-50% if metrics above target
- Example: $75/day → $100/day → $125/day → $150/day
- Creatives: Continue testing new variations alongside winners
- Metric: Maintain ROAS >1.5 (breakeven for brand building)

### Scaling Rule
```
If CPV stays <$0.25: Can scale 20-30% per week
If CPV rising (>$0.35): Slow scaling to 5-10% weekly
If CPV >$0.50: Pause; audience saturated or bidding too aggressive
```

## TROUBLESHOOTING

### Problem: Watch rate <15% (viewers skipping early)
**Diagnosis:** Hook isn't working or ad placement bad
**Solution:**
1. Check hook element (0-2 seconds)—is it pattern-interrupting?
2. Try different templates (Template 2 "Before/After" often highest watch rate)
3. Adjust audience (maybe wrong demographic)
4. Change placement (YouTube channels; exclude certain content types)

### Problem: High views (1000+), zero conversions
**Diagnosis:** Either conversion tracking broken OR audience cold
**Solution:**
1. Test conversion tracking (buy your own product; verify pixel fires)
2. Add retargeting campaign to warm up audience (they saw ad, need second touch)
3. Adjust landing page (video drives awareness; page must convert)

### Problem: CPV rising week-over-week ($0.20 → $0.35 → $0.50)
**Diagnosis:** Audience saturation (same people seeing ad repeatedly)
**Solution:**
1. Expand audience (add lookalikes, broader interests)
2. Create new ad variations (same audience gets bored with same ad)
3. Cap frequency (show ad max 3-5 times per user per week)
4. Pause campaign 1-2 weeks (let audience "refresh")

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Don't launch YouTube first** — Layer after Search/Shopping profitable
2. **Sweet spot duration: 1:30-3:00** (longer watch time; optimal skip avoidance)
3. **7 elements must be present** (hook, value, demo, proof, objection, CTA, branding)
4. **Hook is critical** (0-2 sec; pattern interrupt before 5-second skip)
5. **Use CPV model** (pay only for engaged views 30+ seconds)
6. **Cold vs warm split** (60% awareness / 40% retargeting is optimal)
7. **Use templates** (8 proven templates cover 90% of scenarios)
8. **Phase scaling** (test, optimize, then scale 20-30%)
9. **Watch rate >25%** = healthy ad; <15% = fix the hook
10. **Frequency cap** to prevent audience fatigue; rotate creatives weekly
