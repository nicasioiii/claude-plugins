# Google Full-Funnel Strategy

## HIERARCHY OF GOALS (Top to Bottom)

1. **Finance Goals** -- profits, EBITDA, contribution margin (TOP PRIORITY).
2. **Business Goals** -- total sales, total ad spend, MER (total sales / total ad spend).
3. **Customer Goals** -- LTV, new customer rates, repeat purchase rates.
4. **Channel/Platform Goals** -- ROAS, CPA/CAC (LOWEST -- servant to goals above).

Channel ROAS is the least important goal. Never optimize channel metrics at the expense of business metrics.

---

## DEMAND CAPTURE vs DEMAND GENERATION

### Demand Capture (On-Demand Marketing)
- Active shoppers already looking for solutions.
- Focus: Search + Shopping.
- Best for: auto parts, appliances, saunas, product categories people search for.
- Characteristics: high ROAS, low CAC, efficient.

### Demand Generation (Interruption Marketing)
- People unaware your product exists but would love it.
- Focus: YouTube + Display + Discovery.
- Best for: fashion, novel products, impulse purchases, problem-aware-not-solution-aware audiences.
- Characteristics: scale, rapid growth, brand building.

### Almost ALL Businesses Need Both
- Scale & rapid growth = need demand generation.
- High ROAS / low CAC = need demand capture.
- Reference: Eugene Schwartz -- you can't truly create demand, only channel existing desires.

## INTERRUPTION vs ON-DEMAND MARKETING

| Interruption Marketing | On-Demand Marketing |
|---|---|
| Fashion & accessories | Auto parts |
| New & novel products | Appliances |
| Impulse products | Known product categories |
| Problem aware, not solution aware | Problem AND solution aware |

## BUDGET ALLOCATION GUIDELINES (Across Hundreds of E-Commerce Accounts)

| Channel | % of Total Google Budget |
|---|---|
| Search | 10-30% (10% if demand gen focused, 30% if demand capture) |
| Shopping / PMax | 30-65% (automotive parts example: 65%+) |
| Remarketing | 10-20% |
| YouTube & Display TOF | 10-50% (depends on demand gen needs) |

### By Business Maturity
- **New businesses / new Google accounts:** Start with Search + Shopping + Remarketing. Launch TOF after building conversion data.
- **Mature business, new to Google:** Get several campaigns to 30+ conversions/month first, THEN add YouTube/TOF.
- **Mature business, mature account:** Full funnel deployment.

## THE PORTFOLIO MINDSET (Ezra/Brett)

### Dennis Rodman Analogy
Don't judge individual campaigns in isolation. YouTube is the "Dennis Rodman" -- might not score points (direct conversions) but creates opportunities for other campaigns.

### Light Switch Analogy
TOF campaigns are like a light switch -- the action (flip) happens in one place, but the result (light) happens somewhere else. YouTube drives brand search, which drives conversions on brand campaigns.

### The Rule
**Obsess over portfolio metrics, inspect campaign metrics.** Each campaign does what it was designed to do. Ultimate measure = portfolio performance.

## ROAS-BASED BUDGET MODEL

### Example Scenario
- $140K/month total budget.
- $40K on TOF, $100K on primary.
- YouTube at 50% ROAS (for every $1 spent, $0.50 in directly attributed sales).
- Non-brand consideration at 175% ROAS.
- Brand/purchase at 350% ROAS.
- Remarketing/loyalty at 250% ROAS.
- **Portfolio ROAS: 200%** (translates to ~330-400% MER).

### Portfolio ROAS to MER Translation
In-platform ROAS of 200% translates to ~330-400% MER due to halo effects and cross-channel attribution gaps.

## BUDGET CALCULATION FORMULAS (PPC Hub)

### CPA-Based
Required Monthly Budget = Target Monthly Conversions x Target CPA.
Example: 100 leads x $50 CPA = $5,000/month = $164/day.

### ROAS-Based
Required Monthly Budget = Target Monthly Revenue / Target ROAS.
Example: $400K Q4 revenue / 350% ROAS = $140K quarterly = ~$1,250/day.

### Budget Validation Checklist
- Is there enough search volume / market potential?
- Is there room in search impression share?
- What is competition level per market segment?
- Historical CPA/ROAS trends during scaling?
- Will conversion rate hold when scaling?

- Lower-volume accounts: monthly adjustments.
- Follow the 20% rule for any bid or budget changes.

## CROSS-CHANNEL ATTRIBUTION

### The Problem
If you add conversion value from all channels (Facebook, Google, TikTok), total exceeds actual revenue. Each platform takes full credit for the same purchase.

### Solution
Third-party attribution tools as single source of truth:
- **Northbeam** -- cross-channel attribution.
- **Triple Whale** -- cross-channel attribution.
- **GA4** -- free option.
All leverage first-party data with unique attribution models.

### Cash vs Accrual Reporting
- **Google Ads** = accrual-based (counts conversion on date of CLICK).
- **GA4/Shopify** = cash-based (counts conversion on date of PURCHASE).
- This causes data discrepancies, especially for longer conversion windows.

### Data-Driven Attribution (Recommended)
- Uses your account's data to determine which ads have most influence.
- Compares converter paths vs non-converter paths.
- Different for every advertiser.
- All OMG Commerce clients use Data-Driven Attribution.
