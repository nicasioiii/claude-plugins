# Google Search Campaign Strategy

## KEYWORD CATEGORIES

### Three Categories (Ezra/Brett)
1. **Brand Keywords** -- contains your brand name or product names. Highest conversion rates. Users are problem-aware, solution-aware, AND brand-aware.
2. **Non-Brand Keywords** -- category-specific, not brand-specific. High purchase intent. START HERE for non-brand search.
3. **Top of Funnel Keywords** -- questions, research queries. Problem-aware only. Low return, reserved for advanced strategies.

### What to Look for in Keywords
1. **Relevance** -- closely related to what you sell (more specific = better).
2. **Intent** -- non-brand > TOF for starting.
3. **Volume** -- enough search volume to build conversion data.

## MATCH TYPES

### Exact Match [brackets]
- Same meaning as keyword, including plurals/misspellings.
- Most cost effective. Maximum control.
- Example: [blue bicycle] -> "blue bikes"

### Phrase Match "quotes"
- Same meaning plus added qualifiers. Middle ground.
- Example: "blue bicycle" -> "kids blue bike with training wheels"

### Broad Match (no symbols)
- Widest net, uses additional signals: landing page content, other keywords, user history, location.
- 15% of daily searches are never-before-seen, so broad captures these.
- Getting better with machine learning -- now outperforms expectations in many accounts.

### Match Type Selection by Situation
| Situation | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| New account, limited budget | Phrase + Exact (Ezra/Brett) or Exact only (Shri) |
| Established account, strong data | Broad + smart bidding (PPC Hub) |
| Budget-constrained, winning products | Exact only at $0.05 CPC (Shri) |
| High-volume, scaling | Broad + smart bidding + challenging targets (PPC Hub) |

### Broad Match Additional Signals (Not Available to Phrase/Exact)
- Landing page content (understands context).
- Other keywords in same ad group (understands meaning).
- Previous user searches.
- User location.

## SEARCH CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE

### Brand Campaigns (1-4 Campaigns) -- Ezra/Brett
1. **General/Primary Brand** -- brand keywords -> homepage. EVERYONE should have this.
2. **Brand Products** -- brand + product name -> PDP (optional).
3. **Brand Category** -- brand + category -> category pages (optional).
4. **Brand NCA (New Customer Acquisition)** -- brand keywords segmented by new vs returning.

### Why You MUST Bid on Brand Keywords
1. Prevents competitors from poaching customers.
2. Controls brand messaging through ad copy and extensions.
3. Improves account-level quality score (high CTR).

### Non-Brand Campaigns
- Group by product or category.
- Top products non-brand campaign for standout sellers.
- Smaller budget campaigns for other products.

### Competitor Campaigns
- Advanced strategy -- build everything else first.
- Expect high CPCs and low ROAS. Treat as awareness.
- Always keep in separate campaigns for budget control.

### RLSA Campaigns (Remarketing Lists for Search Ads)
- Target past site visitors when they do non-brand searches.
- **Observation mode recommended for ALL search campaigns** -- doesn't narrow, just reporting.
- Layer viewed video audiences onto branded search to measure YouTube brand lift.

## QUALITY SCORE OPTIMIZATION PLAYBOOK (PPC Hub)

### The Philosophy
QS is NOT optimized directly. It is the RESULT of Google gaining confidence that your ad matches intent, users will click, and the landing page delivers.

### Prioritization Formula
**Priority Score = (10 - Quality Score) x Impressions.**
Sort descending. Work on highest-priority keyword/ad group first.
Fix ONE theme -> validate -> replicate. Never work on dozens at once.

### Phase 1: Ad Relevance (Fix First)
- Below Average -> STOP, fix structural issues.
- Fix: ad group structure, keyword-to-ad alignment.
- Include target keywords in ad copy. Segment ad groups more tightly.
- Timeline: 7-14 days.

### Phase 2: Expected CTR (Fix Second)
- Below Average -> STOP, fix competitiveness.
- Focus: message strength (benefits, social proof, CTAs, differentiation).
- Beef up ad extensions, improve calls to action, clarify benefits.
- Timeline: 7-21 days.

### Phase 3: Landing Page Experience (Fix Third)
- Below Average -> STOP, fix post-click experience.
- Google uses conversion rate as proxy for good LPE.
- Improve page speed, send to most relevant page, align content with keywords.
- Timeline: 14-30 days.

### Definition of Done
- All three components = Average or Above Average.
- Quality Score >= 7/10 (stable for 14+ days).
- CPC stabilized or decreased.

## RSA BEST PRACTICES

### PPC Hub: The 7 Headline Types (30-char max)
1. **Relevance Anchor** -- signal match to query (use DKI: {KeyWord:Fallback}).
2. **Problem/Pain Recognition** -- mirror frustration. [Tired of/Stop/End] + [Problem].
3. **Value Proposition** -- what you offer + primary benefit.
4. **Call-to-Action** -- exact next step. [Action Verb] + [Outcome] + [Modifier].
5. **USP/Benefit** -- differentiate. [Differentiator] + [Quantified Benefit].
6. **Social Proof & Authority** -- trust. [Metric] + [Audience/Context].
7. **Risk Removal** -- lower barrier. [Guarantee Type] + [Reassurance].

### Structure Rules
- **6-8 headlines per RSA** (NOT 15 -- more = data poverty).
- All 7 types represented.
- 25-30 characters per headline.
- Headlines must stand alone AND complement each other.
- 2-3 descriptions per RSA.
- One RSA per ad group (no competing RSAs splitting traffic).

### The Data Poverty Problem
15-headline RSA = up to 2,730 three-headline combinations. Most ad groups never accumulate enough impressions to learn. Solution: limit to 6-8 headlines, use templates across clusters, aggregate data.

### Shri's Approach (Simpler)
- 3 headlines (not 15) -- keep testing efficient.
- 2 descriptions (not 4).
- Repeat SEO keyword in headlines AND descriptions.
- Use emotional/trust words and discount incentives.

## AD GROUP STRUCTURE

### Tightly Themed Ad Groups
- Keywords and ad copy closely aligned.
- High-spend/low-conversion keywords: break into own ad group and optimize for QS.

### Templatize Approach (PPC Hub)
- Group ad groups into clusters with similar intent.
- Create RSA template: H1 = keyword slot (changes per ad group), all other slots identical across cluster.
- 20 ad groups x 5,000 impressions = 100,000 aggregated impressions for one angle.

## SEARCH AD EXTENSIONS (ALL OF THEM)
- Sitelinks, callouts, structured snippets, image extensions, promotion extensions.
- Extensions improve CTR and quality score.
- Use ALL available extensions -- no exceptions.

---

## BIDDING PROGRESSION FOR SEARCH

1. **Start:** Enhanced CPC or Max Conversions / Max Conversion Value.
2. **After 30 conversions in 30 days:** Switch to Target CPA or Target ROAS.
3. **Set initial target** close to current performance.
4. **Optimize gradually:** Max 20% per week (recommended 10%, 1-2x/week).

### Search Term Report Management
- Check DAILY for new campaigns.
- Add negative keywords for: unrelated terms, high spend/no conversions, high CPCs.
- Use phrase match negatives to block categories of irrelevant terms.
- Create negative keyword lists to apply across multiple campaigns.
