# Google YouTube Ads Strategy

## SUCCESS IS 50-80% CREATIVE

Google study confirms: 50-80% of YouTube ad success is the creative. Remaining 20-50% is campaign structure, bidding, and audience strategy.

YouTube ads are fundamentally different from Facebook/TikTok. More similar to short-form infomercials (1-3 minutes).

---

## VIDEO LENGTH SWEET SPOT

### Optimal: 1.5 to 3 Minutes
- Beyond 3 minutes: Google may increase CPM.
- Below 30 seconds: good for engagement/views but poor for conversions.
- A 1-minute version of an ad got 10x the conversions of its 30-second version (exact same content).
- Story/message matters more than production value.

### YouTube Ads Don't Burn Out Like Social Ads
- Audiences refresh regularly on YouTube.
- Winning ads can run for months or even YEARS.
- Unlike Meta/TikTok where creative fatigue hits in weeks.

## 7 MUST-HAVE ELEMENTS (Ezra/Brett)

### 1. The One Thing (Golden Thread)
- Single core message woven throughout the entire ad.
- After watching, what ONE thing do you want them to remember?
- Examples: Boom = "pro-age cosmetics", Grammarly = "grammar mistakes are killing your success."

### 2. The Hook (First 5 Seconds -- CRITICAL)
Users must watch first 5 seconds before skip button appears. Goal: get the RIGHT audience to watch more (OK to repel wrong audience -- they skip and you don't pay).

**Hook Types:**
- Lead with biggest benefit ("gorgeous flawless legs in seconds").
- Lead with biggest problem you solve (FlexSeal: "these repairs will cost thousands").
- Ask a big question prospects already wonder ("do these actually work?").
- Shock and awe (Poo-Pourri: prim British lady talking about bathroom habits).

**Enter the conversation already taking place in your customer's head.** If the ad isn't working, the hook is likely what needs adjusting.

### 3. Product Demo
- Show product in its natural environment.
- Kitchen appliance -> working in a kitchen; outdoor product -> outdoors.
- No excuse not to demo in video format.

### 4. Magical Demo (Optional but Powerful)
Goes above and beyond normal demo:
- Purple Mattress: raw egg test (300lb glass dropped, eggs don't break).
- William Painter: sunglasses as bottle opener + driving over them.
- FlexSeal: sawing a boat in half and sealing it.
- Creates "I've never seen anything like this" moment.

### 5. Objection Busters
Address the "yeah, but..." that follows interest:
- Common objections: price, durability, does it work, past bad experiences.
- Example: Groove Life's "94-Year No BS Guarantee."
- Persistent unaddressed objections = no action taken.

### 6. Social Proof
- Show reviews (website + Amazon).
- Display review count ("Over 1,000 5-star reviews").
- Read interesting/funny reviews on camera.
- Expert endorsements or credentials.
- UGC = pure gold -- real video from real customers.
- Every YouTube ad is made better with social proof.

### 7. Offer / Call to Action
- If you don't tell them what to do, they will do NOTHING.
- Must present: What is the offer? What are the next steps?
- Options: bundles, discounts, starter kits, free trial, subscribe & save.
- The offer + CTA is the close.

## YOUTUBE CAMPAIGN TYPES

### Remarketing Campaigns (Build FIRST)
Foundation of YouTube strategy. Target past visitors with video ads.

**Remarketing Audiences (Most to Least Likely to Convert):**
1. Past Purchasers (most likely)
2. Cart Abandoners
3. PDP Viewers
4. Newsletter/Email Subscribers
5. Viewed Video Audiences
6. All Visitors (largest, least likely)

### Top of Funnel Campaigns
- For demand generation and new customer acquisition.
- Target: custom intent audiences, affinity audiences, demographic targeting.
- Higher CPA expected; measure by brand lift, not direct ROAS.

### Demo-Only Targeting
- Can work extremely well on YouTube.
- Demo-only campaigns often see the LOWEST CPCs, CPVs, and CPMs.
- Broader audiences = cheaper traffic. If conversion rate holds, it outperforms targeted campaigns.

## YOUTUBE BIDDING STRATEGIES

### Conversion-Based (Primary)
1. **Maximize Conversion Value** -- auto-sets bids for highest AOV. 90% of the time: AOV and household income correlate 1:1. Use this 90% of the time.
2. **Target ROAS** -- efficiency target. Needs 15-20 conversions in last 30 days.
3. **Maximize Conversions** -- most conversions regardless of value.
4. **Target CPA** -- most conversions at target cost.

### Target CPA vs Target ROAS for YouTube
- **Target CPA:** High LTV / repeat purchases (supplements, consumables). Get more customers at low cost.
- **Target ROAS:** Low LTV / one-time purchases. Need profitability per transaction.

### Awareness-Based
- **Maximum CPV** -- set bid per view (30 seconds of watch time).
- **Target CPM** -- bid per thousand impressions.
- Good for awareness, NOT for conversion goals.

## THE YOUTUBE HALO EFFECT (OMG Commerce Study)

### Data from All Clients at $30K+/Month YouTube Spend
- Non-YouTube campaigns grew 27% in revenue.
- Clicks up 36%, CTR improved.
- NO changes were made to those campaigns.
- Growth was purely from YouTube's halo effect.

### Case Studies
- Automotive brand: Starting YouTube caused brand searches to MORE THAN DOUBLE.
- Hair care brand: Pausing YouTube for 2 weeks caused Amazon brand search volume to drop by HALF.

### Implication
- YouTube at 50% direct ROAS is acceptable when measured as portfolio.
- Don't judge YouTube by direct ROAS alone.
- YouTube is the "Dennis Rodman" -- creates opportunities for other campaigns.

## YOUTUBE OPTIMIZATION

### Key Metrics
- **View rate:** Indicates hook quality.
- **CTR:** Indicates persuasion quality.
- **Conversion rate:** Affected by landing page/offer (not just creative).

### Process
- Test multiple video creatives.
- Monitor audience performance and adjust bids at ad group level.
- Budget toward best-performing audiences.
