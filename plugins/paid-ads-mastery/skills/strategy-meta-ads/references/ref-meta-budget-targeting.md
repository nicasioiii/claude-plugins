# Meta Budget & Targeting Rules

## BUDGET SIZING FORMULA

### The Core Rule
**Daily budget / Average Cost Per Purchase = Number of ad sets you should run.**

| Daily Budget | Avg CPP | Ad Sets | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| $100 | $30 | 3-4 | Standard setup |
| $200 | $30 | 6-7 | Good range for Crazy Method |
| $500 | $30 | ~16 | Divide by 2 = 8 for safer approach |
| $100 | $100 | 1 | Need higher budget or lower CPP |

- Having 20 ad sets on $100 budget = asking for $5 CPP (impossible if your actual CPP is $30).
- After iOS: max ~10 ad sets recommended per CBO campaign.

### Budget Minimums
- Daily budget should be at least 1x your average cost per purchase.
- $100/day with $30 CPP = reasonable (3-4 ad sets).
- $100/day with $300 CPP = problematic (need higher AOV or lower CPP).

## BUDGET DISTRIBUTION REALITY

### How Meta Actually Spends
- Meta may spend $55 one day, $40 the next, $60 another -- but weekly total respects 7x daily budget.
- Never more than 7x the daily budget in a week.
- CBO distributes more evenly across ad sets than ASC (ASC can give 90% to one ad).

### E-commerce Prime Hours
- Active shopping: ~6 AM to ~12 AM (midnight).
- Dead hours: ~1 AM to ~5 AM.
- Budgets reset daily -- this is fundamental.

## THE 6 AM LAUNCH RULE

### Why Launch at 6 AM (Local Time of Target Market)
1. Budgets reset daily.
2. Launching at 6 AM gives the full day of prime hours for budget to spread.
3. Ads take 1-2 hours to populate, so effective spending starts ~7-8 AM.
4. Launching at 8-9 PM wastes the first-day boost on only 4 hours.
5. The 24-48 hour boost (Meta gives "low-hanging fruit" sales to kickstart) is wasted if you only give it 4 hours on day one.

### The 24-48 Hour Boost
- New campaigns get a boost in first 24-48 hours.
- With trained pixels, this transitions smoothly into sustained performance.
- With untrained pixels, performance often drops after boost period.
- **Relaunch strategy:** When campaign dies after boost, turn off, duplicate, launch next day at 6 AM.
- Warning: Overusing relaunches causes diminishing returns (4 days -> 2 days -> 1 day -> stops working).

## PLACEMENT STRATEGY

### Always Use Advantage+ (Automatic) Placements
Manual placements = higher CPM and worse results (tested extensively by Konstantinos).

### Only 6 Placements Actually Drive Significant Results
1. Facebook Feed
2. Instagram Feed
3. Instagram Stories
4. Facebook Stories
5. Instagram Reels
6. Facebook Reels

All other placements (Messenger, Audience Network, In-Stream, Search) get negligible spend but Meta forces auto to include them.

### The One Exception
If 100% of sales come from only Facebook or only Instagram (verified via breakdown), you can isolate:
- Common for 50+ audience (Facebook only).
- Common for 18-25 audience (Instagram only).

### ACA/Seth Best Practices
- At least 6+ placements across platforms (feed, stories, reels, search, messenger).
- More placements + more variations = LOWER costs (not higher).
- Create all content in 9:16, 1:1, and 4:5 formats minimum.

## TARGETING PROGRESSION

### The Three Layers of Targeting Quality (Konstantinos)
1. **Ad Account** -- separates good traffic from bad; trained by consistent spend and payment history.
2. **Pixel/API** -- forms ideal avatar; targets within the good traffic the account provides.
3. **Targeting settings** -- interest, lookalike, or broad; the most cost-efficient path.

### Progression Path (New to Seasoned)
1. Small interests (2-3M audience) -- start here for new pixels.
2. Normal interests (20M+) -- once initial data flows.
3. Broader interests (30-60M) -- expand reach.
4. Lookalikes 1% (~2.5M US) -- at ~100 purchase events.
5. Lookalikes 10% (~25M US) -- proven lookalike sources.
6. Stacked lookalikes (10% multiple sources) -- multiple entry points.
7. Broad targeting (~150M+ US) -- ultimate goal.

### Audience Size Guidelines
| Type | Typical Size (US) |
|---|---|
| Small interests | 2-3M |
| Normal interests | 20M |
| Broader interests | 30-60M |
| Lookalikes 1% | ~2.5M |
| Lookalikes 10% | ~25M |
| US broad | ~150M+ |

### Interest Targeting for Testing (Deividas)
- Test on interests sized 15-25 million people.
- Avoid broad when testing NEW creatives -- too much randomness.
- Broad only works well when you have enough data or a "home run" product.
- Once proven on interest, THEN test broad.

### Broad Targeting Philosophy (ACA/Seth)
- Target as broadly as possible. Narrow targeting drives UP costs and excludes potential buyers.
- Meta's algorithm doesn't need demographic constraints to find the right people.
- Use Advantage Plus Audience to let algorithm find the audience.
- Creative becomes the targeting -- your ad content determines who engages.
- Example: Bras brand targeted women 22-49, but women outside that range also buy bras.

## CPM BENCHMARKS

| Scenario | Expected CPM |
|---|---|
| Trained pixel, US, broad targeting | $10-20 |
| New pixel, US | $30-60+ |
| Smaller countries | Generally lower |
| Traffic/engagement campaigns | $2-3 (worthless traffic) |

**CPM is the compass** -- always compare CPMs between targeting types and follow the lowest CPM path that produces actual sales.

## CREATIVE FORMAT RULES FOR CAMPAIGNS

### Never Mix Creative Types in One Campaign
- Catalog + video = catalog takes all budget.
- Video + image = video takes all budget.
- The losing format is not necessarily bad -- it just can't compete for initial engagement.

### Budget Hierarchy by Creative Type (Most to Least Spend)
1. Catalog/Dynamic ads (always win)
2. Video post IDs (auto-play engagement)
3. Carousel post IDs (swipeable engagement)
4. Image post IDs (existing engagement helps)
5. Single image (hardest to get initial engagement)
6. Instant Experience / Collection ads (high engagement, low conversions)

### Post IDs
- Always use Existing Post IDs when running ads across multiple campaigns.
- Post IDs carry accumulated social proof (likes, comments, shares).
- Upload creatives to a dummy campaign first, then use Existing Post ID when deploying.
