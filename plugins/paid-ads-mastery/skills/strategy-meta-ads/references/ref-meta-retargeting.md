# Meta Retargeting Strategy

## NEVER EXCLUDE PAST CUSTOMERS (Konstantinos -- Strongest Position)

### The Core Argument
Excluding past customers = removing the BEST data from your optimization chain. A loyal repeat buyer provides better data for finding new similar customers than a first-time buyer.

### iOS Made This Worse
Less data flowing back + excluding customers = even less data for optimization. Every exclusion reduces the algorithm's ability to learn.

### Case Study Proof
Same creatives, same targeting -- campaigns WITH exclusions had dramatically worse results (2x+ higher CPP) vs. campaigns WITHOUT exclusions. The data quality loss from excluding known buyers degrades optimization significantly.

### Meta's Own Behavior Confirms This
Even in ASC campaigns, Meta ignores the "existing customer budget cap" setting and spends on existing customers anyway -- because Meta WANTS that data for optimization.

### The One Exception
If you sell a one-time product that can NEVER be repurchased (e.g., a one-time surgery):
- Exclude purchases.
- NEVER exclude traffic or social media engagers (they still provide valuable optimization data).

### The Principle
"What is the best data for finding a new client? A loyal customer who represents your ideal avatar."

## CATALOG / DYNAMIC RETARGETING

### What It Does
Shows people the exact products they viewed on your website. Carousel format automatically pulls from your product catalog.

### Setup Rules
- Run in a SEPARATE campaign from cold traffic.
- Catalog ads always win budget in mixed campaigns (they have highest engagement).
- Never mix catalog ads with video or image ads in the same campaign.

### Performance
- Naturally better metrics (lower CPP, higher ROAS) because audience is warm.
- Don't compare retargeting metrics to cold traffic metrics.
- Retargeting is NOT the main revenue driver -- cold traffic campaigns generate the bulk of results.

## SOCIAL MEDIA & LONG-TERM RETARGETING

### Audiences to Retarget
- Instagram engagers (365 days maximum)
- Facebook engagers (365 days maximum)
- Video viewers (various retention windows)
- Website visitors (180 days)
- All past purchasers (maximum retention)

### Window Strategy
- Use 180-365 day windows for maximum audience size.
- Longer windows = bigger pool = more data for algorithm.
- Don't exclude past purchasers from retargeting pools (see above).

## RETARGETING BUDGET REALITY

### Key Principles
1. Retargeting naturally has better metrics because the audience is warm.
2. Don't over-invest in retargeting -- it has diminishing returns.
3. Cold traffic campaigns are the primary revenue driver.
4. Retargeting adds incremental revenue on top of cold traffic results.

### Budget Allocation
- Retargeting typically gets 10-20% of total ad spend.
- More than 20% on retargeting = diminishing returns.
- Most of your budget should go to prospecting/cold traffic.

## FACEBOOK RETARGETING VIA SHOPPING ADS (Shri Method -- Cross-Platform)

### Campaign Structure
- Objective: Catalog Sales (not conversions or traffic).
- Campaign Type: Dynamic Product Ads.
- Choose: "Retarget ads to people who interacted with products on and off Facebook."

### Ad Set Structure: Time-Based Escalation
Create separate ad sets for different recency windows with escalating incentives:

| Window | Audience | Coupon |
|---|---|---|
| 3 days | Viewed/ATC, no purchase | 5% off |
| 5 days | Viewed/ATC, no purchase | 5% off |
| 7 days | Viewed/ATC, no purchase | 5% off |
| 14 days | Viewed/ATC, no purchase | 10% off |
| 30 days | Viewed/ATC, no purchase | 10% off |
| 60 days | Viewed/ATC, no purchase | 20-25% off |
| 90 days | Viewed/ATC, no purchase | 20-25% off |

### Budget Rules
- Start at $5/day if less than 1,000 daily visitors.
- Start at $10/day if 1,000+ daily visitors.
- General rule: Retargeting budget = 10% of daily visitor count.

### Kill Criteria
- Kill ad set after $50-$100 spend with zero sales.
- Count by SPEND, not by days.
- Test 3 different ad copies per ad set (keep coupon code consistent within window).

## RETARGETING CREATIVE STRATEGY

### Dynamic Ads
- Let the catalog do the work -- show viewed products automatically.
- Add a simple message: reminder, discount, urgency.

### Custom Retargeting Creatives
- Address specific objections (shipping time, return policy, price).
- Show social proof (reviews, customer photos).
- Escalate urgency as time since visit increases.
- Use different copy styles: "Left something behind?" / seasonal discounts / urgency-based.

### Creative Type Rule (Still Applies)
- Catalog retargeting = its own campaign.
- Video retargeting = its own campaign.
- Image retargeting = its own campaign.
- Never mix types in retargeting campaigns either.

## TIME-BASED ESCALATION DETAIL (Expanded)

### Why Escalating Incentives Work
- 3-day window: Customer still remembers the product, slight nudge needed (5% off)
- 7-day window: Interest is cooling, needs stronger reminder + small incentive
- 14-day window: Customer likely explored alternatives, need to re-engage with meaningful offer (10% off)
- 30-day window: Customer may have purchased elsewhere or forgotten; stronger incentive required
- 60-90 day window: Long-shot recovery; only worth it with significant discount (20-25% off)
- Each tier targets a different psychology: from "reminder" to "win-back"

### Coupon Code Best Practices
- Use unique codes per window (e.g., SAVE5, SAVE10, SAVE25) for tracking
- Set expiration dates on codes matching the retargeting window
- Display the code prominently in the ad creative (overlay text on image/video)
- Include the discount in the headline: "Still thinking about it? Here's 10% off"

### Ad Copy Templates by Window

**3-5 Day Window (Gentle Reminder):**
- "Still browsing? Your [product] is waiting"
- "You left something behind -- here's 5% off to make it easy"
- Focus: product reminder, low-pressure, small incentive

**7-14 Day Window (Re-Engagement):**
- "Back by popular demand -- and 10% off just for you"
- "Hundreds of customers love [product]. Ready to join them?"
- Focus: social proof + moderate incentive + urgency

**30-60 Day Window (Win-Back):**
- "We miss you -- enjoy 20% off your first order"
- "It's been a while. Here's our best offer yet."
- Focus: strong incentive + emotional reconnection + urgency/scarcity

## AUDIENCE SPECIFICATION DETAIL

### Custom Audience Windows for Retargeting
| Audience Source | Max Window | Recommended Window | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Website visitors (all pages) | 180 days | 180 days | Cast wide net |
| Product page viewers | 180 days | 30-60 days | Higher intent |
| Add-to-cart, no purchase | 180 days | 14-30 days | Highest intent |
| Instagram engagers | 365 days | 365 days | Broad but valuable |
| Facebook page engagers | 365 days | 365 days | Broad but valuable |
| Video viewers (25%+) | 365 days | 180 days | Warm but not hot |
| Video viewers (75%+) | 365 days | 90 days | Very warm |
| Past purchasers | Unlimited | All time | Never exclude from optimization |

### Layering Retargeting Audiences
- Combine engagement sources for larger retargeting pools
- Example: Instagram engagers + Facebook engagers + Video viewers 50%+ = one large warm audience
- Larger pools give the algorithm more data to optimize against
- Keep catalog retargeting separate (different audience = product page viewers / ATC)

## RETARGETING CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE TEMPLATE

### Campaign 1: Catalog Retargeting (DPA)
- Objective: Catalog Sales
- Ad set 1: Viewed/ATC 3 days (5% off)
- Ad set 2: Viewed/ATC 7 days (5% off)
- Ad set 3: Viewed/ATC 14 days (10% off)
- Ad set 4: Viewed/ATC 30 days (10% off)
- Budget: $5-10/day per ad set (scale based on traffic volume)

### Campaign 2: Social Retargeting (Video/Image)
- Objective: Conversions (Purchase)
- Ad set 1: All social engagers 365 days
- Creatives: Testimonials, objection handling, social proof
- Budget: $10-20/day total
- Purpose: Re-engage people who engaged with content but never visited site

### Campaign 3: Website Retargeting (Non-Catalog)
- Objective: Conversions (Purchase)
- Ad set 1: Website visitors 30 days (exclude purchasers if one-time product)
- Creatives: Brand story, customer results, urgency-based offers
- Budget: $10-20/day total
