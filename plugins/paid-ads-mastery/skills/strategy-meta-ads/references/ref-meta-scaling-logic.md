# Meta Scaling Logic

## THE BELL THEORY (Konstantinos)

### How Every Ad Works
Every ad follows a bell curve: performance rises, hits a soft spot (peak), then declines as budget increases.

### The Soft Spot
- The soft spot = maximum ROAS for that ad at a given budget level.
- Increasing budget past the soft spot = declining ROAS.
- You cannot infinitely increase budget on one campaign -- each bell has a ceiling.

### Scaling Hack: Duplicate at Higher Budget
- Duplicate the campaign at a higher starting budget.
- The new bell curve has a HIGHER soft spot.
- Example: Campaign peaked at $40/day. Duplicate at $40 -> new soft spot may be at $80/day.
- Multiple campaigns at their respective soft spots = higher total spend with maintained ROAS.

## THE DAILY LOOP THEORY (Konstantinos -- Critical Scaling Concept)

### Core Insight
If Facebook does not want you to scale, adding more campaigns/budget will NOT help -- it redistributes the SAME number of sales across more campaigns.

### Discovery
Netherlands account: 1 campaign = 8 sales. 2 campaigns = 4+4 sales (same total, double the spend). With many campaigns, cannibalization is harder to spot but still occurs.

### How to Detect
Compare total account-level sales/revenue across periods with different campaign counts. If total stays flat while campaigns multiply, you are in a daily loop.

### 5 Ways to Break the Daily Loop
1. **New creatives with different ANGLES** (different hot pockets).
2. **Different products or offers.**
3. **Testing new countries.**
4. **Promotions to inflate overall account data** (see Attack Periods below).
5. **Improving AOV, conversion rate, or offer strength.**

Simply duplicating the same creatives in more campaigns = spending more for the same results.

## HOT POCKETS AS SCALING MECHANISM

### Hot Pockets Form at the Ad Level
- Originally believed to form at the ad set level (from CBO experience).
- After ASC campaigns arrived (one ad set, many ads), realized hot pockets form at the AD level.
- Different ads within the same ad set produce different hot pockets.

### Why Consolidation Caps Scaling (Konstantinos)
- Consolidation = one campaign = one hot pocket = limited reach.
- **Proof:** Single broad ad set spent $1.7M over 6 months, only reached ~20M unique people out of 150M potential, with 8.5 frequency.
- **Case study:** Account stuck at $7K/day with one campaign -> multiple campaigns -> $12-14K/day ($370K/month at 1.72 ROAS).

### Creating Different Hot Pockets for Scale
Each angle creates a different hot pocket that can sustain its own campaign and budget:
- **Product example (car sales):** Lifestyle angle (young men), aesthetics (women), specs (enthusiasts), practicality (older people).
- **Product example (lunch boxes):** Mom angle, teacher angle, office worker angle, health-conscious angle.

### Consolidation as End Goal (Deividas -- Opposing View)
- Ultimate goal: few campaigns, most spend on top 10% of ads.
- Test in ABO -> push winners to CBO/Advantage Shopping -> maintain by continuously testing + refreshing creatives.
- Don't optimize individual ads in consolidated CBO -- look at campaign/ad set level only.

## THE 20% SCALING RULE

### Konstantinos's Position
- Only matters at HIGHER budgets after passing the learning phase.
- At small budgets ($10-$50/day), 20% increase is meaningless.
- At $500+/day, 20% increases ($500 -> $600) are reasonable.
- Learning phase is largely Meta's gamification tool.
- **When to use:** Conservative scaling of proven campaigns at higher budgets.
- **When to ignore:** Small budgets, new campaigns, aggressive scaling.

### Deividas's Position
- Budget increase up to 70% = no learning phase reset (updated from old 20% rule).
- Assets that exit learning phase perform better long-term.
- Aim for 50% of ad spend OUTSIDE learning phase.
- Advantage Shopping: uploading new creatives does NOT reset learning phase.

### The Learning Phase (Context)
- Meta says: Need 50 optimization events per ad set within 7 days to exit.
- Post-iOS: Only 7-day click, 1-day view window (harder to accumulate).
- For high-priced products ($500+ CPA): practically impossible to exit -- just accept it.
- Focus on ROAS and CPP, not learning phase status.

## SCALING STRATEGIES TOOLBOX (Deividas)

### 7 Methods Available
1. **Vertical scaling:** 20-70% budget increase per 24-48 hours. Safe: 20% every 48hr. Aggressive: 70% every 24hr. Cap: ~$1K-$3K/day per ad set before diminishing returns.
2. **Horizontal/Nuke scaling:** Duplicate winning creative into 25-100 ad sets at $25-50 each; kill non-performers fast.
3. **Manual bid scaling:** Test cost caps, bid caps, ROAS value optimization. Start at average CPA, increase by 20% per ad set.
4. **Surge scaling:** One ad set, very high budget (opposite of nuke -- shotgun approach).
5. **Sniper scaling:** Very low manual bid = only spend ~40-50% of budget, captures cheapest audience pockets.
6. **CBO consolidation:** Push all winners into CBO campaigns for automated scaling.
7. **Advantage Shopping:** Evergreen scaling campaign -- continuously add winning creatives.

### When to Scale (Deividas)
- Above-average ROAS for 3-5 consecutive days (NOT just one good day).
- Never judge by single day.
- Only look at ROAS/CPA -- never scale based on CTR improvements alone.

### Downscaling Protocol (Deividas)
- When killing an ad set in CBO: reduce CBO budget by the amount that ad set was spending.
- When performance drops: test LESS, not more. Focus on consolidating what works.

## ATTACK PERIODS / PROMOTIONS FOR SCALING (Konstantinos)

### Strategy
Run promotions during bad periods to inflate data within the platform. The inflated results from promo period CARRY OVER to improve post-promo performance.

### Why It Works
Promotions force more data into the system, which can break the daily loop and improve algorithmic optimization for subsequent non-promo periods.

## BLACK FRIDAY PLAYBOOK (Konstantinos)

### The 4-Step Strategy
1. **Build evergreen campaigns BEFORE Black Friday** (weeks/months in advance).
2. **Run promo campaigns ALONGSIDE evergreen campaigns** during Black Friday.
3. **Evergreen campaigns get inflated** by Black Friday data spillover -- they ride the wave of increased purchase behavior across the entire account.
4. **After closing promo campaigns**, evergreen campaigns perform better due to accumulated data.

### The Critical Mistake to Avoid
Running ONLY promo campaigns during Black Friday, then having nothing after. When promo campaigns close, there are no evergreen campaigns to benefit from the data influx.

### Seasonal Transition in Working Campaigns
- Acceptable to swap seasonal ads within existing campaigns (e.g., swap Black Friday ads for Christmas ads) -- same hot pocket, different offer.
- This is one of two exceptions to the "don't touch working campaigns" rule.

## MULTI-COUNTRY EXPANSION

### Rules
- Test one country at a time to isolate results.
- Smaller countries may require different approaches (broader works faster).
- Canada often tested as expansion from US campaigns.
- Each country may need its own creative testing and optimization cycle.

## WHEN YOU CANNOT SCALE

If lowering budget = good ROAS and increasing budget = bad ROAS, the problem is NOT your Facebook strategy.

### Actual Reasons You Cannot Scale
- Weak offer
- Bad creatives
- Low AOV
- Poor conversion rate
- Too much competition
- Product-market limitations

**"Facebook is just an amplifier of reality."** If the product cannot scale in reality, Facebook cannot force it. Fix business fundamentals first, then try scaling again.
