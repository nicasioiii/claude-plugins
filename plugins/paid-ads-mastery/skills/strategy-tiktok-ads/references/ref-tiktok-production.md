# TikTok Production & Testing

## PRODUCTION BEST PRACTICES

### Sweet Spot Ad Length: 20-35 Seconds
- Long enough to deliver a complete message with hook, key message, and CTA.
- Short enough that 18-25% of viewers reach the end (completion rate benchmark).
- Under 15 seconds risks being too shallow for conversion.
- Over 45 seconds risks significant drop-off.

### Safe Zone Rules
- **Top 20%** of the screen: TikTok username, follow button, caption area.
- **Bottom 20%** of the screen: navigation bar, share/like/comment buttons.
- **Keep ALL text, logos, CTAs, and key elements in the CENTER 60%.**
- Design for the center frame -- everything important must live there.

### Minimum Creative Volume
- **5-7 unique creatives per conversion campaign.**
- Refresh weekly.
- The algorithm needs variety to test and optimize distribution.
- Fewer than 5 creatives starves the algorithm and limits performance.

### Show One Product Maximum
- One product per ad (unless they form a bundle, max 3 in a bundle).
- Multiple products in one ad dilutes focus and confuses the viewer.

### Price Disclosure
- **Disclose price or promotion early in the ad.**
- TikTok audiences are price-sensitive.
- Hiding the price creates distrust and wastes ad spend on viewers who won't convert.

### Casting & Diversity
- Showcase diversity in talent selection.
- Match talent demographics to target audience.
- Multiple creators for the same product = multiple audience entry points.

## THREE-WEEK CREATIVE ITERATION LOOP

### Week 1: Launch and Test
- Create 3-5 creatives per ad group with varied hooks.
- Each creative tests a different approach (hook type, structure, angle).
- Launch at test budget ($50 per ad set, auto placement).
- Measure: 3-second hold rate, CTR, completion rate.

### Week 2: Double Down on Winners
- Identify top 1-2 performers from Week 1.
- Increase budget on winners.
- Kill underperformers (anything below benchmark thresholds).
- Begin planning variations of winners.

### Week 3: Remix and Refresh
- Take winning creatives and create variations:
  - Change the sound/music.
  - Rewrite captions.
  - Swap the first 3 seconds (new hook on same body).
- Launch remixed versions alongside originals.
- The originals may be fatiguing by now -- remixes extend their life.

### Repeat the Cycle
This is a continuous loop. Never stop the cycle. TikTok creative fatigues faster than any other platform.

## SIX-DAY TESTING ACTION PLAN (Detailed)

### Day 1: Research & Script
- Pull audience insights from TikTok Analytics or TikTok Creative Center.
- List 3 subcultures within your target audience (e.g., gym TikTok, clean beauty TikTok, budget cooking TikTok).
- Write 3 scripts targeting different subcultures or pain points.
- Each script should follow one of the 3 ad structures (talent-forward, problem/product-forward, no visible talent).

### Day 2: Produce Variants
- Spin 5 variants using Symphony Generate, CapCut, or manual editing.
- Tag each variant with which creative principle it emphasizes (e.g., "trend-based," "stimulation-heavy," "ASMR/sound-focused").
- This tagging enables you to identify which principles work best for your audience.

### Day 3: Launch
- Set up test campaign with $50 per ad set, auto placement.
- Each ad set gets one creative variant.
- Use conversion objective (purchase or lead, never awareness).
- Let the campaign run without touching it.

### Day 4: First Read
- Check 3-second hold rate: anything below 70% is underperforming.
- Check CTR: anything below 2% for e-commerce is underperforming.
- Kill clear underperformers (below both benchmarks after meaningful spend).
- Do NOT kill ads that haven't spent enough yet.

### Day 5: Remix Winners
- Take the best 1-2 performers.
- Create 2 remix variations of each:
  - Variation A: Change the hook only (different opening 3 seconds, same body + CTA).
  - Variation B: Change the sound/music only (same visuals, different audio).
- Launch remixes alongside originals.

### Day 6: Scale and Document
- Increase budget on top performers (2-3x test budget).
- Discard remaining losers.
- Document learnings in a creative tracker:
  - Which principle worked best?
  - Which ad structure won?
  - Which subculture resonated?
  - What hook style outperformed?
- Use these learnings to inform Week 2 of the iteration loop.

## TIKTOK NATIVE TOOLS (Detailed)

### Symphony Generate
- **What it does:** Paste your product URL, and Symphony generates 5 ready-to-run video ads automatically.
- **How to use:** Submit product URL -> receive 5 videos in minutes -> review and launch best ones.
- **Best for:** Initial creative volume when starting fresh. Quick testing variants. Supplementing manual production.
- **Limitation:** Quality varies. Best used as starting material that you can remix, not as final polished creative.

### Symphony Remix
- **What it does:** Takes an existing ad and creates variations (new hooks, new sounds, new captions).
- **How to use:** Upload winning creative -> Symphony generates remixed versions -> test alongside original.
- **Best for:** Week 3 of the iteration loop. Extending the life of fatiguing winners.

### Avatar Library
- **What it does:** AI avatars lip-sync your scripts in any language.
- **How to use:** Write script -> select avatar matching your target demographic -> generate in target language.
- **Languages:** 30+ languages supported.
- **Best for:** Multi-market expansion without hiring local talent. Testing scripts before investing in real creator production.
- **Limitation:** AI avatars are recognizable as non-human by sophisticated audiences. Best for initial testing, not long-term scaling.

### Daily Video Generations
- **What it does:** Symphony analyzes yesterday's winning ads across your account and proposes fresh creative spins.
- **How to use:** Review daily suggestions -> select promising ones -> launch as new test variants.
- **Best for:** Maintaining creative freshness with minimal manual effort. Catching trends early.

## CREATIVE REFRESH TRIGGERS

### When to Refresh (Hard Rules)
| Signal | Threshold | Action |
|---|---|---|
| CPA rising | 20% above week-one average | Rotate in 3-5 new creatives |
| CTR dropping | 30% from peak performance | Rotate in 3-5 new creatives |
| Frequency climbing | Above 3x per week | Expand audience OR refresh creative |
| Completion rate dropping | Below 10% | The content isn't holding -- new concept needed |

### Refresh Methods (From Fastest to Most Effort)
1. **Sound swap:** Same visual, different music/voiceover (minutes).
2. **Hook swap:** New opening 3 seconds, same body (30 minutes).
3. **Caption rewrite:** Same video, new on-screen text (30 minutes).
4. **Creator swap:** Same script, different person (days).
5. **Full new creative:** New concept from scratch (days to week).

### The Refresh Mindset
On TikTok, creative IS the campaign strategy. Without fresh creative, even perfect targeting and bidding will decline. Budget for continuous creative production -- it is not optional.

## TESTING BUDGET GUIDELINES

| Monthly Ad Spend | Creative Testing Budget | New Creatives/Week |
|---|---|---|
| <$5K | 30-40% of spend | 3-5 |
| $5K-$25K | 25-35% of spend | 5-10 |
| $25K-$100K | 20-30% of spend | 10-20 |
| $100K+ | 15-25% of spend | 20+ |

TikTok requires higher creative volume and testing investment than Meta or Google because of faster fatigue cycles.
