# Creative Volume, Pipeline & Iteration Framework

## The Two-Level Testing Framework

### Level 1: Big Swings (Concept Tests)
- Entirely new creative directions: new angles, formats, audiences, messaging
- Examples: UGC testimonial vs. product demo vs. lifestyle montage vs. meme-style
- Goal: discover new winning CONCEPTS that can spawn dozens of iterations
- Expected success rate: 10-20% (most concepts fail; this is normal)
- Frequency: continuous; never stop concept testing

### Level 2: Variations (Iteration Tests)
- Modifications to proven winners: new hook, different headline, color change, new UGC creator, format swap
- Goal: extend lifespan and increase scale of existing winners
- Expected success rate: 30-50% (much higher than concept tests)
- Frequency: start iterating IMMEDIATELY when a winner emerges; do not wait for fatigue

### Budget Split Between Levels

| Account Performance | Level 1 (Concepts) | Level 2 (Iterations) |
|---|---|---|
| **Performance is UP** | 50% | 50% |
| **Performance is STABLE** | 40% | 60% |
| **Performance is DOWN** | 20% | 80% |

When performance drops, the instinct is to test MORE new concepts. This is wrong. Iterations have higher success rates and stabilize accounts faster.

## Creative Volume by Monthly Ad Spend

### Minimum Creative Output Targets

| Monthly Ad Spend | New Concepts/Month | Iterations/Month | Testing Budget (% of spend) |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0-$1K | 4-6 | 4-6 | 25-30% |
| $1K-$5K | 8-12 | 8-12 | 20-25% |
| $5K-$25K | 15-25 | 15-25 | 20% |
| $25K-$100K | 25-40 | 30-50 | 20% |
| $100K-$500K | 40-60 | 50-80 | 15-20% |
| $500K-$1M | 60-80 | 80-120 | 15% |
| $1M+ | 80-120+ | 120-180+ | 10-15% |

### The 20% Testing Minimum
- Minimum 20% of total ad spend should fund creative testing
- This is non-negotiable for growth accounts
- Below 20%, you are running on borrowed time (existing winners will fatigue)

## Iteration Quantity Formula

At $1K/day spend with $50 CPA:
- 4 ad sets running simultaneously in testing
- ~8 creative batches per week (4 iterations + 4 new concepts)
- Each batch = 4 creatives
- Total: ~32 creatives per week entering testing

### When to Stop Iterating a Specific Winner
- Stop when new iteration batches consistently underperform below account average ROAS
- This is the diminishing returns signal
- Reallocate budget to iterating a different winner or testing new concepts

## Cross-Testing Iteration Method

A diagnostic technique that reveals WHETHER the hook or the body is driving performance:

1. Take the winning hook from Creative A
2. Apply it to Creative B (different body/script)
3. If the combined creative works: the HOOK was the key variable
4. If it fails: the BODY CONTENT of Creative A was the true winner

### Additional Cross-Testing Combinations
- Same script, different UGC creator (tests creator impact)
- Video concept converted to static (tests format impact)
- Video concept converted to VSL (tests length/depth impact)
- Same concept, different platform format (Meta vs. TikTok style)
- Male + female UGC in same video (tests audience broadening)

## The 5 Stages of Iterating a Concept

When you find a winning concept, iterate through these stages sequentially:

### Stage 1: Hook Iterations
- Produce 3-5 different hooks for the same body/script
- Change visual hook, text hook, or both
- This is the highest-impact iteration

### Stage 2: Copy/Headline Iterations
- Keep winning hook and body, change the ad copy
- Test different headlines below the creative
- Revive dead creatives by changing ONLY the headline (quarterly practice)

### Stage 3: On-Ramp Variations
- Change the opening story/setup before the product introduction
- Same product pitch, different emotional entry point

### Stage 4: Sales Sequence Variations
- Modify the order of USPs, social proof, and objection handling
- Test different CTA placements (middle vs. end)

### Stage 5: Rhetorical Reframing
- Same product, same benefits, completely different messaging angle
- Example: functional benefit angle vs. emotional benefit angle vs. social proof angle
- This borders on a new concept but stays within the proven framework

## Creative Testing Tracker Pipeline

### Document Structure (Spreadsheet or Project Tool)

**Columns:**
1. Creative ID (naming convention)
2. Concept Category (new concept / iteration of [parent ID])
3. Format (video / static / carousel / UGC)
4. Hypothesis (what you expect and why)
5. Variable Being Tested
6. Launch Date
7. Kill/Scale Date
8. Spend
9. Conversions
10. ROAS
11. CPA
12. Hook Rate (video) / CTR (static)
13. Result (Winner / Loser / Inconclusive)
14. Next Action (Scale / Iterate / Archive)
15. Notes

### Pipeline Stages (Project Management)
```
Ideas --> To Do --> In Production --> Ready to Launch --> Launched --> Results In --> Archived
```

### Tagging System
Every creative should be tagged with:
- **Concept family** (parent concept it belongs to)
- **Iteration number** (v1, v2, v3...)
- **Variable type** (hook / headline / body / format / creator)
- **Target audience** (if audience-specific)

## Naming Convention System

### Standard Format
```
[YYYY-MM-DD]_[CONCEPT]_[FORMAT]_[VARIATION]_[CREATOR/SOURCE]
```

### Examples
| Name | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `2026-03-15_PainPoint_Video_HookA_SarahUGC` | Pain point concept, video format, hook variation A, creator Sarah |
| `2026-03-15_SocialProof_Static_HeadlineV2_InHouse` | Social proof concept, static, headline version 2, made in-house |
| `2026-03-20_ProductDemo_Carousel_ColorBlue_AI` | Product demo concept, carousel, blue color test, AI-generated |
| `2026-03-22_PainPoint_Video_HookB_SarahUGC` | Iteration of original, new hook, same creator |

### Concept Category Codes (Customize Per Account)
- `PP` = Pain Point
- `SP` = Social Proof
- `PD` = Product Demo
- `UGC` = User Generated Content
- `LS` = Lifestyle
- `ED` = Educational
- `TP` = Testimonial/Proof
- `OF` = Offer/Promotion
- `EM` = Emotional Benefit
- `FN` = Functional Benefit

## Weekly Reporting System

### Monday: Winner Report (Extensive)
- Top 3-5 performing creatives by ROAS
- Top 3-5 performing creatives by spend volume
- Creative fatigue alerts (declining performance over 7 days)
- New winners identified this week
- Iteration opportunities from this week's data
- Concepts that failed (with hypothesis on WHY)

### Mid-Week: Slack/Channel Updates
- Flag potential winners as they emerge (do not wait for Monday)
- Flag urgent kills (creatives burning budget with no conversions)
- Share any surprising results or learnings

## Testing Calendar Template

### Monthly Testing Rhythm

**Week 1:** Launch new concept batch (4-6 concepts) + iterate last month's winners
**Week 2:** Kill underperformers from Week 1, iterate emerging winners, launch iteration batch
**Week 3:** Launch next concept batch + continue iterating, cross-test winning hooks
**Week 4:** Full analysis, prepare next month's concept brief, update testing tracker

### Quarterly Cadence
- **Every quarter:** Test ad copy separately (find one evergreen winner, use across all creatives)
- **Every quarter:** Revive dead creatives by changing ONLY the headline
- **Every quarter:** Review concept families -- which themes consistently win? Double down.

## Meta A/B Testing Tools

### Native Meta Tools Available
- A/B testing (split test campaigns)
- Sales testing
- Meta Experiments
- Brand Survey test (requires Meta rep + higher budget)

### Practitioner Preference
Most experienced media buyers prefer manual side-by-side testing over Meta's built-in A/B tools. Reason: Meta's native testing has simplified to the point of being unreliable for creative-level decisions. Running campaigns side-by-side with identical settings gives cleaner data.

### The 15+ Experiments Rule
Industry data shows: running 15+ creative experiments per year correlates with a 30% improvement in overall ad performance. This is not optional for growth accounts.
