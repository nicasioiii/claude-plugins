# /callreview Command

## Purpose

Analyze a sales call (from transcript, recording notes, or user description) and diagnose which phase broke down, what went well, and what to improve.

## Routing

This command routes across ALL 12 skills based on diagnosis. It is the highest-value command because it connects theory to real performance.

## Workflow

### Step 1: Gather Information

Ask the user for:
- Call transcript, recording link, or detailed description
- What happened (their summary of the call)
- The outcome (closed, objection, ghosted, follow-up needed, etc.)
- What they THINK went wrong (their self-diagnosis)

### Step 2: Diagnose the Phase

Map the breakdown to the sales call flow:

| Phase | Skill | Signs of Breakdown |
|---|---|---|
| **Pre-call** | `sales-mindset-prep` | Low energy, uncertainty, chasing energy, no conviction |
| **Opening/Rapport** | `tonality-delivery`, `presence-power` | Prospect skeptical from start, no rapport built, wrong tone |
| **Discovery** | `discovery-qualification` | Shallow questions, prospect not engaged, gap not built, beliefs not checked |
| **Empathy/Connection** | `tactical-empathy` | Prospect guarded, no emotional connection, "you're right" instead of "that's right" |
| **Pitch/Transition** | `pitch-presentation` | Prospect confused, not tied to their words, features over outcomes |
| **Close Attempt** | `closing-techniques` | Price drop was awkward, no trial close, no temperature check |
| **Objection Handling** | `objection-handling` | Smokescreen not identified, pacing skipped, went alpha vs. alpha |
| **Negotiation** | `negotiation-tactics` | Gave away too much, did not identify negotiator type, no Ackerman structure |
| **Follow-Up** | `pipeline-followup` | No follow-up plan, ghosted, no re-offer designed |
| **Setting/Booking** | `appointment-setting` | No-show, not qualified, not confirmed |

### Step 3: Identify Root Cause

**Critical insight from Gordon:** 90% of the time someone asks about objection handling, it is actually an information-gathering, transition, or pitch issue -- not an objection handling issue. Always look at the front end first.

**Diagnostic hierarchy:**
1. Was discovery deep enough? (Most common root cause)
2. Was the Belief Ladder checked? Which beliefs were missing?
3. Was tactical empathy established before assertion?
4. Was tonality appropriate for each phase?
5. Was the transition to pitch earned (permission-based)?
6. Was the close attempt well-timed (temperature check first)?

### Step 4: Prescribe Improvements

For each issue identified:
1. Name the specific skill and framework that applies
2. Explain what SHOULD have happened at that moment
3. Provide the exact language or technique to use next time
4. Cross-reference the relevant skill for deeper study

### Step 5: Highlight What Went Well

Always identify at least one thing the user did effectively. Reinforce good habits alongside corrections.

## Diagnosis Patterns

### "Prospect Said 'I Need to Think About It'"

**Check in order:**
1. Discovery depth -- was the gap built? (`discovery-qualification`)
2. Belief Ladder -- which beliefs were missing? (`sales-psychology`)
3. Tactical empathy -- was "that's right" achieved? (`tactical-empathy`)
4. Temperature check -- was uncertainty caught before the close? (`closing-techniques`)
5. Pacing -- was the first objection paced correctly? (`objection-handling`)

### "Prospect Went Dark After the Call"

**Check in order:**
1. Was a follow-up plan established on the call? (`pipeline-followup`)
2. Was commitment tested with Rule of Three? (`tactical-empathy`)
3. Was there a genuine close attempt or did the call fizzle? (`closing-techniques`)
4. Use "Have you given up?" as reactivation (`tactical-empathy`)

### "I Couldn't Get Past the Price Objection"

**Check in order:**
1. Was money isolated vs. uncertainty? (`objection-handling`)
2. Was the double tie-down completed before discussing price? (`objection-handling`)
3. Were payment plans held in reserve? (`closing-techniques`)
4. Was the gap built large enough in discovery? (`discovery-qualification`)
5. Was loss aversion leveraged? (`sales-psychology`)

### "The Call Felt Good But They Didn't Buy"

**Check in order:**
1. Were they in the Buying Pocket? (`sales-psychology`)
2. Was there a clear close attempt with investment drop + silence? (`closing-techniques`)
3. Did you get "that's right" or "you're right"? (`tactical-empathy`)
4. Were you in Prize Frame or seeking approval? (`sales-mindset-prep`)
