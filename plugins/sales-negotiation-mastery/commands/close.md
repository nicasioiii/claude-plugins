# /close Command

## Purpose

Help the user plan, execute, or debrief a closing strategy for a specific deal.

## Routing

**Primary skill:** `closing-techniques`
**Supporting skills:**
- `negotiation-tactics` (if pricing/terms are being negotiated)
- `objection-handling` (if objections arise during close)
- `tactical-empathy` (Rule of Three, "that's right," labeling)
- `tonality-delivery` (voice control during investment drop and silence)

## Workflow

1. **Assess the situation.** Where are they in the process?
   - Did they complete thorough discovery?
   - Was the pitch delivered and well-received?
   - Has a temperature check been done? What was the result?
   - Is this a first-call or multi-call close?

2. **Select the closing approach.**
   - **Temperature check system** (Gordon) -- if temp check not yet done
   - **Commitment close** (Alexander) -- get verbal commitment before price
   - **Trial close patterns** (Gordon) -- Sharp Angle, Feel Good, Keep It In Context
   - **Investment drop + silence** -- the moment of truth
   - **HALL method** (Serhant) -- for pre-negotiation composure
   - **Rule of Three** (Voss) -- to test if commitment is real
   - **Push/Pull/Persist** (Serhant) -- for urgency creation

3. **Prepare for objections.** Cross-reference `objection-handling` for common post-price objections. Have the pacing method ready.

4. **Script the close.** Provide exact language for:
   - The temperature check
   - The commitment close (pre-price)
   - The investment drop and silence protocol
   - Post-price trial close
   - Next steps / onboarding script

5. **Plan for deal recovery.** If the close stalls:
   - Five Serhant techniques (Pull, Facts, What Do You Want to Do, Emotions, Reality)
   - Red-button recovery (bring parties together)
   - Five-phase "No" progression (Voss) for boundary setting

6. **Plan the follow-up.** If deal does not close on this call, route to `pipeline-followup` for re-offer design and follow-up sequence.

## Key Decision Points

- If temp check is below 8: DO NOT proceed to price. Handle concerns first.
- If prospect agrees but tonality is uncertain: use Rule of Three to verify.
- If objections arise at close: route to `objection-handling`, then return.
- If price becomes a negotiation: route to `negotiation-tactics`.
- If deal is not closing today: route to `pipeline-followup` for strategic follow-up.
