# /negotiate Command

## Purpose

Help the user prepare for, execute, or debrief a price negotiation or complex deal structuring conversation.

## Routing

**Primary skill:** `negotiation-tactics`
**Supporting skills:**
- `tactical-empathy` (calibrated questions, labeling, mirroring, accusation audits)
- `presence-power` (in-person dynamics, setting control, body language)
- `sales-psychology` (loss aversion, prospect theory, framing effect)
- `sales-mindset-prep` (pre-negotiation mental preparation)

## Workflow

### For Preparation (Before Negotiation)

1. **Gather context.** What is being negotiated? Price, terms, scope? Who is the counterpart? What is the setting?

2. **Identify the negotiator type.** Analyst, Accommodator, or Assertive? Adapt primary tools:
   - Analyst = Labels (not questions)
   - Accommodator = Implementation "how" questions
   - Assertive = Mirrors and summaries

3. **Set the three numbers** (Serhant). First offer, best target, walkaway price. Define before entering the room.

4. **Run opposition research** (CODO Step 2). Research the other party, their representative, and all supporting data. Comp through THEIR eyes, not just yours.

5. **Map the star players** (CODO Step 1). Who is involved beyond the obvious? Who are the deal killers? Who influences the decision maker?

6. **Design the strategy.**
   - **Ackerman model** for structured price bargaining (65% -> 85% -> 95% -> 100% + non-monetary)
   - **Interests vs. Positions** for creative deal structuring
   - **CODO Five Steps** for complex multi-party deals
   - **Fairness framing** (deploy early: "I want you to feel treated fairly at all times")

7. **Prepare tactical empathy tools.**
   - Accusation audit (list everything they might think negatively about you)
   - Calibrated questions (what/how questions ready)
   - Non-monetary items list (things valuable to you, easy for them)
   - Silence protocols (when to deploy strategic silence)

8. **Plan for Black Swans.** What hidden information could change the game? What questions will help discover it? What leverage types exist (positive, negative, normative)?

9. **Work the setting** (CODO Step 5). Where should the negotiation happen? Who controls the environment?

### For Debriefing (After Negotiation)

1. **What worked?** Which techniques landed? What moved the counterpart?
2. **What did not work?** Where did you lose ground? What signals did you miss?
3. **Type assessment.** Were you right about their type? What would you change?
4. **Black Swans discovered?** What hidden information surfaced? How does it change next steps?
5. **Next moves.** What follow-up actions are needed? What concessions are still available?

## Key Decision Points

- If the "negotiation" is really an **objection** (fear, uncertainty, trust), route to `objection-handling`
- If in-person dynamics are critical, load `presence-power` for setting control and body language
- If pre-negotiation anxiety is the issue, load `sales-mindset-prep` for mental preparation
- If the negotiation stalled and needs follow-up, route to `pipeline-followup`
- If dealing with a **committee or multiple stakeholders**, use calibrated questions to surface deal killers and the team behind the table (Voss)
