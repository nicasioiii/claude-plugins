# Appointment Setting

The complete system for the setter role -- the person who qualifies prospects and books them onto a closer's calendar. Covers DM setting, phone setting, triage calls, show-rate optimization, and the path from setter to closer.

**Scope:** This skill covers the SETTING function (booking qualified meetings from inbound or warm leads). It does NOT cover cold outbound prospecting or list building -- that is cold-outbound-mastery. The distinction: setting = working inbound leads or warm lists to book qualified calls. Prospecting = finding and generating leads from scratch.

---

## Core Philosophy: Sell the Conversation, Not the Product

> **Gordon:** The setter's ONLY job is to sell the prospect on the CONVERSATION with a closer -- NOT on the product.

You are the restaurant door greeter. Your job is to make a great first impression, qualify that the person belongs here, and seat them at the right table. You are NOT the chef, the sommelier, or the waiter. Get them in the door and hand them off.

## Section 1: The Three Qualifications

**Source:** Alexander. Every prospect must satisfy these before booking on a closer's calendar.

| Qualification | What to Check | How to Check |
|--------------|--------------|--------------|
| **Money** | Can they afford the offer? | "How much do you have set aside to invest so you can [desired outcome]?" |
| **Commitment** | Do they actually want to do this? | "Is this something you are serious about, or just exploring?" |
| **Show-up** | Will they actually attend the call? | "Nothing is going to come up that prevents you from showing up to that call?" |

If a prospect fails any of these three, either:
1. Down-sell to a lower-ticket offer (if money fails)
2. Build more urgency/pain (if commitment fails)
3. Lock down with certainty language (if show-up fails)

## Section 2: DM Setting Framework (B2C)

**Source:** Alexander. The complete qualify-and-book flow for text-based messaging (Instagram, Twitter, Facebook DMs).

### The Flow

**Step 1: Acknowledge + Initial Qualify**
```
"Hey! Thanks for reaching out. Just have a few quick questions
to see if we can help. You came from the [story/post/ad] --
looks like you want to learn [topic]. Do you work right now?
What's the main reason you're looking to do this?"
```

**Step 2: Current Situation**
Get their job, income level, and pain points through natural conversation.

**Step 3: Goal Quantification**
```
"What do you need to be making to [leave the 9-5 / hit your goal]?"
```
Get a specific number. Vague goals = vague prospects.

**Step 4: Time Gap**
```
"How long have you been wanting to [goal]? What have you tried?"
```

**Step 5: Urgency**
```
"If this has been the goal for [X time], what makes it important NOW?"
```

**Step 6: Financial Qualification**
```
"If this program was exactly what you needed, do you have $[range]
available to invest?"
```

**Step 7: Book**
```
"Perfect. Let's get you on a longer Zoom call with one of our
coaches for a deeper dive. Here's the link. Choose a time you
are 100% good for."
```

**Step 8: Confirm**
```
"Drop me a confirmation whenever you book and what time."
```

### Key Rule
Put this into NORMAL conversational language. Do not sound scripted. You are chatting with a friend, not reading from a telemarketer script.

## Section 3: Phone Setting Framework

**Source:** Alexander (updated May 2025).

### Opening
```
"Hey, is this [Name]?"

"[Name], this is [You] from [Company]. I saw you filled out an
application about [outcome] just like 2 minutes ago. Does that
ring a bell?"
```

If confused, give context: influencer name, email they signed up with, application answers.

```
"Got a few minutes to chat quick? Just 3-5 minutes to see if
we can help."
```

### The Tone
**Sound like a bubbly customer support rep, not a sales rep.** You are the friendly greeter at the front door.

### Discovery (3-5 Minutes)
```
"What caught your attention and made you want to fill out the
application?"

"What do you do for work right now?"

"What's the long-term goal -- get out of the job and do this
full-time, or just a side hustle?"
```

Dig past surface answers:
```
"Everyone says 'extra cash' -- that's easy to say. What
specifically?"
```

### Financial Qualification
```
"Obviously you're starting something here, and starting anything
requires capital. How much would you say you have set aside to
invest so you can [desired outcome]?"
```

**If enough:** Book with closer.
**If borderline:** "Is that what you're HOPING to invest, or is that ALL you have?"
**If insufficient for high-ticket:** Down-sell to lower-ticket offer.
**If financing available:** "We have a buy-now-pay-later option. Do you have a rough idea of your credit score?"

### Booking the Call
```
"Perfect. Let me get you on a call with one of our coaches.
When works best for you?"
```

**Lock down certainty:**
```
"Nothing's going to come up that prevents you from showing up
to that call?"
```

### Turning Uncertain Language into Certain Language
**Source:** Alexander. Critical setter skill.

| Prospect Says | You Say |
|--------------|---------|
| "It should work" | "Is that a yes or no? I just want to make sure nothing pops up." |
| "I'll try to be there" | "I need a yes or no on this. Can you commit to being there?" |
| "Yeah, probably" | "You sound a little uncertain. Is there something that might come up?" |

**Rule:** If you let uncertainty slide, the whole interaction becomes uncertain and ends in a no-show.

## Section 4: Triage Call Process

**Source:** Gordon. When the prospect has already booked a call, the setter runs a triage to confirm, pre-qualify, and reduce no-shows.

### The Triage Flow
```
1. Rapport & Frame (if set triage)
2. Mini Discovery (current situation + desired situation)
3. Sell the Consult (why the call matters)
4. Book / Confirm the Call
5. Qualify AFTER Booking (not before)
```

**Key insight:** If you master the triage call, you master everything else. It is 80% of what a setter needs.

### Messenger Scripts for Triage (Gordon)

**2-Step Indirect Script:**
1. Send value-based content piece
2. Follow up with: "What'd you think? By the way, are you still working on [goal]?"
3. Transition to booking when they show interest

**Direct Script:**
```
"Hey [Name] -- saw you [applied/signed up/expressed interest].
I just want to make sure you're all set for your call on [date].
Quick question: what's the #1 thing you're hoping to get out
of the conversation?"
```

**Cold Lead Script (VSL opt-in who did not book):**
```
"Hey [Name], this is [You] from [Company]. I noticed you signed
up to watch our training but didn't get a chance to book a call
yet. Got 2 minutes? I just want to see if it's even worth
your time."
```

## Section 5: Three Ways Setters Make Money

**Source:** Alexander. Setters are not limited to one revenue stream.

### Revenue Stream 1: Setting Appointments
Primary role. Book qualified prospects on closer calendars.
- Compensation: 3-5% of offer price per closed deal
- Example: $250 on a $5K offer at 5%

### Revenue Stream 2: Down-Selling
When a prospect does not qualify for the high-ticket offer, sell them a lower-ticket product directly.
- Self-liquidating offers, challenges, courses
- Often can close these yourself (no closer needed)
- Commission may be smaller but volume is higher

### Revenue Stream 3: Triaging
Confirm and pre-qualify already-booked appointments. Reduce no-shows, increase show rate.
- May earn a smaller per-call bonus
- Value to the company: higher show rates = more revenue

## Section 6: Setter KPIs & Standards

### KPIs to Track Daily
- Dials made / DMs sent
- Conversations had (connected calls)
- Appointments booked
- Show rate (% of booked who actually showed)
- Quality score (% of sets that were actually qualified)
- Down-sells closed (if applicable)

### Top 1% Setter Standards (Alexander)
- Sound confident and expert -- not uncertain
- Reply speed matters: treat leads like hot potatoes (speed to lead)
- Keep energy bubbly but accountable
- Be the first and best touch point of the business
- Update CRM religiously after every interaction
- Use local area codes when possible for outbound calls
- Leave voicemails that create curiosity, not sales pitches

### Compensation Benchmarks

| Role | Monthly OTE |
|------|-------------|
| Setter (new) | $3,000-$5,000 |
| Setter (experienced) | $5,000-$7,000 |
| Top setter | $7,000-$10,000 |
| Setter daily rate (base) | $100-$150/day |

## Section 7: Setter-to-Closer Transition

**Source:** Alexander. The four KPIs required before you earn the right to close.

### The 4 Requirements
1. **Top 1-2 setter on your offer** with at least 70% show rate
2. **Most dials/output** -- demonstrably outworking the team
3. **Great team member** -- helps others, goes the extra mile, supports the company
4. **Sustained performance** -- not one good week, but months of consistency

### Transition Mechanics
- Start attending closing team meetings
- Run mock calls with closers (aim for 20+ mock calls minimum)
- Ask for "at bats" -- even unqualified leads, just to prove competence
- If your offer will not promote you: "I love working here, but I need to chase my goals. If I can't close here, I'll need to find another offer."

### The Mindset Shift
- Setter: Sell the CONVERSATION
- Closer: Sell the TRANSFORMATION
- The skills overlap (discovery, qualification, tonality), but the closer carries the full weight of the commitment conversation

## Section 8: Show Rate Improvement for Setters

### Speed to Lead (The #1 Factor)
Call within minutes of opt-in. Every hour of delay reduces show rate dramatically.
- Under 5 minutes: highest conversion
- 5-30 minutes: good
- 30+ minutes: prospect has moved on, low pickup rate

### Confirmation Protocol
1. **Immediately after booking:** Text confirmation with time, date, link
2. **24 hours before:** Email reminder with what to expect
3. **30 minutes before:** Text reminder: "Hey [Name], just confirming we're still on for [Time]. You're all set!"

### Post-Sale Strategy
After deal closes, follow up with buyer to check satisfaction. Build relationship -- they may refer others. Ask for referrals explicitly.

## Decision Tree: Setter Call Routing

```
New lead comes in
    |
    +--> Did they book a call already?
    |       |
    |       +--> YES: Run triage call (confirm, pre-qualify, reduce no-show risk)
    |       |
    |       +--> NO: Run phone/DM setting framework
    |
    +--> Do they qualify financially?
    |       |
    |       +--> YES: Book with closer
    |       |
    |       +--> NO but interested: Down-sell to lower-ticket offer
    |       |
    |       +--> NO and not interested: DQ, add to long-term nurture
    |
    +--> Did they show up to their call?
            |
            +--> YES: Closer handles from here
            |
            +--> NO: Setter contacts within 5 minutes, re-books or DQs
```
