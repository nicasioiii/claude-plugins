# Triage, Booking & Setter Operations

## Triage Call Deep Dive (Gordon)

### Purpose
The triage call sits between the raw lead and the closer call. Its job:
1. Confirm the appointment is real (reduce no-shows)
2. Pre-qualify (save closer time on bad fits)
3. Build anticipation (make them WANT the closer call)
4. Gather intel (give closer ammunition for discovery)

### The 80% Rule
If you master the triage call, you master 80% of what a setter needs. Everything else is a variation of this process.

### Full Triage Process
```
Step 1: Rapport & Frame
  "Hey [Name], this is [You]. Just calling to make sure
  everything's set for your call on [date]. Got 2 minutes?"

Step 2: Mini Discovery
  "What's the #1 thing you're hoping to get from the call?"
  "What's your current situation with [topic]?"
  "Where do you want to be in [timeframe]?"

Step 3: Sell the Consult
  "[Coach] has helped a ton of people in your exact situation.
  They're going to give you a clear roadmap."

Step 4: Confirm the Booking
  "You're locked in for [date/time]. Nothing's going to come
  up, right?"

Step 5: Qualify AFTER Booking
  Once committed, check financial qualification:
  "One more thing -- [Coach] is going to walk you through
  our process. If it turns out to be a fit, do you have the
  resources to get started? I just want to make sure the
  call is worth both of your time."
```

**Key insight:** Qualify AFTER booking, not before. If you lead with financial qualification, you lose the people who would have been sold during the closer call. The closer's job is to build enough value to justify the investment.

## Show Rate System

### The 4 Reasons People No-Show (Alexander)

| Reason | Solution |
|--------|----------|
| Did not know what the call is about | Confirmation email with clear agenda |
| Did not know how the call would help them | Pre-call testimonial or case study |
| Forgot they had a call | 30-minute text reminder |
| Scared / nervous about the call | Pre-call email normalizing the experience |

### Confirmation Sequence

**Immediately after booking:**
```
TEXT: "Hey [Name], you're confirmed for [date] at [time] with
[Coach]. Here's the Zoom link: [link]. Save it somewhere safe.
Looking forward to connecting you!"
```

**24 hours before:**
```
EMAIL: Subject: Your call tomorrow with [Coach] at [time]

Hey [Name],

Quick reminder about your call tomorrow at [time].

Here's what to expect:
- [Coach] will ask about your situation and goals
- They'll share how we've helped people in your position
- If it's a fit, they'll walk you through next steps
- The call takes about 30-45 minutes

Come prepared with questions -- the more specific, the
better they can help.

Zoom link: [link]

Talk soon,
[You]
```

**30 minutes before:**
```
TEXT: "Hey [Name], just confirming we're still on for [time]
with [Coach]. You're all set!"
```

### Personalized Pre-Call Assets

Match testimonials to the prospect's specific situation:

| Prospect Profile | Send This |
|-----------------|-----------|
| Scared of being scammed | Student interview: "I was skeptical too, and here's what happened" |
| Money concerns | Student interview: "I invested despite tight finances and here's my ROI" |
| Time concerns | Student interview: "I did this while working full-time" |
| Tried before and failed | Student interview: "Previous programs failed me, but this was different because..." |

Send with:
```
"Hey [Name], found this and thought of you. This person had a
really similar background. Worth a watch before your call --
[Coach] might reference it."
```

## Three Ways Setters Make Money -- Deep Dive

### Revenue Stream 1: Setting (Primary)
- Commission: 3-5% of offer price per closed deal
- Example: $5K offer at 5% = $250 per close
- Volume play: 3 closes/week = $3K/month minimum
- Top setters: 6-8 closes/week = $6K-$8K/month

### Revenue Stream 2: Down-Selling
When prospect does not qualify for high-ticket:
```
"I appreciate you being upfront about where you're at
financially. The coaching program is at a higher price
point, but we have a [challenge/course/bootcamp] for
$[low price] that's a great starting point.

You'd get [specific deliverable] and see exactly how
this works. Sound like something worth trying?"
```

Commission on down-sells is typically smaller but:
- Higher volume (more people qualify)
- Builds pipeline for high-ticket later
- Company loves you for capturing revenue others miss

### Revenue Stream 3: Triaging
Pre-qualifying already-booked calls:
- May earn $10-$25 per completed triage
- Or earn show-rate bonuses (e.g., extra $50 for every call that shows)
- Value: You reduce wasted closer time and increase team revenue

## Post-Sale Strategy for Setters

**Source:** Alexander. Most setters stop at the booking. Top setters build relationships.

### After the Deal Closes
1. Follow up with the buyer: "Hey [Name], congrats on getting started! How are you feeling?"
2. Check satisfaction: "Everything going well with onboarding?"
3. Build relationship for referrals
4. Ask explicitly: "Do you know anyone else in your situation who might benefit from this?"

### Why This Matters
- Referrals are the highest-quality leads
- They convert at 2-3x the rate of cold leads
- Your reputation with the company grows
- You become the type of setter companies fight to keep

## Increasing Pick-Up Percentage

**Source:** Alexander. Tactical methods to get more prospects to answer.

1. **Speed to lead:** Call within minutes of opt-in. Every hour of delay reduces pickup rate.
2. **Local area codes:** Use local numbers when possible. Unknown out-of-state numbers get ignored.
3. **Optimal timing:** Not too early (before 9am), not too late (after 8pm). Lunch hours often work well.
4. **Curiosity voicemails:** Create intrigue, not a sales pitch. "I have something interesting based on what you shared."
5. **Multi-channel approach:** If phone fails, text immediately. If text fails, DM.
6. **Caller ID management:** Make sure your number is not flagged as spam. Check regularly.
