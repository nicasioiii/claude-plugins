# Close Scripts Reference

## Temperature Check Scripts

### Standard Temperature Check (Gordon)
"Just curious, in terms of the process specifically -- how do you feel?"

### Alignment Script (Gordon -- for uncertain responses)
"What's really important to me is ALIGNMENT -- when you come in and work with us, we're rolling up our sleeves and getting in the trenches with you. Our team is ALL IN -- so it's really important that you feel GOOD about the process.

On a scale of 1-10, 1 being [worst case description] and 10 being [best case description], where do you feel like you fall exactly?"

### 1-10 Follow-Up
- 9-10 + certain tonality: "Perfect, let me walk you through what next steps look like."
- 8 or below: "What exactly is keeping you from being a 9 or 10?"
- Handle the reveal, then re-check.

### Commitment Check (Alexander)
"Based on everything we talked about, do you think [offer] is the best thing to get you to [goal]? What makes you say that?"

If uncertain language ("yeah, I guess so"):
- Use 1-10 frame
- "What's keeping you from being a 10?"
- Handle before proceeding to price

## Investment Drop Scripts

### Standard Investment Drop (Gordon)
"And the investment to get you to [HIGH-LEVEL OUTCOME] is just [PRICE]."
[SHUT UP -- let them respond first]

### Pre-Price Commitment Close (Alexander)
"I just want to make sure -- if you decide to do something, you follow through. What would you say your level of commitment is for something like this?"
[They commit verbally]
"Perfect. So for everything we discussed, the investment is [PRICE]."
[Silence]
"Does that sound good? Ready to get started?"

### Next Steps Before Price (Gordon)
"So what you'll do is process the investment with me. Once we take care of that, we set baseline. I'll give you homework right away to get quick wins, have you send in your current [deliverable] for review. Most people find they [early win] just from the first [engagement point]. Then we're ready to rock and roll. Sound good?"

Then: "And the investment to get there is [PRICE]."

### Price Drop Rules
- Do NOT ramble after stating the price
- Do NOT immediately discount
- Do NOT anvil-drop payment plans with the price
- Present pay-in-full first. Payment plans are reserve leverage.
- Be confident, not robotic
- If they asked price earlier, TELL THEM (don't dodge)

## Trial Close Scripts (Gordon)

### Sharp Angle Trial Close
"So if you come in and we show you exactly how to get to [OUTCOME], is it worth it for you to do this?"
"Are you sure?"
"Ready to get started on some homework?"
"What's your billing address?"

### Feel Good Trial Close
"How do you feel? Feel good?"
"Are you sure?"
"Ready to get started?"
"What's your billing address?"

### Keep It In Context Trial Close
Put the investment in perspective:
- "[Price] over 12 months is [monthly amount]. That's less than [relatable comparison]."
- "What's the cost of staying where you are for another year?"
- "You told me [their stated cost of inaction]. This investment is [fraction] of that."

## Soft/Assumptive Close Scripts

### Standard Assumptive
"Does that sound good?"
"Does that make sense?"
"Is that something that would work for you?"

### Continuance Close (Post-Agreement)
Assume they've said yes. Ask WHEN, not IF.
"When would work better for you, morning or afternoon?"
"When should we schedule your first call?"
"Is next Monday or Wednesday better?"

### Full Assumptive Flow
Prospect: "Yeah, this sounds good"
You: "Perfect. When would work for your first session?"
Prospect: "Probably afternoon"
You: "Great. Tuesday or Wednesday?"
Prospect: "Wednesday"
You: "Done. 2pm Wednesday. Sending you the login details now."

No ask for commitment. Just scheduled it.

## Post-Close Onboarding Scripts

### Setting Expectations (Gordon)
"Here's what happens next:
1. You'll get onboarding access within [timeframe]
2. Complete [specific first task] before our first call
3. Our first session is [date/time]
4. You'll need to commit [hours/week] to the process
5. Results typically start showing in [timeframe]
6. You have access to [support channels] for any questions"

### Double Confirmation Message
Send 30 minutes before first engagement:
"Hey [Name], just confirming we're still on for [Time] with [Coach/Team]. You're all set!"

### Homework Assignment (Gordon)
"Before our first call, I want you to:
1. [Specific actionable task]
2. Send me [deliverable for review]
3. Watch [specific resource]

Most people find they [early win metric] just from doing this prep work."

## Handling "I Need More Time" at Close

### Alexander's Approach
Prospect: "Can I take more time to think about it?"
"I completely understand wanting to feel certain. What would make you 110% certain about moving forward?"

Get specific criteria, then provide them immediately if possible.

### Serhant's "What Do You Want to Do?"
"Look, I've laid out everything. The data supports it. The timing works. What do you want to do?"

Puts the decision in their hands. Gives control.

### Voss's No-Oriented Approach
Instead of pushing for yes:
"Is it ridiculous to think this would work for you?"
"Are you against getting started this week?"
"Do you disagree that this addresses what you told me you need?"

Default "no" answers move them toward commitment.

## Closing Under Pressure -- Specific Scenarios

### When They Want to Try It Themselves First
"You might be able to figure it out yourself. But we have coaches who've already done it. Rather than spending 6-12 months figuring it out, they show you exactly what works in 30 days."

### When They've Wanted This for Years But Never Acted
"So you've wanted this for 4 years but never tried anything. What's changed in the last week?"
Reveals if there is real urgency or tire-kicking.

### When Agreement Is Given But They Keep Talking
Close IMMEDIATELY. Do not re-explain.
Prospect: "That's what I've had to do my entire life."
You: "Well let's do it again. You ready to join?"

The window after agreement is narrow. Use it fast.
