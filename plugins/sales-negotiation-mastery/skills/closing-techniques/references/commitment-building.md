# Commitment Building: Creating Lasting Agreements

## Three-Stage Commitment Model

### Stage 1: Pre-Commitment (Building Belief)

Before asking for any commitment, establish three beliefs in this exact order:

#### Belief 1: It's Possible
Show evidence that the outcome is achievable:
- Testimonials from real people
- Case studies with specifics (not generic claims)
- Community wins and progress
- Third-party validation

**Script**:
```
"I can show you testimonials from people who've done this.
They're normal people like you. They weren't special.
But they followed the process and it worked."
```

#### Belief 2: It's Possible For Them Specifically
Get them to see themselves in the success:
- Ask them to identify with people in testimonials
- Highlight similarities (their job, background, challenges)
- Get them to admit: "If they can do it, I probably can too"

**Script**:
```
"These people have no special advantage over you.
Same challenges you have. Same doubts.
But they stuck with it. Do you feel like you could do that?"
```

#### Belief 3: It's Worth It
Show ROI justifies the effort/cost:
- Timeline to results (not "make money forever," but "30 days to X")
- Specific outcomes they get (not vague benefits)
- Cost of NOT doing this (staying stuck)

**Script**:
```
"So if you could have a clear roadmap in 30 days
instead of spinning for another year wondering what to do,
that's worth $3K to you, right?
That's less than a car payment."
```

**Why order matters**: You're building logical agreement first. Then ask for commitment. Prevents "intellectually right, emotionally not ready" freeze.

## Stage 2: Initial Commitment (The Ask)

### The Commitment Progression

#### Micro-Commitment #1: Soft Agreement
```
"Does that sound good?"
"Does that make sense?"
"Are you with me so far?"
```
(They just mentally say "yes"—zero financial commitment yet)

#### Micro-Commitment #2: Acknowledgment
```
"So just to be clear, you see how this could work for you, right?"
[Get "Yes"]
"And you understand the investment is $3K?"
[Get "Yes"]
```
(They've acknowledged understanding—still testing their readiness)

#### Micro-Commitment #3: The Trial Commitment
```
"If we could do this, would you be ready to move forward?"
[Get "Yes"]
```
(They've committed conditionally—now you remove the condition)

#### Micro-Commitment #4: Full Commitment
```
"Let's do this then. When works for your first call?"
[Get specific time]
```
(They're in—they just don't know it yet)

#### Micro-Commitment #5: Deposit/Payment
```
"To lock this in, I need a $500 deposit today. You good with that?"
[Get card/payment]
```
(They're fully committed—money is moving)

### The Psychology of Micro-Commitments

Each small "yes" makes the next "yes" more likely:
- First "yes" = easier (does it make sense?)
- Second "yes" = still easy (you understand the price?)
- Third "yes" = getting harder (would you move forward?)
- Fourth "yes" = much harder (actually scheduling time?)
- Fifth "yes" = hardest (giving money?)

But because of **commitment and consistency principle**, they're more likely to say yes each time because they've already said yes before. Their brain wants to stay consistent.

## Stage 3: Reinforcing Commitment (Post-Close)

### Immediately After Close: Set Expectations

This is critical for preventing refunds/cancellations.

**Script**:
```
"Awesome! Here's what to expect starting Monday:

WHAT YOU'LL GET:
- Onboarding training module (complete before Tuesday)
- Access to community (over 500 members, very active)
- First one-on-one call Tuesday at 2pm with Sarah

WHAT I NEED FROM YOU:
- Show up on time (don't be late, don't cancel)
- Complete all training modules (takes about 30 minutes each)
- Be open to doing things differently than you've done before
- Commit 10 hours per week minimum for first month

TIMELINE TO RESULTS:
- Week 1-2: Learning and understanding the system
- Week 3-4: First real applications
- Week 6-8: You should see measurable progress

SUPPORT:
- Email support within 24 hours
- Community feedback anytime
- Weekly group coaching calls
- Access to all training materials forever

Questions? Any concerns I should know about?"
```

**Why this works**:
- Removes surprises (reduces refund shock)
- Sets success standards upfront
- Creates accountability
- Manages expectations

### 24 Hours Before First Call: Confirmation

**Text/Email Script**:
```
"Hey [Name], just confirming we're still on for tomorrow at 2pm with Sarah.
You should have received:
1. Your login credentials
2. The foundation training (watch this before the call)
3. The Zoom link

If you need anything, hit reply. See you tomorrow!"
```

**Why this works**:
- 30-second confirmation increased show rate from 50% to 75-80%
- Gives them last chance to ask questions
- Reduces cold feet

### Week 1: Nurture Momentum

**Day 1 of Program (After first call)**:
```
"Hey [Name],

Great first call with Sarah today! She told me you asked some killer questions.

Here's what's next:
- Complete Training Module 2 by Thursday
- Practice assignment [specific task]
- Group call tomorrow at 6pm (optional but recommended)

You're doing great. Let's build on this momentum."
```

**Day 3 of Program**:
```
"How's it going so far?

Common thing people get stuck on early: [mention their specific concern]

Here's how to handle it: [provide solution]

Don't hesitate to reach out with questions."
```

**Why this matters**:
- Most refunds happen in days 1-7
- Early nurture dramatically reduces refunds
- Shows you're invested in their success
- Keeps them focused and moving

## Building Commitment Over Time

### The 30-Day Commitment Architecture

**Week 1: Establish Credibility & Clear Direction**
- Deliver on promises made
- Introduce community/support
- Give quick wins
- Answer early questions quickly

**Week 2: Build Results (or evidence of progress)**
- Share specific examples of their progress
- Celebrate small wins publicly (if they're comfortable)
- Introduce them to community members

**Week 3-4: Momentum Building**
- By now, they've invested 20-30 hours
- Results starting to show
- Commitment becomes self-reinforcing

### The "Sunk Cost" Principle

After they've invested:
- Time (10+ hours)
- Money ($3K+)
- Emotional energy (shared goals)

They're much less likely to leave. Their brain says: "I've come this far, might as well finish."

### Preventing Commitment Backslide

**Red flags**:
- Missing group calls
- Not completing assignments
- Vague responses to check-ins
- Posting in community but not taking action

**Intervention script**:
```
"Hey [Name], I noticed you missed the last two group calls
and didn't complete the assignment from week 2.

That's cool—things get busy. But I want to make sure you're getting what you need.

What's going on? Is it time? Is it clarity on what to do?
Let's fix it right now."
```

## Commitment When There's External Resistance

### Scenario: Family/Spouse Against It

**Addressing on initial call** (before they talk to spouse):
```
"I have a question for you. When you tell people about this,
especially your spouse, what do you think they'll say?"

[Listen to answer]

"That's fair. Here's what I'd suggest:
Share with them what you learned. Show them the community.
Explain why YOU feel this is different.

And honestly, when they see your results in 30 days,
they'll be your biggest cheerleaders.
People support winning. Show them you're serious."
```

**Preventing post-call sabotage**:
```
"Before our next meeting, do yourself a favor:
Have a conversation with [spouse/parent] about this.
Tell them what you're doing and why.
If they have concerns, tell them to text me directly.
I'm happy to answer their questions."
```

**Why this works**:
- Removes surprise
- Includes potential saboteurs
- Shows confidence
- Makes spouse an ally instead of enemy

## Special Case: Financial Commitment When They're Struggling

### When They Say "I Just Quit My Job"

**Response** (from Luke Alexander):
```
"Okay, but in terms of liquid capital available—
do you actually have $1,500-$3K that you could invest?
Even if you're spending more day-to-day, do you have savings?"
```

**Why they're still good**:
- Quitting their job = ultimate commitment signal
- They're willing to take risk
- Usually have severance or savings
- Motivation is HIGH

**Reinforce with**: "So the fact that you quit tells me you're serious about this. That's good. That means you're ready to move."

### When They Have Less Capital

**Trial close**:
```
"So you said you only have $500 right now.
The lowest we could do is like three payments of $1,000 each.

If we were able to do that, and our program could help you hit your goals,
would you be in a position to do that?"

[If yes]

"Perfect. So your first payment is $1,000 today,
then $1,000 next month, then $1,000 the month after.
Does that work?"
```

## Guarantee as Commitment Reinforcer

### The Guarantee's Real Purpose

Not an escape hatch—it's a commitment reinforcer.

**Script**:
```
"Here's the deal. If you follow the system for 90 days
and don't hit your goals, we're going to do additional calls to figure out why.
We're going to keep working until it clicks.

But here's what I need from you:
I need to know you're willing to commit. This isn't a 'dip your toe in' situation.
This is 'I'm all in and I'm going to give this everything' situation.

Can you do that?"
```

**Why this works**:
- Shows confidence
- Filters for real commitment
- Makes them recommit verbally
- Creates accountability

## Community as Commitment Reinforcer

### Built-In Accountability

Once they're in a community of 500+ people:
- They don't want to look like they're slacking
- They see others' progress and get motivated
- They make friends (soft lock to stay)
- They have multiple touchpoints with company

**Script when onboarding**:
```
"You're going to be joining a community of 500+ people
doing exactly what you're doing.

These are normal people. Real results.
When you see what they're achieving, you're going to be motivated.
Plus, you'll have people to lean on when things get tough."
```

## Red Flags for Weak Commitment (Recover These)

| Signal | Recovery Script |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Doesn't show up to first call** | "I had you on my calendar. What happened? This is an hour of our closer's time. Let's reschedule and make sure you're serious." |
| **Says "I need to think about it" after close** | "What specifically do you need to think about? Let's talk it through right now." |
| **Keeps delaying payment** | "So we're locked in for [date], and the deposit is due by [date]. If that's not going to work, we need to reschedule." |
| **Doesn't watch pre-call materials** | "Did you get a chance to watch the training? No? Go ahead and do that before the call tomorrow. It'll make the call way more valuable." |
| **Missing group calls/assignments week 1** | "I'm noticing you're missing some components. What's getting in the way? Time? Clarity? Let's solve it." |

## Long-Term Commitment Maintenance

### Monthly Check-In Script (If Higher-Ticket)
```
"Hey [Name], we're 30 days in now.

How are you feeling? Is this aligned with what you expected?
Are you getting what you need from the coaching?

[Listen]

Here's what I'm seeing: You've [specific behavior they did well].
That shows real commitment.

Here's where we can tighten up for month 2: [specific feedback]

Any questions or concerns? You're doing great."
```

### 60-Day Decision Point
```
"We're at 60 days now. You're either seeing results and want to keep going,
or something isn't clicking and you want to try something different.

Which is it? Be honest."

[If second option, offer pause/refund. Don't force it.
Respecting their choice builds trust for future.]
```

### Renewal/Continuation Script (If Recurring)
```
"Your first month is wrapping up. You've gotten [specific results].

A lot of people want to keep going because month 2 is where we [specific benefit].

What do you want to do? Continue with month 2?"
```
