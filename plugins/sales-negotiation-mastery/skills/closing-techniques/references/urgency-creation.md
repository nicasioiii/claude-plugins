# Urgency Creation: Real vs. Fake, and How to Build It

## The Urgency Problem

Most salespeople create FAKE urgency (countdown timers, artificial scarcity) which:
- Damages trust when exposed
- Doesn't actually move prospects who aren't ready
- Makes follow-up harder (they feel manipulated)

The experts in the sales-negotiation-mastery course all emphasize REAL urgency—based on prospect's actual pain and timeline.

---

## Real Urgency: The Foundation

### What Real Urgency Is

Real urgency comes from prospect's situation, not from you creating artificial pressure.

**Sources of real urgency**:
1. **Pain/Timeline** - "My girlfriend got pregnant 6 months ago. I need to provide for a baby NOW."
2. **Opportunity window** - "I just quit my job. I have 3 months of savings. After that, I have to get income."
3. **Consequences of delay** - "If I don't change jobs in the next month, I'll miss the hiring cycle at my target company."
4. **Environmental pressure** - "Wife gave me 6 months to figure something out."

These are real. These move people. These create actual commitment.

### How to Discover Real Urgency

**The Core Question** (Luke Alexander framework):
```
"So you've been wanting to make $10K/month.
Why not just continue what you're doing for another 6 months?
What makes NOW so important?"
```

**Listen for**:
- Time-specific events ("Baby coming," "Deadline," "Lease ends")
- Emotional language ("Can't stand it anymore," "I'm desperate")
- External pressure ("Boss said figure it out," "Wife's timeline")
- Internal urgency ("My kids are asking why I'm not around")

**Don't accept vague answers**:
- ❌ "I want better"
- ❌ "I'm tired of my job"
- ✅ "I'm tired of missing my kids' soccer games because I work till 7pm"

**Dig deeper**:
```
"There's gotta be something. What's going on?"
[Wait for real answer]

"Okay, so when exactly does that need to happen by?"
[Get specific date/timeline]
```

## The Three Layers of Urgency

### Layer 1: Pain (Past/Present)

What current situation is unbearable?

**Setter's question**:
```
"Walk me through what's been the hardest part of your current job."
```

**They answer with pain**: "I come home smelling like dog food every day. I'm exhausted."

**You lock it in**:
```
"So if we could get you out of that situation and making money from home,
that would be a big change for you, right?"
```

### Layer 2: Consequence (Future)

What happens if they DON'T change?

**Closer's question**:
```
"If you stay in your current situation for the next 12 months,
where do you end up? Be honest with me."
```

**They answer**: "I'm still broke, my family's disappointed, and I feel like a failure."

**You lock it in**:
```
"Do you want to be in that situation a year from now?
Or do you want to make a change?"
```

### Layer 3: Opportunity (Timeline)

What window is closing?

**Setter's question**:
```
"How much longer can you stay in your current situation before something has to change?"
```

**They answer**: "Honestly, maybe 3-6 months. After that, I'll have used my savings."

**You lock it in**:
```
"So we're looking at a 3-6 month window to get you set up and making money.
This isn't something to think about for the next year."
```

## How Urgency Affects Close Rate

### No Urgency
- Prospect: "I've wanted to do this for 4 years"
- You: "Okay, let's talk..."
- Reality: They'll say no. They have no timeline.

### Low Urgency
- Prospect: "I want to make more money someday"
- You: "When would that be?"
- Reality: Soft maybe. Likely nurture candidate.

### Medium Urgency
- Prospect: "I want to leave my job in the next 6 months"
- You: "Okay, got it"
- Reality: Decent closer, maybe 50-60% conversion

### High Urgency
- Prospect: "My boss told me to figure it out or I'm fired"
- You: "Then we need to move fast"
- Reality: Much higher conversion. High commitment.

## CODO Framework: Creating Urgency the Right Way

(Ryan Serhant's method from CODO course)

### The CODO Mindset

CODO = "Conviction On Demand Outcome"

The idea: You create urgency not by being pushy, but by helping them see the consequences of their own situation.

### Step 1: Know the Star Players

Who are they?
- Current position
- Income
- Goals
- Pain

**Script**:
```
"So you're a home depot cashier making $16/hour.
You want to make $10K/month. Your savings are $4,500.
You're 24 and want to move out.
Is that right?"
```

### Step 2: Know the Opposing Team

What's fighting them?
- Current job
- Family pressure
- Self-doubt
- Time constraints
- Other opportunities

**Script**:
```
"So home depot gives you flexibility but no upside.
Your family thinks you should stay safe.
You've tried a few things that didn't work.
That's what we're dealing with here, right?"
```

### Step 3: Create Urgency (The Key Part)

Don't manufacture it. Reveal it.

**Script**:
```
"Here's what I see: You can stay at Home Depot forever,
making $16/hour, or you can use the next 90 days
to learn a skill that pays $10K/month.

You have $4,500 saved. That's your window.
After you spend that on rent and life, it's gone.

So the real question is: Are you going to use this next 90 days
to change your life, or use it to stay comfortable and broke?

What's it going to be?"
```

**Why this works**:
- Not artificial ("Limited spots available")
- Not manipulative ("Deadline at 5pm tonight")
- Real ("You have $4,500 and 3 months of runway")
- Gives them autonomy to choose (more likely to commit)

## The Urgency Check

### Before Closing, Ask Yourself

**Questions to validate real urgency exists**:

1. Do they have a specific timeline? (Not "someday" but "by September")
2. Have they taken action before? (Not "been wanting to" but "tried this")
3. Is there external pressure? (Job ending, spouse deadline, life event)
4. Are they running out of time? (Savings running out, opportunity closing)
5. Do they understand consequences? (Can they articulate what stays same if they don't change?)

**If you answer NO to 3+ of these**: They're a nurture candidate, not a close candidate.

---

## Without Real Urgency: The Nurture Path

If they lack urgency but show interest:

**Script**:
```
"You know what, this probably isn't the right time for you yet.
Your situation is different.

Let's stay connected though. I'm going to send you some resources
to think through. And when your situation changes—maybe you get laid off,
maybe you decide you're done, maybe your timeline becomes real—
hit me up and we'll move fast.

Sound fair?"
```

This is often MORE effective than forcing a close with no urgency.

## Artificial Urgency (What NOT to Do)

### What Experts Say About Fake Urgency

All four coaches in the extractions emphasize that fake urgency BACKFIRES:
- Countdown timers ("Offer expires at 5pm!")
- False scarcity ("Only 3 spots left")
- Manufactured deadlines
- Arbitrary bonuses

**Why it fails**:
- If they don't buy, they feel relieved they weren't manipulated
- Follow-up is harder (they resent you)
- Trust is broken
- Real prospects who would have bought feel tricked

### What IS Okay to Mention

These aren't fake—they're real constraints:

**Okay**:
- "I have [X] spots available in my coaching program right now"
- "My calendar for first appointments fills up around mid-month"
- "If you want to start Monday, we need to move today because appointments after that are set"

**Not okay**:
- "Offer expires at midnight!" (arbitrary)
- "Price goes up tomorrow" (if it doesn't)
- "Only spots available if you decide in the next hour" (manufactured)

## Urgency Maintenance Post-Close

Once they close, KEEP the urgency alive:

### Week 1 Script
```
"You made the right call moving fast on this.
The next 7 days are critical.

Here's what most people do wrong:
They procrastinate on the first module.
Then they fall behind the whole month.

Don't be that person. Start today."
```

### Week 2 Script (If Momentum Slowing)
```
"I'm noticing you didn't show up to the group call yesterday.
Remember why you got into this?

Your goal is to hit $10K/month in 90 days.
That timeline doesn't move for us.
So let's pick up the pace."
```

### Week 4 Script (Decision Point)
```
"You're a month in. You're either crushing it
and want to keep going, or you're losing momentum.

Which is it? Because if you're losing steam,
we need to fix what's wrong RIGHT NOW.
Don't let this become another thing you started and didn't finish."
```

## The Urgency Conversation (Full Script)

Use this when you sense lack of urgency before closing:

```
Setter: "So you've been wanting to leave your job for how long?"

Prospect: "About a year now."

Setter: "A year. So why hasn't it happened yet?"

Prospect: "I don't know. Life gets busy. I've been looking into stuff."

Setter: "Right. So my question is—why NOW? Why not just keep doing what you're doing for another year?"

Prospect: "Well, I'm just really unhappy."

Setter: "Okay, but being unhappy hasn't changed anything for a year.
What's different about right now that makes you think it would change?"

Prospect: "Honestly, I got a text from my ex that he's making $20K/month
and now I just feel like I'm falling behind."

Setter: "Okay, so that's real. You got reminded that change is possible.
And you're feeling behind. That's actually good—that's urgency.

But here's what I need to know: Are you going to actually DO something about this,
or are you going to feel bad for a week and then go back to normal?

Because if it's the second one, we're wasting each other's time.
Be honest with me."

Prospect: "No, I'm serious. I'm ready to change."

Setter: "Okay, I believe you. So here's the deal:
You use the next 90 days to learn and build.
After 90 days, you have a real option for income.
Or you spend the next 90 days thinking about it and staying where you are.

Which one is it going to be?"

Prospect: "I want to do it."

Setter: "Perfect. Then we move today. Not tomorrow. Today.
Because if you can't commit 10 minutes right now,
how are you going to commit 10 hours a week for the next 90 days?"

[Now close]
```

## Special Cases: Urgency Variations

### No Urgency but High Motivation
(They WANT to do this but no timeline pressure)

**Approach**: Manufacture accountability, not artificial deadline

```
"Okay, so no hard deadline. But here's what I know about people:
Without a timeline, they procrastinate forever.

So let's create one together. You're starting Monday, yeah?
And we're going to measure results on day 30, day 60, day 90.

If you're crushing it by day 30, we keep going.
If you're not, we figure out what's wrong.

That fair?"
```

### Multiple Urgencies (Job ending + Family deadline + Opportunity window)

**Approach**: Lock in ALL of them

```
"So you're getting laid off in 6 weeks.
Your family will support you for 3 months.
Your friend's business is only taking partners for the next 30 days.

So realistically, you have 30 days to decide and start.
Otherwise you miss the window and you're job hunting.

Do you see why we need to move today?"
```

### False Urgency Revealed

(You sensed urgency but now realize it was fake)

**Approach**: Reset to truth

```
"I'm going to be honest with you.
When we first talked, I thought you had a real deadline.
But it sounds like maybe you were just interested, not urgent.

That's okay. Not everyone is ready right now.
When do you think your situation will change?"

[Reset to nurture]
```

## Urgency Creation: The Bottom Line

**The Rule of Three** (Black Swan principle):

Ask urgency questions three different ways:

```
Q1: "Why is NOW the right time for you?"
[Listen]

Q2: "If you wait 3 more months, what changes?"
[Listen]

Q3: "What happens if you never do this?"
[Listen]
```

The third answer usually reveals the real urgency. That's what you close on.
