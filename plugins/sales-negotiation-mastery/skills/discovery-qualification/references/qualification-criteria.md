# Qualification Criteria & Rubrics

## The Four Must-Have Qualification Criteria

Every prospect must pass a "yes" on all four. If even one is missing or weak, you either need to work on it in discovery or disqualify.

---

## CRITERION 1: PAIN CLARITY

**Definition**: They can articulate the specific problem with concrete details and emotional connection.

### How to Test It

During discovery, listen for:
- **Specificity**: Can they describe the exact problem, not vague feelings?
- **Quantification**: Can they put numbers on it (time, money, frequency)?
- **Emotion**: Do they show genuine frustration/stress when talking about it?
- **Duration**: Can they trace when it started and how long they've tolerated it?

### Green Light Indicators (Strong Pain)

```
✓ They spontaneously mention specific numbers ("I'm losing $5K/month")
✓ They get visibly frustrated/emotional when describing it
✓ They've mentioned this problem unprompted multiple times
✓ They describe the problem with vivid language ("It's killing me", "Complete nightmare")
✓ They can trace secondary impacts ("And that's affecting my ability to...")
✓ Pain has been ongoing (3+ months, not just 2 weeks)
✓ They've lost sleep/energy/focus because of it
```

### Yellow Light Indicators (Moderate Pain)

```
⚠ Vague about numbers ("I think I'm losing some money")
⚠ Mention the problem but seem resigned, not frustrated
⚠ Haven't clearly traced secondary impacts
⚠ Problem is relatively new (1-2 months)
⚠ Only mention pain when directly asked
⚠ Can describe it logically but not emotionally
⚠ Don't know exact numbers (haven't quantified)
```

### Red Light Indicators (Weak/No Pain)

```
✗ Can't or won't articulate the problem specifically
✗ Sound like they're repeating something they heard
✗ Vague about duration ("I don't know, been meaning to fix it")
✗ No emotional activation when discussing it
✗ Never mention numbers or impact
✗ Say things like "It would be nice to..." instead of "I need to..."
✗ Downplay it ("It's not a huge deal, just annoying")
```

### How to Strengthen Weak Pain

If they show yellow/red lights:

1. **Ask the 5-Why drill**: "Why is that a problem? And why? And why?"
2. **Quantify it**: "How much time per week?" "How much money?" "How many people affected?"
3. **Link to secondary impact**: "How does that affect your ability to [what they care about]?"
4. **Create contrast**: "So you're spending $X/month on this workaround. What would freedom from this look like?"

## CRITERION 2: BUDGET/RESOURCE CAPABILITY

**Definition**: They have access to funds or resources to invest in your solution.

### How to Test It

**Direct approach**:
```
"Our solution requires an investment of $[amount].
Is that something that would make sense for you financially right now?"
```

**Or softer**:
```
"What range would you be comfortable investing to solve this?"
"Do you have budget allocated for solving this?"
"What access do you have to invest if you found the right solution?"
```

### Green Light Indicators (Clear Budget)

```
✓ They answer directly ("Yes, that works" or "Yes, but I'd need a payment plan")
✓ They mention they've invested in similar solutions before
✓ They've asked about ROI/payback (signals they can afford it)
✓ They just quit a job / liquidated cash (shows liquidity)
✓ They're a business owner with reported revenue
✓ They don't hesitate on the number - accept immediately
✓ They ask about payment options (vs. deflecting on price)
```

### Yellow Light Indicators (Constrained but Possible)

```
⚠ They hesitate but don't say no ("That's... a lot, but...")
⚠ They just quit their job and say "money is tight for now"
⚠ They mention needing a payment plan
⚠ They say "I can make that work" with effort
⚠ They ask about financing/payment options
⚠ They want to "discuss with partner" (need approval, not "can't afford")
⚠ They have assets but not liquid cash
```

### Red Light Indicators (No Budget)

```
✗ Immediate deflection ("I don't have money right now")
✗ Get defensive about the price
✗ Avoid the question or say "I need to think about it" to budget question
✗ Say they're "homeless" or "broke" and seem resigned
✗ Suggest much lower price without negotiating
✗ Say "I wish I could but..." and trail off
✗ Express skepticism that it's worth the investment
```

### Critical Rule: Separate Price Objection from Budget Reality

Many people say "I don't have the money" when they mean "I'm not sure this is worth the money."

**Test it**: "Is the investment the issue, or is there something about the solution that doesn't feel right?"

If they clarify "No, I genuinely don't have it," that's a real objection. If they hesitate or talk about value/ROI, it's actually an objection to certainty, not budget.

## CRITERION 3: TIMELINE/READINESS

**Definition**: They're ready to make a decision and move NOW (within 30 days), not "someday."

### How to Test It

The key question: **"Why is RIGHT NOW the right time for you?"**

Listen for:
- Specific triggers (hit a breaking point, boss gave ultimatum, market changed)
- Emotional urgency (frustrated, desperate, excited)
- Time-bound deadlines (need results by [specific date])
- NOT: "Well, I've been wanting to change this for 2 years..."

### Green Light Indicators (Ready Now)

```
✓ "I just quit my job yesterday" (forced timeline)
✓ "My boss said I need to fix this by end of quarter"
✓ "I've lost [amount] this month, it has to stop"
✓ "I'm getting married/moving/starting a business next month"
✓ "I've been dealing with this for 3 years and I'm done"
✓ "I have 30 days to get results or..."
✓ They want to get started immediately, ask about next steps
✓ They book follow-up call without hesitation
```

### Yellow Light Indicators (Interested but Not Urgent)

```
⚠ "I've been thinking about this for 6+ months"
⚠ "Next quarter would be ideal"
⚠ "Once [specific event] happens, I'll do this"
⚠ "I'm ready but need to discuss with partner first"
⚠ "I want to explore more before deciding"
⚠ "Yeah, definitely interested but not sure about timing"
⚠ Say they'll "think about it" and decide later
```

### Red Light Indicators (Not Ready/No Urgency)

```
✗ "I've been wanting to do this for 2+ years"
✗ "Maybe next year"
✗ "I'll reach out when I'm ready"
✗ "Just exploring options right now"
✗ "Can't commit to anything for a while"
✗ Vague about timeline when asked
✗ "I need to see if this is even possible before deciding"
```

### The "Right Now" Qualification Question

```
You: "So why is RIGHT NOW the perfect time for you to solve this?"

GOOD answers:
- "I just hit my breaking point"
- "My business is falling apart and I need help immediately"
- "I quit my job and need to make this work fast"
- "My partner gave me a deadline"
- "The market window is closing"

BAD answers:
- "Well, I've been thinking about it"
- "When things slow down next year"
- "I'm just exploring options"
- "I need to think about it first"
```

### How to Strengthen Weak Timeline

If they don't have urgency:

1. **Create cost of inaction**: "If nothing changes in the next 12 months, where will you be?"
2. **Identify the real deadline**: "When does this need to be solved by?"
3. **Add consequence**: "What happens if you wait until [later time]?"
4. **Use scarcity**: "I work with limited clients. I'm booking 30-45 days out"

**But be honest**: If they're genuinely not urgent, **nurture them**. Don't force a close. They'll move slower and may not follow through.

## CRITERION 4: DECISION AUTHORITY

**Definition**: They can make the decision themselves, or they can get approval from decision-maker quickly.

### How to Test It

Ask directly:
```
"Is this a decision you can make on your own, or does someone else need to be involved?"

"Besides you, who would need to sign off on this?"

"Would [partner/boss/board] need to approve?"

"Are they as interested in solving this as you are?"
```

### Green Light Indicators (Clear Authority)

```
✓ "I can decide on my own"
✓ "I'm the owner/decision-maker"
✓ "My wife is on the call, we can decide together"
✓ "My partner will do whatever I want to do"
✓ "I have budget authority for this"
✓ "I'll get my partner's approval by tomorrow"
✓ They don't mention anyone else when asked
```

### Yellow Light Indicators (Partial Authority/Need Approval)

```
⚠ "I need to talk to my partner"
⚠ "My boss would need to approve"
⚠ "I run it past my team before big decisions"
⚠ "My spouse needs to agree"
⚠ "I'll need board/investor approval"
⚠ They say "we" would need to discuss
⚠ They're enthusiastic but say "Let me talk to [person]"
```

### Red Light Indicators (No Authority/Hidden Decision-Maker)

```
✗ "My [spouse/boss/partner] makes the decisions"
✗ They seem hesitant about involving the other person
✗ "I'd need to convince [person] but they probably won't be interested"
✗ "It's not really up to me"
✗ They mention the other person as a blocker
✗ They avoid the decision-maker question
✗ "I'd have to check with [person] and they're hard to reach"
```

### How to Handle Yellow Light (Weak Authority)

If they need someone else's approval:

**Option 1: Get the other person involved**
```
"Would it make sense to get [person] on a call so we're all on the same page?"
```

**Option 2: Give them a talking point**
```
"When you talk to [person], here's what I'd mention: [key benefit for them too]"
```

**Option 3: Move to nurture**
```
"Why don't you have that conversation with them, and if they're interested, let's schedule a call with both of you?"
```

## BONUS CRITERIA: Action-Taking Capability

While not one of the "must-have four," these indicators predict follow-through:

### Green Lights (High Follow-Through)

```
✓ They mention previous investments in similar areas
✓ They've taken action to solve problems in the past
✓ They mention having a coach/mentor/advisor
✓ They ask about onboarding/implementation details
✓ They mention accountability partners
✓ They set personal goals and talk about achieving them
✓ They research before making decisions
```

### Red Lights (Low Follow-Through)

```
✗ "I've been wanting to do this for years but never do"
✗ Started multiple programs and didn't finish
✗ "I'm not really an action person"
✗ No previous investments in growth/solutions
✗ Never mention any accountability structures
✗ Vague about how they make decisions
✗ "I like to think about things a long time"
```

## THE QUALIFICATION MATRIX

Use this matrix to assess overall fit:

| Criterion | Green Light (3 pts) | Yellow Light (2 pts) | Red Light (0 pts) |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Pain Clarity** | Specific, quantified, emotional | Vague but mentioned | No pain articulated |
| **Budget** | Confirmed, ready | Constrained but possible | No budget/deflecting |
| **Timeline** | Ready now, urgency shown | 6+ months out | 2+ years, no urgency |
| **Authority** | They decide | Need approval | Hidden decision-maker |

**Scoring**:
- **10-12 points**: CLOSE - They're ready
- **7-9 points**: NURTURE or WORK - Strengthen weaker areas
- **Under 7**: DISQUALIFY - Not a fit right now

---

## Disqualification Decision Tree

```
Does their pain connect to your solution?
├─ NO → DISQUALIFY (not a fit)
└─ YES ↓

Can they afford it?
├─ NO → NURTURE (come back when they can)
└─ YES ↓

Are they ready now?
├─ NO → NURTURE (set timeline to re-engage)
└─ YES ↓

Can they decide?
├─ NO → CONDITIONAL (depends on getting approval)
└─ YES ↓

→ CLOSE
```

---

## The Disqualification Script

When you've determined they're not a fit:

```
"You know what, I really appreciate you taking the time.
But I'm sensing this probably isn't the right move for you right now.

[Pick one]
- 'The timeline isn't urgent enough'
- 'The investment isn't in the budget currently'
- 'I don't think we're the right fit for your situation'

What I'll do is I'll send you some value anyway [resources/insights]
so you can keep this top of mind.

And whenever [specific condition is met], let's reconnect. Sound fair?"
```

## Qualification Red Flags (Deal-Killers)

**Absolute red flags that should trigger disqualification**:

1. **Dishonesty** - They won't answer questions directly or are evasive
2. **No decision authority** - They can't/won't involve decision-maker
3. **No budget in sight** - Say they have zero access to funds
4. **Conflicting goals** - Want mutually exclusive outcomes
5. **No prior action** - 2+ years of wanting to change with zero attempts
6. **Negative attitude toward solution type** - "I don't think this stuff works"
7. **Just exploring** - "Gathering information" with no urgency
8. **Expecting miracles** - Want massive results with no effort/time

---

## Checklist Before Closing

Before moving to pitch/offer, verify:

- [ ] They've identified specific pain (not vague)
- [ ] They can articulate impact (financial, time, quality of life)
- [ ] They've attempted solutions before (shows action-taking)
- [ ] They stated clear goals (specific numbers, not "more")
- [ ] They explained why now (urgency exists)
- [ ] They confirmed budget is available
- [ ] They confirmed they can decide (or get approval quickly)
- [ ] They showed coachability (open-minded, asking good questions)
- [ ] They asked about next steps (signal of genuine interest)

If 7 of 8 are true: **PROCEED TO CLOSE**
If 5-6 of 8 are true: **WORK ON THE GAPS BEFORE CLOSING**
If less than 5: **DISQUALIFY OR NURTURE**
