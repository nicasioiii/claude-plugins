# Qualification Criteria & Belief Ladder

## The Belief Ladder Checklist (Gordon)

Seven beliefs that must be established BEFORE transitioning to pitch. Check each during discovery. An unchecked belief becomes the objection you face at the close.

### Belief 1: Pain

**What to establish:** They have a real problem keeping them from what they want.
**How to test:** Pain questions, specificity drills, duration questions.

| Signal | Indicator |
|--------|-----------|
| Green | Specific numbers, visible frustration, vivid language, secondary impacts identified, ongoing 3+ months |
| Yellow | Vague about numbers, mention pain only when asked, cannot trace secondary impacts |
| Red | Cannot articulate the problem, sounds like repeating something they heard, no emotional weight |

### Belief 2: Doubt

**What to establish:** They cannot fix this alone (or it wastes unnecessary resources to try).
**How to test:** "What do you think has been the biggest thing keeping you from getting this started by yourself?"

**Anti-pattern:** If prospect already says they want coaching and do not want to reinvent the wheel, doubt is already there. Do NOT spend 20 minutes creating doubt that already exists. Move on (Gordon).

### Belief 3: Cost

**What to establish:** Doing nothing is far more painful than the investment.
**How to test:** Cost/Why Now questions. "What made you draw the line in the sand?"

**Key:** Whatever you skip in discovery becomes the objection at the close. If you skip cost questions because they are uncomfortable, expect "I want to think about it" later.

### Belief 4: Desire

**What to establish:** Solving the problem yields a clearly better future.
**How to test:** Vision questions, future pacing, identity frame.

### Belief 5: Money

**What to establish:** They have financial resources AND willingness to deploy them.
**How to test:** Late-stage financial qualification (see below).

**Special rule:** Leave money to the END. Do not dig into finances prematurely or people disqualify themselves before you pitch. Exception: extreme red flags (food stamps, living in car -- genuine conditions, not objections).

### Belief 6: Support

**What to establish:** People around them (spouse, partner, business partner) will support the decision.
**How to test:** Front-load early: "If you wanted to be part of something like this, is there anyone you'd need to run it by?"

**Cross-reference:** If support objection surfaces at close, see `objection-handling` for the full spouse/partner process (Two Guys Reframe, respect vs. permission distinction).

### Belief 7: Trust

**What to establish:** Your solution is clearly different from and superior to what they have tried before.
**How to test:** Solution questions: "Have you worked with anyone before? How did that go? What was missing?"

**Key:** Position your solution as the EXPLANATION of why past attempts failed, not just another attempt.

## The Buying Pocket Model (Gordon)

A mental model for where the prospect sits on a belief spectrum:

**Left side (low belief):** Does not believe transformation is possible. Use stories, case studies, proof to move them RIGHT toward belief.

**Right side (high ego):** Believes they can do it themselves. Use skilled questioning and chunking down to move them LEFT toward humility.

**Buying Pocket (middle):** Believes transformation is possible, but ONLY with your help. This is where the sale lives.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- If they seem defeated: "What would need to be true for this to work?" (move right)
- If they seem overconfident: "What specifically has stopped you from doing this already?" (move left)

## Financial Qualification

### Timing Rule

Qualify financially LATE, not early. Alexander's setter approach puts it after desire is fully established: "If this program was exactly what you needed, do you have $5-6K available to invest?"

### Gordon's Financial Qualification Process

After double tie-down on the solution (prospect says they are certain this is what they need):

1. Open the conversation: "Are you comfortable having an open and honest conversation about where you're at financially?"
2. Get exact numbers: cash on hand (checking + savings), credit score / available credit, money coming in within 30 days
3. If they willingly share all this AND still cannot afford it = genuine condition (not objection)
4. If they WILL NOT share = trust issue (smokescreen for uncertainty)

### Alexander's Diagnostic

"Is this logistical where you don't have the money, or is it that you have the money but you've never spent this much on something like this?"
- If fear (has money, scared): treat as fear objection
- If logistics (truly does not have it): payment plan, deposit close, financing

### Ethical Line

If someone is a single mom with $500 -- do NOT try to sell them a high-risk program. Have morality (Alexander).

## Support/Partner Qualification

### The Front-Load

Ask early in discovery (not at close): "Are you married? What would your spouse think about something like this?" or "If you wanted to be part of something, is there anyone you'd need to run it by?"

### When Partner Surfaces as Issue

Two critical distinctions (Gordon):
1. **Respect:** They are going to do this regardless, just want to inform their partner first
2. **Permission:** They genuinely need partner approval

**Test:** "Is this a respect thing where you'll do this regardless and just want to let them know? Or do you need their permission to move forward?"

### Handling (Summary)

- If respect: deposit close or CC close (hold spot, follow up with partner)
- If permission: set follow-up call with partner present, use the Two Guys Reframe
- See `objection-handling` for the full spouse/partner objection process

## Disqualification Criteria

### Hard DQ (Do Not Close)

- Cannot articulate any specific problem (zero pain)
- Zero prior action in 2+ years of wanting change
- Undecided between multiple conflicting goals
- Not the decision-maker AND refuses to involve decision-maker
- Extreme financial distress (ethical line)
- "Just exploring" with zero urgency and no willingness to create it

### Soft DQ (Nurture, Do Not Close Today)

- Interest shown but timeline is 6+ months
- Financial constraint exists but commitment and desire are genuine
- Wants to "think about it" with genuine exploration energy (not smokescreen)
- Recently burned -- needs trust-building over time

### The "Not Ready Yet" Framework (Alexander)

When you sense they are not ready, remove pressure:
- "This probably isn't something that makes sense for you right now"
- "Let's book a call when it does make sense"
- "I'll send you value content so you can keep this top of mind"

Removing pressure paradoxically increases follow-up engagement. Route to `pipeline-followup` for nurture sequencing.

## The 1-10 Frame (Alexander, Gordon)

When a prospect gives uncertain or low-energy response to any check-in:

1. "On a scale of 1-10, 1 being this makes no sense, 10 being you're ready -- where are you?"
2. "What's keeping you from being a 10?"
3. Handle whatever they reveal, THEN proceed

Use this throughout discovery (not just at close) to calibrate readiness in real time.

## Socratic Dialogue and Consistency Bias (Gordon)

The highest driver of human behavior is the drive to appear consistent with what we have already said (Cialdini). When prospects express the seven beliefs through YOUR questions, they are telling THEMSELVES. This makes it incongruent for them NOT to move forward.

**The rule:** "If you say it, it's shit. If they say it, it's true." Write down their exact words and weave them into your pitch and close.
