# Discovery Question Frameworks & Libraries

## Overview

This reference contains the most effective discovery questions organized by framework, stage, and offer type. All questions are field-tested across high-ticket B2B and coaching offers.

---

## SECTION 1: Core Discovery Question Bank

### Level 1: Current Situation Questions

**Purpose**: Establish baseline understanding of their world before diagnosing problems.

```
"What do you do for work right now?"
"How long have you been in [their current role/business]?"
"Walk me through how you're currently handling [X]?"
"What's your current process for [their challenge area]?"
"How many people are on your team?"
"What's working in your situation right now?"
"What's not working?"
```

**Why these work**: Open-ended, non-leading, let them establish context.

---

### Level 2: Problem & Pain Questions

**Purpose**: Identify specific gaps between current and desired state.

```
"What's been the biggest challenge with [situation]?"
"How long have you been dealing with this?"
"When did this start becoming a problem?"
"What specifically frustrates you most about [X]?"
"What have you tried so far to solve this?"
"Why didn't that work?"
"What's kept you from trying [alternative solution]?"
```

**Advanced variation**: "What do you mean specifically?" (after any vague answer)
- This forces the prospect to get detailed and emotional

---

### Level 3: Impact & Cost Questions

**Purpose**: Link the pain to real consequences (revenue, time, relationships, opportunity).

```
"How does that impact your [business/work/health/relationships]?"
"How much time does this cost you per week/month?"
"How much money are you losing because of this?"
"What's the ripple effect? What else is affected?"
"If this problem continues, where will you be in 12 months?"
"What opportunities are you missing because of this?"
"How is this affecting your [specific stated value]?"
```

**Pattern**: Primary pain → Secondary impact
- Primary: "I don't have enough leads"
- Secondary: "How does that affect your ability to hire/take time off/plan your future?"

---

### Level 4: Goal & Vision Questions

**Purpose**: Clarify desired outcome and make it tangible.

```
"What's your ideal outcome?"
"Where do you want to be in 6 months/1 year?"
"What would be different if you solved this?"
"What would [desired outcome] make possible for you?"
"What's your target [revenue/lead volume/weight/etc]?"
"How much do you want to make?"
"What would real success look like for you?"
```

**Critical rule**: Get SPECIFIC numbers.
- Bad: "I want to make more money"
- Good: "I want to make $15,000 per month"

---

### Level 5: Consequence & Urgency Questions

**Purpose**: Create urgency by articulating cost of inaction.

```
"So why is RIGHT NOW the right time for you to change this?"
"How long have you been wanting to solve this?"
"What happens if nothing changes in the next 12 months?"
"What's the cost of staying where you are?"
"What are you sacrificing by not solving this?"
"Why haven't you already done this yourself?"
"If I could wave a magic wand and solve this instantly, what would change?"
```

**Most powerful form**: "If nothing changes with [their problem], what does your life/business look like in 5-10 years?"

## SECTION 2: By Offer Type

### B2B/Business Scaling (SaaS, Agency Services, Consulting)

**Current Situation**:
```
"Tell me about your business. What do you do?"
"How many customers/clients are you serving?"
"How many opportunities came in last month?"
"How many did you convert?"
"Where are those opportunities coming from?"
```

**Problems**:
```
"What's the #1 bottleneck in your business right now?"
"Are you stuck on leads, conversion, delivery, or retention?"
"What's your current close rate on conversations?"
"Why aren't you closing at [higher rate]?"
"What's preventing you from hitting $[target revenue]?"
```

**Impact**:
```
"How much revenue is that bottleneck costing you monthly?"
"What would you be able to do if you solved this?"
"Could you scale your team if you had that solution?"
"How much could you increase prices if you removed [problem]?"
```

**Goals**:
```
"What's your revenue target for next year?"
"How many clients do you want to serve?"
"What's the lifestyle you're trying to create?"
"What does a successful business look like to you?"
```

---

### High-Ticket Coaching/Personal Transformation

**Current Situation**:
```
"What are you doing for work/health/life right now?"
"How satisfied are you on a scale of 1-10?"
"Walk me through a typical day for you"
"What's one thing you've been wanting to change?"
```

**Problems**:
```
"What's been the biggest obstacle to changing this?"
"What have you tried in the past?"
"Why didn't those work?"
"What's kept you from getting the body/income/lifestyle you want?"
"What would it cost you if nothing changes?"
```

**Impact**:
```
"How is this affecting your energy/mood/relationships?"
"What opportunities are you missing?"
"How would your life change if you solved this?"
"What would this make possible in your personal relationships?"
"What would this allow you to teach/model for your kids?"
```

**Goals**:
```
"What's your ideal weight/income/lifestyle?"
"How would you feel if you achieved this?"
"What would that freedom give you?"
"Who would you become once you solved this?"
```

---

### Low-Ticket Offers / $40-500 Range

**Current Situation**:
```
"What brings you here today?"
"What's the biggest challenge you're facing?"
"Have you looked into solutions before?"
```

**Interest Level Check**:
```
"On a scale of 1-10, how interested are you in [solution]?"
"What would move you from a [current number] to a 7?"
"Is this something you want to explore?"
```

**Goals**:
```
"What would success look like for you?"
"How would solving this change your situation?"
```

**Much shorter discovery (5-15 min)**. Focus on interest level, not deep pain.

## SECTION 3: The Belief Ladder Deep Dive

Cole Gordon's belief ladder forces you to dig for root motivation.

**Level 1: Surface Problem**
```
"What brought you to reach out?"
→ They state the symptom
```

**Level 2: Why Does That Matter?**
```
"Why is [symptom] important to you?"
→ They state first-level impact
```

**Level 3: What Would That Give You?**
```
"What would [outcome] give you?"
→ They state deeper desire
```

**Level 4: Why Is THAT Important?**
```
"And why is [that outcome] important to you?"
→ They state life-level desire
```

**Level 5: Identity Level (Where emotion lives)**
```
"So at the core, what's this really about for you?"
→ They reveal identity-level belief (freedom, security, significance, legacy)
```

**Example Chain**:
- "I want more leads"
- "Why?" "Because I'm not making enough money"
- "What would more money give you?" "Freedom to take time off"
- "Why is time off important?" "To be present with my family"
- "What does family time give you?" "The ability to be a good father, which defines who I am"

**That's** where the real buy button is: identity-level desire.

## SECTION 4: Qualification Questions (Decision Clarity)

**Does this solution fit them?**
```
"Based on what you've described, what solution would solve this?"
"What have you heard about [your type of solution]?"
"Do you believe this type of solution actually works?"
"Do you think it could work for YOUR situation specifically?"
"What would need to be true for this to work for you?"
```

**Can they invest?**
```
"What access do you have to invest in solving this?"
"What would a reasonable investment feel like to you?"
"If I had the perfect solution, what's the budget range?"
"Is this a now purchase or are we waiting for [specific time]?"
```

**Are they the decision-maker?**
```
"Is this a decision you make solo, or does someone else need to approve?"
"Who else would need to sign off on this?"
"Would [partner/team member] need to be involved?"
"Are they as interested in solving this as you are?"
```

**Are they ready NOW?**
```
"Why is right now the time to solve this?"
"How long have you been wanting to change this?"
"What would happen if we waited until [future time]?"
"Could you see yourself moving on this in the next 30 days?"
"On a scale of 1-10, how ready are you to make a change?"
```

## SECTION 5: When Discovery Isn't Enough (Disqualify Frame)

These questions help identify if someone should be disqualified or nurtured:

**To identify confusion**:
```
"So you're interested in [X], but also considering [Y]. How do you see those connecting?"
"What would make [X] the right choice for you?"
"I'm sensing you're not 100% sure this is the right move. What's holding you back?"
```

**To identify action-taking capability**:
```
"You mentioned you've been wanting to change this for [timeframe]. What's kept you from doing it?"
"Have you invested in similar solutions before?"
"How do you typically make big decisions about your business/life?"
"When you commit to something, how serious are you about following through?"
```

**To identify readiness**:
```
"Does this feel like the right time to move forward, or do you need more time to think?"
"What would need to happen for you to be 100% ready?"
"If I could prove this works and remove all risk, would you be ready to go?"
```

## SECTION 6: Advanced Techniques

### The "Specifically" Drill

When they give vague answers, force specificity:

```
Prospect: "I want to make more money"
You: "What do you mean specifically?"
Prospect: "More like 10K a month"
You: "How much are you making now specifically?"
Prospect: "About 5K"
You: "So you want to 2x your income. What would that make possible for you specifically?"
```

Each "specifically" forces them deeper into emotion and clarity.

### The Stretching Time Technique

Quantifying duration increases pain perception:

```
"So you said you've been dealing with this. How long exactly?"
"Three weeks or three years?"
"Walk me through how it started..."
"And you've been doing [workaround] the whole time?"
"That's [X] weeks/months of your time. What's that cost you?"
```

### The Two Truths Technique

When they're being defensive, lead with the positive:

```
"What do you like about [current situation]?"
→ They acknowledge the positive
"And if you could change one thing, what would it be?"
→ Now they feel safe admitting pain
"What would be different if you could change that?"
```

### The Isolation & Specificity Pattern

Break vague problems into specifics:

```
"You said [general problem]. What do you mean specifically?"
→ Get one specific example
"Has that happened before?"
→ Create pattern recognition
"How does that make you feel when it happens?"
→ Activate emotion
"What would change if this never happened again?"
→ Paint the vision
```

## SECTION 7: Question Selection by Situation

### High Energy/Enthusiastic Prospect
Use deeper vision questions. They're engaged.
```
"What would be possible if you solved this?"
"How would your life change?"
"What would success look like?"
```

### Low Energy/Skeptical Prospect
Use problem/pain questions. Get them activated.
```
"What's the biggest frustration?"
"How long have you been dealing with this?"
"What's it costing you?"
```

### Prospects Who Talk A Lot
Use focused questions that force specificity.
```
"What do you mean specifically?"
"Can you give me one specific example?"
"How much exactly?"
```

### Quiet/Reserved Prospects
Use open-ended questions and create space.
```
"Tell me about your situation"
"What brings you here?"
"Walk me through how you got here"
[Then pause. Let silence work.]
```

### Time-Conscious Prospects
Lead with goals, get to decision faster.
```
"What are you looking to solve?"
"What would success look like?"
"Is this something you'd want to explore?"
```

### Analytical/Data-Driven Prospects
Use specific numbers and logic.
```
"What's your current metric for [X]?"
"What target are you aiming for?"
"How much impact would [solution] need to have?"
```

## SECTION 8: The Questions NOT to Ask

**Don't ask**:
```
❌ "Are you interested?" (yes/no kills conversation)
❌ "Do you want to hear about my solution?" (you haven't earned it)
❌ "Is money an issue?" (too direct, they'll say yes even if it's not)
❌ "Do you think this would help?" (leading, tells them your answer)
❌ "Would you like to get together sometime?" (vague)
❌ Generic statements posing as questions (they can ignore)
```

**Do ask**:
```
✓ "How would that change things?" (open, forces thinking)
✓ "What does that look like?" (makes it tangible)
✓ "Why is that important?" (gets to motivation)
✓ "What have you tried?" (assesses past action)
✓ "When do you want to solve this?" (creates timeline)
✓ Calibrated "how" questions (keeps them engaged and thinking)
```

---

## Quick Reference: The 5-Question Minimum Discovery

If time is limited, ALWAYS ask these 5:

1. **Current Situation**: "What's your situation with [X] right now?"
2. **Pain**: "What's the biggest challenge?"
3. **Have They Tried**: "What have you done to solve this?"
4. **Impact**: "How much is this costing you?"
5. **Urgency**: "Why is now the right time?"

These five tell you everything about whether to close, nurture, or disqualify.
