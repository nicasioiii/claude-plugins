# Discovery Question Frameworks

## Problems First Syntax -- Complete Flow (Gordon)

The default syntax for prospects with diagnosable problems (B2B, agencies, coaching, health/fitness).

### Step-by-Step Execution

**1. Isolate the Problem**
- "What would you say right now is your biggest challenge? What's not working at the level you truly feel like it could be?"
- "What's the #1 bottleneck in your business right now?"

**2. Probe Deeper**
- "Tell me more about that"
- "When you say ___, what do you mean exactly?"
- "What's the worst part about that?"

**3. Widen the Gap (Chunk Down on Pain)**
- Surface emotional impact beyond the stated problem
- "How does that show up in your [business/life/relationships]?"
- Always elicit how the problem impacts ONE other area of life

**4. Duration**
- "How long has this been going on?"
- "Take me back to the day you drew the line in the sand and decided enough was enough -- what happened?"

**5. Doubt Questions**
- "What do you think has been the biggest thing keeping you from getting this started by yourself?"
- "Why haven't you been able to fix this on your own?"

**6. Solution Questions**
- "Have you reached out to anyone else to get help?"
- "Are you working with anyone now?"
- "Have you done coaching/consulting before? How did that go?"

**7. Cost / Why Now**
- "What made you draw the line in the sand and say this has got to become a priority NOW?"
- "What's this costing you -- in revenue, time, stress, opportunity?"

**8. Financial Qualifier (Late Stage)**
- "What access do you have to invest in solving this?"
- Keep it brief; do not dig deep into finances prematurely

**9. Support Check**
- "Are you married? What would your spouse think about something like this?"
- "If you wanted to move forward with something, is there anyone you'd need to loop in?"

**10. Desired Situation**
- "Ultimately, what's the goal? What's the monetary goal? Why that number?"
- "What are the personal goals you want your business to allow you to achieve?"

## Goals First Syntax -- Complete Flow (Gordon)

For new opportunities, confused prospects, or situations where they cannot diagnose the problem.

**1.** "Ultimately, what's the goal?"
**2.** Probe on goal + assign specific numbers
**3.** Monetary goal + "Why that number?"
**4.** Long-term vision
**5.** Non-monetary goals
**6.** Current situation: "What do you do now for work?"
**7.** "Do you like it?" (branch based on answer)
**8.** "After all that time, what was the final straw?"
**9.** Doubt questions
**10.** Solution questions
**11.** Cost / Why Now
**12.** Support questions

**When to switch from Problems First to Goals First:** (1) new opportunity/biz-op, (2) prospect cannot diagnose their problem, (3) going in circles/confused, (4) using a wrong method that does not align with your solution.

## Calibrated Questions (Voss)

Open-ended questions using "what" and "how" that give the prospect the feeling of control while you direct the conversation.

### Word Selection Rules

| Use | Avoid |
|-----|-------|
| What | Can, Is, Are, Do, Does (binary yes/no) |
| How | Who, When, Where (single-word answers) |
| Why (rarely) | Why (usually accusatory) |

**Why exception:** When you want them to defend YOU: "Why would you choose us?"
**Substitute "what" for "why":** "What caused you to..." vs. "Why did you..."

### Standby Calibrated Questions

- "What's the biggest challenge you face?"
- "What about this is important to you?"
- "How can I help make this better for us?"
- "How would you like me to proceed?"
- "What are we trying to accomplish here?"
- "How am I supposed to do that?" (the most powerful calibrated question)

### Bundle Questions in Groups of Three

Ask the same thing THREE DIFFERENT WAYS (not three different things):
- "What's the biggest challenge?" / "What's the key issue?" / "What are we up against?"
- "How does that help?" / "What problem does that solve?" / "What's the objective?"

### The Team Behind the Table

Always probe for hidden stakeholders: "How does this affect the rest of the team?" / "What do your colleagues see as their main challenges?" Ask these MORE THAN ONCE during the call (Voss).

## The Funnel Technique (Serhant)

Three-step questioning from forensic interviewing, especially useful for multi-stakeholder deals.

**Step 1 -- Top of Funnel (Open-ended):**
- Who/what/where/why questions
- Client does 80% of talking
- You fill the funnel with raw information
- "What are you looking for?" / "Walk me through your situation"

**Step 2 -- Mid Funnel (Guided):**
- More targeted questions that test theories
- Shows you care about their lives beyond the transaction
- "How often do you have family visit?" / "What does a typical week look like?"
- Guide them to answer for themselves why they want what you know they actually want

**Step 3 -- Bottom of Funnel (Specific yes/no):**
- Get the nitty-gritty details
- "Do you need X?" / "Is Y a deal-breaker?" / "Will you need Z?"
- Reveals hidden players and deal-breakers

**Key insight:** The funnel reveals hidden decision-makers. Without it, these players surface months later when the deal falls apart.

## Question Banks by Offer Type

### B2B / Business Scaling

**Current Situation:**
- "Tell me about your business. What do you do?"
- "How many customers/clients are you serving?"
- "Where are opportunities coming from? How many convert?"

**Problems:**
- "What's the #1 bottleneck right now?"
- "Are you stuck on leads, conversion, delivery, or retention?"
- "What's your current close rate? Why aren't you at [higher rate]?"

**Impact:**
- "How much revenue is that bottleneck costing you monthly?"
- "Could you scale your team if you solved this?"

**Goals:**
- "What's your revenue target for next year?"
- "What does a successful business look like to you?"

### High-Ticket Coaching / Personal Transformation

**Current Situation:**
- "What are you doing for work/health/life right now?"
- "How satisfied are you on a scale of 1-10?"
- "Walk me through a typical day"

**Problems:**
- "What's been the biggest obstacle to changing this?"
- "What have you tried in the past? Why didn't those work?"

**Impact:**
- "How is this affecting your energy/mood/relationships?"
- "What would this make possible in your personal life?"

**Goals:**
- "What's your ideal outcome?"
- "Who would you become once you solved this?"

### Selling to Affluent Prospects (Alexander)

Different pain points for different income levels:

**Corporate 200-400K earners:** Pain is NOT money. Pain is low leverage, no freedom, no time, ceiling on income, being told what to do. Key question: "What's the extent you have to go to in order to make that money?"

**High-net-worth (7+ figures liquid):** Extra $3-5K/mo does not change their life. Frame as ROI conversation, not emotional one. "Do you think an investment of $15K to make $36K/year with zero additional time is a good investment?"

## Advanced Questioning Techniques

### The "Specifically" Drill
When they give vague answers, force specificity. Each "specifically" forces them deeper into emotion and clarity.

### The Stretching Time Technique
Quantify duration to increase pain perception: "How long exactly? Three weeks or three years?"

### The Anti-Question (Alexander)
When there is not enough pain to work with: "What DON'T you want your life to look like?" Reverses the frame to activate loss aversion.

### The Going Negative Technique (Alexander)
After future pacing positive outcomes, reverse direction: "And do you feel like you're just not able to do that to the extent you want to?" This creates emotional contrast that makes the positive outcome more urgent.

## The 5-Question Minimum Discovery

If time is limited, ALWAYS ask these five:

1. **Current Situation:** "What's your situation with [X] right now?"
2. **Pain:** "What's the biggest challenge?"
3. **Past Attempts:** "What have you done to solve this?"
4. **Impact:** "How much is this costing you?"
5. **Urgency:** "Why is now the right time?"

These five tell you everything about whether to close, nurture, or disqualify.
