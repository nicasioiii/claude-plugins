# Discovery & Qualification Scripts Library

All scripts are word-for-word, field-tested across hundreds of calls. Adapt to your voice, but keep the structure.

---

## SECTION 1: Discovery Opening Sequences

### Opening 1: Direct & Professional

```
"Hi [Name], thanks for hopping on!
I know you're busy, so I'll keep this brief.

Before I dive into anything, tell me - what's your main focus
with [their area] right now?"
```

**Use when**: Closing-style calls, high-ticket, time-conscious prospects

---

### Opening 2: Light & Conversational

```
"Hey [Name]! Hope you're having a good [day/week].

So before we get into anything, I want to understand where you're at.
Can you walk me through your current situation with [area]?"
```

**Use when**: Lower-ticket, coaching, relationship-building focus

---

### Opening 3: Rapport-First (Setter style)

```
"Hey [Name]! I found you on [platform] and thought you'd be a great fit
for something we're doing. You got a quick second?"

[After they say yes]

"So tell me - what are you working on right now?"
```

**Use when**: Cold outreach, appointment setting, building initial interest

## SECTION 2: Discovery Question Sequences

### Sequence 1: The Full Discovery Flow (30-45 min)

**Opening**
```
"Before I tell you what we do, I really want to understand
where you're at right now. Can you walk me through your situation?"
```

**Current Situation**
```
"So what's your current setup look like?"
[Listen, take notes]

"How long have you been doing it this way?"
```

**Identify Pain**
```
"And what's been the biggest challenge with [what they mentioned]?"
[Wait. Let them talk.]

"Can you tell me more about that?"
"What do you mean specifically?"
```

**Dig Deeper on Pain**
```
"How long has this been going on?"
"How has that affected [other area of their business/life]?"
"Have you tried to fix it before?"
"Why didn't that work?"
```

**Quantify the Cost**
```
"So roughly, how much is [problem] costing you per month?"
[If they don't have a number]
"Well, if you're spending [time] on this, and your time is worth $[X],
what's that adding up to?"

"And that's on top of the [other impact they mentioned], right?"
```

**Goals & Vision**
```
"So if we could solve all this, what would be different?"
"What would that look like for you specifically?"
"What's your target [revenue/weight/freedom/etc]?"
```

**Why Now**
```
"So why is right now the right time to solve this?"
"How long have you been wanting to change this?"
"What happens if nothing changes in 12 months?"
```

**Attempt Archaeology (if relevant)**
```
"Have you tried any solutions like this before?"
"What was your experience?"
"Why do you think that didn't work?"
"What would be different this time?"
```

**Readiness Check**
```
"On a scale of 1-10, where are you at in terms of readiness to solve this?"
[If less than 8]
"What would move you to an 8 or 9?"
```

**Timeline & Authority**
```
"Would this be a decision you make on your own,
or does someone else need to be involved?"

"And if you found the right solution, could you move forward within the next 30 days?"
```

### Sequence 2: Short Discovery (B2B, 20-30 min)

**Current Situation** (3 min)
```
"So tell me - what's your current situation with [their area]?"
[Listen. If vague, ask "Can you be more specific?"]
```

**Problem Identification** (5 min)
```
"What's the biggest challenge right now?"
"How much is that costing you roughly?"
"Have you tried fixing it?"
"Why didn't that work?"
```

**Goals** (3 min)
```
"What's the ideal outcome for you?"
"What numbers are we talking about?"
```

**Urgency** (2 min)
```
"Why is now the right time?"
"When would you want this solved by?"
```

**Authority & Budget** (2 min)
```
"Is this a decision you make, or does [person] need to be involved?"
"Our solution requires $[amount]. Is that in the ballpark?"
```

**Close or Next Step** (1-2 min)
```
"Based on everything you've told me, this could be a fit.
Would you want to explore it further?"
```

## SECTION 3: Pain Discovery Deep Dive

### The "Specifically" Drill

```
Prospect: "We're not getting enough leads"

You: "What do you mean specifically?"

Prospect: "Well, we get some, but not consistently"

You: "How many would be consistent for you?"

Prospect: "We need about 20 a month"

You: "How many are you getting now?"

Prospect: "Maybe 5-8"

You: "So you're short 12-15 leads a month.
What does that cost you in lost revenue?"
```

The pattern:
1. Vague statement
2. "What do you mean specifically?"
3. They get more specific
4. "How many/much exactly?"
5. Quantify the gap
6. Link to cost

---

### The Time-Stretching Technique

```
"So you said you've been dealing with this. How long exactly?"

"Is it weeks, months, years?"

"Let's say it's been 3 years. That's 156 weeks of [problem affecting you]."

"What would your life/business look like if you had solved this 3 years ago?"

"So you've been paying [cost] for 3 years. That's $[total cost]."

"And you're probably going to keep paying it unless something changes, right?"
```

This creates urgency by showing the cumulative cost.

---

### The Secondary Impact Pattern

```
"You said [primary pain].

How does that show up in [different area of their life]?"

"So it's affecting your [primary area] AND your [secondary area]?"

"What else is being impacted by this?"

[Keep going until you've found 3-4 ripple effects]

"So really, this isn't just about [primary problem].
It's affecting everything. Is that fair?"
```

## SECTION 4: Qualification Scripts

### Testing Budget Capability

**Version 1: Direct**
```
"Our solution requires an investment of $[amount].
Is that something that would make sense for you financially right now?"

[Wait for answer]

If they hesitate:
"I hear you. Is the investment the issue,
or is it more about whether this is the right solution?"
```

**Version 2: Softer**
```
"What would feel like a reasonable investment to solve this?"

[They give number or range]

"Okay, and we usually work in the $[range] range.
Does that feel like it's in the ballpark for you?"
```

**Version 3: On second call (after they've expressed interest)**
```
"When you're ready on that end of the call,
you can let [person] know all your payment information,
or we can discuss that separately.

But first, let me make sure this is the right solution for you.
Does that sound fair?"
```

---

### Testing Timeline & Urgency

**Direct approach**:
```
"So why is RIGHT NOW the perfect time for you to solve this?"

[Listen to their answer]

If it's vague ("I've been wanting to..."):
"I get that. But what's different about now?
What would be different if you waited until [later time]?"
```

**Consequence approach**:
```
"If nothing changes with [their stated problem],
where are you in 12 months?

[Let them paint the picture]

"Does that feel like the future you want?"

[Usually: No]

"So we need to move fast. Could you see yourself
getting going on this within the next month?"
```

---

### Testing Decision Authority

```
"Is this a decision you make on your own,
or does someone else need to be involved?"

[They answer]

If solo:
"Perfect. So if this makes sense,
you can move forward whenever you're ready."

If someone else involved:
"Got it. Are they as interested in solving this as you are?

[Listen]

"Would it make sense to get them on a quick call
so we're all on the same page?"

Or: "When you talk to them, here's what I'd mention..."
```

## SECTION 5: Disqualification & Nurture Scripts

### The "Not Ready Yet" Script (Disqualify with grace)

```
"You know what, I really appreciate you taking the time to talk.

But I'm sensing this probably isn't the right move for you right now.
[Pick one reason: timing, budget, clarity on solution, etc.]

What I'll do is I'll send you some [resources/testimonials/case studies]
so you can keep this top of mind.

And whenever [condition: you've had the conversation with your partner,
the budget clears, etc.], let's reconnect. Sound fair?"

[They usually say yes]

"Perfect. I'm going to send you [specific thing] today.
Expect it in the next hour."
```

**Why this works**: You're not desperate to close. This removes pressure and often gets them MORE engaged. They'll follow up when ready.

---

### The "Already Confused" Redirect

```
"So you're interested in [X], but also looking at [Y].
Help me understand - how do you see those two connecting?

[They try to explain]

"I ask because usually when someone is juggling multiple things,
it means we're not 100% clear on what the real goal is.

What would be THE priority if you could only focus on one?"

[If they still can't decide]

"You know what, this might be a 'get clear first' situation.
Why don't we reconnect in a couple weeks when you've figured out your priority?

In the meantime, I'll send you some stuff that might help you decide."
```

## SECTION 6: Advanced Pain-Activation Sequences

### The Two-Truths Technique (for defensive prospects)

```
"Can I ask you something? What do you like about [current situation]?"

[They answer - and there's usually something]

"Yeah, okay, that makes sense.

And if you could change one thing about [their situation], what would it be?"

[Now they feel safe admitting pain because you validated the positive first]

"What would be different if you could change that?"

[Paint vision]
```

---

### The Isolation Pattern (Deep on specific pain)

```
"You mentioned [problem]. Can you give me a specific example?"

[They give example]

"Has that happened before, or was that a one-time thing?"

[Get pattern]

"When that happens, how does it make you feel?"

[Activate emotion - this is crucial]

"And if that never happened again, what would be possible?"

[Vision]

"How much would you give to have that freedom?"

[Quantify value]
```

---

### The Belief Ladder (Cole Gordon's deepest technique)

```
"So you want to [their stated goal]. Why is that important?"

[They answer]

"And why is [their reason] important to you?"

[They answer - usually getting more emotional]

"What would [outcome] make possible for you?"

[They answer]

"And at the core, why does that matter?
What does that say about who you are or who you want to be?"

[They hit identity level - this is where the real motivation lives]
```

You'll go from "I want more money" all the way to "I want to be able to provide for my family without working 80 hours a week" or "I want to prove I'm not a failure."

## SECTION 7: When They Give Weak Answers

### "I'm just exploring"

```
You: "That's smart. What specifically are you exploring?"

Them: [vague answer]

You: "I ask because if you're just gathering information
but not in a hurry, that tells me you're probably not ready yet.

Which is fine - are you actually ready to move on this soon,
or are you still in research mode?"

[If research mode: Nurture script]
[If ready: Continue discovery]
```

---

### "I need to think about it"

```
You: "I appreciate that. What specifically do you want to think about?"

Them: [reveals the real objection - price, if it works, etc.]

You: [Address that specific concern, not the vague "thinking"]

"So now that we've talked through [concern],
does it make sense to move forward?"
```

---

### "I can't decide between [two options]"

```
You: "Help me understand. If you could only pick one,
which would move the needle more for your business?"

[If they still can't decide]

You: "The fact that you're torn tells me you might not be 100% clear yet.

Let me ask you this - what does success look like?
Really specifically, what are the numbers?"

[Usually this clarifies what matters most]
```

## SECTION 8: The Financial Conversation

### When they say "I don't have the money"

```
First, clarify if it's real or not:

"I hear that. Is money the only thing,
or is there something about the solution
that's making you hesitant?"

[If it's really money:]

"Okay, I get it. Can I ask -
what access do you have to capital?
Any credit cards, emergency fund, partners who might invest?"

[If they won't answer: They're not serious]

[If they have options:]

"Most of my clients do a deposit upfront,
then break the rest into payments.
Do you have $[deposit] you could get together?"
```

---

### When they say "That's expensive"

```
"I get it. Let me ask you though -
how much is this problem costing you every month?"

[They usually can't quantify it]

"Well, think about it this way -
you're spending $[monthly cost of problem].

To get rid of that permanently would cost you $[your price].

That pays for itself in [X months/weeks]."
```

## SECTION 9: The Bridge to Pre-Pitch

When you've gathered enough discovery material, use this bridge:

```
"Okay, so let me just make sure I'm clear on everything.

You're dealing with [their pain],
and it's been going on for [duration],
costing you [quantified cost].

You've tried [what they tried],
but it didn't work because [why it failed].

And your goal is to [their vision].

Did I get that right?"

[They confirm]

"Perfect. I have some thoughts on how we might approach this.

But before I share that, are you open to some feedback
on what I'm seeing?"

[They say yes]

[Now you transition to pre-pitch]
```

```

"In other words..."
"If I'm hearing you right..."
"What specifically do you mean..."
"Can you give me an example..."
"How much exactly..."

"What would be different if..."

"What happens if nothing changes..."
```

These are the connective tissue that keeps discovery flowing and shows you're listening.

## SECTION 11: Discovery Call Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Say)

```
❌ "Does that sound interesting?" (yes/no, they check out)
❌ "I think you need..." (tells them what to think)
❌ "Most people in your situation..." (centers you, not them)
❌ "Let me tell you about our solution..." (not yet, still discovering)
❌ "So you're probably frustrated, right?" (leading question)
❌ "That must suck" (softens the pain - bad)
❌ "Oh, don't worry, we can fix that" (minimizes their problem)
❌ "Have you heard about [company]?" (about you, not them)
```

## SECTION 12: Your 5-Minute Pre-Call Ritual

Before every discovery call:

1. **Review application/intake** (30 sec) - Know what they said about why they're calling
2. **Look for pain signals** (30 sec) - What problems did they mention?
3. **Prep discovery questions** (1 min) - Know your first 5 questions
4. **Clear your mind** (1 min) - Take 3 deep breaths, get focused
5. **Set your intention** (1 min) - "I'm here to understand their situation deeply"

Then make the call with full presence. Not rushed. Not needy. Just genuinely curious.
