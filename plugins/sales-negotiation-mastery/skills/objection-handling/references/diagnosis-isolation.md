# Objection Diagnosis & Isolation Reference

## The Full Objection Taxonomy

### Category Details

**Smokescreen Objections (Never Real)**
- "I need to think about it" (INTAI)
- "Send me more information"
- "Let me do some research"
- "I want to compare options"
- "Let me check my budget"
- Detection: If you resolve the stated objection and they immediately find another one, the first was a smokescreen.

**Uncertainty/Fear Objections**
- "What if it doesn't work for me?"
- "I'm scared I'll fail"
- "I've never invested like this before"
- "What if I waste my money?"
- "I'm not sure I can do this"
- Root: Lack of belief in self, offer, or seller. Always emotional, not logical.

**Money Objections (Two Sub-Types)**
- Fear-based: Has the money but scared to spend it. Treat as fear objection.
- Logistical: Truly does not have the funds. Solve with payment structuring.
- Key diagnostic from Alexander: "If I came to you with a duffel bag of $3,000 free cash right now, would you use it for this program?" If yes, it is fear, not logistics.

**Partner/Spouse Objections**
- B2B (real partner with authority): Legitimate. Book three-way call.
- B2C (spouse): Often smokescreen. Test with respect vs. permission distinction.
- Detection: "If your spouse was on this call and said 'do whatever is best,' would you be 100% in?"

**Burned in Past Objections**
- "I got scammed before"
- "Last course/coach/program didn't work"
- "I've spent money on this before with no results"
- Root: Trust/solution belief issue. Past experience creates pattern of self-protection.

**Time Objections**
- "I'm too busy right now"
- "Maybe in 6 months"
- "Not the right time"
- Often smokescreen for fear. Test with: "Why is this important NOW rather than in 6 months?"

**Logistical Objections**
- "I get paid next week"
- "I'm in the wrong geographic area"
- "I need to move money around"
- Tactical/practical. Either solvable cooperatively or a genuine disqualifier.

## Isolation Technique Library

### The Three Isolation Questions
1. "What do you mean by that?" (opens the door)
2. "Besides [stated objection], is there anything else keeping you from moving forward?" (tests for additional blockers)
3. "Help me understand -- when you say [objection], what do you mean exactly?" (gets specificity)

### "How vs. Should" Isolation (Gordon)
After pacing the first objection:
- "Just to be clear -- you're not in a 'SHOULD I do this' place... it sounds like you're more in a 'HOW can I do this' place where you're certain, it's just a matter of making it work. Is that fair?"
- If they confirm "how" = route to logistics/money resolution.
- If they hesitate or say "should" = route back to uncertainty resolution.

### The Tie-Down Process (Gordon)

**Tie Down #1:** "Let's pretend money is out of the equation. How do you feel about the process specifically? Do you feel like it's what you need to get to [OUTCOME]?"

Outcomes:
- Bought in --> proceed to Tie Down #2
- Not bought in --> use 1-10 frame to diagnose

**Tie Down #2 (if bought in):**
- Option A ("How vs. Should"): Use when they have NOT yet mentioned money/partner
- Option B (Isolation): "So just to be clear, [OBJECTION] aside, there's nothing keeping you from being less than 100% certain this is what you need?"

**Step 2.5 (if NOT bought in after Tie Down #1):**
"I'd like to acknowledge you for being honest about that. Can I be honest with you for a second? You just told me you were a 9 out of 10 on the process because [their reason]. Now it seems like you're nowhere near that certain. So what's REALLY going on?"

This uncovers the real objection hiding behind money or "think about it."

## The 1-10 Frame (Full Process)

**When to use:** Any time you sense uncertainty but cannot pinpoint the cause.

1. "On a scale of 1-10, 1 being this makes no sense, 10 being you're ready to go -- where are you?"
2. Listen to both the NUMBER and the TONALITY. A "7" with uncertain voice is really a 4-5.
3. "What's keeping you from being a 10?"
4. Handle whatever they reveal.
5. Re-check: "Now where are you?"
6. Only proceed when they are at 9-10 with certain tonality.

**Common patterns:**
- 9-10 with certain tonality = move to close
- 7-8 = one or two unresolved concerns, usually identifiable
- 5-6 = significant doubt, likely missed something in discovery
- Below 5 = fundamental belief gap, may need to loop back to discovery

## Smokescreen Detection Methods

### The Second-Objection Test
Resolve the stated objection. If they immediately produce a new objection, the first was a smokescreen.

### The "Money Aside" Test
"Let me ask you this. Money aside, set that away for a second. When it comes to the program itself, is there anything else keeping you from jumping in?"

This separates real objections (fear, commitment) from price stalling.

### The Duffel Bag Test (Alexander)
"If I came to you with a duffel bag of [price] in free cash right now, would you use it for this program?"
- If "yes" but they "can't afford it" = fear-based, not money-based
- If "no" = different objection entirely, keep isolating

### The Contradiction Call-Out
When a prospect contradicts themselves:
"Help me understand -- earlier you said this was exactly what you needed. You told me you were ready. But now it sounds like this isn't even a priority. What changed?"

Leverages consistency bias -- people experience internal pressure to align words with actions.

## Labeling for Diagnosis (Voss)

Use labels to surface the real emotion behind the objection:

- "It seems like there's something here that's making this feel risky."
- "It sounds like you've been through this before and it didn't go well."
- "It looks like the investment is bringing up some concerns beyond just the number."
- "It seems like this decision feels bigger than just the program."

Labels as truth serum: described by Voss as "unlocking the floodgates of truth-telling." Once you find the primary emotion, their fears diffuse.

## Gordon's Four Reasons for Objections

Use this diagnostic to determine whether you have an objection-handling problem or a process problem:

1. **Logistics** -- Moving money, timing. Easy, cooperative. Handle directly.
2. **Process Breakdown** -- Whatever you missed earlier = the objection now. Fix the root cause.
3. **Conditioned Response** -- Reflexive resistance. Easier to handle if no process breakdown.
4. **Unclosable** -- Genuine disqualifier. Know when to walk away.

**Key insight from Gordon:** If you are consistently getting the same objection, the problem is NOT objection handling. The problem is earlier in your sales process. Look at discovery and pitch first.

## The Belief Ladder as Diagnostic Tool

Map the objection to the belief that is missing:

| Objection Pattern | Missing Belief | Resolution Focus |
|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| "I don't think I have a problem" | Pain (Belief 1) | Loop back to Hell Island |
| "I can figure this out myself" | Doubt (Belief 2) | Challenge with past attempts |
| "It's not that urgent" | Cost (Belief 3) | Consequence questions |
| "I'm not sure what I'd get out of it" | Desire (Belief 4) | Future pacing, Heaven Island |
| "I can't afford it" | Money (Belief 5) | Diagnose fear vs. logistics |
| "My spouse won't go for it" | Support (Belief 6) | Respect vs. permission |
| "I've tried this before" | Trust (Belief 7) | Why past failed + your difference |

## Pre-Handling During Discovery

### The Mental Block Technique (Alexander)

Best time to handle objections is BEFORE they surface at close.

1. During discovery: "In the past [X years] of wanting [outcome], what have you tried?"
2. If "nothing": "Why haven't you done anything?"
3. Dig until you find: "I didn't want to put money into something that wouldn't work out"
4. That is the mental block. Pre-handle it now.
5. "It's like that little voice in your head saying you probably shouldn't. At least we're aware of it now."

### Gordon's Belief Ladder Pre-Build

Build all seven beliefs systematically during discovery. If all seven are established before pitch, the prospect closes themselves.

- Pain: Get them to state their problem out loud
- Doubt: Get them to admit they cannot solve it alone
- Cost: Get them to say the cost of inaction
- Desire: Get them to articulate the future they want
- Money: Qualify financial capacity (late, not early)
- Support: Front-load the partner question
- Trust: Build through case studies and your diagnostic process

## Objection Handling Order (When Multiple Arise)

1. **Handle FEAR first** (removes emotion that clouds everything else)
2. **Then LOGISTICS** (builds confidence and momentum)
3. **Then MONEY last** (only after certainty is established)

Never handle money first. You will lose.
