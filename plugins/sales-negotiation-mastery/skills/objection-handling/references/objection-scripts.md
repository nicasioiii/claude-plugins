# Objection Scripts - Top 10 Common Objections with Rebuttals

This file contains word-for-word scripts for handling the 10 most common sales objections. Use these as templates - adapt them to your voice and offer, but keep the underlying framework.

---

## OBJECTION 1: "I Need to Think About It"

**Classification:** UNCERTAINTY (masquerading as timing)

**Why They Say It:** Not convinced enough yet. Something is missing - uncertain about process, results, or if it's right for them.

### Script Option A (Direct Approach)
**Closer:** "I appreciate that, and I think it's smart to think things through. But let me ask you - what specifically do you want to think about?"

**Why This Works:** They'll reveal the REAL objection. It's rarely about needing more time. Usually uncovers fear, logistics question, or value doubt.

**What They'll Say (Likely):**
- "If it will work"
- "The money"
- "If my partner will support it"
- "Whether I have time"

**Then Address That Specific Objection** (see relevant sections below)

### Script Option B (Temperature Check Anchor)
This works if you got the temperature check BEFORE price.

**Closer:** "Help me understand - thirty seconds ago, you said this was exactly what you needed and you rated it a 9 out of 10. But now it seems like you want to think about it. So what changed? Is it the price, or is there something about the process itself that's making you hesitate?"

**Why This Works:** Consistency bias. They said 9/10. That becomes their anchor. Asking "what changed" creates cognitive dissonance - they have to either move forward or admit something about the process isn't right.

### Script Option C (Isolate the Real Objection)
**Closer:** "Sure, and I totally get wanting to think it through. Let me ask you this - beyond the money and beyond needing time to think, is there any other reason you're not 100% certain this is the right move for you right now?"

**Why This Works:** "Beyond money and time" removes the easy outs. Forces them to articulate real concern (fear, doubt about results, logistics issue).

### Follow-Up (If They Still Want Time)
**Closer:** "Okay, I respect that. Let me ask - on a scale of 1-10, where are you right now? Like, how close are you to saying yes?"

**If 7-8:** "What would move you from a 7 to a 9 or 10?"
**If Below 7:** "What would need to be different for this to be a fit?" (May not be a fit)

### The Anti-Pattern
**DON'T say:** "Take your time, no pressure. Just let me know when you decide."
**This is weak.** It gives permission to ghost. Instead, get them to commit to a follow-up time.

**DO say:** "Perfect. I'm going to send over [specific material] that addresses [specific concern]. Let's reconnect on [specific time] so you can walk me through your thoughts. Sound good?"

## OBJECTION 2: "I Don't Have the Money / I Can't Afford It"

**Classification:** MONEY (sometimes), but often FEAR disguised as money

**Why They Say It:** Either (a) real budget constraint, or (b) not convinced enough of value to justify the cost.

### Script Option A (Isolate if It's Really Money)
**Closer:** "I totally get it, and I want to be real with you. Let me ask - if money wasn't the issue, if I handed you the capital tomorrow, would you do this?"

**Why This Works:** If they say YES → It's not money; it's fear or logistics. If they say NO → Money might be real, but likely combined with doubt.

**If YES (Not really about money):**
"Okay so it's not the money - it's something else. Is it uncertainty about whether this will work? Or is it something about the process?"

**If NO (Actually about money):**
"Got it. So the money is real. Here's what we can do..."

### Script Option B (Money Aside Reframe)
**Closer:** "Let me ask you this - money aside, all that away, right? When it comes to the program/offer itself, is there anything else that would be keeping you from jumping in and getting started?"

**Why This Works:** Separates real objections from price stalling. Often uncovers fear of failure, fear it won't work, or logistics concerns.

### Script Option C (Value vs. Cost Reframing)
**Closer:** "So here's how I'd think about this. You said you're currently losing [consequence they mentioned]. That's costing you roughly $X per month, right? So our investment of $Y is essentially buying back the value you're already hemorrhaging. Plus it prevents future loss. Does that math make sense?"

**Why This Works:** Anchors perceived cost of inaction against the investment. Makes your price look small by comparison.

### Script Option D (Payment Plan Solution)
**Closer:** "I get that cash flow is tight. That's actually common. Here's what we do - most clients don't pay it all upfront. What if we did a $500 deposit today, then two payments of $750 each over the next two months? That spreads it out and shows me you're serious. What do you think?"

**Why This Works:**
- Deposit shows commitment (sunk cost bias)
- Installments are psychologically easier
- You proposed it; they don't get to negotiate down
- Deposit secures the spot

### The Reframe on Investment
**Closer:** "Think about it this way - to get to $240K a year, you really only need 4 clients at $5K a month. That's achievable in 90 days with the right system. So the $5K investment is your ticket to that. Does that make sense?"

**Why This Works:** Chunks up and chunks down. Makes the goal feel achievable. Price feels tiny when you see the outcome.

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Ask "What payment plan works for you?" (gives them control)
- Lower price immediately
- Over-justify the price with features

**DO:**
- Propose specific payment options
- Tie price to ROI/outcome
- Use comparison principle ("vs. cost of staying stuck")

## OBJECTION 3: "I Need to Talk to My Spouse/Partner"

**Classification:** AUTHORITY/LOGISTICS (or sometimes FEAR disguised as authority)

**Why They Say It:** Either (a) real shared decision (legitimate), or (b) avoiding commitment by deferring to someone else, or (c) expecting partner will say no and want ally in rejection.

### Script Option A (Get Buy-In During Call)
**Closer:** "That's smart - I get that. What would your [spouse] want to know from me? What are their questions or concerns?"

**Why This Works:** Gets you inside their partner's head. Often reveals the REAL objection isn't from them, it's what they think their partner will say.

### Script Option B (Make the Partner an Ally)
**Closer:** "Great - that makes sense. You know what, why don't we get them on a quick call real quick so I can address their questions directly? Or I can send over a video/case study they can review. That way they're not just hearing from you, they're hearing from me. What's easier?"

**Why This Works:** Removes the "whisper campaign" dynamic. Partner hears directly. Often they're more sold than the person on the call thinks.

### Script Option C (Get Their Commitment First)
**Closer:** "So here's my question - if your [partner] said 'Yeah, let's do this,' would YOU want to do it? Or is there something about the offer itself that's making you hesitate?"

**Why This Works:** If they say NO → It's not the partner; it's them. If YES → Get them to pitch their partner and come back with approval.

### Script Option D (If Partner Objection is Smoke Screen)
**Closer:** "I'm hearing you need partner buy-in, and that makes sense. But real quick - is your hesitation coming from them, or are you uncertain about this yourself? Because I want to make sure we're solving the right thing."

**Why This Works:** Sometimes they're using the partner as an excuse. This flushes that out.

### Follow-Up Sequence
If they want to discuss with partner:
1. Send summary email with key points
2. Offer to record a video directly for partner
3. Provide case study showing similar family/partner situations
4. Set specific follow-up time: "Let's reconnect Tuesday at 2pm. That gives you time to chat, and we can finalize then."

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Say "Sure, just have them call me whenever" (too loose)
- Assume it's a real objection without isolating
- Let them leave without next step

**DO:**
- Make the partner an ally
- Provide materials/video for partner
- Set specific follow-up

## OBJECTION 4: "I've Tried This Before and It Didn't Work"

**Classification:** FEAR (fear of wasting time/money again) with LIMITING BELIEF

**Why They Say It:** Past failure has created belief that solutions like this don't work. They need to understand why it failed before, and why this is different.

### Script Option A (Three Reasons Framework)
**Closer:** "Yeah, I hear that a lot. Here's what I've found - when people try something in their space and it doesn't work, there are usually three reasons why:

1. **The strategy was outdated or wrong for their situation**
2. **The strategy was good but they lacked the implementation support and accountability**
3. **They had both the strategy AND support, but they didn't show up consistently**

Which of these three resonates most with what you experienced?"

**Why This Works:** Let's them self-diagnose why they failed. Once they identify which, you position your solution as addressing what was missing.

**If #1:** "That makes sense. What we do differently is [how your approach is different]. Here's proof from someone in your exact situation..."

**If #2:** "Got it. That's actually why we built [accountability/support element] into our process. Here's how that works..."

**If #3:** "I appreciate that honesty. That's why we structure it with [frequency of touchpoints]. But real talk - if you're not willing to show up, I need to know that now because I want clients who are actually committed."

### Script Option B (Different Approach Positioning)
**Closer:** "I totally get that, and I'm not going to tell you that approach was bad - it worked for some people. Here's what we've found though - most solutions in your space focus on [old approach]. But what we've learned is the real issue is [different root]. That's why our clients are getting [different results]."

**Why This Works:** Doesn't say they're wrong. Positions your solution as evolved understanding, not just more of the same.

### Script Option C (Personal Credibility Move)
**Closer:** "So I actually went through something similar myself a few years ago. Tried a solution, it didn't work. I thought the model was broken. Turns out, I was missing [key element]. Once I understood that, everything changed. I'm wondering if something similar is at play here..."

**Why This Works:** Shared experience. You're not above them. You understand the frustration.

### Follow-Up Proof
- Share case study of someone in similar situation who failed before, succeeded with you
- Provide before/after of someone with same limiting belief who changed mindset
- Offer testimonial video showing someone addressing their specific concern

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Say "This time will be different" (too vague)
- Blame them for past failure
- Dismiss their experience

**DO:**
- Acknowledge the failure was real
- Identify what was missing
- Position your solution as addressing that gap

## OBJECTION 5: "I'm Not Convinced This Will Work For Me"

**Classification:** FEAR (fear of failure, self-doubt)

**Why They Say It:** They see others' success but don't believe they have the same capability, discipline, or circumstances.

### Script Option A (Three Belief Questions)
**Closer:** "That's fair. Let me ask you three things, and be honest:

1. Do you think this HAS worked for other people? (Usually YES)
2. Is it possible it could work for YOU? (Push here)
3. Would it be WORTH it if it did work for you?

So if the answer to all three is yes, what's the hesitation?"

**Why This Works:** Gets them to build their own logical case for why it should work. Moves from emotion to logic.

### Script Option B (Make Them Identify With Success Stories)
**Closer:** "Okay, so you mentioned you're skeptical. I get it. Let me ask - do you see anyone in your network who's had success like [goal]? Anyone at all?"

**When they identify someone:** "Right, so if [person] can do it, what's different about you that you can't? Because from what I'm hearing, you've got [same capability/background/drive]. So what's actually the difference?"

**Why This Works:** Gets them to admit they have similar capabilities to people who've succeeded.

### Script Option C (Community/Support as Risk Reversal)
**Closer:** "Look, I'm going to be real - you WILL struggle at some point. Everyone does. That's normal. But here's why it's different with us: you're not doing it alone. You've got [community/1-on-1 support/weekly calls]. When you hit the hard part, that's when we're there. That's what makes the difference."

**Why This Works:** Removes isolation fear. They see support as safety net.

### Script Option D (90-Day Guarantee)
**Closer:** "Here's what I want you to know - if you go through the process, implement what we teach, and don't see results in 90 days, we'll set up additional 1-on-1 calls to figure out what's off. With our students, we KNOW the system works. If it's not working, there's a specific reason. We'll find it and fix it."

**Why This Works:** Risk reversal. You're betting on your system. They see you're confident.

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Say "It will definitely work for you" (sounds desperate)
- Dismiss their doubt
- Skip the guarantee/support element

**DO:**
- Get them to identify themselves with success stories
- Emphasize support/accountability
- Offer guarantee

## OBJECTION 6: "Your Price Is Too High"

**Classification:** MONEY (usually perceived value, not actual cost)

**Why They Say It:** Either (a) don't see enough value, or (b) haven't done the math on cost of inaction, or (c) comparing to cheaper alternatives.

### Script Option A (Don't Lower Price - Reframe Value)
**Closer:** "I hear you. And I get that price matters. Let me ask - is it that the price is too high for what it is, or is it that you're not 100% sure it will deliver the result?"

**If "Not sure it will deliver":** Go back to belief-building, not price discussion.

**If "Price seems high for what it is":** "What would feel like the right investment for [specific outcome]?"

**Why This Works:** Separates price from value. Often reveals it's not the price—it's the doubt.

### Script Option B (Cost of Inaction Anchor)
**Closer:** "So right now, you're at [current situation]. If nothing changes, over the next 12 months, what do you think that's costing you? In money, in time, in stress?

[Get number or estimate]

So really, this $X investment is a way to stop the bleeding of $Y per month. Does that math make sense?"

**Why This Works:** Anchors your price against the cost of staying stuck. Makes your investment look cheap.

### Script Option C (Installment Proposal - Don't Ask)
**Closer:** "I get that $5K upfront is a lot. Here's what most of my clients do - $1,500 deposit today, then three payments of $1,200 over three months. Spreads it out. Deposit shows me you're serious, and we'll make sure you hit results in that time. What do you think?"

**Why This Works:**
- You propose; don't ask what works
- Deposit is sunk cost (creates commitment)
- Installments are psychologically easier
- Shows path forward

### Script Option D (Compare to Doing It Alone)
**Closer:** "So think about this - if you try to figure this out on your own, what's that going to cost you? Your time is worth something. Trial and error has a cost. Let's say 6 months of figuring it out yourself = roughly $X in lost revenue/wasted time. Our program is $Y. The difference is we compress that timeline and remove the errors. Does that make sense?"

**Why This Works:** Reframes price as investment that saves money.

### Script Option E (What If I Removed Price)
**Closer:** "Let me ask you something - if price wasn't an object, would you do this? Like, would you want to join?"

**If YES:** Price was the objection. Offer payment plan.
**If NO:** It's not price; it's doubt. Go back to belief-building.

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Lower price immediately
- Overexplain why it costs so much
- Get defensive about pricing
- Ask "What would feel right to you?" (lets them anchor low)

**DO:**
- Reframe value vs. cost
- Use cost of inaction
- Propose payment structures
- Separate price objection from value doubt

## OBJECTION 7: "Can I Get a Discount?"

**Classification:** MONEY (but often a test)

**Why They Say It:** Either (a) testing if you'll negotiate, or (b) trying to get lower price to justify to themselves.

### Script Option A (Direct: No Discounts, But Payment Options)
**Closer:** "I appreciate you asking, and I respect it. Here's the thing - we don't discount the price, but what we DO offer is flexibility on payment. So instead of $5K upfront, you could do $1,500 down and three payments of $1,200. That way you're not taking all the hit at once. Does that work better?"

**Why This Works:** Shows price is firm (increases perceived value), but offers relief through payment terms.

### Script Option B (Limited-Time Offer Variation)
**Closer:** "I can't discount the core program - that keeps the quality consistent for everyone. BUT, if you jump in this week, I can throw in [bonus element] that I usually don't include. That adds about $X of value. So you're getting more, not less, by moving fast."

**Why This Works:**
- Maintains price integrity
- Creates urgency (limited time)
- Adds value without discounting
- Rewards fast action

### Script Option C (Flip the Question)
**Closer:** "I get the question. Here's how I think about it though - if I discount 30%, I'm essentially saying 'The first version wasn't fairly priced.' I can't do that because it's not true. But I AM confident enough to offer a guarantee: if you implement and don't see results, we figure it out together. That's where my confidence is. Does that make sense?"

**Why This Works:** Reframes discount request as challenge to your integrity. Positions guarantee as better than discount.

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Give discounts (teaches them to negotiate)
- Get angry or defensive
- Say "I could give you a discount but..." (then do it)

**DO:**
- Say no to discount
- Offer payment flexibility instead
- Offer bonuses for fast action
- Stay firm

## OBJECTION 8: "Can I Try It First? / Can I Get a Sample?"

**Classification:** RISK AVERSION (fear of commitment without proof)

**Why They Say It:** Want to test before investing. Fair concern, but if you give samples, they often don't convert.

### Script Option A (Reframe: You Already Are Trying)
**Closer:** "That's totally fair. And actually, you kind of already are trying it - we've been talking about your situation, I've given you [specific insight]. That's a sample of how we think. But here's the thing - you can't get the real transformation in a trial. It requires full commitment from you. That's where the magic happens."

**Why This Works:** Validates their concern but shows trial won't prove it works.

### Script Option B (Money-Back Guarantee as Trial)
**Closer:** "I get wanting to test it out. Here's what we offer instead - you get the full program, 90 days, and if you implement everything and don't see results, we refund you. That's your risk-free trial. But you get the full thing, not a watered-down version. Sound fair?"

**Why This Works:**
- Removes risk for them
- You put money where mouth is
- Full program; no diluted sample
- Requires commitment (which drives results)

### Script Option C (Limited-Time Access)
**Closer:** "Here's what I can do - you can jump in for 7 days, get full access to everything, see what it's about, and if it's not for you, you're out no cost. But I'm going to be real - you need to commit to actually going through the process. This isn't a "browse around" trial. Does that make sense?"

**Why This Works:** Time-bound trial. Requires commitment. Tests motivation.

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Give unlimited sample access (kills conversion)
- Let them "try before buying" indefinitely
- Show your best content in trial (saves it for paid)

**DO:**
- Offer money-back guarantee
- Time-bound trials (7-14 days max)
- Require commitment/action
- Charge a small deposit (gets them serious)

## OBJECTION 9: "What About Competitor X?"

**Classification:** COMPARISON (doubt about best choice)

**Why They Say It:** Not convinced you're the best option. May be gathering info before deciding.

### Script Option A (Acknowledge, Don't Bash Competitor)
**Closer:** "[Competitor] is solid, no doubt. They do [what they do well]. Here's how we're different though - we focus on [your unique angle]. And the reason that matters is [specific benefit]. For example, [case study of someone who tried competitor first, got better results with you]."

**Why This Works:**
- You're not insecure about competitors
- You position your differentiation clearly
- You show proof with case study

### Script Option B (Different Philosophy)
**Closer:** "So there's a few options in the market. [Competitor] is more [approach]. We're more [different approach]. Both can work, but the way we see it, [your philosophy on why your approach is better]. That's why our clients stick with us long-term and see sustained results."

**Why This Works:** Positions difference as philosophy, not price/features.

### Script Option C (Get Specific on What Matters to Them)
**Closer:** "That's fair - there are definitely options out there. Here's my question though - what matters most to you in choosing? Like, is it [option A], [option B], or [option C]?"

**When they answer:** "Got it. So if I could show you that we do [that thing] better than [competitor], would that change your mind?"

**Why This Works:** Get them to define their criteria, then show how you win on their criteria.

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Bash the competitor
- Get defensive
- Offer discount to "beat" competitor pricing

**DO:**
- Acknowledge competitor's strengths
- Clearly differentiate
- Use case studies
- Get them to define what matters

## OBJECTION 10: "Not the Right Time Right Now"

**Classification:** TIMING (often smoke screen for FEAR or MONEY)

**Why They Say It:** Genuine seasonal issue, or using timing as excuse to delay.

### Script Option A (Isolate if It's Really Timing)
**Closer:** "I hear you - timing matters. Let me ask though - if it was the perfect time right now, would you want to move forward with this? Or is it timing, or is there something else making you hesitate?"

**If YES to "perfect time":** Set specific start date. "How about we start in [X weeks] when things calm down?"

**If NO:** It's not timing. "So it's not about when, it's about whether. Got it. So what would need to be different for this to feel like the right move?"

### Script Option B (The Circumstances vs. Vision Reframe)
**Closer:** "I get that now's not perfect. Here's what I want to challenge you on though - if we wait for perfect circumstances, we never move. Perfect circumstances never come. It's really about making a decision from vision instead of circumstance.

So the question isn't 'Is now the perfect time?' The question is: 'Is this the move I want to make?' And if yes, we start whenever makes sense. If no, then timing isn't the issue. Which is it?"

**Why This Works:**
- Reframes timing decision as identity/vision decision
- Creates urgency without pressure
- Separates real objection from excuse

### Script Option C (Start Small / Phase It)
**Closer:** "I get that things are crazy now. Here's what we can do - how about you get started with [Phase 1 / minimal commitment] when things settle down in [timeframe]? At least you're in, at least you've made the decision. Then we scale it from there. Does that work?"

**Why This Works:** Reduces friction by phasing. Gets commitment to decision, if not full program.

### Script Option D (Cost of Waiting)
**Closer:** "So if we wait [timeframe], you're essentially pushing back your [goal] by that long too. Every month you wait is a month you're not [desired outcome]. Is the wait worth that cost?"

**Why This Works:** Quantifies cost of delay. Often makes now seem more appealing.

### Follow-Up (For Genuine Delay)
If they genuinely want to wait:
1. Get the specific date: "Let's say March 1st - is that enough time for things to calm down?"
2. Set specific follow-up: "I'll send you a summary, and let's reconnect February 15th to finalize. Does that work?"
3. Send value during wait: Monthly emails with insights, case studies, proof
4. Create urgency for follow-up: "Spots are limited. I want to make sure you're in."

### Anti-Pattern
**DON'T:**
- Accept "later" without specific date
- Let them ghost between now and future date
- Forget to follow up when date comes

**DO:**
- Isolate if it's really timing
- Set specific follow-up date
- Send value during wait
- Create urgency

## Framework Summary

All objections fit into FEAR, MONEY, or LOGISTICS. When you classify correctly, your response becomes obvious:

- **FEAR** → Provide social proof, testimonials, guarantee
- **MONEY** → Show ROI, offer payment plans, anchor cost of inaction
- **LOGISTICS** → Explain process clearly, reduce complexity, answer specific question

Always handle in this order: FEAR → LOGISTICS → MONEY. Never start with money objections.
