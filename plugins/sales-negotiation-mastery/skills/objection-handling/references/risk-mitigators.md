# Risk Mitigators Reference

## When to Use Risk Mitigators

Risk mitigators are leverage tools, NOT crutches. Use them sparingly and strategically.

**Gordon's rule:** Never offer payment plans until the prospect is tied down twice on certainty. Payment plans held in reserve become your most powerful follow-up ammunition (re-offer technique).

**Order of deployment:**
1. First, resolve the objection at the belief/emotional level
2. If genuine logistical barrier remains after belief is established, THEN offer a mitigator
3. Never lead with a mitigator -- it signals desperation and lowers perceived value

## Payment Plans

### Standard Split (2-Pay, 3-Pay)
- "We don't normally do this, but based on your situation, what if we split it into [2/3] payments of [amount]?"
- Present pay-in-full price first. Payment plan is the concession.

### Based on Pay Cycle
- "What do you have right now?"
- "When's the next time you get paid?"
- "How much could you allocate then?"
- Custom plan based on their actual cash flow.

### "Buy" Second Month (Gordon)
- Give 60 days to the second payment instead of 30
- For tight cash flow situations
- "We can push the second payment out to 60 days so you have breathing room"

### "Buy" a Month of Time
- Add weeks to the program timeline
- For timing objections: "What if we added 30 extra days so you don't feel rushed?"

### Financing Options (Alexander)
- "We have buy-now-pay-later options. Do you know your rough credit score?"
- Requirements vary: typically US-based, 18+, 670+ credit score
- Always verify they can afford monthly payments: if financed at $483/mo, can they swing that?

**Anti-pattern from Gordon:** Immediately offering payment plans without isolating. "We can do 2 payments of 5K or one payment of 9K" -- this gives away all your power. They get all info, get off the call, and ghost.

## Deposit Close

### Standard Deposit
- After full agreement on offer, introduce deposit as "locking in" the deal
- Typically 10-25% of total
- Frame as commitment, not cost

### $1K Deposit Close (Gordon -- for spouse objections)
- Refundable deposit to hold spot and draw a line in the sand
- Follow-up call where spouse can join
- "Let's put $1K down today. If after talking to your spouse you decide this isn't right, we'll refund it. But this shows you're serious and holds your spot."

### Deposit Psychology
- Small deposit = high commitment signal
- Creates sunk cost bias (less likely to quit)
- Filters tire-kickers immediately
- Positions you as premium/selective

### Handling "Why do you need a deposit?"
- "It ensures you'll show up. I don't want to waste your time or mine."
- "It's how we know you're committed, and how we justify dedicating resources to your onboarding."

## CC Close (Gordon)

When deposit won't work:
- Take credit card number but do NOT run it
- Acts as commitment device
- "I'll take your info now so it's ready when you are. We won't process until [condition]."
- Creates psychological commitment without financial commitment

## 24-Hour Refund (Gordon)

### When to Use
- Prospect is super close but wants to "sleep on it"
- They've passed both tie-downs on certainty
- The only remaining barrier is decision anxiety

### Script
"The best thing for you isn't hopping in if this isn't right. But the best thing also isn't doing nothing.

Here's what I'd do if I was you -- metaphorically draw the line in the sand and say 'I'm committed to no longer having [PROBLEM] and starting to have [DESIRE]' and step across that line.

And look -- if you wake up tomorrow morning and at the bottom of your bones you know this was NOT the right decision, we can refund you and circle back another time."

### Psychology
- Removes decision paralysis ("I can always reverse it")
- In practice, very few people use it (commitment bias kicks in overnight)
- Shows confidence in your solution

## 7-Day Refund (Gordon)

### When to Use
- Very skeptical prospect
- "Been burned before" objection
- Higher trust barrier than typical

### Framework
- Give them 7 days to experience the program
- Conditions: Must complete onboarding, attend first coaching call, engage with material
- "If after 7 days of actually trying this, you don't see the value -- we'll refund you. No questions."

### Why Conditions Matter
- Prevents tire-kicking (they must actually participate)
- By the time they've done onboarding + first call, they're invested
- The conditions themselves build commitment

## ROI Guarantee

### When to Use
- ROI-based offers only (measurable financial outcomes)
- Not appropriate for lifestyle/health/transformation offers without clear financial metrics

### Structure
- "If you don't hit [specific measurable outcome] in [timeframe], we [specific remedy]"
- Remedy options: additional 1:1 calls, extended program access, continued coaching
- NOT a money-back guarantee -- it's a commitment to their success

### Conditions (Critical)
- Must complete content
- Must attend calls
- Must reach out for help when stuck
- Must implement what's taught

### Script
"We guarantee results. If you do the work -- complete the content, attend the calls, reach out when you're stuck -- and you don't hit [outcome], we'll double down and figure out what's missing. Together."

### Why This Works
- Shows confidence (signals you believe in your solution)
- Most people WON'T use it (commitment builds through participation)
- Filters for commitment (they must accept the work requirement)
- Positions you as trustworthy ("we'll figure it out together")

**Anti-pattern:** Never position any guarantee as "free money back." Frame as "we're committed to your success."

## The Re-Offer Technique (Gordon -- Pipeline Context)

Payment plans held back on the initial call become your best follow-up weapon.

**Design the re-offer to overcome the original objection:**
- Not enough time --> Add 30 days to offer
- Not enough cash flow --> Pricing re-offer (2-pay or 3-pay)
- Trouble with lead gen --> Private workshop on lead gen
- Trouble with sales --> "Sales Intensive" call review
- Trouble getting started --> Reveal exact strategy influencer used

**Re-Offer Script (Messenger):**
"Hey [NAME] -- we're testing something new this week that'll help you [overcome their objection]."
"I know you said [objection] was the thing. When [influencer] brought this up I thought of you. Want me to send info?"
"No worries either way."

## Risk Mitigator Selection Guide

| Situation | Best Mitigator | Why |
|-----------|---------------|-----|
| Close but wants to sleep on it | 24-hour refund | Removes decision anxiety without real risk |
| Spouse needs to approve | $1K refundable deposit | Shows commitment, holds spot, allows spouse conversation |
| Truly cannot afford pay-in-full | Payment plan (2-3 pay) | Addresses logistics without lowering price |
| Very skeptical, been burned | 7-day refund with conditions | Gives them proof before full commitment |
| ROI-based offer, analytical prospect | ROI guarantee with conditions | Speaks their language (results-oriented) |
| Tight cash flow, committed | "Buy" second month (60 days) | Breathing room without changing price |
| Need commitment device, no deposit | CC close (hold, don't run) | Psychological commitment without immediate charge |
| Follow-up after initial call | Re-offer (payment plan as new offer) | Overcomes original objection with fresh approach |

## Trial Close Patterns (Gordon)

Use after applying a risk mitigator to test if it resolved the objection:

**Sharp Angle Trial Close:**
"So if you come in and we show you exactly how to get to [OUTCOME], is it worth it?"
"Are you sure?"
"Ready to get started?"

**Feel Good Trial Close:**
"How do you feel? Feel good?"
"Are you sure?"
"Ready to get started?"

**Keep It In Context Trial Close:**
Chunk up and chunk down to put the investment in perspective relative to the outcomes discussed.
