# Follow-Up Sequences: Scripts, Templates & Timing

## The 8-Touch System -- Detailed Scripts

### Touch 1: Same-Day Recap (Within 2 Hours of Call)

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: Quick recap from our call + one thing you should see

Hi [Name],

Thanks for taking the time today. I enjoyed learning about [specific
challenge they mentioned].

Based on what you told me, here is what a solution looks like:
- [Specific benefit 1 -- use their language]
- [Specific benefit 2]
- [Specific benefit 3]
- Investment: $[amount]
- Timeline: [X weeks/months]

I attached a case study of [similar client] who had the exact same
issue with [their specific pain]. Worth a look.

I will check in tomorrow. In the meantime, reach out if anything
comes up.

[Your name]
```

**Simultaneous Text:**
```
"Hey [Name], just sent you a quick recap email from our call.
Take a look when you get a chance -- I included something I think
you'll find interesting."
```

### Touch 2: Day 1 Phone Call (24 Hours)

**Script:**
```
"Hey [Name], glad I caught you. I was going over my notes from
yesterday, and when you mentioned [specific concern], something
clicked. I remembered a client who had the exact same hesitation,
and here is how we worked through it...

[30-second specific example]

I thought that might be helpful. Anything else come up for you
that we did not cover?"
```

**If voicemail:**
```
"Hey [Name], it's [You] from [Company]. Quick thought about what
you mentioned yesterday regarding [concern]. I found something
that directly addresses it. Shooting you a text with details.
Talk soon."
```

### Touch 3: Day 2 Voice Note or Video

**Voice Note Script (keep under 60 seconds):**
```
"Hey [Name], quick voice note for you. I was just on a call with
a client who literally had your exact same concern about [issue].
They ended up [result] within [timeframe]. I know that was a big
deal for you so I wanted to share. Let me know if you want to hear
more about how they handled it."
```

**Video Message (keep under 90 seconds):**
- Face to camera, natural lighting, casual setting
- "Hey [Name], I recorded this quick video because I wanted to
  share something I think is relevant to what you are working through..."
- Reference their specific situation
- End with: "Anyway, thought you would find that useful. Let me know."

### Touch 4: Day 3 Meme or Social Proof Text

**Social Proof Drop:**
```
"Quick update -- [similar client] just hit [specific result] using
the exact approach we discussed for you. Figured you would want
to know it is working for people in your situation."

[Attach screenshot of result/testimonial]
```

**Meme/Humor Option (for ghosted prospects):**
Send a relatable, industry-appropriate meme about decision-making or
procrastination. Humor breaks ice where formal follow-up fails.

### Touch 5: Day 5 Phone Call

**Script:**
```
"Hey [Name], it's [You]. I know big decisions take time -- totally
get it. I have been thinking about your situation and I wanted to
mention one more thing...

[Share insight, new angle, or additional benefit you did not cover]

What is your biggest remaining question at this point?"
```

### Touch 6: Day 7 Email with Case Study

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: [Client Name] had your exact concern about [topic]

Hi [Name],

It has been about a week since we talked, and I came across something
that made me think of you.

[Client Name] was in a similar spot:
- Challenge: [Their challenge -- same as prospect's]
- Hesitation: They worried about [specific concern prospect raised]
- What happened: [3-step specific approach]
- Result: [Specific metric/outcome] in [timeframe]

They told me the turning point was [specific insight]. I mention
this because you raised [concern], and I think the same approach
applies.

Worth another conversation?

[Your name]
```

### Touch 7: Day 10 Direct Text

```
"Hey [Name], haven't heard from you in a bit. Real talk -- is this
something you still want to move on?

Or should I send you some resources and we circle back when timing
is better?"
```

This forces a response. Either they re-engage or they give you a clear "not now" (which is still valuable information).

### Touch 8: Day 14 Final Push Call

**Script:**
```
"Hey [Name], it's [You]. Quick call -- I realized we have been going
back and forth for a couple weeks, and I want to make sure I am not
missing something.

I genuinely think this is right for you. So let me ask directly:
What is the ONE thing that, if we solved it, would move you forward?"

[Listen. They will say one of:]
- Price / budget concern
- Partner approval needed
- Uncertainty if it works
- Bad timing
- Looking at alternatives

[Then address THAT specific thing:]
If price: "What would feel right financially?"
If approval: "What would it take to get them on board?"
If uncertainty: "What would prove to you it works?"
If timing: "When would be the right time?"
If alternatives: "What are you considering?"

[If still hesitating after 2-3 rounds:]
"Alright, I hear you. This probably is not the right time, and that
is completely fine. Let me send you our latest results. Keep us on
your radar -- whenever you are ready, we are here. Sound fair?"

[Move to dead list -> DR campaign cycle]
```

## Text-Based Follow-Up Variations

### For Younger/DM-Oriented Prospects
Shorter, more casual. Higher frequency tolerance.

**Day 1:** "Hey [Name]! Quick thought -- you mentioned [concern]. Had a client with the exact same worry. Here is what changed for them: [1-2 sentences]. Worth talking through?"

**Day 3:** "Yo, sent you a video earlier of [client]. The part around [timestamp] is exactly what you were asking about. Check it out when you get a sec?"

**Day 7:** "No pressure, but wanted to see where you are at. Any questions I can answer? Or is timing just not right yet?"

**Day 10:** "Haven't heard from you in a bit. Real talk -- is this something you still want to do? Or should I send you some stuff and we reconnect later?"

## Follow-Up Method Selection Guide

| Prospect Profile | Best Methods | Frequency |
|-----------------|--------------|-----------|
| Young/DM-native | Text, voice notes, memes | Can go daily |
| Professional/corporate | Email, phone calls | Every 2-3 days |
| High-ticket/executive | Phone, video, email | Every 3-5 days |
| Previously engaged, now quiet | Meme, social proof, voice note | Pattern interrupt first |
| Explicitly said "not now" | Email with value only | Weekly max |

## Anti-Ghosting Sequence (Escalation)

**Source:** Gordon. Deploy when standard follow-up gets zero response.

### Level 1 (48 hours after last touch)
```
"?"
```
Yes, literally just a question mark. Gets surprisingly high response rates.

### Level 2 (72 hours later)
```
"Just bumping this to the top of your inbox :)"
```
OR
```
"Know it is easy for these to get lost. Still want to [their goal]?"
```

### Level 3 (5 days later)
```
"You alive? Lol"
```
Humor + concern. Low-pressure pattern interrupt.

### Level 4 (7 days later)
Send a GIF: "Ghosted" themed, "Hello from the other side," or
any humorous GIF about being ignored.

### Nuclear Option (14+ days, last resort)
```
"Have you given up on [their stated goal]?"
```
Source: Voss / Dean Jackson hybrid. Triggers loss aversion -- nobody
wants to admit they gave up on their own goals.

## Timing Rules

### When to Use Delay (Gordon)
- After initial no-close: Wait 18-24 hours before first follow-up (same-day email is OK, but no phone call)
- After sending detailed proposal: Wait 48 hours before asking for feedback
- After addressing major objection: Let it settle overnight; follow up next day with proof

### When to Be Relentless (Alexander)
- Prospect showed HIGH urgency on the call
- Deal is large enough for daily contact
- They explicitly said "call me tomorrow"
- Multiple competitors are in play
- They have a hard deadline approaching

### Frequency Caps
- Maximum 1 outreach per day (unless they are responding)
- Never same medium twice in a row
- After 8 touches with no response: move to dead list cadence (every 2 weeks)
- After 30 days dead: quarterly touchpoints only
