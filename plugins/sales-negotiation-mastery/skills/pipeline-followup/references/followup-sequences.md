# Follow-Up Sequences: Detailed Scripts & Timing

## The 6-Step Follow-Up Sequence (Luke Alexander)

This is the core follow-up system that works across different industries and offer types.

### Day 0: Same-Day Follow-Up (Within 2 Hours)

**Purpose**: Capitalize on momentum while call is fresh in their mind.

**What to Send:**
- Email with proposal summary
- Offer details (pricing, terms, timeline)
- Relevant case study or social proof
- Clear next steps

**Sample Email Template:**

```
Subject: Quick recap from our call + one thing you should see

Hi [Name],

Thanks for taking the time to chat today. I really enjoyed learning about [specific challenge they mentioned].

Based on what you told me, here's what a solution would look like:
- [Specific benefit 1]
- [Specific benefit 2]
- [Specific benefit 3]
- Investment: $[amount]
- Timeline: [X weeks/months]

I wanted to send this over while everything we discussed is fresh. One thing that stuck with me was when you mentioned [specific pain point]. I attached a case study of [similar company] who had the exact same issue - here's how we helped them.

I'll check in with you tomorrow to see if any questions came up. In the meantime, feel free to review and reach out if something doesn't make sense.

Talk soon,
[Your name]
```

**Timing**: Send within 2 hours of call ending.

---

### Day 1: First Follow-Up (24 Hours Later)

**Purpose**: Continue momentum, provide value, don't ask "did you decide?"

**Method Options:**
- Phone call (if they answered/seemed engaged on first call)
- Text message (if they're younger/DM-oriented)
- Email (if they're formal/cautious)

**Sample Phone Script:**

```
"Hey [Name], I'm glad I caught you. I was going over our notes from yesterday, and when you mentioned [specific objection/concern],
something clicked. I remembered a client who had the exact same hesitation, and here's how we worked through it..."

[Provide 30-second specific example]

"I thought that might be helpful. Anything else come up for you that we didn't cover?"

[Listen for response. Either address or say:]
"Great, well I'll send that over. Anything else I can help clarify?"
```

**Sample Text Script:**

```
Hey [Name]! Quick thought - you mentioned [concern] yesterday.

We actually have a framework for exactly that. Client [Company] asked the same thing.
Here's what worked: [1-2 sentence explanation]

Worth a quick call about it?
```

**Sample Email Script:**

```
Subject: One more thing about [specific concern they raised]

Hi [Name],

I was thinking about what you mentioned yesterday regarding [concern], and I wanted to add one more thing.

Most of our clients initially worry about [concern] because [reason]. Here's how we mitigate it:
[3-4 specific steps]

Quick example: [Client name] came in with the same concern, and after [timeframe], they saw [result].

Wanted to make sure you had this perspective before making your decision.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Timing**: 24 hours after call (not same day; shows you're not desperate).

**Key Rule**: Add value. Don't ask "did you decide?" or "what do you think?" Provide new information.

---

### Day 2: Second Follow-Up (48 Hours)

**Purpose**: Different medium, social proof, keeps momentum

**Method**: Use different medium than Day 1
- If Day 1 was call → Day 2 is email or text
- If Day 1 was email → Day 2 is call or text

**Sample Email (Social Proof Focus):**

```
Subject: [Client Name] just closed using this approach...

Hi [Name],

Thought you'd appreciate seeing this.

Quick video of [Company] talking about their experience. They had similar concerns
to what you mentioned (pay attention around 2:10 where they talk about [specific concern]).

After [timeframe], they're seeing [specific result].

Figured it'd be helpful context.

[Include video link or attachment]

[Your name]
```

**Sample Text (Short & Casual):**

```
Yo [Name], sent you something. [Client] had your exact concern when we started.

Here's what 90 days looked like for them: [specific metric].

Interested to see if that trajectory works for your situation?
```

**Timing**: 48 hours from initial call.

---

### Day 3: Third Follow-Up (3-Day Call)

**Purpose**: Phone conversation, warmer tone, check for obstacles

**Phone Script:**

```
"Hey [Name], it's [Your name]. I know when I'm thinking about a decision like this,
sometimes questions come up after the call. Anything pop up for you?"

[Listen]

"Cool. I also wanted to mention - I attached a resource earlier that I think directly
addresses [concern]. Have you had a chance to check that out?"

[If yes: "Great, thoughts?"]
[If no: "It's short, maybe 3-minute video. Worth checking out because it might clear things up."]

"The other thing is - just want to make sure I'm available if anything comes up.
What's the best way to reach you if you have questions today or tomorrow?"
```

**Tone**: Helpful, not pushy. You're making it easy for them to say yes.

**Timing**: 72 hours (3 days) from initial call. Morning call is better than evening.

---

### Day 7: Follow-Up with Social Proof (1 Week)

**Purpose**: New case study, show pattern of success

**Email Template:**

```
Subject: [3-4 sentence description of client result]

Hi [Name],

It's been about a week since we talked, and I've had time to think about
your situation more. I wanted to share something specific.

[Client Name] was in a similar spot when we started:
- Challenge: [Their challenge]
- Timeline: They had [X] pressure
- Hesitation: They worried about [specific concern you raised]

Here's what we did: [3-step specific approach]

Result: [Specific metric/outcome], accomplished in [timeframe]

They told me the turning point was [specific insight]. I mention this because
you raised [concern], and I think you'd benefit from the same approach.

Let me know if this is the thing that unlocks it for you.

[Your name]
```

**Why Case Study Works**:
- They can see someone like them succeeded
- Addresses their specific objection indirectly
- Time has passed (not desperate)
- Pattern of success is clear

**Timing**: 7 days from initial call.

---

### Day 14: Final Push Call (2 Weeks)

**Purpose**: Direct, honest, "what would move you forward?"

**Phone Script:**

```
"Hey [Name], it's [Your name]. Quick call - I realized we've been going back
and forth for a couple weeks, and I want to make sure I'm not missing something.

I genuinely think this is right for you. So let me ask directly: What's the
ONE thing that, if we solved for it, would move you forward?"

[Listen carefully. They'll say one of:]
- "The price"
- "I need partner approval"
- "Not sure if it works"
- "Bad timing financially"
- "I'm still looking at other options"

[Then address THAT specific thing, not generic objections:]

If price: "What would feel right to you financially?"
If approval: "What would it take to get them on board?"
If uncertainty: "What would prove to you it works?"
If timing: "When would be the right time?"
If alternatives: "What are the other options you're considering?"

[Listen, then:]

"Okay, so if I [specific solution to their concern], would you move forward?"

[They either say yes or say another concern. If another concern: repeat the process.]

[If still hesitating after 2-3 rounds:]

"Alright, I hear you. Look, this is clearly not the right time, and that's
totally fine. Let me send you our latest case studies and testimonials.
Keep us on your radar - whenever this makes sense, we're here. Sound fair?"

[Move to nurture close - stop pushing.]
```

**Key Moment**: This is the "make or break" call. Be direct but not aggressive. They'll either commit or reveal they're not ready. Either outcome is valuable information.

**Timing**: 14 days after initial call.

## Value-Add Follow-Up Framework

**Instead of**: "Following up on my email, did you have a chance to think about it?"

**Do This**: Find a specific, relevant reason to reach out.

### Resource Follow-Ups

```
"I came across [specific resource] that directly addresses the [concern] you mentioned.
Thought it was worth sharing:

[Resource link + 1-2 sentence explanation]

Let me know if that changes anything."
```

### Social Proof Follow-Ups

```
"Quick update - [similar client] just implemented the [solution] we discussed
for you, and they're already seeing [specific result]. Figured you'd want to know
it's working for people like you."
```

### Insight Follow-Ups

```
"I was working with another prospect in [industry], and they had the exact same
[concern] you raised. Here's what we found: [specific insight].

Thought it might be relevant to your situation."
```

### Industry Update Follow-Ups

```
"Just saw this article about [trend in their industry]. Made me think of our
conversation. The reason I'm sending it: [1-2 sentences on relevance].

Curious if this matches what you're seeing on your end?"
```

## Text-Based Follow-Up Sequence (For SMS/Messenger)

Shorter, casual, frequency can be higher (less intrusive).

### Day 1 Follow-Up (Text)

```
"Hey [Name]! Quick thought - you mentioned [concern] on our call.

Had a client with the exact same worry. Here's what changed for them:
[1-2 sentence explanation]

Worth talking through?"
```

### Day 3 Follow-Up (Text)

```
"Yo, sent you a video earlier of [client name]. The part around 2:10 is exactly
what you were asking about. Check it out when you get a sec?"
```

### Day 7 Follow-Up (Text)

```
"No pressure, but wanted to see where you're at with this.

Any questions I can answer? Or is timing just not right yet?"

[If: "timing not right yet"]
"All good. When would be better? Let's lock it in."

[If: "I have questions"]
"Cool, what's on your mind?"

[If: crickets - give it 3 days, then Day 10 follow-up]
```

### Day 10 Follow-Up (Text)

```
"Haven't heard from you in a bit. Real talk - is this something you still want to move on?

Or should I send you our stuff and let you marinate on it for a bit?"
```

## Email Sequence for Cold Prospects (Reverse Follow-Up)

When someone didn't engage from the start but you want to re-engage.

### Day 1 Email (Subject Line is Everything)

```
Subject: I think I oversold this

Hi [Name],

I realized on my last email I probably came on too strong.

Here's the thing: I'm not sure if [solution] is even right for your situation
because I don't know [specific detail about their situation].

If you have 2 minutes, I'd rather understand YOUR situation than try to force
a fit that doesn't exist.

Sound fair?

[Your name]
```

### Day 7 Email

```
Subject: [Company Name] is doing this differently

Hi [Name],

Just saw that [Competitor/peer company] announced [specific initiative/product].

Made me think of what you're probably dealing with on your end. Curious -
are you seeing pressure to do [same thing]?

Just wanted to reach out because if you are, I think we have a perspective
you'd find valuable.

[Your name]
```

### Day 14 Email

```
Subject: This might not be for you - and that's okay

Hi [Name],

I'm going to level with you. I've reached out a few times, and I'm guessing
either:

1. Wrong timing
2. Wrong fit
3. You're already working with someone

All totally fair. But if it's just #1 (timing), I'd rather keep you warm than
let this go cold.

I'm going to send you our latest case studies and resources. Keep us in your
back pocket for when this does make sense.

Feel free to ignore this - no hard feelings.

[Your name]
```

## The Nurture Close Email

**Use when**: They're interested but clearly not ready to commit now

```
Subject: Let's pick this back up when timing shifts

Hi [Name],

I know I've been checking in, and I realize now might not be the right moment
for you. That's totally fine.

Here's what I'm going to do:

1. I'll send you 3 case studies of clients similar to you (shows it works)
2. I'll send you our latest testimonials (real people, real results)
3. I'll keep this in your back pocket

When things shift on your end - budget opens up, timeline changes, or you get
board approval - we're here and ready to go.

In the meantime, keep me updated on how things go on your end. I genuinely want
to see you succeed, regardless of whether it's with us.

Sound fair?

[Your name]
```

## Timing & Frequency Adjustments

### If Prospect Seems HIGHLY Engaged
- Follow up more frequently (can do daily without being annoying)
- Use phone more than email
- Assume they're ready to close soon
- Example: "Quick call this afternoon? 15 min?"

### If Prospect Seems MODERATELY Engaged
- Stick to 6-step sequence (Day 0, 1, 2, 3, 7, 14)
- Mix mediums (call, email, text)
- Don't exceed 1x per day

### If Prospect Seems LOW ENGAGEMENT
- Extend timeline (Day 0, 3, 7, 14, 21, 30)
- Use nurture close earlier
- Provide value-heavy resources
- Stop after 2-3 weeks if no engagement

### If Prospect Explicitly Says "Not Ready"
- Pivot to nurture immediately
- "When would be a good time to circle back?"
- Send value quarterly
- Don't force more contact

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

1. **Same message on multiple platforms** - If you email them, don't email again 2 hours later. Wait 24 hours, then different medium.

2. **"Did you decide yet?" messages** - Never ask about decision without new information. Always provide something: a case study, social proof, specific answer.

3. **Following up with questions** - Don't say "Have you thought about it?" Say "Here's what I found about [their concern]."

4. **Too much frequency** - More than 1x per day = annoying. More than 2x per week without engagement = desperate.

5. **Copy-paste follow-ups** - Personalize every single one. Use their name, mention something specific from your conversation.

6. **Ignoring objections** - If they said "too expensive," don't send them case studies about speed. Address the price concern directly.
