# Pipeline Management: Systems, Tracking & Daily Rituals

## Overview: Why Pipeline Management Matters

Your pipeline is your business. If you can't see where deals are, you can't move them forward. Top performers treat pipeline management like religion - daily updates, weekly reviews, relentless tracking.

**The Reality:**
- Reps without pipeline discipline lose deals they don't even know they have
- Deals stall because "out of sight, out of mind"
- You can't manage what you don't measure

---

## The 8-Stage Pipeline Model

### Stage 1: Lead
**Definition**: Prospect identified but not yet qualified

**Entry Trigger**:
- You found them (cold outreach)
- They filled out a form
- They replied to your email/DM
- Someone referred them

**Exit Criteria**:
- You've had a conversation to determine if they have pain/need
- You've confirmed basic fit (industry, company size, budget range)

**Your Action**:
- Qualify on: Do they have the problem? Do they have interest? Do they have budget (if applicable)?
- Book a call OR move to disqualify
- **Timeline**: Qualify within 48 hours

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- How you found them
- Initial pain signal (if any)
- Their stated goal
- Urgency indicator (high/medium/low)

---

### Stage 2: Qualified Lead
**Definition**: Prospect confirmed to have pain/problem AND interest in solution

**Entry Trigger**:
- You asked qualifying questions
- They said yes to: "This is a real problem for us" AND "This seems interesting"
- They have budget/resources (if applicable)

**Exit Criteria**:
- They've agreed to a deeper dive call
- You've scheduled a meeting with them
- They said "yes, let's talk to your closer"

**Your Action**:
- Send calendar invite
- Provide pre-call resources/agenda
- Prepare materials specific to their stated problem
- **Timeline**: Schedule call within 48 hours of qualifying

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- Specific problems they mentioned
- Stated goals
- Timeline they mentioned
- Budget range
- Key decision maker info
- Competitors they're looking at (if mentioned)

---

### Stage 3: Call Scheduled / Pre-Call
**Definition**: Meeting booked, prospect is on calendar

**Entry Trigger**:
- Calendar invite sent and accepted
- Pre-call resources sent
- Confirmation email/call happened

**Exit Criteria**:
- Call happens (or they no-show)

**Your Action**:
- Send 3-4 small pre-call videos (max 2 min each)
- Send case study related to their stated problem
- Send confirmation 24 hours before call
- Send reminder text 30 min before call
- **Timeline**: 30 min before call = double confirm (increases show rate 25%)

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- Exact call date/time
- Pre-call materials sent (checkmark)
- Show/no-show status
- Their demeanor (engaged, hesitant, etc.)

---

### Stage 4: Discovery Complete / Deciding (Post-Call, No Decision)
**Definition**: You had the call, they didn't close, they said "I need to think about it"

**Entry Trigger**:
- Call happened
- You made an offer
- They didn't say yes
- They said "let me think about it" or similar

**Exit Criteria**:
- They say yes (move to Stage 6)
- They say no/not interested (move to Stage 8)
- 14 days pass with no contact (move to Stage 8)
- They come back with questions (stay in Stage 4)

**Your Action**:
- Execute 6-step follow-up sequence immediately
- **Day 0 (same day)**: Send proposal email + case study
- **Day 1**: Call with value-add
- **Day 2**: Email with social proof
- **Day 3**: Call to check for obstacles
- **Day 7**: Email with deeper case study
- **Day 14**: Final push call - direct ask
- **Timeline**: Daily/every-other-day cadence for 2 weeks

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- Their stated objection(s)
- Urgency level (timeline they mentioned)
- Decision maker status (are they deciding alone?)
- Each follow-up action and response
- Next scheduled follow-up
- Follow-up sequence progress (Day 0/1/2/3/7/14)

---

### Stage 5: Negotiation / Objection Handling
**Definition**: Prospect is engaged, actively raising objections/concerns

**Entry Trigger**:
- They said "I like it but..." or "I'm interested but..."
- They're asking questions (not disappearing)
- They're showing engagement (responding to emails, answering calls)

**Exit Criteria**:
- Objections resolved (move to Stage 6)
- They ghost (move to Stage 8)
- They say no (move to Stage 8)

**Your Action**:
- Address their SPECIFIC objection (not generic rebuttals)
- Provide proof (testimonial, case study, guarantee)
- Get them on calls if possible (more persuasive than email)
- **Timeline**: Respond to objections within 24 hours

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- Each objection raised (word-for-word)
- How you addressed it
- Their response to your address
- New objections (keep list)
- When it resolves

---

### Stage 6: Ready to Close / Paperwork
**Definition**: Prospect has agreed in principle, no major objections remain

**Entry Trigger**:
- They said "yes" to moving forward
- All objections addressed
- Payment/contract discussion is next

**Exit Criteria**:
- Payment received (move to Closed/Won)
- They back out (move to Stage 8)
- They ask for more time (move to Stage 4)

**Your Action**:
- Send contract/payment link immediately
- Follow up if they don't sign within 24 hours
- Ensure smooth onboarding
- Get them started quickly
- **Timeline**: Payment within 48-72 hours

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- Proposal/contract sent date
- Payment received date
- Deal amount
- Commission earned (if applicable)
- Start date for service/program
- Onboarding point of contact

---

### Stage 7: Closed / Won
**Definition**: Deal is done, payment received, client is active

**Entry Trigger**:
- Payment received
- Contract signed
- Service/program access provided

**Exit Criteria**:
- This is an archive stage (stays here)

**Your Action**:
- Ensure great onboarding (first impression matters)
- Collect testimonial (ask 30 days in)
- Ask for referrals
- Monitor for upsell opportunities
- **Timeline**: Testimonial request = 30 days in; upsell conversation = 60-90 days in

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- Final contract terms
- Payment amount and date
- Client satisfaction notes
- Testimonial (if received)
- Potential upsell/expansion opportunity
- Renewal date (if applicable)
- Referral names (if given)

---

### Stage 8: Dead / Stalled
**Definition**: Deal is not moving forward (could be temporary or permanent)

**Entry Trigger**:
- 7+ days with no response
- They explicitly said "not interested"
- They said "maybe next year"
- They're working with competitor
- Budget was cut
- No contact for 30+ days without explanation

**Exit Criteria**:
- They re-engage (move back to Stage 4)
- You decide to truly give up (archive)
- They say "revisit in 6 months" (put in evergreen nurture)

**Your Action**:
- Send "nurture close" email
- Transition to monthly check-ins
- Stop daily/weekly follow-up
- Keep them on mailing list
- **Timeline**: Can take 3-6 months to convert from cold; don't force it

**CRM Notes to Track**:
- Reason it stalled (budget, timing, not fit, etc.)
- Last contact date
- When to circle back (if applicable)
- Notes on why it didn't work (learn for next time)
- Evergreen nurture cadence (if applicable)

## Daily Pipeline Ritual (15 Minutes)

### Morning (8:30 AM or start of day)

**1. Open your CRM/Pipeline list (1 min)**
- Filter to "Today's Actions"
- See: Who needs follow-up TODAY?

**2. Identify the 3 hottest deals (2 min)**
- Which deals close this week?
- Which deals have you not touched in 2+ days?
- Mark as your "Morning 3"

**3. Plan actions for each (5 min)**
- Deal #1: Send email with [X] or call about [X]
- Deal #2: Send [social proof] or address [objection]
- Deal #3: [Next step]

**4. Schedule your contact time (5 min)**
- Block 30-min call window for Deal #1
- Block email/message time for Deal #2
- Block logistics (re-scheduling, etc.) for Deal #3

**5. Execute immediately (don't wait) (2 min)**
- Send that first email
- Make that first call
- Momentum matters

---

### End of Day (Last 30 Minutes)

**1. Update CRM on all activity (5 min)**
- Log every call (duration, outcome, next step)
- Log every email/message sent
- Log every response received
- Status: Did they move to next stage?

**2. Note next actions clearly (3 min)**
- For each deal, write: "Next: [Specific action] on [Date]"
- This makes tomorrow's planning instant
- Example: "Next: Send case study email on March 7"

**3. Mark follow-up dates (2 min)**
- If you're supposed to call them tomorrow: date stamp it
- If they're on a follow-up sequence: mark Day 1, 2, etc.
- Don't rely on memory; let CRM remind you

**4. Document deal stage changes (3 min)**
- Did anyone move from Stage to another Stage?
- Update their stage in CRM immediately
- This feeds your weekly review

**5. Plan tomorrow (2 min)**
- Look at tomorrow's date
- See what deals need action
- Prepare any materials needed

**EOD Metrics to Track:**
- Calls made
- Calls connected (actual conversations)
- Emails sent
- Follow-ups completed
- Deal stage movements
- New deals added

## Weekly Pipeline Review (Friday Close)

### 30-Minute Friday Ritual

**Setup (2 min):**
- Pull full pipeline report
- Sort by stage (all deals in each)
- Open spreadsheet or CRM dashboard

**Stage 4 Deep Dive (8 min):** Deciding/Stalled Deals
- Which deals have been in Stage 4 more than 7 days?
- Action: Have you done the full 6-step follow-up? If not, execute.
- Which ones are ready to move to Stage 8 (dead)?
- Decision: Continue following up or move to nurture?

**Stage 3 Deep Dive (5 min):** Scheduled Calls
- Which calls are booked for next week?
- Pre-call materials sent? Confirm.
- Reminder emails scheduled? Confirm.
- Any no-shows expected? Address proactively.

**Stage 2 Review (5 min):** Qualified Leads
- How many qualified leads are you sitting on?
- Why aren't they scheduled yet? Schedule them now.
- If you can't schedule them, move to Stage 8.

**Bottleneck Identification (5 min):** Where are deals stuck?
- Are most deals stuck in Stage 4? You're not following up enough.
- Are most deals stuck in Stage 3? Calls aren't converting; check your discovery.
- Are most deals stuck in Stage 8? Your offers/positioning isn't resonating.

**Forecast & Projections (5 min):**
- Which deals are closing THIS MONTH?
- Which deals are closing NEXT MONTH?
- Are you on pace to hit your goals?
- If not, what needs to happen? (More volume? Better close rate? Faster follow-up?)

**Output: Weekly Action Items**
Write down the 3-5 things that must happen next week:
1. [Deal name]: [Action by date]
2. [Deal name]: [Action by date]
3. [Deal name]: [Action by date]
4. [Process improvement]: [Action by date]

## Monthly Pipeline Hygiene

### First Friday of Month: Deep Clean (30 min)

**Duplicate Cleanup (5 min)**
- Search for prospects with same name/company
- Merge duplicates into single contact
- Consolidate history

**Dead Deal Archiving (5 min)**
- Identify deals in Stage 8 longer than 30 days
- Decision: Will you ever re-engage? If no, archive.
- If yes, move to "evergreen nurture" category

**Contact Info Validation (5 min)**
- Verify email addresses are correct
- Update phone numbers if changed
- Note if contact moved to new company

**Stage Review (5 min)**
- Are deals in right stage?
- Example: If someone hasn't responded in 30 days, should be Stage 8, not Stage 4.
- Correct stage classifications

**Metrics Review (5 min)**
- Last month: How many deals closed?
- Average deal value?
- Average close timeline?
- What's trending?

**Output: Monthly Improvements**
- What will you do differently this month?
- Process improvement? Follow-up change? Discovery change?

## HubSpot HotList System (Cole Gordon Method)

If using HubSpot, implement this structure:

### HotList View
**Criteria:**
- Last contact within 48 hours
- Deal stage: Stage 4, 5, or 6
- Probability to close: 50%+
- Expected close date: THIS WEEK

**Purpose**: These deals close this week. Everything else is secondary.

**Daily Ritual**: Review every morning. Take 1-2 actions per deal today.

**Actions**:
- Call to close objection
- Send offer/contract
- Follow up on payment
- Address final concern

### WarmList View
**Criteria:**
- Last contact within 7 days
- Deal stage: Stage 4 or 5
- Probability to close: 30-50%
- Expected close date: THIS MONTH

**Purpose**: These deals need weekly attention to move them forward.

**Weekly Ritual**: Review Friday afternoon. Plan next week's actions.

**Actions**:
- Send social proof email
- Request follow-up call
- Address secondary objection
- Provide additional materials

### ColdList View
**Criteria:**
- Last contact 14+ days ago
- Deal stage: Stage 4 (stalled) or Stage 8
- Probability to close: 10-30%
- Expected close date: TBD or far future

**Purpose**: Long-term nurture. Not forcing anything, but staying warm.

**Monthly Ritual**: Review once per month. One action per deal.

**Actions**:
- Send industry update/insight
- Check in with "anything changed on your end?"
- Provide valuable content (no sales pitch)
- Acknowledge and accept it's not right now

## CRM Field Setup: Minimum Required Fields

### Basic Information
- First Name
- Last Name
- Email
- Phone
- Company Name
- Job Title
- Industry
- Company Size (# of employees)

### Stage & Timeline
- Current Stage (pick from 8-stage model)
- Date Entered Stage (auto-populate)
- Expected Close Date
- Deal Amount / Estimated Value
- Probability to Close (percentage)

### Engagement Tracking
- Last Contact Date
- Last Contact Medium (call/email/text)
- Next Action
- Next Action Date
- Follow-up Sequence Status (Day 0/1/2/3/7/14)

### Communication Log
- Calls (log each one)
  - Call date
  - Duration
  - Notes/summary
  - Outcome
- Emails (track opens/clicks if possible)
  - Email date
  - Subject
  - Opened? (Y/N)
  - Response?
- Messages/Texts
  - Date
  - Content
  - Response?

### Deal-Specific
- Original Source (where did they come from?)
- Stated Problem/Pain
- Stated Goal
- Competitor (if mentioned)
- Budget Range
- Decision Timeline
- Key Objections
- How You Addressed Each Objection

### Notes Field
Free-form notes on:
- Tone/demeanor during call
- Personality quirks (helps build rapport next call)
- Personal details (family, hobbies, etc. - relationship building)
- Specific follow-up needed

## Pipeline Math You Should Know

### Conversion Rates (Benchmarks)

**Lead → Qualified**: 20-40%
- If you have 100 leads, 20-40 will be qualified
- Action: Better qualification questions = higher %

**Qualified → Call Booked**: 80-90%
- If you have 20 qualified leads, 16-18 will agree to call
- Action: Easy - most say yes if truly qualified

**Call Booked → Show**: 60-80%
- Out of 20 booked calls, 12-16 actually show up
- Action: Pre-call materials and confirmations increase this 10-20%

**Call → Stage 4 (Deciding)**: 40-60%
- Out of 12 calls, 5-7 don't immediately close
- They go to follow-up sequence
- Action: Better discovery = higher close rate on call

**Stage 4 → Closed (2-week window)**: 30-50%
- Out of 5 deciding deals, 1-2 close from follow-up
- Action: Better follow-up sequences = higher %

### Working Backwards From Your Goal

**Goal**: Close 10 deals per month

**Required call volume**:
- If 50% of calls close: Need 20 calls/month
- If 40% of calls close: Need 25 calls/month
- If 30% of calls close: Need 33 calls/month

**Required books**:
- If 70% show up: Need 29 books for 20 calls
- If 80% show up: Need 25 books for 20 calls

**Required qualifieds**:
- If 85% of qualifieds book: Need 34 qualified for 29 books
- If 90% of qualifieds book: Need 28 qualified for 25 books

**Required leads**:
- If 30% qualify: Need 113 leads for 34 qualified
- If 40% qualify: Need 85 leads for 34 qualified

## Common Pipeline Problems & Fixes

### Problem: "Deals keep stalling in Stage 4"
**Root cause**: You're not following up consistently

**Fix**:
1. Use the 6-step follow-up sequence religiously
2. Add calendar reminders for every follow-up date
3. Prepare follow-up materials in advance
4. Vary your medium (call, email, text)
5. Add value with every touch

### Problem: "Deals drop off after first call"
**Root cause**: Your discovery/close is weak, or they're not actually qualified

**Fix**:
1. Review recordings of your calls
2. Are you asking deep discovery questions?
3. Are you building urgency around their problem?
4. Are you positioning your solution as answer to THEIR problem?
5. Are you actually closing (asking for commitment)?

### Problem: "I have lots of deals but nothing closes"
**Root cause**: Leads are low quality; you're chasing bad fits

**Fix**:
1. Improve qualification questions
2. Get "yes" on urgency AND commitment AND resources
3. Move unqualified prospects out of pipeline faster
4. Focus on fewer, better-qualified deals
5. Rather than 50 weak leads, have 20 strong ones

### Problem: "No-show rates are high"
**Root cause**: Prospects don't see the value, or logistics are unclear

**Fix**:
1. Send pre-call materials 3 days before
2. Send confirmation text 30 min before (increases show rate 25%)
3. Make call time clear and specific
4. Use calendar link (no confusion on timezone)
5. Call them 2 min before to confirm they're ready

### Problem: "Show-up but don't move deals forward"
**Root cause**: You're collecting information, not moving them through stages

**Fix**:
1. Have a clear next step for every call
2. Don't end a call without knowing: Yes/No/Follow-up?
3. If follow-up, schedule it immediately (don't assume)
4. Update CRM stage immediately (don't wait)
5. Move forward or move them out; don't let deals linger

## Advanced: Deal Scoring

Optional, but useful for bigger operations:

**Score prospects 1-10 based:**
- Urgency (do they have timeline pressure?) 1-3 pts
- Commitment (have they taken previous action?) 1-3 pts
- Resources (do they have budget/capacity?) 1-2 pts
- Fit (does your solution match their problem?) 1-2 pts

**9-10**: Hot - pursue aggressively
**7-8**: Warm - steady follow-up
**5-6**: Lukewarm - nurture only
**3-4**: Cold - very low priority
**1-2**: Poor fit - disqualify

Prioritize your time on 8-10 scored deals; don't waste energy on 1-4s.

## Key Discipline Rules

1. **Update CRM daily** - If it's not in CRM, it didn't happen.
2. **One deal = one next action** - Every deal has a clear "what's next?"
3. **Never leave call without next step** - End of call: "So our next step is [X] on [date]."
4. **Archive or pursue** - No deals in limbo; they're either active or dead.
5. **Follow the sequence** - Don't "check in" randomly; follow 6-step framework.
6. **Weekly review** - Friday close is non-negotiable.
7. **Numbers don't lie** - Track metrics; they reveal where to improve.
8. **Urgency is trainable** - Most stalled deals lack urgency; you create it through good discovery.
