# Re-Offer Techniques: Bringing Dead Deals Back to Life

## When to Re-Offer

A deal is a good candidate for re-offer if it meets these criteria:

**✓ Good Re-Offer Candidates**
- Were interested at one point ("This looks interesting")
- Said "maybe later" or "I need to think about it" (not "I'm not interested")
- It's been 14-90 days with no contact
- No explicit "not interested" message
- Haven't confirmed they're using a competitor
- Offered good company/prospect (not a complete tire-kicker)

**✗ Bad Re-Offer Candidates**
- Explicitly said "I'm not interested" or "not for us"
- Said they're already using competitor
- Time has passed and context has changed (their problem solved)
- They said "never" or "not this year"
- They've gone radio silent for 6+ months
- Budget was cut and unlikely to return

## The Four Re-Offer Strategies

### Strategy 1: The Graceful Re-Offer (Cole Gordon's Approach)

**Philosophy**: You're not desperately chasing them; you're reminding them of an opportunity they were considering.

**When to Use**:
- Deal stalled 2-4 weeks with no contact
- They showed genuine interest initially
- No clear rejection

**The Script**:

```
Subject: [Name], wanted to circle back on this

Hey [Name],

I realized it's been [X weeks] since we talked about [solution].

I wanted to reach out because I genuinely think there's still a strong fit here.
I know you were weighing options - just wanted to see if timing has shifted at all
on your end.

If not now, no worries - I'm happy to keep us on your radar for when it does
make sense.

[Your name]
```

**Why It Works**:
- Assumes they were considering it (positive framing)
- Doesn't seem needy ("no worries if not now")
- Implies you have other options ("keep you on my radar")
- Soft re-engagement, not desperate chase

**Expected Response Rate**: 20-30% of them reply with either "yes" or "let's try this"

**Tone**: Confident, patient, respectful of their timeline

---

### Strategy 2: The Value-Add Re-Offer (Luke Alexander's Approach)

**Philosophy**: Don't ask about decision; offer something NEW they didn't see before.

**When to Use**:
- Deal stalled 3-6 weeks
- You've thought about their specific objection
- You have NEW information, case study, or approach that addresses their concern

**The Script**:

```
Subject: [Name] - came across this, thought of you

Hi [Name],

I was going through some recent case studies and realized we have a client
in [their industry] who had the exact same [concern/objection] you raised.

Here's the cool part: [describe the challenge they had]

The way we solved it for them was [specific 3-step approach], which turned out
to be even better than our standard approach.

They're now doing [specific result - make it concrete].

I thought you'd find this valuable since you asked about [specific concern]
on our call. Worth another 10-minute conversation?

[Your name]
```

**Why It Works**:
- New information changes the math in their head
- Shows you listened to their specific concern
- Proof that others like them succeeded
- Lower pressure ("worth a conversation?")
- Positions you as helpful, not just selling

**Expected Response Rate**: 30-40% (higher than graceful re-offer because new info)

**Tone**: Helpful expert sharing relevant insights

---

### Strategy 3: The Paid Entry Point Re-Offer (Luke's Paid Challenge)

**Philosophy**: They rejected $X price. Offer them $Y smaller offer to build confidence first.

**When to Use**:
- They seemed interested but said "too expensive" or "bad timing financially"
- It's been 4-8 weeks
- You have a lower-ticket entry product/workshop/challenge
- They showed genuine interest in your method, just not the price

**The Script**:

```
Subject: Lower-cost way to get started ([Name])

Hey [Name],

I was thinking about our conversation, and I realize $[high price] is a big
commitment upfront.

What if we started smaller? I'm running a [paid challenge/workshop/bootcamp]
next month for $[low price]. It's [description of what they'll learn/achieve].

In [timeframe], you'll see exactly how this works. Then, if you want to go
deeper with the full program, you'll already know the method works for you.

What do you think? Worth trying at that price point?

[Your name]
```

**How It Works as a Pipeline:**

```
$40 Paid Challenge
    ↓ (70% completion rate)
    ↓
They see value, believe it works
    ↓ (Often their idea to upgrade)
    ↓
$3,000 Full Program (much easier to close)
```

**Why It Works**:
- Removes entry barrier dramatically
- They "prove it to themselves" (not your proof)
- Builds social proof (they completed challenge)
- Creates momentum (easy to invest more after first investment)
- Filters for seriousness (paid = commitment)

**Expected Conversion**: 40-50% of challenge completers upgrade to main offer

**Tone**: Realistic, accommodating to budget constraints

---

### Strategy 4: The Industry/Competitive Re-Offer (Cole's Urgency Creation)

**Philosophy**: New trigger event makes it relevant now (not later).

**When to Use**:
- Deal stalled because "wrong timing" or "we'll do it next quarter"
- Something in their industry changed
- Competitor move, regulation change, market shift, etc.
- Now it's relevant again

**The Script**:

```
Subject: I saw this and immediately thought of you, [Name]

Hi [Name],

Not sure if you've seen, but [competitor/industry announcement], and it made me
think of our conversation from [X weeks] ago.

Here's why I'm reaching out:

[Competitor] just announced [move that affects their space], which means
companies like you will probably feel pressure to [respond similarly].

We've already helped [X clients] prepare for exactly this. Here's what they did:
[quick 2-3 bullet points]

Timing-wise, this is moving faster than most expect. Wanted to make sure you
had a heads up.

Worth a 15-min call to talk through implications for your specific situation?

[Your name]
```

**Why It Works**:
- External trigger makes it urgent NOW (not just "someday")
- Shows you're tracking their industry
- Positions you as helpful advisor, not salesperson
- Creates FOMO (others are moving)
- Gives them a reason to re-engage

**Expected Response Rate**: 35-45% (external triggers are powerful)

**Tone**: Advisor/insider, not salesman

## The Re-Offer Sequence

If one approach doesn't work, don't give up. Try the next.

### Week 1: The Graceful Re-Offer
Use Script 1 (graceful approach).

**If no response**: Wait 5 days, then move to next.

### Week 2: The Value-Add Re-Offer
Use Script 2 (value-add with specific case study).

**If no response**: Wait 7 days, then move to next.

### Week 3: The Trigger Event Re-Offer
Use Script 4 (industry/competitive trigger).

**If no response**: Wait 10 days, then move to next.

### Week 4: The Paid Entry Point Re-Offer
Use Script 3 (offer lower-ticket entry).

**If no response**: Move to evergreen nurture (monthly check-ins, no aggressive close).

## Handling Objections During Re-Offer

### Objection: "We went with someone else"

**Your Response:**
"Totally fair. Out of curiosity, how's that working out? Are you getting the
[specific result you mentioned needing]?"

[Listen]

"Cool. Well, if that changes or if they can't deliver, you know where to find
us. I'm happy to be your backup option."

[Then add them to evergreen nurture list]

**Why This Works**:
- You're not attacking their choice
- You're genuinely interested (builds relationship)
- You're leaving the door open (situation can change)
- You're not pushy (they'll respect you more)

### Objection: "Still not the right time"

**Your Response:**
"Totally get it. Let me ask - what would need to change for it to be the right
time? Timeline? Budget? Something else?"

[Listen]

"Got it. So when you have [what they need], reach out and we'll jump on it.
In the meantime, I'm going to send you [specific resource] that might be
helpful. Sound good?"

[Add to calendar: 30-day check-in]

**Why This Works**:
- You're identifying their real blocker (not just stalling)
- You're setting specific re-engagement trigger
- You're providing value (resource)
- You're patient but not giving up
- 30-day check-in shows follow-through

### Objection: "We're too busy right now"

**Your Response:**
"I hear that. That's actually usually a signal that [solution] would help
ease that pressure, but I get it - not the time to think about it.

What if I send over a case study of how [similar prospect] streamlined things
during their busy season? You can skim it when you have 5 minutes.

If it resonates, we can pick it back up. Deal?"

[Send case study]

**Why This Works**:
- You're reframing "busy" as a reason they NEED you
- You're giving them low-friction way to engage (read case study when ready)
- You're being patient but not giving up

### Objection: "We don't have budget right now"

**Your Response:**
"Totally understand. Two questions real quick:

1) When does budget open up? [Listen for timeline]
2) If we could work something out financially, would that change things?"

[If yes to #2]: "Perfect. What would work for you? Payment plan? Smaller scope?"

[If no to #2]: "No problem. When does budget cycle, let's circle back then."

[Add to calendar: Budget renewal timing]

**Why This Works**:
- You're identifying their real blocker (budget timing)
- You're offering solutions (payment plans, smaller scope)
- You're not giving up, but respecting their constraint
- You're creating specific re-engage trigger

## Advanced: Multi-Channel Re-Offer

Don't rely on email alone. Use multiple channels.

### Channel 1: Email (Primary)
- Initial re-offer message
- Case study with new information
- Calendar link to book call

### Channel 2: Phone Call (48 Hours After Email)
If no response to email, call:

```
"Hey [Name], I sent you an email this morning about [topic]. Just checking
if you got it? Also wanted to see where you're at on [original need]."
```

### Channel 3: LinkedIn Message (5 Days Later)
If no phone response:

```
"Hey [Name], noticed I hadn't heard back. Just want to make sure the email
got through. Sent something I think you'd find valuable. Check your inbox?"
```

### Channel 4: SMS (7 Days Later)
If still no response:

```
"Hey [Name], it's [Your name]. Sent you some case studies about [topic].
Worth a quick conversation? Let me know what works."
```

**Why Multi-Channel Works**:
- Some people respond better to calls, some to texts, some to emails
- Multiple channels feel intentional, not desperate
- Increases response rate 40-60% over email-only

## The Re-Offer Timeline

### Best Re-Offer Windows

**14-30 days since last contact**: GOLD
- Fresh memory of your conversation
- Not too long that they've forgotten you
- Still seriously considering
- Easiest to re-engage

**30-60 days since last contact**: GOOD
- Still remember you
- Beginning to re-evaluate their decision
- Can use new information effectively
- 30-40% response rate

**60-90 days since last contact**: OK
- May have forgotten you
- Might have moved on
- Needs strong trigger (industry change, competitor move)
- 20-30% response rate

**90+ days since last contact**: COLD
- Likely moved on
- Conversation is stale
- Only re-offer with major new info
- <15% response rate

## The Disqualification Decision

**When to stop re-offering and accept the deal is dead:**

✓ They've said "not interested" 2+ times clearly
✓ They said they're using competitor (and you verified it)
✓ 90+ days of silence with zero response attempts
✓ Company context changed (downsizing, pivoted product, etc.)
✓ You've tried all 4 strategies and nothing worked
✓ They asked you to stop contacting them

**When to keep trying (don't give up):**

✗ Just 2-4 weeks of silence
✗ They said "maybe later" or "I need to think"
✗ New information emerged that's relevant
✗ Industry/market event changes their situation
✗ You haven't tried the paid entry point yet

## Success Rates & Expectations

**Realistic Re-Offer Conversion Rates** (all approaches combined):

- Cold prospects (no prior conversation): 5-10%
- Warm prospects (talked once, stalled 2-4 weeks): 20-30%
- Warm prospects (talked once, stalled 4-8 weeks): 10-20%
- Warm prospects (talked once, stalled 8-12 weeks): 5-10%
- Cold prospects (12+ weeks): 1-3%

**Average Re-Offer ROI**:
- 100 re-offer attempts
- 15-20 responses
- 3-5 actual conversions
- 1-2 mid-ticket deals

Not huge, but pure upside. You're not creating these opportunities; you're unlocking ones that were already there.

## Common Re-Offer Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake #1: Re-offering too soon**
- Waiting less than 2 weeks looks desperate
- Wait minimum 2 weeks; 3-4 weeks is better
- Exception: Major industry/competitive trigger

**Mistake #2: Re-offering the same way**
- "Hi [Name], just checking in on [X]"
- Use different angle: new case study, new info, new price point
- Each re-offer should feel fresh

**Mistake #3: Re-offering with no new information**
- They already said no to your offer
- Give them a reason to reconsider
- New case study, new approach, new price point

**Mistake #4: Re-offering after explicit "no"**
- If they said "we're not interested," stop
- If they said "not this year," respect it (circle back in year)
- If they said "using competitor," accept it
- You can keep them in nurture, but don't hard close

**Mistake #5: Giving up too early**
- One "I need to think" is not final answer
- 2-4 weeks of silence doesn't mean dead
- Try 2-3 re-offers before accepting it's dead

**Mistake #6: Being too aggressive**
- Calling daily is annoying
- Using all 4 channels in 48 hours looks desperate
- Space re-offers 7-10 days apart
- Let them respond before trying next angle

## The Re-Offer Mindset

**Key beliefs for successful re-offering:**

1. **Not every "no" is final** - Most stalled deals were genuinely interested; they're just deciding
2. **Patience converts more than aggression** - Re-offer that respects their timeline converts better
3. **New information changes minds** - You don't need to convince them again; show them something they didn't see
4. **External triggers are powerful** - Industry changes, competitor moves, budget cycles create urgency naturally
5. **Dead deals aren't wasted time** - Even unopened re-offers stay in their mind; they convert later
6. **You're not desperate** - You have other deals; this is just unlocking what was already there
7. **Respect the "no"** - Some deals are genuinely not right; accept it and move on without resentment
8. **Evergreen nurture is underrated** - Monthly value emails convert 5-10% over 6-12 months with zero effort

Re-offering is the art of strategic patience + new information. Master it, and you'll unlock 20-30% more revenue from your existing pipeline.
