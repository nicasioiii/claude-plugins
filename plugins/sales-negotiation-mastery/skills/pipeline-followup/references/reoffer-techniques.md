# Re-Offer Techniques: Scripts & Strategy

## The Re-Offer Principle

**Source:** Gordon. A re-offer is NOT repeating your original pitch. It is a new or modified offer specifically designed to overcome the reason the prospect did not buy.

The reason you withhold payment plans on the initial call: they ARE the re-offer. If you give away all your ammunition on call one, you have nothing left for follow-up.

---

## Re-Offer Design Framework

### Step 1: Identify the Original Objection
Review your call notes. What was the real reason they did not buy?

### Step 2: Design the Re-Offer to Solve That Specific Problem

| Original Objection | Re-Offer Type | Example |
|-------------------|---------------|---------|
| Cash flow / "can't afford it" | Payment plan that was NOT offered | "We are testing a 3-pay option this week" |
| Not enough time | Extended timeline | "We added 30 extra days for people in your situation" |
| Uncertain about method | Micro-commitment | Private workshop, challenge, or single module access |
| Specific skill gap (lead gen) | Targeted bonus | "Private 3-day workshop on [gap]" |
| Specific skill gap (sales) | Targeted bonus | "Sales Intensive call review package" |
| Fear of getting started | Proof of concept | "Exact strategy [top client] used for first 3 wins" |
| Too expensive overall | Lower-ticket entry | $40-$97 challenge, bootcamp, or mini-course |

### Step 3: Frame It as Something New
The re-offer must feel like a new opportunity, not a desperate discount. Language matters:
- "We are testing something new..."
- "Based on what you told me, I put this together..."
- "[Owner/influencer] just launched this and I thought of you..."

## Re-Offer Scripts by Channel

### Messenger/Text Re-Offer (Gordon)
```
"Hey [NAME] -- we're testing something new this week that'll help
you [overcome their specific objection]." SEND

"I know you were saying ___ was keeping you from being able to move
forward, so when [influencer] brought this up I thought of you.
Want me to send you some info?" SEND

"No worries either way" SEND
```

### Email Re-Offer (Value-Add)
```
Subject: [Name] -- came across this, thought of you

Hi [Name],

I was going through recent client results and realized we have
someone in [their industry/situation] who had the exact same
[concern/objection] you raised.

Here is what happened: [describe their challenge briefly]

The way we solved it was [specific approach], and they are now
[specific result -- concrete numbers].

I thought you would find this valuable since you mentioned
[specific concern] on our call. Worth another 10-minute conversation?

[Your name]
```

### Phone Re-Offer Script
```
"Hey [Name], it's [You]. Real quick -- I was going through some
things this week and something came up that made me think of you.

You mentioned [objection] was the thing keeping you from moving
forward. Well, we just [launched/created/tested] something that
specifically addresses that.

Basically [1-2 sentence description of re-offer].

I know it was not the right fit last time, but this is different.
Worth hearing about?"
```

## Content Tagging Follow-Up (Gordon)

One of the highest-converting follow-up strategies. Uses brand content as the hook.

### The Flow
1. Brand publishes new content (training, video, post)
2. You tag the prospect in the content
3. Follow up on whether they consumed it
4. Pivot naturally back to their pain point

### Script
```
"Hey -- have you seen the new [training] [influencer] just put out?"
SEND

"I just tagged you in it" SEND

"Did you get the notification?" SEND

[They respond]

"Awesome -- by the way, did you ever get clear on [their pain point]?"
```

### Content Consumption Follow-Up
After they watch/read the content:
```
"What'd you think of the [training name]?"
```

This was the main message behind most of Gordon's long-term conversions. Simple, effective, and the content does the pre-selling for you.

## The Dean Jackson 9-Word Email

The most famous reactivation message in direct response marketing. Works because it is impossibly short and personal.

### Template
```
Subject: [First name]?

Are you still trying to [their stated goal]?
```

### Why It Works
- So short it gets opened and read
- Directly references their goal (personal)
- Asks a yes/no question (easy to respond to)
- Triggers mild guilt/motivation about their abandoned goal
- Does not feel like a sales email

### Response Handling
- If "Yes": "Great! What's changed since we last talked? Still dealing with [pain]?"
- If "No": "Got it. What happened? Did you find another solution?"
- If "Not right now": "Totally understand. When would be better?"
- If no response: Move to next re-offer strategy

## Paid Entry Point Re-Offer

**Source:** Alexander. When the price was the blocker, offer a low-cost entry to prove value.

### The Strategy
They rejected your $3K-$10K offer but seemed genuinely interested. Offer a $40-$97 paid challenge/workshop. They complete it, see value, then upgrade naturally.

### Script
```
"I realize $[big price] is a big commitment upfront.

What if we started smaller? We have a [challenge/workshop/bootcamp]
for $[low price]. You get [specific deliverable] in [timeframe],
and you see exactly how this works.

Then, if you want to go deeper, you already know the method works
for you. Thoughts?"
```

### The Conversion Funnel
```
$40-$97 paid challenge
    -> 70% completion rate
    -> They see value, believe it works
    -> 40-50% of completers upgrade to main offer
```

## Re-Offer Timing Guide

| Window | Strategy | Expected Response |
|--------|----------|-------------------|
| Week 2-3 | Graceful re-offer or content tagging | 20-30% response |
| Week 3-4 | Value-add re-offer with new case study | 25-35% response |
| Week 4-6 | Re-offer designed for their specific objection | 20-30% response |
| Week 6-8 | Paid entry point / lower-ticket offer | 15-25% response |
| Week 8-12 | Dean Jackson 9-word email | 20-30% response |
| Week 12+ | "Have you given up?" nuclear option | 10-15% response |

## Re-Offer Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Re-offering too soon** (under 2 weeks) -- looks desperate
2. **Re-offering the exact same thing** -- must be new/modified
3. **Giving away all payment options on call one** -- save them for re-offer
4. **No new information** -- they already said no to your original offer
5. **Re-offering after explicit "not interested"** -- respect it, move to quarterly nurture
6. **Being too aggressive** -- space re-offers 7-10 days apart minimum
7. **Forgetting the "no worries either way"** -- always include an easy out
