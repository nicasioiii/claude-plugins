# Demo & Delivery Mastery

## Core Principles of Pitch Delivery

### The Tonality Framework

**What Makes Tonality Powerful:**
38% of communication is tonality (tone, pace, inflection)
55% is body language/visual (on video)
Only 7% is the actual words

This means: You could say the RIGHT words in the WRONG tone and lose the deal.

---

## Tonality Types by Situation

### 1. Confidence Tonality (During Offer/Objection Handling)

**Characteristics:**
- Slow, measured pace (not rushed)
- Lower pitch (commands authority)
- Full breath between sentences
- Assured tone (not cocky or arrogant)

**When to use:**
- Making the offer
- Handling objections
- Anchoring value

**Script example with confidence tonality:**
"So here's what we do. The investment is $5,000. And based on working with hundreds of clients in your exact situation, this is what's needed. Sound fair?"

**Physical indicators:**
- Sit back slightly (not leaning in desperately)
- Pause after statements (let it land)
- Lower vocal register
- Slower speech rate

---

### 2. Curious Tonality (During Discovery)

**Characteristics:**
- Slightly higher pitch (genuine interest)
- Questions sound like questions (not statements)
- Pause for them to fill silence
- Not robotic—natural curiosity

**When to use:**
- Asking discovery questions
- Probing for pain
- Inviting answers

**Script example:**
"Tell me—what's been the biggest challenge with this? [pause for real answer]"

**Physical indicators:**
- Lean in slightly (shows interest)
- Eyebrows up (genuine curiosity)
- Pace your questions (don't machine-gun them)
- Let silence exist (don't fill it)

---

### 3. Empathetic Tonality (During Objections)

**Characteristics:**
- Warm, understanding tone
- Slower pace
- Match their energy (don't exceed it)
- Validating without being weak

**When to use:**
- Acknowledging objections
- Responding to concerns
- Addressing hesitation

**Script example:**
"I totally get it. You've been burned before, and that makes sense. A lot of our best clients come with that story..."

**Physical indicators:**
- Soften facial expression
- Nod to show understanding
- Speak more slowly
- Match their energy level (don't bounce high if they're reserved)

## Dialogue Management (Critical to Pitch Success)

### The 45-Second Rule

Beyond 45 seconds without feedback, prospects mentally check out.

**How to structure pitch with built-in dialogue:**

**0:00-0:30** - State pillar 1 (feature + benefit)
**0:30** - "Does that make sense?"
**0:30-1:00** - Deeper explanation on pillar 1
**1:00** - "What are your thoughts so far?"
**1:00-1:30** - Pillar 2
**1:30** - "Does that align with what you needed?"

**Pattern:** Teach 30-45 seconds → Get feedback → Continue

### Types of Feedback Questions

**Light check-in (lower commitment):**
- "Does that make sense?"
- "Follow me?"
- "Good?"

**Moderate check-in (medium commitment):**
- "What do you think about that?"
- "How's that landing?"
- "Does that feel right?"

**Strong check-in (requires thinking):**
- "Do you agree with that?"
- "Are you sure?" (can sound bold, use carefully)
- "Can you see how this would work for you?"

**Strategic tip:** Use light questions when building. Use strong questions when you need actual commitment.

## The 2-3 Minute Pitch Structure (Verbatim Delivery)

### Exact Timing Breakdown

**0:00-0:10 - Opening Frame**
"So what we do for people in your situation is three things. Let me walk you through each one."

**0:10-0:45 - Pillar 1 (Feature + Benefit + Why)**
"First, [specific solution to their pain]. Here's why that matters—[reason]. That means [benefit to them]."

**0:45-1:15 - Pillar 2 (Support System)**
"Second, you get [support detail]. That's important because [why]. Which means [benefit]."

**1:15-1:45 - Pillar 3 (Knowledge/Independence)**
"And third, you're learning [what they learn], not just getting the solution. That means [benefit of independence]."

**1:45-2:05 - Chunk & Anchor**
"The real beauty is, to hit [big goal], you only need [simplified version]. That's it."

**2:05-2:20 - Check-In**
"Does that make sense?"
[They respond]

**2:20-2:30 - Open to Questions**
"Any questions before we go further?"

## Avoiding Common Tonality Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Approval-Seeking Energy

**What it sounds like:**
- Talking faster and faster
- Nervous pacing
- Over-explaining
- Hesitant language ("I think maybe you might want...")

**What it does:**
- Makes prospect feel unsafe
- Creates doubt in YOUR mind about offer
- Prospect mirrors the uncertainty

**How to fix it:**
- Slow down deliberately
- Pause after statements (let them land)
- Use declarative statements: "Here's what we do" (not "I think you might need")
- Sit back instead of leaning in

### Mistake 2: Over-Enthusiasm

**What it sounds like:**
- Too-high pitch
- Rapid speech rate
- Constant excitement
- Sounds salesy

**What it does:**
- Prospect feels pressure
- Seems inauthentic
- Creates resistance

**How to fix it:**
- Lower your pitch intentionally
- Slow your pace
- Match their energy level
- Let quiet moments exist

### Mistake 3: Matching Low Energy with High Energy

**Scenario:**
Prospect is reserved and quiet. You respond by bouncing higher to "engage" them.

**What happens:**
They feel pursued, they pull back further. You look desperate.

**How to fix it:**
- Don't match desperation
- Instead, LEAN OUT and create space
- Lower your energy to their level
- They'll naturally step in
- Psychology: People pursue what has to reach for them

### Mistake 4: Losing Emotional Connection

**What it sounds like:**
- Discovery = warm, connected, slow
- Transition = suddenly logical
- Pitch = pure data, no feeling

**What it does:**
- Kills the urgency you built
- Prospect feels duped (emotional → transactional)
- Less likely to buy

**How to fix it:**
- Maintain emotional tone through pre-pitch
- Keep pain references throughout pitch
- Tie benefits to their stated values
- Use their names (creates connection)

## Screen Share / Demo Delivery Best Practices

### Before You Share Your Screen

**1. Set expectations:**
"I'm going to show you what this looks like. You'll see the dashboard, here's how it flows..."

**2. Have a clear path:**
Know exactly what you're showing. Don't wander.

**3. Zoom properly:**
Make sure text is readable (120-150% zoom minimum on shared screen)

**4. Close other tabs:**
One tab open—the demo. Nothing else visible.

---

### During the Demo

**Rule 1: Don't narrate robotically**
Don't read what's on screen. Show, then explain the impact.

**Rule 2: Pause for questions**
"See how this feeds into [next screen]? Any questions about this part?"

**Rule 3: Highlight key features only**
Don't show everything. Show 3-4 most relevant features.

**Rule 4: Use cursor to point**
Draw attention to where you want eyes. Let cursor lead.

**Rule 5: Tell the story, not the spec**
Bad: "This field captures the data point X..."
Good: "So when you enter this information, the system automatically calculates how much revenue you're leaving on the table."

---

### After Demo

**Don't ask "Do you have questions?"**
Instead: "What stands out most to you about this?" (opens dialogue)

**Connect back to their pain:**
"Remember you mentioned [their issue]? Watch how this solves that automatically."

**Get commitment:**
"Could you see yourself using this?"

## Advanced: Multi-Prospect Presentations

### When Multiple Decision-Makers Are Present

**The Challenge:**
Different people have different needs. Miss one, and they become the objector later.

**The Solution Framework:**

#### 1. Identify Decision-Maker Types Before You Pitch
- Primary buyer (will use it / who needs it)
- Financial decision-maker (pays for it)
- Influencer (has opinion that matters)

#### 2. Tailor Questions to Each During Discovery
"So [primary], what matters most to you in the solution?"
"And [financial decision-maker], what's your main concern about making an investment like this?"

#### 3. During Pitch, Address Both Explicitly
Pillar 1 → How it solves primary person's problem
Pillar 2 → How it addresses financial concern (ROI, payback period)
Pillar 3 → How it impacts the team/organization (broader benefit)

#### 4. Include Both in Check-Ins
"Does that land for you?" [to primary]
"And from a financial perspective, does that address your concern?" [to decision-maker]

#### 5. Close with Both
"If both of you are comfortable, shall we move forward?"

## Pacing Strategies by Offer Type

### High-Ticket B2B Consulting ($5K-$25K+)
- Slower pace overall
- More dialogue (less talking from you)
- Allow longer pauses
- Let them ask detailed questions
- Don't rush to price

### Mid-Ticket Coaching ($2K-$5K)
- Moderate pace
- 2-3 minute pitch
- Check in every 30-45 seconds
- Move through objections quickly
- Get to price within 20 minutes

### Lower-Ticket Digital Products ($200-$1K)
- Faster pace overall
- 1-2 minute pitch
- High energy but not desperate
- Quick commitment ask
- Payment integration ready

## Confidence Builders for Pitch Delivery

### 1. Record Yourself
Listen to your pitch 1.5x speed 5 times. Let the tonality embed.

### 2. Practice the Pause
After key statements, count 2 seconds in silence. Let it land.

### 3. Lower Your Baseline Pitch
Go 10% deeper than feels natural. This is your "confident" register.

### 4. Memorize the Framework, Not the Script
Know the three pillars cold. Don't memorize exact words—this keeps you natural.

### 5. Lead With Proof
Start with what's possible (case studies, results) before explaining your offer.

## Question Library for Throughout the Pitch

### Opening/Framing
- "Can I walk you through how we structure this?"
- "Before I dive in, does this still feel like a fit?"

### Mid-Pitch Check-Ins
- "Does that make sense?"
- "What are your thoughts so far?"
- "How's that landing?"
- "Can you see this working for you?"

### Objection Handling
- "What specifically concerns you?"
- "What would need to be true for this to work?"
- "Besides that, anything else keeping you from moving forward?"

### Closing
- "So how do you feel about the process?"
- "On a scale of 1-10, where would you fall?"
- "What would move you to a 9 or 10?"

### Buying Signals
- "Should we get you set up?"
- "Ready to move forward?"
- "Shall we get started?"

## Red Flags in Your Own Delivery

If you notice these, it's time to adjust:

1. **Talking more than 45 seconds without asking a question** - You're lecturing, not dialoguing
2. **Speaking faster as you go** - Sign of nervousness; slow down intentionally
3. **Repeating yourself** - You're uncertain; commit to what you said
4. **Over-explaining features** - Prospect is confused; step back to benefits
5. **Seeking approval in your tone** - "Does that sound okay?" sounds weak
6. **Not pausing for answers** - You're not actually listening; create space
7. **Prospect nodding but eyes glazed** - Polite but not engaged; ask stronger question
8. **You talking 60% of the call** - Prospect should talk 50%+ of discovery, 40%+ of pitch

## Mental Models for Delivery Excellence

### The Doctor Model
You're diagnosing, not selling. This removes pressure and creates safety.

**How it changes delivery:**
- More curious tonality
- More questions, fewer statements
- Focus on understanding their world
- Present solution as prescription after diagnosis

### The Teacher Model
You're teaching them why this matters, not tricking them into buying.

**How it changes delivery:**
- Educational tone (explain the why)
- Slower pace (let concepts land)
- More structure (pillars, frameworks)
- Respect for their intelligence

### The Advisor Model
You're someone they hired to help them decide, not someone trying to sell them.

**How it changes delivery:**
- Confident but not pushy
- Willing to say "this might not be right for you"
- Focus on their best outcome (not your commission)
- Contrarian points appreciated
