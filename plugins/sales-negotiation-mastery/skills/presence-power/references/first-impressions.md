# First Impressions: 7-Second Rule, Dress, VIP Protocol

## The 7-Second Rule -- Full Breakdown

**Source:** Serhant (Myka -- etiquette expert).

The brain forms a first impression in roughly 7 seconds. In business and sales contexts, this impression includes assessments of:
- Competence
- Trustworthiness
- Status / authority
- Likability
- Confidence

### What Happens in Those 7 Seconds

**Second 1-2: Visual Scan**
- Posture, clothing, grooming
- Facial expression (smile vs. neutral vs. frown)
- Eye contact (or avoidance)

**Second 3-4: Physical Approach**
- How you walk toward them (confident stride vs. tentative shuffle)
- Whether you face them or angle away
- Spatial awareness (too close = aggressive, too far = disengaged)

**Second 5-7: First Contact**
- Handshake quality
- First words spoken
- Vocal tone and energy level

### The 7-Second Preparation Checklist

Before entering any meeting, run through:

- [ ] Am I facing forward? (Never turn your back to the room when entering)
- [ ] Am I smiling? (Start every sentence with a smile)
- [ ] Is my posture upright? (Shoulders back, chin level)
- [ ] Do I know who has VIP status?
- [ ] Am I dressed appropriately for the audience?
- [ ] Do I have a clear opening line planned?
- [ ] Have I memorized names and key details about attendees?

## Dress Code Protocol

### The Principle
**Dress how you want to be addressed** -- everything you wear and carry is being evaluated.

### Context-Dependent Dressing

| Audience | Dress Code | Details |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Corporate / executive | Formal business | Suit, tie, polished shoes |
| Startup / tech | Smart casual | Button-down, no tie, clean sneakers OK |
| Creative / agency | On-brand casual | Express personality but remain polished |
| High-net-worth client | Match their level + one notch up | If they wear Brunello, you wear Brunello |
| Blue collar / tradesman | Relatable business casual | Overdressing signals disconnection |

### Details That Get Noticed
- **Shoes** -- the first thing many people look at. Clean and polished always.
- **Watch / accessories** -- signals status subtly. Do not overdo it.
- **Phone / pen / bag** -- every item you carry is being judged.
- **Grooming** -- hair, nails, facial hair. Polished = trustworthy.

## VIP Status & Location Awareness

### Determining VIP Status

Before any meeting, identify:
1. **Who is the host?** (They have home-field VIP status)
2. **Who has the higher organizational rank?**
3. **Who is the buyer/decision-maker?** (They have economic VIP status)
4. **Where is the meeting?** (Location confers status to whoever "owns" the space)

### Protocol When YOU Are the Guest
- Show respect for the host's space
- Wait to be shown where to sit
- Do NOT put your hand on top in handshake (dominant power play against the host)
- Let them lead the beginning of the conversation
- Accept offered beverages (refusal can be seen as standoffish)

### Protocol When YOU Are the Host
- Greet them at the door (do not make them find you)
- Guide them to their seat (Power Play 1: "Please, after you")
- Offer beverages (demonstrates hospitality and control)
- Choose seating arrangement strategically:
  - Round table: sit next to them (collaborative)
  - Long table: sit at head (authority) or across (negotiation)
  - Couch/lounge: sit at equal level, angled toward them

### Power Positions in Seating
- **Head of table:** Authority position
- **Middle of long side:** Influence position (equidistant from everyone)
- **Across from counterpart:** Negotiation position (face-to-face)
- **Next to counterpart:** Collaborative position (same side)

## The Name Memory Trick

**Source:** Serhant. The brain remembers two related pieces of information better than one isolated fact.

### The Technique
When you meet someone, immediately create a visual association:
- "Ryan with the gray hair"
- "Myka with the red lip"
- "Dave with the blue tie"
- "Sarah with the big smile"
- "Tom with the firm handshake"

### Why It Works
- Creates a mental "hook" for the name
- Visual associations are stored in long-term memory more easily than names alone
- Becomes automatic with practice

### Advanced: Group Setting Memory
When meeting multiple people:
1. Create association for each person as you shake their hand
2. Mentally review the associations after introductions are complete
3. Use their name within the first minute of conversation (reinforces)
4. If you forget: "I am sorry, I did not catch your name clearly" -- better than guessing wrong

## The Smile Technique

**Source:** Serhant.

### The Principle
Start every sentence by smiling. Between natural pauses, bring in a smile.

### The Science
- Smiling triggers endorphin release (your own mood improves)
- Mirror neurons cause the other person to reciprocate (their mood improves)
- Voss: People are 31% more mentally capable in a positive frame of mind
- Smiling literally makes the other person smarter and more collaborative

### The Practice
Record yourself in mock conversations. Watch for:
- Do you smile at the start of sentences?
- Do you smile during pauses?
- Does the smile reach your eyes (genuine) or stop at your mouth (fake)?

> **Serhant:** "Don't fake it until you make it. Practice it until you become it."

## Handshake Power Plays -- When to Use Each

### Standard Professional Handshake
- Firm (not crushing)
- 2-3 pumps
- Eye contact throughout
- Use in: most business meetings, first introductions

### Hand on Top (Dominant)
- Your palm faces down, theirs faces up
- Communicates: "I am in charge"
- Use in: when you ARE the authority figure, your home turf, establishing frame
- Do NOT use: when you are the guest, when meeting someone of equal/higher status

### Two-Handed Shake (Warmth)
- Standard shake + your left hand on top of their hand
- Communicates: "I care about this relationship"
- Use in: meeting long-time clients, emotional moments, relationship-first contexts
- Do NOT use: first meeting with strangers (too intimate too soon)

### Hand on Shoulder After Shake
- Standard shake, then your hand moves to their shoulder
- Communicates: authority + warmth simultaneously
- Use in: getting things back on track, reassuring after frustration, political/leadership contexts
- Do NOT use: with people who have higher status or in cultures where touch is unwelcome
