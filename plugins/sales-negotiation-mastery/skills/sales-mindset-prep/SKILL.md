# Sales Mindset Prep: The Inner Game of Selling

> **Core Truth:** The call is won or lost before it starts. Your identity, certainty, and energy state determine more than any script or technique.

## Why Mindset Comes First

Every instructor agrees on this: the inner game precedes the outer game.

- Gordon: "If you're in your head, you're dead." Flow state and conviction are prerequisites for effective selling.
- Alexander: The Belgian Malinois identity -- relentless, proactive, high-output -- separates top 1% reps from lazy "French Bulldog" reps coasting on inbound calls.
- Voss: Fear of negotiation is the #1 obstacle. People take themselves hostage before they ever reach the table.
- Serhant: CODO (Close On Day One) is a mindset, not a tactic. You are closing from the first moment of contact.

**Bottom Line:** Fix your mental state first. Then apply technique.

## The Three Frames (Hold All Three on Every Call)

### Frame 1: Moral Authority

The prospect believes you have their best interests at heart. This is the most influential frame in post-2008 selling.

**How to establish it:**
- Active listening and skilled Socratic questions (not lecturing)
- Trust-based language over hype-based language
- Genuine belief in your product and your prospect's potential
- Leadership: take a stand for what is best for them, even when it is uncomfortable

**Three Rules of Leadership in Sales (from Taylor Welch via Gordon):**
1. Your only job is to lead the prospect to what is best for them
2. What is best for them is almost always outside their comfort zone
3. The prospect will fight to stay comfortable -- you must hold the line for them

### Frame 2: Prize Frame

You are the prize. Ruthless independence of outcome.

- You are excellent at what you do. They are fortunate if you extend an offer.
- If they say no, it is their loss -- not yours.
- 100% attachment to the PROCESS. Zero attachment to the outcome.
- "If you nailed the process, let the losses go."

**Practical application:** Never chase, beg, or discount out of neediness. Desperation repulses prospects.

### Frame 3: Expert (Doctor) Frame

You are the diagnostic expert with a proven process.

- You will NOT prescribe anything until diagnosis is complete
- Your frame on the proper process must be stronger than their frame
- Hold it through: clear leadership, no premature coaching, downward/equal tonality, silence and pausing, short pointed questions

**Anti-pattern:** Giving advice, coaching, or revealing the solution before discovery is complete. This destroys the doctor frame instantly.

## Identity and Conviction

### The Belgian Malinois vs. French Bulldog (Alexander)

The industry is full of lazy reps who want 4 inbound calls per day and a laptop lifestyle. The future belongs to end-to-end closers who handle pipeline, follow-up, outbound, AND inbound.

**The standard:** Outwork, outhustle, take commission from the lazy reps. High-level business owners universally complain about sales team quality. Be the exception.

### Building Conviction (Gordon)

Conviction is not hype. It is calm, certain resolve -- as if the outcome may as well already be done.

**"Pumped-Up Certainty" vs. "Resolve":**
- Pumped up = Tony Robbins energy, slightly off-putting in 1:1 sales
- Resolve = calm certainty, relaxed, care-free, even slightly humorous. Like playing a game you KNOW you will win.

**Daily conviction builders:**
1. Write and review your Identity Document (who you are becoming)
2. Morning priming routine (affirmations, visualization, physical movement)
3. Honor commitments to yourself (if you set a calendar, follow it)
4. Review testimonials and client wins DAILY before calls
5. Weigh the prospect's alternatives honestly (S&P 500 comparison -- is doing nothing really better?)
6. Faith in your ability to "make it right" if something goes wrong
7. Get into flow state on calls

### The Two Emotions You Must Transfer

1. **Clarity** -- leads to certainty
2. **Certainty** -- leads to decisive action

You cannot transfer what you do not possess. Build both before dialing.

## Flow State on Calls

**Definition:** A calm sense of certainty under pressure. All attention on the prospect and their words, like meditation.

**How to achieve it:**
- Get out of your head. Trust yourself to respond spontaneously.
- Place ALL energy on the prospect, not on your script or your performance
- Lose yourself in enjoyment of the process
- "If you're in your head, you're dead." (Gordon)

**Recommended reading:** "The Inner Game of Tennis" by W. Timothy Gallwey

## Overcoming Fear of Negotiation (Voss)

### The Self-Hostage Problem

People avoid negotiation out of worry it will escalate to personal attacks. They manufacture extreme reactions from the counterpart IN THEIR HEAD.

- "We take ourselves hostage, we defeat ourselves before we even get to the table"
- The human mind envisions 13 of the actual 3 negative consequences we might face
- Sweaty palms signal fear of the CONFLICT itself, not fear of the person

### The Reframe

- Pushing for what you believe is not selfish and not bullying
- The adversary is the SITUATION. The person is your PARTNER.
- Research shows genuine conflict around goals actually ENERGIZES collaborative problem-solving
- Skilled negotiators use conflict productively without personal battles

**Key principle:** Honest, clear differences must not be avoided -- but never plow into them without tactical empathy first. See `tactical-empathy` for the empathy-before-assertion rule.

## The CODO Mindset (Serhant)

CODO = Close On Day One. From the first moment you land a client, you are closing.

**Three core beliefs:**
1. Your business does not operate in phases. It is one continuous closing process.
2. You win the game on day one by setting the stage for the end from the beginning.
3. Every interaction is either moving toward the close or away from it.

**Pre-negotiation ritual (HALL Method):**
- **H** -- Have Empathy. This is emotional, not just transactional. Stay patient and calm.
- **A** -- Always Counter. No matter how insane an offer, counter it. Conversations have to start somehow.
- **L** -- Look for the Sweet Spot. What can be used as incentive to move toward agreement?
- **L** -- Listen. Listen to respond, not just to reply. No rush. Blurting out a reply before thinking will not help.

## Energy Management and Daily Routine

### The Ideal Daily Routine (Alexander + Gordon)

**Morning (pre-calls):**
- Wake up, shower, hydrate, stretch, get sun
- Morning priming: review identity document, visualize outcomes, physical movement
- Review calendar, research prospects, review call notes from prior interactions
- Follow up with pipeline prospects, hit no-shows

**During call hours:**
- Calendar open for calls (keep availability wide)
- Between calls: follow up, update CRM, review calls -- not social media
- Daily team meeting

**Evening:**
- End-of-day report, clean CRM/pipeline, prep for next day
- Review call recordings -- look for missed tells, wrong tonality, talking too much
- 90 days of daily call review = dramatic skill improvement

### The 30-Day Ramp-Up Process (Gordon)

**Days 1-7:** Drink from the firehose. Complete ALL company training. Shadow current closers. Go through 5-10 call recordings per day. Script out and drill questions, pitch, and objection responses.

**Days 7-14:** Take limited call volume. Set your own appointments. Get calls reviewed constantly. Continue shadowing.

**Days 15-30:** Full call volume. Get into KPI within 50 calls. Owners look for: coachability, hunger, engagement, culture fit, and visible progress.

## The Mirror: Your Objections = Their Objections (Gordon)

One of the most powerful mindset principles: whatever internal objections YOU have about the product, price, or process will be reflected in the objections your prospects raise.

- If you think it is too expensive, they will too
- If you doubt the method works, they will doubt it
- If you would not buy it yourself at that price, your energy communicates that

**Fix:** Resolve your own beliefs first. Review results, testimonials, and your own experience until YOU are genuinely certain.

## Instructor Disagreement: Energy Style

**Gordon:** Resolve (calm certainty) beats pumped-up energy in 1:1 sales. The best closers are often introverts.

**Alexander:** Belgian Malinois energy -- relentless output, bubbly on setting calls, sharp conviction on closing calls.

**Serhant:** Chameleon approach -- adapt energy to the situation. Stay steady as a closer on the inside.

**Resolution:** All agree the DEFAULT should be calm confidence, not hype. Alexander's high-output energy applies to activity volume (dials, follow-ups), not call demeanor. Gordon's resolve applies to the tone on the call itself. Serhant's adaptability applies to reading the room.
