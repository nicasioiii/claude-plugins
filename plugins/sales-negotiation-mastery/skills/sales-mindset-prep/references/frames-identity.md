# Frames and Identity: Deep-Dive Reference

## The Three Frames -- Detailed Mechanics

### Moral Authority Frame

**Establishment tactics:**
- Ask Socratic questions that lead prospect to their own conclusions
- Mirror and match their language (not their energy if it is low)
- Use downward or equal tonality on important questions -- never upward/seeking
- Be willing to disqualify: "Based on what you've told me, this might not be the right fit"
- Reference their stated goals, not your assumptions

**Key language patterns:**
- "What I've found works best is..."
- "I want to make sure you feel good about whatever decision you make"
- "If we can help, I'm happy to walk you through what that looks like. If not, I can refer you out or give you some homework."

**Anti-patterns:**
- Telling them what to do before diagnosing
- Sounding overly enthusiastic about getting them enrolled
- Discounting before being asked
- Sharing too much information too early (opposing agenda principle)

### Prize Frame

**Establishment tactics:**
- Never be the one chasing. Let the prospect move toward you.
- Use the sandwich technique in transitions: "Where do you want to go?" + [your suggestion] + "You tell me"
- When they push for information early: redirect to discovery
- If they are not engaged, you can walk: "Is now still a good time?"

**The Opposing Agenda Principle (Gordon):**
- Prospect wants: information without deciding
- You want: a transformation (a decision)
- The more information you disclose freely, the less leadership you hold
- Control information flow to maintain the prize frame

### Expert/Doctor Frame

**Specific holding techniques:**
- Short, pointed questions (not rambling)
- Silence after asking -- do not fill it
- Downward tonality on diagnostic questions
- No coaching, prescribing, or revealing the solution during discovery
- Let the prospect sit in the weight of their own pain
- No judgment -- neutral observation

**The Doctor Frame Script (Gordon):**
"So what I've really found to work best on these calls is first diving deeper into the specifics of your business, how that's working right now, and ultimately what you feel are the specific challenges keeping you from moving forward. Once we get some clarity there -- if we can help, I'm more than happy to walk you through whatever that would look like. And if not, we can figure out whatever else is best."

## Identity Documents and Conviction Builders

### The Identity Document (Gordon)

Write a 1-2 page document describing the person you are becoming. Read it every morning.

**Structure:**
1. Who you are (values, standards, non-negotiables)
2. How you show up on calls (energy, certainty, curiosity)
3. What you believe about your product and your prospects
4. Your commitment to the process regardless of outcomes
5. Your relationship with money, rejection, and discomfort

### The Belgian Malinois Standard (Alexander)

**What it means practically:**
- First to arrive, last to leave (metaphorically in remote work)
- 30-100 DMs per day minimum if in outbound/setting
- Touch ALL leads, not just booked inbound calls
- CRM updated religiously after every interaction
- Call reviews daily -- even when performing well
- Personal brand building so you are never "offerless"
- Helping teammates, supporting the company culture

**The 10K vs. 30K Rep Distinction:**

| $10K/mo Rep | $30K/mo Rep |
|---|---|
| Takes calls, does EOD report | All of that PLUS: |
| Follows the framework | Deeply understands WHY everything works |
| Waits for inbound | Hits no-shows between calls |
| NPC behavior | Ascends existing customers to higher products |
| | Posts content / builds personal brand |
| | Gives ideas to sales manager and offer owner |
| | Creates their own opportunities |

### Four Things That Determine Success (Alexander)

1. **Good things take time** -- skill compounds over years, not weeks
2. **Everything is a numbers game** -- volatile income is normal; control inputs
3. **Stick through things** -- 3 weeks minimum to ramp on any new offer
4. **Help-first mindset** -- reduces refunds, chargebacks, and desperation energy

## Conviction Transfer Mechanics

### The Three Mechanisms That Dictate Idea Receptiveness (Gordon)

1. **Certainty of the communicator** -- Not WHAT you say but HOW you say it
2. **Inner alignment of the communicator** -- Are you walking the walk? (The Mirror principle)
3. **Social proof** -- Others' beliefs around the idea

### The Three Certainty Distinctions

1. **The Sound of Belief:** Iron-clad conviction that every bone in your body knows what you are saying is true
2. **Resolve vs. Pumped-Up:** Resolve = certainty as if it is already done, allowing full relaxation. "Like playing a game you know you've already won."
3. **Shallow vs. Depth in Expression:** A heaviness behind communication that comes from thousands of hours of experience -- cannot be faked, only built through reps

### Sales Myths Debunked (Gordon)

- **Myth:** You must be high-energy/extroverted. **Truth:** Most of the best 1:1 closers are introverts. In the post-trust era, hype causes resistance.
- **Myth:** Men are better salespeople. **Truth:** Nothing to do with gender. Women who step into masculine energy while maintaining feminine energy are "devastating."
- **Myth:** Salespeople are born, not made. **Truth:** It is a learnable skill through repetition.

## Frame Holding Under Pressure

### When Frames Get Challenged

Prospects will test your frames -- consciously or unconsciously. Common frame-breaking attempts:

**Doctor Frame Challenges:**
- "Can you just tell me the price?" (trying to skip diagnosis)
- "I've already done my research" (trying to position as equal expert)
- "My friend/coach told me I should do X" (external authority challenge)

**Response pattern:** Redirect to process. "I appreciate that -- and the more context you can give me, the more specific I can be about whether we can actually help. So let me ask you..."

**Prize Frame Challenges:**
- "I have other calls booked" (trying to create competition)
- "Can you send me more info?" (trying to get you to chase)
- "I'm not sure I'm ready" (testing if you will beg)

**Response pattern:** Release pressure. "Totally fine. This might not be the right time." The more you release, the more they chase.

**Moral Authority Challenges:**
- "Are you just trying to sell me?" (trust test)
- "How do I know this works?" (proof demand)

**Response pattern:** Acknowledge directly. "I get it. And honestly, if this is not the right fit, I will tell you. My job is to figure out if we can help, not to convince you of something that is not true."

### The Frame Stack in Practice

Hold all three frames simultaneously on every call. They reinforce each other:
- Doctor Frame gives you the right to ASK deep questions
- Moral Authority gives you the right to CHALLENGE them
- Prize Frame gives you the right to WALK AWAY

When one frame weakens, lean into the other two. If they challenge your expertise (doctor), lean into moral authority and prize. If they challenge your care (moral authority), lean into doctor and prize.
