# Pre-Call Rituals, Routines, and Ramp-Up

## Morning Priming Routine (Gordon + Alexander)

### The Morning Sequence

1. **Physical foundation:** Wake up, shower, hydrate, stretch, get sun (Alexander)
2. **Identity review:** Read your Identity Document (Gordon)
3. **Testimonial review:** Go through 3-5 recent client wins/testimonials (Gordon)
4. **Visualization:** See yourself on the call -- calm, certain, leading (Gordon)
5. **Calendar review:** Know who you are calling, research prospects, review prior notes (Alexander)
6. **Pipeline hits:** Before inbound calls start, hit no-shows and follow-ups (Alexander)

### Why This Works

- Testimonial review builds conviction BEFORE you need it
- Calendar review removes surprise and anxiety
- Pipeline activity creates momentum before the first booked call
- Physical care puts you in a peak state

## Flow State Protocol (Gordon)

### Definition
"Poise" = a calm sense of certainty under pressure. ALL attention on the prospect and their words.

### The Five Keys to Flow State on Calls

1. **Get out of your head.** Trust yourself to respond spontaneously to whatever happens.
2. **Place ALL energy on the prospect.** Their words, their tone, their pauses. Like meditation -- when your mind wanders to your script or performance, bring it back to THEM.
3. **Lose yourself in enjoyment.** Treat each call as a puzzle to solve, not a test to pass.
4. **Use silence.** Pausing after questions or statements keeps you present and prevents rushing.
5. **Physical anchors:** Sit upright, breathe deeply, smile at the start. Your body signals safety to your brain.

### "If You're In Your Head, You're Dead"

Signs you are in your head:
- Thinking about what to say next while they are talking
- Worrying about how you are being perceived
- Mentally calculating commission
- Rehearsing the next phase of the script
- Second-guessing a question you just asked

**Fix:** Return attention to their WORDS. What exactly did they just say? Repeat it in your mind. Then respond.

## The 30-Day Ramp-Up Process (Gordon)

### Days 1-7: Drink From the Firehose

- Complete ALL company training first -- follow THEIR process, not a generic one
- Shadow current closers as much as possible
- Go through 5-10 call recordings per day
- Script out and drill: questions, pitch, objection responses
- Show hunger: ask questions, be alert in meetings, volunteer for anything

**Key principle:** "Follow THEIR process, not mine, unless you have mastered theirs." (Gordon)

### Days 7-14: Limited Call Volume

- Taking limited live calls
- Set your own appointments to demonstrate initiative
- Get calls reviewed constantly -- more reviews = faster improvement
- Continue shadowing in spare time between calls
- Keep drilling scripts

### Days 15-30: Full Volume

- Full call volume expected
- Get into KPI within approximately 50 calls (statistical significance)
- You are NOT guaranteed 50 calls -- companies assess qualitatively:
  - How you sound on calls
  - Coachability and implementation speed
  - Culture fit and team contribution
  - Hunger and visible progress

**Warning:** At ANY point during the 30 days, if the company is not seeing coachability and engagement, they may let you go. The ramp is earned, not given.

### The 3-Week Rule (Alexander)

Minimum 3 weeks to ramp on any new offer. Offer-hopping kills momentum. Stick through early discomfort unless the offer itself is fundamentally broken (red flags: no leads, no support, scam indicators).

## Energy Management Framework

### Manage Energy, Not Just Time (Gordon via Taylor Welch)

**Energy drains to avoid:**
- Social media scrolling between calls
- Negative team chat or complaining
- Replaying bad calls in your head without action items
- Skipping meals, exercise, or sleep

**Energy builders:**
- Exercise block (calendar it -- treat as non-negotiable)
- 8 hours of sleep
- Proper hydration throughout the day
- Decompression break between call blocks (walk, stretch, not phone)
- Quick wins in CRM (follow-ups, updates) create momentum

### The Performance Sport Analogy (Alexander)

Sales is a performance-based sport, not a lifestyle business. Treat your body accordingly:
- Stretch daily (you sit all day)
- Exercise regularly
- Morning routine creates consistency and peak mental state
- "Rust is real" -- Alexander was "horrible" after a year off. Skills require maintenance.

## Setting Projections (Gordon)

### Projections vs. Goals

- **Projection:** Activity-based, within your control (calls made, proposals sent, follow-ups done)
- **Goal:** Outcome-based (revenue, close rate)
- Set projections first, then reverse-engineer goals from them

### The Offer Equation (Alexander)

Monthly Call Volume x Close Rate x Average Commission = Monthly Income

If the math does not work for your income target, you need either:
1. More call volume
2. Higher close rate (skill improvement)
3. Higher-commission offer

### Two Reasons You Miss Numbers (Gordon)

1. **Volume** -- Not enough activity
2. **Skill** -- Not converting at target rates

It is NEVER acceptable to not know WHY you missed. Diagnose and fix.

### Minimizing Downside (Gordon)

Investors focus on minimizing losses over maximizing gains. Apply the same principle:
- Set your BASELINE as high as possible
- Instead of 5-10% close rate on bad weeks and 20-30% on good weeks
- Aim for 30% on low weeks, 50% on high weeks
- Your floor matters more than your ceiling

## The Preparation Rule (Voss)

**"When the pressure is on, you don't rise to the occasion -- you fall to your highest level of preparation."**

This applies to:
- Knowing your pitch cold before the call
- Having objection responses drilled, not memorized
- Researching the prospect before dialing
- Having your CRM notes from prior interactions open
- Knowing your three numbers (if negotiating): first offer, best target, walkaway
