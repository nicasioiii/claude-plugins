# Sales Psychology: The Theoretical Foundation

> **Core Truth:** People are irrational and emotional, not rational actors. All humans suffer from cognitive bias -- over 150 identified. Understanding these biases is the foundation of effective selling. (Voss, via Kahneman)

## Prospect Theory and Loss Aversion

### The Science (Kahneman & Tversky -- Nobel Prize Winner)

- **Framing Effect:** People respond differently to the same choice depending on how you frame it.
  - "Drug works 90% of the time" vs. "Drug fails 1 in 10 times" = different reactions to identical facts.
- **Loss Aversion:** Reaction to a loss is MORE EXTREME than reaction to the same gain.
  - Published number: losses hurt 2x as much as equivalent gains
  - Kahneman privately believed it was 5-7x (lowered to avoid academic arguments)
  - Sales programs show you need to demonstrate 9x value to make the sale
- **70% of buy decisions are made to AVOID LOSS, not to achieve gain**
- People fear what they HAVE TO LOSE 3-9x more than what they HOPE TO GAIN

### Application to Sales

It is not enough to demonstrate you can deliver a GAIN. Your edge comes when you get them to grasp what they will LOSE by doing nothing.

**But:** You cannot do this without tactical empathy first -- or you seem like a threat. The empathy-before-assertion rule (see `tactical-empathy`) is the prerequisite.

### System 1 vs. System 2 Thinking

- **System 1 (Animal Mind):** Fast, instinctive, emotional. Always running. Like the respiratory system.
- **System 2 (Rational Mind):** Slow, calculating, logical. NOT always active.

**Key insight:** System 1 influences all rational thoughts. It is a spring that feeds the river. If you know how to affect System 1, you can guide System 2 and modify responses.

**Implication:** Logical arguments alone do not close deals. You must first influence the emotional system.

## The Six Human Needs (Gordon via Tony Robbins)

People buy STATES -- both surface-level outcomes and emotional-level outcomes.

| Need | Sales Power | How to Leverage |
|---|---|---|
| **Certainty** | Highest | "You'll know exactly what to do every day" |
| **Significance** | Highest | "You'll be recognized as the authority in your space" |
| **Variety** | Medium | "Every day brings new challenges and clients" |
| **Growth** | Medium | "You'll develop skills that compound over your career" |
| **Contribution** | Medium | "You'll be able to give back to your community/family" |
| **Love/Connection** | Medium | "You'll build relationships with your team and clients" |

**Key insight:** When pitching, tie features of your offer to these emotional destinations -- especially certainty and significance. Your pitches become "absolutely wicked" when you do this. (Gordon)

## The Belief Ladder (Gordon)

Seven beliefs the prospect must hold before they will buy. Every objection traces back to one unmet belief.

| # | Belief | What It Eliminates |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pain** -- They have a problem keeping them from what they want | "I don't have a problem" |
| 2 | **Doubt** -- They cannot fix this alone | DIY/ego objection |
| 3 | **Cost** -- Doing nothing is more painful than the investment | Time/INTAI objections |
| 4 | **Desire** -- Solving yields a better future than staying put | Low motivation |
| 5 | **Money** -- They have resources AND willingness | Financial objections |
| 6 | **Support** -- People around them will support the decision | Spouse/partner objections |
| 7 | **Trust** -- Your solution is different from past failures | "I've been burned before" |

**Operating principle:** Build all seven beliefs BEFORE transitioning to pitch. If all are checked, you get an "objectionless close."

**Money belief rule:** Leave money to the END. Do not dig into finances prematurely or people disqualify themselves before you pitch.

## The Buying Pocket (Gordon)

A spectrum model for where the prospect sits on belief:

- **Far Left:** Does not believe transformation is possible --> Use stories, case studies, proof to move them RIGHT (alleviate doubt)
- **Far Right:** Believes they can do it themselves (ego) --> Use skilled questioning, chunking down, reframes to move them LEFT (create doubt)
- **Buying Pocket (middle):** Believes transformation is possible, but ONLY with your help

**Anti-pattern:** Prospect already says they want coaching and cannot do it alone. Salesperson spends 20 minutes trying to corner them into admitting they cannot do it alone. Doubt is already established. Move on.

## Consistency Bias and Socratic Dialogue

### Consistency Bias (Cialdini)

The highest driver of human behavior is the drive to appear consistent with what we have already said.

### Socratic Dialogue (Gordon)

Asking skilled questions to help the prospect come to the conclusion you want them to reach, but as if it is their own idea.

**Why it works:** When prospects express the seven beliefs through YOUR questions, they are telling THEMSELVES. It becomes incongruent for them NOT to move forward.

**Key:** "If you say it, it's shit. If they say it, it's true." (Gordon) Never tell the prospect something they can tell themselves through a well-placed question.

## Internal vs. External Pressure (Gordon)

| Type | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal pressure** | Prospect motivated by THEIR OWN reasons | Cost of inaction exceeds cost of investment |
| **External pressure** | Prospect motivated by SOMEONE ELSE'S reasons | Fast-action discount, "only 3 spots left" |

**Goal:** Build a healthy balance, weighted toward internal pressure. The wider the gap between current and desired situation, the more internal tension, the more they want to resolve it by buying.

External pressure (discounts, urgency) is not bad -- just do not make it the ONLY lever.

## Bending Reality (Voss)

### Emotional Anchoring Tactics

1. **Anchor their emotions.** Start with a negative anchor: "I've got bad news..." or "You're not going to like this..." Bends perception of what follows.
2. **Let the other side go first on price** (usually). Their naming a price creates actual commitment.
3. **Pivot to non-monetary terms.** Brainstorm things valuable to you and easy for them.
4. **Use odd/precise numbers.** $105,357 carries more authority than $105,000. Odd numbers perceived as calculated and immovable.

### Compromise = Formula for Failure (Voss)

"Paint the bedroom blue or yellow? Compromise = green. Nobody wanted green."

- Compromise always involves giving something up (loss)
- Your view of what you give up exceeds its value to the other side
- Creates a downward spiral
- **No deal is better than a bad deal. A bad deal will bleed you for years.**

## The Mirror Principle (Gordon)

Your objections = their objections. Whatever internal doubts YOU carry about the product, price, or process will be reflected in the prospect's objections.

**Fix:** Resolve your own beliefs first. If you would not buy the product at that price, your energy communicates that uncertainty.

## Improv as Negotiation Skill (Serhant)

**Core principles:**
- **"Yes, and..."** -- Accept what is thrown at you and build on it. Never shut down new information.
- **Specificity** -- Get specific so clients think specifically about what they actually want.
- **Listen to respond, not reply** -- Truly hear before responding.

**Why scripts fail:** "What happens when the other side goes off script? You freeze."

**Instructor resolution on scripts vs. improv:**
- Voss: Use precise scripted tools (Ackerman, calibrated questions)
- Gordon: Drill scripts until they become natural
- Serhant: Scripts are dead; use improv
- **Synthesis:** Scripts are training wheels that become internalized playbooks. Drill scripts (Voss/Gordon) until they flow naturally as improv (Serhant).

## Selling to Affluent Prospects (Alexander)

### Corporate 200-400K Earners
Their pain is NOT money. Their pain is: low leverage, no freedom, no time, ceiling on income.

Key questions: "What's the extent you have to go to in order to make that money?"

### High-Net-Worth Individuals (7+ figures liquid)
Extra $3-5K/month does not change their life. Frame as ROI conversation, not emotional conversation.

"Do you think an investment of $15K to make $36K/year with zero additional time is a good investment?"

### The Empathy Gap

As sales reps, we often project OUR relationship with money onto them. A $5K investment might be your entire bank account but their rounding error. Understand their actual problems, not what you assume.

## "Know Their Religion" (Voss)

Everyone has things they believe in that are larger than what is at stake -- their "religion." Understanding someone's worldview, reason for being, gives you influence.

**How:** Review adjectives they use. Words conveying PASSION are the most revealing. Mirror these words back.

**Application:** Position your objectives within their worldview. If your solution can be framed as aligned with their "religion," decision-making shifts dramatically.
