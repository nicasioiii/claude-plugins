# Persuasion Principles: Deep-Dive Reference

## Prospect Theory -- Full Mechanics (Voss via Kahneman)

### The Framing Effect

People respond differently to identical choices based on presentation:
- "90% success rate" vs. "10% failure rate" = same fact, different emotional response
- In sales: "You'll save $500/month" vs. "You're currently losing $500/month" = same math, but loss frame is 3-9x more motivating

### Loss Aversion -- The Numbers

- Published: losses hurt 2x as much as equivalent gains
- Private Kahneman estimate: 5-7x (lowered for academic credibility)
- Sales application: demonstrate 9x value to make the sale (because the perceived risk of loss from buying must be overcome)
- **70% of buy decisions** are made to AVOID LOSS, not to achieve gain

### Negative Emotions Impact

- Negative emotions have roughly 3x the impact of positive emotions (Voss)
- Negatives create 3x or more the size of obstacles
- Reasons for NOT doing something are more impactful than reasons FOR doing something
- Therefore: **clear the barriers (negatives) FIRST**, then the path to agreement opens

### Bending Reality -- Tactical Application

**Step 1:** Establish tactical empathy (you cannot bend reality without trust)
**Step 2:** Anchor emotions: "I've got bad news..." or "You're not going to like this..."
**Step 3:** Let the other side go first on price (their number = real commitment)
**Step 4:** Pivot to non-monetary terms (things valuable to you, easy for them)
**Step 5:** Use odd/precise numbers ($37,893 not $38,000 -- adds authority and perceived calculation)

### Compromise Is Failure (Voss)

- Compromise always involves giving something up = loss
- Your perception of what you gave up exceeds its value to the other side
- Creates downward spiral of dissatisfaction
- "No deal is better than a bad deal"
- "The negotiator who offers to meet you in the middle is usually a poor judge of distance"

## The Six Human Needs -- Pitching Application (Gordon)

### Certainty (Most Powerful in Sales)

**What it sounds like from prospects:** "I just want to know exactly what I'm getting." / "What's the guarantee?" / "How do I know this will work?"

**How to leverage in pitch:**
- Specific timelines: "In 8 weeks, you'll have..."
- Clear milestones: "Week 1 we do X, Week 2 we do Y"
- Guarantees or risk reversal
- Case studies with specific numbers

### Significance (Most Powerful in Sales)

**What it sounds like:** "I want to be the best." / "I want people to take me seriously." / "I want to stand out."

**How to leverage:**
- "You'll be recognized as the authority in your space"
- "Your team will look to you as the leader who made this happen"
- Status-raising language tied to their identity

### Variety, Growth, Contribution, Love

These are secondary but should be woven into pitch when the prospect has expressed related desires:
- Variety: "Every day brings new challenges"
- Growth: "Skills that compound over your career"
- Contribution: "Give back to your community/family"
- Love: "Build meaningful relationships with clients/team"

## Consistency Bias -- Tactical Application (Gordon)

### The Mechanism

Once someone states a position, they feel compelled to act consistently with it. This is the HIGHEST driver of human behavior (Cialdini).

### In Sales

When prospects express beliefs through YOUR questions, they are telling THEMSELVES:
- "Yes, this is a real problem for me" --> Now inconsistent to say "I don't need help"
- "Yes, I've been dealing with this for 2 years" --> Now inconsistent to say "I can wait"
- "Yes, doing nothing will cost me more" --> Now inconsistent to say "I'll think about it"

### The Socratic Dialogue Method

Ask questions that lead them to your desired conclusion without YOU stating it:
- "What's been the biggest impact of not solving this?"
- "How much longer are you willing to live with this?"
- "If nothing changes in the next 12 months, where does that leave you?"

Each question builds a chain of self-stated beliefs that makes buying the logical conclusion.

### "If You Say It, It's Shit. If They Say It, It's True." (Gordon)

Never tell the prospect something they can tell themselves. Your job is to ask the question that triggers the insight, not deliver the insight as a statement.

## Internal vs. External Pressure -- Detailed Framework (Gordon)

### Internal Pressure (Preferred)

Built through discovery by defining the gap:
- **Hell Island** = current situation (specific numbers, emotional impact)
- **Heaven Island** = desired situation (specific numbers, life impact)
- **The Gap** = the tension that compels action

The wider you make this gap through skilled questioning, the more internal pressure builds.

### External Pressure (Supplementary)

- Fast-action bonuses
- Limited spots/availability
- Deadline-based pricing
- Social proof (others are doing this NOW)

**Balance:** Weight heavily toward internal pressure. External pressure alone creates buyer's remorse and refund requests.

### Urgency Creation (Serhant Additions)

Three types:
1. **Push:** Use market data, timelines, and consequences. "Be their doctor -- tell them they need this medicine."
2. **Pull:** Take the deal away. "Maybe now isn't the right time..." -- reminds them WHY they wanted this.
3. **Persist:** Repeat the same story. Facts tell, stories sell. There is power in repetition.

**Warning:** Creating urgency is NOT creating anxiety. You are creating structure and timelines, not panic.

## The Fairness F-Bomb (Voss)

### Three Ways "Fair" Gets Used

1. **"We just want what's fair"** = defensive/destabilizing. Implied allegation of unfairness. Counter: "I apologize. Go back to where I started treating you unfairly, and we'll fix it."

2. **"We've given you a fair offer"** = manipulative. Counter: Mirror "Fair offer?" + label: "It seems like you're ready to provide evidence that supports that."

3. **"I want you to feel treated fairly at all times"** = positive (USE THIS). Deploy early. Establishes honest dealing and gives counterpart the right to use "fair" honestly.
