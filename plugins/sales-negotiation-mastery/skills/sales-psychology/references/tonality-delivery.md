# Tonality & Delivery Reference Guide

The 7-38-55 rule is clear: only 7% of communication is words. Master tonality and presence, and your words become secondary.

---

## The 7-38-55 Rule: How Communication Works

**The Breakdown:**
- **7% = Words** (what you say)
- **38% = Tonality** (how you say it - tone, pace, inflection, breathing)
- **55% = Body Language** (on video, this becomes presence)

**The Sales Implication:**
You could say the PERFECT words in the WRONG tone and lose the deal.
Conversely, you could say MEDIOCRE words in a confident tone and close.

**Practical Reality:**
- Master tonality BEFORE perfecting scripts
- A mediocre script delivered with perfect tonality beats a perfect script delivered with uncertain tonality
- Your emotional state directly translates to prospect emotional state (mirror neurons)

## The Five Voices: Complete Mechanics

### 1. THE INQUISITIVE VOICE

**Core Mechanics:**
- Curiosity in tone
- Questions, not statements
- Slightly higher pitch than baseline
- Genuine interest (not faked)
- Listening posture (pausing for answers)

**Physical Elements:**
- Lean in slightly when asking
- Eyebrows raised (curiosity)
- Smile in your voice
- Slow down slightly to show you care about the answer

**When to Use:**
- Asking discovery questions
- Building initial rapport
- Seeking to understand their world
- 40-50% of your call should be this voice

**Example Talk Tracks:**
- "Tell me if I'm wrong, but it sounds like..."
- "What makes the most sense to you?"
- "How would you want to approach this?"
- "Walk me through how that's been working for you"
- "What's the biggest challenge you're facing?"

**Why It Works:**
- It invites collaboration instead of confrontation
- People love talking about themselves
- When you sound curious, they feel heard
- It forces them to elaborate and reveal more

**Advanced Technique:** After asking an inquisitive question, COUNT TO FIVE in your head before responding. Let them fill the silence. They'll go deeper.

---

### 2. THE DECLARATIVE VOICE

**Core Mechanics:**
- Statement-based, not question-based
- Matter-of-fact, direct
- Medium to lower pitch
- Confident assertion (not tentative)
- No upward inflection at end
- Full stops, not trailing off

**Physical Elements:**
- Shoulders back (confident posture)
- Level eye contact
- Slower pace
- Firm jaw position
- Measured breathing

**When to Use:**
- Setting boundaries ("Here's what we're going to do")
- Making non-negotiable points
- Presenting solutions with conviction
- Creating clear structure ("The process works like this...")
- ~10-15% of call

**Example Talk Tracks:**
- "Here's what's going to happen..."
- "This is how we operate..."
- "That's not going to work because..."
- "The timeline is X. That's final."

**Why It Works:**
- Shows conviction and certainty
- Prospect mirrors your confidence
- Creates clear direction
- No ambiguity in what you're saying

**Warning:** Overuse (>20% of call) sounds aggressive and closes conversations.

**Advanced Technique:** Use declarative voice ONLY on points you can back up. If you waffle after a declarative statement, credibility drops.

---

### 3. THE POSITIVE PLAYFUL VOICE

**Core Mechanics:**
- Light, warm, genuinely good-natured
- Almost joking but never mocking
- Smile through the phone (they hear it)
- Slightly higher pitch (energy)
- Rhythmic, bouncy cadence
- Humor used to connect, not belittle

**Physical Elements:**
- Smile while speaking
- Slight animation in face
- Open posture
- Relaxed shoulders
- Eye crinkles when smiling (genuine smile)

**When to Use:**
- Building relationship early in call
- Defusing tension or heavy moments
- Creating lightness around difficult topics
- Making hard asks seem less heavy ("We might need you to invest $X")
- Creating connection and liking
- ~20-25% of call

**Example Talk Tracks:**
- "Ha! I love the optimism here."
- "You're really reaching here, and I appreciate the confidence!"
- "We might be in trouble if your boss expects that timeline!"
- "OK that's a good one. But here's actually what happened..."

**Why It Works:**
- Builds trust faster than almost any other voice
- Makes you human (not just a salesman)
- Defuses defensive reactions
- Creates genuine connection
- People want to work with people who seem genuinely good-natured

**Key Rule:** Humor should NEVER be at their expense. Never punch down or mock them.

**Advanced Technique:** Self-deprecating humor works best. Making fun of yourself creates liking.

---

### 4. THE FM DJ VOICE

**Core Mechanics:**
- Slow, measured, calm, almost hypnotic
- Lower pitch, full resonance
- Smooth, rhythmic, melodic
- Pause between phrases (letting words land)
- Sound like you have all the time in the world
- Relaxed authority

**Physical Elements:**
- Posture fully relaxed
- Slow breathing (visible pauses)
- Lower jaw relaxed
- Smooth hand movements
- Calm facial expression

**When to Use:**
- De-escalating emotional situations
- Closing (making offer)
- Handling objections
- When emotions are high
- Creating calm authority
- ~30-35% of call (heaviest use)

**Example Talk Tracks (imagine slow, melodic delivery):**
- "I understand you're frustrated... Let's take a breath and talk through this together..."
- "Here's what I'm hearing... and I want to make sure I understand..."
- "So based on everything you've told me, the core issue is... and here's how we solve it..."

**Why It Works:**
- This is the most powerful voice for de-escalation
- Forces the other person to calm down too (mirror neurons)
- Creates sense of safety and authority
- Unconsciously powerful for trust-building
- Makes big asks feel smaller

**Pro Tip:** Practice this voice until it's genuinely comfortable. It can't be faked or it sounds manipulative.

**Advanced Technique:** Combine FM DJ voice with the inquisitive voice's upward tone on questions. Example:
"I understand you're frustrated... Tell me more about what happened?"
[First sentence in FM DJ, question at end with slight upward inflection - still calm but inviting]

---

### 5. THE DIRECT ASSERTIVE VOICE

**Core Mechanics:**
- Firm, clear, no ambiguity
- No softening language
- Direct confrontation with love
- Zero negotiability
- Professional but unmovable
- Lines in the sand

**Physical Elements:**
- Chest out (assertive posture)
- Direct eye contact (intense)
- Slow, powerful speech
- Jaw forward slightly
- No fidgeting

**When to Use:**
- When you have true walk-away power
- When something is genuinely non-negotiable
- Ending unproductive discussions
- Confronting dishonesty/excuses
- ~5-10% of call (minimal use)

**Example Talk Tracks:**
- "This is not negotiable."
- "Here's the deadline. It's final."
- "This is the limit. Take it or we're done."
- "I need you to be straight with me..."

**Why It Works:**
- Shows you're serious
- Creates respect through clarity
- Stops haggling and game-playing
- Makes prospect understand true stakes

**Warning:** Overuse destroys relationships. Use ONLY when:
1. You mean it
2. You can back it up
3. You're willing to lose the deal

**Advanced Technique:** Use direct assertive voice right BEFORE FM DJ voice. Create the line, then soften with empathy:
"Look, this is not negotiable. [Pause - let it land]
Now, I understand you might have concerns about this. Let me explain why it has to be this way..."

## Tonality Applications by Call Phase

### OPENING & RAPPORT BUILDING (First 5 minutes)
**Optimal Mix:**
- 70% Positive Playful + Inquisitive
- 20% FM DJ (setting calm tone)
- 10% Declarative (setting boundaries/expectations)

**Goal:** Establish safety, warmth, and credibility. They should feel you're a good person before you're a salesman.

### DISCOVERY QUESTIONS (Main body - 30-45 minutes)
**Optimal Mix:**
- 70% Inquisitive
- 15% FM DJ (when they get emotional)
- 10% Positive Playful (keeping it light)
- 5% Declarative (guiding to next question)

**Goal:** Get them talking 70%, you listening and asking 30%. Master silence.

### HANDLING OBJECTIONS (Whenever they arise)
**Optimal Mix:**
- 50% FM DJ (de-escalate)
- 25% Inquisitive (understand the real concern)
- 15% Positive Playful (keep it light, not heavy)
- 10% Declarative (clear on your position)

**Goal:** Defuse emotion, understand real issue, maintain rapport while being clear.

### MAKING THE OFFER (5-10 minutes)
**Optimal Mix:**
- 50% FM DJ (calm authority)
- 30% Declarative (clarity on what you're offering)
- 15% Positive Playful (excitement about possibility)
- 5% Inquisitive (their reaction)

**Goal:** Present with conviction and calm. They should feel safe and assured.

### CLOSING (Final 3-5 minutes)
**Optimal Mix:**
- 60% FM DJ (calm authority)
- 20% Declarative (assumption of yes)
- 15% Inquisitive (getting commitment)
- 5% Positive Playful (celebration energy)

**Goal:** Assumed close. FM DJ creates confidence they're making right decision.

## Specific Tonality Adjustments

### CONFIDENCE TONALITY (For offers, boundaries, statements)
**Mechanics:**
- Slow, measured pace (not rushed)
- Lower pitch (authority)
- Full breath between sentences (no gasping)
- Assured tone without arrogance
- Pauses that land

**How to Develop:**
- Breathe fully before speaking
- Lower pitch intentionally
- Slow down 20% from normal speech
- Pause between major points

**Effect on Prospect:**
- Mirror neurons = they feel confident too
- Confidence creates belief in your solution
- De-escalates pressure and objections

### CURIOUS TONALITY (For discovery, questions)
**Mechanics:**
- Slightly higher pitch than baseline
- Genuine interest in answer
- Pause for them to fill silence
- Not robotic or fake
- Engaged listening

**How to Develop:**
- Lean in slightly
- Genuinely wonder about their answer
- Don't pre-answer your own questions
- Let silence hang (they fill it)

**Effect on Prospect:**
- Feels heard and understood
- Opens up more (you care about them)
- Reveals deeper information

### EMPATHETIC TONALITY (For acknowledging objections/emotions)
**Mechanics:**
- Warm, understanding, slower pace
- Match their energy level
- Validating tone
- No defensiveness

**How to Develop:**
- Take a breath before responding
- Remind yourself you understand them
- Slow down your response
- Validate before explaining

**Effect on Prospect:**
- Trust increases dramatically
- Defensiveness decreases
- They feel safe to keep talking

## Language Certainty: Words Matter

### UNCERTAIN LANGUAGE TO ELIMINATE

These words/phrases signal lack of confidence:

- "I think this could work"
- "It might be able to help"
- "We'll probably be able to"
- "If it goes well"
- "I hope this will"
- "It seems like it could"
- "Hopefully this works"

**The Problem:** Uncertain language makes prospect uncertain too. They mirror your lack of conviction.

### CERTAIN LANGUAGE TO USE

Replace uncertain language with:

- "This WILL work because..." [explain why]
- "Here's exactly what we'll do..."
- "You'll see [specific result] by [date]..."
- "This is how it works..."
- "Based on [reason], this is the right move"

**Example Transformation:**

**Uncertain:** "I think this could help with your lead generation, hopefully. It might increase your calls by like 30 or something."

**Certain:** "This will generate 20-30 qualified leads per week for you. Here's how: [mechanism]. That's based on what we've done with similar clients."

## Advanced Tonality Mechanics

### THE STRATEGIC PAUSE

**Mechanics:**
- Ask a question and STOP
- Count to 3-5 seconds in your head
- Do not fill the silence
- Let prospect feel the vacuum

**Why It Works:**
- Creates psychological pressure to speak
- Prevents you from talking too much
- Forces them to elaborate
- Shows you're genuinely listening

**Example:**
You: "So what's been the biggest challenge with your current situation?"
[PAUSE - count 1, 2, 3, 4, 5]
Them: [elaborates, goes deeper]

### THE EMOTIONAL PACE CHANGE

**Mechanics:**
- When emotions are high, SLOW DOWN
- When energy is low, SPEED UP
- Never match nervous energy (it amplifies)
- Always match calm energy

**Why It Works:**
- Your pace becomes their pace
- Slowing down de-escalates
- Speeding up energizes without overwhelming

### THE BREATH WORK

**Mechanics:**
- Fully breathe before statements (not gasps)
- Pause between phrases to breathe
- Breathing = confidence
- Gasping = anxiety

**Why It Works:**
- Full breathing = calm nervous system
- Shallow breathing = anxiety signal
- Prospect senses your breathing through your voice

## Common Tonality Mistakes

### MISTAKE #1: TALKING TOO FAST
**Problem:** Signals anxiety, makes prospect anxious
**Fix:** Slow down 20% from your normal speed

### MISTAKE #2: TALKING TOO MUCH
**Problem:** Doesn't let them talk, they disengage
**Fix:** Aim for 30% you, 70% them in discovery

### MISTAKE #3: NO PAUSES (RUN-ON SENTENCES)
**Problem:** Doesn't let things land, sounds rushed
**Fix:** Pause after major statements. Let them absorb.

### MISTAKE #4: SAME TONE THROUGHOUT
**Problem:** Gets boring, loses attention
**Fix:** Vary between the five voices intentionally

### MISTAKE #5: FAKE ENTHUSIASM
**Problem:** Comes across as phony, kills trust
**Fix:** Be genuine. If you're not excited, they won't be.

### MISTAKE #6: STARTING OFFERS TOO LOUD/STRONG
**Problem:** Can sound aggressive or sales-y
**Fix:** Start offer with FM DJ voice (calm), then build energy

### MISTAKE #7: ENDING STATEMENTS WITH UPWARD INFLECTION
**Problem:** Sounds like a question, kills authority
**Fix:** End definitive statements with downward inflection

## Presence & Body Language (On Video)

Since 55% of communication is body language:

### CONFIDENCE POSTURE
- Shoulders back
- Chest open
- Slight forward lean (interest)
- Hands visible (not crossed)
- Head level (not tilted down)

### ENGAGED LISTENING
- Lean in when they talk
- Nod occasionally (shows understanding)
- Eyebrows raised (interest)
- Eye contact (but not intense staring)

### OPEN BODY LANGUAGE
- Arms uncrossed
- Hands visible (on desk or used for emphasis)
- Legs uncrossed (open stance)
- Facing them directly

### WHAT KILLS PRESENCE
- Crossing arms (defensive)
- Looking down/away (disengaged)
- Fidgeting (nervous)
- Stiff posture (uncomfortable)
- Phone/laptop visible (distracted)

## Tone Matching & Mirroring

**The Mechanic:** When prospect is emotional, you match their energy level (not exact tone) while staying calm.

**Example:**
- Prospect is frustrated (elevated tone)
- You match energy level (speak more deliberately, show you understand intensity)
- But use FM DJ voice (calm them down)
- This honors their emotion while de-escalating

**Why It Works:** Empathy requires showing you understand their emotional level. Then you guide them toward calm.

## Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Record Yourself
- Record a 10-minute segment of your call
- Listen back for tone shifts
- Note where you sound uncertain
- Identify which voice dominates (should be inquisitive + FM DJ)

### Exercise 2: The Five Voices Solo
- Practice each voice in isolation
- Record yourself using each voice
- Play them back
- Notice the differences

### Exercise 3: Pace Control
- Record yourself speaking at three different speeds: slow (too slow for you), normal, fast
- Listen back
- Find optimal speed (usually 15-20% slower than natural)

### Exercise 4: Pause Practice
- Ask a question
- Count to 10 silently
- Record it
- Notice how much better it sounds with pause

### Exercise 5: Mirror Neuron Test
- Do a call where you intentionally use FM DJ voice 60% of call
- Compare to normal call
- Track if close rate improves (it should)

## Summary: The Tonality Mastery Stack

1. **Master the Five Voices first** - Know when to use each
2. **Slow down 20% from normal** - Confidence comes from pacing
3. **Pause intentionally** - Let things land
4. **Match energy, lead with calmness** - Mirror but don't amplify anxiety
5. **Practice recording yourself** - Can't improve what you don't measure
6. **FM DJ + Inquisitive = 70% of call** - These two carry most of the load
7. **Body language on video** - Open, engaged, present
8. **Eliminate uncertain language** - Replace "maybe" with "will"
9. **Let emotions guide your pace** - Slower when emotions high, faster when energy low
10. **Remember the 7-38-55 rule** - Tonality trumps words

**The Mastery Question:** Could someone listen to your call with their eyes closed (just audio) and feel completely confident in you and your solution? That's the standard.
