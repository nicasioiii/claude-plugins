# Voice Types, Inflection, and Silence Protocols

## The 5x Rule in Detail (Voss)

How you say something is roughly 5x more important than what you say. The counterpart will hear your intention and attitude in your tone.

**Practical implication:** You could say the perfect rebuttal script with the wrong tone and lose the deal. Conversely, you could say mediocre words in a confident, warm tone and close.

**The 7-38-55 Rule (Mehrabian via Voss):**
- 7% of communication = words
- 38% of communication = tone of voice
- 55% of communication = body language/facial expressions (on video)

**Key:** Pay attention to ALIGNMENT of these three, and CHANGES in alignment. When tone and body do not match words = problem. Use labels to probe moments of misalignment.

## Voice Type Deep Mechanics

### Positive/Playful Voice -- Full Breakdown

**Physical technique:**
- Relax your jaw and shoulders
- SMILE before speaking and while speaking
- Let breath flow naturally -- do not hold tension
- Slightly higher pitch than your neutral speaking voice
- Upward inflection on questions (genuine curiosity)

**When to deploy:**
- Opening the call (first 2-3 minutes)
- Asking discovery questions
- Light rapport moments
- After resolving an objection (returning to positive state)
- When prospect is engaged and sharing openly

**Signal it sends:** "I'm safe. I'm here to help. This is going to be a good conversation."

### Late Night FM DJ Voice -- Full Breakdown

**Physical technique:**
- Slow down by 30-40% from your natural pace
- Drop pitch slightly lower than normal
- Downward inflection at end of statements
- Clear articulation -- no mumbling
- Deliberate pauses between phrases

**When to deploy:**
- Labeling emotions: "It seems like this has been really frustrating..."
- After mirroring (the 4-second silence)
- Delivering the price
- Setting boundaries: "I'm sorry, that just doesn't work for me"
- When emotions are running high on the other side
- During accusation audits

**Signal it sends:** "I'm in control. We're going to figure this out. You're safe here."

### Direct/Assertive Voice -- Full Breakdown

**Physical technique:**
- Even-paced, measured delivery
- No upward inflection
- Strong downward inflection
- Direct eye contact (on video)
- Minimal filler words

**When to deploy (RARE):**
- True walk-away moment: "This is not going to work."
- Genuine non-negotiable: "That's not something we can do."
- Final closing authority: "Here's what needs to happen next."

**Warning signs you are overusing it:**
- Prospect becomes quiet and withdrawn
- Prospect becomes argumentative
- Rapport breaks and does not recover
- You feel powerful but the deal dies

## Rapport Level Switching Guide (Gordon)

### Seeking Rapport (upward inflection)
- When to use: Almost never in sales. Reserved for genuine "I don't know" moments.
- Sound: Like asking someone on a date for the first time
- Use case: "I'm not sure I understand -- could you explain that?"

### Neutral Rapport (even inflection)
- When to use: DEFAULT. 60-70% of the call.
- Sound: Two professionals having a peer-level conversation
- Use case: Discovery questions, pitch delivery, most of the call

### Breaking Rapport (downward inflection)
- When to use: Commands, closing moments, establishing authority
- Sound: A doctor delivering a diagnosis
- Use case: "Here's what I think you should do." / "The investment is $X."

**Switching pattern on a typical call:**
1. Opening: Positive/playful (rapport building)
2. Frame setting: Neutral to breaking (establishing authority)
3. Discovery: Neutral with moments of empathetic positive
4. Pain amplification: FM DJ (labeling emotions)
5. Transition: Neutral breaking (leadership)
6. Pitch: Neutral breaking (authority + certainty)
7. Price drop: FM DJ breaking (state price, silence)
8. Objection handling: FM DJ to neutral (grounded, oak tree)
9. Close: Breaking rapport (decisiveness)

## Silence Protocol

### After a Mirror (Voss)
- Repeat their 1-3 key words with upward inflection
- SHUT UP for minimum 4 seconds
- Do NOT continue talking
- Do NOT say "you know what I mean?"
- Do NOT provide rationale
- Let them re-explain and expand

### After a Label (Voss)
- Deploy the label: "It seems like..."
- SHUT UP and listen
- Do NOT expand, provide rationale, or say "have I got that right?"
- If silence continues (they look thoughtful): you are on the right track -- wait longer
- Over-labeling is NOT a problem

### After the Price Drop (Gordon)
- State the investment clearly
- SHUT UP
- Do NOT fill with justification
- Do NOT immediately offer payment plans
- Let them respond first -- their first words reveal what to do next

### Strategic Silence in Negotiation (Serhant)
- Silence can go for DAYS in negotiation (not just seconds)
- "Almost never answer anything too quickly, especially with lots of emotion at play"
- Following up too much reveals you want the deal more than they do

## Tonality Drills

### Daily Practice (5 minutes)

1. Record yourself reading a pitch script three ways: positive/playful, FM DJ, assertive
2. Listen back. Mark where you default to seeking rapport when you should be neutral
3. Practice the same discovery question with upward then downward inflection -- hear the difference
4. Practice the price drop statement: state price, then count silently to 6 before speaking again

### Study Recommendations (Alexander)

Watch these for tonality range:
- Politicians under pressure at press conferences (Obama, Reagan)
- Actors: Leonardo DiCaprio (Wolf of Wall Street conviction scenes), Cillian Murphy (Thomas Shelby calm authority)
- Key: understand WHY they are saying something -- the intention creates the tone naturally
