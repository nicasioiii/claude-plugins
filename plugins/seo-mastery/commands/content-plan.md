# Content Plan Command

Interactive workflow for designing your cluster-based content strategy and publishing roadmap.

## Pre-Planning Questions

Before designing your strategy, answer these:

**Question 1: How many target keywords did you identify?**
(e.g., "80 keywords in 4 potential clusters")

**Question 2: What's your writing capacity?**
(e.g., "I can write 2-3 articles/week" or "I have a writer who can do 4-5/week")

**Question 3: What's your team structure?**
(e.g., "Solo (no team)" or "Designer + writer + me managing")

**Question 4: What's your primary goal?**
(e.g., "Affiliate revenue" or "Lead generation" or "Establish authority")

**Question 5: How long before you need results?**
(e.g., "3 months" or "6 months" or "No deadline, long-term play")

## Phase 1: Cluster Identification

**Task:** Organize keywords into 3-5 coherent clusters based on topic relationships

**Output:** Cluster map with pillar + satellites for each cluster

**Process:**

**Step 1:** List all your "RANK NOW" keywords
- Take output from keyword research
- Focus on opportunities scoring 6-8 out of 10
- Should have 50-80 keywords

**Step 2:** Identify natural groupings
- Which keywords are topically related?
- Do they serve same or similar user intent?
- Can they be served by one pillar + multiple satellites?

**Step 3:** For each cluster, identify:
- **Pillar keyword:** The broad keyword (highest volume)
- **Satellite keywords:** 4-6 specific, long-tail variants
- **Cluster size:** Total articles needed
- **Thematic focus:** What unites this cluster?

**Example clustering (dog nutrition niche):**

```
CLUSTER 1: "Dog Food Types"
- Pillar: "Best dog food" (600 monthly searches)
- Satellites:
  - "Dry dog food" (150 searches)
  - "Canned dog food" (120 searches)
  - "Raw dog food" (80 searches)
  - "Prescription dog food" (60 searches)
- Total articles: 5

CLUSTER 2: "Dog Allergies & Sensitivities"
- Pillar: "Best dog food for allergies" (400 monthly)
- Satellites:
  - "Dog food for sensitive stomach" (180 searches)
  - "Limited ingredient dog food" (140 searches)
  - "Hypoallergenic dog food" (100 searches)
  - "How to identify dog allergies" (90 searches)
- Total articles: 5

CLUSTER 3: "Dog Nutrition by Life Stage"
- Pillar: "Dog nutrition guide" (500 monthly)
- Satellites:
  - "Best puppy food" (120 searches)
  - "Senior dog food" (100 searches)
  - "Pregnant dog nutrition" (40 searches)
  - "Nursing dog diet" (30 searches)
- Total articles: 5
```

**Reference:** Read **content-cluster-templates.md** → "Template Selection" + **content-cluster-strategy.md** → "Cluster Anatomy"

**Time:** 2-3 hours

**Deliverable:** "cluster_map_[date].xlsx" with columns:
- Cluster Name
- Pillar Keyword
- Pillar Volume
- Pillar KD
- Satellite 1-6
- Cluster Size (articles)
- Competitive Advantage
- Priority (1, 2, 3)

## Phase 2: Pillar Page Architecture

**Task:** Design comprehensive pillar pages for each cluster

**Output:** Pillar outline + content specs for each pillar

**Process:**

**Step 1:** For each pillar, define the scope
- What is the high-level topic?
- What aspects will you cover?
- What won't you cover?

**Step 2:** Create pillar outline structure:**

```
1. Introduction & Problem (200-300 words)
2. Foundational Concepts (300-400 words)
3-6. Main Topic Sections (400-600 words each, 4 sections)
7. Comparison/Summary Table
8. FAQ (200-300 words)
9. Conclusion & Next Steps
```

**Step 3:** For each section, map to satellites
- Section 2: "Different types → Link to type satellites"
- Section 3: "Choosing for health conditions → Link to health satellites"
- Section 4: "Cost considerations → Link to budget satellites"
- etc.

**Step 4:** Define content specs:**
- **Target word count:** 2,500-3,500 words
- **Target keyword:** (your pillar keyword)
- **Secondary keywords:** (keywords naturally fitting pillar scope)
- **Satellite links:** (exact list of articles to link to)

**Step 5:** Create sample outline for first pillar

**Reference:** Read **pillar-page-architecture.md** → "Pillar Page Structure & SEO"

**Time:** 1-2 hours per pillar (3-6 hours for all pillars)

**Deliverable:** "pillar_outlines_[date].docx" with:
- Pillar title
- Target keyword
- Outline (H1, H2, H3 structure)
- Word count target
- Satellite links planned
- Unique angle/competitive advantage

## Phase 3: Satellite Page Planning

**Task:** Define unique angle + outline for each satellite

**Output:** Satellite specs showing how each differs + complements pillar

**Process:**

**Step 1:** For each satellite, define unique angle
- How does this satellite differ from pillar?
- What specific question does it answer?
- What's unique vs. competitors?

**Example:**
```
Satellite: "Best dog food for sensitive stomach"
Unique angle: "Step-by-step transition guide" (competitors lack)
Target keyword: "best dog food for sensitive stomach"
Word count: 1,500-2,000
Sections:
- What is sensitive stomach (definition)
- Signs your dog has it (identification)
- Top recommendations (products)
- Transition process (unique!)
- Troubleshooting (if transition fails)
- FAQ
```

**Step 2:** Create quick outline for each satellite
- H1 (title)
- H2 sections (4-6 main sections)
- Approximate word count per section

**Step 3:** Note internal links
- Every satellite links back to pillar
- Every satellite links to 1-2 related satellites
- Document exact anchor text

**Reference:** Read **content-cluster-templates.md** → "Satellite Page Design"

**Time:** 2-3 hours (for all satellites)

**Deliverable:** "satellite_specs_[date].xlsx" with:
- Satellite keyword
- Satellite title
- Unique angle (1-2 sentences)
- Target word count
- Main sections (H2s)
- Back-links (which pages link here?)
- Internal links (which satellites to link to?)

## Phase 4: Internal Linking Strategy

**Task:** Map all internal links between pillar + satellites

**Output:** Complete internal linking specification

**Process:**

**Step 1:** Create interlinking map (visual)
- Center: Pillar
- Around: All satellites
- Arrows: Show which links where

**Step 2:** Create link detail spreadsheet**

**Columns:**
- Source page (pillar or satellite)
- Target page (pillar or satellite)
- Anchor text (exact text to link)
- Location (which section of source page)
- Context (why this link makes sense)

**Example:**
```
| Source | Target | Anchor | Location | Context |
|--------|--------|--------|----------|---------|
| Pillar | S1 | best dog food for sensitive stomach | Section 2 | When discussing health conditions |
| S1 | Pillar | back to dog nutrition guide | Conclusion | Direct reader to broader guide |
| S1 | S2 | see our limited ingredient alternatives | Body | When discussing same problem, different solution |
```

**Step 3:** Ensure all links anchor text is descriptive
- Goal: Natural language + keyword-rich
- Not: "click here," "read more"
- Yes: "best dog food for sensitive stomach"

**Step 4:** Count links
- Pillar: Should have 15-25 outgoing links (to satellites)
- Each satellite: Should have 3-5 outgoing links (to pillar + related satellites)
- Result: Authority concentrated on pillar, spreads to satellites

**Reference:** Read **internal-linking-playbook.md** → "Cluster Interlinking Architecture"

**Time:** 1-2 hours

**Deliverable:** "internal_linking_map_[date].xlsx" with:
- Source Page
- Target Page
- Anchor Text
- Location in Article
- Link Context
- Status (implemented / planned)

## Phase 5: Publishing Roadmap & Timeline

**Task:** Create detailed publishing schedule and cadence

**Output:** Week-by-week publishing plan for next 12-16 weeks

**Process:**

**Step 1:** Calculate publishing capacity
- How many articles/week can you publish?
- Example: "2 articles/week = 8 articles/month"
- Plan realistically (include editing, revisions, delays)

**Step 2:** Prioritize clusters
- **Cluster 1 (First):** Easiest to rank (lowest KD, clear gaps)
- **Cluster 2 (Second):** Medium difficulty
- **Cluster 3 (Third):** Higher difficulty or lower priority

**Step 3:** Create publishing schedule**

**Example (with 2 articles/week capacity):**

```
WEEK 1: Publish Cluster 1 Pillar
- Tuesday: Pillar page "Dog Nutrition Guide" live

WEEK 2: Publish Cluster 1 Satellites
- Tuesday: Satellite "Best Dog Food for Sensitive Stomach"
- Friday: Satellite "Limited Ingredient Dog Food"

WEEK 3: Continue Cluster 1
- Monday: Satellite "How to Calculate Dog Portions"
- Thursday: Satellite "Dog Food Ingredients Glossary"

WEEK 4: Start Cluster 2
- Tuesday: Pillar "Dog Health Complete Guide"
- Friday: Satellite "Best Dog Food for Skin Allergies"

WEEK 5: Continue Cluster 2
- Monday: Satellite "Dog Immune System Nutrition"
- Thursday: Satellite "Supplements for Dogs"

... continue through remaining clusters
```

**Step 4:** Account for delays
- Add 1-2 week buffer for edits/revisions
- Plan for holidays (reduce publishing)
- Account for unexpected delays

**Step 5:** Define post-publishing schedule
- Week 1: Monitor indexing
- Week 2-4: Check rankings, adjust internal links if needed
- Month 2-3: Refresh content if needed (stats updates, new examples)
- Month 6: Consider cluster expansion (new satellites?)

**Reference:** Read **publishing-cadence-optimization.md** → "Publishing Timeline & Optimization"

**Time:** 1-2 hours

**Deliverable:** "publishing_roadmap_[date].xlsx" with:
- Week
- Date
- Content Title
- Article Type (Pillar/Satellite)
- Cluster
- Assigned Writer
- Status (Planned/Drafted/Published)
- Tracking Link (URL when published)

## Phase 6: Competitive Content Analysis

**Task:** For each cluster, analyze top competitors and identify gaps

**Output:** Content gap summary + competitive advantage for each article

**Process:**

**Step 1:** For pillar keyword in each cluster:
- Google the keyword
- Analyze top 3 results
- Note: What they cover
- Identify: What they're missing
- Opportunity: How you can do better

**Step 2:** For key satellites:
- Research top competitor content
- Identify content gaps
- Plan how your satellite will be better/different

**Step 3:** Document competitive advantages
- For pillar: One major advantage
- For each satellite: One unique element

**Example:**
```
Cluster 1: "Dog Food Types"
Pillar: "Best dog food"
Competitors: List 3 top results
Competitive gap: All discuss types, but none have detailed transition guide
Your advantage: Add "How to transition between food types safely"

Satellite: "Raw dog food"
Competitors: Mostly pro-raw blogs with limited criticism
Your advantage: Balanced view with pros/cons + cost analysis
```

**Reference:** Read **competitive-landscape-analysis.md** → "Competitive Landscape Analysis" + **content-gap-identification.md**

**Time:** 2-3 hours (for all clusters)

**Deliverable:** "competitive_analysis_[date].docx" with:
- Cluster name
- Pillar keyword
- Top 3 competitors + URLs
- Content gaps identified
- Your competitive advantage
- Unique element per satellite

## Implementation Checklist

Before starting content production:

- [ ] Clusters identified (3-5 clusters)
- [ ] Pillars defined (pillar keyword + outline for each)
- [ ] Satellites planned (unique angle for each)
- [ ] Internal linking mapped (all links specified)
- [ ] Publishing schedule created (week-by-week)
- [ ] Competitive gaps identified (for each cluster)
- [ ] Team assigned (who writes what)
- [ ] Tools/resources ready (WordPress, images, references)
- [ ] Success metrics defined (what are we measuring?)

---

## Success Metrics to Track

Once you start publishing, track:

**Monthly metrics:**
- Articles published (on schedule?)
- Articles indexed by Google
- Organic traffic (is it growing?)
- Average ranking position (improving?)

**6-month metrics:**
- % of keywords ranking top 10
- % of keywords ranking top 3
- Organic traffic from clusters
- Conversions/revenue from organic traffic

**12-month metrics:**
- Total keywords ranking
- Topical authority level
- Estimated organic revenue
- Domain authority growth

## Next Steps

1. **Start with one cluster**
   - Complete pillar article first
   - Then satellites sequentially
   - Launch interlinking as satellites publish

2. **Monitor & optimize**
   - Check Google Search Console weekly
   - Adjust internal links if needed
   - Update with fresher data (monthly)

3. **Expand clusters**
   - After first cluster stable, launch second
   - Learn from first cluster, apply to second
   - Build momentum

4. **Scale to other clusters**
   - Rinse and repeat
   - Each cluster builds on previous learnings
   - Authority compounds over time
