# Backlink Analysis Framework

Complete system for analyzing competitor backlinks, identifying link sources, and building a link acquisition strategy.

## Backlink Fundamentals

**What is a backlink?** A link from another website pointing to your site.

**Why it matters:** Links are votes of confidence. More links (from quality sites) = higher authority = better ranking.

**Quality hierarchy:**
- Link from DA 70 site = more valuable than 10 links from DA 5 sites
- Editorial link (chosen by editor) > Paid link (bought)
- Topic-relevant link > Unrelated link

## Analyzing Top Competitors' Backlinks

### Phase 1: Backlink Export (Using Ahrefs)

**Step 1:** Go to Ahrefs.com Site Explorer

**Step 2:** Enter top competitor's domain

**Step 3:** Click "Backlinks" tab

**Step 4:** Filter by:
- Backlink type: "Do-follow" only (no-follow links don't pass authority)
- Link type: "External" (internal links aren't relevant)
- Sort by: "Domain Authority" descending (strongest links first)

**Step 5:** Export data (CSV if available) or record top 50-100 links

**Data to collect:**

| Referring Domain | DA | Link Type | Anchor Text | Target URL | Landing Page Topic |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| topicalblog.com | 42 | Editorial | "best dog food" | /buying-guide | Related |
| resourcesite.com | 35 | Editorial | "dog food guide" | /guide | Related |
| newssite.com | 62 | Editorial | "premium dog nutrition" | /products | Related |
| generalblog.com | 28 | Editorial | Domain name | / | Unrelated |

### Phase 2: Link Source Classification

Categorize links by source type:

**Link source types:**

| Type | Example | DA Range | Frequency | Likelihood of Getting Link |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Topical blogs | dog-nutrition-blog.com | 20-50 | High | HIGH—write about dogs |
| Industry news | petcare-news.com | 40-70 | Medium | MEDIUM—need news value |
| Roundups/lists | best-pet-products.com | 30-60 | High | HIGH—submit to roundup |
| Resource pages | pet-resources.com | 30-50 | Medium | MEDIUM—fill requirement |
| Directories | business-directory.com | 10-40 | Low | LOW—usually paid |
| Guest posts | various | 20-50 | Medium | HIGH—pitch guest post |
| Broken links | (where links broken) | Varies | Low | HIGH—broken link building |
| Forum discussions | pet-forums.com | 10-30 | High | MEDIUM—natural participation |
| Social media | twitter.com, linkedin.com | 80-90 | Very High | LOW—not effective |
| Wikipedia | wikipedia.org | 95 | Low | Very Low—hard to edit |

**Strategic insight:** Topical blogs, resource pages, and guest posts = your best targets

### Phase 3: Pattern Recognition

Identify themes in competitor's link profile:

**Questions to ask:**
1. Where do 60%+ of their links come from? (Identify link source clusters)
2. Are they using guest posting? (Look for "guest post by [author]")
3. Are they in directories? (Check for directory links)
4. Do they have links from major publications? (News coverage)
5. Any broken link strategies? (Links from old broken links)
6. Do competitors link to each other? (Internal niche linking)

**Example analysis:**

**Competitor link source breakdown:**
- Topical dog blogs: 35% of links (strongest opportunity)
- Industry news/publications: 20% of links
- Guest posts: 15% of links
- Resource pages: 15% of links
- Directories: 10% of links
- Other: 5% of links

**Your strategy:**
1. Find 20+ dog blogs linking to competitor
2. Research their linking policy (do they accept submissions?)
3. Reach out with better content
4. Repeat for top 3 competitors

### Phase 4: Referring Domain Diversity

Analyze where links come from:

**Good link profile:**
- 100+ referring domains
- Links from 20+ different sources/verticals
- Links from mixture of DA 20-60 sites (not all from same source)
- Regular new links (shows recent activity)

**Weak link profile:**
- <50 referring domains
- Links from 5-10 sources (concentrated)
- All links from DA <30 sites (low authority)
- No recent links (stagnant)

**Template analysis:**

| Metric | Competitor A | Competitor B | Your Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Total referring domains | 450 | 120 | 100-200 |
| Unique link sources | 25+ | 8 | 15+ |
| Links from DA 50+ | 180 (40%) | 30 (25%) | 40-60 |
| Links from DA 20-50 | 220 (49%) | 65 (54%) | 40-80 |
| Links from DA <20 | 50 (11%) | 25 (21%) | 0-20 |
| Newest link date | Last week | 6 months ago | Recent |
| 12-month growth | +80 links | +10 links | +30 links/year |

## Identifying Linkable Asset Opportunities

### Asset Type 1: Research-Based Content

**What attracts links:** Original research, data, studies

**Example:**
- Competitor: "50 dog foods analyzed, ranked by ingredient quality"
- Links from: Veterinary resources, pet blogs, health websites
- Why they link: Citable data; original research

**Your advantage:** Create more comprehensive research
- Test 100 dog foods instead of 50
- Include more breeds
- Add nutritional analysis
- Publish raw data (linkable)

**Expected links:** 20-50 from topical sites

### Asset Type 2: Comprehensive Resource

**What attracts links:** "Everything about X in one place"

**Example:**
- Competitor: "Dog allergy guide" (2,000 words)
- Links from: Pet health blogs, forums, resource pages
- Why they link: Comprehensive reference

**Your advantage:** Go deeper
- 3,500+ words
- Add more examples
- Include decision trees
- Add downloadable checklist
- Create visual flowchart

**Expected links:** 15-30 from resource sites

### Asset Type 3: Tool or Calculator

**What attracts links:** Interactive tools people use and share

**Example:**
- Competitor: None (gap!)
- Link from: Pet calculators, dog health sites, resource pages
- Why they link: Useful tool for their visitors

**Your tool:**
- Calorie calculator (input dog weight/age, get calorie requirement)
- Cost comparison tool
- Allergy risk assessment quiz
- Transition timeline calculator

**Expected links:** 10-20 from sites recommending tools

### Asset Type 4: Expert Interview

**What attracts links:** Unique expert perspective or interview

**Example:**
- Competitor: No interviews
- Link from: Dog health blogs, social proof
- Why they link: Credibility and unique content

**Your content:**
- Interview 3-5 veterinary nutritionists
- Record video or transcribed interview
- Quote throughout article
- Link back from their sites

**Expected links:** 5-10 from expert sites + their own networks

### Asset Type 5: Ultimate Guide

**What attracts links:** "The complete guide to X" (comprehensive, downloadable)

**Example:**
- Create: "Ultimate Guide to Dog Nutrition" (5,000 word guide + downloadable PDF)
- Link from: Dog blogs, resource pages, educational sites
- Why they link: Comprehensive resource to recommend

**Structure:**
- 5,000+ words
- 20+ sections
- Downloadable PDF
- Shareable graphics
- Citation-worthy statistics

**Expected links:** 30-50 from quality sources

## Link Building Outreach Strategy

### Strategy 1: Topical Blog Outreach

**Target:** Dog health blogs, pet nutrition blogs that linked to competitors

**Process:**

1. **Identify blogs** (from competitor backlink analysis)
2. **Research their recent content** (understand their audience)
3. **Find contact email** (usually author page or contact form)
4. **Craft personalized pitch:**

**Email template:**

```
Subject: [Blog name] article on [topic] + dog nutrition resource

Hi [Author name],

I came across your article "[Competitor article title]" on [Blog name]—really valuable
resource on dog allergies. I noticed you mention [specific thing they covered].

We just published a more comprehensive guide on dog nutrition and allergies that
includes [unique element missing from their content: expert interviews, updated data, etc.].
We thought your readers might find it helpful.

[Link to your resource]

No pressure to link—just thought you'd appreciate the resource.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Key elements:**
- Reference their specific article (shows genuine interest)
- Mention what you're offering (be clear)
- Explain why it's valuable
- Include direct link
- No pressure language

**Expected response rate:** 5-10% (1 link from 10-20 outreach emails)

**Effort:** 15-20 minutes per outreach × 20 outreach = 5-6 hours

**Expected links:** 1-2 links

### Strategy 2: Broken Link Building

**Target:** Sites linking to broken competitor content

**Process:**

1. **Find competitor's broken links** (using Ahrefs "broken backlinks" filter)
2. **Identify what those broken links were linking to**
3. **Create similar content** (that covers the same topic)
4. **Reach out:** "Hi, I noticed you linked to [broken page]. We have similar content that might be helpful."

**Email template:**

```
Subject: Updated resource for [topic]

Hi [Site name],

I noticed your article "[Article]" linked to [competitor's broken page] about [topic].
That page appears to be down now.

We have a comprehensive guide on the same topic that might be a good replacement:
[Your link]

Just thought I'd let you know in case it's helpful.

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Expected response rate:** 10-15% (higher than cold outreach—you're solving a problem)

**Effort:** 20-30 minutes per broken link × 10 = 3-5 hours

**Expected links:** 1-3 links

### Strategy 3: Guest Posting

**Target:** Blogs in your niche that accept guest posts

**Process:**

1. **Identify guest post sites** (look at competitor's backlinks for "guest post by" links)
2. **Research their guest post guidelines** (usually on their site)
3. **Pitch unique article idea**
4. **Write article for them** (1,500-2,000 words)
5. **Include bio with link** to your site

**Pitch template:**

```
Subject: Guest post idea: [Headline] for [Blog name]

Hi [Editor name],

I'm a [your expertise] and I've been following [Blog name] for a while. Your audience
clearly cares about [topic], and I have an idea that might fit.

Article: "[Your headline]" — covers [3-4 main points], which I don't see covered
elsewhere on your site.

I'd write a 1,500-2,000 word article, original to your site, covering:
1. [Point 1]
2. [Point 2]
3. [Point 3]

Interested?

Best,
[Your name]
```

**Expected acceptance rate:** 20-30% (1 accepted from 3-5 pitches)

**Effort:** 40-60 minutes per guest post × 10 pitches = 6-10 hours (plus writing time)

**Links per article:** 1-2 (author bio link + possibly body links)

**Expected links:** 2-4 links (plus credibility)

## Link Velocity & Timeline

**Link velocity** = how many new links you acquire per month

**Healthy velocity:**
- Month 1: 5-10 links
- Month 2: 10-15 links
- Month 3: 15-20 links
- Month 4+: 20+ links/month (compounding)

**Timeline expectations:**
- 50 links: 2-3 months of active link building
- 100 links: 4-6 months
- 200+ links: 12+ months

**Ranking timeline (relative to link building):**
- Links acquired Month 1 → Rankings visible Month 3-4
- Links acquired Month 3 → Rankings visible Month 5-6
- Links acquired Month 6 → Rankings visible Month 8-10

**Strategic implication:** Start link building while writing content (not after)

## Linking Site Quality Checklist

Before pursuing a link, verify quality:

- [ ] Site has DA 20+ (authority requirement)
- [ ] Site is topically relevant (related to your niche)
- [ ] Site looks active (recent posts, regular updates)
- [ ] Site appears legitimate (no spam indicators)
- [ ] Site isn't link farm (doesn't have 1000+ random links)
- [ ] Site traffic looks real (not obviously fake)
- [ ] Linking page is relevant to your content
- [ ] No obvious paid link indicators

**Red flags to avoid:**
- Private Blog Networks (PBNs)
- Directory spam sites
- Link farm sites
- Hacked websites
- Expired domains with redirects
- Any site looking "obviously spammy"
