## SOFT SILO MODEL (Authority Hacker)

### Architecture
- Organize content into WordPress categories
- Hub page for each category (the pillar)
- Soft silo = categories organize content but are NOT rigid URL-based silos
- Breadcrumbs: Home > Category > Post
- Navigation menu reflects category structure

### Content Planning Process
1. Expand competitor list (find all competitors in niche)
2. Research monetization (find all affiliate programs, commission rates)
3. Expand keyword list (brainstorm + tool research)
4. Reverse engineer competitor keywords (Ahrefs)
5. Plan skyscraper content (linkable assets)
6. Create sitemap (full site plan with internal linking mapped)

### Three Content Types
| Type | Purpose | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Info content** | Traffic + link building | Question posts, list posts, skyscraper |
| **Commercial content** | Revenue + conversions | Reviews, roundups, vs, alternatives |
| **Support content** | Internal linking glue | Short explainers for commonly referenced concepts |

## THREE-LAYER INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE (Koray)

| Layer | Role | Link Direction |
|---|---|---|
| **Root** | Connected to homepage, links to all seeds, highest quality | Receives PageRank from homepage |
| **Seed** | Mid-level; links to root and to nodes | Bidirectional with root and nodes |
| **Node** | Individual entity-attribute articles | Links UP to seeds |

**Key distinction from soft silos:** Koray uses BOTH taxonomy (hierarchical) and ontology (relational) simultaneously. Not pure silos, not pure hubs.

## THE 6-STEP INTERNAL LINKING PROCESS (Hockman)

**Step 1:** Pick 3-4 target keywords for the page you want to rank

**Step 2:** Find 3-4 relevant pages on your site (must have topical relevance)

**Step 3:** Add exact-match link anchor text on those 3-4 pages (one keyword per page, no repeats)

**Step 4:** Find 3-4 MORE relevant pages. Add exact-match keywords BUT with filler words ("my best workout tips," "working out tips guide")

**Step 5:** Repeat Step 4 with another batch of 3-4 pages (more filler words)

**Step 6:** After 9-12 exact-match links, add partial matches and rearrangements ("tips on working out" instead of "working out tips")

---

## ANCHOR TEXT RATIOS (Hockman -- At Scale)

For pages with 50+ internal links:
- **~30%** exact match keyword anchors
- **~60%** exact match + filler words
- **~10%** partial matches / variations / rearrangements

### Koray's Internal Link Rules (Different Framework)
- Same anchor text maximum 3 times across entire semantic content network
- No reciprocal links by default (only when PageRank return is justified)
- Most important anchor text in root document
- First anchor text in document has most weight
- One link per heading section, not clusters
- Anchor text must appear in target page's H1 AND source page's heading

## INTERNAL LINK POWER -- CASE STUDY (Hockman / Spencer Haws)

Out of 47 pages tested with ONLY internal link additions:
- **36 out of 47 pages increased in rankings**
- Average: 2-3 internal links added per post
- One article: rank 100+ -> **#1**
- Another: #32 -> #1
- Another: #21 -> #1
- Another: #11 -> #1

**Industry estimate:** 3 internal links are roughly equal to 1 external backlink in ranking power.

## CONTENT CALENDAR PLANNING (Nat Eliason)

### 4-Week Sprint Model
- Plan at least 4 weeks initially, targeting 3 articles per week
- From 50 keywords, pick 12 to start (4 weeks x 3)
- Mix broad cornerstone + narrow long-tail each week
- Narrow articles rank faster for newer sites
- Related topics in series (all black tea, then green tea) for easy interlinking
- Lower-difficulty topics earlier (quick wins build domain trust)
- 50 keywords = roughly 1 year of content

### Three Content Buckets (Vahe Arabian)
Execute all three in synergy with increasing velocity:
1. **Informational** -- How-to, TOFU, drives most traffic
2. **Promotional** -- Listicles, curated content, earns links
3. **Tactical** -- Reviews, conversion-focused content

Run in quarterly campaign sprints.

## CONTENT DECAY FRAMEWORK (brightonSEO -- Rich George)

### Keep-Kill-Merge-Refresh Audit

**Not a one-off project but a regular health check.**

Map every page with: page type, primary intent, page topic. Combine crawl data + analytics + GSC.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Sustained traffic drops?
- Declining search interest in original topic?
- Declining UX metrics (bounce rate, time on page)?
- Ranking drops for target keywords?

### Gold/Silver/Bronze Review Tiers

| Tier | Criteria | Review Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Gold** | Top 10-15% of traffic, top-5 rankings | Every 3 months |
| **Silver** | Moderate traffic, rankings 6-20 | Every 6-12 months |
| **Bronze** | Low traffic, rankings 20+ or declining | Every 12-18 months |

### Actions per Audit Result

| Action | When | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Keep** | Content performing well, rankings stable | Monitor only, update stats/dates |
| **Kill** | Zero traffic, no backlinks, irrelevant topic | 301 redirect to closest relevant page OR noindex |
| **Merge** | Multiple thin pages on similar topic | Consolidate into one comprehensive page, 301 old URLs |
| **Refresh** | Declining but topic still relevant | Update content, add new sections, refresh dates |

### Mindset Shift
From "publish and forget" to "publish and manage." Content has a lifecycle: create -> promote -> maintain -> retire.

## PUBLISHING CADENCE OPTIONS

### Option A: Patternless Momentum (Koray)
- Never publish at regular intervals
- Sometimes 3 articles/day, then wait 4 days, then 7 articles
- Initial launch: 20+ articles simultaneously
- Keeps crawler attention through unpredictability

### Option B: Steady Cadence (Authority Hacker / Cromwell)
- 4-5 posts per month for most businesses
- Consistent quality over volume
- Cromwell warning: 100 AI posts = potential penalty
- Authority Hacker: 3 articles per week during growth phase

### Option C: Quarterly Sprints (Vahe Arabian)
- Group content into quarterly campaigns
- Sprint: plan -> produce -> publish -> promote -> measure
- Increase velocity each quarter as systems mature

## CONTENT RE-USAGE STRATEGIES (Vahe Arabian)

Three approaches to extend content lifecycle:
1. **Reformat** -- Same content, different format (article -> video, infographic, podcast)
2. **Repurpose** -- Extract sections for social media, email newsletters, slide decks
3. **Improve** -- Update data, add new sections, refresh examples and screenshots
