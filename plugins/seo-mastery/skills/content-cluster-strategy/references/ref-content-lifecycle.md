# Content Lifecycle -- Complete Reference

## CONTENT DECAY FRAMEWORK (brightonSEO -- Rich George)

### Why Content Decay Matters
- Damages brand perception (outdated info = unreliable brand)
- Wastes original content investment
- Feeds LLM biases and inaccuracies (outdated web content gets amplified through AI training)

### Keep-Kill-Merge-Refresh Audit

**This is NOT a one-off project. It is a regular health check.**

### Step 1: Map Every Page
Combine crawl data + analytics + GSC into a single spreadsheet with:
- URL
- Page type (blog, category, product, landing)
- Primary intent (informational, commercial, transactional)
- Page topic / target keyword
- Monthly traffic (last 3 months average)
- Rankings for target keyword(s)
- Backlinks / referring domains
- Last updated date

### Step 2: Diagnose
For each page, ask:
- Sustained traffic drops over last 3-6 months?
- Declining search interest in original topic? (Google Trends check)
- Declining UX metrics (high bounce rate, low time on page)?
- Ranking drops for target keywords?

### Step 3: Tier Assignment
| Tier | Criteria | Review Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Gold** | Top 10-15% of traffic, top-5 rankings | Every 3 months |
| **Silver** | Moderate traffic, rankings 6-20 | Every 6-12 months |
| **Bronze** | Low traffic, rankings 20+ or declining | Every 12-18 months |

### Step 4: Take Action
| Action | When to Apply | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| **Keep** | Performing well, rankings stable | Monitor only. Update statistics and dates. |
| **Kill** | Zero traffic, no backlinks, topic irrelevant | 301 redirect to closest relevant page, or noindex. |
| **Merge** | Multiple thin pages on similar/overlapping topic | Consolidate into one comprehensive page. 301 redirect old URLs. |
| **Refresh** | Declining traffic but topic still relevant | Update content, add new sections, refresh dates and data. |

### AI-Era Caveat
If content performance drops, it may not be the content's fault. Check if the SERP landscape changed:
- Did AI Overviews appear for those keywords?
- Did a new competitor launch a superior piece?
- Did search volume decline for the topic itself?

## PUBLISHING CADENCE OPTIONS

### Option A: Patternless Momentum (Koray)

**The philosophy:** Be patternless. Never publish at regular intervals.

**Implementation:**
- Sometimes 3 articles in one day, then wait 4 days, then 7 articles
- Initial launch: 20+ articles simultaneously to create a state change
- Sweet spot after launch: 2/day sometimes, 5 over 10 days other times
- Crawl rate correlates with positive rankings

**Rules:**
1. Never publish during core algorithm updates (wait for flat algorithmic season)
2. Do NOT publish 400-500 AI articles per day (Bing delists, Google penalizes)
3. Historical data accrues per article -- earlier articles boost later ones
4. Continue publishing through the wait for core updates (~6 month intervals)

### Option B: Steady Cadence (Authority Hacker / Cromwell)

**For most businesses:**
- 4-5 posts per month (quality over quantity)
- 3 articles per week during active growth phase (Nat Eliason)
- Consistent schedule helps with planning and writer management

**Cromwell's warning:** Publishing 100 AI-generated posts = potential Google penalty. The algorithm detects mass-produced content patterns.

### Option C: Quarterly Sprints (Vahe Arabian)

**Sprint structure:**
1. Plan (Week 1): Keywords, briefs, competitor analysis
2. Produce (Weeks 2-8): Writing, editing, optimizing
3. Publish (Weeks 9-10): Batch publishing with internal links
4. Promote (Weeks 11-12): Outreach, social, syndication
5. Measure (Week 13): Performance review, adjust next sprint

**Velocity progression:** Increase output 25-50% each quarter as systems mature.

## CONTENT RE-USAGE STRATEGIES (Vahe Arabian)

### Three Approaches
1. **Reformat** -- Same content, different format
   - Article -> video (YouTube, embedded in post)
   - Article -> infographic
   - Article -> podcast episode
   - Article -> slide deck (SlideShare, LinkedIn)

2. **Repurpose** -- Extract portions for other channels
   - Key quotes -> social media posts
   - Statistics -> Twitter/LinkedIn snippets
   - Sections -> email newsletter content
   - Data -> downloadable PDF lead magnet

3. **Improve** -- Enhance existing content
   - Update statistics and data points
   - Add new sections covering emerging subtopics
   - Refresh screenshots and examples
   - Add video or interactive elements
   - Improve schema markup

## EVERGREEN VS NEWS CONTENT (Vahe Arabian)

### Evergreen Content
- Stable search demand over time
- Core of organic traffic strategy
- Requires periodic refresh but fundamentals remain relevant
- Examples: how-to guides, product comparisons, educational content
- Higher ROI over time (compounds traffic)

### News Content
- Spiky search demand around events
- Good for Google News, Google Discover, social sharing
- Short shelf life (days to weeks)
- Examples: industry announcements, event coverage, trend analysis
- Lower long-term ROI but drives short-term traffic spikes

### The Balanced Approach
- 70-80% evergreen content (traffic foundation)
- 20-30% timely/news content (spikes + freshness signals)
- CONTRARIAN (Vahe Arabian): Editorial teams default to "write, publish, forget" due to volume. This is working harder, not smarter. Reuse content to extend lifecycle.

## CONTENT LIFECYCLE MANAGEMENT

### The Four Phases
1. **Create** -- Research, write, optimize, publish
2. **Promote** -- Outreach, social, syndication, internal linking
3. **Maintain** -- Monitor performance, refresh data, update for accuracy
4. **Retire** -- Kill or merge when content no longer serves a purpose

### Maintenance Triggers
- Traffic drops 30%+ from peak
- Rankings drop 5+ positions
- Content factually outdated (statistics, tools, processes changed)
- Competitor published superior content on same topic
- Topic seasonality shift (update before next relevant season)

### Retirement Triggers
- Zero organic traffic for 6+ months
- No backlinks or referring domains
- Topic no longer relevant to business
- Content cannibalized by a stronger page on same topic

### Post-Retirement Handling
1. If page has any backlinks: 301 redirect to most relevant active page
2. If page has zero backlinks and zero traffic: noindex or delete
3. Never leave dead pages accessible -- they waste crawl budget and dilute site quality

## CONTENT DECAY IN THE AI ERA

### New Considerations
- Outdated content gets amplified through AI training data -- AI may cite your outdated statistics
- AI Overviews may appear for keywords that previously drove clicks, reducing traffic without content fault
- Content that LLMs can easily answer (definitions, simple facts) loses click value faster

### Mitigation
- Update timestamps in schema (makes AI orchestrator more likely to use fresh content)
- Add unique data and original research (forces LLMs to cite you)
- Build interactive tools (calculators, quizzes) that cannot be answered in AI Overviews
- Focus content decay audits on Gold-tier content first (highest impact)
