# Topical Authority Frameworks

Advanced semantic positioning and entity relationship architecture for cluster mastery.

## Topical Authority Definition

**Definition:** Your domain's expertise in a specific topic, demonstrated through comprehensive content coverage that Google recognizes as authoritative.

**How Google measures it:**
- Number of pages on topic (20+ = strong signal)
- Depth of coverage (comprehensive vs. surface-level)
- Semantic relationships (entities, related concepts)
- User engagement (CTR, time on page, bounce rate)
- Link profile (authoritative sites linking to your content)

**Ranking benefit:** Pages on topic you own authority in rank 2-3x faster than unrelated topics

## Entity-Centric Architecture (Koray Gubur Framework)

**Core concept:** Structure content around entities and their relationships, not just keywords

**Entities vs. Keywords:**
- Keyword: "dog food allergies" (narrow)
- Entity: Dog allergies (entity) + food (entity) + relationship (connection)
- Entity approach: More semantic, more Google-aligned

### Three-Layer Entity Model

**Layer 1: Central Entity**
- The main entity your domain owns authority in
- Appears in all major articles
- Example: "Dog" (for dog nutrition site)

**Layer 2: Core Attributes**
- Main characteristics of central entity
- Deep coverage across multiple articles
- Example: "Dog nutrition," "dog health," "dog breeds"

**Layer 3: Outer Attributes**
- Supporting information, broader context
- Lighter coverage, fewer articles
- Example: "Dog history," "dog psychology," "dog training"

### Semantic Distance Mapping

**Semantic distance:** How "close" two entities are in user search behavior

**Examples (Dog domain):**

**Close distance (high relevance):**
- Dog → Dog nutrition → Dog food (direct relationship)
- Dog → Dog allergies → Food allergies (closely related)
- Dog → Health → Diet (connected via health)

**Medium distance (supporting):**
- Dog → Grooming → Overall health (contextual)
- Dog → Behavior → Exercise needs → Nutrition (indirect chain)

**Far distance (peripheral):**
- Dog → Dog sports → Canine athletics → Training (getting distant)
- Dog → History of dogs → Domestication (historical, not practical)

**Don't use:** Dog → Car → Transportation (no connection)

### Content Frequency as Authority Signal

**Rule:** Repeat certain terms/entities across pages to signal topical dominance

**Example (Dog Nutrition domain):**

**Frequently repeated terms (30%+ of articles):**
- Dog, food, nutrition, protein, ingredients, health

**Often repeated terms (20-30% of articles):**
- Allergies, sensitive stomach, feeding, portions, age

**Occasionally repeated terms (10-20% of articles):**
- Brands, cost, quality, supplements

**Result:** Google recognizes "dog nutrition" as your domain's central topic

## Topical Map Structure (Six Elements)

### Element 1: Central Entity (Required)

The one entity that defines your domain.

**Example:** A dog nutrition site
- Central entity: "Dog" or "Canine nutrition"
- Appears in: Every major article
- Function: Defines domain focus

### Element 2: Core Search Intent (Required)

The macro-level intent users have when they search

**Example:** Dog nutrition site
- Core intent: "Know/Learn about dog nutrition" or "Find best nutrition for dog"
- Not: "Buy dog food quickly" (transaction) or "Train my dog" (different intent)
- Function: Governs all content angles

### Element 3: Source Context (Required)

Who you are and how you monetize

**Example:** Dog nutrition site
- Source context: "Independent dog nutrition expert, affiliate monetization"
- Vs.: "Official brand site selling dog food"
- Vs.: "Veterinary clinic recommending clinical diets"
- Function: Shapes content angle and credibility

### Element 4: Core Section (Deep)

Your deepest, most comprehensive content area

**Example:** Dog nutrition site
- Core section: All dog food types + feeding guidance
- Gets: Most articles (40-50% of cluster)
- Gets: Best internal links (hub of hub)
- Gets: Most optimization effort
- Function: Establishes expertise

### Element 5: Outer Section (Breadth)

Supporting content proving topical relevance

**Example:** Dog nutrition site
- Outer section: Dog health, dog behavior, dog breed characteristics (as they relate to nutrition)
- Gets: Fewer articles (20-30% of cluster)
- Function: Proves you understand broader context

### Element 6: Semantic Distance Boundaries

Which topics to include vs. exclude

**Include (semantic distance 1-2):**
- Dog training (affects exercise, affects nutrition needs)
- Dog health (directly related)
- Dog digestion (core topic)

**Exclude (semantic distance 3+):**
- Veterinary surgery
- Dog grooming
- Dog boarding
- Dog traveling

**Rule:** If you can't clearly explain "how this relates to dog nutrition," exclude it

## Topical Authority Scoring

Measure your topical authority development:

**Scoring system (0-10 per topic):**

| Score | Authority Level | Articles Needed | Ranking Expectation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-2 | None | 0-5 | Can't rank |
| 2-4 | Emerging | 5-10 | Rank for long-tail only |
| 4-6 | Developing | 10-20 | Rank position 15-30 |
| 6-8 | Strong | 20-40 | Rank position 5-15 |
| 8-9 | Very Strong | 40-60 | Rank position 1-10 |
| 9-10 | Dominant | 60-100+ | Dominate top 10 |

**Calculation:**
- Article count: (number of articles on topic / 50) × 2 points
- Content depth: (average article length / 2500 words) × 2 points
- Link profile: (referring domains / 100) × 2 points
- User engagement: (avg CTR / 3%) × 2 points
- Update frequency: (content published this year / 12 months) × 2 points

**Total: 0-10 points**

**Example:**
- 25 articles = 1 point
- Average 2,000 words = 1.6 points
- 50 referring domains = 1 point
- 2% average CTR = 1.3 points
- 15 articles published this year = 2.5 points
- **Total: 7.4 = Strong authority**

## Semantic Distance Testing

### Test 1: The "Related Questions" Test

Use Google's "People Also Ask" to validate semantic relationships

**Process:**
1. Google your main topic
2. Expand "People Also Ask" (click each question)
3. See what Google associates with your topic
4. Include those topics in your topical authority

**Example:**
- Main topic: "Dog nutrition"
- Related questions Google shows: allergies, age-based nutrition, budget options, supplements
- Include in your cluster: all these topics
- Exclude: topics Google doesn't associate

### Test 2: The "Related Searches" Test

At bottom of Google SERP, see related searches

**Process:**
1. Search your main topic
2. Scroll to bottom
3. See "Searches related to [your topic]"
4. Those are your semantic neighbors
5. Include them in your authority map

**Example:**
- Main search: "dog nutrition"
- Related searches shown: dog feeding, dog health, dog breed nutrition, senior dog nutrition
- Include these as subtopics

### Test 3: The "Could I Write This?" Test

If you can't write 1,500+ word article on topic without sounding like outsider = too distant

**Examples:**

**Could you write 1,500 words on this? (Include in cluster)**
- Best dog food (YES—core expertise)
- Identifying dog allergies (YES—nutrition domain)
- Dog training (MAYBE—yes if relates to nutrition)
- Dog breeds (MAYBE—yes if relates to nutrition)

**Too distant to include:**
- Dog behavior psychology (NO—outside expertise)
- Veterinary surgery (NO—not qualified)
- Dog boarding (NO—unrelated)

## Building Topical Authority Step-by-Step

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-3)

**Goal:** Establish core expertise area

**Content:**
- 5-8 core articles on central topic
- Each 2,000+ words, comprehensive
- Well-interlinkied with clear hierarchy
- Example: Dog nutrition cluster (pillar + satellites)

**Authority score:** 3-4

### Phase 2: Expansion (Months 4-6)

**Goal:** Expand to core attributes

**Content:**
- 8-12 more articles on related topics
- Specific angles on nutrition (allergies, age-based, budget)
- Link back to core articles
- Example: Add "dog health" cluster, link to "nutrition" cluster

**Authority score:** 5-6

### Phase 3: Depth (Months 7-12)

**Goal:** Go deeper on high-opportunity topics

**Content:**
- 15-20 more articles, targeting long-tail keywords
- Each satellite topic gets multiple deep-dive articles
- Emerging as authority on specific subtopics

**Authority score:** 7-8

### Phase 4: Dominance (12+ Months)

**Goal:** Own the category

**Content:**
- 40-100+ total articles covering all aspects
- Multiple content formats (guides, comparisons, tools, videos)
- Clear topical map visible to Google
- Ranking for 200+ keywords in category

**Authority score:** 9-10

**Result:** New articles in your category rank in 4-8 weeks (vs. normal 12-16 weeks)

## Common Topical Authority Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Spreading too thin**
- 50 articles across 10 different topics
- No deep authority in any
- Solution: Choose 2-3 core topics, dominate them

**Mistake 2: Missing semantic connections**
- Articles exist but don't interlink
- Authority stays fragmented
- Solution: Create entity map, implement all connections

**Mistake 3: Ignoring outer attributes**
- Only cover core topic, miss supporting context
- Google doesn't recognize full expertise
- Solution: Add 20% outer topic articles (prove broader understanding)

**Mistake 4: Publishing random content**
- One article dog nutrition, next article cat training
- Domain authority diluted
- Solution: Stick to semantic cluster (related topics only)

**Mistake 5: Not updating content**
- Old articles don't mention new developments
- Newer articles don't reinforce earlier learning
- Solution: Quarterly updates to keep topic current
