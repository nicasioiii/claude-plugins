# Commercial vs Informational Content

Master the difference between money-making content and authority-building content, and how to use both strategically.

## The Fundamental Difference

### Commercial Content (80% of Output)

**Goal**: Rank for keywords with buyer intent; drive conversions (affiliate, leads, sales)

**User Intent**: "I want to buy/transact/compare"

**Monetization**: Direct (affiliate links, product sales, lead forms)

**Keywords Targeted**: "Best X", "X vs Y", "X review", "Buy X", "X for [use case]"

**Examples**:
- "Best Natural Sunscreen 2024" (rank → affiliate sales)
- "Organic Sunscreen vs Chemical Sunscreen" (rank → affiliate)
- "MailChimp Alternative Comparison" (rank → affiliate signup)
- "iOS vs Android Phones" (rank → affiliate/ads)

**Ranking Difficulty**: Higher (everyone wants to rank)

**Expected Revenue**: High per article

**Typical Structure**: Reviews, roundups, comparisons, buying guides

### Informational Content (20% of Output)

**Goal**: Establish topical authority; earn backlinks; support money pages

**User Intent**: "I want to learn/understand"

**Monetization**: Indirect (links to money pages, authority boost)

**Keywords Targeted**: "How to", "What is", "Guide to", "Explained"

**Examples**:
- "How to Save for Retirement" (rank → link to money pages → affiliate/product)
- "Natural Ingredients Explained" (rank → authority → link to product reviews)
- "Dog Training Methods" (rank → links to training guides and products)

**Ranking Difficulty**: Lower (less competition, less valuable keywords)

**Expected Revenue**: Low-direct, high-indirect

**Typical Structure**: Guides, how-tos, educational articles

## Commercial Content Strategy

### Commercial Keyword Examples

```
Best [Product] for [Use Case]
- "Best Natural Sunscreen for Sensitive Skin"
- "Best Dog Food for Allergies"
- "Best CRM for Small Business"

[Product] Review
- "Zoom Review 2024"
- "MailChimp Review"
- "Slack Review"

[Product] vs [Competitor]
- "Hubspot vs Salesforce"
- "iPhone vs Android"
- "Intercom vs Zendesk"

[Modifier] [Product] [Modifier]
- "Affordable Web Hosting for Beginners"
- "Lightweight Laptop for Video Editing"

Top [Number] [Products]
- "7 Best Email Marketing Tools"
- "5 Best Project Management Software"

[Product] Alternatives
- "Shopify Alternatives for Dropshipping"
- "WordPress Alternatives for Blogging"

[Product] + [Benefit]
- "Email Marketing Tool with Best Automation"
- "Project Management Software for Remote Teams"
```

### Commercial Content Structure

**Key Elements**:
1. Clear winner/recommendation (not wishy-washy)
2. Honest cons (credibility)
3. Specific pricing and availability
4. Multiple product options (affiliate variety)
5. Use case-based recommendations
6. Affiliate links prominently placed
7. Clear CTA with link

**Typical Monetization**:

```
Article: "Best Email Marketing Tool for Small Business"
Conversion Path:
1. User reads review → clicks affiliate link
2. Goes to tool's website
3. Buys or signs up → you get commission

Revenue: $30-150 per conversion (varies by tool, cookie length, commission %)
Expected: 100 visitors/month × 2-5% conversion = 2-5 conversions = $60-750/month per article
```

### Commercial Content Authority Requirements

**Can Rank Without High Authority**: Sort of
- Google's 2024 update penalizes low-authority reviews
- But niche authority (topical relevance) matters more than domain authority
- Example: 1-year-old site can rank for "Email Tool for Coaches" if site is all about coaching

**Authority Signals Help**:
- Author credentials (expert in category)
- Original testing/research
- High-quality supporting content linking to review
- User reviews/ratings on page
- Long article (2,000+ words)

## Informational Content Strategy

### Informational Keyword Examples

```
How to [Accomplish Goal]
- "How to Save for Retirement"
- "How to Train a Dog"
- "How to Start a Blog"

What is [Concept]
- "What is Affiliate Marketing"
- "What is Email Marketing"
- "What is SEO"

Guide to [Topic]
- "Beginner's Guide to Email Marketing"
- "Complete Guide to Dog Training"
- "Ultimate Guide to Content Marketing"

[Topic] Explained
- "Affiliate Marketing Explained"
- "Domain Authority Explained"
- "Email Deliverability Explained"

Best Practices for [Topic]
- "Best Practices for Email Marketing"
- "Best Practices for User Experience"

Comparison/Difference [Concept A] vs [Concept B]
- "401k vs Roth IRA"
- "Sourdough vs Whole Wheat Bread"
- "Remote vs In-Office Work"
```

### Informational Content Structure

**Key Elements**:
1. Comprehensive coverage (why it ranks)
2. Original data/research if possible
3. Accessible (explains concepts clearly)
4. Actionable (readers can apply)
5. Links to money pages naturally
6. Attracts backlinks (reference-quality)

**Typical Value Flow**:

```
Article: "Complete Guide to Affiliate Marketing"
Value Chain:
1. Ranks for "affiliate marketing explained"
2. Other sites link to it (backlink)
3. Backlink boosts domain authority
4. Link to money page: "Best Affiliate Programs for Beginners"
5. Money page gets ranking boost from authority
6. Money page drives affiliate revenue

Revenue: Indirect (money page → affiliate sales)
Expected: 0 direct revenue, $500-2000/month from linked money page
```

## Strategic Mix: 80/20 Approach

### Monthly Content Plan (5 Articles)

**Distribution**:
```
4 Commercial Articles (Money Pages)
- Article 1: "Best [Product] for [Use Case]"
- Article 2: "[Product] Review"
- Article 3: "Top [Number] [Products]"
- Article 4: "[Product] vs [Competitor]"

1 Informational Article (Authority)
- Article 5: "How to [Accomplish Goal]" or "Guide to [Topic]"
```

### Why 80/20?

**Commercial Content** (80%):
- Ranks for high-intent keywords
- Drives immediate affiliate revenue
- Google rewards user-focused commercial intent

**Informational Content** (20%):
- Ranks for volume keywords
- Attracts backlinks (boosts domain authority)
- Supports ranking of commercial pages
- Establishes expertise

**Result**: Balanced site with strong authority + strong revenue

## Writing Approach Differences

### Tone & Voice

**Commercial Content**:
- Confident (not wishy-washy about recommendations)
- Clear judgment (best overall, best budget, etc.)
- Action-oriented ("Buy now", "Get started")
- Specific details (exact pricing, exact specs)
- Personal experience ("I tested for 90 days")

**Informational Content**:
- Educational (explains concepts)
- Neutral (not pushing toward one option)
- Detailed (thorough explanation)
- Reference-like (comprehensive coverage)
- Data-driven (statistics, research)

### Keyword Integration

**Commercial**:
- Include full keyword in title
- Keyword in H1
- Keywords in first 100 words
- Secondary keywords in different sections

**Informational**:
- Keyword in title (usually)
- Keyword in H1 (usually)
- Keywords less frequently (focus on comprehensiveness)
- More natural variation of keywords

### Examples Comparison

**Same Topic - Different Approaches**:

```
COMMERCIAL ARTICLE:
Title: "Best Organic Dog Food for Sensitive Stomachs | 2024"
Focus: Specific products, pricing, where to buy
H1: "Top 5 Organic Dog Foods for Dogs with Sensitive Stomachs"
Includes: Brands, prices, links to buy, affiliate links
Goal: Reader buys dog food through your link
Revenue: $20-50 per purchase

INFORMATIONAL ARTICLE:
Title: "How to Choose Dog Food for Sensitive Stomachs"
Focus: Understanding sensitivities, ingredients to avoid, selection process
H1: "How to Choose the Best Dog Food for Your Dog's Sensitive Stomach"
Includes: Explanation, decision framework, no product links (until end)
Goal: Reader understands how to choose, links to commercial article
Revenue: Indirect (commercial article ranking boost)
```

## Content Linking Strategy

### Money Page (Commercial) Gets:
- Links from informational content (naturally contextual)
- Homepage links (authority concentration)
- Internal links from pillar pages
- External links from guest posts (future skill)

### Authority Page (Informational) Gets:
- Backlinks (external sites link to it as reference)
- Homepage link (navigation)
- Links from related money pages (natural context)

### Internal Linking Flow:

```
Homepage
├─ Pillar Page: "Complete Guide to Dog Training" (Informational)
│  └─ Links to commercial articles
│     ├─ Money Page: "Best Dog Training Tools"
│     └─ Money Page: "Best Dog Training Treats"
└─ Money Pages link back to pillar (mutual links)
```

## Monetization Models

### Commercial Content Models

**Affiliate Programs**:
- Commission per click/signup/purchase
- Best for: SaaS (MailChimp, Shopify), retail (Amazon)
- Revenue: $5-200 per conversion
- Best platforms: SG partner, Amazon Associates, Brand affiliate programs

**Sponsored Content**:
- Brand pays to feature product
- Disclosure required: "This post is sponsored"
- Revenue: $500-5000 per post

**Lead Generation**:
- User fills contact form
- You sell lead to business
- Revenue: $5-50 per qualified lead

**Direct Sales**:
- Sell own products from article
- Revenue: Whatever you set pricing at
- Best for: Own courses, digital products, services

### Authority Content Models

**Display Ads**:
- Google AdSense or similar
- Revenue: $0.50-5 per thousand impressions (RPM varies)
- Not primary revenue (low for most topics)

**Backlink Building**:
- Authority page ranks, gets backlinks
- Backlinks boost money pages
- Money pages drive revenue
- Revenue: Indirect, 50-500x higher than direct ads

**Lead Magnets**:
- "Download guide" or "Sign up for course"
- Build email list
- Monetize list via email marketing
- Revenue: Indirect, long-term

## Common Strategy Mistakes

**Mistake 1: All Commercial, No Authority**
```
❌ WRONG: 50 product reviews, zero educational content
Result: No backlinks, no topical authority, reviews don't rank

✓ CORRECT: 40 reviews + 10 guides
Result: Guides rank, attract backlinks, boost review rankings
```

**Mistake 2: All Informational, No Commercial**
```
❌ WRONG: 50 "how to" guides, no product recommendations
Result: Great authority, zero direct revenue

✓ CORRECT: Link from guides to commercial articles
Result: Authority + revenue
```

**Mistake 3: Poor Author Credibility on Commercial**
```
❌ WRONG: Review dog food but you have no dog, no expertise
Result: Reads as spammy, Google penalizes low-authority reviews

✓ CORRECT: Author has relevant credentials, tested products
Result: Credibility signals boost ranking
```

**Mistake 4: No Unique Angle**
```
❌ WRONG: "Best Email Tools" like 100 other articles
Result: Outranked by established sites

✓ CORRECT: "Best Email Tool for Coaches" (niche angle)
Result: Less competition, can rank faster
```

**Mistake 5: Honest Cons But Then Wrong Recommendation**
```
❌ WRONG: "Tool A has these cons, Tool B has these cons...
but I recommend Tool A anyway" (no logic)

✓ CORRECT: "Tool A has cons X, Y. Tool B has cons A, B.
For your use case, Tool B wins because..."
```

## Audit Your Current Content

**Ask These Questions**:
1. What % of content is commercial vs informational?
2. Do money pages rank? If not, do you have supporting authority content?
3. Do informational articles link naturally to money pages?
4. Do informational articles have affiliate potential indirect benefits?
5. Is author credibility clear on commercial content?
6. Are recommendations honest (including cons)?
7. Do you have original data/testing in content?

**Scoring**:
- 80/20 split: Good ✓
- All commercial: Rebalance toward 20% informational
- All informational: Add 60-80% commercial content
- No clear recommendations: Edit for clarity and conviction
- No author credentials: Add author bios to all content
