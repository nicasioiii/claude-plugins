# Commercial Content Templates (Authority Hacker)

## The Four Commercial Content Types

### 1. Single Product Review
- In-depth review of one product
- Feature-to-benefit translation is the key skill
- Honest tone: genuine pros AND cons build trust and convert better
- Include personal experience where possible
- Comparison tables against alternatives
- Clear recommendation with affiliate link

### 2. Roundup Post ("Best X for Y")
- Collection of products in a category
- Most lucrative commercial content type
- Structure: brief intro, comparison table at top, detailed reviews below
- Each product gets: summary, pros, cons, who it is best for, price
- Inverted pyramid of value: best product first, worst last
- Readers who do not scroll still see your top pick

### 3. Versus Post ("X vs Y")
- Direct comparison of two specific products
- Works for high-intent searchers who have narrowed to 2 options
- Side-by-side comparison table
- Feature-by-feature breakdown
- Clear winner recommendation with reasoning
- Often converts better than roundups (reader is further in buying cycle)

### 4. Alternatives Post ("X Alternatives")
- People searching for alternatives are dissatisfied with the known product
- High commercial intent -- actively looking to switch
- List 5-10 alternatives with brief descriptions
- Highlight what each alternative does better than the original
- Price comparison is important (often switching for cost reasons)

## Feature-to-Benefit Translation

The #1 skill for commercial content. Features are what a product has. Benefits are what a product does for the reader.

### Process
1. List all product features
2. For each feature, ask: "So what? Why does the reader care?"
3. Connect feature to reader's life/problem/goal
4. Write in terms of outcomes, not specifications

### Examples
| Feature | Benefit |
|---------|---------|
| 5000mAh battery | Lasts 2 full days without charging |
| Titanium frame | Survives drops that would crack other phones |
| 120Hz display | Scrolling feels buttery smooth, no lag |
| 1TB storage | Never delete photos to make room again |

### Common Mistakes
- Listing specs without explanation
- Using manufacturer jargon
- Assuming readers understand technical terms
- Forgetting to connect features to pain points

## Writing Style for Commercial Content

### Honest Reviews (Authority Hacker)
- Never write fake positive reviews -- readers detect it and bounce
- Include genuine cons for every product
- "If this product has no cons, you haven't used it"
- Trust is the conversion mechanism, not hype
- Write as if recommending to a friend

### Pre-Selling Philosophy
- Your job is NOT to sell the product -- that is the merchant's landing page job
- Your job is to pre-sell: help reader decide which product is right for THEM
- Warm the reader up so they arrive at the merchant ready to buy
- This is why honest reviews convert better than positive-only reviews

## Commercial Content Research Process

1. **Use the product** (if possible) -- nothing replaces hands-on experience
2. **Read manufacturer specs** -- get all features and technical details
3. **Read competitor reviews** -- find gaps they missed
4. **Check Amazon/retailer reviews** -- real user feedback, especially complaints
5. **Note common questions** -- FAQ sections from real buyers
6. **Identify unique angles** -- what can you say that nobody else has?

## Affiliate Link Placement Strategy (Authority Hacker)

### Placement Rules
- First link: in the comparison table at top of article (above the fold)
- Second link: within each product's detailed review section
- Third link: at the bottom of the article in a "final recommendation" section
- Do NOT overload with links -- 2-3 per product is sufficient
- Use link management plugin (ThirstyAffiliates, Lasso) for centralized updates

### Link Presentation
- Use cloaked/pretty links: `yourdomain.com/go/product-name`
- Button-style CTAs convert better than text links for primary recommendations
- "Check Price on Amazon" outperforms "Buy Now" (lower commitment language)

## Info Content Templates (Authority Hacker)

### Question Posts
- Answer a specific question searchers ask
- H1 = the question (or close variation)
- First paragraph answers the question directly (targets featured snippets)
- Remaining content provides depth, context, supporting details
- Examples: "How long does X take?", "Is X safe for Y?", "When should you X?"

### List Posts
- Numbered or bulleted compilation
- H1 includes the number: "7 Best Ways to X"
- Each list item gets its own H2
- Brief explanation per item with actionable detail
- Works extremely well for SEO due to clear structure

## Content Promotion Systems (Nat Eliason)

### Automated Promotion
- Zapier: RSS feed detects new post > auto-tweets, auto-posts to Facebook, creates VA tasks
- Connect to Pinterest, LinkedIn, Buffer
- Instagram cannot be automated via web access

### Re-Promotion on Autopilot
- MeetEdgar or Post Planner: schedule automatic re-sharing of old articles
- Create a few text variations per article to avoid seeming repetitive
- Equal variation count per article (no disproportionate sharing)

### Republishing on Owned Platforms
- **Medium:** Use "Import a Story" feature (canonical tag auto-added, no SEO risk). Publish to Medium publications for more exposure. Typical: 3K-5K views/month, spikes to 13K-20K
- **LinkedIn:** Copy article as LinkedIn article. No canonical needed -- LinkedIn articles do not outrank originals
- Wait 2+ days before republishing to ensure Google indexes original first

### External Publications
- Find target publication editors
- Pitch an ORIGINAL article first (not republished)
- After first article performs, ask about republished content
- Build list of all publications your market reads

### Reddit Strategy
- Post to relevant subreddits. 5-10% success rate but can drive 40K-50K views in 24-48 hours
- Check if subreddit allows link posts
- Risk: downvotes, bans

### Influencer Tagging
- Tag influencers/brands mentioned in your article when tweeting
- Do NOT email them saying "I mentioned you"
- Include them naturally in tweet body: "How [Book] by @Author can change your life"

### Facebook Boosting
- Boost articles with $100-250 for extra exposure
- Target: page likes, custom audiences, interest targeting
- Set budget for ~1 week
- More traffic = better ranking signals
