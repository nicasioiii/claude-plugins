# Content Writing Process (Complete)

## The 4-Stage Process (Maddy Osman)

### Stage 1: Content Brief
Essential sections:
- **Administrative:** draft due date, PM task link, writer, priority
- **Client guidelines:** style guide URL, voice/tone (funny vs serious, formal vs casual, respectful vs irreverent, enthusiastic vs matter-of-fact), image guidelines, past examples
- **Audience:** target persona, problem to solve, buyer's journey stage (TOFU/MOFU/BOFU)
- **Competition:** content to beat (URLs), pros/cons of top results
- **Brief details:** goal, CTA, topic, angle, major points, anticipated questions
- **Content scope:** inclusions/exclusions, content type, format, word count, structure template
- **On-page SEO:** primary keyword, secondary/semantic keywords, heading vs body keywords, internal link suggestions, sources
- **Unique angle:** "This is the most important factor"

Start with keyword research before writing to validate the idea.

### Stage 2: Research & Outlining
High-level roadmap for the draft:
- Add subheading labels for major topics
- Add bullet points with specifics under each
- Complete most research during outline stage
- Seek statistics, expert insights, supportive material
- Goal: outline you can draft from without further research distraction

### Stage 3: Drafting
- Knock it out quickly without overthinking
- Turn bullet ideas into sentences rich in detail
- Transform placeholder subheadings into compelling titles
- Editing is for the next stage -- do not self-edit while drafting

### Stage 4: Self-Editing (6 Passes)
1. **Content pass:** flesh out unfinished sections, refine word choice, complete remaining research
2. **Grammar pass:** spelling, grammar, formatting
3. **Read aloud pass:** catch agreement issues, awkward phrasing, run-on sentences
4. **Audience pass:** validate resonance with target reader
5. **Plagiarism pass:** check with Copyscape
6. **SEO pass:** keyword implementation, internal/external links, on-page optimization tasks

## Subtopic Research (Nat Eliason)

Three-step process:

**Step 1 -- Ahrefs "Also Rank For"**
- Plug target keyword into Keyword Explorer
- Check "also rank for" to find related terms ranking pages cover
- Add all relevant subtopics to outline

**Step 2 -- SERP Analysis**
- Google your keyword, open top 3-5 results
- Read through each for subtopics you missed
- Do NOT copy their structure wholesale -- many pad with off-topic content

**Step 3 -- ClearScope (Optional)**
- Run a report to identify terms from top results you may be missing
- After writing, paste article in for grade (aim for A before publishing)
- Validates completeness and competitive readiness

---

## Outlining From Subtopics

1. Take full subtopic list from research
2. Group related subtopics into logical sections (e.g., all cardiovascular topics together)
3. Order from most universally relevant to least
4. Top-level items become H2s, second-level become H3s
5. Delete duplicates as you cluster
6. Should be familiar enough with topic to write most without heavy research

## Introduction Structure (Maddy Osman)

Three parts:
1. **Problem** -- the pain point that brought the reader
2. **Solution** -- your approach or framework
3. **Reveal** -- a hint at how the problem gets solved (hooks the reader)

---

## Conclusion Structure

- Reflects introduction without being exact copy
- Restates problem and key points
- Ends with CTA based on original content goal

---

## Subheading Density
Aim for roughly 1 subheading per ~300 words so sections do not become overly dense.

## On-Page SEO Checklist Per 500 Words (Maddy Osman)

- SEO title: 50-60 characters
- Meta description: under 155 characters based on primary keyword
- Primary keyword used 2+ times
- Primary keyword in title and 1+ subheadings
- Relevant secondary/semantic keywords 1+ times each
- Secondary keywords in subheadings where possible
- 2+ internal links
- 2+ external links to high-quality, relevant sources
- Only one H1 per page
- Images optimized: primary keyword in title, alt tag, filename

## The 8-Step Article Optimization Checklist (Nat Eliason)

1. **Keyword-focused slug** -- only primary keyword, no dates, no categories
2. **Keyword + solution focused title** -- keyword plus compelling reason to click
3. **Keyword + solution meta description** -- make people want to click
4. **Internal links** -- link to your other articles where natural
5. **Good external links** -- authoritative sources only (Wikipedia, academic, .gov, NCBI, major publications). Never link to other blogs as sources
6. **Right headers** -- one H1, H2s for sections, H3s for subsections
7. **Image alt tags** -- every image must have descriptive alt text
8. **Link from old to new** -- after publishing, add links from existing articles back to the new one

## Title Formulas for Articles (Nat Eliason)

**Formula 1:** [Keyword]: [Exciting Pitch]
Example: "Water Fasting Results: Why I Love Not Eating for Five Days"

**Formula 2:** [Answer + Keyword + Curiosity]
Example: "No, Soylent Isn't Healthy. Here's Why"

**Formula 3:** [Keyword + Branding]
Example: "Self-Education: Teach Yourself Anything with the Sandbox Method"

The article title and meta title can differ. Use Yoast or similar to customize separately.

## Content Planning (Nat Eliason)

- Plan at least 4 weeks, targeting 3 articles per week
- From 50 keywords, pick 12 to start (4 weeks x 3 articles)
- Mix broad cornerstone content and narrow long-tail each week
- Do related content in series for easy interlinking
- Put lower-difficulty topics first (rank faster for newer sites)
- Focus on cornerstone content early so you can keep linking back to it
- 50 keywords represent roughly a year of content
- Total planning process: couple of hours

### Content Plan Spreadsheet Structure (6 sheets)
1. High Level Topics (3-10 broad authority topics)
2. Related Keywords (Ahrefs "also rank for" data)
3. Competitor Keywords (position 4-100, volume 250+, difficulty <60)
4. All Keywords (combined master list)
5. Target Keywords (filtered top 50 with validation)
6. Content Plan (keyword, week 1-13, outline link)
