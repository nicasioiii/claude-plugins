# Writing for SEO Checklist

Master keyword integration, readability, and structure to maximize ranking potential while maintaining quality.

## Keyword Integration Strategy

### Primary Keyword Placement (Must Include)

**In Title**: Required
- "Best Natural Sunscreen for Sensitive Skin"
- Not: "Amazing Sunscreen Product Guide"

**In H1**: Required
- Same as title or variation
- "How to Choose the Best Natural Sunscreen"

**In First 100 Words**: Best Practice
- Appear naturally in introduction
- "Natural sunscreen protects your skin while..."

**In Body Text**: 2-3 times per 1,000 words
- First mention: full keyword
- Later mentions: can use variations or pronouns
- Natural context, not forced

**In Meta Description**: Yes (150-160 chars)
- "Discover natural sunscreen options for sensitive skin..."

**Frequency Guide** (per 1,000 words):
```
Keyword Length: 2 words ("natural sunscreen")
- Primary keyword: 2-3 times total
- Variations: 3-5 times

Keyword Length: 3 words ("natural sunscreen for sensitive skin")
- Primary: 1-2 times
- Variations: 2-3 times

Keyword Length: 4+ words
- Primary: 1-2 times
- Variations: 1-2 times
```

### Secondary Keyword Integration

**Definition**: Related keywords that support main topic

**Examples** (main: "natural sunscreen"):
- reef-safe sunscreen
- sunscreen for sensitive skin
- mineral sunscreen
- SPF protection
- chemical-free sunscreen

**Placement Strategy**:
- One secondary per major section (H2)
- Include in different H2/H3 than primary
- 1-2 appearances per secondary keyword
- Naturally, not forced

**Implementation**:
```
H1: Best Natural Sunscreen for Sensitive Skin
H2: Understanding SPF and UV Protection (secondary: "SPF")
H2: Mineral vs Chemical Sunscreen (secondary: "mineral sunscreen")
H2: Reef-Safe Ingredients (secondary: "reef-safe")
H2: Application Tips (no secondary needed)
```

## Readability Standards

### Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level

**Target**: Grade 8-10 (14-16 year old comprehension)

**Tools**: Hemingway Editor (hemingwayapp.com), Yoast

**Achievable By**:
- Short sentences (under 20 words avg)
- Simple words (avoid jargon)
- Active voice (not passive)
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)

**Examples**:

❌ Unreadable (Grade 16+):
"The utilization of mineral-based photoprotective compounds facilitates dermatological preservation through the implementation of physical obstruction mechanisms against ultraviolet radiation."

✓ Readable (Grade 9):
"Mineral sunscreen works by creating a physical barrier that reflects UV rays away from your skin."

### Sentence Structure

**Bad Practice**:
- Average sentence length: 25+ words
- Multiple clauses
- Dense paragraphs

**Good Practice**:
- Average sentence length: 15-18 words
- Mix short (8 words) and medium (18 words) sentences
- 2-4 sentence paragraphs
- Varied structure

**Example**:
```
✓ Good readability:
"Natural sunscreen works differently than chemical sunscreen.
It creates a physical barrier. This barrier reflects UV rays.
Your skin stays protected without harmful chemicals."

(Avg 10.5 words/sentence)

❌ Poor readability:
"Natural sunscreen, which works through physical barrier creation
instead of chemical absorption, creates protection by reflecting UV rays,
which distinguishes it fundamentally from chemical-based alternatives, thus offering
superior safety for sensitive skin types."

(Avg 22 words/sentence)
```

### Paragraph Structure

**Bad**: Walls of text (5+ sentences per paragraph)

**Good**:
- 2-4 sentences per paragraph typical
- One idea per paragraph
- White space improves scannability

**Example**:
```
✓ Well-formatted:
Natural sunscreen works by sitting on your skin's surface.
It creates a physical barrier that reflects UV rays.

This approach is gentler for sensitive skin.
It doesn't absorb into your bloodstream.

Mineral sunscreen is ideal if your skin is reactive.
Look for zinc oxide or titanium dioxide.
```

### Heading Optimization

**Make Headers Compelling & Specific**:

❌ Weak: "Types of Sunscreen"
✓ Strong: "Mineral vs Chemical Sunscreen: Which Protects Better?"

❌ Weak: "Application"
✓ Strong: "How to Apply Sunscreen for Maximum Protection"

❌ Weak: "Benefits"
✓ Strong: "Why Natural Sunscreen is Better for Sensitive Skin"

**Header Best Practices**:
- Front-load benefit or question
- Include secondary keyword if natural
- 8-12 words typical (not 3 words, not 20 words)
- Make reader curious (clickable style)

## Internal Link Strategy

### Linking Rules

**Quantity**: 3-5 internal links per 1,500 words
- Too few: Missed opportunity
- Too many: Looks spammy

**Placement**: Within relevant content context
- Link when you naturally mention related topic
- Don't add links just to hit target
- Feel natural when read aloud

**Anchor Text**: Descriptive, 3-5 words
```
✓ "Learn about reef-safe sunscreen ingredients"
✓ "See our mineral sunscreen buying guide"
❌ "Click here"
❌ "This article on sunscreen ingredients"
```

**Link Placement Map**:
```
Article about: "Best Sunscreen for Sensitive Skin"

Link 1 (Intro/Context): "If you have sensitive skin..."
  → Link to: Pillar page "Natural Skincare Guide"

Link 2 (Body): "Reef-safe options are important..."
  → Link to: "Reef-Safe Sunscreen Guide"

Link 3 (Body): "Ingredient deep dive available..."
  → Link to: "Common Sunscreen Ingredients Explained"

Link 4 (Conclusion): "For water activities..."
  → Link to: Money page "Best Reef-Safe Sunscreen"

Link 5 (Conclusion): "Complete buying guide..."
  → Link to: Money page "Natural Sunscreen Products"
```

## External Links Strategy

### Authority Sources

**Best**: Academic, government, well-known brands
- PubMed (medical research)
- Government (.gov) sites
- Major publications (NYT, BBC)
- Established institutions

**Good**: Industry-specific authority sites
- Dermatology associations
- Scientific journals
- Professional organizations

**Avoid**:
- Low-authority blogs
- Content syndication sites
- Unverified sources
- Competing affiliate sites

### Linking Guidelines

**Quantity**: 3-5 external links per article
- Supports credibility
- Too many dilutes authority flow

**Placement**:
- Cite specific claims with links
- Example: "According to a 2024 study [link], mineral sunscreen..."
- Not: "Sunscreen is important. [Random link]"

**Anchor Text**: Descriptive
```
✓ "According to the FDA's sunscreen guide..."
✓ "Study from University of California..."
❌ "Click for more info"
```

## Content Quality Checklist

### Pre-Publication

- [ ] Primary keyword appears in: Title, H1, first 100 words, body (2-3x), meta description
- [ ] Secondary keywords in different H2/H3 sections (1-2x each)
- [ ] No keyword stuffing (reads naturally when read aloud)
- [ ] Flesch-Kincaid grade 8-10 (Hemingway Editor)
- [ ] Average sentence length: 15-18 words
- [ ] Paragraphs: 2-4 sentences each
- [ ] Headers are compelling (benefit-focused, not generic)
- [ ] 3-5 internal links with descriptive anchor text
- [ ] 3-5 external links to authority sources
- [ ] All external links open in new tab
- [ ] Zero plagiarism (Copyscape check)
- [ ] All claims verified with sources or personal experience
- [ ] Author credentials included
- [ ] Featured image 1200x630px+ with keyword alt text
- [ ] 2-3 supporting images with alt text
- [ ] Article schema markup ready
- [ ] Word count matches target (within 10%)

### Readability Elements

- [ ] Table of contents (if article 2,000+ words)
- [ ] Key takeaway box or summary
- [ ] Lists (bullet or numbered) for scanability
- [ ] Bolded key terms and phrases
- [ ] Logical flow (smooth transitions between sections)
- [ ] Introduction sets expectations clearly
- [ ] Conclusion recaps key points
- [ ] Clear call-to-action at end

### SEO Elements

- [ ] Meta title: 50-60 characters, keyword first
- [ ] Meta description: 160 characters, benefit statement
- [ ] H1 matches meta title (or very similar)
- [ ] One H1 only
- [ ] Proper H2/H3 hierarchy (no skipped levels)
- [ ] Headers include keywords naturally (not forced)
- [ ] Primary keyword in at least one H2
- [ ] Secondary keywords in different sections
- [ ] Internal links strategic (to money pages mostly)
- [ ] External links to authority only
- [ ] Alt text on all images, includes keywords naturally
- [ ] Image filenames include keywords
- [ ] URLs use hyphens, lowercase, include keyword

## Common Writing Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Keyword Stuffing**
```
❌ "Natural sunscreen, reef-safe sunscreen, best sunscreen for sensitive skin,
organic sunscreen with natural sunscreen ingredients for reef-safe sunscreen protection."

✓ "Natural, reef-safe sunscreen with organic ingredients protects sensitive skin."
```

**Mistake 2: Poor Sentence Structure**
```
❌ "The implementation of mineral-based photoprotective compounds, which function through
the utilization of physical obstruction mechanisms against ultraviolet radiation wavelengths,
provides dermatological benefits."

✓ "Mineral sunscreen creates a physical barrier. This barrier protects against UV rays."
```

**Mistake 3: Weak Transitions**
```
❌ No transition between topics (jarring)

✓ "While chemical sunscreen absorbs into skin, mineral sunscreen works differently.
It creates a physical barrier on the surface."
```

**Mistake 4: Generic Headers**
```
❌ "Introduction", "Main Points", "Conclusion"
✓ "Why Natural Sunscreen Matters", "How to Choose the Best Option", "Final Thoughts"
```

**Mistake 5: No Clear CTA**
```
❌ Article ends with "I guess that's it."
✓ "Ready to try natural sunscreen? Check out our top picks here [link]"
```

**Mistake 6: Inconsistent Formatting**
```
❌ Some sections bolded, some not; lists start mid-text
✓ Consistent formatting: headers bolded, lists on own lines, structure clear
```

## Optimization After Publishing

### 30 Days Post-Publication

- Monitor Google Search Console
- Check: Impressions, clicks, CTR, average position
- If CTR < 2%: Rewrite meta title or description
- If impressions low: Content may need updating or more promotion

### 90 Days Post-Publication

- Check ranking position
- If ranking: Analyze what worked, apply to future articles
- If not ranking: Identify gaps (weak sections, missing angles, competitor advantage)
- Add/update internal links from higher-authority pages
- Add external links for additional credibility

### 6-Month Content Refresh

- Update statistics/data if newer info available
- Refresh examples if outdated
- Add new sections for recent developments
- Update "2024" to "2025" if claiming current year
- Re-optimize based on top-ranking competitors

## Reading Level Test

**Test Your Content**:
1. Paste article into Hemingway Editor
2. Aim for Grade Level 8-10
3. Fix any sentences marked as "hard to read"
4. Verify active voice is used (not passive)

**Red Flags**:
- Grade level 14+: Too complex
- Multiple sentences over 25 words: Too long
- Adverbs highlighted: Replace when possible
- Passive voice: Convert to active
