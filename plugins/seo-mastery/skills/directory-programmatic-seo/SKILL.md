# Directory SEO & Programmatic SEO

## Core Philosophy

Directories are digital real estate -- cheap to start, fully remote, inherently scalable, high-margin, and sellable as assets. The key competitive advantage is topical authority: a directory 100% focused on one niche can outrank Yelp and massive competitors despite zero backlinks and DR 0.1.

**Three directory types that survive AI:**
1. **Community-based** -- marketplace directories or shared-interest hubs
2. **Omnipresent** -- present on YouTube, Reddit, Instagram, TV, LLM SEO (not SEO-dependent)
3. **Specialized data** -- data so hard to get that no one else bothers, or data you're uniquely positioned to acquire

## Niche Selection: The 7-Filter Framework

**Philosophy:** 70% data-backed, 30% passion-based. "Most ideas are terrible" -- maintain high standards. Target low competition, evergreen, location-based niches.

### Filter 1: Keyword Difficulty + Search Volume
- Target KD under 20 (Ahrefs), ideally under 5
- Check location sub-keywords for consistent city/state demand (90-150 monthly searches per city at KD 0-2 = massive green flag)
- For display ad directories: aim for 20,000-50,000 monthly visitors

### Filter 2: Competitive Research
- Google target keyword + city. Find other directories (skip Reddit, Yelp)
- Paste competitors into Ahrefs: check monthly visitors, backlinks, referring domains
- Yelp is NEVER a reason to abandon a niche -- topical authority beats Yelp's diluted relevance
- Look for mom-and-pop competitors (simple design, one-person operations)

### Filter 3: Problem Solving
Validate via Google Maps (mismatched categories = opportunity), Reddit (location questions), TikTok (viral videos with "where is this?" comments).

### Filter 4: Search Intent
Broad keywords are traps. Go deeper to KD 0-5 sub-niches with crystal-clear intent. Location-based keywords usually have inherently clear intent.

### Filter 5: Future Workload
Low maintenance (dog parks) vs. high maintenance (AI tool directories). Ask: "What does maintaining this look like in a year?"

### Filter 6: Improvement Potential
Two axes: data enrichment (add info competitors lack) and user experience (fewer clicks to find info).

### Filter 7: Monetization Potential
- **Display ads:** 20K-50K visitors = ~$1K/month. RPM varies by niche.
- **Sponsored listings:** High-AOV businesses will pay. Low-AOV businesses won't.
- **Affiliate:** Hardest. Requires high-AOV + matched intent.
- **Digital products:** Often discovered after traffic comes (e.g., $19 PDF with insider data).

## Directory Building 12-Step Roadmap

1. Find and validate niche (7 filters)
2. Plan monetization (before building -- informs structure)
3. Scrape data (Outscraper for Google Maps, or manual)
4. Clean data (verify listings are contextual to niche)
5. Enrich data (add value beyond Google Maps -- amenities, insider info, user tips)
6. Buy domain (only AFTER data is ready -- enrichment is where people quit)
7. Build website (WordPress + Elementor Pro, or AI tools)
8. Implement on-page SEO (headers, alt text, FAQ, TOC, URL slugs)
9. Set up GA + GSC (submit sitemap)
10. Launch with Reddit post (initial traffic burst + feedback)
11. Wait 1-2 months (let Google index, observe natural ranking ceiling)
12. Build backlinks (link swaps, badges, niche edits) to push past plateau

**Critical rule:** Buy domain only after data is scraped, cleaned, and enriched. Enrichment is where people quit.

## On-Page SEO for Directories

### Keyword Clusters
Never target just one keyword. Find synonyms and layman terms. Check Ahrefs parent topics to confirm shared intent. Highest-volume synonym for H1, lower volume in H2/H3.

### Architecture: Pillar Page vs. Individual Listing
- **Pillar page:** One long page per state/region with all listings. Good for simpler niches, lower listing counts.
- **Individual listing:** Separate page per listing. Good for richer data, higher listing counts. URL: /state/city/business-name.

### Internal Linking
Top 3 most influential SEO tactics for directories. Creates topical authority -- has outranked much higher DR sites through topical authority alone. Avoid orphan pages. Homepage links to city pages > city pages link to listings + neighboring cities + blogs.

### Additional On-Page Elements
FAQ sections (enable FAQ schema), table of contents (essential for pillar pages), foundational pages (About, Contact), breadcrumbs, social links in footer.

## Off-Page SEO for Directories

**Directories use national SEO, not local SEO.** Do NOT buy citations (NAP consistency is for business websites, not directories). In low-competition niches, as few as 2-3-5 backlinks can rank a directory.

### Backlink Tier List (Directory Context)
- **S tier:** Link inserts / link swaps ($50-100/link, full quality control)
- **A tier:** Reddit mentions (free, high-ranking posts)
- **B tier:** Guest posts/HARO, badge/award strategy, niche edits
- **C tier:** PBNs (risky for beginners)
- **F tier:** Web 2.0 backlinks, blog comments, local citations

### Badge/Award Strategy (Unique to Directories)
1. Create "Top 5/10 Best X in [City]" page
2. Design badge in Canva (brand + location + category)
3. Generate embeddable HTML code
4. Email businesses: "We chose you as top 5. Here's a badge for your site"
5. Badge links back to your awards page = backlink
6. Add internal links on award page for equity distribution

## Programmatic SEO Playbook

### TattoosAI Case Study (Danny Postma)
- Hub page lists all sub-categories targeting head keyword ("tattoo ideas")
- Sub-pages target long-tail variations with auto-generated content
- User-generated content + AI categorization = zero content creation cost
- Revenue: $4,000/month on autopilot. Built in one week. Sold for six figures.

### Hub + Sub-Page Template Pattern
- **Hub page:** Lists all sub-categories with links
- **Sub-pages:** Auto-generated title/meta, AI-written description, grid of user content, CTA buttons, internal links back to hub and related sub-pages
- Scale: hundreds to thousands of pages, all interlinked

### pSEO Keyword Expansion
Identify keyword goldmines through expansion patterns (e.g., "[tattoo style] tattoo ideas" generates hundreds of long-tail variations).

## Authority Site Business Model (TASS 3.0 Overview)

### 3-Stage Framework
1. Get traffic via SEO, monetize with affiliate + display ads
2. Add email lists, audience building, email marketing
3. Create own products, paid ads, full business

### Revenue Model Hierarchy
1. Affiliate commissions (primary)
2. Display advertising (add at 50K+ monthly traffic)
3. Own products (Stage 3)

### Niche Qualification Criteria
Competition, monetization potential, SEO viability, community presence, evergreen vs. trending, YMYL risk assessment.
