# Search Intent Classification -- Complete Reference

## THE FOUR INTENT TYPES

### 1. Informational Intent
**User goal:** Learn something. Gather information. Understand a concept.
**Query signals:** how to, what is, why does, guide, tutorial, tips, examples, definition
**Content format:** Blog posts, guides, tutorials, explainers, FAQ pages, listicles
**Monetization:** Display ads, email capture, affiliate links within educational content
**Funnel position:** Top of funnel (awareness)

**Examples:**
- "how to train a puppy"
- "what is topical authority"
- "benefits of intermittent fasting"
- "SEO vs SEM difference"

### 2. Commercial / Investigational Intent
**User goal:** Research before buying. Compare options. Evaluate alternatives.
**Query signals:** best, top, review, vs, comparison, alternative to, [product] pros cons
**Content format:** Roundup reviews, comparison pages, "best of" lists, vs articles
**Monetization:** Affiliate commissions (primary), display ads
**Funnel position:** Middle of funnel (consideration)

**Examples:**
- "best air purifiers 2026"
- "Ahrefs vs SEMrush"
- "top CRM for small business"
- "Mailchimp alternatives"

### 3. Transactional Intent
**User goal:** Take action. Buy. Sign up. Download. Hire.
**Query signals:** buy, price, cost, discount, coupon, free trial, download, hire, near me, order
**Content format:** Product pages, landing pages, collection pages, pricing pages
**Monetization:** Direct sales, lead generation, service bookings
**Funnel position:** Bottom of funnel (decision/action)

**Examples:**
- "buy running shoes online"
- "mailchimp pricing"
- "plumber near me"
- "download free SEO checklist"

### 4. Navigational Intent
**User goal:** Find a specific website or page.
**Query signals:** [brand name], [brand] login, [brand] support, [website] blog
**Content format:** Homepage, specific feature pages, support pages
**Monetization:** Brand reinforcement, customer retention
**Funnel position:** N/A (brand-specific)

**Examples:**
- "Ahrefs login"
- "YouTube"
- "Amazon customer service"

## SERP-BASED INTENT DETECTION

**The definitive method:** Search the keyword in Google and observe what ranks.

| SERP Feature | Indicates |
|---|---|
| Blog posts, guides, tutorials dominate | Informational intent |
| "Best of" lists, review sites dominate | Commercial/investigational intent |
| Product/category pages, store listings dominate | Transactional intent |
| Single brand dominates all results | Navigational intent |
| Mixed results (blogs + products) | Mixed intent -- create content matching dominant format |
| Featured snippet present | Informational with high answer potential |
| Product carousels / Shopping ads | Transactional, e-commerce opportunity |
| Local pack (map results) | Local transactional intent |
| People Also Ask present | Informational with sub-question opportunities |

**The critical rule:** Match your content format to what currently ranks. If Google shows listicles, create a listicle. If Google shows product pages, you need a product page. Fighting the SERP's intent interpretation is a losing battle.

## INTENT-TO-CONTENT FORMAT MAPPING

| Intent | Primary Format | Secondary Format |
|---|---|---|
| Informational | How-to guide, tutorial, explainer | List post, FAQ page, video |
| Commercial | Roundup ("Best X"), comparison ("X vs Y") | Single review, alternatives list |
| Transactional | Product/service page, landing page | Collection page, pricing page |
| Navigational | Homepage, feature page | Support/docs page |

### Content Type Templates (Authority Hacker)

**Informational content types:**
- **Question posts** -- Answer a specific question (how to, what is, why does)
- **List posts** -- Curated lists (10 best, 7 ways to, 15 tips for)

**Commercial content types:**
- **Single review** -- Deep dive on one product/service
- **Roundup review** -- "Best [category]" with 5-15 options ranked
- **Vs comparison** -- Head-to-head "[Product A] vs [Product B]"
- **Alternatives** -- "[Product] alternatives" for users unhappy with market leader

## E-COMMERCE INTENT FRAMEWORK (Cromwell)

### Collections-First Priority
- Target collection/category pages over product pages
- Collections rank easier because:
  - You can build backlinks to collection pages (Google often ignores links to product pages)
  - Consumer psychology: users want options
  - Google shows far more collection pages than product pages in results

### The "Best/Top" Modifier Rule
- Keywords like "best sunscreen" or "top sunscreen" rank listicle/affiliate sites (Forbes, Healthline)
- E-commerce stores should target direct purchase-intent keywords: "natural sunscreen", "organic sunscreen lotion"
- Exception: if you run a content blog alongside your store, target "best" keywords with blog content

### Sub-Collection Revenue Multiplier (Cromwell)
Sell the SAME products across multiple collections, each targeting a different keyword:
- Main: "Natural Sunscreen" (9,900 searches/month)
- Sub 1: "Natural Sunscreen Lotion" (different keyword)
- Sub 2: "Natural Sunblock" (900 searches/month)
- Sub 3: Additional variant keyword

All collections contain the same 5 products. Instead of 1 keyword, you target 3-4 keywords and potentially 3-4x traffic and revenue.

**Why most e-commerce operators miss this:** They think each collection needs unique products. It does not. The same product legitimately fits multiple keyword categories.

## SEARCH FUNNEL POSITIONING

### Mapping Keywords to Funnel Stages

```
AWARENESS (Informational)
  "what is topical authority" -> Blog post / guide
  "how to improve SEO" -> Tutorial / pillar content
     |
CONSIDERATION (Commercial)
  "best SEO tools 2026" -> Roundup / comparison
  "Ahrefs vs SEMrush" -> Vs article
     |
DECISION (Transactional)
  "Ahrefs pricing" -> Pricing page / affiliate link
  "buy Ahrefs subscription" -> Product page
     |
RETENTION (Navigational)
  "Ahrefs login" -> Support / docs
```

### Budget Allocation by Funnel Stage (Gorrono)
- Use Google Ads CPC data to prioritize SEO keywords
- High CPC = high commercial value = worth investing content effort
- DataForSEO SERP parser shows how many paid results sit above organic for any keyword
- More paid ads above organic = higher commercial value = harder to rank organically but more valuable

## INTENT CLASSIFICATION FOR CONTENT PLANNING

### The Three Content Buckets (Vahe Arabian)
1. **Informational** -- How-to content, TOFU, drives the most traffic
2. **Promotional** -- Listicles and curated content designed to earn links (connective tissue)
3. **Tactical** -- Reviews and content focused on conversions (affiliate revenue, leads)

Execute all three in synergy. Run in quarterly campaign sprints.

### Authority Hacker Content Types Mapped to Intent
| Content Type | Intent | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Info content (question posts, list posts) | Informational | Traffic + link building + internal link juice |
| Commercial content (reviews, roundups, vs, alternatives) | Commercial | Revenue + affiliate commissions |
| Support content | Informational (secondary) | Internal linking + concept explanation |

### Keyword Intent Signals Quick Reference

| Signal | Intent |
|---|---|
| how to, what is, why, guide, tutorial | Informational |
| best, top, review, vs, compare, alternative | Commercial |
| buy, price, cost, coupon, near me, hire, download | Transactional |
| [brand name], [brand] login, [brand] support | Navigational |
| [product category] (e.g., "running shoes") | Mixed -- check SERP |
| [product] + [modifier] (e.g., "running shoes for flat feet") | Commercial leaning |
