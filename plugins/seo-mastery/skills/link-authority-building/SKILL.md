# Link Building & Authority Acquisition

## Core Philosophy

Link building is the single most controllable external ranking factor. The goal is not raw volume but a diversified, velocity-safe link profile that signals genuine authority to Google.

**The Hierarchy of Link Building (in order of long-term value):**
1. Entity stack + foundational links (Day 1 -- brand entity establishment)
2. Nofollow diversity layer (trust signals, link diversity)
3. Content-driven link earning (HARO, skyscraper, infographics)
4. Outreach-based acquisition (guest posts, niche edits)
5. Tiered link building (activating nofollow hint directive)

## Entity Stack Construction (Day-One Priority)

Build from Day 1 to create a brand entity in Google -- a terminology cluster around your brand name so branded SERPs show your site, socials, and properties.

**Required properties:** Social profiles, citations, directories, GMB listing, branded properties.

**Critical rule:** Social profiles must be active and engaged -- posts, comments, likes. Not set-and-forget automation. Future algorithm updates will check social profile activity as a trust signal.

**Social signals** (likes, tweets, shares from external users) are separate from social profiles and serve as additional trust signals.

> **Source synthesis:** Floate emphasizes entity stacks as foundational; Authority Hacker builds them through the site setup process; Frey Chu skips entity stacks for directories (national SEO game). Apply entity stacks to money sites and service businesses, not to directory/programmatic SEO sites.

## Foundational Link Types

1. **Social profiles** -- rank for brand entity keywords, establish brand presence
2. **Citations and directories** -- backbone of local SEO; support brand entity signals
3. **Niche blog comments** -- find high/mid-authority sites with manual approval and website field enabled; go slow
4. **Image links** -- non-contextual but powerful; Google counts differently from text links
5. **Forum profiles and posts** -- profiles are easier (URL in profile field); posts are contextual and more powerful but face heavy moderation

**3,180+ free foundation backlink sites available** across categories: social (Instagram, Quora, Twitch), developer (GitHub, StackOverflow), general (Medium, Tumblr, WordPress), business (Eventbrite, Calendly), creative (Behance, Dribbble).

## Nofollow Link Strategy

**Key change (2019):** Google announced nofollow becomes a "hint directive" -- nofollow links can now pass juice at Google's discretion based on page authority, link authority, and social signals.

**Strategic implication:** A higher ratio of nofollow + trust signal links enables more aggressive dofollow links. 100 dofollow links/month is dangerous alone, but 100 dofollow + 200-300 nofollow looks natural because diversity offsets velocity concerns.

### INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: Nofollow Disavow

**Floate (contrarian):** You SHOULD disavow toxic nofollow backlinks. Google's 2012 guidance (no need to disavow nofollows) has not been updated since making nofollow a hint directive. Single-variable testing shows disavowing purely toxic nofollow links has promising impact.

**Most SEOs:** Skip nofollow disavow entirely.

**Resolution:** Floate's position is supported by the 2019 hint directive change. Consider disavowing toxic nofollows for sites where link profile hygiene is critical.

## Anchor Text Strategy

### INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: Anchor Text Approach

**Floate:** Link velocity matters MORE than anchor text ratios. Focus on creating unique anchor text on every single backlink rather than obsessing over ratios. Higher authority sources and higher authority sites reduce anchor text ratio importance.

**Morrison:** Always use keyword anchor text on PBN links -- this is your most powerful link type, so maximize it. Save branded anchors for other link types.

**Safe anchor text distribution (synthesized):**
- ~30% naked URL
- ~35% branded
- ~10% partial match
- ~20% generic ("click here," "read this")
- Exact match: use sparingly, balanced by diversity

**Method to find your ideal ratio:** Check your current distribution in Ahrefs, then compare to top 5-10 competitors. Match their distribution.

## Link Velocity Framework

**Core principle:** Link velocity is page-by-page, not site-wide. More pages on your site = higher total link velocity is safe because links spread across pages.

**Danger signals:**
- Brand new sites: go very slow
- Penalty-recovering sites: go very slow
- Dead pages getting sudden new links without content updates = red flag
- Google's freshness algorithm scores pages with purely single-link niche edits lower if the page hasn't been meaningfully updated

**Social signals offset velocity:** Social signals allow higher link velocities and larger quantities of cheaper links because they balance with the trust element.

## Tiered Link Building

**Strategy for activating the nofollow hint directive:**

Most nofollow links just need an indexing service. But quality nofollow links from large publications need tier 2 links built to them to activate juice passing.

**Target for tiered building:** Large publication links, HARO links, charity links, Wikipedia links.

**How it works:** Grow the authority of the PAGE containing your nofollow link (not your site directly) using links + social signals pointed at that page.

**Tier 2 link sources:** Cheap niche edits (Rhino Rank, Get Me Rankings), niche-specific PBN link providers.

**Quality tiered method (Floate):** Find DR20-DR40 sites, go into Ahrefs "Best by Links," find pages with 50-150 referring domains already. Pay the site owner to insert a link on that page ($60-$70). The existing 50-150 referring domains ARE your tier 2 links already.

## Link Quality Assessment

**Five factors for evaluating any potential link:**
1. Domain Rating / Domain Authority
2. Topical relevance to your site
3. Page freshness and recency of updates
4. Page authority (referring domains to the specific page)
5. Bad neighborhood check (spam, adult, gambling unless that's your niche)

**Deep links vs. homepage links:** Deep links to inner pages are more valuable for ranking specific pages. Homepage links boost overall domain authority.

### INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT: Purchasing Links

**Authority Hacker:** "We've never paid for a single link." Focuses entirely on earned link strategies (HARO, skyscraper, guest posting).

**Gorrono / Floate:** Purchasing links is standard practice. Agencies like Get Me Links, Rhino Ranks, The Hoth provide link services at various quality tiers.

**Resolution:** Both approaches work. The risk spectrum ranges from zero-risk (earned only) to moderate risk (purchased from vetted sources). Document the approach used and the risk tolerance of each project.

## Niche Edits

Way cheaper than guest posts. Safe to use 5-10 niche edits in your link profile while diversifying.

**Link freshness warning:** Google's freshness algorithm penalizes pages updated with ONLY a single link insertion. When buying niche edits, request that the publisher also add content, imagery, or media to the page.

**High-quality niche edits on pages with existing high referring domains are the best value in link building.**
