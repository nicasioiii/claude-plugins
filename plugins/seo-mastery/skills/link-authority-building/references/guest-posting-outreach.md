# Guest Posting Outreach: Full Campaign Workflow

## What Is Guest Posting?
Write article for someone else's site; they publish it with backlink to yours.

**Pros**: Build authority, reach new audiences, showcase expertise, contextual links
**Cons**: Takes 2-3 hours per post; rejection rate 70%+; sites may remove posts later

---

## Part 1: Finding Guest Posting Opportunities (Research Phase)

### Method 1: Google Search (Fastest)
Search for: `[your niche] "write for us" OR "guest post"`

Examples:
- `marketing "write for us"`
- `SEO "guest post"`
- `real estate "contribute"`

Add site: `site:medium.com "write for us"` to find specific platforms

### Method 2: Competitor Backlink Analysis
1. Open Ahrefs
2. Enter competitor domain
3. Filter backlinks by "Referring page type" = "Blog"
4. Look for nofollow/guest post domains
5. List sites that accept guest posts

**Why effective**: If competitor got linked, you can too.

### Method 3: Manual List Creation
Identify 50 quality sites in your niche:
1. Google top keywords in your space
2. Look at top 10 results
3. Check "About Us" for "Guest Post" or "Contribute" page
4. Build spreadsheet of opportunities

## Part 2: Vetting Opportunities (Quality Check)

### Criteria for GOOD Guest Posting Site

**1. Domain Authority**: DR 30+
- Below 30 = minimal value
- Check in Ahrefs/SEMrush

**2. Niche Relevance**: Must be related to your industry
- Good: Guest post on marketing blog for marketing company
- Bad: Guest post on gardening blog for marketing company (no relevance)

**3. Real Audience**: Site has actual traffic
- Check: Traffic estimates in SEMrush (100+ visitors/month minimum)
- Look at social followers (if 1K+ followers, has real audience)

**4. Engagement Signals**: Readers actually engage
- Check recent articles for comments
- Look for shares on social
- If no engagement = dead blog (avoid)

**5. Link Profile**: The link is actually followed
- Check: Is it dofollow or nofollow?
- Dofollow = better; nofollow = still valuable for brand
- Avoid: Sites with 100+ OBLs (external links) per page (spam)

### RED FLAGS to Avoid

❌ "We'll write the article for you" (spam network)
❌ "Available guest posting" site-wide (low quality)
❌ No author byline or bio link (worthless)
❌ Unrelated niche (wasted effort)
❌ Extremely new domain (<6 months old)
❌ No "About" page or contact info (sketchy)
❌ Article footer full of unrelated links (PBN)

## Part 3: Outreach Email Template

### Email Structure (Critical)

**Subject Line**: ⚠️ Most important part
- Good: "Guest Post Idea: [Specific Topic Your Audience Wants]"
- Bad: "Guest Post Opportunity" (generic, ignored)
- Bad: "Can I write for you?" (too casual)

### Email Body Template A (Specific Angle)

```
Subject: Guest Post: 7 Link Building Strategies That Actually Work [BlogName]

Hi [Blogger Name],

I've been following [BlogName] for a while and love your articles on [specific article]. Your audience clearly cares about [topic].

I'd like to contribute a guest post on [specific topic]. Here's what I'm thinking:

TOPIC: "[Catchy Title]: How [X] Benefits [Audience]"

Why this topic:
- Your readers asked about X (I saw in comments of [recent article])
- It's underserved in your niche
- I have new data to share (we surveyed 500 companies)

I'd include:
- Original research/data
- 1500-2000 words
- Inline images
- A relevant link to my site in the author bio

Can I send you a full outline? I'm flexible on the angle if you have a different angle in mind.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Credentials: "5 years SEO marketing" or similar]
[Website]
[Email]
```

## Part 4: Outreach Execution

### Best Practices for Higher Acceptance Rate

**1. Personalize**: Use their name (not "Hi there")
- Research: Google their name; read a recent article
- Mention specific article by title
- Show you've actually read their content

**2. Lead With Value**: What's in it for them?
- Better: "Your readers ask about X; I'll write about it"
- Worse: "I'd like to write for you"

**3. Make It Easy**: Suggest complete topic/outline
- Don't make them brainstorm
- Provide 2-3 topic options
- Be clear on word count, format, turnaround

**4. Include Writing Sample**: Prove you can write
- Link to best article you've written
- If no website, send document via attachment
- If very new: Share detailed outline showing quality

**5. Follow Blog Guidelines**: If they have submission page, follow it exactly
- If they say "1500-2000 words" → deliver exactly that
- If they list topics they want → pitch within those
- If they mention editorial standards → match them

### Outreach Cadence
- Send 5-10 pitches/week
- Spread across 2-3 days (don't blast all at once)
- Track which sites you've contacted (don't repeat)

## Part 5: Writing the Guest Post

### Structure for Best Results

**1. Title** (Do this first)
- Include keyword for SEO value
- Make it benefit-focused
- Example: "3 Link Building Strategies That Increased Our Rankings 45%"

**2. Introduction** (Hook them immediately)
- State the problem
- Why readers should care
- What they'll learn
- Example: "Most link building advice is outdated. In 2024, we're seeing 3 new strategies that actually work. Here's what the data shows..."

**3. Body** (Main content)
- 3-5 main points (match the number in title)
- Each with explanation + example + actionable step
- Use subheadings (H2 format)
- Include 1-2 images (own screenshots or branded graphics)

**4. Internal Links** (Smart placement)
- 2-4 links total (not excessive)
- Link to their most relevant existing articles
- Use natural anchor text
- Example: "As we discussed in [our article on X], the foundation is..."

**5. Author Bio** (Your backlink)
- 2-3 sentences about who you are
- Include 1 link to your site (best: homepage or landing page)
- Example: "Jane Smith is an SEO director with 10 years experience helping SaaS companies rank. She runs [Your Company]. Learn more at [yoursite.com]"

**6. Conclusion** (End strong)
- Restate main benefit
- Call-to-action (optional): Ask readers to try a tool, read more, etc.

### Quality Standards
- **Length**: Match their guidelines (usually 1500-2500 words)
- **Writing**: Professional, clear, no grammatical errors
- **Original**: Content should be new (not repurposed from your blog)
- **Images**: Include 2-3 relevant images (own screenshots, Unsplash, Canva)
- **Formatting**: Use subheadings (H2), bullet points, short paragraphs

## Part 6: Follow-Up Strategy

### Email 1: Initial Pitch
(As described above)

Wait: 5-7 days

### Email 2: Gentle Follow-Up

```
Subject: RE: Guest Post: [Topic] [BlogName]

Hi [Name],

Just following up on my email from last week about guest posting on [blog name]. I understand you're busy—no pressure, but wanted to check in.

Would you be open to a post on [topic]? I can have a draft to you in 48 hours.

Thanks,
[Name]
```

Wait: 3-5 days

### Email 3: Final Follow-Up

```
Subject: One More Idea for [BlogName]

Hi [Name],

Last follow-up—if guest posting isn't a good fit, no worries.

But I did want to suggest an alternative topic that might be more relevant: "[Different topic]"

Let me know if either resonates.

Best,
[Name]
```

### Cut-Off
If no response after 3 emails = move on. Target next site.

## Part 7: After Article is Published

### Amplification (Gets more views = better SEO)
1. Share on your social media (LinkedIn, Twitter, company channels)
2. Mention in your newsletter (if you have one)
3. Tag the blog in social post (helps them see the share)
4. Ask email list to read + comment (increases engagement)

### Track the Link
1. Monitor backlinks in Ahrefs/SEMrush
2. Add to your link tracking spreadsheet
3. Note: Domain, DR, publication date, niche relevance

### Relationship Building
1. Thank blogger in follow-up email
2. Engage with their other articles (comment, share)
3. Mention their site in future content
4. Pitch again in 6 months with new topic

## Part 9: Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Pitching to low-DR sites (waste of time)
❌ Generic pitches (ignored)
❌ Writing for sites unrelated to your niche (no SEO value)
❌ Overwriting (2500 words when they want 1500)
❌ Putting link in article instead of author bio (less effective)
❌ Promotional tone (readers see through it)
❌ Writing first, pitching second (better to pitch first)
❌ Not following editorial guidelines (instant rejection)
