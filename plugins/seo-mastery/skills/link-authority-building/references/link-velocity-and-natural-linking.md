# Link Velocity & Natural Linking Patterns: Timing Strategy

## What Is Link Velocity?
The rate at which you acquire backlinks over time.

**Slow velocity**: 2-3 links per month (looks natural)
**Normal velocity**: 5-20 links per month (depends on site age)
**Fast velocity**: 50+ links per month (looks suspicious)

---

## Part 1: Why Velocity Matters to Google

### The Problem with Fast Links
**Scenario 1 - Fast Links**:
- Your site gets 50 links in week 1
- Google sees: "This looks like a paid campaign"
- Possible outcome: Manual review or algorithmic penalty

**Scenario 2 - Natural Growth**:
- Your site gets 3 links week 1, 2 links week 2, 4 links week 3
- Google sees: "Content is resonating; attracting natural links"
- Outcome: Safe, normal ranking boost

### How Google Interprets Velocity

**Red Flag Velocities**:
- 100+ links in 48 hours = obvious spam campaign
- 50 links from 50 different domains in same week = coordinated
- Bulk link purchase spike = not natural
- Sudden spike then nothing = temporary campaign

**Green Light Velocities**:
- Gradual increase (5 links/month → 10 links/month → 15 links/month) = growing authority
- Steady baseline (10 links/month consistent) = sustainable growth
- Organic spikes from viral content = legitimate
- Mix of sources and anchor texts = natural pattern

## Part 2: Velocity Guidelines by Site Age

### New Site (0-6 months)

**Expected Velocity**: 1-5 links/month
- New sites shouldn't grow fast
- Too many links = immediate red flag

**Strategy**:
- Start slow (1-2 HARO quotes)
- Build foundation (HARO, one guest post)
- Be patient (first 3 months = lowest link acquisition)

**If you do**:
- 50 links in month 1 = Google will review manually
- 100+ links in month 1 = Likely penalized

### Established Site (6-24 months)

**Expected Velocity**: 5-20 links/month
- Can acquire links faster as domain ages
- 20+ links/month still acceptable if diversified

**Strategy**:
- HARO: 1-2 per week (12-15/month)
- Guest posting: 2-3/month
- Skyscraper: 3-5/month
- Total: 18-23 links/month (natural for this stage)

### Mature Site (24+ months)

**Expected Velocity**: 20-50+ links/month
- Established authority; fast growth expected
- High link velocity = normal

**Strategy**:
- Can do PBN safely (mixed with white hat)
- Aggressive skyscraper campaigns (10+ outreach/week)
- Multiple guest posts/month
- Total: 30-60 links/month (natural for mature site)

## Part 3: Natural Linking Patterns (Google's Expectation)

### Pattern 1: Content Goes Viral Naturally

**What It Looks Like**:
- Week 1: 2-3 links
- Week 2: 8-10 links (content is spreading)
- Week 3: 5-7 links (momentum decreasing)
- Week 4: 3-4 links (normalizing)

**Why Google Accepts It**: Articles naturally get more links when fresh; momentum decays.

**Velocity Spike Sign**: NONE (it's legitimate)

### Pattern 2: HARO + Guest Posting Mix

**What It Looks Like**:
- Weeks 1-4: 2 HARO quotes each = 8 total
- Week 2: 1 guest post published = 1 link
- Week 4: 1 guest post published = 1 link
- Total: 10 links spread over 4 weeks

**Why Google Accepts It**: Diverse sources, natural spacing, different types.

### Pattern 3: Campaign Execution (Skyscraper)

**What It Looks Like**:
- Day 1-5: Send 20 outreach emails
- Day 10-14: 3 acceptances; links placed
- Week 3: 2 more links from earlier outreach
- Week 4: 1 late response converts
- Total: 6 links over 4 weeks (spread out)

**Why Google Accepts It**: Links aren't all simultaneous; they come in over time.

### Pattern 4: PBN Strategy (Mixed with White Hat)

**What It Looks Like**:
- Week 1: 1 PBN link
- Week 2: 2 HARO links + 1 guest post
- Week 3: 2 PBN links
- Week 4: 1 skyscraper link + 1 HARO

**Ratio**: 30% PBN (3 links), 70% white hat (7 links)
**Spacing**: All mixed; not grouped by type
**Velocity**: 10 links over 4 weeks (natural pace)

**Why Google Accepts It**: Links are diversified; don't look coordinated.

## Part 4: Velocity Strategies by Budget

### Low Budget Strategy (0-500/month)

**Approach**: HARO + organic growth

**Monthly Breakdown**:
- HARO responses: 5 per week = 20 responses, ~3 accepted = 3 links
- Natural/organic: 1-2 links from inbound interest
- **Total**: 4-5 links/month
- **Velocity**: Slow, natural, risk-free

**Pros**: Safe, no penalty risk, sustainable
**Cons**: Slow growth (need 2-3 years to build 100+ links)

### Medium Budget Strategy ($500-2000/month)

**Approach**: HARO + Guest Posting + Skyscraper

**Monthly Breakdown**:
- HARO: 3 links
- Guest posting: 2 posts × 1 link = 2 links
- Skyscraper outreach: 10 outreach → 2 links
- **Total**: 7 links/month
- **Velocity**: Moderate, natural, acceptable

**Pros**: Good ROI, sustainable, diverse strategy
**Cons**: Requires effort (10+ hours/week)

### Higher Budget Strategy ($2000-5000/month)

**Approach**: HARO + Guest Posting + Skyscraper + PBN

**Monthly Breakdown**:
- HARO: 3 links
- Guest posting: 3 posts = 3 links
- Skyscraper: 20 outreach → 4 links
- PBN: 4 carefully placed links
- **Total**: 14 links/month
- **Velocity**: Faster but still natural (20 links/month is acceptable for established site)

**Pros**: Faster growth, acceleration, mixed strategies
**Cons**: Requires management, PBN risk factor

## Part 6: Velocity Red Flags & Recovery

### Red Flag 1: Sudden Spike

**What Triggered It**: Bought 50 links in bulk

**Google's Response**: Manual review; possible penalty

**Recovery**:
1. Stop immediately (no new bulk links)
2. Disavow the 50 links (disavow file in GSC)
3. Build white-hat links over next 3 months
4. Monitor rankings daily
5. If deindexed: Wait for Google's next manual review cycle (often 30-60 days)

### Red Flag 2: Consistent Spike Every Month

**What Triggered It**: Same time each month (looks coordinated)

**Example**:
- Week 1 of month: 0-1 links
- Week 2: 1-2 links
- Week 3: 15-20 links (blast from paid service)
- Week 4: 1-2 links

**Google's Response**: Algorithm flags as coordinated campaign

**Recovery**:
1. Diversify timing (don't do blasts same week each month)
2. Mix sources (not all from same service)
3. Randomize: 2 links week 1, 5 week 3, 1 week 4, etc.

### Red Flag 3: All Anchors Same Pattern

**What Triggered It**: "Best link building strategies" anchor on 20 links in 4 weeks

**Google's Response**: Unnatural anchor text pattern = penalty risk

**Recovery**:
1. Stop exact-match anchoring
2. Next 20 links: 50% branded, 30% generic, 20% partial
3. Disavow worst offenders if necessary
4. Monitor rankings for 30 days

## Part 7: Natural Anchor Text Distribution

### Distribution by Source

**HARO Quotes**:
- Anchor text: Usually branded (journalist writes it)
- Example: "John's Blog", "Expert's Article"
- Natural: 100% (journalist choice)

**Guest Posts**:
- Author bio link: Usually branded
- Contextual link: Mix of branded/partial/generic
- Natural: 100% (editorial placement)

**Skyscraper**:
- Your control: Try to influence
- Best practice: 50% branded, 50% partial/generic
- Avoid: Pushing exact-match too hard

**PBN**:
- Your full control: Be careful
- Strategy: 40% branded, 40% generic, 20% partial
- Avoid: All exact-match (obvious)

### Monthly Anchor Text Target

For 10 links/month:
```
Branded: 5 links (50%)
  - "John's Blog" = 2
  - "Example.com" = 2
  - "Brand Name" = 1

Generic: 3 links (30%)
  - "Click here" = 1
  - "This resource" = 1
  - "Learn more" = 1

Partial match: 2 links (20%)
  - "Link building guide" = 1
  - "SEO strategies" = 1
```

**Yearly View**: If you maintain this distribution, anchor text looks natural.

## Part 8: Velocity Recovery Plan (If Penalized)

### If You See Rankings Drop

**Immediate Actions** (Day 1):
1. Check GSC for manual actions
2. Look at recent links in Ahrefs (what changed?)
3. Identify the problem (fast velocity? bad anchors? spam links?)

**First Week**:
1. Stop new link acquisition (pause all campaigns)
2. Disavow any suspicious links
3. Re-file disavow file (replace old one)
4. Wait for Google to process (usually 48-72 hours)

**Following Weeks** (If penalty confirmed):
1. Monitor daily (are rankings coming back?)
2. Build white-hat links only (HARO, guest posts)
3. Slow velocity: 2-3 links/month max
4. Diversify sources heavily
5. Wait 4-6 weeks for recovery signal

**Expected Timeline**:
- Unnatural link penalty: 2-4 weeks to recover
- Manual action: Requires human review (4-12 weeks)
- Algorithm penalty: Usually 4-8 weeks with proper fix

## Key Principle
**Patience beats speed in link building.** Google rewards steady, natural growth over aggressive blasts. Think in terms of 12-month strategy, not 30-day campaigns.
