# Link Building Outreach

## Outreach Email Templates

### Guest Post Pitch
**Subject:** Content idea for [Site Name] -- [Topic]

Body: Reference a recent article on their site. Pitch 2-3 topic ideas with 1-sentence descriptions. Explain why their audience would benefit. Include a brief author bio with relevant credentials. Do NOT attach writing samples in the first email -- offer to send if interested.

### Niche Edit / Link Insert Request
**Subject:** Quick suggestion for your [Article Title] article

Body: Compliment the article specifically (reference a section). Suggest your resource as an addition that would benefit their readers. Explain the relevance. Offer a reciprocal value (share on social, link back from your site).

### Skyscraper Outreach
**Subject:** Updated resource for your [Page Title]

Body: Note that they link to [Original Resource] on their [Page]. Explain that you've created an updated/more comprehensive version. Highlight 2-3 specific improvements. Ask if they'd consider linking to or replacing with your version.

### Broken Link Outreach
**Subject:** Broken link on your [Page Title]

Body: Note the specific broken link (include the anchor text and URL). Offer your replacement resource. Explain how your content covers the same topic. Keep it short -- you're doing them a favor by flagging the broken link.

### Follow-Up Sequence (Detailed)
- **Follow-up 1 (Day 5):** Brief check-in, re-state the value proposition in one sentence. Subject: "Re: [Original Subject]" -- keep it in the same thread
- **Follow-up 2 (Day 12):** Offer additional value (social share, testimonial, different angle). Show you've done something for them ("I shared your article on LinkedIn this morning")
- **Follow-up 3 (Day 21):** Final touch, mention you won't follow up again, leave the door open. "I know you're busy -- just wanted to send one last note. No hard feelings if the timing isn't right."
- **Never follow up more than 3 times** -- persistent but not annoying

### Cold Outreach Best Practices
- Keep emails under 150 words (nobody reads long pitches from strangers)
- Personalize the first line to their specific content (proves you actually read it)
- One clear CTA per email (don't ask for three things at once)
- Use their name, not "Dear Webmaster" or "Hi there"
- Avoid spam trigger words: "free", "guarantee", "click here", "act now"

## Email Deliverability

### Setup Requirements
- Use a dedicated outreach domain (not your main domain) to protect sender reputation
- Max 3 email accounts per domain
- Configure SPF/DKIM/DMARC on the outreach domain
- Warm up email accounts for 3+ weeks before full-volume sending
- Email forwarding to main inbox for consolidated management

### Domain Warming Process
1. **Week 1:** Send 5-10 personal emails per day to real contacts (friends, colleagues)
2. **Week 2:** Increase to 15-20 emails per day, mix personal and outreach
3. **Week 3:** Increase to 25-30 emails per day, mostly outreach
4. **Week 4+:** Full volume at 40-50 emails per day maximum
- Engage in real conversations (replies boost sender reputation)
- Use a warming service (Lemwarm, Warmbox) to automate engagement

### Volume Guidelines
- Start: 10-15 emails/day per account during warm-up
- Ramp: Increase by 5/day each week
- Maximum: 50-75 emails/day per account (conservative)
- Never blast 200+ emails from a fresh account
- If bounce rate exceeds 5%, pause and clean your list

## Finding Contact Information

### Hunter.io Workflow (Step-by-Step)
1. Go to Hunter.io and enter the target domain
2. Hunter reveals the email pattern for the company (e.g., first@domain.com)
3. Search for specific people by name + domain
4. Verify email addresses using Hunter's verification tool (reduces bounce rate)
5. Export verified contacts to your outreach spreadsheet
6. Cross-reference with LinkedIn to confirm the person's current role

### Other Contact Discovery Tools
- **LinkedIn** -- find the content manager, editor, or site owner
- **Contact/About pages** -- many publishers list submission emails
- **WHOIS** -- for smaller sites, the domain registrant email may work
- **Twitter/X** -- DM as a secondary channel if email doesn't get responses
- **Snov.io** -- alternative to Hunter with email finder + verification
- **Voila Norbert** -- email finding tool with high accuracy

### Who to Contact
- Blogs/content sites: Editor or Content Manager
- Small sites: Site owner (usually listed on About page)
- Large publications: The specific section editor, not the general inbox
- Guest post opportunities: Look for a "Write for Us" or "Contribute" page
- If no editor found: the author of the article you want a link placed on

## Link Insert / Swap Process (Frey Chu Method)

### Step-by-Step
1. Find competitor in Ahrefs
2. Go to their backlinks list
3. Scan for small publishers (personal blogs, niche sites) -- skip large media companies
4. Click each backlink, assess quality in 15 seconds (traffic, relevance, link count)
5. Find publisher's contact info (footer, contact page)
6. Send personalized email with:
   - Introduction (position yourself as a fellow enthusiast)
   - Reference the specific article and competitor link with screenshot
   - Offer to swap their link for yours (more updated resource)
   - Offer $50 initially, up to $100 if needed
   - Be transparent about who you are and what you built
7. Follow up weekly -- persistent but not annoying

### Link Swap Negotiation Framework
- **Pure swap:** You link to their content from your site, they link to yours. Zero cost.
- **Paid insert:** $50-$100 for them to add your link to an existing article.
- **Content upgrade:** You offer to update/improve their article content in exchange for a link.
- **Combination:** Swap + you share their content on social media + small payment.
- Always start with the lowest-cost option and escalate only if needed.

### Pricing
- Standard link insert/swap: $50-$100 per link
- Quality assessment: verify page traffic, topical relevance, linked domains count
- If a niche edit costs $100 and delivers zero-traffic irrelevant pages, spend $150-200 for quality instead

## Badge/Award Strategy for Link Acquisition (Directory Context)

### Complete Process
1. Create "Top 5/10 Best X in [City]" pages on your site
2. Design embeddable badges in Canva with: your brand + location + category
3. Generate embeddable HTML code (use ChatGPT to generate the embed snippet)
4. Email businesses: "We chose you as one of the top 5. Here's a badge for your site"
5. Badge links back to your awards page = backlink
6. Get creative with categories: "Best mom-and-pop business", "Best customer service", "Best deals"
7. Add internal links on the award page so badge-generated backlink equity flows to other pages

### Badge Strategy Scaling
- Create awards pages for every major city you cover
- Design 3-5 badge variants (different colors, sizes) to increase adoption
- Follow up with businesses that don't respond -- many need a reminder
- Track badge adoption rate -- expect 10-20% of contacted businesses to embed
- Each embedded badge is a permanent, contextual backlink from a topically relevant local site
- Refresh award lists annually to create new outreach opportunities

## Purchasing Backlinks

### Agencies Referenced by Instructors
- **Get Me Links** -- guest posts and niche edits
- **Rhino Ranks** -- niche edits, affordable tier
- **The Hoth** -- full-service link building packages

### Risk Spectrum
- Zero risk: HARO, guest posting with genuine content, broken link building
- Low risk: Niche edits on topically relevant, trafficked pages with content updates
- Medium risk: Purchased guest posts on vetted sites
- Higher risk: Bulk niche edits without quality vetting, link packages from unknown vendors
- Avoid: Fiverr backlinks, PBN links from unknown networks, link farms

### Free High-Authority Platforms (Gorrono)
- Spotify for Podcasters (create a podcast listing with links)
- Mozilla Add-ons (create a simple browser extension)
- Other high-DA platforms that accept user-generated content with profile links

### Event Submission Backlinks (Local Context)
- Local event submission sites are "hidden gems" -- high DA, geo-specific platforms

- Find targets: "[City Name]" + "submit an event" / "events calendar" / "add event"
- Event title = exact same as GBP Event Post

## Outreach Tracking & Organization

### Spreadsheet Columns for Link Outreach Tracking
| Column | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Target URL | The page you want a link on |
| Target DA/DR | Domain authority of the target site |
| Contact Name | Editor, webmaster, or site owner |
| Contact Email | Primary email address |
| Date Contacted | When first email was sent |
| Follow-up 1 | Date of first follow-up |
| Follow-up 2 | Date of second follow-up |
| Status | Pending, Replied, Accepted, Declined, No Response |
| Link Type | Guest post, niche edit, link swap, broken link |
| Cost | Payment amount if applicable |
| Link Live Date | When the link was published |
| Anchor Text | The anchor text used for your link |
| Dofollow/Nofollow | Link attribute status |

### Monthly Outreach Cadence
- Week 1: Prospect 50 new targets, send initial pitches
- Week 2: Follow up on Week 1 pitches, prospect 25 more
- Week 3: Follow up on Week 2, send final follow-ups for Week 1
- Week 4: Review results, update tracking sheet, plan next month
- Target: 5-10 secured links per month from 100+ outreach emails
