# White-Hat Link Building Strategies

## HARO Mastery Workflow (Authority Hacker)

### Prospecting
- Sign up for HARO (now Connectively) and Featured.com
- Set up daily email alerts filtered by your niche categories
- Select "Master HARO" in preferences to get ALL categories (their categorization is poor)
- Fill out profile completely (reporters use this to evaluate you)
- Scan each daily digest for relevant queries within 2 hours of receipt (speed matters)
- Prioritize queries from high-DA publications (DR 60+)
- Look for queries matching your genuine expertise areas

### HARO Email Schedule
- Emails arrive at 5:35am, 12:35pm, 5:35pm EST, Monday through Friday
- Set aside 30-60 minutes daily at the same time for responses
- **Primary queries:** Directly related to your niche (Ctrl+F for niche keywords)
- **Secondary queries:** Anything you can reasonably answer from personal experience
- Skip queries with passed deadlines
- Skip queries requiring specific credentials you lack

### HARO Pitch Formula (Step-by-Step)
1. **Personalize greeting** -- use the reporter's actual name ("Hey Chrissy")
2. **Brief intro** -- name, site, credibility statement (1-2 sentences max)
3. **Bold headline** -- 5-6 words summarizing your specific point (this is KEY -- "significant uptick in successful responses when we started adding this")
4. **Core answer** -- 2-3 concise, quotable sentences directly answering the query
5. **Supporting example or data point** -- a specific number, case study, or anecdote
6. **Polite sign-off** -- "Hope this is useful, let me know if you have questions"
7. **Website URL in signature** -- not in the body of the pitch

### Success Metrics
- Target 20% response-to-placement success rate (Authority Hacker benchmark)
- ~40% of successful placements do NOT notify you -- many journalists publish without telling you
- Monthly audit: search Google for your name + quotes you submitted to find placements you missed
- Track link quality: DA of publishing site, dofollow vs nofollow, anchor text used
- Numbers game: many decent answers consistently beats one perfect answer per week

### Finding Unnotified HARO Links
- Ahrefs > Site Explorer > Your domain > Backlinks > New > Last 30 days > Exact URL (homepage)
- Look for anchor text containing your name + company name
- Google search: "Your Name" + "Your Website" > Tools > Last week
- Check Google Alerts for your name and brand name

### HARO Alternatives
- **Featured.com:** Similar model, different submission flow. Generally lower DA publications but higher acceptance rates. Good for building volume.
- **SourceBottle.com:** Not as effective but useful for scaling outreach volume
- **Qwoted:** Growing platform connecting journalists with expert sources
- **Help a B2B Writer:** Niche HARO alternative focused on B2B content

## Guest Posting Process

### Prospecting
- Search operators: "[niche] + write for us", "[niche] + guest post", "[niche] + contribute"
- Mix keywords: main topic + subtopics + broader terms (dog, puppy, pet, Labrador)
- Mix search phrases: "write for us" / "submit a post" / "guest post" / "contribute" / "writing guidelines"
- Add modifiers: blog, site, resource, article, tips
- Use Ahrefs Content Explorer to find sites accepting guest posts in your niche
- Check competitor backlinks for guest post sources (look for author bios with links)
- Install Ahrefs Chrome toolbar to see DR in search results
- Vet sites: minimum DR 30, real organic traffic, active publishing schedule
- Target: DR equal to or higher than your own site
- Build a prospect list of 30+ sites minimum per batch

### Guest Post Outreach Sequence
- **Email 1:** Pitch 2-3 topic ideas with brief outlines (personalize to the site's content gaps). Follow their specific submission instructions.
- Wait 3-5 business days
- **Email 2:** Follow up with a value-add (share their content on social, reference a recent article of theirs)
- Wait 5-7 business days
- **Email 3:** Final follow-up with a different angle or new topic suggestions
- Stop after 3 emails -- do not become a nuisance

### Guest Post Content Standards
- Quality must match or exceed your own site's content
- Higher DR sites require better content -- invest proportionally
- Use a specific guest post content template (different from info content templates)
- Need at least 10 articles published on your own site first (proves writing quality)
- Show examples of previous guest posts in your pitch (builds credibility)

### Negotiation
- Standard: 1 dofollow link in the body + 1 in author bio
- If they push back on dofollow: accept nofollow from high-DA sites (still valuable under hint directive)
- Never pay for placement on sites that claim to be "editorial" -- that's a paid link, not a guest post
- Push for deep links when possible (to specific pages you're trying to rank)
- Most guest posts default to homepage link in bio -- homepage links are fine for new sites building domain authority

### Post-Placement Optimization (Floate)
- After guest post is accepted, internally link OTHER existing pages on the host site to your new guest post URL
- This passes additional internal link equity from the host site, making the link more powerful
- Many site owners will add an internal link if you identify a relevant existing page and ask politely

## Skyscraper Technique

### Process (Detailed)
1. Find high-performing content in your niche (sort by backlinks in Ahrefs Content Explorer)
2. Identify the top-linked piece (100+ referring domains ideal)
3. Analyze WHY people linked to it -- what value did it provide?
4. Create a significantly better version (more comprehensive, more current, better design)
5. Compile a list of every site linking to the original (Ahrefs backlinks report)
6. Find contact information for each linker (email, contact form, social)
7. Send personalized outreach noting the original, explaining your improvements
8. Follow up once after 5-7 days

### What "Better" Means
- More comprehensive (cover subtopics the original missed)
- More current (updated data, recent examples, current-year statistics)
- Better formatted (visuals, infographics, tables, comparison charts)
- More practical (templates, checklists, downloadable resources)
- More depth (expert quotes, case studies, original data)

### Expected Conversion
- 5-10% of contacted linkers will add your link
- Higher conversion when original content is outdated or broken
- Even higher conversion when the original page returns a 404 or has been deleted

### Skyscraper Content Types That Attract Links
- Definitive guides (5,000+ word comprehensive resources)
- Original research and data studies
- Infographics with unique data visualization
- Free tools and calculators
- Curated resource lists with editorial commentary

## Broken Link Building

### Process
1. Find resource pages in your niche (search: "[niche] + resources", "[niche] + useful links")
2. Run through Ahrefs or Check My Links extension to find broken outbound links
3. Check Wayback Machine to see what the broken page originally contained
4. Create content that replaces the broken resource (match the original's scope and value)
5. Contact the page owner: "I noticed [broken link] on your [page]. I have a similar resource at [URL] that might be a good replacement."

### Tools
- Check My Links (Chrome extension) for quick broken link detection on individual pages
- Ahrefs Broken Link Checker for bulk analysis across entire domains
- Wayback Machine to see what the broken page originally contained
- Screaming Frog for crawling competitor sites to find broken outbound links at scale

### Outreach Template for Broken Links
Subject: Broken link on your [Page Title]
Body: Note the specific broken link (include the anchor text and URL). Offer your replacement resource. Explain how your content covers the same topic. Keep it short -- you are doing them a favor by flagging the broken link.

## Featured.com

- Similar to HARO but with a different submission flow
- Journalists post specific questions; you submit expert answers
- Generally lower DA publications than HARO but higher acceptance rates
- Good for building volume of editorial links
- Lower time investment per pitch than HARO

## Cross-Niching Strategy (Authority Hacker)

- Find related but non-competing niches that share audience overlap
- Example: a fitness site can build links from nutrition sites, sports equipment sites, wellness blogs
- Create content at the intersection of niches (e.g., "nutrition for marathon runners" appeals to both fitness and nutrition audiences)
- Cross-niche links have high topical relevance without direct competition
- Helps diversify your link profile beyond a single topic vertical

## Link Quality Assessment Framework

### Five Evaluation Factors
1. **Domain Rating (DR):** Higher is better, but DR alone is a vanity metric
2. **Topical Relevance:** The linking page should be about a related topic
3. **Page Freshness:** Recently updated pages pass more value than stale ones
4. **Page Authority:** Check referring domains to the specific linking page, not just the domain
5. **Bad Neighborhoods:** Avoid links from spam, adult, gambling, pharma sites (unless that's your niche)

### Red Flags
- DR 80+ site with zero organic traffic = likely a link farm
- Page with 5,000+ outbound links = link equity diluted to nothing
- Site monetized entirely through link sales = Google likely knows
- Link from page with no topical connection to your content

### Spammy Backlink Tells (Frey Chu)
- Very high linked domains count on the page
- Site looks like a link vendor
- Zero page traffic despite high DR
- Sketchy URL structure or foreign language mismatch

### The DR Vanity Metric Warning
- Frey Chu ranked #1 for "Amazon bin store near me" with DR 0.1 and zero backlinks -- pure on-page SEO
- High DR means nothing without page traffic and topical relevance
- If spending $100 on a backlink, spending $150-200 for a quality one is better value

## Link Building Checkpoint (Authority Hacker)

### Goal: First 20 Links Before Commercial Content
- Mix of HARO + guest posts + skyscraper
- Track all links in respective tracking spreadsheets
- Monitor DR growth in Ahrefs
- Only start writing commercial/money content after 20 links are secured
- This establishes baseline domain authority needed to rank for commercial terms
