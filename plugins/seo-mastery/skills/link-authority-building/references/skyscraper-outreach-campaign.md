# Skyscraper Outreach: Reverse-Engineering Competitor Links

## What Is Skyscraper?
Find content ranking for your target keywords → see who links to it → create better version → outreach to linkers with "we made better content".

**Why It Works**: Linkers already know the topic is valuable; you just need to convince them yours is better.

**ROI**: Highest quality links, best conversion rate (20-30% if done right)

---

## Part 1: Research & Opportunity Identification

### Step 1: Find Target Keywords
1. List 5-10 keywords you want to rank for
2. Search each in Google
3. Note the top-ranking article (position #1-3)

**Example**: "link building strategies" → top result is "Ultimate Guide to Link Building" on AuthorityBlog.com

### Step 2: Analyze Top Article

**In Ahrefs**:
1. Enter the article URL
2. Look at "Referring Domains" section
3. Note: Which websites link to this article?
4. Filter by Domain Rating (show DR 30+)
5. Export list of linking domains

**Expected**: 50-100 sites linking to top article

**These are your targets**: Everyone linking to the old article might link to yours.

### Step 3: Quality Filter
From the 50-100 linkers:

**Keep These**:
- DR 20+ (has some authority)
- Relevant niche (related industry)
- Actual blog/site (not spam directory)
- Recent links (linked within last 12 months)

**Remove These**:
- DR <20 (waste of time)
- Unrelated niche (no relevance)
- Spam sites (directories, PBNs)
- Comment spam (not real links)

**Result**: Usually narrows to 20-30 quality targets

## Part 2: Create the Skyscraper Content

### Make It Objectively Better

**Dimensions of "Better"**:

1. **Longer**: Add 30-50% more content
   - Old article: 2500 words
   - Your article: 3500 words
   - Add: New sections, more examples, more data

2. **More Comprehensive**: Cover more angles
   - Old article: 5 strategies
   - Your article: 7 strategies
   - Add: New strategies, emerging tactics, case studies

3. **More Recent**: Update for 2024
   - Old article mentions "Google Penguin update"
   - Your article: Includes 2024 algorithm changes
   - Replace: Outdated tools with current tools
   - Add: Recent case studies/data

4. **Better Formatted**: Easier to read
   - More subheadings
   - More bullet points
   - Better infographics
   - Video embeds
   - Interactive elements (tools, checklists)

5. **Original Data**: Your unique research
   - Survey data: "We surveyed 1000 companies"
   - Case studies: "Here's how we improved X by 150%"
   - Tools: Interactive calculator, checklist
   - Statistics: New statistics not in original

### Content Structure Template

```
TITLE: "[Original Article Title] + Your Angle/Year]"

Example:
Original: "5 Link Building Strategies That Work"
Yours: "7 Link Building Strategies That Work in 2024 (+ 150% ROI Data)"

INTRODUCTION:
- Hook: Acknowledge original article was good
- Your angle: "But here's what's changed in 2024..."
- Preview: "We added 2 new strategies + updated data"
- CTA: "Let's dive in"

BODY:
- Include all original 5 strategies (+ improvements)
- Add 2 new strategies
- Update all case studies to recent (2023-2024)
- Add new data from your research
- Include interactive element (calculator, template, checklist)

CONCLUSION:
- Restate main benefit
- CTA: Download free checklist, try tool, etc.
```

### Optimization for Linkers
Include elements that make linkers want to reference:
- **Infographics** (people love sharing visuals)
- **Statistics/Data** (original research = linkable)
- **Checklists** (downloadable, shareable)
- **Tools** (interactive calculators, templates)
- **Case Studies** (real examples with results)

## Part 3: Outreach Campaign

### Email Template (Critical)

**Subject**: Keep it personal + specific

Bad: "Great content idea"
Good: "Great article on link building strategies - I created an updated version"

### Email Body

```
Subject: Updated Guide to Link Building Strategies [BlogName]

Hi [Author Name],

I really appreciated your article "[Original Title]" that ranks for "link building strategies". You covered some solid fundamentals.

Since your article published in [year], we've noticed three gaps:

1. You didn't cover [New Strategy] - which is actually the fastest-growing technique
2. Data has changed significantly - we ran a survey of 500 marketers and found [surprising stat]
3. [Old tool you mentioned] has been replaced by [newer tool]

So we created an updated guide that covers:
✓ All your original strategies (with 2024 updates)
✓ 2 new strategies with case studies
✓ New survey data + interactive calculator
✓ Updated tools list

Here's the link: [your article]

I thought your readers might find the updated version valuable. No need to link, just wanted to share in case you want to reference it.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Your Title]
[Your Website]
```

### Outreach Best Practices

1. **Personalize**: Use their name, mention their article by title
2. **Specific improvements**: Don't just say "better"; list what's better
3. **Lead with value**: "Your readers would find this useful"
4. **Don't ask for link directly**: Frame as helpful share
5. **Make it easy**: Include link, no homework for them
6. **Timing**: Email when article published (or coincide with news/update)

## Part 5: Conversion Optimization

### Why Most People Don't Link
Common reasons for rejection:
1. "Looks promotional" — Frame as update, not promotion
2. "Too busy" — Make it effortless (link already there)
3. "Don't want to remove old link" — Suggest updating old link, not removing
4. "No relevance to my audience" — Show specific value to THEIR readers

### Increasing Your Link Rate

**Tactic 1: Offer Different Value Props**
- First email: "Updated with new data"
- Second email: "Interactive tool"
- Third email: "Original case study"

One of these usually resonates.

**Tactic 2: Target Different Content Types**
- Blog posts linking to article (smaller audience)
- Industry reports (huge authority)
- Roundup articles (actively looking for content to link)

Roundup articles are GOLD — they're specifically looking to link to great content.

**Tactic 3: Create Linkable Asset**
Instead of just article, create downloadable:
- Checklist (people share)
- Templates (people use + link)
- Infographic (people embed)
- Tool (people bookmark + share)

Tangible assets convert better than articles.

**Tactic 4: Identify Low-Hanging Fruit**
In your linker list:
- New sites (more active in outreach)
- Sites with guest post sections (already open to links)
- Sites by solo bloggers (more likely to engage)

Reach out to these first. High conversion rate = momentum.

## Part 7: Advanced Skyscraper Tactics

### Tactic 1: Automated Outreach
Use tools like Mailshake to automate follow-up sequence:
1. Set up 3-4 email sequence
2. Tool sends automatically
3. You monitor opens/responses
4. Personalize on high-engagement responses

Saves time; increases consistency.

### Tactic 2: Create Infographic
Commission designer to create infographic from your data:
- Cost: $200-500
- Value: High (people love sharing visuals)
- Conversion: 30-40% higher link rate
- ROI: Easily pays for itself in 2-3 links

### Tactic 3: Press Release
Announce your updated article:
- "Company X Releases Updated Guide to Link Building Strategies"
- Distribution: Press release networks
- Result: Journalist coverage = high authority links

### Tactic 4: Find Linking Patterns
Analyze: Why do people link to the original?
- Cite for statistics?
- Case study reference?
- Best practices list?
- Unique methodology?

Mirror that pattern in your article + outreach.

## Part 8: Content Refresh vs. New Article

### When to Skyscraper (New Article):
- Original article still ranks #1-3
- High search volume (10K+ searches/month)
- Multiple linking opportunities (100+ linkers)
- You have significantly better angle

### When to Update Existing Article (Cheaper):
- Your article already ranks but under top 10
- Good article but outdated
- Lower search volume (<5K/month)
- Don't need fresh links, just better ranking

**Rule of Thumb**: Skyscraper is most effective for high-traffic keywords where original is still strong.

## Key Takeaway
Skyscraper is best ROI link building strategy because:
1. Targets proven audiences (people already link to the topic)
2. Clear value proposition (yours is demonstrably better)
3. High conversion rate (20-30% if content is good)
4. Natural link context (they choose to link, not paid)
5. Sustainable (builds authority over time)
