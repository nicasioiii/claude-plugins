# /build-pitch-deck -- Brand Partnership Pitch Deck Builder

## INTAKE QUESTIONS (Ask All Before Building)

### Question 1: Creator Stage
**Where are you in the brand partnership journey?**

| Choice | Context |
|--------|---------|
| A) Never done a brand deal -- building my first pitch deck | Full pitch deck build from scratch |
| B) Done a few deals informally -- need a professional deck | Formalize existing experience into structured deck |
| C) Active brand partnerships -- updating/improving existing deck | Audit and optimize current deck |
| D) Small following (<10K) -- want to start with UGC | Route to UGC section, build UGC portfolio first |

### Question 2: Platform & Content Format
**Where do you create content and what does it look like?**
- Primary platform(s) and follower count on each
- Typical content format (short-form video, long-form, text, carousels)
- Publishing frequency (how many posts per week/month)
- Average views or engagement per post

### Question 3: Audience Definition
**Who is your audience? Be specific.**
- Demographics: age range, gender split, location
- Psychographics: interests, goals, values
- Can you name 2-3 distinct audience segments?
- What transformation does your content provide them?

### Question 4: Brand Deal Experience
**What is your brand partnership history?**
- A) No previous brand deals
- B) 1-3 deals, mostly informal or gifted
- C) 5-10 deals with some paid partnerships
- D) 10+ deals with established rate card
- E) Currently represented by an agent or manager

### Question 5: Pricing Context
**Help me set your pricing floor:**
- What are your total monthly expenses (rough estimate)?
- How many brand integrations per month are you willing to do?
- What is the lowest amount you would accept for a single integration?
- Have any brands already told you what they would pay?

## GENERATION PROCESS

### Step 1: Brand Narrative Setup
1. Load `brand-partnerships` SKILL.md
2. Load `ref-pitch-deck.md`
3. Gather or confirm:
   - Mission statement (from brand-foundation or create one inline)
   - 3-5 competitive advantage bullets
   - Unique positioning angle (what makes them different from other creators in this niche)

### Step 2: Audience Segment Definition
1. Define 3 hyper-specific audience segments with names
2. For each segment: who they are, what they care about, which platforms they use
3. Apply the specificity principle: specific audience = rates determined by quality (defensible); general audience = rates determined by size (replaceable)

### Step 3: Social Proof Collection
1. Guide user to identify best proof elements:
   - DMs or comments showing audience transformation
   - Testimonials or quotes from followers
   - Specific engagement metrics that demonstrate audience quality
   - Any press mentions, features, or notable collaborations
2. Map one proof element per audience segment

### Step 4: Integration Product Design
1. Define their two products:
   - **Dedicated video**: Full video tailored around a brand
   - **Integration**: 60-90 second brand segment within regular content
2. Describe what each product looks like in their specific content style
3. Identify 2-3 sample content links (their best work)

### Step 5: Pricing Calculation
1. Apply the Floor Price Calculator:
   - Monthly expenses + 40% margin = monthly minimum
   - Divide by integrations per month = floor price per integration
   - Round up to clean go-to-market number
2. Build package pricing:
   - Single integration price
   - 3-episode package (10% discount)
   - 6-episode annual (20% discount)
3. Factor in commission assumptions: agent (10%) + lawyer (5%) = 15%
4. Apply fill rate assumption: plan for 50% of inventory actually selling

### Step 6: Inventory Mapping
1. Calculate available-to-sell inventory:
   - Total upload slots per month
   - Subtract breaks, non-brandable content, personal projects
   - Result = available brand deal slots
2. Create quarterly availability overview

### Step 7: Assemble 7-Page Deck Outline
1. Write content for each of the 7 pages following the E03b structure
2. Include specific copy, data points, and layout recommendations for each page
3. Add design direction notes consistent with their brand identity

## OUTPUT FORMAT

Deliver a complete Pitch Deck Package containing:

1. **Page 1 -- Cover**: Channel name, branding direction, design notes
2. **Page 2 -- Mission & Overview**: Mission statement, 3-5 competitive advantage bullets, positioning narrative
3. **Page 3 -- Audience Segments**: 3 named segments with descriptions and platform breakdown
4. **Page 4 -- Social Proof**: Proof elements mapped to audience segments, suggested quote formatting
5. **Page 5 -- Show Description**: Follower count, analytics snapshot, format description, frequency, 3 sample links
6. **Page 6 -- Integration Details**: Integration format description, sample integration links, previous brand partners list
7. **Page 7 -- Pricing & Packaging**: Single integration rate, 3-episode package rate, 6-episode annual rate, date-connected pricing note
8. **Rate Card Summary**: Clean pricing table for quick reference
9. **Inventory Overview**: Quarterly availability with open/sold/break status
10. **Inbound Response Template**: Pre-written email response to attach deck to brand inquiries
11. **North Star Brands List**: Template for identifying ideal brand partners (aspiration, organic-use, proven-budget)
12. **Next Steps**: "Send your deck to target brands" and "Use /write-script to craft integration scripts"
