# /film-checklist -- Pre-Production Planning & Filming Checklist

## INTAKE QUESTIONS (Ask All Before Building Checklist)

### Question 1: Content Type
**What are you filming?**

| Choice | Filming Approach |
|--------|-----------------|
| A) Talking-head (single camera, you speaking to camera) | Standard setup, warmup focus, B-roll planning |
| B) Tutorial / How-to (demonstrating something) | Multi-angle consideration, B-roll of process |
| C) Vlog / Lifestyle (day-in-the-life) | Run-and-gun setup, B-roll throughout the day |
| D) Interview / Conversation (two people) | Two-shot framing, dual audio, lighting for two |
| E) Product showcase / Review | Lighting for product, close-up B-roll, detail shots |
| F) Batch session (multiple videos in one sitting) | Wardrobe changes, script stack, energy management |

### Question 2: Platform Target
**What platform(s) is this for?**

| Choice | Format Implications |
|--------|-------------------|
| A) Instagram Reels / TikTok / Shorts (short-form vertical) | 9:16, 30fps, fast pacing, B-roll heavy |
| B) YouTube long-form (horizontal) | 16:9, 24-30fps, higher production, story pacing |
| C) Both short-form and long-form | Film horizontal, reframe vertical in post |
| D) LinkedIn video | Can be either format, authenticity valued |

### Question 3: Current Equipment
**What equipment do you have?**

| Choice | Setup Tier |
|--------|-----------|
| A) Phone only | Tier 1: Phone + natural light |
| B) Phone + ring light + mic | Tier 2: Essential upgrades |
| C) Dedicated camera + lighting + mic | Tier 3-4: Pro setup |
| D) Full studio setup | Tier 5-6: Professional |
| E) Not sure what I need (help me figure it out) | Equipment recommendation mode |

### Question 4: Number of Videos
**How many videos are you planning to film in this session?**
- 1 single video
- 2-3 videos
- 4-6 videos (batch session)
- 7+ videos (power batch)

### Question 5: Script Status
**Are your scripts or outlines ready?**
- Yes, fully scripted
- Partial -- outlines or bullet points ready
- No -- I need to figure out what to say
- Unscripted -- I plan to speak off the cuff

## OUTPUT FORMAT

After collecting answers, generate the following deliverable:

### Section 1: Equipment Checklist
Customized to their equipment tier:
- [ ] Camera/phone (charged, storage cleared)
- [ ] Lighting (type based on their setup)
- [ ] Audio (mic type, tested)
- [ ] Tripod / phone mount
- [ ] Background / set preparation
- [ ] Wardrobe (number of outfit changes for batch)
- [ ] Props (if applicable for content type)
- [ ] Scripts / outlines (printed or on teleprompter)
- [ ] B-roll shot list (see Section 3)

### Section 2: Filming Day Schedule
Time-blocked schedule based on number of videos and content type:

**Example for 4-video batch session:**

| Time Block | Activity | Notes |
|------------|----------|-------|
| 0:00-0:15 | Setup | Lighting, camera, audio, background |
| 0:15-0:25 | Warmup | Breathing, tongue twisters, energy calibration |
| 0:25-0:50 | Video 1 | [Topic]. Multiple takes of hook. |
| 0:50-0:55 | Quick change | Wardrobe swap, angle adjustment |
| 0:55-1:20 | Video 2 | [Topic]. |
| 1:20-1:25 | Break | Water, vocal rest, review scripts |
| 1:25-1:50 | Video 3 | [Topic]. |
| 1:50-1:55 | Quick change | Wardrobe swap |
| 1:55-2:20 | Video 4 | [Topic]. |
| 2:20-2:45 | B-roll session | Dedicated B-roll capture (see shot list) |
| 2:45-3:00 | Organize | Label files, backup, log what was filmed |

### Section 3: B-Roll Shot List
Platform-appropriate B-roll suggestions based on their content type:
- Minimum 10 B-roll shot ideas specific to their niche/topic
- Duration guide: 3-5 seconds per clip (body), under 1 second per clip (hook)
- Dynamic shots (movement, action) prioritized over static

### Section 4: Pre-Filming Warmup Card
Printable warmup routine:
1. Deep breath: inhale, hold 6 sec, exhale 8 sec (repeat 3x)
2. Tongue twisters (2-3 rounds)
3. Hand/gesture warmup (shake out tension)
4. Visualize camera as younger self or close friend
5. Energy check: amplify natural personality one notch
6. Final deep breath before pressing record

### Section 5: Camera Settings Card
Quick-reference settings based on their platform target:
- Resolution, frame rate, aspect ratio, white balance recommendation

### Section 6: Post-Session Checklist
- [ ] All footage labeled with naming convention
- [ ] Backup initiated (local + cloud if applicable)
- [ ] Log which scripts were filmed + number of takes
- [ ] Note any issues for editing (bad audio sections, lighting changes)
- [ ] Schedule editing session

## FOLLOW-UP RECOMMENDATIONS

After delivering the checklist:

1. **If scripts are not ready:** "Would you like to use /write-script to prepare your scripts before filming day?"
2. **If equipment guidance needed:** Reference the equipment tiers from film-video skill to make specific recommendations.
3. **After filming is complete:** "Ready to edit? Your footage is organized and ready for post-production. The edit-video skill can guide your editing process."

---

## RULES FOR THIS COMMAND

- Always generate a SPECIFIC, actionable checklist -- not generic advice
- Tailor equipment checklist to their actual gear level
- Time estimates should be realistic (not optimistic)
- B-roll suggestions must be specific to their content niche, not generic
- If they mention being nervous on camera, emphasize the warmup section
- If batch filming, include outfit/angle change notes between videos
- Always include the post-session organization checklist
