## STEP 1: POSITION -- Discovery Prompts

### Information Advantage Interview
**Purpose:** Discover your unique expertise and positioning opportunities.

```
I want you to interview me to discover my "Information Advantage" -- the specialized knowledge I have from my unique combination of jobs, hobbies, goals, and experiences.

Ask me these questions one at a time, waiting for my response before moving to the next:

1. What are the 5 jobs or roles you have held in the last 2-5 years?
2. What are 5 significant wins or achievements from that period?
3. What are 5 problems you have been paid to solve?
4. What are 5 skills you have built that others find valuable?
5. What are 5 "odd" or unique experiences that shaped your perspective?

After I answer all 5, analyze my responses and identify:
- 3 potential positioning angles (audience + problem + result)
- Overlapping themes across my answers
- The expertise I likely cannot see because I am too close to it
- 5 content topics I could write about with genuine authority
```

### Positioning Consultant
**Purpose:** Generate comprehensive positioning options.

```
Based on the following information about my expertise and experience:

[Paste your Information Advantage Interview results]

Act as a positioning consultant and generate:
1. 30 potential niche positioning statements (format: "I help [audience] solve [problem] so they can [result]")
2. 20 related industries/fields that would find my expertise valuable
3. 7 LinkedIn headline variations (each under 220 characters)
4. A "Professional Constellation" map showing how one niche position opens doors to adjacent fields
5. The single strongest positioning angle based on specificity + market demand
```

## STEP 2: PROFILE -- Optimization Prompts

### Banner Text Generator
**Purpose:** Create 8 banner text variations.

```
Write 8 LinkedIn banner text options for someone positioned as: [your positioning statement]

Use these 8 templates:
1. Category Authority: [Industry] Expert | [Differentiator] | [CTA]
2. Results & Credibility: [Metric] | [Experience] | [CTA]
3. Social Proof Focus: Featured in [Publications] | [Success Quote] | [CTA]
4. Method/Framework: The [System Name] | [Core Benefit] | [CTA]
5. Problem Agitation: [Pain Point Question]? | [Promise] | [CTA]
6. Authority Positioning: [Expertise] Specialist | [Philosophy] | [CTA]
7. Transformation Story: From [Before] to [After] | Now I show [audience] how | [CTA]
8. Strapline: Simple memorable phrase

Rules: 10-15 words maximum per banner. Must complement the bio, not repeat it. Include specific numbers/metrics when possible.
```

### About Section Writer
**Purpose:** Draft About section using 7 psychological frameworks.

```
Write 7 variations of a LinkedIn About section for someone positioned as: [your positioning statement]

Use these 7 frameworks (250-300 words each):
1. Problem-Agitation-Solution: Problem > agitate (FOMO, costs) > solution + social proof + CTA
2. Achievement-Realisation-Results: Authority marker > personal pivot > results you create + CTA
3. Story-Driven Relatability: Backstory > "I have been there" > how you help > social proof + CTA
4. Contrarian Insight: Common assumption > flip it > fresh perspective + proof + CTA
5. WHO-WHAT-HOW: Who you help > what you help achieve > how (process) + proof + CTA
6. Future Pacing: Desirable future > gap between now and then > your method as bridge + CTA
7. Authority-First Showcase: Big wins > unique insight > tangible client benefits + CTA

Style: Write like explaining your work to someone at a barbecue, not pitching them. Conversational, specific, credible.
```

### Skills Section Optimizer
**Purpose:** Generate 20-25 skills across 4 strategic buckets.

```
Generate a LinkedIn Skills section for someone positioned as: [your positioning statement]

Organize 20-25 skills into 4 buckets:
1. Core Expertise (5-7 skills): what I am best known for
2. Adjacent Skills (5-7 skills): reinforcing skills (storytelling, leadership, etc.)
3. Industry Keywords (5-7 skills): terms prospects/clients would search
4. Proof/Outcome Skills (5-7 skills): results I create (lead generation, organic growth, etc.)

Top 5 must match my headline. Every skill must be specific, search-friendly, and relevant to my positioning.
```

## STEP 3: PLAN -- Content Strategy Prompts

### Brand Identity Builder
**Purpose:** Interactive walkthrough to define brand identity.

```
Guide me through building my brand identity for social media content. Ask me questions in this order:

1. Audience & Target Market: Who specifically do I serve? Demographics, psychographics, pain points?
2. Brand Personality: What 3 adjectives describe how I want to come across?
3. Visual Direction: What look/feel am I going for? Reference brands I like? Colors I prefer or dislike?

After gathering my answers, provide:
- Color palette recommendation (3 HEX codes)
- Font pairing recommendation (headline + body)
- Design recommendations for posts, carousels, and banners
- 5 moodboard search keywords
```

### Content Ideas Mega-Prompt
**Purpose:** Generate hundreds of content ideas using the 5-variable multiplication system.

```
Generate a comprehensive content idea bank using this system:

My content pillars: [list 3-5 pillars]
My audience archetypes: [list 3-5 audience segments]
My positioning: [positioning statement]

For EACH pillar, generate ideas using all 10 Magical Multipliers:
1. Tips -- X tips for [audience] to [outcome]
2. Stats -- X stats that prove [insight]
3. Steps -- X steps to [achieve result]
4. Lessons -- X lessons I learned from [experience]
5. Benefits -- X benefits of [approach/tool]
6. Reasons -- X reasons why [contrarian take]
7. Mistakes -- X mistakes [audience] makes with [topic]
8. Examples -- X examples of [concept] in action
9. Questions -- X questions to ask yourself about [topic]
10. Personal Stories -- A story about [topic] from my experience

Target: generate at minimum 50 unique content ideas organized by pillar and multiplier.
```

### 30-Day Content Calendar Generator
**Purpose:** Create a strategic monthly content plan.

```
Create a 30-day content calendar based on:

Pillars: [list]
Audience archetypes: [list]
Sub-topics: [list]
Posting frequency: [X] posts per week

Rotation logic:
- Day 1: Pillar 1 > Day 2: Pillar 2 > Day 3: Pillar 3 > repeat
- Rotate through all 10 Magical Multipliers without repetition until all used
- Rotate through audience archetypes evenly
- Mix content buckets: Futurism (where industry is headed), Your Story (personal lessons), Actionable Advice (practical guidance)

For each day include:
- Content pillar
- Content bucket (Futurism / Story / Actionable)
- Magical Multiplier angle
- Target audience archetype
- Sub-topic
- Post title/hook suggestion
- Suggested format (short text, long text, carousel, video)
```

## STEP 4: WRITE -- Content Creation Prompts

### Proven Hook Generator (16 Formats)
**Purpose:** Generate hooks using all 16 proven LinkedIn formats.

```
Write 16 hook variations for a post about: [topic]
Target audience: [audience]

Use each of these 16 formats exactly once:
1. "If...Then" Algorithm: {X} "If, then" algorithms for {Topic}
2. Brutal But True Story: The first {Time Period} of my {Activity} were brutal...
3. Average Person Is Wrong: The average person thinks {Common Belief}. They are wrong:
4. Curated Opinion: The problem with {Behavior} in your {Area}...
5. One Habit Huge Outcome: The one habit that {Huge Outcome}...
6. How To Post: How to get "{Outcome}" on {Platform} this {Timeframe}
7. What Is It Really About: {Topic} is not about {Misconceptions}. It is how people feel...
8. Belief Changer: At {Age}, I was: [negatives]. My life was {Adjective}. But {Action} changed everything
9. Here Is How I Do It: Here is what I {Action} every day to {Outcome}
10. Stop/Start: Stop {Undesirable} and start {Desired}, here is how
11. Investment In Myself: I paid {Experts} $X,XXX to attend their {Event}. Here are {X} takeaways
12. Truth Nobody Wants: The truth about {Subject} - great for {Initial Benefit}, but that is about it
13. Shortcut: The shortcut to {Outcome} is not: [accepted practices]. It is {Simple Practice}
14. Powerful Assets: {Number} powerful {Content Type} to change your life
15. Misconception Reframe: {Outcome} is not a destination. It is a daily {Action}
16. Ready To Give Up: After {X} {Timeframe} {habit}, I had {Neg1}, {Neg2}, and I was ready to give up

Rules: Each hook must be under 200 characters (LinkedIn first-line rule). Use "you/your" framing where possible.
```

### Hook Optimizer (20 Rewrites)
**Purpose:** Take one hook concept and generate 20 variations.

```
Here is my current hook: [paste hook]

Generate 20 rewrite variations using these approaches:
- 5 rewrites making it more specific (add numbers, names, dates)
- 5 rewrites changing the framing (you vs I, question vs statement, positive vs negative)
- 5 rewrites increasing contrast (bigger gap between expectation and reality)
- 5 rewrites simplifying (fewer words, clearer language, stronger verbs)

Rules:
- Each must be under 200 characters
- Avoid AI-sounding words (transform, revolutionize, unlock, elevate, game-changer)
- Use "you/your" in at least 12 of the 20 variations
- Keep reading level at 7th grade or below
```

### Contrast Hook Generator
**Purpose:** Create hooks using the contrast psychology framework.

```
Topic: [topic]
Audience: [audience]
Common belief they hold: [what most people think]
Contrarian reality: [what you know to be true]

Generate 10 hooks using contrast patterns:
1. Before/After contrast
2. Common Belief vs Truth contrast
3. What Most Do vs What Works contrast
4. Expected vs Unexpected contrast
5. Old Way vs New Way contrast
6. Myth vs Reality contrast
7. Easy Way vs Right Way contrast
8. Quantity vs Quality contrast
9. Short-term vs Long-term contrast
10. Visible vs Hidden contrast

For each, create both a "stated contrast" version (directly name both sides) and an "implied contrast" version (only state the contrarian side).
```

## STEP 5: CREATE -- Visual Content Prompts

### Carousel Optimizer for Gamma
**Purpose:** Convert a LinkedIn post into carousel-ready format.

```
Convert this LinkedIn post into a 10-slide carousel optimized for Gamma.app:

[Paste your LinkedIn post]

Structure:
- Slide 1: Big Promise / Hook (compelling question or statement)
- Slide 2: Stakes (problems if they do not act)
- Slide 3: Example (real-world application)
- Slides 4-7: Framework (clear steps to follow)
- Slide 8: Checklist (common mistakes to avoid)
- Slide 9: Quick Win (one tool to try now)
- Slide 10: Call to Action (next step or engagement question)

Rules: Keep each slide to 3-5 bullet points maximum. Use headers that deliver value even if someone only reads the headers. Maintain a logical flow where each slide builds on the previous.
```

### Infographic Optimizer for Gamma
**Purpose:** Convert a post into infographic format.

```
Convert this content into a single-slide infographic optimized for Gamma.app:

[Paste your content]

The infographic should:
- Have a clear, compelling headline
- Use visual hierarchy (most important info largest/first)
- Include no more than 7 data points or steps
- Use icons or simple graphics to represent each point
- Have a clear CTA or takeaway at the bottom
- Be readable on mobile without zooming
```

### Video Script Generator (But-So Foreshadow)
**Purpose:** Create video scripts using the proven framework.

```
Write a 40-60 second video script on: [topic]
Target audience: [audience]

Use the But-So Foreshadow framework:
1. HOOK (first 3 seconds): Visual hook + opening line + text overlay. Must give immediate context on topic.
2. FORESHADOW (5-7 seconds): Context/setup + promise + stakes. Why should they keep watching?
3. TRANSITION (2-3 seconds): Bridge line. NO generic phrases like "let us get started" -- use a specific transition.
4. BODY (15-30 seconds): Use BUT-SO framework:
   - Situation (what most people do)
   - BUT complication (why it does not work)
   - SO action (what to do instead)
   - BUT twist (unexpected challenge)
   - SO resolution (final answer)
5. CLOSING (3-5 seconds): Payoff + memorable closer + abrupt ending for retention.

Target: 200 words maximum. Include [VISUAL] notes for what should be on screen.
```

## STEP 6: ENGAGE -- Interaction Prompts

### Engagement Question Generator
**Purpose:** Create 5 engagement questions per post.

```
Post topic: [topic]
Post summary: [1-2 sentence summary]

Generate 5 engagement questions (one of each type):
1. Experience-based: "What is the biggest lesson you learned doing X?"
2. Opinion-based: "Do you agree that X is more important than Y?"
3. Tip-sharing: "What is one tool/strategy that made the biggest difference for you?"
4. Prediction: "Where do you think X will be in 5 years?"
5. Quick poll: "Which do you prefer: A or B?"

Rules: Keep questions to 1-2 sentences. Make them easy to answer without overthinking. Phrase so the person answering feels smart or validated.
```

### Comment Assistant
**Purpose:** Draft 3 comment variations for posts in your niche.

```
Here is a post I want to comment on:
[Paste post or summary]

My positioning: [your positioning]

Generate 3 comment variations:
1. Add a concrete example from my experience
2. Add a contrarian or nuanced perspective
3. Ask a thoughtful follow-up question

Rules: 2-3 sentences each. Use contractions and conversational tone. Must add genuine value (never "Great post!"). Include a specific number, tool, or insight.
```

### DM Assistant
**Purpose:** Draft personalized DM messages.

```
Context: [New connection / lead magnet request / warm lead / etc.]
Their background: [What you know about them]
My offer: [What I can help with]

Generate 3 DM variations:
1. Warm welcome (for new connections)
2. Value-first (share a relevant resource)
3. Conversation starter (ask about their goals)

Rules: Under 3 sentences each. Reference something specific about them. Never pitch in the first message. End with a question to continue the conversation.
```

## STEP 7: CONVERT -- Sales Content Prompts

### CTA Plug Generator
**Purpose:** Create CTA variations for content.

```
My offer: [description]
Post topic: [topic]

Generate 4 CTA plugs:
1. Direct: "Full breakdown is in my free guide -- grab it here"
2. Benefits: "If [problem], this [resource] will [solution]"
3. Social proof: "This process [specific result] -- details in the guide"
4. Conversation: "Comment '[keyword]' and I will DM it"

Rules: Each must feel like a natural extension of the post content. Specific and relevant to the topic. Under 2 sentences each.
```

### Pitch In Public Template
**Purpose:** Draft a transparent offer post.

```
My offer: [description]
Who it is for: [target audience]
Main outcome: [result they get]
Price range: [price]
Social proof: [1-2 results]

Write a "Pitch In Public" post following this structure:
1. Problem statement (2-3 sentences)
2. Stakes -- what happens if they do not solve it (2-3 sentences)
3. Your method -- how you help (3-4 sentences)
4. Proof -- specific results (2-3 sentences)
5. Invitation -- clear, no-pressure CTA (1-2 sentences)

Rules: Transparent and authentic, not manipulative. Under 2,000 characters. Include specific numbers. End with clear next step.
```
