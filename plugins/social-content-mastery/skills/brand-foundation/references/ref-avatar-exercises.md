# Avatar & Audience Exercises

## The IVP Deep-Dive Exercise (E01 Mino Lee)

### Core Concept
Your ideal viewer persona = a specific version of your younger self at a specific point in time. Not a vague "target audience" -- a REAL person (you) at a REAL moment (e.g., "October 2021, freshman year of college"). Never say "you guys" in videos -- talk to ONE person. You are a digital mentor to this past version of yourself.

### The 5-Question Exercise (5 answers each = 25 total responses)

**Question 1: Five situations they are terrified of**
Go deep -- the situations you would literally never tell anybody. The more uncomfortable or embarrassing the fear, the more golden it is as content.
- Example fears: ending up poor working a job I hate; being made fun of for being cringe on social media; wasting my life away; never building something meaningful; being stuck in the same place five years from now

**Question 2: Five specific dream outcomes you have NOW that they wish they had**
Focus on outcomes you have actually achieved. Using "what I have now that they wish they had" is stronger than "here's how to do X" because it includes implicit social proof.
- Examples: hitting first $10K month doing something I love; feeling fulfilled while creating content; having creator friends I can actually call; location freedom; waking up excited about work

**Question 3: Five limiting beliefs they hold**
Each limiting belief = at least one content piece that breaks it down.
- Examples: "You need a massive following to make money"; "You have to be naturally funny to grow"; "The market is too saturated"; "I don't have anything unique to say"; "I'm too old/young to start"

**Question 4: Five hacks/tools/solutions they don't know about yet**
The information you WISH you had back then. These become how-to videos.
- Examples: social media marketing strategy, hook scripting techniques, batch content creation, meditation before filming, specific software tools

**Question 5: Five truths that would change their life**
Life truths you now take for granted but would have been mind-blowing back then.
- Examples: "Posting content will change your life"; "Being vulnerable is a hack for connection"; "Brand promotions can pay $1,000+ for a single video"; "Your first 7 videos don't matter"; "Consistency beats talent every time"

### From IVP to Content Strategy
- **50% how-to videos, 50% list videos** (E01 formula)
- How-to: "Here's exactly how to [dream outcome]. [Social proof]. Step 1..."
- List: "3 reasons why you're never going to [fear/problem]" or "Top 3 [solutions] for [dream outcome]"
- Hooks are sourced directly from the IVP exercise: fears become hooks, dreams become promises, limiting beliefs become video topics

## Identity-Emotion-Action Framework (E03b Colin & Samir)

From BuzzFeed CEO Jonah Peretti's framework, adapted for creators:

### Three Columns Per Audience Segment

| Identity (Who) | Emotion (Feel After Watching) | Action (Do After Watching) |
|---|---|---|
| Aspiring Creators | Empowered, inspired | Go create a video |
| Career Creators | Educated, less alone | Connect with others, build better businesses |
| Creator Industry | Empathy toward creators | Help solve problems, bring opportunities |

### How to Use This Framework
1. Define 3 audience segments (see segmentation below)
2. For each segment, fill in all three columns
3. Lines can cross between identities and emotions (career creators also want empowerment)
4. Use this framework to brainstorm video titles: titles should match the identity, emotion, and action you defined
5. **Value = transformation**: the audience invests time, the return on investment is they transform (new emotion + new action)

## Audience Segmentation (E03b Colin & Samir)

### The Process
Break your audience into 3 specific segments with names and short descriptions.

### Segment Design Rules
1. Each segment should be nameable (not "Segment A" -- use descriptive names)
2. For each segment, identify which platforms they inhabit beyond your main platform (Reddit, Discord, newsletters, LinkedIn)
3. A hyper-specific, hyper-engaged audience is a high-quality audience
4. Specificity of content drives specificity of audience, which drives specificity of advertisers
5. General audience = rates determined by size (replaceable); Specific audience = rates determined by quality (defensible)

### Template
| Segment Name | Description | Platforms | Key Need |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Name 1] | [Who they are, where they are in journey] | [Where they hang out] | [Primary pain/desire] |
| [Name 2] | [Who they are, where they are in journey] | [Where they hang out] | [Primary pain/desire] |
| [Name 3] | [Who they are, where they are in journey] | [Where they hang out] | [Primary pain/desire] |

## Target Avatar Exercise (E03a Gusten Sun)

### Quick Avatar Builder
Create a detailed target avatar answering:
- **Demographics**: Age range, gender, location, income level, occupation
- **Psychographics**: Values, beliefs, aspirations, lifestyle
- **Problems**: What keeps them up at night? What have they tried that failed?
- **Desires**: What does their ideal future look like in 6-12 months?

### The Younger Self Shortcut
If struggling to define your avatar: imagine the target avatar is your younger self. All content should be made for this avatar. If the avatar would not like the video, do not make it.

### Principles and Values Declaration
Write down principles and values your brand stands behind. These make your brand unique and rally like-minded people. Strong opinions on controversial topics attract AND repel -- opinions must be genuine, not manufactured for engagement.

## Audience Archetypes for Content (E02)

Beyond demographic segments, define 3-5 audience archetypes for content targeting:

- **By experience level**: Beginner, intermediate, advanced
- **By role**: Individual contributor, manager, executive
- **By industry**: SaaS, e-commerce, consulting
- **By life stage**: Student, early career, established, transitioning
- **By business size**: Startup, SMB, enterprise

Same core message tailored for different archetypes = genuinely different content pieces serving different reader needs.

## FFGA Framework -- Audience Research (E02)

Research your audience's:
- **Fears**: What are they afraid of? What keeps them stuck?
- **Frustrations**: What annoys them about current solutions?
- **Goals**: What are they trying to achieve?
- **Aspirations**: What does their dream outcome look like?

Source this data from:
- Comments on competitor content
- Reddit threads in your niche
- DM conversations with followers
- Community questions (Facebook groups, Discord)
- Survey responses

## Creator Overview / Competitive Advantage (E03b)

### Exercise
Write 3-5 bullets explaining what makes you different from other creators with similar missions:

1. Study 5-10 other creators in your niche
2. Write down each creator's unique value proposition
3. Identify the gap: what do audiences need that nobody is providing?
4. Your competitive advantage sits in that gap
5. Document these bullets -- they control your narrative when pitching to brands, agents, or collaborators

### Questions to Answer
- What perspective do you bring that others cannot?
- What experience or background gives you unique authority?
- What format or style do you use that differentiates your delivery?
- What audience segment do you serve better than anyone?
- What values or principles guide your content that others ignore?

## Beginner Advantage (E01 Mino Lee -- Contrarian)

Your unique advantage may be that you are a BEGINNER. You relate to beginners better than experts do. An expert explaining hooks has forgotten what it felt like to not understand hooks. A beginner documenting their learning journey creates content that resonates with the majority of any audience (who are also beginners or early-stage).

Do not wait until you are an "expert" to start creating. The journey IS the content.
