# Pitch Deck & Brand Deal Deep-Dive Reference

## COMPLETE 7-PAGE PITCH DECK WALKTHROUGH (E03b)

### Design Principles
- Clean, professional design matching your brand identity
- Consistent colors, fonts, and imagery throughout
- Not overly designed -- content and clarity matter more than flashiness
- PDF format for easy email attachment
- Update quarterly with fresh metrics and social proof

### Page 1: Cover Page
**Goal:** Instant brand recognition and professionalism.
- Channel/brand name prominently displayed
- Your professional photo or logo
- Optional: tagline or one-line positioning statement
- No clutter -- white space is your friend

### Page 2: Mission Statement + Creator Overview
**Goal:** Explain WHY you exist and what makes you unique.

Mission statement formula: "My mission is to [VALUE as verb] [AUDIENCE IDENTITY]"
- Example: "To educate and empower the next generation of creators"

Creator Overview: 3-5 bullets covering your competitive advantage.
- What makes you different from other creators with similar audiences?
- Study other creators in your niche, write down their value props, identify the gap you fill
- This section controls your narrative when agents pitch on your behalf

Sample creator overview bullets:
- "Only creator covering [niche] with [unique angle]"
- "[X] years of [relevant professional experience] before becoming a creator"
- "Known for [specific content format] that [specific audience outcome]"
- "[Notable achievement or recognition]"
- "Community of [X] engaged [specific audience descriptor]"

### Page 3: Audience Segments
**Goal:** Prove your audience is specific, engaged, and valuable to advertisers.

Break audience into 3 hyper-specific segments with names and descriptions.

Example (Colin & Samir model):
1. **Aspiring Creators** -- People who want to start creating content but have not yet; consume content about the creator journey; active on YouTube and Instagram
2. **Career Creators** -- Full-time creators building businesses; need tactical advice on revenue, production, team building; active on YouTube, Twitter, Discord
3. **Creator Industry Professionals** -- Agents, brand managers, platform employees; want to understand creator motivations and trends; active on LinkedIn, YouTube

For each segment include:
- Who they are (1-2 sentences)
- What they care about
- Where they hang out (platforms beyond your main one)
- Why they matter to brands

**Key principle:** A hyper-specific, hyper-engaged audience is a high-quality audience. Specificity of content drives specificity of audience, which drives specificity of brands/advertisers. General audience = rates determined by size (replaceable). Specific audience = rates determined by quality (defensible).

### Page 4: Social Proof
**Goal:** Demonstrate that your audience is transforming, not just consuming.

Map one proof element per audience segment:
- **Aspiring Creators:** DM screenshot from new viewer expressing inspiration
- **Career Creators:** Endorsement from established creator
- **Creator Industry:** Quote from industry leader or platform executive

Collection strategy:
- If audience is truly transforming, they will verbalize it in comments, DMs, tweets
- Screenshot and save ALL positive feedback as it arrives
- Constantly update pitch deck with fresh social proof (quarterly minimum)

Types of social proof to include:
- DM screenshots (with permission) showing specific results
- Notable quote or endorsement
- Press mentions or features
- Community growth metrics
- Event attendance or sell-through numbers

### Page 5: Show/Format Description
**Goal:** Give the brand concrete data on what they are buying into.

Include:
- **Subscriber/follower count** on each platform
- **Growth chart** showing upward trajectory (even if gradual)
- **Average views** per video/post (last 30-90 days)
- **Format description:** What does a typical episode/video/post look like? Length, structure, style
- **Frequency:** How often you publish (weekly, 3x/week, daily)
- **Distribution:** List every platform where content goes
- **3 sample links:** Your best-performing or most brand-friendly content

### Page 6: Integration Details
**Goal:** Show what the brand will actually receive and who has trusted you before.

Include:
- Standard integration description: "60-90 second brand segment within our regular [format], delivered in our natural voice and style"
- Sample integrations: links to 2-3 previous brand partnership examples
- List of previous brand partners: logos or names as social proof for the brand's marketing team
- This answers the brand's internal question: "Will their audience receive our message well?"

If you have no previous brand partners:
- Include unpaid sample content featuring products you genuinely use
- Focus on format description and sample quality over partner logos
- Even one successful partnership opens doors to many more

### Page 7: Pricing & Packaging
**Goal:** Make it easy for the brand to say yes.

Include:
- Price per single integration
- 3-episode package price (with slight discount)
- 6-episode or annual package price (with bigger discount)
- Note: "Pricing connected to date of agreement" (gives flexibility to increase)
- Payment terms: net 30 recommended

## SOCIAL PROOF STRATEGY MAPPED TO AUDIENCE SEGMENTS (E03b)

### Collecting Social Proof
- If audience is transforming, they verbalize it in comments, DMs, tweets
- Actively screenshot and save ALL positive audience feedback
- Create a folder system organized by audience segment
- Ask permission before using in pitch deck (most will gladly agree)

### Mapping Proof to Segments
Every audience segment should have its own type of proof:

| Segment | Proof Type | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Beginners/Aspirational | DM from new viewer | "Your video made me finally start my channel" |
| Experienced/Career | Endorsement from peer | "The best resource for [niche] on YouTube" |
| Industry/Professional | Authority quote | Platform exec or industry publication mention |

### Social Proof for Customers (Brands)
- Different from audience proof -- this proves that working with you delivers business results
- List of previous brand partners (logos on page 6)
- If possible: metrics from previous campaigns (click-through rates, conversion data, engagement rates)
- Constantly update with most recent and most impressive partners

## BRAND DEAL FLOOR PRICE CALCULATOR STEP-BY-STEP (E03b)

### Step 1: List Monthly Expenses
Category all recurring costs:
- Rent/housing
- Software subscriptions (editing, scheduling, analytics)
- Freelancers (editor, VA, designer)
- Equipment amortization (divide annual gear cost by 12)
- Utilities, internet, phone
- Food, transportation
- Insurance, health
- Content production costs (travel, sets, props)
- Professional services (lawyer, accountant)

### Step 2: Add Margin
- Recommended starting point: 40%
- This covers taxes, savings, unexpected expenses, and profit
- As you grow, margin percentage can increase

### Step 3: Calculate
- Total expenses + margin = monthly revenue needed from brand deals
- Divide by number of brand deals per month you are willing to do
- Result = your absolute minimum acceptable price (floor)

### Step 4: Go to Market
- Round floor price UP to a clean number
- Example: $3,500 floor becomes $5,000 market price
- The gap between floor and market price is your negotiation room

### Step 5: Model Scenarios
Use a scratch pad to model:
- "What if we sold 3 deals at $5K?" = $15K
- "What if we sold 2 deals at $8K?" = $16K
- "What if we sold 1 premium deal at $15K?" = $15K
- Pick the model that matches your audience size and content capacity

### Step 6: Adjust Over Time
- When inbound exceeds inventory: stop showing prices
- Let market demand set your rate
- Highest price any brand pays becomes new floor

## FIVE TAKEAWAYS ON COLD OUTREACH (E03b)

### 1. Borrowed Credibility
Social proof is essential for cold outreach. Be aware of what people find when they Google you:
- Clean social profiles with consistent branding
- Press mentions or features
- Previous brand partnerships listed somewhere visible
- Professional website or landing page

### 2. Find an Inside Person
Warm outreach through mutual connections is far more effective than cold emails:
- Ask fellow creators for introductions to brand partnerships teams
- Attend industry events (VidCon, Creator Economy events)
- Join digital communities where brand marketers participate
- Build relationships before you need them

### 3. Have a Clear Ask
- Your pitch deck IS your clear ask
- Do not send rambling emails explaining your life story
- Attach the deck, include 1-2 sentence summary, make it easy to say yes or no
- Include a clear next step: "Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week?"

### 4. Be Cautious of Over-Persistence
- Reputation is your most valuable asset in a small industry
- One follow-up after initial outreach is appropriate
- Two follow-ups maximum before moving on
- Being pushy damages your reputation with that brand AND their network

### 5. Focus on Creating Value
- 80-90% of your time should be making better content
- 10-20% maximum on outreach and deal negotiation
- As your content improves, inbound interest increases naturally
- The best brand deal strategy is making undeniable content

## NORTH STAR BRANDS LIST EXERCISE (E03b)

### Building Your Target List
Create a document with three categories:

**Category 1: Aspiration Brands**
- Brands you have always wanted to work with
- Dream partnerships that would validate your work
- Example: Colin & Samir wanted Nike for 6 years; constantly voiced it; eventually got connected

**Category 2: Organic-Use Brands**
- Tools and products you already use daily
- These make the most authentic partnerships
- Your audience may already know you use these products

**Category 3: Proven-Budget Brands**
- Brands already sponsoring other creators in your niche
- They have allocated budget for creator partnerships
- Lower risk pitch because they understand the value

For each brand document:
- Brand name
- Why it is a good fit (audience overlap, values alignment)
- Mutual connection path (who can introduce you?)
- Contact info if available (marketing team, partnerships coordinator)

## INBOUND/OUTBOUND RESPONSE TEMPLATES (E03b)

### Inbound Response Template (When a Brand Contacts You)
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for reaching out -- [brand] is a great fit for [your channel/content].

I have attached our partnership deck with format details, audience data, and pricing.

Happy to hop on a quick call to discuss how we can make this work. What does your week look like?

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Outbound Template (When You Contact a Brand)
```
Hi [Name],

I am [your name], creator of [channel/brand] where I [what you do] for [audience].

I have been using [their product] for [time period] and my audience regularly asks about [relevant topic]. I think there is a strong fit for a partnership.

I have attached our deck with audience data, sample integrations, and pricing.

Would love to explore this -- are you open to a 15-minute call?

Best,
[Your name]
```

### Key Principles for Both
- Keep it short (under 150 words)
- Always attach the pitch deck
- Include a specific next step (call, meeting)
- Reference genuine product use or audience alignment
- Never lead with your follower count alone

## MEDIA KIT CREATION (E04a)

### For Creators Without a Full Pitch Deck
- Create on Beacons (free, auto-generates from Instagram data)
- Include: bio, audience demographics, engagement rates, content samples
- Link in your bio for brands browsing your profile
- Update monthly with latest metrics

### Media Kit vs. Pitch Deck
- **Media kit:** 1-2 page overview for quick reference; good for inbound inquiries at any stage
- **Pitch deck:** 7-page deep dive for serious partnership discussions; good for outbound and call presentations
- Have both: media kit for initial interest, pitch deck for closing

## KEY BENCHMARKS AND NUMBERS

### Pricing Benchmarks by Platform Size
| Followers | Instagram Integration | YouTube Integration | TikTok |
|-----------|----------------------|--------------------| -------|
| 10K-50K | $500-$2,000 | $1,000-$3,000 | $300-$1,500 |
| 50K-100K | $2,000-$5,000 | $3,000-$8,000 | $1,500-$4,000 |
| 100K-500K | $5,000-$15,000 | $8,000-$25,000 | $4,000-$15,000 |
| 500K-1M | $15,000-$30,000 | $25,000-$50,000 | $15,000-$30,000 |
| 1M+ | $30,000+ | $50,000+ | $30,000+ |

*Note: These are general ranges. Specific niche, engagement rate, and audience quality significantly affect pricing. Finance, B2B, and tech niches command premium rates.*

### Commission Structure
- Agent: 10% of deal value
- Lawyer: 5% of deal value
- Total: 15% typical
- Factor this into your pricing from the start

### Revenue Benchmarks from Sources
- Parker Walbeck at 1.5M subscribers: minimum $10K per 30-second plug; $286K from 11 deals in one year
- Brand deals at ~500K TikTok followers: $2,000-$3,000 per post (E01)
- Colin & Samir: brand partnerships became predictable monthly revenue after implementing Menu Concept
