# Community Building Playbook Reference

## FACEBOOK GROUP GROWTH SOPs (E03c)

### Daily Growth Routine (15-20 Minutes)

**Step 1: Invite 40 (5 minutes)**
- Go to your personal profile's friend list
- Filter by people relevant to your niche (use mutual friends, location, interests)
- Send 40 group invitations
- Prioritize people who have engaged with your content recently

**Step 2: Remind 40 (5 minutes)**
- Check pending invitations in Group Settings > Members > Invited
- For invitations older than 3 days, send a personal DM reminder
- Template: "Hey [name], I started a group for [topic] -- thought you would be a perfect fit. Would love to have you!"
- Personal message converts significantly better than generic invitation

**Step 3: Delete 40 (5 minutes)**
- Remove invitations older than 7 days with no response
- This frees up invitation slots (Facebook limits pending invitations)
- Do not take non-responses personally -- people are busy
- These people can be re-invited later

### Weekly Growth Actions
- Share 1 piece of group content on your main profile/page
- Cross-post in 2-3 related groups (with permission, providing value not spam)
- Feature 1 member win on your stories/main platform with group mention
- Review pending member requests and approve those who fit

### Monthly Growth Review
- Track member count growth rate
- Review most engaging posts (what topics drove most discussion?)
- Identify top 10 most active members (these are your community leaders)
- Adjust content strategy based on engagement data

## 5-DAY LEAD GEN SEQUENCE (E03c)

### Sequence Overview
This repeatable 5-day cycle generates qualified leads from community members. Run continuously, rotating themes each cycle.

### Day 1: The Value Bomb
**Goal:** Establish authority and generosity with no strings attached.

Template:
- Open with a bold statement or contrarian take
- Deliver 3-5 actionable steps on a specific problem
- Include specific examples or numbers
- Close with: "Save this post -- you will want to come back to it"

Example post structure:
```
[Bold statement about a common problem in your niche]

After [your experience], here are the [X] things that actually move the needle:

1. [Specific tactic] -- [why it works] -- [example]
2. [Specific tactic] -- [why it works] -- [example]
3. [Specific tactic] -- [why it works] -- [example]

Most people overcomplicate this. Start with #1 today.

Save this post -- you will want to reference it later.
```

### Day 2: The Story + Lesson
**Goal:** Create emotional connection through vulnerability and shared experience.

Template:
- Open with a specific moment in time (date, location, feeling)
- Share the struggle or failure honestly
- Describe the turning point
- Extract the universal lesson
- Connect to something members are experiencing now

Key: Use specific numbers, names, and dates. Specificity creates imagery; imagery creates emotion; emotion creates connection (E01 Specificity Principle).

### Day 3: The Engagement Post
**Goal:** Get members talking and build peer-to-peer relationships.

Question types that drive engagement:
- **Experience-based:** "What was the single biggest lesson you learned this year about [topic]?"
- **Opinion-based:** "Unpopular opinion time: [your take on a debated topic]. Agree or disagree?"
- **Tip-sharing:** "Drop your #1 [tool/strategy/hack] for [specific outcome]"
- **This-or-that:** "[Option A] or [Option B] for [specific use case]? And why?"

Critical: Respond to every comment personally. Ask follow-up questions. Turn comments into conversations.

### Day 4: The Case Study / Proof
**Goal:** Demonstrate that your method works with specific evidence.

Template:
- Introduce the person (name, starting point, goal)
- Describe what they were doing before (that was not working)
- Explain what changed (your method/framework)
- Share the specific result (numbers, timeline, outcome)
- Connect to the group: "Has anyone else experienced [the starting problem]?"

If you do not have client case studies yet:
- Use your own results
- Use before/after from your own journey
- Reference industry data or research

### Day 5: The Soft Offer
**Goal:** Transition trust into revenue without being pushy.

Template:
```
This week I shared [summary of Days 1-4].

If this resonated with you, I want you to know -- this is exactly what I help people do in [your program/product].

[1-2 sentences about what the offer includes]
[1 sentence about the result people get]

If you want to learn more, [specific CTA: comment "interested," DM me, click this link].

No pressure -- I will keep sharing free value here regardless.
```

Key principle: Frame the offer as a natural next step, not a pitch. The 4 days of value built the trust; Day 5 simply presents the option.

## COMMUNITY ENGAGEMENT SYSTEMS

### New Member Welcome System
- Personal welcome message within 24 hours of joining
- Tag in an introduction thread: "Welcome [name]! Tell us: what is your biggest goal with [topic]?"
- DM with 2-3 best resources/posts in the group to get them started
- Follow up in 7 days: "How are you settling in? Any questions?"

### Weekly Engagement Rituals
- **Monday Wins Thread:** "Share your biggest win from last week (big or small)"
- **Wednesday Q&A:** Open question thread with guaranteed response from leader
- **Friday Resource Drop:** Curated tool/article/resource for the community

### Member Retention Tactics
- Recognize and celebrate member milestones
- Create roles for highly active members (moderators, mentors)
- Run time-limited challenges (7-day, 30-day) to spike engagement
- Survey members quarterly: what do you want more of? Less of?

## DM CLOSER SCRIPTS (E03a)

### Initial Qualification (After Keyword Comment or DM)
```
Hey [name]! Thanks for your interest in [resource/offer].

Quick question -- what specifically caught your attention? I want to make sure I point you to the right thing.
```

### Problem Discovery
```
Got it! That makes sense.

If you do not mind me asking -- what have you tried so far to [solve the problem they mentioned]?

And what would it mean for you if you could [desired outcome] in the next [timeframe]?
```

### Bridge to Offer
```
I hear you. That is exactly the kind of situation where [your program] helps.

[1-2 sentences about what the program does differently]

Would you be open to a quick 15-minute call so I can see if it is the right fit? No pressure -- I will be honest if I think something else would serve you better.
```

### Objection Handling
- **"I cannot afford it":** "I totally understand. Can I ask -- what would need to happen in the next [timeframe] for you to feel like it was worth the investment?"
- **"I need to think about it":** "Absolutely. What specifically are you weighing? I might be able to help clarify."
- **"Is it guaranteed?":** "I cannot guarantee results because that depends on your effort. What I can tell you is that [X] of our members have achieved [specific outcome]."

## POWER PARADOX PRINCIPLES (E03c)

### From the Power Paradox (Book Reference)
- People rise to positions of power through empathy, generosity, and service to others
- People LOSE power through selfishness, extraction, and self-interest
- This principle directly applies to community leadership

### Community Application
- The more you give without expectation, the more authority and influence you gain
- The moment members feel you are extracting more than you are giving, trust erodes rapidly
- Invest your own time and money into group welfare BEFORE expecting financial return
- Make visible sacrifices that prioritize the group over yourself

### Practical Examples
- Spend personal money on tools/resources that benefit the group
- Give away your best content for free inside the group
- Dedicate hours to members without immediate financial return
- Publicly celebrate member achievements instead of your own

## CASE STUDY: $33K FROM <200 MEMBERS (E03c)

### The Setup
- Sky Stack built a Facebook group focused on personal branding and business growth
- Started with direct invitations to people he knew were interested in the topic
- Committed to the daily invite/remind/delete growth system

### The Strategy
- Invested hours of live stream content before any offer
- Applied the community revenue formula: 1 hour per $1K of offer price
- Focused on leadership and genuine value delivery
- Used the 5-Day Lead Gen Sequence on repeat

### The Results
- Less than 200 members in the group
- $33K in sales in the first month of offering paid services
- High conversion rate because every member had significant time invested with him
- Proof that community size matters far less than community depth

### Key Takeaways
1. Small group + deep engagement > large group + shallow engagement
2. Time invested in live streams directly correlated to willingness to buy
3. The go-giver approach built trust that converted without hard selling
4. Community revenue is relationship math, not audience math
