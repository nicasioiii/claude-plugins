## The Content Multiplication System (E02 Dickie Bush & Cole)

### 5 Variables That Generate Infinite Content

**Variable 1: Content Pillars (3-5)** -- from brand-foundation
**Variable 2: Content Buckets (3)**
1. Futurism: Where your industry is headed (trends, predictions)
2. Your Story: Lessons from personal experience
3. Actionable Advice: Specific, practical guidance people can apply immediately

**Variable 3: Magical Multipliers (10 angles)**
Tips, Stats, Steps, Lessons, Benefits, Reasons, Mistakes, Examples, Questions, Personal Stories. Take ANY topic and run through each multiplier = 10 different content pieces.

**Variable 4: Audience Archetypes (3-5 segments)** -- from brand-foundation
**Variable 5: Sub-Topic Expansion** -- vary tools, time frames, budget levels, experience levels, industry applications, scale variations

### The Math
3 pillars x 3 buckets x 10 multipliers x 5 archetypes x 10 sub-topics = **4,500+ unique content ideas** from ONE niche.

## Idea Generation Methods (Synthesized From All Sources)

### Method 1: IVP-Based Generation (E01 Mino Lee)
Pull directly from the 25 IVP responses (brand-foundation). Fears become hooks, dreams become promises, limiting beliefs become video topics. Formula: 50% how-to videos, 50% list videos.

### Method 2: The Lean Process (E02, 7 Steps)
1. Define Content Pillars
2. Identify Audience Archetypes
3. Generate Sub-Topics
4. Select ONE pillar + ONE audience + ONE sub-topic + ONE bucket
5. Generate 10 Magical Multiplier ideas for that combination
6. Test with "Outlines As Content" (short posts)
7. Expand winners into long-form

### Method 3: Five-Step Framework (E03b Colin & Samir)
1. Set consistent brainstorm time (Jerry Seinfeld method -- 30 min daily)
2. Brainstorm within formats (format = storytelling formula with consistent packaging)
3. Determine your ideation style: Reactive (news-driven), Active (experience-driven), or Iterative (building on existing ideas)
4. Develop an idea test (5 questions): Value? Unique to brand? Reinforces mission? Inspires community? Evokes emotion?
5. Evaluate production requirements (timeline, resources, feasibility)

### Method 4: Natural Priming + Active Rest (E04b)
Spend 15-60 min/day consuming content strategically. Save to collections. Ask "why did this work?" Then do semi-intensive activity (driving, walking, training) that occupies mind but not completely. Subconscious processes ideas -- eureka moments emerge during rest.

### Method 5: Anomaly Method (E04a Justas)
Find 10-20 creators in your niche. Analyze last 30 pieces. Find anomalies (content with significantly higher engagement than average). Recreate with your own touch -- aim to make it at least 10% better.

## Content Purpose Types

### The Four-Purpose Model (E04a)
1. **Educational**: People watch to end, replay, save. High retention
2. **Relatable**: People share with friends/family. Shareability = virality driver
3. **Entertainment**: Funny, memes, fails. Most common on internet
4. **Inspiring**: Motivational, transformation stories. Everyone needs motivation

Choose at least one purpose before creating. Can mix (educational + entertaining).

### Likeable / Saveable / Shareable Model (E04b -- Contrarian)
Prefer this categorization over educational/inspirational/entertaining because different niches define those terms differently:
- Likeable content: aim for 1:10 ratio (likes to reach)
- Saveable content: aim for 1:1 ratio (saves to likes)
- Shareable content: aim for 1:10 ratio (shares to likes)

Map: Educational = optimized for saves. Inspirational = optimized for shares. Entertainment = optimized for likes.

## Content Strategy Matrix (E04b, 6 Elements)

1. **Goals**: Growth (80%) vs conversion (20%). Growth split: 80% attraction, 20% nurture
2. **Types**: Attraction, nurture, conversion content
3. **Pillars**: 3-5 content pillars (horizontal axis)
4. **Topics**: Subtopics within each pillar (vertical axis -- go deep)
5. **Formats**: Reels, carousels, single images. Start 33/33/33, shift to 60/20/20 for winning format after 100 days
6. **Personality**: Brand superpowers infused into every piece

## Publishing Cadence

> **Instructor Disagreement -- Posting Frequency:**
> - E01 (Mino Lee): Post daily no exceptions. First 7 videos don't matter -- build the habit
> - E02 (Dickie Bush & Cole): 3-5x per week is sufficient
> - E04b (Dojo): Minimum 3 posts/week. Can scale to 10-14/week
> - **Reconciliation:** Daily during learning phase (first 100 videos). 3-5x/week for established creators optimizing quality.

### Platform-Specific Cadence
- **Instagram**: 1 reel/day (growth phase), 1+ story/day, 1+ regular post/month
- **LinkedIn**: 3-5 posts/week. Mix: 2 short (50-120 words), 1 long (200-450 words), 1 visual every 1-2 weeks
- **YouTube**: 1-2 long-form/week + shorts daily or every other day
- **TikTok**: Daily posting recommended for algorithm momentum

### Cadence Rules
- Maintain predictable windows each week (teach the feed when to expect you)
- Never stop posting once you start. Taking a week off can drop reach by 10x (E04a)
- Batch-create content before any planned break
- Post 30 minutes BEFORE peak follower activity time

## Batch vs. Daily Creation Decision (E04b)

### Daily Creation (Beginners, First 30-100 Days)
Create one full post daily: idea -> copy -> design -> post. Fastest learning curve + strongest habit building.

### Batch Creation (After 100 Days)
Break posts into elements, assign to specific days:
- Monday: Ideas
- Tuesday: Copy/scripts
- Wednesday: Review/edit
- Thursday: Design/film
- Friday: Scheduling/descriptions
- Weekend: Rest (must be scheduled)

### 6-Step Content Board (E04a)
Move cards through stages: Idea Generation -> Scripting/Structuring -> Filming -> Editing -> Scheduling/Description -> Posted. Track all posted videos with view counts for data-driven decisions.

## "Outlines As Content" Method (E02)

"All great writing is basically just a Jenga tower of ideas stacked on top of each other." Write 3-5 short-form posts per week as outlines. Track which outlines resonate. Expand proven outlines into 1-2 long-form posts per week. You are never starting from scratch -- you already know the audience wants this combination.

## 30-Day Content Calendar Framework

### Rotation Logic (E02)
- Day 1: Pillar 1 -> Day 2: Pillar 2 -> Day 3: Pillar 3 -> repeat
- Rotate through Magical Multipliers without repetition
- Rotate through Audience Archetypes and Sub-topics evenly
- Each day includes: pillar, content bucket, multiplier angle, audience archetype, sub-topic, post title/hook, suggested format

### Calendar Rules
- Cover all pillars equally across the month
- Alternate content buckets (Futurism / Your Story / Actionable Advice)
- Include at least 2-3 "Outlines As Content" tests per week
- Reserve 1 day/week for trend-reactive content (leave slot open)
- Schedule rest days explicitly

## Competitive Research System

### Creator List Method (E04b)
Maintain a list of 15-50+ creators in your niche. Rate each 1-3 stars for relevance. Store URLs for quick access when seeking inspiration.

### Anomaly Analysis (E04a)
Use sortfeed.com (Chrome extension) to sort any creator's content by views. Filter for last 200 posts. Open top performers in new tabs. Analyze hooks, formats, topics, and delivery. Run this process every 2-4 weeks.

### Video Breakdown Exercise (E01)
Take 3 favorite creators, find their 2 most viewed videos, screenshot every shot change, note: duration, composition, effects used, total shot changes per minute.
