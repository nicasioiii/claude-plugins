# Content Formats & Types

## The 8 Viral Video Formats (E01 Mino Lee)

### Format 1: Educational Video
- **Value**: Viewer walks away better equipped for a problem
- **Structure**: Hook (persuade this covers THEIR problem) -> Thing 1 -> Thing 2 -> Thing 3 -> Conclusion/CTA
- Within each "thing": Name it -> Why it is crucial -> Quick example or story
- Save the best thing for last (rewatchability)
- Inject personality via stories/humor -- do not be a "textbook on a screen"
- CTA options: lead magnet comment trigger, tease incomplete list ("These are only 3 but I use 7..."), or "Follow for more"

### Format 2: Challenge Video (Three-Act Structure)
- **Value**: Takes viewer through a journey of caring about you achieving a goal
- **Act 1 (Setup)**: Get people bought into the goal. "Today I'm going to try to ace a midterm after studying for just one hour"
- **Act 2 (Conflict)**: Introduce the obstacle. Stack difficulties. "The Avengers without Thanos are just funny characters eating shawarma"
- **Act 3 (Resolution)**: Binary result -- did you achieve it or not? Make it CLEAR. Celebrate or show genuine disappointment
- This format will always exist (every trending YouTube video follows it)

### Format 3: Vlog / Day-in-the-Life
- **Value**: Transparent look into what their life would look like in your situation
- **Structure**: Hook -> Setting Switch 1 -> Setting Switch 2 -> Setting Switch 3
- Setting switches are critical. Film ACTION at each new location (emotion, dialogue, movement)
- Hack: Film your hook at the END of the day -- it does not have to be chronological
- 30-50 B-roll clips per minute for top-tier vlogs

### Format 4: Reaction Video
- **Value**: Unique or applicable perspective on existing content
- **Structure**: Stitched content -> Emotionally striking follow-up -> Expand with storytelling
- Lead with EMOTION first (show reaction in first 1-2 seconds before explaining)
- Human beings respond to emotion before intellect

### Format 5: Life Lesson Video
- **Value**: Learning a lesson vicariously through your experience
- **Structure**: Hook = unique aphorism + twist -> "Then I realized something..." (emotional spike) -> Story -> Takeaway
- "I realized something" or "something bad happened" creates emotional spikes in otherwise flat storytelling

### Format 6: News Story Video
- **Value**: Learning about a helpful/entertaining story
- **Structure**: Hook = "The new thing" + shocking implication -> Background while hinting at reveal -> Reveal/payoff
- Always pair information with an OPINION/reaction
- Story does not have to be recent -- just make it FEEL novel

### Format 7: Comparison / Tier List Video
- **Value**: Helps viewer choose best/worst option
- **Structure**: Hook = collection you compiled + why applicable -> Analyze items -> Verdict
- Mention you "had to compile" the information (implies effort)
- Make applicable beyond niche: not just "iPhone 16 specs" but "Should YOU upgrade?"

### Format 8: Skit / Comedy Video
- **Value**: Comedic bits that catch viewer off guard
- Conflict creates space for humor. Main character must face external pressure
- Character traits must escalate: mild (Beat 1) -> noticeable (Beat 2) -> extreme (Beat 3)
- Without escalation, the extreme reaction feels unearned and unfunny

## LinkedIn Format Reference (E02)

### 5 Core Content Styles
1. **Steps**: "5 steps to [outcome]" -- actionable, process-driven
2. **Stats**: "X stats that prove [claim]" -- data-driven authority
3. **Mistakes**: "X mistakes that [negative outcome]" -- pain-point driven
4. **Lessons**: "X lessons from [experience]" -- story-driven wisdom
5. **Examples**: "X examples of [concept]" -- proof-driven credibility

### Post Length Framework
- Short posts: 50-120 words (quick insights, single ideas)
- Long posts: 200-450 words (frameworks, detailed breakdowns)
- Stay under 2,800 characters (of LinkedIn's 3,000 max) -- non-negotiable

### "Outlines As Content" (E02)
Write 3-5 short-form posts per week as outlines. Track resonance. Expand proven outlines into long-form posts. Short-form validates ideas before long-form investment.

### Carousel Format (E02)
6-10 slides:
1. Big Promise / Hook
2. Stakes (problems if you don't act)
3. Example (real-world application)
4. Slide 4-7: Framework (clear steps)
5. Checklist (common mistakes)
6. Quick Win (one tool to try now)
7. Call to Action (next step or question)

## Content Type Decision Matrix

### By Platform
| Platform | Primary Format | Secondary | Avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram | Reels (70%) | Stories (25%), Posts (5%) | Long text-only posts |
| LinkedIn | Text posts (60%) | Carousels (25%), Video (15%) | Off-topic content, external links in post |
| YouTube | Long-form (main) | Shorts (support) | Inconsistent posting |
| TikTok | Short video (100%) | -- | Over-polished content |

### By Goal (E04b)
| Goal | Content Type | Metric |
|---|---|---|
| Growth (attraction) | Educational, entertaining, shareable | Reach, new followers |
| Nurture | Personal stories, behind-the-scenes | Saves, comments, DMs |
| Conversion | Testimonials, case studies, offers | Link clicks, DMs, sales |

### Recommended Splits
- **Pure growth phase** (E04b): 80% attraction, 20% nurture, 0% conversion in feed
- **Balanced phase**: 60% attraction, 20% nurture, 20% conversion
- **E01 formula**: 50% how-to, 50% list videos
- **Instagram (E04a)**: 70% Reels, 25% Stories, 5% regular posts

## Content Repurposing Framework (E04b)

### 4 Repurposable Elements
Extract from any piece of content:
1. Key quote or insight (for text post or image)
2. Framework or process (for carousel or infographic)
3. Story or example (for video or thread)
4. Data or stat (for stat post or visual)

### On-Platform Repurposing (3 Levels)
1. **Same content, new format**: Text post -> carousel -> video
2. **Same idea, new angle**: New hook, new audience segment, different multiplier
3. **Same framework, new example**: Same structure with fresh case study or application

### Cross-Platform Repurposing
- Short-form video: Post natively to IG, TikTok, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn (do NOT cross-post -- upload natively to each)
- Written content: LinkedIn post -> X thread -> newsletter section
- Long-form video: YouTube long-form -> extract 3-5 shorts -> write text versions

## Reel Length Decision (Instructor Disagreement)

> **Instructor Disagreement -- Ideal Reel Length:**
> - E04a (Justas): 7-15 seconds optimal. Shorter = higher chance of full watch = better algorithm signal
> - E01 (Mino Lee) + E05b (Christl): Up to 60 seconds with strong storytelling
> - **Reconciliation:** Shorter for algorithm tricks and broad reach. Longer for trust-building and coaching-funnel content. Match length to purpose, not arbitrary rules.

## Trending Content Integration

### How to Use Trends (E04a, E04b)
- Combine mainstream events with your niche (e.g., product launch + your expertise)
- Trending audio: slight additional push, not essential. Only use if mood matches video emotion
- Trend-reactive content should fill 1-2 slots per week, not dominate calendar

> **Instructor Disagreement -- Music/Trending Sounds:**
> - General advice: Use trending sounds for algorithmic boost
> - E01 (Mino Lee): Mood first, trend second
> - **Reconciliation:** Trending sound ONLY if it matches video emotion. Wrong-mood trending sound hurts more than no trend helps.
