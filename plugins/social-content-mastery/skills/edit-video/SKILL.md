## THE CORE FRAMEWORK: Story Editing vs. Retention Editing (E05b Nicklas Christl)

This is the most important editing decision you will make. It is a spectrum, not a binary choice.

### Story Editing
**Intention:** Tell the most impactful story with the strongest emotions. Optimize every decision for the story.

| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| In-depth dialogue | Detailed conversations revealing personality, background, motivations |
| Thoughtful pacing | Varied speed emphasizing key moments; sometimes slow, sometimes fast |
| Subtle foreshadowing | Revealing/hiding context at the right time; sprinkling hints |
| Visual storytelling | Using shot sizes, angles, compositions to convey emotion |
| Realistic sound design | Multi-layered ambient sounds and natural SFX for immersion |

### Retention Editing
**Intention:** Maintain viewer engagement to increase views. Optimize every decision for retention.

| Characteristic | Description |
|---|---|
| Hyper dialogue | Bare minimum of most engaging statements; filler removed; rapid |
| Rapid pacing | Scenes kept very short; quick cuts; dynamic transitions; never slows |
| Directly stated goals/stakes | Told to viewer early and pointed out continuously |
| Flashy visuals | Dynamic footage, FPV drone, eye-catching transitions, overlay text, animations |
| Strong/amplified sound design | Upbeat music, loud exaggerated SFX, audio cues signaling what to pay attention to |

### Two Types of Retention (E05b)
1. **Expensive retention (story)** -- harder to achieve, but viewer feels excited and rewarded
2. **Cheap retention (flashy techniques)** -- easier to achieve, but viewer feels manipulated and exhausted

**Key insight:** "The better you are at storytelling, the less you have to use cheap retention hacks." (E05b)

## The Adaptive Hybrid Model (E05b) [CONTRARIAN]

Do NOT choose one style for your entire video. Adjust editing style based on two factors:

### Factor 1: Natural Engagement of the Section
- If section is naturally interesting / has strong emotions -> lean story editing (let it breathe)
- If section is less interesting but context is needed -> use retention techniques (fast cuts, animations, sound)

### Factor 2: Timing Within the Video
- **Beginning** -> lean retention editing (viewer not yet invested, short attention spans)
- **Once viewer is invested** -> shift toward story editing
- Story attention builds over time; retention attention fades over time. These are opposite curves -- use both to capture benefits of each.

### Practical Application for Short-Form
- **Hook (0-5 seconds):** Full retention editing -- rapid visual changes, text on screen, strong audio
- **Body setup (5-15 seconds):** Transition zone -- retention pacing but introducing story elements
- **Core content (15+ seconds):** Story editing -- let strong content breathe, varied pacing, emotional beats
- **CTA (final seconds):** Brief return to retention energy for clear call-to-action

## INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT NOTICE

### Editing Style Philosophy

| Position | Advocate | Core Argument |
|---|---|---|
| **Retention editing maximizes growth** | Common social media advice | Retention = most important metric for algorithm; biggest YouTubers use this; fast cuts/flashy visuals keep viewers watching |
| **Story editing builds deeper trust, even MrBeast is shifting toward it** | E05b Nicklas Christl | Pure retention creates joyless engagement; viewer feels manipulated; MrBeast himself moving toward story after audience feedback; deeper connection = better monetization |
| **Mood-first editing, never use transitions** | E01 Mino Lee | Hard jump cuts ONLY; transitions look amateur; match editing energy to content emotion; cut all dead space ruthlessly |

**Resolution:** Use the adaptive hybrid model. Retention techniques for hooks and weak sections where content alone would not hold attention. Story techniques for strong emotional sections where the content itself carries engagement. Never use cheesy transitions (wipes, fades, spins) in short-form content -- hard jump cuts or nothing (E01). Music selection follows mood of content, not trends (E01).

## Jump Cut Technique (E01)

The fundamental editing technique for all social video:

1. Play clip, find where sentence ends
2. Split at that exact moment
3. Find where next sentence begins in next clip
4. Split again, delete all dead space between
5. Result: crisp, snappy transitions with zero dead air
6. Test by playing back -- it should feel seamless

### Pacing Rules (E01)
- **Every 0.5-second pause** between sentences should be cut out
- Goal: pacing feels "slightly fast" but still natural
- 2-3 minutes of raw footage should become 40-60 second final video
- Only keep "golden nugget" sentences -- cut B-tier and C-tier ruthlessly
- Target 30-50 visual changes (B-roll clips) per minute of finished video

### NEVER Use Transitions (E01)
- Hard jump cuts ONLY for short-form social content
- Wipes, fades, spins, and other transitions look amateur
- The only exception: intentional stylistic VFX transitions for specific creative purposes (see ref-social-vfx.md)

## Caption Strategy (E01)

### Auto-Captions Setup (CapCut)
- Settings: video mode, no template, no bilingual, filler words off
- Captions display 3-4 words at a time (precisely timed)

### Caption Design Rules
- Title text should be 2-3 words SHORTER than first draft
- Do not stack multiple lines of different text -- keep it visually simple
- Make key words/numbers larger or highlighted (yellow, bold) for emphasis
- Captions increase watch time because viewers read along even when audio is clear

## Music Selection (E01)

### Mood First, Trend Second [CONTRARIAN]
- Do NOT use a trending song if it does not match your video's emotion
- Wrong-mood music creates an "uncanny valley" effect: viewer feels confused and scrolls
- A trending sound ONLY if it matches the video emotion; wrong-mood trending sound hurts more than no trend helps

### Music Editing Rules
- Max 3 songs per short-form video (1 is usually enough)
- Musical shifts recapture attention -- use when changing emotional tone in the video
- Use beat drops strategically for reveals and payoffs
- Cut music to silence for emphasis on key sentences (use sparingly -- silence is powerful)

## Color Grading Basics (E01)

| Adjustment | When to Use | How |
|---|---|---|
| **Increase contrast** | Shots look "grayish" or flat | CapCut: Adjust > Contrast |
| **Increase saturation** | Want more vibrant, dopamine-inducing look | Slight increase only; over-saturation looks unnatural |
| **Warm tones** | Calm, empathetic, personal content | Shift temperature toward orange/yellow |
| **Cool tones** | Professional, tech, corporate content | Shift temperature toward blue |
| **High contrast + high saturation** | Exciting, energetic, hype content | Combine both adjustments |

## Sound Design (E05c, E05b, E01)

### Four Layers of Audio

| Layer | Purpose | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Ambient** | Atmosphere and immersion | Record on location or use sound libraries |
| **Music** | Emotional cues and pacing | Royalty-free libraries (MixKit.co, Epidemic Sound) |
| **SFX** | Texture, emphasis, transitions | CapCut built-in, freesound.org, or record foley |
| **Narration** | Voice content | Direct-to-camera or voiceover recording |

### Sound Design by Editing Style (E05b)
- **Story editing:** Realistic multi-layered sound; natural SFX; subtle music; ambient sounds create immersion
- **Retention editing:** Amplified/exaggerated sound; upbeat music; loud SFX; audio cues signal what to pay attention to
- **Adaptive hybrid:** Match sound design intensity to the section -- subtle for story moments, amplified for retention moments

### Sound Effects Toolkit (E01)
- Whoosh for text appearing
- Click/tap for button presses or point emphasis
- Subtle bass hit for reveals
- Record close-up sounds of objects after main filming session for custom foley (E05c)

## B-Roll Editing Rules (E01)

- Each B-roll clip: 1-2 seconds max on screen
- Target 30-50 clips per minute of finished video
- Front-load B-roll changes in first 5 seconds (5 different clips in 5 seconds for hooks)
- Match B-roll to what you are saying -- visual must support or illustrate narration
- Build reusable B-roll library organized by emotion/theme (see film-video for filming system)

## Two Final Tests Before Posting (E04b)

### Test 1: Watch on Mute
- Your video must be understandable without audio
- Captions, text on screen, and visual storytelling should carry the message
- If it makes no sense on mute, add more visual context

### Test 2: Overlay Social Media UI Safe Zones
- Check that no important text/visuals are hidden behind platform UI elements
- Instagram: username overlay at top, caption/like/share buttons at bottom
- TikTok: similar safe zone constraints
- Ensure captions and key text fall within the "safe zone" center of the frame

## Video Editing Process (E04a, adapted)

1. **Organize footage** before opening editing software (name files, group by scene)
2. **Build rough timeline** quickly to visualize overall pacing
3. **Rough cuts** -- clean footage, trim all white space (0.3ms gaps add up)
4. **First draft** -- verify pacing (hook grabs, body delivers, ending does not overstay)
5. **Cleanup** -- noise removal, echo removal, add SFX, color grading
6. **Two final tests** -- watch on mute + overlay social UI safe zones
7. **Optional** -- have a friend watch and note where their attention wanders
