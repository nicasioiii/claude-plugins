## Core Framework: Story Editing vs. Retention Editing Spectrum

(E03 Nicklas Christl -- Story Hero Academy)

Editing style exists on a spectrum. There is no single "right" way to edit. Your position on this spectrum defines your creative voice.

| Dimension | Story Editing | Retention Editing |
|---|---|---|
| **Dialogue** | In-depth. Longer conversations developing character, personality, motivations. | Hyper. Bare minimum of most engaging statements. All filler removed. Rapid. |
| **Pacing** | Thoughtful. Variable speed -- slow for emotional moments, fast for action. Builds tension. | Rapid. Fast information flow. Short scenes. Quick cuts. Dynamic transitions. Never slows down. |
| **Visual stimulation** | Subtle foreshadowing. Hints that stimulate curiosity. Viewers look for connections. | Constant. Flashing elements, zooms, graphics. Brain wired to attend to flashing lights and loud noises. |
| **Emotional tone** | Visual storytelling. 14 visual elements (shot sizes, angles, compositions) convey specific emotions. | Exaggerated reactions. Heightened emotional responses to maintain engagement. |
| **Sound design** | Realistic. Multi-layered ambient sounds and natural SFX. Immersive. | Heavy. Aggressive SFX, music hits, whooshes to trigger attention responses. |

**Reference points:**
- Cinema = optimize for story (Lee Smith / Christopher Nolan)
- Social media = optimize for retention (MrBeast)
- Best creators find their own position on the spectrum

## Editing Decisions Framework (E03 Nicklas)

Three factors drive every editing decision:

1. **Context** -- What information to reveal when. Control the viewer's knowledge.
2. **Emotion** -- What feeling to create at each moment. Match cuts to emotional beats.
3. **Rhythm** -- The pace and flow of cuts. Musical quality of the edit.

**Story Triangle:** Keep edits balanced between context, emotion, and rhythm. Over-indexing on any one creates problems:
- Too much context, no emotion = boring lecture
- Too much emotion, no context = confusing
- Too much rhythm, no substance = empty flash

## Engagement Without Cheap Tricks (E03 Nicklas)

Three factors that create genuine engagement:

1. **Diversity** -- Vary shot types, pacing, and content to avoid monotony
2. **Authenticity** -- Real moments resonate more than manufactured ones
3. **Originality** -- Unique creative voice that stands out from templates

## Six Editing Styles (E01 Frabrand)

| # | Style | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **B-roll driven** | B-roll with text overlay. Popular but don't rely exclusively on it. | Quick tips, how-to, aesthetic content |
| 2 | **Retention-based** | Wild combination of everything. Something must happen every 2 seconds. Most sought-after but expensive/intensive. | Viral short-form, MrBeast-style |
| 3 | **Animated** | Animations (After Effects/Canva). Popularized by Dan Koe. | Educational, concept explanation |
| 4 | **Authentic** | Minimal editing, few angles, no fancy subtitles. | Talking-head, personal stories, vlogs |
| 5 | **Combined** | Mix of animation, B-roll, talking head. | Most versatile; personally recommended by Frabrand |
| 6 | **Picture-driven** | Combination of pictures with simple animations and annotations. | Listicle content, before/after, comparison |

> **[CONTRARIAN] Authentic style predicted to grow** (E01 Frabrand):
> Minimal editing, few angles, no fancy subtitles. This style is predicted to become more popular, even on YouTube, as audiences tire of over-produced content.

## 7-Step Editing Process (E01 Frabrand)

1. **Organize footage** -- BEFORE opening editing software. Label, sort, prepare.
2. **Build timeline quickly** -- Get a rough grasp of pacing and end result. Don't polish yet.
3. **Rough cuts** -- Clean up, trim, remove dead space between sentences.
4. **First draft** -- Verify pacing (intro > body > outro). Re-hook audience if body runs too long.
5. **Cleanup** -- Noise removal, echo cleanup, add transitions + sound effects.
6. **Final version** -- Color grading, audio finalized, animations complete.
7. **Quality tests** -- Two mandatory tests before publishing.

### Two Quality Tests (E01 Frabrand)

**Test 1: Watch on mute.** The video must communicate its key information without audio. If it doesn't pass, add text overlays, captions, or visual cues.

**Test 2: Overlay platform UI.** Ensure nothing important is blocked by like/share/profile/comment buttons. Check all four corners and bottom third.

**Optional Test 3:** Show to a friend and note where their eyes wander from the screen. Those are your weak points.

## The 2-Second Rule (E01 Frabrand)

For retention-based editing: every 2 seconds, something must happen.

**"Something" means any of:**
- Sound effect
- Animation or graphic
- Transition
- Angle change or zoom
- Text pop-up
- B-roll switch
- Music beat sync

**When to use:** Short-form content where retention is critical (Reels, TikTok, Shorts).

**When NOT to use:** Story-driven content where thoughtful pacing matters more (YouTube long-form, documentary-style).

---

## Dead Space Trimming (E01 Frabrand, E01 Mino)

- Cut ALL dead space between sentences -- even 0.2-0.3 second gaps
- These micro-gaps compound across a video and make it feel "sluggish"
- Removing them makes videos "snappier" with no content loss
- Exception: intentional pauses for dramatic effect (story editing approach)

## Caption and Subtitle Rules

### Why Captions Are Mandatory
- 85-90% of social video is watched without sound (E06, E03)
- Captions serve as a visual hook (viewers glance down to read)
- Improves accessibility and international reach
- Instagram, TikTok, and LinkedIn all benefit from captions

### Caption Formatting (E01 Mino)
- Use classic font (no overly decorative fonts)
- Resize slightly smaller than default (not overwhelming)
- Position approximately 30% up from bottom of frame
- No two sentences on the same caption line
- Check for typos in auto-generated captions
- Captions must end before the video clip ends (no black space with lingering text)

### Caption Styles by Platform
| Platform | Recommended Style |
|---|---|
| TikTok | Auto-captions or CapCut styled captions. Word-by-word highlighting trending. |
| Instagram Reels | Clean, branded font. Consistent color. Not too large. |
| YouTube Shorts | Built-in captions or CapCut. Clear, readable. |
| YouTube Long-Form | Upload SRT file or use YouTube auto-captions (edit for accuracy). |
| LinkedIn | Burned-in captions for native video. Professional font. |

## Music and Sound Design

### Music Selection (E01 Mino)
- Find music on device or royalty-free library
- Cut music to start at beginning of video
- Volume at approximately 60% of speech volume
- Music should support, not compete with, the message

### Four Pillars of Audio (E03 Bob Krist)
1. **Natural/ambient sound** -- Environmental audio that places the viewer in the scene
2. **Sound effects** -- Punctuation for actions, transitions, emphasis
3. **Music** -- Emotional undercurrent; sets mood and pace
4. **Voice/narration** -- Primary information delivery

Multi-layered sound design creates immersion regardless of production budget.

### Sound Design for Retention (E03 Creators Club)
- Whooshes for transitions
- Impact sounds for text appearances
- Subtle ambient layers for depth
- Music beat syncing with visual cuts
- **Sound design makes VFX believable** -- without proper audio (whooshes, impacts, ambient transitions), even the best visual effects fall flat. Audio is 50% of perceived quality.

## VFX Concepts for Social Content (E03 Creators Club)

*Note: These are strategic concepts, not software tutorials.*

### High-Impact VFX Techniques
- **3D Tracking + Real Footage:** Combining 3D camera tracking with live footage creates premium feel. Even simple elements (floating UI, text, graphics) tracked to real-world footage creates standout content.
- **HUD (Heads-Up Display) Elements:** Overlaying UI-style animations (selection circles, typing keyboards, icon bars, review cards) on real footage = "Apple Vision Pro" effect. Trending heavily on Instagram/TikTok for product showcases.
- **Rotoscoping for Subject Isolation:** Isolating hands/objects from background creates layered depth effects. Subject in front of graphics = more professional look.

### Transition Techniques That Drive Retention (E03 Creators Club)
1. **Zoom transitions** -- Zoom into wheel, logo, or object to bridge scenes
2. **Masking transitions** -- Object passes frame, wipe reveals next scene
3. **Speed ramping** -- Slow-to-fast or fast-to-slow for dramatic moments
4. **Match cuts** -- Similar shapes/movements bridge two different scenes
5. **3D camera movements** -- Virtual camera flies through/around elements

### Behind-the-Scenes (BTS) Format (E03 Creators Club)
- Showing your creation process as content itself
- "Making of" format educates AND entertains
- Dual purpose: teaches technique while showcasing skill
- Works exceptionally well for video editors and VFX artists on social platforms

## Looping Technique (E01 Frabrand)

Slightly fade sound at end of video. Pick it up at the beginning. Trains rewatchability and signals to algorithm that viewers are watching multiple times.

**How to execute:**
1. Last 0.5-1 second: fade music/ambient audio down
2. First 0.5-1 second: fade same audio up
3. Visual: end frame should visually connect to opening frame
4. Content: ending statement can tease or connect to opening hook

## Video Structure in Editing (E01 Frabrand)

**Three-part structure:**
1. **Intro** -- Set expectations, open curiosity gap. Fast.
2. **Body** -- Deliver value. If too long, add a re-hook moment to pull attention back.
3. **Outro** -- Cut IMMEDIATELY after delivering value. Do not overstay your welcome.

**Common mistake:** Adding long outros with "thanks for watching" or extended CTAs. Cut right after the final value moment.

## Export Settings Quick Reference

| Platform | Resolution | Frame Rate | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instagram Reels | 1080 x 1920 (9:16) | 30fps | MP4 / H.264 |
| TikTok | 1080 x 1920 (9:16) | 30fps | MP4 |
| YouTube Long-Form | 3840 x 2160 (16:9) or 1920 x 1080 | 24fps or 30fps | MP4 / H.264 |
| YouTube Shorts | 1080 x 1920 (9:16) | 30fps | MP4 |
| LinkedIn Video | 1080 x 1920 or 1920 x 1080 | 30fps | MP4 |

## Hiring and Outsourcing Editors

### When to Hire (E02 Gusten, E08)
- Revenue from content exceeds cost of editing
- Editing time blocks you from higher-value activities (content strategy, sales, filming)
- You have a proven system and brand style that can be documented

### Finding Editors (E02 Gusten, E08 Dylan Reynolds)
- Freelance platforms (Fiverr, Upwork)
- Social media communities (editor-specific groups)
- Referrals from other creators
- Instagram/TikTok -- find editors who showcase their work

### Working with Editors
- **Use Loom** to create editing instructions -- saves hours of back-and-forth (E02 Gusten)
- Create a brand style guide: fonts, colors, caption style, pacing, music preferences
- Provide reference videos ("edit it like this")
- Give feedback using one positive + one critical sentence format (E01 Mino)

### Editor Skill Assessment (E08 Dylan Reynolds)
- Look for: storytelling sense, technical proficiency, turnaround speed
- Test with one paid project before committing to ongoing work
- Great editors understand pacing and emotion, not just button-clicking
