# Editing Process Reference

## Complete 7-Step Editing Workflow (Expanded)

### Step 1: Organize Footage
- Create folder structure BEFORE opening editor
- Naming convention: [Date]_[VideoTitle]_[Take#]_[A-roll/B-roll]
- Separate A-roll, B-roll, audio files, graphics, music
- Delete obvious bad takes to reduce clutter
- Star/favorite your best takes if your camera supports it

### Step 2: Build Timeline Quickly
- Import best takes into timeline
- Rough order: Hook > Body sections > Conclusion
- Don't worry about precision -- get the shape of the video
- Listen through once at 1.5x speed to assess overall flow
- Mark sections that need B-roll or graphics

### Step 3: Rough Cuts
- Remove ALL dead space between sentences (0.2-0.3 sec gaps compound)
- Cut stumbles, repeated takes, "ums" and "ahs"
- Tighten transitions between ideas
- Target: video should feel 20-30% shorter than raw footage

### Step 4: First Draft Review
- Watch at normal speed, full attention
- Check pacing: Does intro hook quickly? Does body maintain interest?
- If body runs too long, add a "re-hook" moment (new curiosity element)
- Verify the video delivers on the promise made in the hook
- Check total length against platform best practices

### Step 5: Cleanup
- **Audio cleanup:** Noise removal, echo reduction, volume normalization
- **Transitions:** Add cuts, zooms, or transitions at section breaks
- **Sound effects:** Add punctuation sounds at key moments
- **Text/graphics:** Add text overlays, lower thirds, call-outs
- **B-roll insertion:** Replace talking-head with B-roll at key moments

### Step 6: Final Version
- **Color grading:** Consistent look across all clips. Match white balance.
- **Audio master:** Final volume levels, music ducking under speech
- **Captions:** Add, review for typos, position correctly
- **Animations:** Finalize any motion graphics
- **Brand elements:** Logo, watermark, end card if applicable

### Step 7: Quality Tests
- **Mute test:** Watch entire video on mute. Must communicate key info visually.
- **UI overlay test:** Check all four corners and bottom third for platform UI conflicts.
- **Friend test (optional):** Note where a first-time viewer's attention drifts.
- **Phone test:** Watch on phone screen (not desktop monitor) to verify readability.

## 6 Editing Styles Deep Dive

### 1. B-Roll Driven
**What it looks like:** Full-screen B-roll footage with text overlay narrating the story or tips. Minimal or no talking head visible.
**Characteristics:**
- Text carries the message
- B-roll creates visual interest and mood
- Voice-over optional (some use text-only with music)
- Quick B-roll changes every 2-4 seconds
**Best for:** Quick tips, aesthetic content, "day in the life" style, recipe/how-to
**Tools needed:** B-roll library, text animation capability
**Example creators:** Lifestyle creators, food content, travel

### 2. Retention-Based
**What it looks like:** Constant visual stimulation -- zooms, SFX, graphics, angle changes, animations every 1-2 seconds.
**Characteristics:**
- 2-second rule: something new happens constantly
- Heavy use of sound effects for punctuation
- Zoom ins/outs synced to emphasis words
- Text pops, emojis, graphics layered over footage
- Most expensive/intensive editing style
**Best for:** Viral short-form, educational "fast facts," entertainment
**Tools needed:** Advanced editing software, SFX library, graphic templates
**Example reference:** MrBeast, Ali Abdaal shorts

### 3. Animated
**What it looks like:** Motion graphics, animated text, slide-like presentations with movement.
**Characteristics:**
- Kinetic typography (text that moves, transforms, animates)
- Diagram and concept animations
- Minimal live footage; heavy on designed elements
- Clean, modern aesthetic
**Best for:** Concept explanation, data-heavy content, philosophy/mindset
**Tools needed:** After Effects, Canva animations, or dedicated animation tools
**Example reference:** Dan Koe, educational channels

### 4. Authentic
**What it looks like:** Minimal editing. Single or dual angle. Clean cuts. No fancy effects.
**Characteristics:**
- Jump cuts to remove dead space (but no added effects)
- Single camera angle or simple two-angle setup
- No or minimal subtitles/captions
- Natural audio (light cleanup only)
- Personal, intimate feel
**Best for:** Personal stories, talking-head advice, vlogs, thought leadership
**Tools needed:** Basic editor (iMovie, CapCut)
**Trend note:** [CONTRARIAN] Predicted to grow in popularity as audiences tire of over-produced content (E01 Frabrand)

### 5. Combined (Recommended)
**What it looks like:** Mix of talking head, B-roll, animations, and text. Varied but cohesive.
**Characteristics:**
- Talking head for personal connection
- B-roll for visual variety
- Text overlays for emphasis and captions
- Occasional animations for concepts
- Balanced pacing -- not overwhelming, not boring
**Best for:** Most creators. Versatile across platforms and content types.
**Tools needed:** Mid-level editing software, B-roll library, basic graphics

### 6. Picture-Driven
**What it looks like:** Slideshow of images/screenshots with simple animations and text annotations.
**Characteristics:**
- Ken Burns effect (slow zoom/pan on still images)
- Text callouts and annotations
- Arrow/circle highlights
- Before/after comparisons
- Can be created quickly without video footage
**Best for:** Listicle content, comparison, before/after, reaction, news commentary
**Tools needed:** Basic editor, image library

## Caption Formatting Detailed Guide

### Auto-Caption Workflow (CapCut)
1. Import video into CapCut
2. Use auto-caption feature (supports 20+ languages)
3. Select font style: clean, readable, branded
4. Review EVERY line for errors (auto-captions are 85-90% accurate)
5. Fix timing: captions should appear/disappear in sync with speech
6. Adjust size: slightly smaller than default (not overwhelming)
7. Position: approximately 30% up from bottom (clear of platform UI)

### Caption Design Rules
| Rule | Rationale |
|---|---|
| Maximum 2 lines visible at once | Readability on small screens |
| No two sentences on same caption line | Prevents information overload |
| Consistent font throughout video | Brand consistency |
| High contrast (white text + dark outline or background) | Visible in all lighting conditions |
| Captions end before clip ends | No lingering text on black frames |
| Word-by-word highlighting (optional, trending) | Keeps viewer engaged with text |

### Platform-Specific Caption Notes
- **TikTok:** Native captions or CapCut. Word-by-word highlighting is trending and boosts retention.
- **Instagram:** Clean, branded font. Consistent color. Test that captions don't overlap with Reel UI elements.
- **YouTube:** Upload SRT file for accessibility. Burned-in captions optional for Shorts.
- **LinkedIn:** Burned-in captions required for native video. Professional, understated font.

## Transition Techniques Library

### Basic Transitions (Any Editor)
| Transition | Use When | How |
|---|---|---|
| Hard cut | Default. Most cuts should be hard cuts. | Simply place clips next to each other. |
| Jump cut | Same angle, removing dead space | Cut section from single clip. |
| J-cut | Audio from next scene starts before visual | Extend audio of clip B under clip A. |
| L-cut | Audio from current scene continues into next visual | Extend audio of clip A under clip B. |
| Cross dissolve | Passage of time, gentle transition | Add dissolve between clips. Use sparingly. |

### Advanced Transitions (E03 Creators Club)
| Transition | Effect | Execution |
|---|---|---|
| Zoom transition | Zoom into object, bridge to new scene | Keyframe scale up on clip A, scale down on clip B |
| Masking transition | Object passes frame, reveals new scene | Track object edge, mask wipe |
| Speed ramp | Dramatic slow-to-fast or fast-to-slow | Keyframe speed changes at cut point |
| Match cut | Similar shapes/movements bridge scenes | Find visual similarity between last frame A and first frame B |
| Whip pan | Fast camera movement bridges scenes | Fast pan at end of A, match with fast pan start of B |

### Transition Rules
- **Less is more:** Most cuts should be hard cuts
- **Match transitions to content:** Dramatic transition = dramatic moment
- **Don't distract:** If the viewer notices the transition more than the content, it's too much
- **Consistency:** Pick 2-3 transition types and use them throughout for brand consistency

## Color Grading Quick Guide

### Basic Color Workflow
1. **White balance correction:** Ensure skin tones look natural
2. **Exposure adjustment:** Proper brightness levels
3. **Contrast:** Add depth (lift blacks slightly for social media -- pure black looks harsh on phones)
4. **Saturation:** Slightly boost for mobile viewing (phones display less saturated than monitors)
5. **Color tone:** Apply consistent color grade across all clips

### Color Grading for Social Media
- Slightly warmer tones perform better (inviting, approachable)
- High contrast grabs attention in scrolling feeds
- Consistency across videos builds brand recognition
- Use LUTs (Look-Up Tables) for consistent one-click grading
- Test final color on a phone screen, not just a calibrated monitor

## Music Selection and Audio Mixing

### Finding Royalty-Free Music
- YouTube Audio Library (free for YouTube creators)
- Epidemic Sound (subscription)
- Artlist (subscription)
- CapCut built-in library (free)
- TikTok/Instagram native audio libraries

### Audio Mixing Levels
| Element | Relative Level |
|---|---|
| Voice/narration | 100% (reference level) |
| Background music | 15-25% of voice level |
| Sound effects | 50-75% of voice level (brief punctuation) |
| Ambient/natural sound | 10-20% of voice level |

### Music Rules
- Music starts at beginning of video (E01 Mino)
- Music should match energy/mood of content
- Duck music volume under speech (auto-duck feature in most editors)
- Trending audio on TikTok/IG may boost distribution (separate from background music)
- End music fade should coordinate with looping technique if applicable

## Platform-Specific Editing Notes

### TikTok Editing
- Edit within TikTok app for simplest workflow (E06 Chase Reiner, Jimmy Farley)
- [CONTRARIAN] TikTok REWARDS you for using all their features: effects, sounds, stitch, duet, text-to-speech (E06 JT Barnett)
- Jump cuts every 2-3 seconds maintain attention (E06 Ryan Magin)
- Phone-based editing apps: CapCut, InShot, VN
- Add subtitles/captions to all videos

### Instagram Reels Editing
- CapCut or Instagram native editor
- 30fps output (NOT 60fps -- appears choppy, E01 Mino)
- Volume: increase clips to 200% if needed for audibility
- Auto-captions: classic font, resize slightly smaller, position ~30% up from bottom

### YouTube Long-Form Editing
- Premiere Pro, Final Cut Pro, or DaVinci Resolve
- iMovie is fine for beginners (Sunny grew to 50K subs with it)
- More story-editing approach (pacing, structure, narrative)
- Chapters/timestamps in description
- End screens and cards added in YouTube Studio (not editor)

### LinkedIn Video Editing
- Keep it simple and professional
- Burned-in captions mandatory
- Square (1:1) or vertical (9:16) formats
- Authentic style performs best on LinkedIn
