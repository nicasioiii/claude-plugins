## Editing Style Decision Matrix (E05b Nicklas Christl)

Use this matrix to determine your default editing position on the story-retention spectrum:

| Distribution Channel | Intention | Recommended Style |
|---|---|---|
| Cinema | Story impact | Pure story editing |
| Streaming (Netflix, etc.) | Story + recommendations | Story editing with some retention techniques |
| YouTube (impact-focused) | Impact + views | Hybrid: retention hooks -> story once viewer is invested |
| YouTube (max views) | Views at all costs | Pure retention editing |
| Short-form (Reels/TikTok/Shorts) | Business leads + trust | Hook = retention; body = story-driven value |

### Finding Your Personal Editing Style (E05b)

Answer two questions:
1. **What is your distribution channel?** (Cinema / streaming / social media)
2. **What is your personal intention?** (Impact? Views? Sales?)

The intersection determines your default position. Then adjust per-section using the adaptive hybrid model.

## 5 Characteristics of Story Editing -- Deep Dive (E05b)

### 1. In-Depth Dialogue
- Let subjects speak in complete thoughts
- Reveal personality, background, and motivations through conversation
- Do not cut to only the "best" soundbites -- context builds connection
- Application for social: keep emotional/vulnerable moments intact; do not rush through genuine storytelling

### 2. Thoughtful Pacing
- Varied speed: sometimes fast, sometimes deliberately slow
- Slow moments create emphasis -- the pause before a reveal builds anticipation
- Fast moments create energy -- rapid-fire information delivery during exciting sections
- Application for social: match editing speed to emotional intensity of each section

### 3. Subtle Foreshadowing
- Plant hints early that pay off later
- Reveal and hide context at precisely the right time
- Create curiosity through what you do NOT show
- Application for social: tease the payoff in the first 3 seconds without giving it away

### 4. Visual Storytelling
- Use shot sizes, camera angles, and compositions to convey emotion and psychology
- Close-ups = intimacy, vulnerability; wide shots = isolation, context; low angles = power
- Let the visuals tell part of the story that words cannot
- Application for social: B-roll that creates emotion, not just illustration

### 5. Realistic Sound Design
- Multi-layered ambient sounds create immersion
- Natural SFX over exaggerated ones
- Music supports emotion rather than creating it artificially
- Application for social: subtle background audio that makes viewer feel "present"

## 5 Characteristics of Retention Editing -- Deep Dive (E05b)

### 1. Hyper Dialogue
- Bare minimum of the most engaging statements
- All filler words and pauses removed
- Rapid back-and-forth between speakers or ideas
- Application for social: hook sections, rapid-fire tips, list content

### 2. Rapid Pacing
- Scenes kept very short; quick cuts throughout
- Dynamic transitions between segments
- Never slows down -- constant forward momentum
- Application for social: first 5-10 seconds of any video; weak content sections

### 3. Directly Stated Goals and Stakes
- Tell the viewer early what they will get / what is at stake
- Continuously point out progress toward that promise throughout
- Leave nothing for the viewer to figure out -- everything is explicit
- Application for social: "In this video I'll show you X" + progress markers

### 4. Flashy Visuals
- Dynamic footage, FPV drone shots, eye-catching transitions
- Overlay text graphics, progress bars, animated elements
- Advanced animations and motion graphics
- Application for social: text-on-screen hooks, animated callouts, zoom effects

### 5. Strong/Amplified Sound Design
- Upbeat, energetic music throughout
- Loud, exaggerated SFX for every transition and emphasis point
- Audio cues that signal what the viewer should pay attention to
- Application for social: whoosh sounds, bass drops, notification sounds

## Pros and Cons Summary (E05b)

### Story Editing Pros
1. **Mental engagement** -- viewer challenged to figure things out (give them 2+2, not 4); naturally rewarding
2. **Strong emotions** -- in-depth dialogue + varied pacing + visual storytelling = nuanced emotional connection
3. **Deep connection** -- creates trusting, loyal audience; depth -> connection -> trust -> monetization
4. **Universal appeal** -- everybody likes good stories; broader demographic reach

### Story Editing Cons
1. **Slow attention** -- stories need time to develop; hard to compete in first 10 seconds on social
2. **Dependency on story** -- only works if core story is inherently interesting
3. **High time investment** -- steeper learning curve + longer editing process

### Retention Editing Pros
1. **Quick attention** -- grabs viewer immediately; effective on social platforms
2. **High-view potential** -- retention is the most important metric for algorithms
3. **Low time investment** -- techniques are quick to learn and execute

### Retention Editing Cons
1. **Over-stimulation** -- fast pacing + info overload = overwhelm and exhaustion
2. **Joyless engagement** -- viewer keeps watching without wanting to (like mindless scrolling)
3. **Feeling of manipulation** -- fast cuts and flashy visuals diminish trust
4. **Lack of narrative depth** -- no room for viewer to form own thoughts

## 6 Video Editing Styles for Social (E04b Content Creation Dojo)

These are execution styles, distinct from the story/retention spectrum above:

| Style | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **B-roll driven** | Single or multiple B-roll clips with text overlay | Quick tips, motivational quotes, simple education |
| **Retention-based** | Everything changing every 2 seconds: SFX, animations, angle changes | High-energy educational, entertainment, max views |
| **Animated** | Animations (After Effects or Canva) with text overlays; popularized by Dan Koe | Conceptual content, philosophy, abstract ideas |
| **Authentic** | Minimal editing, few angles, native platform subtitles | Personal stories, vulnerability, BTS, trust-building |
| **Combined** | Mix of all styles; instructor's favorite | Diverse information delivery; serves visual, auditory, and reading learners |
| **Picture-driven** | Combination of pictures with simple animations and on-screen instructions | Listicles, comparisons, tutorials without video footage |

## Emotion-to-Editing Map (E01 Mino Lee)

Match your editing style to the emotion of each video section:

| Content Emotion | Editing Approach |
|---|---|
| **Exciting / Hype** | Fast cuts, high contrast + saturation, upbeat music, beat drops, rapid B-roll changes |
| **Educational / Informative** | Medium pacing, clear captions, supporting B-roll, minimal music, clean sound |
| **Emotional / Vulnerable** | Slower pacing, let moments breathe, soft music, close-up shots, natural sound |
| **Motivational / Inspiring** | Building tempo, music crescendo, uplifting color grade, dynamic movement |
| **Funny / Entertaining** | Tight cuts on punchlines, well-timed SFX, reaction close-ups, silence for comedic timing |
| **Shocking / Surprising** | Build-up with normal pacing, then hard cut to silence or bass drop at reveal |

### Musical Shifts (E01)
- Musical shifts recapture attention -- use when changing emotional tone within the video
- Cut music to silence before a key sentence for maximum emphasis
- Use sparingly: silence is powerful BECAUSE it contrasts with music, not on its own

## The Adaptive Hybrid in Practice (E05b, E01, synthesized)

### Example: 60-Second Educational Reel

| Time | Section | Editing Style | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-3s | Hook | Full retention: rapid B-roll, text on screen, punchy audio | Viewer not invested; must stop scroll |
| 3-8s | Context/Setup | Retention-leaning: fast cuts, stated promise | Building toward investment |
| 8-25s | Core teaching | Transition to story: let strong content breathe, B-roll supports | Viewer is engaged; content quality carries |
| 25-45s | Example/Story | Full story: emotional pacing, varied speed, visual storytelling | Deepest engagement section |
| 45-55s | Recap/Value | Mild retention: tighten pacing, recap key points | Re-energize before CTA |
| 55-60s | CTA | Retention: direct, clear, punchy | Must be unmistakable |

### Example: 15-Second TikTok/Reel

| Time | Section | Editing Style | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-2s | Hook | Retention: text + visual + spoken aligned | Scroll-stopping is everything |
| 2-10s | Value delivery | Hybrid: fast pacing but clear delivery | Short format demands compression |
| 10-15s | CTA or loop | Retention: end strong or loop back to hook | Maximize watch-through and replays |
