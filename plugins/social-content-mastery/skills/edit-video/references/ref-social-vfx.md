# Social Video VFX Reference

> Load when user asks about video effects, transitions for social content, After Effects, 3D tracking, rotobrush, parallax, fire effects, HUD overlays, or production-level social VFX.

---

## VFX Philosophy for Social Content

VFX in social content serves a different purpose than in film or TV. The goal is to enhance attention and storytelling, not to demonstrate technical mastery.

**Key principles:**
- VFX should serve the content, not distract from it
- Sound design is the final layer that sells the effect (E05d)
- Start simple and build complexity step by step (E05d)
- Reference existing viral content for inspiration, then add your own creative spin

---

## Transition Types for Social Content (E05d Creators Club)

### Zoom-Out Transitions
- Mask an object (e.g., car wheel, coffee cup, phone screen), then zoom camera out to reveal full scene
- Creates dramatic reveal effect
- Works well for product showcases and before/after content

### Fire / Particle Effects
- Fire rim animations around objects for dramatic emphasis
- Logo fire animations for brand reveals
- Particle effects for magical/tech-themed content
- Application: opening sequences, product reveals, subscribe CTAs

### Looping Phone Transitions
- Seamless transition from phone screen to real-world scene
- Creates "portal" effect that viewers find mesmerizing
- Works for app demos, social media content about social media, tech content

### 3D Tracking + HUD Overlays
- Track real-world objects and overlay floating UI elements
- Examples: Spotify player hovering over headphones, Google reviews floating near a restaurant, time display on a clock
- Requires After Effects 3D camera tracker or similar
- Plan shots with tracking markers if needed for complex tracking

### Rotobrush Hand Isolation
- Isolate hands using After Effects rotobrush tool
- Create "AR interaction" illusion where hands appear to interact with digital elements
- Application: tutorial content, tech reviews, "magic" effects

### 3D Photo Parallax
- Convert still photos to 3D scenes with camera animation
- Depth layers separated and animated to create parallax motion
- Creates cinematic movement from a single still image
- Application: storytelling with photos, nostalgia content, dramatic reveals

## Sound Design for VFX (E05d)

Sound design is what makes VFX feel real. Without it, even the best visual effect looks like a graphic overlay.

### Layering Approach
1. **Base ambient sound** -- environment audio continues under the effect
2. **Movement SFX** -- whoosh, slide, or impact sound matched to visual motion
3. **Texture SFX** -- secondary sound that adds depth (crackle for fire, hum for tech)
4. **Music integration** -- beat drops or musical accents aligned with effect timing

### SFX to Match Common Effects
| Visual Effect | Recommended SFX |
|---|---|
| Zoom transition | Whoosh + subtle bass impact |
| Fire/particle | Crackle + flame burst |
| 3D element appearing | Soft "pop" or digital chime |
| HUD overlay activation | Tech beep + subtle hum |
| Text appearing | Click or snap |
| Object transformation | Morph/warp sound + impact |

## Creative Process Workflow for VFX Content (E05d)

1. **Find inspiration** -- study viral content with effects you admire
2. **Plan the shot** -- identify what tracking markers or clean footage you need
3. **Capture clean footage first** -- effects are added in post-production
4. **Start with simple elements** -- basic camera animation or basic overlay
5. **Build complexity step by step** -- add elements one at a time
6. **Enter flow state** -- "step by step I come more into the flow" (E05d)
7. **Polish and refine** -- adjust timing, color match, add final SFX
8. **Sound design last** -- layer SFX to match every visual transition and motion

### Key Principle: Iterate, Do Not Plan Everything Upfront
- Start with a rough idea inspired by viral content
- Throw in ideas and iterate -- you do not need to know the final result before starting
- Build step-by-step: basic camera animation -> add elements -> add effects -> polish
- The creative process is emergent, not predetermined

## When to Use VFX vs. Simple Editing

| Content Type | VFX Appropriate? | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Quick educational tip | No | VFX distracts from information delivery |
| Personal story / vulnerability | No | Raw authenticity is the appeal |
| Product showcase | Yes -- subtle | Zoom transitions, clean overlays enhance product presentation |
| Tech / gaming content | Yes | Audience expects and appreciates production value |
| Brand reveal / intro | Yes | Sets professional tone for channel |
| Tutorial / how-to | Sometimes | HUD overlays can enhance but should not overwhelm |
| Entertainment / comedy | Sometimes | When effects serve the joke or premise |
| Trend / challenge content | Depends | Match the trend's production level |

## VFX Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

1. **Do not add effects to every video** -- overuse numbs the audience and sets unsustainable production expectations
2. **Do not use generic wipe/fade transitions** -- hard jump cuts are the standard for short-form (E01)
3. **Do not let effects overshadow the message** -- if viewers remember the effect but not the content, you failed
4. **Do not skip sound design** -- silent effects look like graphics overlays, not integrated elements
5. **Do not copy effects without understanding why they work** -- study the viral content to understand what the effect achieves emotionally, then decide if that emotion serves YOUR content
6. **Do not ignore color matching** -- VFX elements must match the footage's color grade, lighting, and shadow direction
