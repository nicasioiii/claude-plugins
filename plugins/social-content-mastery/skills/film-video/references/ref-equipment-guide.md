## Equipment Philosophy

Three instructors agree on the core principle but differ on investment timing:

- **E01 Mino Lee:** Start with absolute minimum; upgrade incrementally as skills develop; specific budget recommendations below
- **E04a Justas:** Phone is sufficient for all social content; only invest in pro camera if you need it for other purposes too; use back camera, never front camera
- **E05c Bob Krist:** Story clarity matters more than gear; started filmmaking at age 58 with minimal equipment; invest in audio first

**Unified recommendation:** Master the craft with minimal gear first. Each upgrade should solve a specific, identified problem in your content quality.

## The Equipment Progression Ladder

### Level 1: Zero Budget (Start Here)

| Component | Solution | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Camera** | Smartphone (back camera only) | $0 |
| **Lighting** | Window / natural sunlight | $0 |
| **Audio** | Phone built-in mic (quiet room) | $0 |
| **Stabilization** | Prop phone on books, water bottle, or shelf | $0 |
| **Editing** | CapCut (free, phone + desktop) | $0 |

**When to upgrade:** When your audio has noticeable echo/noise, or when natural light is not available for your filming schedule.

### Level 2: Starter Budget (~$50-70)

| Component | Solution | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Lighting** | Ring light (Sensyne or similar, lasted 2+ years per E01) | ~$25-30 |
| **Background** | LED color-changing bulb for accent/backlight | ~$15-20 |
| **Stabilization** | Basic phone tripod | ~$15-20 |

**Result:** Front lighting on face + colored backlight = instantly professional-looking shot (E01). This setup covers 80% of what beginners need.

**When to upgrade:** When audio quality is holding back your content (echo, background noise, tinny sound).

### Level 3: Audio Investment (~$100-200 additional)

| Component | Solution | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Wireless microphone** | DJI Wireless Mic or Rode Wireless GO | ~$100-200 |
| **Alternative** | Hollyland Lark M2 (mentioned in E05a) | ~$100-150 |

**The 2-foot rule (E05c):** Mic must be within 2 feet of your mouth. Wireless lav mics solve this without visible cables on camera.

**When to upgrade:** When you are posting consistently (daily or 5x/week), getting traction, and want to level up visual quality beyond phone capabilities.

### Level 4: Camera Upgrade (~$500-1500 additional)

| Component | Solution | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Camera** | Mirrorless camera (Sony, Canon, Fuji) | ~$500-1500 |
| **Lens** | Kit lens to start; prime lens (35mm or 50mm) for background blur | ~$100-400 |

**E04a critical note:** Only invest in a professional camera if you are using it for other purposes too. Phone is sufficient for Instagram/TikTok.

**E05c note:** Even at the professional level, camera choice is secondary to audio quality and story clarity.

### Level 5: Professional Setup ($3000+)

| Component | Solution | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Softbox lighting** | 2-3 softbox lights for controlled 3-point lighting | ~$200-500 |
| **Quality mic** | Shotgun mic (for documentary/interview) or XLR lav | ~$200-400 |
| **Teleprompter** | Dedicated teleprompter for scripted content | ~$100-200 |
| **Background** | Acoustic panels, professional backdrop | ~$100-300 |
| **Production team** | Editor, camera operator, creative director | $2K-10K/month (E03a) |

## Audio Equipment Hierarchy

Ranked by priority for social content creators:

| Priority | Equipment | Use Case | Budget |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Wireless lav mic** (DJI/Rode/Hollyland) | All talking-head content | $100-200 |
| 2 | **Shotgun mic** (Rode VideoMicro or similar) | On-camera mounted, backup | $60-100 |
| 3 | **USB condenser mic** (Blue Yeti, Audio Technica) | Voiceover recording, podcasting | $50-130 |
| 4 | **XLR setup** (Shure SM7B + interface) | Studio voiceover, professional podcasting | $400-600 |

### Audio Recording Tips
- Always record in a quiet environment if possible (E04a)
- Record voiceover separately from video if ambient noise is uncontrollable (E04a)
- Record 10-30 seconds of room tone (silence in the room) for editing flexibility (E05c)
- Record close-up sounds of objects/actions after main shoot for foley SFX (E05c)
- Test audio levels before a long recording session

## Lighting Equipment Hierarchy

| Priority | Equipment | Setup | Budget |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Natural window light** | Position yourself facing window | $0 |
| 2 | **Ring light** | Directly in front of face, slightly above eye level | $25-30 |
| 3 | **LED color bulb** | Behind you, angled at background/hair for depth | $15-20 |
| 4 | **Small LED panel** | Fill light opposite key light to reduce shadows | $30-60 |
| 5 | **Softbox kit (2-3 lights)** | Full 3-point lighting control | $200-500 |

### 3-Point Lighting Quick Setup (E01)

```
       [BACK LIGHT]
          (behind subject, creates edge light / separation)
           |
    [SUBJECT] ----> [CAMERA]
     /          \
[KEY LIGHT]   [FILL LIGHT]
(brightest,    (softer, opposite side
 in front)     to reduce shadows)
```

### Lighting Mistakes to Avoid
- Overhead-only lighting (creates harsh under-eye shadows)
- Backlit without fill (face becomes silhouette)
- Mixed color temperature (daylight window + warm lamp = unnatural skin tones)
- Android phones typically have worse contrast than iPhone (E01) -- compensate in editing

## Stabilization Solutions

| Solution | Description | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Books/water bottle** | Prop phone at desired angle (E01) | $0 |
| **Phone tripod** | Basic tripod with phone mount | $15-25 |
| **Flexible tripod** | Gorilla-style, wraps around objects for unique angles | $20-40 |
| **Full-size tripod** | For consistent studio setup | $40-100 |
| **Gimbal** | For smooth walking/movement shots | $100-300 |

### Dedicated Tripod Spots (E01)
Remove friction from daily B-roll filming by setting up permanent tripod positions:
- Kitchen/coffee area (morning routine B-roll)
- Desk/workspace (working B-roll)
- Near window with best natural light (main filming spot)

## Platform-Specific Export Settings (E04a)

| Platform | Resolution | Aspect Ratio | Frame Rate | Key Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Instagram Reels** | 1080 x 1920 | 9:16 | 30fps | MUST export 1080p; posting 4K directly causes quality drop from IG compression |
| **TikTok** | 1080 x 1920 | 9:16 | 30fps | Standard; 4K supported but unnecessary |
| **YouTube Shorts** | 1080 x 1920 | 9:16 | 30fps | Same as TikTok |
| **YouTube Long** | 1920 x 1080 (or 3840 x 2160) | 16:9 | 24 or 30fps | 4K preferred if available |
| **LinkedIn** | 1080 x 1080 or 1080 x 1920 | 1:1 or 9:16 | 30fps | Square performs well in feed |
