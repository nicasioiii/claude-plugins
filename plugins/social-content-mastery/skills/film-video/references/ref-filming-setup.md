# Filming Setup Reference

## Equipment by Budget Tier

### Tier 1: $0 -- Phone Only (Start Here)
- **Camera:** Smartphone (iPhone or Android -- both fine)
- **Tripod:** Stack of books, lean phone against a wall
- **Lighting:** Natural window light (position yourself facing window)
- **Audio:** Phone built-in mic in a quiet room
- **Editing:** CapCut (free), InShot (free), or TikTok native editor

**Who used this successfully:**
- Mino Lee grew his initial audience with phone-only setup
- Jimmy Farley (1M+ TikTok) films on phone
- Ryan Magin plans every video in advance, films on phone

**Key insight (E02 Gusten):** Lighting > camera for video quality. "I can shoot with an iPhone and get great quality if lighting is right. Conversely, most expensive camera with bad lighting = bad quality."

## Lighting Priority Hierarchy

### Importance Ranking
1. **Key light** -- Main light source illuminating your face. Most critical.
2. **Fill light** -- Softer light opposite key light. Reduces harsh shadows.
3. **Backlight/Rim light** -- Separates subject from background. Creates depth.
4. **Background light** -- Illuminates backdrop. Adds dimension.

### Natural Light Setup (Best and Free)
- Position yourself FACING a window
- Window acts as a large, soft key light
- Avoid having window behind you (creates silhouette)
- Overcast days = best natural light (diffused, no harsh shadows)
- Morning or late afternoon light is warmest and most flattering
- Avoid direct midday sun (harsh shadows under eyes/nose)

### Artificial Light Setup
- **Ring light:** Good all-in-one starter light. Even illumination for talking-head. Can cause circular catchlights in eyes.
- **Softbox:** More natural, diffused light. Professional look. Requires more space.
- **LED panels:** Versatile, adjustable color temperature. Good for travel.
- **Three-point lighting:** Key (45 degrees from subject) + Fill (opposite side, lower intensity) + Backlight (behind subject). Standard professional setup (E03 100K IG Course).

### Common Lighting Mistakes
- Overhead fluorescent lighting (green cast, unflattering shadows)
- Mixed color temperatures (warm tungsten + cool daylight)
- Backlighting without key light (creates silhouette)
- Over-lighting (flat, washed-out look)

## Audio Setup Guide

### Audio Priority (All Instructors Agree)
Audio is the #1 production element. Viewers tolerate mediocre video. They do NOT tolerate bad audio.

### Microphone Types
| Type | Best For | Examples | Price Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lavalier (lapel) | Talking-head, interviews | Rode Wireless Go, Boya BY-M1 | $20-$250 |
| Shotgun | On-camera, YouTube | Rode VideoMic Pro, VideoMicro | $60-$300 |
| USB condenser | Desk recording, podcasts | Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica AT2020 | $50-$150 |
| Built-in phone | Quick content, run-and-gun | -- | $0 |

### Audio Quality Checklist
- [ ] Room is quiet (no AC hum, traffic, appliances)
- [ ] No echo (soft furnishings absorb sound; hard walls bounce it)
- [ ] Mic positioned close to mouth (6-12 inches for lav; 1-3 feet for shotgun)
- [ ] Test recording before full shoot (listen on headphones)
- [ ] Monitor audio levels during recording if possible
- [ ] Avoid volume spikes (consistent distance from mic)

### Post-Production Audio
- AI editing tools can improve audio quality after recording (E08 Anton Mehto)
- Noise removal in post (Premiere Pro, Audacity, or AI tools)
- Echo cleanup with software
- Volume normalization

## Camera Settings Reference

### Short-Form Video (Instagram, TikTok)
| Setting | Recommended Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Resolution | 4K (export at 1080p) | Allows crop/zoom in post |
| Frame rate | 30fps | 60fps appears choppy on IG/TikTok apps (E01 Mino) |
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 (vertical) | Full-screen mobile experience |
| Exposure | Auto or slightly bright | Bright = more inviting on mobile |
| White balance | Match lighting (auto for beginners) | Consistency across clips |

### Long-Form Video (YouTube)
| Setting | Recommended Value | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Resolution | 4K | Future-proofs content; allows reframing |
| Frame rate | 24fps (cinematic) or 30fps | 24fps for professional feel |
| Aspect ratio | 16:9 (horizontal) | YouTube standard |
| Exposure | Manual | Consistent across session |
| White balance | Manual | Consistent across session |

### B-Roll Settings
- Same settings as A-roll for consistency
- Slow-motion B-roll: 60fps or 120fps (then slow down in edit)
- Always shoot B-roll at highest resolution available

## Talking-Head Framing Guide

### Standard Setup
- Camera at eye level or slightly above
- Subject fills 60-70% of frame vertically
- Eyes at upper third line (rule of thirds)
- Slight offset from center (not dead center) for more dynamic composition
- Headroom: small gap between top of head and frame edge
- Look at the camera LENS, not the screen (E04 InstaCoach Mike)

### Common Framing Mistakes
- Camera too low (unflattering up-nose angle)
- Too much headroom (subject looks small, unused space)
- Subject too far from camera (feels disconnected)
- Cluttered background (distracts from message)

### Creating Visual Variety from One Position (E04 InstaCoach Mike)
- Shoot wide, then crop/zoom in post for medium and close-up
- Multiple "camera angles" from a single take using post-production reframing
- Slight position changes between videos in batch sessions

## Batch Recording Workflow

### Pre-Batch Preparation
1. All scripts/outlines written, printed, or loaded on teleprompter
2. Wardrobe selected and laid out (multiple outfit options for visual variety)
3. Equipment charged, memory cleared
4. Set/background prepared with brand elements

### Filming Day Schedule (Example for Short-Form)
| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0:00-0:15 | Setup: lighting, camera, audio, background |
| 0:15-0:25 | Warmup routine (breathing, tongue twisters, energy calibration) |
| 0:25-1:30 | Film A-roll for 5-8 videos (change shirt/angle between each) |
| 1:30-2:00 | Dedicated B-roll shooting session |
| 2:00-2:15 | Organize and label all footage |

### Filming Day Schedule (Example for YouTube Long-Form)
| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0:00-0:30 | Setup + warmup |
| 0:30-2:00 | Film Video 1 (sections with breaks) |
| 2:00-2:15 | Outfit/set change |
| 2:15-3:45 | Film Video 2 |
| 3:45-4:15 | B-roll and supplementary shots |
| 4:15-4:30 | Organize footage + backup |

### Storage and Organization (E08 Sunny)
- **Google Drive system:** Organized by video, with subfolders for A-roll, B-roll, graphics
- **Triple backup:** Local drive + cloud storage + external hard drive
- **File naming convention:** [Date]_[VideoTitle]_[Take#]

## Studio Setup Tiers (E08 Personal Brand X)

### Simple YouTube Travel Studio
- Laptop or phone on tripod
- Portable LED light
- Wireless mic
- Clean wall or portable backdrop
- Total: Under $500

### Home Office Studio ($1,000-$3,000)
- Dedicated desk or filming area
- Two-point lighting (key + fill)
- Camera on tripod
- Audio: shotgun or lavalier
- Simple backdrop or styled bookshelf

### Dream Home Office ($10,000+)
- Multiple cameras (A + B angle)
- Three-point lighting with diffusion
- Acoustic treatment
- Teleprompter
- Dedicated editing workstation
- Branded set design

**Emphasis from all instructors:** You do NOT need an expensive setup to start. Start with what you have. Upgrade as revenue justifies investment.
