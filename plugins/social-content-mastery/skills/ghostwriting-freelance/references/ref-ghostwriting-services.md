## Sales Call Script (9 Steps Expanded)

### Step 1: Opening (2 minutes)
"Thanks for hopping on. Before we dive in, I'd love to hear a bit about what you're working on and what prompted you to reach out."

### Step 2: Identify the Problem (5 minutes)
"What's your biggest challenge right now when it comes to [content/writing/online presence]?"
Follow-up: "How long has this been a problem?"
Follow-up: "What have you tried so far?"

### Step 3: Quantify the Pain (3 minutes)
"What is this costing you? In terms of time, missed opportunities, or revenue?"
"If nothing changes in the next 6 months, where does that leave you?"

### Step 4: Present the Vision (3 minutes)
"Imagine if [specific outcome]. What would that mean for your business?"
Paint the picture of life AFTER the problem is solved.

### Step 5: Show the Path (5 minutes)
"Here's specifically how I would solve this for you..."
Walk through your productized service step by step.

### Step 6: Share Proof (3 minutes)
"I've done this for [similar client type]. Here's what happened..."
Share 1-2 specific case studies or results.

### Step 7: Present the Offer (3 minutes)
"Based on everything we've discussed, here's what I'd recommend..."
Present specific package, deliverables, and pricing.
**Price anchor first:** "Your email list could generate $X if we improved open rates by Y%. My fee is $Z/month."

### Step 8: Handle Objections (5 minutes)
Common objections and responses:
- "Too expensive" --> Reframe as investment vs. cost (price anchoring)
- "Need to think about it" --> "What specifically do you need to think about?"
- "Can I see samples?" --> Share 2-3 relevant examples
- "What if it doesn't work?" --> Share your process, results, and any guarantees

### Step 9: Close (2 minutes)
"Based on our conversation, would you like to move forward with [package name]?"
If yes: "Great, I'll send over the agreement today."
If not yet: "No problem. What would need to be true for you to feel confident moving forward?"

## Cold Outreach Templates

### DM Template (Warm -- They Engaged With Your Content)
```
Hey [Name], your post about [specific topic] caught my eye.
I noticed [specific observation about their content/presence].
I actually help [type of person] with [specific outcome] --
would love to share a few ideas if you're open to it.
```

### Email Template (Cold -- Research-Based)
```
Subject: Quick idea for [their company/brand] content

Hi [Name],

I've been following [company] and noticed [specific observation --
e.g., "your LinkedIn hasn't been updated in 3 months despite your
recent product launch"].

I specialize in [your service] for [their industry], and I had a
couple ideas that might help with [specific problem].

Would you be open to a 15-minute call this week? No pitch -- just
sharing what I've seen work for similar companies.

[Your name]
[Your bio one-liner]
```

```
Hi [Name], just circling back on my note from last week.

I put together a quick audit of [their specific content/platform]
and found [one specific, actionable insight].

Happy to share the full breakdown on a quick call if helpful.

[Your name]
```

## Scaling Model Benchmarks

### Solo Ghostwriter Growth Path

| Month | Clients | Revenue/Client | Total MRR | Key Action |
|-------|---------|---------------|-----------|------------|
| 1-2 | 1-2 | $1,500-$2,500 | $3,000-$5,000 | Land first clients from network |
| 3-4 | 3-4 | $2,500-$3,500 | $7,500-$14,000 | Raise prices, get referrals |
| 5-6 | 4-6 | $3,000-$5,000 | $12,000-$30,000 | Standardize process, niche down |
| 7-12 | 5-8 | $3,500-$5,000 | $17,500-$40,000 | Max solo capacity |

### Agency Growth Path (After Solo Max)

| Stage | Team | Clients | Revenue | Key Action |
|-------|------|---------|---------|------------|
| Solo max | Just you | 5-8 | $30K-$40K/mo | Hit capacity ceiling |
| First hire | You + 1 writer | 8-12 | $40K-$60K/mo | Hire first writer, train on your process |
| Small agency | You + 2-3 writers | 12-20 | $50K-$70K/mo | Systematize everything |
| Growth | You + team | 20+ | $70K+ /mo | You become strategist, not writer |

### Efficiency Multiplier
- Specializing in one industry + one service type = 2-3x output per hour
- AI-assisted research: 15 seconds vs. 15-20 minutes manual (60x faster)
- AI-assisted drafting: 17 minutes per newsletter on unfamiliar topics (with years of experience)
- Systemizing onboarding: reduces per-client setup from days to hours

## The Advil Test (When to Say No to Clients)

Before accepting a new client, ask:
1. Is this client in my target niche? (Or will it require industry switching costs?)
2. Is this a service I've productized? (Or will it be custom/one-off work?)
3. Can I deliver exceptional results? (Or am I learning on their dime?)
4. Will this client refer more clients like themselves? (Or is it a dead end?)

If any answer is unfavorable, saying yes = taking Advil. Short-term relief, long-term damage to positioning and efficiency.
