## The Reels Algorithm (How Instagram Distributes Content)

### Tiered Distribution Model (IG Black File / InstaCoach Mike)

Instagram pushes reels through progressive audience tiers:

1. **Tier 1 (50-100 viewers):** Algorithm shows your reel to a small initial batch
2. **Metric evaluation:** Compares your metrics against similar content in your niche sphere
3. **Tier 2 (~1,000 views):** If metrics match or exceed niche average, pushes to next tier
4. **Re-evaluation at each tier:** Continues pushing as long as metrics remain constant or improve
5. **Plateau:** When metrics dip below niche average, reach stops expanding

**Why accounts plateau at specific view counts (e.g., 3,000 views):**
- Algorithm categorizes you within your niche and determines a "standing"
- To break through: increase quality, aim for metrics even 0.5% above average
- Algorithm detects upward trends and rewards them with next-tier distribution

### Multiple Distribution Windows

Every reel gets 2-3 chances to blow up:
- **Window 1:** First week after posting
- **Window 2:** 3-5 weeks later
- **Window 3:** ~2 months later

Implication: Do NOT delete underperforming reels quickly. They may resurge.

### The Single Most Important Metric

**Content-view-time (watch time) is THE most important metric.** Aim for average watch time above 100% (meaning the average viewer watches the reel more than once). The algorithm is optimized to find content that keeps people on the platform longest.

## The ACAB Content Formula

Every reel should contain all four elements:

| Element | Purpose | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| **A -- Attention** | Strong hook, speak to emotions, text + voice + visual | First 0-2 seconds |
| **C -- Curiosity** | Keep them wondering, create anticipation | Throughout |
| **A -- Action** | Rewatchability, full watch-through, CTA | End (max 2 sec) |
| **B -- Benefit** | Deliver on the hook's promise, make it shareable | Overall |

**Critical rule:** If you can only nail ONE element, always choose the Attention hook. Reels that underperform almost always have a bad hook or no hook at all.

## Content Mix by Growth Stage

| Stage | Recommended Mix |
|---|---|
| 0 followers / starting out | 90% educational, 10% other |
| Few thousand, want first leads | Mix promotional + entertaining |
| Established, steady growth | 30% educational + 30% expertise-personality + 30% promotional |

**The 70/30 Rule for Content Ideation:**
- 70% content that's already in demand (pre-validated through competitive research)
- 30% your own original ideas
- If no time for original ideas: 100% pre-validated content is fine

**Best-performing content combos:**
1. Educational + Entertaining = highest performance
2. Personal + Entertaining = good
3. Promotional + Entertaining = good for monetization reels
4. Educational + Personal = solid for tribe-building

Entertainment should be in ALMOST every reel. Mix categories freely.

## Five Content Types for Reels

1. **Educational** -- Best for building authority. 100% value, no fluff. Still needs entertainment factor.
2. **Entertaining** -- Best viral potential. Must utilize trends. Recreate viral reels in your niche with your own touch.
3. **Inspirational / Personal** -- NOT designed to blow up. Purpose: build connection, create 1,000 true fans. Use every once in a while.
4. **Promotional** -- Grow revenue or email list. Use sparingly. STILL needs a hook. Don't be overly salesy.
5. **Behind-the-Scenes** -- Critical for connection. Shows the human side. Builds know/like/trust factor.

## The Rule of Seven (Views to Followers)

It takes an average of 7 interactions with your brand before a viewer follows or takes action. Sometimes less (strong positioning), sometimes more. This is why posting frequency matters -- more posts = more touchpoints.

**Follower Conversion Rate = New followers / Views**

If views are high but followers are low, check:
- Content not valuable enough for follow-worthy perception
- Profile not optimized (bio, highlights, grid)
- No clear CTA
- Content too broad (attracts random viewers, not ideal audience)

## Instagram Metrics & Benchmarks

### Under 1,000 Views

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Watch Time (Content View Time) | 85% of reach |
| Likes | 10% of reach |
| Saves | 2% of reach |
| Shares | 4-5% of reach |
| Comments | Not significant at this level |

### Over 1,000 Views

Metrics become slightly less demanding as view count increases. But always aim for the under-1,000 benchmarks as your target.

### 4 Key Metrics to Track Weekly

1. **Views / Reach** -- unique accounts that saw your content
2. **Engagement Rate** -- (likes + comments + shares + saves) / reach
3. **Follower Conversion Rate** -- new followers gained / total views
4. **Average Watch Time** -- percentage of reel watched on average

Track weekly. Look for trends, not individual reel performance. One bad reel means nothing; a downward trend over 2-3 weeks means something needs to change.

## Hashtag Strategy

Hashtags are a minor signal, not a growth lever:
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags (especially effective under 5K followers)
- Never spend more than 2 minutes on hashtags
- Never let hashtag research prevent you from posting
- Mix niche-specific and broader-but-relevant hashtags

> **Instructor Disagreement -- Hashtag Importance:**
> - Jimmy Farley: Hashtags don't matter, never spend more than 2 min
> - 100K IG Course: 3-5 hashtags, mainly useful under 5K followers
> - **Reconciliation:** Use 3-5 hashtags. Minor signal. Don't overthink it.

## Posting Frequency

**Default recommendation:** 1 reel per day minimum (2 if quality allows).

**Efficiency system for non-full-time creators:**
- Batch content creation (dedicate 1-2 days per week to filming)
- Film 5-7 reels in one session
- Edit in batches, schedule or queue for posting
- Not every reel needs to be a masterpiece -- quick takes are fine

**30-Day Reels Challenge:**
1. Post 1 reel per day for 30 consecutive days
2. After publishing, interact with 8 niche-specific hashtags
3. Post 4 meaningful comments morning and 4 evening
4. Extend to 60 days or make permanent

## Profile Optimization Checklist

- **Bio:** Communicate who you help + what transformation you deliver. Keep first line short and clear.
- **Profile picture:** Clear, well-lit photo of your face (or brand logo for business accounts)
- **Highlights:** Organize by content pillar or audience question
- **Pinned reels:** Pin your 3 best-performing reels
- **Link in bio:** Direct to landing page, lead magnet, or link tree
- **Username:** Searchable and memorable
- **CTA in bio:** Tell visitors what to do next

## Competitive Research Process

**Step 1:** Identify 5-10 competitors with overlapping target audience (3 tiers: big influencers, same-level creators, small emerging)

**Step 2:** Track top 3 performing reels of each competitor. Identify 3-5 keywords in your niche. Track top 3 performing reels for each keyword.

**Step 3:** Research TikTok first -- trends hit TikTok first, then migrate to Reels. Set up a "one true fan" dummy profile for unbiased research.

**Step 4:** Use external tools: AnswerThePublic, Quora, Google Trends, ExplodingTopics, TalkBoard.com (trending audio weekly)

**Step 5:** For trending audio, check TalkBoard.com weekly. Click on sounds from performing reels to verify the sound itself is trending (not just the creator).

**From now on, consume as a CREATOR, not a consumer.** Every reel you watch: analyze the hook, storytelling, structure. Save reels into an "Inspiration" list.

## Caption Strategy

**Why captions matter:** Increase conversion rate, provide deeper value, improve engagement metrics (time on post, saves, shares).

**Caption structure:**
1. **First sentence = HOOK** (short enough to read without clicking "read more")
2. **Body = Value delivery** (answer the hook, provide tips/details)
3. **End = CTA** (follow, save, share, comment, DM)

**Three caption opening styles:**
1. **Bold Statement:** "A lot of followers don't equal money"
2. **Question:** "How often should you post to grow on Instagram?"
3. **Intrigue:** "I reached 20 million people with my Reels over the last 90 days"

## When to Delete vs. Keep Reels

**General rule:** Don't delete reels. They get 2-3 distribution windows.

**Exceptions -- consider deleting if:**
- Fundamentally misrepresents your brand
- Contains incorrect information
- 3-5 months with zero traction

**After deleting:** Wait 3-5 months. Don't re-upload exact same video (IG detects this). Create a slightly different version with same core content. Change the hook or angle.

## Stories Strategy

- Share reels to Stories to boost initial view count
- Use polls and questions about your reel topics to warm up audience
- Stories drive reel views -- algorithm favors this signal
- Behind-the-scenes stories build know/like/trust factor
- Use story highlights to organize by content pillar

## Instructor Disagreements

> **Posting Frequency:**
> - Mino Lee / InstaCoach Mike: Post daily, no matter what
> - 100K IG Course: Daily for first 100 days, then sustainable cadence
> - **Reconciliation:** Daily for first 100 days (skill-building). After that, 5-7x/week if quality allows. Never sacrifice quality for quantity.

> **Niche Breadth on IG:**
> - 100K IG Course: Expand niche for more reach on Instagram
> - Sunny Lenarduzzi: Niche down aggressively
> - **Reconciliation:** Broaden hooks/topics for reach, but keep specific value delivery. Niche down in your bio/positioning, broaden in individual content pieces.

> **Shadow Bans on Instagram:**
> - Frabrand: Shadow banning IS real on Instagram
> - **Resolution:** Instagram has confirmed shadow-restricting content. Check Account Status in settings. Actionable advice is the same regardless: keep posting quality content, check for policy violations.
