# Instagram Tactics Reference

## 7 Hook Types for Reels

### 1. Text Overlay Hook (Most Common for Educational)
- Text appears at frame zero before video starts playing
- 90% of the time for educational content, text should be there immediately
- Use visually pleasing text: different fonts, colors, varied sizes
- Example: "Fashion secrets all models use" -- target audience identified + "secrets" creates curiosity

### 2. Caption First-Line Hook
- "Above the fold" first line of caption serves as secondary hook
- If visual isn't 100% clear, the mind glances down to the caption
- This is your second chance to hook them

### 3. Visual / Aesthetic Hook
- Highly visual reels that are inherently gratifying to watch
- No text needed -- the visual IS the hook
- Works for: satisfying processes, beautiful locations, revealing content

### 4. Revealing / Working Progress Hook
- Content that reveals itself over time; the journey is equally important as the end result
- Example: Art being created, food being prepared, transformation

### 5. Fast-Cut Hook
- Cuts slightly faster than the brain can process
- Prevents boredom, breaks pattern recognition

### 6. "Wait For It" Hook
- Text/voice telling viewer to wait for the end
- Use sparingly -- overuse kills trust

### 7. Multi-Angle Hook
- Text says one thing, voice says something complementary but different
- Hits viewer from different brain pathways simultaneously
- Auditory + visual + caption = maximum hook penetration

## Hook Formulas for Educational Content

- "How to [desired outcome]"
- "5 hacks you never knew about [topic]"
- "Top 5 [topic] hacks"
- "Here's how I managed to [impressive result]"
- "Here's why you DON'T [negative framing of same content]"
- Bold statement: "A lot of followers don't equal money"
- Question: "How often should you post to grow on Instagram?"
- Intrigue: "I reached 20 million people with my Reels over the last 90 days"

**[CONTRARIAN] Same content, different hook = different audience.** If "How to look good in every photo" performs average, try "Here's why you don't look good in your photos" -- same content, negative framing, different emotional trigger.

## Competitive Research Process (5 Steps)

**Step 1: Identify Competitors**
- Minimum 5 competitors (ideally 10) with overlapping target audience
- If you can't find 5: your niche may not be viable
- Three tiers: Big influencers (most important), Same-level creators, Small emerging creators

**Step 2: Track Top-Performing Content**
- Write down 3 top-performing reels/TikToks of EACH competitor
- Identify 3-5 keywords in your niche
- Track top 3 performing reels for each keyword

**Step 3: Platform Research**
- **TikTok first** -- trends hit TikTok first, then migrate to Reels
- Set up a "one true fan" dummy profile for unbiased research
- Browse IG reels, hashtags, explore page

**Step 4: External Research Tools**
- AnswerThePublic.com -- content idea generator
- Quora -- questions your niche audience asks
- Google Trends -- spot trending topics
- ExplodingTopics.com -- spot emerging topics
- TalkBoard.com -- weekly updated trending TikTok songs

**Step 5: Trending Songs Research**
- Check TalkBoard.com weekly
- Check official @creators Instagram account for trend reports
- Click on sounds from performing reels to verify trend status
- If only 1-2 reels blew up with that sound, it's probably not a trending sound

## Trending Audio Early Bird Checklist

1. Check TalkBoard.com weekly for trending TikTok songs (they migrate to Reels within a week)
2. Check @creators on Instagram for official trend reports
3. When you find a performing reel, click on its sound
4. Check if other reels with that sound also perform well
5. If yes: create content with that audio within 24-48 hours (early adoption = more reach)
6. If only 1-2 reels blew up: probably not a trending sound, skip it

## Hashtag Strategy (Detailed)

- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags per post
- Mix: 1-2 niche-specific + 1-2 broader-but-relevant + 1 brand/location
- Most effective under 5K followers for discovery
- Never spend more than 2 minutes selecting hashtags
- Never let hashtag research prevent you from posting
- Check what hashtags top competitors use

## S4S (Share for Share) Growth Strategy (Nathan Chan)

**The #1 strategy that grew Foundr to 2M+ followers:**
- 0 to 10K followers in 2 weeks
- 0 to 500K in 1 year
- 0 to 1M in 2 years

### Process
1. Find accounts with similar audience overlap (don't have to be direct competitors)
2. Look for fair exchange in value
3. If follower counts don't match, compensate elsewhere (send product, offer extra post)
4. Each account shares one piece of the other's content
5. Both benefit from exposure to each other's audience

### Results from good S4S
- Extra 1,000 to 5,000 followers per day above daily average
- Key: good crossover of audience interest levels

### Action Items
- Reach out to 5-10 accounts for S4S
- Track which partners drive the most followers
- Build long-term relationships with best performers
- Document everything in a spreadsheet
- Can be systematized with a VA

## Influencer Outreach Machine (Nathan Chan)

### Step 1: Build Influencer List
- Use Instagram search, hashtags, explore page
- Spreadsheet: username, follower count, engagement rate, email, status

### Step 2: Find Contact Info
- Check bio for email > Check website > DM as last resort

### Step 3: Outreach
- Personalized email (not copy-paste)
- Reference specific content they've posted
- Clear value proposition for them
- Make it easy to say yes

### Step 4: Follow-Up
- Follow up 3-5 times over 2-3 weeks
- Each follow-up adds new value or angle
- Most responses come from follow-ups, not initial outreach

### Step 5: Scale with VA
- Once process is proven, hire VA for list building and initial outreach
- You handle relationship management and creative decisions

## Influencer Pricing Guide

**General benchmark:** $10-$15 per 1,000 followers for a single post (varies widely)

**Factors affecting price:** Engagement rate, niche (luxury/finance = premium), content quality, exclusivity, usage rights, number of deliverables

**Cost-saving strategies:**
- Product gifting (works for smaller influencers)
- Affiliate/commission model (pay per sale)
- S4S (zero cost)
- Long-term ambassador deals (lower per-post cost)
- Micro-influencers (1K-10K) often accept product only

**[CONTRARIAN] Don't judge by follower count alone.** A 10K account with 8% engagement beats a 100K account with 0.5% engagement. Check: real comments, saves, shares, audience match.

## Top 15 Engagement Hacks

1. Ask questions in captions (drives comments)
2. Use "Save this for later" CTA (boosts save metric)
3. Create shareable content (relatable or useful enough to send to friends)
4. Reply to every comment (algorithm favors it)
5. Share reels to Stories (drives initial view count)
6. Post carousels between reels (variety keeps audience engaged)
7. Go Live (signals activity to algorithm)
8. Use trending audio (gets pushed to more explore pages)
9. Engage with accounts in your niche (8 hashtags/day, meaningful comments)
10. Pin your best 3 reels (first impression matters)
11. Collaborate with similar-sized accounts (cross-pollination)
12. Create controversy/debates (drives comments -- use carefully)
13. Use polls in Stories about reel topics (warms up audience)
14. Post behind-the-scenes content (builds connection)
15. Create "Part 2" content (people follow for next installment)

## Top 10 Dos and Don'ts of Viral Reels

### DOs
1. Use text overlays on educational content (first frame)
2. Keep videos under 15 seconds when starting out
3. Use trending audio
4. Research competitors before creating
5. Include a CTA in every reel
6. Show your face when possible
7. Make content rewatchable
8. Post consistently (daily)
9. Engage with audience after posting
10. Analyze metrics weekly

### DON'Ts
1. Repost TikToks with watermarks (algorithm PENALIZES)
2. Have dull moments in your reel
3. Start reels without a hook
4. Say "Let's start" or "Alright let's begin" -- just START
5. Make reels longer than needed
6. Be overly promotional
7. Use the same CTA every time
8. Ignore analytics
9. Copy content exactly (always add your own touch)
10. Get discouraged by one underperforming reel

## Faceless Reel Ideas (9 Types)

1. Screen recordings with text-to-speech voiceover
2. Photo collages with transitions and reactions
3. Work-in-progress / process videos (art, cooking, DIY)
4. Point-of-view (POV) shots
5. Text-on-screen listicles with trending audio
6. B-roll footage with voiceover narration
7. Product/item showcases with hand movements only

## Profile Optimization Checklist (Detailed)

### Bio Framework
- Line 1: Who you are / what you do (keyword-rich)
- Line 2: Who you help / what transformation
- Line 3: Social proof or credibility
- Line 4: CTA (free resource, link, next step)

### Highlights Strategy
- Organize by: About Me, Results/Proof, Free Resources, FAQ, Services
- Update regularly with fresh content
- First 4 highlights are most visible -- prioritize

### Grid Aesthetic
- Consistent visual theme (colors, filters, composition)
- Pin 3 best-performing reels at top
- Mix: reels, carousels, single images for variety

### Username & Name Field
- Username: searchable, memorable, no unnecessary characters
- Name field: include primary keyword (e.g., "Sarah | Instagram Coach")

## Posting Frequency & Timing

**Frequency:** 1 reel/day minimum, 1-2/day optimal

**Finding YOUR best time:**
1. Check Instagram Insights for when followers are most active
2. Post 30 min to 1 hour before peak activity
3. Test different times over 2-3 weeks
4. Track which times correlate with best initial metrics

**Batch Content System:**
- Dedicate 1-2 days per week to filming
- Film 5-7 reels in one session
- Edit in batches
- Schedule or queue for posting
- Use templates and repeatable formats

## 30-Day Reels Challenge Rules

1. Post 1 reel per day for 30 consecutive days
2. If you skip a day, restart from Day 1
3. After publishing, interact with 8 niche-specific hashtags
4. Post 4 meaningful comments in the morning, 4 in the evening
5. No repetitive/bot-like comments -- be genuine
6. Extend to 60 days or make permanent habit

## Algorithm Hacks (Content Tricks)

1. **Photo Collage with Reaction** -- photos getting faster, ending with surprise reaction
2. **Work-in-Progress** -- keeps people watching for payoff
3. **"Wait For It"** -- text teasing end result (use sparingly)
4. **Nonsense Content** -- confusing content that drives rewatches
5. **Plot Twists** -- set up expectation, break it
6. **Text-to-Speech** -- trending IG feature, performs well
7. **"Slightly Too Short"** -- visual reels ending just before satisfaction, creating rewatch craving

## Stories Strategy (5X Views Hack)

- Share every new reel to Stories to boost initial views
- Use interactive stickers: polls, questions, quizzes about reel topics
- Post 3-5 stories per day to stay visible
- Use story highlights to organize by content pillar
- Behind-the-scenes stories build know/like/trust
- Polls warm up audience for upcoming content topics
