# Offer Design & Pricing Deep-Dive Reference

## COACHING OFFER DESIGN (E01 Mino Lee)

### The 2-Year Rule
- Help someone who is 2 years behind you in your niche's journey
- You do not need to be the world's top expert
- You need to deeply understand the specific problem you solve
- Your unique advantage may be that you are a BEGINNER -- you relate to beginners better than experts do

### Niche Down on the Problem
- Not "fitness coaching" but "curb binge eating recovery coaching"
- Not "business coaching" but "helping freelance designers land their first $5K client"
- Specificity increases perceived value and reduces competition

### Competition Is a Good Sign
- Competition means the niche works and people are willing to pay
- "You are not competing against other fitness coaches; you are competing against the couch"
- If nobody else is selling in your niche, that is a warning sign, not an opportunity

### ICP (Ideal Client Persona) Exercise
1. **Age range:** Recommend a 2-year range (e.g., 25-27, not "20-40")
2. **Specific problem:** What exact problem do they wake up thinking about?
3. **Enjoyment test:** Who do you genuinely ENJOY helping? Do not just chase the most lucrative client
4. **Above-and-beyond test:** Can you see yourself going above and beyond for this person without it feeling like work?

## THREE SALES FRAMEWORK EXPANDED (E01)

### Sale 1: The Mechanism/Vehicle Is the Best
**Purpose:** Convince the prospect that your METHOD is superior.

How to build this sale:
- Name your system with a branded methodology (e.g., "The Content Flywheel System")
- Explain what makes it different from what they have tried before
- List specific boxes your system checks that alternatives do not
- Use contrast: "Most coaches teach X. My system does Y instead because Z."

Content that makes Sale 1:
- Framework breakdowns showing your unique process
- Behind-the-scenes of your methodology
- "Why most [niche] advice is wrong" content
- Case studies showing the method working

### Sale 2: You Are the Best Authority
**Purpose:** Convince the prospect that YOU specifically should teach them.

Competitive advantage angles:
- **Personal results:** "I went from X to Y using this exact system"
- **Client results:** "147 students have used this to achieve Z"
- **Relatable backstory:** "I was in your exact position 2 years ago"
- **Unique positioning:** Young, bootstrapped, done it in multiple niches, specific industry experience

Content that makes Sale 2:
- Personal story content showing your journey
- Client testimonials and case studies with specific numbers
- Behind-the-scenes of your life/business showing results
- "Here is what I learned after [milestone]" content

### Sale 3: The Customer Believes It Is Possible for THEM
**Purpose:** Overcome their internal objection that "it worked for you but it will not work for me."

How to build this sale:
- Tell stories of people SIMILAR TO THEM who succeeded
- Use specific numbers, dates, pictures to make it feel real
- Address their limiting beliefs directly
- Show diverse success stories (different ages, backgrounds, experience levels)

Content that makes Sale 3:
- Student spotlight content with before/after
- "If they can do it, you can too" narratives
- FAQ content addressing common objections
- Live coaching clips showing transformation in action

## PRICING STRATEGY (E01, E03a)

### Low-Ticket Community Model ($59-69/month)
**Pros:**
- Higher volume, more fulfilling
- Easier sell (low commitment)
- Recurring revenue with low churn if content is good
- Great for building community and social proof

**Best for:**
- Creators building authority
- Niches where audience has lower purchasing power
- Creators who enjoy group interaction
- Early-stage monetization

### Mid-Ticket Course Model ($200-$997)
**Pros:**
- Moderate volume needed
- Can be evergreen (sell while you sleep)
- One-time creation with ongoing revenue

**Best for:**
- Proven transformation methodology
- Strong content library to repurpose
- Creators who prefer building products over live coaching

### High-Ticket Coaching Model ($2K-$15K)
**Pros:**
- Lower volume needed (5-10 clients = significant income)
- Higher per-client revenue
- Deep relationships build powerful testimonials

**Best for:**
- Deep expertise with proven client results
- Creators who enjoy 1:1 or small group work
- Niches with high-value outcomes (business, career, health)

**Requires:**
- More social proof than low-ticket
- Application funnel with qualification
- Booked calls (do not sell high-ticket via checkout page alone)

### Pricing Progression Strategy
1. Start with lowest price you are comfortable with
2. Increase price as you complete the product and gather results
3. Use early bird pricing for launch, then authentic price increase
4. Never discount retroactively -- only offer bonuses

## REVENUE MODEL MATH WITH EXAMPLES (E03a)

### Example 1: Course Creator
- $497 course
- Need 60 sales/month = $30K/month
- 60 sales from 5,000 email list = 1.2% conversion (achievable)
- Build email list via lead magnets from 20K+ followers

### Example 2: Coaching Business
- $3,000/month retainer coaching
- Need 10 clients = $30K/month
- With low churn, only need 1 new client/month to replace attrition
- 1 client from 20 discovery calls = 5% close rate (conservative)

### Example 3: Affiliate + Info Product Stack
- Affiliate income: $1K/day from recurring SaaS referrals
- Info product: $1K/day from $97 course (11 sales/day)
- Combined: $60K/month from two streams
- Risk diversification: if one stream dips, the other sustains

### Example 4: Digital Template Business
- $27 Notion template
- Need 37 sales/day for $1K/day
- Requires high volume audience (50K+ followers)
- But nearly zero fulfillment cost and infinite scalability

## GAMIFICATION STRATEGY FOR PURCHASES (E03a)

### The Motivation Hack
When you want something expensive, calculate how many clients or sales you need to pay for it:
- $15K in camera gear = 5 coaching clients at $3K each
- $5K vacation = 100 template sales at $50 each
- $2K course investment = 2 affiliate referrals

### Business Expense Reframe
- Business-related purchases are tax write-offs
- Content creation equipment, software, courses = deductible
- This effectively reduces cost by your tax rate (25-40%)

### Implementation
- Write down your next desired purchase
- Calculate exact number of sales/clients needed
- Set it as a 30-day challenge
- Track progress daily
- Celebrate when you earn it through the business

## THE SPECIFICITY PRINCIPLE FOR SALES CONTENT (E01)

### Core Rule
The more oddly specific your stories are, the more they resonate. This is contrarian to the common advice of "keep it broad to reach more people."

### How to Apply
- **Name-drop:** Specific companies, tools, locations
- **Quote conversations:** "She looked at me and said..."
- **Describe scenes:** "You just got out of a test. You got a 73. Your dad is calling..."
- **Use precise numbers:** "$1,247" not "about a thousand dollars"
- **Include dates:** "October 14th, 2023" not "last fall"

### Why It Works
The viewer does not need to have the EXACT same experience. They feel it on a visceral level because they can visualize it. Specificity creates imagery; imagery creates emotion; emotion creates connection; connection creates trust; trust creates sales.

### Content Application
- Personal story videos: describe the exact scene, not the summary
- Testimonials: include specific details (name, location, exact result, timeline)
- Sales pages: use case studies with granular before/after data
- Email sequences: open with a specific moment, not a general statement

## CONVERSION TACTICS (E02, E04a)

### Profile CTAs -- Multiple Locations (E02)
- Custom CTA button (LinkedIn Premium): appears on every post
- Bio headline CTA: DM call-to-action
- Banner CTA: design with soft CTA in Canva
- About section CTAs: links to multiple resources
- Featured section CTAs: pin lead magnet, case study, "start here" post

### Autoplugs (E02)
Add a call-to-action to every piece of content. Rotate CTA types:
- **Direct:** "Full breakdown is in my free guide -- grab it here"
- **Benefits:** "If [problem], this [resource] will [solution]"
- **Social proof:** "This process [specific result] -- details in the guide"
- **Conversation:** "Comment 'guide' and I will DM it"

### Pitch In Public (E02)
- Publish one offer post per month
- Structure: problem > stakes > your method > proof > invite
- Write core offer in one sentence: who it is for, outcome, timeline, price range
- Transparent, authentic selling -- not hidden or manipulative

### Warm DM Script After Value Exchange (E02)
1. Acknowledge context: "Saw your comment on [X]"
2. Ask goal/problem: "What is the target you are aiming for?"
3. Offer help: "I have a [resource] -- want it?"
4. If they engage, offer 15-minute audit (clear agenda and outcome, not a pitch call)
