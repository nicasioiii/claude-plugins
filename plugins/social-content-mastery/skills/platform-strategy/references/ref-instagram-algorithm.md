# Instagram Algorithm Deep-Dive

## How the Instagram Algorithm Actually Works (E04b)

### Process Flow
1. **Scan content** -- analyze visual, audio, text, and metadata
2. **Eliminate** -- low quality, spam, TOS violations, clickbait filtered out
3. **Categorize** -- niche taxonomy like tree branches (arts > photography > wedding photography)
4. **Measure signals** -- track engagement metrics on initial distribution
5. **Rank content** -- assign position (1-500) in each user's feed

### Good Signals (Algorithm Rewards)
- Clicking "...more" on captions
- Following directly from reel
- Sharing/embedding post (on and off Instagram)
- Time spent on post (dwell time)
- Saves
- Comments
- Likes

### Bad Signals (Algorithm Punishes)
- Bounce rate / early exit from video
- Unfollowing after seeing content
- Hiding post
- Reporting post
- Marking as "not interested"
- Closing the app immediately after viewing

### Ranking Formula
- Good signals minus bad signals = content rank
- High positive, low negative = top of feed
- Each feed type has its OWN algorithm: stories, explore, feed, hashtags are separate
- Multiple algorithms per platform -- not one monolithic system

### Algorithm Learning Period
- Instagram needs minimum ~30 posts to understand your niche and target audience
- New accounts and narrow niches require more posts for the algorithm to properly categorize content
- Shadow banning exists -- check Account Status in settings (E04b)

## Content Metrics That Matter (E04b)

### Engagement Ratio Benchmarks
| Metric | Target Ratio | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Likes to reach** | 1:10 | 1,000 reach = 100 likes minimum |
| **Saves to likes** | 1:1 | 100 likes = 100 saves minimum |
| **Shares to likes** | 1:10 | 100 likes = 10 shares minimum |
| **Follower to non-follower reach** | Good = 1:1, Phenomenal = 1:3, Viral = 1:10 | |

### Content Analysis Framework (E04b)
Prefer categorizing content as likeable / saveable / shareable over educational / inspirational / entertaining:
- **Likeable content** = entertainment (likes are the signal)
- **Saveable content** = educational (saves are the signal)
- **Shareable content** = inspirational/relatable (shares are the signal)

### Retention Rate (E04b)
- Retention rate is the #1 metric across ALL platforms (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn, Pinterest)
- For video: average watch time should equal or exceed reel duration
- Replays vs. initial plays: replays > initial = strong signal to algorithm

### Retention Graph Diagnosis (E04b)
| Pattern | Diagnosis | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Massive dip at beginning | Wrong audience targeting OR weak hook | Rewrite the hook; test variants via Trial Reels |
| Huge drop near end | People sense video ending and skip | End quickly, do not overstay welcome |
| Wiggly staircase declining | Boring sections in middle | Cut or speed up sections at dip points |

## Hashtag Strategy (E04a)

### The Contrarian Truth
Hashtags have minimal impact. Justas tested across multiple accounts and found no significant difference between using them and not using them.

### When to Use Hashtags
- ONLY for new accounts under 5,000 followers to help algorithm understand your audience
- After 5K followers, hashtags are less important -- Instagram already knows your audience
- If using: 3-5 big hashtags related to your niche, SAME hashtags consistently (do not change per post -- confuses algorithm)

### Hashtag Rules
- If you do not know what hashtags to use, do not use any -- wrong hashtags do more harm than good
- Check if hashtag is banned: search > tags > look for "recent posts are hidden" text
- Put hashtags at END of description, not beginning
- Never use in hooks or first lines

## Trending Audio Strategy (E04a)

### Early Bird Audio Checklist
A trending audio is worth using if:
- 10K-100K reels have been created under that audio
- High views on recent reels (last 4 weeks) using it
- Reels in same or similar niche are using it successfully

### Growth Indicator
- 500+ new reels/day created under that audio = strong viral potential

### Finding Viral Audio
1. Scroll your feed and notice repeated audio
2. Find anomaly reels on top creators and save that audio
3. Follow accounts/communities that share viral audio discoveries

### Audio Selection Rule
Save audio and check next day -- if growing fast, it has viral potential.

**INSTRUCTOR DISAGREEMENT:** General advice says use trending sounds for algorithmic boost. Mino Lee (E01) says mood first, trend second -- do not use a trending song if it does not match your video's emotion. Wrong-mood trending sound hurts more than no trend helps.

**Resolution:** Trending sound ONLY if it matches video emotion. Never sacrifice content mood for an algorithm trick.

## Description/Caption Strategy (E04a)

### Why Descriptions Matter
- Descriptions help with SEO (Instagram analyzes for content categorization)
- Reading time counts as retention (reading the description counts as reel watch time)
- Descriptions add audience value beyond the video

### Description Structure
Hook > Value > Call to action

### Writing Rules
- Write simple, short sentences. No long paragraphs. Lists work best
- Time spent reading description counts as reel watch time -- this is a key algorithm hack
- CTAs with a question convert better: "Want to lose 10kg in one month? DM me 'guide' and I'll send you..."
- Use gaps between sentences for readability, keep total text concise
- Can place CTA both in middle and end of description

## Posting Time Optimization (E04a)

### When to Post
- Post 30 minutes BEFORE peak follower activity time
- Under 1,000 followers: use general best-times table

### General Best Times (EST, E04a)
| Day | Best Times |
|---|---|
| Monday-Friday | 6-9 AM, 12 PM, 5-7 PM |
| Saturday-Sunday | 9-11 AM |

### How to Find Your Peak Times
- Instagram Insights > Audience > Most Active Times
- Track when followers are online and post 30 minutes before that peak

## Story Algorithm (E04a)

### How Story Ranking Works
- Story algorithm ranks by engagement history -- most interacted-with creators show first in story bar
- Replies to stories = interaction = algorithmic push
- Post engagement stickers (questions, polls, quizzes) to drive interaction

### Story Rules
| Guideline | Detail |
|---|---|
| Ideal count | 3 per day (max 5 -- more = more skips = bad algorithm data) |
| Length | Keep SHORT (3-5 seconds). Convert photos to 3-second videos in CapCut |
| Timing | Post during follower peak activity times |
| Engagement prep | Before posting promotional story, post engagement story couple hours earlier |
| Recovery | If story views drastically drop, stop posting stories for 1-3 days. Instagram gives additional push on return |
| Posting window | Most active hours (e.g., 3PM-6PM) |

### 5x Story Views Strategy (E04a)
- Story reply magnets: offer something free if they reply with keyword X
- Photo stories default to 5 seconds -- trick: convert photo to 3-second video in CapCut, upload as video story (shorter = fewer skips)
- Post engagement stickers before any promotional stories
- Replies and sticker interactions train the algorithm that your stories are high-value

### Story Insights to Track
- Minimize exits/skips
- Maximize completions and replays
- Engagement (replies, sticker interactions) pushes stories to more followers

## Content Split Recommendations

### Instagram Course (E04a)
- **70% Reels** (primary growth driver)
- **25% Stories** (retention of existing followers)
- **5% Regular posts** (grid aesthetics)

### Content Dojo (E04b)
- Start with 33% / 33% / 33% split across reels, carousels, single images
- After 100 days of data, shift to 60% / 20% / 20% for your winning format

### Content Purpose Types (E04a)
1. **Educational**: people watch to end, replay, save. High retention
2. **Relatable**: people share with friends/family. Shareability = virality driver
3. **Entertainment**: funny, memes, fails. Most common on internet
4. **Inspiring**: gym, motivational, short films. Everyone needs motivation

Choose at least one purpose before creating any content piece. Can mix (educational + entertaining).
