## Carousel Structure: 10-Slide Framework (E02 Dickie Bush & Nicolas Cole)

Carousels keep people on-platform longer. The algorithm rewards dwell time with more reach.

| Slide | Purpose | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Big Promise / Hook** | Bold headline that creates curiosity; should stop the scroll |
| 2 | **Stakes** | Problems if you do not act; pain agitation |
| 3 | **Example** | Real-world application or case study |
| 4-7 | **Framework** | Clear steps to follow; the main value delivery |
| 8 | **Checklist** | Common mistakes to avoid |
| 9 | **Quick Win** | One tool or action to try immediately |
| 10 | **Call to Action** | Next step, question, or follow CTA |

### Carousel Creation Workflow (E02)
1. **Write a LinkedIn post** covering the topic in text form
2. **Optimize for Gamma** using AI (paste content into Gamma.app)
3. **Select "Social" format** in Gamma and choose a theme
4. **Download as PDF**
5. **Upload to LinkedIn** as a document post

### Why Carousels Work
- Dwell time: each swipe = more time on post = algorithm signal
- Save-worthy: frameworks and checklists get saved for later reference
- Shareable: structured visual content is easier to share than text posts
- Multiple touchpoints: 10 slides = 10 opportunities to deliver value

## Infographic Types (E02)

Infographics drive saves and shares -- the highest-value engagement signals on any platform.

| Type | Description | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Process flow** | Step-by-step visual showing a sequence | How-to content, workflows, systems |
| **Comparison chart** | Side-by-side evaluation of options | Tool comparisons, before/after, decision guides |
| **Checklist** | Visual checklist with checkboxes | Preparation lists, audit frameworks, daily routines |
| **Framework** | Visual representation of a mental model | Strategy concepts, decision trees, matrices |

### Infographic Creation Workflow (E02)
1. Write LinkedIn post covering the topic
2. Optimize for Gamma infographic (single-slide format)
3. Prompt Gamma to make it "more visual"
4. Export as PDF
5. Upload to LinkedIn

## 6 Proven Photo Styles (E02)

Rotate through these photo styles to maintain visual variety while staying on-brand:

| Style | Description | Trust Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **Behind-the-scenes** | Your workspace, process, daily work | Authenticity, shows the real work |
| **"Whiteboard" sketch** | Hand-drawn diagrams, framework sketches | Expertise, teaching authority |
| **Before/after** | Transformation results, progress shots | Proof of outcomes |
| **Milestone** | Achievement moments, celebrations | Social proof, credibility |
| **Counter-example** | What NOT to do (with visual) | Educational, relatable |
| **AI-generated** | Conceptual images, avatars via HeyGen | Visual variety, professional look |

### AI Photo Generation (E02)
- Upload 3 camera shots to HeyGen to create 15-20 "looks" for variety
- For each post, generate 3 complementary image ideas: selfie-style, Studio Ghibli-style, AI conceptual
- BetterPic AI: upload 10-15 casual photos, generates dozens of professional headshot options
- Use AI images to supplement real photos, not replace them

## "Photos as Sawdust" Concept (E02)

Photos do not need to be specially produced. The byproduct of your daily work and life IS the content.

**Document everything:**
- Your workspace setup
- Your working process
- Your wins and celebrations
- Your team interactions
- Your tools and equipment
- Your morning routine

This is "sawdust" -- the leftovers from your real work that become valuable content assets. The key insight: you are already creating photo-worthy moments every day; you just need to capture them.

## Screenshots as Content (E02)

Screenshots feel authentic and unpolished -- a high trust signal on LinkedIn and other platforms.

**Screenshot types that perform well:**
- Results dashboards (revenue, growth, metrics)
- Conversation excerpts (with permission) showing impact
- Tool interfaces showing your process
- Before/after analytics
- Customer feedback or testimonials

**Why screenshots work:** They bypass the "polished marketing" filter in viewers' minds. Raw screenshots feel like evidence, not advertising.

## Thumbnail Packaging Rules (E03b Colin & Samir)

### The 5 Rules
1. **Subtle tension:** Some element of drama or action that creates curiosity; not over-the-top (unbelievable) but enough to earn a click
2. **Maximum 3 core storytelling elements:** More than 3 makes it hard for viewers to quickly understand
3. **Do NOT repeat story in title and thumbnail:** Title and thumbnail are two different levers; they should work off each other, not tell the same story twice
4. **Pass the squint test:** Shrink thumbnail to small size, look at it on your phone next to other thumbnails; must be readable and recognizable
5. **Consistent branding element:** Colors, font, or your face -- something that makes your thumbnails recognizable across your channel

### Mr. Beast's Packaging Advice (E03b)
- "Take your idea and push it to the most simple and extreme place. Then pull it back to where you're comfortable."
- Stretch imagination to see all possibilities of representing the concept, then dial back to what feels right
- The tension between extreme and comfortable is where the best thumbnails live

## Visual Brand Consistency

### Visual Identity Rules (E04b Content Creation Dojo)

| Rule | Application |
|---|---|
| **Clarity over cleverness** | Unless you sell design services, prioritize readability |
| **Left-align text** | Matches Western left-to-right reading pattern; center-align only for 2 lines or less |
| **High contrast** | Test with accessibility tools (Coolors); ensure readability on all devices |
| **Consistent imagery style** | All posts should feel like they belong to the same brand |
| **Functional fonts** | High legibility over decorative; fancy fonts reduce readability |
| **F-pattern reading** | People scan top-left > right > down; place most important info accordingly |
| **Design for mobile** | Ensure readability on small screens with sun glare |

### The Rule of 2 (E04b)
- Two fonts (one for headlines, one for body)
- Two main colors (primary + accent)
- Two imagery styles (e.g., photos + illustrations, or screenshots + graphics)

### Brand DNA: Macro + Micro Consistency (E03c Sky Stack)

| Level | Elements |
|---|---|
| **Macro branding** | Positioning, content type, network/associations, message |
| **Micro branding** | Fonts, colors, visual themes, stock photo/video style, color grading/filters, logos, copy language |

Brand strength = congruency + consistency between macro and micro. Brand DNA weaves through both levels.

### Practical Brand DNA Steps (E03c)
1. Establish strong rules: your colors, fonts, color filters, styles
2. Document in brand guidelines
3. Give guidelines to everyone you work with (editors, freelancers, funnel builders)
4. Apply consistently across all platforms
5. If everything looks a little better, a little different, and consistent -- people perceive you as more advanced than you may be

## LinkedIn Banner Templates (E02)

8 frameworks for banner images. Must COMPLEMENT bio (never repeat it). 10-15 words maximum. Include specific numbers/metrics when possible. Include soft CTA.

| # | Framework | Template |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Category Authority** | [Industry] Expert / [Differentiator] / [CTA] |
| 2 | **Results & Credibility** | [Metric] / [Experience] / [CTA] |
| 3 | **Social Proof Focus** | Featured in [Publications] / [Success Quote] / [CTA] |
| 4 | **Method/Framework** | The [System Name] / [Core Benefit] / [CTA] |
| 5 | **Problem Agitation** | [Pain Point Question]? / [Promise] / [CTA] |
| 6 | **Authority Positioning** | [Expertise] Specialist / [Philosophy] / [CTA] |
| 7 | **Transformation Story** | From [Before] to [After] / Now I show [audience] how / [CTA] |
| 8 | **Strapline** | Simple memorable phrase (think "Think Different" or "Just Do It") |

## Lead Magnet Visual Design (E02)

When creating visual lead magnets (PDFs, cheat sheets, guides):
- 2-5 pages: clean, legible, large headings, short bullets
- One font pair (headline + body)
- Three brand colors maximum
- Simple landing page: clear headline, targeted subhead, 3-5 bullet outcomes, form (name + email), thank-you page
