## Carousel Slide-by-Slide Structure (E02)

### The 10-Slide Framework (Expanded)

#### Slide 1: Big Promise / Hook
- **Goal:** Stop the scroll and create curiosity
- Bold, clear headline stating the value proposition
- Use numbers when possible ("7 Steps to...", "The 3 Rules...")
- Visual: clean background, large text, brand colors
- Do NOT give away the answer -- create a curiosity gap

#### Slide 2: Stakes / Problem Agitation
- **Goal:** Make the reader feel the pain of NOT acting
- What happens if they ignore this? What are they losing?
- Use specific, relatable scenarios
- Visual: contrasting colors or imagery that conveys struggle/frustration

#### Slide 3: Example / Case Study
- **Goal:** Show this works in the real world
- Real numbers, real results, real scenarios
- Screenshots, before/after data, or concrete examples
- Visual: proof-oriented (dashboards, testimonials, data)

#### Slides 4-7: Framework / Core Value
- **Goal:** Deliver the promised value step by step
- One concept per slide (do NOT overcrowd)
- Clear numbering or progression (Step 1, Step 2...)
- Each slide should be independently valuable (people screenshot individual slides)
- Visual: consistent layout across these slides; numbered, structured, clean

#### Slide 8: Checklist / Common Mistakes
- **Goal:** Prevent the reader from failing at implementation
- What do most people get wrong?
- Checkbox format is highly save-worthy
- Visual: checklist format with clear check/X marks

#### Slide 9: Quick Win / Immediate Action
- **Goal:** Give one thing they can do RIGHT NOW
- One specific tool, action, or resource to try today
- Low barrier to entry -- should take less than 5 minutes
- Visual: action-oriented, highlight the single tool/resource

#### Slide 10: Call to Action
- **Goal:** Convert attention into action
- Options: Follow for more, Comment your thoughts, DM me [keyword], Save this for later, Visit link in bio
- Ask a question to drive comments (engagement signal)
- Visual: your face/brand + clear CTA text

## Carousel Design Best Practices

### Typography Rules
- **Headline:** 24-32pt equivalent, bold, brand headline font
- **Body:** 14-18pt equivalent, regular weight, high legibility
- **Maximum lines per slide:** 5-7 (less is more)
- **Left-align** all text (E04b) unless it is 2 lines or fewer

### Color Rules
- Two main colors + one accent (E04b Rule of 2)
- High contrast between text and background (test with Coolors)
- Consistent color scheme across all 10 slides
- Use brand colors for recognition

### Layout Rules
- Consistent margins and padding across all slides
- Consistent placement of slide numbers/progress indicators
- F-pattern reading: most important info at top-left (E04b)
- Mobile-first: test readability on phone screen

## Infographic Creation Workflow (E02)

### Step 1: Write the Content
Write a LinkedIn post that covers the same information the infographic will visualize. This becomes your source text.

### Step 2: Choose Infographic Type

| Type | Structure | Best When |
|---|---|---|
| **Process Flow** | Vertical or horizontal steps with arrows | Explaining a sequence or workflow |
| **Comparison** | Two columns or split layout | Evaluating options, before/after |
| **Checklist** | Numbered or checkbox list | Preparation, audit, requirements |
| **Framework** | Matrix, quadrant, or circular diagram | Mental models, decision frameworks |
| **Timeline** | Horizontal or vertical with dates/stages | History, progression, roadmap |
| **Hierarchy** | Pyramid or nested structure | Priority, importance ranking |

### Step 3: Create in Gamma
- Paste optimized content into Gamma.app
- Select single-slide (infographic) format
- Prompt Gamma to make it "more visual"
- Adjust colors to match brand

### Step 4: Export and Post
- Export as PDF from Gamma
- Upload to LinkedIn as document post
- Write an accompanying text post that complements (not duplicates) the infographic content

## Photo Style Rotation Guide (E02)

Rotate through these 6 styles weekly to maintain variety while building a consistent visual brand:

### Style 1: Behind-the-Scenes
- Your desk setup, workspace, tools in action
- Working on a project, whiteboard sessions
- Team meetings, collaboration moments
- **Trust signal:** "This is what the real work looks like"

### Style 2: "Whiteboard" Sketch
- Hand-drawn frameworks or diagrams
- Napkin sketches of concepts
- Annotated screenshots or documents
- **Trust signal:** "I think deeply about this topic"

### Style 3: Before/After
- Transformation results with visual evidence
- Progress shots over time
- Dashboard comparisons (metrics then vs. now)
- **Trust signal:** "I deliver measurable results"

### Style 4: Milestone
- Achievement celebrations
- Award or recognition moments
- Team celebrations, launch days
- **Trust signal:** "I have a track record of success"

### Style 5: Counter-Example
- What NOT to do (screenshot or visual example)
- Common mistakes visualized
- "Ugly vs. good" comparisons
- **Trust signal:** "I know the pitfalls and can help you avoid them"

### Style 6: AI-Generated
- HeyGen avatar variations (upload 3 shots, generate 15-20 looks)
- Studio Ghibli-style conceptual images
- Professional headshot variations via BetterPic AI
- **Trust signal:** "I use modern tools and stay current"

### Weekly Rotation Schedule
| Day | Photo Style | Pairing |
|---|---|---|
| Mon | Behind-the-scenes | Industry insight post |
| Tue | Whiteboard sketch | Educational framework post |
| Wed | AI-generated | Thought leadership post |
| Thu | Before/after or Milestone | Story or results post |
| Fri | Counter-example | Hot take or lesson learned post |

## Thumbnail Packaging Rules (E03b Colin & Samir -- Expanded)

### Rule 1: Subtle Tension
- Include some element of drama, action, or intrigue
- NOT clickbait (do not promise what you cannot deliver)
- Just enough curiosity to earn their click/time
- Examples: facial expression showing surprise, an unexpected object, a visual question mark

### Rule 2: Maximum 3 Core Storytelling Elements
- More than 3 elements creates visual confusion
- Viewer needs to understand the thumbnail in under 1 second
- The 3 elements: typically (1) person/face, (2) text/title, (3) supporting object/graphic
- Remove anything that does not serve one of these 3 roles

### Rule 3: Title and Thumbnail Tell Different Parts of the Story
- Title and thumbnail are two DIFFERENT levers
- They should work off each other, not duplicate information
- If title says "How I Made $100K," thumbnail should show the EVIDENCE, not repeat the text
- Each adds information the other does not

### Rule 4: The Squint Test
- Shrink thumbnail to the smallest size it will appear (phone search results)
- Look at it next to competitor thumbnails
- If you cannot tell what it is about at a glance, redesign it
- Test on phone, not desktop -- phone is where most viewing happens

### Rule 5: Consistent Branding Element
- One element that makes YOUR thumbnails recognizable in a sea of content
- Options: consistent color scheme, specific font, your face, a recurring graphic element
- Viewers should recognize your content before reading the title
- Build this recognition over 20-30 thumbnails for compound effect

### Mr. Beast Packaging Method (E03b)
1. Take your concept and push it to the most extreme visual representation imaginable
2. Then pull it back to where you are comfortable
3. The sweet spot lives in the tension between extreme and comfortable
4. This applies to both thumbnail imagery AND title wording

## Visual Brand Checklist (E04b, E03c, E02 -- Synthesized)

Use this checklist to audit your visual brand consistency:

### Foundation
- [ ] Two fonts selected (headline + body) with high legibility
- [ ] Two main colors defined (primary + accent) with high contrast
- [ ] Two imagery styles established (e.g., photos + graphics)
- [ ] Mood board created combining typography, colors, textures, imagery

### Application
- [ ] All carousels use the same color scheme and fonts
- [ ] Thumbnails have a recognizable branding element
- [ ] Photos follow a consistent filter/grading style
- [ ] Infographics use the same layout principles
- [ ] LinkedIn banner complements bio (does not repeat it)
- [ ] Lead magnets use brand colors and fonts

### Testing
- [ ] High contrast verified with accessibility tools (Coolors)
- [ ] All text passes the squint test on mobile
- [ ] F-pattern reading flow verified (important info top-left)
- [ ] Thumbnails recognizable at smallest display size
- [ ] Visual consistency when viewing profile grid/feed as a whole

### Documentation
- [ ] Brand guidelines documented (colors, fonts, styles)
- [ ] Guidelines shared with all collaborators (editors, freelancers)
- [ ] Guidelines applied consistently across all platforms
- [ ] Color filter/grading settings saved for reuse
