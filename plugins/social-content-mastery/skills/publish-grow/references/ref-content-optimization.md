# Content Optimization Reference

## THE COMPLETE "WHY YOUR REELS AIN'T POPPIN" CHECKLIST (E01)

### 1. B-Roll / Editing Quality
**Benchmark:** Top creators use 30-50 clips of B-roll per minute of video.

**Diagnostic questions:**
- Does each shot last 1-2 seconds max on screen?
- Do the first 5 seconds have the MOST shot changes? (Some creators change B-roll every 0.5 seconds in the hook)
- Do you have a library of "S-tier" reusable B-roll clips?

**Fix: Video Breakdown Exercise**
1. Take 3 favorite creators in your niche
2. Find their 2 most viewed videos
3. Screenshot every shot change
4. Note for each: duration, composition (wide/close-up/medium), visual effects used
5. Count total shot changes per minute
6. Think from the CREATOR's perspective: "what did they DO to make me feel X?"

**Fix: B-Roll Filming Habit**
- Stack with daily routines: morning coffee, skincare, journaling
- Film 4-5 clips each morning so you are done by noon
- Remove friction by having dedicated tripod spots with preset angles
- Build organized folders: travel, home, family, sports, design, tutorials
- Film B-roll constantly; dump into categorized folders

### 2. Framing & Personal Feel
- Follow Rule of Thirds: eyeline at upper third intersections
- Do not place head too high (shoulders at line) or too low (bottom third)
- Camera should feel personal -- not too far from face
- Eye contact with camera creates connection
- Start close for hooks: creates intimacy and urgency

### 3. Delivery / Voice-Over
**Problem:** Boring or monotone delivery kills even great scripts.

**Fixes:**
- Practice expanding vocal range -- UP for urgent/exciting, DOWN for calm
- First 1-2 sentences: speak slightly FASTER than normal (redo 20+ times if needed)
- Pre-filming energy exercises:
  - Forced laughter for 30 seconds straight
  - Tongue twisters / mouth warm-ups ("avada avada avada")
  - Bioenergetics: jump up and down, yell affirmations
  - Deep breathing: inhale, hold 6 seconds, exhale 8 seconds
- Film right after workouts or caffeine for highest natural energy
- Find the time/place where you feel most confident filming
- Visualize the camera as the ONE person you are talking to -- not an audience
- Speak from diaphragm: hand on belly, feel resonance
- Over-exaggerate as practice; go off-script sometimes to unlock natural energy

### 4. Talking Too Slow / Too Many Pauses
- Cut every "um," "like," and pause in editing
- Cut 0.5-second dead spaces between sentences
- Record only 3-4 sentences at a time to maintain breath control
- Deep breath before each recording segment
- Film 2-3 minutes of footage, cut to 40-second video (keep only golden sentences)

### 5. Script Is Too Wordy
- If viewer needs to remember last 3 sentences for current sentence to make sense, it is too much
- Do not repeat same point with different wording
- ONE main point per video -- know what it is before filming
- Every sentence must relate back to the main point
- No tangents about your business/credentials unless directly relevant to the viewer's problem
- It is OK to make your video shorter than originally planned

### 6. Writing With the Viewer in Mind
- When you say "I," it should really be about "us" and "you"
- Use "you" or "we" or "us" within the first two sentences
- Do not just talk about yourself -- think about what the VIEWER cares about
- Best hooks describe the viewer's situation, not yours
- Your unique advantage may be that you are a BEGINNER -- beginners relate to beginners better than experts do

### 7. Clean Composition
- Simplify your frame -- remove clutter from background
- One focused background element (like a colored light) > 5 random objects
- If talking about salmon and rice, show salmon and rice -- not 10 ingredients
- Clean backgrounds let viewer focus on what matters

### 8. Contrast & Lighting
- If shot looks "grayish," increase contrast (CapCut: Adjust > Contrast)
- Android phones typically have worse contrast than iPhone
- Equipment: ring light (~$25-30) + LED color-changing bulb for background
- Front lighting on face + colored backlight = professional-looking shot with minimal investment
- Stand facing brightest light source -- spin in a circle to find angle where face is brightest

### Bonus: Never Use Transitions (E01)
- No fade-ins, fade-outs, wipes, or built-in CapCut transitions between clips
- Only hard jump cuts
- This is non-negotiable for short-form social content

## ANTI-PATTERNS — WHAT NOT TO DO (E01)

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| Chase trending sounds/formats without understanding WHY they work | You become a bottom-feeder relying on the next trend |
| Leave dead space at beginning (even 0.1 seconds of silence) | Costs viewers immediately |
| Repeat same information with slightly different wording | Redundancy kills pacing |
| Talk about random personal details that do not serve the viewer | Narcissistic tangents |
| Use niche jargon without explaining it | Alienates non-niche viewers (kills viral potential) |
| Say "you guys" | Talk to ONE person |
| Start with slow introductions ("Hey guys, welcome back") | Get to the point immediately |
| Spaghetti-ball visuals (multiple text, split screens, random overlays) | Best videos are visually SIMPLE |
| Film with cluttered backgrounds | Simplifies frame, focus on what matters |
| Use wrong mood music just because it is trending | Creates uncanny valley, viewer scrolls |
| Analyze while creating | Separate creative flow from analytical revision |
| Expect results before 60-100 videos | You never gave it a real chance |
| Over-optimize for dopamine only | Emotional journeys outperform pure excitement |
| Be overtly salesy | People DM you to buy when you provide genuine value |
| Copy hooks word-for-word without understanding WHY they work | You never develop your own voice |
| Film entire vlog at one location | Setting switches are critical for interest |
| Forget binary resolution in challenge videos | Viewers need a clear YES or NO answer |

## STUDY FRAMEWORK FOR COMPETITOR ANALYSIS (E01)

**Repeat this process monthly:**

1. List 3 favorite creators in your niche
2. Sort their content by most popular/viewed (use Sort Feed on Instagram)
3. Pick their top 2 videos each (6 total)
4. Watch first 30 seconds of each, 5-6 times
5. Write down 3 "tricks" per video (18 total) analyzing:
   - Text overlays and captions
   - Editing style and pacing
   - Lighting setup
   - Camera angle and distance
   - Speaking style and tonality
   - Music selection and timing
   - Sound effects usage
   - Visual effects
6. Think from CREATOR perspective: not "this made me feel X" but "what did the creator DO to make me feel X?"

## CONTENT REVIEW FRAMEWORK — PEER FEEDBACK (E01)

### How to Give Effective Feedback
Always: one positive sentence + one critical sentence.

### Evaluation Questions
- Were the visuals engaging?
- Was any part confusing as a viewer?
- Was the lighting/color engaging, too much, or dull?
- Were the captions well-executed or hard to read?
- How did the storytelling make you feel?
- At which point did the video slow down?
- Was the music too loud? Audio muffled?
- Was the script hard to follow? Boring? Not personal enough?

### Why Peer Review Matters
- Giving feedback to others trains YOUR content analysis muscle
- The "second pair of eyes" is crucial -- you cannot objectively evaluate your own content
- Content review is a two-way growth accelerator

## RETENTION METRICS BENCHMARKS

### Instagram Reel Benchmarks (E04a, E04b)
| Metric | Good | Great | Viral |
|---|---|---|---|
| Likes to reach | 1:10 | 1:7 | 1:5 |
| Saves to likes | 1:1 | 2:1 | 3:1+ |
| Shares to likes | 1:10 | 1:5 | 1:3 |
| Follower to non-follower reach | 1:1 | 1:3 | 1:10 |
| Average watch time vs reel length | 80%+ | 100%+ | 150%+ (replays) |

### Video Quality Checklist (E04a)
- [ ] Not blurry
- [ ] Not over/underexposed
- [ ] Clean audio (no background noise, echo)
- [ ] 1080p export resolution (not 4K directly to Instagram)
- [ ] 9:16 aspect ratio
- [ ] Captions included (90% watch without sound)
- [ ] Visual change every 3-5 seconds

### Two Final Tests Before Posting (E04b)
1. **Watch on mute:** Must be understandable without audio
2. **Overlay social media UI:** Check safe zones for text, captions, and animations -- ensure nothing important is hidden behind platform UI elements

## BURNOUT PREVENTION (E04b)

- Never overwork in single sessions -- spread editing over several days
- Hard to maintain focus for more than 3 hours
- When burned out: take 5 days off Instagram but still post 1 pre-made piece daily (takes 5 min)
- Edit a batch of content BEFORE taking break
- Recreate old successful content when lacking ideas -- saves time, reduces idea pressure
- Rest must be scheduled -- otherwise you will rationalize skipping it and burn out
- 1,500+ consecutive days posting without missing one (Dojo instructor's record) is possible WITH scheduled rest and batching

## CONTENT CREATION WORKFLOW REMINDER (E01)

### Daily Workflow
1. **Morning:** Film 4-5 B-roll clips stacked with daily routines
2. **Ideation:** Pick one topic from IVP exercise or hook template vault
3. **Brain dump:** 10-minute timer, write everything about the topic
4. **Script:** Structure into Hook > Body > Conclusion. Cut to essentials.
5. **Revise:** Apply CGOVE framework to hook. Apply 4 storytelling revision rules.
6. **Pre-film prep:** Deep breathing, energy exercises, warm up voice
7. **Film:** Record hook 5-20 times. Record body in 3-4 sentence chunks.
8. **Edit:** Cut dead space, add B-roll, auto-captions, music matching mood, SFX, color grade
9. **Review:** Watch as stranger. Hook capture in 3 seconds? Pacing fast enough? Ending satisfying?
10. **Post:** Upload before going to sleep -- no exceptions during learning phase
11. **Community:** Drop in reviews for peer feedback. Review 2 other videos.

### Batch Creation Method (E04a, E04b)
- Create content for 3-7 days ahead (3 days recommended for beginners)
- 6-step content board: Idea Generation > Scripting > Filming > Editing > Scheduling > Posted
- Track all posted videos with view counts for data analysis
- Creator habits to track daily: post 1 content piece, engage with 10 creators, film 1 shot, post 1 story
