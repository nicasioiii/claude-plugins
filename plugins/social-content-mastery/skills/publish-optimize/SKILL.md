# Publishing Optimization & Analytics

Help creators maximize content performance through strategic publishing, analytics-driven optimization, and systematic diagnosis of underperforming content. This skill covers the complete post-creation workflow from pre-publish checklist through performance analysis and iteration.

## CROSS-PLATFORM PUBLISHING WORKFLOW

### Pre-Publish Checklist (Every Post)

Run before hitting publish on ANY platform:

1. **Hook passes the stranger test** -- Would someone with zero context about you stop scrolling?
2. **Audio is clean** -- No background noise, clear voice, proper levels (200% boost if needed)
3. **Captions/subtitles present** -- 85% of social video is watched on mute
4. **First 3 seconds are tight** -- No dead space, no "hey guys," no throat clearing
5. **CTA is clear and benefit-oriented** -- Not "follow me" but "follow for daily [topic] tips"
6. **Thumbnail/cover image passes squint test** -- Legible at phone size next to other content
7. **Title and thumbnail do NOT repeat** -- Two separate levers working together
8. **No external links in post** (platforms suppress) -- Move links to bio/comments/DMs
9. **Hashtags applied** (2 min max) -- 3-5 relevant tags on IG, skip or geo-tag on TikTok
10. **Export settings correct** -- 4K, 30fps (NOT 60fps), vertical 9:16 for short-form

### Platform-Specific Publishing Rules

| Platform | Native Upload | Frequency | Best Timing |
|----------|--------------|-----------|-------------|
| Instagram Reels | Always native, never cross-post with watermarks | 1x/day (first 100 days), then 5-7x/week | When YOUR audience is active (check Insights) |
| TikTok | Native upload | 1-3x/day if quality maintained | Test and iterate; no universal "best time" |
| YouTube | Native, scheduled uploads | 1x/week minimum | Consistent day/time each week |
| LinkedIn | Native text + video | 3-5x/week | Weekday mornings, business hours |
| Twitter/X | Native | Daily minimum | Throughout the day, engagement-focused |
| Facebook Reels | Upload natively (not from IG) | Repost from IG but upload separately | Varies by audience demographic |

### Cross-Platform Repurposing Strategy (3 Tiers)

**Tier 1: Direct Repost (Low Effort)**
- Same content uploaded natively to each platform
- Remove watermarks (SnapTik for TikTok, screen record if needed)
- Adjust aspect ratio if needed (9:16 for short-form, 16:9 for YouTube)
- Change caption formatting per platform norms

**Tier 2: Adapted Repost (Medium Effort)**
- Same core content, platform-optimized hook
- LinkedIn: Add text-based hook above video
- Twitter: Thread version of video content
- YouTube: Expand short-form into long-form deep dive

**Tier 3: Content Multiplication (High Effort)**
- One idea becomes 5+ pieces across formats
- Video becomes: reel, carousel, thread, newsletter section, podcast segment
- Apply Lean Content Multiplication (5 variables): Pillars x Buckets x Multipliers x Archetypes x Sub-topics

### Platform Suppression Awareness

Platforms actively suppress content with:
- External links in captions (move to bio/comments)
- Competitor platform watermarks (TikTok logo on IG Reels)
- Overtly promotional content (algorithm deprioritizes "buy my product" posts)
- Low engagement in first hour (front-load engagement with story priming)

## ANALYTICS FRAMEWORK

### The 4 Metrics That Matter (InstaCoach Mike)

Track weekly, not daily:

1. **Reach** -- How many unique accounts saw your content
2. **Engagement Rate** -- (Likes + Comments + Saves + Shares) / Reach
3. **Follower Growth Rate** -- Net new followers per week
4. **Profile Visits to Follow Rate** -- What % of profile visitors become followers

### Engagement Ratio Benchmarks

| Metric | Target Ratio | What It Means |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Likes-to-Reach | 1:10 (10%) | 100 likes per 1,000 reach = healthy |
| Saves-to-Likes | 1:1 | Equal saves to likes = high-value content |
| Shares-to-Likes | 1:10 | 10 shares per 100 likes = strong virality signal |

### Content Type to Primary Metric

| Content Type | Primary Metric | Secondary |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|
| Educational | Saves | Comments |
| Inspirational | Shares | Likes |
| Entertainment | Likes | Shares |
| Promotional | Profile visits | DMs |

### Reach Distribution Levels

| Level | Follower:Non-Follower Reach | Rating |
|-------|----------------------------|--------|
| Good | 1:1 | Analyze and replicate |
| Phenomenal | 1:3 | Consistent growth guaranteed |
| Viral | 1:10 | 100K follower reach to 1M non-follower reach |

## RETENTION GRAPH DIAGNOSIS

Retention rate is THE metric that matters across all video platforms. Every platform tracks it.

### Reading Retention Graphs

**Pattern: Big dip at the beginning (0-3 seconds)**
- Diagnosis: Hook failure or audience targeting mismatch
- Fix: Rewrite hook, test different hook archetype, improve first-frame visual

**Pattern: Steep drop at the end**
- Diagnosis: Outro too long, value already delivered
- Fix: Cut ending immediately after delivering core value. No "thanks for watching."

**Pattern: Wiggly staircase declining throughout**
- Diagnosis: Pacing problems in the middle body
- Fix: Cut boring sections, add visual changes every 2-3 seconds, increase rate of revelation

**Pattern: Flat or rising retention**
- Diagnosis: Content is working -- analyze and replicate
- Fix: Double down on this format/topic/hook style

### The 2-Second Rule (Editing)

If any moment in your video could lose a viewer, you have approximately 2 seconds before they scroll. Every cut, transition, and visual change should serve retention.

## CONTENT AUDIT AND OPTIMIZATION

### The Anomaly Method (7 Steps)

Systematic process for identifying what works and doing more of it:

1. Sort all content by views (highest to lowest)
2. Identify the top 10% (your anomalies)
3. For each anomaly, document: topic, hook style, format, length, thumbnail/cover, posting time
4. Identify patterns across your anomalies
5. Create 5-10 new content pieces using those patterns
6. Track performance of pattern-based content
7. Repeat monthly -- your anomaly patterns will evolve

### Doubling Down System

When a piece of content overperforms:

1. **Same topic, different angle** -- If "5 mistakes in X" worked, try "5 rules for X"
2. **Same hook style, different topic** -- If "Stop doing X" hooked, use format on other pillars
3. **Same format, deeper dive** -- If listicle worked, expand each point into its own video
4. **Series creation** -- Turn one-off winner into a recurring series

### Competitive Benchmarking (4-Step Process)

1. **Identify 5-10 competitors** in your niche currently growing
2. **Sort their content by performance** (views, engagement) using platform tools or Social Blade
3. **Document patterns** -- What topics, hooks, formats, thumbnails consistently win?
4. **Adapt (don't copy)** -- Apply patterns to your unique expertise and voice

### Weekly Analytics Review Checklist

Run every Sunday or Monday:
- [ ] Review reach and engagement for each post this week
- [ ] Identify best and worst performer -- WHY did each succeed/fail?
- [ ] Check follower growth rate (up, flat, or declining?)
- [ ] Review retention graphs on video content
- [ ] Note any anomalies to replicate
- [ ] Check comment sentiment (positive engagement vs. confusion)
- [ ] Plan next week's content based on findings
- [ ] Update content calendar with learnings

## A/B TESTING METHODOLOGY

### What to Test (Priority Order)

1. **Hooks** -- Biggest impact on performance. Test 2-3 variations of same content
2. **Thumbnails/Covers** -- Test different images, text overlays, expressions
3. **Posting times** -- Test same content type at different times over 2-4 weeks
4. **Content length** -- Test 30s vs. 60s vs. 90s on same topic
5. **CTA placement** -- Test end-of-video vs. mid-video vs. caption-only

### Testing Rules

- Change ONE variable at a time
- Minimum sample size: 5-10 posts per variation before drawing conclusions
- Account for external factors (trending topics, holidays, algorithm changes)
- Document everything in a testing log
- Never test on your most important content -- use secondary content for experiments

### The 100-Day Testing Method

For the first 100 days of content creation:
- Post daily (no exceptions)
- Track every metric
- Try different formats, hooks, topics each week
- At day 100, you have 100 data points to analyze
- Your anomalies will reveal your winning formula

## ENGAGEMENT OPTIMIZATION

### Rule of Seven

A viewer needs to see your content approximately 7 times before they follow, engage, or buy. Implications:
- Consistency matters more than any single viral post
- Posting frequency directly impacts how quickly you hit 7 touchpoints
- Cross-platform presence multiplies touchpoints

### Comment Engagement Strategy

- Reply to every comment in the first hour (signals to algorithm)
- Ask questions back to commenters (creates conversation threads)
- Pin a comment with additional value or a question
- Use "comment magnet" CTAs: "Comment [keyword] for my free [resource]"
- DM automation (ManyChat) to deliver lead magnets from comment triggers

### Pre-Post Engagement Hack

15-30 minutes before posting:
- Engage with 10-20 accounts in your niche (genuine comments, not emojis)
- Post a story teasing upcoming content
- This primes the algorithm to show your next post to engaged accounts

### Content-View-Time Optimization

Maximize the time viewers spend with your content:
- Use open loops ("but here's the thing..." / "and the third one is the most important")
- Rate of revelation: give one new insight every 3-5 seconds
- Looping technique: end connects to beginning, encouraging rewatches
- Strategic cliffhangers between carousel slides or video segments

## POSTING FREQUENCY RESOLUTION

Different instructors disagree. Use this decision framework:

| Stage | Recommended Frequency | Rationale |
|-------|----------------------|-----------|
| First 100 days | Daily (all platforms) | Skill-building phase, algorithm data gathering |
| Established (short-form) | 5-7x/week | Quality over quantity once baseline skill achieved |
| YouTube | 1x/week minimum | Long-form requires more production time |
| LinkedIn | 3-5x/week | Professional audience, weekday-focused |
| TikTok (growth phase) | 1-3x/day | Only if quality maintained; 1x/day minimum |
| Twitter/X | Daily minimum | Text-based, lower production cost |

**Contrarian view (Jimmy Farley):** Every other day is fine if you maintain B-to-A quality per video. Supported by his 1M+ follower results. Valid ONLY after proving consistent quality.
