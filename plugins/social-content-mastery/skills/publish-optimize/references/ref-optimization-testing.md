# Optimization & Testing Quick Reference

## The Anomaly Method (Frabrand / Content Creation Dojo -- 7 Steps)

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Sort all content by views** -- highest to lowest, across last 90 days minimum
2. **Identify top 10%** -- these are your anomalies (content that significantly outperformed)
3. **Document each anomaly:**
   - Topic / content pillar
   - Hook type used (question, statement, story, stat, controversy)
   - Format (educational, challenge, listicle, story, comparison)
   - Length (seconds or word count)
   - Thumbnail/cover style
   - Posting time and day
   - Caption approach
4. **Identify patterns** -- What do your top performers have in common?
5. **Create 5-10 new pieces** using those exact patterns
6. **Track performance** -- Do the pattern-based pieces outperform your average?
7. **Repeat monthly** -- Anomaly patterns evolve as your audience grows

### Common Anomaly Patterns

| Pattern | What It Means | Action |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| Same topic wins repeatedly | Audience has strong interest in this area | Create series around this topic |
| Same hook style wins | Your audience responds to specific hook psychology | Standardize this hook approach |
| Same format wins | Algorithm favors this format for your account | Make this your primary format |
| Same length wins | Optimal attention span for your audience | Standardize content length |

## Doubling-Down System

When content overperforms (2x+ your average views/engagement):

### Strategy 1: Same Topic, Different Angle
- Original: "5 mistakes new creators make"
- Double down: "5 rules every creator should follow" / "What I wish I knew as a new creator"

### Strategy 2: Same Hook Style, Different Topic
- Original hook: "Stop doing X if you want Y" (performed well)
- Double down: Apply "Stop doing X" format to your other content pillars

### Strategy 3: Same Format, Deeper Dive
- Original: Listicle with 5 surface-level points
- Double down: Take point #3 and create a full deep-dive video on just that point

### Strategy 4: Series Creation
- Original: One-off viral video
- Double down: "Part 2," "Advanced version," "Beginner version," weekly series

### Strategy 5: Cross-Platform Expansion
- Original won on TikTok
- Double down: Adapt for Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn video

## A/B Testing Framework

### Testing Priority Order (Highest Impact First)

1. **Hooks** -- Test 2-3 different hooks on same content body
2. **Thumbnails/Covers** -- Different images, text, expressions
3. **Content length** -- 30s vs. 60s vs. 90s same topic
4. **Posting time** -- Morning vs. afternoon vs. evening
5. **CTA type** -- "Follow" vs. "Save" vs. "Comment [keyword]" vs. "DM me"
6. **Format** -- Talking head vs. B-roll heavy vs. text-on-screen

### Testing Rules

- **One variable at a time** -- If you change hook AND thumbnail, you cannot attribute results
- **Minimum sample: 5-10 posts** per variation before conclusions
- **Control for externals** -- Holidays, trending topics, algorithm changes can skew results
- **Document everything** -- Maintain a testing log with hypothesis, variable, result, learning
- **Use secondary content** -- Never test on your highest-stakes content

### Testing Log Template

| Date | Hypothesis | Variable Tested | Version A | Version B | Result | Learning |
|------|-----------|----------------|-----------|-----------|--------|----------|
| ___ | Hook questions outperform statements | Hook type | Question hook | Statement hook | A won by 2.3x reach | Questions create curiosity gap |

## Competitive Benchmarking Process (4 Steps)

### Step 1: Identify Competitors (5-10 accounts)
**Criteria for valid competitors:**
- Currently posting (not dormant accounts)
- Posting on YOUR platform
- Following is actively growing (check Social Blade)
- Getting meaningful engagement (not just followers)
- In your niche or adjacent niche

### Step 2: Sort Their Content
- Use platform tools to sort by most viewed/engaged
- Install Sort Feed Chrome extension for Instagram sorting
- On YouTube, sort channel videos by "Most Popular"
- On TikTok, view profile and sort by views

### Step 3: Document Patterns
For their top 10 performers, note:
- Topic and angle
- Hook approach (first line / first frame)
- Format and length
- Visual style and editing approach
- Caption/description strategy
- Engagement patterns (comments sentiment)

### Step 4: Adapt (Do NOT Copy)
- Apply their successful PATTERNS to your unique expertise
- Use their topic choices as market validation
- Study their hook formats but write your own hooks
- Match their production quality level but maintain your style

## Engagement Optimization Tactics

### Comment Engagement as Growth Strategy

**First-Hour Comment Strategy:**
- Reply to every comment in the first 60 minutes
- Ask a follow-up question in each reply (creates conversation threads)
- Each reply counts as additional engagement signal to the algorithm
- Longer reply threads = more dwell time = better distribution

**Comment Magnet System:**
- End videos with: "Comment [keyword] for my free [resource]"
- Set up DM automation (ManyChat) to deliver lead magnet
- Converts engagement into email list growth
- Works on Instagram and Facebook

**Pinned Comment Strategy:**
- Pin a comment that adds extra value not in the video
- OR pin a question to encourage more comments
- OR pin a controversial take to spark debate

### Pre-Post Engagement Hack

**15-30 minutes before posting:**
1. Open your platform and engage genuinely with 10-20 accounts in your niche
2. Leave thoughtful comments (not emojis or "great post")
3. Watch/read their content fully (gives YOUR account engagement signals)
4. Post a story teasing your upcoming content
5. Then publish your post

**Why it works:** Primes the algorithm to recognize your account as active. Your post gets shown to the accounts you just engaged with, and their audiences.

### The R.E.S.U.L.T. Method (100K IG Course)

1. **R**esearch -- Study competitors and trending content in your niche
2. **E**ngage -- Comment on 20-30 posts before and after your own post
3. **S**tory -- Post to stories with poll/question sticker about your main post topic
4. **U**pload -- Post at your analytics-proven best time
5. **L**ink -- Cross-promote on other platforms
6. **T**rack -- Review metrics next day and add to testing log

## The Rule of Seven (Applied to Content)

A viewer needs approximately 7 touchpoints with your brand before they take action (follow, engage, buy).

### Implications for Publishing Strategy

| Touchpoint | Channel | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| 1 | FYP / Explore | Viewer sees your reel, watches 50% |
| 2 | FYP again | Different video, same creator -- "oh I've seen them" |
| 3 | Search | Viewer searches topic, your content appears |
| 4 | Stories | Viewer taps into your story from the top bar |
| 5 | Profile visit | Viewer checks your bio and other content |
| 6 | Email/DM | Viewer gets your lead magnet |
| 7 | Follow/Purchase | Trust threshold reached, action taken |

### How to Accelerate the 7 Touchpoints
- **Post daily** -- More content = more chances to appear in someone's feed
- **Use stories** -- Additional touchpoint channel on IG/Facebook
- **Cross-platform** -- Viewer sees you on TikTok AND Instagram = 2 touchpoints per day
- **Email list** -- Every newsletter is a guaranteed touchpoint (no algorithm filter)
- **Comment engagement** -- Your replies show up in their notifications = additional touchpoint

## Delete/Repurpose Decision Framework (InstaCoach Mike)

### When to Delete a Post
- Under 500 views after 48 hours (for accounts with 1K+ followers)
- Significantly below your average engagement
- Content feels off-brand or was posted impulsively
- Information is outdated or incorrect

### When to Repurpose Instead
- Content got decent views but low engagement --> Refilm with better hook
- Good topic, poor execution --> Reshoot with improved production
- Performed well 3+ months ago --> Repost (audience has refreshed)
- Platform-specific winner --> Adapt for other platforms

### Greatest Hits Strategy
- Every 2-3 months, repost your all-time best performers
- Audience turns over -- new followers have never seen your old content
- Minor edits (new cover, updated caption) make it feel fresh
- Proven content typically performs well again
