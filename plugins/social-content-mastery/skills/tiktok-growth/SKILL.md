## The TikTok Algorithm

### The "America's Got Talent" Model (Jimmy Farley)

The algorithm works like talent show rounds:

1. **Post a video** -> gets sent to random For You pages (small initial batch)
2. **TikTok sends to people it THINKS might like it** based on content signals
3. **If that batch engages** (like, share, comment, full watch, follow) -> sent to MORE pages
4. **If next batch engages** -> sent to even MORE pages
5. **If engagement drops** -> reach stops

**Evidence:**
- Videos grow in "spurts" -- view counts increase in progressively bigger jumps
- When engagement ratio stays steady (e.g., 1:4 view-to-like), reach continues
- When ratio drops (e.g., 1:7), reach stops
- Zero-follower accounts still get views via FYP -- this is what makes TikTok unique

**Key insight:** TikTok is PERFECT competition. A zero-follower account competes on equal footing with million-follower accounts on the FYP.

### The Authority Effect (Ryan Magin)

Every million-view video raises your account's BASELINE permanently:
- Account with 1K followers: bad video gets ~100 views
- After a breakthrough video (40-100K views): bad videos get ~1K views
- Near 1M followers: even terrible content gets 100K views
- More million-view videos = more account authority

**Viral Video Math (Alex Hormozi case study):**
- Goal: 100K followers in 90 days
- Required: 5-7 videos hitting 1M+ views
- Each 1M-view video yields 10-20K followers
- 3M-view video produced ~30K followers
- Hit 100K in 96 days

### FYP Distribution Reality

- FYP is the PRIME real estate on TikTok
- Most viewers on FYP are NOT your followers
- **[CONTRARIAN] Followers on TikTok are not an effective statistic** -- you are not guaranteed follower views like on Instagram (Jimmy Farley)
- Implication: funnel TikTok viewers to platforms where followers DO matter (Instagram, email list, YouTube)

## The "Generally Specific" Framework (Ryan Magin)

**Four causes of virality:**
1. General topic that can reach 1M+ viewers (don't limit by gender, age, sub-niche)
2. Instantly specific, actionable advice (tell them exactly what to do)
3. Two-hook structure
4. Anti-CUB compliance

**The key insight:** Start GENERAL in your opening, get SPECIFIC in the content.
- BAD: "Moms with two kids who want financial advice" (too narrow for FYP)
- GOOD: "If you're under 25, here's how to plan your finances" (broad reach)

Give the video the CAPACITY to get distributed. Ryan could NOT get over 100K views with video editing content (too niche). Made first video about making friends -- INSTANTLY got 5M views.

## The Anti-CUB Framework (Ryan Magin)

Videos that flop are **C**onfusing, **U**nbelievable, or **B**oring:

| Killer | Problem | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| **Confusing** | Using words/concepts your audience doesn't understand | Simplify everything. If your grandmother wouldn't understand it, rewrite it. |
| **Unbelievable** | Bold claims without proof | Show screenshots, bank statements, receipts. If you say $100K/month, PROVE IT. |
| **Boring** | Not giving tangible, actionable information | Make videos for THEM not you. Include something they can use immediately. |

## The 4 Content Boxes (Ryan Magin)

Never run out of ideas by pulling from 4 categories:

1. **Profession/Expertise** -- things you've been PAID for (proof exists)
2. **Competent Skill Sets** -- things you're good at but don't monetize; friends ask you for advice
3. **Relationships & Family** -- most relatable category; everyone has relationships
4. **Hobbies & Interests** -- things you do for fun

**Content planning with boxes:**
- Post ~30 videos/month
- Pull 5-7 videos per week from one box
- Rotate through boxes continuously -- never run out of topics

## Series vs. Trends (JT Barnett)

- **75% of content should be SERIES** (original, recurring content themes)
- **25% should be TRENDS** (use as boosts, not foundation)
- Series builds community; people follow because they want MORE of that series
- Trends get views but don't convert to followers -- visitors see you just doing trends, no reason to follow

**Series ideas:** Founding story (Part 1, 2, 3...), "Package an order with me," industry tips, day-in-the-life, what I eat in a day. Run 3-4 series simultaneously.

## The Video Flow Formula (JT Barnett)

Every successful TikTok follows this arc:
1. **Hook** -- pull attention in first 3 seconds
2. **Conflict** -- present a problem or tension
3. **Resolution** -- solve the problem / deliver the value
4. **Call to Action** -- subtle push to follow/click/engage

## B-to-A Content Concept (Jimmy Farley)

- Keep ALL content at B-minus level or higher (top 80%)
- Your videos should be interchangeable with viral videos on the FYP
- Most videos will "miss" -- and that's OKAY if you maintain B-to-A quality
- Good content will ALWAYS prevail in the macro even if short-term is inconsistent
- A million-follower account will still have 10-20K view videos regularly -- that's normal

**[CONTRARIAN] TikTok is a CREATIVE game, not a work-harder game.** Post on a schedule that preserves your creativity. No amount of posting frequency can override a bad video.

## Optimal Video Length

- **Best performing:** 20-40 seconds (Ryan Magin)
- First 6 seconds = the two hooks
- Everything after 6 seconds = meat/information/actionable content
- Goal: high completion rate -> distribution -> shares -> likes/comments/followers

## Context Captions (Jimmy Farley)

The caption overlaid on the video that explains what the viewer is about to see. Can transform a video from zero engagement to millions of views.

**Without context caption:** Viewer sees random action, doesn't understand, scrolls.
**With context caption:** Viewer IMMEDIATELY knows what's happening, stops to watch.

**Examples:**
- Without: Guy putting tape on a broom (scroll past)
- With: "Spider-Man web transition tutorial" -> 10M+ views

**Rule:** If it is NOT clear as day what your video is about in the first moment, ADD A CONTEXT CAPTION. Always.

---

## Engagement Bait & Controversy (Jimmy Farley)

**The #1 most powerful engagement tactic:** Guide people to engage WITHOUT them knowing you're doing it. Make it look NATURAL -- never obviously forced.

**Types of engagement bait:**
- Plant "comment bait" elements throughout videos
- Purposeful "mistakes" that viewers want to correct
- Polarizing statements that split the audience
- Sexual jokes / double entendres that look accidental
- Highlighting flaws in your own product

**The psychology:** Viewers think they're the FIRST to notice the "mistake." They engage with others who noticed the same thing. This creates massive comment threads.

## Account Setup Checklist

- **Account type:** Use Personal or Creator account (NOT Business -- gets less reach)
- **Instagram connected** (built-in integration)
- **YouTube connected** (built-in integration)
- **Link in bio** (website, link tree, landing page)
- **Email in profile settings**
- **Clean profile picture** (human face, not faceless brand)
- **Clear bio** communicating what you do and what value you provide
- **Q&A feature enabled** (Creator accounts only)

## Instructor Disagreements

> **Posting Frequency:**
> - Jimmy Farley (CONTRARIAN): Every other day is fine. 1M+ followers posting every other day. Quality non-negotiable, quantity negotiable.
> - JT Barnett: 3x/day minimum for 90 days (brands need volume for valid sample size)
> - Ryan Magin: Focus on having content ready, not specific timing
> - **Reconciliation:** Default to daily for first 100 days. After proven quality, reduce to sustainable cadence. For brands: higher volume (2-3x/day) for 90 days to test. For creators: 1x/day or every other day with B-to-A quality.

> **Hashtag Importance:**
> - Jimmy Farley: Doesn't use them. Had 35M-view video with zero hashtags. Never spend more than 2 minutes.
> - JT Barnett: Useful for categorization and geo-targeting. 4-5 location hashtags for local business.
> - **Reconciliation:** Hashtags are a minor signal. Use 3-5 for categorization if you want. For geo-targeting, use location-specific hashtags. Never let hashtags prevent you from posting.

> **Shadow Bans:**
> - Jimmy Farley: Likely not real. TikTok has NOT confirmed they exist. Believing in shadow bans removes your responsibility for content quality.
> - JT Barnett: If you think you're shadow banned, just keep posting quality content for 90 days.
> - **Reconciliation:** No concrete evidence. Natural algorithm fluctuation is more likely. Better mindset: "I am causing everything. I need to make better content."
