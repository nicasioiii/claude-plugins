# TikTok Tactics Reference

## The Two-Hook System (Ryan Magin)

### Hook 1 -- "Stop the Scroll" (Seconds 1-3)
- ONLY goal: make them stop scrolling
- You are INTERRUPTING them -- their thumbs are a "well-tuned machine"
- Must be attention-grabbing, controversial, or polarizing

### Hook 2 -- "Fall in Line" / "Reason to Watch" (Seconds 3-6)
- Gives them a reason to keep watching
- Generally blends smoothly from Hook 1
- This is the "real" hook that delivers the promise

**Real estate example:**
- Hook 1: "Here's how I made $40K flipping my first house"
- Hook 2: "...with a credit score of 492 and less than $5K in my bank"

**Every million-view video on your feed has BOTH hooks. You just never noticed until now.**

## 8 Hook Types (Ryan Magin)

1. **Controversial/Triggering** -- gets under their skin; causes emotional/irrational commenting
2. **Statistics** -- start with stat + proof screenshot
3. **Celebrities** -- reference celeb drama or pop culture events
4. **Prices** -- saying any price in first 6 seconds grabs attention
5. **Big Numbers** -- large money figures or extreme statistics
6. **Extreme Statements** -- said with enormous confidence and conviction
7. **Popular Social Media Topics** -- reference influencer drama
8. **Exclusive Secrets** -- behind-the-scenes, things they "shouldn't" see

## Hook Component Framework (JT Barnett)

Every hook has THREE components working together:
1. **Visual** -- what they see in the video itself
2. **Audio** -- what they hear (voice, sound)
3. **Text Overlay** -- title/captions written on screen

All three must engage simultaneously for maximum impact.

## Controversy and Engagement Bait (Jimmy Farley)

### Purposeful "Mistakes" (The #1 Engagement Tactic)
- Plant "comment bait" elements throughout videos
- Make it look NATURAL -- never obviously forced
- Viewers think they're the FIRST to notice

**Types of engagement bait:**
- Obvious "mistakes" viewers want to correct
- Polarizing statements splitting the audience
- Sexual jokes / double entendres that look accidental
- Highlighting flaws in your own product
- Including a controversial person

### Sparking Emotion
The single thread connecting ALL viral content: it sparks emotion.
- Types: humor, controversy, heartwarming, shock, relatability, anger, nostalgia

## Posting Frequency Debate (3 Perspectives)

### Jimmy Farley (CONTRARIAN -- quality over quantity)
- "Post 3-5 times a day to grow" is PROVABLY FALSE
- 1M+ followers posting every other day
- Quality is NON-NEGOTIABLE; quantity is negotiable
- Burning out and quitting = worse than posting less
- TikTok is a CREATIVE game, not a work-harder game
- Target: stay below 30 minutes per video

### JT Barnett (volume for brands)
- Minimum 3x/day for 90 days for valid sample size
- Need to try all content types before judging
- Without sufficient volume, can't determine if platform works

### Ryan Magin (batch-focused)
- Filmed 200 videos with a client in a SINGLE DAY
- Plan content in advance so filming is fast
- Doesn't believe in posting times -- post whenever
- Focus on having content ready, not specific timing

## Hashtag Debate (2 Perspectives)

### Jimmy Farley (don't matter much)
- Had 35M-view video with no hashtags
- Theory: no significant positive OR negative effect
- NEVER spend more than 2 minutes on hashtags

### JT Barnett (useful for categorization)
- Two uses: categorize account + geo-target
- For geo-targeting: use location-specific hashtags
- Don't use massive hashtags (14.9B views = too competitive)
- Target under 500M views
- 4-5 location-specific for local business
- Create 5-6 different hashtag SETS matched to video topics

## Reposting & Greatest Hits Strategy (Ryan Magin)

### Reposting Rules
- If a video gets 500K+ views, wait 7 days and post it AGAIN (exact same)
- Second post often does almost as well; sometimes BETTER
- Continue reposting every 7-10 days until views decline

### Case Studies
- Aaron Marino (Alpha M): reposted for 90 days, no new content -> gained 600,000 followers
- Ryan Magin: reposted 60 days -> gained 20,000 followers
- Ryan on IG Reels: reposted old TikToks -> 3M views, 40K Instagram followers

### Greatest Hits Strategy
- Once a week, take best video from previous week and repost
- After a big follower spike (50K+), take a week off creating and repost best videos
- New followers have NEVER SEEN your old content

### Remixing Strategy
- Take winning video, make VARIATIONS
- Change title/hook, keep core content
- Change hashtags, slightly different angle

## Cross-Platform Repurposing

- Create on TikTok, repost to: YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, Facebook Reels, Snapchat
- Remove TikTok watermark before posting to other platforms (IG penalizes)
- Use free desktop tools for watermark removal (not phone apps)
- Same content can work across all short-form platforms

## TikTok Features (7 Key Features)

1. **Reply-with-Video** -- Reply to comments with a new video (great for engagement)
2. **Duet** -- Side-by-side reaction/response to another video
3. **Stitch** -- Take a clip from another video and add your own
4. **Lives** -- Live streaming for direct engagement
5. **Q&A** -- Creator accounts can enable Q&A for audience questions
6. **SEO/Search** -- TikTok increasingly functions as a search engine (optimize captions)
7. **Series** -- Organize related content into playlists

## Brand TikTok Strategy (JT Barnett + Konstantinos)

### For Companies
- 75% series content (original, recurring themes)
- 25% trends (use as boosts, not foundation)
- Define brand face: ideally a HUMAN, not faceless brand
- "Humans trust humans"
- 3x/day for 90 days minimum to get valid sample size
- Run 3-4 series simultaneously

### Series Ideas for Brands
- Founding story (Part 1, 2, 3...)
- "Package an order with me" (behind-the-scenes)
- Industry tips ("Did you know these ingredients are bad for your skin?")
- Day-in-the-life format

### Konstantinos (CONTRARIAN on niche)
- Don't niche to your CLIENT base -- niche to a BROAD AUDIENCE
- Broader you go on TikTok, the better -- give videos room to breathe
- He focused on "all things digital" instead of just "marketing agency clients"

## Shadow Ban Reality

### Jimmy Farley (CONTRARIAN -- likely not real)
- TikTok has NOT confirmed shadow bans exist
- No concrete evidence, only correlations
- Too many variables to conclude it's a shadow ban
- If TikTok wanted to punish you, they'd just take down your account
- Objectively good content always seems to work even for "shadow banned" accounts

### Mindset Reframe
- Believing in shadow bans removes responsibility for content quality
- Better: "I am causing everything. I need to make better content."
- You can't say wins are you but losses are TikTok -- "that's literally a sore loser"

### Recovery (JT Barnett)
- Keep posting quality content for 90 days
- The algorithm naturally fluctuates
- Evaluate honestly after 90 days of consistent posting
