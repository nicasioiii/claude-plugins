## Tweet Psychology -- 5 Cognitive Biases (JK Molina)

Every viral tweet exploits at least one psychological bias:

### 1. Optimism Bias
People believe they are less likely to end up with a bad outcome. Exploit this: make people believe their effort/lifestyle makes them immune to common pitfalls.

### 2. Reactive Devaluation
People devalue ideas that appear to originate from someone they oppose. Most viral tweets contrast the author vs. consensus.

### 3. Us vs. Them
Everyone likes believing their group is better than others. Your followers believe something others don't -- show them why they are right. **[CONTRARIAN] Attacking the opposing group works better than praising your own.**

### 4. Confirmation Bias
People interact with tweets that validate their self-image. We all believe we are rational, smart, and ethical. Remind them of this and engagement spikes.

### 5. Make Me Look Good
People retweet content because they want their followers to see it. Four "look good" angles: compassionate, smart, mature, visionary.

## The 14 "Bow" Types (Content Hook Categories)

1. **Us vs. Them** -- contrast your group vs. others
2. **Make Me Look Good** -- reader feels smart/ethical when they RT
3. **Confirmation Bias** -- validate reader's existing beliefs
4. **Metaphors** -- compare concept to familiar object (high quality, high memorability)
5. **Memes** -- ultimate tool for getting a point across
6. **Awareness** -- describe the FEELING, not the experience
7. **Timeliness** -- tweet about current events
8. **Contrasts** -- the most popular bow on Twitter; X vs. Y
9. **Going Against Consensus** -- attack the other side
10. **Calling BS Out** -- controversy drives drama (avoid gender/racial = ban risk)
11. **Calling People Out** -- people think praise is about them, criticism is about others
12. **Flaw Fuel** -- tell audience their flaws can be fuel
13. **Pride From Indifference** -- "I honestly don't care..." format crushes engagement
14. **Vulnerability** -- share struggles, mindset shifts, wins/losses

## The Problem-Pivot Technique

Start with a controversial topic, pivot to a forgiving conclusion:
- Gets attention from EVERYONE: people who agree AND people who don't
- Example: "College isn't the problem. The problem is thinking college is the only place to learn."
- Not everyone agrees college is the problem, but everyone agrees not learning is

## The Atomic Essay Format (Ship 30 for 30)

- 250 words per essay
- One clearly defined idea per essay
- Fits within a single screenshot
- Write in 30-60 minutes; edit for 5-10 minutes MAX (grammar/punctuation only)
- A 60,000-word book = 250 words + 250 words, over and over

## 3 Content Buckets Framework

1. **General Audience** (e.g., Productivity) -- broadest reach
2. **Niche Audience** (e.g., Project Management Software) -- targeted reach
3. **Industry Audience** (e.g., The Future of Productivity Software) -- thought leadership

**[CONTRARIAN] "The myth that you should only write about 1 thing and 1 thing only is false."** Use all three buckets.

## The Endless Idea Generator (3-Step Formula)

### Step 1: The 4A Framework (Direction)
- **Actionable** (here's how)
- **Analytical** (here are the numbers)
- **Aspirational** (yes, you can)
- **Anthropological** (here's why)

### Step 2: 10 Proven Approaches (Structure)
How To, Lessons Learned, Mistakes, Quotes, Ways, Tools, Trends, Stats, Reasons, Examples

### Step 3: Credibility (Why Me)
- Expert: "I am an expert. Here's what I think."
- Curator: "I talked to all the experts. Here's what they think."
- Opinion: "My opinion is the most relevant/articulate one."

**Formula: 4A + Approach + Credibility = Unlimited Ideas**

## The Golden Intersection

Tell a personal story AND give actionable advice simultaneously:
- "Sandwich" structure: advice opener -> story in middle -> actionable takeaway at end
- If you only tell a story: "That's great but what does it have to do with me?"
- If you only give advice: "Why should I trust you over everyone else?"
- Golden Intersection solves both problems

## Proof-Based Selling System

**Core principle:** "Make it so abundantly clear that your stuff works that the selling is already done by the time you ask for the credit card details."

### Do The Work, Share The Work
Share: headlines, techniques, before/afters, screenshots, testimonials, videos, sales call excerpts, how you solved problems. "You wake up every day to sell the idea of you."

### Show Numbers, Not Claims
- Bad: "You're gonna be broke if you don't focus"
- Good: "If you can't work 4 hours a day with no distractions, don't be surprised when everyone makes more money than you"
- Numbers hurt. They also sell.

## Twitter as Networking Platform (David Perell)

### 5 Mental Models for Twitter
1. **Network** -- connects based on ideas, not hierarchy
2. **School** -- rewarded for being insightful
3. **Coffee Shop** -- random interactions with half-recognized people
4. **Conference** -- follow experts from any field
5. **Newspaper** -- personalized, curated

### The 10 Club Framework (John Lilly)
"The mistake of life is thinking the mentors you need are older than you." Find 10 people who are up-and-coming, genuine, humble, kind, generous, ambitious. These become your core network.

### DMs: The Dark Matter of Twitter
- 50-80% of top experts in any field are on Twitter
- When they follow you, it unlocks DM -- very powerful
- Build relationship on main feed first (reply, engage over weeks/months)
- Then DM to meet: "Hey, I'll be in [city] if you're around for coffee"

## Profile Setup

### Bio Strategy (David Ogilvy Principle)
- Not about telling people what you can do
- Find a BIG IDEA that evokes: "You've got to be kidding me"
- Make it benefit-oriented: what's in it for them?
- Bad: "Mindset. Sales. Here to teach you affiliate marketing"
- Good: "Ideas into cash and men into kings"

### Pinned Tweet Options
- Most popular tweet
- Thread of all your best threads
- Top performing essay/content piece
- Tweet that defines your account philosophy

## Content Repurposing Chain

1. For every chapter you read, write down your thoughts
2. From those thoughts, create an email
3. From that email, create some tweets
4. For every tweet that does well, create a thread
5. From that thread, create a module/course

**"If you're only using ideas once, you're making Twitter harder than it has to be."**

## Writing Mindset Principles

- **"The more you write, the more you write."** Inverse is also true. You are always practicing one of two habits: writing or not-writing.
- **"You can't steer a stationary ship."** Discover your path BY writing, not before writing.
- **Permission to create junk.** Get comfortable creating whatever you feel compelled to create. Atomic Essays that perform well = signals. Those that don't = signals to adjust.
- **Write for your past self.** Everything in the gap between who you were and who you are = valuable content.
- **[CONTRARIAN] "For the first year or longer, editing is largely a waste of time."** Only edit 5-10 minutes for grammar.
