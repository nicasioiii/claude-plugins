## 27 Wordplay Templates (JK Molina)

1. **Good vs. Great** -- "This starts with X... and ends with -X"
2. **Dialogue Comparisons** -- add dialogues to make comparisons funnier
3. **If X then Y / If -X then -Y** -- people who do this also do this embarrassing thing
4. **Maybe-Ridiculous** -- "Maybe if we did this to something ridiculous they could become something serious"
5. **Root Cause** -- "The reason this happens is because most people suck at this"
6. **Negative Twist** -- "If this is negative quality, imagine this seemingly unrelated thing"
7. **Immunity** -- "You can't do this if I'm somehow immune to it"
8. **Problem Reframe** -- "This isn't the problem. The problem is your attitude towards the problem"
9. **Cure Doesn't Fix** -- "This with a cure is still this"
10. **Leveling** -- Level 1 / Level 2 / Level 3
11. **More-Less** -- "The more positive quality you have, the less you negative quality"
12. **This Over That** -- "This will do more for you than this thing people usually do"
13. **Metaphor Disguise** -- "The best X are actually this in disguise"
14. **Stop-Start** -- Negative vs. Positive (placing the negative first works best)
15. **Unconventional Methods** -- "The best this have unconventional methods"
16. **We're All Like This** -- "We're all like this. Some people just do something differently."
17. **Permission** -- "It's ok to do X and not do quality associated with X"
18. **Goal-Need-Don't Need** -- 1. introductory phrase, 2. Things you don't need, 3. Things you need
19. **Self-Deception** -- "You're not X, you're just something else"
20. **Disguised As** -- "This is this thing disguised as this other thing"
21. **Not-Because** -- "You're not X because you have this. You're X because you don't have that."
22. **Middle Ground** -- "X side is ok. Y side is ok. What's not ok is Z."
23. **Don't Envy** -- "Don't envy this, envy that"
24. **If-Love** -- "If you like X, you'll love Y"
25. **Born For** -- "You were not born for X"
26. **Realization** -- "I used to think X. Now I realize it's Y."
27. **Easier Than** -- "People haven't realized that X is easier than Y"

## The Atomic Essay Format (Ship 30 for 30)

- 250 words per essay
- One clearly defined idea
- Fits within a single screenshot
- Write in 30-60 minutes
- Edit 5-10 minutes MAX (grammar/punctuation only)
- Goal statement template: "I am going to write every day for 60 minutes at [TIME] at [PLACE]. I will write 250-word Atomic Essays about [TOPICS], published on Twitter."

## The Endless Idea Generator (Complete)

### Step 1: 4A Framework (Direction)
- **Actionable** -- "here's how" (tactical, step-by-step)
- **Analytical** -- "here are the numbers" (data, research, stats)
- **Aspirational** -- "yes, you can" (motivational, possibility)
- **Anthropological** -- "here's why" (observation, human behavior)

### Step 2: 10 Proven Approaches (Structure)
1. How To
2. Lessons Learned
3. Mistakes
4. Quotes
5. Ways
6. Tools
7. Trends
8. Stats
9. Reasons
10. Examples / Embarrassing Moments

### Step 3: Credibility (Why Me)
- **Expert:** "I am an expert on this topic. Here's what I think."
- **Curator:** "I talked to all the trusted experts. Here's what they think."
- **Opinion:** "My opinion is the most relevant/articulate one of all."

### Example Outputs for "Quitting Your 9-5":
- [Actionable + How To + Expert]: "I Just Quit My 9-5 And Doubled My Monthly Earnings. Here Are 5 Steps"
- [Analytical + How To + Curated]: "A New Study Shows Millennials Are The Poorest Generation. Here's Advice From 10 Financial Planners"
- [Aspirational + How To + Personal Story]: "I Used To Have $150,000 Of Debt. Here's My Step-By-Step Guide For Financial Freedom"
- [Anthropological + How To + Curated]: "Mark Cuban Just Said Something That Reveals Why So Many Never Achieve Financial Freedom"

## Thread Writing Best Practices

### Structure
1. **Tweet 1 (Hook):** Promise what the thread delivers. Use curiosity + specificity.
2. **Tweet 2-N (Body):** One point per tweet. Use 1/3/1 rhythm within tweets.
3. **Final Tweet:** Summarize + CTA (follow, retweet, link)

### Viral Mechanics
- Threads get more engagement than single tweets
- First tweet must stand alone as a great tweet
- Number format in hook: "7 lessons I learned from..."
- Each tweet should provide standalone value
- End every 2-3 tweets with a mini-hook to keep reading

---

## The 1/3/1 Writing Rhythm

For any text block (tweet, essay, post):
- **1 sentence** -- strong opening
- **3 sentences** -- supporting detail/explanation
- **1 sentence** -- punchy conclusion

This creates natural reading flow. Avoids walls of text.

## The Golden Intersection

Tell a personal story AND give actionable advice simultaneously:
- **Sandwich structure:** advice opener -> story in middle -> actionable takeaway
- Solves two problems: "why should I trust you?" (story) + "what's in it for me?" (advice)

## Proof-Based Selling System (Complete)

### Do The Work, Share The Work
Share: headlines, ads, techniques, secrets, before/afters, screenshots (lots), testimonials, videos, pictures, sales call excerpts, how you handled objections, how you solved problems.

### It Ain't Bragging If It's True
- Share open rates, conversion rates, client results
- Screenshot testimonials and post immediately
- Save in a folder AND post

### Show Numbers, Not Claims
- Numbers hurt. They also sell.
- Bad: "You're gonna be broke someday"
- Good: "If you can't work 4 hours/day with no distractions, don't be surprised when everyone makes more money"

## DM Strategy (David Perell)

### The Dark Matter of Twitter
- 50-80% of top experts in any field are on Twitter
- When they follow you, it unlocks DM

### Best Practices
**DO:**
- Send relevant articles/resources
- Compliment SPECIFIC work (not generic praise)
- Offer to help with something concrete
- Keep it short: "Hey, are you in [city]? Would love to grab coffee"
- Do the work on the main feed FIRST

**DON'T:**
- "Hi, I need your help" from strangers
- "Hello, sir" (sounds like scam)
- Long messages about yourself
- Me-me-me focused messages

### The 10 Club Framework (John Lilly)
Find 10 people who are up-and-coming, genuine, humble, kind, generous, ambitious. These become your core network. "The mistake of life is thinking the mentors you need are older than you."

## Profile Optimization

### Bio Strategy (Big Idea Principle)
- Find a BIG IDEA that evokes: "You've got to be kidding me"
- Make it benefit-oriented
- Bad: "Mindset. Sales. Here to teach you affiliate marketing"
- Good: "Ideas into cash and men into kings"
- Good: "$50K/month email marketing agency" (big accomplishment = big idea)

### Pinned Tweet Options
1. Most popular tweet (highest engagement)
2. Most popular article link
3. Thread of all your best threads
4. Top performing essay/content piece
5. Tweet that defines your account philosophy

### Pseudonymous Account Best Practices
- Use faces, not logos (people connect with characters)
- Build a language: slang, speech patterns, jokes
- Stand for something: "The algorithmic lords reward loud opinions"
- Social capital IS transferable to real name
- Historical precedent: Ben Franklin, Hamilton, J.K. Rowling, Stephen King

## Building In Public (Pre-Selling)

### Pre-Selling Checklist
1. Agitate the problem with content (use numbers)
2. Show proof that your method works
3. Promise something bold
4. Show the unique mechanism
5. Show a unique selling point
6. Show an irresistible offer
7. Show a big idea

### Pre-Launch Content Strategy
- 5 tweets pointing out the problem
- 10 tweets building in public
- "Leak" product to a few people for before/after
- Write threads on lessons with waiting list CTA
- Post screenshots of results
- Share thread on table of contents

### Dissolving Objections With Content
"Solve objections with your content, collect payments with your website."

## Reposting Strategy

- Best tweets at 5K followers: release again at 10K (those 5K new people haven't seen it)
- Search your best tweets: `yourusername min_retweets:x`
- Reword, repurpose, and repost
- Drafts: save good ideas you can't word yet, come back with fresh mind
- "Wasting thoughts is a sin"
