# Write Hooks

## WHY HOOKS MATTER MORE THAN ANYTHING ELSE

**Spend 30-40% of total video creation time on your hook (Kallaway, E03).** This ratio is not a typo.

**Funnel math:** The second a viewer churns, everything after that point might as well be a black screen. If they churn at 4 seconds of a 55-second video, you wasted 51 seconds of effort.

**Hook = headline + intro + thumbnail combined.** On short-form, videos are fed automatically -- viewers react with reflex, not choice. Your hook must be that much better.

**The #1 reason posts fail (Frabrand, E01):** Hook and concept -- not algorithm, not packaging, not CTAs, not information quality.

## THE 4 HOOK COMPONENTS (Kallaway, E03)

In order of importance:

1. **Text Hook** -- What you write on screen. Consumed first because people read faster than they hear.
2. **Visual Hook** -- What you show (B-roll, graphics, A-roll, movement).
3. **Spoken Hook** -- What you say verbally.
4. **Audio Hook** -- Non-spoken audio (music + sound effects).

**Key insight:** Videos without spoken audio can still perform; spoken words with weak visuals/text rarely do. Viewer attention flow: visual/text > spoken > visual/text.

---

## MAX ALIGNMENT -- THE SECRET TO WINNING HOOKS (Kallaway, E03)

The difference between 500 and 500,000 views is achieving max alignment across all 4 components.

**Comprehension** = the degree to which the viewer actually understands what they see and hear.

**Good alignment:** Spoken, visual, text, and audio all reinforce the same message from different angles. Complementary, not contradictory.

**Bad alignment:** Spoken says one thing, visual shows something slightly different = comprehension loss = confusion = bounce.

### Alignment Self-Test

After creating your hook, run three tests:
1. **Silent test:** Watch on mute. Are you clear on topic + curiosity loop?
2. **Audio-only test:** Listen with eyes closed. Are you clear?
3. **Combined test:** Watch and listen. Is comprehension immediate?

If any test fails, your alignment needs work.

## VIEWER PSYCHOLOGY -- ON-TARGET + CURIOSITY LOOP (Kallaway, E03)

To hook a viewer you need two things:

### On-Target (Is this video for me?)

- Communicate what the video is about in the first sentence -- rapid context
- Use fewest words (distillation) that make clearest point (clarity)
- Use "you/your" instead of "me/my/I" -- makes viewer feel spoken to directly
- Saying "me/I" forces the viewer to decide if they see themselves in you -- this paralysis drives comprehension down

### Curiosity Loop (Why should I keep watching?)

Best way to create curiosity = **contrast**.

**Contrast = distance between expectations (common belief) and reality (your contrarian take).** The bigger the gap, the more hooked they are.

**Two types of contrast:**

1. **Stated Contrast:** Explicitly name the common belief, then present contrarian reality.
   - Example: "Stop putting money in your 401K. Instead open a smart-yield harvest account."
   - Lower comprehension loss, but more specific (may rule out viewers)

2. **Implied Contrast:** Never state common belief directly; let viewer construct baseline.
   - Example: "Use a smart-yield harvest account. Biggest hack rich people use."
   - Relates to more people, but higher comprehension risk

## THE 7 SPOKEN HOOK ARCHETYPES (Kallaway, E03)

1. **Fortuneteller** -- Compare existing present to a new future.
   - "This backpack is going to change how people hike in the future."

2. **Experimentation** -- Frame a pain point being solved through a live experiment.
   - "You can pack more hiking equipment if you just do this..."

3. **Educational/Tutorial** -- Frame a pain point solved through a method/tool.
   - "If you want to fit more in your backpack, use the 2-1-3 method."

4. **Secret Reveal** -- Reveal a hidden truth or finding.
   - "There's a backpack brand nobody knows about but all the celebrities are wearing."

5. **Contrarian/Negative** -- State a non-obvious belief immediately.
   - "People spend way too much time obsessing over their backpack design."

6. **Comparison** -- Compare many versions.
   - "These are the top 5 hiking backpack brands...but which one is actually best?"

7. **Question** -- Post a question they want answered.
   - "Why are so many people wearing this backpack when they go hiking?"

**Bonus layer (combine with any):**
- **Raw Shock** -- Instant scroll-stop word/action/sound.

## THE CGOVE FRAMEWORK (Mino Lee, E01)

Analyze hooks AFTER writing/filming. Do NOT use during creative flow.

1. **C -- Clarity:** Hook must be simple and easy to understand. Leave ONE question in mind, not multiple. Don't spaghetti-ball the hook with 3 different text lines and 4 visuals.

2. **G -- (Curiosity) Gap:** Must leave something out for the viewer to wonder about. Know what question you're creating. Make clear what question you are solving WITHOUT giving the answer.

3. **O -- Originality:** Viewer shouldn't assume they already know the answer. Most information is not new -- make it FEEL new through your unique experience or a recent discovery.

4. **V -- (Perceived) Value/Trust:** Viewer must believe you will deliver. Speed = perceived value. If you take 10 seconds to convey a simple point, they think the video is not worth their time.

5. **E -- Emotion:** Must make them FEEL something. Without emotion, you have an indifferent question. The WORST reception = indifference. Even anger is better than indifference.

**WARNING:** Right-brain (creative flow) for writing/filming. Left-brain (CGOVE analysis) for revision. Never interrupt creative flow with analysis.

## THE 10-MINUTE HOOK WRITING FRAMEWORK (Kallaway, E03)

### Step 0: Understand Your Visuals

Check what B-roll/footage you already have. Build hook around available footage if strong.

### Step 1: Write Spoken Hook

**Easy Mode:** Copy a validated hook structure from research.

**Normal Mode:** Answer two questions:
1. What context must I communicate so viewer can decide to opt in?
2. What contrast between their current belief and mine creates interest?

Can you do both in one sentence? If not, take two. Max contrast = 100 people believe one thing, you are the only one who believes the other. Fewer words = better.

### Step 2: Write Text Hook

Fewest words possible. Font as big as possible. Easy to read. White/colored background behind text. Keep close to "eye level" of subject.

### Step 3: Build Visual Hook

Visuals need enough motion and color. Brain reacts to color and motion subconsciously.
- If A-roll: The more attractive and happy you look, the better. Include initial motion. Use "Pope in the Pool" -- do passive action while explaining.
- If B-roll: Switch visuals every 1-2 seconds. Use arrows/circles. High contrast.

### Step 4: Record Multiple Takes

### Step 5: Edit Hook -- Final Check

Run the 3 alignment tests (silent, audio-only, combined).

## HOOK FAILURE DIAGNOSIS (Kallaway, E03)

### Patterns in Hooks That Flopped

- "In my opinion" wastes most valuable real estate
- "Me/I" framing forces viewer to decide if they relate
- No curiosity loop / no contrast
- Visual and spoken hooks misaligned
- Pace too slow
- Title text too long, not on screen long enough to read
- Points feel disconnected
- AI voice sounds fake, intonation unclear
- Confusing word combinations (comprehension loss)
- Speed to value too slow (contrarian solution not until 3rd sentence)

### Patterns in Hooks That Hit (900K-14M views)

- Immediate context clarity in first line
- Contrast embedded in frame naturally
- Visual hook perfectly matches spoken hook
- Super clear, impossible to misunderstand
- Text hook + spoken hook alignment perfect
- Relatable even when using "I/me" if universally relatable situation

## TWO-HOOK SYSTEM (Ryan Magin, E06)

**Hook 1 -- "Stop the Scroll" (seconds 1-3):**
- ONLY goal: make them stop scrolling
- Must be attention-grabbing, controversial, or polarizing

**Hook 2 -- "Fall in Line" / "Reason to Watch" (seconds 3-6):**
- Gives them a reason to keep watching
- This is the "real" hook that delivers the promise

**Example:** "Here's how I made $40K flipping my first house" (Hook 1 -- stops scroll) + "...with a credit score of 492 and less than $5K in my bank" (Hook 2 -- reason to watch).

## CONTEXT CAPTIONS (Jimmy Farley, E06)

The caption overlaid on the video that explains what the viewer is about to see. This can transform a video from zero engagement to millions of views.

**Rule:** If it is NOT clear as day what your video is about in the first moment, ADD A CONTEXT CAPTION. Always.

**Without context:** Guy putting tape on a broom (scroll past).
**With context:** "Spider-Man web transition tutorial" > 10M+ views.

## HOOK REVISION FRAMEWORK

### The Compression Process

1. Write the hook as a full thought
2. Remove every unnecessary word
3. Compress 2-3 words into one where possible
4. Cut any word that does not add meaning
5. Read aloud -- does it flow?

### A/B Testing Strategy

- Create 3-5 hook variations for important content
- Use IG Trial Reels to test multiple hooks simultaneously
- Best metric for hook effectiveness: average watch time
- After testing, reuse winning patterns; discard losing patterns
