# Hook Formulas Reference

## 7 SPOKEN HOOK ARCHETYPES -- EXPANDED (Kallaway, E03)

### 1. Fortuneteller

Compare existing present to a new future.
- "This [product] is going to change how people [activity] in the future."
- "In 5 years, nobody will [current practice]. Here's why."
- "The future of [industry] looks nothing like today."

### 2. Experimentation

Frame a pain point being solved through a live experiment.
- "You can [better outcome] if you just do this..."
- "I tested [X] for 30 days. Here's what happened."
- "Let's see if [method] actually works..."

### 3. Educational/Tutorial

Frame a pain point solved through a method/tool.
- "If you want to [outcome], use the [method name]."
- "Here's the exact system I use to [result]."
- "3 steps to [desirable outcome] that nobody talks about."

### 4. Secret Reveal

Reveal a hidden truth or finding.
- "There's a [thing] nobody knows about but [group] is using."
- "The [industry] doesn't want you to know this."
- "I just discovered something about [topic] that changes everything."

### 5. Contrarian/Negative

State a non-obvious belief immediately.
- "People spend way too much time on [common activity]."
- "Stop doing [popular practice]. It's ruining your [outcome]."
- "[Common advice] is the worst advice you can follow."

### 6. Comparison

Compare many versions.
- "These are the top 5 [category]...but which one is actually best?"
- "I tried every [product type] so you don't have to."
- "[Option A] vs. [Option B] -- the answer surprised me."

### 7. Question

Post a question they want answered.
- "Why are so many people [doing unexpected thing]?"
- "What if everything you know about [topic] is wrong?"
- "How do [successful people] always manage to [result]?"

## 8 INSTAGRAM HOOK TYPES (E04)

### 1. Text Overlay Hook (Most common for educational)
Text appears at frame zero before video starts playing. First frame IS the hook.

### 2. Caption First-Line Hook
If no text overlay, the first line of caption serves as secondary hook.

### 3. Visual / Aesthetic Hook
Highly visual reels that are inherently gratifying. No text needed.

### 4. Revealing / Working Progress Hook
Content that reveals itself over time. Journey as important as result.

### 5. Fast-Cut Hook
Cuts slightly faster than the brain can process. Prevents boredom.

### 6. "Wait For It" Hook
Text or voice telling viewer to wait for the end. Use sparingly.

### 7. Multi-Angle Hook
Text says one thing, voice says something complementary but different. Hits viewer from different brain pathways.

### Hook Formulas for Educational IG Content
- "How to [desired outcome]"
- "5 hacks you never knew about [topic]"
- "Top 5 [topic] hacks"
- "Here's how I managed to [impressive result]"
- "Here's why you DON'T [negative framing]"
- Bold statement: "[Counterintuitive claim]"
- Question: "[Question your audience asks]"
- Intrigue: "[Your impressive result/stat]"

## HOOK DIAGNOSTIC TOOL

### Quick Diagnosis Checklist

| Question | If No... |
|---|---|
| Is the topic clear in the first sentence? | Add context caption or rewrite for clarity |
| Does it create a curiosity loop? | Add contrast (stated or implied) |
| Are all 4 components aligned? | Fix the component that diverges |
| Is pacing fast enough? | Cut words, speed delivery |
| Does it create emotion? | Add stakes, specificity, or controversy |
| Would your target avatar stop scrolling? | The topic may not resonate -- reframe or change |

### CGOVE Scoring

Rate each element 1-5:

| Element | Score | Fix If Low |
|---|---|---|
| Clarity | /5 | Simplify, reduce to one question |
| Gap (Curiosity) | /5 | Add contrast or withhold the answer |
| Originality | /5 | Add your unique angle or recent discovery |
| Value (Perceived) | /5 | Add credibility signal, increase pace |
| Emotion | /5 | Add stakes, specificity, tonality |

**Target:** 20+ out of 25. Below 15 = rewrite completely.

## PSYCHOLOGY PRINCIPLES FOR HOOKS (Frabrand, E01)

1. **Cocktail Party Effect** -- People care only about information that applies to them. Use "you."
2. **Bandwagon Effect** -- Social proof in hooks increases trust. "Join 450,000 others."
3. **Numerology** -- Odd numbers perform better than even numbers in headlines.
4. **Serial Positioning** -- People remember beginnings and endings most. Strong hook + strong conclusion.
5. **Loss Aversion** -- Pain-cure hooks outperform benefit/vitamin hooks.
6. **Anchoring Bias** -- First information anchors all subsequent. Lead with your strongest point.

---

## TWEET PSYCHOLOGY -- 5 COGNITIVE BIASES FOR HOOKS (JK Molina, E07)

1. **Optimism Bias** -- Make people believe their effort makes them immune to common pitfalls
2. **Reactive Devaluation** -- Contrast yourself vs. consensus
3. **Us vs. Them** -- Show why your group/approach is superior
4. **Confirmation Bias** -- Validate the reader's existing self-image
5. **Make Me Look Good** -- If your content makes someone look smart when they share it, they will engage

## HOOK RESEARCH METHOD (Kallaway, E03)

1. Build a Creator List: 20-30 accounts in your niche, 50K+ followers, 50+ posts
2. Find them: Pick one you know, go to their "Following" and scan
3. Use sortfeed.com (Chrome extension). Go to each creator's Reels tab, filter last 200 videos, sort by views
4. Open top performers in new tabs, analyze the hook across all 4 components
5. Run this process every 2-4 weeks

## SAME CONTENT, DIFFERENT HOOK (E04)

Same content can reach different audiences through different hooks:
- "How to look good in every photo" (positive framing)
- "Here's why you don't look good in your photos" (negative framing)
- Same information, different emotional trigger, different audience segment
