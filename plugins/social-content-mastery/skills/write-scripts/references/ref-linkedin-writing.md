# LinkedIn Writing Guide

## 5 Keys to Writing on LinkedIn (E02 Dickie Bush & Cole)

### Key 1: Write to One Reader
Every post speaks to one specific person with one specific problem. Not "everyone in marketing." One marketer struggling with one challenge. The more specific, the more universal it feels.

### Key 2: Use Proper Formatting
LinkedIn has limited formatting -- no bold H1s. Create "headers" by giving each section its own line with numbering (Item #1:, 1/, 1., etc.).

Headers accomplish two goals:
1. Act as section dividers for skimming
2. Deliver on hook promise -- reader should extract 80% of value from headers alone

Use sentence-style headers: full sentences, first word capitalized, punctuation at end. Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences). White space. Clear section lines. Avoid walls of text.

### Key 3: Post 3-5x Per Week
More posts = faster improvement + more chances to attract right opportunities. Having a plan eases content creation anxiety.

### Key 4: Use Proven Post Formats
5 core content styles (see below). These mirror what LinkedIn readers want to know.

### Key 5: Analyze Metrics 1x Per Week
30 minutes every Saturday with coffee. Track per-post: saves, comments, profile visits, follows, link clicks. Clone your winner 3 ways: new hook, new audience segment, new format.

## 5 Content Styles (E02)

### 1. Steps
"5 steps to [outcome]"
- Most actionable format
- Number each step clearly
- Each step: what to do + why it matters + quick example
- Works best for solution-aware audience

### 2. Stats
"X stats that prove [claim]"
- Builds authority through data
- Each stat: number + source (if available) + implication
- Pair surprising stats with your interpretation
- Works best for credibility building

### 3. Mistakes
"X mistakes that [negative outcome]"
- Pain-point driven (loss aversion = strong engagement)
- Each mistake: what people do wrong + why it fails + what to do instead
- Works best for problem-aware audience

### 4. Lessons
"X lessons from [experience]"
- Story-driven wisdom format
- Each lesson: the situation + what you learned + how to apply it
- Personal experience makes it unique and hard to replicate
- Works best for building trust

### 5. Examples
"X examples of [concept in action]"
- Proof-driven credibility format
- Each example: who/what + what they did + the result
- Real examples > hypothetical scenarios
- Works best for product/solution-aware audience

## Post Structure Framework (E02)

### The Full Structure
- **Hook** (first 200 chars): ~200 characters. Must be both hook and cliffhanger
- **Introduction** (~300-400 chars): 4 blocks stacked:
  - Block 1: Opening hook (before "See more")
  - Block 2: Promise -- niche-specific problem or outcome (3-5 sentences or bullets)
  - Block 3: Solution -- 1-2 sentences telling reader you will help
  - Block 4: Transition sentence ("Let's walk through each one")
- **Each Main Section** (~300-450 chars):
  - Declarative opening (the "what")
  - 3-5 tactical pieces (the "how")
  - One-sentence insight (the "why")
- **Conclusion** (~100-200 chars): Summary, engagement question, or CTA

### Critical Formatting Rule
Alternate format between bulleted lists and paragraph style across sections. Section 1: bullets. Section 2: paragraph. Section 3: bullets. This visual variety keeps readers engaged.

### Character Limits
- Total: stay under 2,800 characters (of 3,000 max). Non-negotiable
- Short posts: 50-120 words (quick insights, single ideas)
- Long posts: 200-450 words (frameworks, detailed breakdowns)
- Mix weekly: 2 short posts + 1 long post + 1 visual every 1-2 weeks

## Voice Archetype System (E02)

### Finding vs. Creating Your Voice
Your voice is CREATED, not found. It emerges through consistent practice and deliberate choices.

### How to Develop Voice
1. Write consistently for 30+ days
2. Notice patterns in what feels natural and what gets engagement
3. Use AI to analyze your writing patterns
4. Codify patterns into a replicable voice profile

### AI Voice Profile
Create a voice profile document that captures:
- Sentence length preferences (short punchy vs. flowing)
- Vocabulary tendencies (simple vs. sophisticated)
- Structural patterns (list-heavy vs. narrative)
- Tone markers (authoritative vs. conversational)
- Forbidden words/phrases (what you never say)

This profile can be fed to AI tools to maintain consistent voice when accelerating content production.

## "Outlines As Content" Method (E02)

"All great writing is basically just a Jenga tower of ideas stacked on top of each other."

### The Process
1. Write 3-5 short-form posts per week as outlines (50-120 words)
2. Track which outlines resonate with audience (saves, comments, shares)
3. Expand proven outlines into 1-2 long-form posts per week (200-450 words)
4. You are never starting from scratch -- you already validated the idea

### Why This Works
- Low risk per post (15-20 min investment)
- Data-driven expansion (only invest in proven ideas)
- Builds writing habit and audience simultaneously
- Creates a feedback loop between short-form tests and long-form investment

## Carousel Text Structure (E02)

### 10-Slide Framework
1. **Slide 1**: Big Promise / Hook (make them swipe)
2. **Slide 2**: Stakes (problems if you don't act)
3. **Slide 3**: Example (real-world application)
4. **Slides 4-7**: Framework (clear steps to follow)
5. **Slide 8**: Checklist (common mistakes to avoid)
6. **Slide 9**: Quick Win (one tool to try now)
7. **Slide 10**: Call to Action (next step or question)

### Creation Workflow
Write a LinkedIn text post first -> Optimize for Gamma.app using AI -> Paste into Gamma ("Social" format) -> Select theme -> Download as PDF -> Upload to LinkedIn.

### Carousel Writing Rules
- Each slide: one idea only
- Large text, minimal words per slide
- Visual consistency across all slides (brand colors, fonts)
- Swipe incentive on each slide (cliffhanger or "next...")

## LinkedIn Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

### Content Mistakes
- Walls of text with no formatting or section breaks
- Starting with "I" or your name (boring, self-focused)
- Generic motivational quotes without personal insight
- Posting external links in the post body (algorithm penalizes)
- Using more than 3-5 hashtags (spam signal)
- Being clever instead of clear ("prioritizing the pun" over clarity)

### Structural Mistakes
- No clear hook in first 200 characters
- No sections or headers (readers skim, not read)
- Same format for every section (no visual variety)
- Conclusion that just trails off (no CTA or engagement question)
- Exceeding 2,800 characters

### Voice Mistakes
- Corporate jargon when audience wants conversational tone
- Writing like you write, not like you speak
- Being too formal when building personal brand
- "Write like you're explaining your work to someone at a barbecue, not pitching them" (E02)

## Engagement Question Strategy (E02)

End every post with an engagement question that:
- Is specific to the post topic (not generic "thoughts?")
- Invites readers to share their own experience
- Creates a low barrier to respond
- Examples: "What's the #1 mistake you've made with [topic]?" or "Which of these 5 steps is hardest for you?"

Comments = someone read post, processed it, formulated response, typed it out = 2-3 minutes of dwell time. When others read those comments = even more time on your post. This is the flywheel.

## Pitch In Public (E02)

Once per month, publish one offer post: problem -> stakes -> your method -> proof -> invite. Write core offer in one sentence: who it is for, outcome, timeline, price range. This is transparent, authentic selling -- not hidden or manipulative.
