# Script Structures Reference

## 8 VIRAL VIDEO FORMATS -- DETAILED STRUCTURES

### Format 1: Educational Video

**Full Structure:**
```
HOOK: Persuade viewer this covers THEIR problem (0-3 sec)
THING 1: State it > WHY it's crucial > Quick example or story
THING 2: State it > WHY it's crucial > Quick example or story
THING 3: State it > WHY it's crucial > Quick example or story (BEST saved for last)
CONCLUSION: Plug lead magnet or "follow for more"
```

**Scripting Rules:**
- Every sentence must justify its existence
- After stating a "thing" (e.g., "hammer curl"), always explain WHY it's crucial
- Embellish with opinion -- you're not a textbook
- Inject personality through small stories/tangents
- Viewers could ask ChatGPT -- they watch YOU for entertainment too
- Save the best point for LAST -- improves satisfaction and rewatchability

### Format 2: Challenge Video

**Three-Act Structure:**
```
ACT 1: GOAL SETUP (hook) -- What's the challenge? What are the stakes?
ACT 2: SETBACK -- What went wrong? What obstacle appeared?
ACT 3: PAYOFF -- How was it resolved? Satisfying conclusion
```
- Video without any challenge/obstacle is boring
- Need stakes -- what happens if you fail?
- Tension drives retention

### Format 3: Vlog / Day-in-the-Life

**Minimum Viable Vlog System (Mino Lee, E01):**
1. Collect B-roll throughout your day
2. Film base A-roll (talking head narrating)
3. Edit together

**Key rule:** Nobody cares about the events in your life. Partner events with lessons learned.

### Format 4: Reaction Video

- Hooks already optimized by original creator (built-in advantage)
- Con: trust accrual to you is slower since value per second from you is lower
- May be worth it at 100x views compared to original content

### Format 5: Life Lesson Video

- Common among cinematic TikTok/Instagram creators
- Personal wisdom delivered with emotional resonance
- Usually paired with atmospheric B-roll and music

### Format 6: News Story Video

- Cover specific news in your niche
- MKBHD, Philip DeFranco style
- Timeliness is critical -- first to report gets the views

### Format 7: Comparison Video

- Tier lists, versus formats, ranking systems
- Natural curiosity driver -- everyone wants to know the "best"

### Format 8: Skit Video

- POV skits, comedy sketches
- High shareability when relatable

## H.O.T. FORMULA -- YOUTUBE SCRIPTS (Sunny, E08)

**H -- Hook:**
- First 15-30 seconds
- Address the viewer's problem/desire directly
- Create immediate relevance
- Use keyword in spoken hook for SEO

**O -- Outcome:**
- Tell them exactly what they'll learn/get
- "By the end of this video, you'll know exactly how to..."
- Sets expectations and creates commitment

**T -- Testimonial/Trust:**
- Brief credibility signal
- "I've helped 500+ students do this" or "After 10 years of..."
- Don't brag -- establish relevance
- Can also be a result: "This method grew my channel to 50K"

## YOUNGER-SELF SCRIPTING PROCESS -- COMPLETE (Mino Lee, E01)

### Step 1: Visualization
Close eyes for 20 seconds. Think of yourself exactly 1 year ago. Remember that person -- their fears, doubts, daily life.

### Step 2: List Wins
Write 5 specific things that happened that your younger self would be excited about. Be concrete: "Got my first 10K view video" not "Got better at content."

### Step 3: Identify the Change
For each win: What is ONE specific change that made it happen? Not "work hard" -- something like "I started scripting every video instead of winging it."

### Step 4: Add the Doubt (KEY HACK)
What doubt would your younger self have said back? "But I don't have time to script every video" or "That won't work in my niche."

### Step 5: Convince Them
What do you say back? 3-4 sentences using specific numbers and evidence. "I went from 200 views to 10K views in 30 days by spending just 15 extra minutes scripting."

### Step 6: Close
Final short message of encouragement or call to action.

## AIDA FORMULA -- VARIATIONS

### Classic AIDA
```
ATTENTION: Hook / first line / Slide 1
INTEREST: Expand on the hook, build context (2-3 sentences / Slides 2-3)
DESIRE/DETAIL: Body of content -- the value delivery
ACTION: Call to action
```

### Middle CTA Variation
```
EDUCATION SECTION 1
CTA (soft -- "Save this post")
EDUCATION SECTION 2
FINAL CTA (stronger -- "Comment X for my guide")
```

### Heavy Education Variation
```
HOOK
PURE EDUCATION (maximum value, no interruption)
CTA at end only
```

### Storytelling Variation
```
INTEREST (intriguing opening)
OPEN LOOPS (create questions in reader's mind)
CURIOSITY GAPS (answer some, open new ones)
CTA (resolution + action)
```

## POST ENDINGS -- 6 CATEGORIES COMPLETE (Authority Marketing, E05)

### Pre-Writing: 3 Questions Before Every Post Ending

1. Who are you talking to?
2. What ONE message are you delivering?
3. What behavior are you trying to change? (Motivate, Challenge, Connect to outcome, State opinion)

### 5 Post-Ending Writing Principles

1. Avoid complex terminology -- keep punchy and simple
2. Keep language SIMPLE -- imagine reader is a 12-year-old
3. One row, one sentence, space in between
4. Use POWERFUL words: you, imagine, discover, proven, secret, now, today, free, bonus, step-by-step, easy, because, results, win, master, value
5. Use Verbs of ACHIEVEMENT: build, master, discover, learn, surpass, win, maximize, transform, overcome, improve, grow, accelerate, create, attract, generate

### Body-to-Ending Connectors

- "That's it."
- "To sum up:"
- "Remember:"
- "Don't forget:"
- "A friendly advice:"

### Category 1: Connect With Successful Outcome

- "Master [SKILL], and [POSITIVE OUTCOME]."
- "Make [X], and you'll [ACHIEVE SUCCESSFUL OUTCOME]."
- "Want to improve your [KEY METRIC]? Give this a shot."

### Category 2: Connect With Bad Outcome

- "[X] is complex enough. Don't complicate it further."
- "You can [DO AMAZING THING]. But if you [BAD THING] -- you [FACE BAD OUTCOME]."
- "Hello, [GOOD OUTCOME]. Goodbye, [BAD OUTCOME]."

### Category 3: Challenge

- "There's hardly anything as powerful as [X]. Take this seriously."
- "An average [X] [ACHIEVES A]. A great [X] [ACHIEVES B]."
- "[COMMON ADVICE] -- Yeah, but [CHALLENGE THAT ADVICE]?"
- "Still not taking [X] seriously?"

### Category 4: Motivate

- "Give [X] a go. You'll see [WHY X IS USEFUL]."
- "[STATE FACT]. There has never been a better time to [START X]."

### Category 5: Summarize

- "Step 1. [DO X]. Step 2. [DO Y]. Step 3. [ACHIEVE SUCCESS]."
- "It's simple -- [STATE SOMETHING]. Making [X] will help you [ACHIEVE Y]."

### Category 6: Ask For A Sale

- "P.S. If you like this, you will love [X]. Go to [LINK] and grab it."
- "P.S. If you want [BENEFIT] without [PAIN], my [PRODUCT] launches in [X] days."
- "Tomorrow, I'll go deeper on [X]. Join [X] subscribers: [LINK]"

## 1/3/1 WRITING RHYTHM -- ADVANCED (Dickie Bush, E07)

### The Core Pattern

```
[1 sentence -- strong opener]

[3 sentences -- the explanation, the context, the depth]

[1 sentence -- the punchline, the takeaway, the transition]
```

### Variations and Rules

- 1/4/1, 1/5/1, 1/7/1 are all valid
- Maximum 7 sentences in a single paragraph for online readers
- The longer the middle paragraph, the shorter the surrounding singles
- "Creating rhythms in your online writing is a game of opposites"
- Short sentences create urgency. Long sentences create reflection.
- Alternate rhythm throughout the piece to maintain novelty

### Application by Section

| Section | Pattern | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | 1/3/1 | Strong hook, quick context, transition |
| Body sections | 1/5/1 or 1/7/1 | Depth with boundaries |
| Conclusion | 1/3/1 or 1/1 | Resolution, clarity, power |

## RATE OF REVELATION -- CHECKLIST

### Words to Always Cut

- That
- As (when used as "because")
- Therefore / However / Furthermore
- Practically / Honestly / Basically
- All adverbs (-ly words) unless essential
- "In order to" (replace with "to")
- "The fact that" (delete entirely)
- "I think" / "I believe" (just state the belief)

### Sentence-Level Cuts

- If a sentence repeats something already said: DELETE
- If a sentence adds no new information: DELETE
- If a sentence exists only as transition: COMPRESS to one clause

### The Compression Test

Read your piece aloud. Every time you feel bored or your mind wanders, you've found a slow spot. Cut it or restructure.

## THE GOLDEN INTERSECTION (Dickie Bush, E07)

### Structure

```
[Actionable advice opener -- 1-2 sentences]

[Personal story that illustrates the advice -- 3-7 sentences]

[Actionable takeaway derived from the story -- 1-3 sentences]
```

### Why It Works

- Story alone: "That's great but what does it have to do with me?"
- Advice alone: "Why should I trust you over everyone else?"
- Golden Intersection: Reader learns AND is entertained simultaneously
- Creates trust (story proves experience) AND delivers value (advice is actionable)

## 5 SHORT-FORM CONTENT FORMATS (Cole, E05)

### 1. Paragraph Style
Concise insight or opinion. 3-5 sentences. One clear point.

### 2. Listicle
"X things you don't need to [achieve Y]" -- numbered items, easy to scan.

### 3. Old vs. New
Contrast outdated approach with modern approach. Two columns or before/after.

### 4. Framework/Steps
Tactical how-to. Numbered steps with brief explanation per step.

### 5. One-liner/Punchline
Compressed wisdom in a single powerful sentence. Hardest to write well.

## VIDEO FLOW FORMULA (JT Barnett, E06)

Every successful short-form video follows this arc:
1. **Hook** -- pull attention in first 3 seconds
2. **Conflict** -- present a problem or tension
3. **Resolution** -- solve the problem / deliver value
4. **Call to Action** -- subtle push to follow/click/engage

## WRITING PROCESS (Frabrand, E01)

### Two-Step Method

1. **Write everything** from mind to paper (first draft -- NO editing)
2. **Edit 12-24 hours later** with fresh eyes

### Editing Checklist

- Cut all unnecessary words (rate of revelation)
- Check hook strength (is it the strongest version?)
- Verify each section adds NEW information
- Read aloud for flow and rhythm
- Check CTA placement and strength
- Verify formatting for platform (line breaks, character limits)
