# YouTube Tactics Reference

## 50+ Ideas System (10 Sources)

Research across 10 sources for unlimited content ideas:

1. **"Master That" List** -- 10 things you know you're good at (expertise, trends, tips)
2. **FAQs** -- 10 most frequently asked questions from clients/audience
3. **AnswerThePublic.com** -- All questions/topics around your keyword (limited to 2 free searches/day)
4. **YouTube Search Bar + Alphabet Trick** -- Type keyword + go through A-Z for auto-complete suggestions
5. **Relevant Channels** -- Filter YouTube search by "Channel" to find competitors
6. **Top 10 Most Popular Videos** on relevant channels (sort by Most Popular)
7. **Gateway Videos** -- Recent videos with abnormally high views relative to channel size
8. **Six Degrees of Separation** -- Recurring themes/sub-topics across competitor channels
9. **Amazon Book Reviews** -- Read table of contents + reviews (especially NEGATIVE reviews -- they show what's MISSING)
10. **Blogs, Podcasts, Courses** -- Google "top blogs/podcasts for [topic]" + browse Udemy/Skillshare curricula

Store all research in a master spreadsheet with tabs: You, Close Relationships, Niche, Audience, Content Plan, Viewer Avatar.

## Ideal Viewer Avatar (Complete)

Define your viewer with:
- Gender, age, income, education, geographic location, time zone
- Profession, interests/hobbies, dislikes/villains, gurus/experts
- Books, blogs, podcasts they consume
- Goals and challenges
- Values (map to Maslow's hierarchy)
- Transformation statement: "I want to go from X to Y so that I can Z"
- Name the avatar (e.g., "Sally the Single Entrepreneur")

### Audience Validation Methods
1. **Content Survey** -- Google Forms/Typeform with specific questions
2. **Zoom Call Interviews** -- 15-min deep-dive with ideal viewers
3. **Gold Question:** "If you were to search for help with this topic on Google or YouTube, what would you search?"
4. **Build subscriber list** -- Collect emails before launch

## SEO Deep-Dive

### Description Fundamentals
- First 3 lines are CRITICAL (factor into CTR)
- Must sound conversational, human (NO keyword stuffing -- YouTube penalizes)
- 500-1,000 characters recommended (5,000 max)
- Don't give away the outcome in description
- Write as recap/teaser of what's covered
- Include affiliate disclaimer if using affiliate links

### Tags Strategy
- 500 characters available
- ONLY relevancy matters (no search volume parameters)
- Against YouTube TOS to include irrelevant tags
- Order doesn't matter
- Channel name should ALWAYS be a tag

### Finding Tags (4 Methods)
1. Use your TITLE as the first tag
2. YouTube Search Bar + Alphabet Trick
3. Look at highest-ranking videos for your topic (via TubeBuddy)
4. TubeBuddy Chrome extension for research

### Tags-First Process
- Find tags first, THEN write description using those tags
- Set up Upload Defaults (Settings > Upload Defaults) to template your description

### Video Hashtags
- Go at very bottom of description
- Show as blue clickable links above title
- Use top 3 most relevant keywords + channel name
- Max 3 hashtags

## Thumbnail Strategy (Detailed)

### CTR Measures 4 Factors
1. Thumbnail (most impactful)
2. Title
3. First line of description
4. Relevancy to ideal viewers

### Thumbnail Checklist
- Fill the frame with your subject (tiny on mobile)
- Large, readable text (minimum words, maximum impact)
- Contrasting colors so text stands out
- Shallow depth of field to make subject pop
- Create emotional response (curiosity, shock, excitement)
- Test different styles for YOUR audience

### Title-Thumbnail Rule
- Title and thumbnail should NOT repeat same words
- They should COMPLEMENT each other
- Thumbnail shows emotion/curiosity, title provides context

### A/B Testing
- If video has bad CTR vs. channel average, create new thumbnail
- Changing thumbnail is LOW RISK (doesn't reset metadata)
- Changing title is HIGH RISK (resets metadata, can ruin momentum)
- Good CTR benchmark: ~5%

## H.O.T. Script Formula (Sunny Lenarduzzi)

- **H -- Hook:** Opening that grabs attention and keeps viewers watching
- **O -- Outcome:** What viewers will get/learn by watching
- **T -- Testimonial/Trust:** Proof that the content delivers (social proof, credentials, results)

Apply to every video opening. Then transition into the content body.

## Batch Recording System

### Monthly Schedule
- Block same 2 days every month for filming
- **Day 1:** B-roll (screencasts, visuals)
- **Day 2:** A-roll (on-camera)
- Shoot 4-8 videos over those 2 days
- Only change your TOP between videos (that's all viewers see)

### File Organization (Google Drive)
Master folder: YouTube-[Your Name]
- Subfolder by year
- Subfolder per video: SEO Brief, Graphics, Newsletter, Script, Teasers, Transcription, Raw files, Final video

### Triple Backup
1. Google Drive (in video folder)
2. External hard drive at home
3. Off-site backup (monthly)

## Upload Process Checklist

1. Upload as UNLISTED or SCHEDULED (never public until fully optimized)
2. Copy/paste SEO brief into YouTube fields (title, description, tags, hashtags)
3. Upload custom thumbnail
4. Set monetization settings
5. Add end screens and cards
6. Schedule publish time

### [CONTRARIAN] Do NOT Use YouTube Premieres
- Counterproductive to velocity and views
- Notifies audience but not everyone interested in THAT topic
- Results in initial click but POOR retention
- Hurts long-term video performance

## Monetization Settings

### Requirements
- 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 hours watch time

### Revenue Optimization
- Videos 10+ minutes: Enable mid-roll ads AND post-roll ads
- Turning on mid-roll + post-roll can TRIPLE AdSense revenue
- Skippable video ads are least intrusive (always leave on)
- CPM varies by: geographic location (US pays most) and niche (cleaner = higher CPM)

### Revenue Streams (Priority Order)
1. Own products and services -- PRIMARY
2. AdSense -- bonus, not main
3. Affiliate income
4. Speaking engagements
5. Brand sponsors
6. Merch

### AdSense Reality Check
- ~$1 per 1,000 views (Parker Walbeck experience)
- Unless getting millions of views, AdSense isn't significant
- YouTube is a lead generation machine, not ad revenue machine

## Keyword Ownership Strategy (Parker Walbeck)

### How It Works
- Being first result for a search term = continuous organic traffic
- Parker owns: "how to shoot a commercial", "how to shoot real estate videos", "how to film a wedding"
- Gets wedding inquiries weekly purely from YouTube keyword ownership (no paid ads)

### Process
1. Research keywords with TubeBuddy (search volume + competition)
2. Create definitive content for that keyword
3. Optimize title (keyword FIRST in title)
4. Optimize description and tags
5. Monitor ranking and iterate

### Title Tip
- Changed "how to film a wedding" to "how to shoot a wedding" after discovering higher search volume
- Small wording changes can dramatically change search positioning

## Internal vs. External Traffic

- **Internal (weighted MORE by algorithm):** Search, Browse, Suggested
- **External (creates momentum, weighted less):** Social media, email, embeds

### Playlist Strategy
- Playlists help algorithm categorize related content
- Increases session watch time (videos auto-play in sequence)
- Creates advantage in browse and suggested traffic

## 3 Keys to Viral Content

1. **Relatability** -- subject relates to LARGEST number of people possible
2. **Relevancy** -- timing; release when topics are trending
3. **Shareability** -- "wow factor" or shock that makes people share

When all 3 present: chances of virality SIGNIFICANTLY increase. Even 2 out of 3 can hit 1M+ views.

## CEO vs. Creator Channel Model (Detailed)

### Type 1 -- The CEO
- System: Research > Scripting > Filming > Editing > Thumbnail/Titles > Distribution > Short-form clips
- Outsource everything except key creative decisions
- Some making millions with very little personal involvement
- Built for scale

### Type 2 -- The Creator
- Does everything or most things personally
- Can still outsource specific weaknesses
- Built for enjoyment and authenticity
- [CONTRARIAN] Better to make $50K/month enjoying it than $500K/month hating it

### 7-Figure Content Funnel Steps
1. Business (what you sell, who, what outcome)
2. Content Pillars
3. Format (tutorials, behind-scenes, challenges, podcasts, breakdowns)
4. Content (titles, thumbnails, tests)
5. Production (filming, lighting, audio, gear)
6. Editing (style + workflow)
7. Distribution (YouTube, short form, written)
8. Monetization (offers, funnels)

## 60-Day YouTube Launch Plan

### Phase 1: Research & Setup (Days 1-14)
- Complete niche formula (Skills + Profit - Passion)
- Build Ideal Viewer Avatar
- Generate 50+ ideas from 10 sources
- Set up channel (name, art, description, About)
- Set up Upload Defaults template

### Phase 2: First 8 Videos (Days 15-45)
- Create SEO briefs for first 8 videos
- Script using H.O.T. formula
- Batch record (2 filming days)
- Edit and optimize
- Publish 1 per week

### Phase 3: Analyze & Iterate (Days 45-60)
- Review analytics: CTR, watch time, traffic sources
- Identify which topics/formats perform best
- Double down on winners
- Adjust content plan for next month
- Set up playlist strategy

### Key Benchmarks for First 60 Days
- 8 videos published
- 100+ subscribers (proof for algorithm + social proof for others)
- Understand which content types YOUR audience responds to
- SEO brief process locked in
- Batch recording rhythm established

## Mindset Frameworks

### The Scientist Mindset
Test > Fail > Learn > Grow > Repeat. Each video is a PROTOTYPE.

### The Snowflake Effect
Each video, viewer, subscriber seems small but accumulates into an avalanche.

### 1,000 True Fans
A true fan buys ANYTHING you produce. 1,000 true fans = sustainable living. Viral success does NOT equate to revenue.

### [CONTRARIAN] Progress Metrics
- If you only measure success by views/subscribers, it will make you crazy
- First 100 subscribers are HARDER than first 1,000
- Care more about the VIEWER than the VIEWS
